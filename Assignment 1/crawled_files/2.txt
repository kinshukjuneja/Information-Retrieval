It is the only airport serving the Isles of Scilly, handling all air traffic to and from the Islands. href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s_Airport,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Earl_Amherst_at_St_Mary%27s_Airport_Opening.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Earl_Amherst_at_St_Mary%27s_Airport_Opening.png/220px-Earl_Amherst_at_St_Mary%27s_Airport_Opening.png" width="220" height="186" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Earl_Amherst_at_St_Mary%27s_Airport_Opening.png/330px-Earl_Amherst_at_St_Mary%27s_Airport_Opening.png 1.5x, data-file-height="1086" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Earl_Amherst_at_St_Mary%27s_Airport_Opening.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Western Morning News. Thursday 17 August 1939. <a href="/wiki/Jeffery_Amherst,_5th_Earl_Amherst" title="Jeffery Amherst, 5th Earl Amherst">Jeffery Amherst, 5th Earl Amherst</a>, general manager of the Great Western and Southern Air Lines Ltd., marking the opening of the new airport. Stideford (captain of the golf club), and Mr. A.C.V. Stephens (treasurer of the golf club).</div></div></div>\n<p>On 15 September 1937, <a href="/wiki/Olley_Air_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Olley Air Service">Olley Air Service</a>\'s subsidiary Channel Air Ferries started the first scheduled service between <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End_Airport" title="Land&#39;s End Airport">Land\'s End</a> and St Mary\'s, flying <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Dragon" title="De Havilland Dragon">de Havilland Dragons</a>. Initially, however, the planes landed on the St Mary\'s golf course.<sup id="cite_ref-cwlife_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwlife-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> St Mary\'s Airport was first opened in August 1939, after being converted from High Cross Farm.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1938 Great Western and Southern Airlines took over Olley Air Service and Channel Air Ferries. It continued the service throughout <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, during which it replaced the Dragons with <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Dragon_Rapide" title="De Havilland Dragon Rapide">de Havilland Dragon Rapides</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bea5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bea5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 1 February 1947 this operation was taken over by <a href="/wiki/British_European_Airways" title="British European Airways">British European Airways</a> (BEA).<sup id="cite_ref-bea5_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bea5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In August 1949 a <a href="/wiki/Control_tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Control tower">control tower</a> and a passenger waiting room were completed at St Mary\'s.<sup id="cite_ref-Wickstead_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wickstead-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg/220px-DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg/330px-DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg/440px-DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="670" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DH.89A_G-AHLL_BEA_St_Marys_08.59_edited-3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>British European Airways De Havilland Dragon Rapide at St Mary\'s airfield in 1958 before departure to Lands End airport.</div></div></div>\n<p>On 2 May 1964, BEA replaced its Dragon Rapides on the Land\'s End route with a single <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_S-61" title="Sikorsky S-61">Sikorsky S-61</a> helicopter, operated by <a href="/wiki/BEA_Helicopters" class="mw-redirect" title="BEA Helicopters">BEA Helicopters</a>. From 1 September 1964 the route was to the new <a href="/wiki/Penzance_Heliport" title="Penzance Heliport">Penzance Heliport</a>. BEA Helicopters later became <a href="/wiki/British_Airways_Helicopters" title="British Airways Helicopters">British Airways Helicopters</a>, and subsequently <a href="/wiki/British_International_Helicopters" title="British International Helicopters">British International Helicopters</a>. A second helicopter was eventually added to the service in the summers.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Mayflower Air Services started services to the airport in 1961; this operation was taken over by Scillonian Air Services in 1963, itself taken over by <a href="/wiki/British_Westpoint_Airlines" title="British Westpoint Airlines">British Westpoint Airlines</a> in 1964. Scillonia Airways operated services to the airport from 1966 to 1970. Westward Airways, not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Westward_Airways_(United_Kingdom)" title="Westward Airways (United Kingdom)">Westward Airways (Lands End)</a>, operated services from 1967 to 1970. <a href="/wiki/Brymon_Airways" title="Brymon Airways">Brymon Airways</a> operated services from June 1972 to March 1991,<sup id="cite_ref-Wickstead_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wickstead-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> initially with <a href="/wiki/Britten-Norman_Islander" class="mw-redirect" title="Britten-Norman Islander">Britten-Norman Islander</a> aircraft, and two years later, <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada_DHC-6_Twin_Otter" title="De Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter">de Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1975, a new terminal was opened by the then incumbent Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Harold_Wilson" title="Harold Wilson">Harold Wilson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1984 <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus" title="Isles of Scilly Skybus">Isles of Scilly Skybus</a> started flights to St Mary\'s from Land\'s End, initially freight and <a href="/wiki/Air_charter" title="Air charter">charter</a>. Scheduled services started on 1 April 1987,<sup id="cite_ref-Wickstead_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wickstead-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> flying Islanders and, later, Twin Otters.<sup id="cite_ref-cwlife_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwlife-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The current 600m asphalt runway, 15/33, was built in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-hist_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hist-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After being in operation for 48 years, the helicopter service between St Mary\'s and the mainland UK ceased operations in October 2012, leaving Skybus as the sole remaining air link for the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-mdg_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mdg-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A helicopter service is due to resume between Land\'s End Airport and St. Mary\'s on in May 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" title="Isles of Scilly Steamship Company">Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</a> and the Council of the Isles of Scilly submitted a joint bid for finance from the <a href="/wiki/European_Regional_Development_Fund" title="European Regional Development Fund">European Regional Development Fund</a> for improvements to the terminal, new lighting and navigational systems and runway resurfacing, together with runway resurfacing at Land\'s End Airport.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In May 2014 the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> gave its approval. The upgrades at St Mary\'s are expected to cost \xc2\xa36.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s_Airport,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg/220px-Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg/330px-Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg/440px-Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Taking_off_by_helicopter_from_St_Mary%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_-_geograph.org.uk_-_357408.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The airport building (before the recent redevelopment); <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a> is seen just beyond the airport.</div></div></div>\n<p>The terminal at the airport is open all year round whilst the airport is in operation. It has a buffet, toilets, as well as access to wheelchairs upon request. The airport is used as a landing area for some emergency services such as the HM Coastguard Search and Rescue Aircraft (based out of Newquay Airport) and the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Air_Ambulance" title="Cornwall Air Ambulance">Cornwall Air Ambulance</a>, as well as being the administrative base for the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Fire_and_Rescue_Service" title="Isles of Scilly Fire and Rescue Service">Isles of Scilly Fire and Rescue Service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A public footpath passes within a few metres of the southern end of the runway;<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> it is closed by warning lights and bells a few minutes before a take-off or landing is due.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
