href="http://www.cricinfo.com/ci/content/player/14752.html">Cricinfo</a>, <span class="nowrap">3 May 2010</span></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Sir <b>Frederick Thomas Arthur Hervey-Bathurst</b>, born <b>Frederick Hutchison Hervey</b> (6 June 1807 \xe2\x80\x93 29 October 1881) was a famous English <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricketer</a>. Hervey-Bathurst was a right-handed <a href="/wiki/Batsman_(cricket)" class="mw-redirect" title="Batsman (cricket)">batsman</a> who bowled right-RM <a href="/wiki/Roundarm_bowling" title="Roundarm bowling">roundarm fast</a>.\n</p><p>Hervey-Bathurst made his <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class</a> debut in 1831 for the Bs against an early <a href="/wiki/England_national_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="England national cricket team">England</a> side.\n</p><p>He made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Marylebone_Cricket_Club" title="Marylebone Cricket Club">Marylebone Cricket Club</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Town_Club" title="Cambridge Town Club">Cambridge Town Club</a>. Hervey-Bathurst would represent the MCC in 28 first-class matches up until 1855. In his 28 matches for the club, he scored 306 runs at a <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a> of 7.46 and with a high score of 34. With the ball he took 63 wickets at a <a href="/wiki/Bowling_average" title="Bowling average">bowling average</a> of 12.00, with best figures of in an innings of 6/?.\n</p><p>In 1842, he made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_county_cricket_teams" title="Hampshire county cricket teams">Hampshire</a> against the Marylebone Cricket Club. Hervey-Bathurst represented Hampshire in 12 first-class matches between 1842 and 1861. In his 12 matches, he scored 203 runs at an average of 9.22 and a high score of 46. With the ball he took 72 wickets at an average of 14.23, with a best return of 7 wickets in an innings, although his exact best figures are unknown.\n</p><p>Hervey-Bathurst was one of three local gentlemen, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Chamberlayne_(cricketer)" title="Thomas Chamberlayne (cricketer)">Thomas Chamberlayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barker-Mill_Baronets" class="mw-redirect" title="Barker-Mill Baronets">Sir John Barker-Mill</a>, who financed the development of the <a href="/wiki/Antelope_Ground" title="Antelope Ground">Antelope Ground</a> and installed the former <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_county_cricket_teams" title="Hampshire county cricket teams">Hampshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surrey_County_Cricket_Club" title="Surrey County Cricket Club">Surrey</a> cricketer <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Day_(cricketer)" title="Daniel Day (cricketer)">Daniel Day</a> in the Antelope Hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-Profile_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As well as representing the above major sides, he also represented the Gentlemen in 20 <a href="/wiki/Gentlemen_v_Players" title="Gentlemen v Players">Gentlemen v Players</a> fixtures, where he took 73 wickets at an average of 11.25, with best figures of 7/?. He also represented the <a href="/wiki/Gentlemen_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Gentlemen of England">Gentlemen of England</a> in 12 first-class matches, where he took 88 wickets at an average of 19.40, with a best return of 6 wickets in an innings. Hervey-Bathurst also represented A to K, England, the Gentlemen of Marylebone Cricket Club, the Gentlemen of the South, the <a href="/wiki/South_of_England_cricket_team" title="South of England cricket team">South of England</a> and the West of England.\n</p><p>In his overall first-class career he played 92 matches, scoring 817 runs at an average of 9.92, with a high score of 46. With the ball he took 349 wickets at an average of 13.02, with 32 <a href="/wiki/Five_wicket_haul" class="mw-redirect" title="Five wicket haul">five wicket hauls</a>, 8 ten wicket hauls in a match and a best return of 7 wickets in an innings, although his exact figures are unknown.\n</p><p>Hervey-Bathurst died at <a href="/wiki/Clarendon_Park,_Wiltshire" title="Clarendon Park, Wiltshire">Clarendon Park</a>, Wiltshire on 19 October 1881.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_Frederick_Hervey-Bathurst,_3rd_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Hervey-Bathurst was the start of a cricketing family. He was the father of <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Hervey-Bathurst,_4th_Baronet" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick Hervey-Bathurst, 4th Baronet">Frederick Hervey-Bathurst, 4th Baronet</a> who represented both the MCC and the Hampshire team as well as <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Hampshire County Cricket Club">Hampshire County Cricket Club</a>. His other son, <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Hervey-Bathurst" title="Lionel Hervey-Bathurst">Lionel Hervey-Bathurst</a> represented Hampshire in two first-class matches in 1875. His great-grandson <a href="/wiki/Hervey_Tudway" title="Hervey Tudway">Hervey Tudway</a> played one first-class match for <a href="/wiki/Somerset_County_Cricket_Club" title="Somerset County Cricket Club">Somerset</a> in 1910 and would go on to fight in the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a> where he was to be killed in action in 1914.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_Frederick_Hervey-Bathurst,_3rd_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Profile-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Profile_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://content.cricinfo.com/england/content/ground/57308.html">"Antelope Ground, Southampton, England"</a>. www.cricinfo.com<span class="reference-accessdate">.
