href="/wiki/HarperCollins" title="HarperCollins">HarperCollins Publishers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harper_Perennial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Harper Perennial has divisions located in New York, London, <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, and Sydney. The imprint is descended from the Perennial Library imprint founded by <a href="/wiki/Harper_(publisher)" title="Harper (publisher)">Harper &amp; Row</a> in 1964. In Fall of 2005, Harper Perennial rebranded with a new logo (an Olive) and a distinct editorial direction emphasizing fiction and non-fiction from new and young authors.  In the end matter, books often feature a brand-specific P.S. section that features extra material such as interviews, essays, etc.  Carrie Kania is the publisher.\n</p><p>Recent notable books include <i>I Am Not Myself These Days</i> by <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kilmer-Purcell" title="Josh Kilmer-Purcell">Josh Kilmer-Purcell</a>, <i>The Yacoubian Building</i> by <a href="/wiki/Alaa_Al_Aswany" title="Alaa Al Aswany">Alaa Al Aswany</a>, <i>This Will Be My Undoing</i> by Morgan Jerkins, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Paradox_of_Choice" title="The Paradox of Choice">The Paradox of Choice</a></i> by Barry Schwartz, <i>Lullabies for Little Criminals</i> by Heather O\'Neil, <i>Grab On to Me Tightly as If I Knew the Way</i> by Bryan Charles, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Yiddish_Policemen%27s_Union" title="The Yiddish Policemen&#39;s Union">The Yiddish Policemen\'s Union</a></i> by Michael Chabon.  In November, 2011, they released <i>The Shakespeare Guide to Italy: Retracing the Bard\'s Unknown Travels</i> by Richard Paul Roe, a detailed examination of the locales mentioned in ten plays by Shakespeare.\n</p><p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harper_Perennial_Modern_Classics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Harper Perennial Modern Classics (page does not exist)">Harper Perennial Modern Classics</a>, a direct offshoot of the imprint, publishes eminent authors such as <a href="/wiki/Peter_Singer" title="Peter Singer">Peter Singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harper_Lee" title="Harper Lee">Harper Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zora_Neale_Hurston" title="Zora Neale Hurston">Zora Neale Hurston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aldous_Huxley" title="Aldous Huxley">Aldous Huxley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russell_Banks" title="Russell Banks">Russell Banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pynchon" title="Thomas Pynchon">Thomas Pynchon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan_Kundera" title="Milan Kundera">Milan Kundera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Garcia_Marquez" class="mw-redirect" title="Gabriel Garcia Marquez">Gabriel Garcia Marquez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Plath" title="Sylvia Plath">Sylvia Plath</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Thornton_Wilder" title="Thornton Wilder">Thornton Wilder</a> among many others.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span
