id="Topography">Topography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Topography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Howardian Hills form 79 square miles of well-wooded undulating countryside between the flat agricultural <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Pickering" title="Vale of Pickering">Vales of Pickering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_York" title="Vale of York">York</a>. The irregular 558 feet (170&#160;m) high ridges of the Howardian Hills are a southern extension of the rocks of the <a href="/wiki/Hambleton_Hills" title="Hambleton Hills">Hambleton Hills</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_York_Moors" title="North York Moors">North York Moors</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a> limestone and a network of pasture, extensive woodland, trees and woodland combine to create a strong visual character in the higher ground overlooking the agricultural plains below. On the eastern edge, the <a href="/wiki/River_Derwent,_Yorkshire" title="River Derwent, Yorkshire">River Derwent</a> cuts through the Hills in the Kirkham Gorge, a deep winding valley formed as an overflow channel from glacial <a href="/wiki/Lake_Pickering" title="Lake Pickering">Lake Pickering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nateng_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nateng-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NMPReport_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NMPReport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlement">Settlement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Settlement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Although there are no towns within the AONB, the market towns of <a href="/wiki/Helmsley" title="Helmsley">Helmsley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malton,_North_Yorkshire" title="Malton, North Yorkshire">Malton</a> lie just beyond the boundary. From Malton to <a href="/wiki/Hovingham" title="Hovingham">Hovingham</a> is a line of <a href="/wiki/Spring_line_village" class="mw-redirect" title="Spring line village">spring line villages</a>. The majority of older buildings are of locally quarried <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> with red pantile roofs and those which developed as part of the grand country house estates have largely retained a coherent identity. The village of <a href="/wiki/Ampleforth" title="Ampleforth">Ampleforth</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Ampleforth_Abbey" title="Ampleforth Abbey">Abbey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ampleforth_College" title="Ampleforth College">College</a> lie within the area.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_use">Land use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Land use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg/220px-Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg/330px-Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg/440px-Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dry_Stone_Wall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>New stone walling conservation work in the Howardian Hills AONB</div></div></div>\n<p>High grade arable land, pasture and managed woodland makes this  rich farming country whose diversity contributes to its attractive rural character.<sup id="cite_ref-nateng_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nateng-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Howardian Hills AONB is a key area for several nationally important  <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity_Action_Plan" class="mw-redirect" title="Biodiversity Action Plan">Biodiversity Action Plan</a> (BAP) Priority habitats including lowland broadleaved woodland, wood pasture, veteran trees, limestone and neutral grasslands and fen meadows. Characteristic species include brown hare, lapwing, tree sparrow and barn owl as well as several local rarities such as knapweed broomrape and baneberry.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Howardian_Hills_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty">Howardian Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Howardian Hills Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a designated <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB); one of 46 areas in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> which, along with <a href="/wiki/National_Parks" class="mw-redirect" title="National Parks">National Parks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Coasts" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage Coasts">Heritage Coasts</a>, represent some of the most spectacular and unspoilt landscapes in the country.\n</p><p>The Howardian Hills are designated an AONB because of the following Special Qualities:\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>Unusual landform</b>: The Howardian Hills are the only area of Jurassic limestone in Northern England with AONB designation. Kirkham Gorge on the <a href="/wiki/River_Derwent,_Yorkshire" title="River Derwent, Yorkshire">River Derwent, Yorkshire</a> is a unique glacial overflow channel of great scientific importance.</li>\n<li><b>A richly varied landscape</b>: The landscape comprises a complex system of hills and valleys of woodland, arable fields, pasture, fens, hedgerows, stone walls, designed parklands and scattered settlements.</li>\n<li><b>Landscape of high visual quality</b>: Diverse landform and land use with contrasting  scale, colour, texture and form alongside historic houses, extensive woodland, sweeping views, farming landscapes and traditional building styles provide great aesthetic appeal.</li>\n<li><b>Remarkable heritage</b>: A concentration of archaeological and historic features including Iron Age earthworks, medieval castles and monasteries, grand houses and designed landscapes including <a href="/wiki/Castle_Howard" title="Castle Howard">Castle Howard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newburgh_Priory" title="Newburgh Priory">Newburgh Priory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hovingham_Hall" title="Hovingham Hall">Hovingham Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gilling_Castle" title="Gilling Castle">Gilling Castle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nunnington_Hall" title="Nunnington Hall">Nunnington Hall</a> contribute to the dramatic landscape.</li>\n<li><b>An important wildlife resource</b>: The area boasts outstanding wild plants and animals within the unique River Derwent and has nationally important fens as well as relatively extensive remnants of Ancient Semi-Natural Woodland.</li></ul>\n<p>Each of these attributes is important in its own right, but it is their combination in a relatively small area that has produced a landscape of national significance.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="What_the_Howardian_Hills_AONB_partnership_does">What the Howardian Hills AONB partnership does</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: What the Howardian Hills AONB partnership does">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a working landscape, the interests of agriculture, forestry, rural enterprise and the economic and social needs of communities in the AONB are important. The Joint Advisory Committee and professional staff in the AONB unit help to conserve and enhance the AONB landscape by:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Preparing and implementing the AONB Management Plan on behalf of the three constituent Local Authorities<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Encouraging and facilitating communities to work collaboratively.</li>\n<li>Providing advice, practical support and grant aid to farmers and landowners to protect and enhance wildlife habitat and landscape features.</li>\n<li>Working with the County Council\'s Rights of Way Team to improve access.</li>\n<li>Restoring historic field boundaries including hedgerows and stone walls.</li>\n<li>Implementing habitat restoration and management projects.</li>\n<li>Safeguarding the historic environment by renovating traditional features and buildings.</li>\n<li>Scutinising strategies and planning applications to avoid intrusive development.</li></ul>\n<p>Visit <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.howardianhills.org.uk">[3]</a> for further details.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howardian_Hills&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-nateng-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-nateng_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-nateng_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080725045041/http://www.countryside.gov.uk/LAR/Landscape/DL/aonbs/aonb_howardianhills.asp">"AONB Howardian Hills"</a>.
