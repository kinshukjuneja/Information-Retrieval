class="external text" href="http://www.dominicansummerleague.com/">Official website</a> <span class="languageicon">(in Spanish)</span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Dominican Summer League</b> is a branch of affiliated <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> which is played in the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. The league was founded in 1985. After the demise of the <a href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Summer_League" title="Venezuelan Summer League">Venezuelan Summer League</a> after the 2015 season, it is the only Latin American based rookie league.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The 2018 72-game season starts June 2 and ends August 25 with the playoffs starting on August 27.\n</p><p>The league will be played with forty-four teams in six divisions. With the exception of the cooperative team formed by the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Brewers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Indians</a>, each team is affiliated with a different major league franchise with thirteen organizations fielding two teams: the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">Cardinals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Cubs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Diamondbacks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Dodgers</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">Mets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Phillies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pirates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Rangers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Rays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Red Sox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Rockies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Royals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Tigers</a>.\n</p><p>The six division winners advance to the playoffs. The two division champions with the best winning percentages receive byes to the semifinals.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox"
