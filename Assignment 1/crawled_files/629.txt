Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Oona Tamsyn King, Baroness King of Bow</b> (born 22 October 1967)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Chief_diversity_officer" title="Chief diversity officer">chief diversity officer</a> of <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>.\n</p><p>She was a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green_and_Bow_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bethnal Green and Bow (UK Parliament constituency)">Bethnal Green and Bow</a>, serving from <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_1997" title="United Kingdom general election, 1997">1997</a> until <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_2005" title="United Kingdom general election, 2005">2005</a>. That year she was defeated by <a href="/wiki/George_Galloway" title="George Galloway">George Galloway</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Respect_Party" title="Respect Party">Respect Party</a> candidate.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Oona King was born in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Preston_King_(academic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Preston King (academic)">Preston King</a>, an <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African-American</a> academic, and his British Jewish wife, Murreil Hazel (n\xc3\xa9e Stern), a social justice activist.  She is the niece of the medical doctor <a href="/wiki/Miriam_Stoppard" title="Miriam Stoppard">Miriam Stoppard</a> (her maternal aunt);<sup id="cite_ref-Emma_Brockes_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Emma_Brockes-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> her cousin is the actor <a href="/wiki/Ed_Stoppard" title="Ed Stoppard">Ed Stoppard</a>. On her father\'s side, she comes from a line of American civil rights activists and successful entrepreneurs.  Her paternal grandfather, <a href="/wiki/Civil_rights_activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil rights activist">civil rights activist</a> <a href="/wiki/Clennon_Washington_King,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Clennon Washington King, Sr.">Clennon Washington King, Sr.</a>, and his wife had a daughter and seven sons, including her uncle <a href="/wiki/Chevene_Bowers_King" title="Chevene Bowers King">C.B. King</a>, a pioneering civil rights attorney in <a href="/wiki/Albany,_Georgia" title="Albany, Georgia">Albany, Georgia</a>.\n</p><p>King was educated at <a href="/wiki/Haverstock_School_Business_%26_Enterprise_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Haverstock School Business &amp; Enterprise College">Haverstock Comprehensive Secondary School</a> on Crogsland Road in <a href="/wiki/Chalk_Farm" title="Chalk Farm">Chalk Farm</a> (borough of Camden), London. She was a contemporary of fellow Labour politicians <a href="/wiki/David_Miliband" title="David Miliband">David Miliband</a> and his younger brother <a href="/wiki/Ed_Miliband" title="Ed Miliband">Ed</a>. It was at Haverstock that she first showed an interest in politics, telling her careers teacher she wanted to become <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister</a> and was promptly told to become a librarian instead.<sup id="cite_ref-Indy1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>At the beginning of her period as an undergraduate at <a href="/wiki/University_of_York" title="University of York">University of York</a>, King was briefly a member of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Workers_Party_(UK)" title="Socialist Workers Party (UK)">Socialist Workers Party</a><sup id="cite_ref-King34_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-King34-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During her second year (1988\xe2\x80\x9389), she gained a scholarship to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a> and graduated with a <a href="/wiki/First_class_honours" class="mw-redirect" title="First class honours">first class honours</a> Politics degree in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-King34_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-King34-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Before becoming a member of parliament, King was a researcher for the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She also worked as a political assistant to <a href="/wiki/Glyn_Ford" title="Glyn Ford">Glyn Ford</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEP</a>, the Labour Party Leader in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>, and later <a href="/wiki/Glenys_Kinnock" class="mw-redirect" title="Glenys Kinnock">Glenys Kinnock</a> MEP.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1995\xe2\x80\x9397, she was a political organiser for the <a href="/wiki/GMB_(trade_union)" title="GMB (trade union)">GMB</a> Southern Region.<sup id="cite_ref-CBB_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBB-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>She was selected to represent the seat of Bethnal Green and Bow early in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-CBB_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBB-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Shore" title="Peter Shore">Peter Shore</a> had announced his retirement early, but faction fighting in the <a href="/wiki/Constituency_Labour_Party" title="Constituency Labour Party">constituency Labour Party</a> led to party headquarters delaying the selection and imposing its own shortlist.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Some leading competitors from the local <a href="/wiki/British_Bangladeshi" title="British Bangladeshi">Bangladeshi</a> community were not included.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliamentary_career">Parliamentary career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Parliamentary career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Winning the seat in 1997, King became the second black woman to be elected as a member of parliament, the first being <a href="/wiki/Diane_Abbott" title="Diane Abbott">Diane Abbott</a>. In her "truly first-class maiden speech",<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> King described the racial abuse she and her family had suffered as a child. She referred to herself as "multi-ethnic", representing "a truly multicultural constituency where hardship and deprivation gave birth to Britain\'s greatest social reforms." She described <a href="/wiki/William_Beveridge" title="William Beveridge">William Beveridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clement_Attlee" title="Clement Attlee">Clement Attlee</a>, as "surrounded by an East End infant mortality rate of 55%" and said this led to social reforms, including the <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">NHS</a>. She emphasised a need for coherence in the strategy for eradicating poverty, and the importance of education in its elimination.<sup id="cite_ref-maiden_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maiden-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>King was a passionate advocate of international aid and human rights.  She served on the international development select committee, and as the Vice-Chair of the All-Parliamentary Group on Bangladesh.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She was selected to second the Queen\'s Speech debate in November 2002, where she also discussed her views on genocide and a visit to Rwanda.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> King served as the Parliamentary Private Secretary to the Secretary of State for Trade and Industry and the Minister for e-commerce.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2003 she was selected as one of "100 Great Black Britons".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>King supported the <a href="/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 invasion of Iraq">2003 invasion of Iraq</a>, which was controversial for her constituency\'s large Muslim population. Her support for the controversial invasion was used against her in the electoral campaign of <a href="/wiki/Respect_Party" title="Respect Party">Respect</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/George_Galloway" title="George Galloway">George Galloway</a>, who defeated her at the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_2005" title="United Kingdom general election, 2005">2005 general election</a>. The campaign for the seat contained strong comments from both candidates.<sup id="cite_ref-street_fight_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-street_fight-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>King changed her views, after seeing how poorly the United States had handled the crisis of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a> and its aftermath in New Orleans:\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>it shows that America has no grasp whatever on the activity needed to rebuild a destroyed city. And if they can\'t do that in their own country, then it\'s obvious why they can\'t do it in Iraq. So ... I regret that we went to war with a country that has shown itself to be incapable of the very basic actions required to deal with post-conflict reconstruction.<sup id="cite_ref-Emma_Brockes_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Emma_Brockes-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<p>King has said that she does not regret voting for the war in Iraq, "I could never have voted against getting rid of Saddam Hussein. He was responsible for the deaths of one million people."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_general_election">2005 general election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2005 general election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Bethnal Green and Bow, with a population of approximately 45,000 Muslim residents, was George Galloway\'s best chance to defeat a Labour candidate in what became a "bitter single issue campaign."<sup id="cite_ref-street_fight_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-street_fight-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> King described the contest as "one of the dirtiest ..we have ever seen in British politics" and complained of "quite disturbing" anti-semitic and racial abuse.<sup id="cite_ref-beeb_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beeb-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> She was the putative target of vegetable and egg throwers during a memorial service commemorating the Second World War German bombing of a block of flats that had predominantly Jewish victims.<sup id="cite_ref-Statesman_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Statesman-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> King claimed it was a deliberate part of Respect\'s campaign,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> but Galloway\'s representative Ron McKay denied any accusation of racial abuse.<sup id="cite_ref-beeb_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beeb-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Both candidates were given police protection, King after her car tyres were slashed and Galloway after receiving a death threat.<sup id="cite_ref-street_fight_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-street_fight-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> King lost the seat by 823 votes, a 26.2% swing from King to Galloway.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> King said that whilst the war in Iraq had been a major issue, false claims in the Bangladeshi press that she wanted to get rid of <a href="/wiki/Halal" title="Halal">halal</a> meat had played a part in her defeat.<sup id="cite_ref-beeb_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-beeb-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2005.E2.80.932009"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005\xe2\x80\x932009">2005\xe2\x80\x932009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2005\xe2\x80\x932009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>King had said that she would remain in Bethnal Green and Bow with her constituency office funded from the <a href="/wiki/GMB_(trade_union)" title="GMB (trade union)">GMB</a> trade union, attempting to act as an unofficial MP. However, later in 2005, she began a career in the media, saying "I wanted to be an MP all my life, and when it didn\'t work, I thought, well then, I\'ll just have to go down a different path."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007, King published her autobiography, <i>The Oona King Diaries: House Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\nIn 2008, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> appointed her to act as his Senior Policy Adviser on Equalities and Diversity and Faith.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2009, King was appointed head of diversity at <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> She continues to live in <a href="/wiki/Mile_End" title="Mile End">Mile End</a>, in a converted pub, in the East End of London.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_London_mayoral_campaign">2010 London mayoral campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010 London mayoral campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2010, King unsuccessfully challenged <a href="/wiki/Ken_Livingstone" title="Ken Livingstone">Ken Livingstone</a> for the Labour Party nomination in the <a href="/wiki/London_mayoral_election,_2012" title="London mayoral election, 2012">2012 election</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_London" title="Mayor of London">Mayor of London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> King\'s first campaign speech, at Haverstock school, focused on "engagement with young people" as a way of reducing <a href="/wiki/Knife_legislation" title="Knife legislation">knife crime</a> and helping them achieve their potential. In June 2010, she was shortlisted for the nomination. In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i>, King emphasised both her experience of "pushing and pulling the levers of power", i.e. her experience of negotiating with top ministers, and also her willingness to work with political opponents.<sup id="cite_ref-Indy1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Her opponent, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Livingstone" title="Ken Livingstone">Ken Livingstone</a>, accused her of using inappropriate methods of obtaining email addresses of Labour Party supporters; King denied the allegation.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> King had the backing of <a href="/wiki/Neil_Kinnock" title="Neil Kinnock">Neil Kinnock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Bradshaw" title="Ben Bradshaw">Ben Bradshaw</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Johnson" title="Alan Johnson">Alan Johnson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Indy1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On 24 September 2010, Livingstone won the nomination.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peerage">Peerage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Peerage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 26 January 2011, King was created a <a href="/wiki/Life_peer" title="Life peer">life peer</a> as <b>Baroness King of Bow</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Bow,_London" title="Bow, London">Bow</a> in the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">London Borough of Tower Hamlets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> She was <a href="/wiki/Introduction_(House_of_Lords)" title="Introduction (House of Lords)">introduced</a> in the House of Lords on 31 January 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> where she sits on the Labour benches. When her appointment was announced in November 2010, she resigned as a constituency representative to the Labour <a href="/wiki/National_Executive_Committee" title="National Executive Committee">National Executive Committee</a>, to which she had recently been elected, before attending her first meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Upon taking her seat in the Lords, King stood down from her Diversity Officer role with Channel 4.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2012, King was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Progress_(organisation)" title="Progress (organisation)">Progress</a> strategy board as a parliamentarian. In 2016, she took a leave of absence from the Lords to take a role as YouTube Diversity Director.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_work">Media work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Media work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>King has made appearances on television shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/This_Week_(BBC_One_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="This Week (BBC One TV series)">This Week</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Politics" class="mw-redirect" title="The Daily Politics">The Daily Politics</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_All_Star_Talent_Show" title="The All Star Talent Show">The All Star Talent Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Have_I_Got_News_for_You" title="Have I Got News for You">Have I Got News for You</a></i>. She hosted a <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> documentary on <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King_Jr" class="mw-redirect" title="Martin Luther King Jr">Martin Luther King</a> and the deep South entitled <i>American Prophet</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> aired on 29 March 2008. She made appearances on the new comedic show <i>Jews at Ten</i> on Channel 4, 9 October 2012. In January 2013, she appeared on the skating show <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_on_Ice_(series_8)" title="Dancing on Ice (series 8)">Dancing On Ice</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> being voted off 20 January.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oona_King&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1994, King married <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> Tiberio Santomarco,<sup id="cite_ref-DontLook_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DontLook-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> while working for an MEP in Brussels. The couple have adopted three children, and have a fourth child born to a surrogate mother in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> She speaks <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> fluently.<sup id="cite_ref-DontLook_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DontLook-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
