Yount</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\xcb\x88</span><span title="/j/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;yes&#39;">j</span><span title="/a\xca\x8a/: &#39;ou&#39; in &#39;mouth&#39;">a\xca\x8a</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span></span>/</a></span></span>; nicknamed,<b>"The Kid"</b>, and <b>"Rockin\' Robin</b>", born September 16, 1955) is an American former professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> player. He spent his entire 20-year career in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Shortstop" title="Shortstop">shortstop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Center_fielder" title="Center fielder">center fielder</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a> (1974\xe2\x80\x9393).\n</p><p>After growing up in California, Yount spent a couple of months in minor league baseball and advanced to the major leagues at the age of 18. He won two <a href="/wiki/American_League_Most_Valuable_Player" class="mw-redirect" title="American League Most Valuable Player">American League Most Valuable Player</a> awards. In his best season, 1982, the Brewers made a <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> appearance. In <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_balloting,_1999" title="Baseball Hall of Fame balloting, 1999">1999</a>, Yount was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Baseball Hall of Fame</a> in his first year of eligibility. Since his retirement as a player, Yount has held several roles as a baseball coach.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Yount was born in <a href="/wiki/Danville,_Illinois" title="Danville, Illinois">Danville, Illinois</a>. He lived briefly in <a href="/wiki/Covington,_Indiana" title="Covington, Indiana">Covington, Indiana</a>, but his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">southern California</a> when he was an infant; his father had gotten a job testing rocket engines with <a href="/wiki/Rocketdyne" title="Rocketdyne">Rocketdyne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sondheimer_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sondheimer-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Robin attended <a href="/wiki/William_Howard_Taft_High_School_(Los_Angeles,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="William Howard Taft High School (Los Angeles, California)">William Howard Taft High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Woodland_Hills,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Woodland Hills, California">Woodland Hills</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Yount was the third pick overall in the June <a href="/wiki/1973_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="1973 Major League Baseball draft">1973 Major League Baseball draft</a>, one slot ahead of fellow Hall of Famer and 3,000 Hit Club member <a href="/wiki/Dave_Winfield" title="Dave Winfield">Dave Winfield</a>.  Yount made his major league debut the following April, at eighteen years old.  After going hitless in his first four games, Yount hit a game-winning home run in his sixth. Yount is currently the last 18-year-old to hit a home run in the Major Leagues (<a href="/wiki/Andruw_Jones" title="Andruw Jones">Andruw Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Harper" title="Bryce Harper">Bryce Harper</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Soto" title="Juan Soto">Juan Soto</a> are the most recent teenagers to have hit Major League home runs, but did so as 19-year-olds).  On September 14, 1975 (two days before his 20th birthday), Yount broke <a href="/wiki/Mel_Ott" title="Mel Ott">Mel Ott</a>\'s 47-year-old record for most games played in the major leagues before turning 20.\n</p><p>Yount courted controversy in the winter of 1978. He threatened to retire from the game and take up professional golf rather than be underpaid or moved to the outfield by the Brewers. Early in the season, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Molitor" title="Paul Molitor">Paul Molitor</a> was called up from the Brewers Class A affiliate to the major league team because of Yount\'s absence. Yount\'s demands were met; when he returned to the team, Molitor was moved from shortstop to second base to make room for Yount.<sup id="cite_ref-Okrent_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Okrent-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was an early proponent of weight training \xe2\x80\x93 then uncommon in baseball \xe2\x80\x93 and by 1980 Yount\'s power hitting had improved, particularly for a shortstop. Yount was an <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star</a> in 1980, 1982, and 1983. No other Brewer was voted a starter in consecutive years until <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Braun" title="Ryan Braun">Ryan Braun</a> started each year between 2008 and 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1982_season">1982 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1982 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Yount led the American League with 210 hits in 1982. The 1982 AL East race was tied on the final day of the season, with the race coming down to a winner-take-all game between the Brewers and the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>. With the title on the line, Yount hit home runs in each of his first two at-bats against Orioles starter <a href="/wiki/Jim_Palmer" title="Jim Palmer">Jim Palmer</a>. Yount finished with a four-hit game, as the Brewers won 10-2. In addition to his only 200-hit season, he registered career highs with 29 home runs, 114 RBI, and a .331 batting average (.001 behind the league leader, <a href="/wiki/Willie_Wilson_(baseball)" title="Willie Wilson (baseball)">Willie Wilson</a>). Yount finished with a .578 slugging percentage and .957 OPS on his way to gaining 367 total bases \xe2\x80\x93 leading the major leagues in all three categories.<sup id="cite_ref-Robin_Yount_Statistics_and_History_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Robin_Yount_Statistics_and_History-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His slugging percentage was the second highest ever by a shortstop, and his 129 runs set the record for that position.<sup id="cite_ref-Yount_Wins_AL_MVP_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yount_Wins_AL_MVP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>That year, Yount also won his only <a href="/wiki/Gold_Glove_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Gold Glove Award">Gold Glove Award</a> and his first <a href="/wiki/MLB_Most_Valuable_Player_award" class="mw-redirect" title="MLB Most Valuable Player award">Most Valuable Player Award</a>. His performance garnered 27 of 28 possible first place votes in the 1982 MVP balloting.<sup id="cite_ref-1982_awards_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1982_awards-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The year ended with the Brewers making their only <a href="/wiki/1982_World_Series" title="1982 World Series">World Series</a> appearance.  Although Yount became the only player in history to have two 4-hit games in one World Series, Milwaukee lost to the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> in seven games. Yount batted .414 in the Series, with one home run and 6 RBI\'s.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1985, a shoulder problem forced Yount to move to the outfield.<sup id="cite_ref-library_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-library-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> After splitting time between center field and left field, Yount became the Brewers\' regular center fielder in 1986. He played more than 1,200 games in the outfield in his career, with a .990 fielding percentage. He made a game-ending, diving catch to preserve a no-hitter by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Nieves" title="Juan Nieves">Juan Nieves</a> early in the 1987 season.\n</p><p>Yount narrowly won a second MVP Award in 1989, making him only the third player to win MVPs at two positions, joining <a href="/wiki/Hank_Greenberg" title="Hank Greenberg">Hank Greenberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stan_Musial" title="Stan Musial">Stan Musial</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alex_Rodriguez" title="Alex Rodriguez">Alex Rodriguez</a> would later join this group).<sup id="cite_ref-library_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-library-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Yount was the first AL player to win multiple MVP\'s in over twenty-five years, since the Yankees\' <a href="/wiki/Roger_Maris" title="Roger Maris">Roger Maris</a> (1960 &amp; 1961) and <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mantle" title="Mickey Mantle">Mickey Mantle</a> (1956, 1957, and 1962). Yount collected more hits (1731) in the decade of the 1980s than any other player.<sup id="cite_ref-urlBaseball_Hall_of_Fame_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlBaseball_Hall_of_Fame-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<table style="width: 100px; font-size: 90%; float:right; border: 2px solid #aaa; margin: 0em 1em 0em 0em;">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;"><a href="/wiki/File:Milret19.PNG" class="image"><img alt="Milret19.PNG" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Milret19.PNG/95px-Milret19.PNG" width="95" height="64" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Milret19.PNG/143px-Milret19.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Milret19.PNG/190px-Milret19.PNG 2x" data-file-width="414" data-file-height="280" /></a>\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Robin Yount\'s number 19 was <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_retired_numbers" title="List of Major League Baseball retired numbers">retired</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers#Retired_numbers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a> in 1994.\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>After the 1989 season, Yount was a free agent and he spoke with several teams about contract offers. The <a href="/wiki/California_Angels" class="mw-redirect" title="California Angels">California Angels</a> were prepared to make a serious offer,<sup id="cite_ref-Penner_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penner-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> but Yount signed a three-year contract with the Brewers worth $9.6 million in February 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-Contract_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Contract-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 1991, Yount was briefly on the <a href="/wiki/Disabled_list" title="Disabled list">disabled list</a> (DL) with a kidney stone, only the second stint on the DL in his career; the first one was in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-DL_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DL-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 9, 1992, Yount collected his 3,000th career hit, becoming the 17th player (and the third youngest) to reach the mark.<sup id="cite_ref-HOF3000_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOF3000-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He announced his retirement after the 1993 season. The Brewers retired his number the next year.<sup id="cite_ref-MLB_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MLB-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Yount was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Athletic_Hall_of_Fame" title="Wisconsin Athletic Hall of Fame">Wisconsin Athletic Hall of Fame</a> in 1995. He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Baseball Hall of Fame</a> in 1999, his first year of eligibility. That same year, he was included in the balloting for the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Century_Team" title="Major League Baseball All-Century Team">Major League Baseball All-Century Team</a>, finishing fifth among shortstops.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Yount holds Brewers career records for games, at-bats, runs, hits, doubles, triples, RBIs, total bases, walks and strikeouts.  He was the last active major leaguer to have been a teammate of <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron" title="Hank Aaron">Hank Aaron</a> (1975\xe2\x80\x931976). He posted a career .285 batting average with 251 home runs, 1632 runs scored and 1406 runs batted in. His 11,008 career at-bats is the seventh-most in Major League Baseball history, and he ranks 17th on the all-time hit list. His three All-Star appearances are tied with <a href="/wiki/Ferguson_Jenkins" title="Ferguson Jenkins">Ferguson Jenkins</a> for the second-fewest of any Hall of Famer from the post-All-Star Game era, and he won a second MVP Award in 1989 without making the All-Star Team.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Yount served as first base coach and bench coach for the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> from 2002 to 2004. He resigned after the dismissal of Arizona manager <a href="/wiki/Bob_Brenly" title="Bob Brenly">Bob Brenly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-urlUSATODAY.com_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlUSATODAY.com-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He, <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron" title="Hank Aaron">Hank Aaron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warren_Spahn" title="Warren Spahn">Warren Spahn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Uecker" title="Bob Uecker">Bob Uecker</a> threw out the ceremonial first pitches at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2002 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">2002 Major League Baseball All-Star Game</a> at <a href="/wiki/Miller_Park_(Milwaukee)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miller Park (Milwaukee)">Miller Park</a>.\n</p><p>In 2005, Brewers manager <a href="/wiki/Ned_Yost" title="Ned Yost">Ned Yost</a> convinced <a href="/wiki/Dale_Sveum" title="Dale Sveum">Dale Sveum</a>, a teammate of Yount\'s, to become Milwaukee\'s new third base coach. Yount followed suit a few weeks later, accepting a post as the Brewers\' bench coach.<sup id="cite_ref-urlUSATODAY.com_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlUSATODAY.com-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In November 2006, Yount announced he would not return to the team as bench coach for the 2007 season.  However, on September 15, 2008 Sveum, by now the team\'s manager, chose Yount as his bench coach.<sup id="cite_ref-urlYount_reveling_in_another_postseason_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlYount_reveling_in_another_postseason-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2012, when Sveum was named the Chicago Cubs new manager, rumors quickly spread that Sveum would ask Yount to coach with him, even though the Brewers and Cubs had become bitter rivals. Sveum very quickly confirmed that he was not even considering such a move. As of 2014, Yount is a special instructor in spring training for the Brewers.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Yount&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg/170px-Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg/255px-Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg/340px-Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="3072" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Robin_Yount_-_Rubenstein_-_2008_All_Star_Game_Red_Carpet_Parade.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Yount in 2008</div></div></div>\n<p>Yount met his wife Michele at Taft High School and they have been married since 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-Hunt_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hunt-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Yount\'s brother <a href="/wiki/Larry_Yount" title="Larry Yount">Larry</a> was a pitcher and was briefly called up to play in the major leagues. While taking his warmup tosses for his debut as a <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> reliever in 1971, he experienced elbow pain.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He never threw an official pitch in any MLB game. Yount\'s son Dustin played baseball in the minor leagues for several years.<sup id="cite_ref-Brazzle_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brazzle-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Yount\'s nephew Austin Yount played professional baseball for the Dodgers organization. Another nephew, Cody Yount, played college baseball for <a href="/wiki/Pepperdine_University" title="Pepperdine University">Pepperdine University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Roots_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roots-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since retiring from baseball, Yount has increased his participation in two of his other passions, professional motorcycle and auto racing.<sup id="cite_ref-library_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-library-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In June 2008, Yount announced the creation of a new all-natural lemonade drink, <i>Robinade</i>. A portion of the proceeds of the sales goes to charity.<sup id="cite_ref-urlRobinade_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urlRobinade-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Yount sometimes goes hunting with Sveum. While hunting in Arizona in 2012, Yount accidentally shot Sveum with pellets in the back and ear. Sveum\'s injuries were minor.<sup id="cite_ref-TSN_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSN-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2012, Yount became a minority owner of the <a href="/wiki/Lakeshore_Chinooks" title="Lakeshore Chinooks">Lakeshore Chinooks</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Northwoods_League" title="Northwoods League">Northwoods League</a>, a collegiate summer baseball league. The Chinooks play at Kapco Park at <a href="/wiki/Concordia_University_Wisconsin" title="Concordia University Wisconsin">Concordia University Wisconsin</a> where the right field fence is 319 feet in his honor.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2014, Yount was honored with the "Lombardi Award of Excellence" from the Vince Lombardi Cancer Foundation. The award was created to honor <a href="/wiki/Vince_Lombardi" title="Vince Lombardi">Lombardi</a>\'s legacy, and is awarded annually to an individual who exemplifies the spirit of the acclaimed football coach.\n</p><p>On October 20th, 2018, Yount threw out the first pitch before Game 7 of the National League Championship Series between the Dodgers and the Brewers. The Dodgers won 5-1, subsequently winning the series.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
