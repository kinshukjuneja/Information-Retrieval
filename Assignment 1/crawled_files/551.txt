id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Second_Cameron_ministry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Premiership_of_David_Cameron" title="Premiership of David Cameron">Premiership of David Cameron</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum,_2016" title="United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016">United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016</a></div>\n<p>Cameron announced his choice for <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Foreign_and_Commonwealth_Affairs" title="Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs">Foreign Secretary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Defence" title="Secretary of State for Defence">Defence Secretary</a> on the afternoon of 8 May 2015, with <a href="/wiki/George_Osborne" title="George Osborne">George Osborne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theresa_May" title="Theresa May">Theresa May</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philip_Hammond" title="Philip Hammond">Philip Hammond</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Fallon" title="Michael Fallon">Michael Fallon</a> retaining their posts. Osborne was also given the <a href="/wiki/Title_of_honor" title="Title of honor">honorific</a> title of <a href="/wiki/First_Secretary_of_State" title="First Secretary of State">First Secretary of State</a>, which had been held by <a href="/wiki/William_Hague" title="William Hague">William Hague</a> in the preceding ministry.<sup id="cite_ref-PMO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMO-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Cameron\'s choices for other ministers were announced through the week.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eric_Pickles" title="Eric Pickles">Eric Pickles</a>, previously the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Communities and Local Government">Communities Secretary</a> left the Cabinet and received a knighthood, being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Greg_Clark" title="Greg Clark">Greg Clark</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Gove" title="Michael Gove">Michael Gove</a> moved to the position of <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Justice" title="Secretary of State for Justice">Justice Secretary</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Chris_Grayling" title="Chris Grayling">Chris Grayling</a>, who became the new <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_House_of_Commons" title="Leader of the House of Commons">Leader of the House of Commons</a>.\n</p><p>Several vacant Cabinet posts previously held by Liberal Democrats were subsequently filled by Conservative ministers \xe2\x80\x93 the positions of <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Business,_Innovation_and_Skills" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Business, Innovation and Skills">Business Secretary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Energy_and_Climate_Change" title="Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change">Energy Secretary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Scotland" title="Secretary of State for Scotland">Scottish Secretary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chief_Secretary_to_the_Treasury" title="Chief Secretary to the Treasury">Chief Secretary to the Treasury</a> were given to <a href="/wiki/Sajid_Javid" title="Sajid Javid">Sajid Javid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amber_Rudd" title="Amber Rudd">Amber Rudd</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Mundell" title="David Mundell">David Mundell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Hands" title="Greg Hands">Greg Hands</a> respectively, with <a href="/wiki/John_Whittingdale" title="John Whittingdale">John Whittingdale</a> replacing Javid as <a href="/wiki/Culture_Secretary" class="mw-redirect" title="Culture Secretary">Culture Secretary</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mark_Harper" title="Mark Harper">Mark Harper</a> replaced Gove as <a href="/wiki/Chief_Whip" title="Chief Whip">Chief Whip</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Hancock" class="mw-redirect" title="Matthew Hancock">Matthew Hancock</a> became the new <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_the_Cabinet_Office" title="Minister for the Cabinet Office">Minister for the Cabinet Office</a>, being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Anna_Soubry" title="Anna Soubry">Anna Soubry</a> as Minister of State for Small Business, Industry and Enterprise. Also <a href="/wiki/Priti_Patel" title="Priti Patel">Priti Patel</a> became the new Minister of State for Employment and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Halfon" title="Robert Halfon">Robert Halfon</a> became a
