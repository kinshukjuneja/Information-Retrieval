class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">Major</a> Arthur Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith was born on 28 January 1876 in Oxfordshire. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Algernon_Smith-Dorrien-Smith" title="Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith">Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith</a> and Edith Anna Maria Tower. He married Eleanor Salvin Bowlby (d. 13 Apr 1978), daughter of Edward Salvin Bowlby and Elizabeth Vans Agnew, on 11 May 1909 at <a href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity_Sloane_Street" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Trinity Sloane Street">Holy Trinity Church, Sloane Street, London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\nThere were four sons and three href="/wiki/Claud_Phillimore,_4th_Baron_Phillimore" title="Claud Phillimore, 4th Baron Phillimore">Claud Phillimore, 4th Baron Phillimore</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-Commander" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant-Commander">Lieutenant-Commander</a> Thomas Mervyn Dorrien-Smith <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">RN</a> (12 May 1913 \xe2\x80\x93 5 Dec 1973) married href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG/220px-Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG" width="220" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG/330px-Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG/440px-Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1819" data-file-height="1134" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Memorial_to_Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien_Smith.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Memorial to Arthur Algernon Dorrien Smith in <a href="/wiki/St_Nicholas%27s_Church,_Tresco" title="St Nicholas&#39;s Church, Tresco">St Nicholas\'s Church, Tresco</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Dorrien-Smith was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a>.\n</p><p>He was commissioned a <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Rifle_Brigade" class="mw-redirect" title="Rifle Brigade">Rifle Brigade</a> on 4 May 1898, and promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_(British_Army_and_Royal_Marines)" title="Lieutenant (British Army and Royal Marines)">lieutenant</a> on 3 February 1900. On the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a>, his battalion was sent to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, and he was mentioned in dispatches, and received the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">Distinguished Service Order</a> (DSO) for his services during the war. He was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(British_Army_and_Royal_Marines)" title="Captain (British Army and Royal Marines)">captain</a> on 22 January 1902.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Following the end of hostilities in June 1902, he left <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a> for England and returned to <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a> in early August.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 1904-5 he was an extra <a href="/wiki/Aide-de-camp" title="Aide-de-camp">Aide-de-camp</a> to <a href="/wiki/Henry_Northcote,_1st_Baron_Northcote" title="Henry Northcote, 1st Baron Northcote">Henry Northcote, 1st Baron Northcote</a>, <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Australia" title="Governor-General of Australia">Governor-General of Australia</a>, and retired in 1906, joining the Special Reserve. In the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a> he was Major, 6th Battalion (Reserve) Rifle Brigade, 1914; Brigade Major <a href="/wiki/17th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="17th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">17th Infantry Brigade</a>, 1914\xe2\x80\x9317; On the Staff 59th Division and 8th Corps, 1917-18.\n</p><p>On the death of his father in 1918, he succeeded as <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#Governors_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Lord Proprietor of the Isles of Scilly</a>, a position he held until 1920 when the lease for the majority of the Isles of Scilly was handed back to the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a>, Dorrien-Smith retaining control only over Tresco.\n</p><p>He continued the work of his father and developed <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">Tresco Abbey Gardens</a> making many trips to South Africa looking for suitable trees and plants. He went on the <a href="/wiki/1907_Sub-Antarctic_Islands_Scientific_Expedition" title="1907 Sub-Antarctic Islands Scientific Expedition">1907 Sub-Antarctic Islands Scientific Expedition</a>, which had as its primary object magnetic observation in the Auckland and Campbell Islands. Following the expedition he travelled widely in New Zealand, as well as making a shorter visit to Australia. In 1909 he again visited Australia, New Zealand and the Chatham Islands, and had a total collection of plants and seeds of about 2280 specimens. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Medal_of_Honour" title="Victoria Medal of Honour">Victoria Medal of Honour</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Horticultural_Society" title="Royal Horticultural Society">Royal Horticultural Society</a> in 1944.\n</p><p>He was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Peace">Justice of the Peace</a> for Cornwall, and on 31 January 1938, <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy Lieutenant">Deputy Lieutenant</a> for Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was a <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Linnean_Society_of_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Fellow of the Linnean Society of London">Fellow of the Linnean Society of London</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bibliography">Bibliography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bibliography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>The southern islands expedition.
