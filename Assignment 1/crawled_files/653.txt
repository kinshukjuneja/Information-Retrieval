<b>buffer stop</b>, <b>bumper</b>, <b>bumping post</b>, <b>bumper block</b> or <b>stopblock</b> (US), is a device to prevent <a href="/wiki/Railroad_car" title="Railroad car">railway vehicles</a> from going past the end of a physical section of <a href="/wiki/Track_(rail_transport)" title="Track (rail transport)">track</a>.\n</p><p>The design of the buffer stop is dependent, in part, on the kind of couplings that the railway uses, since the <a href="/wiki/Railway_coupling" title="Railway coupling">coupling gear</a> is the first part of the vehicle that the buffer stop touches. The term "buffer stop" is of British origin, since railways in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> principally use <a href="/wiki/Buffers_and_chain_coupler" title="Buffers and chain coupler">buffer-and-screw couplings</a> between vehicles.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" href="/w/index.php?title=Buffer_stop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Several different types of buffer stop have been developed. They differ depending on the type of coupler used and on the intended application.\n</p>\n<ul><li>Buffer stops with <a href="/wiki/Anticlimber" class="mw-redirect" title="Anticlimber">anticlimbers</a>. These are particularly important for passenger railway applications, because the anticlimbers prevent <a href="/wiki/Telescoping_(railway)" class="mw-redirect" title="Telescoping (railway)">telescoping</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Passenger_car_(rail)" title="Passenger car (rail)">railroad cars</a> during a head-on impact.</li>\n<li>Buffer stops for a <a href="/wiki/Janney_coupler" title="Janney coupler">knuckle coupler</a> or an <a href="/wiki/SA3_coupler" title="SA3 coupler">SA3 coupler</a> (centrally positioned between the two rails)</li>\n<li>Buffer stops with traditional "<a href="/wiki/Buffer_(rail_transport)" title="Buffer (rail transport)">buffers</a>" on either side</li>\n<li>Hydraulic buffer stops</li>\n<li>Friction buffer stops (bolted down to the rail)</li></ul>\n<p>If there is extra room behind the bumper block, there is usually a sand or ballast drag that is designed to further retard a runaway train. One such accident occurred when a Northern Line train powered past the bumper block at <a href="/wiki/Moorgate_tube_crash" title="Moorgate tube crash">Moorgate station in 1975</a> on the <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> system.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy-absorbing">Energy-absorbing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buffer_stop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Energy-absorbing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Largely because of its mass, a train transfers an enormous amount of <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_energy" title="Kinetic energy">kinetic energy</a> in a collision with a buffer stop. Rigid buffers can safely cope only with very low-speed impacts (<i>i.e.</i>, nearly stationary). To improve stopping performance, a way of dissipating this energy is needed, through compression or friction. Following a buffer stop accident at <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt-am-Main" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurt-am-Main">Frankfurt am Main</a> in 1902, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rawie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rawie (page does not exist)">Rawie</a> company developed a large range of energy-absorbing buffer stops. Similar hydraulic buffer stops were developed by <a href="/wiki/Ransomes_%26_Rapier" title="Ransomes &amp; Rapier">Ransomes &amp; Rapier</a> in the UK.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. They press upwards against the wheels, and may optionally be turned off as required.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
