Diocese of Bacolod">Roman Catholic Diocese of Bacolod</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Saint Sebastian</b> (died <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;AD&#160;288</span>) was an early <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saint</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyr</a>. According to traditional belief, he was killed during the Roman emperor <a href="/wiki/Diocletian" title="Diocletian">Diocletian</a>\'s persecution of Christians. He is commonly depicted in art and literature tied to a post or tree and shot with arrows. Despite this being the most common artistic depiction of Sebastian, he was, according to legend, rescued and healed by <a href="/wiki/Irene_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Irene of Rome">Irene of Rome</a>. Shortly afterwards he went to Diocletian to warn him about his sins, and as a result was clubbed to death.<sup id="cite_ref-independent_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independent-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SHMI_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SHMI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is venerated in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Orthodox Church</a>.\n</p><p>The details of Saint Sebastian\'s martyrdom were first spoken of by 4th-century bishop <a href="/wiki/Ambrose" title="Ambrose">Ambrose of Milan (Saint Ambrose)</a>, in his sermon (number 22) on <a href="/wiki/Psalm_118" title="Psalm 118">Psalm 118</a>. Ambrose stated that Sebastian came from <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> and that he was already venerated there at that time. Saint Sebastian is a popular male saint, especially among athletes.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" Stricken</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Josse_Lieferinxe" title="Josse Lieferinxe">Josse Lieferinxe</a>, 1497\xe2\x80\x931499, <a href="/wiki/The_Walters_Art_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="The Walters Art Museum">The Walters Art Museum</a></div></div></div>\n<p>According to Sebastian\'s 18th-century entry in <i><a href="/wiki/Acta_Sanctorum" title="Acta Sanctorum">Acta Sanctorum</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> still attributed to Ambrose by the 17th-century <a href="/wiki/Hagiography" title="Hagiography">hagiographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Jean_Bolland" title="Jean Bolland">Jean Bolland</a>, and the briefer account in the 14th-century <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Legend" title="Golden Legend">Legenda Aurea</a></i>, he was a man of <a href="/wiki/Gallia_Narbonensis" title="Gallia Narbonensis">Gallia Narbonensis</a> who was taught in <a href="/wiki/Mediolanum" title="Mediolanum">Mediolanum</a> (<a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>). In 283, Sebastian entered the army in Rome under Emperor <a href="/wiki/Carinus" title="Carinus">Carinus</a> to assist the <a href="/wiki/Christian_martyrs" title="Christian martyrs">martyrs</a>. Because of his courage he became one of the captains of the <a href="/wiki/Praetorian_Guard" title="Praetorian Guard">Praetorian Guards</a> under Diocletian and <a href="/wiki/Maximian" title="Maximian">Maximian</a>, who were unaware that he was a Christian.<sup id="cite_ref-SHMI_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SHMI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to tradition, Marcus and Marcellian were twin brothers from a distinguished family and were <a href="/wiki/Deacon" title="Deacon">deacons</a>. Both brothers married, and they resided in Rome with their wives and children. The brothers refused to sacrifice to the Roman gods and were arrested. They were visited by their parents Tranquillinus and Martia in prison, who attempted to persuade them to renounce Christianity. Sebastian succeeded in converting Tranquillinus and Martia, as well as <a href="/wiki/Saint_Tiburtius" title="Saint Tiburtius">Saint Tiburtius</a>, the son of Chromatius, the local prefect. Another official, Nicostratus, and his wife Zoe were also converted. It has been said that Zoe had been a mute for six years; however, she made known to Sebastian her desire to be converted to Christianity. As soon as she had, her speech returned to her. Nicostratus then brought the rest of the prisoners; these 16 persons were converted by Sebastian.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Chromatius and Tiburtius converted; Chromatius set all of his prisoners free from jail, resigned his position, and retired to the country in <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>. Marcus and Marcellian, after being concealed by a Christian named <a href="/wiki/Castulus" title="Castulus">Castulus</a>, were later martyred, as were Nicostratus, Zoe, and Tiburtius.<sup id="cite_ref-Butler_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butler-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Martyrdom">Martyrdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Sebastian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Martyrdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stsebastian.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Stsebastian.jpg/170px-Stsebastian.jpg" width="170" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Stsebastian.jpg/255px-Stsebastian.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b2/Stsebastian.jpg 2x" data-file-width="323" data-file-height="450" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stsebastian.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Reliquary" title="Reliquary">Reliquary</a> of Saint Sebastian, around 1497<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>, London)</div></div></div>\n<p>Sebastian had prudently concealed his faith, but in 286 it was detected. Diocletian reproached him for his supposed betrayal, and he commanded him to be led to a field and there to be bound to a stake so that certain archers from <a href="/wiki/Mauretania" title="Mauretania">Mauritania</a> would shoot arrows at him. "And the archers shot at him till he was as full of arrows as an <a href="/wiki/Hedgehog" title="Hedgehog">urchin</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;Note 1&#93;</a></sup> is full of pricks, and thus left him there for dead."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Miraculously, the arrows did not kill him. The widow of Castulus, <a href="/wiki/Irene_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Irene of Rome">Irene of Rome</a>, went to retrieve his body to bury it, and she discovered he was still alive. She brought him back to her house and nursed him back to health.<sup id="cite_ref-SHMI_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SHMI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sebastian later stood by a staircase where the emperor was to pass and harangued Diocletian for his cruelties against Christians. This freedom of speech, and from a person whom he supposed to have been dead, greatly astonished the emperor; but, recovering from his surprise, he gave orders for his being seized and beat to death with cudgels, and his body thrown into the common sewer. A pious lady, called Lucina, admonished by the martyr in a vision, privately removed the body, and buried it in the catacombs at the entrance of the cemetery of Calixtus,<sup id="cite_ref-Butler_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butler-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> where now stands the <a href="/wiki/San_Sebastiano_fuori_le_mura" title="San Sebastiano fuori le mura">Basilica of St. Sebastian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SHMI_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SHMI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sebastian was said to be a defense against <a href="/wiki/Plague_(disease)" title="Plague (disease)">the plague</a>. The <i>Golden Legend</i> transmits the episode of a great plague that afflicted the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a> in the time of King Gumburt, which was stopped by the erection of an altar in honor of Sebastian in the Church of Saint Peter in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Pavia" title="Province of Pavia">Province of Pavia</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location_of_remains">Location of remains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Sebastian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location of remains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:217px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Sebastia.jpg/215px-Sebastia.jpg" width="215" height="292" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Sebastia.jpg/323px-Sebastia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1a/Sebastia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="354" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>St. Sebastian</i> (detail), <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Mantegna" title="Andrea Mantegna">Andrea Mantegna</a>, 1480, <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_du_Louvre" class="mw-redirect" title="Mus\xc3\xa9e du Louvre">Mus\xc3\xa9e du Louvre</a>, Paris</div></div></div>\n<p>Remains reputed to be those of Sebastian are housed in Rome in the <i>Basilica Apostolorum,</i> built by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Damasus_I" title="Pope Damasus I">Pope Damasus I</a> in 367 on the site of the provisional tomb of Saints <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Peter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Apostle" title="Paul the Apostle">Paul</a>. The church, today called <a href="/wiki/San_Sebastiano_fuori_le_mura" title="San Sebastiano fuori le mura">San Sebastiano fuori le mura</a>, was rebuilt in the 1610s under the patronage of <a href="/wiki/Scipione_Borghese" title="Scipione Borghese">Scipione Borghese</a>.\n</p><p>St. Ado, Eginard, Sigebert, and other contemporary authors relate that, in the reign of Louis Debonnair, Pope Eugenius II gave the body of St. Sebastian to Hilduin, Abbot of St. Denys, who brought it into France, and it was deposited at <a href="/wiki/Abbey_of_St._Medard,_Soissons" class="mw-redirect" title="Abbey of St. Medard, Soissons">Saint Medard Abbey</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Soissons" title="Soissons">Soissons</a>, on 8 December, in 826.<sup id="cite_ref-Butler_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butler-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sebastian\'s <a href="/wiki/Cranium" class="mw-redirect" title="Cranium">cranium</a> was brought to the town of <a href="/wiki/Ebersberg" title="Ebersberg">Ebersberg</a> (<a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>) in 934. A Benedictine abbey was founded there and became one of the most important pilgrimage sites in southern Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Sebastian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In art and literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg/240px-Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg" width="240" height="308" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg/360px-Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg/480px-Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2024" data-file-height="2601" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Georges_de_La_Tour_003.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>St. Sebastian tended by <a href="/wiki/Irene_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Irene of Rome">Saint Irene</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Georges_de_La_Tour" title="Georges de La Tour">Georges de La Tour</a> <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 1645</div></div></div>\n<p>The earliest representation of Sebastian is a mosaic in the <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_Sant%27Apollinare_Nuovo" title="Basilica of Sant&#39;Apollinare Nuovo">Basilica of Sant\'Apollinare Nuovo</a> (Ravenna, Italy) dated between 527 and 565. The right lateral wall of the basilica contains large mosaics representing a procession of 26 martyrs, led by Saint Martin and including Sebastian. The martyrs are represented in Byzantine style, lacking any individuality, and all have identical expressions.\n</p><p>Another early representation is in a mosaic<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> in the Church of <a href="/wiki/San_Pietro_in_Vincoli" title="San Pietro in Vincoli">San Pietro in Vincoli</a> (Rome, Italy), probably made in the year 682. It shows a grown, bearded man in court dress but contains no trace of an arrow.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The archers and arrows begin to appear by 1000, and ever since have been far more commonly shown than the actual moment of his death by clubbing, so that there is a popular misperception that this is how he died.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As protector of potential plague victims (a connection popularized by the <i>Golden Legend</i><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>) and soldiers, Sebastian occupied an important place in the popular medieval mind. He was among the most frequently depicted of all saints by Late Gothic and Renaissance artists, in the period after the <a href="/wiki/Black_Death" title="Black Death">Black Death</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The opportunity to show a semi-nude young male, often in a contorted pose, also made Sebastian a favourite subject.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> His shooting with arrows was the subject of the largest <a href="/wiki/Engraving" title="Engraving">engraving</a> by the <i><a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Playing_Cards" title="Master of the Playing Cards">Master of the Playing Cards</a></i> in the 1430s, when there were few other current subjects with male nudes other than Christ. Sebastian appears in many other <a href="/wiki/Old_master_print" title="Old master print">prints</a> and paintings, although this was due to his popularity with the faithful. Among many others, <a href="/wiki/Botticelli" class="mw-redirect" title="Botticelli">Botticelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perugino" class="mw-redirect" title="Perugino">Perugino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Titian" title="Titian">Titian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pollaiuolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Pollaiuolo">Pollaiuolo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Bellini" title="Giovanni Bellini">Giovanni Bellini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guido_Reni" title="Guido Reni">Guido Reni</a> (who painted the subject seven times), <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Mantegna" title="Andrea Mantegna">Mantegna</a> (three times), <a href="/wiki/Hans_Memling" title="Hans Memling">Hans Memling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gerrit_van_Honthorst" class="mw-redirect" title="Gerrit van Honthorst">Gerrit van Honthorst</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Signorelli" title="Luca Signorelli">Luca Signorelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Greco" title="El Greco">El Greco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honor%C3%A9_Daumier" title="Honor\xc3\xa9 Daumier">Honor\xc3\xa9 Daumier</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Singer_Sargent" title="John Singer Sargent">John Singer Sargent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louise_Bourgeois" title="Louise Bourgeois">Louise Bourgeois</a> all painted Saint Sebastians. An early work by the sculptor <a href="/wiki/Gianlorenzo_Bernini" class="mw-redirect" title="Gianlorenzo Bernini">Gianlorenzo Bernini</a> is of <a href="/wiki/St._Sebastian_(Bernini)" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Sebastian (Bernini)">Saint Sebastian</a>.\n</p><p>The saint is ordinarily depicted as a handsome youth pierced by arrows. <a href="/wiki/Predella" title="Predella">Predella</a> scenes when required, often depicted his arrest, confrontation with the Emperor, and final beheading. The illustration in the infobox is the <i>Saint Sebastian</i> of <a href="/wiki/Il_Sodoma" title="Il Sodoma">Il Sodoma</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Pitti" title="Palazzo Pitti">Pitti Palace</a>, Florence.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Woodblock_of_St_Sebastian.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" Maxima</a></i> (1612)</div></div></div>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a>, Sebastian is commemorated by an optional memorial on 20 January. In the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Greece" title="Church of Greece">Church of Greece</a>, Sebastian\'s feast day is on 18 December.\n</p><p>As a protector from the <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">bubonic plague</a>, Sebastian was formerly one of the <a href="/wiki/Fourteen_Holy_Helpers" title="Fourteen Holy Helpers">Fourteen Holy Helpers</a>. The connection of the martyr shot with arrows with the plague is not an intuitive one, however. In Greco-Roman myth, <a href="/wiki/Apollo" title="Apollo">Apollo</a>, the archer god, is the deliverer from pestilence; the figure of Sebastian <a href="/wiki/Christianization" title="Christianization">Christianizes</a> this <a href="/wiki/Folkloric" class="mw-redirect" title="Folkloric">folkloric</a> association. The <a href="/wiki/Chronicler" class="mw-redirect" title="Chronicler">chronicler</a> <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Deacon" title="Paul the Deacon">Paul the Deacon</a> relates that, in 680, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> was freed from a raging pestilence by him.\n</p><p>In Catholicism, Sebastian is the patron saint of archers, athletes, and of a holy death.\n</p>\n<div href="/wiki/Palencia_Cathedral" title="Palencia Cathedral">Cathedral of San Antol\xc3\xadn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palencia" title="Palencia">Palencia</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Sebastian is one of the <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saints</a> of the city of <a href="/wiki/Qormi" title="Qormi">Qormi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> along with Saint George.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Sebastian is the <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of Acireale, <a href="/wiki/Caserta" title="Caserta">Caserta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petilia_Policastro" title="Petilia Policastro">Petilia Policastro</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melilli" title="Melilli">Melilli</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n_(Spain)" class="mw-redirect" title="San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n (Spain)">San Sebasti\xc3\xa1n</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Palma,_Majorca" class="mw-redirect" title="Palma, Majorca">Palma de Mallorca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huelva" title="Huelva">Huelva</a> in Spain. He is the patron saint of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. Informally, in the tradition of the <a href="/wiki/Afro-Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Afro-Brazilian">Afro-Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Syncretic_religion" class="mw-redirect" title="Syncretic religion">syncretic religion</a> <a href="/wiki/Umbanda" title="Umbanda">Umbanda</a>, Sebastian is often associated with <a href="/wiki/Oxossi" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxossi">Oxossi</a>, especially in the state of Rio de Janeiro itself. \n</p><p>Feast of St. Sebastian is celebrated among <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> communities of <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a> in India, with lot of elegance and colour. Churches are grandiosely illuminated and decorated, with fireworks being a main event in every Catholic home to commemorate the saint. Every parish has its own date of celebration, especially in the districts of <a href="/wiki/Thrissur" title="Thrissur">Thrissur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernakulam" title="Ernakulam">Ernakulam</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Andrew%27s_Basilica,_Arthunkal" title="St. Andrew&#39;s Basilica, Arthunkal">St. Andrew\'s Basilica, Arthunkal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kottayam" title="Kottayam">Kottayam</a>. Besides this, many pilgrim centres, churches, shrines and many educational institutions too, throughout Kerala, bear the name of the saint.\n</p><p>He is the patron of <a href="/wiki/San_Sebastian_College_-_Recoletos" class="mw-redirect" title="San Sebastian College - Recoletos">a college named for him</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> which is adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Parish_of_San_Sebastian" class="mw-redirect" title="Parish of San Sebastian">Parish of San Sebastian</a>.\n</p><p>At the Catholic Newman Community at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rochester" title="University of Rochester">University of Rochester</a>, the St. Sebastian Society is an organization of campus-wide Christian athletes that works to serve the greater <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a> area through methods of <a href="/wiki/Restorative_justice" title="Restorative justice">restorative justice</a>, special needs <a href="/wiki/Fundraising" title="Fundraising">fundraising</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Community_service" title="Community service">community service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sebastian is the patron saint of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Bacolod" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Bacolod">Roman Catholic Diocese of Bacolod</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Negros_Occidental" title="Negros Occidental">Negros Occidental</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lipa_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Lipa City">Lipa City</a> in <a href="/wiki/Batangas" title="Batangas">Batangas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.\n</p><p>Also, Saint Sebastian is the patron saint of <a href="/wiki/Le%C3%B3n,_Guanajuato" title="Le\xc3\xb3n, Guanajuato">Leon</a> City <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. A representation of the Saint in his martyrdom is present in the upper left corner of the city coat of arms.\n</p><p>Saint Sebastian is the patron of <a href="/wiki/Knights_of_Columbus" title="Knights of Columbus">Knights of Columbus</a> Council #4926 in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_San_Jose_in_California" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of San Jose in California">Roman Catholic Diocese of San Jose in California</a>, serving the cities of <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View,_California" title="Mountain View, California">Mountain View</a> and <a href="/wiki/Los_Altos,_California" title="Los Altos, California">Los Altos</a>.\n</p><p>Saint Sebastian is the patron saint of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_War_Veterans" title="Catholic War Veterans">Catholic War Veterans</a> of the United States of America. The highest award given by the CWV is the Honor Legion of the Order of St. Sebastian. \n</p><p>In his 1906 <i>Reminiscences</i>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Schurz" title="Carl Schurz">Carl Schurz</a> recalls the annual "bird shoot" pageant of the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhenish</a> town of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Liblar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Liblar (page does not exist)">Liblar</a> which was sponsored by the Saint Sebastian Society, a club of <a href="/wiki/Sharpshooter" title="Sharpshooter">sharpshooters</a> and their sponsors to which nearly every adult member of town belonged.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/St._Sebastian_River" title="St. Sebastian River">St. Sebastian River</a> is named after him. It is a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_River_Lagoon" title="Indian River Lagoon">Indian River Lagoon</a> and comprises part of the boundary between <a href="/wiki/Indian_River_County,_Florida" title="Indian River County, Florida">Indian River County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brevard_County,_Florida" title="Brevard County, Florida">Brevard County</a> in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>. The adjacent city of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian,_Florida" title="Sebastian, Florida">Sebastian, Florida</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._Sebastian_River_Preserve_State_Park" title="St. Sebastian River Preserve State Park">St. Sebastian River Preserve State Park</a> are also named for Saint Sebastian.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
