href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">13</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In early times one group of islands was in the possession of a confederacy of hermits. King <a href="/wiki/Henry_I_of_England" title="Henry I of England">Henry I</a> gave it to <a href="/wiki/Tavistock_Abbey" title="Tavistock Abbey">Tavistock Abbey</a> which established a <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">priory</a> on Tresco; it was abolished during the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Monasteries" title="Dissolution of the Monasteries">Dissolution of the Monasteries</a>. The priory was given the care of souls in the secular islands by the lord of the fief.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1233, a prior here, known as Alan of Cornwall, was made <a href="/wiki/Abbot_of_Tavistock" title="Abbot of Tavistock">abbot of Tavistock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The original name for the island (including Bryher) was the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Ryn Tewyn</i> meaning "promontory of sand-dunes". In 1193 when the island was granted to the abbot of Tavistock by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Celestine_III" title="Pope Celestine III">Pope Celestine III</a> the island was known as <i>St. Nicholas\'s island</i> and by 1305 it is called <i>Trescau</i> (farm of elder-trees). By 1540 this has changed to <i>Iniscaw</i> (island of elder-trees).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The island was named as <i>Trescaw</i> in an 1814 publication.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The island is administered for the Crown by the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and is leased to the Dorrien-Smith estate,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which runs it as a timeshare business. The Dorrien-Smith family (descended from <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a>) held the position of <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Scilly Isles">Lord Proprietors of the Scilly Islands</a> between 1834 and 1920.\n</p><p>From 2001 until 2009, the island hosted a <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a> run organised in aid of Cystic Fibrosis. The course consists of 7\xc2\xbd laps around the island. The event is always held on the same day as the <a href="/wiki/London_Marathon" title="London Marathon">London Marathon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Past winners include <a href="/wiki/Dara_O%27Kearney" title="Dara O&#39;Kearney">Dara O\'Kearney</a> and Bob Brown. The Marathon has now been replaced with a Sprint Triathlon.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2007 a rebuild of the Abbey Farm/Shed area was completed; this area served as <a href="/wiki/RNAS_Tresco" title="RNAS Tresco">RNAS Tresco</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Seaplane" title="Seaplane">seaplane</a> base during the First World War.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The development included rental cottages, a swimming pool and spa and the Flying Boat Bar &amp; Bistro.\n</p><p>In 2012 the Island Hotel was closed. Parts of the complex were converted into luxury holiday cottages; other parts of the hotel were demolished with new cottages built in its place. The Sea Garden Cottages now offer flexible accommodation with an on-site spa and tennis court.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg/220px-Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg/330px-Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg/440px-Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3012" data-file-height="2258" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tresco_-_aerial_photo6.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial view of Tresco.</div></div></div>\n<p>A variety of scenery is found on the island, including rugged <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> outcrops, heathland of the exposed north coast and mainly shell beaches in the east and south. The variety of its scenery and geomorphology is partly a result of the last ice age, when the <a href="/wiki/Devensian_glaciation" class="mw-redirect" title="Devensian glaciation">Devensian</a> ice sheet clipped the north side of the island, leaving deformation till deposits.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The main settlements are <a href="/wiki/New_Grimsby" title="New Grimsby">New Grimsby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Old_Grimsby" title="Old Grimsby">Old Grimsby</a> in the central part of the island. Combined, their facilities include a convenience store (with a post office sub-branch), an art gallery, a pub, and two caf\xc3\xa9/restaurants, all of which are owned and run by the Tresco Estate. At the south of the island are the sub-tropical <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">Tresco Abbey Gardens</a>, including the Valhalla Figurehead Collection, and <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Heliport" title="Tresco Heliport">Tresco Heliport</a>. To the north of New Grimsby are King Charles\'s Castle and Cromwell\'s Castle.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_parish_and_ward">Civil parish and ward</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Civil parish and ward">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/220px-Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/330px-Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/440px-Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tresco_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The isles of Scilly comprising the civil parish and ward of Tresco shown in red.</div></div></div>\n<p>Tresco is one of the five <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parishes</a> of the Isles of Scilly, which are also <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">wards</a>. Farm tractors with passenger trailers are used to transport overnight visitors to and from <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Heliport" title="Tresco Heliport">Tresco Heliport</a> and from the various quays, and a few golf carts are available for disabled visitors.\n</p><p>From 1983 to October 2012 <a href="/wiki/British_International_Helicopters" title="British International Helicopters">British International Helicopters</a> operated from <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Heliport" title="Tresco Heliport">Tresco Heliport</a>, providing a year-round helicopter service to <a href="/wiki/Penzance_Heliport" title="Penzance Heliport">Penzance Heliport</a>. A new helicopter service from a new Penzance Heliport will operate to Tresco and St Mary\'s from 2019 or 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Tresco Boat Services (formerly Bryher Boats) run passenger boat services to and from the other inhabited islands, as well as occasional circular sightseeing tours.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife_and_ecology">Wildlife and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Wildlife and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Tresco is unique amongst the off islands in that its habitat ranges from a windswept northern plateau with waved heath to sheltered bulb fields, wetland and lakes, to beautiful beaches backed by a sand dune system on the south coast.<sup id="cite_ref-Cooper_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cooper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Subsequently, the forerunner of <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a> designated three Sites of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI) in 1971 and 1976. They are the <a href="/wiki/Castle_Down_(Tresco)" class="mw-redirect" title="Castle Down (Tresco)">Castle Down (Tresco) SSSI</a>, Great Pool (Tresco) SSSI and <a href="/wiki/Round_Island_Light,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Round Island Light, Isles of Scilly">Pentle Bay, Merrick and Round Islands SSSI</a>. Castle Down is a SSSI for its waved maritime heath, its <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichen flora</a>, a breeding colony of Common Tern (<i><a href="/wiki/Sterna_hirundo" class="mw-redirect" title="Sterna hirundo">Sterna hirundo</a></i>) and for its <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>. Great Pool is an SSSI because it is the largest area of fresh water in the islands and important for its breeding birds, and as a sheltering and feeding area for migrants. Pentle Bay is designated for the transition from a flora-rich sand dune system to lichen-rich heath.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> \n<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 2012, four male and one female Red Squirrel (<i><a href="/wiki/Red_Squirrel" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Squirrel">Sciurus vulgaris</a></i>), on permanent loan from the British Wildlife Centre, were transported to Tresco by helicopter. Only two survived, but in September 2013 a further twenty were transported to Tresco and released in Abbey Woods, near <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">the Abbey Gardens</a>. Tresco is considered to be a \xe2\x80\xb3safe haven\xe2\x80\xb3 for the endangered mammal as it is free from predators such as <a href="/wiki/Red_fox" title="Red fox">foxes</a>, and from <a href="/wiki/Eastern_gray_squirrel" title="Eastern gray squirrel">grey squirrels</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Squirrel_parapoxvirus" title="Squirrel parapoxvirus">squirrel pox</a> they carry.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In June 2014, an unknown number of baby squirrels have been pictured in the Abbey Gardens, proving the squirrels are successfully breeding.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vagrant_birds">Vagrant birds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vagrant birds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Among the many <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant birds</a> which have been found here, the following were firsts for Britain:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Common Nighthawk (<i><a href="/wiki/Chordeiles_minor" class="mw-redirect" title="Chordeiles minor">Chordeiles minor</a></i>), collected on 17 September 1927.</li>\n<li>Black-billed Cuckoo (<i><a href="/wiki/Coccyzus_erythrophthalmus" class="mw-redirect" title="Coccyzus erythrophthalmus">Coccyzus erythrophthalmus</a></i>), collected on 27 October 1932.</li>\n<li>Northern Parula (<i><a href="/wiki/Setophaga_americana" class="mw-redirect" title="Setophaga americana">Setophaga americana</a></i>), seen 16\xe2\x80\x9317 October 1966.</li>\n<li>Western Sandpiper (<i><a href="/wiki/Calidris_mauri" class="mw-redirect" title="Calidris mauri">Calidris mauri</a></i>), originally identified as a Semipalmated Sandpiper, 19 August 1969.</li>\n<li>Yellow-bellied Sapsucker (<i><a href="/wiki/Sphyrapicus_varius" class="mw-redirect" title="Sphyrapicus varius">Sphyrapicus varius</a></i>), seen 26 September \xe2\x80\x93 6 October 1975.</li></ul>\n<p>The island also briefly hosted the second British record of Great Blue Heron (<i><a href="/wiki/Ardea_herodias" class="mw-redirect" title="Ardea herodias">Ardea herodias</a></i>) in April and May 2015, which also visited both Bryher and St Mary\'s during its 23-day stay.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span><span brought up on the Isles of Scilly. Tresco is mentioned several times.</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Why_the_Whales_Came" title="Why the Whales Came">Why the Whales Came</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Morpurgo" title="Michael Morpurgo">Michael Morpurgo</a> is set in the Isles of Scilly and features Tresco several times.</li>\n<li><i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hell_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hell Bay (page does not exist)">Hell Bay</a></i>, by Sam Llewellyn, is set on Tresco and fictionalises the events leading up to <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> taking ownership of the island.</li>\n<li><i><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Sea_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Sea Garden (page does not exist)">The Sea Garden</a></i>, also by Sam Llewellyn, is set on a fictional island based heavily on Tresco.
