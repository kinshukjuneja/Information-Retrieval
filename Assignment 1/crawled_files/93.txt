links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tresco_Abbey">Tresco Abbey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco_Abbey_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tresco Abbey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Augustus Smith chose Tresco as the site of his home because the site was more or less central in relation to the rest of the islands. It is also close to the <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Priory" title="Tresco Priory">original abbey ruins</a>, is near a fresh water pool and overlooks the sand dunes and beach at Carn Near. The area at the time was barren land and the original building, designed by Smith and started in 1835, was small in comparison to the current building. He made additions to the house in 1843 and 1861. The <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Grade II listed house</a> consists of roughly coursed granite with <a href="/wiki/Ashlar" title="Ashlar">ashlar</a> dressings and a slate roof. Some of the timbers from the 1861 wreck of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_shipwrecks_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly#1861" title="List of shipwrecks of the Isles of Scilly"><i>Award</i></a> were used for the panelling and roof of the new dining room, as well as panelling of the rooms Annet, Rosevean and Rosevear.<sup id="cite_ref-award_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-award-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His successor, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Algernon_Smith-Dorrien-Smith" title="Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith">Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith</a> added the tower in 1891.<sup id="cite_ref-vyvyan_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vyvyan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abbey_Gardens">Abbey Gardens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco_Abbey_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Abbey Gardens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When Augustus Smith chose the area for his house and garden one of his first acts was to build a granite wall for shelter and to scatter gorse (<i><a href="/wiki/Ulex_europaeus" title="Ulex europaeus">Ulex europaeus</a></i>) seeds. The seeds were brought from the mainland which suggests that the main gorse plant on the islands was western gorse (<i><a href="/wiki/Ulex_gallii" title="Ulex gallii">Ulex gallii</a></i>) which, being a low growing plant, would not provide as much shelter.<sup id="cite_ref-parslow_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parslow-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Within the gardens are the remains of a <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Benedict" title="Order of Saint Benedict">Benedictine abbey</a> founded in 964 AD, although the majority of what remains today comes from the <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Priory" title="Tresco Priory">Priory of St Nicholas</a> founded by monks from <a href="/wiki/Tavistock_Abbey" title="Tavistock Abbey">Tavistock Abbey</a> in 1114.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There were hardly any trees on the island and the gorse did not provide enough protection so he planted <a href="/wiki/Windbreak" title="Windbreak">shelterbelts</a>. The first were mainly deciduous trees such as, elm (<i><a href="/wiki/Ulmus" class="mw-redirect" title="Ulmus">Ulmus sp</a></i>), sycamore (<i><a href="/wiki/Acer_pseudoplatanus" title="Acer pseudoplatanus">Acer pseudoplatanus</a></i>), oak (<i><a href="/wiki/Rubus" title="Rubus">Rubus sp</a></i>) and poplar (<i><a href="/wiki/Populus" title="Populus">Poplar sp</a></i>), and later he planted Monterey cypress (<i><a href="/wiki/Cupressus_macrocarpa" title="Cupressus macrocarpa">Cupressus macrocarpa</a></i>) and Monterey pine (<i><a href="/wiki/Pinus_radiata" title="Pinus radiata">Pinus radiata</a></i>) which are fast growing and suited to coastal conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-king_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-king-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A large expansion to the collection was undertaken by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien-Smith" title="Arthur Algernon Dorrien-Smith">Arthur Algernon Dorrien-Smith</a> in the early years of the 20th century. He made many trips to South Africa looking for suitable trees and plants. He went on the <a href="/wiki/1907_Sub-Antarctic_Islands_Scientific_Expedition" title="1907 Sub-Antarctic Islands Scientific Expedition">1907 Sub-Antarctic Islands Scientific Expedition</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-NZETC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NZETC-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which had as its primary object magnetic observation in the Auckland and Campbell Islands. Following the expedition he travelled widely in New Zealand, as well as making a shorter visit to Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 1909 he again visited Australia, New Zealand and the Chatham Islands, returning on <a href="/wiki/SS_Athenic" title="SS Athenic">SS Athenic</a>. By this time he had amassed a total collection of plants and seeds of about 2280 specimens.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Because of the mild winter climate, the long hours of summer sunshine, and the high walls and hedges around the garden protecting it from the Atlantic winds, the garden is now home to exotic plants from all over the world: the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Head_Gardeners">Head Gardeners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco_Abbey_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Head Gardeners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg/220px-In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg/330px-In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg/440px-In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:In_memory_of_John_Hartley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bench in Memory of John Hartley, Head Gardener 1948-1958</div></div></div>\n<ul><li>G.D. Only two survived so in 2013 the British Wildlife Centre in Surrey provided a new colony which was flown to Tresco by helicopter on a routine flight from <a href="/wiki/RNAS_Culdrose" class="mw-redirect" title="RNAS Culdrose">RNAS Culdrose</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Valhalla_Museum">Valhalla Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco_Abbey_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Valhalla Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Valhalla,_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Valhalla%2C_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg/220px-Valhalla%2C_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Valhalla%2C_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg/330px-Valhalla%2C_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Valhalla%2C_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg/440px-Valhalla%2C_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Valhalla,_Tresco_Abbey_Gardens_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1766334.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Valhalla</div></div></div>\n<p>The Valhalla Museum within Tresco Abbey Gardens features the Valhalla Collection containing some 30 <a href="/wiki/Figurehead_(object)" title="Figurehead (object)">figureheads</a>, as well as name-boards and other decorative carvings from the days of sail. The collection was started by Augustus Smith. Most of the figureheads date from the middle and end of the 19th century and come from merchant sailing vessels or early steamships that were wrecked on the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Some of the ships which are represented in the collection are:\n</p>\n<ul><li>a 17th-century stern decoration of the Greek god <a href="/wiki/Anemoi#Boreas" title="Anemoi">Boreas</a> and possibly carved by <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Paul_Puget" class="mw-redirect" title="Pierre Paul Puget">Pierre Puget</a>, is said to be from a French ship wrecked on Annet in that century.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Association_(1697)" title="Association (1697)">HMS Association</a> - wrecked on the night of 22 October 1707 <span style="font-size:85%;">(<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">Old style</a>)</span> in the <a href="/wiki/Scilly_naval_disaster_of_1707" title="Scilly naval disaster of 1707">Scilly naval disaster of 1707</a>. The bronze cannon was salvaged from this wreck in 1970. It is a French 18-pounder bronze gun, probably a trophy from the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toulon_(1707)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Toulon (1707)">siege of Toulon (1707)</a>. The main decoration shows the arms of France and Navarre surrounded by the collars of the orders of St Michel and the St Esprit, surmounted by a crown.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/SS_Thames" title="SS Thames">SS <i>Thames</i></a> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 4 January 1841 near <a href="/wiki/Western_Rocks,_Isles_of_Scilly#Gorregan" title="Western Rocks, Isles of Scilly">Gorregan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosevear,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Rosevear, Isles of Scilly">Rosevear</a>.</li>\n<li><i>Alessandro II Grande</i> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 1 January 1851 when she was blown on the Mare ledges, off the south shore of Tresco. There was no loss of life. The figurehead is of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_I_of_Russia" title="Alexander I of Russia">Tsar Alexander I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>Mary Hay</i> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 13 April 1852 after hitting the Steeple Rock, on the Bream Ledges which is between Mincarlo and Samson.<sup id="cite_ref-larn92_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-larn92-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>Chieftain</i> \xe2\x80\x94 said to be wrecked in 1856 off St Martin\'s Head. There is no record of a ship with that name in the <a href="/wiki/Lloyd%27s_Register" title="Lloyd&#39;s Register">Lloyd\'s Register</a><sup id="cite_ref-larn92_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-larn92-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>Award</i> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 19 March 1861 when she was driven onto <a href="/wiki/Gweal" class="mw-redirect" title="Gweal">Gweal</a> during a force 8 to 9 NNW gale. Her crew of 24 managed to scramble ashore.<sup id="cite_ref-award_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-award-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>Primos</i> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 24 June 1871 on the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Stones_reef" class="mw-redirect" title="Seven Stones reef">Seven Stones reef</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-larn92_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-larn92-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>River Lune</i> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 27 June 1879 on the Brothers Rock in Muncoy Neck, between <a href="/wiki/Western_Rocks,_Isles_of_Scilly#Melledgan" title="Western Rocks, Isles of Scilly">Melledgan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Annet,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Annet, Isles of Scilly">Annet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-larn92_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-larn92-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>Bernardo</i> \xe2\x80\x93 sank on Annet in 1888. Her figurehead is said to be <a href="/wiki/Bernard_of_Clairvaux" title="Bernard of Clairvaux">St Bernard of Clairvaux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i>Lofaro</i> \xe2\x80\x94 wrecked 2 February 1902 struck Merrick Rock, St Martin\'s with the loss of her crew.<sup id="cite_ref-larn92_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-larn92-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco_Abbey_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Listed_buildings_in_Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Listed buildings in Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Listed buildings in Tresco, Isles of Scilly</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_shipwrecks_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly" title="List of shipwrecks of the Isles of Scilly">List of shipwrecks of the Isles of Scilly</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Tresco_Priory" title="Tresco Priory">Tresco Priory</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tresco_Abbey_Gardens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
