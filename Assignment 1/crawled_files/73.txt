<b>Bricktown Showdown</b>, is a single championship game held annually between the league champions of the <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a> (IL) and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> (PCL) affiliated <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> leagues of <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> (MiLB) to determine an overall champion of the classification. The championship consists of a single nine-inning game to determine a champion. As the game is usually played at a neutral site, the host league has its team designated as the home team.\n</p><p>From 2006 to 2010, the game was held annually at <a href="/wiki/Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark" title="Chickasaw Bricktown Ballpark">AT&amp;T Bricktown Ballpark</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">Oklahoma City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>. Since 2011, the game has been hosted in a different Triple-A city each year.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Durham_Bulls" title="Durham Bulls">Durham Bulls</a> have made four appearances in the Triple-A Championship Game, more than any other team. Durham, the IL\'s <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Clippers" title="Columbus Clippers">Columbus Clippers</a>, and the PCL\'s <a href="/wiki/Omaha_Storm_Chasers" title="Omaha Storm Chasers">Omaha Storm Chasers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_River_Cats" title="Sacramento River Cats">Sacramento River Cats</a> have each won two championships, more than any others. Five other teams have won one championship each. Eight titles have been won by PCL teams, while the IL has won only five titles.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" href="/w/index.php?title=Triple-A_National_Championship_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg/220px-Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg/330px-Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg/440px-Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg 2x" data-file-width="760" data-file-height="570" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark" title="Chickasaw Bricktown Ballpark">AT&amp;T Bricktown Ballpark</a>, site of the first five Triple-A National Championship Games (2006\xe2\x80\x932010)</div></div></div>\n<p>Off and on from 1904 to 1975, the league champions of the three highest-classification <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> leagues met in the postseason to determine a classification champion. The <a href="/wiki/Junior_World_Series" title="Junior World Series">Little World Series</a> (1904\xe2\x80\x931931) and <a href="/wiki/Junior_World_Series" title="Junior World Series">Junior World Series</a> (1932\xe2\x80\x931975) usually consisted of a best-of-seven (or eight) series modeled on the <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. Most often it was held between the champions of the <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(20th_century)" title="American Association (20th century)">American Association</a> (AA), leaving the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> out of the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-pastclasschamps_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastclasschamps-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A one-time <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_World_Series" title="Triple-A World Series">Triple-A World Series</a> was held in 1983 as a <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin tournament</a> featuring the champions of all three Triple-A leagues.<sup id="cite_ref-pastclasschamps_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastclasschamps-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The IL and AA champions met in the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_Classic" title="Triple-A Classic">Triple-A Classic</a>, a best-of-seven series, from 1988 to 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-pastclasschamps_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastclasschamps-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> From 1998 to 2000, the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_World_Series" title="Triple-A World Series">Las Vegas Triple-A World Series</a> pitted the IL and PCL champs (as the American Association had folded in 1997) in a best-of-five championship series.<sup id="cite_ref-pastclasschamps_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastclasschamps-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In 2006, Triple-A Baseball announced the creation of a single championship game between the league champions of the International League and the Pacific Coast League to determine an overall champion of the classification. The game, called the Bricktown Showdown, was to be played at <a href="/wiki/Chickasaw_Bricktown_Ballpark" title="Chickasaw Bricktown Ballpark">AT&amp;T Bricktown Ballpark</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">Oklahoma City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>. In addition to serving as the pinnacle of the Triple-A and MiLB season, the leagues sought for the championship game to develop and prosper like the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_All-Star_Game" title="Triple-A All-Star Game">Triple-A All-Star Game</a> did since its creation in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-btsd01_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsd01-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The first Bricktown Showdown was played on September 19, 2006. The PCL\'s <a href="/wiki/Tucson_Toros" title="Tucson Toros">Tucson Sidewinders</a> defeated the IL\'s <a href="/wiki/Toledo_Mud_Hens" title="Toledo Mud Hens">Toledo Mud Hens</a>, 5\xe2\x80\x932, in front of an announced paid attendance of 12,572 and a national television audience watching on <a href="/wiki/ESPN2" title="ESPN2">ESPN2</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2006box_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006box-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The initial Showdown was approved only as a one-time meeting by Major League Baseball,<sup id="cite_ref-btsd03_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsd03-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> but subsequent meetings were planned following the event\'s success.<sup id="cite_ref-btsd03_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsd03-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The game was rebranded as the Triple-A Baseball National Championship Game in 2009, and later as simply the Triple-A National Championship Game. This was done to increase the event\'s national appeal and to emphasize its significance as a championship game.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The championship continued to be held in Oklahoma City through 2010. Since 2011, the game has been held in a different Triple-A city each year.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The first city to host under this new format was <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">Albuquerque, New Mexico</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque_Isotopes" title="Albuquerque Isotopes">Albuquerque Isotopes</a> of the PCL. Games have since been held in other Triple-A cities. No host city\'s team has ever participated in the championship game.\n</p><p>From 2006 to 2016, the league that won the Triple-A All-Star Game earned the distinction of having its team designated as the home team.<sup id="cite_ref-btsd01_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsd01-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This changed in 2017, when home team status began being awarded to the team from the hosting league.<sup id="cite_ref-2017homefield_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017homefield-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>The event has been televised nationally every year. It aired on <a href="/wiki/ESPN2" title="ESPN2">ESPN2</a> from 2006 to 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-results_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The game has been broadcast on the <a href="/wiki/NBCSN" title="NBCSN">NBC Sports Network</a> (formerly known the Outdoor Life Network and Versus) since 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-results_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Triple-A_National_Championship_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg/220px-RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg/330px-RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg/440px-RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3550" data-file-height="1997" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:RailRiders_celebrate_championship_game_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Scranton/Wilkes-Barre_RailRiders" title="Scranton/Wilkes-Barre RailRiders">Scranton/Wilkes-Barre RailRiders</a> celebrate winning the 2016 Triple-A Championship.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Triple-A National Championship Game consists of a single nine-inning game to determine a champion. The only championship game to ever go beyond the prescribed nine innings was the 2009 contest which went to eleven innings.<sup id="cite_ref-2009box_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009box-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The host league\'s team serves as the home team.<sup id="cite_ref-2017homefield_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017homefield-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> (Prior to 2017, the league that won the Triple-A All-Star Game was designated as the home team.<sup id="cite_ref-btsd01_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsd01-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) <a href="/wiki/Designated_hitter" title="Designated hitter">Designated hitters</a> bat in place of the pitchers.\n</p><p>Players wear their respective team\'s uniforms. Players on the home team wear their club\'s white home uniforms, while players on the away team wear their club\'s gray road uniforms. Usually, a patch depicting the game\'s logo is sewn onto their jerseys and caps.\n</p><p>The game is <a href="/wiki/Umpire_(baseball)" title="Umpire (baseball)">umpired</a> by a four-man crew with one umpire behind <a href="/wiki/Baseball_field#Home_plate" title="Baseball field">home plate</a> and the others covering each base. Two of the umpires work in the IL, while two work in the PCL.
