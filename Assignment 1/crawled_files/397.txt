class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Atlantic_Coast_Line_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The canalised River Par</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Par River</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Dowr Gwernan</b></i>, meaning <i>alder tree river</i>), also known as the <b>Luxulyan River</b> is a <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> draining the area north of <a href="/wiki/St_Blazey" title="St Blazey">St Blazey</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Par_River,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Par River rises on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Criggan_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Criggan Moor (page does not exist)">Criggan Moor</a>, with tributaries rising near <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crift&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crift (page does not exist)">Crift</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bokiddick_Downs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bokiddick Downs (page does not exist)">Bokiddick Downs</a>, and the villages of <a href="/wiki/Lockengate" title="Lockengate">Lockengate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lanivet" title="Lanivet">Lanivet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tregullon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tregullon">Tregullon</a> near <a href="/wiki/Bodmin" title="Bodmin">Bodmin</a>. It flows southwards via <a href="/wiki/Bokiddick" title="Bokiddick">Bokiddick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bodwen" title="Bodwen">Bodwen</a>, and through the <a href="/wiki/Luxulyan_Valley" title="Luxulyan Valley">Luxulyan Valley</a>, to flow into <a href="/wiki/St_Austell_Bay" title="St Austell Bay">St Austell Bay</a> at <a href="/wiki/Par,_Cornwall" title="Par, Cornwall">Par</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Par_River,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Until the 16th-century the valley below St Blazey contained an estuary and the crossing at St Blazey was the lowest crossing point on the river.<sup id="cite_ref-CISI_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CISI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ponts_Mill" title="Ponts Mill">Ponts Mill</a> was once a port, up-river of St Blazey, and as late as 1720, 80 ton sea going vessels could reach the port.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2017 <a href="/wiki/Imerys" title="Imerys">Imerys Minerals</a> were fined \xc2\xa375,000 with \xc2\xa325,000 costs for polluting the tributary, Rocks Stream. An estimated 474 litres (104&#160;imp&#160;gal; 125&#160;US&#160;gal) of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jayfloc_85&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jayfloc 85 (page does not exist)">Jayfloc 85</a>, a  substance, harmful to aquatic life, was, in July 2013, flushed from a redundant storage tank, through drains and settlement lagoons and into the stream, near <a href="/wiki/Bugle,_Cornwall" title="Bugle, Cornwall">Bugle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Par_River,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Smith, John R (1988). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://mapping.cornwall.gov.uk/EventRecord/1988/1988R005.pdf">"The Luxulyan Valley"</a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>.
