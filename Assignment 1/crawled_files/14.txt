It is the westernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Society_Islands" title="Society Islands">Society Islands</a>\' <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands_(Society_Islands)" title="Leeward Islands (Society Islands)">Leeward Islands</a>, located 217&#160;mi. (350&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Bora_Bora" title="Bora Bora">Bora Bora</a> and 342&#160;mi. (550&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Papeete" title="Papeete">Pape\'ete</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Geography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Geography</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Administration"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Administration</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Nature_Reserve"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Nature Reserve</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Other_Names"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Other Names</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-7"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">7</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuae_(Society_Islands)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Manua\'e consists of a ring of islands approximately 6\xe2\x80\x937&#160;mi. in diameter, separated by unnavigable passages, rising just a few feet above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Motu_(geography)" class="mw-redirect" title="Motu (geography)">motus</a> are covered primarily with <a href="/wiki/Coconut_palm" class="mw-redirect" title="Coconut palm">coconut palms</a> and tropical scrub with sandy beaches, and comprise a total land area of about 3.5&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (1.4&#160;sq&#160;mi).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuae_(Society_Islands)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The atoll is administratively part of the <a href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Commune in France">commune</a> (municipality) of <a href="/wiki/Maupiti_(commune)" title="Maupiti (commune)">Maupiti</a>, itself in the <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia#Administrative_divisions" title="French Polynesia">administrative subdivision</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands_(Society_Islands)" title="Leeward Islands (Society Islands)">Leeward Islands</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature_Reserve">Nature Reserve</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuae_(Society_Islands)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nature Reserve">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The atoll\'s lagoon, home to a number of rare species, has been a <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a> since 1992. The beaches are popular with <a href="/wiki/Sea_turtle" title="Sea turtle">sea turtles</a>, including the endangered <a href="/wiki/Green_Sea_Turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Green Sea Turtle">Green Sea Turtle</a>, which come annually to lie on Manua\'e\'s beaches beginning each November. The atoll is also home to large <a href="/wiki/Oyster" title="Oyster">oyster</a> beds, containing up to 3 or 4 million oysters.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuae_(Society_Islands)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Manua\'e\'s first <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> visitor was <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> navigator <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Wallis" title="Samuel Wallis">Samuel Wallis</a>, in 1767. A small village was established there at the time, the remains of which can still be found on the northern part of the main island. The atoll was the site of the 1855 <a href="/wiki/Shipwreck" title="Shipwreck">shipwreck</a> of the three-masted <i>Julia Ann</i>. Its crew and passengers, including twenty-four women and children, lived on the atoll for two months, during which time they were able to build a boat with which they were able to sail to <a href="/wiki/Raiatea" title="Raiatea">Ra\'i\xc4\x81tea</a>. There is no evidence that the islands were ever permanently inhabited prior to the shipwreck of the <i>Julia Ann</i>, a ship whose passengers were mainly <a href="/wiki/Latter-day_Saints" class="mw-redirect" title="Latter-day Saints">Latter-day Saints</a> on their way from Australia to the United States. Beginning in 1952, the islands were regularly visited by <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a> merchants. During the 1960s, these casual visitors began capturing large numbers of <a href="/wiki/Sea_turtle" title="Sea turtle">sea turtles</a> until their activities were legally restricted in 1971. Today the atoll is visited regularly by fishermen from Ra\'i\xc4\x81tea. \n</p><p>The islands, which have no regular ship or airline connections with other locations in French Polynesia, are owned by the "Compagnie Fran\xc3\xa7aise de Tahiti", which cultivated 57,000 coconut palms there in the 1920s. The now-abandoned plantation once produced up to 70 tons of copra annually.\n</p><p>On June 25, 2005, the Silverwood family of California crashed into the reef in a 55-foot catamaran, the <i>Emerald Jane</i>. Thanks to an <a href="/wiki/EPIRB" class="mw-redirect" title="EPIRB">EPIRB</a> distress call, the family was spotted by a French Navy airplane, picked up by the resident Taputo family, and flown out in a helicopter on June 26. John Silverwood, the captain of the <i>Emerald Jane</i>, would lose his leg, and the experience is described in the book "Black Wave". It was also featured in an episode of "<a href="/wiki/I_Shouldn%27t_Be_Alive" title="I Shouldn&#39;t Be Alive">I Shouldn\'t Be Alive</a>".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Names">Other Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuae_(Society_Islands)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Other names given to Manua\'e include <b>Fenua \'Ura</b> and <b>Putai</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tahitian_language" title="Tahitian language">Tahitian</a>), <b>Isla de P\xc3\xa1jaros</b> ("Island of Birds", in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Span.</a>), and <b>Scilly</b> (<a
