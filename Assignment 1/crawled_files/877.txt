<a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EGHQ</b>) is the main commercial <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> for <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England. At <a href="/wiki/St_Mawgan" title="St Mawgan">Mawgan in Pydar</a>, 4&#160;<a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">NM</a> (7.4&#160;km; 4.6&#160;mi) northeast of the resort town of <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">Newquay</a> on Cornwall\'s north coast, its runway was operated by <a href="/wiki/RAF_St_Mawgan" title="RAF St Mawgan">RAF St Mawgan</a> before 2008 and is now owned by <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a>.\n</p><p>In 2017, the airport handled 461,300 passengers, a 24.2% increase compared with 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Newquay has a <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">CAA</a> Public Use Aerodrome Licence that allows flights for the public transport of passengers or for flying instruction. The <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Air_Ambulance" title="Cornwall Air Ambulance">Cornwall Air Ambulance</a> is based at the airport. Since 2012, the airport has hosted the <b>Aerohub</b> <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise zone">enterprise zone</a>.\n</p><p>The runway is able to take the largest and fastest of civil and military aircraft, having been built and maintained for decades as an RAF maritime operations base. The US Navy were present with the USN AWD storage facility and Joint Maritime Facility. With the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> and changes in American political priorities, the Americans pulled out of all involvement with the base by the end of 2009. The last RAF flying squadron based at St Mawgan was <a href="/wiki/No._203_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 203 Squadron RAF">203(R) Squadron</a> which moved out in 2008, but the RAF continue to use part of the site.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newquay_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_use">Military use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newquay_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The airfield was opened in 1933 as a civilian facility, but was requisitioned at the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and named RAF Trebelzue to support other bases in the Cornwall area. The base was renamed RAF St Mawgan 1943, after expansion. The facility was then handed to the USAAF and a number of improvements took place, including the building of a new <a href="/wiki/Control_tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Control tower">control tower</a> and expansion of the current runway. The airfield was put under maintenance in 1947, and reopened as a <a href="/wiki/RAF_Coastal_Command" title="RAF Coastal Command">Coastal Command</a> base in 1951.\n</p><p>Since 1951, the base has seen various squadrons based; including <a href="/wiki/No._22_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 22 Squadron RAF">22 Sqn.</a>, <a href="/wiki/No._203_Squadron_RAF" title="No. 203 Squadron RAF">203 Sqn.</a> 220 Sqn. (later renamed 201), 228 Sqn. (later renamed 206), both Long Range Reconnaissance Squadrons, 42 Sqn., 7 Sqn., <a href="/w/index.php?title=No._2625_Squadron_RAF_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="No. 2625 Squadron RAF Regiment (page does not exist)">2625 Sqn</a> (<a href="/wiki/Royal_Auxiliary_Air_Force" title="Royal Auxiliary Air Force">Royal Auxiliary Air Force</a>) and <a href="/wiki/No._1_Squadron_RAF_Regiment" title="No. 1 Squadron RAF Regiment">1 Sqn.</a> (<a href="/wiki/RAF_Regiment" title="RAF Regiment">RAF Regiment</a>). 2625 Sqn was disbanded on 1 November 2006, whilst 1 Sqn relocated to <a href="/wiki/RAF_Honington" title="RAF Honington">RAF Honington</a>. The others relocated or closed. In 2005, RAF St Mawgan was one of the bases shortlisted to house the new <a href="/wiki/Joint_Combat_Aircraft" title="Joint Combat Aircraft">Joint Combat Aircraft</a> (JCA) in 2013, but in November 2005 it was announced by <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_State_for_the_Armed_Forces" title="Minister of State for the Armed Forces">Minister of State for the Armed Forces</a> <a href="/wiki/Adam_Ingram_(Labour_politician)" title="Adam Ingram (Labour politician)">Adam Ingram</a> that the operation would be based from Scotland. Helicopter maintenance (HMF) also ceased here in late 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Flying operations at RAF St Mawgan (on the opposite side of the runway to the civil terminal) ceased in December 2008.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition_to_fully_civilian_airport">Transition to fully civilian airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newquay_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transition to fully civilian airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nqyramp.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Nqyramp.JPG/220px-Nqyramp.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Nqyramp.JPG/330px-Nqyramp.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Nqyramp.JPG/440px-Nqyramp.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nqyramp.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Looking over the ramp at some of the airlines that serve NQY in Summer 2007</div></div></div>\n<p>In 2006 the airport introduced a \xc2\xa35 per passenger airport development fee, payable by all departing passengers via an <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" title="Automated teller machine">ATM</a> type machine prior to passing security.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This resulted in <a href="/wiki/Monarch_Airlines" title="Monarch Airlines">Monarch Airlines</a> axing its route from the airport (to <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_Airport" title="M\xc3\xa1laga Airport">M\xc3\xa1laga</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> reducing the number of flights, though this was later restored. In 2015, it was announced that the \xc2\xa35 development fee would be scrapped in March 2016.\n</p><p>In 2008 the airport closed from 1 to 19 December, to allow time for the takeover of the airfield navigation services (i.e. <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">Air Traffic Control</a>) from <a href="/wiki/RAF_St_Mawgan" title="RAF St Mawgan">RAF St Mawgan</a> and to fully meet CAA standards. Following further inspections by the CAA, flights recommenced on 20 December 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By virtue of a recent Statutory Instrument issued on 22 September 2009, Newquay Airport is subject to current airport bylaws, per Section 63 of the Airports Act 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>An extension opened in 2006 increased the <a href="/wiki/Airport_terminal" title="Airport terminal">terminal</a>\'s size by 20%, allowing the airport to handle up to 450,000 passengers a year. In January 2008, <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_County_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornwall County Council">Cornwall County Council</a> approved plans for an expanded arrivals hall and departure lounge; a new retail outlet; a business lounge and new airfield structures. In May 2008, the new arrivals and <a href="/wiki/Baggage_claim" class="mw-redirect" title="Baggage claim">baggage reclaim</a> area was opened, allowing domestic and international flights to simultaneously be processed unlike the old arrivals hall. This also added a <a href="/wiki/Gate_(airport)" title="Gate (airport)">gate</a> to the departures lounge.\n</p><p>The expansion of the airport was criticised in 2007 by a number of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmental groups</a>. This opposition was based not only on environmental concerns (given that the majority of flights are <a href="/wiki/Flight_length" title="Flight length">short-haul</a> and to destinations that could be served by rail), but also <a href="/wiki/Socioeconomics" title="Socioeconomics">socio-economic</a> concerns that airport expansion would lead to a greater demand for second homes in Cornwall, adding to already inflated local house prices.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_airport_operation">Civil airport operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newquay_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Civil airport operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2006 the Irish low cost carrier <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> pulled out of the airport, ceasing flights to <a href="/wiki/London_Stansted" class="mw-redirect" title="London Stansted">London Stansted</a> and <a href="/wiki/Girona-Costa_Brava_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Girona-Costa Brava Airport">Girona-Costa Brava Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In November 2008, Newquay Airport released their draft master plan, outlining their plans for the airport until 2030.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2011, the airport\'s main carrier, <a href="/wiki/Air_Southwest" title="Air Southwest">Air Southwest</a>, announced the end of all flights from Newquay with effect 30 September 2011, leaving the airport with only three year-round scheduled routes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2013, <a href="/wiki/Flybe" title="Flybe">Flybe</a> announced they would cease flying to <a href="/wiki/Gatwick_Airport" title="Gatwick Airport">Gatwick</a>, leaving Newquay with year-round flights to just the Isles of Scilly and Manchester. On 2 December 2013, Flybe announced they had agreed a deal with Newquay which would secure the future of the Gatwick route until the end of October 2014, so that a <a href="/wiki/Public_service_obligation" title="Public service obligation">public service obligation</a> (PSO) subsidy could be finalized. Under the new deal Flybe would operate two daily flights, with the afternoon service being dropped.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2016, Ryanair returned to the airport after the \xc2\xa35 development fee was scrapped. <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aerohub_enterprise_zone">Aerohub enterprise zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newquay_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aerohub enterprise zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In August 2011, the UK government announced that the airport\'s bid to host an <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise zone">enterprise zone</a> for aerospace businesses had been successful.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Aerohub enterprise zone was launched in April 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-aw_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aw-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In September 2014, the UK\'s <a href="/wiki/Homes_and_Communities_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Homes and Communities Agency">Homes and Communities Agency</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Regional_Development_Fund" title="European Regional Development Fund">European Regional Development Fund</a> agreed to fund the construction of a \xc2\xa36&#160;million Aerohub Business Park.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Organisations attracted to Aerohub by 2014 included <a href="/wiki/Classic_Air_Force" title="Classic Air Force">Classic Air Force</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bloodhound_SSC" title="Bloodhound SSC">Bloodhound Supersonic Car</a> project.<sup id="cite_ref-aw_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aw-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 29 March 2013, Classic Air Force has operated from the airport using a variety of aircraft,<sup id="cite_ref-FlyPastMarch2013_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FlyPastMarch2013-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> including the world\'s oldest flying British jet aircraft and the only flying <a href="/wiki/Gloster_Meteor" title="Gloster Meteor">Gloster Meteor</a> T7.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Since April 2013, it has also operated a museum in the 70,000 square feet (6,500&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) Hangar 404, which was previously used to service the <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Siddeley_Nimrod" title="Hawker Siddeley Nimrod">Hawker Siddeley Nimrods</a> of RAF St Mawgan.<sup id="cite_ref-FlyPastMarch2013_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FlyPastMarch2013-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It has now (April 2017) closed, though some of its exhibits remain elsewhere on the airfield under different ownership.\n</p><p>Testing of Bloodhound at speeds of over 200 miles per hour (320&#160;km/h) was performed on the runway in 2017<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> in preparation for a 500 miles per hour (804 km/h) test run on its new specially created race track at Hakskeen Pan, South Africa in 2019, leading to an attempt on the land speed record in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Potential_future_uses">Potential future uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newquay_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Potential future uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In September 2013, the National Aeronautical Centre (NAC) at <a href="/wiki/Aberporth_Airport" title="Aberporth Airport">Aberporth Airport</a> announced it would use Newquay as its second airport for testing <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">unmanned aerial vehicles</a> (UAVs).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The NAC said it was attracted by the length of the runway and the facilities of Aerohub. The test flights would require 3,000 square miles (7,800&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of segregated airspace over the sea.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In March 2015, the airport reported that the establishment of this segregated area had not yet been agreed with the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">Department for Transport</a>, who stated that negotiations with international bodies to create an appropriate regulatory framework for UAVs were still taking place.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2014, Newquay was one of eight airports shortlisted by the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">Civil Aviation Authority</a> as a possible site for a <a href="/wiki/British_commercial_spaceport" class="mw-redirect" title="British commercial spaceport">UK commercial</a> <a href="/wiki/Spaceport" title="Spaceport">spaceport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The shortlist was reduced to six airports in March 2015, with Newquay still a candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The selection process was abandoned in May 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-ths20160520_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ths20160520-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and
