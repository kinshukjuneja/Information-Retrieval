id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The place name element <i>Lis</i>, along with ancient privileges accorded the town, indicates that the settlement was once a high status \'court\'. A Norman castle was built here after the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Conquest</a>, which eventually fell into disuse in the later Middle Ages. By 1538 when visited by <a href="/wiki/John_Leland_(antiquary)" title="John Leland (antiquary)">John Leland</a> only a few insignificant remains were to be seen.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Sir <a href="/wiki/Sir_Richard_Carew,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Richard Carew, 1st Baronet">Richard Carew</a> writing in 1602 concurred;\n</p>\n<table class="rquote pullquote floatcenter" role="presentation" style="border-collapse:collapse; border-style:none; float:center; margin:0.5em 0.75em; width:33%;">\n\n<tbody><tr style="text-align:left; vertical-align:top;">\n<td style="color:#b2b7f2; font-size:3.3em; font-family:&#39;Times New Roman&#39;,serif; font-weight:bold; padding:4px 2px 2px; width:0.5em; line-height: 1em;">\xe2\x80\x9c\n</td>\n<td style="padding:0 10px;"><i>Of later times, the Castle serued the Earle of Cornwall for one of his houses; but now, that later is worm-eaten out of date and vse. Coynages, Fayres, and markets, (as vitall spirits in a decayed bodie) keepe the inner partes of the towne aliue, while the ruyned skirtes accuse the iniurie of time, and the neglect of industrie.</i><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</td>\n<td style="color:#b2b7f2; font-size:3.3em; font-family:&#39;Times New Roman&#39;,serif; font-weight:bold; padding:4px 2px 2px; text-align:right; vertical-align:bottom; width:0.5em; line-height: 1em;">\xe2\x80\x9d\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td class="rquotecite" colspan="3" style="padding-top:10px; font-size:smaller; line-height:normal; text-align:right;">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Liskeard was one of the 17 <a href="/wiki/Antiqua_maneria" title="Antiqua maneria">Antiqua maneria</a> of the Duchy of Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The market <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">charter</a> was granted by <a href="/wiki/Richard,_Earl_of_Cornwall" class="mw-redirect" title="Richard, Earl of Cornwall">Richard, Earl of Cornwall</a> (brother of <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_England" title="Henry III of England">Henry III</a>) in 1240. Since then, it has been an important centre for agriculture. The seal of the borough of Liskeard was Ar. a fleur-de-lis and perched thereon and respecting each other two birds in chief two annulets and in flank two feathers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Wilkie_Collins" title="Wilkie Collins">Wilkie Collins</a> wrote of his visit to the town in his <i>Rambles Beyond Railways</i> he had a low opinion of it: "that <a href="/wiki/Abomination_of_desolation" title="Abomination of desolation">abomination of desolation</a>, a large agricultural country town".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The town went through a period of economic prosperity during the pre-20th century boom in tin mining, becoming a key centre in the industry as a location for a <a href="/wiki/Stannary" title="Stannary">stannary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tin_coinage" title="Tin coinage">coinage</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" The <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">MPs</a> have included <a href="/wiki/Edward_Gibbon" title="Edward Gibbon">Edward Gibbon</a>, author of <i><a href="/wiki/The_History_of_the_Decline_and_Fall_of_the_Roman_Empire" title="The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire">The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Foot" title="Isaac Foot">Isaac Foot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Liskeard is now part of the <a href="/wiki/South_East_Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="South East Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">South East Cornwall</a> constituency, and is represented by <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Sheryll_Murray" title="Sheryll Murray">Sheryll Murray</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The first school in Liskeard was founded in 1550 on Castle Hill. For a time it was maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Earls_of_St_Germans" class="mw-redirect" title="Earls of St Germans">Earls of St Germans</a>, but it closed around 1834 due to a decline in numbers and financial difficulties. From 1835 a series of private schools existed in the borough, until 1908 when Cornwall Education Committee built the County School at Old Road. From 1945 it was known as <a href="/wiki/Liskeard_Grammar_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Liskeard Grammar School">Liskeard Grammar School</a> until September 1978 when it became the Lower School site of Liskeard School, following amalgamation with the town\'s <a href="/wiki/Secondary_modern_school" title="Secondary modern school">secondary modern school</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Liskeard County Secondary School received its first pupils on Monday 12 September 1960, and was formally opened by the <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister of Education">Minister of Education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_David_Eccles" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir David Eccles">Sir David Eccles</a> on 7 July the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Costing \xc2\xa3100,000, it was built to accommodate around 500 pupils on the site of the current school at Luxtowe. Its glass and steel structure made "free use of fresh air and sunlight" according to local newspaper reports, whilst other modern features included a well-equipped gymnasium, automated central heating and synchronised clocks across the school, operated from the secretary\'s office.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> A new block was opened by the Right Honourable <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Thatcher" title="Margaret Thatcher">Margaret Thatcher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Education_and_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Education and Science">Secretary of State for Education and Science</a> in 1974, following the raising of the school leaving age from 15 years to 16, two years earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Like many similar secondary schools in Cornwall, from the late 1970s it housed the Upper School (3rd Year / Year 9 upwards), when it merged with the town\'s grammar school to create a split-site <a href="/wiki/Comprehensive_school" title="Comprehensive school">comprehensive school</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Twenty years later, with increased pupil numbers requiring many to be taught in temporary buildings, the need for improvements to Liskeard\'s secondary and primary schools was being raised in Parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> By the late 1990s, <a href="/wiki/Liskeard_School_and_Community_College" title="Liskeard School and Community College">Liskeard School and Community College</a> had been extended at Luxstowe, and the Old Road site closed and redeveloped for housing. Further multimillion-pound science and technology facilities were added in 2002, and the original 1960s and 1970s buildings were completely modernised by 2011. As Cornwall\'s only school with an engineering speciality,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> it now caters for approximately 1300 students aged between 11 and 19, and employs around 200 teaching and non-teaching, full- and part-time staff. It also has a creche, a teenage advice and information service, a centre for children with <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a>, and facilities at <a href="/wiki/Moorswater" title="Moorswater">Moorswater</a> where some engineering-based courses are taught.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There are two primary schools in Liskeard: St Martin\'s Church of England (Voluntary Aided) School in Lake Lane and Hillfort Primary School on Old Road. The latter was opened in September 2006 following the renaming of Liskeard Junior School after its merger with Liskeard Infant School.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120324093233/http://www.caradonsss.ik.org/p_Home.ikml">Caradon Short Stay School</a> (previously known as a <a href="/wiki/Pupil_Referral_Unit" title="Pupil Referral Unit">Pupil Referral Unit</a>) is located in West Street, on the site of the former Liskeard Infant School. It provides education for students aged 11\xe2\x80\x9316 from across south east Cornwall who are unable to attend a <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Mainstream school">mainstream school</a> or <a href="/wiki/Special_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Special school">special school</a>. The nearest <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent schools</a> are in Plymouth and Tavistock, Devon.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Liskeard_railway_station" title="Liskeard railway station">Liskeard railway station</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/London_Paddington" class="mw-redirect" title="London Paddington">London Paddington</a> to <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Main_Line" title="Cornish Main Line">Cornish Main Line</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/A38_road" title="A38 road">A38</a> trunk road provide the town with rapid access to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>, the rest of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> and the motorway network. The town is also served by the <a href="/wiki/Looe_Valley_Line" title="Looe Valley Line">Looe Valley branch line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Looe" title="Looe">Looe</a>. There are regular bus services to various parts of Cornwall.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leisure_and_sports">Leisure and sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Leisure and sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There is a leisure centre at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lux_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lux Park (page does not exist)">Lux Park</a> on the north side of the town: there is a bowling club on the southern side. The town has a <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">Non-League football</a> club <a href="/wiki/Liskeard_Athletic_F.C." title="Liskeard Athletic F.C.">Liskeard Athletic F.C.</a> who play at Lux park. The town also has a rugby and cricket club who are both well-supported. The town has a <a href="/wiki/King_George%27s_Fields" title="King George&#39;s Fields">King George V Playing Field</a>. Live music and various theatrical events frequently take place in the unusual but acoustically good <a href="/wiki/Carnglaze_Caverns" title="Carnglaze Caverns">Carnglaze Caverns</a> just to the north.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leisure_trails">Leisure trails</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Leisure trails">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are three trails, each has its own blue commemorative plaque (these were unveiled by former town mayor, Sandra Preston).\n</p>\n<ul><li>Footpath from the town to the railway station: the path was built by Thomas Lang, who was a former mayor, in 1890.</li>\n<li>Trail around the north of the town centre, including the Parade and the ornamental fountain. The fountain was given to the town by Michael Loam, whose father (also called <a href="/wiki/Michael_Loam" title="Michael Loam">Michael Loam</a>) invented the <a href="/wiki/Man_engine" title="Man engine">Man engine</a> (a device for lifting men up and down mineshafts, and used in many mines throughout Cornwall &amp; West Devon).</li>\n<li>Trail around the southern part of the town, commemorating <a href="/wiki/John_Richards_Lapenoti%C3%A8re" title="John Richards Lapenoti\xc3\xa8re">Lt. Lapenoti\xc3\xa8re</a>, who brought back the news of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Trafalgar" title="Battle of Trafalgar">Battle of Trafalgar</a> to <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. For this Lt. Lapenoti\xc3\xa8re was given a silver spice sprinkler by <a href="/wiki/King_George_III" class="mw-redirect" title="King George III">King George III</a>. The sprinkler is still owned by the mayor\'s office, and is exhibited occasionally.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Liskeard has its own community radio station \'Liskeard Radio\'. Broadcasting online and at 87.7FM and operating on a temporary annual licence.
