href="/w/index.php?title=Land%27s_End_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Cobham_Air_Routes" title="Cobham Air Routes">Cobham Air Routes</a> started to plan the route linking the Isles of Scilly to the mainland in 1935.  Cobham was subsequently acquired by <a href="/wiki/Olley_Air_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Olley Air Service">Olley Air Service</a>, whose subsidiary Channel Air Ferries developed the Land\'s End airport and started a service to the island of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a> on 15 September 1937, flying <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Dragon" title="De Havilland Dragon">de Havilland Dragons</a>.  A <a href="/wiki/Hangar" title="Hangar">hangar</a> was brought from <a href="/wiki/Squires_Gate_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Squires Gate Airport">Squires Gate Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Blackpool" title="Blackpool">Blackpool</a>.  On St Mary\'s, until its own airport opened in 1939, the planes landed on the golf course.<sup id="cite_ref-cwlife_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwlife-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1938 Great Western and Southern Airlines took over Olley Air Service and Channel Air Ferries. It continued the service throughout <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, during which it replaced the Dragons with <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Dragon_Rapide" title="De Havilland Dragon Rapide">de Havilland Dragon Rapides</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bea5_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bea5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 1 February 1947 the airline and the service were taken over by <a href="/wiki/British_European_Airways" title="British European Airways">BEA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bea5_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bea5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On 2 May 1964, BEA replaced the Dragon Rapides with a single <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_S-61" title="Sikorsky S-61">Sikorsky S-61</a> helicopter, operated by <a href="/wiki/BEA_Helicopters" class="mw-redirect" title="BEA Helicopters">BEA Helicopters</a>.  On 1 September 1964 the helicopter service was moved from Land\'s End to the new <a href="/wiki/Penzance_Heliport" title="Penzance Heliport">Penzance Heliport</a>, although BEA occasionally chartered <a href="/wiki/Britten-Norman_Islander" class="mw-redirect" title="Britten-Norman Islander">Britten-Norman Islanders</a> to fly from Land\'s End when the helicopter was unavailable.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Between 1966 and 1970, Scillonia Airways operated from the airport, flying Rapides. Westward Airways was founded at the airport in 1970,<sup id="cite_ref-Wickstead_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wickstead-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and operated a flying school there from 1971 to 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-cwlife_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwlife-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1984 Isles of Scilly Skybus started operations at the airport, initially freight and <a href="/wiki/Air_charter" title="Air charter">charter</a>, then scheduled services from April 1987,<sup id="cite_ref-Wickstead_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wickstead-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> flying Islanders and <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada_DHC-6_Twin_Otter" title="De Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter">de Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cwlife_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cwlife-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Skybus became the sole remaining air link for the Isles of Scilly from October 2012, when the helicopter service from Penzance ended.<sup id="cite_ref-mdg_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mdg-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 9 April 2013 a new \xc2\xa31 million passenger terminal was officially opened. Work had begun in July 2012 and included new baggage handling and arrivals facilities and a new <a href="/wiki/Control_tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Control tower">control tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Up to 2014, all of the airport\'s runways were grass. During the winters of 2012/13 and 2013/14 the airport closed for long periods when the runways became waterlogged, and flights were temporarily moved from Land\'s End to <a href="/wiki/Newquay_Cornwall_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Newquay Cornwall Airport">Newquay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mdg_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mdg-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In May 2013, ISSC and the Council of the Isles of Scilly submitted a bid for finance from the <a href="/wiki/European_Regional_Development_Fund" title="European Regional Development Fund">European Regional Development Fund</a> for runway resurfacing at Land\'s End, together with various improvements at St Mary\'s.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In May 2014 the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> gave its approval. The cost of the planned <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">asphalting</a> at Land\'s End was stated to be \xc2\xa32.6 million,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> for which the European fund\'s contribution was \xc2\xa31.3 million. The airport closed on 4 July 2014 for the asphalting of two runways, with Skybus flights diverted to Newquay, and it reopened on 29 July.<sup id="cite_ref-asphalted_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-asphalted-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airlines_and_destinations">Airlines and destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
