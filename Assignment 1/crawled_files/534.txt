(1807-1813)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>John Allen</b> (December 30, 1771<sup id="cite_ref-kye_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kye-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lanham_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lanham-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> \xe2\x80\x93 January 22, 1813) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> politician and army officer who was killed in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>.\n</p><p>Allen was born in <a href="/wiki/Rockbridge_County,_Virginia" title="Rockbridge County, Virginia">Rockbridge County, Virginia</a>, and moved with his father to <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a> in 1779.<sup id="cite_ref-Lanham_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lanham-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  He went to school in <a href="/wiki/Bardstown,_Kentucky" title="Bardstown, Kentucky">Bardstown, Kentucky</a> and studied law in <a href="/wiki/Staunton,_Virginia" title="Staunton, Virginia">Staunton, Virginia</a>. He then returned to Kentucky to law practice in <a href="/wiki/Shelbyville,_Kentucky" title="Shelbyville, Kentucky">Shelbyville</a>.  He was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_Senate" title="Kentucky Senate">Kentucky Senate</a> in 1807, and served until his death.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cemetery,_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Cemetery%2C_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg/220px-Cemetery%2C_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg" width="220" height="434" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Cemetery%2C_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg/330px-Cemetery%2C_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Cemetery%2C_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg/440px-Cemetery%2C_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="462" data-file-height="912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cemetery,_Military_Monument-3_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Allen\'s name on the Kentucky War Memorial in <a href="/wiki/Frankfort,_Kentucky" title="Frankfort, Kentucky">Frankfort, KY</a><br /> along with his fellow officers who died at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Frenchtown" title="Battle of Frenchtown">River Raisin Massacre</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Early in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>, Allen raised the 1st Regiment of Riflemen to serve under General <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Harrison" title="William Henry Harrison">William Henry Harrison</a>. Allen was commissioned its colonel. He was killed in action while leading his men at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Frenchtown" title="Battle of Frenchtown">Battle of Frenchtown</a> on the <a href="/wiki/River_Raisin" title="River Raisin">River Raisin</a> in southeast <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Allen_County,_Kentucky" title="Allen County, Kentucky">Allen County, Kentucky</a> was named for him in 1815.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is also honored by <a href="/wiki/Allen_County,_Ohio" title="Allen County, Ohio">Allen County, Ohio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Allen_County,_Indiana" title="Allen County, Indiana">Allen County, Indiana</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Allen_(soldier)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Helen Winemiller Wood.
