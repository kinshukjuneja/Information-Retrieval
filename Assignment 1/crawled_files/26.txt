links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The island has a length of 2 kilometres (1.2&#160;mi), a maximum width of 1 kilometre (0.62&#160;mi) and an area of 134 hectares (330 acres), including Shipman Head, which rises to 42 metres (138&#160;ft) at the northern end of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Bryher lies to the west of <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>, and is separated from that island by the Tresco Channel, once the main anchorage for the islands and now an area where sandflats are exposed at low tide. Off the southern end of Bryher is the uninhabited island of <a href="/wiki/Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Samson, Isles of Scilly">Samson</a>. It is possible to walk between the three islands at the lowest <a href="/wiki/Springs_(tide)" class="mw-redirect" title="Springs (tide)">spring</a> tides.\n</p><p>The settlement at the Pool / Hell Bay Hotel is the westernmost in England. Without the <a href="/wiki/Tidal_island" title="Tidal island">tidal island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gugh" title="Gugh">Gugh</a> included, <a href="/wiki/St_Agnes,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Agnes, Isles of Scilly">St Agnes</a> is marginally smaller than Bryher in either population or area; however if Gugh is included with St Agnes, which is the common interpretation, then Bryher is (again, marginally) the smallest of the populated isles of Scilly in area and population.\n</p><p>The centre of Bryher is mainly low-lying with arable fields, pasture and housing and is where most of the population of 84 live. On the west side is the <a href="/wiki/Brackish" class="mw-redirect" title="Brackish">brackish</a> Great Pool overlooked by the Hell Bay Hotel and in the south are sandy beaches, a common feature on the island, Rushy Bay being an example. The island lies within the Isles of Scilly <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage Coast">Heritage Coast</a>, is part of the Isles of Scilly <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> and untennanted land is leased by the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust" title="Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a> which is using ponies and <a href="/wiki/North_Devon_cattle" title="North Devon cattle">red ruby</a> cattle<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> to graze the overgrown areas as part of the <i>Waves of Heath</i> project.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The name of the island is recorded as <i>Brayer</i> in 1336 and <i>Brear</i> in 1500.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hell_Bay">Hell Bay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hell Bay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HellBay.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/HellBay.JPG/180px-HellBay.JPG" width="180" height="135" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/HellBay.JPG/270px-HellBay.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/HellBay.JPG/360px-HellBay.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="1728" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HellBay.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hell Bay, Bryher.</div></div></div>\n<p>The infamous Hell Bay can be found on Bryher. This Atlantic-facing cove became a notorious place for shipwrecks over the 18th and 19th century though there is little evidence to support this, most ships having been wrecked before they even reached here.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bar_Quay">Bar Quay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bar Quay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the centre of the island is Bar Quay, which was built in 1990 by volunteers for the television programme <i><a href="/wiki/Challenge_Anneka" title="Challenge Anneka">Challenge Anneka</a></i>. It is known to many islanders as \'Anna-Quay\'. In 2007 it was replaced by a new concrete quay, as part of a Scilly-wide programme of quay rebuilding.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_history">Natural history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Natural history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are three <a href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" class="mw-redirect" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI) on Bryher. The Shipman Head and Shipman Down SSSI was first designated in 1971 and covers over 40 ha of the northern part of the island. Waved maritime <a href="/wiki/Heath" title="Heath">heath</a> grows over shallow <a href="/wiki/Podzolic" class="mw-redirect" title="Podzolic">podzolic</a> soils which are underlain by <a href="/wiki/Hercynian" class="mw-redirect" title="Hercynian">Hercynian</a> <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a>. Rare plants include the Red Data Book (RDB) Orange Bird\xe2\x80\x99s-foot (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ornithopus_pinnatus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ornithopus pinnatus (page does not exist)">Ornithopus pinnatus</a></i>) and the nationally scarce Hairy Bird\xe2\x80\x99s-foot trefoil (<i><a href="/wiki/Lotus_subbiflorus" title="Lotus subbiflorus">Lotus subbiflorus</a></i>). <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">Lichens</a> include (<i><a href="/wiki/Lobaria_pulmonaria" title="Lobaria pulmonaria">Lobaria pulmonaria</a></i>) and (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teleoschistes_flavicans&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Teleoschistes flavicans (page does not exist)">Teleoschistes flavicans</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Shipman_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shipman-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>On the west side of the island is Great Pool which is part of the Pool of Bryher and Popplestone Bank SSSI. It is separated from the sea by a <a href="/wiki/Storm_beach" title="Storm beach">storm beach</a> and small dune system, and is the only natural <a href="/wiki/Brackish" class="mw-redirect" title="Brackish">brackish</a> lagoon on Scilly with plants such as Saltmarsh Rush (<i><a href="/wiki/Juncus_gerardii" title="Juncus gerardii">Juncus gerardii</a></i>) and Beaked Tasselweed (<i><a href="/wiki/Ruppia_maritima" title="Ruppia maritima">Ruppia maritima</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Pool_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pool-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Covering 12 hectares (30 acres) of the southern part of the island is the Rushy Bay and Heathy Hill SSSI which has a number of nationally rare plants. An Isles of Scilly speciality is the Dwarf Pansy (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viola_kitaibeliana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Viola kitaibeliana (page does not exist)">Viola kitaibeliana</a></i>) which grows nowhere else in Great Britain. It is locally abundant on Bryher and thousands can be found in May in short turf and bare sand.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Unfortunately a storm in 2008 reduced the numbers of plants seen; there are also small colonies on <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Te%C3%A4n" title="Te\xc3\xa4n">Te\xc3\xa4n</a>. Orange Bird\xe2\x80\x99s-foot, Small Adder\xe2\x80\x99s-tongue (<i><a href="/wiki/Ophioglossum_azoricum" title="Ophioglossum azoricum">Ophioglossum azoricum</a></i>) and Autumn\xe2\x80\x99s Lady\xe2\x80\x99s-tresses (<i><a href="/wiki/Spiranthes_spiralis" title="Spiranthes spiralis">Spiranthes spiralis</a></i>) grow on Heathy Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-Rushy_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rushy-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding_birds">Breeding birds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding birds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Shipman head has seven species of breeding seabirds:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Kittiwake (<i><a href="/wiki/Rissa_tridactyla" class="mw-redirect" title="Rissa tridactyla">Rissa tridactyla</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Herring Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_argentatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus argentatus">Larus argentatus</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Greater Black-backed Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_marinus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus marinus">L marinus</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Lesser Black-backed Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_fuscus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus fuscus">L fuscus</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Razorbill (<i><a href="/wiki/Alca_torda" class="mw-redirect" title="Alca torda">Alca torda</a></i>)</li>\n<li>Shag (<i><a href="/wiki/Phalacrocorax_aristotelis" class="mw-redirect" title="Phalacrocorax aristotelis">Phalacrocorax aristotelis</a></i>)</li>\n<li>European Storm Petrel (<i><a href="/wiki/Hydrobates_pelagicus" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydrobates pelagicus">Hydrobates pelagicus</a></i>)</li></ul>\n<p>Ringed Plover (<i><a href="/wiki/Charadrius_hiaticula" class="mw-redirect" title="Charadrius hiaticula">Charadrius hiaticula</a></i>) breed on Shipman Down.<sup id="cite_ref-Shipman_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shipman-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_parish_and_ward">Civil parish and ward</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Civil parish and ward">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Bryher_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/220px-Bryher_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Bryher_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/330px-Bryher_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png 1.5x, parishes in England">civil parishes</a> of the Isles of Scilly, which are also <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">wards</a>. The civil parish and ward include several uninhabited islands and rocks, including the <a href="/wiki/Norrard_Rocks" title="Norrard Rocks">Norrard Rocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gweal" class="mw-redirect" title="Gweal">Gweal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zantman%27s_Rock" title="Zantman&#39;s Rock">Zantman\'s Rock</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Crim_Rocks" title="Crim Rocks">Crim Rocks</a> (the westernmost place of England).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Bryher returns two councillors to the Council of the Isles of Scilly, the same as the other "off-island" wards. The civil parish is not functional however, and there is no <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">council</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parish_meeting" title="Parish meeting">meeting</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visiting_the_island">Visiting the island</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Visiting the island">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Varied accommodation is available on the island. There are guesthouses and self-catering cottages scattered across the island. The campsite is located close to the north end of the island overlooking both coasts. The Hell Bay Hotel is located close to the coast on the west side. \n</p><p>Two quays are used (depending on tides) by boats which take tourists between Bryher and other islands, including <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>. On some low tides it is possible to walk between Bryher and Tresco and even Samson, the uninhabited island to the south. There is also safe anchorage for small yachts in the channel and Green Bay.\nIn the north of the island are the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bryher.co">Fraggle Rock Bar</a>, Vine Cafe and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://bryhershop.co.uk/">Bryher shop</a>, which opened in 2011.\n</p><p>The Golden Eagle Gig shed on the west coast is the studio of the artist <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.rpearce.net/">Richard Pearce</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Church,_Bryher" title="All Saints&#39; Church, Bryher">All Saints\' Church, Bryher</a> is located on the island.\n</p><p>Local activities include boating, walking and watching wildlife.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Other_uses_of_.22Bryher.22"></span><span
