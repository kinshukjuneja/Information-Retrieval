href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, England.  It lies close to sea level south of the <a href="/wiki/Polden_Hills" title="Polden Hills">Polden Hills</a>, historically largely marsh (or "moor" in its older sense).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The eastern part is known as <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Sedgemoor" title="King&#39;s Sedgemoor">King\'s Sedgemoor</a>, and the western part <a href="/wiki/West_Sedgemoor" title="West Sedgemoor">West Sedgemoor</a>.  Sedgemoor is part of the area now known as the <a href="/wiki/Somerset_Levels_and_Moors" class="mw-redirect" title="Somerset Levels and Moors">Somerset Levels and Moors</a>.  Historically the area was known as the site of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sedgemoor" title="Battle of Sedgemoor">Battle of Sedgemoor</a>.\n</p><p>Sedgemoor has given its name to a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> formed on 1 April 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, by a merger of the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bridgwater" title="Bridgwater">Bridgwater</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Burnham-on-Sea" title="Burnham-on-Sea">Burnham-on-Sea</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bridgwater_Rural_District" title="Bridgwater Rural District">Bridgwater Rural District</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/Axbridge_Rural_District" title="Axbridge Rural District">Axbridge Rural District</a>.  The district covers a larger area than the historical Sedgemoor, extending north of the Polden Hills across the Somerset Levels and Moors to the <a href="/wiki/Mendip_Hills" title="Mendip Hills">Mendip Hills</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sedgemoor" title="Battle of Sedgemoor">Battle of Sedgemoor</a> 1685</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sedgemoor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Light industry now predominates, but traditional trades including <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">peat</a> extraction, <a href="/wiki/Willow" title="Willow">willow</a> crafts and <a href="/wiki/Cider" title="Cider">cider</a> making may still be found, in addition to livestock farming.  The <a href="/wiki/River_Parrett" title="River Parrett">River Parrett</a> provides a source of eels (<a href="/wiki/Anguilla_anguilla" class="mw-redirect" title="Anguilla anguilla">anguilla anguilla</a>) and elvers from January through to May.\n</p><p>Also notable is the new Isleport trading estate at Highbridge, which houses many global businesses such as Geest (Isleport Foods) who make yoghurt under franchise to Ski &amp; Muller, Brake Brothers who supply the catering trade, BFP wholesale who supply dry goods to bakeries etc., Woodbury &amp; Haines who supply furniture globally, Polybeam Limited who supply GRP radio masts to customers such as Marconi, and also AT&amp;T whose centre there controls all internet cable traffic to and from the US.\n</p><p>Industry in Bridgwater has also seen major growth recently with the opening of "Express Park" which houses Gerber Foods (a global fruit juice supplier), <a href="/wiki/NHS_Supply_Chain" title="NHS Supply Chain">NHS Logistics</a> depot and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Stobart_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Eddie Stobart Ltd.">Eddie Stobart</a> depot.  South of Bridgwater, at Huntworth, is a large <a href="/wiki/Somerfield_(UK_retailer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Somerfield (UK retailer)">Somerfield</a> depot which supplies their Somerfield and former Kwik Save stores in the south west.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sedgemoor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sedgemoor_District_Council">Sedgemoor District Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sedgemoor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sedgemoor District Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Sedgemoor District Council has traditionally been <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> run since its creation in 1974 when it was merged with the old Bridgwater &amp; Burnham-On-Sea Urban District Councils. Past voting trends have placed strong cores of <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> voters in Bridgwater Town, with Conservative support coming from the Villages such as <a href="/wiki/Pawlett" class="mw-redirect" title="Pawlett">Pawlett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wedmore" title="Wedmore">Wedmore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shipham" title="Shipham">Shipham</a>. <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrats</a> fared well in Burnham and Highbridge areas. In 1995, the Liberal Democrats and Labour took 26 seats together, out-numbering the Conservatives 24 seats, and they formed a coalition. This continued until 1999, when the Conservatives re-took Sedgemoor, crushing the Liberal Democrats from 12 seats, to just 2. The Liberal Democrats lost even their safe seat of Highbridge to Labour.\n</p><p>In 2000, a Conservative Councillor died, and a By-Election was called in his seat of Huntpsill and Pawlett. Previous Liberal Democrat Councillor Marilyn Wallace fought the seat, and re-took it with 56% of the vote, bringing the Liberal Democrats back up to 3 seats.\n</p><p>In 2003, the Conservatives extended their majority to 35 seats of 50, leaving the Liberal Democrats on just 1 Seat, Which was held by long term veteran Liberal Democrat, Mike Mansfield, who actually took the seat from the Conservatives in Burnham South, a previously Liberal Democrat safe seat. Labour remained on 14.\n</p><p>In 2007, the Conservatives held a status quo of 35 seats, however the Liberal Democrats gained 3 up to 4 total, all of which were in their traditional safe seat of Highbridge. This included the election of their youngest Councillor in the South west, Councillor Joe Leach, who was aged just 19 years. Mike Mansfield was elected Leader of the Liberal Democrat group, until the untimely death of his wife. Newly elected Councillor Danny Titcombe then was elected leader of the Group. on 14 April 2008, Cllr. Danny Titcombe announced his defection to the Conservative Party, effectively boosting their majority to 36 seats. Despite calls of resignation from all sides, Cllr. Titcombe continued in his role. At 20 Years old, Cllr Joe Leach became the youngest group Leader in the Country, and still continues to lead his party on the Council at 23. The Labour Party dropped to 11 seats, losing 2 in highbridge to the Liberal Democrats, and 1 in Bridgwater Bower to the Conservatives. The Conservatives lost 1 in Highbridge to the Liberal Democrats (before defection). In 2010, Woolavington Councillor Roger Lavers, who was the Labour Group Leader, died suddenly of a brain haemorrhage, triggering a by-election in the 1 Member ward, which saw Labour drop from holding the seat, to third behind the Liberal Democrats who hadn\'t stood in the seat since 1991, and the Conservatives gain the seat, boosting their total number to 37 of 50 councillors. Labour dropped to 10. Councillor Kathy Pearce, Labour member for Bridgwater Hamp, was elected as the new Labour group Leader.\n</p><p>In the 2011 Local Council elections, The Conservatives held the council with a reduced number of seats. Labour took back all Bridgwater seats except the Windham ward, and the Independents gained 2 seats at the expense of the Liberal Democrats, who held their leader\'s seat in Highbridge &amp; Burnham Marine, and Burnham Central. Notable casualties included Vice-Chairman of the Council Mike Creswell, in Bridgwater Fairfax.\n</p><p>After the elections, Labour Leader Kathy Pearce stood down as Leader of the Opposition and Labour group, and was replaced by newly elected Councillor, Mick Lerry. In 2012, Liberal Democrat Leader, Joe Leach, resigned his seat on the council to pursue a new career path in Banking. His replacement was Councillor Helen Groves, who was the newly elected LibDem for Highbridge &amp; Burnham Marine in the following by-election.\n</p><p>The Conservative Leader is Cllr. Duncan McGinty, who is also leader of council. He succeeded Cllr. John Lang in 2006.\n</p><p>The Labour Leader is Cllr. Mick Lerry, who succeeded Cllr. Kathy Pearce in 2011.\n</p><p>The Liberal Democrat Leader is Cllr. Helen Groves, who succeeded Cllr.
