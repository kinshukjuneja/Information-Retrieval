href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is centred on the town of <a href="/wiki/Swindon" title="Swindon">Swindon</a> and forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial county</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a>.  It was formed in 1974 as the <b>Thamesdown</b> district of Wiltshire, from former areas of the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> of Swindon (created 1900) and Highworth Rural District (created 1894).\n</p><p>On 1 April 1997 it was made administratively independent of <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_County_Council" title="Wiltshire County Council">Wiltshire County Council</a> (with its council becoming a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  It adopted the name Swindon on 24 April 1997. The former Thamesdown name and logo are used by the main local bus operator, <a href="/wiki/Thamesdown_Transport" class="mw-redirect" title="Thamesdown Transport">Thamesdown Transport Limited</a>.<sup id="Swindon_Borough_Council">Swindon Borough Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borough_of_Swindon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Swindon Borough Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Swindon_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon local elections">Swindon local elections</a></div>\n<p>The council follows a leader and cabinet model<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and has 57 elected members. Elections are held in three out of every four years, with one third of the seats being elected at each election. From the first election in 1996 to the <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Council_election,_2000" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon Council election, 2000">2000 election</a>, Labour had a majority on the council. Following a period where no party had a majority the Conservatives gained a majority at the <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Council_election,_2003" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon Council election, 2003">2003 election</a> and have held control since then.
