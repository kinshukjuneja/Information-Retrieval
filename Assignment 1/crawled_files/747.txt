Together with the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_League" title="Arizona League">Arizona League</a>, it forms the lowest rung on the North American minor-league ladder.\n</p><p>GCL teams play at the minor league spring training complexes of their parent <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> clubs and are owned by those parent clubs. Admission is not charged and no concessions are operated at the teams\' games. The players assigned to this level are first-year players who are drafted in the MLB entry draft a few weeks prior to the start of the GCL season, and emphasis is therefore placed on skill id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_Coast_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Prior to the formation of this league, three separate leagues used the Gulf Coast League name, a 1907\xe2\x80\x931908 Class D league, a 1926 class D league and a 1950\xe2\x80\x931953 Class C League. All three leagues operated around the Gulf coasts of <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Complex-based baseball leagues, which played before sparse crowds and often scheduled morning games to avoid the summer heat and afternoon thunderstorms, were adopted after the drastic shrinking of minor league baseball during the 1950s and 1960s. MLB teams needed an entry level to professional baseball for 18- and 19-year-old players graduating from high schools or signed from Latin America. They are typically considered the lowest rung on the minor league ladder, a notch below other Rookie-level leagues such as the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_League" title="Appalachian League">Appalachian</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_League_(baseball)" title="Pioneer League (baseball)">Pioneer</a> circuits.\n</p><p>The league was founded in 1964 as the <b>Sarasota Rookie League</b> with four teams playing in <a href="/wiki/Sarasota" class="mw-redirect" title="Sarasota">Sarasota</a>. It was originally intended to be the Gulf Coast division of a statewide rookie league, with the eastern division based in <a href="/wiki/Cocoa,_Florida" title="Cocoa, Florida">Cocoa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  However, the eastern and western teams never played each other. The SRL\'s four teams consisted of squads sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Braves_(1953%E2%80%9365)" class="mw-redirect" title="Milwaukee Braves (1953\xe2\x80\x9365)">Milwaukee Braves</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_Braves" class="mw-redirect" title="Gulf Coast Braves">SRL Braves</a>, managed by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Snyder_(baseball)" title="Paul Snyder (baseball)">Paul Snyder</a>, future Atlanta <a href="/wiki/Farm_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Farm system">farm system</a> director, won the championship with a 36\xe2\x80\x9323 record.\n</p>\n<div>\n\n&#32;\n<table class="multicol" role="presentation" style="border-collapse: collapse; padding: 0; border: 0; background:transparent; width:100%;"><tbody><tr>\n<td style="width: 50%;text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/SRL_Braves" class="mw-redirect" title="SRL Braves">SRL Braves</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/SRL_Cardinals" class="mw-redirect" title="SRL Cardinals">SRL Cardinals</a></li></ul>\n<p>&#32;\n</p>\n</td>\n<td style="width: 50%;text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/SRL_White_Sox" class="mw-redirect" title="SRL White Sox">SRL White Sox</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/SRL_Yankees" class="mw-redirect" title="SRL Yankees">SRL Yankees</a></li></ul>\n<p>&#32;\n</p>\n</td></tr></tbody></table></div>\n<p>The league added teams in <a href="/wiki/Bradenton" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradenton">Bradenton</a> in 1965 and changed its name to the <b>Florida Rookie League</b>.\n</p>\n<div>\n\n&#32;\n<table class="multicol" role="presentation" style="border-collapse: collapse; vertical-align: top;">\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/FRL_Twins" class="mw-redirect" title="FRL Twins">FRL Twins</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/FRL_White_Sox" class="mw-redirect" title="FRL White Sox">FRL White Sox</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/FRL_Yankees" class="mw-redirect" title="FRL Yankees">FRL Yankees</a></li></ul>\n<p>&#32;\n</p>\n</td></tr></tbody></table></div>\n<p>The league adopted its current name, Gulf Coast League, for the 1966 season. It expanded to Florida\'s east coast in the 1990s.\n</p><p>On June 21, 2016, the GCL hired Jen Pawol, the first female umpire in <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> since 2007, and the first in the GCL since 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  In 2017 the GCL hired another woman umpire, Emma Charlesworth-Seiler.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="League_procedures">League procedures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_Coast_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: League procedures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The league plays a 60-game season that runs from mid-June to late August. Teams in the league are divided into four divisions: <b>East</b>, <b>Northeast</b>, <b>Northwest</b>, and <b>South</b>. The four division winners play in a one-game semifinal; the team with the best regular-season record plays the division winner with the lowest record, while the division winner with the second-best record plays the division winner with the third-best record. Should the Northeast and Northwest Divisions finish 1st and 4th, the semifinal matchups place 1st vs. 3rd and 2nd vs. 4th. The semifinal winners meet in a best-of-3 game series for the Gulf Coast League championship.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_teams">Current teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulf_Coast_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Current teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>GCL teams are not referred to by their home city, but simply by their parent club\'s name, the prefix "GCL" or "Gulf Coast" if necessary to differentiate between them and another club sharing the nickname, and a cardinal number if the parent club sponsors more than one team in the league. Some of these teams share stadiums with their club\'s High-A affiliate in the <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_League" title="Florida State League">Florida State League</a>, which can lead to confusion, as FSL teams <i>do</i> use the city name (e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Tarpons" title="Tampa Tarpons">Tampa Tarpons</a>, three levels up from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_League_Yankees" title="Gulf Coast League Yankees">GCL Yankees 1 and GCL Yankees 2</a>, who also play in Tampa).\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a>, <a
