class="mw-body-content"><!-- CentralNotice --></div><div class="mw-indicators mw-body-content">\n</div>\n<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">1998 Major League Baseball expansion</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>1998 Major League Baseball expansion</b> resulted in the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB)\'s 29th and 30th teams beginning play for the <a href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1998 Major League Baseball season">1998 season</a>. After initiating an expansion committee in March 1994, 27 groups representing nine cities submitted bids for the proposed expansion teams. After a yearlong process, on March 9, 1995, the league awarded the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> franchise to <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> franchise to <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Devil_Rays" class="mw-redirect" title="Tampa Bay Devil Rays">Tampa Bay Devil Rays</a> (currently the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</a>), would subsequently begin play for the 1998 season.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>With the successes of the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" class="mw-redirect" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> following the 1993 expansion, Major League Baseball by 1994 was looking to expand again from 28 to 30 teams. On March 2, 1994, MLB created an expansion committee to evaluate the feasibility of expansion.<sup id="cite_ref-March_3,_1994_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-March_3,_1994-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Although both Phoenix and Tampa were believed to be overwhelming favorites for the franchises, other cities were invited to bid for the proposed franchises.<sup id="cite_ref-March_3,_1994_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-March_3,_1994-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The committee was headed by <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> General Partner <a href="/wiki/John_Harrington_(Red_Sox_CEO)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Harrington (Red Sox CEO)">John Harrington</a>, with others serving on the committee including <a href="/wiki/Bill_Giles_(baseball)" title="Bill Giles (baseball)">Bill Giles</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Reinsdorf" title="Jerry Reinsdorf">Jerry Reinsdorf</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Steinbrenner" title="George Steinbrenner">George Steinbrenner</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, Stanton Cook of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Jacobs_(businessman)" title="Richard Jacobs (businessman)">Richard Jacobs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stan_Kasten" title="Stan Kasten">Stan Kasten</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a>, the president of the National League, <a href="/wiki/Leonard_S._Coleman,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Leonard S. Coleman, Jr.">Leonard S. Coleman, Jr.</a>, and the president of the American League, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Brown_(third_baseman)" title="Bobby Brown (third baseman)">Bobby Brown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-March_3,_1994_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-March_3,_1994-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By June, the league announced that all cities with interest in the proposed franchises should submit their intentions by June 24.<sup id="cite_ref-June_10,_1994_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-June_10,_1994-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By June 30, the committee released the nine communities that submitted information to MLB for the proposed franchises.<sup id="cite_ref-July_1,_1994_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-July_1,_1994-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> placed expansion requests,<sup id="cite_ref-July_1,_1994_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-July_1,_1994-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Other cities mentioned as potential expansion locations which did not officially submit an application to the league included <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-June_10,_1994_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-June_10,_1994-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After whittling down the field to four finalists (<a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa</a>), MLB announced Phoenix and Tampa as the two expansion franchises on March 9, 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-March_10,_1995_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-March_10,_1995-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Announced to begin play for the 1998 season, each ownership group paid a $130 million expansion fee to enter the league.<sup id="cite_ref-March_10,_1995_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-March_10,_1995-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The ownership groups paid $32 million in July 1995, $25 million in July 1996, $40 million in July 1997 and $33 million in November 1997. In addition, the two expansion teams gave away their rights to $5 million from baseball\'s central fund for each of the five years following expansion (1998\xe2\x80\x932002).\n</p><p>The adding of the 29th and 30th team caused the American League and the National League to have fifteen teams each. Because of the odd number of teams, only seven games could possibly be scheduled in each league on any given day. Thus, one team in each league would have to be idle on any given day. This would have made it difficult for scheduling, in terms of travel days and the need to end the season before October. In order for MLB officials to continue primarily intraleague play, both leagues would need to carry an even number of teams, so the decision was made to move one club from the AL Central to the NL Central - it was ultimately then-acting Commissioner <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a> (who at the time also owned the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>) who agreed to have his own franchise change leagues.\n</p><p>The 1998 expansion remains, as of 2018, the most recent to be conducted in Major League Baseball. MLB would later reverse the imbalance created by the 1998 expansion when, following the sale of the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>, the new owners agreed to move their franchise to the American League. Following that transaction, MLB consequently altered its season structure so as to include interleague play throughout the regular season.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-March_3,_1994-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-March_3,_1994_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-March_3,_1994_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-March_3,_1994_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation news">Justice, Richard (March 3, 1994). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/washingtonpost/access/72228611.html?dids=72228611:72228611&amp;FMT=ABS&amp;FMTS=ABS:FT&amp;type=current&amp;date=Mar+03%2C+1994&amp;author=Richard+Justice&amp;pub=The+Washington+Post+%28pre-1997+Fulltext%29&amp;desc=Baseball+Eyes+Addition+Of+Its+29th%2C+30th+Teams&amp;pqatl=google">"Baseball eyes addition of its 29th, 30th teams"</a>.
