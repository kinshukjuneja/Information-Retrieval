title="Oomycete">oomycetes</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Hosts</th><td>\nplants</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Downy mildew</b> refers to any of several types of <a href="/wiki/Oomycete" title="Oomycete">oomycete</a> microbes that are <a href="/wiki/Obligate_parasite" title="Obligate parasite">obligate parasites</a> of plants. Downy mildews exclusively belong to <a href="/wiki/Peronosporaceae" title="Peronosporaceae">Peronosporaceae</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commercial</a> <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, they are a particular problem for growers of <a href="/wiki/Cruciferae" class="mw-redirect" title="Cruciferae">crucifers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grape" title="Grape">grapes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">vegetables</a> that grow on vines. The prime example is <i><a href="/wiki/Peronospora_farinosa" title="Peronospora farinosa">Peronospora farinosa</a></i> featured in NCBI-Taxonomy<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and HYP3.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This pathogen does not produce survival structures in the northern states of the United States, and overwinters as live mildew colonies in Gulf Coast states.  It progresses northward with cucurbit production each spring. Yield loss associated with downy mildew is most likely related to soft rots that occur after plant canopies collapse and sunburn occurs on fruit. Cucurbit downy mildew only affects leaves of <a href="/wiki/Cucurbitaceae" title="Cucurbitaceae">cucurbit</a> plants.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="toclevel-1 tocsection-16"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symptoms">Symptoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Symptoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Initial symptoms include large, angular or blocky, yellow areas visible on the upper surface.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  As lesions mature, they expand rapidly and turn brown.  The under surface of infected leaves appears watersoaked.  Upon closer inspection, a purple-brown mold (see arrow) becomes apparent.  Small spores shaped like footballs can be observed among the mold with a 10x hand lens.  In disease-favorable conditions (cool nights with long dew periods), downy mildew will spread rapidly, destroying leaf tissue without affecting stems or petioles.<sup id="cite_ref-purdue1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purdue1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment_and_management">Treatment and management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Treatment and management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_options">Cultural options</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cultural options">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Because the downy mildew pathogen does not overwinter in midwestern fields, crop rotations and tillage practices do not affect disease development. The pathogen tends to become established in late summer. Therefore, planting early season varieties may further reduce the already minor threat posed by downy mildew.<sup id="cite_ref-purdue1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purdue1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_control">Chemical control</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chemical control">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Fungicides applied specifically for downy mildew control may be unnecessary.  Broad spectrum protectant fungicides such as <a href="/wiki/Chlorothalonil" title="Chlorothalonil">chlorothalonil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mancozeb" title="Mancozeb">mancozeb</a>, and fixed <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> are at least somewhat effective in protecting against downy mildew infection. Systemic fungicides are labeled for use against <a href="/wiki/Cucurbitaceae" title="Cucurbitaceae">cucurbit</a> downy mildew, but are recommended only after diagnosis of this disease has been confirmed.<sup id="cite_ref-purdue1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purdue1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the United States, the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Protection_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> has approved <a href="/wiki/Oxathiapiprolin" title="Oxathiapiprolin">oxathiapiprolin</a> for use against downy mildew.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In Canada, a mixture of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zoxamide&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zoxamide (page does not exist)">zoxamide</a> and mancozeb was registered for control of the mildew under the trademark <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gavel_(fungicide)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gavel (fungicide) (page does not exist)">Gavel (fungicide)</a> as early as 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organic_control">Organic control</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Organic control">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>One way to control downy mildew is to eliminate moisture and humidity around the impacted plants. Watering from below, such as with a drip system, and improve air circulation through selective pruning. In enclosed environments, like in the house or in a greenhouse, reducing the humidity will help as well.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plant_specific_mildews">Plant specific mildews</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Plant specific mildews">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basil">Basil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Basil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Downy mildew of <a href="/wiki/Basil" title="Basil">basil</a> caused by <i>Peronospora belbahrii</i> has been a huge problem for both commercial producers and home growers. The disease was first reported in Italy in 2004,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  was reported in the U.S. in 2007 and 2008<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and has been steadily increasing in prevalence, distribution, and economic importance since then.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cucurbitaceae">Cucurbitaceae</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cucurbitaceae">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Cucurbitaceae" title="Cucurbitaceae">Cucurbitaceae</a> downy mildew (caused by <i><a href="/wiki/Pseudoperonospora_cubensis" title="Pseudoperonospora cubensis">Pseudoperonospora cubensis</a></i>) is specific to cucurbits (e.g., cantaloupe (<i><a href="/wiki/Cucumis_melo" class="mw-redirect" title="Cucumis melo">Cucumis melo</a></i>), cucumber (<i><a href="/wiki/Cucumis_sativus" class="mw-redirect" title="Cucumis sativus">Cucumis sativus</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Pumpkin" title="Pumpkin">pumpkin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Squash_(plant)" class="mw-redirect" title="Squash (plant)">squash</a>, watermelon (<i><a href="/wiki/Citrullus_lanatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Citrullus lanatus">Citrullus lanatus</a></i>) and other members of the gourd family).  The disease is one of the most significant diseases of cucurbits worldwide.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grapes">Grapes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Grapes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><i><a href="/wiki/Plasmopara_viticola" title="Plasmopara viticola">Plasmopara viticola</a></i> is the causal agent of grapevine downy mildew.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hops">Hops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Hop Downy Mildew (caused by <i><a href="/wiki/Pseudoperonospora_humuli" title="Pseudoperonospora humuli">Pseudoperonospora humuli</a></i>) is specific to <a href="/wiki/Hops" title="Hops">hops</a> (<i>Humulus lupulus</i>). The disease is the single most devastating disease in Western United States hopyards, since the microbe thrives in moist climates. Infected young hop <a href="/wiki/Bine_(botany)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bine (botany)">bines</a> become stunted with thickened clusters of pale curled leaves. These spikes have a silvery upper surface, while the undersides of leaves become blackened with spores. These dwarfed spikes are called "basal spikes". \'Lateral\' or \'terminal\' spikes occur further up the vine.  An entire hop crop could be devastated in only a few days.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ornamentals">Ornamentals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ornamentals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A new and particularly aggressive form of <a href="/wiki/Impatiens_downy_mildew" class="mw-redirect" title="Impatiens downy mildew">impatiens downy mildew</a> has recently emerged as a major threat to the cultivation of ornamental <a href="/wiki/Impatiens" title="Impatiens">impatiens</a> in the United States, where they are one of the most popular ornamental plants.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soybeans">Soybeans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Downy_mildew&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Soybeans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><i><a href="/wiki/Peronospora_manshurica" title="Peronospora manshurica">Peronospora manshurica</a></i> infects soybeans, reducing photosynthetic activity, yield, and quality.<sup id="cite_ref-SoyResearch_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoyResearch-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The fungus spreads by <a href="/wiki/Oospore" title="Oospore">oospores</a> on diseased leaves and/or on infected seed. The disease spreads in environments with high humidity and favors temperatures between 20-22&#160;\xc2\xb0C. Tufts of grayish to pale-colored <a href="/wiki/Sporangium" title="Sporangium">sporangiophores</a> on the underside of leaves easily distinguish the infection from other foliar diseases.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The disease is often controlled using the fungicides <a href="/wiki/Mancozeb" title="Mancozeb">mancozeb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maneb" title="Maneb">maneb</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Zineb" title="Zineb">zineb</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SoyResearch_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoyResearch-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunflowers">Sunflowers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
