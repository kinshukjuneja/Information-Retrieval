class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CivilRightsGame5.PNG" class="image"><img alt="CivilRightsGame5.PNG" data-file-height="214" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CivilRightsGame5.PNG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Civil Rights Game</b> was an annual <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> game that honors the history of <a href="/wiki/Civil_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil rights">civil rights</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and marked the unofficial end to the league\'s <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a>.\n</p><p>The first Civil Rights game was played in 2007 .The first two games were held at <a href="/wiki/AutoZone_Park" title="AutoZone Park">AutoZone Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis, Tennessee</a>. The intent of the game was to "embrace baseball\'s history of <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> players", as well as to generate interest for future black players, after a demographics survey revealed that the percentage of black players in the league has dwindled over the past twelve years to just 8.4 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-8.4_percent_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8.4_percent-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The survey also gave the diversity of players in Major League Baseball an A+ grade: while African-Americans in the sport since 1996 dropped from 17 percent to 8 percent, the percentage of Hispanic players (many of them blacks from the Caribbean) increased during that period from 20 percent to 29 percent, and Asian and other minorities increased from 1 percent to 3 percent. The percentage of non-Hispanic white players went down from 62 percent to 60 percent during that period.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, the game became a regular season game. It has not been held since 2016, for reasons unknown.\n</p><p>Baseball Commissioner <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a> commented on air during the first Civil Rights game that the 8 percent total for African-Americans was "a problem that needed to be looked at." <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> news releases related to the game focused on the drop in African-Americans, and quoted former Cleveland pitcher <a href="/wiki/CC_Sabathia" title="CC Sabathia">CC Sabathia</a> on the idea that baseball must do more to promote the game in inner cities, saying, "It\'s not just a problem\xe2\x80\x94it\'s a crisis."<sup id="cite_ref-8.4_percent_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8.4_percent-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In conjunction with the Civil Rights Game, Major League Baseball honors three pioneers of civil rights with the Beacon Awards (Beacon of Life Award, Beacon of Change Award and Beacon of Hope Award).\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Inaugural_game">Inaugural game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Inaugural game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The inaugural game was played on Saturday, March 31, 2007 at 5:30 p.m. and was broadcast nationally on <a href="/wiki/ESPN_Major_League_Baseball" title="ESPN Major League Baseball">ESPN</a> and <a href="/wiki/ESPNHD" class="mw-redirect" title="ESPNHD">ESPNHD</a>. It was announced by ESPN\'s #1 broadcast team of <a href="/wiki/Jon_Miller" title="Jon Miller">Jon Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Morgan" title="Joe Morgan">Joe Morgan</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gammons" title="Peter Gammons">Peter Gammons</a> serving as a field analyst (his role during <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Night_Baseball" title="Sunday Night Baseball">Sunday Night Baseball</a></i> telecasts on ESPN). The game featured the <a href="/wiki/2007_St._Louis_Cardinals_season" title="2007 St. Louis Cardinals season">St. Louis Cardinals</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Cleveland_Indians_season" title="2007 Cleveland Indians season">Cleveland Indians</a>. Both teams wore uniforms reminiscent of those worn in <a href="/wiki/Negro_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Negro League">Negro League</a> games. The Cardinals won 5&#8211;1, receiving a solid five-inning start out of their 2006 closer <a href="/wiki/Adam_Wainwright" title="Adam Wainwright">Adam Wainwright</a>, who got the <a href="/wiki/Win_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Win (baseball)">win</a>.\n</p><p>Selig came under heavy fire from <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a>, who feel that the involvement of the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> was a slap in the face to the <a href="/wiki/Cherokee" title="Cherokee">Cherokee</a> people who still live in the <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis</a> area after the infamous <a href="/wiki/Trail_of_Tears" title="Trail of Tears">Trail of Tears</a> passed through less than 200 years ago. The <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a></i> called the situation a "primer on how to inadvertently stage an ironic insult to a local and large population of Natives" and insinuated that the league has (inadvertently) sabotaged the game by inviting the Indians.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The sports blog <a href="/wiki/Deadspin" title="Deadspin">Deadspin</a> asked the question "If the Indians win, do Native Americans get civil rights?"<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_game">2008 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>On December 3, 2007, league officials announced details for the second annual game. It was played on March 29, 2008. The <a href="/wiki/2008_New_York_Mets_season" title="2008 New York Mets season">New York Mets</a> beat the <a href="/wiki/2008_Chicago_White_Sox_season" title="2008 Chicago White Sox season">Chicago White Sox</a> 3&#8211;2.  <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King_III" title="Martin Luther King III">Martin Luther King III</a> threw out the first pitch.  This has been the lowest attended game, to date, primarily because it was held at a minor league park during cool, rainy weather.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_game">2009 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2009 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>On June 20, 2009, the Civil Rights Game was played for the first time as part of the regular season schedule.  The game took place at <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Ball_Park" title="Great American Ball Park">Great American Ball Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a> between the host <a href="/wiki/2009_Cincinnati_Reds_season" title="2009 Cincinnati Reds season">Cincinnati Reds</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2009_Chicago_White_Sox_season" title="2009 Chicago White Sox season">Chicago White Sox</a>. The White Sox defeated the Reds, 10&#8211;8.  For this game, the teams wore replicas of their 1965 uniforms.  The White Sox became the first team to participate in two Civil Rights Games. The game was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/MLB_Network" title="MLB Network">MLB Network</a> except in the home markets of the two teams that played in the game, Cincinnati (<a href="/wiki/FSN_Ohio" class="mw-redirect" title="FSN Ohio">FSN Ohio</a>) and Chicago (<a href="/wiki/CSN_Chicago" class="mw-redirect" title="CSN Chicago">CSN Chicago</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of the end of the 2011 season, this game marks the last time the White Sox have actually worn white socks.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_game">2010 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Cincinnati again hosted the Civil Rights Game on May 15, 2010. The Reds defeated the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> in a dramatic 4\xe2\x80\x933 game that ended when Reds SS Orlando Cabrera took a relay throw from LF Chris Heisey at the base of the left field wall and gunned down Skip Schumaker at home plate trying to score the tying run from first base on Joe Mather\'s double.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  Both teams wore replicas of their 1954 uniforms for this game, the first season both teams <a href="/wiki/List_of_first_black_Major_League_Baseball_players_by_team_and_date" class="mw-redirect" title="List of first black Major League Baseball players by team and date">fielded their first black players</a>. Again, MLB Network telecast the game except in Cincinnati (Fox Sports Ohio) and St. Louis (<a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_Midwest" title="Fox Sports Midwest">Fox Sports Midwest</a>).\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_game">2011 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2011 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>During the 2010 season, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta, Georgia</a> had been selected to host the 2011 and 2012 Civil Rights Games at <a href="/wiki/Turner_Field" title="Turner Field">Turner Field</a>, home of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Atlanta_Braves_season" title="2011 Atlanta Braves season">Atlanta Braves</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the start of the 2011 season, the 2011 Game was announced to be the May 15 series finale between the Atlanta Braves and <a href="/wiki/2011_Philadelphia_Phillies_season" title="2011 Philadelphia Phillies season">Philadelphia Phillies</a>. Additionally, the festivities have been expanded from two days to four days.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_on_TBS" title="Major League Baseball on TBS">TBS</a> carried the game outside the Philadelphia and Atlanta <a href="/wiki/Media_market" title="Media market">DMAs</a>.\n</p><p>For the Civil Rights Game, the Braves and the Phillies wore their 1974 throwback jerseys to honor <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron" title="Hank Aaron">Hank Aaron</a>\'s record-breaking 715th home run in 1974 as a member of the Atlanta Braves. In the previous game of the series, both teams wore <a href="/wiki/Negro_league_baseball" title="Negro league baseball">Negro League</a> uniforms from their respective cities, the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Black_Crackers" title="Atlanta Black Crackers">Atlanta Black Crackers</a> for the Braves and the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Stars_(baseball)" title="Philadelphia Stars (baseball)">Philadelphia Stars</a> for the Phillies.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The game was a pitching duel against two of the National League\'s best pitchers, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hudson" title="Tim Hudson">Tim Hudson</a> of the Braves and <a href="/wiki/Roy_Halladay" title="Roy Halladay">Roy Halladay</a> of the Phillies. Both pitchers had very good starts, with Hudson going seven and giving up just two runs on a <a href="/wiki/John_Mayberry_Jr." title="John Mayberry Jr.">John Mayberry Jr.</a> home run. Halladay worked eight innings, giving up a leadoff eighth-inning home run to <a href="/wiki/Dan_Uggla" title="Dan Uggla">Dan Uggla</a> to put the Braves in front. <a href="/wiki/Craig_Kimbrel" title="Craig Kimbrel">Craig Kimbrel</a> of the Braves closed the game out, earning his tenth save of the season.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_game">2012 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2012 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Like the 2011 Game, the 2012 Game was held at <a href="/wiki/Turner_Field" title="Turner Field">Turner Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta, Georgia</a>. The Atlanta Braves organization announced on February 21 that the game would be the August 19 series finale versus the <a href="/wiki/2012_Los_Angeles_Dodgers_season" title="2012 Los Angeles Dodgers season">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>. The three-game weekend series from August 17 to 19 will again be incorporated into the Civil Rights Game Weekend, an event that will honor those on and off the field who have paved the way for equal rights for all Americans.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_game">2013 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2013 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The 2013 Game was held at <a href="/wiki/US_Cellular_Field" class="mw-redirect" title="US Cellular Field">US Cellular Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago</a> on August 24, when the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">White Sox</a> beat the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> by a score of 3\xe2\x80\x932.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_game">2014 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2014 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>It was announced on November 19, 2013 that the 2014 Game will be held at <a href="/wiki/Minute_Maid_Park" title="Minute Maid Park">Minute Maid Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston</a> on May 30, when the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Astros</a> host the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Orioles</a>. Houston beat Baltimore 2\xe2\x80\x931. For the game, the Astros were known as the Houston Eagles (after the only Negro League team in Texas) and Baltimore played as the Baltimore Elite Giants.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_game">2015 game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Civil_Rights_Game&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2015 game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The 2015 Game was held on April 15 at <a href="/wiki/Dodger_Stadium" title="Dodger Stadium">Dodger Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> which saw the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Dodgers</a> host the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Mariners</a>. It was the first Civil Rights Game played on the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">West Coast</a>. This is, to date, the last Civil Rights game ever played.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beacon_Awards">Beacon Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
