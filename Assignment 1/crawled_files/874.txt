href="http://www.americanassociationbaseball.com/">americanassociationbaseball.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>American Association of Independent Professional Baseball</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Independent_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent league baseball">independent</a> professional baseball league founded in 2005. It operates in the central United States and Canada, mostly in cities not served by MLB teams or their <a href="/wiki/Minor_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor league baseball">minor league affiliates</a>. Miles Wolff is the league commissioner. League offices are located in <a href="/wiki/Durham,_North_Carolina" title="Durham, North Carolina">Durham, North Carolina</a>.  Though a separate entity, the league shares a commissioner and director of umpires with the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_American_Association_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Canadian American Association of Professional Baseball">Canadian American Association of Professional Baseball</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toclevel-1 tocsection-11"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">8</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Association_of_Independent_Professional_Baseball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The American Association was founded in October 2005 when the <a href="/wiki/St._Paul_Saints" title="St. Paul Saints">St. Paul Saints</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Saltdogs" title="Lincoln Saltdogs">Lincoln Saltdogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sioux_City_Explorers" title="Sioux City Explorers">Sioux City Explorers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sioux_Falls_Canaries" title="Sioux Falls Canaries">Sioux Falls Canaries</a> announced they were leaving the <a href="/wiki/Northern_League_(baseball,_1993%E2%80%932010)" title="Northern League (baseball, 1993\xe2\x80\x932010)">Northern League</a>. Around the same time, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Baseball_League" title="Central Baseball League">Central Baseball League</a> announced it was disbanding after four seasons. The <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth_Cats" title="Fort Worth Cats">Fort Worth Cats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shreveport-Bossier_Captains" title="Shreveport-Bossier Captains">Shreveport-Bossier Sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pensacola_Pelicans" title="Pensacola Pelicans">Pensacola Pelicans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coastal_Bend_Aviators" title="Coastal Bend Aviators">Coastal Bend Aviators</a>, and <a href="/wiki/El_Paso_Diablos" title="El Paso Diablos">El Paso Diablos</a> joined the four former Northern League teams and the expansion <a href="/wiki/St._Joe_Blacksnakes" title="St. Joe Blacksnakes">St. Joe Blacksnakes</a> to form the American Association as a ten-team league. The new league began play in 2006, with a 96-game schedule, which has since expanded to 100 games.\n</p><p>2008 saw the league lose the Blacksnakes and Aviators, with the <a href="/wiki/Texas_AirHogs" title="Texas AirHogs">Grand Prairie AirHogs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wichita_Wingnuts" title="Wichita Wingnuts">Wichita Wingnuts</a> joining in their place. In 2011 and 2012 the league went through a significant shift. Fort Worth left the league to join <a href="/wiki/United_League_Baseball" title="United League Baseball">United League Baseball</a>, while Shreveport and Pensacola both relocated. The Pelicans moved to <a href="/wiki/Amarillo,_Texas" title="Amarillo, Texas">Amarillo, Texas</a> and became the Amarillo Sox (later the <a href="/wiki/Amarillo_Thunderheads" title="Amarillo Thunderheads">Amarillo Thunderheads</a>) while Shreveport, who had changed their name to the Shreveport-Bossier Captains, moved to <a href="/wiki/Laredo,_Texas" title="Laredo, Texas">Laredo, Texas</a> and became the <a href="/wiki/Laredo_Lemurs" title="Laredo Lemurs">Laredo Lemurs</a>. In addition, four more Northern League franchises (<a href="/wiki/Fargo-Moorhead_RedHawks" title="Fargo-Moorhead RedHawks">Fargo-Moorhead RedHawks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_SouthShore_RailCats" title="Gary SouthShore RailCats">Gary SouthShore RailCats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_T-Bones" title="Kansas City T-Bones">Kansas City T-Bones</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg_Goldeyes" title="Winnipeg Goldeyes">Winnipeg Goldeyes</a>) joined the American Association as that league\'s stability came into question.\n</p><p>For the 2012 season, the American Association began interleague play with the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_American_Association_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Canadian American Association of Professional Baseball">Can-Am League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The two leagues are both headquartered in Durham, North Carolina, and both have Miles Wolff as their commissioner. This was similar to interleague play in Major League Baseball, but American Association and Can-Am League are separate legal entities and have separate playoffs/championships.\n</p><p>At the end of the 2013 season, due to the <a href="/wiki/Tucson_Padres" title="Tucson Padres">Tucson Padres</a> relocating to their city, the El Paso Diablos suspended operations. The team was eventually revived and relocated, operating as the <a href="/wiki/Joplin_Blasters" title="Joplin Blasters">Joplin Blasters</a>. The Blasters ceased operations after the conclusion of the 2016 season.\n</p><p>On November 19, 2015, Miles Wolff announced that there would no longer be interleague play. It also was announced that for the <a href="/wiki/Amarillo_Thunderheads" title="Amarillo Thunderheads">Amarillo Thunderheads</a> and <a href="/wiki/Texas_AirHogs" title="Texas AirHogs">Grand Prairie AirHogs</a> would operate as a joint team (<a href="/wiki/Texas_AirHogs" title="Texas AirHogs">Texas AirHogs</a>) playing 25 games in Amarillo and 25 games in Grand Prairie to make up a 12-team league.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The team remained in Grand Prairie full-time in 2017, with the <a href="/wiki/Cleburne_Railroaders" title="Cleburne Railroaders">Cleburne Railroaders</a> joining league the same season. Shortly before the 2017 season, the <a href="/wiki/Laredo_Lemurs" title="Laredo Lemurs">Laredo Lemurs</a> withdrew from the league.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They were temporarily replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Salina_Stockade_(baseball)" title="Salina Stockade (baseball)">Salina Stockade</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Pecos_League" title="Pecos League">Pecos League</a> for the season. The <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Dogs" title="Chicago Dogs">Chicago Dogs</a> joined for 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_model">Business model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Association_of_Independent_Professional_Baseball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Typically the American Association recruits college, ex-major and ex-minor players. Former affiliated-league players that get injured or have other circumstances join the AA as an opportunity to get re-signed by major league organizations. Other players consist of college players that were not drafted into MiLB, but seek the opportunity to be seen by major league scouts and possibly get signed by major league organizations. Other former MLB players join the AA as a way to stay involved in baseball after their career as a major league player, often as coaches and managers.\n</p><p>As of 2008, the salary cap for each team was $100,000, with a minimum salary of $800 per month.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The price of an expansion team is also about $750,000.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This is in stark contrast with the minor and major leagues. Commissioner Miles Wolff stated in an interview that "We have to pay the salaries of the players, which they don\'t in an affiliated [league]. It\'s a much riskier business. Just because of the longevity and tradition, we usually don\'t get the best cities, either, so some of the markets we\'re in are not great markets. But as I say, I think it\'s a better product."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" href="/w/index.php?title=American_Association_of_Independent_Professional_Baseball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: All-Star Game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The American Association hosted an annual <a href="/wiki/All-Star_Game" class="mw-redirect" title="All-Star Game">All-Star Game</a> from 2006 to 2010. The league\'s first All-Star game was played in <a href="/wiki/El_Paso,_Texas" title="El Paso, Texas">El Paso, Texas</a>, on July 18, 2006, which pit a team of American Association All-Stars against an All-Star team from the <a href="/wiki/Canadian-American_Association_of_Professional_Baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian-American Association of Professional Baseball">Can-Am League</a>. Its current format pits the all-stars from each division against each other. There was no All-Star game in 2011, 2012, or 2013. The <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg_Goldeyes" title="Winnipeg Goldeyes">Winnipeg Goldeyes</a> hosted the 2014 All-Star game.\n</p>\n<dl><dt>Game results</dt></dl>\n<ul><li>2006 \xe2\x80\x93 AA 5, Can-Am 3</li>\n<li>2007 \xe2\x80\x93 South 6, North 4</li>\n<li>2008 \xe2\x80\x93 South 11, North 4</li>\n<li>2009 \xe2\x80\x93 North 6, South 2</li>\n<li>2010 \xe2\x80\x93 South 12, North 3</li>\n<li>2011 \xe2\x80\x93 No game played</li>\n<li>2012 \xe2\x80\x93 No game played</li>\n<li>2013 \xe2\x80\x93 No game played</li>\n<li>2014 \xe2\x80\x93 South 7, North 0</li>\n<li>2015 \xe2\x80\x93 No game played</li>\n<li>2016 \xe2\x80\x93 North 6, South 1</li></ul>\n<dl><dt>Most Valuable Players</dt></dl>\n<ul><li>2006 \xe2\x80\x93 Jake Whitesides, (St. Joe Blacksnakes)</li>\n<li>2007 \xe2\x80\x93 Jorge Alvarez, (El Paso Diablos)</li>\n<li>2008 \xe2\x80\x93 Brian Fryer, (Fort Worth Cats)</li>\n<li>2009 \xe2\x80\x93 Trevor Lawhorn, (Sioux Falls Canaries)</li>\n<li>2010 \xe2\x80\x93 Chris Garcia, (Shreveport-Bossier Captains)</li>\n<li>2011 \xe2\x80\x93 Lee Cruz, (Amarillo Sox)</li>\n<li>2012 \xe2\x80\x93 Nic Jackson, (Fargo-Moorhead RedHawks)</li>\n<li>2013 \xe2\x80\x93 C.
