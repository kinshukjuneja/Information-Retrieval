/>.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Purbeck</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The district is named after the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Purbeck" title="Isle of Purbeck">Isle of Purbeck</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> that forms a large proportion of the district\'s area. However the district extends significantly further north and west than the traditional boundary of the Isle of Purbeck along the <a href="/wiki/River_Frome,_Dorset" title="River Frome, Dorset">River Frome</a>. The district council is based in the town of <a href="/wiki/Wareham,_Dorset" title="Wareham, Dorset">Wareham</a>, which is itself north of the River Frome.\n</p><p>The district was formed under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> on 1 April 1974, from the former <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> of Wareham, <a href="/wiki/Swanage" title="Swanage">Swanage</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wareham_and_Purbeck_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wareham and Purbeck Rural District (page does not exist)">Wareham and Purbeck Rural District</a>.\nThe district and its council will be abolished on 1st April 2019 and, together with the other 4 districts outside the greater Bournemouth area, will form a new <a href="/wiki/Dorset_(unitary_authority)" title="Dorset (unitary authority)">Dorset</a> unitary authority.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Its name is recorded in 948 AD as <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Anglo-Saxon</a> <i>Purbicinga</i>, meaning "of the people of Purbic", where Purbic may be a former Celtic name, or may contain a supposed Anglo-Saxon word *<i>pur</i> or "male <a href="/wiki/Domestic_sheep" class="mw-redirect" title="Domestic sheep">lamb</a>".\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en"
