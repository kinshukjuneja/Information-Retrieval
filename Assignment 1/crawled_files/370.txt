href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">Saorst\xc3\xa1t \xc3\x89ireann</i> <small>pronounced&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Irish" title="Help:IPA/Irish">[\xcb\x88s\xcb\xa0i\xcb\x90\xc9\xbe\xcb\xa0.s\xcb\xa0t\xcc\xaa\xcb\xa0a\xcb\x90t\xcc\xaa\xcb\xa0 \xcb\x88e\xcb\x90\xc9\xbe\xca\xb2\xc9\x99n\xcc\xaa\xcb\xa0]</a></span>; 6 December 1922&#160;&#8211;&#32;29 December 1937) was a <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">state</a> established in 1922 under the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish_Treaty" title="Anglo-Irish Treaty">Anglo-Irish Treaty</a> of December 1921. That treaty ended the three-year <a href="/wiki/Irish_War_of_Independence" title="Irish War of Independence">Irish War of Independence</a> between the forces of the self-proclaimed <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republic" title="Irish Republic">Irish Republic</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army_(1919%E2%80%9322)" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Republican Army (1919\xe2\x80\x9322)">Irish Republican Army</a> (IRA), and <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">British Crown</a> forces.\n</p><p>The Free State was established as a <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">Dominion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Commonwealth_of_Nations" class="mw-redirect" title="British Commonwealth of Nations">British Commonwealth of Nations</a>. It comprised 26 of the 32 <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Ireland" title="Counties of Ireland">counties of Ireland</a>. <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, which comprised the remaining six counties, exercised its right under the Treaty to opt out of the new state. The Free State government consisted of the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Governor-General of the Irish Free State">Governor-General</a>, the representative of the King, and the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Council_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Executive Council of the Irish Free State">Executive Council</a> (cabinet), which replaced both the revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_2nd_D%C3%A1il" title="Government of the 2nd D\xc3\xa1il">D\xc3\xa1il Government</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_Ireland_(1922)" title="Provisional Government of Ireland (1922)">Provisional Government</a> set up under the Treaty. <a href="/wiki/W._T._Cosgrave" title="W. T. Cosgrave">W. T. Cosgrave</a>, who had led both of these governments since August 1922, became the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Executive_Council_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="President of the Executive Council of the Irish Free State">President of the Executive Council</a> (prime minister). The <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas_(Irish_Free_State)" class="mw-redirect" title="Oireachtas (Irish Free State)">Oireachtas</a> or legislature consisted of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann_(Irish_Free_State)" title="D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann (Irish Free State)">D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann</a> (the lower house) and <a href="/wiki/Seanad_%C3%89ireann_(Irish_Free_State)" title="Seanad \xc3\x89ireann (Irish Free State)">Seanad \xc3\x89ireann</a>, also known as the Senate. Members of the D\xc3\xa1il were required to take an <a href="/wiki/Oath_of_Allegiance_(Ireland)" title="Oath of Allegiance (Ireland)">Oath of Allegiance to the Constitution of the Free State and to declare fidelity to the king</a>. The oath was a key issue for opponents of the Treaty, who refused to take the oath and therefore did not take their seats. Pro-Treaty members, who formed <a href="/wiki/Cumann_na_nGaedheal" title="Cumann na nGaedheal">Cumann na nGaedheal</a> in 1923, held an effective majority in the D\xc3\xa1il from 1922 to 1927, and thereafter ruled as a minority government until 1932. \n</p><p>In 1931, with the passage of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> relinquished its remaining authority to legislate for the Free State and the other dominions. This had the effect of making the dominions fully sovereign states. The Free State thus became the first internationally recognised independent Irish state.\n</p><p>In the first months of the Free State, the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Civil_War" title="Irish Civil War">Irish Civil War</a> was waged between the newly established <a href="/wiki/National_Army_(Ireland)" title="National Army (Ireland)">National Army</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army_(1922%E2%80%9369)" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Republican Army (1922\xe2\x80\x9369)">anti-Treaty IRA</a>, who refused to recognise the state. The Civil War ended in victory for the government forces, with the anti-Treaty forces dumping their arms in May 1923. The anti-Treaty political party, <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a>, refused to take its seats in the D\xc3\xa1il, leaving the relatively small <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Ireland)" title="Labour Party (Ireland)">Labour Party</a> as the only opposition party. In 1926, when Sinn F\xc3\xa9in president <a href="/wiki/%C3%89amon_de_Valera" title="\xc3\x89amon de Valera">\xc3\x89amon de Valera</a> failed to have this policy reversed, he resigned from Sinn F\xc3\xa9in and founded <a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna F\xc3\xa1il">Fianna F\xc3\xa1il</a>. Fianna F\xc3\xa1il entered the D\xc3\xa1il following the <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_June_1927" title="Irish general election, June 1927">1927 general election</a>, and entered government after the <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_1932" title="Irish general election, 1932">Irish general election, 1932</a>, when it became the largest party.\n</p><p>De Valera abolished the Oath of Allegiance and embarked on an <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish_Trade_War" title="Anglo-Irish Trade War">economic war</a> with the UK. In 1937 he drafted a new <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Constitution of Ireland">constitution</a>, which was passed by a <a href="/wiki/Irish_constitutional_plebiscite,_1937" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish constitutional plebiscite, 1937">referendum</a> in July of that year. The Free State came to an end with the coming into force of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Constitution of Ireland">new constitution on 29 December 1937</a>, which with the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_Act_1948" title="Republic of Ireland Act 1948">Republic of Ireland Act 1948</a> officially became the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" <span class="toctext">Further reading</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_background">Historical background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Easter_Rising" title="Easter Rising">Easter Rising</a> of 1916, and particularly the execution of fifteen people by firing squad, the imprisonment or internment of hundreds more, and the imposition of martial law, caused a profound shift in public opinion towards <a href="/wiki/Irish_republicanism" title="Irish republicanism">the republican cause in Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, opposition increased to <a href="/wiki/Ireland_in_World_War_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Ireland in World War I">Ireland\'s participation in World War I</a> in Europe and the Middle East. This came about when the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Parliamentary_Party" title="Irish Parliamentary Party">Irish Parliamentary Party</a> supported the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allied cause</a> in World War I in response to the passing of the <a href="/wiki/Home_Rule_Act_1914" class="mw-redirect" title="Home Rule Act 1914">Third Home Rule Bill in 1914</a>. Many people had begun to doubt whether the Bill, passed by Westminster in September 1914, but suspended for the duration of the war, would ever come into effect. Due to the war situation <a href="/wiki/Spring_offensive" class="mw-redirect" title="Spring offensive">deteriorating badly</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a> in April 1918, which coincided with the publication of the final report and recommendations of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Convention#Europe_intervenes" title="Irish Convention">Irish Convention</a>, the British Cabinet drafted a doomed "dual policy" of introducing Home Rule linked to <a href="/wiki/Compulsory_military_service" class="mw-redirect" title="Compulsory military service">compulsory military service</a> for Ireland which it eventually had to drop.  Sinn F\xc3\xa9in, the Irish Party, and all other Nationalist elements joined forces in opposition to the idea during the <a href="/wiki/Conscription_Crisis_of_1918" title="Conscription Crisis of 1918">Conscription Crisis of 1918</a>.  At the same time, the Irish Parliamentary Party lost in support on account of the crisis.  Irish republicans felt further emboldened by successful anti-monarchical revolutions in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> (1917), the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> (1918), and the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Austro-Hungarian Empire">Austro-Hungarian Empire</a> (1918).  In the <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_1918" title="Irish general election, 1918">December 1918 General Election</a>, <a href="/wiki/History_of_Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="History of Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a> won a large majority of the Irish seats in the Westminster parliament of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a>: 73 of the 105 constituencies returned Sinn F\xc3\xa9in members (25 uncontested).  The Sinn F\xc3\xa9in party, founded by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Griffith" title="Arthur Griffith">Arthur Griffith</a> in 1905, had espoused non-violent separatism. Under <a href="/wiki/%C3%89amon_de_Valera" title="\xc3\x89amon de Valera">\xc3\x89amon de Valera</a>\'s leadership from 1917, it campaigned <a href="/wiki/Physical_force_Irish_republicanism" title="Physical force Irish republicanism">aggressively and militantly</a> for an Irish republic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>On 21 January 1919, Sinn F\xc3\xa9in MPs (who became known as <i><a href="/wiki/Teachta_D%C3%A1la" title="Teachta D\xc3\xa1la">Teachta D\xc3\xa1la</a></i>, TDs), refusing to sit at <a href="/wiki/Westminster" title="Westminster">Westminster</a>, assembled in Dublin and formed a single-chamber Irish parliament called <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann_(Irish_Republic)" title="D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann (Irish Republic)">D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann</a> (Assembly of Ireland). It affirmed the formation of an Irish Republic and passed a <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence</a>, calling itself <i>Saorst\xc3\xa1t \xc3\x89ireann</i> (Irish Free State) in <a href="/wiki/Irish_Gaelic" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Gaelic">Irish Gaelic</a>:\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>"...the Irish people is resolved... to promote the common weal, to re-establish justice... with equal rights and equal opportunity for every citizen."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></p></blockquote><p>\nAlthough the less than overwhelming majority<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> of Irish people accepted this course,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Soviet Russia</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> were targeted to recognise the Irish Republic internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Message to the Free Nations of the World called on: </p><blockquote><p>"...every free nation to support the Irish Republic by recognizing Ireland\'s national status... the last outpost of Europe towards the West... demanded by the Freedom of the Seas."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Cathal_Brugha" title="Cathal Brugha">Cathal Brugha</a>, elected President of the Ministry Pro-Tem, warned, "Deputies you class="mw-headline" id="A_war_for_a_new_independent_Ireland">A war for a new independent Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: A war for a new independent Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Irish War">War of Independence</a> (1919\xe2\x80\x931921) pitted the army of the Irish Republic, the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army_(1917%E2%80%9322)" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Republican Army (1917\xe2\x80\x9322)">Irish Republican Army</a> (known subsequently as the "Old IRA" to distinguish it from later organisations of that name), against the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Black_and_Tans" title="Black and Tans">Black and Tans</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Irish_Constabulary" title="Royal Irish Constabulary">Royal Irish Constabulary</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Auxiliary_Division" title="Auxiliary Division">Auxiliary Division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Metropolitan_Police" title="Dublin Metropolitan Police">Dublin Metropolitan Police</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Special_Constabulary" title="Ulster Special Constabulary">Ulster Special Constabulary</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Volunteer_Force" title="Ulster Volunteer Force">Ulster Volunteer Force</a>. On 9 July 1921 a truce came into force. By this time the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Parliament of Northern Ireland">Ulster Parliament</a> had opened, established under the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland_Act_1920" title="Government of Ireland Act 1920">Government of Ireland Act 1920</a>, and presenting the republican movement with a <i>fait accompli</i> and guaranteeing the British permanent entanglement in Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On 11 October negotiations opened between the British Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/David_Lloyd_George" title="David Lloyd George">David Lloyd George</a>, and Arthur Griffith, who headed the Irish Republic\'s delegation.  The Irish Treaty delegation (Griffith, Collins, Duggan, Barton, and Gavan Duffy) set up headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Hans_Place" title="Hans Place">Hans Place</a>, Knightsbridge. On 5 December 1921 at 11:15 am the delegation decided during private discussions at 22 Hans Place to recommend the negotiated agreement to the D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann; negotiations continued until 2:30 am on 6 December 1921, after which the parties signed <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish_Treaty" title="Anglo-Irish Treaty">Anglo-Irish Treaty</a>.\nNobody had doubted that these negotiations would produce a form of Irish government short of the independence wished for by republicans.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (May 2016)">original research?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The United Kingdom could not offer a republican form of government without losing prestige and risking demands for something similar throughout the Empire.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Furthermore, as one of the negotiators, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(Irish_leader)" title="Michael Collins (Irish leader)">Michael Collins</a>, later admitted (and he would have known, given his leading role in the independence war), the IRA at the time of the truce was weeks, if not days, away from collapse, with a chronic shortage of ammunition. "Frankly, we thought they were mad", Collins said of the sudden British offer of a truce \xe2\x80\x93 although the republicans would probably have continued the struggle in one form or another, given the level of public support.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Since Lloyd George had already, after the truce had come into effect, made it clear to <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Irish_Republic" title="President of the Irish Republic">President of the Republic</a>, \xc3\x89amon de Valera, "that the achievement of a republic through negotiation was impossible",<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> de Valera decided not to become a member of the treaty delegation and so not to risk more militant republicans labelling him as a "sellout". Yet his own proposals \xe2\x80\x93 published in January 1922 \xe2\x80\x93 fell far short of an autonomous all-Ireland republic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Sinn F\xc3\xa9in\'s abstention was unambiguous.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>As expected, the Anglo-Irish Treaty explicitly ruled out a republic. It offered Ireland <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">dominion</a> status, as a state within the then <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> \xe2\x80\x93 equal to Canada, Newfoundland, Australia, New Zealand and South Africa. Although less than expected by the Sinn F\xc3\xa9in leadership, this deal offered substantially more than the initial form of <a href="/wiki/Irish_Home_Rule_movement" title="Irish Home Rule movement">home rule</a> within the United Kingdom sought by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stewart_Parnell" title="Charles Stewart Parnell">Charles Stewart Parnell</a> from 1880, and represented a serious advance on the Home Rule Bill of 1914 that the Irish nationalist leader <a href="/wiki/John_Redmond" title="John Redmond">John Redmond</a> had achieved through parliamentary proceedings. However, it all but confirmed the <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_Ireland" title="Partition of Ireland">partition of Ireland</a> between <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> and the Irish Free State. The <a href="/wiki/Second_D%C3%A1il" title="Second D\xc3\xa1il">Second D\xc3\xa1il</a> in Dublin ratified the Treaty (7 January 1922), splitting Sinn F\xc3\xa9in in the process.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Arthur Griffith addressing the Dail on 14 December 1921 said "Well, we have brought back Irish freedom and Irish independence ... Ireland is as free as Canada and Australia".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  Speaking in Dail Eireann on 21 September 1922, Gavan Duffy TD, one of the signatories of the treaty, stated: "The Governor-General will have to do exactly as he is told by Dominion Ministers, and it is proposed, and warmly advocated, that he should cease altogether to be the representative of Great Britain. Let him represent the Imperial Crown, but let Great Britain send Ambassadors to her Dominions, as she does to other countries, because they are in fact independent countries voluntarily uniting themselves with Great Britain and not a gang of subject States."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Northern_Ireland_.22opts_out.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland_&quot;opts_out&quot;">Northern Ireland "opts out"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland &quot;opts out&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Treaty, and the legislation introduced to give it legal effect, implied that Northern Ireland would be a part of the Free State on its creation,<sup id="cite_ref-historical-debates.oireachtas.ie_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historical-debates.oireachtas.ie-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> but legally the terms of the Treaty applied only to the 26 counties, and the government of the Free State never had any powers\xe2\x80\x94even in principle\xe2\x80\x94in Northern Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Treaty was given legal effect in the United Kingdom through the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State_Constitution_Act_1922" title="Irish Free State Constitution Act 1922">Irish Free State Constitution Act 1922</a>. That act, which established the Free State, allowed Northern Ireland to "opt out" of it.<sup id="cite_ref-historical-debates.oireachtas.ie_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historical-debates.oireachtas.ie-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Under Article 12 of the Treaty, Northern Ireland could exercise its option by presenting an address to the King requesting not to be part of the Irish Free State. Once the Treaty was ratified, the Houses of Parliament of Northern Ireland had one month (dubbed the "Ulster month") to exercise this option during which month the Government of Ireland Act continued to apply in Northern Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Realistically it was always certain that Northern Ireland would opt out of the Free State.  The <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Prime Minister of Northern Ireland">Prime Minister of Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_James_Craig" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir James Craig">Sir James Craig</a>, speaking in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Parliament of Northern Ireland">Parliament</a> in October 1922 said that "when 6 December is passed the month begins in which we will have to make the choice either to vote out or remain within the Free State". He said it was important that that choice be made as soon as possible after 6 December 1922 "in order that it may not go forth to the world that we had the slightest hesitation".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On the following day, 7 December 1922, the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Parliament of Northern Ireland">Parliament</a> resolved to make the following address to the King so as to opt out of the Irish Free State:<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>MOST GRACIOUS SOVEREIGN, We, your Majesty\'s most dutiful and loyal subjects, the Senators and Commons of Northern Ireland in Parliament assembled, having learnt of the passing of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State_Constitution_Act_1922" title="Irish Free State Constitution Act 1922">Irish Free State Constitution Act, 1922</a>, being the Act of Parliament for the ratification of the Articles of Agreement for a Treaty between Great Britain and Ireland, do, by this humble Address, pray your Majesty that the powers of the Parliament and Government of the Irish Free State shall no longer extend to Northern Ireland.\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>Discussion in the Parliament of the address was short. Prime Minister Craig left for London with the memorial embodying the address on the night boat that evening, 7 December 1922. The King received it the following day, <i>The Times</i> reporting:<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>YORK COTTAGE, SANDRINGHAM, DEC. 8. The <a href="/wiki/Rowland_Baring,_2nd_Earl_of_Cromer" title="Rowland Baring, 2nd Earl of Cromer">Earl of Cromer</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lord_Chamberlain" title="Lord Chamberlain">Lord Chamberlain</a>) was received in audience by <a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">The King</a> this evening and presented an Address from the Houses of Parliament of Northern Ireland, to which His Majesty was graciously pleased to make reply.\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>If the Houses of Parliament of Northern Ireland had not made such a declaration, under Article 14 of the Treaty Northern Ireland, its Parliament and government would have continued in being but the <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas" title="Oireachtas">Oireachtas</a> would have had jurisdiction to legislate for Northern Ireland in matters not delegated to Northern Ireland under the Government of Ireland Act. This, of course, never came to pass.\n</p><p>On 13 December 1922 Prime Minister Craig addressed the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Parliament of Northern Ireland">Parliament</a> informing them that the King had responded to the Parliament\'s address as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I have received the Address presented to me by both Houses of the Parliament of Northern Ireland in pursuance of Article 12 of the Articles of Agreement set forth in the Schedule to the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State_(Agreement)_Act_1922" title="Irish Free State (Agreement) Act 1922">Irish Free State (Agreement) Act, 1922</a>, and of Section 5 of the Irish Free State Constitution Act, 1922, and I have caused my Ministers and the Irish Free State Government to be so informed.\n</p></blockquote>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governmental_and_constitutional_structures">Governmental and constitutional structures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Governmental and constitutional structures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Saorst%C3%A1t_%C3%89ireann.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/23/Saorst%C3%A1t_%C3%89ireann.png/200px-Saorst%C3%A1t_%C3%89ireann.png" width="200" height="233" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/23/Saorst%C3%A1t_%C3%89ireann.png 1.5x" data-file-width="293" data-file-height="341" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Saorst%C3%A1t_%C3%89ireann.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A symbol most often associated with the new state\'s <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Posts_and_Telegraphs" title="Minister for Posts and Telegraphs">postal system</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Treaty established that the new Irish Free State would be a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>, with a Governor-General as representative of the Crown.  The <i><a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Constitution of the Irish Free State">Constitution of the Irish Free State</a></i> made more detailed provision for the state\'s system of government, with a three-tier parliament, called the <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Oireachtas of the Irish Free State">Oireachtas</a>, made up of the King and two houses, D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann and <a href="/wiki/Seanad_%C3%89ireann_(Irish_Free_State)" title="Seanad \xc3\x89ireann (Irish Free State)">Seanad \xc3\x89ireann</a> (the Irish Senate). \n</p><p>Executive authority was vested in the King, with the Governor-General as his representative. He appointed a cabinet called the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Council_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Executive Council of the Irish Free State">Executive Council</a> to "aid and advise" him. The Executive Council was presided over by a prime minister called the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Executive_Council_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="President of the Executive Council of the Irish Free State">President of the Executive Council</a>. In practice, most of the real power was exercised by the Executive Council, as the Governor-General was almost always bound to act on the advice of the Executive Council.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Representative_of_the_Crown">The Representative of the Crown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Representative of the Crown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Governor-General of the Irish Free State">Governor-General of the Irish Free State</a></div>\n<p>The King in the Irish Free State was represented by a <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Governor-General of the Irish Free State">Governor-General of the Irish Free State</a>. The office replaced the previous <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_Ireland" title="Lord Lieutenant of Ireland">Lord Lieutenant</a>, who had headed English and British administrations in Ireland since the Middle Ages. Governors-General were appointed by the King initially on the advice of the British Government, but with the consent of the Irish Government. From 1927 the Irish Government alone had the power to advise the King whom to appoint.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oath_of_Allegiance">Oath of Allegiance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Oath of Allegiance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>As with all dominions, provision was made for an Oath of Allegiance. Within dominions, such oaths were taken by parliamentarians personally towards the monarch. The <a href="/wiki/Oath_of_Allegiance_(Ireland)" title="Oath of Allegiance (Ireland)">Irish Oath of Allegiance</a> was fundamentally different. It had two elements; the first, an <i>oath to the Free State, as by law established</i>, the second part a promise of <i>fidelity, to His Majesty, King George V, his heirs and successors</i>. That second fidelity element, however, was qualified in two ways. It was to the King <i>in</i> Ireland, not specifically to the King of the United Kingdom. Secondly, it was to the King explicitly in his role as part of the Treaty settlement, not in terms of pre-1922 British rule. The Oath itself came from a combination of three sources, and was largely the work of Michael Collins in the Treaty negotiations. It came in part from a draft oath suggested prior to the negotiations by President de Valera. Other sections were taken by Collins directly from the Oath of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Brotherhood" title="Irish Republican Brotherhood">Irish Republican Brotherhood</a> (IRB), of which he was the secret head. In its structure, it was also partially based on the form and structure used for \'Dominion status\'.\n</p><p>Although \'a new departure\', and notably indirect in its reference to the monarchy, it was criticised by nationalists and republicans for making any reference to the Crown, the claim being that it <i>was</i> a direct oath to the Crown, a fact demonstrably incorrect by an examination of its wording.  But in 1922 Ireland and beyond, it was the perception, not the reality, that influenced public debate on the issue.  Had its original author, Michael Collins, survived, he might have been able to clarify its actual meaning, but with his assassination in August 1922, no major negotiator to the Oath\'s creation on the Irish side was still alive, available or pro-Treaty. (The leader of the Irish delegation, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Griffith" title="Arthur Griffith">Arthur Griffith</a>, had also died in August 1922). The Oath became a key issue in the resulting <a href="/wiki/Irish_Civil_War" title="Irish Civil War">Irish Civil War</a> that divided the pro- and anti-treaty sides in 1922\xe2\x80\x9323.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Irish_Civil_War">The Irish Civil War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Irish Civil War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Irish_Civil_War" title="Irish Civil War">Irish Civil War</a></div>\n<p>The compromises contained in the agreement caused the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Civil_War" title="Irish Civil War">civil war</a> in the 26 counties in June 1922&#8212;April 1923, in which the pro-Treaty <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_the_Irish_Free_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Provisional Government of the Irish Free State">Provisional Government</a> defeated the anti-Treaty Republican forces. The latter were led, nominally, by <a href="/wiki/%C3%89amon_de_Valera" title="\xc3\x89amon de Valera">\xc3\x89amon de Valera</a>, who had resigned as President of the Republic on the treaty\'s ratification. His resignation outraged some of his own supporters, notably <a href="/wiki/Se%C3%A1n_T._O%27Kelly" title="Se\xc3\xa1n T. O&#39;Kelly">Se\xc3\xa1n T. O\'Kelly</a>, the main Sinn F\xc3\xa9in organizer. On resigning, he then sought re-election but was defeated two days later on a vote of 60\xe2\x80\x9358. The pro-Treaty Arthur Griffith followed as President of the Irish Republic. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(Irish_leader)" title="Michael Collins (Irish leader)">Michael Collins</a> was chosen at a meeting of the members elected to sit in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Southern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Commons of Southern Ireland">House of Commons of Southern Ireland</a> (a body set up under the Government of Ireland Act 1920) to become <a href="/wiki/Chairman_of_the_Provisional_Government_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Chairman of the Provisional Government of the Irish Free State">Chairman of the Provisional Government of the Irish Free State</a> in accordance with the Treaty. The <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_1922" title="Irish general election, 1922">general election</a> in June gave overwhelming support for the pro-Treaty parties. <a href="/wiki/W._T._Cosgrave" title="W. T. Cosgrave">W. T. Cosgrave</a>\'s Crown-appointed Provisional Government effectively subsumed Griffith\'s republican administration with the death of both Collins and Griffith in August 1922.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="The_.22freedom_to_achieve_freedom.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_&quot;freedom_to_achieve_freedom&quot;">The "freedom to achieve freedom"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The &quot;freedom to achieve freedom&quot;">edit</a><span State passport</a> (holder\'s name removed)</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The following were the principal parties of government of the Irish Free State between 1922 and 1937:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Cumann_na_nGaedheal" title="Cumann na nGaedheal">Cumann na nGaedheal</a> under W. T. Cosgrave (1922\xe2\x80\x9332)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna F\xc3\xa1il">Fianna F\xc3\xa1il</a> under \xc3\x89amon de Valera (1932\xe2\x80\x9337)</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Constitutional_evolution">Constitutional evolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Constitutional evolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg/200px-Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg" width="200" height="126" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg/300px-Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg/400px-Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg 2x" data-file-width="627" data-file-height="394" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stamp_irl_1922_2N6se.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Overprint" title="Overprint">Overprinted</a> stamp</div></div></div>\n<p>Michael Collins described the Treaty as "the freedom to achieve freedom". In practice, the Treaty offered most of the symbols and powers of independence. These included a functioning, if disputed, <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">parliamentary democracy</a> with its own executive, judiciary and written constitution which could be changed by the <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Oireachtas of the Irish Free State">Oireachtas</a>.  Although an <a href="/wiki/Irish_republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish republic">Irish republic</a> had not been on offer, the Treaty still afforded Ireland more internal independence than it had possessed in over 400 years, and far exceeded the most optimistic goals of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Home_Rule_movement" title="Irish Home Rule movement">Home Rule movement</a>. However, a number of conditions existed:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The King remained king <i>in</i> Ireland;</li>\n<li>Britain retained the so-called strategic <a href="/wiki/Treaty_Ports_(Ireland)" title="Treaty Ports (Ireland)">Treaty Ports</a> on Ireland\'s south coast which were to remain occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>;</li>\n<li>Prior to the passage  of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster</a>, the UK government continued to have a role in Irish governance. Officially the representative of the King, the Governor-General also received instructions from the British Government on his use of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Assent" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Assent">Royal Assent</a>, namely a Bill passed by the D\xc3\xa1il and Seanad could be Granted Assent (signed into law), Withheld (not signed, pending later approval) or Denied (vetoed).  The <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">letters patent</a> to the first Governor-General, <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Michael_Healy" class="mw-redirect" title="Timothy Michael Healy">Tim Healy</a>, explicitly named Bills that were to be rejected if passed by the D\xc3\xa1il and Seanad, such as any attempt to abolish the Oath. In the event, no such Bills were ever introduced, so the issue was moot.</li></ul>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Irish_Free_State_Butter,_Eggs_and_Bacon_for_our_Breakfasts_Du_beurre,_des_%C5%93ufs_et_du_bacon_de_l%E2%80%99%C3%89tat_libre_d%E2%80%99Irlande_au_d%C3%A9jeuner.jpg" class="image"><img title="Enlarge"></a></div>Poster promoting Irish Free State farm goods for breakfast to Canadians ("Irish Free State butter, eggs and bacon for our breakfasts").</div></div></div>\n<ul><li>As with the other dominions, the Irish Free State had a status of association with the UK rather than being completely legally independent from it. However the meaning of \'Dominion status\' changed radically during the 1920s, starting with the <a href="/wiki/Chanak_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="Chanak crisis">Chanak crisis</a> in 1922 and quickly followed by the directly negotiated <a href="/wiki/Halibut_Treaty" title="Halibut Treaty">Halibut Treaty</a> of 1923. The <a href="/wiki/1926_Imperial_Conference" title="1926 Imperial Conference">1926 Imperial Conference</a> declared the equality [including the UK] of all member states of the Commonwealth.  The Conference also led to a reform of the King\'s title, given effect by the <i><a href="/wiki/Royal_and_Parliamentary_Titles_Act_1927" title="Royal and Parliamentary Titles Act 1927">Royal and Parliamentary Titles Act 1927</a></i>, which changed the King\'s royal title so that it took account of the fact that there was no longer a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a>. The King adopted the following style by which he would be known in all of his Empire: <i>By the Grace of God, of Great Britain, Ireland and the British Dominions beyond the Seas King, Defender of the Faith, Emperor of India</i>. That was the King\'s title in Ireland just as elsewhere in his Empire.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>In the conduct of external relations, the Irish Free State tried to push the boundaries of its status as a Dominion.  It \'accepted\' credentials from international ambassadors to Ireland, something no other dominion up to then had done.  It registered the treaty with the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> as an international document, over the objections of the United Kingdom, which saw it as a mere <i>internal</i> document between a dominion and the United Kingdom.  Entitlement of citizenship of the Irish Free State was defined in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Free State Constitution">Irish Free State Constitution</a>, but the status of that citizenship was contentious. One of the first projects of the Irish Free State was the design and production of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Seal_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Great Seal of the Irish Free State">Great Seal of Saorst\xc3\xa1t \xc3\x89ireann</a> which was carried out on behalf of the Government by <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Kennedy" title="Hugh Kennedy">Hugh Kennedy</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster</a> (of 1931), embodying a decision of an Imperial Conference, enabled each dominion to enact new legislation or to change any extant legislation, without resorting to any role for the British parliament that may have enacted the original legislation in the past. This change made the dominions, including the Free State, <i>de jure</i> sovereign nations \xe2\x80\x94 fulfilling Collins\' vision of having "the freedom to achieve freedom". \n</p><p>The Free State symbolically marked these changes in two mould-breaking moves soon after winning internationally recognised independence:\n</p>\n<ul><li>It sought, and got, the King\'s acceptance to have an Irish minister, to the complete exclusion of British ministers, formally advising the King in the exercise of his powers and functions as King in the Irish Free State.  Two examples of this are the signing of a treaty between the Irish Free State and the <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese Republic</a> in 1931, and the act recognising the <a href="/wiki/Abdication_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="Abdication crisis">abdication</a> of <a href="/wiki/King_Edward_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="King Edward VIII">King Edward VIII</a> in 1936 separately from the recognition by the British Parliament.</li>\n<li>The unprecedented replacement of the use of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Seal_of_the_Realm" title="Great Seal of the Realm">Great Seal of the Realm</a> and its replacement by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Seal_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Great Seal of the Irish Free State">Great Seal of the Irish Free State</a>, which the King awarded to the Irish Free State in 1931.  (The Irish Seal consisted of a picture of \'King George V\' enthroned on one side, with the Irish state harp and the words <i>Saorst\xc3\xa1t \xc3\x89ireann</i> on the reverse. It is now on display in the Irish National Museum, <a href="/wiki/Collins_Barracks_(Dublin)" class="mw-redirect" title="Collins Barracks (Dublin)">Collins Barracks</a> in Dublin.)</li></ul>\n<p>When \xc3\x89amon de Valera became President of the Executive Council (prime minister) in 1932 he described Cosgrave\'s ministers\' achievements simply. Having read the files, he told his son, Vivion, "they were magnificent, son".\n</p><p>The Statute of Westminster allowed de Valera, on becoming President of the Executive Council (February 1932), to go even further.  With no ensuing restrictions on his policies, he abolished the <a href="/wiki/Oath_of_Allegiance_(Ireland)" title="Oath of Allegiance (Ireland)">Oath of Allegiance</a> (which Cosgrave intended to do had he won the <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_1932" title="Irish general election, 1932">1932 general election</a>), the Senate, university representation in the D\xc3\xa1il, and appeals to the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Privy Council">Privy Council</a>.\nOne major policy error occurred in 1936 when he attempted to use the abdication of <a href="/wiki/Edward_VIII" title="Edward VIII">King Edward VIII</a> to abolish the crown and governor-general in the Free State with the "Constitution (Amendment No. 27 Act)". He was advised by senior law officers and other constitutional experts that, as the crown and governor-generalship existed separately from the constitution in a vast number of acts, charters, orders-in-council, and letters patent, they both still existed.  A second bill, the "<a href="/wiki/Executive_Powers_(Consequential_Provisions)_Act_1937" title="Executive Powers (Consequential Provisions) Act 1937">Executive Powers (Consequential Provisions) Act, 1937</a>" was quickly introduced to repeal the necessary elements. De Valera retroactively dated the second act back to December 1936.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Currency">Currency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Currency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The new state continued to use sterling from its inception; there is no reference in the Treaty or in either of the enabling Acts to currency.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Nonetheless and within a few years, the D\xc3\xa1il passed the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/1926/en/act/pub/0014/print.html">Coinage Act, 1926</a> (which provided for a <i>Saorst\xc3\xa1t</i> [Free State] coinage) and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.irishstatutebook.ie/1927/en/act/pub/0032/print.html">Currency Act, 1927</a> (which provided <i>inter alia</i> for banknotes of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Pound#Second_pound" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Pound">Saorst\xc3\xa1t pound</a>). The new Saorst\xc3\xa1t pound was defined by the 1927 Act to have exactly the same weight and fineness of gold as was the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_(British_coin)" title="Sovereign (British coin)">sovereign</a> at the time, making the new currency <a href="/wiki/Fixed_exchange-rate_system" title="Fixed exchange-rate system">pegged</a> at 1:1 with sterling. The State circulated its new national coinage in 1928, marked <i>Saorst\xc3\xa1t \xc3\x89ireann</i> and a <a href="/wiki/Series_A_Banknotes" title="Series A Banknotes">national series of banknotes</a>. British coinage remained  acceptable in the Free State at an equal rate. In 1937, when the Free State was superseded by Ireland (<i>\xc3\x89ire</i>), the pound became known as the "Irish pound" and the coins were marked <i>\xc3\x89ire</i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>According to one report, in 1924, shortly after the Irish Free State\'s establishment, the new dominion had the "lowest birth-rate in the world". The report noted that amongst countries for which statistics were available (<a href="/wiki/Ceylon" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceylon">Ceylon</a>, Chile, Japan, Spain, South Africa, Netherlands, Canada, Germany, Australia, United States, Britain, New Zealand, Finland and the Irish Free State). Ceylon had the highest birth rate at 40.8 per 1,000 while the Irish Free State had a birth rate of just 18.6 per 1,000.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_Irish_Free_State">After the Irish Free State</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: After the Irish Free State">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1937 the <a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna F\xc3\xa1il">Fianna F\xc3\xa1il</a> government presented a draft of an entirely new Constitution to <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann" title="D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann">D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann</a>.  An amended version of the draft document was subsequently approved by the D\xc3\xa1il.  A <a href="/wiki/Irish_constitutional_plebiscite,_1937" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish constitutional plebiscite, 1937">referendum</a> was held on  1 July 1937, the same day as the <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_1937" title="Irish general election, 1937">1937 general election</a>, when a relatively narrow majority approved it.  The new <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Constitution of Ireland">Constitution of Ireland</a> (<i>Bunreacht na h\xc3\x89ireann</i>) repealed the 1922 Constitution, and came into effect on 29 December 1937.\n</p><p>The state was named Ireland (<a href="/wiki/%C3%89ire" title="\xc3\x89ire">\xc3\x89ire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a>), and a new office of <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President of Ireland</a> was instituted in place of the Governor-General of the Irish Free State. The new constitution claimed jurisdiction over all of Ireland while recognising that legislation would not apply in Northern Ireland (see <a href="/wiki/Articles_2_and_3_of_the_Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Articles 2 and 3 of the Constitution of Ireland">Articles 2 and 3</a>). Articles 2 and 3 were <a href="/wiki/Nineteenth_Amendment_of_the_Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Nineteenth Amendment of the Constitution of Ireland">reworded</a> in 1998 to remove jurisdictional claim over the entire island and to recognise that "a united Ireland shall be brought about only by peaceful means with the consent of a majority of the people, democratically expressed, in both jurisdictions in the island".\n</p><p>With respect to religion, a section of Article 44 included the following:\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The State recognises the special position of the Holy Catholic Apostolic and Roman Church as the guardian of the Faith professed by the great majority of the citizens. The State also recognises the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Ireland" title="Church of Ireland">Church of Ireland</a>, the Presbyterian Church in Ireland, the Methodist Church in Ireland, the <a href="/wiki/Religious_Society_of_Friends" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious Society of Friends">Religious Society of Friends</a> in Ireland, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Jew">Jewish Congregations</a> and the other religious denominations existing in Ireland at the date of the coming into operation of this Constitution.\n</p></blockquote><p> Following a referendum, this section was <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Amendment_of_the_Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Fifth Amendment of the Constitution of Ireland">deleted</a> in 1973.\n</p><p>It was left to the initiative of de Valera\'s successors in government to achieve the country\'s <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_Act_1948" title="Republic of Ireland Act 1948">formal transformation into a republic</a>. A small but significant minority of Irish people, usually attached to parties like Sinn F\xc3\xa9in and the smaller <a href="/wiki/Republican_Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Republican Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Republican Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a>, denied the right of the twenty-six county state to use the name <i>Ireland</i> and continued to refer to the state as the Free State.  With Sinn F\xc3\xa9in\'s entry into <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann" title="D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann">D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> at the close of the 20th century, the number of those who refuse to accept the legitimacy of the state, which was already in a minority, declined further.
