economy of the islands as it is today.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Smith was born in 1804 "at the house of a fashionable <a href="/wiki/Obstetrics_and_gynaecology" title="Obstetrics and gynaecology">accoucheur</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harley_Street" title="Harley Street">Harley Street</a>", London<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> before returning to the family home of <a href="/wiki/Ashlyns_Hall" title="Ashlyns Hall">Ashlyns Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berkhamsted" title="Berkhamsted">Berkhamsted</a>, Hertfordshire, England. He became known locally as a <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropist</a>, doing his best to improve educational standards in his home district, opening several non-denominational schools with his own money. In 1834 he acquired the <a href="/wiki/Lease" title="Lease">lease</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> for \xc2\xa320,000, and set about changing the islanders\' way of life, expelling those who could not find a job locally and reforming the long-neglected leaseholds of the smaller islands, where systematic sub-letting and underinvestment had left the population in a state where subsistence was the best that could be hoped for, and famine a constant threat.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stemmata_Smithiana-Ferraria">Stemmata Smithiana-Ferraria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stemmata Smithiana-Ferraria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1861, Augustus Smith Esq, <a href="/wiki/Member_of_parliament#United_Kingdom" title="Member of parliament">MP</a> published <i>A True and Faithful History of the Family of Smith, Originally Cradled at Wiverton and Cropwell-Butler, in the Parish of Titheby, and more recently established at <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>, in the County of Nottingham</i>. As the title suggests, it traces the family name from its early beginnings and purports \xe2\x80\xb3the county of Nottingham may justly be considered as the fatherland of Smiths, who have always abounded within its borders\xe2\x80\xb3.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Berkhamsted_Common">Berkhamsted Common</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Berkhamsted Common">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1866 <a href="/wiki/Baron_Brownlow" title="Baron Brownlow">Lord Brownlow</a>  tried to enclose <a href="/wiki/Berkhamsted#Development_of_the_modern_town_(19th_and_20th_century)" title="Berkhamsted">Berkhamsted Common</a> with 5\' steel fences built by Woods of Berkhamsted and therefore, claim it as part of his estate. Augustus Smith MP brought out a gang of navvies on a specially chartered train to roll up the fence and leave it within sight of Brownlow\'s house, demonstrating his will to protect Berkhamsted Common for the people of Berkhamsted. This and his actions in promoting <a href="/wiki/Working_class_education" title="Working class education">working class education</a> are commemorated by the award of the Augustus Smith scholarship for state school students in Berkhamsted.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lord_Proprietor_of_Scilly">Lord Proprietor of Scilly</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lord Proprietor of Scilly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1834, Augustus Smith acquired the lease on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> from the Duchy of Cornwall for \xc2\xa320,000, created the quasi-aristocratic title <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#Lord_Proprietors_of_the_Scilly_Islands" title="Isles of Scilly">Lord Proprietor</a> for himself, and set about changing the islanders\' way of life. Besides building a new quay at <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a> on <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St. Mary\'s</a>, he sowed <a href="/wiki/Gorse" class="mw-redirect" title="Gorse">gorse</a> and trees to provide shelter for the agricultural land.  He built schools on the five principal islands, and instituted compulsory education some thirty years before it became the rule on the mainland. Some of his actions were unpopular; in 1855, he moved the ten impoverished inhabitants of the island of <a href="/wiki/Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Samson, Isles of Scilly">Samson</a> to more salubrious quarters and turned the island into a deer park (the deer did not like the habitat, and escaped.) The merit of his reforms is demonstrated by the fact that most of the male alumni of his schools who went to sea ended their career behind the mast, and very few of the female alumni went into service.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Smith lived at <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">Tresco Abbey</a> and developed the gardens on <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>. He never married, but is believed to have had illegitimate children by several of his female servants. He had a close relationship and a lifelong correspondence with Lady Sophia Tower, a married woman (and only daughter of the <a href="/wiki/John_Cust,_1st_Earl_Brownlow" title="John Cust, 1st Earl Brownlow">1st Earl Brownlow</a>) who often visited him at Tresco. The estate was inherited by his nephew <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Algernon_Smith-Dorrien-Smith" title="Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith">Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith</a>, and his descendants retain the lease of Tresco to the present day.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG/250px-Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG/375px-Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG/500px-Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Smith_Monument_Plaque.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The plaque on the front of a monument to Smith, located in the churchyard of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">St Mary\'s Old Church, St Mary\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. Click to enlarge.</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_offices">Public offices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Public offices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>He was <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> Member of Parliament (MP) for <a href="/wiki/Truro_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Truro (UK Parliament constituency)">Truro</a> from 1857 to 1865. He served as President of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Geological_Society_of_Cornwall" title="Royal Geological Society of Cornwall">Royal Geological Society of Cornwall</a> from 1857\xe2\x80\x931862,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and also of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institution_of_Cornwall" title="Royal Institution of Cornwall">Royal Institution of Cornwall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Smith died of gangrene of the lungs in <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> on 31 July 1872. He was laid to rest in <a href="/wiki/St_Buryan%27s_Church" title="St Buryan&#39;s Church">St Buryan\'s Church</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Buryan" title="St Buryan">St Buryan</a>, Cornwall. A tall stone monument was erected in the churchyard of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">St Mary\'s Old Church, St Mary\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> to recognise Smith\'s involvement with the Islands.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Augustus_Smith_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Emperor Smith, The Man who Built Scilly, Sam Llewellyn, Dovecote Press 2005</span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">SMITH, Esq., Augustus (1861). <i><span></span></i>A True and Faithful History of the Family of Smith, Originally Cradled at Wiverton and Cropwell-Butler, in the Parish of Titheby, and more recently established at Nottingham, in the County of Nottingham<i><span></span></i>.
