<b>International League</b> (<b>IL</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> league that operates in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_United_States" title="Eastern United States">eastern United States</a> and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Dublin,_Ohio" title="Dublin, Ohio">Dublin, Ohio</a>. Like the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_League_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican League (baseball)">Mexican League</a>, it plays at the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> level, which is one step below <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>.\n</p><p>It was so named because throughout its history the International League has had teams in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> as well as those in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. However, since the relocation of the <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Lynx" title="Ottawa Lynx">Ottawa Lynx</a> to <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown, Pennsylvania</a>, to become the <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley_IronPigs" title="Lehigh Valley IronPigs">Lehigh Valley IronPigs</a> for the 2008 season, all of the league\'s teams are now based in the U.S. Today, the league is composed of 14 teams across 9 states stretching from <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis, Indiana</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Pawtucket,_Rhode_Island" title="Pawtucket, Rhode Island">Pawtucket, Rhode Island</a>, and from <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Lawrenceville,_Georgia" title="Lawrenceville, Georgia">Lawrenceville, Georgia</a>. \n</p><p>A league champion is determined at the end of every season. The <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Red_Wings" title="Rochester Red Wings">Rochester Red Wings</a> have won 19 <a href="/wiki/List_of_International_League_champions" title="List of International League champions">International League titles</a>, the most in the league\'s history, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bisons" title="Buffalo Bisons">Buffalo Bisons</a> (12) and the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs_(International_League)" title="Toronto Maple Leafs (International League)">Toronto Maple Leafs</a> (11). Since the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Governors%27_Cup" title="Governors&#39; Cup">Governors\' Cup</a> in 1933, the most cup titles have been won by Rochester and the <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Clippers" title="Columbus Clippers">Columbus Clippers</a> (10), followed by the <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_Mets" class="mw-redirect" title="Syracuse Mets">Syracuse Mets</a> (8) and the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Royals" title="Montreal Royals">Montreal Royals</a> (7). After the season, the IL champion plays in the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_National_Championship_Game" title="Triple-A National Championship Game">Triple-A National Championship Game</a> against the Pacific Coast League champion to determine an overall champion of Triple-A baseball. The Columbus Clippers and <a href="/wiki/Durham_Bulls" title="Durham Bulls">Durham Bulls</a> have each won two national championships, more than any other IL teams.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/International_League_executives_in_1915.jpg/220px-International_League_executives_in_1915.jpg" width="220" height="148" class="thumbimage" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:International_League_executives_in_1915.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>International League baseball executives in 1915</div></div></div>\n<p>The International League was created from the mergers of member teams from three precursor leagues: the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_(1884)" title="Eastern League (1884)">Eastern League</a>, which was itself a re-organization of the  Interstate Association of 1883; the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_League_(1885%E2%80%931917)" title="New York State League (1885\xe2\x80\x931917)">New York State League</a>, formed in 1885; and the Ontario League, also organized in 1885. The New York State and Ontario leagues merged in 1886 to form the International League, and in 1887 the Eastern League was absorbed to create a 10-club league.\n</p><p>The league collapsed soon afterwards, when the northern teams claimed that it was too onerous to travel to the south and formed the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_for_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="International Association for Professional Base Ball Players">International Association</a>. Teams and league names came and went over the years. The league was also affected by the effort to establish the <a href="/wiki/Federal_League" title="Federal League">Federal League</a> as a new third major league from 1914 to 1915, with franchises being added and dropped and new ballparks built. In 1954, a franchise was awarded to <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana, Cuba</a>, but due to political upheaval in that country it had to be moved \xe2\x80\x94 to <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City,_New_Jersey" title="Jersey City, New Jersey">Jersey City, New Jersey</a> \xe2\x80\x94 in the middle of the 1960 season. Another foray into the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> failed when the newly created team in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a>, added in 1961, had to be moved to <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_West_Virginia" title="Charleston, West Virginia">Charleston, West Virginia</a>, in mid-season.\n</p><p>In 1971, an International League all-star team beat the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> in an exhibition game in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>, before 11,000 people. In 1984, the all-stars lost to the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> in 11 innings before 11,032 fans in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>, to commemorate the league\'s 100th anniversary.\n</p><p>The International League and the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(20th_century)" title="American Association (20th century)">American Association</a>, another Triple-A league that operated in the Midwest, voted in 1988 to play interleague games as part of the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_Alliance" title="Triple-A Alliance">Triple-A Alliance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TripleATimeLine_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TripleATimeLine-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The league also split into two divisions that year. The interleague concept ended in 1992, but the two league divisions remained.\n</p><p>In 1998, with the addition of three new teams from the disbanded American Association and the <a href="/wiki/Durham_Bulls" title="Durham Bulls">Durham Bulls</a> who previously played in the <a href="/wiki/Carolina_League" title="Carolina League">Carolina League</a>, the International League reorganized into three divisions for the first time.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure_and_season">Structure and season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure and season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The International League is divided into three divisions: the <b>North Division</b>, <b>South Division</b>, and <b>West Division</b>. The North Division consists of six teams, while the South and West Divisions each have four teams.<sup id="cite_ref-standings_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-standings-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams are slated to play 140 games in 2018, reduced from 142 in 2017 and 144 during the years 1998-2016.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The season typically begins during the first week of April and concludes on <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">Labor Day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2017season_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2017season-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The league plays by the same rules listed in the <i>Official Baseball Rules</i> published by Major League Baseball.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" All-Stars at the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_All-Star_Game" title="Triple-A All-Star Game">Triple-A All-Star Game</a></div></div></div>\n<p>At the end of each season, the three divisional leaders and a <a href="/wiki/Wild_card_(sports)" title="Wild card (sports)">wild card</a> team square off in <a href="/wiki/Shaughnessy_playoff_system" title="Shaughnessy playoff system">best-of-five series playoffs</a> to determine a <a href="/wiki/List_of_International_League_champions" title="List of International League champions">league champion</a>, with the winner awarded the <a href="/wiki/Governors%27_Cup" title="Governors&#39; Cup">Governors\' Cup</a>, the league\'s championship trophy. Under this format, the North Division champion plays the wild card team, while the champions of the South and West Divisions play one another in best-of-five series. The winners then play each other in a best-of-five series to determine the champion.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since 2006, the IL champion has played against the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a>\'s champion in the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_National_Championship_Game" title="Triple-A National Championship Game">Triple-A National Championship Game</a>, a single game to determine an overall champion of Triple-A baseball. Previously, the IL champion also competed in the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_World_Series" title="Triple-A World Series">Triple-A World Series</a> (1983, 1998\xe2\x80\x932000), <a href="/wiki/Junior_World_Series" title="Junior World Series">Junior World Series</a> (1919), and other sporadic postseason competitions throughout the league\'s history.\n</p><p>Other interleague play occurs during the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_All-Star_Game" title="Triple-A All-Star Game">Triple-A All-Star Game</a>. Traditionally, the game has taken place on the day after the mid-summer <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">Major League Baseball All-Star Game</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Omaha2015_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Omaha2015-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The game is meant to mark a symbolic halfway-point in the season (though not the mathematical halfway-point which, for most seasons, is usually one month prior). During the All-Star break, no regular-season games are scheduled for two days before the All-Star Game itself.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AwardWinners-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hall_of_fame">Hall title="International League Hall of Fame">International League Hall of Fame</a></div>\n<p>The International League Hall of Fame was established in 1947 to honor league players, managers, and executives who have made significant contributions to the league. The Hall of Fame inducted its first class of 9&#160;men in 1947. A plaque was unveiled at the IL\'s New York City offices located in the Ruppert Building at 535 Fifth Avenue. Today, the plaque has no permanent home, but exists as a traveling display which visits a number of the league\'s ballparks each season. The Hall became dormant after 1963, but was revived in 2007.
