Fr., (1832)</small><br />\n<i>Uredo cruciferarum</i> <small>DC.</small>\n</p>\n</td></tr>\n\n</tbody></table>\n<p><i><b>Albugo candida</b></i>, commonly known as <b>white rust</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oomycete" title="Oomycete">oomycete</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Albuginaceae" title="Albuginaceae">Albuginaceae</a>. It is sometimes called a fungus, but in fact forms part of a distinct lineage of fungus-like microorganisms, Oomycetes, commonly known as <a href="/wiki/Water_mould" class="mw-redirect" title="Water mould">water moulds</a>. <i>A. candida</i> is an <a href="/wiki/Obligate_parasite" title="Obligate parasite">obligate</a> <a href="/wiki/Plant_pathogen" class="mw-redirect" title="Plant pathogen">plant pathogen</a> that infects <a href="/wiki/Brassicaceae" title="Brassicaceae">Brassicaceae</a> species and causes the disease known as <a href="/wiki/White_rust_(disease)" title="White rust (disease)">white rust</a> or white blister rust.<sup id="cite_ref-Saharan2014_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saharan2014-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It has a relatively smaller genome than other oomycetes.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" reading</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albugo_candida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>A. candida</i> has a <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_distribution" title="Cosmopolitan distribution">cosmopolitan distribution</a> and is known from many countries where cruciferous crops are grown in Europe, Asia, Africa, Australasia, North, Central, and South America. Areas, where it has not been recorded, include northern Scandinavia, northern and central Siberia, northern China, western and central Africa, Alaska, northern and central Canada, and southern and western South America.<sup id="cite_ref-CABI_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CABI-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hosts">Hosts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albugo_candida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hosts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>This pathogen infects plants in the family <a href="/wiki/Brassicaceae" title="Brassicaceae">Brassicaceae</a>; the growth stages involved include the seedling stage, the growing stage, the flowering stage, and the fruiting stage.<sup id="cite_ref-CABI_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CABI-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It has been recorded on almost all the varieties and species of the rapeseed-mustard group of crops as well as many wild brassicas. It has also been recorded on plants in the families <a href="/wiki/Aizoaceae" title="Aizoaceae">Aizoaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capparaceae" title="Capparaceae">Capparaceae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleomaceae" title="Cleomaceae">Cleomaceae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Amaranthaceae" title="Amaranthaceae">Amaranthaceae</a>. There are many different races and varieties of <i>A. candida</i>, each infecting its own group of species; for example, one infects <i><a href="/wiki/Capsella_(plant)" title="Capsella (plant)">Capsella</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Arabis" title="Arabis">Arabis</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Lepidium" title="Lepidium">Lepidium</a></i>, while another infects <i><a href="/wiki/Brassica" title="Brassica">Brassica</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Diplotaxis_(plant)" title="Diplotaxis (plant)">Diplotaxis</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Sinapis" title="Sinapis">Sinapis</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Saharan_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saharan-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symptoms">Symptoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albugo_candida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Symptoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>White rust can infect plants both locally and systemically. On stems, leaves, and inflorescences it appears as a mass of white or cream-coloured pustules, each about 2&#160;mm (0.08&#160;in) in diameter, packed with <a href="/wiki/Sporangium" title="Sporangium">sporangia</a>. The systemic version causes distortion, abnormal growth forms, and sterile inflorescences. The abnormal growth forms are sometimes known as "stagheads".<sup id="cite_ref-Ferreira_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferreira-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Infection with white rust predisposes a crop to develop <a href="/wiki/Downy_mildew" title="Downy mildew">downy mildew</a>, caused by another oomycete plant pathogen <i><a href="/wiki/Phytophthora_nicotianae" title="Phytophthora nicotianae">Phytophthora nicotianae</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life-cycle">Life-cycle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albugo_candida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Life-cycle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When liberated, the sporangia inside the pustules are spread by wind, rain, and insects. After landing on a susceptible plant, each sporangium gives rise to about six <a href="/wiki/Zoospore" title="Zoospore">zoospores</a> which, under suitable conditions of moisture and light, form germ tubes which invade the plant\'s tissues. Thick-walled sexual spores, called <a href="/wiki/Oospore" title="Oospore">oospores</a> are produced which germinate, producing either vesicles inside the plant tissue, exit tubes with vesicles at the tip, or germ tubes. Further zoospores develop inside the vesicles. The infection is spread by either oospore-infected seed or by mechanical movement of sporangia.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferreira_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferreira-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albugo_candida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.speciesfungorum.org/GSD/GSDspecies.asp?RecordID=122113">"<i>Albugo candida</i> (Pers.)
