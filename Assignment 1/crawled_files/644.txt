(2009)</div></div></div></div>\n<p>Whether an islet is considered a rock or not can have significant economic consequences under Article 121 of the <a href="/wiki/UN_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" class="mw-redirect" title="UN Convention on the Law of the Sea">UN Convention on the Law of the Sea</a>, which stipulates that "Rocks which cannot sustain human habitation or economic life of their own shall have no <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone" title="Exclusive economic zone">exclusive economic zone</a> or <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a>." One long-term dispute over the status of such an islet was that of <a href="/wiki/Snake_Island_(Black_Sea)" title="Snake Island (Black Sea)">Snake Island (Black Sea)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lathrop_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lathrop-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a> jurisprudence however sometimes ignores islets, regardless of inhabitation status, in deciding territorial disputes; it did so in 2009 in adjudicating the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_delimitation_between_Romania_and_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime delimitation between Romania and Ukraine">Romania-Ukraine dispute</a>, and previously in the dispute between Libya and Malta involving the islet of <a href="/wiki/Filfla" title="Filfla">Filfla</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lathrop_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lathrop-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Smith1991_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smith1991-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_islets">List of islets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Islet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: List of islets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are thousands of islets on Earth: approximately 24,000 islands and islets in the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_archipelago" title="Stockholm archipelago">Stockholm archipelago</a> alone.
