template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>British\xe2\x80\x93Irish Council</b> (<b>BIC</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Intergovernmental organisation">intergovernmental organisation</a> that aims to improve collaboration between its members in a number of areas including transport, the environment, and energy.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its membership comprises <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Devolution_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Devolution in the United Kingdom">devolved governments</a> of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, and the governments of the <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependencies" title="Crown dependencies">Crown dependencies</a> of the UK: <a href="/wiki/Bailiwick_of_Guernsey" title="Bailiwick of Guernsey">Guernsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>. <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> does not have a <a href="/wiki/Devolution_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Devolution in the United Kingdom">devolved</a> administration, and as a result is not individually represented on the Council.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The British and Irish governments, and political parties in Northern Ireland, agreed to form a Council under the British\xe2\x80\x93Irish Agreement, part of the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a> reached in 1998. The Council was formally established on 2 December 1999, when the Agreement came into effect. The Council\'s stated aim is to "promote the harmonious and mutually beneficial development of the totality of relationships among the peoples of these islands". The BIC has a standing secretariat, located in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, and meets in semi-annual summit session and more frequent <a href="/wiki/Government_minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Government minister">ministerial</a> meetings.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div href="/wiki/Summit_(meeting)" title="Summit (meeting)">summits</a> twice per year. Additionally, there are regular meetings that deal with specific sectors and are attended by the corresponding ministers. Representatives of members operate in accordance with whatever procedures for democratic authority and accountability are in force in their respective elected legislatures.\n</p><p>England, unlike the other countries of the United Kingdom, is not represented separately, as it does not have its own devolved administration. It is thus solely represented on the Council as part of the United Kingdom.\n</p><p>The work of the Council is financed by members through mutual agreement as required.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At the ninth meeting of the Council,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (September 2014)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> it was decided that with devolved government returned to Northern Ireland that an opportune time existed "to undertake a strategic review of the Council\'s work programmes, working methods and support arrangements." This decision included the potential for a permanent standing secretariat, which was established in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, on 4 January 2012.\n</p><p>At its June 2010 summit, the Council decided to move forward on recommendations to enhance the relationship between it and the <a href="/wiki/British-Irish_Parliamentary_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="British-Irish Parliamentary Assembly">British-Irish Parliamentary Assembly</a> (BIPA). The British-Irish Parliamentary Assembly is made up of members from the parliaments and assemblies of the same states and regions as the members of the British\xe2\x80\x93Irish Council. The Council tasked its secretariat with moving this work forward in conjunction with the BIPA\'s secretariat.\n</p><p>In addition to the above members <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall</a> has been a full observer member since 2010 due to the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish language</a> falling under the Council\'s areas of work.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_areas">Work areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British%E2%80%93Irish_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Council agrees to specific work areas for which individual members take responsibility. The Belfast Agreement suggested transport links, agriculture, environmental issues, culture, health, education and approaches to the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> as suitable topics for early discussion. However, these work areas can be expanded or reduced as the Council decides. It is also open to the Council to make agreement on common policies. These agreements are made through consensus, although individual members may opt not to participate in implementing any of these.\n</p><p>The current list of work areas and the member responsible are:\n</p>\n<div>\n\n&#32;\n<table class="multicol" role="presentation" style="border-collapse: collapse; padding: 0; border: 0; background:transparent; width:100%;"><tbody><tr>\n<td style="width: 50%;text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">\n<ul><li>Collaborative spatial planning (Northern Ireland)</li>\n<li>Demography (Scotland)</li>\n<li>Digital inclusion (Isle of Man)</li>\n<li>Early years policy (Wales)</li>\n<li>Energy (United Kingdom - Electricity Grids, and Scotland - Marine)</li>\n<li>Environment (United Kingdom)</li></ul>\n<p>&#32;\n</p>\n</td>\n<td style="width: 50%;text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">\n<ul><li>Housing (Northern Ireland)</li>\n<li>Indigenous, minority and lesser-used languages (Wales)</li>\n<li>Misuse of Substances (drugs and alcohol) (Ireland)</li>\n<li>Social inclusion (Scotland and Wales)</li>\n<li>Transport (Northern Ireland)</li>\n<li>Creative Industries (Jersey)</li></ul>\n<p>&#32;\n</p>\n</td></tr></tbody></table></div>\n<p>Demography was adopted as a work area at the 2006 meeting of the Council. It was proposed by the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Executive</a>, who also took responsibility for it. During the 2007 meeting of the Council the Scottish Government further proposed that <a href="/wiki/Energy_policy" title="Energy policy">energy</a> become a work area of the Council. Past work sector areas included knowledge economy, e-health / telemedicine and tourism.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_of_the_Council">Name of the Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British%E2%80%93Irish_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Name of the Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Initial suggestions for the council included the names <i>Council of the British Isles</i><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> or <i>Council of the Isles</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the council has sometimes been known by the latter name. However, owing to <a href="/wiki/British_Isles_naming_dispute" title="British Isles naming dispute">sensibilities around the term <i>British Isles</i></a>, particularly in Ireland, the name <i>British-Irish Council</i> was agreed.\n</p><p>The official name of the Council is represented in minority and lesser-used languages of the council as:\n</p>\n<div>\n\n&#32;\n<table class="multicol"
