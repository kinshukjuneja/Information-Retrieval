Counsell</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">General Manager</th><td>\n<a href="/wiki/David_Stearns" title="David Stearns">David Stearns</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Milwaukee Brewers</b> are an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee, Wisconsin</a>.  The Brewers compete in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) as a member club of the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> (NL) <a href="/wiki/National_League_Central" title="National League Central">Central</a> division.  The team is named for the city\'s association with the <a href="/wiki/Brewing_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Brewing industry">brewing industry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Since 2001, the Brewers have played their home games at <a href="/wiki/Miller_Park_(Milwaukee)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miller Park (Milwaukee)">Miller Park</a>, which has a seating capacity of 41,900.\n</p><p>The team was founded in 1969 as the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Pilots" title="Seattle Pilots">Seattle Pilots</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion team</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> (AL), in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle, Washington</a>. The Pilots played their home games at <a href="/wiki/Sick%27s_Stadium" title="Sick&#39;s Stadium">Sick\'s Stadium</a>. After only one season, the team <a href="/wiki/Relocation_of_professional_sports_teams" title="Relocation of professional sports teams">relocated</a> to Milwaukee, becoming known as the Brewers and playing their home games at <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_County_Stadium" title="Milwaukee County Stadium">Milwaukee County Stadium</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1998 Major League Baseball season">1998</a>, the Brewers joined the National League. They are the only franchise to play in four divisions since the advent of divisional play in Major League Baseball in 1969. They are also one of two current MLB franchises to switch leagues in their history, the other one being the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>. \n</p><p>The team\'s only <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> appearance came in <a href="/wiki/1982_World_Series" title="1982 World Series">1982</a>. After winning the <a href="/wiki/1982_American_League_Championship_Series" title="1982 American League Championship Series">ALCS</a> against the <a href="/wiki/1982_California_Angels_season" title="1982 California Angels season">California Angels</a>, the <a href="/wiki/1982_Milwaukee_Brewers_season" title="1982 Milwaukee Brewers season">Brewers</a> faced off against the <a href="/wiki/1982_St._Louis_Cardinals_season" title="1982 St. Louis Cardinals season">St. Louis Cardinals</a> in the World Series, losing 4\xe2\x80\x933. In 2011, the <a href="/wiki/2011_Milwaukee_Brewers_season" title="2011 Milwaukee Brewers season">Brewers</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/2011_Arizona_Diamondbacks_season" title="2011 Arizona Diamondbacks season">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> to win the <a href="/wiki/2011_National_League_Division_Series" title="2011 National League Division Series">NLDS</a> 3\xe2\x80\x932, but lost in the <a id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Milwaukee_Brewers" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the Milwaukee Brewers">History of the Milwaukee Brewers</a></div>\n<p>Originating as an <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion team</a> in 1969, in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Pilots" title="Seattle Pilots">Seattle Pilots</a>, the club played for one season in the American League West Division before being acquired in bankruptcy court by <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a>, who then moved the team to <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a>. They would continue to play in the West Division for two more years.  Before the beginning of the 1972 season the Brewers agreed to switch over to the American League East to make room for the Texas Rangers who had relocated from Washington.  Beginning in 1994, due to divisional re-alignment, the Brewers moved to the newly created American League Central division. In all, the Brewers were part of the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> from their creation in 1969 through the 1997 season, after which they moved to the <a href="/wiki/National_League_Central_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="National League Central Division">National League Central Division</a>. Milwaukee had previously been a <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> city when its team was the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Braves_(1953%E2%80%9365)" class="mw-redirect" title="Milwaukee Braves (1953\xe2\x80\x9365)">Milwaukee Braves</a> (1953\xe2\x80\x931965).\n</p><p>In 1981, Milwaukee won the <a href="/wiki/American_League_East_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="American League East Division">American League East Division</a> in the second half of the strike-shortened season. In the playoffs, they lost the divisional series to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, three games to two.\n</p><p>In 1982, Milwaukee won the <a href="/wiki/American_League_East_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="American League East Division">American League East Division</a> and the American League Pennant, earning their only <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> appearance to date as the Brewers. In the <a href="/wiki/1982_World_Series" title="1982 World Series">Series</a>, they lost to the <a href="/wiki/1982_St._Louis_Cardinals_season" title="1982 St. Louis Cardinals season">St. Louis Cardinals</a> four games to three.\n</p><p>In 1998, the Brewers changed leagues, going from the American League to the National League.\n</p><p>In 2008, for the first time in the 26&#160;years since their World Series appearance, the Brewers advanced to postseason play by winning the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_wild_card" title="Major League Baseball wild card">National League wild card</a>. They were eliminated in the <a href="/wiki/2008_National_League_Division_Series" title="2008 National League Division Series">National League Division Series</a> by the eventual <a href="/wiki/2008_World_Series" title="2008 World Series">World Series</a> champion <a href="/wiki/2008_Philadelphia_Phillies_season" title="2008 Philadelphia Phillies season">Philadelphia Phillies</a>.\n</p><p>On September 23, 2011, the Milwaukee Brewers clinched their first division title in 29&#160;years. They won the <a href="/wiki/2011_National_League_Division_Series" title="2011 National League Division Series">National League Division Series</a> in five games over the <a href="/wiki/2011_Arizona_Diamondbacks_season" title="2011 Arizona Diamondbacks season">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>, but lost the <a href="/wiki/2011_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2011 National League Championship Series">National League Championship Series</a><sup id="cite_ref-history_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> to the eventual <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Series" title="2011 World Series">World Series</a> champion <a href="/wiki/2011_St._Louis_Cardinals_season" title="2011 St. Louis Cardinals season">St. Louis Cardinals</a> in six games.\n</p><p>In 2018, the Brewers clinched a spot in the post-season for the first time since 2011 with a 2\xe2\x80\x931 victory over the <a href="/wiki/2018_St._Louis_Cardinals_season" title="2018 St. Louis Cardinals season">St. Louis Cardinals</a> on September 26, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 29, they tied with the Cubs for first place in the National League Central, with a record of 95\xe2\x80\x9367; at the end of the day on September 30, the Cubs and Brewers were still tied. This tie was broken on October 1st, when the Brewers defeated the Cubs 3\xe2\x80\x931 in the <a href="/wiki/2018_National_League_Central_tie-breaker_game" title="2018 National League Central tie-breaker game">NL Central tiebreaker</a> to improve to 96\xe2\x80\x9367 and win the division by one game. They went on to defeat the <a href="/wiki/2018_Colorado_Rockies_season" title="2018 Colorado Rockies season">Colorado Rockies</a> 3\xe2\x80\x930 to win the <a href="/wiki/2018_National_League_Division_Series" title="2018 National League Division Series">NLDS</a>, but in the following <a href="/wiki/2018_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2018 National League Championship Series">NLCS</a>, they lost out to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> in 7 games.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_uniforms">Team uniforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Team uniforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uniforms">Uniforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uniforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span id="1970.E2.80.9377"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1970\xe2\x80\x9377">1970\xe2\x80\x9377</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1970\xe2\x80\x9377">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The first Brewers uniforms were "hand-me-downs" from the Seattle Pilots. Because the move to Milwaukee received final approval less than a week before the start of the season, there was no time to order new uniforms. Selig had originally planned to change the Brewers\' colors to navy blue and red in honor of the <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">minor league</a> <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(20th_century)" title="American Association (20th century)">American Association\'s</a> <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers_(American_Association)" title="Milwaukee Brewers (American Association)">Milwaukee Brewers</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> but was forced to simply remove the Seattle markings from the Pilots\' blue-and-gold uniforms and sew "BREWERS" on the front. However, the outline of the Pilots\' logo remained visible. The uniforms had unique striping on the sleeves left over from the Pilots days. The cap was an updated version of the Milwaukee Braves cap in blue and yellow. Ultimately, it was decided to keep blue and gold as the team colors, and they have remained so ever since.\n</p><p>The Brewers finally got their own flannel design in 1971. This design was essentially the same as the one used in 1970, but with blue and yellow piping on the sleeves and collar. In 1972, the Brewers entered the double-knit era with uniforms based upon their flannels: all white with "BREWERS" on the front and blue and yellow trim on the sleeves, neck, waistband and down the side of the pants. This is the uniform that <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron" title="Hank Aaron">Hank Aaron</a> wore with the club in his final seasons and that Robin Yount wore in his first. During this period, the logo of the club was the <a href="/wiki/Beer_Barrel_Man" title="Beer Barrel Man">Beer Barrel Man</a>, which had been used by the previous minor league Brewers since at least the 1940s. The Brewers mascot, <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Brewer" title="Bernie Brewer">Bernie Brewer</a> (a man with a large yellow mustache wearing a Brewers hat) was introduced in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span id="1978.E2.80.9393"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1978\xe2\x80\x9393">1978\xe2\x80\x9393</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1978\xe2\x80\x9393">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The Brewers unveiled new uniforms for the 1978 season. The uniforms featured pinstripes with a solid blue collar and waistband. The road uniforms continued to be powder blue, but for the first time the city name, "MILWAUKEE", graced the chest in an upward slant. In addition, this season saw the introduction of the logo that was to define the club: "M" and "B" in the shape of a baseball glove. The logo was designed by Tom Meindel, an art history student at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin-Eau_Claire" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire">University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire</a>. The home cap was solid blue, and the road cap was blue with a yellow front panel. Additionally, their batting helmets had a white front panel. The club wore these uniforms in their pennant-winning season of 1982. Only minor changes were made until 1990; the color of the road uniforms changed to gray in 1986, while the blue-yellow-blue road cap and white-paneled batting helmets were abandoned at the same time.\n</p><p>In 1990, the Brewers made significant modifications to their uniforms, switching from pullover to button-down jerseys (the last American League team to do so). Their individual uniforms showed other changes as well; at home, the blue piping was removed and the block lettered "BREWERS" was changed to a script version similar to the script used on road uniforms, while those outfits had their piping changed from blue-yellow-blue to blue-yellow. The road jerseys were the first uniforms in franchise history to feature player names on the back; names were added to the home jerseys beginning in 1993.\n</p>\n<h4><span id="1994.E2.80.9399"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1994\xe2\x80\x9399">1994\xe2\x80\x9399</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1994\xe2\x80\x9399">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In 1994, in collaboration with the Brewers celebrating their 25th year in Milwaukee, the team did a radical makeover of their uniforms. The ball-in-glove logo was removed and replaced with a stylized interlocking "M" and "B" set on a pair of crossed bats and a diamond background. The royal blue changed to navy blue, while the yellow changed to a metallic gold. Forest green was added as a third color. The jerseys swapped pinstripes for retro-themed piping around the collar, buttons, and sleeves, following a trend that was popular in the 1990s. The uniforms\' lettering had the same style of letters as the new cap logo with heavily stylized "BREWERS" lettering on the home jerseys and "MILWAUKEE" on the road grays. For the first time, an alternative jersey was introduced. It was navy blue with the home "BREWERS" lettering on the front and featured the Brewers\' logo on the lower left side. The caps featured the interlocking "MB" logo (without the bats or diamond) on both the home and away versions. The home cap was completely navy blue, while the away cap featured a navy blue crown and a forest green bill.\n</p><p>In 1997, the uniforms were slightly modified, with the main logo being removed from the caps and replaced with an "M". All navy caps were worn with both the home and away uniforms; the home hats featured a white "M" and the road caps had a gold "M." The green socks that had previously been worn on the road were changed to navy blue. The blue alternate jersey placed the player\'s number on the lower left side instead of the logo.\n</p>\n<h4><span id="2000.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000\xe2\x80\x93present">2000\xe2\x80\x93present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2000\xe2\x80\x93present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Before the 2000 season, to coincide with the anticipated opening of Miller Park, the Brewers changed their uniforms again. The block letters on the front were replaced with "Brewers" in a flowing script, and green was removed as the third color. The cap logo was a script "M", similar in style to the Miller logo, with a head of barley underlining it, symbolizing Milwaukee\'s beer-making industry. The home uniforms also featured a patch on the left sleeve consisting of the cap logo with a gold outline of the state of Wisconsin behind it, showing the Brewers statewide appeal. The road uniforms were grey and featured the same script "Brewers" on the front, with a simple patch on the left sleeve bearing a script "Milwaukee". There was also an alternate navy blue jersey that had the same features as the home jersey.\n</p><p>Although the uniforms were supposed to debut with the opening of Miller Park, the <a href="/wiki/Big_Blue_(crane)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Blue (crane)">Big Blue crane collapse</a> in July 1999, which cost the lives of three workers and caused damage to the first base side of the stadium, delayed the opening of Miller Park for one year, so the uniforms actually debuted at <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_County_Stadium" title="Milwaukee County Stadium">Milwaukee County Stadium</a> in the ballpark\'s final year.\n</p><p>In 2006, the Brewers introduced Retro Sundays, when the Brewers would wear uniforms featuring the "ball-in-glove" logo. The uniforms are similar to the uniforms worn from 1978 to 1989, but with some modern modifications, such as the uniforms being button-up instead of pullover, displaying players\' last names on the backs of the jerseys, and a "ball-in-glove" logo patch on the left sleeve. In 2007, the Retro day was changed from Sunday to Friday, though they may also be worn outside of those days if a <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> chooses the retro uniforms to wear during his start. In 2010, the Brewers debuted a new alternate road jersey which, like the other alternate jersey, is navy blue, but bears a script "Milwaukee" on the front. In 2013, a gold alternate jersey with "Brewers" on the front was introduced, as well.\n</p><p>During the off-season before the 2013 season, the Brewers allowed fans to design their own Milwaukee Brewers uniforms. Three finalists were chosen, which fans were given the opportunity to vote for their favorite through the Brewers website. The winning uniform was designed by Ben Peters of Richfield, Minnesota, and was worn by the Brewers for two spring training games.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016, the Brewers replaced their road navy and home gold alternates with a new navy alternate jersey. The uniform is similar to the previous road navy alternate but with yellow replacing gold as the trim color, and will be paired with a navy cap featuring the "ball-and-glove" logo. Since 2017, both alternate navy uniforms are used regardless of home or road games.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Achievements">Achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milwaukee_Brewers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Milwaukee_Brewers_award_winners_and_All-Stars" title="List of Milwaukee Brewers award winners and All-Stars">List of Milwaukee Brewers award winners and All-Stars</a></div>\n<p>Three Brewers have won <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="Major League Baseball Most Valuable Player Award">MVP awards</a> during their career with the team. While in the American League, <a href="/wiki/Rollie_Fingers" title="Rollie Fingers">Rollie Fingers</a> won the award in 1981, and <a href="/wiki/Robin_Yount" title="Robin Yount">Robin Yount</a> received the honor in 1982 and 1989. <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Braun" title="Ryan Braun">Ryan Braun</a> won the National League MVP award in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-MVPCYA_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MVPCYA-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Two pitchers have won the <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a> in the American League. Rollie Fingers won in 1981, and <a href="/wiki/Pete_Vuckovich" title="Pete Vuckovich">Pete Vuckovich</a> won in 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-MVPCYA_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MVPCYA-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Two players have been named <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" title="Major League Baseball Rookie of the Year Award">Rookie of the Year</a>. <a href="/wiki/Pat_Listach" title="Pat Listach">Pat Listach</a> won the American League\'s award in 1992, and Ryan Braun won the National League award in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Christian_Yelich" title="Christian Yelich">Christian Yelich</a> won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_batting_champions" title="List of Major League Baseball batting champions">National League Batting title</a> in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>The batting title was the first in Milwaukee Brewers history.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hall_of_Famers">Hall of Famers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a data-file-width="1093" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bob_Uecker.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a Brewers\' <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> radio station is <a href="/wiki/WTMJ_(AM)" title="WTMJ (AM)">WTMJ</a> (620 <a href="/wiki/AM_broadcasting" title="AM broadcasting">AM</a>/103.3 FM). <a href="/wiki/Bob_Uecker" title="Bob Uecker">Bob Uecker</a>, a winner of the <a href="/wiki/Ford_C._Frick_Award" title="Ford C. Frick Award">Ford C. Frick Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Baseball Hall of Fame</a>, joined the Brewers in 1970, when the team moved from Seattle, and has been there ever since. Alongside Uecker are Jeff Levering and Lane Grindle. Levering joined the team\'s radio broadcast in 2015 as a fill-in for Uecker on select road games<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and Grindle joined the team in 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> replacing Joe Block, who had left to join the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> after the 2015 season.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Block replaced Cory Provus who had left to become the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> lead broadcaster on radio after the 2011 season.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Provus, formerly of <a href="/wiki/WGN_(AM)" title="WGN (AM)">WGN radio</a> in Chicago, replaced Jim Powell, who left Milwaukee for the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> radio network. Powell in turn replaced <a href="/wiki/Pat_Hughes_(sportscaster)" title="Pat Hughes (sportscaster)">Pat Hughes</a>, who departed to do play-by-play for the Cubs on WGN in 1996. The Brewers radio broadcasts usually feature a 2-2-2-1-2 format where Uecker does solo play-by-play for the first, middle and last 2 innings, while Levering does innings 3-4 and 7, and both doing analysis throughout and varied presentation for extra innings games. Starting with the 2014 season Uecker cut back on the amount of road games he works due to health concerns, mainly involving West Coast trips and distant road games in <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta</a>; Block handled the play-by-play, with former Brewer and Met <a href="/wiki/Darryl_Hamilton" title="Darryl Hamilton">Darryl Hamilton</a> on color for the first series at Atlanta.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Select daytime home games were formerly broadcast in Spanish over <a href="/wiki/Waukesha,_Wisconsin" title="Waukesha, Wisconsin">Waukesha</a>-licensed <a href="/wiki/ESPN_Deportes_Radio" title="ESPN Deportes Radio">ESPN Deportes Radio</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/WRRD" title="WRRD">WRRD</a> (1510), with Jaime Cano serving as play-by-play announcer. In 2017 the station was purchased by another party which instituted an English-language talk format, effectively ending that arrangement.\n</p><p>Most of the team\'s television broadcasts are aired on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_Wisconsin" title="Fox Sports Wisconsin">Fox Sports Wisconsin</a>. <a href="/wiki/Brian_Anderson_(broadcaster)" class="mw-redirect" title="Brian Anderson (broadcaster)">Brian Anderson</a>, who has worked on <a href="/wiki/The_Golf_Channel" class="mw-redirect" title="The Golf Channel">The Golf Channel</a>, took over as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Milwaukee_Brewers_broadcasters" title="List of Milwaukee Brewers broadcasters">Brewers\' play-by-play announcer</a> for the 2007 season. He replaced <a href="/wiki/Daron_Sutton" title="Daron Sutton">Daron Sutton</a>, who joined the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>. The color commentator is <a href="/wiki/Bill_Schroeder_(baseball)" title="Bill Schroeder (baseball)">Bill Schroeder</a>, a former major league catcher who played six of his eight seasons for the Brewers. As of 2014 Schroeder is in his 20th season as the Brewers\' color commentator. The 2010 season was the first year where all of Fox Sports Wisconsin\'s games were broadcast in <a href="/wiki/High_definition_television" class="mw-redirect" title="High definition television">high definition</a>. Anderson (who also is a part of <a href="/wiki/MLB_on_TBS" class="mw-redirect" title="MLB on TBS">TBS</a> playoff coverage) also provided play-by-play for the 2011 NLCS due to <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Johnson,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Ernie Johnson, Jr.">Ernie Johnson</a> stepping aside for the year due to a medical situation with his son.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Since 2014, as Anderson\'s <a href="/wiki/Turner_Sports" title="Turner Sports">Turner Sports</a> duties have increased along with the addition of NCAA college basketball and <a href="/wiki/NBA_on_TNT" title="NBA on TNT">NBA on TNT</a> play-by-play duties, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Badgers" title="Wisconsin Badgers">Wisconsin Badgers</a> football and men\'s college basketball radio announcer <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lepay" title="Matt Lepay">Matt Lepay</a> has served as play-by-play man on days when Anderson has other assignments for Turner Sports.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 2007\xe2\x80\x932011, the Brewers and FSN Wisconsin subcontracted to <a href="/wiki/Weigel_Broadcasting" title="Weigel Broadcasting">Weigel Broadcasting</a> a package of 15 games and one spring training game over-the-air on <a href="/wiki/WBME-CD" title="WBME-CD">WMLW-CA</a> (then-Channel 41/58.2) in Milwaukee each season with FSN Wisconsin producing the telecasts and Weigel selling air time for each of those games and additional games added depending on weather postponements and pennant race standings (WMLW-CA games would air on the outstate FSN Wisconsin network for the remainder of the state). The deal was ended before the 2012 season in order to facilitate full-season HD coverage on FSN Wisconsin and distribution complications, along with the addition of a "Plus" channel for <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> play-by-play conflict situations. Weigel continues to air a few Sunday home broadcasts per year with Spanish language play-by-play on <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/WYTU-LD" title="WYTU-LD">WYTU-LD</a> (Channels 63/58.4), which produces their own broadcasts using FSN\'s camera positions with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hector_Molina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hector Molina (page does not exist)">Hector Molina</a> on play-by-play and bilingual WDJT sports anchor Kevin Holden on color.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Five of the six major network television stations in Milwaukee, along with WMLW-CA, have carried game broadcasts over the years, with <a href="/wiki/WTMJ-TV" title="WTMJ-TV">WTMJ-TV</a> being the original broadcaster in the 1970s. <a href="/wiki/WVTV" title="WVTV">WVTV</a> carried the team for the bulk of the 1980s and early 1990s, with <a href="/wiki/WVTV-DT2" title="WVTV-DT2">WCGV-TV</a> following from 1994 until 2004, and <a href="/wiki/WISN-TV" title="WISN-TV">WISN-TV</a> carrying select Sunday games at the beginning of the 2000s. <a href="/wiki/WITI_(TV)" title="WITI (TV)">WITI</a> is the only station not to have carried local coverage of the team through its history (though former WITI sports anchor and current <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Bucks</a> play-by-play man <a href="/wiki/Jim_Paschke" title="Jim Paschke">Jim Paschke</a> was the team\'s TV announcer during its time with WVTV and portions of WCGV\'s coverage contract), although it has aired national games from <a href="/wiki/MLB_on_CBS" class="mw-redirect" title="MLB on CBS">CBS</a> and <a href="/wiki/MLB_on_Fox" class="mw-redirect" title="MLB on Fox">Fox</a> involving the Brewers through the years.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
