A species that is the sole <i>extant</i> member of its genus (e.g. the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Homo_(genus)" class="mw-redirect" title="Homo (genus)">Homo</a></i>) is not necessarily <i>sui generis</i>; extinctions have eliminated other congenerous species.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creative_arts">Creative arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Creative arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A book, movie, television series, or other artistic creation is called <i>sui generis</i> when it does not fit into standard genre boundaries. Movie critic Richard Schickel identifies<i> <a href="/wiki/Joe_Versus_the_Volcano" title="Joe Versus the Volcano">Joe Versus the Volcano</a></i> as a <i>sui generis</i> movie.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Film critic Michael Brooke used the term to describe <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Planet" title="Fantastic Planet">Fantastic Planet</a></i>, a 1973 Franco-Czech sci-fi film directed by <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Laloux" title="Ren\xc3\xa9 Laloux">Ren\xc3\xa9 Laloux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law">Law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a>, it is a <a href="/wiki/Term_of_art" class="mw-redirect" title="Term of art">term of art</a> used to identify a legal classification that exists independently of other categorizations, either because of its singularity or due to the specific creation of an entitlement or obligation.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> For example, a court\'s contempt powers arise<i> sui generis</i> and not from statute or rule.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/New_York_Court_of_Appeals" title="New York Court of Appeals">New York Court of Appeals</a> has used the term in describing cooperative apartment corporations, mostly because this form of housing is considered <a href="/wiki/Real_property" title="Real property">real property</a> for some purposes and <a href="/wiki/Personal_property" title="Personal property">personal property</a> for other purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>When citing cases and other authorities, lawyers and judges may refer to "a <i>sui generis</i> case", or "a <i>sui generis</i> authority", meaning it is a special one confined to <i>its own</i> facts, and therefore may not be of broader application.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aboriginal_law_and_education">Aboriginal law and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Aboriginal law and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The motto "Sui Generis" has been adopted by the <a href="/wiki/Akitsiraq_Law_School" title="Akitsiraq Law School">Akitsiraq Law School</a> because it is a <i>sui generis</i> <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_title" title="Aboriginal title">(aboriginal) title</a> in all of <a href="/wiki/Canadian_aboriginal_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian aboriginal law">Canadian aboriginal law</a> institutes by dint of its title being Inuktitut, the Aboriginal language of the Inuit in the far north of Canada. More importantly, in aboriginal professional legal education, the work of Aboriginal people to define and create contemporary aboriginal education is a <i>thing of its own kind</i> having <i>sui generis</i> admissions and <i>sui generis</i> curriculum.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Intellectual_property_law">Intellectual property law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Intellectual property law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Generally speaking, protection for <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual property</a> extends to intellectual creations in order to incentivize innovation, and depends upon the nature of the work and its <i>characteristics</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The main types of intellectual property law are: <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyright</a>, which protects creative works; <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a>, which protects invention; <a href="/wiki/Trade_secret" title="Trade secret">trade secret</a>, which protects information not generally known or readily ascertainable that is valuable to the secret holder; and <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademark</a>, which protects branding and other exclusive properties of products and services. Any matter that meets these criteria can be protected.\n</p><p>However, <i>sui generis</i> statutes exist in many countries that extend intellectual property protection to matter that does not meet characteristic definitions: <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit_layout_design_protection" title="Integrated circuit layout design protection">integrated circuit layouts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hull_(ship)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hull (ship)">ship hull designs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">fashion designs</a> in France, <a href="/wiki/Database_right" class="mw-redirect" title="Database right">databases</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Plant_variety_(law)" title="Plant variety (law)">plant varieties</a> require <i>sui generis</i> statutes because of their unique characteristics. The United States, Japan, and many EU countries protect the topography of semiconductor chips and <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuits" class="mw-redirect" title="Integrated circuits">integrated circuits</a> under <i>sui generis</i> laws, which borrow some aspects from patent or copyright law. In the U.S. this <i>sui generis</i> law is known as the <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_Chip_Protection_Act_of_1984" title="Semiconductor Chip Protection Act of 1984">Semiconductor Chip Protection Act of 1984</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Statutory_law">Statutory law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Statutory law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. The word <i>large</i> is ambiguous per se, but may be considered <i>heavy</i>.  Thus the relevant legislation (in Australian law) contains a section called <i>Terms used</i> or <i>Definitions</i> that itemises all words considered ambiguous, and confers specific interpretations consistent with natural language.  So, this list tells us that <i>heavy vehicle</i> means a vehicle with a <i>GVM</i> over 4.5 tons;<i> and </i>GVM<i> means </i>gross vehicle mass<i>\xe2\x80\x94the maximum loaded mass of the vehicle. Further explanations cover various contingencies.  Thus </i>large<i> is equivalent to </i>heavy<i> and is (for this unique case) clearly defined, </i>sui generis<i>.</i>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Town_planning_law">Town planning law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Town planning law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In United Kingdom <a href="/wiki/Town_and_country_planning_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Town and country planning in the United Kingdom">town planning</a> law, in particular relating to the <a href="/wiki/Town_and_Country_Planning_(Use_Classes)_Order_1987" title="Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987">Town and Country Planning (Use Classes) Order 1987</a>, many common types of land use are categorised into specific "use classes". Change of use of land <i>within</i> a use class does not require planning permission; however, changing <i>between</i> use classes might require planning permission, and permission is always required if the new use is <i>sui generis</i>.\n</p><p>Examples of <i>sui generis</i> uses include <a href="/wiki/Embassy" class="mw-redirect" title="Embassy">embassies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amusement_arcade" title="Amusement arcade">amusement arcades</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laundrette" class="mw-redirect" title="Laundrette">laundrettes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taxicab" title="Taxicab">taxi</a> or <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_hire" class="mw-redirect" title="Vehicle hire">vehicle hire</a> businesses, <a href="/wiki/Gas_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Gas station">petrol filling stations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scrapyard" class="mw-redirect" title="Scrapyard">scrapyards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nightclub" title="Nightclub">nightclubs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Car_dealership" title="Car dealership">motor car showrooms</a>, retail warehouses, clubs and <a href="/wiki/Hostel" title="Hostel">hostels</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The grant of private hire vehicle (taxicab) operators licences by local authorities frequently has a condition attached that the appropriate <i>sui generis</i> change of use planning permission is granted to those premises to ensure those businesses cannot trade lawfully without the appropriate planning consents.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philosophy">Philosophy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Philosophy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Analytic_philosophy" title="Analytic philosophy">Analytic philosophy</a> often uses the expression to indicate an idea, an <a href="/wiki/Entity" title="Entity">entity</a>, or a reality that cannot be reduced to a lower <a href="/wiki/Concept" title="Concept">concept</a> or included in a higher concept.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_society">Politics and society</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Politics and society">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table The legal nature of the EU is widely debated because its mixture of <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental</a> and <a href="/wiki/Supranational" class="mw-redirect" title="Supranational">supranational</a> elements causes it to share characteristics with both <a href="/wiki/Confederal" class="mw-redirect" title="Confederal">confederal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Federalism" title="Federalism">federal</a> entities. It is generally considered more than a confederation but less than a federation,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> thus being appropriately classified as an instance of neither political form. Compared to other international organizations, the EU is often considered "sui generis" because its legal system comprehensively rejects any use of retaliatory sanctions by one member state against another.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A similar case that led to the use of the label <i>sui generis</i> is the relationship of <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a> relative to France, because the legal status of New Caledonia can aptly be said to lie somewhere between a French <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">overseas collectivity</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Sovereign nation">sovereign nation</a>. Although other examples of such status for other <a href="/wiki/List_of_dependent_territories" class="mw-redirect" title="List of dependent territories">dependent</a> or <a href="/wiki/List_of_disputed_or_occupied_territories" class="mw-redirect" title="List of disputed or occupied territories">disputed</a> territories may exist, this arrangement is unique within the French realm.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Legal_status_of_the_Holy_See" title="Legal status of the Holy See">legal status of the Holy See</a> has also been described as a <i>sui generis</i> entity possessing an international personality.\n</p><p>In local government, a <i>sui generis</i> entity is one that does not fit with the general scheme of local governance of a country. For example in England, the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> are the two <i>sui generis</i> localities, as their forms of local government are both (for historical or geographical reasons) very different from those of elsewhere in the country. \nIn a press conference during which reporters were trying to analyse his political personality,  <a href="/wiki/Huey_Long" title="Huey Long">Huey Long</a> said, "...say that I am <i>sui generus</i>, and let it go at that."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Joint_Council_of_Municipalities" class="mw-redirect" title="Joint Council of Municipalities">Joint Council of Municipalities</a> in <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> is a <i>sui generis</i> council of municipalities in the east of the country that formed after an international agreement to protect the rights of the ethnic Serb community in the region and is, as such, unique in Croatia.<sup id="cite_ref-ZVO1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZVO1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ZVO2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZVO2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ZVO3_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ZVO3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sociology">Sociology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sociology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In sociology, the <i>sui generis</i> is what has been externalized, then internalized in the overall public and becomes a part of society that simply exists in its construct. It is not something that is not thought to have been created, because it is imbedded in everyone\'s way of thinking and being. Instances include the idea of love, or going to school, or clothing belonging to a specific gender. These examples are <i>sui generis</i> for they simply exist in society and are widely accepted without thoughts of where they come from or how they were created.<sup id="cite_ref-berger_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-berger-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Ad_hoc" title="Ad hoc">Ad hoc</a></i></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_Latin_phrases" title="List of Latin phrases">List of Latin phrases</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_legal_Latin_terms" class="mw-redirect" title="List of legal Latin terms">List of legal Latin terms</a></li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Status_aparte" title="Status aparte">Status aparte</a></i></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sui_generis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite id="CITEREFReference-OED-sui_generis" class="citation"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://oed.com/search?searchType=dictionary&amp;q=sui+generis">"sui generis"</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" title="Oxford English Dictionary">Oxford English Dictionary</a></i> (3rd ed.).
