(<small>French pronunciation:&#160;&#8203;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[\xc9\xa1a\xca\x81 m\xc9\x94\xcc\x83pa\xca\x81nas]</a></span>) (<i>Montparnasse Station</i>), officially <b>Paris-Montparnasse</b>, is one of the six large <a href="/wiki/List_of_railway_stations_in_Paris" class="mw-redirect" title="List of railway stations in Paris">Paris railway termini</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/14th_arrondissement_of_Paris" title="14th arrondissement of Paris">14th</a> and <a href="/wiki/15th_arrondissement_of_Paris" title="15th arrondissement of Paris">15th arrondissements</a> of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. It was opened in 1840, rebuilt in 1852, and then rebuilt completely further south in 1969. A <a href="/wiki/Steam_train" class="mw-redirect" title="Steam train">steam train</a> crashed through the station in 1895; there is a <a href="/wiki/Montparnasse_derailment" title="Montparnasse derailment">well-known photograph</a> of the event, and full scale reproductions outside a museum chain in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It is used by intercity <a href="/wiki/TGV" title="TGV">TGV</a> trains to the west and south-west of France including <a href="/wiki/Tours" title="Tours">Tours</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bordeaux" title="Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rennes" title="Rennes">Rennes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nantes" title="Nantes">Nantes</a>, and by suburban and regional services on the <a href="/wiki/Transilien_Paris_%E2%80%93_Montparnasse" class="mw-redirect" title="Transilien Paris \xe2\x80\x93 Montparnasse">Transilien Paris&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 Montparnasse</a> routes. There is also a <a href="/wiki/Montparnasse_%E2%80%93_Bienven%C3%BCe_(Paris_M%C3%A9tro)" title="Montparnasse \xe2\x80\x93 Bienven\xc3\xbce (Paris M\xc3\xa9tro)">metro station</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Gare_Montparnasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The station opened in 1840 as <i>Gare de l\'Ouest</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> later being renamed. A second station was built between 1848 and 1852.\n</p><p>On 25 August 1944, the <a href="/wiki/German_occupation_of_France_during_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="German occupation of France during World War II">German</a> military governor of Paris, <a href="/wiki/Dietrich_von_Choltitz" title="Dietrich von Choltitz">General von Choltitz</a>, surrendered his garrison to the French General <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Leclerc" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippe Leclerc">Philippe Leclerc</a> at the old station, after disobeying <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>\'s direct order to destroy the city (see <a href="/wiki/Liberation_of_Paris" title="Liberation of Paris">Liberation of Paris</a>).\n</p><p>During the 1960s, a newer station integrated into a complex of office buildings was built. In 1969, the old station was torn down and the <a href="/wiki/Tour_Montparnasse" title="Tour Montparnasse">Tour Montparnasse</a> built on its site. An extension was built in 1990 to host the <a href="/wiki/TGV_Atlantique" class="mw-redirect" title="TGV Atlantique">TGV Atlantique</a>.\n</p>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-traditional">\n\t\t<li derailment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gare_Montparnasse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1895 derailment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg/200px-Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg" width="200" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg/300px-Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg/400px-Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1750" data-file-height="2100" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Train_wreck_at_Montparnasse_1895.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Granville\xe2\x80\x93Paris Express wreck on 22 October 1895.</div></div></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Montparnasse_derailment" title="Montparnasse derailment">Montparnasse derailment</a></div>\n<p>The Gare Montparnasse became famous for the derailment on 22 October 1895, of the <a href="/wiki/Granville,_Manche" title="Granville, Manche">Granville</a>\xe2\x80\x93Paris Express, which overran the <a href="/wiki/Buffer_stop" title="Buffer stop">buffer stop</a>. The engine careered across almost 30 metres (100&#160;ft) of the station concourse, crashed through a 60-centimetre (2&#160;ft) thick wall, shot across a terrace and smashed out of the station, plummeting onto the Place de Rennes 10 metres (33&#160;ft) below, where it stood on its nose. Two of the 131 passengers sustained injuries, along with the fireman and two conductors. The only fatality was a woman on the street below, Marie-Augustine Aguilard, who was temporarily taking over her husband\'s work duty while he went out to get the newspapers. She was killed by falling masonry.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The railway company later paid for her funeral and provided a pension to look after her two children. The accident was caused by a faulty <a href="/wiki/Westinghouse_Air_Brake_Company" title="Westinghouse Air Brake Company">Westinghouse</a> brake and the engine driver, who was trying to make up lost time.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\nA conductor was given a 25-<a href="/wiki/French_franc" title="French franc">franc</a> fine and the engine driver a 50-franc fine. \n</p><p>Replicas of the train crash are recreated outside the Mundo a Vapor ("Steam World") museum chain buildings in Brazil, in the southernmost state, <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a>, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Canela,_Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Canela, Rio Grande do Sul">Canela</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Train_services">Train services</span><span
