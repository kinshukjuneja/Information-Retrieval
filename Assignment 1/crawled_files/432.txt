Hams</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> on the south coast of <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, England. It is administered partly by <a href="/wiki/South_Hams_District_Council" title="South Hams District Council">South Hams District Council</a>, which has its headquarters in the town of <a href="/wiki/Totnes" title="Totnes">Totnes</a>, and partly by <a href="/wiki/Devon_County_Council" title="Devon County Council">Devon County Council</a> in the nearby city of <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a>. The area also contains the towns of <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth,_Devon" title="Dartmouth, Devon">Dartmouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingsbridge" title="Kingsbridge">Kingsbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salcombe" title="Salcombe">Salcombe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ivybridge" title="Ivybridge">Ivybridge</a>, the last of which is the largest, with a population of 11,851.\n</p><p>To the north, it includes part of <a href="/wiki/Dartmoor_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Dartmoor National Park">Dartmoor National Park</a>, to the east borders <a href="/wiki/Torbay" title="Torbay">Torbay</a>, and to the west <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>. It contains some of the most unspoilt coastline on the south coast, including the promontories of <a href="/wiki/Start_Point,_Devon" title="Start Point, Devon">Start Point</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bolt_Head" title="Bolt Head">Bolt Head</a> The entire coastline, along with the lower Avon and Dart valleys, form most of the <a href="/wiki/South_Devon_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="South Devon Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">South Devon Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a>. The South Hams, along with nearby <a href="/wiki/Broadsands" title="Broadsands">Broadsands</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paignton" title="Paignton">Paignton</a>, is the last <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> refuge of the <a href="/wiki/Cirl_bunting" title="Cirl bunting">cirl bunting</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Geography"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Geography</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Settlements"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Settlements</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Hams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The South Hams were originally part of the <a href="/wiki/Britons_(historical)" class="mw-redirect" title="Britons (historical)">Brythonic</a> (Celtic) Kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Dumnonia" title="Dumnonia">Dumnonia</a> later reduced to the modern boundary at the <a href="/wiki/River_Tamar" title="River Tamar">River Tamar</a> as <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> presumably during the tenth-century reign of <a href="/wiki/%C3%86thelstan" title="\xc3\x86thelstan">\xc3\x86thelstan</a>. Post-Roman settlement on coastal <a href="/wiki/Hill_fort" class="mw-redirect" title="Hill fort">promontory hillforts</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Burgh_Island" title="Burgh Island">Burgh Island</a>, follows the established pattern of trading\xe2\x80\x94of <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> in particular\xe2\x80\x94found across the western, so-called \'Celtic\', Atlantic coastal regions. In the later <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Saxon England">Anglo-Saxon era</a>, the South Hams was a feudal estate consisting of all of the land between the <a href="/wiki/River_Plym" title="River Plym">River Plym</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Dart" title="River Dart">River Dart</a> and south of <a href="/wiki/Dartmoor" title="Dartmoor">Dartmoor</a> with the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> forming the southern boundary. There is some evidence that <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a> was spoken and understood in the area until the late Middle Ages.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1917, the village of <a href="/wiki/Hallsands" title="Hallsands">Hallsands</a> was abandoned after much of it was lost to the sea. This happened because the shingle bank protecting the shore was removed to help build <a href="/wiki/HMNB_Devonport" title="HMNB Devonport">Devonport dockyard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1944 several villages were evacuated so that training for <a href="/wiki/Normandy_landings" title="Normandy landings">D-Day</a> could be carried out in secret. The area was chosen because of the resemblance of its beaches to those of <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>. Preparations were disrupted, and secrecy nearly compromised, by a  devastating <a href="/wiki/E-boat" title="E-boat">E-boat</a> attack during <a href="/wiki/Exercise_Tiger#1944" title="Exercise Tiger">Exercise Tiger</a>. In 1967, the suburban towns of <a href="/wiki/Plympton" title="Plympton">Plympton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plymstock" title="Plymstock">Plymstock</a> were  amalgamated with the City of <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The current district was formed on 1 April 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, by the merger of:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Borough of Dartmouth</li>\n<li>Borough of Totnes</li>\n<li>Kingsbridge Rural District</li>\n<li>Kingsbridge Urban District</li>\n<li>Plympton St Mary Rural District</li>\n<li>Salcombe Urban District</li>\n<li>Totnes Rural District</li></ul>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum,_2016" title="United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016">2016 EU Referendum</a> it was one of few rural areas to vote to remain with a very high (80.3%) turnout.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Hams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Geography divides into three unequal, fuzzy bands, one with bays, headlands, the birdlife, fishing and small harbour towns\' estuaries and <a href="/wiki/Ria" title="Ria">rias</a>; an unequal wide-ranging elevations middle band with the main, well-conserved towns and; a sparsely populated, upland <a href="/wiki/National_parks_of_England_and_Wales" title="National parks of England and Wales">National Park</a> moorland in the north.  For over a century its tourism was concentrated around the <a href="/wiki/Exeter_to_Plymouth_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Exeter to Plymouth Line">railway</a>, with most stations built here from 1847-1872 so tourism to its beaches and fishing villages began in earnest much later than to the \'<a href="/wiki/English_Riviera" class="mw-redirect" title="English Riviera">English Riviera</a>\' east of the area.  South Hams\' widespread tourism multiplied on the dualling of the <a href="/wiki/A38_road_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="A38 road (Great Britain)">A38</a> and time-cutting construction of the <a href="/wiki/M5_motorway" title="M5 motorway">M5</a> and <a href="/wiki/A303_road" title="A303 road">A303</a> across other parts of <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">south-west England</a>.\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In the north, there is...the "wildscape"\xe2\x80\x94...[a new] bypass [to the A30, the <a href="/wiki/A30_road_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="A30 road (Great Britain)">A38</a>]...cut[s] through some of the most beautiful country in Britain, that of the Dartmoor national park....while in the south-west there is <a href="/wiki/China_clay_country" class="mw-redirect" title="China clay country">extensive mining of china clay</a>...[an abortive] tungsten mine in the <a href="/wiki/Shaugh_Prior" title="Shaugh Prior">Shaugh Prior</a> \xe2\x80\x94 <a href="/wiki/Sparkwell" title="Sparkwell">Sparkwell</a> area will be [would have been] amongst the largest in the world and probably the largest in Europe. [There is] an island in the shape of <a href="/wiki/Burgh_Island" title="Burgh Island">Burgh Island</a>. There are rolling hills, deep wooded valleys, estuaries full of fish and a coastline full of <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lobster" title="Lobster">lobsters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oyster" title="Oyster">oysters</a>. The climate is warm and mellow. Some regard it as a <a href="/wiki/Garden_of_Eden" title="Garden of Eden">garden of Eden</a>. We even have a naturist beach and hotel. ...forgive me if I wax lyrical about this beautiful, delightful area. Tourism is an important livelihood...the other is farming...for many.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></p><div
