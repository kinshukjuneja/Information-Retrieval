href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> enjoy a friendly economic relationship,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but a tense political relationship. Saudi Arabia has an embassy in <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a> and a consulate\xe2\x80\x93general in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, and Turkey has an embassy in <a href="/wiki/Riyadh" title="Riyadh">Riyadh</a> and a consulate\xe2\x80\x93general in <a href="/wiki/Jeddah" title="Jeddah">Jeddah</a>. Both countries are members of the <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Organization" title="World Trade Organization">World Trade Organization</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a>.\n</p><p>According to a 2013 <a href="/wiki/Pew_Research_Center" title="Pew Research Center">Pew</a> global opinion poll, <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turks</a> hold the most negative view of Saudi Arabia out of any surveyed <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> country, with 26% expressing a favourable view and 53% expressing an unfavourable view.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input href="/w/index.php?title=Saudi_Arabia%E2%80%93Turkey_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Hashimite" class="mw-redirect" title="Hashimite">Hashimite</a> rulers of the <a href="/wiki/Hejaz" title="Hejaz">Hejaz</a> pledged allegiance to the Ottoman Sultan in 1517 following the Ottoman conquest of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, placing the holy cities of <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medina" title="Medina">Medina</a> under Ottoman Protection until the <a href="/wiki/Arab_revolt" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab revolt">Arab revolt</a> in 1916 when <a href="/wiki/Sharif_Hussayn" class="mw-redirect" title="Sharif Hussayn">Sharif Hussayn</a> of Mecca expelled them with the aid of Britain.\nAlso, the Ottomans had conflicts with <a href="/wiki/House_of_Saud" title="House of Saud">House of Saud</a>, the first Saudi state which resulted to the <a href="/wiki/Wahhabi_War" title="Wahhabi War">Ottoman\xe2\x80\x93Wahhabi war</a>. Many Saudi leaders were executed in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> or killed during the war.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saudi_Arabia%E2%80%93Turkey_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Relations between Turkey and Saudi Arabia began in 1932, after the creation of the new Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.\n</p><p>Due to the <a href="/wiki/2017_Qatar_diplomatic_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 Qatar diplomatic crisis">2017 Qatar diplomatic crisis</a>, the relationship between Turkey and Saudi Arabia faces problems, with Turkey supporting <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a> against Saudi Arabia in the ongoing diplomatic dispute.\n</p><p>World Pensions Council\'s M. Nicolas J. Firzli has argued that the Turkish government has sought to use the crisis to its own advantage, by advancing an <a href="/wiki/Expansionism" title="Expansionism">expansionist</a> <a href="/wiki/Neo-Ottomanism" title="Neo-Ottomanism">Neo-Ottoman</a> agenda at the expense of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Cooperation_Council" title="Gulf Cooperation Council">Gulf Cooperation Council</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Turkey and a resurgent Iran for their part are cynically using the festering crisis to advance a distinct agenda: rebuilding military and economic beachheads along the \xe2\x80\x98eastern flank\xe2\x80\x99 of the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> from Oman to Southern Iraq, a part of the world from which they were forced out by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> in 1917, precisely one hundred years ago. The local chess board is getting crowded with too many avid players, at a time when many British and American policy makers seem to have lost interest in that part of the world: this doesn\xe2\x80\x99t bode well for the long-term stability of the <a href="/wiki/MENA" title="MENA">MENA</a>.\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>Saudi Arabia, in response, has threatened to impose sanctions against Turkey, and has conducted discussions with the <a href="/wiki/UAE" class="mw-redirect" title="UAE">UAE</a> on the topic of curbing \xe2\x80\x9cTurkish expansionist policy\xe2\x80\x9d. In turn, Turkish President <a href="/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan" title="Recep Tayyip Erdo\xc4\x9fan">Recep Tayyip Erdo\xc4\x9fan</a> accused Saudi Arabia of being non-Islamic and <a href="/wiki/Heretics" class="mw-redirect" title="Heretics">heretics</a>. Furthermore, Turkey has deployed troops to defend the government of Qatar from an attempted coup by Saudi Arabia and the UAE.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 2, 2018, Saudi journalist and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> writer <a href="/wiki/Jamal_Khashoggi" title="Jamal Khashoggi">Jamal Khashoggi</a> was killed in the Saudi Arabian consulate in Istanbul. It has been widely alleged that he was killed by the Saudi government.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
