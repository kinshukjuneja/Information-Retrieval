(2015\xe2\x80\x93present)</div></div></div><p><b>Shortstop</b>, abbreviated <b>SS</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_positions" title="Baseball positions">baseball or softball fielding position</a> between <a href="/wiki/Second_base" class="mw-redirect" title="Second base">second</a> and <a href="/wiki/Third_base" class="mw-redirect" title="Third base">third base</a>, which is considered to be among the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_spectrum" title="Defensive spectrum">most demanding</a> defensive positions. Historically the position was assigned to defensive specialists who were typically poor at batting and were often placed at the bottom of the <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(baseball)" title="Batting order (baseball)">batting order</a>. Today shortstops are often able to hit well and many are placed at the top of the lineup. In the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_positions" title="Baseball positions">numbering system</a> used by <a href="/wiki/Baseball_scorekeeping" title="Baseball scorekeeping">scorers</a> to record defensive plays, the shortstop is assigned the number 6.\n</p><p>More hit balls go to the shortstop than to any other position, as there are more <a href="/wiki/Right-handedness" class="mw-redirect" title="Right-handedness">right-handed</a> hitters in baseball than <a href="/wiki/Left-handedness" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-handedness">left-handed</a> hitters, and most hitters have a tendency to <a href="/wiki/Pull_hitter" title="Pull hitter">pull the ball</a> slightly. Like a <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a>, a shortstop must be agile, for example when performing a <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_baseball_(0%E2%80%939)" title="Glossary of baseball (0\xe2\x80\x939)">4-6-3</a> <a href="/wiki/Double_play" title="Double play">double play</a>. Also, like a <a href="/wiki/Third_baseman" title="Third baseman">third baseman</a>, the shortstop fields balls hit to the left side of the <a href="/wiki/Infield#Baseball" title="Infield">infield</a>, where a strong arm is needed to <a href="/wiki/Out_(baseball)" title="Out (baseball)">throw out</a> a <a href="/wiki/Baserunning#The_batter-runner" class="mw-redirect" title="Baserunning">batter-runner</a> before they reach the safety of first base.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortstop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Doc_Adams" title="Doc Adams">Doc Adams</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knickerbockers" title="New York Knickerbockers"> Knickerbockers</a> created the concept of the shortstop position, according to baseball historian <a href="/wiki/John_Thorn" title="John Thorn">John Thorn</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">Baseball Hall of Fame</a> researcher Freddy Berowski.<sup id="cite_ref-SABR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SABR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-invent_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-invent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the first five years the Knickerbockers played, the team fielded anywhere from eight to eleven players. The only infielders were the players covering each of the bases; if there were more than eight players, extra <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielders</a> were sometimes used. The outfielders had difficulty throwing baseballs into the infield, because of the balls\' light weight. Adams\' shortstop position, which he started playing at some time from 1849 to 1850, was used to field throws from the outfielders and throw to the three infielders.<sup id="cite_ref-SABR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SABR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> With the advent of higher-quality baseballs, Adams moved to the infield, since the distance the balls could travel increased.<sup id="cite_ref-SABR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SABR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Adams had a long playing career with the Knickerbockers: he remained a player with the team until 1860.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Positioning">Positioning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortstop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Positioning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Unlike the <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catcher" title="Catcher">catcher</a>, who must start every play in a designated area (the pitcher must be on the <a href="/wiki/Pitcher%27s_mound" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitcher&#39;s mound">pitcher\'s mound</a>, with one foot in contact with the <a href="/wiki/Pitcher%27s_rubber" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitcher&#39;s rubber">pitcher\'s rubber</a>, and the catcher must be behind <a href="/wiki/Home_plate" class="mw-redirect" title="Home plate">home plate</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Catcher%27s_box" class="mw-redirect" title="Catcher&#39;s box">catcher\'s box</a>) the shortstop and the other fielders can vary their positioning in response to what they anticipate will be the actions of the batter and runner(s) once the play begins.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The shortstop ordinarily is positioned near second base on the third-base side. Because right-handed hitters tend to hit the ball more toward third base, a shortstop will generally move closer to third base if the batter is batting right-handed, and more toward first base if the batter is batting left-handed. A shortstop typically has a strong throwing arm, because he has a relatively long throw to first base, and often has less time in which to make a throw, given that the <a href="/wiki/Ground_ball" class="mw-redirect" title="Ground ball">ground balls</a> he fields have often traveled relatively far. A shortstop must also be extremely agile, because balls hit to or near the shortstop position are usually hit harder than to other infield positions.\n</p><p>Shortstops are required to <a href="/wiki/Covering_a_base" title="Covering a base">cover second base</a> in <a href="/wiki/Double_play" title="Double play">double play</a> situations when the ball is hit to the second baseman or first baseman. They also cover second when a runner is attempting a <a href="/wiki/Stolen_base" title="Stolen base">stolen base</a>, but only when a left-handed hitter is batting. This is because the infield will respond to a left-handed batter by shifting toward first base, resulting in the shortstop being the infielder who is closest to second base. Shortstops also must cover third at various times, including the rotation play; the latter occurs when there are runners on first and second and a <a href="/wiki/Sacrifice_bunt" title="Sacrifice bunt">sacrifice bunt</a> is attempted toward third base, requiring the third baseman to move in away from third base in order to field it.  Shortstops generally are given precedence on catching pop-ups in the <a href="/wiki/Infielder" title="Infielder">infield</a> as well, so they end up calling off other players many times, although on deep pop-ups they generally fall back when called off by an <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a>. They often become the cutoff man on balls to any part of the outfield that are being directed towards third base and all balls to left and center field that are destined for second base. Depending on the system the shortstop may cut balls from left field heading home; however, this is usually the job of the third baseman.\n</p><p>The emphasis on defense makes the position unusually difficult to fill. Historically, a strong shortstop did not have to be a good hitter. Some of the weakest hitters in Major League Baseball have played the position, including <a href="/wiki/Mario_Mendoza" title="Mario Mendoza">Mario Mendoza</a>, for whom <a href="/wiki/George_Brett" title="George Brett">George Brett</a> popularized the eponymous <a href="/wiki/Mendoza_Line" title="Mendoza Line">Mendoza Line</a> to describe a batting average below .200. Since the 1960s, however, such mediocre hitting has become rarer as teams increasingly demand players with ability to both field and hit.<sup id="cite_ref-seminara20100706_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seminara20100706-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In practice, a marginal fielder as a shortstop who hits well can be moved to almost any other position, especially second base or third base, whether early in their careers (examples: George Brett and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Schmidt" title="Mike Schmidt">Mike Schmidt</a> were both tried early in their careers as shortstops)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> or later due to diminished <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fielding_range&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fielding range (page does not exist)">fielding range</a>, slower reflexes, weaker throwing arms, increased risk of injury, or co-existence with another dominant shortstop, as with <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Banks" title="Ernie Banks">Ernie Banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cal_Ripken_Jr." title="Cal Ripken Jr.">Cal Ripken Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Rodr%C3%ADguez" class="mw-redirect" title="Alex Rodr\xc3\xadguez">Alex Rodr\xc3\xadguez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Young_(baseball)" title="Michael Young (baseball)">Michael Young</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Tejada" title="Miguel Tejada">Miguel Tejada</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Significant_shortstops">Significant shortstops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortstop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Significant shortstops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shortstops_inducted_to_the_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame">Shortstops inducted to the Baseball
