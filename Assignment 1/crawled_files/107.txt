href="/wiki/Chichester" title="Chichester">Chichester</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Solent" title="Solent">Solent</a>. It straddles the boundary of West Sussex and Hampshire. It is one of four natural harbours in that area of the coastline, the others being <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_Harbour" title="Portsmouth Harbour">Portsmouth Harbour</a>, <a href="/wiki/Langstone_Harbour" title="Langstone Harbour">Langstone Harbour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pagham" title="Pagham">Pagham</a> Harbour. The harbour and surrounding land is managed by Chichester Harbour Conservancy.<sup id="cite_ref-Conservancy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conservancy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chichester_Harbour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Chichester Harbour is one of the few remaining undeveloped coastal areas in Southern England and remains relatively wild. Its wide expanses and intricate creeks are at the same time a major wildlife haven and among some of Britain\'s most popular boating waters.\n</p><p>The massive stretch of tidal flats and saltings are of outstanding ecological significance. Very large populations of wildfowl and waders use the mudflats feeding on the rich plant life and the huge populations of intertidal invertebrates. More than 7,500 Brent geese overwinter on the intertidal mud-land and adjacent farmland and more than 50,000 birds reside in or visit the Harbour throughout the year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The harbourside villages are: <a href="/wiki/West_Wittering" title="West Wittering">West Wittering</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Itchenor" title="West Itchenor">West Itchenor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birdham" title="Birdham">Birdham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apuldram#Dell_Quay" title="Apuldram">Dell Quay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fishbourne,_West_Sussex" title="Fishbourne, West Sussex">Fishbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bosham" title="Bosham">Bosham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chidham" class="mw-redirect" title="Chidham">Chidham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southbourne,_West_Sussex" title="Southbourne, West Sussex">Prinsted</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thorney_Island_(West_Sussex)" title="Thorney Island (West Sussex)">Thorney Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emsworth" title="Emsworth">Emsworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Langstone,_Hampshire" title="Langstone, Hampshire">Langstone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northney" title="Northney">Northney</a>. The nearest towns are <a href="/wiki/Havant" title="Havant">Havant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chichester" title="Chichester">Chichester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hayling_Island" title="Hayling Island">Hayling Island</a>. The harbour lowlands contain high quality arable farmland. Boatyards, marinas and commercial fishing are important elements of the local economy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>This is one of the south coast\'s most popular sailing waters with as many as 12,500 craft regularly using the harbour, with competitive racing taking place among the 14 sailing clubs of the Chichester Harbour Federation.<sup id="cite_ref-Chifed_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chifed-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The villages, sea walls and footpaths are a popular leisure area for residents and tourists alike.\n</p><p>Set up by Act of Parliament in 1971, Chichester Harbour Conservancy has the duty to conserve, maintain and improve the harbour and amenity area for recreation, natural conservation and natural beauty. As well as being the statutory harbour authority, the Conservancy manages the Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty (AONB).<sup id="cite_ref-Conservancy_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Conservancy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>To the south west of the entrance to Chichester Harbour is Chichester Bar, a shallow <a href="/wiki/Spit_(landform)" title="Spit (landform)">spit</a> which can present a significant <a href="/wiki/Navigation" title="Navigation">navigation</a> hazard at all states of the <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">tide</a>. The entrance to the harbour is deep with a fast tidal stream and to the east of the main harbour entrance channel is a gravel bank known as The Winner.\n</p><p>The east side of the harbour entrance is an area of geographical, recreational and conservation interest known as East Head. It is a large sand dune linked to land by a narrow area known as The Hinge. In recent years The Hinge has been breached by several storms and then repaired. There is much debate about whether and how it should continue to be repaired.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The western boundary with <a href="/wiki/Langstone_Harbour" title="Langstone Harbour">Langstone Harbour</a> is defined by a historic causeway known as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wade_way&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wade way (page does not exist)">wade way</a>, once the principal access from <a href="/wiki/Hayling_Island" title="Hayling Island">Hayling Island</a> to the mainland, but since bisected by a deep channel for the Portsmouth and Chichester Canal in the 1820s, and no longer safely traversable.\n</p><p>Chichester Harbour has three main channels. The Emsworth Channel, the Thorney Channel and the Chichester Channel, which also branches off into the Bosham Lake and Itchenor Reach.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chichester_Harbour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg/220px-Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg/330px-Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg/440px-Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg 2x" data-file-width="891" data-file-height="593" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Salt-marsh_Bosham_5.2.2003.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Salt-marsh near Bosham</div></div></div>\n<p>Chichester Harbour is an <a href="/wiki/Areas_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty_in_England_and_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty in England and Wales">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a>. The harbour is of national and international importance for nature conservation. It is a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> a wetland of international importance,a Special Protection Area for wild birds and a Special Area of Conservation. The harbour is of particular importance for wintering wildfowl and waders of which five species reach numbers which are internationally important.\n</p><p>There are a number of habitats including a large area of <a href="/wiki/Salt_marsh" title="Salt marsh">salt marsh</a> <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat (ecology)">habitat</a> and mudflats which are exposed at low tide. These areas are particularly important for wintering birds. \n</p><p>Chichester Harbour is a designated <a href="/wiki/Bass_(fish)" title="Bass (fish)">Bass</a> Nursery Area.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recreation">Recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chichester_Harbour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Langstone_Sailing_Club,_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Langstone_Sailing_Club%2C_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg/220px-Langstone_Sailing_Club%2C_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Langstone_Sailing_Club%2C_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg/330px-Langstone_Sailing_Club%2C_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Langstone_Sailing_Club%2C_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg/440px-Langstone_Sailing_Club%2C_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Langstone_Sailing_Club,_Hampshire_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720045.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Langstone Sailing Club</div></div></div>\n<p>Chichester Harbour is used for a wide variety of <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailing</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Sailing_dinghy" class="mw-redirect" title="Sailing dinghy">dinghy</a> racing; it is the venue for the \'Itchenor Gallon\' race for the <a href="/wiki/International_14" title="International 14">International 14</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> There are several <a href="/wiki/Yacht" title="Yacht">yacht</a> <a href="/wiki/Marinas" class="mw-redirect" title="Marinas">marinas</a>. It is also used for <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>.\n</p><p>Harbour tours depart year round from Itchenor in either a traditional boat or in <i>Solar Heritage</i>, a solar powered boat. During the summer regular trips depart from Emsworth on <i>Solar Heritage</i> and on the Victorian oyster boat <i><a href="/wiki/The_Terror_(boat)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Terror (boat)">Terror</a></i>.\n</p><p>There is a small dory that operates as a ferry service between Itchenor and Bosham.\n</p><p>The harbour is a very popular area for <a href="/wiki/Birdwatching" title="Birdwatching">birdwatching</a>; guided bird walks and boat trips are offered during the winter months by Chichester Harbour Conservancy.\n</p><p>There is a network of footpaths for walkers and a cycle route from Chichester to West Wittering which passes through harbour countryside.\n</p><p>West Wittering Beach and East Head is the only sandy beach on the West Sussex coast and is a popular family and tourist destination on warm weekends.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Harbour_Master%27s_Office,_Chichester,_Sussex.jpg"
