Its council is based in <a href="/wiki/Newton_Abbot" title="Newton Abbot">Newton Abbot</a>.\n</p><p>Other towns in the district include <a href="/wiki/Ashburton,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashburton, England">Ashburton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dawlish" title="Dawlish">Dawlish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teignmouth" title="Teignmouth">Teignmouth</a>.  It is named for the old <a href="/wiki/Teignbridge_Hundred" title="Teignbridge Hundred">Teignbridge</a> <a href="/wiki/Hundred_(administrative_division)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hundred (administrative division)">hundred</a>.\n</p><p>The district was formed on 1 April 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, as a merger of the Ashburton, <a href="/wiki/Buckfastleigh" title="Buckfastleigh">Buckfastleigh</a>, Dawlish, Newton Abbot and Teignmouth <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> along with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Newton_Abbot_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Newton Abbot Rural District (page does not exist)">Newton Abbot Rural District</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/St_Thomas_Rural_District" title="St Thomas Rural District">St Thomas Rural District</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teignbridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Teignbridge_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="Teignbridge local elections">Teignbridge local elections</a></div>\n<p>Elections to the borough council are held every four years, with all of the 46 seats on the council being elected at each election. The council had been under <a href="/wiki/No_overall_control" title="No overall control">no overall control</a> since the 1983, until the Conservatives gained a majority in the 2011 elections.
