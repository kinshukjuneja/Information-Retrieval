Gordon Thomas</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a British <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a> politician and former property developer.  He has been the <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament (UK)">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) for <a href="/wiki/St_Ives_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="St Ives (UK Parliament constituency)">St Ives</a> since the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_2015" title="United Kingdom general election, 2015">general election in May 2015</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derek_Thomas_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Thomas was born on the 20 July 1972 in Cornwall to parents who were <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionaries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-StIvesConsv_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StIvesConsv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  He grew up in West Cornwall<sup id="cite_ref-StIvesConsv_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StIvesConsv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  and went on to complete a traditional Cornish Mason Apprenticeship.<sup id="cite_ref-StIvesConsv_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StIvesConsv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WestBriton01082015_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WestBriton01082015-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He studied in <a href="/wiki/South_London" title="South London">South London</a> and then returned to West Cornwall to be a development manager for <a href="/wiki/Mustard_Seed_Ministries" title="Mustard Seed Ministries">Mustard Seed</a>, a voluntary organisation in <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">Helston, Cornwall</a><sup id="cite_ref-StIvesConsv_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StIvesConsv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which he used to use to demonstrate his strong religious beliefs to local school children, and Chapel Street Methodist Church, <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WestBriton01082015_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WestBriton01082015-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  He later started his own small construction business.<sup id="cite_ref-StIvesConsv_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StIvesConsv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At this time he helped develop community projects to support vulnerable adults.<sup id="cite_ref-SBurtonDerekThomas_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SBurtonDerekThomas-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derek_Thomas_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Thomas was first elected as a candidate for the Conservative Party at a by-election in November 2005 for Penzance Central ward on <a href="/wiki/Penwith" title="Penwith">Penwith</a> District Council. He narrowly beat the Liberal Democrat candidate and subsequently opposed the creation of a unitary Cornwall Council. In May 2009, he did not stand for the new Penzance Central ward on the amalgamated <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> and the seat was narrowly won by the Liberal Democrat candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-StIvesConsv_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StIvesConsv-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He unsuccessfully contested the parliamentary seat of St Ives for the Conservative Party at the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_2010" title="United Kingdom general election, 2010">2010 general election</a>, coming 1,719 votes behind the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrat</a> incumbent <a href="/wiki/Andrew_George_(politician)" title="Andrew George (politician)">Andrew George</a>. However, in the following <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_2015" title="United Kingdom general election, 2015">general election in May 2015</a> he subsequently took the seat.\n</p><p>In January 2016, Thomas was one of 72 MPs who voted down an amendment in Parliament on rental homes being \xe2\x80\x9cfit for human habitation\xe2\x80\x9d who were themselves landlords who derived an income from a property.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Thomas supported Brexit in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum,_2016" title="United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016">2016 European Union Referendum</a> <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Thomas once again contended for the St Ives seat in the 2017 general election, his campaign including a visit by <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" title="Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Leadsom" title="Andrea Leadsom">Andrea Leadsom</a> to his Penzance office. He went on to be re-elected with an increased vote share, but a diminished majority of 312.\n</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a> he sits on the Health and Social Care Committee. He has previously sat on the Science and Technology Committee.<sup id="cite_ref-parliamentbiothomas_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parliamentbiothomas-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Thomas is currently the chair of the All-Party Parliamentary Group on Brain Tumours.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derek_Thomas_(politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
