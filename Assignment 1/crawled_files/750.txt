authority</a> of Wiltshire (excluding the separate UA of <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Swindon" title="Borough of Swindon">Swindon</a>) in <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>, created in 2009. It is the successor authority to <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_County_Council" title="Wiltshire County Council">Wiltshire County Council</a> (1889\xe2\x80\x932009) and the four <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">district councils</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kennet_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennet (district)">Kennet</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Wiltshire" title="North Wiltshire">North Wiltshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salisbury" title="Salisbury">Salisbury</a>, and <a href="/wiki/West_Wiltshire" title="West Wiltshire">West Wiltshire</a>, all of which were <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">created in 1974</a> and <a href="/wiki/2009_structural_changes_to_local_government_in_England" title="2009 structural changes to local government in England">abolished in 2009</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="toclevel-1 tocsection-8"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">8</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment_of_the_unitary_authority">Establishment of the unitary authority</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Establishment of the unitary authority">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ceremonial county of <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a> consists of two unitary authority areas, Wiltshire and <a href="/wiki/Swindon" title="Swindon">Swindon</a>, administered respectively by Wiltshire Council and <a href="/wiki/Swindon_(borough)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon (borough)">Swindon Borough Council</a>. Before 2009, Wiltshire was administered as a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_county" title="Non-metropolitan county">non-metropolitan county</a> by <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_County_Council" title="Wiltshire County Council">Wiltshire County Council</a>, with four districts, <a href="/wiki/Kennet_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennet (district)">Kennet</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Wiltshire" title="North Wiltshire">North Wiltshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salisbury" title="Salisbury">Salisbury</a>, and <a href="/wiki/West_Wiltshire" title="West Wiltshire">West Wiltshire</a>. Swindon, in the north of the county, had been a separate unitary authority since 1997, and on 5 December 2007 the Government announced that the rest of Wiltshire would move to unitary status. This was later put into effect by a <a href="/wiki/Statutory_instrument" title="Statutory instrument">statutory instrument</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/2009_structural_changes_to_local_government_in_England" title="2009 structural changes to local government in England">2009 structural changes to local government in England</a>. \n</p><p>With the abolition of the District of Salisbury, a new <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_City_Council" title="Salisbury City Council">Salisbury City Council</a> was created at the same time to carry out several citywide functions and to hold the City\'s <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">charter</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Functions">Functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The unitary authority provides local government services to 435,000 Wiltshire residents and is also the biggest employer in Wiltshire, being responsible for schools, social services, rubbish collection and disposal, county roads, planning, and leisure services.\n</p><p>Most executive decisions are taken by the authority\'s <a href="/wiki/Cabinet-style_council" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet-style council">cabinet</a>, each member of which has a particular area of responsibility. <a href="/wiki/Development_control_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Development control in the United Kingdom">Development control</a> is undertaken by five <a href="/wiki/Planning_committee" title="Planning committee">planning committees</a>, the powers of which cannot be exercised by the cabinet. Members of the authority are appointed to a wide range of outside bodies, providing them with some element of democratic accountability, such as the <a href="/wiki/Kennet_and_Avon_Canal" title="Kennet and Avon Canal">Kennet and Avon Canal Trust</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Victoria_County_History" title="Wiltshire Victoria County History">Wiltshire Victoria County History</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Historic_Buildings_Trust" title="Wiltshire Historic Buildings Trust">Wiltshire Historic Buildings Trust</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_balance">Political balance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political balance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Council_elections" title="Wiltshire Council elections">Wiltshire Council elections</a></div>\n<p>As a result of the first elections to the new authority, plus two by-elections held in September 2009 and December 2010, an Independent joining UKIP while remaining in the Independent group,<sup id="cite_ref-forum_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forum-3">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> a defection in May 2012 by a Liberal Democrat councillor to the Conservatives,<sup id="cite_ref-defection_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-defection-4">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and in November 2012 by another Lib Dem and a Conservative to the Independents,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> by the beginning of 2013 Wiltshire Council consisted of 61 Conservatives, 22 Liberal Democrats, ten Independents including one UKIP member, three <a href="/wiki/Devizes_Guardians" title="Devizes Guardians">Devizes Guardians</a>, and two Labour members.\n</p><p>The elections of <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Council_election,_2013" title="Wiltshire Council election, 2013">2013</a> produced an unexpected swing to the Liberal Democrats, who made a net gain of five seats, while all three Devizes Guardians lost their seats to the Conservatives and the Labour group increased from two to four. The overall result was 58 Conservatives, 27 Liberals, eight Independents, four Labour, and one UKIP member.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, during the following year four Liberal Democrats defected from their political group: one to the Conservatives and three to the Independents. In a by-election in May 2015 at Chippenham Hardenhuish the Conservatives gained a seat from the Liberal Democrats, and in November they gained another at Salisbury St Edmund, then at a by-election in May 2016 the Liberal Democrats gained Amesbury East from the Conservatives.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Council_election,_2017" title="Wiltshire Council election, 2017">2017</a> election resulted in the Conservatives increasing their share of seats at the expense of the Liberal Democrats, Independents, Labour and UKIP.\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable"
