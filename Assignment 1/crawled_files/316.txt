href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">S. Colom Veur</i><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) is a town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom. Often referred to locally as <i>St Columb</i>, it is situated approximately seven miles (11&#160;km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Wadebridge" title="Wadebridge">Wadebridge</a> and six miles (10&#160;km)  east of <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">Newquay</a>\n<sup id="cite_ref-OS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The designation <i>Major</i> distinguishes it from the nearby settlement and parish of <a href="/wiki/St_Columb_Minor" title="St Columb Minor">St Columb Minor</a> on the coast. An electoral ward simply named <i>St Columb</i> exists with a population at the 2011 census of 5,050.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Twice a year the town plays host to "<a href="/wiki/Cornish_hurling" title="Cornish hurling">hurling</a>", a medieval game once common throughout Cornwall but now only played in St Columb and <a href="/wiki/St_Ives,_Cornwall" title="St Ives, Cornwall">St Ives</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is played on <a href="/wiki/Shrove_Tuesday" title="Shrove Tuesday">Shrove Tuesday</a> and then again on the Saturday eleven days later. The game involves two teams of several hundred people (the \'townsmen\' and the \'countrymen\') who endeavour to carry a silver ball made of apple wood to goals set two miles (3&#160;km) apart, making the parish, around 25 <a href="/wiki/Square_mile" title="Square mile">square miles</a> (65&#160;<a href="/wiki/Square_kilometre" title="Square kilometre">km<sup>2</sup></a>) in area, the <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a> largest sports ground in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" id="History_and_antiquities">History and antiquities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and antiquities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_St_Columb_Major" title="Timeline of St Columb Major">Timeline of St Columb Major</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bronze_and_Iron_Ages">Bronze and Iron Ages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bronze and Iron Ages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Monuments that date from this period include: <a href="/wiki/Castle_an_Dinas,_St._Columb_Major" class="mw-redirect" title="Castle an Dinas, St. Columb Major">Castle an Dinas</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> <a href="/wiki/Hillfort" title="Hillfort">hillfort</a>.;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Maidens_stone_row" title="Nine Maidens stone row">Nine Maidens stone row</a>, the largest row of standing stones in Cornwall;<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the Devil\'s <a href="/wiki/Dolmen" title="Dolmen">Quoit</a> (sometimes recorded as <i><a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>\'s Quoit</i>) in the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Quoit,_Cornwall" title="Quoit, Cornwall">Quoit</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and King Arthur\'s Stone (this long lost stone is said to be not far from the Devil\'s Quoit near St. Columb, on the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Goss_moor" class="mw-redirect" title="Goss moor">Goss moor</a>). It was a large stone with four deeply impressed <a href="/wiki/Horseshoe" title="Horseshoe">horseshoe</a> marks. Legend has it that the marks were made by the horse upon which Arthur rode when he resided at Castle An Dinas and hunted on the moors.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_Ages_and_early_modern_period">Middle Ages and early modern period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Middle Ages and early modern period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are four Cornish crosses in the parish: two are in the churchyard, one is at the hamlet of Black Cross and another (defaced) at Black Rock.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> (one of the crosses is illustrated below, under Church.)\n</p><p>In 1333 <a href="/wiki/Edward_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward III">Edward III</a> granted a market in St Columb Major to Sir John Arundell. This was as a reward for supplying troops to fight the Scottish at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Halidon_Hill" title="Battle of Halidon Hill">Battle of Halidon Hill</a> near <a href="/wiki/Berwick-on-Tweed" class="mw-redirect" title="Berwick-on-Tweed">Berwick-on-Tweed</a>.\n</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Prayer_Book_Rebellion" title="Prayer Book Rebellion">Prayer Book Rebellion</a> of 1549, <a href="/wiki/William_Mayow" title="William Mayow">William Mayow</a> the Mayor of St. Columb was hanged by <a href="/wiki/Provost_Marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Provost Marshal">Provost Marshal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Kingston" title="Anthony Kingston">Anthony Kingston</a> outside a tavern in St Columb as a punishment leading an uprising in Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-a_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The link between the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish language</a> and Catholicism was also exhibited in the activities of John Kennall, at St Columb, where he was still holding <a href="/wiki/Mass_(liturgy)" title="Mass (liturgy)">Mass</a> as late as 1590.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1645 during the English Civil War, Sir <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fairfax,_3rd_Lord_Fairfax_of_Cameron" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas Fairfax, 3rd Lord Fairfax of Cameron">Thomas Fairfax</a>\'s troops were advancing from <a href="/wiki/Bodmin" title="Bodmin">Bodmin</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a>; on 7 March the army held a rendezvous, and halted one night, four miles (6&#160;km) beyond Bodmin. The King\'s forces were quartered at this time near St. Columb, where a smart skirmish took place between the Prince\'s regiment and a detachment of the <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentary">Parliamentary</a> army under <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Rich_(soldier)" title="Nathaniel Rich (soldier)">Colonel Rich</a>, in which the latter was victorious.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the year 1676, the greatest part of the church of St Columb was blown up with gunpowder by three youths of the town.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twentieth_century">Twentieth century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Twentieth century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Royal visits were made to St Columb in 1909, 1977 and 1983. On 9 June 1909 the town was visited by the Prince of Wales (<a href="/wiki/George_V_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="George V of the United Kingdom">George V</a>) and his wife, the Princess of Wales (<a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Teck" title="Mary of Teck">Mary of Teck</a>). The visit was to open the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Cornwall_Agricultural_Show" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Cornwall Agricultural Show">Royal Cornwall Agricultural Show</a>. The Prince gave 2 silver cups: one for the best bull and another for the best horse.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In August 1977 <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">The Queen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Philip">Prince Philip</a> visited the town during their <a href="/wiki/Silver_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II" title="Silver Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Silver Jubilee</a> tour of Cornwall. On 27 May 1983: The town was visited by the Prince and Princess of Wales (<a href="/wiki/Prince_Charles" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Charles">Charles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diana_Spencer" class="mw-redirect" title="Diana Spencer">Diana</a>). The visit was to commemorate the 650th anniversary of the signing of the town charter by <a href="/wiki/Edward_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward III">Edward III</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A plaque commemorates this visit outside the former Conservative club in Union Square.\n</p><p>In 1992 Australian <a href="/wiki/Stuntman" class="mw-redirect" title="Stuntman">stuntman</a> Matt Coulter aka <a href="/wiki/The_Kangaroo_Kid" title="The Kangaroo Kid">The Kangaroo Kid</a> set the record for the longest jump with a crash on a quad bike at Retallack Adventure Park, St Columb Major.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span The north part of the town (known as \'Bridge\') descends into the Vale of Lanherne, having a minimum elevation of approximately 165 feet (50&#160;m).  It was originally a <a href="/wiki/Linear_settlement" title="Linear settlement">linear settlement</a> built on the main road running north-east to south-west, but modern estates have since been built, extending the town to the south and east.  In the older part of the settlement there is much high-density housing with relatively narrow streets, and a number of retail outlets and public houses; the more modern estates have housing which is generally lower in density.  To the south there is an industrial estate.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlements">Settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Settlements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Besides the town, there are numerous villages and hamlets in the parish, including <a href="/wiki/Talskiddy" title="Talskiddy">Talskiddy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gluvian" title="Gluvian">Gluvian</a> in the north, <a href="/wiki/Ruthvoes" title="Ruthvoes">Ruthvoes</a> (southeast), <a href="/wiki/Trebudannon" title="Trebudannon">Trebudannon</a> (south), <a href="/wiki/Tregaswith" title="Tregaswith">Tregaswith</a> (southwest), <a href="/wiki/Tregatillian" title="Tregatillian">Tregatillian</a> (east) and a large number of smaller farming settlements and isolated dwellings. There are also Halloon, Lanhizey, Rosedinnick, <a href="/wiki/Tregamere" title="Tregamere">Tregamere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trekenning" title="Trekenning">Trekenning</a>, Trevarron, Trevolgas and Trugo.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivers">Rivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A number of small rivers and streams flow through St Columb parish, most rising in the eastern part and flowing west.  One of the sources of the <a href="/wiki/River_Fal" title="River Fal">River Fal</a> lies just within the boundary on the <a href="/wiki/Goss_Moor" title="Goss Moor">Goss Moor</a>; this flows southwest to the South Coast.  The <a href="/wiki/River_Menalhyl" title="River Menalhyl">River Menalhyl</a>, which flows through the north part of St Columb (Bridge), has three branches with a confluence at Gilbert\xe2\x80\x99s Water, just to the east of the town.  The longest of these rises next to the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Maidens_stone_row" title="Nine Maidens stone row">Nine Maidens</a> standing stones in the north part of the parish.  The Menalhyl was historically important in the area, powering a number of mills along its course.  A smaller river rises near Winnard\'s Perch (north of <a href="/wiki/Talskiddy" title="Talskiddy">Talskiddy</a>), later joining the Menalhyl near its mouth at <a href="/wiki/Mawgan_Porth" title="Mawgan Porth">Mawgan Porth</a>.  The other main river of the parish is the unnamed one (often called the River Porth) that rises to the east of <a href="/wiki/Ruthvoes" title="Ruthvoes">Ruthvoes</a>, and that in its latter course fills the <a href="/wiki/Porth_Reservoir" title="Porth Reservoir">Porth Reservoir</a> and enters the sea at <a href="/wiki/Porth,_Cornwall" title="Porth, Cornwall">St Columb Porth</a>.  This is the river that, according to legend, was begun by the blood of the murdered <a href="/wiki/Columba_the_Virgin" title="Columba the Virgin">Saint Columba</a> running down the valley.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protected_areas">Protected areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Protected areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><i>Borlasevath and Retallack Moor</i>  (<a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">SSSI</a>), noted for its <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">biological characteristics</a>, is located south of Nine Maidens and north of the resort of <a href="/wiki/Retallack" title="Retallack">Retallack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/A39_road" title="A39 road">A39</a> main road runs north to south through the parish.  Until the late 1970s it went through the town but a <a href="/wiki/Bypass_(road)" title="Bypass (road)">bypass</a> now carries traffic east of St Columb.  The <a href="/wiki/A30_road" title="A30 road">A30</a> dual carriageway also runs through the southeastern part of the parish north of <a href="/wiki/Goss_Moor" title="Goss Moor">Goss Moor</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Coast_Line,_Cornwall" title="Atlantic Coast Line, Cornwall">Par-Newquay railway line</a> does not enter St Columb parish but forms part of its southern boundary. For most of the Middle Ages the church belonged to the Arundells of <a href="/wiki/Lanherne" title="Lanherne">Lanherne</a> and was lavishly endowed. Within the church were two chantry chapels served by six priests altogether (five for the Arundell chantry).<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The tower is a fine example of a fifteenth-century building, consisting of four stages with <a href="/wiki/Battlement" title="Battlement">battlements</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pinnacle" title="Pinnacle">pinnacles</a>. It is 80 feet (24&#160;m) high and contains eight bells re-hung in 1950. In 1920 the chiming clock was added as a memorial to the men of St. Columb who died in the <a href="/wiki/Great_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Great War">Great War</a>. In 1860 plans were drawn up by <a href="/wiki/William_Butterfield" title="William Butterfield">William Butterfield</a>, in hope of St. Columb church becoming the cathedral of the future diocese of Cornwall, but the cathedral was built at Truro.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The church is the venue for the "Classic Cornwall" music class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arms_of_St_Columb_Major.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/7d/Arms_of_St_Columb_Major.jpg/220px-Arms_of_St_Columb_Major.jpg" width="220" height="265" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/7d/Arms_of_St_Columb_Major.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="236" data-file-height="284" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arms_of_St_Columb_Major.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Town <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The town <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a> consists of a hand holding a silver hurling ball with the motto "Town and Country do your best". The design originally appeared on medals awarded to winners of the hurling game and were first awarded in the 1930s. Later the design was adopted by the town council as a symbol of civic pride. The emblem appears on the <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayoral</a> chains and it is used on the uniform of St Columb School.  Roadsigns at each end of the town also bear the design.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>St Columb Major is served, at primary level, by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.st-columb-major.cornwall.sch.uk/">St Columb Major ACE Academy</a> - part of the Atlantic Centre of Excellence Multi Academy Trust.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Amenities.2C_large_employers_and_tourist_attractions"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Amenities,_large_employers_and_tourist_attractions">Amenities, large employers and tourist attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Amenities, large employers and tourist attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_employers">Main employers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Columb_Major&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Main employers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Pall_Corporation" title="Pall Corporation">Pall Corporation</a> \xe2\x80\x93 Filtration and separation technology.
