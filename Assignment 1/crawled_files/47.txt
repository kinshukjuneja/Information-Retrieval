href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Word origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The word <i>archipelago</i> is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> \xe1\xbc\x84\xcf\x81\xcf\x87\xce\xb9- (<i>arkhi-</i>, "chief") and \xcf\x80\xce\xad\xce\xbb\xce\xb1\xce\xb3\xce\xbf\xcf\x82 (<i>p\xc3\xa9lagos</i>, "sea") through the Italian <i>arcipelago</i>. In <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, possibly following a tradition of <a href="/wiki/Classical_Antiquity" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical Antiquity">antiquity</a>, "Archipelago" (from <a href="/wiki/Medieval_Greek" title="Medieval Greek">medieval Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Linguistic_reconstruction" title="Linguistic reconstruction">*</a>\xe1\xbc\x80\xcf\x81\xcf\x87\xce\xb9\xcf\x80\xce\xad\xce\xbb\xce\xb1\xce\xb3\xce\xbf\xcf\x82 and <a href="/wiki/Medieval_Latin" title="Medieval Latin">Latin</a> <i>archipelagus</i>) was the proper name for the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a> and, later, usage shifted to refer to the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Islands" title="Aegean Islands">Aegean Islands</a> (since the sea is remarkable for its large number of islands).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_types">Geographic types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographic types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Archipelagos may be found isolated in large amounts of water or neighbouring a large land mass. For example, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> has more than 700&#160;islands surrounding its mainland which form an archipelago.\n</p><p>Archipelagos are often volcanic, forming along island arcs generated by subduction zones or hotspots, but may also be the result of <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deposition_(sediment)" class="mw-redirect" title="Deposition (sediment)">deposition</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Land_elevation" class="mw-redirect" title="Land elevation">land elevation</a>. Depending on their geological origin, islands forming archipelagos can be referred to as <i>oceanic islands</i>, <i>continental fragments</i>, and <i>continental islands</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oceanic_islands">Oceanic islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Oceanic islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Oceanic islands are mainly of volcanic origin, and widely separated from any adjacent continent. The <a href="/wiki/Hawai%27ian_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Hawai&#39;ian Islands">Hawai\'ian Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a>, and <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Amsterdam" title="\xc3\x8ele Amsterdam">\xc3\x8ele Amsterdam</a> in the south <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> are examples.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continental_fragments">Continental fragments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Continental fragments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Continental fragments correspond to land masses that have separated from a continental mass due to <a href="/wiki/Tectonic" class="mw-redirect" title="Tectonic">tectonic</a> displacement. The <a href="/wiki/Farallon_Islands" title="Farallon Islands">Farallon Islands</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> are an example.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continental_archipelagos">Continental archipelagos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Continental archipelagos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Sets of islands formed close to the coast of a continent are considered continental archipelagos when they form part of the same continental shelf, when those islands are above-water extensions of the shelf. The islands of the <a href="/wiki/Inside_Passage" title="Inside Passage">Inland passage</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> are an example.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_examples">Further examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
