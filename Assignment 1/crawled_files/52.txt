href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> midfielder and <a href="/wiki/Manager_(association_football)" title="Manager (association football)">manager</a>. \n</p><p>He spent 12 of his 15 years as a professional with <a href="/wiki/CS_Universitatea_Craiova" title="CS Universitatea Craiova">Universitatea Craiova</a>, appearing in more than 300 official games with the club and winning seven major titles.\n</p><p>Balaci is considered one of the greatest Romanian footballers of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-FRF_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FRF-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilie_Balaci&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilie_Balaci&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Balaci was a product of <a href="/wiki/CS_Universitatea_Craiova" title="CS Universitatea Craiova">Universitatea Craiova</a>\'s youth system. He made his first team debut in 1973, at the age of 16, and won the <a href="/wiki/1973%E2%80%9374_Divizia_A" title="1973\xe2\x80\x9374 Divizia A">league title</a> in his first season. He soon managed to become one of the youngest players ever to score in the <a href="/wiki/Romanian_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Romanian League">Romanian League</a>. Balaci went on to play twelve seasons with Craiova, helping them to win two consecutive league titles in <a href="/wiki/1979%E2%80%9380_Divizia_A" title="1979\xe2\x80\x9380 Divizia A">1980</a> and <a href="/wiki/1980%E2%80%9381_Divizia_A" title="1980\xe2\x80\x9381 Divizia A">1981</a>. He also won the <a href="/wiki/Romanian_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Romanian Cup">Romanian Cup</a> four times, in the years 1977, 1978, 1981 and 1983. He became an integral part of the team helping them reach the <a href="/wiki/1982%E2%80%9383_UEFA_Cup" title="1982\xe2\x80\x9383 UEFA Cup">1982\xe2\x80\x9383 UEFA Cup</a> semi-finals, before moving to Olt Scornice\xc5\x9fti in the winter of 1984. In December 1982, he signed a pre-contract with Italian side <a href="/wiki/AC_Milan" class="mw-redirect" title="AC Milan">AC Milan</a>, but the transfer did not materialize because of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Romania" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist Romania">communist regime</a> in Romania.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%9387_Divizia_A" title="1986\xe2\x80\x9387 Divizia A">1986\xe2\x80\x9387</a> season, he jointed Bucharest rivals <a href="/wiki/FC_Dinamo_Bucure%C5%9Fti" class="mw-redirect" title="FC Dinamo Bucure\xc5\x9fti">Dinamo Bucure\xc5\x9fti</a> together with Craiova teammate <a href="/wiki/Rodion_C%C4%83m%C4%83taru" title="Rodion C\xc4\x83m\xc4\x83taru">Rodion C\xc4\x83m\xc4\x83taru</a>. He spent two seasons at the club, under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Mircea_Lucescu" title="Mircea Lucescu">Mircea Lucescu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, Balaci did not win any major trophies whilst he was playing for them and following a knee injury he had to retire from football.<sup id="cite_ref-5" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilie_Balaci&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Balaci made his full international debut on 23 March 1974 in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> at the age of 17. He earned 65 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> and scored 8 goals for the <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romanian national team</a> between 1974 and 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He also became captain of the national team during the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1984_qualifying_Group_5" title="UEFA Euro 1984 qualifying Group 5">UEFA Euro 1984 qualifying</a> campaign, but he was unable to play for Romania at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1984" title="UEFA Euro 1984">UEFA Euro 84</a> due to an injury.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilie_Balaci&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After retiring as a footballer, Balaci went on to coach lower league sides <a href="/wiki/CS_Pandurii_T%C3%A2rgu_Jiu" title="CS Pandurii T\xc3\xa2rgu Jiu">Pandurii T\xc3\xa2rgu Jiu</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Drobeta-Turnu_Severin" title="FC Drobeta-Turnu Severin">Drobeta-Turnu Severin</a>. In 1991, he coached clubs in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab world</a>, he became one of the most successful managers there having won over 22 major trophies domestic and international.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He took charge of Tunisia\xe2\x80\x99s <a href="/wiki/Club_Africain" title="Club Africain">Club Africain</a> and Moroccan club Olympique Casablanca in the early 1990s guiding them to their first <a href="/wiki/CAF_Champions_League" title="CAF Champions League">African Champions League</a>, the league title and the Tunis Cup. He then managed UAE, Qatari and Saudi Arabian clubs <a href="/wiki/Al_Shabab_Al_Arabi_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Al Shabab Al Arabi Club">Al Shabab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Nassr_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Al Nassr FC">Al Nassr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al-Hilal_FC" title="Al-Hilal FC">Al-Hilal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Ain_FC" title="Al Ain FC">Al Ain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sadd_Sports_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Sadd Sports Club">Al Sadd</a> winning the championship and domestic cup with each side.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In June 2003, he was appointed manager of <a href="/wiki/Al_Ahli_Club_(Dubai)" class="mw-redirect" title="Al Ahli Club (Dubai)">Al Ahli</a> until January 2005, where he won the <a href="/wiki/UAE_President%27s_Cup" title="UAE President&#39;s Cup">UAE President\'s Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He then went on to join Qatari side <a href="/wiki/Al-Arabi_SC_(Qatar)" title="Al-Arabi SC (Qatar)">Al-Arabi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Qatar_Stars_League" title="2005\xe2\x80\x9306 Qatar Stars League">2005\xe2\x80\x9306</a> season, before moving to the United Arab Emirates club <a href="/wiki/Al_Shabab_Al_Arabi_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Al Shabab Al Arabi Club">Al Shabab</a> for a second spell, but without success.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In August 2007, Balaci was hired as general manager of Universitatea Craiova whom he also coached in two periods, and remained there for two seasons. On 22 July 2009, Balaci was named head coach of Kuwait club <a href="/wiki/Kazma_Sporting_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Kazma Sporting Club">Kazma</a>. He had an impressive start with the Kuwaiti side at the <a href="/wiki/2010_AFC_Cup" title="2010 AFC Cup">2010 AFC Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 11 July 2011, he returned to Morocco signing for <a href="/wiki/Raja_Casablanca" title="Raja Casablanca">Raja Casablanca</a> but was sacked following a financial dispute with the club in September after just two months. Raja appointed <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Marchand" title="Bertrand Marchand">Bertrand Marchand</a> as his replacement.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 4 June 2013, Balaci signed a contract with newly promoted <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Premier_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Saudi Premier League">Saudi Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Al-Nahda_(RSA)" class="mw-redirect" title="Al-Nahda (RSA)">Al-Nahda</a> saving the club from relegation.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In September 2013, he was sacked as coach due to a poor start to the season with only one point from the first four matches.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2016, he took over the head coach position at <a href="/wiki/Omdurman" title="Omdurman">Omdurman</a>-based club <a href="/wiki/Al-Hilal_Club_(Omdurman)" title="Al-Hilal Club (Omdurman)">Al-Hilal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Balaci was sacked in September 2016 after the team clinched the title with three games left to play.  Balaci thought that the club\'s owners did not want the title win associated with his name.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2017, Balaci was unveiled as the new manager of Omani side <a href="/wiki/Suwaiq_Club" title="Suwaiq Club">Al-Suwaiq</a>. Although the team was assured of top of the championship with 38 points from 15 games Balaci decided to terminate his contract in March 2018 with 12 games left on the season.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_overview">Managerial overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
