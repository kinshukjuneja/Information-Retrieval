(1613\xe2\x80\x931679)</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\n\n<b>Anthony Buller</b> (1613\xe2\x80\x931679) was an English soldier and politician who sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_England" title="House of Commons of England">House of Commons</a> between 1659 and 1660. He fought in the <a href="/wiki/Roundheads" class="mw-redirect" title="Roundheads">Parliamentary</a> army in the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>.\n</p><p>Buller was the son of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Buller" title="Richard Buller">Sir Richard Buller</a>, of Shillingham, Cornwall and his wife Alice Hayward, daughter of Sir Rowland Hayward. He was baptised on 14 November 1613. The Buller family was originally from Somerset and acquired Shillingham in around 1555.<sup id="cite_ref-Henning_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Henning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the Civil War, he was a captain on the horse in the Parliamentary army becoming major in 1646. His own reputation for valour was high, but his troopers were given to disorderly and licentious behaviour. He was governor of the <a href="/wiki/Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Scilly Isles">Scilly Isles</a> after they were surrendered by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin_(died_1667)" class="mw-redirect" title="Francis Godolphin (died 1667)">Francis Godolphin</a> in 1647 until 1648 when he was captured in the Royalist revolt. He was held prisoner but treated with special kindness as a "gallant soldier". He served in an expedition to the West Indies and  was a colonel of foot from 1654 to 1655.<sup id="cite_ref-Henning_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Henning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1659, Buller was elected <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for  <a href="/wiki/Callington_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Callington (UK Parliament constituency)">Callington</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Third_Protectorate_Parliament" title="Third Protectorate Parliament">Third Protectorate Parliament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Willis_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Willis-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1660, he was elected MP for <a href="/wiki/Saltash_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Saltash (UK Parliament constituency)">Saltash</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Convention_Parliament_(1660)" title="Convention Parliament (1660)">Convention Parliament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was a captain in the Duke of York\'s Horse Guards from 1660 to 1661. In 1661 he stood unsuccessfully for parliament for <a href="/wiki/Bossiney_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bossiney (UK Parliament constituency)">Bossiney</a>.  He was deputy governor of the Scilly Isles from 1666 to 1667. He served in the admiralty regiment from 1667 until his death. He raised 300 men for the defence of the Scilly Isles during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Second Anglo-Dutch War">Second Dutch War</a>, and was given a newly built Sloop HMS Portsmouth.<sup id="cite_ref-Henning_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Henning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Buller died before August 1679 when his will was proved. He was married and had a son who inherited his estates in Westminster and Weybridge and four daughters. His brothers <a href="/wiki/Francis_Buller_(Parliamentarian)" title="Francis Buller (Parliamentarian)">Francis Buller</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Buller_(MP)" title="George Buller (MP)">George Buller</a> were also MPs for Saltash.<sup id="cite_ref-Henning_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Henning-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
