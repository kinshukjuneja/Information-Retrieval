/>.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>North Somerset Council</b> is the local authority of <a href="/wiki/North_Somerset" title="North Somerset">North Somerset</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority</a>, having the powers of a non-metropolitan county and district council combined. It provides a full range of local government services including Council Tax billing, libraries, social services, processing planning applications, waste collection and disposal, and it is a local education authority. The council meets at <a href="/wiki/Weston-super-Mare" title="Weston-super-Mare">Weston-super-Mare</a> Town Hall.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Powers_and_Functions"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Powers and Functions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Political_Composition"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Political Composition</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-3"><a href="#Current_Councillors"><span class="tocnumber">2.1</span> <span class="toctext">Current Councillors</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_Functions">Powers and Functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Somerset_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Powers and Functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The local authority derives its powers and functions from the Local Government Act 1972 and subsequent legislation. For the purposes of local government, North Somerset is within a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">non-metropolitan</a> area of England. As a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority</a>, North Somerset Council has the powers and functions of both a non-metropolitan county and district council combined. In its capacity as a district council, it is a <a href="/wiki/Billing_authorities_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Billing authorities in England">billing authority</a> that collects Council Tax and business rates, processes local planning applications and is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Council_house" title="Council house">housing</a>, waste collection and environmental health. In its capacity as a county council, it is a <a href="/wiki/Local_education_authority" title="Local education authority">local education authority</a>, responsible for social services, libraries and waste disposal.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_Composition">Political Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Somerset_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>North Somerset unitary council is elected every four years, electing a total of 50 councillors in 20 single-member wards and 15 two-member wards. Since the first election to the unitary authority in 1995, the council has either been under <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative party</a> control, or no party has held a majority. The Conservatives gained a majority at the <a href="/wiki/North_Somerset_Council_election,_2007" title="North Somerset Council election, 2007">2007 election</a> and have retained control since then.\n</p><p>The current political composition of the council is:\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable" style="text-align:right">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th colspan="2" rowspan="2">Political group\n</th>\n<th
