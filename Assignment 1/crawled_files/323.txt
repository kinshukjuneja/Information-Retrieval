format">best-of-seven series</a> played in October in the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_postseason" title="Major League Baseball postseason">Major League Baseball postseason</a> that determines the winner of the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> (NL) pennant. The winner of the series advances to play the winner of the <a href="/wiki/American_League_Championship_Series" title="American League Championship Series">American League Championship Series</a> (ALCS) in the <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>\'s championship series.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_Championship_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Prior to 1969, the National League champion (the "<a href="/wiki/List_of_National_League_pennant_winners" title="List of National League pennant winners">pennant winner</a>") was determined by the best win-loss record at the end of the regular season. There were four <i>ad hoc</i> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_tie-breakers" title="List of Major League Baseball tie-breakers">three-game playoff series</a> due to ties under this formulation (in 1946, 1951, 1959, and 1962). (The <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> had to resolve a tie in 1948, but used a single-game playoff.)\n</p><p>A structured postseason series began in 1969, when both the National and American Leagues were reorganized into two divisions each, East and West. The two division winners within each league played each other in a best-of-five series to determine who would advance to the World Series. In 1985, the format changed to best-of-seven.\n</p><p>The NLCS and ALCS, since the expansion to seven games, are always played in a 2\xe2\x80\x933\xe2\x80\x932 format: games 1, 2, 6, and 7 are played in the stadium of the team that has home field advantage, and games 3, 4, and 5 are played in the stadium of the team that does not. Home field advantage is given to the team that has the better record, with the exception that the team that made the postseason as the <a href="/wiki/Wild_card_(sports)#Major_League_Baseball" title="Wild card (sports)">Wild Card</a> cannot get home field advantage. From 1969 to 1993, home field advantage was alternated between divisions each year regardless of regular season record and from 1995 to 1997 home field advantage was predetermined before the season.\n</p><p>In 1981, a <a href="/wiki/1981_National_League_Division_Series" title="1981 National League Division Series">divisional series</a> was held due to a split season caused by a <a href="/wiki/1981_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1981 Major League Baseball strike">players\' strike</a>.\n</p><p>In 1994, the league was restructured into three divisions, with the three division winners and a wild-card team advancing to a best-of-five postseason round, the <a href="/wiki/National_League_Division_Series" title="National League Division Series">National League Division Series</a> (NLDS). The winners of that round advance to the best-of-seven NLCS.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>, an American League team between 1969 and 1997, and the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>, a National League team between 1962 and 2012, are the only franchises to play in both the ALCS and NLCS. The Astros are the only team to have won both an NLCS (<a href="/wiki/2005_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2005 National League Championship Series">2005</a>) and an ALCS (<a href="/wiki/2017_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2017 American League Championship Series">2017</a>). The Astros made four NLCS appearances before moving to the AL in 2013. Every current National League franchise has appeared in the NLCS.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_Trophy">Championship Trophy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_Championship_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship Trophy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Warren_C._Giles_Trophy" class="mw-redirect" title="Warren C. Giles Trophy">Warren C. Giles Trophy</a>, named for the <a href="/wiki/Warren_Giles" title="Warren Giles">president of the NL</a> from 1951 to 1969, is awarded to the NLCS winner.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Most_Valuable_Player_Award">Most Valuable Player Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_Championship_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Most Valuable Player Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dd><i>See: <a href="/wiki/League_Championship_Series_Most_Valuable_Player_Award#National_League_winners" title="League Championship Series Most Valuable Player Award">League Championship Series Most Valuable Player Award#National League winners</a></i></dd></dl>\n<p>A Most Valuable Player (<a href="/wiki/MVP" class="mw-redirect" title="MVP">MVP</a>) award is given to the outstanding player in each series, though voters can consider performances made during the divisional series.  The MVP award has been given to a player on the losing team twice, in 1986 to Mike Scott of the Houston Astros and in 1987 to Jeff Leonard of the San Francisco Giants.\n</p><p>Although the National League began its LCS MVP award in 1977, the American League did not begin its LCS MVP award until 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="NLCS_results_.281969.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="NLCS_results_(1969\xe2\x80\x93present)">NLCS results
