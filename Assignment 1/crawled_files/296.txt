Edmund Sharrock Moore</b> <a href="/wiki/Associate_of_the_Royal_College_of_Science" title="Associate of the Royal College of Science">ARCS</a> (10 May 1870 \xe2\x80\x93 15 January 1947) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Biologist" title="Biologist">biologist</a>, best known for leading two expeditions to <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika" title="Tanganyika">Tanganyika</a>.  He is often cited as John Edward Sharrock Moore, and was listed in <a href="/wiki/Who%27s_Who_(UK)" title="Who&#39;s Who (UK)">Who\'s Who (UK)</a> as John Edmund Shorrock Moore-Salvin until 1954\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Edmund_Sharrock_Moore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Born at Swinshaw near <a href="/wiki/Loveclough" title="Loveclough">Loveclough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rossendale" class="mw-redirect" title="Rossendale">Rossendale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, John was the son of Henry Moore, Cotton Manufacturer and Mary Elizabeth Moore (n\xc3\xa9e Margerison).  After 1878 his father became a Colliery agent and moved the family to <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a> and then to <a href="/wiki/Chiswick" title="Chiswick">Chiswick</a> before 1891.  His father became a Sculptor, as did his sister Esther Mary Moore who exhibited at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Arts" title="Royal Academy of Arts">Royal Academy of Arts</a>.\nJohn studied at <a href="/wiki/Tonbridge_School" title="Tonbridge School">Tonbridge School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Science" title="Royal College of Science">Royal College of Science</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Kensington" title="South Kensington">South Kensington</a>.\nIn 1892 he created the biological term \xe2\x80\x98<a href="/wiki/Synapsis" title="Synapsis">synapsis</a>\xe2\x80\x99.  Later in 1905 he would co-publisher the term \xe2\x80\x98<a href="/wiki/Meiosis" title="Meiosis">meiosis</a>\xe2\x80\x99.\nBetween 16 October 1893 and 9 June 1894 he worked at the <a href="/wiki/Stazione_Zoologica" title="Stazione Zoologica">Stazione Zoologica</a> in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>. \nHe led the First and Second Tanganyika Expeditions (1894-1897 and 1899-1900).  During the second expedition he was the first to reach the snowline of the <a href="/wiki/Ruwenzori_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Ruwenzori Mountains">Rwenzori Mountains</a>, attaining 14,900 feet and proved the existence of permanent glaciers.\nHe was awarded the Huxley Gold Medal for Research in 1900 by Royal College of Science.\nAppointed Demonstrator in Zoology at The Royal College of Science.  \nIn 1904 he married Heloise Salvin, daughter of naturalist <a href="/wiki/Osbert_Salvin" title="Osbert Salvin">Osbert Salvin</a>.  They had one child <a href="/wiki/Nanamoli_Bhikkhu" title="Nanamoli Bhikkhu">Osbert John S Moore</a> born 25 June 1905.\nAppointed in 1906 as Professor of Experimental and <a href="/wiki/Cytopathology" title="Cytopathology">Pathological Cytology</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Liverpool" title="University of Liverpool">University of Liverpool</a>.\nHe retired in 1908 after the death of his father.  In 1911 he is living with his wife and son in Chiswick with four of his sisters.\nDuring the 1920s he moved to <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>.  Heloise died on 4 Nov 1927.  He died of <a href="/wiki/Heart_failure" title="Heart failure">heart failure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arteriosclerosis" title="Arteriosclerosis">arteriosclerosis</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Cornwall_Hospital" title="West Cornwall Hospital">West Cornwall Hospital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> on 15 January 1947.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Edmund_Sharrock_Moore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Moore, J.E.S., 1897a.
