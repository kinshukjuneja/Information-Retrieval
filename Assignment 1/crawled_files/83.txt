MT&#39;,serif"><b>&#8224;</b></span></a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Maarten Harpertszoon Tromp</b> (23 April 1598 \xe2\x80\x93 10 August 1653) was an officer and later <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Navy" title="Navy">navy</a>. His first name is also spelled <i>Maerten</i>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Early_life"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Early life</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Supreme_commander_of_the_confederate_fleet"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Supreme commander of the confederate fleet</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Legends"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Legends</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maarten_Tromp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Brielle" title="Brielle">Brill</a>, Tromp was the oldest son of Harpert Maertensz, a naval officer and captain of the frigate <i>Olifantstromp ("Elephant Trunk")</i>. The surname <i>Tromp</i> probably derives from the name of the ship; it first appeared in documents in 1607. His mother supplemented the family\'s income as a washerwoman. At the age of nine, Tromp went to sea with his father, and he was present in a squadron covering the Dutch main fleet fighting the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gibraltar_(1607)" title="Battle of Gibraltar (1607)">Battle of Gibraltar</a> in 1607.\n</p><p>In 1610, after his father\'s discharge because of a navy reorganization, the Tromps were on their way to <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a> on their merchantman when they were attacked by a squadron of seven ships under command of the English pirate <a href="/wiki/Peter_Easton" title="Peter Easton">Peter Easton</a>. During the fight, Tromp\'s father was slain by a cannonball. According to legend, the 12-year-old boy rallied the crew of the ship with the cry "Won\'t you avenge my father\'s death?" The pirates seized him and sold him on the slave market of <a href="/wiki/Sal%C3%A9" title="Sal\xc3\xa9">Sal\xc3\xa9</a>. Two years later, Easton was moved by pity and ordered his redemption.\n</p><p>Set free, he supported his mother and three sisters by working in a <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> shipyard. Tromp went to sea again at 19, briefly working for the navy, but he was captured again in 1621 after having rejoined the merchant fleet, this time by <a href="/wiki/Barbary" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbary">Barbary</a> corsairs off <a href="/wiki/Tunis" title="Tunis">Tunis</a>. He was kept as a slave until the age of 24 and by then had so impressed the <a href="/wiki/Bey_of_Tunis" class="mw-redirect" title="Bey of Tunis">Bey of Tunis</a> and the corsair <a href="/wiki/Jack_Ward" title="Jack Ward">John Ward</a> with his skills in gunnery and navigation that the latter offered him a position in his fleet. When Tromp refused, the Bey was even more impressed by this show of character and allowed him to leave as a free man.\n</p><p>He joined the Dutch navy as a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> in July 1622, entering service with the <a href="/wiki/Admiralty_of_the_Maze" class="mw-redirect" title="Admiralty of the Maze">Admiralty of the Maze</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>. On 7 May 1624, he married Dignom Cornelisdochter de Haes, the daughter of a merchant; in the same year he became captain of the <i>St. Antonius</i>, an advice yacht (fast-sailing messenger ship). His first distinction was as Lieutenant-Admiral <a href="/wiki/Piet_Hein_(Netherlands)" class="mw-redirect" title="Piet Hein (Netherlands)">Piet Hein</a>\'s flag captain on the <i>Vliegende Groene Draeck</i> during the fight with <a href="/wiki/Ostend" title="Ostend">Ostend</a> privateers in 1629 in which Hein was killed.\n</p><p>In 1629 and 1630, the year that he was appointed full captain on initiative of <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">stadtholder</a> <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Henry,_Prince_of_Orange" title="Frederick Henry, Prince of Orange">Frederick Henry</a> himself, Tromp was very successful in fighting the <a href="/wiki/Dunkirkers" title="Dunkirkers">Dunkirkers</a> as a squadron commander, functioning as a <a href="/wiki/Commandeur" class="mw-redirect" title="Commandeur">commandeur</a> on the <i>Vliegende Groene Draeck</i>. Despite receiving four honorary golden chains, he was not promoted further. The <i>Vliegende Groene Draeck</i> foundered and new heavy vessels were reserved for the flag officers while Tromp was relegated to the old <i>Prins Hendrik</i>.\n</p><p>In 1634, Tromp\'s first wife died, and he left the naval service in 1634 in disappointment. He became a <a href="/wiki/Deacon" title="Deacon">deacon</a> and married Alijth Jacobsdochter Arckenboudt, the daughter of Brill\'s wealthy <a href="/wiki/Schepen" title="Schepen">schepen</a> and tax collector, on 12 September 1634.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supreme_commander_of_the_confederate_fleet">Supreme commander of the confederate fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maarten_Tromp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Supreme commander of the confederate fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Tromp was promoted from captain to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-Admiral" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant-Admiral">Lieutenant-Admiral</a> of Holland and West Frisia in 1637, when Lieutenant-Admiral Philips van Dorp and other flag officers were removed due to incompetence. Although formally ranking under the <a href="/wiki/Admiral-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Admiral-General">Admiral-General</a> <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Henry_of_Orange" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick Henry of Orange">Frederick Henry of Orange</a>, he was the <i>de facto</i> supreme commander of the Dutch fleet, as the <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">stadtholders</a> never fought at sea. Tromp was mostly occupied with blockading the privateer port of <a href="/wiki/Dunkirk" title="Dunkirk">Dunkirk</a>.\n</p><p>In 1639, during the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years&#39; War">Dutch struggle for independence from Spain</a>, Tromp defeated a large <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> fleet bound for <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Downs" title="Battle of the Downs">Battle of the Downs</a>, marking the end of Spanish naval power. In a preliminary battle, the <a href="/wiki/Action_of_18_September_1639" title="Action of 18 September 1639">Action of 18 September 1639</a>, Tromp was the first fleet commander known for the deliberate use of <a href="/wiki/Line_of_battle" title="Line of battle">line of battle</a> tactics. His flagship in this period was the <i><a href="/wiki/Aemilia_(1632)" class="mw-redirect" title="Aemilia (1632)">Aemilia</a></i>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cornelis_Tromp.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Cornelis_Tromp.jpg/250px-Cornelis_Tromp.jpg" width="250" height="313" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Cornelis_Tromp.jpg/375px-Cornelis_Tromp.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Cornelis_Tromp.jpg/500px-Cornelis_Tromp.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="875" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cornelis_Tromp.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><b>Cornelis Tromp</b>, 1629&#8211;1691</i> by Sir <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lely" title="Peter Lely">Peter Lely</a>, painted c. 1675.</div></div></div>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/First_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="First Anglo-Dutch War">First Anglo-Dutch War</a> of 1652 to 1653, Tromp commanded the Dutch fleet in the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Goodwin_Sands" title="Battle of Goodwin Sands">Dover</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dungeness" title="Battle of Dungeness">Dungeness</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Portland" title="Battle of Portland">Portland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Gabbard" title="Battle of the Gabbard">the Gabbard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Scheveningen" title="Battle of Scheveningen">Scheveningen</a>. In the latter, he was killed by a sharpshooter in the rigging of <a href="/wiki/William_Penn_(admiral)" class="mw-redirect" title="William Penn (admiral)">William Penn</a>\'s ship. His acting flag captain, <a href="/wiki/Egbert_Bartholomeusz_Kortenaer" title="Egbert Bartholomeusz Kortenaer">Egbert Bartholomeusz Kortenaer</a>, on the <i><a href="/wiki/Dutch_ship_Brederode" title="Dutch ship Brederode">Brederode</a></i> kept up fleet morale by not lowering Tromp\'s standard, pretending Tromp was still alive.\n</p><p>Tromp\'s death was a severe blow to the <a href="/wiki/Naval_history_of_the_Netherlands" title="Naval history of the Netherlands">Dutch navy</a> but also to the Orangists, who sought the defeat of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_England" title="Commonwealth of England">Commonwealth of England</a> and the restoration of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Stuart" title="House of Stuart">Stuart</a> monarchy. Republican influence strengthened after Scheveningen, which led to peace negotiations with the Commonwealth, culminating in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Westminster_(1654)" title="Treaty of Westminster (1654)">Treaty of Westminster</a>.\n</p><p>During his career, his main rival was Vice-Admiral <a href="/wiki/Witte_de_With" class="mw-redirect" title="Witte de With">Witte de With</a>, who also served the <a href="/wiki/Admiralty_of_Rotterdam" title="Admiralty of Rotterdam">Admiralty of Rotterdam</a> (de Maze) from 1637. De With temporarily replaced him as supreme commander for the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kentish_Knock" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Kentish Knock">Battle of Kentish Knock</a>. Tromp\'s successor was Lieutenant-Admiral <a href="/wiki/Jacob_van_Wassenaer_Obdam" title="Jacob van Wassenaer Obdam">Jacob van Wassenaer Obdam</a>.\n</p><p>Tromp, a "sea hero", was immensely popular with the common people, a sentiment expressed by the greatest of Dutch poets, <a href="/wiki/Joost_van_den_Vondel" title="Joost van den Vondel">Joost van den Vondel</a>, in a famous poem describing his marble grave monument in <a href="/wiki/Delft" title="Delft">Delft</a> showing the admiral on his moment of death with a burning English fleet on the foreground:\n</p>\n<dl><dd><i>Here rests the hero Tromp, the brave protector</i></dd>\n<dd><i>of shipping and free sea, serving free land</i></dd>\n<dd><i>his memory alive in artful spectre</i></dd>\n<dd><i>as if he had just died at his last stand</i></dd>\n<dd><i>His knell the cries of death, guns\' thunderous call</i></dd>\n<dd><i>a burning Brittany too Great for sea alone</i></dd>\n<dd><i>He\'s carved himself an image in the hearts of all</i></dd>\n<dd><i>more lasting than grave\'s splendour and its marble stone</i></dd></dl>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Cornelis_Tromp" title="Cornelis Tromp">Cornelis Tromp</a>, the second son of Tromp by his first wife, Dignom Cornelisdochter de Haes, later became Commander of the Dutch navy, in the rank of Lieutenant-Admiral-General, after previously having commanded the Danish navy.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maarten_Tromp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In traditional British histories, Tromp is often wrongly called "Van Tromp".  There is also a story that, after his victory at Dungeness, Tromp attached a broom to his mast as a symbol that he had swept the English from the sea.  The following year, the English admiral <a href="/wiki/Robert_Blake_(admiral)" title="Robert Blake (admiral)">Robert Blake</a> supposedly attached a whip to his mast as a symbol that he had whipped the Dutch off the sea. The legend inspired a song <i>The Admiral\'s Broom</i>, famously covered by Australian baritone <a href="/wiki/Peter_Dawson_(bass-baritone)" title="Peter Dawson (bass-baritone)">Peter Dawson</a>. This is now regarded by historians as dubious.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maarten_Tromp&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Roger Hainsworth and Christine Churches, <i>The Anglo Dutch Naval Wars 1652-1674</i>, Sutton Pub Ltd, 1998</li>\n<li>Oliver Warner, <i>Great Sea Battles</i>, Spring Books London, 1973</li>\n<li>R.
