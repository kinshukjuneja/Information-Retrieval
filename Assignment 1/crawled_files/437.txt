Godolphin</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Sidney Godolphin</b>  (1610 (baptised) \xe2\x80\x93 9 February 1643) was an English <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">poet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Courtier" title="Courtier">courtier</a> and  politician who sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_England" title="House of Commons of England">House of Commons</a> between 1628 and 1643. He died fighting in the <a href="/wiki/Cavaliers" class="mw-redirect" title="Cavaliers">Royalist</a> army in the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Biography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Biography</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Poems"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Poems</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Haunting"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Haunting</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Notes"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sidney_Godolphin_(poet)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin was the second son of <a href="/wiki/William_Godolphin_(1567%E2%88%921613)" class="mw-redirect" title="William Godolphin (1567\xe2\x88\x921613)">Sir William Godolphin</a> (died 1613) of <a href="/wiki/Godolphin_Estate" title="Godolphin Estate">Godolphin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, by his wife, Thomasin, and was baptised on 15 January 1610. He was admitted a commoner of <a href="/wiki/Exeter_College,_Oxford" title="Exeter College, Oxford">Exeter College, Oxford</a>, 25 June 1624, aged 18,<sup id="cite_ref-Alumni_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alumni-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> remained there for three years, and afterwards entered one of the <a href="/wiki/Inns_of_court" class="mw-redirect" title="Inns of court">inns of court</a>, and travelled abroad. He was elected <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> MP for <a href="/wiki/Helston_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Helston (UK Parliament constituency)">Helston</a> in 1628 and sat until 1629 when King Charles decided to rule without parliament for eleven years.<sup id="cite_ref-Willis_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Willis-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Godolphin was elected again as MP for Helston to the <a href="/wiki/Short_Parliament" title="Short Parliament">Short Parliament</a> in March 1640, and to the <a href="/wiki/Long_Parliament" title="Long Parliament">Long Parliament</a> in October 1640.<sup id="cite_ref-Willis_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Willis-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was known as an adherent of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Wentworth,_1st_Earl_of_Strafford" title="Thomas Wentworth, 1st Earl of Strafford">Strafford</a>, and was one of the last royalist members to leave the house. When the civil war broke out, he made a final speech of warning, and left to raise a force in Cornwall. Despite his diminutive stature he, refusing an officer\'s commission and choosing to serve as a trooper, joined the cavalry of the army commanded by Sir <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Hopton" class="mw-redirect" title="Ralph Hopton">Ralph Hopton</a>, which crossed the <a href="/wiki/River_Tamar" title="River Tamar">Tamar</a> and advanced into Devon.<sup id="cite_ref-hop_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hop-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Their declaration signed by Godolphin is in <i>\xe2\x80\x98Lismore Papers\xe2\x80\x99</i>. Godolphin, whose advice, according to Clarendon, was highly valued by the commanders in spite of his lack of military experience, was shot in a skirmish at <a href="/wiki/Chagford" title="Chagford">Chagford</a>.\n</p><p>Godolphin was buried in the chancel of <a href="/wiki/Okehampton" title="Okehampton">Okehampton</a> Church on 10 February 1643.<sup id="cite_ref-Alumni_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alumni-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His will, dated 23 June 1642, contained a bequest of \xc2\xa3200 to <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hobbes" title="Thomas Hobbes">Thomas Hobbes</a>. He gave some plate to Exeter College, Oxford.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Poems">Poems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sidney_Godolphin_(poet)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Poems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin left poems which were never collected in a separate volume. "<i>The Passion of Dido for \xc3\x86neas</i>, as it is incomparably expressed in the fourth book of Virgil," finished by <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Waller" title="Edmund Waller">Edmund Waller</a>, was published in 1658 and 1679, and is in the fourth volume of <a href="/wiki/Dryden" class="mw-redirect" title="Dryden">Dryden</a>\'s <i>Miscellany Poems</i>. He was one of "certain persons of quality" whose translation of <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Corneille" title="Pierre Corneille">Pierre Corneille</a>\'s The <a href="/wiki/La_Mort_de_Pomp%C3%A9e" class="mw-redirect" title="La Mort de Pomp\xc3\xa9e">La Mort de Pomp\xc3\xa9e</a> was published in 1664. A song is in <a href="/wiki/George_Ellis_(poet)" title="George Ellis (poet)">George Ellis</a>\'s <i>Specimens of the Early English Poets</i>, and another in the <i>Tixall Poetry</i>. Other poems in manuscript are in the Harleian MSS. (6917) and the Malone MSS. in the <a href="/wiki/Bodleian_Library" title="Bodleian Library">Bodleian Library</a>. His elegy on <a href="/wiki/John_Donne" title="John Donne">John Donne</a> was included in the second edition of the poet\'s collected poetry (1635)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Commendatory_verse" class="mw-redirect" title="Commendatory verse">commendatory verses</a> by him are prefixed to Sandys\'s <i>Paraphrase</i> (1638), and an "Epitaph upon the Lady Rich" is in <a href="/wiki/John_Gauden" title="John Gauden">John Gauden</a>\'s <i>Funerals made Cordial</i> (1658).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Haunting">Haunting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sidney_Godolphin_(poet)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Haunting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>It is alleged that Godolphin\'s ghost haunts <a href="/wiki/The_Three_Crowns_Hotel" title="The Three Crowns Hotel">The Three Crowns Hotel</a> in Chagford.
