spelled and often pronounced <b>Porthlow</b>, (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Porth Logh</i>, \'cove of the deep water inlet\')) is a coastal settlement on the island of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is situated about half a mile northeast of <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a> and is a popular tourist spot with a number of guest houses, a restaurant, several gift shops and art galleries.\n</p><p>The only <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a> club on the Isles of Scilly is situated just to the north of Porthloo. The course consists of nine holes, each with two tees. The club was founded in 1904 and the course and clubhouse are open to visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Beach"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Beach</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Boat_yard"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Boat yard</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#In_television"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">In television</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beach">Beach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthloo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Beach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Porthloo Beach faces west - there are two tidal islets called Taylor\'s Island and Newford Island which are to the north and south of the beach respectively. At the top of the beach is a small remnant sand dune system with the grassy area behind used for boat standing and repairs. Due to the disturbance hairy buttercup (<i><a href="/wiki/Ranunculus_sardous" title="Ranunculus sardous">Ranunculus sardous</a></i>), along with other arable weeds appear here in one of its few sites on Scilly, and the only known site for wall\xe2\x80\x93rue (<i><a href="/wiki/Asplenium_ruta-muraria" title="Asplenium ruta-muraria">Asplenium ruta-muraria</a></i>) in the Isles of Scilly is just to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Porthloo is within an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Beauty" class="mw-redirect" title="Area of Outstanding Beauty">Area of Outstanding Beauty</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Geological_Conservation_Review" title="Geological Conservation Review">Geological Conservation Review</a> site and was designated a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI) in 1996. The 0.66 hectares (1.6 acres) site is notified for a succession of <a href="/wiki/Quaternary" title="Quaternary">Quaternary</a> sediments listed below with youngest at the top of the list:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Aeolian_processes" title="Aeolian processes">Aeolian</a> <a href="/wiki/Loess" title="Loess">loess</a> deposits dated to 18,000 years <a href="/wiki/Before_Present" title="Before Present">BP</a> (before present)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Head_(geology)" title="Head (geology)">Head</a> deposits with <a href="/wiki/Breccia" title="Breccia">breccia</a> recognised as the <a href="/wiki/Type_locality_(geology)" title="Type locality (geology)">type locality</a> and known as the Porthloo Breccia. Dated to the <a href="/wiki/Devensian" class="mw-redirect" title="Devensian">Late Devensian</a> 30,000 BP the pollen from organic layers indicate a <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> climate</li>\n<li>Storm beach laid down during the <a href="/wiki/Ipswichian" class="mw-redirect" title="Ipswichian">Ipswichian</a> <a href="/wiki/Interglacial" title="Interglacial">interglacial</a> which started 130,000 years ago and ended about 114,000 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boat_yard">Boat yard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthloo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Boat yard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> runs a "boat park" at Porthloo, providing ground storage of boats, including ones too large for storing on-land elsewhere on St Mary\'s.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> There is a wide slipway leading across the beach, partially sheltered by Newford Island, which is used (in addition to the boat park) by landing barges, loading items which the St Mary\'s Harbour quay (at Hugh Town) cannot handle, such as large vehicles and machinery. There are boat maintenance sheds adjacent to the slipway.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_television">In television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthloo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Post_box" title="Post box">postbox</a> at Porthloo Terrace was the starting point in a <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i> challenge to <a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_Races#Car_versus..._challenges" title="Top Gear Races">race the Royal Mail\'s delivery of a letter</a> to <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthloo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150531101628/http://www.islesofscillygolfclub.co.uk/index">"Isles of Scilly Golf Club"</a>.
