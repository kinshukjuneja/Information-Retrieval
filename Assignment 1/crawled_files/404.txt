prospects in <a href="/wiki/Minor_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor league baseball">minor league baseball</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arizona_Fall_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Each August, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> clubs hold a position draft to determine the players who will go to Arizona.<sup id="cite_ref-AboutHistory_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHistory-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Most are Double-A and Triple-A Minor League players.<sup id="cite_ref-AboutHistory_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHistory-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Each club can opt to send two players below Double-A. Foreign players are allowed, as long as the player is not on his native country\'s primary protected player list.  The league is designed for these prospects to refine their skills and perform in game settings in front of major and minor league baseball scouts and team executives, who are in attendance at almost every game. Play begins shortly after the end of MLB\'s regular season in early October and ends in mid-November with a championship game between the winners of the East and West divisions.\n</p><p>The Arizona Fall League teams are each affiliated with five separate Major League teams; each affiliated Major League team provides seven players who team up to fill the 35-man roster of each team.<sup id="cite_ref-AboutHistory_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHistory-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, teams may elect to send <a href="/wiki/Taxi_squad" class="mw-redirect" title="Taxi squad">Taxi Squad</a> players who are only eligible on Wednesdays and Saturdays. MLB teams also provide managers, coaches, and trainers.<sup id="cite_ref-AboutHistory_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AboutHistory-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_players">Notable players</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arizona_Fall_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable players">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A number of famous ballplayers have had stints in the Arizona Fall League. In 1994, the league got worldwide attention as <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jordan" title="Michael Jordan">Michael Jordan</a>\'s second professional baseball league, when he played for the <a href="/wiki/Scottsdale_Scorpions" title="Scottsdale Scorpions">Scottsdale Scorpions</a>. Likewise, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Tebow" title="Tim Tebow">Tim Tebow</a> played for the Scorpions during his transition to professional baseball.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Other notable ballplayers who have played in the Arizona Fall League include <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jeter" title="Derek Jeter">Derek Jeter</a>, <a
