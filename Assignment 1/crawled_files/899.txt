rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.ziggogo.tv">Watch live</a> (Netherlands only)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>BBC Two</b> is the second flagship <a href="/wiki/Television_channel" title="Television channel">television channel</a> of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">British Broadcasting Corporation</a> in the United Kingdom, <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>. It covers a wide range of subject matter, but tends to broadcast more "<a href="/wiki/Highbrow" title="Highbrow">highbrow</a>" programmes than the more mainstream and popular <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>. Like the BBC\'s other domestic TV and radio channels, it is funded by the <a href="/wiki/Television_licence" title="Television licence">television licence</a>, and is therefore free of commercial advertising. It is a comparatively well-funded <a href="/wiki/Public_broadcasting" title="Public broadcasting">public-service</a> network, regularly attaining a much higher audience share than most public-service networks worldwide.\n</p><p>Originally styled <b>BBC2</b>, it was the third British television station to be launched (starting on 21 April 1964), and from 1 July 1967, Europe\'s first television channel to broadcast regularly in colour. It was envisaged as a home for less mainstream and more ambitious programming, and while this tendency has continued to date, most special-interest programmes of a kind previously broadcast on BBC Two, for example the <a href="/wiki/The_Proms" title="The Proms">BBC Proms</a>, now tend to appear on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a> instead.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch">Launch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Launch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>British television at the time of BBC2\'s launch consisted of two channels: the <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC Television Service</a> and the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> network made up of smaller regional companies. Both channels had existed in a state of competition since ITV\'s launch in 1955, and both had aimed for a populist approach in response. The 1962 <a href="/wiki/Pilkington_Committee_on_Broadcasting" title="Pilkington Committee on Broadcasting">Pilkington Report</a> on the future of broadcasting noticed this, and that ITV lacked any serious programming. It therefore decided that Britain\'s third television station should be awarded to the BBC.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Prior to its launch, the new BBC2 was promoted on the BBC Television Service: the soon to be renamed <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC1</a>. The animated adverts featured the campaign mascots "Hullabaloo", a mother <a href="/wiki/Kangaroo" title="Kangaroo">kangaroo</a>, and "Custard", her <a href="/wiki/Joey_(marsupial)" class="mw-redirect" title="Joey (marsupial)">joey</a>. Prior to, and several years after, the channel\'s formal launch, the channel broadcast "<a href="/wiki/Trade_test_colour_films" title="Trade test colour films">Trade Test Transmissions</a>", short films made externally by companies such as <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Shell</a> and <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>, which served to enable engineers to test reception, but became <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult</a> viewing.\n</p><p>The channel was scheduled to begin at 19:20 on 20 April 1964, showing an evening of light entertainment, starting with the comedy show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Alberts" title="The Alberts">The Alberts</a></i>, a performance from <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> comedian <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Raikin" title="Arkady Raikin">Arkady Raikin</a>, and a production of <a href="/wiki/Cole_Porter" title="Cole Porter">Cole Porter</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_Me,_Kate" title="Kiss Me, Kate">Kiss Me, Kate</a></i>, culminating with a fireworks display. However, at around 18:45 a huge power failure, originating from a fire at <a href="/wiki/Battersea_Power_Station" title="Battersea Power Station">Battersea Power Station</a>, caused <a href="/wiki/BBC_Television_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Television Centre">Television Centre</a>, and indeed much of west London, to lose all power. BBC1 was able to continue broadcasting via its facilities at <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_Palace" title="Alexandra Palace">Alexandra Palace</a>, but all attempts to show the scheduled programmes on the new channel failed. <a href="/wiki/Associated-Rediffusion" title="Associated-Rediffusion">Associated-Rediffusion</a>, the London weekday ITV franchise-holder, offered to transmit on the BBC\'s behalf, but their gesture was rejected. At 22:00 programming was officially postponed until the following morning. As the BBC\'s news centre at Alexandra Palace was unaffected, they did in fact broadcast brief bulletins on BBC2 that evening, beginning with an announcement by the newsreader <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Priestland" title="Gerald Priestland">Gerald Priestland</a> at around 19:25.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There was believed to be no recording made of this bulletin, but a videotape was discovered in early 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_Launch_BBC_news_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2_Launch_BBC_news-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By 11:00 on 21 April, power had been restored to the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Television_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Television Centre">studios</a> and programming began, thus making <i><a href="/wiki/Play_School_(UK_TV_series)" title="Play School (UK TV series)">Play School</a></i> the first programme to be shown officially on the channel. The launch schedule, postponed from the night before, was then successfully shown that evening, albeit with minor changes. In reference to the power cut, the transmission opened with a shot of a lit candle which was then sarcastically blown out by presenter <a href="/wiki/Denis_Tuohy" title="Denis Tuohy">Denis Tuohy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_Launch_BBC_news_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2_Launch_BBC_news-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>To establish the new channel\'s identity and draw viewers to it, the BBC decided that a widely promoted, lavish series would be essential in its earliest days. The production chosen was <i><a href="/wiki/The_Forsyte_Saga_(1967_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Forsyte Saga (1967 series)">The Forsyte Saga</a></i> (1967), a no-expense-spared adaptation of the novels by <a href="/wiki/John_Galsworthy" title="John Galsworthy">John Galsworthy</a>, featuring well-established actors <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_More" title="Kenneth More">Kenneth More</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Porter" title="Eric Porter">Eric Porter</a>. Critically for the future of the fledgling channel, the BBC\'s gamble was hugely successful, with an average of six million viewers tuning in per episode: a feat made more prominent by the fact that only 9 million were able to receive the channel at the time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Technological_advancements">Technological advancements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Technological advancements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Unlike <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC1</a> and <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>, BBC2 was broadcast only on the <a href="/wiki/576i" title="576i">625 line</a> <a href="/wiki/Ultra_high_frequency" title="Ultra high frequency">UHF</a> system, so was not available to viewers still using sets on the <a href="/wiki/405-line_television_system" title="405-line television system">405-line</a> <a href="/wiki/Very_high_frequency" title="Very high frequency">VHF</a> system. This created a market for dual standard receivers which could switch between the two systems. Set manufacturers ramped up production of UHF sets in anticipation of a large market demand for the new BBC2, but the market did not materialise. The early technical problems, which included being unable to transmit US-recorded videotapes due to a lack of <a href="/wiki/Television_standards_conversion" title="Television standards conversion">system conversion</a> from the US <a href="/wiki/NTSC" title="NTSC">NTSC</a> system, were resolved by a committee headed by <a href="/wiki/James_Redmond_(broadcaster)" title="James Redmond (broadcaster)">James Redmond</a>.\n</p><p>On 1 July 1967, during the <a href="/wiki/1967_Wimbledon_Championships" title="1967 Wimbledon Championships">Wimbledon Championships</a>, BBC2 became the first channel in Europe to begin regular broadcasts in colour, using the <a href="/wiki/PAL" title="PAL">PAL</a> system.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The thirteen part series <i><a href="/wiki/Civilisation:_A_Personal_View" class="mw-redirect" title="Civilisation: A Personal View">Civilisation</a></i> (1969) was created as a celebration of two millennia of western art and culture to showpiece the new colour technology.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> BBC1 and ITV later joined BBC2 on 625-line UHF band, but continued to simulcast on 405-line VHF until 1985. BBC1 and ITV simultaneously introduced PAL colour on UHF on 15 November 1969, although they both had broadcast some programmes in colour "unofficially" since September 1969.\n</p><p>In 1979, the station adopted the first computer-generated channel identification (ident) in Britain, with its use of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_%27Computer_Generated_2%27_ident" title="BBC Two &#39;Computer Generated 2&#39; ident">double striped, orange \'2\' logo</a>. The ident, created in house by BBC engineers, lasted until March 1986 and heralded the start of computer-generated logos.\n</p><p>As the <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television_in_the_United_Kingdom#Digital_switchover" title="Digital terrestrial television in the United Kingdom">switch to digital-only terrestrial transmission</a> progressed, BBC Two was (in each region in turn) the first <a href="/wiki/Analogue_terrestrial_television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Analogue terrestrial television in the United Kingdom">analogue TV channel</a> to be replaced with the BBC multiplex, at first four, then two weeks ahead of the other four channels. This was required for those relay transmitters that had no current <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a> service giving viewers time to purchase the equipment, unless they had already selected a satellite or cable service. The last region for BBC Two to end on analogue terrestrial television was <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> on 10 October 2012.\n</p><p>At the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_International_Television_Festival" title="Edinburgh International Television Festival">Guardian Edinburgh International Television Festival</a>, BBC Two was named "Terrestrial Channel of the Year".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high-definition</a> simulcast of BBC Two began broadcasting on 26 March 2013, replacing the standalone <a href="/wiki/BBC_HD" title="BBC HD">BBC HD</a> channel.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Two_HD-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operation">Operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_television_programmes_broadcast_by_the_BBC" title="List of television programmes broadcast by the BBC">List of television programmes broadcast by the BBC</a></div>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>BBC Two\'s remit is to be a mixed-genre channel appealing to a broad adult audience with programmes of depth and substance. It should carry the greatest amount and range of <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_building" title="Knowledge building">knowledge building</a> programming of any BBC television channel, complemented by distinctive comedy, drama and arts programming.</p><div class="templatequotecite">\xe2\x80\x94&#8201;<cite><b>BBC Two remit</b><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Two_Remit_2011_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Two_Remit_2011-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></cite></div>\n</blockquote>\n<p>BBC Two\'s remit historically was one screening programmes targeting the arts, culture, some comedy and drama, and appealing to audiences not already served by BBC One or ITV. Over its first thirty or so years the channel developed a reputation for screening highly praised and prestigious drama series, among these <i><a href="/wiki/Boys_from_the_Blackstuff" title="Boys from the Blackstuff">Boys from the Blackstuff</a></i> (1982) or 1996\'s, critically acclaimed <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Friends_in_the_North" title="Our Friends in the North">Our Friends in the North</a></i>. The channel\'s "highbrow" profile is also in part attributable to a long history of demanding documentaries of all types, beginning with <i><a href="/wiki/Civilisation:_A_Personal_View" class="mw-redirect" title="Civilisation: A Personal View">Civilisation</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ascent_of_Man" title="The Ascent of Man">The Ascent of Man</a></i> in the 1960s. Like the early <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>, BBC Two also established for itself a reputation as a champion of independent and international cinema, under the <i>Screen 2</i> brand.\n</p><p>The channel has sometimes been judged, increasingly in more recent years, to have moved away from this original role and to have moved closer to the mainstream. Since the launch of the digital-only <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a>, the BBC has been accused in particular of shifting its more highbrow output to the new channel, which, until the end of the UK\'s digital TV switchover in October 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> a minority (7.5% in the final quarter of 2010)<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> of viewers did not receive. BBC Four\'s remit is very similar to that of the earlier remit of BBC2, and contains many documentaries and arts programmes. It has been perceived by some that this strategy is to allow BBC Two to show more popular programmes and to secure higher ratings.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Since 2004 there have been some signs of an attempt to return closer to parts of BBC Two\'s earlier output with the arts strand <i><a href="/wiki/The_Culture_Show" title="The Culture Show">The Culture Show</a></i>. Its most popular programme at the moment is <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i>.\n</p><p>Much of BBC Two\'s output has previously or subsequently been shown on other channels. Some of these programmes are repeats of popular or flagship programmes from BBC Four in a late night strand, originally called <i>BBC Four on Two</i> but now unbranded, for the benefit of audiences without access to BBC Four. Other programmes are moved to the channel as a result of their success on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Three_(former)" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Three (former)">BBC Three</a> or Four, so that subsequent series are well received. An example of this is the BBC Three series <i><a href="/wiki/Torchwood" title="Torchwood">Torchwood</a></i> that was transferred to the channel following the success of the first series. BBC Two is also used as a testing ground for programmes prior to their moving to the flagship <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>: such examples include <i><a href="/wiki/Have_I_Got_News_for_You" title="Have I Got News for You">Have I Got News for You</a></i> and popular comedies <i><a href="/wiki/Absolutely_Fabulous" title="Absolutely Fabulous">Absolutely Fabulous</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Miranda_(TV_series)" title="Miranda (TV series)">Miranda</a></i>, which moved to BBC One after success on Two. Also in August 2014 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_British_Bake_Off" title="The Great British Bake Off">The Great British Bake Off</a></i> moved to BBC One due to its success the previous year on BBC Two.\n</p><p>Another founding part of BBC Two was to provide educational and community programming on the BBC, as part of its public service remit. The educational section of this commitment saw BBC2 broadcast a large amount of programming for the <a href="/wiki/Open_University" title="Open University">Open University</a>, who co-produced programming with the corporation, and saw the channel broadcast <a href="/wiki/BBC_Schools" title="BBC Schools">BBC Schools</a> programmes from 1983 until the programmes were transferred to the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Learning_Zone" title="BBC Learning Zone">BBC Learning Zone</a> in 2010.\n</p><p>As a result of the channel\'s commitment to community broadcasting, the channel produced the symbolic <i><a href="/wiki/Open_Space_(BBC_TV)" class="mw-redirect" title="Open Space (BBC TV)">Open Space</a></i> series, a strand developed in the early 1970s in which members of the public would be allotted half an hour of television time, and given a level of editorial and technical training in order to produce for themselves a film on an issue most important to them. BBC2\'s <a href="/wiki/Community_Programme_Unit" title="Community Programme Unit">Community Programme Unit</a> kept this aspect of the channel\'s tradition alive into the 1990s in the form of <i>Video Diaries</i> and later <i><a href="/wiki/Video_Nation" title="Video Nation">Video Nation</a></i>. The Community Programmes Unit was disbanded in 2004.\n</p><p>Since January 2013, BBC Two stopped showing children\'s programmes and replaced the weekday morning schedule with repeats of the previous BBC One daytime schedule. They also started showing <i>Sign Zone</i> in the early hours, which BBC One used to show before 2013.\n</p><p>From October 2013, BBC Two has shown classic programmes like <i><a href="/wiki/Bergerac_(TV_series)" title="Bergerac (TV series)">Bergerac</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cagney_and_Lacey" class="mw-redirect" title="Cagney and Lacey">Cagney and Lacey</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rockford_Files" title="The Rockford Files">The Rockford Files</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/%27Allo_%27Allo!" title="&#39;Allo &#39;Allo!">\'Allo \'Allo!</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Are_You_Being_Served%3F" title="Are You Being Served?">Are You Being Served?</a></i> on weekday afternoons, with the retro logos from 1970s and 1980s, between the current programmes.\n</p><p>In October 2014, <i><a href="/wiki/Russell_Howard%27s_Good_News" title="Russell Howard&#39;s Good News">Russell Howard\'s Good News</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Backchat_(2013_TV_series)" title="Backchat (2013 TV series)">Backchat</a></i> moved to BBC Two from BBC Three.\n</p><p>In 2014, BBC Two commissioned Britain\'s first transgender sitcom, <i><a href="/wiki/Boy_Meets_Girl_(BBC_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Boy Meets Girl (BBC TV series)">Boy Meets Girl</a></i>, which follows the developing relationship between Leo, a 26-year-old man and Judy, a 40-year-old transgender woman.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 7 April 2015, the morning <i>Sign Zone</i> was shown before <i><a href="/wiki/Victoria_Derbyshire" title="Victoria Derbyshire">Victoria Derbyshire</a></i> between 8:00am-9:00&#160;am including <i>See Hear</i> on Wednesday morning.\n</p><p>Since 2017, it has shown the UK selection show for the Eurovision Song Contest, <i>Eurovision: You Decide</i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presentation">Presentation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Presentation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_BBC_television_idents" title="History of BBC television idents">History of BBC television idents</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BBC_Two_Paint_ident.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/31/BBC_Two_Paint_ident.jpg/220px-BBC_Two_Paint_ident.jpg" Several idents from this set were reinstated in July 2014 with the teal box logo.</div></div></div>\n<p>As well as programmes, BBC Two has also proved memorable for its numerous <a href="/wiki/Station_identification" title="Station identification">idents</a>\xe2\x80\x94various short sequences shown between programme junctions that serve as the channel identity. Nearly all of the identity packages used since the channel\'s launch in 1964 have featured a prominent numeral <i>2</i> in the design. Notable designs include the electronic <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_%27Computer_Generated_2%27_ident" title="BBC Two &#39;Computer Generated 2&#39; ident">double-striped <i>2</i></a>, the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_%27Two%27_ident" title="BBC Two &#39;Two&#39; ident">white <i>TWO</i></a> ident (the only ident not to use a numeral <i>2</i>), and most notably the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_%271991%E2%80%932001%27_idents" title="BBC Two &#39;1991\xe2\x80\x932001&#39; idents">1991 <i>2</i>s</a>.\n</p><p>The 1991 idents featured a <a href="/wiki/Sans-serif" title="Sans-serif">sans-serif</a> numeral <i>2</i> at the centre of an initially art-related scene; however the idents moved away from this style as the station\'s style changed. Although highly praised, this expansive set of idents was ended in November 2001. The <a href="/wiki/Logo_of_the_BBC" title="Logo of the BBC">BBC corporate logo</a> was updated within the idents in October 1997, though the idents moved away from the original <a href="/wiki/Viridian" title="Viridian">viridian</a> colour scheme in these latter years. The <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_%27Personality%27_idents" title="BBC Two &#39;Personality&#39; idents">subsequent presentation style</a> was introduced on 19 November 2001 and kept the same figure <i>2</i>, but in a yellow background and given a personality. At the time, BBC Two became the first BBC channel to feature a box logo.\n</p><p>In 2007, BBC Two debuted the new theme, a "Window on the World", with the <i>2</i> numeral providing that view. Introduced on 18 February 2007, the new look also had the channel adopt a teal-coloured box logo, featuring the BBC logo above the word <i>TWO</i>, in the font <a href="/wiki/Avenir_(typeface)" title="Avenir (typeface)">Avenir</a>.\n</p><p>In 2014, in honour of the channel\'s 50th anniversary, some of the 1990s idents were re-introduced; from 2015, BBC Two Northern Ireland opted to use nearly forty idents from the 1991\xe2\x80\x932001 set.\n</p><p>On 27 September 2018, the 1991-2001 idents were retired once again and BBC Two introduced a new set of idents, based on scenes incorporating a curve motif resembling the number 2. The new branding is designed to reflect BBC Two\'s "constant evolution, constant eclecticism, [and] constant sense of quality". The new idents are produced by various artists and studios, including <a href="/wiki/Aardman_Animations" title="Aardman Animations">Aardman Animations</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Mill_(company)" title="The Mill (company)">The Mill</a> and others. The new identity was co-developed by BBC Creative and Superunion.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_variations">Regional variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Regional variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>BBC Two also has regional variations in the nations: <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_Scotland" title="BBC Two Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_Wales" title="BBC Two Wales">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_Northern_Ireland" title="BBC Two Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. These versions of BBC Two share the same idents, but with the nation name in the BBC Two box. BBC Two Scotland shows a lot of specifically Scottish programming on the channel, as well as its sister channel <a href="/wiki/BBC_One_Scotland" title="BBC One Scotland">BBC One Scotland</a>, and the schedules are often mixed around to match. BBC Two Northern Ireland and BBC Two Wales both have the option to opt out of the main network schedule, but generally stick to it, only opting out a couple of times each week. Until December 2008, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Wales">BBC Wales</a> broadcast a special, digital-only channel, <a href="/wiki/BBC_2W" title="BBC 2W">BBC 2W</a>, which contained more opt-outs than analogue-only BBC Two Wales. <a href="/wiki/BBC_Scotland" title="BBC Scotland">BBC Scotland</a> occasionally broadcast Gaelic-language programmes under the banner <a href="/wiki/BBC_Alba" title="BBC Alba">BBC Two Alba</a>.\n</p><p>In England, many of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_English_regions" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC English regions">BBC English regions</a> were combined to form "super-regions", such as the entire North or Midlands. These had the option to opt out of the network programming on the analogue feed, and replace it with local programming. However this was usually only done in exceptional circumstances, as all regular regional programming has been transferred to BBC One, and the English regions are not available on digital on BBC Two. There is no specific "BBC Two England"; this role is fulfilled by the network BBC Two.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Availability_outside_the_UK">Availability outside the UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Availability outside the UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>BBC Two is widely available in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> on satellite and <a href="/wiki/Cable_television_in_the_Republic_of_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Cable television in the Republic of Ireland">cable</a>, as well as being received directly in areas bordering <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, or in coastal areas from <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. It is also available on cable and <a href="/wiki/IPTV" title="IPTV">IPTV</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, Belgium, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein" title="Liechtenstein">Liechtenstein</a>. On 27 March 2013, it began being carried by <a href="/wiki/BFBS" class="mw-redirect" title="BFBS">British Forces Broadcasting Service (BFBS)</a> to members of HM Forces and their families around the world, replacing the <a href="/wiki/BFBS#BFBS_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="BFBS">BFBS2</a> TV channel, which already carried a selection of BBC Two programmes.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It shares a channel with <a href="/wiki/CBBC" title="CBBC">CBBC</a>, which broadcasts from early morning until the early evening.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accessibility">Accessibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Accessibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The BBC announced in May 2008 that it had achieved its aim for all programming to have <a href="/wiki/Subtitles" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtitles">subtitles</a> for viewers with hearing difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> These are available on the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Red_Button" title="BBC Red Button">BBC Red Button</a>, and until 23 October 2012, via the <a href="/wiki/Ceefax" title="Ceefax">Ceefax</a> teletext service.\n</p><p>The BBC also offers <a href="/wiki/Audio_description" title="Audio description">audio description</a> on some popular programmes<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> for visually impaired-viewers as well as <a href="/wiki/Sign_language" title="Sign language">sign language</a> interpretation on some of its programmes for <a href="/wiki/Deaf" class="mw-redirect" title="Deaf">deaf</a> and hard-of-hearing viewers. The percentage of the BBC\'s total television output with audio description available is 10%, having been increased from 8% in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="BBC_Two_HD">BBC Two HD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Two&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: BBC Two HD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BBC_Two_HD.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/05/BBC_Two_HD.svg/200px-BBC_Two_HD.svg.png" width="200" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/05/BBC_Two_HD.svg/300px-BBC_Two_HD.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/05/BBC_Two_HD.svg/400px-BBC_Two_HD.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="146" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BBC_Two_HD.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>BBC Two HD logo</div></div></div>\n<p>Originally, programmes from BBC Two were shown in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high definition</a> on the dedicated <a href="/wiki/BBC_HD" title="BBC HD">BBC HD</a> channel, alongside programmes from <a href="/wiki/BBC_Three_(former)" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Three (former)">BBC Three</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a>, as well as some select series from <a href="/wiki/CBBC" title="CBBC">CBBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/CBeebies" title="CBeebies">CBeebies</a>. However, in plans outlined by the director general <a href="/wiki/Mark_Thompson_(television_executive)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mark Thompson (television executive)">Mark Thompson</a> on 6 October 2011, BBC HD would close to be replaced by <i>BBC Two HD</i>, a high-definition simulcast of BBC Two that would work much the same way as <a href="/wiki/BBC_One#BBC_One_HD" title="BBC One">BBC One HD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> This move allowed the corporation to save \xc2\xa32.1&#160;million, used to count towards its budget deficit following the freezing of the licence fee and the additional financial responsibility of addition services.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 19 February 2013, it was announced that BBC Two HD would replace BBC HD from 6.05&#160;am on 26 March 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Two_HD_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Two_HD-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Channel numbers for the BBC\'s HD channels also changed on Sky, to allow BBC One HD and BBC Two HD to sit side-by-side on channels 141, and 142 respectively on the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_program_guide" title="Electronic program guide">EPG</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Two_HD_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Two_HD-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 16 July 2013, the BBC indicated that it wants to launch Northern Irish, Scottish and Welsh variations of BBC Two HD; however, this would require the approval of the BBC Trust, with a proposal due to be presented within six months.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 10 December 2013, BBC Two HD was swapped with the SD channel in England on Sky\'s EPG for HD subscribers.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
