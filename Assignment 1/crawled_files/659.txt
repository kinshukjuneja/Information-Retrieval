id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\n\n<b>Colonel Sidney Godolphin</b> (1652&#8211;1732) of Thames Ditton, Surrey was a British Army officer and politician who sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons" title="House of Commons">House of Commons</a> for over 40 years (non-continously) between 1685 and 1737.\n</p><p>Godolphin came from an ancient <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornish</a> family and was born the only surviving son of John Godolphin, judge of the Admiralty court, of St. Thomas\xe2\x80\x99s, near Launceston, Cornwall and St. James\xe2\x80\x99s, Clerkenwell.  \n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Baron_Godolphin" title="Baron Godolphin">Godolphin</a> family had held a long lease of <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Scilly</a> since the late 16th century. He was Lieutenant-governor of The Isles of Scilly from 1690 and <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Scilly Isles">Governor</a> for life from 1700. He was also Auditor of the Revenues in Wales.  \n</p><p>Godolphin was returned as <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Helston_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Helston (UK Parliament constituency)">Helston</a> in 1685 and held the seat until 1687. <sup id="cite_ref-HOP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was returned as MP for <a href="/wiki/Penryn_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Penryn (UK Parliament constituency)">Penryn</a> in 1690 and held the seat until 1695. In 1698  he was elected again as MP for Helston which he held until 1713. He was also returned at <a href="/wiki/West_Looe_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="West Looe (UK Parliament constituency)">West Looe</a> in 1702 but chose to stay at Helston.<sup id="cite_ref-HOP2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOP2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He regained the seat at Helston in 1715 but in 1722 changed to <a href="/wiki/St_Mawes_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="St Mawes (UK Parliament constituency)">St Mawes</a>. In 1727 he was returned instead for <a href="/wiki/St_Germans_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="St Germans (UK Parliament constituency)">St Germans</a> which he held until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-HOP3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOP3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He served in Parliament long enough to become \'<a href="/wiki/Father_of_the_House" title="Father of the House">Father of the House</a>\'.\n</p><p>The Assessment Book of <a href="/wiki/Thames_Ditton" title="Thames Ditton">Thames Ditton</a> showing assessments for the relief of the poor gives the Colonel\'s address as \'Ditton Street\' &#8212; but it was probably Forde\'s Farm (later known as <a href="/wiki/Boyle_Farm" class="mw-redirect" title="Boyle Farm">Boyle Farm</a>):\n</p>\n<dl><dd>"He spent his time in arms till his state of health, requiring ease and quiet, made him quit a military life, but not the service of his Country, which he represented in Parliament near fifty years, and died Father of the House.  It was his felicity to be valued and countenanced by his Sovereign, esteemed and loved by his friends and relations..."</dd></dl>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Private_life">Private life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sidney_Godolphin_(colonel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Private life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin died at the age of 81 in 1732.  He has a portrait-bust on his memorial, erected by his youngest daughter Ellen, in The Lady Chapel of <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St._Nicholas,_Thames_Ditton" class="mw-redirect" title="Church of St. Nicholas, Thames Ditton">St Nicholas Church</a>.\n</p><p>He was married to Susannah Tannat; they had one son: \n</p>\n<ul><li>Tannat Godolphin</li></ul>\n<p>and five daughters\n</p>\n<ul><li><ul><li>Margaret</li>\n<li>Mary</li>\n<li>Penelope, who married Francis Hoblyn</li>\n<li>Elizabeth</li>\n<li>Ellen</li></ul></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sidney_Godolphin_(colonel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-HOP-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-HOP_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.historyofparliamentonline.org/volume/1660-1690/member/godolphin-sidney-ii-1652-1732">"GODOLPHIN, Sidney II (1652-1732), of Broniarth, Mont. and Thames Ditton, Surr"</a>.
