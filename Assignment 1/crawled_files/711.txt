scope="row">Parent(s)</th><td>\nThomas Coulthard<br />Elizabeth Fleming</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>George Coulthard</b> (1 August 1856 \xe2\x80\x93 22 October 1883) was an Australian <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricketer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules footballer</a>.\n</p><p>Born and raised on a farm outside <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>, Coulthard led the <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Football_Club" title="Carlton Football Club">Carlton Football Club</a> to premiership success in the fledgling <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Football_League" title="Victorian Football League">Victorian Football Association</a> (VFA), and was a key member of the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Australian_rules_football_team" title="Victoria Australian rules football team">Victorian side</a> that dominated the first <a href="/wiki/Interstate_matches_in_Australian_rules_football" title="Interstate matches in Australian rules football">intercolonial matches</a>. A fast, versatile and highly skilled footballer, Coulthard was, in the opinion of many of his contemporaries, the greatest player yet seen in the Australian game. However, his football career ended in controversy in 1882 when he received a season-long suspension\xe2\x80\x94then the harshest punishment ever handed down by the VFA\xe2\x80\x94for brawling and using "bad language" during play. Recognised today as the game\'s "first bona fide superstar", he was an inaugural inductee into the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="Australian Football Hall of Fame">Australian Football Hall of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECardosi2014_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECardosi2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports" title="Professional sports">professional</a> cricketer, he played at club level for <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cricket_Club" title="Melbourne Cricket Club">Melbourne</a>, represented <a href="/wiki/Victoria_cricket_team" title="Victoria cricket team">Victoria</a> in five <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Intercolonial_cricket_in_Australia" title="Intercolonial cricket in Australia">intercolonial matches</a>, and made one <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test</a> appearance for <a href="/wiki/Australian_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian cricket team">Australia</a>, against <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="English cricket team">England</a> in 1882. He also umpired some of the earliest Tests, and is perhaps best known in cricket for instigating <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Riot_of_1879" title="Sydney Riot of 1879">the sport\'s first international riot</a> when he dismissed <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_cricket_team" title="New South Wales cricket team">New South Wales</a> batsman <a href="/wiki/Billy_Murdoch" title="Billy Murdoch">Billy Murdoch</a> during an 1879 match in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> against <a href="/wiki/George_Harris,_4th_Baron_Harris" title="George Harris, 4th Baron Harris">Lord Harris</a>\' touring <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_and_New_Zealand_in_1878%E2%80%9379" title="English cricket team in Australia and New Zealand in 1878\xe2\x80\x9379">English team</a>. Coulthard was co-officiating the game with <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Barton" title="Edmund Barton">Edmund Barton</a>, later the first <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister of Australia</a>.\n</p><p>Coulthard\'s sporting exploits made him a household name throughout the Australian colonies.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Off the field, he ran a tobacco and sporting goods store in <a href="/wiki/Lygon_Street,_Melbourne" title="Lygon Street, Melbourne">Lygon Street</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlton,_Victoria" title="Carlton, Victoria">Carlton</a>, and was involved in several notable events: he survived a shark attack in <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="Sydney Harbour">Sydney Harbour</a>, fought bare-knuckle boxing champion <a href="/wiki/Jem_Mace" title="Jem Mace">Jem "The Gypsy" Mace</a>, and, based on a dream he allegedly had, convinced thousands of punters to back a horse with long odds in the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cup" title="Melbourne Cup">Melbourne Cup</a> (the horse finished close to last). Coulthard is also credited with being Australian football\'s first "<a href="/wiki/Umpire_(Australian_rules_football)#Attire" title="Umpire (Australian rules football)">man in white</a>", for he umpired an 1880 match in the now-traditional all-white uniform to stand out from the players. He died of <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a>, aged 27, after suffering from the illness for over a year. He was survived by his wife and baby daughter.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div Carlton Imperial, a junior side then referred to as the "training establishment" of the senior <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Football_Club" title="Carlton Football Club">Carlton Football Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2012108_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2012108-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He proved to be a match-winner for the Imperials with his goal-kicking, and in 1876, was recruited by Carlton, then a powerhouse of Victorian football.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2012116,_121,_128_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2012116,_121,_128-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Starting off as one of Carlton\'s <a href="/wiki/Follower_(Australian_rules_football)" title="Follower (Australian rules football)">followers</a>, he was described by <i>The Footballer</i> as a "rising and most promising player".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014128Weston2008105_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014128Weston2008105-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Carlton topped the ladder that year and looked to win its fourth premiership in a row, but in the decider against archrivals <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Football_Club" title="Melbourne Football Club">Melbourne</a>, a controversial umpiring call secured the trophy for the latter club.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2012132\xe2\x80\x93134_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2012132\xe2\x80\x93134-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Football_League" title="Victorian Football League">Victorian Football Association</a> (VFA) was established the following year with Carlton as one of its twelve foundation member clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201417_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201417-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Despite switching between attacking and defensive positions during the <a href="/wiki/1877_VFA_season" title="1877 VFA season">1877 VFA season</a>, Coulthard still managed to rank equal-first on Carlton\'s goal-kicking tally with eight goals, and his elusive dashes with the ball in hand\xe2\x80\x94fully 100 metres up the field at times\xe2\x80\x94became a celebrated aspect of his game:\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>His speciality is, undoubtedly, running with the ball; many are the runs he has made, warding off his opponents with his long, muscular arms. This peculiar style of passing is really a treat to witness, and we may well say that Coulthard is unequalled at it, being a custom almost his own.</p><div class="templatequotecite">\xe2\x80\x94&#8201;<cite><i><a href="/wiki/The_Australasian" class="mw-redirect" title="The Australasian">The Australasian</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings20143132,_233_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings20143132,_233-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></cite></div>\n</blockquote>\n<p>Mid-season, Carlton pioneered <a href="/wiki/Interstate_matches_in_Australian_rules_football" title="Interstate matches in Australian rules football">intercolonial football</a> in Australia when it travelled north to <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> to take on the Waratahs, a local <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby</a> club, in two matches: one under rugby rules, the other under Australian rules. As was expected, each club won the match played by its own rules, and Coulthard was appraised as one of two Carlton footballers who adapted best to rugby.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201422_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201422-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The clubs met again in Melbourne, repeating the code switch. Carlton won playing Australian rules and claimed a 1\xe2\x80\x931 draw in the rugby match in defiance of the opposing team\'s umpire, who disputed their goal; the Waratahs eventually allowed it under protest in order for the game to continue. Coulthard stood out in both fixtures<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and briefly joined the Waratahs to play rugby. He quickly dominated at the sport, scoring all five goals and four <a href="/wiki/Try" title="Try">tries</a> in his second and last game for the club.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Although he appeared in only two of the Waratahs\' twelve rugby matches in 1877, Coulthard scored more goals than any other member that season, and tied equal first in tries.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was remembered decades later for "[showing] the Rugby men how their game should be played".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shark_attack">Shark attack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shark attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg/220px-Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg/330px-Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5c/Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg 2x" data-file-width="414" data-file-height="235" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sydney_Harbour_Sedgfield.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The waters near <a href="/wiki/Shark_Island_(Port_Jackson)" title="Shark Island (Port Jackson)">Shark Island</a> where the shark attack took place.</div></div></div>\n<p>Following the matches against Carlton, the Waratahs adopted Australian rules, and for a time, the colonial game threatened to supersede rugby in Sydney.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERichards2011186_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERichards2011186-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard accepted an invitation from Phil Sheridan, a trustee of the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Cricket_Ground" title="Sydney Cricket Ground">Sydney Cricket Ground</a>, to travel to Sydney with the aim of fostering Australian rules in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEField195727_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEField195727-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 15 September 1877, soon after his arrival, Coulthard joined several local footballers on a fishing trip in <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="Sydney Harbour">Sydney Harbour</a>. The group was anchored near <a href="/wiki/Shark_Island_(Port_Jackson)" title="Shark Island (Port Jackson)">Shark Island</a> when Coulthard, sitting on the boat\'s <a href="/wiki/Gunwale" title="Gunwale">gunwale</a> with the back of his tailcoat hanging over the side, was pulled overboard by "a monster shark, 13 feet long". The shark had seized his coattails trailing on the water and dragged him beneath the surface "some ten or twelve feet" until it tore the coat from his body. After kicking at the shark, Coulthard swam to the surface alongside the boat, "into which he threw, with the aid of his friends, a kind of somersault, just about as quickly as he had been taken overboard."<sup id="cite_ref-thrill_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thrill-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The incident was said to be "one of the most marvellous escapes from a fearful death on record" and "probably without parallel in Australian waters".<sup id="cite_ref-thrill_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thrill-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Shaken by the event, Coulthard returned to Melbourne within a week, where he resumed playing for Carlton.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The club denied widely circulated rumours that it had lured its star player back with financial incentives, stating that Coulthard returned due to a falling out with his associates in Sydney.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Nonetheless, the shark entered sporting folklore as the reason why Coulthard abandoned his plans in Sydney, which, as the story goes, kept Australian rules from becoming the city\'s most popular football code.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEField195727_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEField195727-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-shark_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shark-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Champion_of_Victoria">Champion of Victoria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Champion of Victoria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg/220px-Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg" width="220" height="104" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg/330px-Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg/440px-Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg 2x" data-file-width="647" data-file-height="305" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Intercolonial_Football_Match_1879.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coulthard was declared best-on-ground in the first <a href="/wiki/Interstate_matches_in_Australian_rules_football" title="Interstate matches in Australian rules football">intercolonial football</a> match: Victoria v. South Australia, 1879, <a href="/wiki/East_Melbourne_Cricket_Ground" title="East Melbourne Cricket Ground">East Melbourne Cricket Ground</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Back in Victoria, in the lead-up to the final match of 1877, against Melbourne, Carlton was already acknowledged as having won the premiership\xe2\x80\x94its fifth such honour in seven years\xe2\x80\x94based on the results of previous encounters between the two clubs that season. Coulthard was instrumental in maintaining Carlton\'s supremacy and was voted by <i>The Australasian</i> in its end-of-season review as one of the VFA\'s best backline players.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201431\xe2\x80\x9332,_232_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201431\xe2\x80\x9332,_232-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Carlton was considered the best side early on in the <a href="/wiki/1878_VFA_season" title="1878 VFA season">1878 VFA season</a> with Coulthard putting in best-on-ground efforts for the club.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201439\xe2\x80\x9340_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201439\xe2\x80\x9340-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> However, 1878 saw provincial <a href="/wiki/Geelong_Football_Club" title="Geelong Football Club">Geelong</a> develop a dynasty that would dominate the competition well into the 1880s.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201436\xe2\x80\x9337_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201436\xe2\x80\x9337-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard capped off the season with 18 goals, the most of any player that year, and was singled out for his prowess in the ruck.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014280\xe2\x80\x93281_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014280\xe2\x80\x93281-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Coulthard was Carlton\'s best in its first match of the <a href="/wiki/1879_VFA_season" title="1879 VFA season">1879 VFA season</a>, a 4\xe2\x80\x930 win over <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park_Football_Club_(VFA)" title="Albert Park Football Club (VFA)">Albert Park</a> in which he scored a goal after using an innovative dodging tactic that, according to one observer, left his opponents "standing looking on at the cool operation like a lot of demented geese".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201453_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201453-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In July, in Melbourne, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Australian_rules_football_team" title="Victoria Australian rules football team">Victoria</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/South_Australia_Australian_rules_football_team" title="South Australia Australian rules football team">South Australia</a> in the first football contest between two colonies. Coulthard contributed two goals for Victoria in a best-on-ground display, and again led the way when his colony trounced South Australia in the return match a few days later.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201456\xe2\x80\x9356_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201456\xe2\x80\x9356-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cricket_Ground" title="Melbourne Cricket Ground">Melbourne Cricket Ground</a> (MCG) the following month, in the first club football match staged at night under electric lights, Carlton triumphed over Melbourne 3\xe2\x80\x930, two of the goals coming from Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolfo200922\xe2\x80\x9323_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolfo200922\xe2\x80\x9323-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He ended 1879 with a record 21 goals, seven more than the runner-up.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014329_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014329-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> At the conclusion of the season, <i>The Australasian</i> declared:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201463_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201463-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>There can be no two opinions as to who is entitled first mention\xe2\x80\x94George Coulthard, of Carlton. Back, forward, or following, and nowhere out of place, the grandest player of the day, it is doubtful if for general excellence his equal has ever been seen in Victoria.\n</p></blockquote>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg/170px-E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg" width="170" height="244" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg/255px-E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg/340px-E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="689" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:E._A._Prevot_and_George_Coulthard.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coulthard (right) wearing a Carlton guernsey and holding a football with club secretary E. A. Pr\xc3\xa9v\xc3\xb4t, 1880</div></div></div>\n<p>Coulthard kicked all five goals in the first game of the <a href="/wiki/1880_VFA_season" title="1880 VFA season">1880 VFA season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201478_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201478-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Carlton was still undefeated in June when it recorded another victory, against Melbourne, in a <a href="/wiki/Testimonial_match" title="Testimonial match">testimonial match</a> for Coulthard, who complimented the occasion with a best-on-ground effort.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201479\xe2\x80\x9480_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201479\xe2\x80\x9480-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In July, he officiated a match between Melbourne and South Australia\'s touring <a href="/wiki/Norwood_Football_Club" title="Norwood Football Club">Norwood Football Club</a> in the now-traditional all-white umpiring uniform, and is thus recognised as football\'s first "<a href="/wiki/Umpire_(Australian_rules_football)#Attire" title="Umpire (Australian rules football)">man in white</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPiesse1995272_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPiesse1995272-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Later that month, in a major match against reigning premiers Geelong, Coulthard fainted after opponent George "Hercules" Watson felled and injured him behind play. He tried squaring up to Watson but was pulled back by a police constable and taken from the field to recover. The incident failed to stop Carlton from recording an upset victory, ending Geelong\'s 44 match winning streak.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings201483_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings201483-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard finished on top of the goal-kicking ladder for the third consecutive season with 21 goals, and was again recognised as a champion of the colony.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014381\xe2\x80\x93382_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014381\xe2\x80\x93382-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July of the <a href="/wiki/1881_VFA_season" title="1881 VFA season">1881 VFA season</a>, Coulthard, attempting a <a href="/wiki/Mark_(Australian_rules_football)" title="Mark (Australian rules football)">mark</a> in front of Carlton\'s goal, was tackled by a Melbourne opponent and accidentally kicked behind the right ear. It left a wound that required immediate surgical treatment, forcing him to sit out the match.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was still suffering the effects of the injury one week later when he returned to the field to face Geelong.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He struggled during Carlton\'s 1881 tour of <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>, the local press stating that he was manned so persistently due to his footballing reputation that "he does not get the same chance of showing his sterling qualities".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Although not up to his usual standard that year, Coulthard was still acknowledged as one of Victoria\'s best forwards, and secured 18 majors to finish second in the goal-kicking stakes.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014429_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014429-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career-ending_suspension">Career-ending suspension</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Career-ending suspension">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Coulthard was serving as Carlton\'s vice-captain in <a href="/wiki/1882_VFA_season" title="1882 VFA season">1882</a> when events conspired to end his VFA career. During a club training session in April, he clashed with teammate <a href="/wiki/Joey_Tankard" title="Joey Tankard">Joey Tankard</a>, a new recruit who subsequently returned to his original club of <a href="/wiki/North_Melbourne_Football_Club" title="North Melbourne Football Club">Hotham</a>. They reignited their feud in August when Carlton and Hotham met on the <a href="/wiki/East_Melbourne_Cricket_Ground" title="East Melbourne Cricket Ground">East Melbourne Cricket Ground</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014103,_112_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014103,_112-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> With Carlton leading into the second half, Coulthard fell on Tankard in a scrimmage, either accidentally or on purpose "with both fists shut", depending on the eyewitness account.<sup id="cite_ref-tribunal_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tribunal-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Upon rising, Tankard struck Coulthard in the face, who returned punches in kind and used "foul language" before other players broke up the fight.<sup id="cite_ref-tribunal_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tribunal-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The crowd then <a href="/wiki/Pitch_invasion" title="Pitch invasion">invaded the pitch</a> and an eruption of mob violence seemed imminent until the police intervened.<sup id="cite_ref-gero_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gero-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sportsman_(1865_newspaper)" title="The Sportsman (1865 newspaper)">The Sportsman</a></i> called it "one of the most disgraceful affairs witnessed on a football field".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014112_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014112-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Later in the match, Coulthard challenged Tankard to a fight in the pavilion, but he refused.<sup id="cite_ref-gero_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gero-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg/220px-Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg/330px-Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg/440px-Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2700" data-file-height="1800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Young_and_Jacksons_Melbourne.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coulthard received his suspension at the <a href="/wiki/Young_and_Jackson_Hotel" title="Young and Jackson Hotel">Young and Jackson Hotel</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>Six days later, the VFA held a special meeting at the <a href="/wiki/Young_and_Jackson_Hotel" title="Young and Jackson Hotel">Young and Jackson Hotel</a> regarding the incident. After assessing the evidence, the bulk of which showed Tankard to be the aggressor, the VFA determined that Coulthard was "more to blame" for the fracas, with chairman <a href="/wiki/H._C._A._Harrison" title="H. C. A. Harrison">H. C. A. Harrison</a> expressing the opinion that "bad language is far worse than blows."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Both players received a season-long suspension\xe2\x80\x94the first punishment of its kind carried out by the association.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014112_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014112-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-week_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-week-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> After the verdict was handed down, Hotham secretary E. J. Lawrence accused Coulthard of insulting and threatening Tankard outside the meeting, and considered going to the police.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Coulthard-Tankard affair was seen as the culmination of a recent trend in the sport harking back to the violence and brutality of 1860s football.<sup id="cite_ref-gero_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gero-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-arg_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arg-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Argus_(Melbourne)" title="The Argus (Melbourne)">The Argus</a></i> supported the VFA, saying "it ought to be thanked and applauded by footballers, as it assuredly is by the public."<sup id="cite_ref-arg_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arg-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Other publications considered the sentence too severe on the grounds that no similar charge had previously been brought against Coulthard, and that the VFA had shown leniency in similar, if not worse cases.<sup id="cite_ref-week_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-week-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tank_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tank-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The incident also served as a fulcrum for debate on the role of the media; one journalist in particular was criticised for <a href="/wiki/Sensationalism" title="Sensationalism">sensationalising</a> the fight, and in turn, influencing the VFA\'s decision to investigate it.<sup id="cite_ref-tank_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tank-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>While Hotham abided by the ruling, Carlton was heavily censured for refusing to enter the field in its next arranged match, against Melbourne, unless Coulthard was allowed to play.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lead_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lead-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Melbourne would only accept their demand if Carlton "assumed the responsibility of defying the association". Carlton rejected the offer, and the match was abandoned.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> By this stage, Carlton was in talks to secede from the VFA, but later decided to play out the season.<sup id="cite_ref-lead_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lead-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> It still sought to exonerate Coulthard, and at the VFA\'s next meeting, the case was reconsidered.<sup id="cite_ref-vfa_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vfa-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In a move that quickly turned public opinion against the VFA, Tankard\'s suspension was uplifted while Coulthard\'s remained in place.<sup id="cite_ref-vfa_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vfa-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Australasian</i> accused the VFA of basing its decision on "various jealousies and petty personal interests", and called for the governing body to be completely restructured.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> It was said that Coulthard\'s status as a lower class professional sportsman made him a convenient <a href="/wiki/Scapegoat" title="Scapegoat">scapegoat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-weeklyobit_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weeklyobit-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Coulthard, despite missing five matches due to his suspension, ranked first for Carlton and fourth overall in the 1882 goal-kicking ladder, tallying 14 majors.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennings2014423_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennings2014423-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He never played senior-level For the 1877\xe2\x80\x9378 season, he transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cricket_Club" title="Melbourne Cricket Club">Melbourne Cricket Club</a> (MCC), which took him on as a professional ground bowler (a job that involved bowling to members in the nets<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>). In 1878\xe2\x80\x9379, he went in as a substitute for a Victorian XV during a match against the <a href="/wiki/Australian_cricket_team_in_England_and_North_America_in_1878" title="Australian cricket team in England and North America in 1878">first representative Australian team</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> and batted in the <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(cricket)#Lower_order_or_tail" title="Batting order (cricket)">lower order</a> for the MCC when it hosted New Zealand\'s visiting <a href="/wiki/Canterbury_cricket_team" title="Canterbury cricket team">Canterbury XI</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Coulthard was only twenty-two when <a href="/wiki/George_Harris,_4th_Baron_Harris" title="George Harris, 4th Baron Harris">Lord Harris</a>, captain of the <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_and_New_Zealand_in_1878%E2%80%9379" title="English cricket team in Australia and New Zealand in 1878\xe2\x80\x9379">touring England XI</a>, put him on trial as the team\'s umpire on the advice of the MCC.<sup id="cite_ref-banquet_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-banquet-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard officiated the team\'s first match in Melbourne, against a Victorian XV, on <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a> 1878,<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> and fulfilled the same duty in the lone <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test match</a> of the tour, against Australia, held at the MCG on 2\xe2\x80\x934 January 1879.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Satisfied with Coulthard\'s umpiring, Harris invited him to accompany the team and stand in as umpire for the remainder of the tour.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sydney_Riot_of_1879">Sydney Riot of 1879</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sydney Riot of 1879">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Riot_of_1879" title="Sydney Riot of 1879">Sydney Riot of 1879</a></div>\n<p>The England team\'s next first-class match was against <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_cricket_team" title="New South Wales cricket team">New South Wales</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Cricket_Ground" title="Sydney Cricket Ground">Association Ground</a> in Sydney. As a hired professional, Coulthard was viewed with suspicion by many Sydneysiders (the local custom was to use amateur umpires), and, in an era of intense <a href="/wiki/Intercolonial_cricket_in_Australia" title="Intercolonial cricket in Australia">rivalry between the colonies</a>, the fact that he was a Victorian only deepened their distrust.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBirley2013125\xe2\x80\x93126_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBirley2013125\xe2\x80\x93126-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> He was also considered "a mere <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/tyro" class="extiw" title="wikt:tyro">tyro</a>", out of depth with his new responsibilities.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Even so, New South Wales was tipped to win based on England\'s losses in Victoria, and <a href="/wiki/Gambling" title="Gambling">illegal gamblers</a> had placed heavy bets in favour of the home side.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolton200140_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolton200140-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The first game passed off without incident, New South Wales winning by five wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETibballs200326\xe2\x80\x9327_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETibballs200326\xe2\x80\x9327-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> England fought back in the return match, which began on 7 February. In reply to the tourists\' first innings total of 267, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Murdoch" title="Billy Murdoch">Billy Murdoch</a>, the star of New South Wales, <a href="/wiki/Carry_the_bat" title="Carry the bat">carried his bat</a> for 82 out of 177 and had reached 10 in the <a href="/wiki/Follow-on" title="Follow-on">follow-on</a> when Coulthard gave him <a href="/wiki/Run_out" title="Run out">run out</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETibballs200326\xe2\x80\x9327_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETibballs200326\xe2\x80\x9327-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> The dismissal caused an uproar in the crowd of 10,000, incited, it was alleged, by bookmakers and their cohorts in the pavilion, who told Murdoch to stay on the field.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolton200041_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolton200041-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> It was not Coulthard\'s first controversial call; on the first day, he gave the caught Harris a second life, a "blatant error" according to one match reporter.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBirley2013125\xe2\x80\x93126_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBirley2013125\xe2\x80\x93126-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ignoring his team\'s umpire <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Barton" title="Edmund Barton">Edmund Barton</a>, who deemed the run out fair,<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> New South Wales captain <a href="/wiki/Dave_Gregory_(cricketer)" title="Dave Gregory (cricketer)">Dave Gregory</a> threatened to abandon the match unless Harris had Coulthard replaced.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClowes200727_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClowes200727-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> While the captains conferred, one of the English fieldsmen inflamed the situation by addressing hecklers in the crowd as "nothing but \'sons of <a href="/wiki/Convicts_in_Australia" title="Convicts in Australia">convicts</a>\'".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClowes200726_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClowes200726-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> At this point, up to 2,000 "roughs and <a href="/wiki/Larrikin" title="Larrikin">larrikins</a>" surged onto the pitch.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETibballs200328\xe2\x80\x9329_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETibballs200328\xe2\x80\x9329-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> Some of the England players armed themselves with <a href="/wiki/Stump_(cricket)" title="Stump (cricket)">stumps</a> as defensive weapons.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETibballs200328\xe2\x80\x9329_70-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETibballs200328\xe2\x80\x9329-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> Harris, in defending Coulthard, was struck with a heavy stick, and <a href="/wiki/A._N._Hornby" title="A. N. Hornby">A. N. "Monkey" Hornby</a> collared and dragged the assailant to the pavilion, taking punches and nearly losing his shirt in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolton200041Lazenby2015245_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolton200041Lazenby2015245-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> Powerless to restore order, the mounted police were able to rescue Coulthard only after a lengthy struggle and with the help of volunteers.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> Two attempts were made to resume the match, but when the rioters learned of Harris\' refusal to withdraw Coulthard, they again rushed the ground and stayed there until the scheduled end of play.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClowes200727_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClowes200727-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Outside the ground, Coulthard was cornered by a 200-strong mob but escaped without further trouble when a group of sailors intervened and "polished off" his would-be attackers.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following a joint apology from New South Wales cricket officials, Harris agreed to continue the match after Sunday break on the condition that Coulthard stood in as umpire. England won by an innings and 41 runs, and then cancelled its remaining fixtures in Sydney.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClowes200727Tibballs200328_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClowes200727Tibballs200328-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> The riot was reported on widely as a national disgrace and a blow to <a href="/wiki/Australia%E2%80%93United_Kingdom_relations" title="Australia\xe2\x80\x93United Kingdom relations">Anglo-Australian relations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolton200041\xe2\x80\x9344_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolton200041\xe2\x80\x9344-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> The Sydney press maintained that Coulthard was either incompetent or "wilfully corrupt" as umpire.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> He wrote an open letter to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Evening_News_(Sydney)" title="The Evening News (Sydney)">The Sydney Evening News</a></i> denying accusations that he had bets on the match,<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> and Harris stated publicly that had he suspected his umpire of taking any interest in the result, he would not have employed him.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Speaking at a banquet given to the England team in Melbourne at the end of the tour, Lord Harris reaffirmed his belief that Murdoch was correctly given out, and, to the applause of the audience, stated that he and his men "had met with no better or fairer umpire than Mr. Coulthard".<sup id="cite_ref-banquet_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-banquet-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First-class_career">First-class career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: First-class career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Coulthard was brought into a Victoria XV in March 1880 to take on that season\'s Australian representative team. He was the leading wicket-taker for his side with 5/52 and 4/28.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> In his <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class</a> debut for <a href="/wiki/Victoria_cricket_team" title="Victoria cricket team">Victoria</a> later that year, against <a href="/wiki/South_Australia_cricket_team" title="South Australia cricket team">South Australia</a> on the East Melbourne Cricket Ground, he took 3/29 and contributed 31 runs to a first innings total of 329, but his performance with the leather slumped in the second innings, conceding 49 runs for a single wicket.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> The following month, after top scoring (51) for a Victorian XV in a match against the Australian XI,<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard played in his first of three first-class contests against New South Wales. He failed to make much of a statistical impact for Victoria in any of these <a href="/wiki/Intercolonial_cricket_in_Australia" title="Intercolonial cricket in Australia">intercolonials</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Given the inconsistency of his first-class outings, it is considered an oddity that, during the 1881\xe2\x80\x9382 season, Coulthard, then Victoria\'s <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_cricket_terms#T" title="Glossary of cricket terms">twelfth man</a>, was selected to play for Australia against <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Shaw" title="Alfred Shaw">Alfred Shaw</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_and_New_Zealand_in_1881%E2%80%9382" title="English cricket team in Australia and New Zealand in 1881\xe2\x80\x9382">touring England team</a> following the withdrawal through injury of <a href="/wiki/Alick_Bannerman" title="Alick Bannerman">Alick Bannerman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fred_Spofforth" title="Fred Spofforth">Fred Spofforth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> It was the second Test of the tour, held at the Association Ground in Sydney on 17\xe2\x80\x9321 February.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup> Batting at number eleven, he scored 6 <a href="/wiki/Not_out" title="Not out">not out</a> in a useful last wicket stand of 29 with fellow Test debutant <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Jones" title="Sammy Jones">Sammy Jones</a>. It would be Coulthard\'s solitary Test appearance, earning him the rare distinction shared only with fellow Australian <a href="/wiki/Paddy_McShane" class="mw-redirect" title="Paddy McShane">Paddy McShane</a> of playing in a Test after umpiring in one.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Also, by a "twist of fate", his captain in the match was Billy Murdoch.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETibballs200429_87-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETibballs200429-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard umpired the fourth and final Test of the tour at the MCG on 10\xe2\x80\x9314 March.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_sports">Other sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Beyond cricket and football, Coulthard excelled at other sports, and was remembered as "one of the best all-round sportsmen of all time".<sup id="cite_ref-allround_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allround-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>An admired <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">amateur boxer</a>, he sparred with and competed against English bare-knuckle champion <a href="/wiki/Jem_Mace" title="Jem Mace">Jem "The Gypsy" Mace</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-allround_89-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allround-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_personal_life">Family and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Family and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Coulthard was born on 1 August 1856 in Boroondara (near <a href="/wiki/Kew,_Victoria" title="Kew, Victoria">Kew</a>) to Thomas Coulthard and his wife Elizabeth (n\xc3\xa9e Fleming), both of whom migrated to Victoria from England in 1854. Born in 1811 outside <a href="/wiki/Stanhope,_County_Durham" title="Stanhope, County Durham">Stanhope</a>, Thomas, a lead ore miner, married in 1843 to Elizabeth, an <a href="/wiki/Ireshopeburn" title="Ireshopeburn">Ireshopeburn</a> native two years his junior.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolfo200925_91-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolfo200925-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> George was their seventh child. Thomas and Elizabeth died in 1866 and 1873 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBolfo200926_92-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBolfo200926-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Coulthard was educated at St. Matthew\'s School, Carlton. As an adult, he lived in Carlton\'s <a href="/wiki/Lygon_Street,_Melbourne" title="Lygon Street, Melbourne">Lygon Street</a>, where he ran a sporting goods store that doubled as a clubroom and <a href="/wiki/Smoking_room" title="Smoking room">smoking divan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup> He married a woman named Letitia Ann Jackson in July 1880 with whom he had two daughters, one of whom died in infancy.\n</p><p>In March 1879, Coulthard spied on and captured William Grieves, a notorious criminal who had eluded detection for five years, and delivered him into police custody.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was later reportedly admitted as a member of the Victoria detective force.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard also donated a <a href="/wiki/Koala" title="Koala">koala</a> to the precursor of the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Zoo" title="Melbourne Zoo">Melbourne Zoo</a> in 1880.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Coulthard\'s youngest brother William, the only sibling to survive past the age of 30, lived until 1935 and served on the board of the Carlton Football Club.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup> He named his son after George.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness_and_death">Illness and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Illness and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg/170px-George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg/255px-George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg/340px-George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1536" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:George_Coulthard_gravesite.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coulthard\'s gravesite, Melbourne General Cemetery</div></div></div>\n<p>In November 1882, Coulthard was appointed umpire for <a href="/wiki/Ivo_Bligh" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivo Bligh">Ivo Bligh</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_in_1882%E2%80%9383" title="English cricket team in Australia in 1882\xe2\x80\x9383">touring England XI</a> on its famous quest to recover <a href="/wiki/The_Ashes" title="The Ashes">The Ashes</a>. Coulthard fell ill during a sea voyage early on in the tour, and on the second day of a match in <a href="/wiki/Newcastle,_New_South_Wales" title="Newcastle, New South Wales">Newcastle</a>, suffered "severe indisposition" and retired from his post.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard had contracted <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a>, and was feared to be on the verge of death by the start of the <a href="/wiki/1883_VFA_season" title="1883 VFA season">1883 VFA season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup> It was arranged that all proceeds from a June match between Carlton and Melbourne on the MCG be donated to Coulthard. The Melbourne press, noting Coulthard\'s popularity, anticipated a record attendance for football in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup> However, stormy weather kept the turnout to no more than 5,000.<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup> Hotham, the club with which Coulthard had feuded the previous year, was among the contributors to his fund.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 20 October 1883, Coulthard was reportedly "confined to his bed in a dangerous state".<sup id="cite_ref-weekly_105-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-105">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup> Delirious as he succumbed to the disease, Coulthard made apparent references to his suspension from the VFA, saying "It is not true they\'re going to disqualify me. Surely they won\'t disqualify me."<sup id="cite_ref-weeklyobit_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weeklyobit-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> News of his death on 22 October was met with an outpouring of public grief, and a large procession followed Coulthard\'s remains from his Lygon Street home to his funeral.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> He was buried at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_General_Cemetery" title="Melbourne General Cemetery">Melbourne General Cemetery</a> next to <a href="/wiki/Princes_Park,_Carlton" title="Princes Park, Carlton">Princes Park</a>, home of the Carlton Football Club.<sup id="cite_ref-weekly_105-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-105">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">&#91;107&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>One sportswriter closed his obituary to Coulthard with the following words:<sup id="cite_ref-obit1_108-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit1-108">&#91;108&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In all my experience I never met with one who better deserved to have written for epitaph <i>"He was a man."</i>\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Benefit_concert" title="Benefit concert">benefit concert</a> was staged later that year in Carlton for Coulthard\'s surviving wife and daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">&#91;109&#93;</a></sup> On the night of the event, Melbourne identity <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Finn" title="Edmund Finn">Edmund Finn</a> recited to the packed venue an original poem about Coulthard, which read in part:<sup id="cite_ref-globe1_110-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe1-110">&#91;110&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<blockquote>\n<div class="poem">\n<p>The foremost he, for football\'s manly game,<br />\nWas ever linked with poor George Coulthard\'s name.<br />\nHe never changed\xe2\x80\x94to Carlton always true,<br />\nHe donned thro\' good and ill the same dark blue.\n</p>\n</div></blockquote>\n<h3><span id=".22The_Dream.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="&quot;The_Dream&quot;">"The Dream"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: &quot;The Dream&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg/220px-Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg" width="220" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg/330px-Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg/440px-Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg 2x" data-file-width="935" data-file-height="663" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Melbourne_Cup_1883.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The 1883 <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cup" title="Melbourne Cup">Melbourne Cup</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Coulthard earned a place in the annals of Australian <a href="/wiki/Horse_racing" title="Horse racing">horse racing</a> for a dream he reported having in the weeks before his death. Lying on his deathbed, he dreamt that Martini-Henri would win the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Derby" title="Victoria Derby">Victoria Derby</a>, Dirk Hatteraick the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Cup" title="Melbourne Cup">Melbourne Cup</a>, and that he himself would die before the first-named race was run.<sup id="cite_ref-Sporting_Gossip_111-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sporting_Gossip-111">&#91;111&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">&#91;112&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard\'s dream was "the great topic of the day" and received significant media coverage.<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113">&#91;113&#93;</a></sup> When he died before Martini-Henri won the Derby, a "rush of superstitious punters" placed bets on Dirk Hatteraick to win the Cup, causing a sharp drop in odds, despite the fact that, in the words of one turf writer, the horse was "as fat as a bacon hog".<sup id="cite_ref-Sporting_Gossip_111-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sporting_Gossip-111">&#91;111&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114">&#91;114&#93;</a></sup> Dirk Hatteraick finished in the tail end of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115">&#91;115&#93;</a></sup> A former member of <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Victoria" title="Parliament of Victoria">Victorian Parliament</a> was among those who believed in Coulthard\'s dream, but admitted backing Dirk Hatteraick "was an idiotic thing to do".<sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116">&#91;116&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Published by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Patchett_Martin" title="Arthur Patchett Martin">Arthur Patchett Martin</a> in <i>Oak-Bough and Wattle-Blossom: Stories and Sketches by Australians in England</i> (1888), author <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Sladen" title="Douglas Sladen">Douglas Sladen</a>\'s short story "At the Melbourne Cup" follows a punter who bets on Dirk Hatteraick after learning of Coulthard\'s dream.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGelderWeaver66_117-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGelderWeaver66-117">&#91;117&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Coulthard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg/170px-Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg" width="170" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg/255px-Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg/340px-Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg 2x" data-file-width="705" data-file-height="912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Victorian_Footballers_1884.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1884 poster showing star VFA footballers of the day. The central portrait of Coulthard is surrounded by two players from each club, all contained in an oval football.</div></div></div>\n<p>Coulthard is often ranked alongside <a href="/wiki/Jack_Worrall" title="Jack Worrall">Jack Worrall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Thurgood" title="Albert Thurgood">Albert Thurgood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fred_McGinis" title="Fred McGinis">Fred McGinis</a> as one of the greatest Australian rules footballers to emerge in the first fifty years of the game.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECardosi2014_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECardosi2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1908, the year of Australian rules football\'s jubilee celebrations, sports journalist <a href="/wiki/Donald_Macdonald_(journalist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Donald Macdonald (journalist)">Donald Macdonald</a> wrote of Coulthard:<sup id="cite_ref-118" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-118">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>He could not be misplaced. His clever handling, his pace, his expertness in dodging, his sureness in the air, and his masterful kicking were items that proved invaluable to his team. He was the brightest star in the galaxy, such as does not, even to-day, shed its effulgent beams on Carlton.\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>Early football historian C. C. Mullen retrospectively named Coulthard the "<a href="/wiki/Champion_of_the_Colony" title="Champion of the Colony">Champion of the Colony</a>" for the years 1876, 1877 and 1879.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020_56-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams200020-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Football_Club#Hall_of_Fame" title="Carlton Football Club">Carlton Football Club Hall of fame</a> in 1990,<sup id="cite_ref-119" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-119">&#91;119&#93;</a></sup> and is one of the few players of his generation to be a member of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="Australian Football Hall of Fame">Australian Football Hall of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECardosi2014_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECardosi2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
