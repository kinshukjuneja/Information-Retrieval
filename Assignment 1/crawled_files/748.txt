href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom, run by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" She operates the principal ferry service to the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> and is one of only three ships in the world still carrying the status of <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mail_Ship" title="Royal Mail Ship">Royal Mail Ship</a> (hence <i>RMV</i> \xe2\x80\x93 Royal Mail Vessel).\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RMV_Scillonian_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>RMV <i>Scillonian III</i> was purpose built for the Steamship Company by <a href="/wiki/Appledore_Shipbuilders" title="Appledore Shipbuilders">Appledore Shipbuilders Ltd</a> of <a href="/wiki/Appledore,_North_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Appledore, North Devon">Appledore</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> and was christened by <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">H.R.H. Prince Charles, Duke of Cornwall</a> on 17 May 1977<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and entered service later in the same month. She is the third passenger ship to carry the <i>Scillonian</i> name and made her first trip to Scilly on 19 May 1977, sailing from <a href="/wiki/Bideford" title="Bideford">Bideford</a> to <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-details_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-details-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On the arrival of the new ship, critics found it "too big, they will never hold her, not suitable or not as good a sea boat as the old boat" (the same had happened when the first <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_(1925)" title="Scillonian (1925)">Scillonian</a></i> went into service in 1926 and again with <a href="/wiki/Scillonian_(1955)" title="Scillonian (1955)">her replacement</a> in 1956).<sup id="cite_ref-backinservice_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backinservice-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Scillonian III</i> has a length of 68 m, a beam of 11.85 m, a draft of 2.89 m, a gross tonnage of 1255.25, and a service speed of 15.15 knots (28.06&#160;km/h). Due to the number of tidal currents which meet off Land\'s End, and the need for a shallow draught to allow access to the Islands, the sea journey can be rough, causing <a href="/wiki/Sea_sickness" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea sickness">sea sickness</a>. For this reason the ship is fitted with a <a href="/wiki/Antiroll_Tanks#Passive_Antiroll_Tanks" class="mw-redirect" title="Antiroll Tanks">"Flume" antiroll stabiliser</a> system. The ship is also designed to have a shallow draft and can sit on the seabed when there is insufficient water.<sup id="cite_ref-harbours_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harbours-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>Scillonian III</i> is currently in service for passengers and cargo 8 months of the year, carrying up to 485 passengers (the current licensed limit)<sup id="cite_ref-harbours_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harbours-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> for day trips or longer holidays to the Islands. As a preferred means of transport between Scilly and the mainland, she is a familiar sight in <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a> \xe2\x80\x93 often photographed by tourists and also featured in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/An_Island_Parish" title="An Island Parish">An Island Parish</a></i>.\n</p><p>In 1998, faced with the cost of building a new ship, the Company decided to give <i>Scillonian III</i> a major refit, during which over 50 tons of steel were used to increase the standard of the ship. The ship also had three new generators and a new bow thruster fitted. The cost was \xc2\xa31.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-details_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-details-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In early 2007, press reports indicated that <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_County_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornwall County Council">Cornwall County Council</a> was expected to approve plans for the construction of a new <a href="/wiki/Ro-ro" class="mw-redirect" title="Ro-ro">ro-ro</a>  ferry at a cost of \xc2\xa317.5 million. This vessel would be leased to the Isles of Scilly Steamship Company and would replace both <i>Scillonian III</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-smcl_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smcl-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The scheme subsequently collapsed.<sup id="cite_ref-abandoned_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abandoned-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During the winter of 2012\xe2\x80\x9313 the ship underwent a \xc2\xa32&#160;million overhaul which refurbished the passenger accommodation and also extended the ship\'s service life to 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-harbours_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harbours-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The ship\'s 40th anniversary was in May 2017 and during that time she has made more than 9,000 return journeys, travelled more than 648,000 nautical miles (1,200,000&#160;km) and carried more than 1,458,000 passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schedule">Schedule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RMV_Scillonian_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Schedule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>From new in 1977 <i>Scillonian III</i> operated a year-round route through all weather conditions to service the cargo and passenger transport to and from Penzance and the island of St. Mary\'s, in the Isles of Scilly. At that time she would run a regular single daily service on weekdays leaving at 0915 from Penzance and then returning to Penzance from St. Mary\'s leaving at 1530. On Saturdays she was scheduled to run a double service leaving earlier in the morning and making a quick turnaround at lunchtime in St. Mary\'s and then performing a second return voyage in the afternoon. In 1990 it was decided to change the service and only run a seasonal operation. The Isles of Scilly Steamship Company Ltd. later purchased a smaller cargo vessel purely for freight and <i>Scillonian III</i> was put into maintenance and repair during the winter months.\n</p><p>These days<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2017)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  <i>Scillonian III</i>  runs between Penzance and Hugh Town, St. Mary\'s for about eight months only, from early spring (March/April) until autumn (October/November).<sup id="cite_ref-islander_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islander-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-channelimages_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-channelimages-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BySea_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BySea-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Through the winter months, she is laid up in Penzance harbour and a cargo-only service to the Isles of Scilly is then provided by <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-simplonpc_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simplonpc-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The annual winter period is used to carry out a maintenance programme, during which <i>Scillonian III</i> is painted and undergoes technical inspections as well as surveys for <a href="/wiki/Passenger_vessel" class="mw-redirect" title="Passenger vessel">passenger vessel</a> certification by the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_and_Coastguard_Agency" title="Maritime and Coastguard Agency">Maritime and Coastguard Agency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-shipshape_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shipshape-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The ferry\'s season thus largely coincides with the tourist season on the Isles of Scilly,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> which normally runs from <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> to the first weekend in October. The ferry is in fact so closely associated with the summer season on Scilly that for many people, "the sight of the ship Scillonian III [arriving in the Isles of Scilly] is a celebrated indication that spring has sprung."<sup id="cite_ref-BySea_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BySea-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During her months of operation, <i>Scillonian III</i> follows a regular schedule. The ferry  normally sails six days a week from Monday to Saturday,<sup id="cite_ref-islander_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islander-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> typically leaving Penzance at 09:15 am and arriving in Hugh Town around 12:00 noon, which means a sailing time of approximately 2 hours 40 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-BySea_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BySea-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This is the vessel\'s standard schedule, which only sees occasional variations on Saturdays.<sup id="cite_ref-seville_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seville-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On most days, when <i>Scillonian III</i> sails from the <a href="/wiki/Mainland" title="Mainland">UK mainland</a> at 09:15 am, she will stay in Hugh Town until 16:30 pm.<sup id="cite_ref-timesanddates_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesanddates-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On some occasions, when changing <a href="/wiki/Tides" class="mw-redirect" title="Tides">tides</a> or <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecast" class="mw-redirect" title="Weather forecast">weather forecasts</a> affect her sailings so that she has to leave Penzance at about 10:30, she will leave Hugh Town again around 15:00, and on the few days when she needs to sail from Penzance at about 08:30, she will depart Hugh Town at about 11:15.<sup id="cite_ref-timesanddates_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesanddates-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Times and dates may also change due to school holidays, special occasions and unexpected events. Two sailings a day, Sunday or night sailings are rare and usually only arranged by the Steamship Company when an exceptionally high number of passengers depend on the ferry service. This may be the case on busy weekends at the start of the summer season, around the <a href="/wiki/World_Pilot_Gig_Championships" title="World Pilot Gig Championships">World Pilot Gig Championships</a>, and more regularly during the year when the air services between the Isles of Scilly and the mainland are grounded due to poor visibility (see <a href="/wiki/RMV_Scillonian_III#Notable_events" title="RMV Scillonian III">"Notable events"</a> below).\n</p><p><i>Scillonian III</i> is part of the emergency response and civil contingency for incidents occurring on the Isles of Scilly.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_events">Notable events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RMV_Scillonian_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 26 March 1997, when the 300-ft <a href="/wiki/Container_vessel" class="mw-redirect" title="Container vessel">container vessel</a> <a href="/wiki/MV_Cita" title="MV Cita"><i>Cita</i></a> hit rocks off the south coast of the Isles of Scilly, the crew of the stricken vessel were rescued by <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Lifeboat" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mary&#39;s Lifeboat">St Mary\'s Lifeboat</a> with the support of a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> from <a href="/wiki/RNAS_Culdrose_(HMS_Seahawk)" title="RNAS Culdrose (HMS Seahawk)">RNAS Culdrose</a>. They returned to the UK mainland on board <i>Scillonian III</i> later that afternoon. As air traffic to Scilly was suspended for the day due to poor visibility on the mainland, the government authorities chartered the <i>Scillonian III</i> to make a night sailing from Penzance to bring officials, shipwreck specialists and police reinforcements to St Mary\'s.<sup id="cite_ref-Luxton_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luxton-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On Monday 12 August 2002, <i>Scillonian III</i> could not sail because of a technical fault, leaving hundreds of tourists temporarily stranded on St Mary\'s. The ferry had to remain docked in Penzance while engineers worked on the fault. Travellers booked in for the sailing were advised to make alternative arrangements; however <a href="/wiki/British_International_Helicopters" title="British International Helicopters">British International</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus" title="Isles of Scilly Skybus">Skybus</a> services struggled to cope with the massive extra demand for seats, the problem made worse by one of British International\xe2\x80\x99s two helicopters being grounded. Cancellation of <i>Scillonian III</i>\'s sailing mainly affected day trip passengers to Scilly and visitors staying in <a href="/wiki/Guesthouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Guesthouse">guesthouses</a>, as in many cases they could not be re-accommodated in the same guesthouse. Freight services to St Mary\'s were also disrupted by the cancellation, so <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i> had to make a rescheduled Monday night sailing to deliver fruit and vegetables to the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-Stranded_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stranded-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 17 June 2010, a <a href="/wiki/Scilly_shrew" class="mw-redirect" title="Scilly shrew">Scilly shrew</a> made headlines on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Cornwall" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Cornwall">BBC Cornwall</a> when it <a href="/wiki/Stowaway" title="Stowaway">stowed away</a> from the Isles of Scilly on <i>Scillonian III</i>. The small 2 in (5&#160;cm) mammal was discovered in a corner of the Upper Deck as the ferry was about to arrive in Penzance and staff were clearing the area near the gangway. Paul Semmens, the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust" title="Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a>\'s onboard marine guide, identified the animal as a shrew about two months old and looked after it for the night. The next day, the shrew was flown back from Penzance to Scilly on a <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus" title="Isles of Scilly Skybus">Skybus</a> plane and released back into its natural environment. Managers at the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" title="Isles of Scilly Steamship Company">Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</a> said they thought it could have been the smallest passenger ever to travel on <i>Scillonian III</i> and the Skybus.<sup id="cite_ref-shrew_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shrew-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 21 April 2011, <i>Scillonian III</i> was issued with her new certificate by the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_and_Coastguard_Agency" title="Maritime and Coastguard Agency">Maritime and Coastguard Agency</a> so her  carrying capacity of 600 passengers could be reinstated. This came in time for the start of the summer season on the Isles of Scilly and the <a href="/wiki/World_Pilot_Gig_Championships" title="World Pilot Gig Championships">World Pilot Gig Championships</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-certificateissued_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-certificateissued-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 2 May 2011, the cancellation of a <i>Scillonian III</i> sailing on the <a href="/wiki/Bank_holiday" title="Bank holiday">bank holiday</a> after the <a href="/wiki/World_Pilot_Gig_Championships" title="World Pilot Gig Championships">World Pilot Gig Championships weekend</a> left visitors stranded on St Mary\'s. Two sailings were scheduled that day to take <a href="/wiki/Gig_rowing" class="mw-redirect" title="Gig rowing">gig rowers</a> home. <i>Scillonian III</i> departed for the first sailing as planned, but with weather conditions worsening in the strong <a href="/wiki/Easterlies" class="mw-redirect" title="Easterlies">Easterlies</a> and waves crashing onto the pier in Penzance it became unsafe to <a href="/wiki/Berth_(moorings)" title="Berth (moorings)">berth</a> and she had to spend three hours circling in <a href="/wiki/Mount%27s_Bay" title="Mount&#39;s Bay">Mount\'s Bay</a>, waiting until <a href="/wiki/High_tide" class="mw-redirect" title="High tide">high tide</a> with 450 passengers on board. When the second sailing was then cancelled, council staff opened up emergency accommodation at their Carn Thomas offices and <a href="/wiki/Town_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Town Hall">Town Hall</a> to provide shelter for visitors stranded on St Mary\'s.<sup id="cite_ref-chaos1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chaos1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n<sup id="cite_ref-chaos2_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chaos2-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 29 June 2011, a passenger had to be <a href="/wiki/Airlift" title="Airlift">airlifted</a> from <i>Scillonian III</i> by a rescue <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> from <a href="/wiki/RNAS_Culdrose_(HMS_Seahawk)" title="RNAS Culdrose (HMS Seahawk)">RNAS Culdrose</a> after complaining about severe chest pains. The day trip visitor had become unwell around 30 minutes out of Penzance, so a doctor travelling on board asked for the helicopter. The ferry retreated to a more sheltered part of the coast, near <a href="/wiki/Mousehole" title="Mousehole">Mousehole</a>, so the patient could be winched up without having to cope with the strong winds.<sup id="cite_ref-airlifted_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-airlifted-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 24 May 2013, <i>Scillonian III</i> ran <a href="/wiki/Aground" class="mw-redirect" title="Aground">aground</a> in St Mary\'s Harbour after being caught by a gust of wind, while attempting to <a href="/wiki/Berth_(moorings)" title="Berth (moorings)">berth</a> by normal approach at <a href="/wiki/Low_tide" class="mw-redirect" title="Low tide">low tide</a>. The captain was intending to retreat and wait for the tide to rise when wind blew her shoreward and she made contact with the bottom, stranding 203 passengers for more than an hour. Two <a href="/wiki/Mooring_(watercraft)" title="Mooring (watercraft)">moorings</a> were lost as a result and a smaller boat received superficial damage, but nobody was hurt and a later inspection by divers revealed that there was no damage to the <i>Scillonian III</i>. She left as scheduled at 4.30pm that afternoon.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gallery">Gallery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RMV_Scillonian_III&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1977 <i>Scillonian III</i> went into service with her hull and superstructure painted white, her <a href="/wiki/Crane_(machine)" title="Crane (machine)">crane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funnel" title="Funnel">funnel</a> davits and masts were buff (yellow) without further markings. Some time after <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i> was acquired, <i>Scillonian III\'</i>s funnel and crane were painted blue to closer match the colours of the other ship, despite the Gry Maritha being a lighter colour blue with a white superstructure. For two seasons after the change of schedule post 1990, the lower half of her hull was painted dark blue with the upper half and superstructure, remaining white. The funnel was painted blue and white striped with a  house flag, painted on the sides in the white stripe. The crane, davits and masts were painted blue.\n</p><p>Thereafter the hull was returned to its original white colour with the flag on the side of the funnel becoming a stylized fluttering company house flag without the red lettering. Following customer feedback and comments about the change of colour, the colour scheme was returned to the original white and buff, but with the addition of the fluttering house flag, complete with the red lettering. For much of the first decade of the 21st century, <i>Scillonian III</i> remained in this appearance.\n</p><p>Shortly before the start of the 2010 season, the colours of the ferry\'s funnel were again changed, this time to white and a black soot ring around the top with a smaller emblem from the marketing material, of the Steamship Company consists of a stylized, flag like, overall shape made up of four quadrant shapes that are not parallel, the left being blue and the right being red.
