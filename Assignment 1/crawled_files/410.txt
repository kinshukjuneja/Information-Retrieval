class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The origin of the name Lostwithiel is a subject much debated. In the 16th century it was thought that the name came from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> name <i>Uzella</i>, translated as <i>Les Uchel</i> in <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>. In the 17th century popular opinion was that the name came from a translation of <i>Lost</i> (a tail) and <i>Withiel</i> (a lion), the lion in question being the lord who lived in the castle.\n</p><p>Current thinking is that the name comes from the Old Cornish <i>Lost Gwydhyel</i> meaning "tail-end of the woodland".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The view from <a href="/wiki/Restormel_Castle" title="Restormel Castle">Restormel Castle</a> looking towards the town shows how this may have come to be.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lostwithiel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Lostwithiel is a historic borough. The <a href="/wiki/Lostwithiel_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Lostwithiel (UK Parliament constituency)">Lostwithiel constituency</a> elected two members to the <a href="/wiki/Unreformed_House_of_Commons" title="Unreformed House of Commons">Unreformed House of Commons</a>, but was disenfranchised by the <a href="/wiki/Reform_Act_1832" title="Reform Act 1832">Reform Act 1832</a>.  It remained a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> until the 1960s, when it became a <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a>.\n</p><p>The seal of the borough of Lostwithiel was a shield charged with a castle rising from water between two thistles, in the water two fish, with the legend "Sigillum burgi de Lostwithyel et Penknight in Cornubia".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Its <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayoral_regalia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mayoral regalia (page does not exist)">mayoral regalia</a> includes a silver oar, signifying its former jurisdiction over the River Fowey.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lostwithiel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The town is situated in the Fowey river valley, positioned between the <a href="/wiki/A390_road" title="A390 road">A390 road</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tavistock,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tavistock, Devon">Tavistock</a> to <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a> and the upper tidal reaches of the river.<sup id="cite_ref-osmap_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osmap-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lostwithiel_railway_station" title="Lostwithiel railway station">Lostwithiel railway station</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Main_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Western Main Line">Cornish Main Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> to <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>. It is situated on the south side of the town, just across the medieval bridge. The line was originally built for the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Railway" title="Cornwall Railway">Cornwall Railway</a> which built its main workshops here, but the surviving workshop buildings were transformed into apartments in 2004.  <a href="/wiki/Lostwithiel_and_Fowey_Railway" title="Lostwithiel and Fowey Railway">A branch line</a> takes <a href="/wiki/Kaolinite" title="Kaolinite">china clay</a> trains to <a href="/wiki/Fowey" title="Fowey">Fowey</a>.\n</p><p>The town contains the suburbs of <b>Bridgend</b> to the east and Rosehill and Victoria to the west of the River Fowey.<sup id="cite_ref-osmap_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osmap-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lostwithiel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Lostwithiel\'s most notable buildings are <a href="/wiki/St_Bartholomew%27s_Church,_Lostwithiel" title="St Bartholomew&#39;s Church, Lostwithiel">St Bartholomew\'s Church</a> and <a href="/wiki/Restormel_Castle" title="Restormel Castle">Restormel Castle</a>. There is a small museum devoted to the history of the town. Once a <a href="/wiki/Stannary_town" class="mw-redirect" title="Stannary town">stannary town</a>, and for a period the most important in Cornwall, it is now much reduced in importance. There is a fine early fourteenth-century bridge with five pointed arches, and nearby the remains of the <a href="/wiki/Lostwithiel_Stannary_Palace" title="Lostwithiel Stannary Palace">Lostwithiel Stannary Palace</a>, with its Coinage Hall \xe2\x80\x93 this was the centre of royal authority over tin-mining, and \'<a href="/wiki/Tin_coinage" title="Tin coinage">coinage</a>\' meant the knocking off of the corner of each block of tin for the benefit of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a>. The small <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/guildhall" class="extiw" title="wikt:guildhall">Guildhall</a> has an arcaded ground floor.  The old <a href="/wiki/Grammar_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammar School">Grammar School</a> has been converted into dwellings.\n</p>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-traditional">\n\t\t<li activities including an arts and crafts festival, a beer festival, a week-long carnival in the summer, food and cider festivals in the October, and a Dickensian evening in December.<sup href="/w/index.php?title=Lostwithiel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are two primary schools in Lostwithiel: <a href="/wiki/St_Winnow" title="St Winnow">St Winnow</a> C E School and Lostwithiel Primary School. Both schools are academies. Lostwithiel Primary School is part of the Peninsula Learning Trust Multi Academy Trust and St Winnow C E School is part of The Saints Way Multi Academy Trust. The majority of children aged between 11 and 16 attend Fowey River Academy or Bodmin College.\n</p><p>Lostwithiel Educational Trust is a local charity which makes "grants to local schools and churches, as well as to individuals, for educational purposes" <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lostwithiel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>From <a href="/wiki/Lostwithiel_railway_station" title="Lostwithiel railway station">Lostwithiel railway station</a> trains operated by <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway_(train_operating_company)" title="Great Western Railway (train operating company)">Great Western Railway</a> run approximately every two hours towards <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_railway_station" title="Plymouth railway station">Plymouth</a> or <a href="/wiki/Penzance_railway_station" title="Penzance railway station">Penzance</a>. Some through services to and from <a href="/wiki/London_Paddington_station" title="London Paddington station">London Paddington station</a> and those operated by <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> between Penzance and <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> also stop.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> provides a regular coach service to London which runs via Plymouth for connections to other destinations. The coach stop is located outside the Royal Talbot Hotel.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bus_stop" title="Bus stop">Bus stops</a> in Lostwithiel are outside the Royal Talbot Hotel and Cott Road phone box.\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:602px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lostwithiel.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Lostwithiel.jpg/600px-Lostwithiel.jpg" width="600" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Lostwithiel.jpg/900px-Lostwithiel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Lostwithiel.jpg/1200px-Lostwithiel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2735" data-file-height="1164" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lostwithiel.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lostwithiel looking from the west</div></div></div></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Twinning">Twinning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lostwithiel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Twinning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Lostwithiel was twinned with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pleyber_Christ&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pleyber Christ (page does not exist)">Pleyber Christ</a> in Brittany, France in 1979.
