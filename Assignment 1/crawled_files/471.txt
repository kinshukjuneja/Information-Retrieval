class="external text" href="http://www.californialeague.com/">www.californialeague.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>California League</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> league which operates throughout <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. it is classified at the <a href="/wiki/Class_A-Advanced" class="mw-redirect" title="Class A-Advanced">Class A-Advanced</a>, three steps below <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. Most players reach this level in their third or fourth year of professional play.\n</p><p>All of the current teams are playing in stadiums that have been built or extensively renovated since 1990. They also are affiliated with MLB teams located west of the Rockies. League attendance continues to increase each season, with over one million fans attending games per year, part of a general nationwide growth and expansion to smaller towns, cities, and regions below those in the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> or <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> with Minor League Baseball at various levels of play in growing popularity in the last few decades. The league is divided into a Northern Division and a Southern Division.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=California_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There were various attempts in the late 1800s and early 1900s to form a "California League" on the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">West Coast</a>, considering the distance of the two current <a href="/wiki/Major_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Major league baseball">major leagues</a> which generally had teams only in the Northeast and were restricted at first until World War I by long distance train travel. The first organized California League lasted from 1887\xe2\x80\x931889, then another followed in 1891, and 1893, and finally in 1899\xe2\x80\x931902. After the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Professional_Baseball_Leagues" class="mw-redirect" title="National Association of Professional Baseball Leagues">National Association of Professional Baseball Leagues</a>, an organization of <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">minor leagues</a> was formed in 1902, (following the "truce" and agreements  between the older <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> of 1876 and the newly "upstart" <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> of 1901), the California League operated outside the NAPBL system as an <a href="/wiki/Independent_baseball_league" title="Independent baseball league">independent league</a> in 1902 and again from 1907\xe2\x80\x931909. This led to huge differences in the quality of teams competing with each other. In 1907, the San Francisco team was 3-34, while later in 1908 San Francisco was 9-67 and Oakland was 4-71. Oakland and San Francisco competed in every year of these various state leagues, with San Francisco having two teams during 1887-88.\n</p><p>The current California League  was founded in 1941, and included teams in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield,_California" title="Bakersfield, California">Bakersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fresno,_California" title="Fresno, California">Fresno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merced,_California" title="Merced, California">Merced</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riverside,_California" title="Riverside, California">Riverside</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino,_California" title="San Bernardino, California">San Bernardino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">Santa Barbara</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stockton,_California" title="Stockton, California">Stockton</a>. The following year, as a result of World War II, the league dropped to four teams, then ceased and suspended operations altogether, although major league baseball and some minor leagues continued as much as possible with limited availability of players during the war years. It reorganized and came back in 1946, adding teams in <a href="/wiki/Visalia,_California" title="Visalia, California">Visalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ventura,_California" title="Ventura, California">Ventura</a> by 1947. Further east, <a href="/wiki/Reno,_Nevada" title="Reno, Nevada">Reno, Nevada</a> joined the league in 1955 with the movement of the old <a href="/wiki/Channel_Cities_Oilers" title="Channel Cities Oilers">Channel Cities Oilers</a> in Santa Barbara and continued as a member for 37 years.\n</p><p>Though nicknames and affiliations shifted, the California League\'s postwar configuration was largely stable by the late 1950s; four of the six cities in the league in 1960 would still be part of the league 50 years later. The league reached eight clubs in 1966 and would hold that for ten years, briefly dipped to six before wavering between eight and nine clubs in the early eighties, then reached ten in 1986 and held that configuration for thirty-one seasons. From 1996 to 2016, the league had a remarkably stable alignment for Class A baseball, with no teams moving or folding for twenty-one years. After the 2016 season, the <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield_Blaze" title="Bakersfield Blaze">Bakersfield Blaze</a>, long dogged by inadequate facilities and unable to negotiate significant repairs, and the <a href="/wiki/High_Desert_Mavericks" title="High Desert Mavericks">High Desert Mavericks</a>, suffering from falling attendance and a lease dispute with the city of <a href="/wiki/Adelanto,_California" title="Adelanto, California">Adelanto</a>, were folded; the High-A level replaced them by expanding the <a href="/wiki/Carolina_League" title="Carolina League">Carolina League</a> to ten teams.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_teams">Current
