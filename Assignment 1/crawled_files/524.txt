Water</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>River Ottery</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Otri</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a small river in northeast <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. The river is about twenty miles (32&#160;km) long from its source southeast of <a href="/wiki/Otterham" title="Otterham">Otterham</a> to its confluence with the <a href="/wiki/River_Tamar" title="River Tamar">River Tamar</a> at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nether_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nether Bridge (page does not exist)">Nether Bridge</a>, two miles (3.2&#160;km) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Launceston,_Cornwall" title="Launceston, Cornwall">Launceston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Source_(river_or_stream)" class="mw-redirect" title="Source (river or stream)">headwaters</a> of the River Ottery are within the <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> of <a href="/wiki/Otterham" title="Otterham">Otterham</a> but formerly came under the parish of <a href="/wiki/Forrabury_and_Minster" title="Forrabury and Minster">Forrabury and Minster</a>. In 1311, the rector of that parish wrote: "..the river Ottery takes its rise in this parish and flows to Canworthy Water and so by Yeolmbridge to the river Tamar."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Hydrology_and_course"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Hydrology and course</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Wildlife_and_conservation"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Wildlife and conservation</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Location"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Location</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hydrology_and_course">Hydrology and course</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Ottery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hydrology and course">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>With its <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributaries</a>, the River Ottery forms a major sub-catchment of the Tamar system, draining 48 square miles (120&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of north Cornwall. The River Ottery\'s <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">catchment area</a> is within the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Carboniferous</a> geological formation known as the <a href="/wiki/Culm_Measures" title="Culm Measures">Culm Measures</a> which stretches from  <a href="/wiki/Dartmoor" title="Dartmoor">Dartmoor</a> to north Devon and across northeast Cornwall as far west as <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The infant river initially flows north until it reaches the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Trengune" title="Trengune">Trengune</a>. From here it adopts an east-southeasterly course which it follows to its confluence with the River Tamar.\n</p><p>The largest tributaries to the River Ottery are Caudworthy Water and Bolsbridge Water which join the River Ottery from the north and Canworthy Water which joins from the south.\n</p><p>The River Ottery system was severely affected by flooding in north Cornwall on 16 August 2004 when up to eight inches of rain fell during a single afternoon.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Ottery headwaters at Marshgate and Otterham were the most severely affected.\n</p><p>The river flows through an isolated area of mixed farming and the only riverside settlements of any size are <a href="/wiki/Canworthy_Water" title="Canworthy Water">Canworthy Water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yeolmbridge" title="Yeolmbridge">Yeolmbridge</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife_and_conservation">Wildlife and conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Ottery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wildlife and conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Cornwall Rivers Project notes five <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI)</a> in the Ottery catchment including the Ottery Valley SSSI which has 33 hectares of increasingly threatened culm habitat and Kernick and Ottery Meadows SSSI, also noted for its <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">biological interest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The project\'s page also notes: "The Ottery supports a broad range of wildlife including populations of <a href="/wiki/Trout" title="Trout">trout</a> and migratory fish, <a href="/wiki/Otter" title="Otter">otters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingfisher" title="Kingfisher">kingfishers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sand_martin" title="Sand martin">sand martins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dipper" title="Dipper">dippers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Curlew" title="Curlew">curlews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snipe" title="Snipe">snipe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Marsh_fritillary" title="Marsh fritillary">marsh fritillary</a> butterfly." <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Ottery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The majority of the boundary between Cornwall and Devon follows the River Tamar, and Celtic place-names, such as those including the element <i>tre-</i>, stop for the most part exactly at the Tamar, reflecting this ancient boundary. However, the part of Cornwall north of the River Ottery has few place-names including <i>tre-</i> and far more of Anglo-Saxon origin (such as those ending in <i>-dun</i> and <i>-cot</i>) than the rest of the county. This indicates that the river formed an early boundary between the rump of <a href="/wiki/Dumnonia" title="Dumnonia">Dumnonia</a> and the westward expansion of the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxon</a> kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Ottery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Source coordinates <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=River_Ottery&amp;params=50.67944_N_4.5946_W_"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">50\xc2\xb040\xe2\x80\xb246\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">4\xc2\xb035\xe2\x80\xb241\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff;
