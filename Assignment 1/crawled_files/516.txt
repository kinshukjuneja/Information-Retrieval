title="Non-departmental public body">non-departmental public body</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Digital,_Culture,_Media_and_Sport" title="Department for Digital, Culture, Media and Sport">Department for Digital, Culture, Media and Sport</a>. Its role is to build the foundations of a community sport system by working with national governing bodies of sport, and other funded partners, to grow the number of people doing sport; sustain participation levels; and help more talented people from all diverse backgrounds excel by identifying them early, nurturing them, and helping them move up to the elite level.\n</p><p>Nick Bitel is the Chairman of Sport England and Debbie Jevans CBE is Vice Chair.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sport_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Sport England was established as the <b>English Sports Council</b> in September 1996 as an executive non-departmental public body by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a>. It began operating in 1997 as Sport England.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It has two statutory, functions: (1) a lottery distributor for sport;<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and (2) the protection of playing fields, through its role as a statutory consultee on planning applications that affect playing fields, under SI No. 1817 (1996)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. The funding it distributes comes from both the <a href="/wiki/HM_Treasury" title="HM Treasury">Treasury</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Lottery_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Lottery (United Kingdom)">National Lottery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since 1997, it has invested over \xc2\xa32bn of Lottery funds and \xc2\xa3300 million from the Exchequer into sports in England.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sport England is organised into nine regions. Following restructuring in 2009, its former <i>Regional Sports Boards</i> have been disbanded and regional staffing considerably reduced. Closer working with the sports national governing bodies (NGBs) is key to its new strategy to achieve its target of one million people doing more sport by 2012-13. Sport England is headquartered in Victoria House, Bloomsbury Square in London.\n</p><p>It is encourages sports venues to enhance their development potential by registering under Sport England\'s SASP (significant areas for sport) programme as either a National or Regional centre for their particular sport.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Its \'Active Places\' website is designed to help the public find sports facilities anywhere in England. Searching can be through an interactive map, within a given locality or to discover more information about a known facility location.\n</p><p>In 2007 Sport England produced Active Design, which provided a set of design guidelines to help promote opportunities for sport and physical activity in the design and layout of new development. The guide was revised, retaining \xe2\x80\x98The 3 A\xe2\x80\x99s\xe2\x80\x99 and refining the criteria based approach to the Ten Principles of Active Design. The revised <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.sportengland.org/activedesign">Active Design</a> was published in 2015, and was supported by Public Health England.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sport_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Derek Mapp resigned as Chair of Sport England on 29 November 2007 after 13 months in the post, claiming he had been forced to leave his position by <a href="/wiki/James_Purnell" title="James Purnell">James Purnell</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Culture,_Media_and_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport">Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport</a>, in a dispute over funding.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hansard_26Jan09_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard_26Jan09-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Mapp was succeeded by Michael Farrar as interim Chair from December 2007 to March 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-Hansard_26Jan09_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard_26Jan09-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_20Feb09_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_20Feb09-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Purnell appointed <a href="/wiki/Richard_Lewis_(tennis_and_rugby_league)" title="Richard Lewis (tennis and rugby league)">Richard Lewis</a> (former Chief Executive of the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Rugby Football League</a>) to review Sport England\'s funding priorities, and he was subsequently appointed Chair on 1 April 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_20Feb09_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_20Feb09-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On 22 April 2013, Nick Bitel was appointed as new Chairman, succeeding Richard Lewis.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clubmark">Clubmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sport_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Clubmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Clubmark is an accreditation which Sport England makes available to community sports clubs which fulfil certain criteria. Its declared aims are: "Higher standards of welfare, equity, coaching and management in community sports club" and "Making sure the nation\'s sports club infrastructure is safer, stronger and more successful". As of  April 2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sport_England&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> over 12,000 clubs had the Clubmark accreditation.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Logo_and_Branding">Logo and Branding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sport_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Logo and Branding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The sport England logo is based on the \'sport for all\' logo which was used from the 1970s onwards.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sport_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Year</th>\n<th>Award</th>\n<th>Category</th>\n<th>Nominee</th>\n<th>Result\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>2015\n</td>\n<td>National Lottery Good Causes Award\n</td>\n<td>Best Sports Project\n</td>\n<td>Sport England TASS\n</td>\n<td style="background: #FFD;
