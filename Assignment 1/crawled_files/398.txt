title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Lannvowsedh</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> opposite <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Cornwall" title="Falmouth, Cornwall">Falmouth</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Roseland_Peninsula" title="Roseland Peninsula">Roseland Peninsula</a> on the south coast of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom.  It lies on the east bank of the <a href="/wiki/Carrick_Roads" title="Carrick Roads">Carrick Roads</a>, a large waterway created after the <a href="/wiki/Ice_Age" class="mw-redirect" title="Ice Age">Ice Age</a> from an ancient valley which flooded as the melt waters caused the sea level to rise dramatically. The immense natural <a href="/wiki/Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="Harbour">harbour</a> created is often claimed to be the third largest in the world.  It was once a busy fishing port, but the trade declined during the 20th century and it now serves as a popular <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourist</a> location, with many properties in the town functioning as holiday accommodation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The town is in the civil parish of <a href="/wiki/St_Just_in_Roseland" title="St Just in Roseland">St Just in Roseland</a>.\n</p><p>A year-round ferry provides a service to Falmouth, which is less than a mile away by boat, but due to its proximity to the <a href="/wiki/River_Fal" title="River Fal">Fal</a> <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a> it is some 30 miles (48&#160;km) away by road. The Place Ferry links the <a href="/wiki/South_West_Coast_Path" title="South West Coast Path">South West Coast Path</a> and operates from <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a> to the end of October.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mawes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The town takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Christianity" title="Celtic Christianity">Celtic</a> saint <a href="/wiki/Saint_Maudez" title="Saint Maudez">Saint Maudez</a> (Mawe), who may have come from Ireland but is mainly venerated in Brittany.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A name: \'Musidum\' in Roman times, has subsequently been applied to St. Mawes, although the source is dubious.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>St Mawes was once an important town and was made a borough in 1563, returning two members to parliament. It was disfranchised in 1832. The town was described, in 1880, by an anonymous writer,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<blockquote>\n<p>... as a quiet little fishing village, and consists of a long straggling street, fronting the water; it has, however a good pier, which was erected in 1854; and a sea-wall, with a parapet was built not long ago, along the centre front of the town<i>.</i>\n</p>\n</blockquote>\n<p><a href="/wiki/St_Mawes_Castle" title="St Mawes Castle">St Mawes Castle</a> is a well-preserved coastal fortress from the time of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII</a>, built to counter the invasion threat from the <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Continent</a>. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Henderson_(historian)" title="Charles Henderson (historian)">Charles Henderson</a>, writing in 1925, says of St Mawes, "an ancient fishing town which in late years has assumed the different and more sophisticated character of a watering place". The seal of St Mawes was Az. a bend lozengy Or between a tower in the sinister chief Arg. and a ship with three masts the sail furled in the dexter base of the second, with the legend "Commune Sigillum Burgi de St. Mawes al Mauditt.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Just outside the town is a closed <a href="/wiki/British_Leyland" title="British Leyland">British Leyland</a> garage on Polvarth Road which retains the British Leyland logo on a hoarding outside.\n</p><p>St Mawes lies within the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB). Almost a third of Cornwall has AONB designation, with the same status and protection as a National Park.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_family">Royal family</span><span frequent private visits to St Mawes by members of the Royal Family including <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_the_Queen_Mother" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth the Queen Mother">HM Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">HRH Princess Margaret</a> and more recently the <a Its existence in 1427 is mentioned in <a href="/wiki/George_Oliver_(1781-1861)" class="mw-redirect" title="George Oliver (1781-1861)">George Oliver</a>\'s <i>Monasticon</i> and it remained in use until the reign of Elizabeth I when it was abandoned. From that time until <i>ca</i> 1838 there was no chapel for the townspeople until a private chapel built in 1807 by the Marquis of Buckingham was licensed by the Bishop. This was on a different site and was rebuilt in 1881. St Mawes continued however to be in the parish of <a href="/wiki/St_Just_in_Roseland" title="St Just in Roseland">St Just in Roseland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/St_Mawes%27_Church,_St_Mawes" title="St Mawes&#39; Church, St Mawes">St Mawes\' Church, St Mawes</a> was opened in 1884. There is also a Methodist church. It was built in the first half of the 19th century and is a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-he1312736_9-0" class="reference"><a
