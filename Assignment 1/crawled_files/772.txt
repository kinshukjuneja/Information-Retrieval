designated <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>-<a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a> border in east <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It comprises the area around the <a href="/wiki/River_Stour,_Suffolk" title="River Stour, Suffolk">River Stour</a> between <a href="/wiki/Manningtree" title="Manningtree">Manningtree</a> and Smallbridge Farm, 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/Bures,_England" title="Bures, England">Bures</a>, including the village of <a href="/wiki/Dedham,_Essex" title="Dedham, Essex">Dedham</a> in Essex. \n</p><p>It is part of the area known as <i>Constable Country</i>, as it was made famous by the paintings of the English <a href="/wiki/Romanticism" title="Romanticism">Romantic</a> painter <a href="/wiki/John_Constable" title="John Constable">John Constable</a>. Among many other works of the area are his <i>Dedham Vale</i> paintings of 1802 and 1828, held in the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a> in London and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Gallery" title="Scottish National Gallery">Scottish National Gallery</a> in Edinburgh.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sng_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sng-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" id="Land_use">Land use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dedham_Vale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Land use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Dedham Vale supports a viable and diverse agriculture with a mix of farm sizes. The majority of the land in the valley is still farmed despite development pressures. Farming is the primary tool for supporting the area\xe2\x80\x99s landscape and wildlife. <a href="/wiki/Arger_Fen" title="Arger Fen">Arger Fen</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>, contains areas of ancient woodland, <a href="/wiki/Meadow" title="Meadow">meadow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fen" title="Fen">fen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sssi_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sssi-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dedham_Vale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Throughout the valley <a href="/wiki/Eocene" title="Eocene">Eocene</a> and glacial deposits overlay chalk deposited during the <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous_period" class="mw-redirect" title="Cretaceous period">Cretaceous period</a>. <a href="/wiki/London_Clay" title="London Clay">London Clay</a> and sands are often exposed on the valley sides as the river and its tributaries cut through the deposits. The composition of these layers and where they occur is paramount in determining what species will grow, which habitats can occur and how the area is farmed.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="River">River</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dedham_Vale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: River">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The River Stour is the key landscape focus for the valley, its course is defined by bank-side trees and <a href="/wiki/Wet_meadows" class="mw-redirect" title="Wet meadows">wet meadows</a>. It supports a variety of riparian (river) habitats. \n</p><p>The valley floor has a large areas of functioning <a href="/wiki/Floodplain" title="Floodplain">floodplain</a>. <a href="/wiki/Water_quality" title="Water quality">Water quality</a> is good, meeting levels demanded in regulations. The catchment meets sustainable demands for <a href="/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water supply</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flood_control" title="Flood control">flood control</a> and recreational use, whilst retaining an unspoilt character and healthy <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The river has become an important method of controlling water levels both in the surrounding countryside and irrigating
