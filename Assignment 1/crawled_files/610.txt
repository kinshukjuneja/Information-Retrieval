class="external text" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b006t6m6">Website</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><i><b>An Island Parish</b></i> is a British television documentary made by <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Aspect_Productions" title="Tiger Aspect Productions">Tiger Aspect Productions</a> for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>. Series 1 and 2 covered the lives of residents of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> parish of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. These series were supported by the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Truro" title="Diocese of Truro">Diocese of Truro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Truro_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Truro-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They followed <i><a href="/wiki/A_Seaside_Parish" title="A Seaside Parish">A Seaside Parish</a></i>, which covered the work of the Reverend Christine Musser in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Boscastle" title="Boscastle">Boscastle</a> in Cornwall, and also <i><a href="/wiki/A_Country_Parish" title="A Country Parish">A Country Parish</a></i> before that. Series 3 and 4 looked particularly at the work of the <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist Church</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, as seen through the eyes of the local minister, the Revd David Easton. Series 5 moved north and followed Fr John Paul in his first year as the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> priest at <a href="/wiki/Castlebay" title="Castlebay">Castlebay</a> on island of <a href="/wiki/Barra" title="Barra">Barra</a>, the second southernmost inhabited island in the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Hebrides" title="Outer Hebrides">Outer Hebrides</a> (after <a href="/wiki/Vatersay" title="Vatersay">Vatersay</a>, to which it is linked by a causeway). Series 6 was filmed on Barra by Tiger Aspect Productions during 2011 and aired in early 2012, while series seven began in late 2012 and follows the Anglican priest and Methodist minister on <a href="/wiki/Sark" title="Sark">Sark</a>. Series 7 and 8 (2013 and 2014 respectively) remained on Sark, while Series 9 and 10(2015) focuses on the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>. The eleventh series is set in The Shetland Islands, broadcast in March and April 2016. The twelfth series, broadcast from January 2017, focuses on <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">Anguilla</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" After much soul-searching, <a href="/wiki/The_Reverend" title="The Reverend">the Reverend</a> Guy Scott decided to leave his parish in <a href="/wiki/Mullion,_Cornwall" title="Mullion, Cornwall">Mullion</a> on the mainland in Cornwall and apply for the position.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC6173819_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC6173819-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The series subsequently followed his eventual arrival on the islands with his family and the difficulties he has faced in adjusting to island life.<sup id="cite_ref-Truro_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Truro-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It also followed the fortunes of islands police force Summer Attachment, PC Nikki Green, local fishermen Martin and Joel Bond and the veterinarian who
