id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg/220px-St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg/330px-St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg/440px-St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="919" data-file-height="919" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary%27s_island_Scilly.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Mary\'s (in red) and the other isles of Scilly</div></div></div>\n<p>St Mary\'s has an area of 6.58 square kilometres (2.54&#160;sq&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-ons_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ons-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> \xe2\x80\x94 40 percent of the total land area of the Isles of Scilly \xe2\x80\x94 this includes four small <a href="/wiki/Tidal_island" title="Tidal island">tidal islands</a> which connect with St Mary\'s at low tide: Toll\xe2\x80\x99s Island, Taylor\xe2\x80\x99s Island, Newford Island and the island at <a href="/wiki/Innisidgen" title="Innisidgen">Innisidgen</a>.\n</p><p>With a population of 1,723 (out of a total population for Scilly of 2,203)<sup id="cite_ref-ons_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ons-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> St Mary\'s is relatively densely populated, with twice the population density than the Isles of Scilly average. The majority of St Mary\'s residents live in the western half of the island, with Hugh Town alone having a population of 1,097.\n</p><p>The main settlement, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a> <i>(<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: Tre Huw)</i>, was sold to the inhabitants by the Crown in 1949 (it had belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> \xe2\x80\x94 which still owns much of the rest of the island). Other settlements on the island are <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porthloo" title="Porthloo">Porthloo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pelistry" title="Pelistry">Pelistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trenoweth,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Trenoweth, Isles of Scilly">Trenoweth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holy_Vale" title="Holy Vale">Holy Vale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maypole,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Maypole, Isles of Scilly">Maypole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Normandy,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Normandy, Isles of Scilly">Normandy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Longstone,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Longstone, Isles of Scilly">Longstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Hill,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Rocky Hill, Isles of Scilly">Rocky Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Telegraph,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Telegraph, Isles of Scilly">Telegraph</a>.\n</p><p>The island became the home of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">British Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Harold_Wilson" title="Harold Wilson">Harold Wilson</a> and his final resting place after his death in 1995. His grave is at the cemetery at <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">Old Town Church</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_parish_and_ward">Civil parish and ward</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Civil parish and ward">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>St Mary\'s is one of the five <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parishes</a> of the Isles of Scilly, which are also <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">wards</a>. St Mary\'s returns 13 councillors to the Council of the Isles of Scilly; this compares with 2 councillors for each of the four "off-island" wards. The civil parish is not functional however, and there is no <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">council</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parish_meeting" title="Parish meeting">meeting</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>St Mary\'s is the only island of the Isles of Scilly with a significant road network and the only island with public highways, including three <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_road_numbering_scheme#A_roads" title="Great Britain road numbering scheme">A roads</a> (measuring 4.7 miles in total length) which are numbered in <a href="/wiki/A_roads_in_Zone_3_of_the_Great_Britain_numbering_scheme" title="A roads in Zone 3 of the Great Britain numbering scheme">Zone 3 of the British numbering scheme</a> (the <a href="/wiki/A3110_road" title="A3110 road">A3110</a>, A3111 and A3112); these \'A\' roads are generally <a href="/wiki/Country_lane" title="Country lane">country lanes</a> in nature. Roads and streets across Scilly have very few signs or markings, and route numbers are not marked at all.\n</p><p>In 2005 there were 619 cars and vans on St Mary\'s; the island also has <a href="/wiki/Taxicab" title="Taxicab">taxis</a> and a conducted <a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">bus</a> tour. Vehicles in Scilly are exempt from annual <a href="/wiki/MOT_test" title="MOT test">MOT tests</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By air, the island is served by <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Airport_(Isles_of_Scilly)" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mary&#39;s Airport (Isles of Scilly)">St Mary\'s Airport</a>. Currently only <a href="/wiki/Fixed-wing_aircraft" title="Fixed-wing aircraft">fixed-wing aircraft</a> services are scheduled, operated by <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus" title="Isles of Scilly Skybus">Isles of Scilly Skybus</a>, to and from <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End_Airport" title="Land&#39;s End Airport">Land\'s End</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newquay_Cornwall_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Newquay Cornwall Airport">Newquay</a>, and (except in winter) <a href="/wiki/Exeter_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Exeter International Airport">Exeter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">Helicopter</a> services were operated by <a href="/wiki/British_International_Helicopters" title="British International Helicopters">British International Helicopters</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Penzance_Heliport" title="Penzance Heliport">Penzance Heliport</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-bih_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bih-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> this service ceased at the end of October 2012.\n</p><p>By sea, the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" title="Isles of Scilly Steamship Company">Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</a> provides a passenger and cargo service from <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>, which is currently operated by the <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Scillonian III">Scillonian III</a></i> passenger ferry, supported by the <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i> cargo vessel. The other islands are linked to St. Mary\'s by a network of inter-island <a href="/wiki/Launch_(boat)" title="Launch (boat)">launches</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ioss_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioss-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The main harbour of St Mary\'s (and the Isles of Scilly) is at Hugh Town, and is called <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mary&#39;s Harbour">St Mary\'s Harbour</a>. Further slipways and small quays exist around the island, at natural harbour sites, such as at Old Town, Pendrathen, <a href="/wiki/Watermill_Cove" title="Watermill Cove">Watermill Cove</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Porthloo" title="Porthloo">Porthloo</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="RNLI">RNLI</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: RNLI">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Lifeboat_Station" title="St Mary&#39;s Lifeboat Station">St Mary\'s Lifeboat Station</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hugh_Town_Harbour,_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Hugh_Town_Harbour%2C_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg/220px-Hugh_Town_Harbour%2C_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Hugh_Town_Harbour%2C_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg/330px-Hugh_Town_Harbour%2C_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Hugh_Town_Harbour%2C_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg/440px-Hugh_Town_Harbour%2C_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hugh_Town_Harbour,_St_Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1197680.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The harbour at Hugh Town, looking east; the quay is where the <i>Scillonian III</i> berths.</div></div></div>\n<p>There has been an <a href="/wiki/RNLI" class="mw-redirect" title="RNLI">RNLI</a> lifeboat station on St Mary\'s since 1837. The first two lifeboats were kept in a boathouse on the town beach at Hugh Town. Following closure in 1855 the lifeboat station was re-opened in 1874 and a boathouse was built on the beach at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Porth_Cressa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Porth Cressa (page does not exist)">Porth Cressa</a>. In 1899 a new boathouse and slipway were built at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carn_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carn Thomas (page does not exist)">Carn Thomas</a> and the first motor boat arrived in 1919.\n</p><p>The lifeboatmen of St Mary\'s have saved many lives in the dangerous seas around the islands, and many have been awarded bravery medals for their courage.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Churches">Churches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Churches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>For the <a href="/wiki/Anglican" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> churches see <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Church, St Mary&#39;s">St Mary\'s Church, St Mary\'s</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">St Mary\'s Old Church, St Mary\'s</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/John_Wesley" title="John Wesley">John Wesley</a> preached here on 13 September 1743. The Wesleyan Methodist Society was established in 1788 and the first chapel erected in 1790. This was replaced in 1828. The present <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a> church was built in 1899 by A.J. Trenear in <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a> and is part of the Isles of Scilly Methodist Circuit.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Telegraph_Tower">Telegraph Tower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Telegraph Tower">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Coastguard\'s Lookout Tower is also known as the Telegraph Tower. It was used by <a href="/wiki/Radio_Scilly" title="Radio Scilly">Radio Scilly</a> for broadcasting. Dating from 1803, it was one of three gun towers built on St Mary\'s by Major Daniel Lyman. They were intended to accommodate 8-10 men and mount a 32-pounder <a href="/wiki/Carronade" title="Carronade">carronade</a> at the top. It was at this tower about 1898 that <a href="/wiki/Guglielmo_Marconi" title="Guglielmo Marconi">Guglielmo Marconi</a> heard wireless signals transmitted from <a href="/wiki/Porthcurno" title="Porthcurno">Porthcurno</a>, a distance of 30 miles.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Harry.27s_Walls"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry\'s_Walls">Harry\'s Walls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Harry&#039;s Walls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Harry%27s_Walls,_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Harry%27s_Walls%2C_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg/220px-Harry%27s_Walls%2C_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Harry%27s_Walls%2C_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg/330px-Harry%27s_Walls%2C_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Harry%27s_Walls%2C_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg/440px-Harry%27s_Walls%2C_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Harry%27s_Walls,_St._Mary%27s_-_geograph.org.uk_-_936169.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Harry\'s Walls, St. Mary\'s</div></div></div>\n<p>Harry\'s Walls are the remains of an unfinished artillery castle situated on a hilltop to the north-east of Hugh Town. It was begun in 1551 as part of a major phase of fortification on the Isles of Scilly, undertaken to counter threats from the French. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Garrison and Star Castle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Star_Castle,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Star Castle, Isles of Scilly">Star Castle</a> is at the centre of a fortification system around the west side of St Mary\xe2\x80\x99s known as the Garrison. The walls of the castle take the shape of an eight-pointed star. It comprises an outer wall around the outcrop of Hew Hill, protecting the town and the castle, with strategically placed gun batteries at regular intervals around the outer wall, allowing covering fire at all angles. A redundant windmill was converted after 1831 into <a href="/wiki/Garrison_Tower" title="Garrison Tower">Garrison Tower</a> as a lookout tower for <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Coastguard" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Coastguard">HM Coastguard</a>. \n</p><p>The Star Castle was built in 1593 by the Surveyor of the Royal Works and mapmaker Robert Adams (d.1595), under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin_(1540-1608)" class="mw-redirect" title="Francis Godolphin (1540-1608)">Francis Godolphin</a>, following the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">Spanish Armada</a> of 1588.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Fearing another Spanish invasion, in May 1593 <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Elizabeth I">Queen Elizabeth I</a> ordered the construction of a fort and two <a href="/wiki/Sconce_(fortification)" title="Sconce (fortification)">sconces</a> as a lookout for any intruder ships.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In 1740 Master Gunner Abraham Tovey transformed the Garrison building walls with gun batteries, including Colonel Boscawen\'s Battery,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> in a circular shape following the coast line of The Hoe. Star Castle is now a hotel.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg/220px-Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg" width="220" height="56" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg/330px-Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg/440px-Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1497" data-file-height="379" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Scillies_2011_081_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Name plate on Colonel George Boscawen\'s Battery</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peninnis_Head">Peninnis Head</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Peninnis Head">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The 14 metre metal tower lighthouse on <a href="/wiki/Peninnis_Head" title="Peninnis Head">Peninnis Head</a> was built in 1911 as a replacement for the 1680 lighthouse in the centre of St Agnes. The lighthouse was converted from acetylene gas to electricity in 1992 and the original range of 17 nautical miles was reduced to nine in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The headland is within the Isles of Scilly <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> and part of the <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Coast" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage Coast">Heritage Coast</a>. It is also a <a href="/wiki/Geological_Conservation_Review" title="Geological Conservation Review">Geological Conservation Review</a> site for its <a href="/wiki/Quaternary" title="Quaternary">Quaternary</a> <a href="/wiki/Geomorphology" title="Geomorphology">geomorphology</a> and was first designated a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of
