href="http://www.easternleague.com/">www.easternleague.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Eastern League</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> league, which operates primarily in the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">northeastern United States</a>, although it has had a team in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> since <a href="/wiki/1989_in_baseball" title="1989 in baseball">1989</a>.  The Eastern League has played at the <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> level since <a href="/wiki/1963_in_baseball" title="1963 in baseball">1963</a>.  The league was founded in <a href="/wiki/1923_in_baseball" title="1923 in baseball">1923</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/New_York%E2%80%93Pennsylvania_League_(1923%E2%80%9337)" title="New York\xe2\x80\x93Pennsylvania League (1923\xe2\x80\x9337)">New York\xe2\x80\x93Pennsylvania League</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1936_in_baseball" title="1936 in baseball">1936</a>, the first team outside the two original states was created, when the <a href="/wiki/York_White_Roses" title="York White Roses">York White Roses</a> of <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">York, Pennsylvania</a>, moved to <a href="/wiki/Trenton,_New_Jersey" title="Trenton, New Jersey">Trenton, New Jersey</a>, and was renamed the <a href="/wiki/Trenton_Senators" class="mw-redirect" title="Trenton Senators">Trenton Senators</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1938_in_baseball" title="1938 in baseball">1938</a>, when the <a href="/wiki/Scranton_Miners" title="Scranton Miners">Scranton Miners</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scranton,_Pennsylvania" title="Scranton, Pennsylvania">Scranton, Pennsylvania</a>, moved to <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford, Connecticut</a>, and became the <a href="/wiki/Hartford_Bees" class="mw-redirect" title="Hartford Bees">Hartford Bees</a>, the league was renamed the <b>Eastern League</b>.\n</p><p>Since 1923, there have been Eastern League teams in 51 different cities, located in 12 different states and two Canadian provinces. The league consisted of six to eight teams from 1923 until 1993. In <a href="/wiki/1994_in_baseball" title="1994 in baseball">1994</a>, the league expanded to 10 teams, with the addition of the <a href="/wiki/Portland_Sea_Dogs" title="Portland Sea Dogs">Portland Sea Dogs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire_Fisher_Cats" title="New Hampshire Fisher Cats">New Haven Ravens</a>, and split into two divisions, the Northern Division and the Southern Division. In <a href="/wiki/1999_in_baseball" title="1999 in baseball">1999</a>, the league expanded to 12 teams, with the addition of the <a href="/wiki/Altoona_Curve" title="Altoona Curve">Altoona Curve</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Erie_SeaWolves" title="Erie SeaWolves">Erie SeaWolves</a>. The two divisions were restructured and renamed for the 2010 season, as the Eastern Division and the Western Division, because the <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Defenders" title="Connecticut Defenders">Connecticut Defenders</a> moved to <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>, after the 2009 season, where they are now known as the <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Flying_Squirrels" title="Richmond Flying Squirrels">Richmond Flying Squirrels</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" League champions">List of Eastern League champions</a></div>\n<p>League champions have been determined by different means since the Eastern League\'s formation in 1923. Before 1934, the champions were simply the league pennant winners. A formal playoff system to determine league champions was established in 1934.<sup id="cite_ref-champions_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-champions-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Binghamton_Triplets" title="Binghamton Triplets">Binghamton Triplets</a> won 10 championships, the most among all teams in the league, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Elmira_Pioneers" title="Elmira Pioneers">Elmira Colonels/Pioneers/Royals</a> (8) and the <a href="/wiki/Scranton_Miners_(baseball)" title="Scranton Miners (baseball)">Scranton Miners/Red Sox</a> (7). Among active franchises, the <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg_Senators" title="Harrisburg Senators">Harrisburg Senators</a> have won 6 championships, the most in the league, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Akron_RubberDucks" title="Akron RubberDucks">Akron Aeros/RubberDucks</a> (5) and the <a href="/wiki/Reading_Fightin_Phils" title="Reading Fightin Phils">Reading Fightin Phils</a> (4).<sup
