href="/wiki/Motorboat" title="Motorboat">motorboat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a Prior to the era of engines on small craft, a launch was the largest boat carried on a sailing vessel, powered by sail or by oars. In competitive rowing, a launch is a motorized boat used by the coach during training.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Civilian_use_in_the_UK"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Civilian use in the UK</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Military_launches_in_the_UK"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Military launches in the UK</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Sports"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Sports</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Launch_(boat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Originally a launch was the largest boat carried by a warship in the age of sail. The word comes from the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> <i>lancha</i> ("barge") and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lancaran_(ship)" title="Lancaran (ship)">lancharan</a></i> ("boat"), from <i>lanchar</i> ("velocity without effort").<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Sail" title="Age of Sail">Age of Sail</a>, a ship carried a variety of <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_boat" title="Ship&#39;s boat">ship\'s boats</a> of different sizes and used for different purposes. In addition to the launch, examples include the <a href="/wiki/Jolly_boat" title="Jolly boat">jolly boat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Captain%27s_gig" title="Captain&#39;s gig">captain\'s gig</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pinnace_(ship%27s_boat)" title="Pinnace (ship&#39;s boat)">pinnace</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(boat)" title="Cutter (boat)">cutter</a>. Distinctions among the smaller vessels were clear, both in design and purpose.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the age of motorized ships, these distinctions of size and purpose have largely disappeared, but the terms continue in use.\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Demak_Sultanate" title="Demak Sultanate">Demak Sultanate</a> attack on <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca" title="Portuguese Malacca">Portuguese Malacca</a> of 1513, lancaran were used as armed troop transports for landing alongside <a href="/wiki/Penjajap" title="Penjajap">penjajap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelulus" title="Kelulus">kelulus</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/Javanese_junk" class="mw-redirect" title="Javanese junk">Javanese junks</a> were too large to approach shore.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 18th century, a launch was used to set the large anchors on a ship. The launch of that era had a square <a href="/wiki/Transom_(nautical)" title="Transom (nautical)">transom</a> and was about 24 feet long. In 1788 <a href="/wiki/Captain_Bligh" class="mw-redirect" title="Captain Bligh">Captain Bligh</a> was set adrift in <a href="/wiki/HMS_Bounty" title="HMS Bounty"><i>Bounty</i></a>\xe2\x80\x99s launch.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civilian_use_in_the_UK">Civilian use in the UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Launch_(boat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Civilian use in the UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> the term "launch" is used to mean any motorised pleasure boat. The usage arises from the legislation<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> governing the management of the Thames and laying down the categories of boats and the tolls for which they were liable.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_launches_in_the_UK">Military launches in the UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Launch_(boat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Military launches in the UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Motor_launch" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor launch">motor launch</a></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Motor_launch" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor launch">Motor launch</a> was the designation for large (typically 60-to-115-foot or 18-to-35-metre long) vessels used in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and some other navies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> They were used for inshore work in defending the coast from submarines and carried relatively light armament: a few <a href="/wiki/Depth_charge" title="Depth charge">depth charges</a>, a gun and a few <a href="/wiki/Machine_gun" title="Machine gun">machine guns</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Launch_(boat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In competitive <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(Sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowing (Sport)">rowing</a> the term "launch" is used to refer to any motorized boat used by the coach to follow practicing boats during workouts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Launch_(boat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Type_Two_63_ft_HSL" title="Type Two 63 ft HSL">RAF rescue launch</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cabin_cruiser" title="Cabin cruiser">Cabin cruiser</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Slipper_launch" title="Slipper launch">Slipper launch</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Naphtha_launch" title="Naphtha launch">Naphtha launch</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Picket_boat" title="Picket boat">Picket boat</a>, a naval launch</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Launch_(boat)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/launch">"Launch (3) noun"</a>. <i>Merriam Webster</i><span class="reference-accessdate">.
