title="British Summer Time">BST</a>)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Southern England</b>, or the <b>South of England</b>, also known as <b>the South</b>, refers roughly to the southern <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_England" title="Counties of England">counties</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The extent of this area can take a number of different interpretations depending on the context, including geographical, cultural, political and economic.\n</p><p>Geographically, the extent of the south of England may vary from the southern quarter (below the M4/Northern M25), via one-third of the country (excluding central England), to the southern half, bordering <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">northern England</a>. The South is often considered a principal <a href="/wiki/Cultural_area" title="Cultural area">cultural area</a> of England, along with <a href="/wiki/The_Midlands" title="The Midlands">the Midlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>. Many consider the area to have a distinct identity from the rest of England, however without universal agreement on what cultural, political, and economic characteristics of the South are.\n</p><p>For statistical purposes, Southern England is divided into four <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_England" title="Regions of England">regions</a>: <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">South East England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">London</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/East_of_England" title="East of England">East of England</a>. Combined, these have a total area of 62,042 square kilometres (23,955&#160;sq&#160;mi), and a population of 28 million.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#People"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">People</span></a>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-2 tocsection-2"><a href="#Health"><span class="tocnumber">1.1</span> <span class="toctext">Health</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Sport"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Sport</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Divisions"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Divisions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>People often apply the terms "southern" and "south" loosely, without deeper consideration of the geographical identities of Southern England. This can cause confusion over the depth of affiliation between its areas. As in much of the rest of England, people tend to have a deeper affiliation to their county or city. Thus, residents of <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> are unlikely to feel much affinity with people in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>. Similarly, there is a strong distinction between natives of the south-west and south-east. The broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Maconie" title="Stuart Maconie">Stuart Maconie</a> has noted that culturally "there\'s a bottom half of England [...] but there isn\'t a south in the same way that there\'s a north".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:EWHealthMap.svg" class="image"><img alt="Map of districts in England and Wales shaded by life expectancy." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/EWHealthMap.svg/260px-EWHealthMap.svg.png" width="260" height="265" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/EWHealthMap.svg/390px-EWHealthMap.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/EWHealthMap.svg/520px-EWHealthMap.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="510" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:EWHealthMap.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Life expectancy at birth in England and Wales 2012 to 2014. Lighter colours indicate longer life expectancy.</div></div></div>\n<p>One major manifestation of the North\xe2\x80\x93South divide is in health and <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> statistics.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> All three Northern England statistical regions have lower than average life expectancies and higher than average rates of <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Circulatory_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Circulatory disease">circulatory disease</a> and <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_disease" title="Respiratory disease">respiratory disease</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LifeExpect_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LifeExpect-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The South of England has a higher life expectancy than the North, however, regional differences do seem to be slowly narrowing: between 1991\xe2\x80\x931993 and 2012\xe2\x80\x932014, life expectancy in the North East increased by 6.0 years and in the North West by 5.8 years, the fastest increase in any region outside London, and the gap between life expectancy in the North East and South East is now 2.5 years, down from 2.9 in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-LifeExpect_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LifeExpect-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Rugby_League" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Rugby League">History of Rugby League</a></div>\n<p>The sport of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a> experienced a <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">schism</a> in 1895 with many teams based in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> and surrounding areas breaking from the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a> and forming their own <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Rugby League">League</a>. The disagreement that led to the split was over the issue of professional payments, and "broken time" or injury payments. There is a perception that league is the code of rugby played in the north, whilst union is the code played in the south.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions">Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In most definitions, Southern England includes all the counties on/near the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a>. In terms of the current ceremonial counties:\n</p>\n<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em;">\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/West_Sussex" title="West Sussex">West Sussex</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/East_Sussex" title="East Sussex">East Sussex</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a></li></ul>\n</div>\n<p>Despite the general acceptance of these counties as Southern, those that comprise the <a href="/wiki/West_Country" title="West Country">West Country</a> are occasionally considered mutually exclusive to Southern England.\n</p><p>The exact northern extent varies and as with most geographical regions, people sometimes debate the boundaries. In the west, Southern England is generally taken to include <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>; in central Southern England, the counties of <a href="/wiki/Bedfordshire" title="Bedfordshire">Bedfordshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a>; to the east, <a href="/wiki/Essex,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Essex, England">Essex</a> and the counties of <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia" title="East Anglia">East Anglia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a>).\n</p><p>Despite these definitions, the northern boundary is generally taken to correspond to an imaginary line from the <a href="/wiki/Severn_Estuary" title="Severn Estuary">Severn Estuary</a> to <a href="/wiki/The_Wash" title="The Wash">the Wash</a> (or expressed in terms of towns, from <a href="/wiki/Gloucester" title="Gloucester">Gloucester</a> to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Lynn" title="King&#39;s Lynn">King\'s Lynn</a>).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
