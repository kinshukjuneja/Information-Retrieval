the <b>Baseball Challenge League</b><span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">\xe3\x83\x99\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xb9\xe3\x83\x9c\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x83\xab\xe3\x83\xbb\xe3\x83\x81\xe3\x83\xa3\xe3\x83\xac\xe3\x83\xb3\xe3\x82\xb8\xe3\x83\xbb\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xb0</span><span style="display:none">,</span>&#32;<i>B\xc4\x93sub\xc5\x8dru Charenji R\xc4\xabgu</i>)</span>, is an independent minor <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_league" title="Sports league">league</a> in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The league\'s abbreviated designation is "BC League<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">BC\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xb0</span>)</span>."\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#League_structure"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">League structure</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Teams"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Teams</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Franchise_locations"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Franchise locations</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Yearly_standings"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Yearly standings</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-7"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">7</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="League_structure">League structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Challenge_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: League structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Baseball Challenge League has two divisions, Future\xe2\x80\x94East and Advance\xe2\x80\x94West, with five teams in each division. Not every team has a home stadium; instead, the team travels around its home <a href="/wiki/Prefectures_of_Japan" title="Prefectures of Japan">prefecture</a>, playing in different stadiums, each one called "home\xe2\x80\x9d for that game.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The 72-game season runs from April\xe2\x80\x93October, split into two half-terms, with the division champion from each half-term meeting in a playoff at the end of the year to determine which two teams compete for the league championship. Each team carries 27 players.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Typically, players earn 150,000 <a href="/wiki/Yen" class="mw-redirect" title="Yen">yen</a> (c. U.S. $2,000) per month, with another 50,000 yen in potential bonuses.<sup id="cite_ref-Patrick_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patrick-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The league imposes a 7.2 million <a href="/wiki/Yen" class="mw-redirect" title="Yen">yen</a> (c. U.S. $60,600) salary limit for team managers.<sup id="cite_ref-jhongo_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jhongo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Challenge_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The BC League began play in 2007 as the <b>Hokushinestu Baseball Challenge League</b>. It originally consisted of four teams based in the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_region" title="Hokuriku region">Hokuriku region</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Ishikawa_Million_Stars" title="Ishikawa Million Stars">Ishikawa Million Stars</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Albirex_Baseball_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Niigata Albirex Baseball Club">Niigata Albirex Baseball Club</a>, the Shinano Grandserows, and the <a href="/wiki/Toyama_Thunderbirds" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyama Thunderbirds">Toyama Thunderbirds</a>. In 2008 the league added two teams, <a href="/wiki/Gunma_Diamond_Pegasus" title="Gunma Diamond Pegasus">Gunma Diamond Pegasus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Fukui_Miracle_Elephants" class="mw-redirect" title="Fukui Miracle Elephants">Fukui Miracle Elephants</a>, and split into two divisions, <a href="/wiki/Shin%27etsu_region" title="Shin&#39;etsu region">J\xc5\x8dshin\'etsu</a> (Gunma, Shinano, and Niigata) and <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_region" title="Hokuriku region">Hokuriku</a> (Fukui, Ishikawa, and Toyama).\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ishikawa_Million_Stars" title="Ishikawa Million Stars">Ishikawa Million Stars</a> Infielder <a href="/wiki/Kensuke_Uchimura" title="Kensuke Uchimura">Kensuke Uchimura</a> led the league in steals in 2007, which led to him being drafted by <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Rakuten_Golden_Eagles" class="mw-redirect" title="Rakuten Golden Eagles">Rakuten Golden Eagles</a>. In 2008, he became the first player to reach NPB after playing in the BC League.\n</p><p>In February 2014, the league agreed to a <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> deal with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Route_Inn_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Route Inn Group (page does not exist)">Route Inn Group</a>, becoming the <b>Route Inn BC League</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gen_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gen-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>That same month, it was announced that a new team, the Musashi Heat Bears, based out of <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, would join the league in time for the 2015 season.<sup id="cite_ref-Gen_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gen-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In July 2014, it was announced that a second new team, the Fukushima Hopes, would also join the league for the 2015 season.<sup id="cite_ref-Yakyubaka_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yakyubaka-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Late in the summer of 2014, along with the <a href="/wiki/Shikoku_Island_League_Plus" title="Shikoku Island League Plus">Shikoku Island League Plus</a>, the Route Inn BCL formed the Japan Independent Baseball League Organization.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>With the addition of two new teams, in 2015 the league realigned and renamed its divisions, replacing the previous divisions J\xc5\x8dshin\'etsu and Hokuriku with Future\xe2\x80\x94East and Advance\xe2\x80\x94West.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">Teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
