title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a>&#160;</div></div></div></div></div></div></div>\n<p>Much of China\'s <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a> Province is west of the strait, having the main islands of <a href="/wiki/Kinmen" title="Kinmen">Kinmen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xiamen_Island" title="Xiamen Island">Xiamen</a> (a major city overspilling into the mainland and including <a href="/wiki/Gulangyu_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Gulangyu Island">Gulangyu Island</a>), <a href="/wiki/Pingtan_Island" title="Pingtan Island">Pingtan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matsu_Islands" title="Matsu Islands">Matsu</a> just off its coast. The <a href="/wiki/Penghu" title="Penghu">Penghu</a> or Pescadores Islands lie in the east of the strait. Fishermen use the strait as a fishing resource.  All of Fujian\'s rivers (the largest being the <a href="/wiki/Min_River_(Fujian)" title="Min River (Fujian)">Min</a> and, second-largest, the <a href="/wiki/Jiulong_River" title="Jiulong River">Jiulong</a>) run into the strait, except the <a href="/wiki/Ting_River" title="Ting River">Ting</a>. Taiwan\'s government administers Penghu, Kinmen, and Matsu.  The strait is on the continental shelf, it is almost entirely less than 150&#160;m deep, with a brief ravine of that depth off the south-west of Taiwan. It hosts major shipping lanes including over a bank, around 25&#160;m in depth (40&#8211;60km north of the Penghu archipelago).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Strait has been the theatre for several military confrontations between the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army_Navy" title="People&#39;s Liberation Army Navy">PLAN</a> and <a href="/wiki/ROCN" class="mw-redirect" title="ROCN">ROCN</a> since the last days of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> in 1949 when the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (KMT) forces led by Generalissimo <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek" title="Chiang Kai-shek">Chiang Kai-shek</a> retreated across the Strait and relocated <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Government of the Republic of China">their government</a> to their final stronghold of Taiwan.  A theoretical median maritime border known as the cross-strait median (<span lang="zh-Hant" title="Chinese language text">\xe6\xb5\xb7\xe5\xb3\xbd\xe4\xb8\xad\xe7\xb7\x9a</span>) also exists on the water to prevent certain transportation from passing.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_link">Proposed link</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit href="/wiki/Taiwan_Strait_Tunnel_Project" title="Taiwan Strait Tunnel Project">Taiwan Strait Tunnel Project</a>, <a href="/wiki/G3_Beijing-Taipei_Expressway" class="mw-redirect" title="G3 Beijing-Taipei Expressway">G3 Beijing-Taipei Expressway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing%E2%80%93Taipei_High-Speed_Rail_Corridor" class="mw-redirect" title="Beijing\xe2\x80\x93Taipei High-Speed Rail Corridor">Beijing\xe2\x80\x93Taipei High-Speed Rail Corridor</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kunming%E2%80%93Taipei_High-Speed_Rail_Corridor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kunming\xe2\x80\x93Taipei High-Speed Rail Corridor (page does not exist)">Kunming\xe2\x80\x93Taipei High-Speed Rail Corridor</a></div>\n<p>As part of the People\'s Republic of China\'s National Expressway Plan, a tunnel or possibly a bridge, was <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Strait_Tunnel_Project" title="Taiwan Strait Tunnel Project">proposed</a> in 2005 to link the city of <a href="/wiki/Fuzhou" title="Fuzhou">Fuzhou</a> with <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> across the strait.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> If such an extreme construction were ever built, it would by far exceed the length of any man-made tunnel in the world today. Engineers in Beijing state that a tunnel is technically feasible. However, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> government in Taiwan has refused to open direct links out of concern for Taiwan\'s security and in fear that by doing so it would have to recognize the People\'s Republic of China\'s <a href="/wiki/One-China_policy" title="One-China policy">one-China policy</a>, since <a href="/wiki/Two_Chinas" title="Two Chinas">China split into two</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> and they still have an <a href="/wiki/Cross-strait_relations" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross-strait relations">adversarial relationship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taiwan_Strait&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 22em;
