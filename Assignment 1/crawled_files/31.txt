Killigrew</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Sir William Godolphin</b> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_parliament" title="Member of parliament">MP</a> (1567\xe2\x88\x921613), of <a href="/wiki/Godolphin_Estate" title="Godolphin Estate">Godolphin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, was an English knight, soldier, knight, and politician who sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_England" title="House of Commons of England">House of Commons</a>  from 1604 to 1611.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Godolphin_(1567%E2%80%931613)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin was the older son of <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin_(1540%E2%80%931608)" title="Francis Godolphin (1540\xe2\x80\x931608)">Sir Francis Godolphin (1540\xe2\x80\x931608)</a>, also an MP and <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Scilly Isles">Governor of the Scilly Isles</a> and his first wife Margaret Killigrew. He matriculated at <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_College,_Cambridge" title="Emmanuel College, Cambridge">Emmanuel College, Cambridge</a> in 1585 and was admitted at <a href="/wiki/Lincoln%27s_Inn" title="Lincoln&#39;s Inn">Lincoln\'s Inn</a> on 29 January 1587.<sup id="cite_ref-Venn_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Venn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He accompanied the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Devereux,_2nd_Earl_of_Essex" title="Robert Devereux, 2nd Earl of Essex">Earl of Essex</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Essex_in_Ireland" title="Essex in Ireland">military expedition of 1599\xe2\x80\x931600 to Ireland</a>, and was knighted on 13 July 1599 for his gallantry in an action at <a href="/wiki/Arklow" title="Arklow">Arklow</a>. He was  subsequently put in command of a brigade of cavalry, and he was credited with playing an important part in victory at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Kinsale" title="Siege of Kinsale">Siege of Kinsale</a> on 24 December 1601, when his troops broke through the enemy line and captured the Spanish commander.\n</p><p>Godolphin was <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) for <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">Cornwall</a> in the first parliament of James I from 1604 to 1611.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWillis1750157_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWillis1750157-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was Lieutenant-governor of the Scilly Isles from 1597, becoming full Governor on the death of his father in 1608 until his own death in 1613.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He had married Thomasine, the daughter and heiress of Thomas Sidney of Wighton, Norfolk and had 3 sons and a daughter.\n</p>\n<h2><span
