have been chosen as symbols to represent specific geographic areas. Some countries have a country-wide <b>floral emblem</b>; others in addition have symbols representing subdivisions. Different processes have been used to adopt these symbols \xe2\x80\x93 some are conferred by government bodies, whereas others are the result of informal public polls. biodiversity.\n</p><p>Each of the 33 <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Indonesia" title="Provinces of Indonesia">provinces of Indonesia</a> also has a native plant as its <a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=75" title="Edit section: County flowers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>A <b>county flower</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> chosen to symbolise a county. They exist primarily in the United Kingdom, but some counties in other countries also have them.\n</p><p>One or two county flowers have a long history in England \xe2\x80\x93 the <a href="/wiki/Red_rose_of_Lancashire" class="mw-redirect" title="Red rose of Lancashire">red rose of Lancashire</a> dates from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, for instance. However, the county flower concept was only extended to cover the whole United Kingdom in 2002, as a <a href="/wiki/Promotional_tool" class="mw-redirect" title="Promotional tool">promotional tool</a> by a charity. In that year, the plant conservation charity <a href="/wiki/Plantlife" title="Plantlife">Plantlife</a> ran a competition to choose county flowers for all counties, to celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II" title="Golden Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Golden Jubilee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Queen Elizabeth II</a>.\n</p><p>Plantlife\'s scheme is loosely based on Britain\'s <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Counties of the United Kingdom">historic counties</a>, and so some current <a href="/wiki/Local_government_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Local government of the United Kingdom">local government areas</a> are not represented by a flower, and some of the counties included no longer exist as administrative areas. Flowers were also chosen for thirteen major cities: <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>, Birmingham, Bristol, <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>, Edinburgh, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, Liverpool, London, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> was also treated as a county (distinct from Cornwall) for the purpose of the scheme. The <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> was included, even though it is not a county, but a self-governing territory outside of the United Kingdom with an existing national flower: the ragwort or <i>cushag</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a> were not included.\n</p><p>A total of 94 flowers was chosen in the competition. 85 of the 109 counties have a unique county flower, but several species were chosen by more than one county. <a href="/wiki/Digitalis_purpurea" title="Digitalis purpurea">Foxglove</a> or <i>Digitalis purpurea</i> was chosen for four counties \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Argyll" title="Argyll">Argyll</a>, Birmingham, <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monmouthshire_(historic)" title="Monmouthshire (historic)">Monmouthshire</a> \xe2\x80\x93 more than any other species. The following species were chosen for three counties each:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bog_Rosemary" class="mw-redirect" title="Bog Rosemary">Bog Rosemary</a> <i>Andromeda polifolia</i> (<a href="/wiki/Ceredigion" title="Ceredigion">Cardiganshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirkcudbrightshire" title="Kirkcudbrightshire">Kirkcudbright</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Tyrone" title="County Tyrone">Tyrone</a>)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Primula_veris" title="Primula veris">Cowslip</a> <i>Primula veris</i> (<a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Harebell" class="mw-redirect" title="Harebell">Harebell</a> <i>Campanula rotundifolia</i> (<a href="/wiki/County_Antrim" title="County Antrim">Antrim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dumfriesshire" title="Dumfriesshire">Dumfriesshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Armeria_maritima" title="Armeria maritima">Thrift</a> <i>Armeria maritima</i> (<a href="/wiki/County_of_Bute" title="County of Bute">Buteshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pembrokeshire" title="Pembrokeshire">Pembrokeshire</a> and the Isles of Scilly)</li></ul>\n<p>And the following species were chosen for two counties:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Grass-of-parnassus" class="mw-redirect" title="Grass-of-parnassus">Grass-of-parnassus</a> <i>Parnassia palustris</i> (<a href="/wiki/Cumberland" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>)</li></ul>\n<p>In addition, <a href="/wiki/Lychnis_viscaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Lychnis viscaria">Sticky Catchfly</a> <i>Lychnis viscaria</i> was chosen for both (ecology)">native</a> species were chosen, but for a small number of counties, <a href="/wiki/Alien_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alien (biology)">non-natives</a> were chosen, mainly <a href="/wiki/Archaeophyte" title="Archaeophyte">archaeophytes</a>. For example, <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a> has a <a href="/wiki/Tudor_rose" title="Tudor rose">Tudor rose</a> as its county flower, even though it is not a native species.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Unofficial_flowers">Unofficial flowers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=76" title="Edit section: Unofficial flowers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Armenia">Armenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=77" title="Edit section: Armenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>No plant or flower seems to be among the current official symbols.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> Some flowering plants from the area include <i><a href="/wiki/Althaea_armeniaca" title="Althaea armeniaca">Althaea armeniaca</a></i>, Armenian Basket, <i><a href="/wiki/Muscari_armeniacum" title="Muscari armeniacum">Muscari armeniacum</a></i>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenian_Poppy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Armenian Poppy (page does not exist)">Armenian Poppy</a>, Armenian vartig (vargit), and <i><a href="/wiki/Tulipa_armena" title="Tulipa armena">Tulipa armena</a></i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=78" title="Edit section: Azerbaijan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Azerbaijan currently has no official national flower. Traditionally, various regions have different designations where national symbols are concerned. The city of <a href="/wiki/Shusha" title="Shusha">Shusha</a> named the <a href="/wiki/Khari_Bulbul" class="mw-redirect" title="Khari Bulbul">Khari Bulbul</a> (<i>Ophrys caucasica</i>) the floral emblem of the <a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh" title="Nagorno-Karabakh">Nagorno-Karabakh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=79" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MeihuaShan_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/MeihuaShan_1.jpg/220px-MeihuaShan_1.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/MeihuaShan_1.jpg/330px-MeihuaShan_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/MeihuaShan_1.jpg/440px-MeihuaShan_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MeihuaShan_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Plum_blossom" class="mw-redirect" title="Plum blossom">Plum blossom</a>, or <i>meihua</i> (<span lang="zh" title="Chinese language text">\xe6\xa2\x85\xe8\x8a\xb1</span>).</div></div></div>\n<p>China currently has no official national flower. Traditionally, various regions have different designations where national symbols are concerned.\n</p><p>In 1903, the <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> named the <a href="/wiki/Paeonia_suffruticosa" title="Paeonia suffruticosa">peony</a> (<span lang="zh" title="Chinese language text">\xe7\x89\xa1\xe4\xb8\xb9</span>) the floral emblem of the nation. The peony has long been considered a flower of wealth and honor in China.\n</p><p>The puppet state <a href="/wiki/Manchukuo" title="Manchukuo">Manchukuo</a> followed Japan\'s model of dual floral emblems: the "spring orchid" (Cymbidium goeringii) for the Emperor and the imperial household, and the sorghum blossom (Sorghum bicolor) for the state and the nation.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Plum_blossom" class="mw-redirect" title="Plum blossom">plum blossom</a>, <i>meihua</i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh">\xe6\xa2\x85\xe8\x8a\xb1</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>&#58; <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">m\xc3\xa9ihu\xc4\x81</span></i>), has also been one of the most beloved flowers in Chinese culture.  The Republic of China government named the plum blossom as the national flower in 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-roc_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-roc-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The plum blossom is symbol for resilience and perseverance in the face of adversity, because plum blossoms often bloom most vibrantly even amidst the harsh winter snow.<sup id="cite_ref-roc_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-roc-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-flagroc_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flagroc-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-npm-roc_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-npm-roc-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The People\'s Republic of China, which has controlled mainland China since 1949, has no official floral emblem. There have been several petitions in recent years to officially adopt one. However, the government has not taken any action yet. A poll in 2005 showed that 41% of the public supports peony as the national flower while 36% supported the plum blossom.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Some scholars have suggested that the peony and plum blossoms may be designated as dual national flowers.  In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Orchid" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchid">orchid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jasmine" title="Jasmine">jasmine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daffodil" class="mw-redirect" title="Daffodil">daffodil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum" title="Chrysanthemum">chrysanthemum</a> have also been held as possible floral symbols of China.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Denmark_2">Denmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=80" title="Edit section: Denmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Denmark has no official floral emblem. The <a href="/wiki/Bellis_perennis" title="Bellis perennis">daisy</a> won an unofficial competition on a national flower in the 1980s, but it was not officially adopted. In 1936, the Danish foreign office responded to Argentina that it would be the <a href="/wiki/Red_clover" class="mw-redirect" title="Red clover">red clover</a>, due to its significance in agriculture. The letter is obscure and was soon forgotten.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> Denmark has never used a floral emblem.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecuador">Ecuador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=81" title="Edit section: Ecuador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>No flower has been officially declared as a national symbol. Unofficially the <a href="/wiki/Rose" title="Rose">rose</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Orchid" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchid">orchid</a> are claimed to hold that title.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Egypt">Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=82" title="Edit section: Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Nymphaea_caerulea" title="Nymphaea caerulea">Blue Egyptian Lotus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nymphaea_lotus" title="Nymphaea lotus">White Egyptian Lotus</a> are regarded as <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>\'s national flowers, and they appear in many <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Ancient Egyptian</a> paintings and monuments.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>While the Netherlands does not have an official national flower, the tulip is widely considered to be its national flower.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vietnam">Vietnam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Floral_emblem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=85" title="Edit section: Vietnam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>While Vietnam does not have an official flower, four plants are traditional regarded as the <i>four graceful plants</i>, namely: the lotus, the pine, bamboo, and the chrysanthemum.<sup id="cite_ref-ASEANnationalflowers_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASEANnationalflowers-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> The lotus (<i>Nelumbo nucifera</i>) is generally regarded as the unofficial national flower of Vietnam,<sup id="cite_ref-77" href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>  In Vietnamese tradition, the lotus is regarded as <i>the symbol of purity, commitment and optimism for the future.</i><sup id="cite_ref-ASEANnationalflowers_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASEANnationalflowers-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
