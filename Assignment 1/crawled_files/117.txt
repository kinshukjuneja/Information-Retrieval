href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, England. Its council is based in <a href="/wiki/Barnstaple" title="Barnstaple">Barnstaple</a>. Other towns and villages in the North Devon District include <a href="/wiki/Braunton" title="Braunton">Braunton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fremington,_Devon" title="Fremington, Devon">Fremington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilfracombe" title="Ilfracombe">Ilfracombe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Instow" title="Instow">Instow</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Molton" title="South Molton">South Molton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lynton" title="Lynton">Lynton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lynmouth" title="Lynmouth">Lynmouth</a>. The district was formed on 1 April 1974 as a merger of the Barnstaple <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a>, the Ilfracombe and Lynton <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a>, and the Barnstaple and South Molton <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural districts</a>.\n</p><p>The wider geographic area of North Devon is divided between North Devon District and the district of <a href="/wiki/Torridge" title="Torridge">Torridge</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Bideford" title="Bideford">Bideford</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>North Devon is popular with retired people. The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 census</a> showed that 18% of residents were aged 15 years and under, 60% were aged 16\xe2\x80\x9364 and 23% were aged 65 and over. This compares to the 20% of the population who were aged 65 and over when the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 census</a> was taken. For comparison, the same age distributions across England were 19%, 64% and 17% respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Life expectancy for men, at 77.7, is close to the English average. Female life expectancy is good at 83.1, around 1 year above the English average.<sup id="cite_ref-apho.org.uk_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apho.org.uk-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There is a gap of 6 years in the life expectancy of men in the least deprived fifth of <a href="/wiki/Ward_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ward (politics)">wards</a> and the most deprived fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-apho.org.uk_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apho.org.uk-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\nThe region has one of the most ethnically homogenous populations in England, with 97.9% reporting their ethnicity as \'white\' in the 2011 census of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  However, this is a decrease on the 99.0% of the population who declared themselves to be White on the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 census</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/North_Devon_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="North Devon local elections">North Devon local elections</a></div>\n<p>North Devon District Council is elected every four years, with currently 43 <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">councillors</a> being elected at each election. From the first election to the council in 1973 to 1987 the council was controlled by <a href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent (politician)">independents</a>. This was followed by a period under <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrat</a> control until the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a> took control at the <a href="/wiki/North_Devon_Council_election,_2007" class="mw-redirect" title="North Devon Council election, 2007">2007 election</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/North_Devon_Council_election,_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="North Devon Council election, 2011">election in 2011</a> the Conservatives lost their majority <sup class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Along with its neighbours to the east and west, <a href="/wiki/Torridge" title="Torridge">Torridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Somerset" title="West Somerset">West Somerset</a>, the area has a fairly sparse transport infrastructure. The rump of the post-<a href="/wiki/Beeching_Axe" class="mw-redirect" title="Beeching Axe">Beeching</a> railway network has left a <a href="/wiki/Tarka_Line" title="Tarka Line">branch line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a> as the area\'s sole railway service. Despite being served only by one railway line, the borough is served by 5 railway stations, which is a large amount comparable with more urbanised boroughs such as <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mid_Devon" title="Mid Devon">Mid Devon</a>.\n</p><p>The region is served by three <i>A</i> roads. The primary link is the <a href="/wiki/A361_road" title="A361 road">A361</a> (known locally as the <i>Link Road</i>) which was constructed between 1986 and 1989. It heads north-west from the <a href="/wiki/M5_motorway" title="M5 motorway">M5 motorway</a>, past <a href="/wiki/South_Molton" title="South Molton">South Molton</a>, to Barnstaple, with the classification continuing then northwards along older roads to <a href="/wiki/Ilfracombe" title="Ilfracombe">Ilfracombe</a>. The modern <i>Link Road</i> continues westwards from Barnstaple as the <a href="/wiki/A39_road" title="A39 road">A39</a> where it is designated the <i>Atlantic Highway</i>, and runs via <a href="/wiki/Bideford" title="Bideford">Bideford</a> into <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>. The eastern section of the A39 links Barnstaple to <a href="/wiki/Lynton" title="Lynton">Lynton</a>, then traverses the northern coastal hills of <a href="/wiki/Exmoor" title="Exmoor">Exmoor</a> into <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>.\n</p><p>The other two <i>A</i> roads in North Devon are the <a href="/wiki/A399_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A399 road">A399</a>, a minor local route between Ilfracombe and South Molton (used as a <i>de facto</i> Barnstaple-bypass to <a href="/wiki/Ilfracombe" title="Ilfracombe">Ilfracombe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woolacombe" title="Woolacombe">Woolacombe</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/A377_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A377 road">A377</a>, which is the main road between Barnstaple and the <a href="/wiki/County_town" title="County town">county town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a>.\n</p><p>Due to significant peak time traffic delays in Barnstaple, coupled with severe congestion at both peak and non-peak times in the summer when tourist traffic is at its busiest, the <a href="/wiki/Barnstaple_Western_Bypass" title="Barnstaple Western Bypass">Barnstaple Western Bypass</a> was opened in 2007.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a class="mw-selflink selflink">North Devon</a> is some distance from the UK\'s traditional areas of industrial activity and population yet boasts some major manufacturing sites that export around the world. TDK Lambda and Pall Europe in Ilfracombe, Eaton Aerospace and Norbord in South Molton, Perrigo in Braunton as well as multiple businesses in Barnstaple. Due to the historically agricultural nature of the economy alongside a strong tourism industry employment can be seasonal, as a result some areas of North Devon and are considered deprived. The overall average income for the district is 80% of the average for the United Kingdom as a whole. \nWhilst the 1989 opening of the improved <a href="/wiki/A361_road" title="A361 road">A361</a> connection to the motorway network helped to promote trade, it had a temporary detrimental effect on a number of distribution businesses. The latter had previously viewed the town as a base for local distribution networks, a need that was removed with an approximate halving of travelling time to the <a href="/wiki/M5_motorway" title="M5 motorway">M5 motorway</a>. The region adapted, in 2005 unemployment in North Devon was 1.8\xe2\x80\x932.4% but in 2018 unemployment in North Devon had come down significantly since its 2010 high to 1.1%. Median weekly full time pay stands at \xc2\xa3440 per week, average house prices stand at \xc2\xa3230,000 and the number of businesses registered has increased to 4895 up 370 from 2010. 2018 has also seen significant Government investment in the area through Coastal Community grants, Housing Infrastructure funds as well at \xc2\xa383 million to further upgrade the North Devon Link Road. [26] \n</p><p><br />\nBecause Barnstaple is the main shopping area for North Devon, retail work is a contributor to the economy. There are many generic <a href="/wiki/Chain_stores" class="mw-redirect" title="Chain stores">chain stores</a> in the town centre and in the Roundswell Business Park, on the western fringe of the town. <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> has several stores in the area, which includes a <a href="/wiki/Tesco_Extra" class="mw-redirect" title="Tesco Extra">Tesco Extra</a> hypermarket, a large Tesco <a href="/wiki/Superstore" class="mw-redirect" title="Superstore">superstore</a>. There is also a <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury\'s</a> superstore, a <a href="/wiki/Lidl" title="Lidl">Lidl</a> supermarket. And, the multi-million pound redevelopment of the former <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leaderflush_Shapland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leaderflush Shapland (page does not exist)">Leaderflush Shapland</a> works at Anchorwood Bank, and the surrounding area, is creating a conservation area near the River Taw, hundreds of new homes, a commercial retail area with new shops, restaurants and leisure facilities. A new <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a> superstore and petrol filling station is part of the redevelopment. The whole scheme will be completed by the end of 2017, and the new Asda store opened in November 2016. On the same side of the riverbank a new housing development and Leisure Centre is planned to start in 2019. However, the largest employer in the region is local and central Government. The two main government employers in the area are the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Marines_Base_Chivenor" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Marines Base Chivenor">Royal Marines Base Chivenor</a>, 3 miles (4.8&#160;km) west of the town, and <a href="/wiki/North_Devon_District_Hospital" title="North Devon District Hospital">North Devon District Hospital</a>, 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) to the north.\n</p><p><br />\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg/250px-Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg" width="250" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg/375px-Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg/500px-Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ilfracombe.view1.arp.750pix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ilfracombe, seen from 447 feet (136 metres) above.
