This was the 20th <a href="/wiki/Census_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Census in the United Kingdom">UK census</a> and recorded a resident population of 58,789,194.\n</p><p>The 2001 UK census was organised by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a> (ONS) in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a>, the <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_for_Scotland" title="General Register Office for Scotland">General Register Office for Scotland</a> (GROS) and the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Statistics_and_Research_Agency" title="Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency">Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency</a> (NISRA). Detailed results by region, council area, ward and <a href="/wiki/ONS_coding_system" title="ONS coding system">output area</a> are available from their respective websites.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Similar to previous UK censuses, the 2001 census was organised by the three statistical agencies, ONS, GROS, and NISRA, and coordinated at the national level by the Office for National Statistics. The <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council#Orders_in_Council_as_Statutory_Instruments" title="Order in Council">Orders in Council</a> to conduct the census, specifying the people and information to be included in the census, were made under the authority of the <a href="/wiki/Census_Act_1920" title="Census Act 1920">Census Act 1920</a> in Great Britain, and the <a href="/wiki/Census_Act_(Northern_Ireland)_1969" title="Census Act (Northern Ireland) 1969">Census Act (Northern Ireland) 1969</a> in Northern Ireland. In England and Wales these regulations were made by the Census Order 2000 (SI 744/2000), in Scotland by the Census (Scotland) Order 2000 (SSI 68/2000), and in Northern Ireland by the Census Order (Northern Ireland) 2000 (SRNI 168/2000).<sup id="cite_ref-definitions_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-definitions-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The census was administered through self-completion forms, in most cases delivered by <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Special:Search/enumerator" class="extiw" title="wikt:Special:Search/enumerator">enumerators</a> to households and communal establishments in the three weeks before census night on 29 April. For the first time return by post was used as the main collection method, with enumerators following up in person where the forms were not returned. The postal response rate was 88% in England and Wales,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 91% in Scotland,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and 92% in Northern Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A total of 81,000 field staff were employed across the UK (70,000 in England and Wales, 8,000 in Scotland and 3,000 in Northern Ireland).<sup id="cite_ref-definitions_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-definitions-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The census was conducted at the height of the <a href="/wiki/2001_United_Kingdom_foot-and-mouth_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 United Kingdom foot-and-mouth crisis">foot-and-mouth crisis</a>, which led to extra precautions being adopted by the field staff, and suggestions that the census may have to be postponed.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, it was reported that the disease outbreak did not affect the effectiveness of the collection process.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The census was estimated to cost \xc2\xa3259m over its 13-year cycle from the start of planning in 1993 to the delivery of final results in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-vidler_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidler-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Printing of the 30 million census forms was subcontracted to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Polestar_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Polestar Group (page does not exist)">Polestar Group</a>, and processing of the returned census forms was subcontracted to <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">Lockheed Martin</a> in a contract worth \xc2\xa354m.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The forms were initially scanned into digital format, then read with <a href="/wiki/Optical_mark_recognition" title="Optical mark recognition">OMR</a> and <a href="/wiki/Optical_character_recognition" title="Optical character recognition">OCR</a>, with manual entry where the automatic process could not read the forms. The forms were then <a href="/wiki/Pulp_(paper)" title="Pulp (paper)">pulped</a> and recycled, and the digital copies printed onto <a href="/wiki/Microfilm" class="mw-redirect" title="Microfilm">microfilm</a> for storage and release after 100 years. Once the data were returned to the statistics agencies it underwent further processing to ensure consistency and to impute missing values.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Enumeration">Enumeration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Enumeration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The overall response rate for the census, that is the proportion of the population who were included on a census form, was estimated to be 94% in England and Wales,<sup id="cite_ref-ons-response_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ons-response-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 96.1% in Scotland<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and 95.2% in Northern Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This was due to a number of factors: households with no response, households excluding residents from their returns, and addresses not included in the enumeration. In Manchester for example 25,000 people from 14,000 addresses were not enumerated because the address database was two years out of date.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Local Authority with the lowest response was <a href="/wiki/Royal_Borough_of_Kensington_and_Chelsea" title="Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea">Kensington and Chelsea</a> with 64%. <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Hackney" title="London Borough of Hackney">Hackney</a> had the next lowest response at 72%. Out of all local authorities, the ten lowest response rates were all in London.<sup id="cite_ref-ons-response_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ons-response-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The results still represent 100 per cent of the population, however, because some individuals not completing their forms were instead identified by census enumerators, and through the use of cross-matching with a follow-up survey.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="One_Number_Census">One Number Census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: One Number Census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The results from the 2001 census were produced using a methodology known as the One Number Census.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> This was an attempt to adjust the census counts and impute answers to allow for estimated under-enumeration measured by the Census Coverage Survey (sample size 320,000 households), resulting in a single set of <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> estimates.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Although the 1851 census had included a question about religion on a separate response sheet, whose completion was not compulsory, the 2001 census was the first in Great Britain to ask about the <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> of respondents on the main census form.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="date-April 2009 (April 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> An amendment to the 1920 Census Act (the <a href="/wiki/Census_(Amendment)_Act_2000" title="Census (Amendment) Act 2000">Census (Amendment) Act 2000</a>) was passed by Parliament to allow the question to be asked, and to allow the response to this question to be optional.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The inclusion of the question enabled the <a href="/wiki/Jedi_census_phenomenon" title="Jedi census phenomenon">Jedi census phenomenon</a> to take place in the United Kingdom. In England and Wales 390,127 people stated their religion as <a href="/wiki/Jediism" title="Jediism">Jedi</a>, as did 14,052 people in Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalstatistics_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalstatistics-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\nThe percentages of religious affiliations were:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>: 72.0%</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>: 3%</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a>: 1%</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Sikh" title="Sikh">Sikh</a>: 0.6%</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>: 0.5%</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a>: 0.3%</li>\n<li>Any other religion: 0.3%</li></ul>\n<p>15% declared themselves of <a href="/wiki/Irreligion" title="Irreligion">no religion</a> (including Jedi at 0.7%, so more than declared themselves as Sikh, Jewish or Buddhist) and 8% did not respond to the question.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Neopaganism">Neopaganism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Neopaganism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After the 2001 census it became clear that the statistics for those adhering to the <a href="/wiki/Neopaganism" class="mw-redirect" title="Neopaganism">Neopagan</a> group of religions were inaccurately recorded. This was caused by a dilution of statistics, with some adherents entering "Pagan" and others entering their individual religions such as "<a href="/wiki/Wicca" title="Wicca">Wiccan</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Neo-druidism" class="mw-redirect" title="Neo-druidism">Druid</a>", which fall under the umbrella term of "Pagan", leaving a significant number of people apparently unaccounted for. The situation was worsened when the <a href="/wiki/Germanic_Neopaganism" class="mw-redirect" title="Germanic Neopaganism">Heathenism</a> statistics were grouped in with <a href="/wiki/Atheism" title="Atheism">Atheism</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Pagan_Federation" title="Pagan Federation">Pagan Federation</a> and the "PaganDash" campaign lobbied for a separate class="mw-headline" id="English_identity">English identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: English identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Controversy surrounding the classification of ethnic groups began as early as 2000, when it was revealed that respondents in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> would be able to check a box describing themselves as Scottish or Irish, an option not available for English respondents.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> With an absence of an English tick-box, the only other tickbox available was "white-British", "Irish", or "other". However, if \'English\' was written in under the "any other white background" it was not clear whether it would be counted as an ethnic group in same the way as the Welsh.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Following criticism, English was included as a tick-box option in the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Welsh_identity">Welsh identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Welsh identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>It is sometimes claimed that the 2001 census revealed that two-thirds of the population of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> described themselves as of <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh nationality</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc20030214_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20030214-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In fact, the 2001 census did not collect any information on nationality. Controversy surrounding the classification of ethnic group began as early as 2000, when it was revealed that respondents in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> would be able to check a box describing themselves as Scottish or Irish, an option not available for Welsh respondents.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc20000923_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20000923-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the census, <i><a href="/wiki/Plaid_Cymru" title="Plaid Cymru">Plaid Cymru</a></i> backed a petition calling for the inclusion of a Welsh tickbox and for the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Wales">National Assembly of Wales</a> to have primary law-making powers and its own National Statistics Office.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc20000923_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20000923-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> With an absence of a Welsh tickbox, the only other tickbox available was "white-British", "Irish", or "other".<sup id="cite_ref-bbc20030214_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20030214-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc20000923_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc20000923-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cornish_identity">Cornish identity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2001&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cornish identity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>For the first time in a UK census, those wishing to describe their ethnicity as <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish</a> were given their own code number (06) on the 2001 UK census form, alongside those for people wishing to describe themselves as English, <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a>, Irish or Scottish. About 34,000 people in Cornwall and 3,500 people in the rest of the UK wrote on their census forms in 2001 that they considered their ethnic group to be Cornish.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> This represented nearly 7% of the population of Cornwall.
