class="dtstart">2018-08-24</span>&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 <span class="dtend">2018-08-26</span>)</span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Players Weekend</b> is an annual <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) event in which players on all 30 MLB teams wear colorful <a href="/wiki/Baseball_uniform" title="Baseball uniform">baseball uniforms</a> based on youth sports designs and sport nicknames on the back of their jerseys during regular season games. Players also wear brightly-colored and custom-designed gear. The event is designed to give players the opportunity to express their personal style, appeal to the youth demographic, and acquaint hometown fans with newer team members.\n</p><p>The inaugural Players Weekend took place on the weekend of August 25\xe2\x80\x9327, 2017. Players were encouraged, but not required, to wear nicknames on the back of their jerseys in place of their <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">last names</a> (or, in the case of <a href="/wiki/Ichiro_Suzuki" title="Ichiro Suzuki">Ichiro Suzuki</a>, his given name). All uniforms, whether or not the players chose to use nicknames, had names on the back\xe2\x80\x94including those of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, a team that had never before placed names on the back of any <a href="/wiki/Logos_and_uniforms_of_the_New_York_Yankees" title="Logos and uniforms of the New York Yankees">official jersey</a>. The league also relaxed the rules for <a href="/wiki/Cleat_(shoe)#Baseball" title="Cleat (shoe)">cleats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Batting_glove" title="Batting glove">batting gloves</a>, wristbands, compression sleeves, <a href="/wiki/Catcher" title="Catcher">catcher</a>\'s masks, and <a href="/wiki/Baseball_bat" title="Baseball bat">bats</a>, allowing players to use brightly colored and custom-designed gear.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" href="/wiki/CKE_Restaurants" title="CKE Restaurants">the chain\'s parent company</a> uses the <a href="/wiki/Hardee%27s" title="Hardee&#39;s">Hardee\'s</a> name east of the Mississippi).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Game-used jerseys were sold after the event, with proceeds benefiting the MLB-MLBPA Youth Development Foundation.<sup id="cite_ref-mlb_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Players were also allowed to wear T-shirts sporting the name and logo of the charity of their choice during <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_baseball_(B)#batting_practice" title="Glossary of baseball (B)">batting practice</a>, pregame workouts, and post-game interviews.<sup id="cite_ref-cbssports_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbssports-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mlb_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> debuted their Players Weekend uniforms earlier in the week for their August 20 game in the <a href="/wiki/2017_MLB_Little_League_Classic" title="2017 MLB Little League Classic">MLB Little League Classic</a> in <a href="/wiki/Williamsport,_Pennsylvania" title="Williamsport, Pennsylvania">Williamsport, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mlb_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>For the 2018 event, <a href="/wiki/Brad_Boxberger" title="Brad Boxberger">Brad Boxberger</a> had his name on the back of his jersey, but in the form of <a href="/wiki/Emoji" title="Emoji">emojis</a> instead of letters.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caps_and_socks">Caps and socks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Players_Weekend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Caps and socks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/New_Era_Cap_Company" title="New Era Cap Company">New Era Cap Company</a> supplied caps.<sup id="cite_ref-mlb_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Socks in multicolored hues were the same for all 30 teams; players had the option of rolling up the cuffs of their pants to show off the design.<sup id="cite_ref-lukas_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lukas-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The socks were supplied by <a href="/wiki/Stance_(brand)" title="Stance (brand)">Stance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mlb_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Cleats.2C_batting_gloves.2C_bats.2C_and_other_gear"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cleats,_batting_gloves,_bats,_and_other_gear">Cleats, batting gloves, bats, and other gear</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Players_Weekend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cleats, batting gloves, bats, and other gear">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Players were allowed to wear brightly colored and custom-designed <a href="/wiki/Cleat_(shoe)#Baseball" title="Cleat (shoe)">cleats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Batting_glove" title="Batting glove">batting gloves</a>, wristbands, compression sleeves, and <a href="/wiki/Catcher" title="Catcher">catcher</a>\'s masks that are typically not permitted under MLB rules.<sup id="cite_ref-mlb_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cbssports_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbssports-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lukas_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lukas-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The league did prohibit the use of white as a color for the batting gloves, wristbands, and compression sleeves, since it might interfere with umpires\' ability to judge a play.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Players could also custom-design their <a href="/wiki/Baseball_bat" title="Baseball bat">bats</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sam_Bat" title="Sam Bat">Sam Bat</a>, a bat supplier to the league, produced dozens of custom-ordered bats for the weekend that were painted with different colors, images, and country flags.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Curtis_Granderson" title="Curtis Granderson">Curtis Granderson</a> raffled off his three pairs of customized cleats online; anyone who donated at least $50 to his charity, Grand Kids Foundation, during the weekend was eligible to win.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Players_Weekend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Players Weekend was hailed by MLB players as a long-overdue opportunity to express their personal style on the field.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/SB_Nation" title="SB Nation">SB Nation</a>, 58 players across the league declined to choose a nickname, instead using their first or last name on their jerseys.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> outfielder <a href="/wiki/Brett_Gardner" title="Brett Gardner">Brett Gardner</a>, whose team had never had a name on the back of the jersey, wanted no name at all, but he was overruled; he ended up printing his surname.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> While most players enjoyed custom designing their cleats and other gear, <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Chafin" title="Andrew Chafin">Andrew Chafin</a> opted to wear his regular black cleats.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span
