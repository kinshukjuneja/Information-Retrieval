Coast AONB, in green.</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Solway Coast</b> is a designated <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It incorporates two areas of coastline along the <a href="/wiki/Solway_Firth" title="Solway Firth">Solway Firth</a>, the first running from just north of the city of <a href="/wiki/Carlisle,_Cumbria" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlisle, Cumbria">Carlisle</a>, at the estuary of the rivers <a href="/wiki/River_Esk,_Dumfries_and_Galloway" title="River Esk, Dumfries and Galloway">Esk</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Eden,_Cumbria" title="River Eden, Cumbria">Eden</a>, in a westerly direction as far as \n<a href="/wiki/Silloth-on-Solway" class="mw-redirect" title="Silloth-on-Solway">Silloth-on-Solway</a>, including the villages of <a href="/wiki/Bowness-on-Solway" title="Bowness-on-Solway">Bowness-on-Solway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burgh-by-Sands" class="mw-redirect" title="Burgh-by-Sands">Burgh-by-Sands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Carlisle" title="Port Carlisle">Port Carlisle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Skinburness" title="Skinburness">Skinburness</a>. The second area begins just north of the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Beckfoot" title="Beckfoot">Beckfoot</a>, and runs south down the coast to the southern end of <a href="/wiki/Allonby_Bay" title="Allonby Bay">Allonby Bay</a> near the village of <a href="/wiki/Crosscanonby" title="Crosscanonby">Crosscanonby</a>. Included in this area are the villages of <a href="/wiki/Mawbray" title="Mawbray">Mawbray</a> and <a href="/wiki/Allonby" title="Allonby">Allonby</a>, and the hamlets of <a href="/wiki/Dubmill" title="Dubmill">Dubmill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hailforth" title="Hailforth">Hailforth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salta,_Cumbria" title="Salta, Cumbria">Salta</a>. The hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Wolsty" title="Wolsty">Wolsty</a> lies just outside the AONB.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Beginning at Silloth,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/B5300_road" title="B5300 road">B5300 coast road</a> runs in a south-westerly direction, entering the Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty just north of Beckfoot, and exiting near Crosscanonby. As indicated by its local name, the road sticks close to the coast, and travels the entire length of the southern section of the Solway Coast AONB.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solway_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty in England and Wales are designated as such because of their beautiful scenery.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The designating body is <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a>, and the reason for the Solway Coast\'s designation was to conserve what is considered one of the most scenic sections of English coastline.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solway_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG/220px-Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG" width="220" height="93" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG/330px-Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG/440px-Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3263" data-file-height="1375" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Robin_Rigg_from_Allonby.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Robin_Rigg_Wind_Farm" title="Robin Rigg Wind Farm">Robin Rigg wind farm</a> (pictured here while still under construction in 2009) has proven controversial with residents and supporters of the AONB.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Solway Coast was designated an AONB because of its scenic vistas. However, the massive increase in construction of <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbines" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind turbines">wind turbines</a>, both off-shore and on-shore, has left many residents feeling that the area\'s natural beauty is being tainted. The section of coast between <a href="/wiki/Silloth-on-Solway" class="mw-redirect" title="Silloth-on-Solway">Silloth-on-Solway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sellafield" title="Sellafield">Sellafield</a> has recently been dubbed "Britain\'s Energy Coast",<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> something which many see as being incompatible with the Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. However, the increase in investment brought by the establishment of the Energy Coast, as well as the promise of new jobs for an area which has seen higher than average unemployment following the closure of mines and factories in the 1970s and 1980s, has proven popular with many residents of the area.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solway_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty is not designed to conserve wildlife or plants, a side-effect of conserving the landscape is the protection of the habitats of several species. Multiple species of butterfly, including some rare specimens, live on <a href="/wiki/Mawbray" title="Mawbray">Mawbray</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolsty" title="Wolsty">Wolsty</a> banks,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> a designated Site of Special Scientific Interest within the Solway Coast AONB. Additionally, Mawbray and Wolsty banks along with Silloth dunes to the north are home to the very rare <a href="/wiki/Natterjack_toad" title="Natterjack toad">natterjack toad</a> <i>Bufo calamita</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Great_crested_newt" class="mw-redirect" title="Great crested newt">great crested newt</a>\n<i>Triturus cristatus</i>. These sand dunes are among only three in Cumbria, and are doubly protected by their status as an SSSI and an AONB.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre">Solway Coast Discovery Centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solway_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solway Coast Discovery Centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg/220px-Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg" width="220" height="86" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg/330px-Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg/440px-Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="249" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Solway_Coast_Discovery_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_40355.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Solway Coast Discovery Centre in Silloth.</div></div></div> \n<p>In the town of Silloth-on-Solway is the Solway Coast Discovery Centre, an exhibition detailing the geography and history of the Solway Coast AONB. As of 2014, the centre attracts around 12,000 visitors per year, and also provides tourist information for other attractions within the AONB.\n</p><p>The main exhibition in the discovery centre looks at the wildlife, geography, and communities along the Solway Coast, and how things have changed over time, from the last ice age to the present day. This includes looking at the arrival of various groups which conquered the region, including the Romans, Vikings, and Normans, and how the establishment of the Abbey at nearby Abbeytown had an effect on the coastal region.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The exhibition was designed to be interactive. There is also an art exhibition, where local artists showcase their work, as well as a cafe and a tourist information office.\n</p><p>To commemorate the 40th anniversary of the Solway Coast\'s designation as an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty, a film was produced and is available on DVD at the Discovery Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Discovery Centre opened in 2002, and was funded by a number of local, national, and trans-national agencies, including <a href="/wiki/Cumbria_County_Council" title="Cumbria County Council">Cumbria County Council</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Nuclear_Fuels" class="mw-redirect" title="British Nuclear Fuels">British Nuclear Fuels</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/European_Regional_Development_Fund" title="European Regional Development Fund">European Regional Development Fund</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solway_Coast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.solwaycoastaonb.org.uk/info.php">"Solway Coast AONB - Contact, directions, Other information (map)"</a><span class="reference-accessdate">.
