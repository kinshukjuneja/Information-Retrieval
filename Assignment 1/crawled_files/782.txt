\n<p><b>Borough of Poole</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> responsible for <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_England" title="Local government in England">local government</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Poole" title="Poole">Poole</a>, Dorset, England. It was created on 1 April 1997 following a review by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Commission_for_England_(1992)" title="Local Government Commission for England (1992)">Local Government Commission for England (1992)</a>, becoming administratively independent from <a href="/wiki/Dorset_County_Council" title="Dorset County Council">Dorset County Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its council comprises 16 wards and 42 councillors and is controlled by a <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_administration" title="Public administration">administration</a>.\n</p><p>In February 2018 the \'Future Dorset\' plan was approved by the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Housing,_Communities_and_Local_Government" title="Secretary of State for Housing, Communities and Local Government">Secretary of State for Housing, Communities and Local Government</a> <a href="/wiki/Sajid_Javid" title="Sajid Javid">Sajid Javid</a>, which means that <a href="/wiki/Bournemouth_Borough_Council" title="Bournemouth Borough Council">Bournemouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a> and Poole borough councils will be merged into one <a href="/wiki/Bournemouth,_Christchurch_and_Poole" title="Bournemouth, Christchurch and Poole">singular unitary authority</a> in April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input elections</a>, 42 <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">councillors</a> are elected across 16 <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">wards</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and elections take place every four years. The last election took place in May 2015, resulting in a Conservative majority.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Mayor is Ann Stribley, a Conservative councillor for Parkstone ward since 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Council is made up of 32 Conservative, 6 <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrat</a> 3 Poole People and 1 UKIP councillors. Following these elections, a Conservative administration was formed <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> with a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_(government)" title="Cabinet (government)">cabinet</a> of seven councillors who are responsible for deciding how the Council\'s strategies and policies are implemented and how the budget is spent.\nThe Council Leader is Janet Walton (Conservative).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Mayor is Ann Stribley, a Conservative councillor for Parkstone ward since 1977.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sheriff" title="Sheriff">Sheriff</a>, a position created by the town\'s charter of 1568 and
