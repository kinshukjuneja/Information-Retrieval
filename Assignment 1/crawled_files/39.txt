href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> is a protected landscape in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>, England.  It covers over 450 km<sup>2</sup> of coastal and agricultural land from <a href="/wiki/The_Wash" title="The Wash">The Wash</a> in the west through coastal marshes and cliffs to <a href="/wiki/Winterton_Dunes" title="Winterton Dunes">the sand dunes at Winterton</a> in the east.  It was designated <a href="/wiki/AONB" class="mw-redirect" title="AONB">AONB</a> in 1968, under the <a href="/wiki/National_Parks_and_Access_to_the_Countryside_Act_1949" title="National Parks and Access to the Countryside Act 1949">National Parks and Access to the Countryside Act 1949</a>. \n</p><p>The area includes; <a href="/wiki/Hunstanton" title="Hunstanton">Hunstanton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wells-next-the-Sea" title="Wells-next-the-Sea">Wells-next-the-Sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blakeney,_Norfolk" title="Blakeney, Norfolk">Blakeney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheringham" title="Sheringham">Sheringham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cromer" title="Cromer">Cromer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mundesley" title="Mundesley">Mundesley</a>. The AONB boundary on the seaward side is the mean <a href="/wiki/Low_water" class="mw-redirect" title="Low water">low water</a> mark, corresponding to the limit of the planning authority of its local authority partners. The terrain behind the coast is rolling <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a> land and glacial <a href="/wiki/Moraine" title="Moraine">moraine</a>, including the almost 300 foot (90m) high <a href="/wiki/Cromer_Ridge" title="Cromer Ridge">Cromer Ridge</a>. \n</p><p>Nature reserves in the area include two <a href="/wiki/National_nature_reserves_in_Norfolk" title="National nature reserves in Norfolk">National Nature Reserves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blakeney_Point" title="Blakeney Point">Blakeney Point</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Winterton_Dunes" title="Winterton Dunes">Winterton Dunes</a> (one of the country\'s finest <a href="/wiki/Dune" title="Dune">dune</a> systems). The <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Coast_(England_and_Wales)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage Coast (England and Wales)">Heritage Coast</a> stretch of the AONB is a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>, a candidate <a href="/wiki/Special_Area_of_Conservation" title="Special Area of Conservation">Special Area of Conservation</a> (SAC) and a <a href="/wiki/Special_Protection_Area" title="Special Protection Area">Special Protection Area</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Peddars_Way" title="Peddars Way">Peddars Way</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Coast_Path" title="Norfolk Coast Path">Norfolk Coast Path</a> National Trail pass through the AONB. \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norfolk_Coast_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>East of <a href="/wiki/Weybourne,_Norfolk" title="Weybourne, Norfolk">Weybourne</a> there is severe coastal erosion. <a href="/wiki/Managed_retreat" title="Managed retreat">Managed retreat</a> is likely to be the long-term solution to rising sea levels along much of the rest of the North Norfolk coast,<sup id="cite_ref-EA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norfolk_Coast_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-EA-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-EA_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20121216153828/http://environment-agency.gov.uk/research/planning/108980.aspx">"Long term planning: North Norfolk coast"</a>. <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a>.
