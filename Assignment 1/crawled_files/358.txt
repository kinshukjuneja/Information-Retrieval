class="mw-headline" id="Clinic_history">Clinic history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogenesis_baseball_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Clinic history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Biogenesis of America was a health clinic briefly operating in <a href="/wiki/Coral_Gables,_Florida" title="Coral Gables, Florida">Coral Gables, Florida</a>, specializing in <a href="/wiki/Weight_loss" title="Weight loss">weight loss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hormone_replacement_therapy" title="Hormone replacement therapy">hormone replacement therapy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was first registered in state corporation records in March 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-fish_01282013_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fish_01282013-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and was founded by Anthony Bosch (also listed as the <a href="/wiki/Program_director" title="Program director">program director</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-fish_01282013_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fish_01282013-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His father, Dr. Pedro Bosch, was listed as the <a href="/wiki/Medical_director" title="Medical director">medical director</a>, and Bosch\'s younger brother, attorney Ashley Bosch, was listed as managing member.<sup id="cite_ref-fish_01282013_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fish_01282013-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Porter Fischer was listed as marketing director.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Several employees quit in the fall of 2012 after they were not paid,<sup id="cite_ref-fish_01282013_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fish_01282013-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the clinic closed months later in December 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-elfrink_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elfrink-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accusations_and_investigation">Accusations and investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogenesis_baseball_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accusations and investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On January 22, 2013, the <i><a href="/wiki/Miami_New_Times" title="Miami New Times">Miami New Times</a></i> obtained documents from former Biogenesis employee Porter Fisher which it said linked three players \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Melky_Cabrera" title="Melky Cabrera">Melky Cabrera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bartolo_Col%C3%B3n" title="Bartolo Col\xc3\xb3n">Bartolo Col\xc3\xb3n</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yasmani_Grandal" title="Yasmani Grandal">Yasmani Grandal</a> \xe2\x80\x93 who had tested positive for performance-enhancing drugs in 2012 to the clinic. Additionally, the paper said several star players including <a href="/wiki/Alex_Rodriguez" title="Alex Rodriguez">Alex Rodriguez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Braun" title="Ryan Braun">Ryan Braun</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Cruz" title="Nelson Cruz">Nelson Cruz</a> could be tied to the clinic. The paper, however, refused to hand the documents over to Major League Baseball (MLB) authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Florida Department of Health, and MLB, both targeted the clinic\'s owner, Anthony Bosch, each separately taking action against him.\n</p><p>In March, MLB sued Bosch, and his business partners, Carlos Acevedo, Ricardo Martinez, Marcelo Albir, and Paulo da Silveira in an attempt to obtain information. The suit alleged that the six had "actively participated in a scheme&#160;... to solicit or induce Major League players to purchase or obtain PES (performing-enhancing substances)".<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Subsequently, the MLB claimed to have found evidence that a representative of Rodriguez had purchased his medical records. It then paid a former Biogenesis employee for documents.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April, Bosch received a complaint from the Florida Department of Health for practicing medicine without a license. The complaint urged him to sign a cease and desist agreement.\n</p><p>In May, Bosch agreed to work with MLB investigators in exchange for his name being removed from the lawsuit.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> MLB conducted a large number of interviews with players it believed may be connected with Biogenesis in June. Every player interviewed was provided legal counsel by the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association" title="Major League Baseball Players Association">Major League Baseball Players Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August, <a href="/wiki/Wifredo_A._Ferrer" title="Wifredo A. Ferrer">Wifredo A. Ferrer</a>, U.S. attorney for the southern district of Florida, announced that Bosch intended to plead guilty to one charge of conspiracy to distribute testosterone.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Player_suspensions">Player suspensions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogenesis_baseball_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Player suspensions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On July 22, 2013, MLB suspended <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a> player <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Braun" title="Ryan Braun">Ryan Braun</a> for the remainder of the 2013 season (65 games and the postseason) for his involvement with the Biogenesis clinic. Braun, who lost $3.25 million as a result, did not appeal the suspension. ESPN reported that Braun decided to "strike a deal" with MLB after being presented with the evidence against him.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Braun had previously tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Testosterone_(medication)" title="Testosterone (medication)">testosterone</a> in December 2011, but maintained his innocence and ultimately avoided suspension for that violation on a technicality that his test sample had been improperly handled.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 5, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Rodriguez" title="Alex Rodriguez">Alex Rodriguez</a> was suspended through the 2014 season (211 games at the time of the decision), but was allowed to play in 2013 pending his appeal of that decision. An arbitrator later upheld the suspension in January 2014, after being allowed to play in the 49 games between the decision and the hearing, technically reducing the suspension to 162 games, representing the entire 2014 regular season and postseason.  Twelve other players connected to the Biogenesis case agreed to 50-game suspensions without the right to appeal: <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Bastardo" title="Antonio Bastardo">Antonio Bastardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Everth_Cabrera" title="Everth Cabrera">Everth Cabrera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Cervelli" title="Francisco Cervelli">Francisco Cervelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Cruz" title="Nelson Cruz">Nelson Cruz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fautino_de_los_Santos" title="Fautino de los Santos">Fautino de los Santos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Escalona" title="Sergio Escalona">Sergio Escalona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Mart%C3%ADnez_(baseball)" title="Fernando Mart\xc3\xadnez (baseball)">Fernando Mart\xc3\xadnez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Montero" title="Jes\xc3\xbas Montero">Jes\xc3\xbas Montero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Norberto" title="Jordan Norberto">Jordan Norberto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jhonny_Peralta" title="Jhonny Peralta">Jhonny Peralta</a>, <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Puello" title="C\xc3\xa9sar Puello">C\xc3\xa9sar Puello</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jordany_Valdespin" title="Jordany Valdespin">Jordany Valdespin</a>. Cabrera, Cruz, and Peralta were <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Stars</a> in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Rodriguez, who received the longest suspension of all the players linked to Biogenesis, was punished for "his use and possession of numerous forms of prohibited performance-enhancing substances, including testosterone and human growth hormone, over the course of multiple years" and "for his attempts to cover up those violations and obstruct a league investigation", according to MLB.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The 13 player suspensions are the most to be imposed simultaneously in the history of organized baseball, the previous record being <a href="/wiki/Kenesaw_Mountain_Landis" title="Kenesaw Mountain Landis">Kenesaw Mountain Landis</a>\' <a href="/wiki/Black_Sox_Scandal" title="Black Sox Scandal">banning of eight players for life</a> for <a href="/wiki/Match_fixing" title="Match fixing">throwing</a> the <a href="/wiki/1919_World_Series" title="1919 World Series">1919 World Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Melky Cabrera, Bartolo Col\xc3\xb3n, and Yasmani Grandal each had previously been suspended in 2012 and already served 50-game suspensions for their involvement with Biogenesis.<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Two players mentioned in Biogenesis documents, <a href="/wiki/Gio_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="Gio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez">Gio Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Valencia" title="Danny Valencia">Danny Valencia</a>, were cleared of any wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_suspensions">List of suspensions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span system<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Appeals">Appeals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogenesis_baseball_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Appeals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>All of the suspended players, with the exception of Rodriguez, reached agreement with the League on the length of that suspension, and as part of that agreement waived their contractual right to appeal it to an arbitrator.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Rodriguez was the only player who appealed his suspension. His appeal was heard by arbitrator Fredric Horowitz, who succeeded Shyam Das as baseball\'s designated arbitrator in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-Fredric_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fredric-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newsday_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Das was removed from his position as baseball\'s long-time arbitrator as a direct result of his overturning Braun\'s original 50-game suspension for PEDs.<sup id="cite_ref-Fredric_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fredric-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-newsday_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Horowitz ruled the original suspension from time of ruling until the end of the 2014 season would stand (though technically reduced from 211 to 162 games since he was allowed to play the 49 games between the ruling and the appeal), leaving Rodriguez\'s career in limbo.<sup id="cite_ref-Fredric_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fredric-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Rodriguez was allowed to play while his appeal was heard.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Players Association said it agreed with his decision to appeal, adding "We believe that the Commissioner has not acted appropriately under the Basic Agreement."<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogenesis_baseball_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>MLB commissioner <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a> remarked "We conducted a thorough, aggressive investigation guided by facts so that we could justly enforce our rules&#160;... we pursued this matter because it was not only the right thing to do, but the only thing to do."<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The other players involved all agreed to deals that included a waiver of the right to appeal.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Cruz blamed a gastrointestinal infection for his drug use and remarked that faced with the weight loss from the infection he was unsure he would be physically able to play and "made an error in judgment that I deeply regret, and I accept full responsibility for that error."<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> An emotional Cabrera said he had taken a banned substance for four days in 2012 to aid in injury recovering before stopping because "I realized it wasn\'t necessary. My heart and my conscience was killing me."<sup id="cite_ref-ESPN_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Peralta remarked "I take full responsibility for my
