navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The following persons served as Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, off the coast of Cornwall. An early governor of Scilly was <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Godolphin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thomas Godolphin (page does not exist)">Thomas Godolphin</a>, whose son <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin_(1540%E2%80%931608)" title="Francis Godolphin (1540\xe2\x80\x931608)">Francis</a> received a lease on the Isles in 1568. They were styled <i>Governors of Scilly</i> and the Godolphins and their Osborne relatives held this position until 1834. In 1834 <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus John Smith</a> acquired the lease from the Duchy for \xc2\xa320,000.<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Smith created the title <i>Lord Proprietor of the Isles of Scilly</i> for himself, and many of his actions were unpopular. The lease remained in his family until it expired for most of the Isles in 1920 when ownership reverted to the Duchy of Cornwall. Today, the Dorrien-Smith estate still holds the lease for the island of <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>.\n</p>\n<ul><li>1568\xe2\x80\x931608 <a
