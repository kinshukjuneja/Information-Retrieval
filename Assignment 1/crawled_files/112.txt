id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_League_Championship_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Prior to 1969, the American League champion (the "<a href="/wiki/List_of_American_League_pennant_winners" title="List of American League pennant winners">pennant winner</a>") was determined by the best win-loss record at the end of the regular season. There was one <i>ad hoc</i> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_tie-breakers" title="List of Major League Baseball tie-breakers">single-game playoff</a> held, in <a href="/wiki/1948_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1948 Major League Baseball season">1948</a>, due to a tie under this formulation. (The <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> had to resolve ties four times, but used three-game playoff series.)\n</p><p>The ALCS started in 1969, when the AL reorganized into two divisions, <a href="/wiki/American_League_East" title="American League East">East</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_League_West" title="American League West">West</a>. The winners of each division played each other in a best-of-five series to determine who would advance to the World Series. In 1985, the format changed to best-of-seven. \n</p><p>In 1981, a <a href="/wiki/1981_National_League_Division_Series" title="1981 National League Division Series">division series</a> was held due to a split season caused by a <a href="/wiki/1981_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1981 Major League Baseball strike">players\' strike</a>. \n</p><p>In 1994, the league was restructured into three divisions, with the three division winners and a wild-card team advancing to a best-of-five postseason round, known as the <a href="/wiki/American_League_Division_Series" title="American League Division Series">American League Division Series</a> (ALDS). The winners of that round then advanced to the best-of-seven ALCS. In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Playoff" class="mw-redirect" title="Playoff">playoffs</a>  were expanded again so that two wild card teams face off in a one-game wild card round to determine which team advances to the division series, with the playoffs then continuing as it had before 2012 (though with the possibility of a fifth seed being in the playoffs and a fourth seed being out) after the end of the wild card round. This is the system currently in use.\n</p><p>The ALCS and NLCS, since the expansion to best-of-seven, are always played in a 2\xe2\x80\x933\xe2\x80\x932 format: Games 1, 2, 6, and 7 are played in the stadium of the team that has <a href="/wiki/Home_field_advantage" class="mw-redirect" title="Home field advantage">home field advantage</a>, and Games 3, 4, and 5 are played in the stadium of the team that does not. The series concludes when one team records its fourth win. Since 1998, home field advantage has been given to the team that has the better regular season record, unless that team happens to be the Wild Card team. In that case, the other team gets home field advantage, because by rule the Wild Card team is never allowed home field advantage in a Division Series or LCS. In the event that both teams have identical records in the regular season, home field advantage goes to the team that has the winning head-to-head record. From 1969 to 1993, home field advantage alternated between the two divisions, and from 1995 to 1997 home field advantage was determined before the season.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>, an American League team between 1969 and 1997, and the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>, a National League team between 1962 and 2012, are the only franchises to play in both the ALCS and NLCS. The Astros are the only team to have won both an NLCS (<a href="/wiki/2005_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2005 National League Championship Series">2005</a>) and an ALCS (<a href="/wiki/2017_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2017 American League Championship Series">2017</a>). Every current American League franchise has appeared in the ALCS.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_Trophy">Championship Trophy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_League_Championship_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship Trophy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_awards#William_Harridge_Trophy" title="List of Major League Baseball awards">William Harridge Trophy</a> is awarded to the ALCS champion. The trophy\'s namesake comes from the <a href="/wiki/Will_Harridge" title="Will Harridge">American League president</a> from 1931 to 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Most_Valuable_Player_Award">Most Valuable Player Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_League_Championship_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Most Valuable Player Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dd><i>See: <a href="/wiki/League_Championship_Series_Most_Valuable_Player_Award#American_League_winners" title="League Championship Series Most Valuable Player Award">League Championship Series Most Valuable Player Award#American League winners</a></i></dd></dl>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lee_MacPhail#Lee_MacPhail_MVP_Award" title="Lee MacPhail">Lee MacPhail</a> Most Valuable Player (MVP) award is given to the outstanding player in the ALCS. No MVP award is given for Division Series play.\n</p><p>Although the National League began its LCS MVP award in 1977, the American League did not begin its LCS MVP award till 1980.
