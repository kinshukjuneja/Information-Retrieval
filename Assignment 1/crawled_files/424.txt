href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_(Royal_Navy)" title="Admiral of the Fleet (Royal Navy)">Admiral of the Fleet</a> <b>Sir Cloudesley Shovell</b> (c. November 1650 \xe2\x80\x93 22 October or 23 October 1707), was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> naval officer. As a junior officer he saw action at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Solebay" title="Battle of Solebay">Battle of Solebay</a> and then at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Texel" title="Battle of Texel">Battle of Texel</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Third Anglo-Dutch War">Third Anglo-Dutch War</a>. As a captain he fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bantry_Bay" title="Battle of Bantry Bay">Battle of Bantry Bay</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Williamite_War_in_Ireland" title="Williamite War in Ireland">Williamite War in Ireland</a>.\n</p><p>As a flag officer Shovell commanded a division at the <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Barfleur_and_La_Hogue" class="mw-redirect" title="Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue">Battle of Barfleur</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Years%27_War" title="Nine Years&#39; War">Nine Years\' War</a> and during the battle distinguished himself by being the first to break through the enemy\'s line. Along with Admiral <a href="/wiki/Henry_Killigrew_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="Henry Killigrew (Royal Navy officer)">Henry Killigrew</a> and Admiral <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Delaval" title="Ralph Delaval">Ralph Delaval</a>, Shovell was put in joint command of the fleet shortly afterwards.\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a> Shovell commanded a squadron which served under Admiral <a href="/wiki/George_Rooke" title="George Rooke">George Rooke</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Gibraltar" title="Capture of Gibraltar">capture of Gibraltar</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_M%C3%A1laga_(1704)" title="Battle of M\xc3\xa1laga (1704)">Battle of M\xc3\xa1laga</a>. Working in conjunction with a landing force under the <a href="/wiki/Charles_Mordaunt,_3rd_Earl_of_Peterborough" title="Charles Mordaunt, 3rd Earl of Peterborough">Earl of Peterborough</a> his forces undertook the siege and <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Barcelona_(1705)" title="Siege of Barcelona (1705)">capture of Barcelona</a>. He was appointed commander-in-chief of the Navy while at Lisbon the following year. He also commanded the naval element of a combined <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toulon_(1707)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Toulon (1707)">attack on Toulon</a>, base of the main French fleet, in coordination with the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Austrian</a> army under <a href="/wiki/Prince_Eugene_of_Savoy" title="Prince Eugene of Savoy">Prince Eugene of Savoy</a> in the summer of 1707. His life was brought to an end in a <a href="/wiki/Scilly_naval_disaster_of_1707" title="Scilly naval disaster of 1707">disastrous shipwreck in the Isles of Scilly</a> later that year. He also served as <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rochester_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Rochester (UK Parliament constituency)">Rochester</a> from 1695 to 1701 and from 1705 until his death in 1707.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" title="Battle of Sole Bay">Battle of Sole Bay</a>, 28 May 1672.</div></div></div>\n<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Cockthorpe,_Norfolk" title="Cockthorpe, Norfolk">Cockthorpe</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the son of John Shovell, a <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> gentleman, and Anne Shovell (n\xc3\xa9e Jenkinson), Shovell was born into a family \'of property and distinction\'<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which, although not poor, was by no means wealthy.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The unusual first name of Cloudesley derives from the surname of his maternal grandmother Lucy Cloudisley, who was the daughter of Thomas Cloudisley.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He went to sea as a <a href="/wiki/Cabin_boy" title="Cabin boy">cabin boy</a> in the care of a paternal relative, Admiral <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Myngs" title="Christopher Myngs">Sir Christopher Myngs</a>, in 1663. After Myngs\' death he remained at sea in the care of Admiral <a href="/wiki/John_Narborough" title="John Narborough">Sir John Narborough</a>. Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Midshipman" title="Midshipman">midshipman</a> on 22 January 1672, he was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/First-rate" title="First-rate">first-rate</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Prince_(1670)" title="HMS Prince (1670)">HMS <i>Royal Prince</i></a>, flagship of the <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_England" title="James II of England">Duke of York</a>, and saw action when a combined British and French fleet was surprised and attacked by the Dutch, led by Admiral <a href="/wiki/Michiel_de_Ruyter" title="Michiel de Ruyter">Michiel de Ruyter</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Solebay" title="Battle of Solebay">Battle of Solebay</a> off the Suffolk coast in May 1672, during the <a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Third Anglo-Dutch War">Third Anglo-Dutch War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_mate" title="Master&#39;s mate">master\'s mate</a> on 17 September 1672, Shovell transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Third-rate" title="Third-rate">third-rate</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Fairfax_(1660)" class="mw-redirect" title="HMS Fairfax (1660)">HMS <i>Fairfax</i></a> later that month and then moved to the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Henrietta_(1654)" title="HMS Henrietta (1654)">HMS <i>Henrietta</i></a> in November 1672. He saw action again when a combined British and French fleet attempting to land troops in the Netherlands was repelled by a smaller Dutch force, again led by Admiral de Ruyter, at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Texel" title="Battle of Texel">Battle of Texel</a> in August 1673.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> on 25 September 1673, he transferred to the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Harwich_(1674)" title="HMS Harwich (1674)">HMS <i>Harwich</i></a> in 1675 and took part in an action against the pirate stronghold at <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>. Shovell led a surprise attack on the pirates, sinking a number of their ships in January 1676. For this action he received the sum of \xc2\xa380 from Narborough. Two months later he undertook a second raid against the pirates, for which he was awarded a gold medal from <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">King Charles II</a>. In a letter from the Admiralty, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Pepys" title="Samuel Pepys">Samuel Pepys</a> recorded the King\'s satisfaction with Shovell\xe2\x80\x99s actions; he transferred to the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Plymouth_(1653)" title="HMS Plymouth (1653)">HMS <i>Plymouth</i></a> in May 1677 and was sent to the Mediterranean.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_(1689).png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_%281689%29.png/220px-Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_%281689%29.png" width="220" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_%281689%29.png/330px-Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_%281689%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_%281689%29.png/440px-Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_%281689%29.png 2x" data-file-width="562" data-file-height="385" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bataille_de_Bantry_Bay_(1689).png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bantry_Bay" title="Battle of Bantry Bay">Battle of Bantry Bay</a>, 11 May 1689.</div></div></div>\n<p>Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(Royal_Navy)" title="Captain (Royal Navy)">captain</a> 17 September 1677, Shovell was given command of the <a href="/wiki/Fifth-rate" title="Fifth-rate">fifth-rate</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Sapphire_(1675)" title="HMS Sapphire (1675)">HMS <i>Sapphire</i></a>. He transferred to the fourth-rate <a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Phoenix_(1671)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="HMS Phoenix (1671) (page does not exist)">HMS <i>Phoenix</i></a> in April 1679 and returned to HMS <i>Sapphire</i> in May 1679 before transferring to the fifth-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Nonsuch_(1668)" title="HMS Nonsuch (1668)">HMS <i>Nonsuch</i></a> in July 1680. He returned to HMS <i>Sapphire</i> again in September 1680 and then transferred to the sixth-rate <a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_James_Galley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="HMS James Galley (page does not exist)">HMS <i>James Galley</i></a> in April 1681, to the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Anne_(1678)" title="HMS Anne (1678)">HMS <i>Anne</i></a> in April 1687 and to the fourth-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Dover_(1654)" title="HMS Dover (1654)">HMS <i>Dover</i></a> in April 1688. Throughout this period Shovell was engaged in the defence of <a href="/wiki/Tangier" title="Tangier">Tangier</a> from <a href="/wiki/Sal%C3%A9" title="Sal\xc3\xa9">Sal\xc3\xa9</a> raiders.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Shovell transferred to the command of the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Edgar_(1668)" title="HMS Edgar (1668)">HMS <i>Edgar</i></a> in April 1689 and saw action at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bantry_Bay" title="Battle of Bantry Bay">Battle of Bantry Bay</a> in May 1689, when a French fleet tried to land troops in Southern Ireland to fight <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">Prince William of Orange</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Williamite_War_in_Ireland" title="Williamite War in Ireland">Williamite War in Ireland</a>. After the battle, Commodore <a href="/wiki/John_Ashby_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="John Ashby (Royal Navy officer)">John Ashby</a> and Shovell were <a href="/wiki/Knight_Bachelor" title="Knight Bachelor">knighted</a>. He transferred to the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Monck_(1659)" title="HMS Monck (1659)">HMS <i>Monck</i></a> in October 1689 and ordered to patrol the area between Ireland and the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. In June 1690 he was commodore of a small squadron, which convoyed King William across <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Channel" title="St George&#39;s Channel">St George\'s Channel</a> to <a href="/wiki/Carrickfergus" title="Carrickfergus">Carrickfergus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_command">Senior command</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cloudesley_Shovell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Senior command">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paton,_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Paton%2C_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg/220px-Paton%2C_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg" width="220" height="139" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Paton%2C_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg/330px-Paton%2C_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Paton%2C_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg/440px-Paton%2C_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="810" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paton,_Battle_of_Barfleur.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Barfleur_and_La_Hogue" class="mw-redirect" title="Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue">Battle of Barfleur</a>, 19 May 1692.</div></div></div>\n<p>Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Rear-admiral" class="mw-redirect" title="Rear-admiral">rear-admiral</a> on 3 June 1690, Shovell hoisted his flag in the first-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Prince_(1670)" title="HMS Prince (1670)">HMS <i>Royal William</i></a>. He provided naval support for <a href="/wiki/Percy_Kirke" title="Percy Kirke">Percy Kirke</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Waterford" title="Capture of Waterford">Capture of Waterford</a> in July 1690. He commanded a division of the Red squadron at the <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Barfleur_and_La_Hogue" class="mw-redirect" title="Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue">Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue</a> in May 1692, in which <a href="/wiki/Edward_Russell,_1st_Earl_of_Orford" title="Edward Russell, 1st Earl of Orford">Russell\'s</a> Anglo-Dutch fleet intercepted and defeated the French fleet under <a href="/wiki/Anne_Hilarion_de_Tourville" title="Anne Hilarion de Tourville">Tourville</a>, on its way along the Channel to provide an escort for an invasion of England. At <a href="/wiki/Action_at_Barfleur" title="Action at Barfleur">Barfleur</a> Shovell\'s flagship was the first ship to break through the enemy\'s line, and in the latter stages of the battle he organised a fireship attack. He received a wound in the thigh during the action, which later incapacitated him during preparations for the attack which destroyed the French ships that had taken refuge at <a href="/wiki/Action_at_La_Hogue_(1692)" title="Action at La Hogue (1692)">La Hogue</a>. Along with Admirals <a href="/wiki/Henry_Killigrew_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="Henry Killigrew (Royal Navy officer)">Henry Killigrew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Delaval" title="Ralph Delaval">Ralph Delaval</a>, Shovell was put in joint command of the fleet in January 1693. After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Smyrna_Convoy" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of the Smyrna Convoy">disastrous attack</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Smyrna" title="Smyrna">Smyrna</a> convoy off <a href="/wiki/Lagos,_Portugal" title="Lagos, Portugal">Lagos, Portugal</a>, in June 1693, all three admirals were dismissed from their joint command. Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Vice_admiral" title="Vice admiral">vice admiral</a> on 16 April 1694, Shovell commanded a squadron on expeditions to <a href="/wiki/Dieppe" title="Dieppe">Dieppe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dunkirk" title="Dunkirk">Dunkirk</a>, later in the year.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Shovell set up residence with his wife at May Place in <a href="/wiki/Crayford" title="Crayford">Crayford</a> in 1694 and was elected <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rochester_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Rochester (UK Parliament constituency)">Rochester</a> in 1695.<sup id="cite_ref-parl_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parl-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was responsible for the restoration of St.&#160;Paulinus\' Church in Crayford and was a great benefactor to <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_Kent" title="Rochester, Kent">Rochester</a>, providing at his own expense the fine decorated plaster ceilings in the Guildhall and the market bell, clock and decorated brick facade for the Butchers\' Market (now the Corn Exchange).<sup id="cite_ref-thomas_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was also Commissioner of the Sewers, responsible for the upkeep of the embankments of the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">Thames</a> between <a href="/wiki/Deptford" title="Deptford">Deptford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gravesend" title="Gravesend">Gravesend</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He did not stand for re-election to Parliament in December 1701.<sup id="cite_ref-parl_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parl-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="War_of_the_Spanish_Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cloudesley_Shovell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: War of the lived</div></div></div>\n<p>Promoted to full <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a> on 6 May 1702, Shovell brought home the spoils of the French and Spanish fleets, which had been captured by Admiral <a href="/wiki/George_Rooke" title="George Rooke">George Rooke</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vigo_Bay" title="Battle of Vigo Bay">Battle of Vigo</a> at an early stage of the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession" title="War of the Spanish Succession">War of the Spanish Succession</a>, arriving in England in late 1702. After commanding a fleet dispatched to take troops to <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> in Spring 1703, he commanded a squadron which served under Rooke at the <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Gibraltar" title="Capture of Gibraltar">capture of Gibraltar</a> in August 1704 and also repulsed the French fleet at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_M%C3%A1laga_(1704)" title="Battle of M\xc3\xa1laga (1704)">Battle of M\xc3\xa1laga</a> later that month. He was appointed a member of the council of the Lord High Admiral (an office vested at that time in <a href="/wiki/Prince_George_of_Denmark" title="Prince George of Denmark">Prince George of Denmark</a>) in December 1704, appointed <a href="/wiki/Rear-Admiral_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Rear-Admiral of the United Kingdom">Rear-Admiral of England</a> on 26 December 1704 and promoted to <a href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_(Royal_Navy)" title="Admiral of the Fleet (Royal Navy)">Admiral of the Fleet</a> on 13 January 1705.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-parl_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parl-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was elected Member of Parliament for Rochester again in 1705.<sup id="cite_ref-parl_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parl-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Working in conjunction with a landing force under the <a href="/wiki/Charles_Mordaunt,_3rd_Earl_of_Peterborough" title="Charles Mordaunt, 3rd Earl of Peterborough">Earl of Peterborough</a> his forces undertook the siege and <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Barcelona_(1705)" title="Siege of Barcelona (1705)">capture of Barcelona</a> in September 1705. He was appointed commander-in-chief of the Navy while at Lisbon in November 1706.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He commanded the naval element of a combined <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toulon_(1707)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Toulon (1707)">attack on Toulon</a>, base of the main French fleet, in coordination with the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Austrian</a> army under <a href="/wiki/Prince_Eugene_of_Savoy" title="Prince Eugene of Savoy">Prince Eugene of Savoy</a> in the summer of 1707. The allies failed to capture the city, but bombardment by Shovell\'s forces panicked the French into scuttling their own fleet. Shovell was subsequently ordered to bring his fleet home in late October 1707.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_in_the_Scilly_naval_disaster">Death in the Scilly naval disaster</span><span the campaign at Toulon, Shovell\'s flagship, the second-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Association_(1697)" class="mw-redirect" title="HMS Association (1697)">HMS <i>Association</i></a>, struck the rocks near the Isles of Scilly at 8&#160;pm on 22 October (2 November, by the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">modern calendar</a>) 1707. HMS <i>Association</i> went down in three or four minutes, with none of the 800&#160;men that were on board saved, according to sailors watching on the first-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Charles_(1668)" title="HMS Charles (1668)">HMS <i>St George</i></a>. Four large ships, HMS <i>Association</i>, the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Eagle_(1679)" title="HMS Eagle (1679)">HMS <i>Eagle</i></a>, the fourth-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Romney_(1694)" title="HMS Romney (1694)">HMS <i>Romney</i></a> and the <a href="/wiki/Fire_ship" title="Fire ship">fire ship</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Firebrand_(1694)" title="HMS Firebrand (1694)">HMS <i>Firebrand</i></a> all sank.<sup id="cite_ref-Sobel,_p._6_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sobel,_p._6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>With nearly 2,000 sailors lost that night, the <a href="/wiki/Scilly_naval_disaster_of_1707" title="Scilly naval disaster of 1707">Scilly naval disaster</a> was recorded as one of the greatest <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">maritime</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_disasters_in_Great_Britain_and_Ireland_by_death_toll" title="List of disasters in Great Britain and Ireland by death toll">disasters in British history</a>.<sup title="Navigator">navigators</a>\' inability accurately to calculate their <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a>, although no public discussion of the events specifically raising the question of longitude is known, prior to a pamphlet published on the eve of Parliament\'s vote on the <a href="/wiki/Longitude_Act" title="Longitude Act">Longitude Act</a>, seven years later.<sup id="cite_ref-sobel11_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sobel11-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Shovell\'s body and those of both his stepsons were all found in <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly#Porthellick_Cove" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">Porthellick Cove</a> on <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>, almost 7 miles (11&#160;km) from where his ship was wrecked. It was possible that Shovell left his flagship in one of its boats along with his two stepsons and the captain of HMS <i>Association</i>, Edmund Loades, and that they were drowned while trying to get to shore.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Shovell\'s body was identified by the <a href="/wiki/Purser" title="Purser">purser</a> of the third-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Warspite_(1758)" title="HMS Warspite (1758)">HMS <i>Arundel</i></a> who knew the admiral well. It was identified by "a black mole under his left ear, also by the first joint of one of his forefingers being broken inwards. He had likewise a shot in his right arm, another in his left thigh".<sup id="cite_ref-cooke_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooke-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Shovell was temporarily buried on the beach at Porthellick Cove.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> By order of <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Queen_of_Great_Britain" title="Anne, Queen of Great Britain">Queen Anne</a> the body was later exhumed and brought back in the fourth-rate <a href="/wiki/HMS_Salisbury_(1707)" title="HMS Salisbury (1707)">HMS <i>Salisbury</i></a> to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>, where it was embalmed by <a href="/wiki/James_Yonge_(surgeon)" title="James Yonge (surgeon)">Dr James Yonge</a>. It was later carried in state to London. During the journey from the <a href="/wiki/West_Country" title="West Country">West Country</a>, large crowds turned out to pay their respects. He was interred in <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a> on 22 December 1707: his large marble monument in the <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey_Burials_and_Memorials#South_Choir_Aisle" class="mw-redirect" title="Westminster Abbey Burials and Memorials">south choir aisle</a> was sculpted by <a href="/wiki/Grinling_Gibbons" title="Grinling Gibbons">Grinling Gibbons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-westminster_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-westminster-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, his two stepsons were buried in <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a> Church on St Mary\xe2\x80\x99s.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Local legend has it that Shovell was alive, at least barely, when he reached the shore of Scilly at Porthellick Cove but was murdered by a woman for the sake of his priceless <a href="/wiki/Emerald" title="Emerald">emerald</a> ring, which had been given to him by a close friend, Captain <a href="/wiki/James_Berkeley,_3rd_Earl_of_Berkeley" title="James Berkeley, 3rd Earl of Berkeley">James Lord Dursley</a>. At that time, the Scillies had a wild and lawless reputation. It is claimed that the murder came to light only some thirty years later when the woman, on her deathbed, confessed to a clergyman to having killed the admiral and produced the stolen ring, which was sent back to Dursley.<sup id="cite_ref-royalnavalmuseum_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royalnavalmuseum-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sobel11_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sobel11-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Several historians doubt the murder story as there is no indication that the ring was recovered and the legend stems from a romantic and unverifiable deathbed confession.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Another legend alleges that a common sailor on the flagship tried to warn Shovell that the fleet was off course but Shovell had him hanged at the yardarm for inciting <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">mutiny</a>. The story first appeared in the Scilly Isles in 1780, with the common sailor being a Scilly native, who recognized the waters as being close to home but was punished for warning the admiral.<sup id="cite_ref-cooke_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooke-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> While it is possible that a sailor may have debated the vessel\'s location and feared for its fate (such debates were common upon entering the English Channel, as noted by <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Pepys" title="Samuel Pepys">Samuel Pepys</a> in 1684), the story has been repeatedly discredited by naval scholars, who noted the lack of any evidence in contemporary documents, its fanciful stock conventions and dubious origins.<sup id="cite_ref-cooke_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooke-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After his death Shovell became a popular British hero.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cloudesley_Shovell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1691 Shovell married Elizabeth, Lady Narborough (n\xc3\xa9e Hill), the widow of his former commander, Rear Admiral Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Narborough" title="John Narborough">John Narborough</a>. Through her, he had two stepsons (Sir John Narborough, 1st Baronet, and James Narborough) who both entered naval careers and died aged 23 and 22, at the sinking of HMS <i>Association</i> in October 1707.<sup id="cite_ref-cooke_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cooke-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Shovell and his wife also had two daughters: Elizabeth and Anne. Elizabeth married <a href="/wiki/Robert_Marsham,_1st_Baron_Romney" title="Robert Marsham, 1st Baron Romney">Lord Romney</a>, whilst Anne married John Blackwood.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cloudesley_Shovell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.shovell1714.crayfordhistory.co.uk/online-exhibition/lord-manor-of-crayford/">"Sir Cloudesley Shovell - Lord of the Manor of Crayford"</a><span class="reference-accessdate">.
