class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin_(1540%E2%80%931608)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The nephew of <a href="/wiki/William_Godolphin_(1515%E2%80%931570)" title="William Godolphin (1515\xe2\x80\x931570)">Sir William Godolphin (1515\xe2\x80\x931570)</a>, who left no male issue, he succeeded to his uncle\'s estates early in <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Queen Elizabeth\'s</a> reign. He was one of the leading citizens of Cornwall, described by that county\'s 17th century historian, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Carew_(antiquary)" title="Richard Carew (antiquary)">Richard Carew</a>, as one "whose zeal in religion, uprightness in justice, providence in government, and plentiful housekeeping, have won him a very great and reverent reputation in his country". (<i>The Survey of Cornwall</i>, 1602, quoted in <i>Burke\'s Extinct Peerage</i>).\n</p><p>His father, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Godolphin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thomas Godolphin (page does not exist)">Thomas</a>, had been <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Scilly Isles">Governor of the Scilly Isles</a> and they were leased to Francis who became governor in his turn (see <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#History" title="Isles of Scilly">Governors of Scilly</a>). On royal instructions he improved the defences of the islands which were, in Carew\'s words "reduced to a more defensible plight by him, who with his invention and purse, bettered his plot and allowance, and therein so tempered strength and delight, and both with use, as it serveth for a sure hold, and a commodious dwelling." Chief among this work of fortification was the building of <a href="/wiki/Star_Castle,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Star Castle, Isles of Scilly">Star Castle</a>. He was also an innovative manager of Cornwall\'s <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Cornwall" class="mw-redirect" title="Mining in Cornwall">tin mines</a>, his inventions greatly increasing their productivity by extracting metal from material; that would previously have been discarded as refuse, so materially improving both the prosperity of Cornwall and the revenue that the Crown derived from it.\n</p><p>Sir Francis represented <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">Cornwall</a> in the Parliament of 1588\xe2\x80\x939 and <a href="/wiki/Lostwithiel_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Lostwithiel (UK Parliament constituency)">Lostwithiel</a> in that of 1593; he was also twice <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Cornwall" title="High Sheriff of Cornwall">High Sheriff of Cornwall</a> (1580 and 1604), <a href="/wiki/Custos_Rotulorum_of_Cornwall" title="Custos Rotulorum of Cornwall">Custos Rotulorum</a> for a number of years, and Vice-Warden of the <a href="/wiki/Stannaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Stannaries">Stannaries</a> from 1584 to 1603.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin_(1540%E2%80%931608)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin married Margaret Killigrew, daughter of Sir John Killigrew of <a href="/wiki/Arwennack" class="mw-redirect" title="Arwennack">Arwennack</a> and accused pirate <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Trewinnard" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth Trewinnard">Elizabeth Trewinnard</a>; and two of his sons, <a href="/wiki/William_Godolphin_(1567%E2%80%931613)" title="William Godolphin (1567\xe2\x80\x931613)">Sir William Godolphin (1567\xe2\x80\x931613)</a> (his heir) and <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin_(died_about_1640)" title="Francis Godolphin (died about 1640)">Sir Francis Godolphin (died 1640)</a>, followed him in becoming members of parliament. His daughter Thomasine married <a href="/wiki/George_Carew_(diplomat)" title="George Carew (diplomat)">George Carew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a widower he married Alice Skerret (1545\xe2\x80\x931632) of <a href="/wiki/Tavistock,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tavistock, Devon">Tavistock, Devon</a> widow of Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Glanville_(judge)" title="John Glanville (judge)">John Glanville (judge)</a> (1542\xe2\x80\x931600)\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_fiction">In fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin_(1540%E2%80%931608)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the historical novel <i>The Grove of Eagles</i>, by <a href="/wiki/Winston_Graham" title="Winston Graham">Winston Graham</a>, Godolphin is shown as a sympathetic figure. The main characters are the family of his first wife, the Killigrews: both out of family feeling and his concern for law and order, Godolphin warns them that their reputation for <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a>  and  their general lawlessness will lead them into ruin.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin_(1540%E2%80%931608)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/12px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png" width="12" height="13" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/18px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/24px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="410" data-file-height="430" />&#160;<cite class="citation encyclopaedia">"<a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Carew,_George_(d.1612)_(DNB00)" class="extiw" title="s:Carew, George (d.1612) (DNB00)">Carew, George (d.1612)</a>". <i><a href="/wiki/Dictionary_of_National_Biography" title="Dictionary of National Biography">Dictionary of National Biography</a></i>.
