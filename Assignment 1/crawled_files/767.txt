Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Nidderdale AONB</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, England, bordering the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales_National_Park" title="Yorkshire Dales National Park">Yorkshire Dales National Park</a> to the east and south.  It comprises most of <a href="/wiki/Nidderdale" title="Nidderdale">Nidderdale</a> itself, part of lower <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale" title="Wharfedale">Wharfedale</a>, the <a href="/wiki/River_Washburn" title="River Washburn">Washburn valley</a> and part of lower <a href="/wiki/Wensleydale" title="Wensleydale">Wensleydale</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Jervaulx_Abbey" title="Jervaulx Abbey">Jervaulx Abbey</a> and the side valleys west of the <a href="/wiki/River_Ure" title="River Ure">River Ure</a>. It covers a total area of 233 square miles (600&#160;km<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  The highest point in the Nidderdale AONB is <a href="/wiki/Great_Whernside" title="Great Whernside">Great Whernside</a>, 704 metres (2,310&#160;ft) above sea level, on the border with the Yorkshire Dales National Park.\n</p><p>The only town in the AONB is <a href="/wiki/Pateley_Bridge" title="Pateley Bridge">Pateley Bridge</a> in Nidderdale.  <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a> lie just to the south of the AONB, and <a href="/wiki/Masham" title="Masham">Masham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ripon" title="Ripon">Ripon</a> are just to the east.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1947, when the <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Hobhouse" title="Arthur Hobhouse">Hobhouse Report</a> recommended the creation of the Yorkshire Dales National Park, it proposed that Nidderdale should be excluded and given a different conservation status.  Accordingly, Nidderdale was not included in the National Park when it was designated in 1954.  In 1963 the then <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_County_Council" title="West Riding County Council">West Riding County Council</a> proposed that Nidderdale should be added to the National Park, but the proposal met with opposition from the <a href="/wiki/Rural_District_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural District Council">district councils</a> which would have lost some of their powers to the county council.  The West Riding National Park Committee, and its successor the Yorkshire Dales National Park Committee, did not entirely abandon the idea until 1988, when the alternative AONB designation was proposed.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The eastern boundary of the National Park had been set at <a href="/wiki/East_Witton" title="East Witton">East Witton</a>, as recommended by the Hobhouse Report.  The section of Wensleydale below East Witton had not been considered for national park status, but was added to the new AONB.  Similarly, the new AONB also included the Washburn Valley and the area of lower Wharfedale between the Washburn Valley and the National Park, which had not been considered for national park status.  The AONB was designated in 1994 with its current boundaries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nidderdale">Nidderdale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nidderdale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nidderdale" title="Nidderdale">Nidderdale</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG/220px-Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG/330px-Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG/440px-Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Near-Pateley-Bridge-04.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Countryside near Pateley Bridge</div></div></div>\n<p>The AONB includes all of Nidderdale except a small area in the lower dale around <a href="/wiki/Hampsthwaite" title="Hampsthwaite">Hampsthwaite</a>.  In the lower dale, below Pateley Bridge, attractions include <a href="/wiki/Brimham_Rocks" title="Brimham Rocks">Brimham Rocks</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Coldstones_Cut" class="mw-redirect" title="Coldstones Cut">Coldstones Cut</a>.  In the less populated upper dale there are a series of reservoirs, <a href="/wiki/Gouthwaite_Reservoir" title="Gouthwaite Reservoir">Gouthwaite Reservoir</a> (a nature reserve), <a href="/wiki/Scar_House_Reservoir" title="Scar House Reservoir">Scar House Reservoir</a> and at the very top of the dale <a href="/wiki/Angram_Reservoir" title="Angram Reservoir">Angram Reservoir</a>, dominated by Great Whernside and <a href="/wiki/Little_Whernside" title="Little Whernside">Little Whernside</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" Wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swinsty_Reservoir" title="Swinsty Reservoir">Swinsty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fewston_Reservoir" title="Fewston Reservoir">Fewston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thruscross_Reservoir" title="Thruscross Reservoir">Thruscross</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lower_Wensleydale_and_side_valleys">Lower Wensleydale and side valleys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lower Wensleydale and side valleys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The eastern part of the AONB includes <a href="/wiki/Colsterdale" title="Colsterdale">Colsterdale</a> and the valleys of the <a href="/wiki/River_Laver" title="River Laver">River Laver</a> and the <a href="/wiki/River_Skell" title="River Skell">River Skell</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Fountains_Abbey" title="Fountains Abbey">Fountains Abbey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jervaulx_Abbey" title="Jervaulx Abbey">Jervaulx Abbey</a> both lie within the AONB, and <a href="/wiki/Hackfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Hackfall">Hackfall Wood</a> is on the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/River_Ure" title="River Ure">River Ure</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Walking is a popular activity.  Two long-distance trails traverse the AONB, the <a href="/wiki/Six_Dales_Trail" title="Six Dales Trail">Six Dales Trail</a> from south to north, and the <a href="/wiki/Nidderdale_Way" title="Nidderdale Way">Nidderdale Way</a> from south east to north west.  Upper Nidderdale is a popular area for <a href="/wiki/Mountain_biking" title="Mountain biking">mountain biking</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and caving takes place in the <a href="/wiki/Nidderdale_Caves" title="Nidderdale Caves">Upper Nidderdale caves</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Grouse_moor" class="mw-redirect" title="Grouse moor">grouse moors</a> on the higher land offer some of the best shooting in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Like most AONBs, and unlike national parks, the Nidderdale AONB is managed by local authorities.  The entire AONB is within North Yorkshire, and almost all of it is within the area of <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Harrogate" title="Borough of Harrogate">Harrogate Borough Council</a>.  Harrogate Borough Council is lead authority for the Nidderdale AONB. Small areas of land to the north are within <a href="/wiki/Richmondshire" title="Richmondshire">Richmondshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hambleton_District" title="Hambleton District">Hambleton</a> districts.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>AONB policy and programmes are set by the Nidderdale AONB Joint Advisory Committee, consisting of county, district and parish councillors, and representatives of community interest groups.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A small team based at Pateley Bridge reports to the JAC.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nidderdale_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.nidderdaleaonb.org.uk/">Nidderdale AONB website</a></span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Ray Woolmore (May 2002). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://webarchive.nationalarchives.gov.uk/20140605090108/http://naturalengland.org.uk/Images/ydnp-dh_tcm6-35861.pdf">"Yorkshire Dales National Park"</a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>.
