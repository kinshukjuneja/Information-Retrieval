This total includes the population estimate for <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, where only the term \'White\' is used in ethnic classification. National identity is listed separately in NI, where 40% classified themselves as British, making up a significant portion of the population, along with those specifying their national identity as <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Irish" class="extiw" title="wikt:Irish">Irish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nisra.gov.uk_4-1" class="reference"><a id="Census_classifications">Census classifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_British&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Census classifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>For the 2011 census, in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, the White British self-classification option included the subcategories of White <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, White <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a>, White <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> and White <a href="/wiki/People_of_Northern_Ireland" title="People of Northern Ireland">Northern Irish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-questionnaireE&amp;W_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-questionnaireE&amp;W-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In Scotland, the White British category was broken down into two different categories: White Scottish and Other White British.<sup id="cite_ref-questionnaireS_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-questionnaireS-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In Northern Ireland, the White British classification did not appear, the only choice being \'White\'.<sup id="cite_ref-questionnaireNI_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-questionnaireNI-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The 2011 census for England, Wales and Scotland also included additional White ethnic classifications of <a href="/wiki/White_Irish" class="mw-redirect" title="White Irish">White Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_Travellers" title="Irish Travellers">Irish Traveller</a> and <a href="/wiki/White_Other" class="mw-redirect" title="White Other">White Other</a>. There were calls for the 2011 national census in England and Wales to include an extra subcategory so people could identify their ethnic group as <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_British&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population_and_distribution">Population and distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_British&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Population and distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The White British census classification have their ages more evenly distributed in their <a href="/wiki/Population_pyramid" title="Population pyramid">population pyramid</a> and have the highest percent female population of all ethnic-based classifications. About 64% percent of the White British classification are between the ages of 16 and 64 while about 19% percent are under 16 and 19% percent are over 64. All other census classifications have a higher percentage of their population under 16 and a lower percentage over 64. Of those aged 65 or over, White British are 41% percent male and 59% percent female, making them have the lowest percent male population among all census classifications defined as "ethnic" in the census.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to the 2011 UK Census results, White British people make up the largest percentage of the population in rural areas, such as <a href="/wiki/Allerdale" title="Allerdale">Allerdale</a> (99.4%) and <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Copeland" title="Borough of Copeland">Copeland</a> (99.3%) in <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryedale" title="Ryedale">Ryedale</a> (99.4%) in North Yorkshire, <a href="/wiki/North_Norfolk" title="North Norfolk">North Norfolk</a> (99.2%) and <a href="/wiki/North_Devon" title="North Devon">North Devon</a> (99%). Cities across the UK regions with high White British populations include <a href="/wiki/Swansea" title="Swansea">Swansea</a>, Wales (91.5%), <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> (92.2%), <a href="/wiki/Darlington" title="Darlington">Darlington</a>, England (93.7%), <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> (96.4% - NI classification "white"),<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a>, England (84.7%), <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford" title="Chelmsford">Chelmsford</a>, England (90.0%) and <a href="/wiki/Lichfield" title="Lichfield">Lichfield</a>, England (94.6%). Within London, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Havering" title="London Borough of Havering">Havering</a> has the highest White British percentage with 83.3%, followed by <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bromley" title="London Borough of Bromley">Bromley</a> with 77.4%, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bexley" title="London Borough of Bexley">Bexley</a> with 77.3% and <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Richmond_upon_Thames" title="London Borough of Richmond upon Thames">Richmond upon Thames</a> with 71.4%.<sup id="cite_ref-CensusEngland_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CensusEngland-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since the 2011 UK Census was returned, London contains by far the lowest percentage of English and other White British people of all the UK regions, where they make up less than half of the population in 24 of the 32 <a href="/wiki/London_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="London Borough">boroughs</a>, including: <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Newham" title="London Borough of Newham">Newham</a> (16.7%), <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Brent" title="London Borough of Brent">Brent</a> (18.0%), <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Ealing" title="London Borough of Ealing">Ealing</a> (30.4%), <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Harrow" title="London Borough of Harrow">Harrow</a> (30.9%), <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">Tower Hamlets</a> (31.2%), <a href="/wiki/City_of_Westminster" title="City of Westminster">Westminster</a> (35.2%) and <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Hackney" title="London Borough of Hackney">Hackney</a> (36.2%). The city with the lowest White British population as a percentage is <a href="/wiki/Leicester" title="Leicester">Leicester</a> (45.1%). id="Economic_status_and_education">Economic status and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_British&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economic status and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>According to official UK Government figures from 2016, the employment rate for White British people stood at 75%, with the overall employment rate in the UK standing at 74%.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> UK Government figures also demonstrate that 31% of White British people work in professional and managerial occupations, higher than the Mixed (30%), Pakistani/Bangladeshi (27%) and Black (25%) groups, but lower than the Indian ethnic group (43%).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At GCSE level, official UK Government statistics state that 63% of White British pupils attained A* to C grades in English and Mathematics in the 2015-16 academic year, higher than Black Caribbean (51%) and Pakistani (58%) pupils, but lower than Bangladeshi (67%), Indian (77%) and Chinese (83%) pupils. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> According to a report by the <a href="/wiki/Sutton_Trust" title="Sutton Trust">Sutton Trust</a>, "White working class pupils achieve the lowest grades at GCSE of any main ethnic group, with just a quarter of boys and a third of girls achieving 5 good GCSEs."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At A-Level, in the 2015-16 academic year, 11% of White British pupils achieved at least 3 \'A\' grades at A-Level; the only major ethnic groups to achieve the same benchmark at a higher rate were Indian (14%) and Chinese (24%) pupils.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_British&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Statistically, White British are more likely to be <a href="/wiki/History_of_Christianity_in_Britain" title="History of Christianity in Britain">Christian</a> than other ethnic-based classifications. According to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 UK Census</a>, White British are 64% Christian, mostly <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglican</a> in England (or <a href="/wiki/Presbyterian#Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Presbyterian">Presbyterian in Scotland</a>), while the percentage for all groups is about 59%. About 27% of the White British population reported having "no religion". The 27% percent figure for "<a href="/wiki/Irreligion_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Irreligion in the United Kingdom">no religion</a>" is about the same for all groups.
