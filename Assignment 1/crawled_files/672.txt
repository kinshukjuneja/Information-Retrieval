<b>Local Government Board</b> (<b>LGB</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/British_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="British Government">British Government</a> supervisory body overseeing local administration in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a> from 1871 to 1919.\n</p><p>The LGB was created by the <b>Local Government Board Act 1871</b> (C. 70) and took over the public health and local government responsibilities of the <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Privy Council">Privy Council</a> and all the functions of the <a href="/wiki/Poor_Law_Board" title="Poor Law Board">Poor Law Board</a>, which was abolished.\n</p>\n<div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The board was headed by a <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Local_Government_Board" title="President of the Local Government Board">president</a>, appointed by and serving at the pleasure of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">British monarch</a>. The president was permitted to hold a seat and vote in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom House of Commons">House of Commons</a>, and the position was generally held by a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet minister">cabinet minister</a>.\n</p><p>In addition the board had a number of <i><a href="/wiki/Ex_officio" class="mw-redirect" title="Ex officio">ex officio</a></i> members consisting of the <a href="/wiki/Lord_President_of_the_Council" title="Lord President of the Council">Lord President of the Council</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_(United_Kingdom)" title="Secretary of State (United Kingdom)">Principal Secretaries of State</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Privy_Seal" title="Lord Privy Seal">Lord Privy Seal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a>. The <i>ex officio</i> members were not paid a salary. The board itself seldom met, with policy being decided by the president of the board. He was assisted by a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Secretary_to_the_Local_Government_Board" title="Parliamentary Secretary to the Local Government Board">Parliamentary Secretary</a> (also a member of the Commons), and a permanent secretary (a <a href="/wiki/Civil_servant" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil servant">civil servant</a>). The practical work of the LGB was carried out by its salaried officers.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Officers">Officers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Officers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Local Government Board was permitted to appoint such secretaries, assistant secretaries, inspectors, auditors, clerks, messengers, "and other officers" as they deemed fit, subject to the approval of the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Treasury" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Treasury">treasury</a>. Salaries paid to officers were required to be confirmed by <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Parliament">parliament</a>. On the formation of the LGB, the existing staff of the Poor Law Board, the Local Government Act Office and the Medical Department of the Privy Council were transferred to the new body.<sup id="cite_ref-bellamy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bellamy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_duties">Powers and duties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Powers and duties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The purpose of the LGB was stated to be <i>"the supervision of the laws relating to the public health, the relief of the poor, and local government"</i>. href="#cite_note-bellamy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_of_the_board">Work of the board</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Work of the board">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The LGB carried its work in the following ways:<sup id="cite_ref-bellamy_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bellamy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Delegated_legislation">Delegated legislation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Delegated legislation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The board was empowered to make general orders and regulations enforcing the various statutes for which it was responsible. These orders and regulations had the same force of law as those made by a secretary of state.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inspection_and_regulation">Inspection and regulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Inspection and regulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The LGB had broad powers of regulation of the bodies under its control. The board\'s inspectors were able to visit local authorities and ensure that they were performing satisfactorily. They could also act as a medium for resolving disputes between local bodies and ratepayers or other interests.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Provisional_orders">Provisional orders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Provisional orders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The board possessed quasi-judicial powers, being able to make legally binding decisions on the bodies under its supervision. Examples included the changing of boundaries, raising of loans or the taking on of additional powers. These <i>provisional orders</i> were subject to confirmation by parliament.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Auditing_and_accountability">Auditing and accountability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Auditing and accountability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The board compiled and published financial summaries for each local authority annually. It also appointed district auditors and supplied such statistics as might be required by parliament.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abolition">Abolition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Abolition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Health_Act_1919" title="Ministry of Health Act 1919">Ministry of Health Act 1919</a> abolished the Local Government Board, and all of its powers and duties were transferred to the new ministry, which also combined the duties of the Insurance Commissioners, the Welsh Insurance Commissioners, the medical duties of the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Board of Education">Board of Education</a>, the duties of the Privy Council under the Midwives Acts and the powers of the Home Secretary in relation to the <a href="/wiki/Children_Act_1908" title="Children Act 1908">Children Act 1908</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Board_for_Scotland" title="Local Government Board for Scotland">Local Government Board for Scotland</a>, a similar body in Scotland from 1894 &#8211;
