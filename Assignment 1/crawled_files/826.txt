titles</th><td style="text-align: left;">\n<a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> (17)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>National League West</b> is one of the three divisions of the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> in North America (including Canada). This Division was formed for the 1969 season when the National League expanded to 12 teams by adding the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a>. For purpose of keeping a regular-season of 162 games, half of the teams were put into the new <a href="/wiki/National_League_East" title="National League East">East Division</a> and half into the new West Division. Within each division, the teams played 18 games each against their five division mates (90 games), and also 12 games against the teams in the opposite division (72 games), totaling 162 games.\n</p><p>Despite the geography, the owners of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> insisted that their team be placed into the East Division along with the teams in New York City, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>. Also, the owners of the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> wanted that team to be in the same division with their natural rivals of the Cubs. The league could have insisted on a geographical alignment like the American League did. But the owners were also concerned about what they thought would be a large imbalance in the strength of the divisions. In the previous two seasons prior to realignment, the Cardinals, Giants, and Cubs finished 1-2-3 in the National League standings. The owners were concerned about putting those teams in the same division, thereby creating one very strong division (West) and one weak one (East). Given all of this, the owners of the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a> consented to being placed into the West Division, even though <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a> are both in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">Eastern Time Zone</a>. Hence, the West Division had teams spread all the way from the East to the Pacific Coast, and scattered over four time zones. The East Division was spread over the Eastern Time Zone and the <a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone" title="Central Time Zone">Central Time Zone</a> - despite the fact that the National League had six teams in the Eastern Time Zone and six teams spread between the Central Time Zone and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Time_Zone" title="Pacific Time Zone">Pacific Time Zone</a>.\n</p><p>All of this increased the traveling distances and times for all of the teams, and it also made <a href="/wiki/Radio_broadcasting" title="Radio broadcasting">radio broadcasting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">TV broadcasts</a> of the games more difficult to schedule. The Braves and the Reds had to travel all the way to <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> three times during each baseball season, and the three teams in California had to travel to Atlanta, Cincinnati, and <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> three times also. Sometimes, the trouble could be alleviated for them by playing some games in Chicago, St. Louis, or <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> on the same long road trips. The 1994 addition of the Central Division would completely remedy these problems, when the Reds and Braves moved to the NL Central and NL East respectively.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: First season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The very first baseball season of division play, 1969, resulted in what might be considered by many<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (June 2012)">who?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> to be two of the best pennant races in Major League baseball history. In the National League West, five of the teams battled for the divisional championship - with only the expansion team, the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>, failing to be a contender. The remaining five teams were separated by as few as one-and-one-half games in the standings on August 18, despite the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> having lost 20 of its first 24 games.\n</p><p>Beginning in mid-August the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> started collapsing, leaving the Braves, the Reds, and the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> in contention. Following a very-long hot streak in July, August, and September, the Braves clinched the divisional championship by winning their next-to-last game. The Giants finished in a close second place. One of the main factors in the big surge by the Braves was that the slugging <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a> <a href="/wiki/Rico_Carty" title="Rico Carty">Rico Carty</a> returned to the team after missing the first half of the season while he was recovering from <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a>. Carty immediately resumed his starring role, adding to the powerful offensive line-up of the Braves that also featured the sluggers <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron" title="Hank Aaron">Hank Aaron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Cepeda" title="Orlando Cepeda">Orlando Cepeda</a> and the good singles hitter <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9lix_Mill%C3%A1n" title="F\xc3\xa9lix Mill\xc3\xa1n">F\xc3\xa9lix Mill\xc3\xa1n</a> who was on base to score a lot of runs. Aaron finished in third place for the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="Major League Baseball Most Valuable Player Award">N.L. Most Valuable Player Award</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> <a href="/wiki/Phil_Niekro" title="Phil Niekro">Phil Niekro</a> finished in second place for the <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">N.L. Cy Young Award</a>.\n</p><p>Perhaps this latter pennant race was overshadowed by that of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>, with the Mets coming back from trailing by nine-and-one-half games near midseason to overtake the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> for the East championship. The big star for the Mets was their starting pitcher <a href="/wiki/Tom_Seaver" title="Tom Seaver">Tom Seaver</a>, who won the <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a>. The so-called <a href="/wiki/1969_New_York_Mets_season" title="1969 New York Mets season">"Miracle Mets"</a> won the first <a href="/wiki/National_League_Championship_Series" title="National League Championship Series">National League Championship Series</a> over the Braves three games to none, and then the Mets defeated the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a> four games to one in the <a href="/wiki/1969_World_Series" title="1969 World Series">World Series</a> in October 1969 \xe2\x80\x94 in the Mets\' first winning season and first appearance in any playoff series whatsoever in their seven year history up to that point.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisional_membership">Divisional membership</span><span champions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Before the forming of a third division in both leagues in 1994, the winners of each division competed in a best-of-five series, with the series being lengthened by two possible games in 1985 to a best-of-seven series, dubbed the "<a href="/wiki/League_Championship_Series" title="League Championship Series">League Championship Series</a>" to determine the winner of the league <a href="/wiki/Pennant_(sports)" title="Pennant (sports)">pennant</a>. This format was to be changed in 1994, though it was not carried out until 1995 due to the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1994\xe2\x80\x9395 Major League Baseball strike">1994\xe2\x80\x9395 Major League Baseball strike</a> that started on August 12, 1994. There was the addition of two further teams in the playoffs in each league. This has led to the creation of a "<a href="/wiki/Division_Series" title="Division Series">Division Series</a>" round of the playoffs, in which two best-of-five series are conducted to determine the participants of the League Championship Series. As before, the winners of each league\'s pennant compete in the best-of-seven <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> to determine the champion of Major League
