href="/wiki/Sui_generis" title="Sui generis">sui generis</a> unitary local government authority covering the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> off the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>. It is currently made up of 16 seats, with all councillors being <a href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent (politician)">Independents</a> as of 2 May 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The council was created in 1890 as the <b>Isles of Scilly Rural District Council</b> and was renamed in 1974.\n</p>\n<div links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Historically, the Isles of Scilly were administered as one of the <a href="/wiki/Hundreds_of_Cornwall" title="Hundreds of Cornwall">hundreds of Cornwall</a>, although the Cornwall <a href="/wiki/Quarter_sessions" class="mw-redirect" title="Quarter sessions">quarter sessions</a> had limited jurisdiction there. For judicial purposes, <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff">shrievalty</a> purposes, and <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Lieutenant">lieutenancy</a> purposes, the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> provided that the Isles of Scilly are "deemed to form part of the county of Cornwall".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The archipelago is part of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a><sup id="cite_ref-duchysite_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-duchysite-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> \xe2\x80\x93 the duchy owns the freehold of most of the land on the islands and the duke exercises certain formal rights and privileges across the territory, as he does in Cornwall proper.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1888" title="Local Government Act 1888">Local Government Act 1888</a> allowed the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Board" title="Local Government Board">Local Government Board</a> to establish in the Isles of Scilly "councils and other local authorities separate from those of the county of Cornwall"... "for the application to the islands of any act touching local government." Accordingly, in 1890 the Isles of Scilly Rural District Council (the RDC) was formed as a <a href="/wiki/Sui_generis" title="Sui generis">sui generis</a> local government authority, outside the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_county" title="Administrative county">administrative county</a> of Cornwall. Cornwall County Council provided some services to the Isles, for which the RDC made financial contributions. The Isles of Scilly Order 1930<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> granted the Council the "powers, duties and liabilities" of a <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county council</a>. Section 265 of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> allowed for the continued existence of the RDC, but renamed as the Council of the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-iosuatt_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iosuatt-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-iosrdtt_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iosrdtt-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>This unusual status also means that much administrative law (for example relating to the functions of local authorities, the health service and other public bodies) that applies in the rest of England applies in modified form in the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Council of the Isles of Scilly is a separate authority to the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> unitary authority, and as such the islands are not part of the administrative county of Cornwall. However the islands are still considered to be part of the <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial county</a> of Cornwall. With a total population of just over 2,000, the Council represents fewer inhabitants than many English <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish councils</a>, and is by far the smallest <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">English unitary council</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Council is headquartered at Town Hall, by The Parade park in <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a>, and also performs the administrative functions of the <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">AONB</a> Partnership<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Inshore_Fisheries_and_Conservation_Authority" title="Inshore Fisheries and Conservation Authority">Inshore Fisheries and Conservation Authority</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Some aspects of local government are shared with Cornwall, including <a href="/wiki/Healthcare_in_Cornwall" title="Healthcare in Cornwall">health</a>, and the Council of the Isles of Scilly together with <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> form a <a href="/wiki/Local_Enterprise_Partnership" class="mw-redirect" title="Local Enterprise Partnership">Local Enterprise Partnership</a>. In July 2015 a <a href="/wiki/Devolution_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Devolution in the United Kingdom">devolution</a> deal was announced by the <a href="/wiki/Second_Cameron_ministry" title="Second Cameron ministry">government</a> under which Cornwall Council and the Council of the Isles of Scilly are to create a plan to bring health and social care services together under local control. The Local Enterprise Partnership is also to be bolstered.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of  2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, 130 people are employed <a href="/wiki/Full-time_equivalent" title="Full-time equivalent">full-time</a> by the Council<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> to provide local services (including water supply and <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">air traffic control</a>). These numbers are significant, in that almost ten per cent of the adult population of the islands is directly linked to the Council, as an employee or a councillor.<sup id="cite_ref-acca2002_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acca2002-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdivisions">Subdivisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subdivisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Scilly_wards_parishes.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Scilly_wards_parishes.png/220px-Scilly_wards_parishes.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Scilly_wards_parishes.png/330px-Scilly_wards_parishes.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Scilly_wards_parishes.png/440px-Scilly_wards_parishes.png 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Scilly_wards_parishes.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The five <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">wards</a> (which are also the <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parishes</a>) of the Isles of Scilly; red is St Agnes, blue is Bryher, orange is Tresco, green is St Martin\'s, and grey is St Mary\'s.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Council consists of 16 elected councillors \xe2\x80\x94 12 of which are returned by the <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">ward</a> of St Mary\'s, and one from each of four "off-island" wards (St Martin\'s, St Agnes, Bryher, and Tresco). The <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly_election,_2017" title="Council of the Isles of Scilly election, 2017">latest elections</a> took place on 4 May 2017; all 16 elected were <a href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent (politician)">independents</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The number of councillors elected in each ward was reduced by one for the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly_election,_2017" title="Council of the Isles of Scilly election, 2017">2017 local elections</a>, with the islands previously being represented by 21 councillors representing unchanged ward boundaries.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Whilst each of the inhabited isles is formally a <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a>, none of them possess a <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">council</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parish_meeting" title="Parish meeting">meeting</a> in their own right.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Elections">Elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>See also: <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly_elections" title="Council of the Isles of Scilly elections">Council of the Isles of Scilly elections</a></i>\n</p><p>Elections to the council were last held in <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly_election,_2017" title="Council of the Isles of Scilly election, 2017">May 2017</a>.
