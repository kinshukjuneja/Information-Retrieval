Lake</a></div></div></div>\n<p><b>Northamptonshire</b> is a county in the <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands" title="East Midlands">East Midlands</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-counties_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-counties-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It has an area of 236,700 hectares (914&#160;sq&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and a population estimated in mid-2015 at 723,000.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire" title="Warwickshire">Warwickshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rutland" title="Rutland">Rutland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bedfordshire" title="Bedfordshire">Bedfordshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>. It is governed by <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire_County_Council" title="Northamptonshire County Council">Northamptonshire County Council</a> and seven district and borough councils, <a href="/wiki/Corby" title="Corby">Corby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daventry_district" class="mw-redirect" title="Daventry district">Daventry</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Northamptonshire" title="East Northamptonshire">East Northamptonshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Kettering" title="Borough of Kettering">Kettering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northampton" title="Northampton">Northampton</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Northamptonshire" title="South Northamptonshire">South Northamptonshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wellingborough" title="Wellingborough">Wellingborough</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-counties_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-counties-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/County_flower" class="mw-redirect" title="County flower">county flower</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Primula_veris" title="Primula veris">cowslip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A ridge of low <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a> hills runs through the county, separating the basins of the <a href="/wiki/River_Welland" title="River Welland">Welland</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Nene" title="River Nene">Nene</a> rivers. The county has good communications as it is crossed by two main railway lines and the <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1 motorway</a>, and it has many small industrial centres rather than large conurbations. The main architectural feature is its country houses and mansions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As of May 2017, there are 18 <a href="/wiki/Local_Nature_Reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="Local Nature Reserve">Local Nature Reserves</a> in Northamptonshire,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and there is public access to all sites. Twelve are managed by the <a href="/wiki/Wildlife_Trust_for_Bedfordshire,_Cambridgeshire_and_Northamptonshire" title="Wildlife Trust for Bedfordshire, Cambridgeshire and Northamptonshire">Wildlife Trust for Bedfordshire, Cambridgeshire and Northamptonshire</a>, and two are also <a href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" class="mw-redirect" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_site" title="Ramsar site">Ramsar</a> internationally important wetland sites, and <a href="/wiki/Special_Protection_Area" title="Special Protection Area">Special Protection Areas</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Birds_Directive" title="Birds Directive">European Union Directive on the Conservation of Wild Birds</a>. The largest site is <a href="/wiki/Titchmarsh_Nature_Reserve" title="Titchmarsh Nature Reserve">Titchmarsh Nature Reserve</a> at 72.7 hectares (180 acres), which has nationally important numbers of <a href="/wiki/Goosander" class="mw-redirect" title="Goosander">goosanders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wigeon" title="Wigeon">wigeons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gadwall" title="Gadwall">gadwalls</a> in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The smallest is <a href="/wiki/Great_Oakley_Meadow" title="Great Oakley Meadow">Great Oakley Meadow</a>, which has prominent surviving <a href="/wiki/Ridge_and_furrow" title="Ridge and furrow">ridge and furrow</a> from the medieval ploughing system.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<table class="noprint infobox" id="GeoGroup" style="width: 23em; font-size: 88%; line-height: 1.5em">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<td><b>Map all coordinates
