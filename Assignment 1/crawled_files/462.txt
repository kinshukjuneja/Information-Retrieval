Hazen</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Arizona Diamondbacks</b>, often shortened as the <b>D-backs</b>, are an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. The club competes in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) as a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> (NL) <a href="/wiki/National_League_West" title="National League West">West</a> division. The team has played every home game in franchise history at <a href="/wiki/Chase_Field" title="Chase Field">Chase Field</a>, formerly known as <i>Bank One Ballpark</i>. The Diamondbacks have won one <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> championship (defeating the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> in <a href="/wiki/2001_World_Series" title="2001 World Series">2001</a>) \xe2\x80\x93 becoming the fastest <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion team</a> in the Major Leagues to win a championship, which it did in only the fourth season since <a href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_expansion" title="1998 Major League Baseball expansion">the franchise\'s inception</a>. href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_expansion" title="1998 Major League Baseball expansion">1998 Major League Baseball expansion</a></div>\n<p>On March 9, 1995, Phoenix was awarded an expansion franchise to begin play for the <a href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1998 Major League Baseball season">1998</a> season. A $130 million franchise fee was paid to Major League Baseball and on January 16, 1997, the Diamondbacks were officially voted into the National League.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Diamondbacks\' first major league game was played against the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a> on March 31, <a href="/wiki/1998_Arizona_Diamondbacks_season" title="1998 Arizona Diamondbacks season">1998</a>, at Bank One Ballpark. The <a href="/wiki/Baseball_park" title="Baseball park">ballpark</a> was renamed Chase Field in 2005, as a result of <a href="/wiki/Bank_One_Corporation" title="Bank One Corporation">Bank One Corporation</a>\'s merger with <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase &amp; Co.</a>\n</p><p>Since their debut, the Diamondbacks have won five NL West division titles, one NL pennant, one Wild Card game, and the <a href="/wiki/2001_World_Series" title="2001 World Series">2001 World Series</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Logos">Logos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arizona_Diamondbacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Logos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Diamondbacks\' original colors were <a href="/wiki/Purple" title="Purple">purple</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black" title="Black">black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Teal_(color)" class="mw-redirect" title="Teal (color)">teal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copper_(color)" title="Copper (color)">copper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-purplesedonared_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purplesedonared-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Their first logo was an italicized block letter "A" with a diamond pattern, and the crossbar represented by a snake\'s tongue. Prior to their inaugural season, they released their baseball caps. The home cap had a cream color crown with a purple visor and button. The road cap was black and had a turquoise visor and button. Their alternate cap had a turquoise crown with a purple visor and button. Depending on the cap, the "A" logo on the front of the cap had different color variations.\n</p><p>In the Diamondbacks\' second season, they introduced a new logo which was a copper color snake in the shape of a letter "D". It was used on a solid black cap, which in the beginning, was worn as a road cap.\n</p><p>The franchise unveiled new uniforms and colors of Sedona Red, Sonoran Sand and black on November 8, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Red" title="Red">red</a> shade is named for the <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> <a href="/wiki/Canyon" title="Canyon">canyon</a> at <a href="/wiki/Red_Rock_State_Park" title="Red Rock State Park">Red Rock State Park</a> near <a href="/wiki/Sedona,_Arizona" title="Sedona, Arizona">Sedona</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Beige" title="Beige">beige</a> (sand) shade is named for the <a href="/wiki/Sonoran_Desert" title="Sonoran Desert">Sonoran Desert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-purplesedonared_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purplesedonared-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A sleeve patch was added featuring a lowercase "d" and "b" configured to look like a snake\'s head.<sup id="cite_ref-purplesedonared_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purplesedonared-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The team also kept the "D" logo, but was slightly altered and put on an all red cap to be used as their game cap. They also kept the "A" logo with the new colors applied to it, with a solid black cap used as the alternate cap. A similar color scheme is currently used by the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Coyotes" title="Arizona Coyotes">Arizona Coyotes</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a>.\n</p><p>Prior to the 2016 season, the Diamondbacks reincorporated teal into its color scheme while keeping Sedona Red, Sonoran Sand and black. They also unveiled eight different uniform combinations, including two separate home white and away grey uniforms. One major difference between the two sets is that the non-teal uniforms feature a snakeskin pattern on the shoulders, while the teal-trimmed uniforms include a charcoal/grey snakeskin pattern on the back. Arizona also kept the throwback pinstriped sleeveless uniforms from their 2001 championship season for use during Thursday home games.<sup id="cite_ref-GilbertDbacks_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GilbertDbacks-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_and_television">Radio and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arizona_Diamondbacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radio and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The primary television play-by-play voice for the team\'s first nine seasons of play was <a href="/wiki/Thom_Brennaman" title="Thom Brennaman">Thom Brennaman</a>, who also broadcasts baseball and <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> games nationally for <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_on_Fox" class="mw-redirect" title="Major League Baseball on Fox">Fox Television</a>. Brennaman was the TV announcer for the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a> (along with his father <a href="/wiki/Marty_Brennaman" title="Marty Brennaman">Marty Brennaman</a>) before being hired by Diamondbacks founder Jerry Colangelo in 1996, two years before the team would begin play.\n</p><p>In October 2006, Brennaman left the Diamondbacks to call games with his father for the Reds beginning in 2007, signing a four-year deal (his FOX duties remained unchanged).\n</p><p>The English language flagship radio station is <a href="/wiki/KTAR_(AM)" title="KTAR (AM)">KTAR</a>. <a href="/wiki/Greg_Schulte" title="Greg Schulte">Greg Schulte</a> is the regular radio play-by-play voice, a 25-year veteran of sports radio in the Phoenix market, also well known for his previous work on <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Cardinals" title="Arizona Cardinals">Arizona Cardinals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a> (ASU) broadcasts.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jeff_Munn" title="Jeff Munn">Jeff Munn</a> is a backup radio play-by-play announcer; he served as the regular public address announcer at <a href="/wiki/Chase_Field" title="Chase Field">Chase Field</a> in the early days of the franchise. He is well known to many Phoenix area sports fans, having also served as the public address announcer for the Suns at America West Arena (now <a href="/wiki/Talking_Stick_Resort_Arena" title="Talking Stick Resort Arena">Talking Stick Resort Arena</a>) in the 1990s. He is also the play-by-play radio voice for ASU women\'s basketball.\n</p><p>On November 1, 2006, the team announced that the TV voice of the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a> since 2002, <a href="/wiki/Daron_Sutton" title="Daron Sutton">Daron Sutton</a>, would be hired as the Diamondbacks primary TV play-by-play voice. Sutton was signed to a five-year contract with a team option for three more years. Sutton is considered one of the best of the younger generation of baseball broadcasters. His signature chants include "let\'s get some runs" when the D-backs trail in late innings. Sutton\'s father is <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Major League Baseball Hall of Fame">Hall of Fame</a> pitcher and current <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Don_Sutton" title="Don Sutton">Don Sutton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mlb3_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlb3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Former Diamondbacks and Chicago Cubs first baseman <a href="/wiki/Mark_Grace" title="Mark Grace">Mark Grace</a> and former Major League <a href="/wiki/Knuckleball" title="Knuckleball">knuckleball</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Tom_Candiotti" title="Tom Candiotti">Tom Candiotti</a> were the Diamondbacks primary color analysts for the 2006 and 2007 seasons. Former Diamondbacks third baseman <a href="/wiki/Matt_Williams_(third_baseman)" title="Matt Williams (third baseman)">Matt Williams</a> also did color commentary on occasion, as did former <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">Cardinals</a> and <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> broadcast legend <a href="/wiki/Joe_Garagiola,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joe Garagiola, Sr.">Joe Garagiola, Sr.</a>, a longtime Phoenix-area resident and father of <a href="/wiki/Joe_Garagiola,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joe Garagiola, Jr.">Joe Garagiola, Jr.</a>, the first GM of the Diamondbacks (as head of the Maricopa County Sports Authority in the early 1990s, Garagiola, Jr. was one of the primary people involved in Phoenix obtaining a Major League Baseball franchise).\n</p><p>The Diamondbacks announced in July 2007<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> that for the 2008 season, all regionally broadcast Diamondbacks TV games will be shown exclusively on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_Arizona" title="Fox Sports Arizona">Fox Sports Arizona</a>, and a few could possibly be shown on the national <a href="/wiki/Fox_Major_League_Baseball" title="Fox Major League Baseball">Fox MLB</a> telecasts. Fox Sports Arizona (or FS Arizona) is currently seen in 2.8&#160;million households in Arizona and New Mexico. The previous flagship station, since the inaugural 1998 season, was <a href="/wiki/KTVK" title="KTVK">KTVK</a>, a popular over-the-air independent station (and former longtime <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> affiliate) in Phoenix.\n</p><p>From 2009 to 2012, Mark Grace and Daron Sutton were tagged as the main broadcasters of the Diamondbacks with pre-game and postgame shows on Fox Sports Arizona, being hosted by former big-league closer <a href="/wiki/Joe_Borowski_(baseball)" title="Joe Borowski (baseball)">Joe Borowski</a>.\n</p><p>On June 21, 2012, Daron Sutton was suspended indefinitely, amid rumors of insubordination.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Then on August 24, the team announced that Mark Grace had requested an indefinite leave of absence after being arrested for his second DUI in less than two years <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> (Grace was later indicted on four DUI counts<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>). For the remainder of the 2012 season, Sutton was replaced by Greg Schulte (Jeff Munn replaced Schulte on the radio broadcast) and Grace was replaced by Luis Gonzalez. At the end of the 2012 season, the team announced that neither Sutton nor Grace would be returning for the 2013 season.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 18, 2012, the team announced that <a href="/wiki/Bob_Brenly" title="Bob Brenly">Bob Brenly</a> would be returning as a broadcaster, and that he would be joined by then-<a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> personality <a href="/wiki/Steve_Berthiaume" title="Steve Berthiaume">Steve Berthiaume</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_broadcasts">Spanish broadcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arizona_Diamondbacks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spanish broadcasts">edit</a><span
