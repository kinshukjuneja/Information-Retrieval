href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom, run by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" title="Isles of Scilly Steamship Company">Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Marine_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marine-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gry_Maritha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>Gry Maritha</i> was built by Moen Slip in Norway, in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-Marine_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marine-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The ship was named after the daughter of the first captain, Tor Sevaldsen.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Purchased in 1989 by the Isles of Scilly Steamship Company, she is a lifeline to the communities on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> as she provides the only method of bulk freight transport from the United Kingdom mainland.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She carries just six passengers and was acquired for all year cargo and winter passenger services, as <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Scillonian III">Scillonian III</a></i> is laid up through the winter.<sup id="cite_ref-simplonpc_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simplonpc-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Between 12,000 and 14,000 tonnes of cargo a year are normally carried by <i>Scillonian III</i> and <i>Gry Maritha</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ivorb_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ivorb-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gry_Maritha.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Gry_Maritha.jpeg/220px-Gry_Maritha.jpeg" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Gry_Maritha.jpeg/330px-Gry_Maritha.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Gry_Maritha.jpeg/440px-Gry_Maritha.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="1946" data-file-height="1314" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gry_Maritha.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Gry Maritha</i> preparing to sail from Penzance in 2015.</div></div></div>\n<p>Most notably, <i>Gry Maritha</i> carries all the fuel requirements of the Isles of Scilly, using transportable fuel tanks on her deck. These are loaded and unloaded by the deck crane. Towards the stern, the deck can accept standard freight containers.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gry_Maritha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Marine-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-Marine_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-Marine_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text">Marine news, Volume 44.
