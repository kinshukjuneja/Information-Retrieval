links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan was founded on the river <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6ta_%C3%A4lv" title="G\xc3\xb6ta \xc3\xa4lv">G\xc3\xb6ta \xc3\xa4lv</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Trollh%C3%A4ttan_Falls" title="Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan Falls">Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan Falls</a>. The site was first mentioned in literature from 1413. For centuries, Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan was an obstacle for boats travelling the river, until a <a href="/wiki/Canal_lock" class="mw-redirect" title="Canal lock">lock</a> system was completed in the 19th century. It has since been updated several times, and the present locks were finished in 1916.\n</p><p>In the late 19th century, <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a> was developed in Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan. The Swedish energy corporation <a href="/wiki/Vattenfall" title="Vattenfall">Vattenfall</a> ("waterfall") took its name from the falls in Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan. Today the city has two operational hydropower stations, <a href="/wiki/Olidan_Power_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Olidan Power Station">Olidan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hojum_Power_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Hojum Power Station">H\xc3\xa5jum</a>. The hydropower has helped the city in its <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial revolution">industrial revolution</a>.\n</p><p>Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan was granted <a href="/wiki/City_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="City rights">city rights</a> (which today have no legal effect) in 1916 at which time it had about 15,000 inhabitants, now grown to 54,000.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Trollh%C3%A4ttan_Falls" title="Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan Falls">Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan Falls</a></div>\n<p>The name Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan is translated as "<a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">troll</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Bonnet_(headgear)" title="Bonnet (headgear)">bonnet</a>". The latter part "<i>h\xc3\xa4tta</i>" could also mean mountain top.\n</p><p>Other former names of the site are <i>Ei\xc3\xb0ar</i> and <i>Stora Edet</i>; the latter lives on in the name of the south-bordering municipality of <a href="/wiki/Lilla_Edet" title="Lilla Edet">Lilla Edet</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The following sports clubs are located in Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/IFK_Trollh%C3%A4ttan" title="IFK Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan">IFK Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/FC_Trollh%C3%A4ttan" title="FC Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan">FC Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Skoftebyns_IF" title="Skoftebyns IF">Skoftebyns IF</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Gripen_Trollh%C3%A4ttan_BK" title="Gripen Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan BK">Gripen Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan BK</a></li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" Bandy World Championship">2017 Bandy World Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industries">Industries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Industries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Saab_trolls.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Saab_trolls.jpg/220px-Saab_trolls.jpg" width="220" height="271" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Saab_trolls.jpg/330px-Saab_trolls.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Saab_trolls.jpg/440px-Saab_trolls.jpg 2x" houses the headquarters and a production plant of <a href="/wiki/National_Electric_Vehicle_Sweden" title="National Electric Vehicle Sweden">National Electric Vehicle Sweden</a>.\n</p><p>It also has a
