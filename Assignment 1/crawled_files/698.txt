flag, for a while also painted on <i>Scillonian III\'</i>s funnel</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</b> (ISSC) operates the principal shipping service from <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> county of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, located 28 miles (45&#160;km) to the southwest. It provides a year-round cargo service together with a seasonal passenger service in summer. The name of the company\'s principal ferry, the <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Scillonian III">Scillonian III</a></i>, is perhaps better known than that of the company itself.\n</p><p>The company is based in the Isles of Scilly Travel Centre Penzance.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It also owns <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus" title="Isles of Scilly Skybus">Isles of Scilly Skybus Ltd</a>, an operator of <a href="/wiki/Fixed-wing_aircraft" title="Fixed-wing aircraft">fixed-wing aircraft</a> services to the Isles of Scilly, and sight seeing flights.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010, the company had close to 1,000 shareholders, around half of whom live on the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-record_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-record-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Due to the Isles of Scilly\'s remote location, the shipping service is often called \'a life line\' for the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ivorb_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ivorb-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In addition to passengers, 12,000 to 14,000 tonnes of cargo are normally carried by <i>Scillonian III</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i> every year.<sup id="cite_ref-ivorb_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ivorb-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="toclevel-1 tocsection-9"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>From 1858 services were provided by the <a href="/wiki/Scilly_Isles_Steam_Navigation_Company" title="Scilly Isles Steam Navigation Company">Scilly Isles Steam Navigation Company</a> which was replaced in 1870 by the <a href="/wiki/West_Cornwall_Steam_Ship_Company" title="West Cornwall Steam Ship Company">West Cornwall Steam Ship Company</a>. This was wound up in 1907 and the services operated by John Banfield until 1917. For the rest of the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a> the services were operated by Government vessels. At the end of the war, a transport vacuum arose when the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Shipping_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Shipping (United Kingdom)">Ministry of Shipping</a> withdrew the wartime service between <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-islander_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islander-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 26 March 1920 the current Isles of Scilly Steamship Company was founded with \xc2\xa320,000 (equivalent to \xc2\xa3740,000 in 2016)<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> raised by selling shares, mostly in the Isles of Scilly. The company was formed with the express purpose of securing a regular shipping service between the islands and the mainland.<sup id="cite_ref-islander_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islander-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It came into service when the company received its <a href="/wiki/Certificate_of_Incorporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Certificate of Incorporation">Certificate of Incorporation</a>, giving the right to commence business, dated 27 March 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-backinservice_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backinservice-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Initially services were operated by the <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Argus_(1904)" title="HMS Argus (1904)">Peninnis</a></i> (ex-<i>HMS Argus</i>), which the company purchased from the Admiralty Disposals for \xc2\xa38,000 (equivalent to \xc2\xa3290,000 in 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-backinservice_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backinservice-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Following the necessary alterations at a cost of \xc2\xa35,000 (equivalent to \xc2\xa3180,000 in 2016)<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>,<sup id="cite_ref-backinservice_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backinservice-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> this former fishery protection vessel served as the company\'s ship from 1920 until 1927 but it soon became clear that a purpose-built, new steamer was required.<sup id="cite_ref-islander_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islander-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During 1924 the directors of the Steamship Company began considering the purchase of a larger ship and at an Extraordinary General Meeting of Shareholders on 16 June 1925, the directors were empowered to place the order with <a href="/wiki/Ailsa_Shipbuilding_Company" title="Ailsa Shipbuilding Company">Ailsa Shipbuilding Company</a> Ltd of <a href="/wiki/Troon" title="Troon">Troon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. In order to keep their yard open during the economic slump, this work was undertaken at cost, the contract price was \xc2\xa324,500 (equivalent to \xc2\xa31,280,000 in 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The new ferry, built to carry 400 passengers and cargo, was launched on 17 November 1925 and named <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_(1925)" title="Scillonian (1925)">Scillonian</a></i> by Mrs. A.A. Dorrien-Smith of <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey" class="mw-redirect" title="Tresco Abbey">Tresco Abbey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-backinservice_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-backinservice-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> At 11 pm on 25 January 1926 she completed her maiden voyage from Troon and docked at <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>. She made her first time-tabled, commercial trip from on Thursday 2 February 1926 from St. Mary\'s to Penzance. It took her three and a quarter hours.<sup id="cite_ref-islander_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islander-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Scillonian was grounded in the St Marys, Annet area on 10 September 1951<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> but refloated the next day, and kept in service until 1956.\n</p><p>Following this ship <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Scillonian II">Scillonian II</a></i> entered into service in 1956, purpose built for the Steamship Company by <a href="/wiki/John_I._Thornycroft_%26_Company" title="John I. Thornycroft &amp; Company">John I. Thornycroft &amp; Company</a> of <a href="/wiki/Woolston,_Southampton" title="Woolston, Southampton">Woolston, Southampton</a> at a cost of \xc2\xa3250,000, and was in service until 1977. A second ship operated for a while named <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_of_the_Isles" title="Queen of the Isles">Queen of the Isles</a></i>. At approx 500 tons she was smaller than <i>Scillonian II</i> and was used to augment the larger ship at busy times. She was also used to run charter services to the Islands from other ports. On her sale she went to operate an inter-island service in the Pacific.\n</p><p>The present <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Scillonian III">Scillonian III</a></i> was also purpose built for the Steamship Company and entered into service in  May 1977. <i>Scillonian III</i> is the third passenger ship of this name and currently in service for passengers and cargo 8 months of the year. Due to the number of tidal currents which meet off Lands End, and the need for a shallow draught to allow access to the Islands, the sea journey to Scilly can be rough. For this reason <i>Scillonian III</i> was fitted with a <a href="/wiki/Antiroll_Tanks#Passive_Antiroll_Tanks" class="mw-redirect" title="Antiroll Tanks">"Flume" antiroll stabilizer</a> system.\n</p><p>On 1 September 2009, the Steamship Company took over the lease to operate and manage <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> <a href="/wiki/Dry_Dock" class="mw-redirect" title="Dry Dock">Dry Dock</a>. Established in 1834, it is one of the UK\xe2\x80\x99s longest established dry docks and provides facilities for vessels requiring repair and conversion. It takes in 15 to 20 ships a year.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/Annual_General_Meeting" class="mw-redirect" title="Annual General Meeting">AGM</a> in September 2010, the following corporate figures were published: During the previous fiscal year, Steamship Company craft had carried 73,000 passengers to Scilly by air and sea. This accounted for 61.4% of the total passenger market on the route between Scilly and the mainland. <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus" title="Isles of Scilly Skybus">Skybus Ltd</a> had a market share to 50.2% of all passengers flying to <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-record_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-record-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2011, the Steamship Company acquired Nike Engineering of the Porthmellon Industrial Estate on <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>, which provides marine and mechanical engineering services to the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In February 2015, the Steamship Company acquired Island Carriers (also of the Porthmellon Industrial Estate) which provides haulage and courier services on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2016, the Steamship Company acquired the <i>Mali Rose</i>, a Norwegian freight ship that was expected to replace the smaller and older <i>Gry Maritha</i> in the autumn of that year. However as of August 2018, the <i>Gry Maritha</i> is still providing the freight service to the islands, as work to fit out and trial the new ship for its new role has taken longer than expected.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sole_Transport_Operator">Sole Transport Operator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sole Transport Operator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Since <a href="/wiki/British_International_Helicopters" title="British International Helicopters">British International Helicopters</a> ceased its service from Penzance in 2012, the Isles of Scilly Steamship Company has been the sole transport operator to the Isles of Scilly.\n</p><p>In February 2017 the Penzance Heliport<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> was unanimously granted planning permission by the Cornwall Council to reinstate a helicopter service from Penzance to the Isles of Scilly. In May of the same year, the Isles of Scilly Steamship Company began proceedings for a Judicial Review of the planning decision. These Judicial Review proceedings began after an anonymous appeal to the Secretary of State to review the planning permission failed a month earlier. The actions of the Steamship Company were widely condemned by businesses, residents, and visitors to Penzance and the Isles of Scilly. The judicial review was opposed by a petition started by an Isles of Scilly resident, which accumulated over 10,000 signatures in just a few days. Penzance Heliport withdrew and resubmitted an amended planning application in January 2018, and is still aiming to begin operating in 2019.\n</p><p>Following the objection to the Penzance Heliport plans, in February 2018 the Isles of Scilly Steamship Company announced its own helicopter service operating from Land\'s End, to begin in May 2018. This announcement met widespread condemnation from those aware of the Isles of Scilly Steamship Company\'s actions. On 21 May 2018, the steamship company inaugurated its new service from <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End_Airport" title="Land&#39;s End Airport">Land\'s End Airport</a> to St Mary\xe2\x80\x99s with a 10-seater <a href="/wiki/AgustaWestland_AW169" title="AgustaWestland AW169">AgustaWestland AW169</a> helicopter.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
