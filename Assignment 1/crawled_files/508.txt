href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> occur in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_latitudes" title="Middle latitudes">middle latitudes</a>, which span between the <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropics</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Polar_regions_of_Earth" title="Polar regions of Earth">polar regions of Earth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> These zones generally have wider <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> ranges throughout the year and more distinct <a href="/wiki/Season" title="Season">seasonal</a> changes compared to <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climates</a>, where such variations are often small. \n</p><p>The <b>temperate zones</b> (latitudes from 35\xc2\xb0 to the <a href="/wiki/Polar_circle" title="Polar circle">polar circles</a> at about 66.5\xc2\xb0, <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">north</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circle" title="Antarctic Circle">south</a>) are where the widest seasonal changes occur, with most climates found in it having somewhat balanced influence from both the tropics and the <a href="/wiki/Geographical_pole" title="Geographical pole">poles</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">subtropics</a> (latitudes from about 23.5\xc2\xb0 to 35\xc2\xb0, <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">north</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">south</a>) have temperate climates that show further similarities with the tropics, usually having warmer summers and milder winters. The poleward outskirts of the temperate zone, is where the coldest but yet temperate climates such as the <a href="/wiki/Subarctic_climate" title="Subarctic climate">boreal climate</a> are found, with colder winters and milder summers, showing further similarities with the poles. \n</p><p>It must be reminded that not only latitudinal positions influence temperature changes and that sea currents, <a href="/wiki/Air_mass" title="Air mass">air masses</a>, continentality, maritimity, and altitude will also be defining factors, with climates considered temperate being found even in tropical areas or milder climates in polar regions. Humidity and precipitation changes are also taken into account.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="K\xc3\xb6ppen climate classification">K\xc3\xb6ppen climate classification</a> defines a climate as "temperate" when the coldest month has a mean temperature above \xe2\x88\x923&#160;\xc2\xb0C (26.6&#160;\xc2\xb0F) but below 18&#160;\xc2\xb0C (64.4&#160;\xc2\xb0F).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="Zones_and_climates">Zones and climates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Zones and climates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <b>north temperate zone</b> extends from (approximately 23.5\xc2\xb0 north) to the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a> (approximately 66.5\xc2\xb0 north latitude). The <b>south temperate zone</b> extends from (approximately 23.5\xc2\xb0 south) to the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circle" title="Antarctic Circle">Antarctic Circle</a> (at approximately 66.5\xc2\xb0 south).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>In some climate classifications</i>, the temperate zone is often divided into several smaller climate zones, based on monthly temperatures, the coldest month, and rainfall. These include <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental climate</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Humid_subtropical_climates">Humid subtropical climates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Humid subtropical climates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:257px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png/255px-Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png" width="255" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png/383px-Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png/510px-Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png 2x" data-file-width="4231" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Koppen_World_Map_Cwa_Cfa.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Regions where the Humid (Cfa) and Dry-Winter Subtropical (Cwa) climates are found.</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">Subtropical climates</a> are generally located between 23.5\xc2\xb0 and 35\xc2\xb0 north or south on the eastern or leeward sides of landmasses. This climate has long hot summers, and short, mild winters, with annual rainfall often concentrated in the warmest part of the year. These climates may occur in southern Asia, the southeastern United States, parts of eastern Australia, and in eastern coastal South America.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dry-winter_subtropical_climates">Dry-winter subtropical climates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dry-winter subtropical climates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>This is a variant of the Subtropical climate that experiences a much drier winter. Similar in characteristic to the Mediterranean Climate which is also a Subtropical Climate with a dry season, in this case a dry summer. The drier season is usally due to proximity towards drier continental regions, so this climate is more commonly found in the subtropical countryside, near dry areas, such as Central-West <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mediterranean_climates">Mediterranean climates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mediterranean climates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Koppen_World_Map_C.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Koppen_World_Map_C.png/260px-Koppen_World_Map_C.png" width="260" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Koppen_World_Map_C.png/390px-Koppen_World_Map_C.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Koppen_World_Map_C.png/520px-Koppen_World_Map_C.png 2x" data-file-width="4231" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Koppen_World_Map_C.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Regions where the Dry Summer Subtropical or Mediterranean Climates are found.</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Dry Summer Subtropical or Mediterranean climates</a> occur generally between 30\xc2\xb0 and 42\xc2\xb0 north and south, on the western sides of landmasses. This climate has warm to hot summers and short mild winters; however, seasonal rainfall is the opposite of that of the humid subtropical type, with a winter or cool season rainfall peak being typical, and summer as drier season. These climates occur near the rimlands of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, in western and southern <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> region of North America, in southwestern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, and in the western areas of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oceanic_climates">Oceanic climates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Oceanic climates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">Oceanic climates</a> occur in the higher middle latitudes, between 45\xc2\xb0 and 60\xc2\xb0 north and south. They are created by the onshore flow from the cool high latitude oceans to their west. This causes the climate to have cool summers and cool (but not cold) winters. These climates are frequently cloudy, and are fairly milder both in winter and summer in comparison to other temperate climates. Annual rainfall is spread throughout the entire year. Regions with this climate include <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a>, northwestern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, southeastern and southwestern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, southeastern <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Koppen_World_Map_Cfb_Cfc_Cwb_Cwc.png" It is found on large landmasses, away from the moderating effects of an ocean, generally at <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitudes</a> from 50\xc2\xb0 to 70\xc2\xb0 N poleward of the <a class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Human aspects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span id="Demography.2C_fauna_and_flora"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography,_fauna_and_flora">Demography, fauna and flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Demography, fauna and flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The vast majority of the world\'s human population resides in temperate zones, especially in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>, due to its greater mass of land.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The biggest described number in temperate region in the world is found in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">southern Africa</a>, where some 24,000 taxa (species and infraspecific taxa) have been described, but the native fauna and flora of this region does not have much cultural importance for the majority of the human population of the world that lives in Temperate Zones and that live in the Northern Hemisphere, only environmental importance.<sup id="cite_ref-ger_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ger-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Farming is a large-scale practice in the temperate regions (except for Boreal/Subarctic regions) due to the plentiful rainfall and warm summers, because most agricultural activity occurs in the spring and summer, cold winters have a small effect on agricultural production. Extreme winters or summers have a huge impact on the productivity of agriculture.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Urbanization">Urbanization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Urbanization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Temperate regions have the majority of the world\'s population, which leads to large cities. There are a couple factors why the climate of large city landscapes differs from the climate of rural areas. One factor is the strength of the absorption rate of builds and asphalt, which is higher than natural land. The other large factor is the burning of fossil fuels from buildings and vehicles. These factors have led to the average climate of cities to be warmer than surrounding areas.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Misconceptions">Misconceptions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Misconceptions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>"Temperate Climate" refers to climates between Polar and Tropical. However, there is a gradual change from polar to tropical climates across the middle latitude temperate zones. Normally the northern portions of the temperate zone feature <a href="/wiki/Boreal_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Boreal climate">Boreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">Continental</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">Oceanic climates</a>, while the southern portions of the temperate zone are often <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climates</a>. These different climates all fall within the temperate zone which has a basic definition as being any climate with a mean temperature above \xe2\x88\x923&#160;\xc2\xb0C (26.6&#160;\xc2\xb0F) but below 18&#160;\xc2\xb0C (64.4&#160;\xc2\xb0F) in the coldest month. Obviously winter temperatures will be more mild in southerly portions and colder in the more northerly portions of the temperate zone.\n</p><p>From a floristic standpoint, each of these climate zones have generally temperate vegetation. There is however, an obvious change in flora from the far northern portions of the temperate zone (Boreal) to the far southern portion (humid subtropical). Pines and conifers are endemic and more abundant in the northern portion of the temperate, while palms may be endemic to far southern portions of the temperate zone. Along with this, the humid subtropical climate may be wrongly separated from the Temperate group of climates, and further associated with the Tropical climates, much because of its name, temperatures and the vegetation found in Subtropical zones which can be somewhat similar to that of Tropical regions (For instance the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Forest" title="Atlantic Forest">Atlantic Forest</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> is spread across both Temperate and Tropical zones of the country). Additionally, both temperate and tropical species can be found side by side in the far southern portions of the temperate zone (for example in central Florida), while subarctic species might be found growing in northern portions of the continental zone (for example in Northwest Europe).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Geographical_zone" title="Geographical zone">Geographical zone</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">Habitat</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="K\xc3\xb6ppen climate classification">K\xc3\xb6ppen climate classification</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Middle_latitudes" title="Middle latitudes">Middle latitudes</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Polar_circle" title="Polar circle">Polar circle</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">Subtropics</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temperate_climate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
