href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Tre Huw</i>) is the largest settlement on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> and its administrative centre. The town is situated on the island of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the largest and most populous island in the archipelago, and is located on a narrow <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a> which joins the peninsula known as the Garrison (historically the Hugh) with the rest of the island.\n</p><p>The population recorded by the 2011 census was 1,097<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (up from 1,068 in 2001). Unlike the rest of the Isles of Scilly, the <a href="/wiki/Freehold_(law)" title="Freehold (law)">freehold</a> title to land in the town is <i>not</i> held by the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> \xe2\x80\x94 in 1949 it was sold to the inhabitants. The harbour however continues to be owned and run by the Duchy.<sup id="cite_ref-harbour_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harbour-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Hugh Town becomes particularly busy during the <a href="/wiki/May_Day" title="May Day">May Day</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Public holidays in the United Kingdom">bank holiday</a> weekend (ie the first weekend in May) when the <a href="/wiki/World_Pilot_Gig_Championships" title="World Pilot Gig Championships">World Pilot Gig Championships</a> are held. The competing teams base themselves in the several bays of St Mary\'s Pool (the large natural bay to the north of Hugh Town) and the numerous races finish at the end of the harbour quay.\n</p>\n<div id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugh_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The name Hugh is derived from an Old English word \'hoh\' meaning a promontory or elevated ground. The Garrison was originally known as The Hugh or Hugh Hill. The same word is used in <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Hoe" title="Plymouth Hoe">Plymouth Hoe</a> and Hoe Point on <a href="/wiki/Gugh" title="Gugh">Gugh</a>. Cemeteries containing native British people, were found in 1949 and 1960 on the Porthcress side of the town, when housing was built. The remains are dated from the 1st to 4th century. A Roman altar was found on The Garrison in the 19th century and is now in <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">Tresco Abbey Gardens</a>. The area has not been excavated so there may be an undiscovered shrine or temple.<sup id="cite_ref-ks4_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ks4-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During medieval times <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a> was the main centre of population but from the 17th century onwards, due to its sheltered harbour, Hugh Town began to grow. Star Castle was built in 1593, the quay in 1601 and in the 18th century, as the military defences increased, The Hugh became known as The Garrison.<sup id="cite_ref-ks4_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ks4-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amenities_and_landmarks">Amenities and landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugh_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Amenities and landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Hugh Street is the main shopping area on the islands and business is quite reliant on <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>. There are several pubs, restaurants and hotels, as well as two banks and a <a href="/wiki/Post_office" title="Post office">post office</a>. A notable building is the <a href="/wiki/Star_Castle,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Star Castle, Isles of Scilly">Star Castle</a>, which overlooks the town from The Garrison and is now a hotel. Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Tregarthen%27s_Hotel" title="Tregarthen&#39;s Hotel">Tregarthen\'s Hotel</a> is a Hugh Town landmark. It was originally the home of Captain Tregarthen who introduced the first sloop in 1849, \'Ariadne\', that serviced the Hugh Town from Penzance.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Being on an isthmus, there are two beaches, the northern one (Town Beach) being along the bay which forms the island\'s main <a href="/wiki/Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="Harbour">harbour</a>; the southern beach and bay is named Porthcressa.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Scilly_Isles,_Hughtown,_-i.e.,_Hugh_Town-,_from_Garrison,_Cornwall,_England-LCCN2002696609.tif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Scilly_Isles%2C_Hughtown%2C_-i.e.%2C_Hugh_Town-%2C_from_Garrison%2C_Cornwall%2C_England-LCCN2002696609.tif/lossy-page1-220px-Scilly_Isles%2C_Hughtown%2C_-i.e.%2C_Hugh_Town-%2C_from_Garrison%2C_Cornwall%2C_England-LCCN2002696609.tif.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Scilly_Isles%2C_Hughtown%2C_-i.e.%2C_Hugh_Town-%2C_from_Garrison%2C_Cornwall%2C_England-LCCN2002696609.tif/lossy-page1-330px-Scilly_Isles%2C_Hughtown%2C_-i.e.%2C_Hugh_Town-%2C_from_Garrison%2C_Cornwall%2C_England-LCCN2002696609.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Scilly_Isles%2C_Hughtown%2C_-i.e.%2C_Hugh_Town-%2C_from_Garrison%2C_Cornwall%2C_England-LCCN2002696609.tif/lossy-page1-440px-Scilly_Isles%2C_Hughtown%2C_-i.e.%2C_Hugh_Town-%2C_from_Garrison%2C_Cornwall%2C_England-LCCN2002696609.tif.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3722" data-file-height="2791" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Scilly_Isles,_Hughtown,_-i.e.,_Hugh_Town-,_from_Garrison,_Cornwall,_England-LCCN2002696609.tif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Photocrom" class="mw-redirect" title="Photocrom">Photocrom</a> picture of Hugh Town, circa 1900. The Duchy of Cornwall, which owns almost all the freehold land in the Isles of Scilly, as well as being the port authority, has its Isles of Scilly offices at Hugh House, located within the Garrison walls.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Church, St Mary&#39;s">St Mary\'s Church</a>, the islands\' <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> Church and their largest <a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> Church are all located on or near Church Street. The Isles of Scilly Museum is also located on Church Street.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The town has a public library and a police station. On the eastern outskirts of the town (heading towards Peninnis Head) are the NHS community hospital and local health centre, as well as other important public amenities such as the island\'s power station, the island\'s refuse depot and the town\'s fire station. The island\'s primary and secondary schools (both part of the federated <a href="/wiki/Five_Islands_School" title="Five Islands School">Five Islands School</a>) are situated to the southeast of the town, at Carn Gwaval, on the road to <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Airport_(Isles_of_Scilly)" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mary&#39;s Airport (Isles of Scilly)">island\'s airport</a> is located approximately 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) to the east of the centre of Hugh Town.\n</p>\n<h2><span data-file-height="427" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary%27s_Harbour_-_geograph.org.uk_-_812175.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <i>Scillonian III</i> berthed at the (former Rat Island) end of the quay</div></div></div>\n<p>The principal harbour of the Isles of Scilly (known as <b>St Mary\'s Harbour</b>) is located on the northern edge of the town, naturally formed by the bay at Town Beach (itself part of a larger bay named St Mary\'s Pool) and improved by the construction of a <a href="/wiki/Quay" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay">quay</a> on its western side, which acts also as a <a href="/wiki/Breakwater_(structure)" title="Breakwater (structure)">breakwater</a>. The quay is the terminal of the <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a> to <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>, currently the <a href="/wiki/RMV_Scillonian_III" title="RMV Scillonian III"><i>Scillonian III</i></a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the cargo vessel <i><a href="/wiki/Gry_Maritha" title="Gry Maritha">Gry Maritha</a></i>; both vessels have St Mary\'s as their <a href="/wiki/Port_of_registry" class="mw-redirect" title="Port of registry">port of registry</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> is the owner and the harbour authority (the St Mary\xe2\x80\x99s Harbour and Pilotage Authority).<sup id="cite_ref-harbour_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harbour-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The original quay was built in 1593 and an extension (or offshoot) began in 1836 to Rat Island under a contract of \xc2\xa34,000 to William Martin of Penzance. Martin resigned in November 1837 and <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> organised the completion. The enlargement of the harbour produced an increase in harbour dues as larger and deeper-draughted boats were now able to dock for loading, unloading, repairs or <a href="/wiki/Victualling" class="mw-redirect" title="Victualling">victualling</a>. In 1889 Augustus Smith\'s successor <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Algernon_Smith-Dorrien-Smith" title="Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith">T A Dorrien-Smith</a> extended the pier a further 250 feet eastwards, at his own expense, so that the <a href="/wiki/West_Cornwall_Steam_Ship_Company" title="West Cornwall Steam Ship Company">West Cornwall Steam Ship Company</a> ships could dock. Despite Parliamentary opposition a Pier and Harbour Provisional Order Bill passed in June 1890 gave him revenue from the harbour dues.<sup id="cite_ref-vyvyan_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vyvyan-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The quay is currently (as of 2015) being extended further, with new passenger and freight facilities being constructed too. The extension to the quay will allow for vessels of a greater draft (up to 3.9m). A small amount of land reclamation will take place by Rat Island too, enlarging the space around the buildings there.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifeboat_station">Lifeboat station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugh_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lifeboat href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Lifeboat_Station" title="St Mary&#39;s Lifeboat Station">St Mary\'s Lifeboat Station</a>, first operated in 1837 and run by the <a
