Godolphin</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\n\n<b>Francis Godolphin, 2nd Baron Godolphin</b> (2 November 1706 &#8211; 25 May 1785) was a British <a href="/wiki/Peerage" title="Peerage">peer</a> and politician. <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was the eldest surviving son of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Godolphin" title="Henry Godolphin">Henry Godolphin</a>, provost of Eton and <a href="/wiki/Dean_of_St_Paul%E2%80%99s" class="mw-redirect" title="Dean of St Paul\xe2\x80\x99s">Dean of St Paul\xe2\x80\x99s</a>, and was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a> (1718\xe2\x80\x931721) and <a href="/wiki/Queen%E2%80%99s_College,_Oxford" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen\xe2\x80\x99s College, Oxford">Queen\xe2\x80\x99s College, Oxford</a> (1723). In 1733 he succeeded to his father\'s estates in Buckinghamshire.\n</p><p>He was appointed <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Scilly Isles">Lieutenant-governor of the Scilly Isles</a> from 1739 to 1766 and full Governor from 1766 to his death. He was also recorder for Helston from 1766 to his death.\n</p><p>He was elected a <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) for the borough of <a href="/wiki/Helston_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Helston (UK Parliament constituency)">Helston</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> from 1741<sup id="cite_ref-stooks-smith_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stooks-smith-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> to 1766,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> when he succeeded to the Barony of Helston on the death of his cousin <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin,_2nd_Earl_of_Godolphin" title="Francis Godolphin, 2nd Earl of Godolphin">Francis Godolphin, 2nd Earl of Godolphin</a>.\n</p><p>He married twice:firstly Lady Barbara Bentinck, the daughter of <a href="/wiki/William_Bentinck,_1st_Earl_of_Portland" title="William Bentinck, 1st Earl of Portland">William Bentinck, 1st Earl of Portland</a> and secondly Lady Anne Fitzwilliam, the daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Fitzwilliam,_2nd_Earl_Fitzwilliam" title="John Fitzwilliam, 2nd Earl Fitzwilliam">John Fitzwilliam, 2nd Earl Fitzwilliam</a>. He had no children and thus the Barony became extinct.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin,_2nd_Baron_Godolphin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"> <cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.historyofparliamentonline.org/volume/1715-1754/member/godolphin-francis-1706-85">"GODOLPHIN, Francis (1706-85), of Baylis, Bucks"</a>.
