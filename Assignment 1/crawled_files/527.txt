member of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B8nneberg_family" title="R\xc3\xb8nneberg family">R\xc3\xb8nneberg family</a>. He was the brother of <a href="/wiki/Erling_R%C3%B8nneberg" title="Erling R\xc3\xb8nneberg">Erling R\xc3\xb8nneberg</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a training.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On the maternal side he was a nephew of <a href="/wiki/Ole_R%C3%B8mer_Aagaard_Sandberg" title="Ole R\xc3\xb8mer Aagaard Sandberg">Ole R\xc3\xb8mer Aagaard Sandberg</a>, and thus a grandnephew of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ole_R%C3%B8mer_Aagaard_Sandberg_(1865%E2%80%931925)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ole R\xc3\xb8mer Aagaard Sandberg (1865\xe2\x80\x931925) (page does not exist)">Ole R\xc3\xb8mer Aagaard Sandberg, Sr</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nbl_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbl-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On the paternal side he was a second great grandson of <a href="/wiki/Carl_R%C3%B8nneberg" title="Carl R\xc3\xb8nneberg">Carl R\xc3\xb8nneberg</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-nbl_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbl-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and a grandnephew of politician <a href="/wiki/Anton_Johan_R%C3%B8nneberg" title="Anton Johan R\xc3\xb8nneberg">Anton Johan R\xc3\xb8nneberg</a>, whose mother was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Holmboe_(family)" title="Holmboe (family)">Holmboe family</a>&#8212;hence Joachim\'s middle name.\n</p><p>On 19 September 1949, he married Liv Foldal, a crafts teacher born in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-nbl_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbl-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> where a statue honouring him was unveiled by <a href="/wiki/Princess_Astrid_of_Norway" class="mw-redirect" title="Princess Astrid of Norway">Princess Astrid</a> at the end of August 2014.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_R%C3%B8nneberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>R\xc3\xb8nneberg reported for <a href="/wiki/National_service" title="National service">national service</a> in 1938, being told to report for duty with the <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">surveying</a> department in 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-NFF_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NFF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_R%C3%B8nneberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> broke out when R\xc3\xb8nneberg was a young adult, and <a href="/wiki/Occupation_of_Norway_by_Nazi_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Occupation of Norway by Nazi Germany">Norway was occupied by Germany</a> from April 1940. He joined <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Independent_Company_1" title="Norwegian Independent Company 1">Norwegian Independent Company 1</a> (NOR.I.C.1) (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <i lang="no">Kompani Linge</i>) in 1941,<sup id="cite_ref-snl1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snl1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> having escaped Norway with eight friends by boat to <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-NFF_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NFF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-new_mission_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-new_mission-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He received military training in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-snl1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snl1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and held the rank of <a href="/wiki/Second_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Second Lieutenant">Second Lieutenant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nkl_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nkl-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Heavy_water_sabotage">Heavy water sabotage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_R%C3%B8nneberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Heavy water sabotage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg/220px-Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg/330px-Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg/440px-Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg 2x" data-file-width="617" data-file-height="406" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vemork_Hydroelectric_Plant_1935.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Vemork hydroelectric plant in 1935. The heavy water was produced in the front building (the Hydrogen Production Plant).</div></div></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_heavy_water_sabotage" title="Norwegian heavy water sabotage">Norwegian heavy water sabotage</a></div>\n<p>R\xc3\xb8nneberg, now a <a href="/wiki/First_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="First Lieutenant">First Lieutenant</a> and put in charge of training, selected and led the six-man <a href="/wiki/Operation_Gunnerside" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation Gunnerside">Operation Gunnerside</a> team, reinforcing the five-man team <i>Grouse</i> sent in earlier, during the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_heavy_water_sabotage" title="Norwegian heavy water sabotage">heavy water sabotage action</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-new_mission_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-new_mission-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After landing at a location 45 kilometres (28&#160;mi) from the other team <i>Gunnerside</i> spent five days waiting out an intense blizzard in an uninhabited hunting cabin before meeting up with <i>Grouse</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The combined Norwegian team went into action against the <a href="/wiki/Norsk_Hydro" title="Norsk Hydro">Norsk Hydro</a> <a href="/wiki/Heavy_water" title="Heavy water">heavy water</a> production plant in <a href="/wiki/Vemork" title="Vemork">Vemork</a> in 1943, parachuting into the <a href="/wiki/Hardangervidda" title="Hardangervidda">Hardangervidda</a> plateau on 16 February.<sup id="cite_ref-NFF_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NFF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> R\xc3\xb8nneberg led the demolition team when the saboteurs, on the night of 27\xe2\x80\x9328 February 1943, entered the Norsk Hydro plant and set explosive charges. The team then escaped from the factory as the explosives went off, without the German guards discovering the saboteurs or indeed noticing that there had been an attack on the plant, probably believing that the heavy snow had set off one of their own <a href="/wiki/Land_mine" title="Land mine">land mines</a>. R\xc3\xb8nneberg recalled the dawn as they escaped: "It was a mackerel sky, it was a marvellous sunrise. We sat there very tired, very happy. Nobody said anything. That was a very special moment."<sup id="cite_ref-new_mission_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-new_mission-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Although chased by 2,800 German troops,<sup id="cite_ref-Moland9_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moland9-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> five of the saboteurs, led by R\xc3\xb8nneberg, escaped safely to <a href="/wiki/Sweden_during_World_War_II" title="Sweden during World War II">neutral Sweden</a> by way of a 14-day march over a distance of 400 kilometres (250&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-Voks\xc3\xb8311_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Voks\xc3\xb8311-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> after the successful completion of their mission.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The six other members of the sabotage team hid out in various locations in Norway without being caught by the Germans.<sup id="cite_ref-Voks\xc3\xb8311_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Voks\xc3\xb8311-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Eighteen heavy water cells and around 500 kilograms (1,100&#160;lb) of heavy water was destroyed during the attack,<sup id="cite_ref-nk2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nk2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> as well as a loss of production of 400 kilograms (880&#160;lb) of heavy water.<sup id="cite_ref-Moland9_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moland9-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After the factory was reported to have been rebuilt in the summer of 1943 a new saboteur attack was planned, but eventually scrapped in favour of an <a href="/wiki/Airstrike" title="Airstrike">air strike</a>. On 16 November 1943 161 <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> <a href="/wiki/B-17_Flying_Fortress" class="mw-redirect" title="B-17 Flying Fortress">B-17</a> and <a href="/wiki/B-24_Liberator" class="mw-redirect" title="B-24 Liberator">B-24</a> <a href="/wiki/Heavy_bomber" title="Heavy bomber">heavy bombers</a> attacked the Vemork heavy water plant, and another 12 bombers the <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a> plant at nearby <a href="/wiki/Rjukan" title="Rjukan">Rjukan</a>. The attack had not been cleared with the Norwegian government in exile in London and led to a diplomatic crisis between the Norwegian and other <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> governments. Of particular concern for the Norwegian government was the targeting of the Rjukan nitrogen plant, as it supposedly only produced products for Norwegian <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nk3_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nk3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Twenty-one civilian lives were lost in the bombing raid.<sup id="cite_ref-Moland9_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moland9-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Following the bombing raid the Germans decided to move the production to Germany, leading the <a href="/wiki/Churchill_war_ministry" title="Churchill war ministry">British War Cabinet</a> to order Norwegian saboteur <a href="/wiki/Knut_Haukelid" title="Knut Haukelid">Knut Haukelid</a> to sink the Norwegian ferry <a href="/wiki/SF_Hydro" title="SF Hydro">SF <i>Hydro</i></a> carrying the containers of heavy water across <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tinn" class="mw-redirect" title="Lake Tinn">Lake Tinn</a>. The ferry was sunk with hidden explosives on 19 February 1944, going down with 15,000 litres (3,300&#160;imp&#160;gal) of heavy water and killing 14 Norwegian civilians, ending the struggle for the Norwegian heavy water.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The sabotage action against the Vemork plant was portrayed in the Franco-Norwegian 1948 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fight_Over_the_Heavy_Water" class="mw-redirect" title="The Fight Over the Heavy Water">Operation Swallow: The Battle for Heavy Water</a></i>, where R\xc3\xb8nneberg was portrayed by Norwegian actor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Claus_Wiese_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Claus Wiese (actor) (page does not exist)">Claus Wiese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 1965 the  less-than-accurate American film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heroes_of_Telemark" title="The Heroes of Telemark">The Heroes of Telemark</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Douglas" title="Kirk Douglas">Kirk Douglas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Harris" title="Richard Harris">Richard Harris</a>, was released by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pictures" title="Columbia Pictures">Columbia Pictures</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> R\xc3\xb8nneberg dismissed this film as a "hopeless" portrayal, when he told his memories in 2010 after many years of silence.<sup id="cite_ref-new_mission_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-new_mission-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_World_War_II_work">Other World War II work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_R%C3%B8nneberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other World War II work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>R\xc3\xb8nneberg subsequently commanded other raids against the Germans, including the <a href="/wiki/Fieldfare_Cabin" title="Fieldfare Cabin">Fieldfare operation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sunnm%C3%B8re" title="Sunnm\xc3\xb8re">Sunnm\xc3\xb8re</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-nkl_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nkl-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> in preparation for attacks against German supply lines in the <a href="/wiki/Romsdal" title="Romsdal">Romsdal</a> valley.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In January 1945 R\xc3\xb8nneberg had led a three-man unit of NOR.I.C.1 on a mission to destroy the <a href="/wiki/Stugufl%C3%A5t_Bridge" title="Stugufl\xc3\xa5t Bridge">Stugufl\xc3\xa5t railway bridge</a>, blowing up the bridge with a 130-kilogram (290&#160;lb) charge of <a href="/wiki/Plastic_explosive" title="Plastic explosive">plastic explosives</a>, putting it out of service for three weeks. The team then escaped without casualties.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>His service with NOR.I.C.1 ended with the liberation of Norway in 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-snl1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snl1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours_and_awards">Honours and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_R%C3%B8nneberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Honours and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1943, he was awarded Norways\'s highest decoration for military gallantry, the <a href="/wiki/War_Cross_with_sword" class="mw-redirect" title="War Cross with sword">War Cross with sword</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Krigskorset_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Krigskorset-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> For his war service R\xc3\xb8nneberg also received <a href="/wiki/St._Olav%27s_Medal_With_Oak_Branch" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Olav&#39;s Medal With Oak Branch">St. Olav\'s Medal With Oak Branch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defence_Medal_1940%E2%80%931945" title="Defence Medal 1940\xe2\x80\x931945">Defence Medal 1940\xe2\x80\x931945</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haakon_VIIs_70th_Anniversary_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="Haakon VIIs 70th Anniversary Medal">Haakon VIIs 70th Anniversary Medal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrkdok_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrkdok-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In addition to his Norwegian decorations, he was also decorated by the British with the Distinguished Service Order (<a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">DSO</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-DSO_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSO-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> by the Americans with the <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Freedom_(1945)" title="Medal of Freedom (1945)">Medal of Freedom with silver palm</a> and by the French with the <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Honour" title="Legion of Honour">Legion of Honour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Croix_de_guerre_1939%E2%80%931945" class="mw-redirect" title="Croix de guerre 1939\xe2\x80\x931945">Croix de Guerre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nrkdok_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nrkdok-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war_career">Post-war career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joachim_R%C3%B8nneberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-war career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After the war he began a career in broadcasting. He was hired in <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Broadcasting_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian Broadcasting Corporation">NRK</a> \xc3\x85lesund in 1948, was promoted to programme secretary in 1954 and sub-editor in 1977. He retired in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-snl1_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snl1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 1970s, from 1971, R\xc3\xb8nneberg was governor of <a href="/wiki/Rotary_International" title="Rotary International">Rotary International</a>\'s 128th district.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He also participated in the rebuilding of <a href="/wiki/Fieldfare_Cabin" title="Fieldfare Cabin">Fieldfare Cabin</a> in the valley <a href="/w/index.php?title=Veltedalen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Veltedalen (page does not exist)">Veltedalen</a> in the summer of 1990, where he had hidden out the last year of the war with two other officers from NOR.I.C.1. Fieldfare Cabin today gives an image of Norwegian resistance during the war.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In his later years R\xc3\xb8nneberg was involved in war information work, holding lectures for audiences around Norway. He said that he was particularly fond of holding talks for school children. R\xc3\xb8nneberg was highly critical of the current situation for the Norwegian military, stating that its capacity for mobilisation was only 9% of the 1990 level.<sup id="cite_ref-NFF_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NFF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1995, R\xc3\xb8nneberg, together with fellow World War II resistance leader <a href="/wiki/Gunnar_S%C3%B8nsteby" title="Gunnar S\xc3\xb8nsteby">Gunnar S\xc3\xb8nsteby</a> and Norwegian businessman <a href="/wiki/Erling_Lorentzen" title="Erling Lorentzen">Erling Lorentzen</a>, received the Norwegian-American Chamber of Commerce Achievement Award for "individuals whose outstanding personal accomplishments exemplify the spirit of commitment, perseverance and endeavor that sustains the strong relations between Norway and the United States of America".<sup id="cite_ref-NACC_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NACC-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> R\xc3\xb8nneberg was a member of the Linge Club, a Norwegian veterans\' association, until it was disbanded on 17 October 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2013, R\xc3\xb8nneberg was presented with a <a href="/wiki/Union_Jack" title="Union Jack">Union Jack</a> during a ceremony at the <a href="/wiki/Special_Operations_Executive" title="Special Operations Executive">Special Operations Executive</a> (SOE) monument in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> to mark 70 years since the successful Gunnerside mission.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>R\xc3\xb8nneberg died on 21 October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bibliography">Bibliography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
