style="white-space:nowrap;padding-right:0.65em;">Language</th><td>\nSpanish</td></tr></tbody></table> \n<p><i><b>La Flor</b></i> (English: <i>The Flower</i>) is a 2018 Argentine film written and directed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariano_Llin%C3%A1s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mariano Llin\xc3\xa1s (page does not exist)">Mariano Llin\xc3\xa1s</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariano_Llin%C3%A1s" class="extiw" title="es:Mariano Llin\xc3\xa1s">es</a>)</span>. With a length of 868 minutes including intermissions, it is the <a href="/wiki/Longest_film" class="mw-redirect" title="Longest film">longest film</a> in the history of <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Argentina" title="Cinema of Argentina">Argentine cinema</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cronk-cinema-scope_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cronk-cinema-scope-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The film is a joint project by the production group El Pampero Cine and the acting company Piel de Lava, made up of actresses Elisa Carricajo, Valeria Correa, Pilar Gamboa, and Laura Paredes. It premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires_International_Festival_of_Independent_Cinema" title="Buenos Aires International Festival of Independent Cinema">Buenos Aires International Festival of Independent Cinema</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Plot"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Plot</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Production"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Production</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Release"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Release</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Flor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>La Flor</i> is broken into six separate episodes, connected only by an on-screen appearance by Llin\xc3\xa1s explaining the film\'s structure.<sup id="cite_ref-cronk-cinema-scope_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cronk-cinema-scope-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The first four episodes have the beginning of a story but finish <i><a href="/wiki/In_medias_res" title="In medias res">in medias res</a></i>. The fifth episode is the only one to proceed from start to end, and the last episode has just the conclusion of a story.\n</p><p>The first episode is shot as a <a href="/wiki/B_movie" title="B movie">B movie</a>, where a group of researchers encounter a mummy and its supernatural curse. The second episode has two parallel plots. One follows a couple that has broken up as they reunite to record a song together, and the other is a mystery about a secret society formulating a potion for eternal life using a supposedly extinct scorpion. The third episode is about a group of spies, with extended flashbacks describing each of their backstories.\n</p><p>The fourth episode uses an experimental <a href="/wiki/Metanarrative" title="Metanarrative">metanarrative</a> in which the four lead actresses play actresses who turn against their director and his elaborate narrative structure. After the director disappears, an investigator develops a theory about the actresses after reading a story about <a href="/wiki/Casanova" class="mw-redirect" title="Casanova">Casanova</a>. The fifth episode is a black-and-white, largely silent remake of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Renoir" title="Jean Renoir">Jean Renoir</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Partie_de_campagne" title="Partie de campagne">Partie de campagne</a></i>. The sixth episode is told through the diary of an Englishwoman living in the Americas during the nineteenth century. She and three other women leave the desert after being held captive many years.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Flor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Llin\xc3\xa1s first met the actresses of Piel de Lava in 2005 after seeing them in the play <i>Neblina</i> (English: <i>Mist</i>). They pitched him the idea of adapting it into a film. The group began working on this initial concept but eventually shifted to the idea of a single film encompassing multiple smaller films.<sup id="cite_ref-cronk-cinema-scope_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cronk-cinema-scope-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zimerman-clarin_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zimerman-clarin-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The film\'s structure is linked to its title through a drawing that illustrates the arrangement of the six episodes in the shape of a flower.<sup id="cite_ref-pereyra-andes_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pereyra-andes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Filming <i>La Flor</i> began in 2009, with each episode being shot sequentially.<sup id="cite_ref-cronk-cinema-scope_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cronk-cinema-scope-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zimerman-clarin_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zimerman-clarin-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The first episodes were shot on <a href="/wiki/MiniDV" class="mw-redirect" title="MiniDV">MiniDV</a>, which gave the images a <a href="/wiki/Shallow_focus" title="Shallow focus">shallow focus</a>. While filming the second episode, the crew switched to a <a href="/wiki/Canon_EOS_7D" title="Canon EOS 7D">Canon EOS 7D</a> camera.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Llin\xc3\xa1s worked closely off of a script for the first two episodes, often recording multiple takes.<sup id="cite_ref-mandelbaum-monde_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mandelbaum-monde-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The third episode, shot from 2011 to 2016, took the longest to film.<sup id="cite_ref-cronk-cinema-scope_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cronk-cinema-scope-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The crew began shooting without a script in order to capture more natural performances from the cast. <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">Voice-over</a> narration was added later.<sup id="cite_ref-mandelbaum-monde_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mandelbaum-monde-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Filming locations included Argentina, Colombia, Nicaragua, Honduras, England, France, Germany, Hungary, Bulgaria, Russia, and Mongolia.<sup id="cite_ref-pereyra-andes_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pereyra-andes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Flor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span <i>La Flor</i> at the <a href="/wiki/2018_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2018 Toronto International Film Festival">2018 Toronto International Film Festival</a></div></div></div>\n<p>While the film was still in production, the first two episodes of <i>La Flor</i> premiered in 2016. They were shown at a surprise screening at Festifreak in <a href="/wiki/La_Plata" title="La Plata">La Plata</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Several other screenings of the incomplete version were held throughout Argentina.<sup id="cite_ref-pereyra-andes_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pereyra-andes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>La Flor</i> premiered in its entirety at the 2018 BAFICI. The film was screened in three sections that began on April 14. It was awarded Best Film, and Piel de Lava won for Best Female Performance.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i>La Flor</i> was selected to screen
