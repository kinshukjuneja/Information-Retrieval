href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Dowr an Velinji</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a short river in north <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, UK, with many small tributaries. After running past <a href="/wiki/Lesnewth" title="Lesnewth">Lesnewth</a> it cuts a valley before entering the sea at the harbour of the village of <a href="/wiki/Boscastle" title="Boscastle">Boscastle</a>. One of its tributaries is the Jordan, which runs 2 kilometres (1.2&#160;mi) north before joining the Valency in <a href="/wiki/Boscastle" title="Boscastle">Boscastle</a> just before the B3263 road bridge.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg/220px-Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg/330px-Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg/440px-Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Source_of_the_Valency_-_geograph.org.uk_-_729319.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Source of the Valency.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Valency valley is steep-sided and the sides of the lower section are wooded. The valley has been flooded many times, most seriously in the <a href="/wiki/Boscastle_flood_of_2004" title="Boscastle flood of 2004">Boscastle flood of 2004</a> when significant channel erosion occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (Heavy rainfall for 7 hours over a wide area in the afternoon of 16 August 2004 led to severe flooding and structural damage.)  The agricultural use and fast drainage within the catchment areas of the Valency and Jordan are related to the extent of the Boscastle flooding.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Valency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The name has been explained as a corruption of the Cornish Melinjy (i.e. Melin-Chy = Mill-house) from the mill which existed in medieval times.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Valency&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.magakernow.org.uk/default.aspx?page=520">Place-names in the Standard Written Form (SWF)</a>&#160;: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.magakernow.org.uk/idoc.ashx?docid=79ba408d-7c02-499e-8cd6-b18dd48de58d&amp;version=-1"><b>List of place-names agreed by the MAGA Signage Panel</b></a>. <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Language_Partnership" title="Cornish Language Partnership">Cornish Language Partnership</a>.</span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/cornwall/6631795.stm">BBC News May 2007 -
