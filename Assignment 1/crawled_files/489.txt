href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><i>for other uses of the word, see <a href="/wiki/Lide_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Lide (disambiguation)">Lide (disambiguation)</a></i>\n</p><p><b>Saint Lide</b>, also known as <b>Elid</b> or <b>Elidius</b>, was a legendary bishop who lived on the island of <a href="/wiki/St_Helen%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Helen&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Helen\'s</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. The name of the Island of St Helen\'s is supposed to be a corruption of Lide\'s name. His feast day is on 8 August and is celebrated on Scilly by the local churches holding a service on St Helen\'s on a mid-August Sunday.\n</p><p>According to Rev. <a href="/wiki/T._F._Thiselton-Dyer" title="T. F. Thiselton-Dyer">T. F. Thiselton-Dyer</a>\'s 1876 <i>British Popular Customs,</i> the first Friday in March is so called from Lide, Anglo-Saxon for March. This day is marked by a seriocomic custom of sending a young lad onto the highest mound or hillock of the work [i.e. tin mine], and allowing him to sleep there as long as he can, the length of his siesta being the measure of the afternoon nap for the <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Cornwall_and_Devon" title="Mining in Cornwall and Devon">tinners</a> throughout the ensuing twelve months.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="External_links">External links</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: External links">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.orthodox.net/western-saints/lide-of-the-scilly-isles.html">St Nicholas Russian Orthodox Church</a> St Lide</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20100302052850/http://www.scillyarchive.com/history/">Scilly Archive</a> History of the Isles of Scilly</li></ul>\n<p><br />\n</p>\n<table class="metadata plainlinks stub" role="presentation" style="background:transparent"><tbody><tr><td><a href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Cornwall.svg" class="image"><img alt="Stub icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Flag_of_Cornwall.svg/30px-Flag_of_Cornwall.svg.png" width="30" height="20" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Flag_of_Cornwall.svg/45px-Flag_of_Cornwall.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Flag_of_Cornwall.svg/60px-Flag_of_Cornwall.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="400" /></a></td><td><i>This <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> article is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Stub" title="Wikipedia:Stub">stub</a>.
