class="mw-redirect" title="Officer of the Order of the British Empire">OBE</a>,&#32;<a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Literature" class="mw-redirect" title="Fellow of the Royal Society of Literature">FRSL</a>,&#32;<a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_King%27s_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Fellow of King&#39;s College">FKC</a>,&#32;<a href="/wiki/Deputy_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" author, poet, playwright, and <a href="/wiki/Librettist" class="mw-redirect" title="Librettist">librettist</a> who is known best for <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_novels" class="mw-redirect" title="Children&#39;s novels">children\'s novels</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/War_Horse_(novel)" title="War Horse (novel)">War Horse</a></i> (1982).  His work is noted for its "magical storytelling",<sup id="cite_ref-guardian1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> for recurring themes such as the triumph of an outsider or survival, for characters\' relationships with nature, and for vivid settings such as the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornish coast</a> or <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Morpurgo became the third <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Laureate" title="Children&#39;s Laureate">Children\'s Laureate</a>, from 2003 to 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-laureate_3-0" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Morpurgo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Morpurgo was born in 1943 as <b>Michael Andrew Bridge</b>; his biological father was actor <a href="/wiki/Tony_Van_Bridge" title="Tony Van Bridge">Tony Van Bridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His mother, Kippe Cammaerts (otherwise Catherine Noel Kippe, daughter of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89mile_Cammaerts" title="\xc3\x89mile Cammaerts">\xc3\x89mile Cammaerts</a>), who had been an actress, met, and later in 1963 married Jack Morpurgo (subsequently <a href="/wiki/Academic_ranks_in_the_United_Kingdom#Professors" title="Academic ranks in the United Kingdom">professor</a> of American Literature at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Leeds" title="University of Leeds">University of Leeds</a> from 1969-82<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>) while Van Bridge was away during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.\n</p><p>Morpurgo did not learn who his biological father was until he was 19 years old,<sup id="cite_ref-horse_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-horse-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> while watching the 1962 film version of <i><a href="/wiki/Great_Expectations" title="Great Expectations">Great Expectations</a></i>, with his mother, in which Tony Van Bridge appeared.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Morpurgo attended schools in central London, <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>;<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> his <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school#Psychological_issues" title="Boarding school">unhappy experiences at boarding school</a> would inform his novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Butterfly_Lion" title="The Butterfly Lion">The Butterfly Lion</a></i>. He attended the independent <a href="/wiki/Preparatory_school_(United_Kingdom)" title="Preparatory school (United Kingdom)">prep school</a> Mount House in Devon until he was 13.  He then went to <a href="/wiki/The_King%27s_School,_Canterbury" title="The King&#39;s School, Canterbury">the King\'s School</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent school</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canterbury" title="Canterbury">Canterbury</a>, prior to studying at <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College_London" title="King&#39;s College London">King\'s College London</a> and graduating with a <a href="/wiki/British_undergraduate_degree_classification#Third-class_honours" title="British undergraduate degree classification">third class degree</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> before entering the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Military_Academy_Sandhurst" title="Royal Military Academy Sandhurst">Royal Military Academy Sandhurst</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-horse_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-horse-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  He then joined the teaching profession<sup id="cite_ref-horse_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-horse-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> with a job in a <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary</a> school in <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>. He also, from 1968, briefly taught at <a href="/wiki/St._Faith%27s_School" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Faith&#39;s School">St. Faith\'s School</a> in Cambridge.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Morpurgo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_teaching_to_writing">From teaching to writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Morpurgo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: From teaching to writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Morpurgo\'s writing career was inspired by <a href="/wiki/Ted_Hughes" title="Ted Hughes">Ted Hughes</a>\' <i>Poetry in the Making</i>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gallico" title="Paul Gallico">Paul Gallico</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Snow_Goose" class="mw-redirect" title="The Snow Goose">The Snow Goose</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Hemingway" title="Ernest Hemingway">Ernest Hemingway</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Old_Man_and_the_Sea" title="The Old Man and the Sea">The Old Man and the Sea</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Hughes and another poet, <a href="/wiki/Se%C3%A1n_Rafferty" title="Se\xc3\xa1n Rafferty">Se\xc3\xa1n Rafferty</a>, were influential in his career, with Hughes becoming a friend, mentor and neighbour. It was not until he was teaching in Kent that Morpurgo discovered his vocation in life, of which he later said "I could see there was magic in it for them, and realized there was magic in it for me."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Farms_for_city_children">Farms for city children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Morpurgo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Farms for city children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Farms_for_City_Children" title="Farms for City Children">Farms for City Children</a></div>\n<p>Morpurgo married Clare, eldest daughter of Sir <a href="/wiki/Allen_Lane" title="Allen Lane">Allen Lane</a>, the founder of <a href="/wiki/Penguin_Books" title="Penguin Books">Penguin Books</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>   In 1976 Michael and Clare established the charity <a href="/wiki/Farms_for_City_Children" title="Farms for City Children">Farms for City Children</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> with the primary aim of providing children from inner city areas with experience of the countryside.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The programme involves the children spending a week at a countryside farm, during which they take part in purposeful farmyard work.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>About 85,000 children have taken part in the scheme since it was set up, and the charity now has three farms in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a> and Wales.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Children.27s_Laureate"></span><span class="mw-headline"
