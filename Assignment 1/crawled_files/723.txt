href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB) in <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> that was subsumed into the <a href="/wiki/New_Forest_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="New Forest National Park">New Forest National Park</a> when it was established on 1 April 2005. It lies between the New Forest and the west shore of the <a href="/wiki/Solent" title="Solent">Solent</a>.\n</p><p>It includes freshwater <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salt-marsh" class="mw-redirect" title="Salt-marsh">salt-marsh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shingle_beach" title="Shingle beach">shingle</a>,   tidal mudflats, wooded coastal lowlands and the estuaries of the <a href="/wiki/Beaulieu_river" class="mw-redirect" title="Beaulieu river">Beaulieu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lymington" title="Lymington">Lymington</a> rivers.\n</p><p>The entire length of the AONB\'s coast is covered by <a href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" class="mw-redirect" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest</a> and the estuaries in particular are notable for wildlife.
