<a href="/wiki/Hill" title="Hill">hills</a> in the <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is a designated <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB), and the highest area of land in eastern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> between <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>. They run roughly parallel with the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> coast, from the <a href="/wiki/River_Humber" class="mw-redirect" title="River Humber">River Humber</a> in the north-west to the edge of the <a href="/wiki/The_Fens" title="The Fens">Lincolnshire Fens</a> in the south-east.<sup id="cite_ref-OS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NCA43_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCA43-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincolnshire_Wolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Wolds comprise a series of low hills and steep valleys underlain by calcareous (<a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> rock, laid down in the <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> period. The characteristic open valleys of the Wolds were created during the last <a href="/wiki/Glacial_period" title="Glacial period">glacial period</a> through the action of <a href="/wiki/Glaciation" class="mw-redirect" title="Glaciation">glaciation</a> and meltwater.\n</p><p>Geologically, the Lincolnshire Wolds is a continuation of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Wolds" title="Yorkshire Wolds">Yorkshire Wolds</a> which runs through the <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>; the point at which the ranges of hill crosses the Humber is known as the <a href="/wiki/Humber_Gap" title="Humber Gap">Humber Gap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NCA43_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCA43-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg/220px-Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg" width="220" height="88" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg/330px-Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg/440px-Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7024" data-file-height="2804" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lincolnshire_Wolds.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View from Red Hill in August</div></div></div>\n<p>The Lincolnshire Wolds can be divided into four distinct areas:<sup id="cite_ref-NCA43_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCA43-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<ol><li>the main area of chalk hills in the north,</li>\n<li>the north west <a href="/wiki/Escarpment" title="Escarpment">scarp</a>,</li>\n<li>an area of ridges and valleys in the south west,</li>\n<li>the <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">claylands</a> in the south east.</li></ol>\n<p>The Red Hill <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a> near the village of <a href="/wiki/Goulceby" class="mw-redirect" title="Goulceby">Goulceby</a> is notable for the unusual red colour of its soil and underlying chalk.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wolds_Top" title="Wolds Top">Wolds Top</a> is the highest point in the whole of Lincolnshire and is class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincolnshire_Wolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: AONB">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Lincolnshire Wolds were designated an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB) in 1973, and are managed as such by the <i>Lincolnshire Wolds Countryside Service</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Wolds Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty covers 560 square kilometres, while the Countryside Service recognise a wider <i>Lincolnshire Wolds Character/Natural Area</i> which incorporates the AONB and the neighbouring areas of the (geographical) Wolds to the north and south, which are not covered by the AONB.<sup id="cite_ref-NCA43_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCA43-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Wolds AONB cuts across the council boundaries of Lincolnshire County Council, <a href="/wiki/East_Lindsey" title="East Lindsey">East Lindsey</a> District Council, <a href="/wiki/West_Lindsey" title="West Lindsey">West Lindsey</a> District Council and <a href="/wiki/North_East_Lincolnshire" title="North East Lincolnshire">North East Lincolnshire</a> Council.<sup id="cite_ref-OS_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NCA43_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCA43-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The boundaries of the AONB are marked by <a href="/wiki/Tourist_sign" title="Tourist sign">tourist signs</a> incorporating stylized hills and trees, placed on roads leading into and out of the Wolds. The placing of these signs has not been uncontroversial, with some residents unhappy that they outline only the Wolds AONB and not the cultural/geographical area as a whole.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People_and_places">People and places</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincolnshire_Wolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: People and places">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Wolds are sparsely populated and have a <a href="/wiki/Rural" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural">rural</a> character. They are \'ringed\' by several small <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market towns</a> that lie around their edge:<sup id="cite_ref-OS_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Alford,_Lincolnshire" title="Alford, Lincolnshire">Alford</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Horncastle,_Lincolnshire" title="Horncastle, Lincolnshire">Horncastle</a>, billed on tourist signs as the "gateway to the Wolds", lies just outside the south end of the AONB</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Louth,_Lincolnshire" title="Louth, Lincolnshire">Louth</a>, "the capital of the Wolds"</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Market_Rasen" title="Market Rasen">Market Rasen</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Caistor" title="Caistor">Caistor</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Spilsby" title="Spilsby">Spilsby</a></li></ul>\n<p>Many of the placenames in the Wolds indicate a strong <a href="/wiki/Viking" class="mw-redirect" title="Viking">Viking</a> influence in the area\'s history. There is also an abundance of <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">mediaeval</a> \'<a href="/wiki/Abandoned_village" title="Abandoned village">lost villages</a>\' \xe2\x80\x93 settlements abandoned due to changes in land use, <a href="/wiki/Soils_retrogression_and_degradation" class="mw-redirect" title="Soils retrogression and degradation">soil exhaustion</a> and disease.\n</p><p>Several notable roads and paths run over the Wolds. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caistor_High_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caistor High Street (page does not exist)">Caistor High Street</a>, the path of a <a href="/wiki/Roman_road" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman road">Roman road</a> and now the route of the B1225, runs from Caistor to <a href="/wiki/Baumber" title="Baumber">Baumber</a> near Horncastle. The ancient <a href="/wiki/Bluestone_Heath_Road" title="Bluestone Heath Road">Bluestone Heath Road</a> follows the course of an ancient <a href="/wiki/Drove_road" class="mw-redirect" title="Drove road">drove road</a> from west to east across the Wolds, and several <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_road_numbering_scheme" title="Great Britain road numbering scheme">"A" roads</a> also run through the AONB.<sup id="cite_ref-OS_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Wolds are now promoted as a tourist destination: the area\'s connection with <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Tennyson,_1st_Baron_Tennyson" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfred Tennyson, 1st Baron Tennyson">Tennyson</a> (who was born in Somersby) is being exploited,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>   and <a href="/wiki/Farmer" title="Farmer">farmers</a> are being encouraged to diversify into the tourism industry. The roads of the Wolds are particularly popular with <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcyclists</a>, and the area is home to <a href="/wiki/Cadwell_Park" title="Cadwell Park">Cadwell Park</a>, one of the UK\'s top <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">race</a> circuits.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The area is also popular with <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hikers</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Viking_Way" title="Viking Way">Viking Way</a> <a href="/wiki/Long-distance_trail" title="Long-distance trail">long-distance footpath</a> runs from <a href="/wiki/Barton-upon-Humber" title="Barton-upon-Humber">Barton-upon-Humber</a> in North Lincolnshire across the Lincolnshire Wolds and into <a href="/wiki/Rutland" title="Rutland">Rutland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  and there is a <a href="/wiki/Youth_Hostel" class="mw-redirect" title="Youth Hostel">Youth Hostel</a> in the middle of the Wolds at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.geograph.org.uk/photo/483429">Woody\'s Top</a> near the village of <a href="/wiki/Tetford" title="Tetford">Tetford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
