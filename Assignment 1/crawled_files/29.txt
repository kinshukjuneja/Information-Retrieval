/>.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Stroud</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in Gloucestershire, England. It is named after its largest town, <a href="/wiki/Stroud,_Gloucestershire" class="mw-redirect" title="Stroud, Gloucestershire">Stroud</a>, and has its administrative headquarters in Ebley Mill, in the <a href="/wiki/Ebley" title="Ebley">Ebley</a> area on the western outskirts of the town. \n</p><p>The district is mixed and consists of part of the <a href="/wiki/Cotswolds" title="Cotswolds">Cotswolds</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Berkeley" title="Vale of Berkeley">Vale of Berkeley</a>, an area of the flat, fertile valley of the <a href="/wiki/River_Severn" title="River Severn">River Severn</a>. The town of <a href="/wiki/Stroud,_Gloucestershire" class="mw-redirect" title="Stroud, Gloucestershire">Stroud</a> is by some way the largest in the area. The southern portion of the district is served mostly by its own market towns, chief among which are <a href="/wiki/Dursley" title="Dursley">Dursley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wotton-under-Edge" title="Wotton-under-Edge">Wotton-under-Edge</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div href="/w/index.php?title=Stroud_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Stroud District Council was formed under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, on 1 April 1974, by a merger of <a href="/wiki/Nailsworth" title="Nailsworth">Nailsworth</a> and Stroud <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dursley_Rural_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Dursley Rural District">Dursley Rural District</a>, Stroud Rural District, and parts of <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_Rural_District" title="Gloucester Rural District">Gloucester Rural District</a>, Sodbury Rural District and <a href="/wiki/Thornbury_Rural_District" title="Thornbury Rural District">Thornbury Rural District</a>.\n</p><p>The area is rich in <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> remnants and is of particular interest to <a href="/wiki/Archaeologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Archaeologist">archaeologists</a> for its <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> burial grounds, of which there are over 100. Much of its wealth was built on the cloth industry during the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian era</a>, and its many mills, most of which are now listed buildings, survive as testament to this. Much of the landscape in this area is designated as an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a>. The esteemed <a href="/wiki/Cotswold_Way" title="Cotswold Way">Cotswold Way</a> walk leads through the area. There are <a href="/wiki/Gliding" title="Gliding">gliding</a> clubs at <a href="/wiki/Aston_Down" title="Aston Down">Aston Down</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nympsfield" title="Nympsfield">Nympsfield</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stroud_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Stroud_District_Council" title="Stroud District Council">Stroud District Council</a></div>\n<p>Elections to the district council are held in three out of every four years, with one third of the seats on the council being elected at each election. At the <a href="/wiki/Stroud_District_Council_election,_2016" title="Stroud District Council election, 2016">2016 election</a>, the coalition of <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a>, Greens and the Liberal Democrats retained its majority on Stroud District Council.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stroud_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170215135902/http://www2.stroud.gov.uk/info/members/cms_documentation/Cl_mins_29_Nov_12.pdf">"Council Meeting 29 November 2012, Minutes"</a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>.
