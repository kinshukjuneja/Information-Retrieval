The city of <a href="/wiki/Salisbury" title="Salisbury">Salisbury</a> is notable for its <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_Cathedral" title="Salisbury Cathedral">mediaeval cathedral</a>. class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The county, in the 9th century written as <i>Wiltunscir</i>, later <i>Wiltonshire</i>, is named after the former county town of <a href="/wiki/Wilton,_Wiltshire" title="Wilton, Wiltshire">Wilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Wiltshire" title="History of Wiltshire">History of Wiltshire</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg/220px-Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg/330px-Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg/440px-Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1752" data-file-height="1196" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stonehenge_back_wide.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Stonehenge" title="Stonehenge">Stonehenge</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Wiltshire is notable for its pre-<a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mesolithic" title="Mesolithic">Mesolithic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> people that occupied southern Britain built settlements on the hills and downland that cover Wiltshire. <a href="/wiki/Stonehenge" title="Stonehenge">Stonehenge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Avebury" title="Avebury">Avebury</a> are perhaps the most famous Neolithic sites in the UK.\n</p><p>In the 6th and 7th centuries Wiltshire was at the western edge of <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Saxon</a> Britain, as <a href="/wiki/Cranborne_Chase" title="Cranborne Chase">Cranborne Chase</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Somerset_Levels" title="Somerset Levels">Somerset Levels</a> prevented the advance to the west. The Battle of <a href="/wiki/Bedwyn" class="mw-redirect" title="Bedwyn">Bedwyn</a> was fought in 675 between <a href="/wiki/Aescwine_of_Wessex" class="mw-redirect" title="Aescwine of Wessex">Escuin</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">West Saxon</a> nobleman who had seized the throne of <a href="/wiki/Seaxburh_of_Wessex" title="Seaxburh of Wessex">Queen Saxburga</a>, and <a href="/wiki/King_Wulfhere" class="mw-redirect" title="King Wulfhere">King Wulfhere</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mercia" title="Mercia">Mercia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 878 the <a href="/wiki/Danes_(Germanic_tribe)" title="Danes (Germanic tribe)">Danes</a> invaded the county. Following the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Conquest">Norman Conquest</a>, large areas of the country came into the possession of the crown and the church.\n</p><p>At the time of the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Survey" class="mw-redirect" title="Domesday Survey">Domesday Survey</a> the industry of Wiltshire was largely agricultural; 390 <a href="/wiki/Mill_(grinding)" title="Mill (grinding)">mills</a> are mentioned, and <a href="/wiki/Vineyards" class="mw-redirect" title="Vineyards">vineyards</a> at Tollard and Lacock. In the succeeding centuries sheep-farming was vigorously pursued, and the Cistercian monastery of <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Abbey" title="Stanley Abbey">Stanley</a> exported wool to the <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florentine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flemish</a> markets in the 13th and 14th centuries.\n</p><p>In the 17th century <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> Wiltshire was largely <a href="/wiki/Roundhead" title="Roundhead">Parliamentarian</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Roundway_Down" title="Battle of Roundway Down">Battle of Roundway Down</a>, a Royalist victory, was fought near <a href="/wiki/Devizes" title="Devizes">Devizes</a>.\n</p><p>In 1794 it was decided at a meeting at the Bear Inn in Devizes to raise a body of ten independent troops of <a href="/wiki/Yeomanry" title="Yeomanry">Yeomanry</a> for the county of Wiltshire, which formed the basis for what would become the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Wiltshire_Yeomanry" title="Royal Wiltshire Yeomanry">Royal Wiltshire Yeomanry</a>, who served with distinction both at home and abroad, during the <a href="/wiki/Boer_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Boer War">Boer War</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The Royal Wiltshire Yeomanry currently lives on as Y (RWY) Squadron, based in Swindon, and B (RWY) Squadron, based in Salisbury, of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Wessex_Yeomanry" title="Royal Wessex Yeomanry">Royal Wessex Yeomanry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Around 1800 the <a href="/wiki/Kennet_and_Avon_Canal" title="Kennet and Avon Canal">Kennet and Avon Canal</a> was built through Wiltshire, providing a route for transporting cargoes from <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a> to London until the development of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a>.\n</p><p>Information on the 261 civil parishes of Wiltshire is available on Wiltshire Council\'s Wiltshire Community History<sup id="cite_ref-wiltshire_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiltshire-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> website which has maps, demographic data, historic and modern pictures and short histories.\n</p><p>The local nickname for Wiltshire natives is "<a href="/wiki/Moonrakers" title="Moonrakers">Moonrakers</a>". This originated from a story of <a href="/wiki/Smuggler" class="mw-redirect" title="Smuggler">smugglers</a> who managed to foil the local <a href="/wiki/Excise" title="Excise">Excise</a> men by hiding their alcohol, possibly French <a href="/wiki/Brandy" title="Brandy">brandy</a> in barrels or kegs, in a village pond. When confronted by the excise men they raked the surface to conceal the submerged <a href="/wiki/Contraband" title="Contraband">contraband</a> with ripples, and claimed that they were trying to rake in a large round cheese visible in the pond, really a reflection of the full moon. The officials took them for simple yokels or mad and left them alone, allowing them to continue with their illegal activities. Many villages claim the tale for their own village pond, but the story is most commonly linked with The Crammer in <a href="/wiki/Devizes" title="Devizes">Devizes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-devizesheritage_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-devizesheritage-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Geology.2C_landscape_and_ecology"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology,_landscape_and_ecology">Geology, landscape and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology, landscape and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_hills_of_Wiltshire" title="List of hills of Wiltshire">List of hills of Wiltshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_rivers_of_Wiltshire" title="List of rivers of Wiltshire">List of rivers of Wiltshire</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg/220px-Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg/330px-Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg/440px-Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="375" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cherhillwhitehorse.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Cherhill_White_Horse" title="Cherhill White Horse">Cherhill White Horse</a>, east of Calne</div></div></div>\n<p>Two-thirds of Wiltshire, a mostly rural county, lies on <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a>, a kind of soft, white, porous limestone that is resistant to erosion, giving it a high <a href="/wiki/Downland" title="Downland">chalk downland</a> landscape. This chalk is part of a system of chalk downlands throughout eastern and southern England formed by the rocks of the <a href="/wiki/Chalk_Group" title="Chalk Group">Chalk Group</a> and stretching from the <a href="/wiki/Dorset_Downs" title="Dorset Downs">Dorset Downs</a> in the west to <a href="/wiki/Dover" title="Dover">Dover</a> in the east. The largest area of chalk in Wiltshire is <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_Plain" title="Salisbury Plain">Salisbury Plain</a>, which is used mainly for <a href="/wiki/Agronomy" title="Agronomy">arable</a> agriculture and by the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> as training ranges. The highest point in the county is the <a href="/wiki/Tan_Hill,_Wiltshire" title="Tan Hill, Wiltshire">Tan Hill</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/Milk_Hill" title="Milk Hill">Milk Hill</a> ridge in the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Pewsey" title="Vale of Pewsey">Pewsey Vale</a>, just to the north of Salisbury Plain, at 295&#160;m (968&#160;ft) above sea level.\n</p><p>The chalk uplands run northeast into <a href="/wiki/West_Berkshire" title="West Berkshire">West Berkshire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Marlborough_Downs" class="mw-redirect" title="Marlborough Downs">Marlborough Downs</a> ridge, and southwest into Dorset as <a href="/wiki/Cranborne_Chase" title="Cranborne Chase">Cranborne Chase</a>. Cranborne Chase, which straddles the border, has, like Salisbury Plain, yielded much Stone Age and Bronze Age <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a>. The Marlborough Downs are part of the <a href="/wiki/North_Wessex_Downs_AONB" class="mw-redirect" title="North Wessex Downs AONB">North Wessex Downs AONB</a> (Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty), a 1,730&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (670-square-mile) conservation area.\n</p><p>In the northwest of the county, on the border with <a href="/wiki/South_Gloucestershire" title="South Gloucestershire">South Gloucestershire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bath_and_North_East_Somerset" title="Bath and North East Somerset">Bath and North East Somerset</a>, the underlying rock is the resistant <a href="/wiki/Oolite" title="Oolite">oolite</a> <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cotswolds" title="Cotswolds">Cotswolds</a>. Part of the Cotswolds AONB is also in Wiltshire, in the county\'s northwestern corner.\n</p><p>Between the areas of chalk and limestone downland are <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a> <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_valley" class="mw-redirect" title="River valley">vales</a>. The largest of these vales is the <a href="/wiki/River_Avon,_Bristol" title="River Avon, Bristol">Avon Vale</a>. The Avon cuts diagonally through the north of the county, flowing through <a href="/wiki/Bradford-on-Avon" title="Bradford-on-Avon">Bradford-on-Avon</a> and into <a href="/wiki/Bath,_Somerset" title="Bath, Somerset">Bath</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>. The Vale of Pewsey has been cut through the chalk into <a href="/wiki/Greensand" title="Greensand">Greensand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Clay" title="Oxford Clay">Oxford Clay</a> in the centre of the county. In the south west of the county is the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Wardour" title="Vale of Wardour">Vale of Wardour</a>. The southeast of the county lies on the sandy soils of the northernmost area of the <a href="/wiki/New_Forest" title="New Forest">New Forest</a>.\n</p><p>Chalk is a <a href="/wiki/Porous" class="mw-redirect" title="Porous">porous</a> rock, so the chalk hills have little surface water. The main settlements in the county are therefore situated at wet points. Notably, Salisbury is situated between the chalk of Salisbury Plain and marshy flood plains.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_belt">Green belt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Green belt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Avon_Green_Belt" title="Avon Green Belt">Avon Green Belt</a></div>\n<p>The county has <a href="/wiki/Green_belt_(United_Kingdom)" title="Green belt (United Kingdom)">green belt</a> mainly along its western fringes as a part of the extensive Avon green belt, reaching as far as the outskirts of Rudloe/Corsham and Trowbridge, preventing urban sprawl particularly from the latter in the direction of Bradford-on-Avon, and affording further protection to surrounding villages and towns from Bath in Somerset.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Along with the rest of <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>, Wiltshire has a temperate climate which is generally wetter and milder than the rest of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-weather_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weather-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The annual mean temperature is approximately 10&#160;\xc2\xb0C (50.0&#160;\xc2\xb0F). <a href="/wiki/Temperateness" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperateness">Seasonal temperature variation</a> is less extreme than most of the United Kingdom because of the adjacent sea temperatures. The summer months of July and August are the warmest with mean daily maxima of approximately 21&#160;\xc2\xb0C (69.8&#160;\xc2\xb0F). In winter mean minimum temperatures of 1&#160;\xc2\xb0C (33.8&#160;\xc2\xb0F) or 2&#160;\xc2\xb0C (35.6&#160;\xc2\xb0F) are common.<sup id="cite_ref-weather_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weather-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In the summer the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> high pressure affects southwest England; however, <a href="/wiki/Convective" class="mw-redirect" title="Convective">convective</a> cloud sometimes forms inland, reducing the number of hours of sunshine. Annual sunshine rates are slightly less than the regional average of 1,600 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-weather_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weather-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In December 1998 there were 20 days without sun recorded at Yeovilton. Most of the rainfall in the southwest is caused by <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">Atlantic depressions</a> or by <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a>. Most of the rainfall in autumn and winter is caused by the Atlantic depressions, which is when they are most active. In summer, a large proportion of the rainfall is caused by sun heating the ground leading to convection and to showers and thunderstorms. Average rainfall is around 700&#160;mm (28&#160;in). About 8\xe2\x80\x9315 days of snowfall is typical. November to March have the highest mean wind speeds, and June to August have the lightest winds. The predominant wind direction is from the southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-weather_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weather-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>This is a chart of trend of regional <a href="/wiki/Gross_value_added" title="Gross value added">gross value added</a> (GVA) of Wiltshire at current basic prices<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> with figures in millions of British Pounds Sterling.\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Year</th>\n<th>Regional gross value added<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></th>\n<th>Agriculture<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></th>\n<th>Industry<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></th>\n<th>Services<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>1995</td>\n<td><b>4,354</b></td>\n<td>217</td>\n<td>1,393</td>\n<td>2,743\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>2000</td>\n<td><b>5,362</b></td>\n<td>148</td>\n<td>1,566</td>\n<td>3,647\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>2003</td>\n<td><b>6,463</b></td>\n<td>164</td>\n<td>1,548</td>\n<td>4,751\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The Wiltshire economy benefits from the "<a href="/wiki/M4_corridor" title="M4 corridor">M4 corridor</a> effect", which attracts business, and the attractiveness of its countryside, towns and villages. The northern part of the county is richer than the southern part, particularly since Swindon is home to national and international corporations such as <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motorola" title="Motorola">Motorola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patheon" title="Patheon">Patheon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalent" title="Catalent">Catalent</a> (formerly known as <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Health" title="Cardinal Health">Cardinal Health</a>), <a href="/wiki/Becton-Dickinson" class="mw-redirect" title="Becton-Dickinson">Becton-Dickinson</a>, <a href="/wiki/WHSmith" title="WHSmith">WHSmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Early_Learning_Centre" title="Early Learning Centre">Early Learning Centre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nationwide_Building_Society" title="Nationwide Building Society">Nationwide</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Dyson_(company)" title="Dyson (company)">Dyson</a> located in nearby <a href="/wiki/Malmesbury" title="Malmesbury">Malmesbury</a>. Wiltshire\xe2\x80\x99s employment structure is distinctive in having a significantly higher number of people in various forms of <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing</a> (especially <a href="/wiki/Electrical_equipment" title="Electrical equipment">electrical equipment</a> and apparatus, food products, and beverages, furniture, rubber, <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceuticals" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmaceuticals">pharmaceuticals</a>, and plastic goods) than the national average.\n</p><p>In addition, there is higher-than-average employment in <a href="/wiki/Public_administration" title="Public administration">public administration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Defense_(military)" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense (military)">defence</a>, due to the military establishments around the county, particularly around <a href="/wiki/Amesbury" title="Amesbury">Amesbury</a> and <a href="/wiki/MoD_Corsham" title="MoD Corsham">Corsham</a>. There are sizeable <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> barracks at <a href="/wiki/Tidworth_Camp" title="Tidworth Camp">Tidworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulford_Camp" title="Bulford Camp">Bulford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warminster" title="Warminster">Warminster</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_School_of_Artillery" title="Royal School of Artillery">Royal School of Artillery</a> is at Larkhill. Further north, <a href="/wiki/RAF_Lyneham" title="RAF Lyneham">RAF Lyneham</a> was home to the RAF\'s Hercules C130 fleet until 2011; the <a href="/wiki/MoD_Lyneham" title="MoD Lyneham">MoD Lyneham</a> site is now a centre for Army technical training. Wiltshire is also distinctive for the high proportion of its working-age population who are economically active (86.6% in 1999\xe2\x80\x932000) and its low unemployment rates. The gross domestic product (GDP) level in Wiltshire did not reach the UK average in 1998, and was only marginally above the rate for South West England.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_infrastructure">Government and infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span Wiltshire">List of schools in Wiltshire</a></div>\n<p>Wiltshire has thirty county secondary schools, publicly funded, of which the largest is <a href="/wiki/Warminster_Kingdown" class="mw-redirect" title="Warminster Kingdown">Warminster Kingdown</a>, and eleven independent secondaries, including <a href="/wiki/Marlborough_College" title="Marlborough College">Marlborough College</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_School,_Calne" title="St Mary&#39;s School, Calne">St Mary\'s Calne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dauntsey%27s_School" title="Dauntsey&#39;s School">Dauntsey\'s</a> near <a href="/wiki/Devizes" title="Devizes">Devizes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Warminster_School" title="Warminster School">Warminster School</a>. The county schools are nearly all <a href="/wiki/Comprehensive_school" title="Comprehensive school">comprehensives</a>, with the older pattern of education surviving only in <a href="/wiki/Salisbury" title="Salisbury">Salisbury</a>, which has two <a href="/wiki/Grammar_schools_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammar schools in the United Kingdom">grammar schools</a> (<a href="/wiki/South_Wilts_Grammar_School_for_Girls" title="South Wilts Grammar School for Girls">South Wilts Grammar School for Girls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Wordsworth%27s_School" title="Bishop Wordsworth&#39;s School">Bishop Wordsworth\'s School</a>) and three non-selective schools. All but two of the county secondary schools in the former districts of <a href="/wiki/West_Wiltshire" title="West Wiltshire">West Wiltshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Wiltshire" title="North Wiltshire">North Wiltshire</a> have <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form" title="Sixth form">sixth forms</a>, but only half of those in the rest of the county.\n</p><p>There are four <a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further education</a> colleges, which also provide some higher education: <a href="/wiki/New_College,_Swindon" title="New College, Swindon">New College (Swindon)</a>; <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_College" title="Wiltshire College">Wiltshire College</a> (Chippenham, Trowbridge and Salisbury); <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_Sixth_Form_College" title="Salisbury Sixth Form College">Salisbury Sixth Form College</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Swindon_College" title="Swindon College">Swindon College</a>. In Salisbury, <a href="/wiki/South_Wiltshire_UTC" title="South Wiltshire UTC">South Wiltshire UTC</a> specialises in science and engineering for students aged 14 to 19.\n</p><p>Wiltshire is one of the few remaining English counties without a university or university college; the closest university to the county town of Trowbridge is the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bath" title="University of Bath">University of Bath</a>.  However, <a href="/wiki/Bath_Spa_University" title="Bath Spa University">Bath Spa University</a> has a centre at <a href="/wiki/Corsham_Court" title="Corsham Court">Corsham Court</a> in <a href="/wiki/Corsham" title="Corsham">Corsham</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Brookes_University" title="Oxford Brookes University">Oxford Brookes University</a> maintains a minor campus in <a href="/wiki/Swindon" title="Swindon">Swindon</a> (almost 50&#160;km from Oxford). Outline plans for a projected University of Swindon or University of Wiltshire were announced by the <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Swindon" title="Borough of Swindon">Borough of Swindon</a> in November 2008, but the scheme remains uncommitted. Swindon therefore remains the UK\'s largest centre of population without its own university.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The county registered a population of 680,137 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 Census</a>. id="Council">Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wiltshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Council" title="Wiltshire Council">Wiltshire Council</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="Wiltshire local elections">Wiltshire local elections</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Borough_Council" title="Swindon Borough Council">Swindon Borough Council</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Swindon_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon local elections">Swindon local elections</a></div>\n<p>The ceremonial county of Wiltshire consists of two unitary authority areas, Wiltshire and Swindon, governed respectively by <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Council" title="Wiltshire Council">Wiltshire Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swindon_(borough)" class="mw-redirect" title="Swindon (borough)">Swindon Borough Council</a>.\n</p><p>Until the <a href="/wiki/2009_structural_changes_to_local_government_in_England" title="2009 structural changes to local government in England">2009 structural changes to local government in England</a>, Wiltshire (apart from Swindon) was a two-level county, divided into four local government districts, <a href="/wiki/Kennet_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennet (district)">Kennet</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Wiltshire" title="North Wiltshire">North Wiltshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Salisbury (district)">Salisbury</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Wiltshire" title="West Wiltshire">West Wiltshire</a>, which existed alongside <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_County_Council" title="Wiltshire County Council">Wiltshire County Council</a>, covering the same area and carrying out more strategic tasks, such as education and county roads. However, on 1 April 2009 these five local authorities were merged into a single <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> called Wiltshire Council. With the abolition of the District of Salisbury, a new <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_City_Council" title="Salisbury City Council">Salisbury City Council</a> was created at the same time to carry out several citywide functions and to hold the City\'s charter.\n</p><p>As a result of elections held in 2017, Wiltshire Council comprises 68 <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservatives</a>, 20 <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrats</a>, seven <a href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent (politician)">Independents</a> and three <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> members. After <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_City_F.C." title="Salisbury City F.C.">Salisbury City</a> went into liquidation in 2014, a new club, <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_F.C." title="Salisbury F.C.">Salisbury</a>, was formed and plays in the Southern Premier League. \n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Wiltshire County Cricket Club">Wiltshire County Cricket Club</a> play in the <a
