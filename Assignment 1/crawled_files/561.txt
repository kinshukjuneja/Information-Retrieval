navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>South West Peninsula</b> is an unofficial region of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, usually defined as the <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> of land between the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Channel" title="Bristol Channel">Bristol Channel</a> to the north and the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> to the south. It comprises the counties of <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> (with some definitions including the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>). It has a population of 2.2 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It corresponds to the south-western part of the official region called <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>, excluding <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>.\n</p><p>Some official bodies such as the South West Peninsula Strategic Health Authority (which existed prior to a 2006 reorganization), and the <a href="/wiki/Met_Office" title="Met Office">Met Office</a>, use the term.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/South_West_Peninsula_League" title="South West Peninsula League">South West Peninsula Football League</a>, which covers only Devon and Cornwall, also uses the name.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The name of <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_College_of_Medicine_and_Dentistry" title="Peninsula College of Medicine and Dentistry">Peninsula College of Medicine and Dentistry</a>, a medical school established by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Exeter" title="University of Exeter">University of Exeter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Plymouth University">Plymouth University</a> which operated between 2000 and 2013, also referred to the South West Peninsula.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The region\'s <a href="/wiki/Deanery_(NHS)" title="Deanery (NHS)">postgraduate NHS Deanery</a>, South West Peninsula Postgraduate Medical Education (formerly South West Peninsula Deanery until April 2013), also uses the term.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
