Many claim to be descendants of <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Gypsies#Abram_Wood" title="King of the Gypsies">Abram Wood</a>, who was the first Romani to reside permanently and exclusively in Wales in the early 18th century, though Romanies have appeared in Wales since the 15th century.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Generally speaking, the Kale have employed a tribal structure in which a group of several family units would be under the authority of a male chieftain. However some Kale families are matriarchal with a senior woman being chosen by consensus among the other women of the family to take the leadership role.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Language"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Language</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Integration_into_Welsh_culture"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Integration into Welsh culture</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Notes"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kale_(Welsh_Romanies)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Kale spoke <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Romani" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh Romani">Welsh Romani</a>. Originally the variants of Welsh Romani and the <a href="/wiki/Angloromani" class="mw-redirect" title="Angloromani">Angloromani</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Romanichal" title="Romanichal">Romanichal</a> constituted a common "British Romani" language.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Both Welsh and English Romani share characteristics and are historically closely related to Romani dialects spoken in France, Germany (<a href="/wiki/Sinti" title="Sinti">Sinti</a>), Scandinavia, Spain, Poland, North Russia and the Baltic states. Such dialects are descended from the first wave of Romani immigrants into western, northern and southern Europe in the late Middle Ages.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>The Welsh Romani language survived in North Wales until at least 1950; in South Wales, a sort of "<a href="/wiki/Pidgin" title="Pidgin">pidgin</a>" dialect arose in the late 19th century, mostly consisting of Romani and English but with other loanwords from various languages (Arabic, Iranian, Greek, Romanian, German, French and Welsh) that indicate the long journey of the Kale.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Integration_into_Welsh_culture">Integration into Welsh culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kale_(Welsh_Romanies)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Integration into Welsh culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>While preserving their travelling lifestyle the Kale grew to claim several aspects of Welsh culture, including conversion to Christianity, taking on Welsh surnames, and participating in regional and national <a href="/wiki/Eisteddfodau" class="mw-redirect" title="Eisteddfodau">eisteddfodau</a>. Notably, John Robert Lewis, the husband of Abram Wood\'s granddaughter, would win prizes for harping in 1842, 1848 and 1850. \n</p><p>Another descendant, John Roberts,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> earned the sobriquet "Telynor Cymru", and taught his whole family various instruments.  His illustrious career culminated in a performance before <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Llandderfel#Pal\xc3\xa9_Hall" title="Llandderfel">Pal\xc3\xa9 Hall</a> on 24 August 1889 on the occasion of the Royal Visit to Wales. John Roberts played with his nine sons, all of them on the harp.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kale_(Welsh_Romanies)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Romanichal" title="Romanichal">Romanichal</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Finnish_Kale" title="Finnish Kale">Finnish Kale</a></li>\n<li>Spanish <a href="/wiki/Romani_people_in_Spain" title="Romani people in Spain">Cal\xc3\xa9</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kale_(Welsh_Romanies)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080621144356/http://www.valleystream.co.uk/romany-welsh%20.htm">"Archived copy"</a>.
