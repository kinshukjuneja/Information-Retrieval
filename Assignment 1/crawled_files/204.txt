\n</p><p>Jamal Khashoggi was the nephew of the high-profile Saudi Arabian arms dealer <a href="/wiki/Adnan_Khashoggi" title="Adnan Khashoggi">Adnan Khashoggi</a>, known for his part in the <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Contra_affair" title="Iran\xe2\x80\x93Contra affair">Iran\xe2\x80\x93Contra scandal</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> who was estimated to have had a net worth of US$4&#160;billion in the early 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-VOA2018_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VOA2018-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SZ2018_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SZ2018-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Adnan Khashoggi had claimed that their family grandfather was of <a href="/wiki/Jewish" class="mw-redirect" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> descent.<sup href="#cite_note-some-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>\n<p>Jamal Khashoggi began his career as a regional manager for Tihama Bookstores from 1983 to 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-wef_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wef-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Later he worked as a correspondent for the <i><a href="/wiki/Saudi_Gazette" title="Saudi Gazette">Saudi Gazette</a></i> and as an assistant manager for <i><a href="/wiki/Okaz" title="Okaz">Okaz</a></i> from 1985 to 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-wef_22-1" class="reference"><a class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">Political views</span></h3>\n<p>He wrote in a <i>Post</i> column on 3 April 2018 that Saudi Arabia "should return to its <a href="/wiki/Grand_Mosque_seizure" title="Grand Mosque seizure">pre-1979 climate</a>, when the government restricted hard-line <a href="/wiki/Wahhabism" title="Wahhabism">Wahhabi</a> traditions. Women today should have the same rights as men. And all citizens should have the right to speak their minds without fear of imprisonment."<sup id="cite_ref-wpostcolumns_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpostcolumns-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In a posthumous (17 October 2018) article, "What the Arab world needs most is free expression", Khashoggi described the hopes of Arab world press freedom during the <a href="/wiki/Arab_Spring" title="Arab Spring">Arab Spring</a> and his hope that an Arab world free press independent from national governments would develop so that "ordinary people in the Arab world would be able to address the structural problems their societies face."<sup id="cite_ref-Khashoggi_posthumous_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Khashoggi_posthumous-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the <i>Post</i>, he criticized the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Qatar_diplomatic_crisis" title="2017\xe2\x80\x9318 Qatar with Lebanon</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-wpostcolumns_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpostcolumns-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Saudi Arabia\'s <a title="Women&#39;s rights in Saudi Arabia">allowing women to drive</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> but he condemned Saudi Arabia\'s arrest of <a href="/wiki/Loujain_al-Hathloul" title="Loujain al-Hathloul">Loujain al-Hathloul</a>, who was ranked third in the list of "Top 100 Most Powerful Arab Women 2015", <a href="/wiki/Eman_al-Nafjan" title="Eman al-Nafjan">Eman al-Nafjan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aziza_al-Yousef" title="Aziza al-Yousef">Aziza al-Yousef</a>, and several other women\'s rights advocates involved in the <a male-guardianship campaign">anti male-guardianship campaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wpostcolumns_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpostcolumns-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Speaking to the BBC\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Newshour" title="Newshour">Newshour</a></i>, Khashoggi criticized <a href="/wiki/Israeli_settlement" title="Israeli settlement">Israel\'s settlement building</a> in the occupied <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_territories" title="Palestinian territories">Palestinian territories</a>, saying: "There was no international pressure on the Israelis and therefore the Israelis got away with building settlements, demolishing homes."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Khashoggi criticized the <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabian-led_intervention_in_Yemen" title="Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen">Saudi war on Yemen</a>, writing "The longer this cruel war lasts in Yemen, the more permanent the damage will be. The people of Yemen will be busy fighting poverty, <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9318_Yemen_cholera_outbreak" title="2016\xe2\x80\x9318 Yemen cholera outbreak">cholera</a> and water scarcity and rebuilding their country. The crown prince [Mohammed bin Salman] must bring an end to the violence," and "Saudi Arabia\'s crown prince must restore dignity to his country \xe2\x80\x93 by ending Yemen\'s cruel war."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>CNN reported that Khashoggi was "journalist simply doing his job who evolved from an Islamist in his twenties to a more liberal position by the time he was in his forties."<sup id="cite_ref-cnnkhashoggi_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnnkhashoggi-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He has admitted that: "Yes, I joined the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood" title="Muslim Brotherhood">Muslim Brotherhood</a> organization when I was at university. And, I was not alone. Some of the current ministers and deputies did but later every one of us developed their own political tendencies and views."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Politically, Khashoggi was supportive of Muslim Brotherhood as an exercise in democracy in the Muslim world. In one of his own blogs he argued for Muslim Brotherhood, and wrote that: "there can be no political reform and democracy in any Arab country without accepting that political Islam is a part of it."<sup id="cite_ref-ftimes_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ftimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> According to <i>The Spectator</i>, "Khashoggi and his fellow travellers believe in imposing Islamic rule by engaging in the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_democracy" title="Islamic democracy">democratic process</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-mobsterstate_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mobsterstate-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> According to other Khashoggi was critical of <a href="/wiki/Salafi_movement" title="Salafi movement">Salafism</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> the ultra-conservative Sunni movement.<sup id="cite_ref-ftimes_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ftimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In order to how Saudi Arabia should confront Iran, Al-Jazeera reported that Khashoggi has stated that Saudi Arabia "must re-embrace its proper religious identity as a Wahhabi Islamic revivalist state and build alliances with organisations rooted in political Islam such as the Muslim Brotherhood", and that it would be a\'"big mistake" if Saudi Arabia and Muslim Brotherhood cannot be friendly.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Khashoggi also said that Saudis "must find a way where we can accommodate <a href="/wiki/Secularism" title="Secularism">secularism</a> and Islam, something like what they have in Turkey."<sup id="cite_ref-cnnkhashoggi_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnnkhashoggi-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_Osama_bin_Laden">Relationship with Osama bin Laden</span></h3>\n<p>Khashoggi was acquainted with <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">Osama bin Laden</a> in the 1980s and 1990s in Afghanistan while Bin Laden was championing his <a href="/wiki/Soviet_war_in_Afghanistan" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet war in Afghanistan">jihad against the Soviets</a>. Khashoggi interviewed bin Laden several times, usually meeting bin Laden in <a href="/wiki/Tora_Bora" title="Tora Bora">Tora Bora</a>, and once more in <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-laden_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laden-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> During that period, he was employed by Saudi Arabian intelligence agencies to try to influence bin Laden into making a compromise with the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Saud" title="House of Saud">Saudi royal family</a> in their rivalry.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Al Arabiya reported that Khashoggi once tried to persuade bin Laden to quit violence.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Khashoggi said: "I was very much surprised [in 1997] to see Osama turning into radicalism the way he did."<sup id="cite_ref-cnnkhashoggi_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnnkhashoggi-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Khashoggi was the only non-royal Saudi Arabian who knew of the royals\' intimate dealing with <a href="/wiki/Al-Qaeda" title="Al-Qaeda">al-Qaeda</a> in the lead-up to the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">11 September terrorist attacks</a>. He dissociated himself from bin Laden following the attacks.<sup id="cite_ref-mobsterstate_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mobsterstate-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Khashoggi wrote in response to the 11 September attacks: "The most pressing issue now is to ensure that our children can never be influenced by extremist ideas like those 15 Saudis who were misled into hijacking four planes that fine September day, piloting them, and us, straight into the jaws of hell."<sup id="cite_ref-laden_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laden-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saudi_Arabia">Saudi Arabia</span></h3>\n<p>Khashoggi briefly became the editor-in-chief of the Saudi Arabian daily <a href="/wiki/Al-Watan_(Saudi_Arabia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Al-Watan (Saudi Arabia)"><i>Al Watan</i></a> in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-speakers_7-5" class="reference"><a Information</a> because he had allowed a columnist to criticize the Islamic scholar <a href="/wiki/Ibn_Taymiyyah" title="Ibn Taymiyyah">Ibn Taymiyyah</a> (1263\xe2\x80\x931328), who is considered the founding father of <a href="/wiki/Wahhabism" title="Wahhabism">Wahhabism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blan5jun_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blan5jun-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> This incident was fired, Khashoggi went to London in voluntary exile. There he became an adviser to Prince <a href="/wiki/Turki_bin_Faisal_Al_Saud" title="Turki bin Faisal Al Saud">Turki Al Faisal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He then served as a media aide to Al Faisal while the latter was Saudi Arabia\'s ambassador to the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In April 2007, Khashoggi began to work as editor-in-chief of <i>Al Watan</i> for a second time.<sup id="cite_ref-ahs25apr_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ahs25apr-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A column by poet Ibrahim al-Almaee challenging the basic <a href="/wiki/Salafi" class="mw-redirect" title="Salafi">Salafi</a> premises was published in <i>Al Watan</i> in May 2010 and led to Khashoggi\'s second departure, on 17 May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc17may_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc17may-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <i>Al Watan</i> announced that Khashoggi resigned as editor-in-chief "to focus on his personal projects". However, it is thought that he was forced out due to official displeasure with articles critical of the Kingdom\'s harsh Islamic rules.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc17may_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc17may-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> After his second resignation, Khashoggi maintained ties with Saudi Arabian elites, including those in its intelligence apparatus. In 2015, he launched the satellite news channel <a href="/wiki/Al-Arab_News_Channel" title="Al-Arab News Channel">Al-Arab</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a> outside Saudi Arabia, which does not allow independent news channels to operate within its borders. The news channel was backed by Saudi Arabian billionaire Prince <a href="/wiki/Alwaleed_bin_Talal" class="mw-redirect" title="Alwaleed bin Talal">Alwaleed bin Talal</a> and partnered with U.S. financial news channel <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Television" title="Bloomberg Television">Bloomberg Television</a>. However, it was on air for less than 11 hours before it was shut down by Bahrain.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He was also a political commentator for Saudi Arabian and international channels, including <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazeera" title="Al Jazeera">Al Jazeera</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dubai_TV" title="Dubai TV">Dubai TV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wef_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wef-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Between June 2012 and September 2016, his href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Citing a report from <a href="/wiki/Middle_East_Eye" title="Middle East Eye">Middle East Eye</a>, the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">Independent</a></i> said in December 2016 that Khashoggi had been banned by Saudi Arabian authorities from publishing or appearing on television "for criticising U.S. President-elect <a 2018.</div></div></div>\n<p>Khashoggi relocated to the United States in June 2017<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> and began writing for <i><a Saudi Arabia used an online army of <a href="/wiki/Internet_troll" title="Internet troll">Twitter trolls</a> to harass Khashoggi and other critics of the Saudi regime.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spectator" title="The Spectator">The Spectator</a></i>, "With almost two million Twitter followers, he was the most famous political pundit in the Arab world and a regular guest on the major TV news networks in Britain and the United States."<sup id="cite_ref-mobsterstate_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mobsterstate-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, Khashoggi established a new political party called <i>Democracy for the Arab World Now</i>, posing a political threat to Crown Prince Mohammed.<sup id="cite_ref-mobsterstate_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mobsterstate-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Killing">Killing</span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Killing_of_Jamal_Khashoggi" title="Killing of Jamal Khashoggi">Killing of Jamal Khashoggi</a></div>\n<p>Khashoggi entered the <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia%E2%80%93Turkey_relations" title="Saudi Arabia\xe2\x80\x93Turkey relations">Saudi Arabian consulate in Istanbul</a> on 2 October 2018 in order to obtain documents related to his planned marriage. As no <a href="/wiki/CCTV" class="mw-redirect" title="CCTV">CCTV</a> recorded him exiting the consulate,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> he was declared a missing person<sup id="cite_ref-TurkeyToSearch_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TurkeyToSearch-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> amid news reports claiming that he had been killed and <a href="/wiki/Dismember" class="mw-redirect" title="Dismember">dismembered</a> inside the consulate.<sup id="cite_ref-ES_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ES-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-missing_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-missing-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> An inspection of the consulate, by both Saudi Arabian and Turkish officials, took place on 15 October. Turkish officials found evidence of "tampering" during the inspection and evidence that supported the belief that Khashoggi had been killed.<sup id="cite_ref-SearchEvidence_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SearchEvidence-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Initially, the Saudi Arabian government denied the death and claimed that Khashoggi had left the consulate alive<sup id="cite_ref-NDTV_Fiancee_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NDTV_Fiancee-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> but 18 days later admitted he had died inside during a fistfight. Eighteen Saudis were arrested, including the team of 15 who had been sent to "confront him".<sup id="cite_ref-fight_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fight-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cnndeath_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnndeath-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> There is concern that many Saudi critics have gone missing in suspicious ways.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> The US president and several US senators remain divided as to which, if any economic or other sanctions should be applied to Saudi Arabia.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>\n<p>At the time of his death, Khashoggi was planning to marry Hatice Cengiz, a 36-year-old PhD candidate at a university in Istanbul. The two had met in May 2018, during a conference in the city. Khashoggi, a Saudi national, visited the Saudi consulate on 2 October to get paperwork that would allow him to marry Cengiz.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Khashoggi was married and divorced three times.  His first marriage was to Rawia al-Tunisi by whom he had two sons and two daughters.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
