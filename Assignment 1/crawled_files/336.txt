Grounds">Haymakers\' Grounds</a> (1880\xe2\x80\x931881) in the upstate New York city of <a href="/wiki/Troy,_New_York" title="Troy, New York">Troy</a>, and at <a href="/wiki/Troy_Ball_Clubs_Grounds" title="Troy Ball Clubs Grounds">Troy Ball Clubs Grounds</a> (1882) across the Hudson in <a href="/wiki/Watervliet,_New_York" title="Watervliet, New York">Watervliet</a>, or "West Troy" as it was known at the time.\n</p><p>Overall, the franchise won 131 games and lost 194. The Trojans, along with the <a href="/wiki/Worcester_Worcesters" title="Worcester Worcesters">Worcester NL team</a>, were expelled from the league shortly before the end of the 1882 season, as Troy and Worcester were seen as too small for the league\'s ambitions, but were encouraged to play out the rest of their seasons as lame-duck teams.\n</p><p>On September 28, 1882, only six fans appeared to watch Worcester host the Trojans in the second-to-last game of the season, then only 25 arrived for the last game between the two teams. Among games that have had at least one paying attendee, the attendance figure of six is the lowest attendance ever recorded at a Major League baseball game.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/1883_in_baseball" title="1883 in baseball">1883</a> the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Gothams" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Gothams">New York Gothams</a>, later known as the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">Giants</a>, took the Trojans\' former slot in the National League. Four of the original Gotham players were former members of the disbanded Trojans, including three Hall of Famers: <a href="/wiki/Buck_Ewing" title="Buck Ewing">Buck Ewing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Connor" title="Roger Connor">Roger Connor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Welch" title="Mickey Welch">Mickey Welch</a>.\n</p><p>A previous team named the <a href="/wiki/Union_Base_Ball_Club_Lansingburgh" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Base Ball Club Lansingburgh">Union Base Ball Club Lansingburgh</a> was organized in 1860, the successor to the Victories of Troy, and was a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Base Ball Players">National Association of Base Ball Players</a>.  That team was given the nickname <i>Haymakers</i> by a defeated New York City team.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Notable players for the Trojans included <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Hall of Famers</a> <a href="/wiki/Dan_Brouthers" title="Dan Brouthers">Dan Brouthers</a>, Connor, Ewing, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Keefe" title="Tim Keefe">Tim Keefe</a>, and Welch.\n</p><p>Another <a href="/wiki/Troy_Trojans_(minor_league_baseball)" title="Troy Trojans (minor league baseball)">Troy Trojans</a> minor league team continued play until at least 1916.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
