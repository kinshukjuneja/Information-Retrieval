lang="kw">Lanndreth</i>) is a small town in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom.\n</p><p><b>St Blaise</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in which St Blazey is situated; the name St Blaise is also used by the town council. The village of  <a href="/wiki/Biscovey" class="mw-redirect" title="Biscovey">Biscovey</a> and the settlements of <a href="/wiki/St_Blazey_Gate" title="St Blazey Gate">St Blazey Gate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bodelva" title="Bodelva">Bodelva</a> and West <a href="/wiki/Par,_Cornwall" title="Par, Cornwall">Par</a> lie within the parish boundaries.<sup id="cite_ref-cccsbtc_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cccsbtc-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> An electoral ward also exists in the name of St Blaise. The population at the 2011 census was 4,674.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\nOnce an important engineering centre for the local mine and railway industries, the parish is now dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Eden_Project" title="Eden Project">Eden Project</a>.\n</p><p>St Blazey is situated 3 miles (4.8&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/St_Austell" title="St Austell">St Austell</a>, 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Tywardreath" title="Tywardreath">Tywardreath</a> and 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/Par,_Cornwall" title="Par, Cornwall">Par</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-osexp1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osexp1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The town takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenian</a> <a href="/wiki/Saint_Blaise" title="Saint Blaise">Saint Blaise</a> and holds a procession and service on his feast day, 3 February.<sup class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Blazey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The namesake and <a href="/wiki/Patron_hallow" class="mw-redirect" title="Patron hallow">patron hallow</a> of St Blazey is <a href="/wiki/Saint_Blaise" title="Saint Blaise">Saint Blaise</a>, who in high church Christianity, is believed to have "cure[d] toothache, sore throats and cattle diseases".<sup id="cite_ref-DeaneShaw2009_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DeaneShaw2009-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>An <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> hillfort <a href="/wiki/Prideaux_Castle" title="Prideaux Castle">Prideaux Castle</a> lies a mile to the north west of the town in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Luxulyan" title="Luxulyan">Luxulyan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TownCouncil_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TownCouncil-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Biscovey Stone is the shaft of an ancient <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> cross. It was inscribed, but the text is no longer readable. There are several theories about the stone; one says it dates from around 600 AD to show the <a href="/wiki/Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saxon">Saxon</a> advance into the county, another puts the date at around 900 AD. The head is thought to have been removed during the <a href="/wiki/Reformation" title="Reformation">Reformation</a>. The stone served as a gate post near the St Blazey turnpike gate. In 1896 it was moved to St Mary\'s Church, Biscovey.<sup id="cite_ref-TownCouncil_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TownCouncil-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Arthur Langdon described the stone in 1896: he read the inscription as "+ Alroron Ullici + filius". It was also described by <a href="/wiki/William_Borlase" title="William Borlase">William Borlase</a> in his <i>Antiquities of Cornwall</i> (1754), pp.&#160;363\xe2\x80\x9364.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The church was built between 1440 and 1445 and is dedicated to Saint Blaise. It replaces an earlier church mentioned in 1294. The parish was administered by <a href="/wiki/Tywardreath#Tywardreath_Priory" title="Tywardreath">Tywardreath Priory</a> until the Reformation. It was split from St Austell parish in 1834.<sup id="cite_ref-CISI_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CISI-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The site was originally known by the Cornish names Landrait or Landreath, meaning Church on the Sand.<sup id="cite_ref-CISI_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CISI-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A stone Gothic Latin cross stands in the churchyard but nothing is known about its history.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Until the 16th century the valley below St Blazey was an estuary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Par_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="River Par (UK)">River Par</a> and the St Blazey was the lowest crossing point on the river. <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Cornwall_and_Devon" title="Mining in Cornwall and Devon">Tin mining</a> up river caused the estuary to silt up and it had become marsh land by the early 19th century. The <a href="/wiki/Par_Canal" class="mw-redirect" title="Par Canal">Par Canal</a> was built by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Treffry" title="Joseph Treffry">Joseph Treffry</a> between 1829 and 1835; it forms part of the boundary with the parish of <a href="/wiki/Tywardreath_and_Par" title="Tywardreath and Par">Tywardreath and Par</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CISI_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CISI-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The town was once dominated by the local mining industries and their associated transport infrastructure. Historically copper and <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> were mined in and around the parish, whilst more recently <a href="/wiki/Kaolinite" title="Kaolinite">china clay</a> has been the principal commodity mined. "The Par &amp; St Blazey Consols" or "South Prideaux Wood" was a small tin mine just north of the town<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Par_Consols_Mine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Par Consols Mine (page does not exist)">Par Consols Mine</a> lies to the south west.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The more extensive <a href="/wiki/Fowey_Consols_mine" title="Fowey Consols mine">Fowey Consols mine</a> lies to the east near <a href="/wiki/Tywardreath" title="Tywardreath">Tywardreath</a>.\n</p><p>The port of <a href="/wiki/Par_Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="Par Harbour">Par Harbour</a>, which lies within the parish, was developed to ease the transport of these minerals, and initially connected to the mines by the <a href="/wiki/Par_Canal" class="mw-redirect" title="Par Canal">Par Canal</a>. Whilst the port of Par is within the parish, the village of <a href="/wiki/Par,_Cornwall" title="Par, Cornwall">Par</a> is actually just across the <a href="/wiki/River_Par_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="River Par (UK)">River Par</a>, and hence lies in the civil parish of Tywardreath.<sup id="cite_ref-osexp1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osexp1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Par Canal was soon replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Minerals_Railway" title="Cornwall Minerals Railway">Cornwall Minerals Railway</a>, which had a depot and station in the town, and still exists as part of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Coast_Line,_Cornwall" title="Atlantic Coast Line, Cornwall">Atlantic Coast Line</a>. Whilst <a href="/wiki/St_Blazey_engine_shed" title="St Blazey engine shed">St Blazey depot</a> is still in use, <a href="/wiki/St_Blazey_railway_station" title="St Blazey railway station">St Blazey station</a> closed to passengers in 1925, and the town is now served by <a href="/wiki/Par_railway_station" title="Par railway station">Par station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Main_Line" title="Cornish Main Line">Cornish Main Line</a> in Par village.<sup id="cite_ref-osexp1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osexp1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Blazey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:362px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg/360px-Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg" width="360" height="112" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg/540px-Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg/720px-Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3700" data-file-height="1155" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Panoramic view of the geodesic biome domes at the Eden Project</div></div></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Eden_Project" title="Eden Project">Eden Project</a> is located within the civil parish, and about a mile and half (2&#160;km) from the centre of the town. The large number of visitors this attracts has led to the development of tourism in the town. Other attractions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Treffry_Viaduct" title="Treffry Viaduct">Treffry Viaduct</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Luxulyan_Valley" title="Luxulyan Valley">Luxulyan Valley</a>, are also close by, although actually within the adjoining parish of <a href="/wiki/Luxulyan" title="Luxulyan">Luxulyan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-osexp1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-osexp1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Blazey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Primary education is provided by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.biscovey-inf.cornwall.sch.uk/">Biscovey Nursery and Infant Community School</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070929125546/http://www.biscovey-jnr.cornwall.sch.uk/">Biscovey Junior School</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Blazey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The town\'s football team, <a href="/wiki/St_Blazey_A.F.C." title="St Blazey A.F.C.">St Blazey A.F.C.</a>, was the first club of England International goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Martyn" title="Nigel Martyn">Nigel Martyn</a>.\n</p><p>The town is also home to St Blazey Cricket Club which has three teams.\nThe cricket club is based at the top of Middleway Road.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Blazey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Notable people from the town include <a href="/wiki/Edward_Long" title="Edward Long">Edward Long</a>, the historian, who was born at Roselyon, in 1734, and educated at <a href="/wiki/Liskeard" title="Liskeard">Liskeard</a>. It is claimed by some as the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Allen" title="Ralph Allen">Ralph Allen</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> notable architect of <a href="/wiki/Bath,_Somerset" title="Bath, Somerset">Bath</a>, although his christening took place at <a href="/wiki/St_Columb_Major" title="St Columb Major">St Columb Major</a>. <a href="/wiki/John_Rogers_(divine)" title="John Rogers (divine)">John Rogers</a>, who supported the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Man_engine" title="Man engine">man engine</a> to Cornish mines, was curate here for a time.<sup id="cite_ref-dnb_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Charles_Jeffries" title="Charles Jeffries">Charles Jeffries</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Commissioner_in_The_Salvation_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Commissioner in The Salvation Army">Commissioner</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Salvation_Army" title="The Salvation Army">The Salvation Army</a>, was posted to the town in 1883.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
