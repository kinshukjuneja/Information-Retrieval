class="nowrap">&#160;</span>October 2015, 21-year-old Anton Lundin Pettersson attacked Kronan School in <a href="/wiki/Trollh%C3%A4ttan" title="Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan">Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan</a>, Sweden, with a sword. He killed a teaching assistant and a male student, stabbed another male student and a teacher, and died later of the gunshot wounds he received during his apprehension.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The second teacher who was wounded died in hospital six weeks after the attack, on 3<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>December.\n</p><p>The attack was Sweden\'s first deadly attack on a school since the 1961 <a href="/wiki/Kung%C3%A4lv_school_shooting" title="Kung\xc3\xa4lv school shooting">Kung\xc3\xa4lv school shooting</a> when one person was killed and six injured. It is also the deadliest attack on a school in Swedish history.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The initial police investigation concluded that Pettersson was motivated by racism and had chosen the school as his target due to its location in a neighbourhood with a high immigrant population.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan_school_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The city of Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan has had a long history of <a href="/wiki/Hate_crime" title="Hate crime">hate crimes</a>, starting with <a href="/wiki/Arson" title="Arson">arson</a> at a mosque in the 1990s. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Sundell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anders Sundell (page does not exist)">Anders Sundell</a> has found it to be the most ethnically segregated city in Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Surveys frequently show Sweden to be the European country which is most tolerant of immigration, but its population is highly polarised on the issue. Sundell\'s research showed that consistently over the last 20 years, a plurality of Swedes have wanted to limit the number of refugees coming to the country, but "There is a large difference between what the politicians think and the general public, and the media have not questioned the politicians until recently."  As a consequence, Swedish anti-immigrant forces believe that there is a conspiracy opposing their views.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attack">Attack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan_school_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Pettersson entered the school in a German World War II helmet and a mask similar to either that of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> villain <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Darth Vader</a> or those worn by police in <i><a href="/wiki/Jin-Roh:_The_Wolf_Brigade" title="Jin-Roh: The Wolf Brigade">Jin-Roh: The Wolf Brigade</a></i>. At first, eyewitnesses believed it was a <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a> prank.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Pettersson immediately attacked 20-year-old teaching assistant Lavin Eskandar.  Eskandar, a <a href="/wiki/Feyli_Kurds" title="Feyli Kurds">Feyli Kurd</a> from Iraq,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> was stabbed and died at the scene. Pettersson then stabbed the abdomen of Ahmed Hassan, a 15-year-old born in Somalia.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hassan died in the hospital. After that, while wandering the halls, Pettersson posed with two students who thought he was playing a prank, and took a picture of Pettersson with blood dripping from his sword. Shortly after, when 42-year-old teacher Nazir Amso asked Pettersson to remove his mask, Amso was stabbed. He died six weeks later at the hospital. At 10.16&#160;a.m., Pettersson was shot by police. He died in the hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On the morning of 23<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>October, Swedish police and media confirmed that the motive behind the attack had "racist motives" and that it was a "hate crime".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Niclas Hallgren, the city\'s police chief, stated that all of the victims were "dark-skinned".<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Head of investigation Thord Haraldsson said that CCTV footage showed that Pettersson spared the lives of students with white skin.<sup id="cite_ref-mail_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mail-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Nazir Amso, the mathematics teacher who was stabbed, died of his injuries in hospital on 3<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>December, at the age of 42.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Perpetrator">Perpetrator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="#cite_note-TV2.no-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> (22<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>June 1994<sup id="cite_ref-TV2.no_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TV2.no-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>&#160;&#8211;&#32;22<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>October 2015) was identified as the attacker at 20:00 CET (18:00 <a href="/wiki/GMT" class="mw-redirect" title="GMT">GMT</a>) by <i><a href="/wiki/Expressen" title="Expressen">Expressen</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  According to <i><a href="/wiki/Aftonbladet" title="Aftonbladet">Aftonbladet</a></i>, the perpetrator had visited right-wing extremist groups at social media sites <a href="/wiki/Neo-Nazism" title="Neo-Nazism">supporting Adolf Hitler and Nazi Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-aftonbladet.se_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aftonbladet.se-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He had also joined a group on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> that wants to stop immigration to Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-aftonbladet.se_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aftonbladet.se-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Security_Service" title="Swedish Security Service">Swedish Security Service</a> were called in to investigate these findings.<sup id="cite_ref-aftonbladet.se_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aftonbladet.se-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Pettersson had no criminal record and was not a member of any political organization, but had supported a petition by the <a href="/wiki/Sweden_Democrats" title="Sweden Democrats">Sweden Democrats</a> to initiate a referendum on immigration.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He left a handwritten note at his home in which he declared that something had to be done about immigration, and that he did not expect to survive his spree.<sup id="cite_ref-mail_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mail-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Pettersson graduated from Lichron Teknikgymnasium with a diploma in technology.<sup id="cite_ref-mail_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mail-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He lived in an apartment in the neighborhood of Stavre, but chose to attack the Kronan School in Kronog\xc3\xa5rden, where there are more immigrants; police cited this as more evidence towards his motive.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Former classmates described the perpetrator as a lonely person who "lived in his own world" and always dressed in black clothes influenced either by the <a href="/wiki/Emo" title="Emo">emo</a> or rock scene.<sup id="cite_ref-aftonbladet.se_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aftonbladet.se-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Bj\xc3\xb8rn Ihler, a survivor of Norwegian far-right extremist <a href="/wiki/Anders_Behring_Breivik" title="Anders Behring Breivik">Anders Behring Breivik</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/2011_Norway_attacks" title="2011 Norway attacks">terror attacks in 2011</a>, wrote in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> that in 2013, Pettersson had "liked" a YouTube video of former <a href="/wiki/Ku_Klux_Klan" title="Ku Klux Klan">Ku Klux Klan</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Lee_Clary" title="Johnny Lee Clary">Johnny Lee Clary</a> testifying how a positive experience with a black man had caused him to disavow his previously-held beliefs.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trollh%C3%A4ttan_school_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Sweden" title="Prime Minister of Sweden">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Stefan_L%C3%B6fven" title="Stefan L\xc3\xb6fven">Stefan L\xc3\xb6fven</a> travelled to Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan after news of the attack, calling it a "black day" for the country.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Interior Minister <a href="/wiki/Anders_Ygeman" title="Anders Ygeman">Anders Ygeman</a> wrote on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, "It is with sadness and dismay I received the news of the attack on the school in Trollh\xc3\xa4ttan. My thoughts go to the victims and their families".<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> King <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf_of_Sweden" title="Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden">Carl XVI Gustaf</a> said that the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish Royal Family">royal family</a> received the news "with great dismay and sadness".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the days following the attack, there were reports of people wearing suspicious outfits or brandishing weapons, which were discovered to be people celebrating Halloween. The police warned the public not to carry imitation weapons with their Halloween costumes, in order to avoid potentially dangerous misunderstandings.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The school remained closed until 2<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>November, when it reopened with higher security. An Afghan father told <i>The Local</i> that he wished to leave the neighbourhood for his family\'s safety.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 29 September 2017, the first book about the school attack was published: <i>Det som aldrig fick ske</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The book contains completely new facts, for example a secret suicide note that the police and the Pettersson family did not know existed. In the book the brother of Anton Lundin Pettersson speaks out for the first time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The author \xc3\x85sa Erlandsson 2017 won the Stora Journalistpriset (the finest prize in journalism in Sweden) for the book.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
