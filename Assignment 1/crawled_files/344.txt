id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">A powerful warm western boundary current in the north Atlantic Ocean that extends the Gulf Stream northeastward</div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"North Atlantic Drift" redirects here. For the 2003 rock album by Ocean Color Scene, see <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Drift_(album)" title="North Atlantic Drift (album)">North Atlantic Drift (album)</a>.</div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:North_Atlantic_currents.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/North_Atlantic_currents.svg/220px-North_Atlantic_currents.svg.png" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/North_Atlantic_currents.svg/330px-North_Atlantic_currents.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/North_Atlantic_currents.svg/440px-North_Atlantic_currents.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="455" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:North_Atlantic_currents.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The North Atlantic Current is the first leg in the North Atlantic Subpolar Gyre.</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>North Atlantic Current</b> (<b>NAC</b>), also known as <b>North Atlantic Drift</b> and <b>North Atlantic Sea Movement</b>, is a powerful warm <a href="/wiki/Western_boundary_current" class="mw-redirect" title="Western boundary current">western boundary current</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> that extends the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">Gulf Stream</a> northeastward.<sup id="cite_ref-Rossby-Abst_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rossby-Abst-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The NAC originates from where the Gulf Stream turns north at the Southeast Newfoundland Rise, a submarine ridge that stretches southeast from the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Banks_of_Newfoundland" title="Grand Banks of Newfoundland">Grand Banks</a>.  The NAC flows northward east of the Grand Banks, from 40\xc2\xb0N to 51\xc2\xb0N, before turning sharply east to cross the Atlantic.  It transports more warm tropical water to northern latitudes than any other boundary current; more than 40&#160;<a href="/wiki/Sverdrup" title="Sverdrup">Sv</a> in the south and 20&#160;Sv as it crosses the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_Ridge" title="Mid-Atlantic Ridge">Mid-Atlantic Ridge</a>.  It reaches speeds of 2 knots near the <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> coast.  Directed by topography, the NAC meanders heavily, but in contrast to the meanders of the Gulf Stream, the NAC meanders remain stable without breaking off into eddies.<sup id="cite_ref-Rossby-Abst_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rossby-Abst-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The colder parts of the Gulf Stream turn northward near the "tail" of the Grand Banks at 50\xc2\xb0W where the <a href="/wiki/Azores_Current" title="Azores Current">Azores Current</a> branches off to flow south of the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>.  From there the NAC flows northeastward, east of the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Cap" title="Flemish Cap">Flemish Cap</a> (47\xc2\xb0N, 45\xc2\xb0W).  Approaching the Mid-Atlantic Ridge, it then turns eastward and becomes much broader and more diffuse.  It then splits into a colder northeastern branch and a warmer eastern branch.  As the warmer branch turns southward, most of the subtropical component of the Gulf Stream is diverted southward, and as a consequence, the North Atlantic is mostly supplied by subpolar waters, including a contribution from the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Current" title="Labrador Current">Labrador Current</a> recirculated into the NAC at 45\xc2\xb0N.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>West of <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">Continental Europe</a>, it splits into two major branches. One branch goes southeast, becoming the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Current" title="Canary Current">Canary Current</a> as it passes northwest <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and turns southwest. The other major branch continues north along the coast of <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_Europe" title="Northwestern Europe">Northwestern Europe</a>.\nOther branches include the <a href="/wiki/Irminger_Current" title="Irminger Current">Irminger Current</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Current" title="Norwegian Current">Norwegian Current</a>. Driven by the global <a href="/wiki/Thermohaline_circulation" title="Thermohaline circulation">thermohaline circulation</a>, the North Atlantic Current is part of the wind-driven Gulf Stream, which goes further east and north from the North American coast across the Atlantic and into the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean" title="Arctic Ocean">Arctic Ocean</a>.\n</p><p>The North Atlantic Current, together with the Gulf Stream, have a long-lived reputation for having a considerable warming influence on European climate.  However, the principal cause for differences in winter climate between North America and Europe seems to be winds rather than ocean currents (although the currents do exert influence at very high latitudes by preventing the formation of <a href="/wiki/Sea_ice" title="Sea ice">sea ice</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Atlantic_Current&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Oscillation" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic Oscillation">North Atlantic Oscillation</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Ocean_gyre" title="Ocean gyre">Ocean gyre</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Physical_oceanography" title="Physical oceanography">Physical oceanography</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Atlantic_Current&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dt>Notes</dt></dl>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Rossby-Abst-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-Rossby-Abst_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-Rossby-Abst_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><a href="#CITEREFRossby1996">Rossby 1996</a>, Abstract</span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a href="#CITEREFLozierOwensCurry1995">Lozier, Owens &amp;
