href="/wiki/Granville,_Manche" title="Granville, Manche">Granville</a>\xe2\x80\x93Paris Express overran the <a href="/wiki/Buffer_stop" title="Buffer stop">buffer stop</a> at its <a href="/wiki/Gare_Montparnasse" title="Gare Montparnasse">Gare Montparnasse</a> terminus. With the train several minutes late and the driver trying to make up for lost time, it entered the station too fast and the train air brake failed to stop it. After running through the buffer stop, the train crossed the station concourse and crashed through the station wall; the locomotive fell onto the Place de Rennes below, where it stood on its nose. A woman in the street below was killed by falling masonry.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montparnasse_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Derailment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 22 October 1895, the Granville to Paris and Montparnasse express, operated by <a href="/wiki/Chemins_de_fer_de_l%27Ouest" title="Chemins de fer de l&#39;Ouest">Chemins de fer de l\'Ouest</a>, was made up of steam locomotive No. 721 (a <a href="/wiki/2-4-0" title="2-4-0">type 2-4-0</a>, French notation 120) hauling three luggage vans, a post van, and six passenger coaches.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERichou1895369\xe2\x80\x93370_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERichou1895369\xe2\x80\x93370-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The train had left <a href="/wiki/Gare_de_Granville" title="Gare de Granville">Granville</a> on time at 08:45, but was several minutes late as it approached its Paris Montparnasse terminus with 131 passengers on board. In an effort to make up lost time,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERichou1895369\xe2\x80\x93370_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERichou1895369\xe2\x80\x93370-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Danger_Fan_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danger_Fan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the train entered the station too fast, at a speed of 40\xe2\x80\x9360&#160;km/h (25\xe2\x80\x9337&#160;mph), and the <a href="/wiki/Westinghouse_Air_Brake_Company" title="Westinghouse Air Brake Company">Westinghouse air brake</a> failed to stop it.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERichou1895369\xe2\x80\x93370_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERichou1895369\xe2\x80\x93370-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-musee-orsay_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-musee-orsay-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Without sufficient braking, the momentum of the train carried it slowly into the buffers, and the locomotive crossed the almost 30-metre (98&#160;ft) wide station concourse, crashing through a 60-centimetre (24&#160;in) thick wall, before falling onto the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Place_de_Rennes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Place de Rennes (page does not exist)">Place de Rennes</a> 10 metres (33&#160;ft) below, where it stood on its nose. A woman in the street below was killed by falling masonry, and two passengers, the fireman, two guards and a passerby in the street sustained injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-Danger_Fan_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danger_Fan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The woman, Marie-Augustine Aguilard, had been standing in for her husband, a newspaper vendor, while he went to collect the evening newspapers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montparnasse_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The locomotive driver was fined 50&#160;<a href="/wiki/French_franc" title="French franc">francs</a> for approaching the station too fast. One of the guards was fined 25&#160;francs as he had been preoccupied with paperwork and failed to apply the handbrake.<sup id="cite_ref-Danger_Fan_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Danger_Fan-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The passenger carriages were undamaged and were easily removed. It took 48 hours before the legal process and investigation allowed the railway to start removing the locomotive and <a href="/wiki/Tender_(rail)" title="Tender (rail)">tender</a>. An attempt was made to move the locomotive with 14 horses, but this failed. A 250&#160;tonne winch, with 10 men, first lowered the locomotive to the ground and then lifted the tender back into the station. When the locomotive reached the railway workshops it was found to have suffered little damage.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERichou1895370_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERichou1895370-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montparnasse_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg/220px-Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg/330px-Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg/440px-Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mundo_a_vapor_-_Canela.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Reconstruction at Mundo a Vapor theme park in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The wreckage remained outside the station for several days<sup id="cite_ref-musee-orsay_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-musee-orsay-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and a number of photographs were taken, such as those attributed to Studio L\xc3\xa9vy and Sons,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> L. href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The L\xc3\xa9vy and Sons photograph (at the top of the article) has become one of the most famous in transportation history.<sup id="cite_ref-8"
