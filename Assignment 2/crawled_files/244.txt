title="Shropshire">Shropshire</a>, is designated as an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (<a href="/wiki/AONB" class="mw-redirect" title="AONB">AONB</a>). It is located in the south of the county, extending to its border with <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. Designated in 1958,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the area encompasses 802 square kilometres (310&#160;sq&#160;mi) of land primarily in south-west Shropshire, taking its name from the upland region of the <a href="/wiki/Shropshire_Hills" title="Shropshire Hills">Shropshire Hills</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A49_road" title="A49 road">A49 road</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Marches_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh Marches Line">Welsh Marches Railway Line</a> bisect the area north-south, passing through or near <a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury" title="Shrewsbury">Shrewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Church_Stretton" title="Church Stretton">Church Stretton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Craven_Arms" title="Craven Arms">Craven Arms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludlow" title="Ludlow">Ludlow</a>.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shropshire_Hills_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Shropshire Hills, located in the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Marches" title="Welsh Marches">Welsh Marches</a>, are relatively high: the highest point in the county, <a href="/wiki/Brown_Clee_Hill" title="Brown Clee Hill">Brown Clee Hill</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Ludlow" title="Ludlow">Ludlow</a>, has an altitude of 540 metres (1,772&#160;ft). This gives Shropshire the <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_counties_by_highest_point" title="List of English counties by highest point">13th</a> tallest hill per county<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> in England. <a href="/wiki/Titterstone_Clee_Hill" title="Titterstone Clee Hill">Titterstone Clee Hill</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Clee_Hills" title="Clee Hills">Clee Hills</a>, is nearly as high as Brown Clee, at 533 metres (1,749&#160;ft), making it the third largest hill in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Stiperstones" title="Stiperstones">Stiperstones</a> are the second highest, at 536 metres (1,759&#160;ft), and are notable for their <a href="/wiki/Tor_(rock_formation)" title="Tor (rock formation)">tors</a> of <a href="/wiki/Quartzite" title="Quartzite">quartzite</a>; particularly notable are <b>Devil\'s Chair</b> (<span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Shropshire_Hills_AONB&amp;params=SO368991_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SO368991</a></span>) and <b>Shepherd\'s Rock</b> (<span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Shropshire_Hills_AONB&amp;params=SO373998_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SO373998</a></span>).\n</p><p>More accessible hills are the <a href="/wiki/Long_Mynd" title="Long Mynd">Long Mynd</a>, which covers an area of 5,436 acres (22.0 km<sup>2</sup>) and peaks at Pole Bank at a height of 516 metres (1,693 feet), is near <a href="/wiki/Church_Stretton" title="Church Stretton">Church Stretton</a>. One of the most famous hills is the <a href="/wiki/Caer_Caradoc" title="Caer Caradoc">Caer Caradoc</a>, at 459 metres (1,506 ft) which is just by the village of <a href="/wiki/Leebotwood" title="Leebotwood">Leebotwood</a>. It includes <a href="/wiki/Carding_Mill_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Carding Mill Valley">Carding Mill Valley</a>, a popular recreational area which was developed as a <a href="/wiki/Honeypot_(geography)" class="mw-redirect" title="Honeypot (geography)">honeypot</a> to draw tourists away from the more sensitive/protected areas of the Mynd. <a href="/wiki/The_Wrekin" title="The Wrekin">The Wrekin</a> (407 metres (1,335&#160;ft)), located in the far northeastern <a href="/wiki/Salient_(geography)" title="Salient (geography)">panhandle</a> of the AONB, is an extremely popular hill with a well-used trail. Located near <a href="/wiki/Wellington,_Shropshire" title="Wellington, Shropshire">Wellington</a>, its position close to the major population centres of Shropshire, and good transport links (<a href="/wiki/A5_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A5 road (Great Britain)">A5</a>/<a href="/wiki/M54_motorway" title="M54 motorway">M54</a>) make it easy to access. <a href="/wiki/Ercall_Hill" class="mw-redirect" title="Ercall Hill">Ercall Hill</a>, a notable geological site, is located just to the north of The Wrekin.\n</p><p>Another prominent hill is <a href="/wiki/Corndon_Hill" title="Corndon Hill">Corndon Hill</a>, whose summit is in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Towns_and_villages">Towns and villages</span><span
