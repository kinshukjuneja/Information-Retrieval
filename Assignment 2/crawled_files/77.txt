Zanzibar</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_novel" class="mw-redirect" title="Children&#39;s novel">children\'s novel</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Morpurgo" title="Michael Morpurgo">Michael Morpurgo</a>. It was first published in Great Britain by <a href="/wiki/William_Heinemann" title="William Heinemann">William Heinemann</a> Publishers in 1995. The book won the <a href="/wiki/1995_Whitbread_Awards" title="1995 Whitbread Awards">Whitbread Children\'s Book Award</a> in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Wreck_of_the_Zanzibar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The story unfolds in journal entries and watercolor illustrations made by 14-year-old Laura Perryman in 1907 and 1908. She tells of her life on one of Britain\'s <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Scilly Isles</a>, where her family\'s survival depends on the mercy of the elements and the sea. One winter is particularly harsh, with the family\'s cows sickening and dying, the weather destroying houses and boats, the food stores dwindling and Laura\'s twin brother, Billy, running away to join a ship\'s crew. As bleak as Laura\'s days are, she is gentle enough to protect a sea turtle that might otherwise serve as food, and hopeful enough to dream of rowing in the island gig despite repeated declarations that a girl will never be allowed to handle one of the oars. Laura gets her chance in a dramatic storm and shipwreck, and helps save the island.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
