class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Francesco_della_Vigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Along with <a href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_Gloriosa_dei_Frari" title="Santa Maria Gloriosa dei Frari">Santa Maria Gloriosa dei Frari</a>, this is one of two <a href="/wiki/Franciscan" class="mw-redirect" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> churches in Venice. The site, originally a vineyard (<i>Vigna</i>), was donated by Marco Ziani in 1253 for construction of the monastery. A tiny chapel already on the site recalled the spot where an angel supposedly had pronounced <i>Pax tibi Evangelista meus</i> to the shipwrecked apostle St. Mark, patron of Venice.\n</p><p>The first church at the site was a <a href="/wiki/Hallenkirche" class="mw-redirect" title="Hallenkirche">triple-nave</a> <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> church by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marino_da_Pisa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marino da Pisa (page does not exist)">Marino da Pisa</a>. A monastery housed the Frati Minori dell\'Osservanza, while the Conventuali occupied the Frari across town. By the 16th century, the church building was in need of repair. Two main impulses led to the reconstruction of this church; one was the reform sweeping the order of the Franciscan Observants, and the other was the wishes of Doge <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gritti" title="Andrea Gritti">Andrea Gritti</a>, whose family palace neighbored the church. In 1534, this Doge laid the foundation stone for the new church. The nave was roofed over by 1554.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exterior">Exterior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Francesco_della_Vigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Exterior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The church was designed in sober <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> style by <a href="/wiki/Jacopo_Sansovino" title="Jacopo Sansovino">Jacopo Sansovino</a> in 1554, with the advice of the Franciscan friar, Fra Francesco <a href="/wiki/Zorzi" title="Zorzi">Zorzi</a>. Fra Zorzi based the sizing of the various elements on the number three, because of its association with the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Trinity">Trinity</a>: the nave should be nine paces wide and 27 paces long, each side chapel three paces wide. However the white marble fa\xc3\xa7ade (1564-1570) was not based on Sansovino\'s designs, but instead was a product of a <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Palladio" title="Andrea Palladio">Andrea Palladio</a>. It is thought that the patrician Daniele Barbaro lobbied for the commission to be switched from Sansovino to Palladio in 1562, convincing the then Patriarch, Giovanni Grimani.\n</p><p>Palladio addressed the challenge of linking the central nave with the side aisles in the fa\xc3\xa7ade in an innovative manner. The Corinthian columns in both the center and the sides are perched on the same high plinth, though the central four columns are taller and wider than the lateral ones. The lateral columns support half-pediments that mimic the angle of the central full pediment. Above the central portal is a hemicircular window, interrupted superiorly by a bracket and subdivided into three parts. The fa\xc3\xa7ade contains two large bronze statues of <i>Moses</i> and <i>Saint Paul</i> (1592), actively stepping forward from their shallow niches, sculpted by <a href="/wiki/Tiziano_Aspetti" title="Tiziano Aspetti">Tiziano Aspetti</a>.\n</p><p>At the top of the <a href="/wiki/Tympanum_(architecture)" title="Tympanum (architecture)">tympanum</a>, an eagle unfolds a banner stating <i>Renovabitur</i>. Below, a frieze dedication of the church states: <i>Deo utriusque templi aedificatori ac reparatori</i> (God, builder and restorer of both temples)  is engraved. Four marble plaques state:  Ac cede ad hoc / ne deseras spirituale / non sine iugi exteriori / interiorique bello (Enter here / not abandoning the spirit / not without detaching yourself from the exterior world / and making your interior beautiful).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Inside the church, each chapel was sold for 200-350 ducats to aristocratic donors, raising much needed construction funds, granting the rights to place their coat-of-arms in the chapel and to bury their family there. For the right to be buried in the floor of the chancel in front of the high altar, Doge <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gritti" title="Andrea Gritti">Andrea Gritti</a> paid 1000 ducats. In 1542, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vettor_Grimani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vettor Grimani (page does not exist)">Vettor Grimani</a> and his brother Cardinal Marino acquired rights to build the facade for the church. The three sarcophagi for two cardinals and a patriarch from the <a href="/wiki/Grimani" class="mw-redirect" title="Grimani">Grimani</a> family are no longer in place. The facade of the church was ultimately commissioned from <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Palladio" title="Andrea Palladio">Andrea Palladio</a> (1562) by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Grimani" title="Giovanni Grimani">Giovanni Grimani</a>.\n</p><p>In the 1950s were cast the 5 bells in B rung with the <a href="/wiki/Veronese_bellringing_art" title="Veronese bellringing art">Veronese bellringing class="mw-headline" id="Interior">Interior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Francesco_della_Vigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Interior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The interior of the church has a simplicity and severity befitting a Franciscan church, with plain <a href="/wiki/Istrian_stone" title="Istrian stone">Istrian stone</a> <a href="/wiki/Doric_Order" class="mw-redirect" title="Doric Order">Doric pilasters</a>. The choir, which in the past was occupied by the monks during services, is behind the altar. The interior contains an alabaster sculpture of <i>St. Louis of Toulouse</i> and an early 15th-century Gothic painting of the <i><a href="/wiki/Madonna_of_humility" title="Madonna of humility">Madonna of humility</a></i>.\n</p><p>The Grimani chapel (first in the left aisle) is decorated with ceiling paintings by <a href="/wiki/Battista_Franco" class="mw-redirect" title="Battista Franco">Battista Franco</a>, and murals and altarpiece by <a href="/wiki/Federico_Zuccari" title="Federico Zuccari">Federico Zuccari</a>. The chapel has been restored by the British charity <a href="/wiki/Venice_in_Peril_Fund" title="Venice in Peril Fund">Venice in Peril</a>, which also restored Palladio\'s facade in the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-Peril_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peril-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The chapels of the church house masterpieces that both were made for this church or were moved here mainly in the 19th century from shuttering churches, oratories, and monasteries. They belong to some of the most prominent aristocratic families in Venice. Among the chapels, and works therein, are the following (starting from the entrance):<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Right">Right</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Francesco_della_Vigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Right">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<dl><dt>1st, <b>Bragadin</b> or of <i>St Catherine of Alexandria</i></dt>\n<dd>First chapel on the right, dedicated to the Bragadin family, and patronized by <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Alexandria" title="Catherine of Alexandria">Saint Catherine of Alexandria</a>. The painting of the altarpiece depicts Catherine of Alexandria with Saints Jerome, John the Baptist, and the Apostle James (Middle of the 16th century) by <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Porta" title="Giuseppe Porta">Giuseppe Porta</a>.</dd></dl>\n<p>On the right, a painting depicting "the Last Supper" by <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Rizzo_da_Santacroce" title="Francesco Rizzo da Santacroce">Francesco Rizzo da Santacroce</a>. On the left, the "Resurrection of Christ"  by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_da_Asola" title="Giovanni da Asola">Giovanni da Asola</a>.\nIn the middle of the chapel, the tombstone of Bragadin Girolamo Micheli\n</p>\n\t\t\t</div>\n\t\t</div></li>\n</ul>\n<dl><dt>3rd left, <b><a href="/wiki/Sagredo_family" title="Sagredo family">Sagredo</a></b></dt>\n<dd>The chapel was designed by <a href="/wiki/Tommaso_Temanza" title="Tommaso Temanza">Tommaso Temanza</a>. It is dedicated to Saint Gherardo Sagredo, an ancestor who died in 1046, and was declared blessed in 1076. The pendedentives depict the four evangelists and four virtues by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Battista_Tiepolo" title="Giovanni Battista Tiepolo">Tiepolo</a>. The cupola has a fresco painted sotto in su by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerolamo_Pellegrini&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gerolamo Pellegrini (page does not exist)">Pellegrini</a>. The chapel has statue of the Blessed Gherardo by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Cominelli" title="Andrea Cominelli">Andrea Cominelli</a>, and other sculptures by Justus Le Court and Enrico Merengo. On the walls are two funeral monuments with busts to the Doges <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%B2_Sagredo" title="Nicol\xc3\xb2 Sagredo">Nicol\xc3\xb2 Sagredo</a> (ruled 1675-76) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Giovanni_Sagredo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Giovanni Sagredo (page does not exist)">Giovanni Sagredo</a> (ruled 1676-91), with busts by Giovanni Gai. The chapel underscores the complex machinations in an oligarchic and aristocratic Venice to uphold the family name.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<dl><dt><b>Chapel Priuli</b></dt>\n<dd>Dedicated to the family <a href="/wiki/Priuli" title="Priuli">Priuli</a> and patronized by <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Baylon" class="mw-redirect" title="Pascal Baylon">Pascal Baylon</a>. It is closed by a polychrome marble balustrade of the XVe . On the right wall a painting of an Addolorate XVII attributed to <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Vassilacchi" title="Antonio Vassilacchi">Antonio Vassilacchi</a>. Below in the wall is the stone where <a href="/wiki/Justine_of_Padua" class="mw-redirect" title="Justine of Padua">Justine of Padua</a> knelt for his martyrdom (comes from the old church of Santa Giustina, now destroyed). The polychrome marble altar is surmounted by a niche in red marble from Verona, where one can see a wooden statue of <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Baylon" class="mw-redirect" title="Pascal Baylon">Pascal Baylon</a> (1691) by Marchi\xc3\xb2 Molziner (withdrawn in 1898 from the Chapel Giustiniani where it was previously exhibited). On the altar a glass sarcophagus containing the body of Santa Cristina (martyred at 13 years in the year 297) relic deposited here following the destruction of the church Santa Giustina in 1810.</dd>\n<dt>The Monument to the Doge Marcantonio Trevisan</dt>\n<dd>Above the door giving access to the Saint-Chapelle, the sacristy and the cloisters. The Monument is by Girolamo Campagna; The Doge\'s tomb is in the center of the transept facing the choir. On the right side of the door is a statue of St. Louis of Toulouse.</dd>\n<dt>The Holy Chapel</dt>\n<dd>Accessible from the left arm of the <a href="/wiki/Transept" title="Transept">transept</a>, the From the same author on the right side <a href="/wiki/Antony_of_Padua" class="mw-redirect" title="Antony of Padua">Antony of Padua</a>. The altar of right is attributed to <a href="/wiki/Jacopo_Sansovino" title="Jacopo Sansovino">Sansovino</a> in 1559. id="Choir">Choir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=San_Francesco_della_Vigna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Choir">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The choir is complex with a lateral vestibule on either side of the liturgical choir and a choir of brothers the two are separated by the main altar and a partition. On each side an apsidal chapel.\nOn the right side of the vestibule is a picture of <a href="/wiki/Palma_il_giovane" class="mw-redirect" title="Palma il giovane">Palma il giovane</a>: the flagellation, below an icon (<a href="/wiki/Tempera" title="Tempera">tempera</a> on wood) anonymous work of the end of the XIVth: Madonna dell\'Umilt\xc3\xa0 <i>.</i>\n</p>\n<ul><li>On the left wall the funerary monument and the epitaph of the Doge <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gritti" title="Andrea Gritti">Andrea Gritti</a> (died in 1538), a work of the Palladian school of the mid-16th century. On the right wall is the monument of Senator Tridiano <a href="/wiki/Gritti" title="Gritti">Gritti</a> who died in 1474.</li>\n<li>The high altar: The design and the sculptures are attributed to <a href="/wiki/Girolamo_Campagna" title="Girolamo Campagna">Girolamo Campagna</a> in the second half of the XVIth. Initially the two groups of four wooden columns contained the statues of <a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assis" class="mw-redirect" title="Francis of Assis">Francis of Assis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antony_of_Padua" class="mw-redirect" title="Antony of Padua">Antony of Padua</a>. The center has the particularity of not having an altarpiece but a cross. In 1649 Baltasarre Longhena was commissioned to modify the altar. Above the eight Corinthian columns it has set up a cornice in the baroque style. In the second half of the 19th century, a large tabernacle was built from the ruined church of Santa Lucia, the statues of St. Francis and St. Anthony were removed from the altar and out of the side doors of the choir brothers. In 1939 the tabernacle deemed too ostentatious for the Franciscan ideal was changed. The pediment of the triumphal arch: Eternal Father, on the sides: the Annunciation in painted wood (second half of the sixteenth century), are attributed to <a href="/wiki/Girolamo_Campagna" Chapel <i>Giustinian dei Vescovi</i></dt></dl>\n<p>The absidial chapel of right; It is under the patronage of <a href="/wiki/Peter_of_Alc%C3%A1ntara" class="mw-redirect" title="Peter of Alc\xc3\xa1ntara">Peter of Alc\xc3\xa1ntara</a>. It was financed and dedicated to the Giustinian "dei vescovi" family (bishops) because this branch of the family had a good number of prelates in these ranks. The vault is decorated with stucco of the eighteenth century, with a central medallion <i>Saint Peter of Alcantara in Glory</i> (1765) by <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Fontebasso" title="Francesco Fontebasso">Francesco Fontebasso</a>. On either side of the stalls of the eighteenth century; Above the tables with gypseries. On the right wall: <i>The death of Peter of Alc\xc3\xa1ntara</i> (1765) Francesco Fontebasso, <i>Penance and meditation</i> (1789) by <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Maggiotto" title="Francesco Maggiotto">Francesco Maggiotto</a>, <i>Peter of Alc\xc3\xa1ntara and the Queen of Spain</i>, (1765) Francesco Fontebasso. The altar: the table of the altar represents <a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis of Assisi</a> receiving the infant Jesus of the Virgin beginning of the seventeenth century by <a href="/wiki/Sante_Peranda" title="Sante Peranda">Sante Peranda</a>. On the left wall: <i>The ecstasy of Saint Peter</i> (1765) Francesco Fontebasso, <i>The theological virtues</i> (1785) by <a href="/wiki/Jacopo_Marieschi" title="Jacopo Marieschi">Jacopo Marieschi</a>, <i>Peter of Alc\xc3\xa1ntara shows FRancesc ad\'Avila the way to paradise (1765) Francesco Fontebasso</i>\n</p>\n<ul class="gallery mw-gallery-packed">\n\t\t<li class="gallerybox"
