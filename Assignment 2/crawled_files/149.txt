title="Enlarge"></a></div>The River Lerryn at Tallowater</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>River Lerryn</b> is a river in east <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, UK, a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Fowey" title="River Fowey">River Fowey</a>. The Lerryn is the largest of the tributaries which enter the estuary of the Fowey. The river is tidal up to the village of Lerryn. The landscape of the Lerryn catchment is rural and includes heathland, moorland and rough pasture in the upper reaches and broadleaf, coniferous and mixed plantation woodland in the lower. This catchment includes four <a href="/wiki/SSSI" class="mw-redirect" title="SSSI">SSSIs</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Redlake_Meadows_%26_Hoggs_Moor" title="Redlake Meadows &amp; Hoggs Moor">Redlake Meadows &amp; Hoggs Moor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Lerryn rises at Fairy Cross (Grid ref. SX1262) on the southern slopes of <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a> and flows south-southwest until it enters the Fowey estuary (Grid ref. SX1255).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The name of the river is <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>; the earliest record of it is as "Leryan" and "Lerion" in 1289. In modern Cornish its name would be Dowr Leryon, meaning "river of floods".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The village of <a href="/wiki/Lerryn" title="Lerryn">Lerryn</a> is named after the river.  \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Footnotes">Footnotes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Lerryn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Footnotes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cornwallriversproject.org.uk/geography/fowey.htm">River catchments -- Fowey and Lerryn</a>; Cornwall Rivers Project</span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Ordnance Survey <i>One-inch Map of England &amp; Wales; Bodmin and Launceston, sheet 186</i>. 1946</span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-3"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-3">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"> Weatherhill, Craig (2009). <i>A Concise Dictionary of Cornish Place-Names</i>.
