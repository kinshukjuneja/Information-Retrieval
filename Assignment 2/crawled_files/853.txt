The team was based in <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford, Connecticut</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Notable_alumni"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Notable alumni</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hartford_Dark_Blues&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Hartford Dark Blues were a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Professional Base Ball Players">National Association of Professional Base Ball Players</a> in 1874 and 1875 and the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> in 1876 and 1877. In 1877 the team played in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a> as the <b>Brooklyn Hartfords</b>.\n</p><p>\nPlaying at the <a href="/wiki/Hartford_Ball_Club_Grounds" title="Hartford Ball Club Grounds">Hartford Ball Club Grounds</a>, in 1876 they joined the <a href="/wiki/19th_century_National_League_teams" class="mw-redirect" title="19th century National League teams">National League</a> as a charter member. The team\'s owner, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_G._Bulkeley" class="mw-redirect" title="Morgan G. Bulkeley">Morgan G. Bulkeley</a>, was also the first president of the National League. Managed by their third baseman, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Ferguson_(infielder)" title="Bob Ferguson (infielder)">Bob Ferguson</a>, the Dark Blues went on to finish third in 1876 with a record of 47\xe2\x80\x9321. </p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White,_1875.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White%2C_1875.jpg/220px-Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White%2C_1875.jpg" width="220" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White%2C_1875.jpg/330px-Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White%2C_1875.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White%2C_1875.jpg/440px-Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White%2C_1875.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1217" data-file-height="922" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hartford_Dark_Blues_by_Prescott_%26_White,_1875.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1875 Hartford Dark Blues.</div></div></div><p>The team\'s strong suit was pitching, with both <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Bond_(baseball)" title="Tommy Bond (baseball)">Tommy Bond</a> and future Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Candy_Cummings" title="Candy Cummings">Candy Cummings</a> finishing with an <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a> under 2.  The pitching staff recorded the most complete games (69) and allowed the lowest number of home runs throughout the 70-game 1876 campaign (the Philadelphia Athletics also accomplished this feat that season). The team\'s best hitter was right fielder <a href="/wiki/Dick_Higham" title="Dick Higham">Dick Higham</a>, who led the team in most offensive categories.\n</p><p>The team left Hartford and moved to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a> for the 1877 season to become the <b>Brooklyn Hartfords</b>. Managed again by Ferguson, the team finished in third again, with a record of 31\xe2\x80\x9327. With Bond, Cummings and Higham all having left the team, the team\'s best player this year was undoubtedly right fielder <a href="/wiki/John_Cassidy_(baseball)" title="John Cassidy (baseball)">John Cassidy</a>, who batted .378 and also led the team in many other categories.\n</p><p>The team disbanded after the 1877 season. Author <a href="/wiki/Mark_Twain" title="Mark Twain">Mark Twain</a> was a fan of the team.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
