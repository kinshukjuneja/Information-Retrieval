6th Duke of Leeds</b>, <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:100;"><a href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Order_of_the_Garter" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight of the Order of the Garter">KG</a>,&#32;<a of Carmarthen</b> from 1789 to 1799, was a British peer and politician. He served as <a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Horse" title="Master of the Horse">Master of the Horse</a> between 1827 and 1830. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Osborne,_6th_Duke_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Leeds was born in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Francis_Osborne,_5th_Duke_of_Leeds" title="Francis Osborne, 5th Duke of Leeds">Francis Osborne, 5th Duke of Leeds</a>, and his first wife, <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Osborne,_Marchioness_of_Carmarthen" title="Amelia Osborne, Marchioness of Carmarthen">Amelia, Baroness Darcy de Knayth</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Darcy,_4th_Earl_of_Holderness" title="Robert Darcy, 4th Earl of Holderness">Robert Darcy, 4th Earl of Holderness</a>. <a href="/wiki/Francis_Osborne,_1st_Baron_Godolphin" title="Francis Osborne, 1st Baron Godolphin">Francis Osborne, 1st Baron Godolphin</a>, was his younger brother. His parents divorced in 1779. In January 1784, aged eight, he succeeded as 13th <a href="/wiki/Baron_Darcy_de_Knayth" title="Baron Darcy de Knayth">Baron Darcy de Knayth</a> and 10th <a href="/wiki/Baron_Conyers" title="Baron Conyers">Baron Conyers</a> on the early death of his mother. In 1799 he also succeeded his father in the dukedom of Leeds.<sup id="cite_ref-thepeerage.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thepeerage.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Osborne,_6th_Duke_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Leeds was appointed <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_the_North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of the North Riding of Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant of the North Riding of Yorkshire</a> in 1802, a post he held until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In May 1827 he entered <a href="/wiki/George_Canning" title="George Canning">George Canning</a>\'s government as <a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Horse" title="Master of the Horse">Master of the Horse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He continued in this office under <a href="/wiki/Frederick_John_Robinson,_1st_Viscount_Goderich" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick John Robinson, 1st Viscount Goderich">Lord Goderich</a> between August 1827 and January 1828 and under the <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Wellesley,_1st_Duke_of_Wellington" title="Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington">Duke of Wellington</a> between January 1828 and November 1830. He was sworn of the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Most_Honourable_Privy_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Most Honourable Privy Council">Privy Council</a> in 1827<sup id="cite_ref-gazette_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and made a <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Garter" title="Order of the Garter">Knight of the Garter</a> the same month.<sup id="cite_ref-gazette_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span
