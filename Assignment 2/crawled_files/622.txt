Pont Pill is a tidal river and is only navigable at <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">high water</a>.\n</p><p>Unspoiled by development, Pont Pill is popular with <a href="/wiki/Birdwatching" title="Birdwatching">bird watchers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">walkers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">sport fishermen</a>. The best way to see Pont Pill is by small boat at high water when it is possible to reach the bridge a mile-and-a-half upriver at <b>Pont</b> (Old <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Pont Pyll</b></i>, meaning <i>bridge of the creek</i>) the end of the navigable water.\n</p><p>Walkers can see Pont Pill from hills that surround the river by following a section of the <a href="/wiki/South_West_Coast_Path" title="South West Coast Path">South West Coast Path</a> between <a href="/wiki/Bodinnick" title="Bodinnick">Bodinnick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fowey" title="Fowey">Fowey</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Literary_associations">Literary associations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pont_Pill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Literary associations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Pont Pill is thought by many local people to be part of the inspiration for Mole, Ratty, Toad and Badger\'s adventures in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wind_in_the_Willows" title="The Wind in the Willows">The Wind in the Willows</a></i> because author <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Grahame" title="Kenneth Grahame">Kenneth Grahame</a> holidayed in nearby <a href="/wiki/Lerryn" title="Lerryn">Lerryn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCGrahame_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCGrahame-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Grahame\'s time spent near the river may have inspired the bedtime stories he told to his son, and later developed into the famous children\'s book.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Leo_Walmsley" title="Leo Walmsley">Leo Walmsley</a> wrote several novels while living in a hut by the creek. It is the setting for his <a href="/wiki/Autobiographical_novel" title="Autobiographical novel">autobiographical novel</a> <i>Love in the Sun</i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pont_Pill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-BBCGrahame-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-BBCGrahame_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/insideout/southwest/series7/wind_in_willows.shtml">"BBC Inside Out - The animals of Wind in the Willows"</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/BBC_Online" title="BBC Online">BBC Online</a></i><span class="reference-accessdate">.
