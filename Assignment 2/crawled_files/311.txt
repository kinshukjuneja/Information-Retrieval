class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>A8 countries</b>, also referred to as the <b>EU8</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> are a group of eight of the 10 countries that joined the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> during its <a href="/wiki/2004_enlargement_of_the_European_Union" title="2004 enlargement of the European Union">2004 enlargement</a>. They are commonly grouped together separately from the other two states that joined in 2004, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, because of their relatively lower per capita income levels in comparison to the EU average.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The countries were:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a></li></ul>\n<p>A particular reason for grouping the A8 countries was an expectation that they would be the origin for a new wave of increased migration to wealthier European countries.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They subsequently did prove to be the origin of a new wave of migration, with many immigrants travelling from these countries to wealthier countries
