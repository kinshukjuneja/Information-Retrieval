template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>British\xe2\x80\x93Irish Intergovernmental Conference</b> (<b>BIIGC</b>) was established under an Agreement between the Governments of Ireland and the United Kingdom made on 8 March 1998. It was provided for under Strand Three of the <a href="/wiki/Belfast_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Belfast Agreement">Belfast Agreement</a>. The inaugural meeting took place at <a href="/wiki/10_Downing_Street" title="10 Downing Street">10 Downing Street</a> on 17 December 1999 and was chaired by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Ahern" title="Bertie Ahern">Bertie Ahern</a> and attended by representatives of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish government">Irish government</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">British government</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a>.\n</p><p>The BIIGC replaced the Anglo-Irish Conference which was established under Article 2 of the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish_Agreement" title="Anglo-Irish Agreement">Anglo-Irish Agreement</a> of 1985. Under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Belfast_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Belfast Agreement">Belfast Agreement</a>, the BIIGC is supported by officials of the British and Irish Governments, including a standing Joint Secretariat of officials dealing with non-devolved Northern Ireland matters. The BIIGC secretariat has approximately 21 staff (10 for the British side, 11 for the Irish side).<sup id="cite_ref-Hansard,_14_July_2008:_Column_WA103_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard,_14_July_2008:_Column_WA103-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The staff comprise a mix of grades from senior civil servants to administrative support grades.<sup id="cite_ref-Hansard,_14_July_2008:_Column_WA103_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard,_14_July_2008:_Column_WA103-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On suspension, devolved matters revert to the Conference\'s remit. The latest meeting of the British Irish Intergovernmental Conference took place in Dundalk, 26 February 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The BIIGC guarantees the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Government of Ireland</a> a say in areas of bilateral co-operation and on those matters not yet <a href="/wiki/Devolved" class="mw-redirect" title="Devolved">devolved</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> or the <a href="/wiki/North/South_Ministerial_Council" title="North/South Ministerial Council">North/South Ministerial Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The BIIGC is normally chaired by the Irish <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Foreign_Affairs_and_Trade" title="Minister for Foreign Affairs and Trade">Minister for Foreign Affairs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Taoiseach&#39;s_website_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Taoiseach&#39;s_website-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Provision is made however for meetings at <a href="/wiki/Summit_(meeting)" title="Summit (meeting)">summit level</a>, i.e. between the <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> and Prime Minister, as required. Summit meetings of the BIIGC took place in 1999 and 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-Taoiseach&#39;s_website_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Taoiseach&#39;s_website-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> There is also provision under the Belfast Agreement for <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Legislative_Assembly_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Member of the Legislative Assembly (Northern Ireland)">Members of the Legislative Assembly</a> to be involved in the intergovernmental conference but they do not have the power to block decisions taken by the two governments.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_justice_co-operation">Criminal justice co-operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British%E2%80%93Irish_Intergovernmental_Conference&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criminal justice co-operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Under an international agreement between the UK and Ireland on "Co-operation on Criminal Justice Matters" signed in 2005, the Northern Ireland minister responsible for justice reports to the BIIGC on certain matters. This because the Agreement provides that the Ministers of the governments of the United Kingdom and Ireland (hereinafter referred to as "the Ministers") responsible for criminal justice matters in the two jurisdictions (Ireland and Northern Ireland) shall meet at least annually for the purpose of facilitating more effective co-operation and co-ordination on criminal justice matters, including in combating criminal behaviour, working together in the prevention of crime and on community safety issues, and dealing with offenders after conviction. Such meetings shall be referred to hereinafter as Ministerial Meetings on criminal justice co-operation. The Agreement provides that such meetings shall operate under the auspices of, and be accountable to, the British\xe2\x80\x93Irish Intergovernmental Conference.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meetings">Meetings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
