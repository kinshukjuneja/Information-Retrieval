<span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The name <i>Yilan</i> name derives from the <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_aborigines" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiwanese aborigines">aboriginal</a> <a href="/wiki/Kavalan_people" title="Kavalan people">Kavalan</a> tribe. Other former names in reference to this area in the <a href="/wiki/Yilan_Plain" title="Yilan Plain">Yilan Plain</a> include <i>Kabalan</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i>Kavalan</i>, <i>La-A-Lan</i>,<i>E-A-Lan</i>, and <i>Gilan</i>. Before 2009, the county\'s official name was transliterated as <b>Ilan</b>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/1915%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3%E5%AE%9C%E8%98%AD%E5%BB%B3%E7%BD%B2_Giran_Ch%C5%8D_-_Yilan_Government_Hall_of_Taiwan_%E3%81%8E%E3%82%89%E3%82%93%E3%81%A1%E3%82%87%E3%81%86.jpg/440px-1915%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3%E5%AE%9C%E8%98%AD%E5%BB%B3%E7%BD%B2_Giran_Ch%C5%8D_-_Yilan_Government_Hall_of_Taiwan_%E3%81%8E%E3%82%89%E3%82%93%E3%81%A1%E3%82%87%E3%81%86.jpg href="/wiki/File:1915%E8%87%BA%E7%81%A3%E5%AE%9C%E8%98%AD%E5%BB%B3%E7%BD%B2_Giran_Ch%C5%8D_-_Yilan_Government_Hall_of_Taiwan_%E3%81%8E%E3%82%89%E3%82%93%E3%81%A1%E3%82%87%E3%81%86.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Yilan Government building in 1915</div></div></div>\n<p>Since early ages, many people have traveled from far places to Yilan. <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_aborigines" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiwanese aborigines">Indigenous tribes</a> that have settled in Yilan are <a href="/wiki/Kavalan_people" title="Kavalan people">Kavalan people</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atayal_people" title="Atayal people">Atayal people</a>.\n</p><p>The Kavalan people came by the sea and lived by the river at <a href="/wiki/Yilan_Plain" title="Yilan Plain">Yilan Plain</a> since around 1,000 years ago. They mostly speak the <a href="/wiki/Austronesian_languages" title="Austronesian languages">Austronesian languages</a>. Their settlements consisted of small villages along rivers with around 40-50 communities scattered around the area with a total population of approximately 10,000 people. The Atayal people came by crossing the Xiyuan Pass and settled in the mountain areas.\n</p><p>The Atayal people arrived in Yilan around 250 years ago and settled along the upper Dazhuoshui River. Later, the tribes crossed the Siyuan Pass to reach the valley upstream of the <a href="/wiki/Zhuoshui_River" title="Zhuoshui River">Zhuoshui River</a>. These people are the current residents of <a href="/wiki/Datong,_Yilan" title="Datong, Yilan">Datong Township</a>. Other parts of the Atayal people headed east to enter and settle along the Nan\'ao North River and Heping North River. These groups are now settled in <a href="/wiki/Nan-ao,_Yilan" class="mw-redirect" title="Nan-ao, Yilan">Nan-ao Township</a>.\n</p><p>Around 200 years ago, at the end of the 18th century, the <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a> traversed the mountain range and settled in Yilan. Large populations began taming the wilderness, cultivating the fields and building irrigation channels. They used various means to seize lands from the Kavalans. Some Kavalans left their homes while some others migrated southwards to <a href="/wiki/Hualien_County" title="Hualien County">Hualien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taitung_County" title="Taitung County">Taitung</a> coastlines and established settlements.<sup id="cite_ref-e-land.gov.tw_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-e-land.gov.tw-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_Formosa">Spanish Formosa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spanish Formosa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spaniards</a> began arriving in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Formosa" title="Spanish Formosa">Taiwan</a> in the 17th century. In 1626, the Spaniards led an invasion under the pretext of ship crews having been slain by Taiwanese barbarians. They then torched harbors and surrounding villages, and even went as far as taking over <a href="/wiki/Su-ao,_Yilan" class="mw-redirect" title="Su-ao, Yilan">Su\'ao Town</a> and established a city called Saint Lorenzo.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dutch_Formosa">Dutch Formosa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dutch Formosa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Spaniards were subsequently ousted by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Empire" title="Dutch Empire">Dutch</a> who had taken over the southern part of Taiwan and established <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Formosa" title="Dutch Formosa">Dutch Formosa</a>. In 1640, the Dutch began contacting Han Chinese merchants for trade and levying taxes on various commercial goods. The merchants had to pay all company taxes but also enjoyed the right to monopolize trade.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kingdom_of_Tungning">Kingdom of Tungning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Kingdom of Tungning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Tungning" title="Kingdom of Tungning">Kingdom of Tungning</a> era, the previous economy monopoly system developed during the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Formosa" title="Dutch Formosa">Dutch Formosa</a> continued to be practiced.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qing_Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Qing Dynasty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>When the Qing Dynasty annexed Taiwan, they established the <b>Kavalan</b> sub-prefecture in Yilan. In 1806, armed conflicts broke out among various ethnic immigrants, followed by pillaging by pirates. The <a href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" class="mw-redirect" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> government subsequently realized that if they continued to disregard the Kavalan people and did not establish rule of law and a system of defense, Yilan would become a haven for criminals and outlaws, a thorn in the side for Taiwan. In 1809, <a href="/wiki/Jiaqing_Emperor" title="Jiaqing Emperor">Jiaqing Emperor</a> incorporated Kavalan into the domain of the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Qing_rule" title="Taiwan under Qing rule">empire</a>. Troops were dispatched to quell pirate attacks and chart local territory.\n</p><p>Local government systems in Taiwan underwent many changes during the Qing Dynasty period. But with regards to administrative levels lower than the county, including local villages, there were no major changes. The earliest organization and planning of Yilan consisted of seven citadels. In 1835, the seven citadels were further divided into 12 citadels based on the needs of the changing population and environment. This arrangement remained unchanged until the end of Qing Dynasty rule.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Taiwan_(1874)" title="Japanese invasion of Taiwan (1874)">Mudan Incident</a> in 1874, Qing rulers changed their passive attitude and took a more ambitious approach in ruling Taiwan. The original aboriginal term <b>Kavalan</b> district was renamed with a more Han-centric <b>Yilan</b> name and the administrative system was also changed accordingly from the original temporary "district" to a formally governed "county".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 1875, the newly created <a href="/wiki/Taipeh_Prefecture" title="Taipeh Prefecture">Taipeh Prefecture</a> included modern-day Yilan County.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Empire_of_Japan">Empire of Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Empire of Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a> in 1894, the Qing government handed over Taiwan to the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a> in accordance with the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Shimonoseki" title="Treaty of Shimonoseki">Treaty of Shimonoseki</a>. Modern-day Yilan County covers Giran District<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">\xe5\xae\x9c\xe8\x98\xad\xe9\x83\xa1</span>)</span>, Rat\xc5\x8d District<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">\xe7\xbe\x85\xe6\x9d\xb1\xe9\x83\xa1</span>)</span>, Su\xc5\x8d District<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">\xe8\x98\x87\xe6\xbe\xb3\xe9\x83\xa1</span>)</span>, and Giran City<span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">\xe5\xae\x9c\xe8\x98\xad\xe5\xb8\x82</span>)</span> as they existed from 1920 to 1945, all under <a href="/wiki/Taihoku_Prefecture" title="Taihoku Prefecture">Taihoku Prefecture</a>, during <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Japanese_rule" title="Taiwan under Japanese rule">Japanese rule</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Republic_of_China">Republic of China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Republic of China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Retrocession_Day" title="Retrocession Day">handover of Taiwan from Japan</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of China">Republic of China</a> in October 1945, the present-day area of Yilan County was incorporated under <a href="/wiki/Taipei_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Taipei County">Taipei County</a>. On 16 August 1950, <b>Yilan County</b> was established as a <a href="/wiki/County_(Taiwan)" title="County (Taiwan)">county</a> of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Province" title="Taiwan Province">Taiwan Province</a> with <a href="/wiki/Yilan_City" title="Yilan City">Yilan City</a> as the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg/220px-Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg/330px-Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg/440px-Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1505" data-file-height="1000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Yilan_Plain_2011-05-11.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Yilan_Plain" title="Yilan Plain">Yilan Plain</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Yilan County sits on the <a href="/wiki/Yilan_Plain" title="Yilan Plain">Yilan Plain</a>, a combined alluvial plain created by <a href="/wiki/Lanyang_River" title="Lanyang River">Lanyang River</a> and other minor streams with a rough shape of triangle. On the three <a href="/wiki/Vertex_(geometry)" title="Vertex (geometry)">vertices</a> of the triangle sit the <a href="/wiki/Toucheng,_Yilan" title="Toucheng, Yilan">Toucheng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanxing_Township" title="Sanxing Township">Sanxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Su-ao,_Yilan" class="mw-redirect" title="Su-ao, Yilan">Su-ao Townships</a> with a roughly equal distance of 30 km on the three sides. The <a href="/wiki/Xueshan_Range" title="Xueshan Range">Xueshan Range</a> sits on the northwest of Yilan County from Toucheng to Sanxing. The county is geographically divided into the cliffs and the plains. The <a href="/wiki/Central_Mountain_Range" title="Central Mountain Range">Central Mountain Range</a> sits to the south from Sanxing to Su\'ao.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Upper Lanyang River is steep and the <a href="/wiki/Rapid" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapid">rapid</a> <a href="/wiki/Current_(stream)" title="Current (stream)">current</a> is highly erosive. Large amount of <a href="/wiki/Silt" title="Silt">silt</a> carried by the river have little time to settle because of the high slope of the lands where it flows out of the mountains and valleys. An <a href="/wiki/Alluvial_fan" title="Alluvial fan">alluvial fan</a> is formed as a result of scattered sand and <a href="/wiki/Gravel" title="Gravel">gravel</a> settling down. Large amount of gravel accumulate in the shallow <a href="/wiki/Stream_bed" title="Stream bed">stream bed</a>, creating alluvial fans that often forms into web-like pattern. The river tends to change courses after floods caused by heavy rainfall or river overflow. The Lanyang River slows down as it reaches mid and downstream where silt begins to settle.\n</p><p>Yilan County is located in the northeastern Taiwan Island which covers an area of 2,143 km<sup>2</sup>. The longest distance from east to west is 63 km and from north to south is 74 km. From the mountain areas downwards, the land falls in altitude in the stages of mountains, alleys, alluvial plains, lowlands, swamps, sand hills and finally coastline.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_County,_Taiwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span Folkgame Festival">Yilan International Children\'s Folklore and Folkgame Festival</a>, \xe2\x80\x9cA dreamland for the children of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, a magnet for art from around the world, a garden of culture for the people of Ilan.\xe2\x80\x9d Seven years ago, these were the concepts that launched the first ICFFF, and over the years the people of Yilan have been making it happen step by step. Of all <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>\xe2\x80\x99s folk festivals, the ICFFF is probably the best known internationally.</li>\n<li>Each summer, <a href="/wiki/Dongshan_River_Water_Park" title="Dongshan River Water Park">Dongshan River Water Park</a> attracts countless children and the themes of the various years have become for many part of the collective memory. The number of people attending has grown each year since the festival was first held in 1996, surpassing one million visits for the first time in 2002. Not only does the event enrich Yilan\xe2\x80\x99s cultural soil and create business opportunities, it offers a highly successful example which other cities and counties across Taiwan can draw on as they also attempt to bring more culture and art into the life of their communities.</li>\n<li>Started from 2004, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_Green_International_Film_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yilan Green International Film Festival (page does not exist)">Yilan Green International Film Festival</a> (<b>GIFT</b>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh-Hant">\xe5\xae\x9c\xe8\x98\xad\xe5\x9c\x8b\xe9\x9a\x9b\xe7\xb6\xa0\xe8\x89\xb2\xe5\xbd\xb1\xe5\xb1\x95</span>) is a non-competition film festival which launch its 3rd edition from 28/4 to 06/5, 2006. GIFT also aims to build up an archive collecting the selected works for the purposes of relevant research as well as education. GIFT is an international film festival taking place in a beautiful country side of Taiwan. As always, our guests will notice the clean and peaceful landscape of Yilan County and the hospitality of Yilan people. We hope that GIFT is a film festival that could fulfill the expectations of all kind of the audience</li>\n<li>Yilan International Collegiate Invitational Regatta<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yilan_Green_Expo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Yilan Green Expo (page does not exist)">Yilan Green Expo</a> started from 2000. The Expo hold in spring every year and always locate on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wulaokeng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wulaokeng (page does not exist)">Wulaokeng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Su%27ao" title="Su&#39;ao">Su\'ao</a>. The spindle of Expo normally has three parts and there are agricultural life, ecological conservation, sustainable future.
