church</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s, Isles of Scilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>. The Church was consecrated on 7 September 1838 and replaced the <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">church</a> at <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a> which was inconvenient for the Hugh Town population and in need of repair.<sup id="cite_ref-vyvyan_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vyvyan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s_Church,_St_Mary%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary%27s,_Hugh_Town.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/St_Mary%27s%2C_Hugh_Town.jpg/220px-St_Mary%27s%2C_Hugh_Town.jpg" width="220" height="295" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/St_Mary%27s%2C_Hugh_Town.jpg/330px-St_Mary%27s%2C_Hugh_Town.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/St_Mary%27s%2C_Hugh_Town.jpg/440px-St_Mary%27s%2C_Hugh_Town.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1936" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary%27s,_Hugh_Town.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nave and chancel</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> although not an <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> designed the church and according to a letter, penned by himself to the secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> was very pleased with his architectural skill. Smith laid the foundation stone on 31 October 1836, on a site at the eastern end of Hugh Town on high ground between Carn Thomas and Buzza Hill. Vyvyan (1960) describes the building as \xc2\xb0 <i>... bold rather than pleasing, plain rather than ornate, characteristics which mark the interior also</i>\xe2\x80\xb3. <a href="/wiki/King_William_IV" class="mw-redirect" title="King William IV">King William IV</a> granted \xc2\xa31,500 towards its erection, it was completed in August 1838 and was consecrated on 7 September 1838.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The proposed inscription for the church, according to a memorandum in Smith\'s handwriting was to be \xe2\x80\xb3<i>This Church was erected by the munificence of His Majesty, King William the Fourth, a lasting memorial of his zeal and of his paternal regard for the inhabitants of these Islands</i>.\xc2\xb0 It actually reads \xe2\x80\xb3<i>In the year of our Lord, 1837, this Church was erected by the munificence of His Majesty King William IV. The same was completed at the expense of Augustus Smith, Esq</i>\xe2\x80\xb3.<sup id="cite_ref-vyvyan_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vyvyan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The interior contains the coloured and gilded wooden lion from the flagship of <a href="/wiki/Cloudesley_Shovell" title="Cloudesley Shovell">Sir Cloudesley Shovell</a>, wrecked in 1707. Outside the church are two lead water cisterns, with heraldic plaques and cartouches set in strapwork panels, dating from 1727 and brought here from <a href="/wiki/Star_Castle,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Star Castle, Isles of Scilly">Star Castle</a>. The church was repaired by Corfield (C. Russell and Son), Architects of <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Cornwall" title="Falmouth, Cornwall">Falmouth</a> between 1979 and 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stained_glass">Stained glass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s_Church,_St_Mary%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stained glass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG/220px-Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG/330px-Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG/440px-Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3428" data-file-height="2570" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lifeboat_in_stained_glass.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Detail of window by Alfred Wilkinson from 1967</div></div></div>\n<p>The east window dates from 1899 and is by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Eamer_Kempe" title="Charles Eamer Kempe">Charles Eamer Kempe</a>. The west window dates from 1937 and was designed by Arthur Lucien Ward. Two north windows by Alfred Wilkinson were added in 1967.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bells">Bells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Mary%27s_Church,_St_Mary%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The peal of eight bells were cast together at <a href="/wiki/Whitechapel_Bell_Foundry" title="Whitechapel Bell Foundry">Whitechapel Bell Foundry</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Gillett_%26_Johnston" title="Gillett &amp; Johnston">Gillett &amp; Johnston</a> 1922 profile in 2008. The bell frame was constructed and bells hung by Nicholson Engineering of <a href="/wiki/Bridport" title="Bridport">Bridport</a> 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The dedication of the eight new bells, clock and chimes was by the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Truro" title="Bishop of Truro">Bishop of Truro</a> <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Martin_Thornton" class="mw-redirect" title="Timothy Martin Thornton">Timothy Martin Thornton</a>, on his first visit to the Islands.
