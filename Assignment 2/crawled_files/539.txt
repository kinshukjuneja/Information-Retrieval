class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fauna_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are few pre-19th century records for animals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Do we have a source that explicitly states so, or is it WP:SYNTH/WP:OR based on the dearth of information that can be found on pre-19th century records? (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/William_Borlase" title="William Borlase">William Borlase</a> published <i>The Natural History of Cornwall</i> in 1758, commenting on the number of rabbits, and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Couch" title="Jonathan Couch">Jonathan Couch</a>\'s <i>A Cornish Fauna</i> gave an account of some the animals known in Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly. In the 19th century, following the fashion of the time, birds were shot and stuffed, especially by <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> and his predecessors on <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>. <a href="/wiki/Egg_collecting" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg collecting">Egg collecting</a> was allowed and in the <a href="/wiki/Natural_History_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural History Museum">Natural History Museum</a> there are, in the collection, forty-five eggs taken between 1880 and 1936 from <a href="/wiki/Annet,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Annet, Isles of Scilly">Annett</a>, even though it was a bird sanctuary.<sup id="cite_ref-Robinson_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Robinson-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Newspapers recorded some of the fish caught but there was little recording of other groups of animals.\n</p><p>It was in the 20th century when regular accounts can be found for other animal groups following visits from naturalists who often published their observations in the scientific literature. For example, <a href="/wiki/W._S._Bristowe" title="W. S. Bristowe">William Bristow</a> visited the islands on three occasions from 1927\xe2\x80\x931934, recording spiders including on some of the uninhabited islands.<sup id="cite_ref-parslow_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parslow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Cornwall Bird Watching and Preservation Society published bird reports from the 1930s onwards and the Isles of Scilly Bird Group (founded 2000) took over publishing their own annual reports \xe2\x80\x93 <i>Isles of Scilly Bird and Natural History Review</i> \xe2\x80\x93 which included other groups of animals such as the <a href="/wiki/Diptera" class="mw-redirect" title="Diptera">diptera</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Planarian_.28flatworms.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Planarian_(flatworms)"><a href="/wiki/Planarian" title="Planarian">Planarian</a> (flatworms)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fauna_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Planarian (flatworms)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Fourteen species of terrestrial flatworms have been recorded in Britain and Ireland with five found on the Isles of Scilly. Only three or four of the fourteen species are native with two recorded on Tresco.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The Australian flatworm (<i><a href="/wiki/Australoplana_sanguinea" class="mw-redirect" title="Australoplana sanguinea">Australoplana sanguinea</a></i>) \xe2\x80\x93 a flatworm from Australia and New Zealand and first found on Tresco in 1960. Now found in many parts of Britain. Feeds on earthworms.</li>\n<li><i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australopacifica_coxii&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Australopacifica coxii (page does not exist)">Australopacifica coxii</a></i> \xe2\x80\x93 a flatworm from Australia and found on Tresco in 1975. Feeds on earthworms.</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Kontikia_andersoni" class="mw-redirect" title="Kontikia andersoni">Kontikia andersoni</a></i> \xe2\x80\x93 found on Tresco in 1984; it is native to Australia and/or New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Microplana_terrestris" title="Microplana terrestris">Microplana terrestris</a></i> \xe2\x80\x93 recorded on Tresco (1982). Native</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Microplana_scharffi" class="mw-redirect" title="Microplana scharffi">Microplana scharffi</a></i> \xe2\x80\x93 recorded on Tresco (1984) and St Mary\'s (1985). Native</li></ul>\n<h2><span id="Opiliones_.28harvestmen.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Opiliones_(harvestmen)"><a href="/wiki/Opiliones" title="Opiliones">Opiliones</a> (harvestmen)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fauna_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit Innisvouls.<sup id="cite_ref-&quot;wsb&quot;29_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-&quot;wsb&quot;29-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Leiobunum_rotundum" title="Leiobunum href="/wiki/Phalangium_opilio" title="Phalangium opilio">Phalangium opilio</a></i> \xe2\x80\x93 found on St Martin\'s, St Mary\'s, Samson and Tresco.<sup id="cite_ref-&quot;wsb&quot;29_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-&quot;wsb&quot;29-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Odonata"><a href="/wiki/Odonata" title="Odonata">Odonata</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fauna_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Odonata">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>The Atlas of the Dragonflies of Britain and Ireland</i> published in 1996 listed just three species; blue-tailed damselfly, common darter (both breeding residents) and the migrant hawker.
