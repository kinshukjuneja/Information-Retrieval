(<b>LCBP</b>), also known as the <b>Colombian Winter League</b>, is a professional baseball league based in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>. class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colombian_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Colombian Professional Baseball League is commonly divided into three eras: from 1948 to 1958, from 1979 to 1988, and from 1993 to the present.  In the middle of 2004 the Colombian Professional Baseball League was accepted in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribbean_Confederation_of_Professional_Baseball&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caribbean Confederation of Professional Baseball (page does not exist)">Caribbean Confederation of Professional Baseball</a>, although for the moment they will not be allowed to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Series" title="Caribbean Series">Caribbean Series</a> until the level of play and the quality of baseball facilities have been deemed to have improved.<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20100208&amp;content_id=8036314&amp;vkey=news_mlb&amp;fext=.jsp&amp;c_id=mlb">[1]</a>  The Colombian League reportedly intends to continue pursuing participation in the Caribbean Series in 2012.  \n</p><p>The league added two teams for the 2010\xe2\x80\x9311 season: the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Potros_de_Medell%C3%ADn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Potros de Medell\xc3\xadn (page does not exist)">Potros de Medell\xc3\xadn</a> (Medell\xc3\xadn Colts), based in <a href="/wiki/Medell%C3%ADn" title="Medell\xc3\xadn">Medell\xc3\xadn</a>, Colombia, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%81guilas_de_Bogot%C3%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="\xc3\x81guilas de Bogot\xc3\xa1 (page does not exist)">\xc3\x81guilas de Bogot\xc3\xa1</a> (Bogot\xc3\xa1 Eagles), based in <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogot\xc3\xa1">Bogot\xc3\xa1</a>, Colombia.  Additionally, the Toros (Bulls) moved from <a href="/wiki/Sincelejo" title="Sincelejo">Sincelejo</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cali" title="Cali">Cali</a> for economic reasons.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a the 2009 season.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership">Ownership</span><span class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In addition, players such as former <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a league has six teams around the country. The season is played from October to January.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The top four teams at the end of the regular season, a first round robin phase of 50 games per team, advance to another round-robin (12 games for every team) with the two best teams contesting a best-of-seven final series to determine the league champion.<sup
