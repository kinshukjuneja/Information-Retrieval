class="mw-parser-output"><p>Lieutenant-Colonel <b>John Lambrick Vivian</b> (1830\xe2\x80\x931896<sup id="cite_ref-genealogy.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genealogy.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) Inspector of Militia and Her Majesty\'s Superintendent of Police and Police Magistrate for <a href="/wiki/St_Kitts" class="mw-redirect" title="St Kitts">St Kitts</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, was a genealogist and historian who edited editions of the <a href="/wiki/Heraldic_Visitation" class="mw-redirect" title="Heraldic Visitation">Heraldic Visitations</a> of Devon and of Cornwall,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> standard reference works for historians of these two counties. Both works contain an extensive pedigree of the Vivian family of Devon and Cornwall (see <a href="/wiki/Vivian_family_(of_Trewan_Hall)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vivian family (of Trewan Hall)">Vivian family</a>), produced largely as a result of his own researches.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He was the only son of John Vivian (1791\xe2\x80\x931872) of Rosehill, <a href="/wiki/Camborne" title="Camborne">Camborne</a>, Cornwall, by his wife Mary Lambrick (1794\xe2\x80\x931872), eldest daughter of John Lambrick (1762\xe2\x80\x931798) of Erisey, Ruan Major, and co-heiress of her infant brother John Lambrick (1798\xe2\x80\x931799).<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His maternal grandmother was Mary Hammill, eldest daughter of Peter Hammill (d. 1799) of Trelissick in Sithney, Cornwall, the ancestry of which family he traced back to the holders of the 13th century French title <a href="/w/index.php?title=Comte_de_Hamel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Comte de Hamel (page does not exist)">Comte de Hamel</a>. He stated himself to be the senior representative of the family of Hamel.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Visitations_of_Cornwall">Visitations of Cornwall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Visitations of Cornwall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1874_edition">1874 edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1874 edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<ul><li><cite class="citation book">Vivian, J. It contains not only the genealogical data from the Heraldic Visitations stated, but also a great deal of further research and annotations by Vivian, much taken from parish registers and published family histories. Frequently he extends pedigrees back as far as the Norman Conquest and forward to his own times. He also notes where church monuments exist for certain individuals, using the letters "MI".</li></ul></li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Visitations_of_Devon">Visitations of Devon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Visitations of Devon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1895_edition">1895 edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1895 edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<ul><li>Vivian, Lt.Col. J. L., (Ed.) <i>The Visitations of the County of Devon: Comprising the Heralds\' Visitations of 1531, 1564 &amp; 1620</i>, Exeter, 1895.\n<ul><li>This is a work on the lines of his 1887 work on Cornwall and contains a huge amount of additional annotation derived from Vivian\'s own researches. Much of the information from the 1887 Cornwall work is repeated, due to the extensive inter-marriages of the gentry families of Devon and Cornwall, which can be explained due to the geographic isolation of these two adjoining counties on the south western peninsula of England. Several prominent Devon families had their origins in Cornwall and vice versa. The pedigree for the Vivian family, in all its branches, occupies pages 747 to 763 of his 1895 work, surpassing in volume even that of the great Devon family of Courtenay, extending to only 10 pages.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He commences the pedigree of Vivian with the entry "Henry III, King of England", whose descendants  actually play no role in the family\'s pedigree until the 16th century.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_works">Other works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li><i>Collection of Wills from Devon &amp; Cornwall</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-genealogy.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genealogy.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Residence">Residence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Residence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>His residence in 1882 was 7 Clifton Road, Camden Square, London NW.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sources">Sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lambrick_Vivian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Vivian, Lt.Col.
