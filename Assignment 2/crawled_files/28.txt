id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Jim Crane</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>James Robert Crane</b> (born January 17, 1954) is a <a href="/wiki/Businessman" class="mw-redirect" title="Businessman">businessman</a> from <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.  Crane is chairman and chief executive of Crane Capital Group, the majority shareholder of <a href="/wiki/Champion_Energy" title="Champion Energy">Champion Energy</a>, Crane Worldwide Logistics, and Crane Freight &amp; Shipping.  He is also the owner and chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Early_life_and_education"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Early life and education</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Crane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Crane, who grew up in the north St. Louis suburb of <a href="/wiki/Dellwood,_Missouri" title="Dellwood, Missouri">Dellwood, Missouri</a>, attended <a href="/wiki/Lutheran_High_School_North_(Missouri)" title="Lutheran High School North (Missouri)">Lutheran High School North</a> and graduated in 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-HollinshedD-2004-09-23_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HollinshedD-2004-09-23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  He gave the school a donation of $1 million to improve their athletic facilities and enlarge the size of the campus from 40 acres to 67 in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-HollinshedD-2004-09-23_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HollinshedD-2004-09-23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The school named the James R. Crane Athletic Complex for his contribution.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Crane earned a degree in <a href="/wiki/Industrial_safety" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial safety">industrial safety</a> from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Missouri" title="University of Central Missouri">Central Missouri State University</a><sup id="cite_ref-WGP-10K-2011_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WGP-10K-2011-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (now the University of Central Missouri) in 1976, where he became a member of the fraternity, <a href="/wiki/Tau_Kappa_Epsilon" title="Tau Kappa Epsilon">Tau Kappa Epsilon</a>. He pitched for the <a href="/wiki/Central_Missouri_Mules_and_Jennies" title="Central Missouri Mules and Jennies">Central Missouri Mules baseball</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  Crane\'s father died between his freshman and sophomore years, and he seriously thought about dropping out of college; however, his coach Robert N. Tompkins drove to his home in north St. Louis and talked Crane into returning to school.<sup id="cite_ref-UCMO-Crane-2006_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UCMO-Crane-2006-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1998 Crane was the largest donor in a $1.2 million upgrade of the CMSU baseball stadium, which was then named James R. Crane Stadium.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Crane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After graduating from college he followed his father and went into the insurance business.  He moved to Houston in 1980, and in March 1984 Crane borrowed $10,000 from his sister and founded Eagle USA Airfreight, an air freight logistics business.<sup id="cite_ref-UCMO-Crane-2006_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UCMO-Crane-2006-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WGP-S1-2008-01-23_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WGP-S1-2008-01-23-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2000, Eagle USA was subject of an <a href="/wiki/Equal_Employment_Opportunity_Commission" title="Equal Employment Opportunity Commission">Equal Employment Opportunity Commission</a> investigation regarding hiring practices of minorities and women.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Eagle USA Airfreight later became Eagle Global Logistics, and then EGL, Inc., and moved into the supply chain management and information services business.  In August 2007 EGL merged with United Kingdom-based <a href="/wiki/CEVA_Logistics" title="CEVA Logistics">CEVA Logistics</a>, owned by American private equity capital management and leveraged buyout firm <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Apollo Management">Apollo Management L.P.</a>.  Crane owned approximately 17.6% of EGL\'s stock at the time of the merger.  He had wanted to do a leveraged, management, or combination of the two, buyout of EGL, prior to the merger with CEVA, but was unable to obtain financing on acceptable terms.  Later, after he obtained equity backing he initially offered $36 per-share for EGL, but Apollo then engaged in a bidding war and the shareholders eventually went with the Apollo/CEVA offer of $47.50 per share.  He was opposed to the EGL and CEVA merger.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Crane took his profits from EGL and formed Crane Capital Group.  He also became a director and shareholder (less than 1%) in Western Gas Holdings, LLC, the general partner in Western Gas Partners, LP, a mid-stream (mainly pipelines and natural gas gathering assets) energy company operating in the southeastern Rocky Mountain region and southern Mid-Continent area (Oklahoma and Texas) of the United States.  Western Gas Holdings and Western Gas Partners are majority owned by <a href="/wiki/Anadarko_Petroleum_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Anadarko Petroleum Corporation">Anadarko Petroleum Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WGP-S1-2008-01-23_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WGP-S1-2008-01-23-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Crane is also the majority shareholder in Crane Worldwide Logistics LLC.,<sup id="cite_ref-WGP-10K-2011_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WGP-10K-2011-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> director of Fort Dearborn Life Insurance Company (a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Health_Care_Service_Corporation" title="Health Care Service Corporation">Health Care Service Corporation</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-WGP-10K-2011_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WGP-10K-2011-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and a director and executive officer in <a href="/wiki/Champion_Energy" title="Champion Energy">Champion Energy</a> Holdings LLC.\n</p><p>A scratch golfer, Crane has been ranked as the nation\'s top CEO golfer. In April 2010 he purchased <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Floridian_National_Golf_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Floridian National Golf Club (page does not exist)">The Floridian National Golf Club</a> from <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Huizenga" title="Wayne Huizenga">Wayne Huizenga</a>. The Floridian in Palm City, Florida recently completed major renovations and additions under the supervision of architect <a href="/wiki/Tom_Fazio" title="Tom Fazio">Tom Fazio</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Crane reached an agreement to purchase the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> from <a href="/wiki/Drayton_McLane" title="Drayton McLane">Drayton McLane</a> for a reported $680 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He was unanimously approved by MLB on November 17, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> MLB Commissioner <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a> intended for Houston to move from the 6-club <a href="/wiki/National_League_Central" title="National League Central">National League Central</a> to the 4-club <a href="/wiki/American_League_West" title="American League West">American League West</a>. Crane received $35 million bumps from McLane and the MLB in exchange for this league-change stipulation, which McLane had previously committed to.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>"It was two things (owning a team and golf course) that I never thought I would be able to accomplish," Crane mused at his Floridian National Golf Club\'s reopening. "I was able to pull it together and I am very happy about it."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Crane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Crane and his ex-wife, Franci Neely Crane, are financial supporters of <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Fine_Arts,_Houston" title="Museum of Fine Arts, Houston">The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>For her 60th birthday party Franci was serenaded by the rock-pop band <a href="/wiki/The_B-52%27s" title="The B-52&#39;s">The B-52\'s</a> at the Crane\'s summer home in <a href="/wiki/Nantucket,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Nantucket, Massachusetts">Nantucket, Massachusetts</a> in July 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a> weekend in 2017, Crane married Whitney Wheeler. The lavish ceremony took place at Crane\'s Floridian National Golf Club. This is Crane\'s third marriage. Crane and Wheeler have a son together, James Robert Crane II.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Crane is a supporter of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a> and frequently golfs with former U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Crane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation news">McTaggart, Brian (November 22, 2011). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://houston.astros.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20111122&amp;content_id=26027000&amp;vkey=news_hou&amp;c_id=hou">"Crane group officially takes control of Astros: Ownership transfer from McLane completed on Tuesday"</a>. <i>MLB.com</i>.
