<b>Philadelphia White Stockings</b> were an early professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team.  They were a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Professional Base Ball Players">National Association</a> from 1873 to 1875.  Their home games were played at the <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Street_Grounds" title="Jefferson Street Grounds">Jefferson Street Grounds</a>.  They were managed by <a href="/wiki/Fergy_Malone" title="Fergy Malone">Fergy Malone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wood" title="Jimmy Wood">Jimmy Wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Craver" title="Bill Craver">Bill Craver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_McGeary" title="Mike McGeary">Mike McGeary</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Addy" title="Bob Addy">Bob Addy</a>.\n</p><p>During their three-year existence the White Stockings won 102 games and lost 77 for a <a href="/wiki/Winning_percentage" title="Winning percentage">winning percentage</a> of .570.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
