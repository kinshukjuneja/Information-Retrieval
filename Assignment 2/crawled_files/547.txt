The Western Rocks are the archipelago in the lower left of this image.</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Western Rocks</b> are a group of uninhabited islands and rocks in the south\xe2\x80\x93western part of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and are renowned for the numerous <a href="/wiki/Shipwrecks" class="mw-redirect" title="Shipwrecks">shipwrecks</a> in the area and the nearby <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Rock,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Bishop Rock, Isles of Scilly">Bishop Rock lighthouse</a>. In 1971, the rocks and islands were designated a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> for their breeding sea birds.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Landing on the islands is both difficult and discouraged and there are few published records of visits by naturalists.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_2-0" class="reference"><a reserve</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Rocks,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nature reserve">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The islands are largely managed as <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserves" class="mw-redirect" title="Nature reserves">nature reserves</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust" title="Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a>, principally for breeding seabirds and grey seals (<i><a href="/wiki/Halichoerus_grypus" class="mw-redirect" title="Halichoerus grypus">Halichoerus grypus</a></i>). There are eleven species of breeding seabirds with the European shag (<i><a href="/wiki/Phalacrocorax_aristotelis" class="mw-redirect" title="Phalacrocorax aristotelis">Phalacrocorax aristotelis</a></i>) and European storm petrel (<i><a href="/wiki/Hydrobates_pelagicus" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydrobates pelagicus">Hydrobates pelagicus</a></i>) of national importance. The other species are kittiwake (<i><a href="/wiki/Rissa_tridactyla" class="mw-redirect" title="Rissa tridactyla">Rissa tridactyla</a></i>), razorbill (<i><a href="/wiki/Alca_torda" class="mw-redirect" title="Alca torda">Alca torda</a></i>), guillemot (<i><a href="/wiki/Uria_aalge" class="mw-redirect" title="Uria aalge">Uria aalge</a></i>), cormorant (<i><a href="/wiki/Phalacrocorax_carbo" class="mw-redirect" title="Phalacrocorax carbo">Phalacrocorax carbo</a></i>), fulmar (<i><a href="/wiki/Fulmarus_glacialis" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulmarus glacialis">Fulmarus glacialis</a></i>), puffin (<i><a href="/wiki/Fratercula_arctica" class="mw-redirect" title="Fratercula arctica">Fratercula arctica</a></i>), great black\xe2\x80\x93backed gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_marinus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus marinus">Larus marinus</a></i>), lesser black\xe2\x80\x93backed gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_fuscus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus fuscus">Larus fuscus</a></i>) and herring gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_argentatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus argentatus">Larus argentatus</a></i>). \n</p><p>The only breeding sites for European storm petrel in England are on the Isles of Scilly with eleven colonies and an estimated 1475 occupied sites (i.e.  breeding pairs). Melledgan did have the third largest colony on Scilly with 140 occupied sites recorded during the Seabird 2000 survey declining to 69 occupied sites in a repeat survey in 2006. It was replaced by Roseveor in 2006 with 129 breeding pairs. Other Western Rock colonies are on Gorregan and Rosevean.<sup id="cite_ref-Seabird2000_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seabird2000-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Rocks,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>All of the Western Rocks are composed of <a href="/wiki/Variscan_orogeny" title="Variscan orogeny">Hercynian</a> <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> of late <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Carboniferous</a> age with thin <a href="/wiki/Podzolic" class="mw-redirect" title="Podzolic">podzolic</a> soils on the larger ones. They are located south-west of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Annet,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Annet, Isles of Scilly">Annet</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._Agnes,_Isles_of_Scilly" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Agnes, Isles of Scilly">St Agnes</a> immediately to the north-east and the Bishop Rock to the west. The larger islands in this <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a> are, from north to south: Great Crebwethan, <a href="/wiki/Rosevear,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Rosevear, Isles of Scilly">Rosevear</a>, Rosevean, Daisy and <a href="/wiki/Pednathise_Head" title="Pednathise Head">Pednathise Head</a> (the most southerly point of the United Kingdom). Rosevear Ledge and the Gilstone Reef are just to the west of this group. Smaller islands within the Western Rocks are: Codnors Rocks, Crebwethan, Jolly Rock, Jacky\'s Rock, Silver Carn, Gorregan and Melledgan.<sup id="cite_ref-OS_4-0" class="reference"><a The island was used as a base camp, during 1709 and 1710, for the Herbert salvage expedition which worked the wrecks of the <a href="/wiki/HMS_Association_(1697)" class="mw-redirect" title="HMS Association (1697)"><i>Association</i></a> and other vessels wrecked in <a href="/wiki/Scilly_naval_disaster_of_1707" title="Scilly naval disaster of 1707">1707</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the 1840s and 1850s it was again inhabited by workmen, this time building the Bishop Rock lighthouse.\n</p><p>The island has replaced Melledgan as the site of the third largest colony of European storm petrel in England with 57 occupied sites recorded during the Seabird 2000 survey increasing to 129 occupied sites in the repeat survey in 2006. The colony of shag on Rosevear and the rest of the Western Rocks is of national importance.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a 1.5x, href="/wiki/Bishop_Rock,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Bishop Rock, Isles of Scilly">Bishop Rock lighthouse</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Unlike the rest of the islands there is no evidence of any human occupation on the Western Rocks, apart from the temporary worksite on Rosevear in the mid\xe2\x80\x93nineteenth century.<sup id="cite_ref-Thomas_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thomas-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Navigation, especially in cloudy weather, was often an informed guess based on assumed speed and direction. With a poor understanding of the sea current, in the western approaches, which pushed sailing ships further north than expected, and the problem of pre\xe2\x80\x931750 charts showing the rocks a further ten miles north than they actually are, it is not surprising that throughout history the Western Rocks have witnessed a great number of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_shipwrecks_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly" title="List of shipwrecks of the Isles of Scilly">shipwrecks recorded in the Isles of Scilly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Reid_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reid-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Particularly the Gilstone Reef<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> has been the cause of many <a href="/wiki/Maritime_disasters" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime disasters">maritime disasters</a>, most notably the sinking of <a href="/wiki/Cloudesley_Shovell" title="Cloudesley Shovell">Admiral Sir Cloudesley Shovell\'s</a> flagship <i><a href="/wiki/HMS_Association_(1697)" class="mw-redirect" title="HMS Association (1697)">HMS Association</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Scilly_naval_disaster_of_1707" title="Scilly naval disaster of 1707">naval disaster of 1707</a> with the loss of over 1400 lives. \n</p><p>Seventy\xe2\x80\x93seven years later, in 1784, the <a href="/wiki/Packet_ship" class="mw-redirect" title="Packet ship">packet ship</a> <a href="/wiki/Nancy_(1774_EIC_ship)" title="Nancy (1774 EIC ship)"><i>Nancy</i></a> hit the Gilstone. Some of the crew and passengers took to a small boat which was dashed on to Rosevear killing all those aboard. A total of 36 crew, 12 passengers, and 1 prisoner drowned including the infamous actress <a href="/wiki/Ann_Cargill" title="Ann Cargill">Ann Cargill</a>. She was returning to England from India and her body was found clinging to a young child and they were buried on Rosevear along with other bodies found. She was later interred in the <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">churchyard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a> on St Mary\'s. \n</p><p>In 1841 the captain of a paddle steamer the <i><a href="/wiki/SS_Thames" title="SS Thames">SS <i>Thames</i></a></i> mistook the lighthouse on St Agnes for the <a href="/wiki/Longships" title="Longships">Longships</a>, changed course to north and steamed onto the Cribewidden Rocks with the loss of 62 of the 66 passengers and crew.<sup id="cite_ref-Wreck_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wreck-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  The second known heaviest loss of life occurred in 1875 when the German liner the <a href="/wiki/SS_Schiller" title="SS Schiller">SS <i>Schiller</i></a> hit the Retarrier Ledges with the loss of 335 lives. Her captain knew she was near the Bishop Light but didn\'t realise he was within the Western Rocks.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The various wrecks are popular diving sites for both commercial and amateur divers with searches made to identify and record the wrecks.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a
