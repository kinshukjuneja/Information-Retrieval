<span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span id="Landscape.2C_flora_and_fauna"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Landscape,_flora_and_fauna">Landscape, flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Landscape, flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Cannock Chase is located between <a href="/wiki/Cannock" title="Cannock">Cannock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lichfield" title="Lichfield">Lichfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugeley" title="Rugeley">Rugeley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stafford" title="Stafford">Stafford</a>. It comprises a mixture of natural deciduous woodland, coniferous plantations, open heathland and the remains of early industry, such as <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal mining</a>. The landscape owes much to the underlying <a href="/wiki/Triassic" title="Triassic">Triassic</a> <a href="/wiki/Bunter_(geology)" title="Bunter (geology)">bunter</a> formations. Cannock Chase was designated as an <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB) on 16 September 1958 and is the smallest area so designated in mainland <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>, covering 68&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (26&#160;sq&#160;mi). Much of the area is also designated as a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI). Despite being relatively small in area, the chase provides a remarkable range of landscape and wildlife, including a herd of around 800 <a href="/wiki/Fallow_deer" title="Fallow deer">fallow deer</a> and a number of rare and endangered birds, including <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> <a href="/wiki/European_nightjar" title="European nightjar">nightjars</a>. A feeding station at the Marquis Drive Visitors\' Centre, sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/West_Midland_Bird_Club" title="West Midland Bird Club">West Midland Bird Club</a>, attracts many species, including <a href="/wiki/Brambling" title="Brambling">brambling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yellowhammer" title="Yellowhammer">yellowhammer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_bullfinch" title="Eurasian bullfinch">bullfinch</a>. \n</p><p>Efforts are<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2009)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> underway to increase the amount of <a href="/wiki/Heathland" class="mw-redirect" title="Heathland">heathland</a> on the chase, reintroducing shrubs such as <a href="/wiki/Calluna" title="Calluna">heather</a> in some areas where <a href="/wiki/Bracken" title="Bracken">bracken</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> forest have crowded out most other plants. The local flora also includes several species of <a href="/wiki/Vaccinium" title="Vaccinium">Vaccinium</a>, including the eponymous Cannock Chase berry (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vaccinium_%C3%97intermedium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vaccinium \xc3\x97intermedium (page does not exist)">Vaccinium \xc3\x97intermedium</a></i> Ruthe).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In January 2009, an outbreak of the plant pathogen <i><a href="/wiki/Phytophthora_ramorum" title="Phytophthora ramorum">Phytophthora ramorum</a></i> was discovered on the chase, at Brocton Coppice. Various restrictions were put in place in an attempt to prevent its spread.<sup id="cite_ref-WMBC-SOD_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMBC-SOD-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visitor_sites_and_landmarks">Visitor sites and landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Visitor sites and landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg/200px-Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg" width="200" height="138" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg/300px-Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg/400px-Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3095" data-file-height="2136" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Katyn_Memorial_Cannock.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Katyn Memorial at Cannock Chase.</div></div></div>\n<p>There are a number of visitor centres, museums and <a href="/wiki/Waymarking" class="mw-redirect" title="Waymarking">waymarked</a> paths, including the <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_England_Way" title="Heart of England Way">Heart of England Way</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire_Way" title="Staffordshire Way">Staffordshire Way</a>. There are also accessible trails to enable people to experience the health benefits of Cannock Chase, such as The Route to Health.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, there are many unmarked public paths. On the Chase\'s north-eastern edge can be found <a href="/wiki/Shugborough_Hall" title="Shugborough Hall">Shugborough Hall</a>, ancestral home of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Lichfield" title="Earl of Lichfield">Earls of Lichfield</a>. At its southern edge are the remains of <a href="/wiki/Castle_Ring" title="Castle Ring">Castle Ring</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> <a href="/wiki/Hill_fort" class="mw-redirect" title="Hill fort">hill fort</a>, which is the highest point on the Chase. Several <a href="/wiki/Glacial_erratic" title="Glacial erratic">glacial erratic</a> boulders are also found on the Chase, remnants of <a href="/wiki/Glaciation" class="mw-redirect" title="Glaciation">glaciation</a>. One is mounted on a plinth.\n</p><p>The Chase has several <a href="/wiki/War_memorial" title="War memorial">war memorials</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Cannock_Chase_German_war_cemetery" class="mw-redirect" title="Cannock Chase German war cemetery">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_War_Graves_Commission" title="Commonwealth War Graves Commission">Commonwealth</a> war cemeteries. A memorial to the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Katyn_massacre" title="Katyn massacre">Katyn massacre</a> was unveiled by Stefan Staniszewski, whose father Hillary Zygmunt Staniszewski (a high court judge) died in the massacre. Preserved below the memorial are phials of soil from both Warsaw and the Katyn forest. Freda, the Harlequin <a href="/wiki/Great_Dane" title="Great Dane">Great Dane</a> mascot of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Rifle_Brigade_(Earl_of_Liverpool%27s_Own)" title="New Zealand Rifle Brigade (Earl of Liverpool&#39;s Own)">New Zealand Rifle Brigade (Earl of Liverpool\'s Own)</a> is also buried on the chase marked with a memorial marble headstone.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountain_biking">Mountain biking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mountain biking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Chase is popular with cross-country mountain bike users. The purpose-built XC \'Follow the Dog\' trail<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is an 11&#160;km (6.8&#160;mi) technically-challenging route, opened in 2005, starting and finishing at the Birches Valley Visitors/Cycle Centre. It is open to all; however, it is not recommended for beginners. A new section of XC trail was opened in April 2010. The \'Monkey Trail\' (11.2&#160;km (7.0&#160;mi)) is a more technical trail that splits from \'Follow the Dog\' at about the halfway point, then rejoins slightly further on. There are several features mountain bikers can look out for when riding on the chase, such as Kitbag Hill, Rabbit Hill, Quagmire Bridge, Roots Hall and Brocton Shorts to name a few.\n</p><p>Increasing popularity of the MTB trails led National Rail to install a cycle bridge in 2013 at Moors Gorse to replace the previous pedestrian level crossing where multiple near misses indicated a high risk to cyclists.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment">Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Since 2006, the forest has been used as an open-air music venue as part of the <a href="/wiki/Forestry_Commission" title="Forestry Commission">Forestry Commission</a> nationwide <a href="/wiki/Forest_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Forest Tour">Forest Tour</a>, with acts such as <a href="/wiki/The_Zutons" title="The Zutons">The Zutons</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Feeling" title="The Feeling">The Feeling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Status_Quo_(band)" title="Status Quo (band)">Status Quo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jools_Holland" title="Jools Holland">Jools Holland</a> playing in a forest clearing.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cannock_Chase_murders">Cannock Chase murders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cannock Chase murders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The area gained notoriety in the late 1960s when the <a href="/wiki/Cannock_Chase_murders" title="Cannock Chase murders">Cannock Chase murders</a> made national headlines; the remains of three young girls were found buried on the Chase after going missing from areas along the <a href="/wiki/A34_road" title="A34 road">A34 road</a> between there and <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>. Raymond Leslie Morris, a motor engineer from <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a>, was found guilty at <a href="/wiki/Stafford" title="Stafford">Stafford</a> assizes of one of the murders in 1968 and was sentenced to <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a>. He died in prison in March 2014, aged 84, after serving 45 years.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Model_WW_I_battlefield">Model WW I battlefield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Model WW I battlefield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In September 2013 <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire_County_Council" title="Staffordshire County Council">Staffordshire County Council</a> allowed a team of local archaeologists and volunteers to excavate the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> model battlefield near <a href="/wiki/Brocton,_Staffordshire" title="Brocton, Staffordshire">Brocton, Staffordshire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which had been constructed by German <a href="/wiki/Prisoners_of_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Prisoners of war">prisoners of war</a> held in a camp on nearby Cannock Chase and guarded by soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Rifle_Brigade_(Earl_of_Liverpool%27s_Own)" title="New Zealand Rifle Brigade (Earl of Liverpool&#39;s Own)">New Zealand Rifle Brigade (Earl of Liverpool\'s Own)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The model of the village and surrounding area of <a href="/wiki/Mesen" title="Mesen">Messines</a> in Belgium, which included replica trenches and dugouts, railway lines, roads, and accurate contours of the surrounding terrain, was open to public view for a few weeks before being buried over again to ensure its preservation.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The excavation revealed many new details of the well-preserved 40 metre square battlefield.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> "Staffordshire County Council will be using laser-scanning technology to re-create the site as a 3D interactive model that can be explored online." <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cannock_Chase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Since the nineteenth century, sightings of <a href="/wiki/Black_dog_(ghost)" title="Black dog (ghost)">Black Dogs</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">Werewolves</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/British_big_cats" title="British big cats">British big cats</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_flying_object" title="Unidentified flying object">UFOs</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Black-eyed_children" title="Black-eyed children">black-eyed children</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and even <a href="/wiki/Bigfoot" title="Bigfoot">Bigfoot</a> have appeared in the local press. However no conclusive evidence has ever been produced verifying these claims, and they  may best be thought of as forming part of local <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a>.\n</p><p>The 1972 <a href="/wiki/Labi_Siffre" title="Labi Siffre">Labi Siffre</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Crying_Laughing_Loving_Lying" title="Crying Laughing Loving Lying">Crying Laughing Loving Lying</a></i> features a track entitled "Cannock Chase".\n</p><p>Cannock Chase  has also achieved national notoriety for its association with the sexual practice of <a href="/wiki/Dogging_(sexual_slang)" title="Dogging (sexual slang)">dogging</a>. This occurred in March 2004 when  the ex-England footballer <a href="/wiki/Stan_Collymore" title="Stan Collymore">Stan Collymore</a> was revealed in a  <a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">News of the World</a>  investigation, to have regularly engaged in the activity   at a car park near Anson\'s Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Cannock Chase also has a darker side associated with it. In 2015 a woman came out to state she had been sexually abused from the age of 6 by senior politicians and other powerful members of society, often referred to as "The Elite". She claims this took place in the woodlands of Cannock Chase, as well as other locations in the midlands.
