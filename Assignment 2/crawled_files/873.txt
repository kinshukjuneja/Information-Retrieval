Camel</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>River Allen</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Dowr Lehen</b></i>, meaning <i>slate river</i>) in north Cornwall is one of two rivers of the same name in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> which share this name. In this case the name is the result of a mistake made in 1888 by Ordnance Survey, replacing the name <i>Layne</i> with <i>Allen</i> which is the old name for the lower reaches of the Camel.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The other <a href="/wiki/River_Allen_(Truro)" title="River Allen (Truro)">River Allen</a> runs through Truro.\n</p><p>The River Allen is a major tributary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Camel" title="River Camel">River Camel</a>. It springs northeast of <a href="/wiki/Camelford" title="Camelford">Camelford</a> and flows south-southwest through the Allen Valley passing <a href="/wiki/St_Teath" title="St Teath">St Teath</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Kew_Highway" class="mw-redirect" title="St Kew Highway">St Kew Highway</a> to join the Camel near <a href="/wiki/Sladesbridge" title="Sladesbridge">Sladesbridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Allen,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Weatherhill, Craig.
