The team was also known as the <b>Washington Statesmen</b> and the <b>Washington Nationals</b>. The team played at <a href="/wiki/Boundary_Field" title="Boundary Field">Boundary Field</a>.\n</p><p>The team started out in the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">American Association</a> as the Washington Statesmen in 1891. The American Association folded after that season, and the team was purchased by <a href="/wiki/J._Earl_Wagner" title="J. Earl Wagner">J. Earl Wagner</a>, who would own the team for the remainder of its existence. The Statesmen moved to the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> for the 1892 season, becoming the Senators. When the NL contracted from twelve teams to eight after the 1899 season, the Senators were one of the teams eliminated.\n</p><p>The Senators did not fare well in their nine years as a franchise, which might have been the reason they were contracted. Washington never had a winning season and compiled a winning percentage of 0.366. Among their more famous players were <a href="/wiki/Deacon_McGuire" title="Deacon McGuire">Deacon McGuire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Hall of Famer</a> <a href="/wiki/Jim_O%27Rourke_(baseball)" title="Jim O&#39;Rourke (baseball)">Jim O\'Rourke</a>.\n</p><p>After a one-year hiatus, the Senators returned, but they were no longer the same franchise that played at Boundary Field. In fact the Original Senators were the first of three teams, all called the Washington Senators, and were in the Capital continuously until the third Senators franchise left to become the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a>. The second had left the city in 1960 becoming the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> and were followed immediately by a new expansion team of the same name, ultimately leaving for Texas in 1971. Baseball returned to the Capital in 2005 when the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> became the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a>. The "Washington Senators" name was still owned by the Texas Rangers, so organizers sought other options. Washington, D.C., mayor <a href="/wiki/Anthony_A._Williams" title="Anthony A. Williams">Anthony A. Williams</a> supported the name "Washington Grays," in honor of the Negro-league team the <a href="/wiki/Homestead_Grays" title="Homestead Grays">Homestead Grays</a> (1929-1950), which had been based in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, but played many of their home games in Washington. In the end, the team owners chose the name "Washington Nationals," which had been the official name of the American League\'s Washington Senators from 1905 to 1955.\n</p>\n<h2><span
