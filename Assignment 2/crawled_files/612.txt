class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Writers%27_Association_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The BBWAA was founded on October 14, 1908,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to improve working conditions for <a href="/wiki/Sports_journalism" title="Sports journalism">sportswriters</a> in the early part of the 20th century; It also sought to promote uniformity of scoring methods, and to professionalize the press box, such that access was limited only to working reporters, telegraphers, and others who had a reason to be there. The forty-three founding members of the Baseball Writers Association first met in mid-October 1908.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They included <a href="/wiki/Joe_S._Jackson" title="Joe S. Jackson">Joe S. Jackson</a>, who became the association\'s first president. At that time, Jackson was the sporting editor (today called <i>sports editor</i>) of the <i><a href="/wiki/Detroit_Free_Press" title="Detroit Free Press">Detroit Free Press</a></i>. Also selected as officers were <a href="/wiki/Sy_Sanborn" title="Sy Sanborn">Irving E. Sanborn</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i>, syndicated columnist <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Fullerton" title="Hugh Fullerton">Hugh Fullerton</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Globe" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i> baseball writer <a href="/wiki/Tim_Murnane" title="Tim Murnane">Tim Murnane</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A second meeting was held in New York City in December; Sanborn decided he could not serve as an officer at that time, and he was replaced by William Weart of the <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Ledger_(Philadelphia)#The_Ochs_era" title="Public Ledger (Philadelphia)">Philadelphia Times</a></i>. The slate of officers was ratified, and anyone who wrote about baseball in major league cities was eligible for membership. This policy changed, however, in December 1913, at which time it was decided that minor league baseball writers could also become members.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Then, Jackson became a dominant force in the early years of the baseball writers, being elected as president of the association during nine consecutive terms.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Jackson finally retired in 1919, while Sanborn returned to assume the position of president. After that, Jackson became a member of the BBWAA Board of Directors.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Writers%27_Association_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">organization</a>\'s primary function is to work with <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> and individual <a href="/wiki/Team" title="Team">teams</a> to assure <a href="/wiki/Locker_room" class="mw-redirect" title="Locker room">clubhouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Press_box" title="Press box">press-box</a> access for BBWAA members. In addition, BBWAA members also elect <a href="/wiki/Baseball_positions" title="Baseball positions">players</a> to the <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">National Baseball Hall of Fame</a>, which is the organization\'s most public function. All writers with 10 continuous years of membership in the BBWAA, plus active BBWAA membership at any time in the preceding 10 years, are eligible to vote for the Hall of Fame. The BBWAA also votes annually for the Kenesaw Mountain Landis <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="Major League Baseball Most Valuable Player Award">Most Valuable Player Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a>, Jackie Robinson <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" title="Major League Baseball Rookie of the Year Award">Rookie of the Year Award</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manager_of_the_Year_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Manager of the Year Award">Manager of the Year Award</a> in each of the two major <a href="/wiki/Sports_league" title="Sports league">leagues</a>. The Hall of Fame also empowers the BBWAA\'s Historical Overview Committee, made up of 11 or 12 veteran BBWAA members, to formulate the annual ballot for the <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Committee" title="Veterans Committee">Veterans Committee</a>.\n</p><p>Considering the ready availability of <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting_of_sports_events" title="Broadcasting of sports events">television broadcasts</a> for the majority of baseball games, plus instant access to information through the Internet, some have called into question why the BBWAA has not broadened its membership rules to include <a href="/wiki/Sports_commentator" title="Sports commentator">broadcasters</a> and researchers.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> (Similar arguments were made for the inclusion of Web-based journalists, before the BBWAA added Web writers to its ranks in December 2007.)<sup id="cite_ref-baseballanalysts2007_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baseballanalysts2007-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Others have openly questioned why the BBWAA is involved in the award and Hall of Fame <a href="/wiki/Voting" title="Voting">voting</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bbwaa.com/hof-elec-req/">processes</a> at all,<sup id="cite_ref-chron2007_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chron2007-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> citing in some cases journalistic integrity and the need to remain unbiased in their coverage of newsworthy events.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_voting">Awards voting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Writers%27_Association_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards voting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The BBWAA\'s most public function is to annually vote on candidates for the National Baseball Hall of Fame.\n</p><p>In addition, the BBWAA is responsible for voting on several annual awards in each major league, including:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Kenesaw_Mountain_Landis" title="Kenesaw Mountain Landis">Kenesaw Mountain Landis</a> <a href="/wiki/MLB_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="MLB Most Valuable Player Award">Most Valuable Player Award</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Jackie_Robinson" title="Jackie Robinson">Jackie Robinson</a> <a href="/wiki/MLB_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="MLB Rookie of the Year Award">Rookie of the Year Award</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Manager_of_the_Year_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Manager of the Year Award">Manager of the Year Award</a></li></ul>\n<p>In or about 2000, the BBWAA took over the voting responsibility for the <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Mart%C3%ADnez_Award" title="Edgar Mart\xc3\xadnez Award">Edgar Mart\xc3\xadnez Award</a>, given each year to the outstanding designated hitter in the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a>.\n</p><p>From 1953 to 1962, the BBWAA presented a "Sophomore of the Year Award" in each league.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1997, a 36-member BBWAA panel selected the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Time_Team" title="Major League Baseball All-Time Team">Major League Baseball All-Time Team</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_display">Awards display</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Writers%27_Association_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards display">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Replicas of various BBWAA awards and lists of past winners are displayed at the <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum#Third_floor" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum</a>, in the Records Room, which also has other exhibits, including charts showing active and all-time leaders in various baseball statistical categories.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="J._G._Taylor_Spink_Award">J. G. Taylor Spink Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Writers%27_Association_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: J. G. Taylor Spink Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/J._G._Taylor_Spink_Award" title="J. G. Taylor Spink Award">J. G. Taylor Spink Award</a></div>\n<p>The annual J. G. Taylor Spink Award is the highest award given by the BBWAA. The award is open to both BBWAA members and nonmembers, but only one winner\xe2\x80\x94<a href="/wiki/Roger_Angell" title="Roger Angell">Roger Angell</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i>, the 2014 recipient\xe2\x80\x94had never been a BBWAA member in his career. Despite having written on baseball for more than a half-century, Angell never worked a specific baseball writing beat, thereby making him ineligible for BBWAA membership.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Web_membership">Web membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
