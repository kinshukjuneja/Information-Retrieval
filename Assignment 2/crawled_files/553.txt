expansion</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>1977 Major League Baseball expansion</b> resulted in the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion franchises</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a> began play in the <a href="/wiki/1977_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1977 Major League Baseball season">1977 Major League Baseball season</a>, becoming the 25th and 26th teams in Major League Baseball.\n</p><p>Seattle and Toronto were included in a list of potential expansion cities in a survey conducted by the American League in 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESarasota_Herald-Tribune1960_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESarasota_Herald-Tribune1960-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The others were <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex" title="Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth metroplex">Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oakland" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland">Oakland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a> (all of which received franchises eventually through expansion or relocation except Buffalo).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESarasota_Herald-Tribune1960_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESarasota_Herald-Tribune1960-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Seattle"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Seattle</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Toronto"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Toronto</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Expansion_draft"><span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#Further_reading"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">Further reading</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Seattle">Seattle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1977_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Seattle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Seattle_Mariners" title="History of the Seattle Mariners">History of the Seattle Mariners</a></div>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion" title="1969 Major League Baseball expansion">1969 Major League Baseball expansion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, and Seattle were each granted a franchise; the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Pilots" title="Seattle Pilots">Seattle Pilots</a> were added to the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a> joined the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a>. The Seattle Pilots only played <a href="/wiki/1969_Seattle_Pilots_season" title="1969 Seattle Pilots season">one season</a>, during which they faced financial difficulties owing to no television coverage, a poorly performing team, a stadium with problems, and the highest ticket and <a href="/wiki/Concession_stand" title="Concession stand">concession</a> prices in the league.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Owners of other American League teams wanted <a href="/wiki/Dewey_Soriano" title="Dewey Soriano">Dewey Soriano</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_R._Daley" title="William R. Daley">William R. Daley</a> to sell the team to a Seattle owner who would improve the team and address issues at <a href="/wiki/Sick%27s_Stadium" title="Sick&#39;s Stadium">Sick\'s Stadium</a>, particularly uncovered seats with bad views.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After several attempts to sell the team to a Seattle owner failed, on 1 April 1970 judge Sidney Volinn declared the team officially bankrupt; it was sold to <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a>, who moved the team to <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a> and renamed it the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2006_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2006-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Selig had negotiated a deal for the purchase with Soriano during <a href="/wiki/1969_World_Series#Game_1" title="1969 World Series">Game 1 of the 1969 World Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2006_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2006-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a result of the relocation of the Seattle Pilots to Milwaukee, in 1970 the city of Seattle, <a href="/wiki/King_County,_Washington" title="King County, Washington">King County</a> and the state of <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> sued the American League for <a href="/wiki/Breach_of_contract" title="Breach of contract">breach of contract</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECour1999C16_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECour1999C16-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The 32.5 million dollar lawsuit<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010802_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010802-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> proceeded until 1976, when at trial the American League offered the city a franchise in exchange for the city, county, and state to drop the suit.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECour1999C16_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECour1999C16-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 2 November 1972, King County had broken ground on the <a href="/wiki/Kingdome" title="Kingdome">Kingdome</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMacIntosh2000_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMacIntosh2000-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which would come to be used by the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> for <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> and by the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Seahawks" title="Seattle Seahawks">Seahawks</a> for <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a>.\n</p><p>On 15 January 1976, the expansion franchise was approved, becoming the 13th franchise in the American League. It was owned by Lester Smith and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Kaye" title="Danny Kaye">Danny Kaye</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010802_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010802-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> who paid an expansion fee of <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>6.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJozsa200658_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJozsa200658-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Owing to the history surrounding the franchise, <a href="/wiki/Sports_journalism" title="Sports journalism">sportswriter</a> Emmett Watson of the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Post-Intelligencer" title="Seattle Post-Intelligencer">Seattle Post-Intelligencer</a> joked that the team should be named the <i>Seattle Litigants</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010802_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010802-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Toronto">Toronto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1977_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toronto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="History of the Toronto Blue Jays">History of the Toronto Blue Jays</a></div>\n<p>Toronto had a long history of interest in baseball. Its first professional baseball team was established in 1885, and in 1886 <a href="/wiki/Sunlight_Park" title="Sunlight Park">Sunlight Park</a> was built to host its games. In 1897, <a href="/wiki/Hanlan%27s_Point_Stadium" title="Hanlan&#39;s Point Stadium">Hanlan\'s Point Stadium</a> was built at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Islands" title="Toronto Islands">Toronto Islands</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs_(International_League)" title="Toronto Maple Leafs (International League)">Toronto Maple Leafs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a>, and on 5 September 1914 it was the site of baseball legend <a href="/wiki/Babe_Ruth" title="Babe Ruth">Babe Ruth</a>\'s first professional home run and only minor league home run.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECreamer200598_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECreamer200598-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1926, <a href="/wiki/Maple_Leaf_Stadium" title="Maple Leaf Stadium">Maple Leaf Stadium</a> was built for the Toronto Maple Leafs. The team was successful, sometimes drawing more fans than established Major League Baseball teams.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010844_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010844-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The team was owned by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kent_Cooke" title="Jack Kent Cooke">Jack Kent Cooke</a>, who in the mid-1950s sought municipal financing of a major league baseball park on the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_National_Exhibition" title="Canadian National Exhibition">Canadian National Exhibition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010844_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010844-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was also involved in the failed attempt to establish the <a href="/wiki/Continental_League" title="Continental League">Continental League</a> as a third league in Major League Baseball.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010844_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010844-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Maple Leaf Stadium was demolished in 1968 after the Toronto Maple Leafs were sold to Walter Dilbeck and moved to <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>. (The team moved again, and is now the <a href="/wiki/Pawtucket_Red_Sox" title="Pawtucket Red Sox">Pawtucket Red Sox</a>.)\n</p><p>In the early 1970s, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_City_Council" title="Toronto City Council">Toronto City Council</a> alderman <a href="/wiki/Paul_Godfrey" title="Paul Godfrey">Paul Godfrey</a> proposed a plan for a publicly financed domed stadium in the city, but it was opposed by taxpayers and amateur sports groups.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010844_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010844-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Undeterred, he proposed renovation of  <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_Stadium" title="Exhibition Stadium">Exhibition Stadium</a> to support baseball. The stadium was renovated in anticipation of the city being awarded a Major League Baseball team, costing <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">C$</a>15,000,000.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2011Last_audio_clip_in_the_post,_from_CBC_Radio_in_1977_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2011Last_audio_clip_in_the_post,_from_CBC_Radio_in_1977-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Half of the funds were provided by the municipal government, and the other half from an interest-free loan from the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ontario" title="Government of Ontario">Government of Ontario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010844_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010844-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Its seating capacity was increased to 40,000.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There had been strong interest from several groups and individuals to own a Major League Baseball team in the city. The most prominent was <a href="/wiki/Labatt_Brewing_Company" title="Labatt Brewing Company">Labatt Brewing Company</a>, who wanted to use ownership of a sports team as a means to establish a visible presence in the Toronto market.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010845_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010845-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 9 January 1976, the National Exhibition Company, owners of the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>, established an <a href="/wiki/Agreement_in_principle" title="Agreement in principle">agreement in principle</a> to sell the franchise to a consortium owned by Labatt Brewing Company, Vulcan Assets, and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Imperial_Bank_of_Commerce" title="Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce">Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce</a> for <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">C$</a>13,250,000.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The Giants had failed to repay a <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>500,000 loan from Major League Baseball, and had experienced declining revenues since the relocation of the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Athletics" class="mw-redirect" title="Kansas City Athletics">Kansas City Athletics</a> to <a href="/wiki/Oakland" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland">Oakland</a> in 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Of the sale price, <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>5,250,000 was to be placed in <a href="/wiki/Escrow" title="Escrow">escrow</a> to "meet certain possible obligations with respect to the transaction", especially the lease of <a href="/wiki/Candlestick_Park" title="Candlestick Park">Candlestick Park</a>, which would expire in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The new owners of the Giants, led by <a href="/wiki/Don_McDougall_(baseball)" title="Don McDougall (baseball)">Don McDougall</a>, would move the team to Toronto pending approval from the other eleven <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> teams, which would be sought on 14 January at the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Meetings" title="Winter Meetings">Winter Meetings</a> of <a href="/wiki/General_manager_(baseball)" title="General manager (baseball)">General Managers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a>. The team would be known as the Toronto Giants<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1976-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and would begin play during the <a href="/wiki/1976_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1976 Major League Baseball season">1976 Major League Baseball season</a>. The deal was scuttled by a <a href="/wiki/Superior_Courts_of_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Superior Courts of California">Superior Court of California</a>, which issued an injunction blocking the sale on 11 February 1976; the injunction was requested by the city of <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> on 10 January.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESakamoto1976_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESakamoto1976-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The National Exhibition Company eventually accepted a purchase proposal from <a href="/wiki/Bob_Lurie" title="Bob Lurie">Bob Lurie</a> in a deal brokered by <a href="/wiki/George_Moscone" title="George Moscone">George Moscone</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_San_Francisco" title="Mayor of San Francisco">Mayor of San Francisco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESakamoto1976_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESakamoto1976-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The American League provided an opportunity for a Toronto franchise, and two groups bid for the rights to franchise ownership in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2011Last_audio_clip_in_the_post,_from_CBC_Radio_in_1977_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2011Last_audio_clip_in_the_post,_from_CBC_Radio_in_1977-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Ultimately, an ownership group named Metro Baseball Ltd. consisting of Labatt Brewing Company, the Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Imperial_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Imperial Trust (page does not exist)">Imperial Trust</a> won the bid for a franchise fee of <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">C$</a>7,000,000.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJozsa200658_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJozsa200658-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESgambati1976_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESgambati1976-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEToronto_Blue_Jays:_Franchise_Timeline_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEToronto_Blue_Jays:_Franchise_Timeline-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010845_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010845-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The other bid was made by Atlantic Packaging.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESgambati1976_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESgambati1976-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After the city was awarded the franchise, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">U.S. President</a> <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Ford" title="Gerald Ford">Gerald Ford</a> attempted to pressure Major League Baseball to instead award the franchise to Washington, D.C., which he claimed should have a team before Toronto. Both the American League and National League dismissed his request.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHowden1976_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHowden1976-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In preparation for the <a href="/wiki/1977_Toronto_Blue_Jays_season" title="1977 Toronto Blue Jays season">1977 season</a>, Toronto City Council approved a further <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">C$</a>2,800,000 for renovation to Exhibition Stadium.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010845_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010845-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Beeston" title="Paul Beeston">Paul Beeston</a> was hired as the team\'s first employee, and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Bavasi" title="Peter Bavasi">Peter Bavasi</a> was hired as the team\'s first president and general manager.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2010845_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2010845-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_draft">Expansion draft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1977_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion draft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1976_Major_League_Baseball_expansion_draft" title="1976 Major League Baseball expansion draft">1976 Major League Baseball expansion draft</a></div>\n<p>In order to stock the <a href="/wiki/Schedule_(workplace)" title="Schedule (workplace)">roster</a> of each team, a draft was held on 5 November 1976 in which each of the extant teams would make available to the expansion franchises some of the players on their major league and minor league rosters. Each team was allowed to protect fifteen players on the major league roster, and an additional three players after each of the first and second round of the draft.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHoltzman1997_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHoltzman1997-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The Seattle Mariners and Toronto Blue Jays each selected 30 players in the
