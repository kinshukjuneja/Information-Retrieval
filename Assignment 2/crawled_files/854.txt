<b><a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a></b> in England, on the border between <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, adjoining <a href="/wiki/Morecambe_Bay" title="Morecambe Bay">Morecambe Bay</a>. One of the smallest AONBs, it covers 29 square miles (75&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) between the <a href="/wiki/River_Kent" title="River Kent">Kent</a> Estuary, the <a href="/wiki/River_Keer" title="River Keer">River Keer</a> and the <a href="/wiki/A6_road_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="A6 road (Great Britain)">A6 road</a>. It was designated in 1972.\n</p><p>The area is characterised by low hills of <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous_Limestone" title="Carboniferous Limestone">Carboniferous Limestone</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Arnside_Knott" title="Arnside Knott">Arnside Knott</a> (522 feet) and <a href="/wiki/Warton_Crag" title="Warton Crag">Warton Crag</a> (535 feet), interspersed with grassland. Much of the area is covered by <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> woodland, in which <a href="/wiki/Ash_tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Ash tree">ash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hazel" title="Hazel">hazel</a> predominate. The coastal area contains large extents of <a href="/wiki/Salt_marsh" title="Salt marsh">salt marsh</a>, although these are under threat from the shifting channel of the <a href="/wiki/River_Kent" title="River Kent">Kent Estuary</a>. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.english-nature.org.uk//science/natural/NA_HAbDetails.asp?Name=Morecambe+Bay&amp;N=118&amp;H=15&amp;S=&amp;R=2">[1]</a>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/RSPB_Leighton_Moss" class="mw-redirect" title="RSPB Leighton Moss">Leighton Moss</a> nature reserve, owned by the <a href="/wiki/RSPB" class="mw-redirect" title="RSPB">RSPB</a>, is the largest area of <a href="/wiki/Reedbed" class="mw-redirect" title="Reedbed">reedbeds</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_West_England" title="North West England">North West England</a>, and is an <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">Important Bird Area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bittern" title="Bittern">bittern</a>, one of the resident species, has been adopted as the logo of the AONB. In addition, there are fifteen <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">SSSIs</a> in the area; one of these, Gait Barrows <a href="/wiki/National_nature_reserve_(United_Kingdom)" title="National nature reserve (United Kingdom)">National Nature Reserve</a>, is home to some rare species of <a href="/wiki/Butterfly" title="Butterfly">butterfly</a> including the <a href="/wiki/High_brown_fritillary" title="High brown fritillary">high brown fritillary</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Arnside" title="Arnside">Arnside</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silverdale,_Lancashire" title="Silverdale, Lancashire">Silverdale</a> are the main villages in the area. Other settlements include <a href="/wiki/Warton,_Lancaster" title="Warton, Lancaster">Warton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yealand_Redmayne" title="Yealand Redmayne">Yealand Redmayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beetham" title="Beetham">Beetham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Storth" title="Storth">Storth</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnside_and_Silverdale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">BirdLife International (2015) Important Bird Areas factsheet: Leighton Moss.
