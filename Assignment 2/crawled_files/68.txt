href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Reskammel</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) is a town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in north <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom, situated in the <a href="/wiki/River_Camel" title="River Camel">River Camel</a> valley northwest of <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a>. The town is approximately ten miles (16&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/Bodmin" title="Bodmin">Bodmin</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is governed by Camelford Town Council.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <b>Lanteglos-by-Camelford</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_parish" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecclesiastical parish">ecclesiastical parish</a> in which the town is situated (<i>not to be confused with</i> <a href="/wiki/Lanteglos-by-Fowey" title="Lanteglos-by-Fowey">Lanteglos-by-Fowey</a>). The ward population at the 2011 Census was 4,001.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Town population at the same census was 865 only<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Camelford is in the <a href="/wiki/North_Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="North Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">North Cornwall parliamentary constituency</a> represented by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Mann_(politician)" title="Scott Mann (politician)">Scott Mann</a> MP since 2015. Until 1974, the town was the administrative headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Camelford_Rural_District" title="Camelford Rural District">Camelford Rural District</a>.\n</p><p>The two main industrial enterprises in the area are the slate quarry at <a href="/wiki/Delabole" title="Delabole">Delabole</a> and the cheese factory at <a href="/wiki/Davidstow" title="Davidstow">Davidstow</a> and there is a small industrial estate at Highfield.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/A39_road#Atlantic_Highway" title="A39 road">A39 road</a> (dubbed \'Atlantic Highway\') passes through the town centre: a <a href="/wiki/Bypass_(road)" title="Bypass (road)">bypass</a> has been discussed for many years. <a href="/wiki/Camelford_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Camelford railway station">Camelford Station</a>, some distance from the town, closed in 1966; the site was subsequently used as a <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a> museum.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input North Cornwall Railway</div></div></div>\n<p>Its position near the highest land in Cornwall makes the climate rather wet. On 8 June 1957, 203 millimetres (8.0&#160;in) of rain fell at Camelford. <a href="/wiki/Roughtor" class="mw-redirect" title="Roughtor">Roughtor</a> is the nearest of the hills of Bodmin Moor to the town and numerous prehistoric remains can be found nearby as well. The Town Hall was built in 1806, but is now used as a branch public library. By the riverside is Enfield Park; hamlets in the parish include Helstone, <a href="/wiki/Tregoodwell" title="Tregoodwell">Tregoodwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valley_Truckle" title="Valley Truckle">Valley Truckle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hendra,_Cornwall" title="Hendra, Cornwall">Hendra</a>, Lanteglos, <a href="/wiki/Slaughterbridge" title="Slaughterbridge">Slaughterbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tramagenna" title="Tramagenna">Tramagenna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treforda" title="Treforda">Treforda</a> and Trevia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The economy depends largely on agriculture and tourism. There was a china clay works at Stannon.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg/220px-North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg/330px-North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg/440px-North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="682" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1755007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>North Cornwall Museum and Gallery</div></div></div>\n<p>Camelford is the home of the <a href="/wiki/North_Cornwall_Museum_and_Gallery" class="mw-redirect" title="North Cornwall Museum and Gallery">North Cornwall Museum and Gallery</a> which contains paintings and objects of local historical interest. To the northwest at <a href="/wiki/Slaughterbridge" title="Slaughterbridge">Slaughterbridge</a> is an Arthurian Centre and at nearby <a href="/wiki/Camelford_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Camelford Station">Camelford Station</a> is the Cycling Museum (temporarily closed since 2010). To the east are the hills of <a href="/wiki/Roughtor" class="mw-redirect" title="Roughtor">Roughtor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brown_Willy" title="Brown Willy">Brown Willy</a> and to the south the old parish churches at Lanteglos and Advent.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The main road through Camelford is the <a href="/wiki/A39_road" title="A39 road">A39</a> (Atlantic Highway) and there is a thrice-daily <a href="/wiki/Western_Greyhound" title="Western Greyhound">Western Greyhound</a> bus service from <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">Newquay</a> to <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a> via <a href="/wiki/Launceston,_Cornwall" title="Launceston, Cornwall">Launceston</a> that serves the town. A tentatively-planned bypass is on hold; traffic problems continue to crowd the town especially during summer weekends.  From 1893 to 1966 the town had a station on the <a href="/wiki/North_Cornwall_Railway" title="North Cornwall Railway">North Cornwall Railway</a>. The nearest national railway station is <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Parkway" class="mw-redirect" title="Bodmin Parkway">Bodmin Parkway</a>, 14 miles distant.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="image"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" width="20" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small><i>(April 2010)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Camelford has been linked to the legendary <a href="/wiki/Camelot" title="Camelot">Camelot</a>, and the battle of <a href="/wiki/Camlann" class="mw-redirect" title="Camlann">Camlann</a>, but historians have refuted these suggestions. The name comes from the original, <a href="/wiki/Common_Brittonic" title="Common Brittonic">Brythonic</a> name of the river (Allen) in combination with cam- = crooked and the English \'ford\',<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> though this is not accepted by all.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (September 2015)">who?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Camelford has sometimes been linked to Gafulford the site of a battle against the West Saxons which is more likely to have been at <a href="/wiki/Gafulford" title="Gafulford">Galford</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>.) Nearby <a href="/wiki/Slaughterbridge" title="Slaughterbridge">Slaughterbridge</a> has been supposed to be the site of a battle; an error arising because the derivation of "slaughter" in this case from an Anglo-Saxon word for "marsh" was not understood.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Manor_of_Helston_in_Trigg">Manor of Helston in Trigg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Manor of Helston in Trigg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Helstone" title="Helstone">Helstone</a> (or Helston in Trigg) was in the Middle Ages one of the chief manors of the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_of_Trigg" class="mw-redirect" title="Hundred of Trigg">Hundred of Trigg</a> and perhaps in Celtic times the seat of a chieftain. In the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> this manor was held by <a href="/wiki/Robert,_Count_of_Mortain" title="Robert, Count of Mortain">Earl Robert of Mortain</a>: there were 2 hides, land for 15 ploughs; the lord had 4 ploughs &amp; 18 serfs; 20 villagers &amp; 18 smallholders had 8 ploughs; 10 acres (40,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of woodland; 6 square leagues of pasture; five kinds of livestock, in total 195 beasts. The manor of <a href="/wiki/Penmayne" title="Penmayne">Penmayne</a> was a dependency of this manor.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It was one of the 17 <a href="/wiki/Antiqua_maneria" title="Antiqua maneria">Antiqua maneria</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The town elected two members to the <a href="/wiki/Unreformed_House_of_Commons" title="Unreformed House of Commons">Unreformed House of Commons</a>: the first MPs sat in the Parliament of 1552. It was later considered a <a href="/wiki/Rotten_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotten borough">rotten borough</a>, and in 1832 the <a href="/wiki/Camelford_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Camelford (UK Parliament constituency)">Camelford parliamentary constituency</a> was abolished and the town became part of the <a href="/wiki/East_Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="East Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">East Cornwall</a> constituency.\n</p><p>The seal of the borough shows: Arg. a camel passing through a ford of water all proper with legend "Sigillum Vill: de Camelford".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_pollution_incident">Water pollution incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camelford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Water pollution incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Camelford_water_pollution_incident" title="Camelford water pollution incident">Camelford water pollution incident</a></div>\n<p>In July 1988, the water supply to the town and the surrounding area was contaminated when 20 tons of <a href="/wiki/Aluminium_sulphate" class="mw-redirect" title="Aluminium sulphate">aluminium sulphate</a> was poured into the wrong tank at the <a href="/wiki/Lowermoor_Water_Treatment_Works" title="Lowermoor Water Treatment Works">Lowermoor Water Treatment Works</a> on <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a>. An independent inquiry into <a href="/wiki/Camelford_water_pollution_incident" title="Camelford water pollution incident">the incident</a>, the worst of its kind in British history, started in 2002, and a draft report was issued in January 2005, but questions remain as to the long-term effects on the health of residents. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Meacher" title="Michael Meacher">Michael Meacher</a>, who visited Camelford as environment minister, called the incident and its aftermath, "A most unbelievable scandal."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a
