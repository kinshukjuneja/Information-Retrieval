life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johnny_Dee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Johnny Dee was born on November 4, 1992, in <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" class="mw-redirect" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>, US.<sup id="cite_ref-playerprofile_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-playerprofile-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Dee\'s grandfather, <a href="/wiki/Don_Dee" title="Don Dee">Don Dee</a>, played for the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Pacers" title="Indiana Pacers">Indiana Pacers</a> and was on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_1968_Summer_Olympics" title="Basketball at the 1968 Summer Olympics">1968 Olympic gold-medal-winning basketball team</a>. His father, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Donnie_Dee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Donnie Dee (page does not exist)">Donnie Dee</a>, played for the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Colts" title="Indianapolis Colts">Indianapolis Colts</a> in the NFL for two seasons, while his mother, Jackie, was on the cross country team at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tulsa" title="University of Tulsa">University of Tulsa</a>. He has a younger sister Jenny who played basketball at <a href="/wiki/UC_Irvine" class="mw-redirect" title="UC Irvine">UC Irvine</a>. Johnny Dee was a part of the Rancho Buena Vista Little League All-Star team that advanced to the <a href="/wiki/2005_Little_League_World_Series" title="2005 Little League World Series">2005 Little League World Series</a>, playing shortstop. He attended <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Buena_Vista_High_School" title="Rancho Buena Vista High School">Rancho Buena Vista High School</a> and led the San Diego Section in scoring as a senior with 32.8 points per game. <a href="/wiki/Portland_State_Vikings_men%27s_basketball" title="Portland State Vikings men&#39;s basketball">Portland State</a> was the only Division I school to offer him a scholarship, but Dee decided to walk on at <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Toreros_men%27s_basketball" title="San Diego Toreros men&#39;s basketball">San Diego</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johnny_Dee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When Dee arrived at San Diego, the team was coming off a 6\xe2\x80\x9324 season and <a href="/wiki/Point-shaving" class="mw-redirect" title="Point-shaving">point-shaving</a> scandal. Dee received a scholarship the summer before his freshman year after a player announced he was leaving the program. He considered redshirting, but coach <a href="/wiki/Bill_Grier" title="Bill Grier">Bill Grier</a> talked him out of the idea and he averaged 13.7 points per game. Dee increased his scoring to 15.0 points per game as a sophomore.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He twisted his left ankle playing against <a href="/wiki/Loyola_Marymount_Lions_men%27s_basketball" title="Loyola Marymount Lions men&#39;s basketball">Loyola Marymount</a> but still managed to score 19 points in a win over <a href="/wiki/BYU_Cougars_men%27s_basketball" title="BYU Cougars men&#39;s basketball">BYU</a> three nights later. As a junior, Dee played most of the season with a knee injury.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During this time, he led NCAA Division I in free throw shooting percentage at 94.5% while averaging 16.6 points per game.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In order to calm his nerves at the line, Dee silently recited the Bible verse Philippians 4:13: "I can do all things through him who gives me strength."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As a senior, Dee averaged 14.0 points per game and shot 37.9 percent from behind the arc. Dee is San Diego\'s all-time leading scorer, finishing with 2,046 points, the all-time leader in three-pointers with 333, and was tied for second in games played with 131.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was a two-time All-<a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Conference" title="West Coast Conference">West Coast Conference</a> First Team selection.<sup id="cite_ref-warriors_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warriors-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johnny_Dee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After going undrafted, Dee signed with the <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Summer_League" title="NBA Summer League">NBA Summer League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-warriors_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warriors-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Dee began his professional career with <a href="/wiki/HKK_%C5%A0iroki" title="HKK \xc5\xa0iroki">\xc5\xa0iroki</a> and averaged over 19 points per game. On November 9, 2015, he signed with Polish club <a href="/wiki/SKS_Starogard_Gda%C5%84ski" title="SKS Starogard Gda\xc5\x84ski">Starogard Gda\xc5\x84ski</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He averaged 11 points per game in Poland. In the 2017\xe2\x80\x932018 season, Dee joined <a href="/wiki/Araberri_BC" title="Araberri BC">Araberri</a> of the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Liga_Espa%C3%B1ola_de_Baloncesto" title="Liga Espa\xc3\xb1ola de Baloncesto">LEB Oro</a>. He posted 15.9 points, and 2.5 assists per game in 24 games. On August 6, 2018, he signed a two-year deal with <a href="/wiki/CB_Sevilla" title="CB Sevilla">Sevilla</a> in the same league.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to his player profile, he is 6&#160;ft 0&#160;in (1.83&#160;m) and weighs 185&#160;lb (84&#160;kg).<sup id="cite_ref-playerprofile_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-playerprofile-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johnny_Dee_(basketball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-playerprofile-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-playerprofile_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-playerprofile_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://basketball.realgm.com/player/Johnny-Dee/Summary/30428">"Johnny Dee G  #3"</a>. <i>RealGM</i><span class="reference-accessdate">.
