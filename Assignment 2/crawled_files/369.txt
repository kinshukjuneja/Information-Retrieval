/>.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>East Dorset</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.  Its council is based in <a href="/wiki/Wimborne_Minster" title="Wimborne Minster">Wimborne Minster</a> since 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The district was formed on 1 April 1974 by the merger of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wimborne_Minster_Urban_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wimborne Minster Urban District (page does not exist)">Wimborne Minster Urban District</a> with part of the <a href="/wiki/Ringwood_and_Fordingbridge_Rural_District" title="Ringwood and Fordingbridge Rural District">Ringwood and Fordingbridge Rural District</a> and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wimborne_and_Cranborne_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wimborne and Cranborne Rural District (page does not exist)">Wimborne and Cranborne Rural District</a>.  The district was originally known as <b>Wimborne</b>, and adopted its present name in 1988.\n</p><p>The popularity of the area, being close to the New Forest, Bournemouth and the Dorset coast, has seen a rapid expansion in housing from the 1970s with the <a href="/wiki/Verwood" title="Verwood">Verwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferndown" title="Ferndown">Ferndown</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Moors" title="West Moors">West Moors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corfe_Mullen" title="Corfe Mullen">Corfe Mullen</a> populations more than quadrupling. There still remains a rural feel, however, especially in the north and west of the area. <a href="/wiki/Wimborne_Minster" title="Wimborne Minster">Wimborne Minster</a> has retained its identity as a historic market town.\n</p><p>East Dorset also contains a large amount of the remaining lowland <a href="/wiki/Heath_(habitat)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heath (habitat)">heath</a>, managed by East Dorset Countryside Management Services in partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Forestry_Commission" title="Forestry Commission">Forestry Commission</a>. The expansion of housing has led to a massive decrease in the area of this unusual and unique habitat, which once covered 500&#160;km\xc2\xb2 but now covers only 15% of that.\n</p><p>Statistics released<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a> show that <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> at birth for males in East Dorset was 80.1 years in 2001-2003, the highest in the United Kingdom.  Female life expectancy at birth for the same period was 83.4 years, ranking seventh in the UK.  The figures for East Dorset during 1991-1993 were 77.9 years for males and 82.5 for females.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Governance"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Governance</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Settlements"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Settlements</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=East_Dorset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/East_Dorset_District_Council_elections" title="East Dorset District Council elections">East Dorset District Council elections</a></div>\n<p>East Dorset District Council is elected every four years, with currently 36 <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">councillors</a> being elected at each election.
