5.680\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">50.124; -5.680</span></span></span></a></span></span></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>St Just</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Lannust</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) is a town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Penwith" title="Penwith">Penwith</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom. It lies along the <a href="/wiki/B3306_road" title="B3306 road">B3306 road</a> which connects <a href="/wiki/St_Ives,_Cornwall" title="St Ives, Cornwall">St Ives</a> to the <a href="/wiki/A30_road" title="A30 road">A30 road</a>. The parish encompasses the town of St Just and the nearby settlements of  <a href="/wiki/Trewellard" title="Trewellard">Trewellard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pendeen" title="Pendeen">Pendeen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelynack" title="Kelynack">Kelynack</a>: it is bounded by the parishes of <a href="/wiki/Morvah" title="Morvah">Morvah</a> to the north-east, <a href="/wiki/Sancreed" title="Sancreed">Sancreed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madron" title="Madron">Madron</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/St_Buryan" title="St Buryan">St Buryan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sennen" title="Sennen">Sennen</a> to the south and by the sea in the west. The parish consists of 7,622 acres (3,085&#160;ha) of land, 12 acres (4.9&#160;ha) of water and 117 acres (47.3&#160;ha) of foreshore. The town of St Just is the most westerly town in mainland Britain and is situated approximately 8 miles (13&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_road_numbering_scheme" title="Great Britain road numbering scheme">A3071</a>. St Just parish, which includes <a href="/wiki/Pendeen" title="Pendeen">Pendeen</a> and the surrounding area, has a population of 4690 (4,637 at the 2011 census).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> An electoral ward also exists: the population of this ward at the same census was 4,812.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>St Just lies within the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB). Almost a third of Cornwall has AONB designation, with the same status and protection as a National Park. href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The identity of Saint Just is not known.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Cornwall\'s long resistance to the edicts of Canterbury and Rome makes it most unlikely that the saint was <a href="/wiki/Justus" title="Justus">Archbishop Justus</a> of Canterbury, as some sources claim.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Another possibility is the 6th- or 7th-century <a href="/wiki/Saint_Iestyn" title="Saint Iestyn">Saint Iestyn</a>, said to be the son of a ruler of <a href="/wiki/Dumnonia" title="Dumnonia">Dumnonia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 1478 William of Worcester found that the church was believed to contain the bones of <a href="/wiki/Justus_of_Trieste" title="Justus of Trieste">Justus of Trieste</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Among the prehistoric antiquities nearby is <a href="/wiki/Ballowall_Barrow" title="Ballowall Barrow">Ballowall Barrow</a>, a chambered tomb. St Just is one of the most ancient mining districts in Cornwall, and remains of ancient pre-industrial and more modern mining activity have had a considerable impact on the nearby landscape.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Churches_and_schools">Churches and schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a St Just</a> is a fine 15th-century building. In 1336 the church was rebuilt and dedicated by <a href="/wiki/John_Grandisson" title="John Grandisson">John Grandisson</a>, Bishop of Exeter; however only the chancel of this church survives and the nave and aisles are 15th-century in date.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There are two Methodist chapels.\n</p><p>St Just is the home of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cornwall_School" title="Cape Cornwall School">Cape Cornwall School</a> which also serves Sennen, Sancreed, Pendeen, St Buryan and other places in the district.\n</p><p>There are seven Cornish crosses in the parish; there are two in the vicarage garden and one built into the church wall. Other crosses are at Leswidden, Nanquidno and Kenidjack (two).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining">Mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The ancient settlement has a strong <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Cornwall_and_Devon" title="Mining in Cornwall and Devon">mining</a> history and was during the 19th century one of the most important mining districts in Cornwall both for copper and for tin. Mines within the area included Boscaswell Downs, Balleswidden, Parknoweth, Boscean, <a href="/wiki/Wheal_Owles" title="Wheal Owles">Wheal Owles</a>, Wheal Boys, <a href="/wiki/Levant_Mine_%26_Beam_Engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Levant Mine &amp; Beam Engine">Levant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botallack" title="Botallack">Botallack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geevor_Tin_Mine" title="Geevor Tin Mine">Geevor</a> (which closed in 1990).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Geevor mine is now a tourist attraction which allows visitors to explore <a href="/wiki/Mining_in_Cornwall_and_Devon" title="Mining in Cornwall and Devon">Cornish Mining</a> heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  The boom in 19th-century mining saw a dramatic increase in the population of St Just, the 1861 census records the population figure as being 9,290, however like other areas in Cornwall the population declined with the collapse in the tin trade in the 19th century. The town also suffered from the decision of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a> to abandon its plans to make St Just the terminus of the London mainline to Cornwall. It was announced in July 2006 that the St Just mining district and the rest of the historic mining areas of Cornwall had become the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_and_West_Devon_Mining_Landscape" title="Cornwall and West Devon Mining Landscape">Cornwall and West Devon Mining Landscape</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The nearby <a href="/wiki/Cot_Valley" title="Cot Valley">Cot Valley</a> has a stream which runs to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">sea</a>. The area has been heavily mined, as was the area around St Just. The round boulders in the Cot Valley Cove here are of specific scientific interest.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Also nearby is <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cornwall" title="Cape Cornwall">Cape Cornwall</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>For the purposes of local government classification St Just is a <a href="/wiki/Town_Council#United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Town Council">town</a> and elects a Mayor every 12 months from among the St Just Town Councillors. The St Just Town Council was created following the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">re-structuring of English Local Government in 1974</a>, St Just having been an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_council" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban district council">urban district council</a> until then. Principal local government functions are now undertaken by <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a>.\n</p><p>St Just was originally part of the Penzance Poor Law Union until 1894 when it was incorporated into the West Penwith Rural District. In 1897 St Just in Penwith parish formed the sole basis of St Just Urban District. In 1974 the urban district was included in Penwith District, until that was abolished in 2009.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_local_traditions">Culture and local traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture and local traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg/220px-The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg" width="220" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg/330px-The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg/440px-The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="469" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_green_at_st_just_in_penwith_cornwall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Plen an Gwarry, common green near the clock tower in the centre of St Just</div></div></div>\n<p>St Just is home to the popular <a href="/wiki/Lafrowda_Day" title="Lafrowda Day">Lafrowda</a> festival<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> a seven-day community and arts celebration usually held in Mid July.\n</p><p>A more ancient celebration associated with the town is St Just Feast which is held every year to celebrate the dedication of the parish church in 13 July 1336.  Feast celebrations were moved to the Sunday nearest to All Saints\' Day in 1536 following an Act of Henry VIII which means it usually take place at the end of October / beginning of  November. Feast itself is a two-day event with a church service and civic procession being held on Feast Sunday and a larger scale popular celebration being held on Feast Monday (which includes a meeting of the Western Hunt). A description of St Just Feast, from 1882, follows:\n</p>\n<blockquote>\n<p>"Rich and poor still at this season keep open house, and all the young people from St. Just who are in service for many miles around, if they can possibly be spared, go home on the Saturday and stay until the Tuesday morning. A small fair is held in the streets on Monday evening, when the young men are expected to treat their sweethearts liberally, and a great deal of "foolish money" that can be ill afforded is often spent"\n</p>\n</blockquote>\n<p>St Just also has a \'<a href="/wiki/Plen_an_Gwarry" class="mw-redirect" title="Plen an Gwarry">Plen an Gwarry</a>\' (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a> for "playing place"), locally pronounced \'Plain an Gwarry\'. These sites were used historically for open-air performance, entertainment and instruction. St Just\'s Plen an Gwarry occasionally hosts productions of the Cornish <a href="/wiki/Ordinalia" title="Ordinalia">Ordinalia</a> <a href="/wiki/Mystery_plays" class="mw-redirect" title="Mystery plays">mystery plays</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>St Just has a healthy artistic scene, including the painter <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Jackson" title="Kurt Jackson">Kurt Jackson</a> who has made several television appearances. Contemporary singer and comedian <a href="/wiki/Jethro_(comedian)" title="Jethro (comedian)">Jethro</a> from nearby <a href="/wiki/St_Buryan" title="St Buryan">St Buryan</a> played for St Just Rugby Club and has recorded a song entitled "St Just".\n</p><p>The folk singer <a href="/wiki/Martha_Tilston" title="Martha Tilston">Martha Tilston</a> released an album in 2010 called <i>Lucy and the Wolves</i>. This features a range of songs inspired by Cornwall. The first song on the album is called <i>The Cape</i> and is based upon Cape Cornwall in St Just. She has performed in Penzance a number of times and now lives in Penwith.\n</p><p>The children\'s animated television series <i><a href="/wiki/Jungle_Junction" title="Jungle Junction">Jungle Junction</a></i>, broadcast by the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Junior" title="Disney Junior">Disney Junior</a> network, is produced by <a href="/wiki/Spider_Eye_Productions" title="Spider Eye Productions">Spider Eye Productions</a> of St. Just.\n</p><p>The local <a href="/wiki/Community_radio_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Community radio in the United Kingdom">community radio</a> station is <a href="/wiki/Coast_FM_(West_Cornwall)" title="Coast FM (West Cornwall)">Coast FM</a> (formerly Penwith Radio), which broadcasts on 96.5 and 97.2 <a href="/wiki/FM_broadcasting" title="FM broadcasting">FM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_services">Bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>St Just bus station is served by <a href="/wiki/First_South_West" title="First South West">First South West</a>, who run routes 10 and 10A, both to <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pendeen" title="Pendeen">Pendeen</a>. In summer it is served by the route 300 open-top bus, to Penzance via <a href="/wiki/St_Ives,_Cornwall" title="St Ives, Cornwall">St Ives</a> or <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End" title="Land&#39;s End">Land\'s End</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Twinning">Twinning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Twinning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>St Just is <a href="/wiki/Twin_towns_and_sister_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin towns and sister cities">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Huelgoat" title="Huelgoat">Huelgoat, Bro-Gernev, Brittany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The town is also twinned, in partnership with Penzance, with Bendigo in the State of Victoria, Australia and with Nevada City in California, USA as a result of the historical links through the many people from the area who emigrated to the two destinations, mostly in the late 19th century, to find a better life in the mines there. There is still an area of Bendigo known as St. Just Point.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Just_in_Penwith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Edward_Grenfell,_1st_Baron_St_Just" title="Edward Grenfell, 1st Baron St Just">Edward Grenfell, 1st Baron St Just</a>, raised to the peerage as Baron St Just, of St Just in Penwith</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Francis_Oats" title="Francis Oats">Francis Oats</a> (1848\xe2\x80\x931918), Cornish miner who became chairman of De Beers diamond company</li>\n<li>Lt Col <a href="/wiki/James_Howard_Williams" title="James Howard Williams">J.
