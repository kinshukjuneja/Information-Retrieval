For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/RIA_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="RIA (disambiguation)">RIA (disambiguation)</a>.</div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg/220px-PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg/330px-PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg/440px-PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg 2x" data-file-width="768" data-file-height="511" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PortHacking-Sydney-Ria.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Georges_River" title="Georges River">Georges River</a>, in the southern suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia, is a ria, or drowned river valley. The deeply indented shape of the ria reflects the <a href="/wiki/Dendrite_(mathematics)" title="Dendrite (mathematics)">dendritic</a> pattern of drainage that existed before the rise in sea level that flooded the valley.</div></div></div>\n<p>A <b>ria</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/i\xcb\x90/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">i\xcb\x90</span><span title="/\xc9\x99/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">\xc9\x99</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span><span title="/\xc9\x99/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">\xc9\x99</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a coastal inlet formed by the partial submergence of an unglaciated <a href="/wiki/River_valley" class="mw-redirect" title="River valley">river valley</a>. It is a drowned river valley that remains open to the sea. Typically, rias have a <a href="/wiki/Drainage_system_(geomorphology)#Dendritic_drainage_pattern" title="Drainage system (geomorphology)">dendritic</a>, treelike outline although they can be straight and without significant branches. This pattern is inherited from the dendritic drainage pattern of the flooded river valley. The drowning of river valleys along a stretch of coast and formation of rias results in an extremely irregular and indented coastline. Often, there are naturally-occurring islands, which are summits of partly submerged, preexisting hill peaks.  (Islands may also be artificial, such as those constructed for the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay_Bridge-Tunnel" class="mw-redirect" title="Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel">Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel</a>.)\n</p><p>A <b>ria coast</b> is a coastline having several parallel rias separated by prominent ridges, extending a distance inland.<sup id="cite_ref-Cotton1956a_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cotton1956a-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Goudie2004a_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Goudie2004a-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bird2008a_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bird2008a-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The sea level change that caused the submergence of a river valley may be either <a href="/wiki/Sea_level#Local_and_eustatic_sea_level" title="Sea level">eustatic</a> (where global sea levels rise), or <a href="/wiki/Isostasy" title="Isostasy">isostatic</a> (where the local land sinks). The result is often a very large estuary at the mouth of a relatively insignificant river (or else sediments would quickly fill the ria). The <a href="/wiki/Kingsbridge_Estuary" title="Kingsbridge Estuary">Kingsbridge Estuary</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, England, is an extreme example of a ria forming an estuary disproportionate to the size of its river; no significant river flows into it at all, only a number of small streams.<sup id="cite_ref-Bird2008a_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bird2008a-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/ria#Portuguese" class="extiw" title="wikt:ria">ria</a></i> or <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician Rias are present all along the <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galician</a> coast in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. As originally defined, the term was restricted to drowned river valleys cut parallel to the structure of the <a href="/wiki/Country_rock_(geology)" title="Country rock (geology)">country rock</a> that was at right angles to the coastline. However, the definition of ria was later expanded to other flooded river valleys regardless of the structure of the country rock.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>For a period of time, European geomorphologists<sup id="cite_ref-Gulliver1899a_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gulliver1899a-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> regarded rias to include any broad estuarine river mouth, including <a href="/wiki/Fjord" title="Fjord">fjords</a>. These are long, narrow <a href="/wiki/Inlet" title="Inlet">inlets</a> with steep sides or cliffs, created in a valley carved by <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glacial activity</a>. In the 21st century, however, the preferred usage of <i>ria</i> by geologists and geomorphologists is to refer solely to drowned unglaciated river valleys. It therefore excludes fjords by definition, since fjords are products of glaciation.<sup id="cite_ref-Cotton1956a_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cotton1956a-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Goudie2004a_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Goudie2004a-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bird2008a_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bird2008a-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ria&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>.
