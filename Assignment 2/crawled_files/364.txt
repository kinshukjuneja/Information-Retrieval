Manfred Jr.</b> (born September 28, 1958) is an American lawyer and business executive who is the tenth and current <a href="/wiki/Commissioner_of_Baseball" title="Commissioner of Baseball">Commissioner of Baseball</a>. He previously served as the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Operating_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Operating Officer">Chief Operating Officer</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) and succeeded <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a> as Commissioner on January 25, 2015.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Manfred&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Manfred was born on September 28, 1958 in <a href="/wiki/Rome,_New_York" title="Rome, New York">Rome, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Rome_Free_Academy" title="Rome Free Academy">Rome Free Academy</a> and graduated in 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Manfred enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Le_Moyne_College" title="Le Moyne College">Le Moyne College</a> from 1976 through 1978 before transferring to <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a>. He graduated from Cornell\'s <a href="/wiki/School_of_Industrial_and_Labor_Relations" class="mw-redirect" title="School of Industrial and Labor Relations">School of Industrial and Labor Relations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Law_School" title="Harvard Law School">Harvard Law School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coo_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After law school, he clerked for Judge <a href="/wiki/Joseph_L._Tauro" title="Joseph L. Tauro">Joseph L. Tauro</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Massachusetts" title="United States District Court for the District of Massachusetts">United States District Court for the District of Massachusetts</a>. He became a <a href="/wiki/Partnership" title="Partnership">partner</a> at <a href="/wiki/Morgan,_Lewis_%26_Bockius" title="Morgan, Lewis &amp; Bockius">Morgan, Lewis &amp; Bockius</a>, focusing on labor and employment law.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt1995_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1995-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-espn2014_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn2014-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_League_Baseball">Major League Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Manfred&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Major League Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1987, Manfred began working with <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) during <a href="/wiki/Collective_bargaining" title="Collective bargaining">collective bargaining</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-espn2014_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn2014-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1994\xe2\x80\x9395 Major League Baseball strike">1994\xe2\x80\x9395 MLB strike</a>, he served as outside counsel for the owners.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt1995_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt1995-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He joined MLB on a full-time basis in 1998, serving as the Executive Vice President of Economics and League Affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-coo_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Manfred negotiated MLB\'s first drug testing agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association" title="Major League Baseball Players Association">Major League Baseball Players Association</a> (MLBPA) in 2002, and represented MLB in negotiations with the MLBPA when forming new collective bargaining agreements in 2002, 2006 and 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-nydn_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nydn-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Manfred led MLB\'s investigation of the <a href="/wiki/Biogenesis_baseball_scandal" title="Biogenesis baseball scandal">Biogenesis scandal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a> promoted Manfred to <a href="/wiki/Chief_operating_officer" title="Chief operating officer">chief operating officer</a> of MLB.<sup id="cite_ref-coo_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The position had been vacant since <a href="/wiki/Bob_DuPuy" title="Bob DuPuy">Bob DuPuy</a> resigned in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Following the announcement of Selig\'s retirement, effective after the 2014 season, Manfred became a finalist to succeed him as Commissioner.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sportingnews_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportingnews-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On August 14, 2014, MLB owners elected Manfred to succeed Selig, beating <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> chairman <a href="/wiki/Tom_Werner" title="Tom Werner">Tom Werner</a> and MLB executive vice president of business Tim Brosnan.<sup id="cite_ref-espn2014_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn2014-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Manfred assumed office on January 25, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-new_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-new-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He stated that his primary goals as commissioner were youth outreach, embracing technology, quickening the pace of play, strengthening player relations, and creating a more unified business operation.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As commissioner, Manfred instituted rules before the start of the 2015 season to address the <a href="/wiki/Pace_of_play" title="Pace of play">pace of play</a>, including having batters remain in the <a href="/wiki/Batters_box" class="mw-redirect" title="Batters box">batters box</a> and the installation of time clocks to limit the time spent around <a href="/wiki/Commercial_break" class="mw-redirect" title="Commercial break">commercial breaks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Before the 2018 season, Manfred introduced more rule changes to affect the pace of play, including reducing the time in <a href="/wiki/Commercial_break" class="mw-redirect" title="Commercial break">commercial breaks</a> and limiting player visits to the <a href="/wiki/Pitcher%27s_mound" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitcher&#39;s mound">pitcher\'s mound</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He has also advocated for expansion franchises, listing <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> as possible locations for new teams.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Manfred&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Growing up in <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">Upstate New York</a>, Manfred was a fan of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His father led the <a Brass</a>, while his mother was a schoolteacher.<sup id="cite_ref-nydn_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nydn-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-espn2015_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn2015-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He has an older sister and a younger brother.<sup id="cite_ref-espn2015_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn2015-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Manfred is married and has four children.<sup id="cite_ref-coo_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coo-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He serves as a Board member at Catholic School of Holy Child in <a href="/wiki/Rye,_New_York" title="Rye, New York">Rye, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> His daughter Megan Manfred married Timothy Petrella of <a href="/wiki/Minnetonka,_Minnesota" title="Minnetonka, Minnesota">Minnetonka, Minnesota</a>, son of the president of <a href="/wiki/UnitedHealth_Group" title="UnitedHealth Group">UnitedHealthcare Community and State</a>, at Immaculate Conception Catholic Church in <a href="/wiki/Sleepy_Hollow,_New_York" title="Sleepy Hollow, New York">Sleepy Hollow, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> His son Michael married Ashley Allen at Catholic Church of the Transfiguration in <a href="/wiki/Tarrytown,_New_York" title="Tarrytown,
