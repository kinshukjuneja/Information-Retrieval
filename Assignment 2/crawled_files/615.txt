href="http://www.southatlanticleague.com/">www.southatlanticleague.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>South Atlantic League</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> league with teams along the Atlantic coastline of the United States from <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> to <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Class_A_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Class A (baseball)">Class A</a> league that plays a full season, and its teams are composed of players in their second or third year of professional play. \n</p><p>A number of different leagues known as the South Atlantic League have existed since 1904. The current league of that name adopted the moniker in 1980, having previously been the <a href="/wiki/Western_Carolinas_League" title="Western Carolinas League">Western Carolinas League</a>, founded in 1963.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Atlantic_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:John_Henry_Moss.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/John_Henry_Moss.png/220px-John_Henry_Moss.png" width="220" height="186" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/John_Henry_Moss.png/330px-John_Henry_Moss.png 1.5x, class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:John_Henry_Moss.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Portion of plaque displaying likeness of John Henry Moss at Municipal Stadium, Hagerstown, Maryland</div></div></div>\n<p>There have been several South Atlantic Leagues in the history of minor league baseball, spanning from 1904 to the present with a few breaks. The league ran from 1904 to 1917 as a class C league, then started up again in 1919, also class C. This time it ran from 1919 to 1930, moving up to class B beginning in 1921. <a href="/wiki/William_G._Bramham" title="William G. Bramham">William G. Bramham</a> became league president in mid-1924 and served until 1930. The league was restarted again as a class B from 1936 to 1942, shut down as a result of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and returned in 1946 as a class A league. The AA <a href="/wiki/Southern_Association" title="Southern Association">Southern Association</a> (which never integrated) died after the 1961 season and so the SAL was promoted to AA in 1963 to take its place; a year later the name was changed to the <a href="/wiki/Southern_League_(baseball)" title="Southern League (baseball)">Southern League</a>. Out of the 51 seasons of operation, <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Georgia" title="Augusta, Georgia">Augusta, Georgia</a> competed in 46, <a href="/wiki/Macon,_Georgia" title="Macon, Georgia">Macon, Georgia</a> was around for 46, and <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Carolina" title="Columbia, South Carolina">Columbia, South Carolina</a> was in 45. <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a>; <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Georgia" title="Columbus, Georgia">Columbus, Georgia</a>; each competed for at least 29 years also, making for a relatively stable lineup.\n</p><p>The South Atlantic League name went unused for 16 years, but in 1980 the <a href="/wiki/Western_Carolinas_League" title="Western Carolinas League">Western Carolinas League</a> brought back the name when it sought to change its identity. For nearly 60 years, 1948 through 2007, the dominant figure in the WCL/SAL was league founder and president <a href="/wiki/John_Henry_Moss" title="John Henry Moss">John Henry Moss</a>, who started the WCL as a young man in 1948, refounded it in 1960 and then led it into the new century. Moss retired at the close of the 2007<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> South Atlantic League season. He died at age 90 on July 1, 2009, at <a href="/wiki/Kings_Mountain,_North_Carolina" title="Kings Mountain, North Carolina">Kings Mountain, North Carolina</a>\xe2\x80\x94a town where he had also been mayor for 23 years.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2005, the SAL had the highest <a href="/wiki/Sports_league_attendances" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports league attendances">attendance</a> in 101 years with over 3,541,992 fans (while minor league baseball set a second straight record with 41,333,279 attendees). Currently, the league has 14 teams, divided into two divisions of seven clubs.\n</p><p>In 2015, the <a href="/wiki/Savannah_Sand_Gnats" title="Savannah Sand Gnats">Savannah Sand Gnats</a> relocated to <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Carolina" title="Columbia, South Carolina">Columbia, South Carolina</a>, becoming the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Fireflies" title="Columbia Fireflies">Columbia
