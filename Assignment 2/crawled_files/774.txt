class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fluorescent_flower_display.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flower display in a US supermarket</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Cut flowers</b> are <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> or <a href="/wiki/Flower_bud" class="mw-redirect" title="Flower bud">flower buds</a> (often with some <a href="/wiki/Plant_stem" title="Plant stem">stem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaf</a>) that have been cut from the plant bearing it. It is usually removed from the plant for decorative use. Typical uses are in <a href="/wiki/Vase" title="Vase">vase</a> displays, <a href="/wiki/Wreath" title="Wreath">wreaths</a> and <a href="/wiki/Garland" title="Garland">garlands</a>. Many gardeners harvest their own cut flowers from domestic gardens, but there is a significant <a href="/wiki/Floral_industry" title="Floral industry">floral industry</a> for cut flowers in most countries. The plants cropped vary by climate, culture and the level of wealth locally. Often the plants are raised specifically for the purpose, in field or glasshouse growing conditions. Cut flowers can also be harvested from the wild.\n</p><p>The cultivation and trade of flowers is a specialization in <a href="/wiki/Horticulture" title="Horticulture">horticulture</a>, specifically <a href="/wiki/Floriculture" title="Floriculture">floriculture</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_flowers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A common use is for <a href="/wiki/Floristry" title="Floristry">floristry</a>, usually for decoration inside a house or building. Typically the cut flowers are placed in a vase. A number of similar types of decorations are used, especially in larger buildings and at events such as weddings. These are often decorated with additional foliage.\nIn some cultures, a major use of cut flowers is for worship; this can be seen especially in south and southeast <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.\n</p><p>Sometimes the flowers are picked rather than cut, without any significant leaf or stem. Such flowers may be used for wearing in hair, or in a button-hole. Masses of flowers may be used for sprinkling, in a similar way to <a href="/wiki/Confetti" title="Confetti">confetti</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Garland" title="Garland">Garlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wreath" title="Wreath">wreaths</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flower_bouquet" title="Flower bouquet">bouquets</a> are major derived and value added products.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Longevity">Longevity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_flowers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Longevity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Once flowers are removed from the <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plant</a> they continue to grow slowly, but have a diminished capability of receiving the <a href="/wiki/Nutrients" class="mw-redirect" title="Nutrients">nutrients</a> that are vital for their survival. In most countries, cut flowers are a local crop because of their perishable nature. In India, much of the product has a shelf life of only a day. Among these are <a href="/wiki/Marigold_(common)" class="mw-redirect" title="Marigold (common)">marigold</a> flowers for garlands and temples, which are typically harvested before dawn, and discarded after use the same day.\n</p><p>The majority of cut flowers can be expected to last several days with proper care. This generally requires standing them in water in shade. They can be treated in various ways to increase their life. According to James C. Schmidt, a <a href="/wiki/Horticulture" title="Horticulture">horticulturist</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana-Champaign" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign">University of Illinois</a>, originally putting cut flowers in a <a href="/wiki/Sterilization_(microbiology)" title="Sterilization (microbiology)">sterilized</a> vase is important to extending the life of the flowers. Vases can be cleaned using a household dish detergent or a combination of water and <a href="/wiki/Bleach" title="Bleach">bleach</a>. Using these <a href="/wiki/Disinfectants" class="mw-redirect" title="Disinfectants">disinfectants</a> ensures that there will be less <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a> growing within the vase that could potentially cause the plant to <a href="/wiki/Wilting" title="Wilting">wilt</a> and die at a faster rate.  Schmidt also claims that cutting the flowers diagonally with a sharp knife under running water ensures that they can immediately take up fresh and clean water.  Re-cutting the <a href="/wiki/Plant_stem" title="Plant stem">stems</a> periodically will ensure that there is a fresh surface from which the stems can take up water. This will allow the flowers to last even longer. Other ways to care for vase flowers includes keeping flowers away from ceiling fans and air-conditioning vents as this can lead to <a href="/wiki/Dehydration" title="Dehydration">dehydration</a>, keeping flowers away from fresh fruit of vegetables, using filtered water rather than tap water so as to avoid both <a href="/wiki/Chlorine" title="Chlorine">chlorine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fluoride_in_water" class="mw-redirect" title="Fluoride in water">fluoride</a>, and keeping flowers away from your television<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>.\n</p><p>There is also a market for \'everlasting\' or dried flowers, which include species such as <a href="/wiki/Xerochrysum_bracteatum" title="Xerochrysum bracteatum">Xerochrysum bracteatum</a>. These can have a very long shelf life.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Additives">Additives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_flowers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Additives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Botanical_Garden" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn Botanical Garden">Brooklyn Botanical Garden</a>, different additives can be used to prolong the lives of fresh cut flowers.  Experiments were performed with various substances mixed with water, including <a href="/wiki/Aspirin" title="Aspirin">aspirin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Multivitamin" title="Multivitamin">vitamin pills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinegar" title="Vinegar">vinegar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennies" class="mw-redirect" title="Pennies">pennies</a>, and flower food to test their effect on cut flowers\' lifespans.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Each plant was placed in the same environment and the same type of plant was used in each vase.\n</p><p>This research found that the best additive for flowers was the retailer-provided "flower food" that is usually given with a bouquet. Plants are known to thrive in an environment where there are few bacteria, plenty of food for <a href="/wiki/Energy_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Energy (biology)">energy</a>, and water uptake is encouraged.  Flower foods contain an <a href="/wiki/Acidifier" title="Acidifier">acidifier</a> that helps to adjust the water\'s <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a>.  With a lower pH the water and food conducting system within the flower can work at maximum efficiency.  The <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a> in the food will be used by the plant as an energy source, which had been lost when the flower was cut away from its root. With these <a href="/wiki/Nutrient" title="Nutrient">nutrients</a> the plant will be able to fully develop.  Finally, there are stem unpluggers that will make sure that the flower can easily take up water and nutrients that can later be used to take care of the needs of the rest of the plant.<sup id="cite_ref-Additives_for_cut_flowers_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Additives_for_cut_flowers-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  This combination gives the fresh cut flowers everything that they need to survive longer. When tests were carried out in St. Mary\'s College C.S.S.p, Rathmines, Dublin, however, results showed that glucose was more effective at prolonging the life of cut flowers than the commercial plant food. The experiment was carried out as part of the Junior Certificate Science Examination set by the Irish State Examinations Commission 2008.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trade">Trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_flowers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Trade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The largest producers are, in order of cultivated area, China, India, and the United States. The largest importer and exporter by value is the Netherlands, which is both a grower and a redistributor of crops imported from other countries. Most of its exports go to its European neighbours.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In recent decades, with the increasing use of <a href="/wiki/Air_freight" class="mw-redirect" title="Air freight">air freight</a>, it has become economic for high value crops to be grown far from their point of sale; the market is usually in industrialised countries. Typical of these is the production of <a href="/wiki/Rose" title="Rose">roses</a> in Ecuador and Colombia, mainly for the US market, and production in Kenya and Uganda for the European market. Some countries specialise in especially high value products, such as <a href="/wiki/Orchid" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchid">orchids</a> from Singapore and Thailand.\n</p><p>As with the production of fruit and vegetables, the industry depends on significant amounts of water, which may be collected and stored by the farm owners. The <a href="/wiki/Patel_Dam_failure" title="Patel Dam failure">Patel Dam failure</a> in May 2018, associated with a large Kenyan rose farm, killed dozens of people. \n</p><p>The total market value in most countries is considerable. It has been estimated at approximately GBP 2 billion in the United Kingdom, of the same order as that of music sales.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_reading">Further reading</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cut_flowers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Further reading">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>Arbel\xc3\xa1ez, Mar\xc3\xada Ang\xc3\xa9lica , Marcela Mel\xc3\xa9ndez, and Nicol\xc3\xa1s Le\xc3\xb3n"The Emergence of Fresh Cut-Flower Exports in Colombia" In <i>Export Pioneers in Latin America</i>  Edited by Charles Sabel et al., Baltimore: Inter-American Development Bank 2012.</li>\n<li>Austin,  James  E.  1990.  <i>Cut Flower Industry in Colombia</i>  (Abridged).
