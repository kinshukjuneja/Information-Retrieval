href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In March 1912, organizers of the proposed league&#8211;described by members of the sports establishment as an "outlaw league"&#8211;met in New York\'s Hotel Imperial.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt-03-16-12_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-03-16-12-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The U.S. Baseball League subsequently organized teams in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Pennsylvania" title="Reading, Pennsylvania">Reading, Pennsylvania</a>; <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-LA84_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA84-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The league president was William Witmann.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sports historian Rudolf K. Haerle observed that the U.S. Baseball League "stressed the inherent \'good\' of baseball for all individuals and communities, and indicated that it wished to conduct its business in the accepted capitalist style&#8211;free competition in the marketplace".<sup id="cite_ref-LA84_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA84-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Despite these lofty ambitions, the league quickly incurred the scorn and hostility of the baseball establishment.<sup id="cite_ref-LA84_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA84-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Additionally burdened with weak leadership, limited financing, poor attendance, and a lack of skillful players, the U.S. Baseball League "folded after about one month of action".<sup id="cite_ref-LA84_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA84-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The League tried again in May 1913, with a slightly different medley of teams. Among the newcomers were the Lynchburg Shoemakers, who had been ejected from the Virginia League the season before. They played their home games at the Fairgrounds in Lynchburg, and sported an old-style L on their uniform jerseys. Another entry from the Nation\'s capitol played at Georgetown Park in Georgetown. The revived Washington team lost their season opener at Georgetown Park in an exciting game to the visiting Brooklyn Bandits, 9-8. <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.covehurst.net/ddyte/brooklyn/bandits.html">http://www.covehurst.net/ddyte/brooklyn/bandits.html</a> \nMeanwhile, the Shoemakers also dropped their home opener against Baltimore at the Fairgrounds 7-4. The Baltimore team finished with the best record in the drastically foreshortened 2nd season; as things became unravelled very quickly again for the U.S. League, due in particular to the flop of the New York and Newark teams, who quarreled about the pitiful proceeds from the gate at the opener played in Newark.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Many sports historians view the U.S. Baseball League as "a major precursor to the <a href="/wiki/Federal_League" title="Federal League">Federal League</a> of 1914&#8211;1915".<sup id="cite_ref-LA84_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LA84-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Federal League, which was the last independent major league, was financed by magnates including oil "baron" <a href="/wiki/Harry_F._Sinclair" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry F. Sinclair">Harry F. Sinclair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Suehsdorf_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suehsdorf-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">Teams</span><span
