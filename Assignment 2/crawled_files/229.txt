href="http://www.ios-wildlifetrust.org.uk/">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</b>, formed in 1985, is a <a href="/wiki/The_Wildlife_Trusts_partnership" class="mw-redirect" title="The Wildlife Trusts partnership">wildlife trust</a> covering the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, a group of islands off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It became the 46th member of The Wildlife Trusts in 2001 and is dedicated to ensuring that the archaeological and historical remains on the islands, as well as the flora and fauna, are protected and maintained.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Trust leases all of the uninhabited islands, islets and rocks and much of the untenanted land (including almost all the coast) on the inhabited islands from the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> for a rent of one <a href="/wiki/Daffodil" class="mw-redirect" title="Daffodil">daffodil</a> per year.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The trust previously worked in conjunction with the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Wildlife_Trust" title="Cornwall Wildlife Trust">Cornwall Wildlife Trust</a>, and jointly produced a thrice yearly magazine called <i>Wild Cornwall &amp; Wild Scilly</i> which ended in the Summer 2014 edition. Members are now sent an e-newsletter.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="Isles_of_Scilly_Seabird_Recovery_Project">Isles of Scilly Seabird Recovery Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Isles of Scilly Seabird Recovery Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2013 the Isles of Scilly Seabird Recovery Project was set up by the Duchy of Cornwall, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_for_the_Protection_of_Birds" title="Royal Society for the Protection of Birds">Royal Society for the Protection of Birds</a> (RSPB), <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" class="mw-redirect" title="Isles of Scilly Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Isles of Scilly Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> and the Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust. The five-year project aims to keep the islands of <a href="/wiki/St_Agnes,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Agnes, Isles of Scilly">St Agnes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gugh" title="Gugh">Gugh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brown_rat" title="Brown rat">brown rat</a> (<i>Rattus norvegicus</i>) free, to help breeding sea birds, which lost 25% of their populations between 1983 and 2006. The rats eat eggs and kill the chicks of those birds that nest in burrows or on the ground. Rat removal began in October 2013 by a team of thirty volunteers led by Wildlife Management International Limited (WMIL) of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and there has been no signs of rats on St Agnes and Gugh since December 2013. WMIL will return to the islands to do a final check for rats in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2014 <a href="/wiki/Manx_shearwater" title="Manx shearwater">Manx shearwater</a>  (<i>Puffinus puffinus</i>) bred on both Gugh and St Agnes for the first time in living memory, and a survey of St Agnes in July 2015 found <a href="/wiki/European_storm_petrel" title="European storm petrel">European storm petrel</a> (<i>Hydrobates pelagicus</i>)  at six nests.  A follow-up in early September, to confirm breeding, found storm petrel chicks at each of the sites. Storm petrel also bred on Gugh in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/The_Wildlife_Trusts" title="The Wildlife Trusts">The Wildlife Trusts</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation journal">Gurr, Mike (2008). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cornwallwildlifetrust.org.uk/Resources/Cornwall%20Wildlife%20Trust/PDF%20Documents/Wild_Cornwall_and_Wild_Scilly_magazine_Winter_2008_issue_107.pdf">"A History of the Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust: 1985-2006"</a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>. <i>Wild Cornwall and Wild Scilly</i> (107): 32<span class="reference-accessdate">.
