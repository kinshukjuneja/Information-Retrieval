a professional, <a href="/wiki/Independent_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent league baseball">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> organization located in the <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">Midwestern United States</a>. It operates mostly in cities not served by <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major</a> or <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> teams and is not affiliated with either. The league was formed in 1993, and is the oldest currently running independent league. It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Sauget,_Illinois" title="Sauget, Illinois">Sauget, Illinois</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frontier_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description and history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Teams in the Frontier League must recruit and sign their own players, who usually are undrafted college players or one-time prospects who have been released by their teams. Frontier League rules limit teams to three "veterans" (those with three or more years of professional experience), two two-year players and seven one-year players (those with at least 150 professional at-bats or 50 innings pitched). The other half of the 24-man roster is confined to rookies. Players cannot be older than 27 as of January 1.\n</p><p>Pay in the Frontier League is minimal. As of the 2013 season, each team has a salary cap of $75,000,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the player salaries range from a minimum of $600 up to $1600 a month. Due to the low pay, players typically live with host families and receive meal money during the season.\n</p><p>The first league champions were the <a href="/wiki/Zanesville_Greys" class="mw-redirect" title="Zanesville Greys">Zanesville Greys</a>. Only six teams have won more than one championship: Springfield in 1996 and 1998; Johnstown in 1995 (as the <a href="/wiki/Johnstown_Johnnies" class="mw-redirect" title="Johnstown Johnnies">Steal</a>) and in 2000 (as the <a href="/wiki/Johnstown_Johnnies" class="mw-redirect" title="Johnstown Johnnies">Johnnies</a>), <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Indiana" title="Richmond, Indiana">Richmond</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Traverse_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Traverse City">Traverse City</a>) in 2001 and 2002, Windy City in 2007 and 2008, and the Evansville Otters in 2006 and 2016. The <a href="/wiki/Florence_Freedom" title="Florence Freedom">Florence Freedom</a> (including years as Erie and Johnstown), the <a href="/wiki/Rockford_RiverHawks" class="mw-redirect" title="Rockford RiverHawks">Rockford RiverHawks</a> (including years as Portsmouth and Springfield), and the <a href="/wiki/Schaumburg_Boomers" title="Schaumburg Boomers">Schaumburg Boomers</a> are currently tied for the most league championships with each franchise winning three.\n</p><p>On June 20, 2000, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Tollberg" title="Brian Tollberg">Brian Tollberg</a> debuted with the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>, becoming the first player from the Frontier League to make it to the Majors. A week later <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Burkhart" title="Morgan Burkhart">Morgan Burkhart</a> made his debut with the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>.\n</p><p>Although the league does not have any teams located in the same city as Major League teams, it does nonetheless have teams located within the markets of Major League teams. <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> (<a href="/wiki/Joliet_Slammers" title="Joliet Slammers">Joliet Slammers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schaumburg_Boomers" title="Schaumburg Boomers">Schaumburg Boomers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Windy_City_ThunderBolts" title="Windy City ThunderBolts">Windy City ThunderBolts</a>) has three teams and <a href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gateway_Grizzlies" title="Gateway Grizzlies">Gateway Grizzlies</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/River_City_Rascals" title="River City Rascals">River City Rascals</a>) has two teams while <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie_Crushers" title="Lake Erie Crushers">Lake Erie Crushers</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a> (<a href="/wiki/Florence_Freedom" title="Florence Freedom">Florence Freedom</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> (<a href="/wiki/Washington_Wild_Things" title="Washington Wild Things">Washington Wild Things</a>) each have one. The Wild Things in particular have been able to market themselves as a successful alternative to the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> due to the latter franchise\'s long stretch of losing seasons, which lasted from the Frontier League\'s founding in 1993 until 2013 when the Pirates finished with a record of 94\xe2\x80\x9368.<sup
