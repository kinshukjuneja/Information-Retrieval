Chicago White Sox celebrate after defeating the Minnesota Twins 1\xe2\x80\x930 to win the 2008 American League Central.</div></div></div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Tiebreaker" title="Tiebreaker">tie-breaker</a> is required in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) when two or more teams are tied at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Regular_season" class="mw-redirect" title="Regular season">regular season</a> for a playoff position including a <a href="/wiki/Pennant_(sports)" title="Pennant (sports)">pennant</a> (prior to the introduction of the League Championship Series in 1969), a division title, or a <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_wild_card" title="Major League Baseball wild card">wild card</a> spot. Both the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American</a> (AL) and <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National Leagues</a> (NL) currently use a <a href="/wiki/One-game_playoff" title="One-game playoff">one-game playoff</a> format for <span class="nowrap">tie-breakers</span>, although the NL used a <a href="/wiki/Playoff_format" title="Playoff format">best-of-three</a> series prior to 1969, when the leagues were split into divisions. Fourteen <span class="nowrap">tie-breakers</span>&#8212;ten single-game and four series&#8212;have been played in MLB history. In <a href="/wiki/Baseball_statistics" title="Baseball statistics">baseball statistics</a>, <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span> games count as regular season games with all events in them counted towards regular season statistics. This can have implications on statistical races, such as when <a href="/wiki/Matt_Holliday" title="Matt Holliday">Matt Holliday</a> won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_batting_champions" title="List of Major League Baseball batting champions">batting average</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_annual_runs_batted_in_leaders" title="List of Major League Baseball annual runs batted in leaders">runs batted in titles</a> thanks in part to his performance in the <a href="/wiki/2007_NL_Wild_Card_tie-breaker" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 NL Wild Card tie-breaker">2007</a> <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-battitle_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-battitle-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Home-field advantage for <span class="nowrap">tie-breakers</span> was determined by a coin flip through the 2008 season, after which performance-based criteria, such as head-to-head record of the tied teams, were put in place.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Although there have been no situations requiring a <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span> between more than two teams it is possible. In <a href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2007 Major League Baseball season">2007</a>, for example, the <a href="/wiki/2007_Philadelphia_Phillies_season" title="2007 Philadelphia Phillies season">Philadelphia Phillies</a>, <a href="/wiki/2007_New_York_Mets_season" title="2007 New York Mets season">New York Mets</a>, <a href="/wiki/2007_San_Diego_Padres_season" title="2007 San Diego Padres season">San Diego Padres</a>, <a href="/wiki/2007_Colorado_Rockies_season" title="2007 Colorado Rockies season">Colorado Rockies</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2007_Arizona_Diamondbacks_season" title="2007 Arizona Diamondbacks season">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> finished the season within two games of one another.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The possibility existed for as many as four teams to be locked in a series of <span class="nowrap">tie-breakers</span> that year to decide the NL East, West, and Wild Card.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, late in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2012 Major League Baseball season">2012 season</a> the possibility existed for the <a href="/wiki/2012_New_York_Yankees_season" title="2012 New York Yankees season">New York Yankees</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Baltimore_Orioles_season" title="2012 Baltimore Orioles season">Baltimore Orioles</a>, and either the <a href="/wiki/2012_Texas_Rangers_season" title="2012 Texas Rangers season">Texas Rangers</a> or <a href="/wiki/2012_Oakland_Athletics_season" title="2012 Oakland Athletics season">Oakland Athletics</a> to all finish with the same record. This could have required the teams to play a complex set of multiple games to determine divisional and wild card winners, a situation which <a href="/wiki/Jayson_Stark" title="Jayson Stark">Jayson Stark</a> described as potentially "baseball\'s worst scheduling nightmare."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Major_League_Baseball_tie-breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The first <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span>, <a href="/wiki/1946_National_League_tie-breaker_series" title="1946 National League tie-breaker series">held in 1946</a>, decided the winner of the NL pennant between the <a href="/wiki/1946_St._Louis_Cardinals_season" title="1946 St. Louis Cardinals season">St. Louis Cardinals</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1946_Brooklyn_Dodgers_season" title="1946 Brooklyn Dodgers season">Brooklyn Dodgers</a>, who had finished the season tied at 96\xe2\x80\x9358.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Cardinals won the series in two games and went on to win the <a href="/wiki/1946_World_Series" title="1946 World Series">1946 World Series</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> one of four <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span> winners who have gone on to win the <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a>.  Three <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span> games have gone into <a href="/wiki/Extra_innings" title="Extra innings">extra innings</a>: the decisive second game of the 1959 series, the 2007 Wild Card <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span>, and the 2009 game.<sup id="cite_ref-1959t_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1959t-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2007t_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2007t-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2009t_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2009t-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The 2008 <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span>, a 1\xe2\x80\x930 victory for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Chicago_White_Sox_season" title="2008 Chicago White Sox season">White Sox</a>, was the lowest scoring game, while the 2007 match-up with 17 total runs was the highest scoring. The Dodgers franchise has participated in six <span class="nowrap">tie-breakers</span>, twice while the team was based in Brooklyn and four times in Los Angeles, the most for any team. <a href="/wiki/Dodger_Stadium" title="Dodger Stadium">Dodger Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ebbets_Field" title="Ebbets Field">Ebbets Field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fenway_Park" title="Fenway Park">Fenway Park</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Polo_Grounds" title="Polo Grounds">Polo Grounds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wrigley_Field" title="Wrigley Field">Wrigley Field</a> are the only venues which have hosted multiple <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span> games. Both games at the Polo Grounds came in the 1951 series.\n</p><p>One of the most famous moments in MLB history came in the final game of the 1951 National League <span class="nowrap">tie-breaker</span> series. Entering the bottom of the ninth inning the <a href="/wiki/1951_New_York_Giants_(MLB)_season" title="1951 New York Giants (MLB) season">New York Giants</a> were trailing the Dodgers 4\xe2\x80\x931. <a href="/wiki/Al_Dark" class="mw-redirect" title="Al Dark">Al Dark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Don_Mueller" title="Don Mueller">Don Mueller</a> each singled to put <a href="/wiki/Base_running" title="Base running">runners</a> on <a href="/wiki/First_baseman" title="First baseman">first</a> and <a href="/wiki/Third_baseman" title="Third baseman">third base</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1951t_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1951t-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Whitey_Lockman" title="Whitey Lockman">Whitey Lockman</a> hit a <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a>, scoring Dark to make the game 4\xe2\x80\x932. Finally, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Thomson" title="Bobby Thomson">Bobby Thomson</a> hit a <a href="/wiki/Walk-off_home_run" title="Walk-off home run">walk-off home run</a> which has come to be known as the "<a href="/wiki/Shot_Heard_%27Round_the_World_(baseball)" title="Shot Heard &#39;Round the World (baseball)">Shot Heard \'Round the World</a>" to give the Giants the 5\xe2\x80\x934 victory and the National League pennant. <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/SportsCentury" title="SportsCentury">SportsCentury</a></i> ranked it as the second greatest game of the 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The 2000s saw three years of consecutive one-run tiebreaker games. The Colorado Rockies stormed back from a 2-run deficit in the 13th in 2007, winning 9\xe2\x80\x938 in a surprising run to the World Series that year. In 2008, a Jim Thome home run and a stellar performance by John Danks helped the White Sox edge out the Twins 1\xe2\x80\x930. The Twins ended up on the winning side the following year, tying the game in the 10th after going down a run and then walking off in the 12th inning.\n</p><p>In 2018, two tiebreakers were played to conclude the season for the first time in MLB history. The NL West was decided between the Los Angeles Dodgers and the Colorado Rockies, while the NL Central was decided between the Milwaukee Brewers and the Chicago Cubs. The Dodgers and Brewers, the winners of these games, advanced to the NLDS, while the losers played in the Wild Card game.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The two 2018 tie-breaker losers were the first to advance to the postseason despite losing a tie-breaker game. The unusual circumstance of two tie-breakers was made possible by all four teams winning their final game of the season (just 6% odds, although the same circumstance could have been achieved with all four teams losing their final game, or with the Cubs and Brewers winning and the Dodgers and Rockies losing or with the Cubs and Brewers losing and the Dodgers and Rockies winning), thus finishing with an identical record as their divisional counterpart.\n</p><p>Despite one team playing on their home field, tie-breakers have not favored the home team statistically, with the home team having gone 11-11 since the first tie-breaker game was played.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Key">Key</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Major_League_Baseball_tie-breakers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Key">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable plainrowheaders">\n<caption>Key to the tie-breakers\n</caption>\n<tbody><tr>\n<th scope="row">Title\n</th>\n<td>The pennant, division, or Wild Card which the tie-breaker decided.
