Council</b> is the local authority of <a href="/wiki/Bournemouth" title="Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, England. The council is now a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Unitary authorities in England">unitary authority</a>, although between 1974 and 1997 it was an administrative <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">district council</a> with Dorset. Previously most of the borough was part of <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_cou_hist_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_cou_hist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Borough can trace its history back to 27 August 1890 when the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal Borough">Municipal Borough</a> of Bournemouth was created by <a href="/wiki/Boroughs_incorporated_in_England_and_Wales_1882_-_1974#1890_-_1899" class="mw-redirect" title="Boroughs incorporated in England and Wales 1882 - 1974">Royal Charter</a>. On 1 April 1900 it received <a href="/wiki/County_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="County Borough">County Borough</a> status which lasted until 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_cou_hist_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_cou_hist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In February 2018 the \'Future Dorset\' plan was approved by the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Housing,_Communities_and_Local_Government" title="Secretary of State for Housing, Communities and Local Government">Secretary of State for Housing, Communities and Local Government</a> <a href="/wiki/Sajid_Javid" title="Sajid Javid">Sajid Javid</a>, which means that Bournemouth, <a href="/wiki/Christchurch,_Dorset" title="Christchurch, Dorset">Christchurch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poole_Borough_Council" title="Poole Borough Council">Poole</a> borough councils will be merged into one <a href="/wiki/Bournemouth,_Christchurch_and_Poole" title="Bournemouth, Christchurch and Poole">singular unitary authority</a> in April 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Bournemouth_Arms_on_BIC_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1504608.jpg/330px-Bournemouth_Arms_on_BIC_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1504608.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Bournemouth_Arms_on_BIC_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1504608.jpg/440px-Bournemouth_Arms_on_BIC_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1504608.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bournemouth_Arms_on_BIC_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1504608.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> of Bournemouth</div></div></div>\n<p>The arms of  Bournemouth were granted on 24 March 1891. The crest (above the shield) consists of four English roses surmounted by a pine tree. The motto (below the shield) is <i>Pulchritudo et Salubritas</i>, Latin for "beauty and health". The colours of the shield, the main part of the coat of arms, are taken from the royal arms of <a href="/wiki/King_Edward_the_Confessor" class="mw-redirect" title="King Edward the Confessor">King Edward the Confessor</a>, in whose royal estate the area now known as Bournemouth was situated. The four <a href="/wiki/Salmon" title="Salmon">salmon</a> represent those to be found in the <a href="/wiki/River_Stour,_Dorset" title="River Stour, Dorset">River Stour</a>, which marks the boundary between <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a> and Bournemouth. Each of the lions holds a rose between its paws. The six birds, also taken from Edward the Confessor\'s arms, are martlets, heraldic birds with no legs (based on the folk belief that swallows never stopped flying and so did not need legs). The roses in the arms are emblems both of England and of Hampshire, which Bournemouth historically belonged to.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bournemouth_Borough_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-BBC_cou_hist-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_cou_hist_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_cou_hist_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Bournemouth Borough Council. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070911001519/http://www.bournemouth.gov.uk/Residents/Historical_Bournemouth/council_history.asp">"History of the Council"</a>.
