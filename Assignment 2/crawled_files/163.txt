\xe2\x80\x93 stylised as <b>O<sub>2</sub></b>) is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_telephone_operating_companies#United_Kingdom" title="List of telephone operating companies">telecommunications services provider in the United Kingdom</a>, owned by the Spanish multinational <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telef\xc3\xb3nica">Telef\xc3\xb3nica</a>, and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Slough" title="Slough">Slough</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> With 25 million subscribers (as of December 2017),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> O2 is the second-largest <a href="/wiki/List_of_mobile_network_operators_of_Europe#United_Kingdom" title="List of mobile network operators of Europe">mobile network operator in the United Kingdom</a> after <a href="/wiki/EE_Limited" title="EE Limited">EE</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Vodafone_UK" title="Vodafone UK">Vodafone</a> in third place.\n</p><p>O2 was formed in 1985 as <b>Cellnet</b>, a 60:40 <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">BT Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Securicor" title="Securicor">Securicor</a>. In 1999, BT Group acquired Securicor\'s share of Cellnet and the company was later rebranded as <b>BT Cellnet</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In June 2000, BT Cellnet launched the world\'s first commercial <a href="/wiki/General_Packet_Radio_Service" title="General Packet Radio Service">General Packet Radio Service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> BT Cellnet, together with BT Group\'s mobile telecommunications businesses in Germany, Ireland and the Netherlands, was part of the BT Wireless division. This was spun off from the BT Group in 2002 to form a new holding company, mmO2 plc, which introduced the new "O2" brand for the businesses. In 2005, mmO2 plc was renamed O2 plc.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>O2 plc was purchased by the Spanish telecommunications company Telef\xc3\xb3nica in 2006 for \xc2\xa318&#160;billion. Under the terms of the acquisition, Telef\xc3\xb3nica agreed to retain the "O2" brand and the company\'s UK headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> O2 plc became <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica_Europe" title="Telef\xc3\xb3nica Europe">Telef\xc3\xb3nica Europe</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input id="1985_to_2005">1985 to 2005</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1985 to 2005">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Between 1985 and 1989, John Carrington was the <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> of <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">British Telecom</a>\'s Mobile Division and the Chairman of Cellnet. It was during this period that Carrington launched Cellnet\'s first cellular service, following the innovative development work by BT Spectrum, who initiated the chain of cells between London Heathrow and BT Tower in January 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Cellnet was established in 1985 as a joint subsidiary of BTCR, British Telecom Cellular Radio, providing the engineering knowledge, and TSCR, Telecom Securicor Cellular Radio Limited, providing the financial investment, resulting in a 60:40 <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">British Telecommunications</a> and <a href="/wiki/Securicor" title="Securicor">Securicor</a>.\n</p><p>The equipment used was primarily a <a href="/wiki/Motorola" title="Motorola">Motorola</a> system initially designed for the American <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Mobile_Phone_System" title="Advanced Mobile Phone System">Advanced Mobile Phone System</a> (AMPS) and had to be adapted for the British system, <a href="/wiki/Total_Access_Communication_System" title="Total Access Communication System">Total Access Communication</a> (TACS). The system was so unready that the initials that Motorola used to designate the network exchanges, EMX, became popularly known as \'European Motorola Experiment\' and the exchanges had to be programmed in machine code loaded by tape. In the early days of the system, mobile calls cost \xc2\xa31 per minute.<sup id="cite_ref-secretdeal_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-secretdeal-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After months of rumours and speculation, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Bonfield" title="Peter Bonfield">Peter Bonfield</a> publicly announced on 27 July 1999 that BT had agreed to buy Securicor\'s 40 per cent share of Cellnet for \xc2\xa33.15 billion. Cellnet had five million customers at the time of its acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-secretdeal_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-secretdeal-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The company was later rebranded as BT Cellnet<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2015)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, and it became a part of BT Wireless, a group of companies owned by BT.\n</p><p>BT announced on 3 September 2001 that the BT Wireless business would be spun off from the main group as a newly listed holding company, mmO2 plc, operating under the "O2" brand.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Shareholders approved the plan at an <a href="/wiki/Extraordinary_general_meeting" title="Extraordinary general meeting">extraordinary general meeting</a> on 23 October 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>BT Cellnet relaunched as "O2" on 18 June 2002, along with other former BT subsidiaries: <a href="/wiki/O2_(Ireland)" title="O2 (Ireland)">Esat Digifone</a> in Ireland, <a href="/wiki/O2_(Germany)" class="mw-redirect" title="O2 (Germany)">Viag Interkom</a> in Germany and <a href="/wiki/Telfort" title="Telfort">Telfort Mobiel</a> in the Netherlands. The rebranding was supported by a European advertising campaign, which began on 16 April 2002, across all four countries, at a cost of \xc2\xa3130 million. The main launch campaign ran from 18 June and was developed by Vallance Carruthers Coleman Priest, working alongside brand consultancy <a href="/wiki/Lambie-Nairn" title="Lambie-Nairn">Lambie-Nairn</a>, creators of the "O2" brand identity.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2005, mmO2 restructured its shares and the company was relisted as O2 plc.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Telef.C3.B3nica_acquisition"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Telef\xc3\xb3nica_acquisition">Telef\xc3\xb3nica acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Telef\xc3\xb3nica acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 30 November 2005, O2 agreed to a takeover by <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telef\xc3\xb3nica">Telef\xc3\xb3nica</a>, a Spanish telecommunications company, for \xc2\xa317.7&#160;billion (\xc2\xa32 per share) in cash. It went through finally in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> According to the merger announcement, O2 retained its name and continued to be based in the United Kingdom, keeping both the brand and the management team. The merger became unconditional on 23 January 2006.\n</p><p>Following the acquisition of O2, Telef\xc3\xb3nica undertook a corporate organisational change that saw the merging of its fixed and mobile businesses in Spain, and the transfer of Telef\xc3\xb3nica\'s non-Spanish European telecommunications properties into the O2 brand. Thus, the <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%BD_Telecom" class="mw-redirect" title="\xc4\x8cesk\xc3\xbd Telecom">\xc4\x8cesk\xc3\xbd Telecom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eurotel" title="Eurotel">Eurotel</a> operations in the Czech Republic as well as the <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica_Germany" title="Telef\xc3\xb3nica Germany">Telef\xc3\xb3nica Deutschland</a> business in Germany were brought under the control of O2, which retained its UK-registered public company status with its own board of directors and corporate structures and processes. Telef\xc3\xb3nica chose to keep its existing mobile phone operations in the rest of the world under the brand <a href="/wiki/Movistar" title="Movistar">Movistar</a>.  This name is used in Spain and in most of the Latin American countries, operated by a separate management team.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Since_2007:_diversification">Since 2007: diversification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Since 2007: diversification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 15 July 2009, O2 entered the <a href="/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">financial services</a> industry with the launch of <a href="/wiki/O2_Money" title="O2 Money">O2 Money</a>, which was the first step in the process of incorporating financial services into <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a>. Future plans included manufacturing <a href="/wiki/Near_Field_Communication" class="mw-redirect" title="Near Field Communication">Near Field Communication</a> (NFC) technology in mobile phones in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>O2 and <a href="/wiki/Vodafone_UK" title="Vodafone UK">Vodafone</a> signed a deal in June 2012 which will see the two companies \'pool\' their network technology, creating a single national grid of 18,500 <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone_transmitter" class="mw-redirect" title="Mobile phone transmitter">transmitter</a> sites. Both networks will continue to carry their own independent mobile spectrum.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2012, O2 had to apologise to almost 8 million customers after a <a href="/wiki/Network_switching_subsystem" title="Network switching subsystem">network switching subsystem</a> failure led to a 24-hour blackout of the service across the UK and Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The problem, which prevented a third of its customers\' phones registering on the network, also affected customers of <a href="/wiki/Mobile_virtual_network_operator" title="Mobile virtual network operator">MVNO</a> networks <a href="/wiki/Tesco_Mobile" title="Tesco Mobile">Tesco Mobile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giffgaff" title="Giffgaff">Giffgaff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> To apologise for this, O2 announced that it would be giving hundreds of thousands of its customers compensation for the issue.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Pay monthly customers received a 10 per cent discount on their bill whereas Pay As You Go users received a 10 per cent refund on their first top up in September.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sale_attempts">Sale attempts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ka-shing</a>, owner of <a href="/wiki/Hutchison_Whampoa" title="Hutchison Whampoa">Hutchison Whampoa</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hutchison_3G" class="mw-redirect" title="Hutchison 3G">Three</a> network (which operates in the UK) entered talks to buy O2 for up to \xc2\xa310.25bn ($15.4bn). However, the move faced tough scrutiny from competition regulators as it would reduce the number of major operators in the UK, when combined with a potential purchase of EE by BT, from four to three.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Hutchison Whampoa had previously acquired the O2 network from Telef\xc3\xb3nica in the Republic of Ireland, which it has since merged into Three.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The combined network would have surpassed EE to create the largest mobile network in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-Hutchison_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hutchison-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The deal was subject to regulatory approval.<sup id="cite_ref-Hutchison_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hutchison-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The European Commission decided in December 2015 not to refer Hutchison\'s takeover of Telef\xc3\xb3nica\'s O2 business in the UK to the country\'s <a href="/wiki/Competition_and_Markets_Authority" title="Competition and Markets Authority">Competition and Markets Authority</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> which had asked to be allowed to investigate the planned acquisition, arguing that as the British competition regulator, it \xe2\x80\x93 and not the Commission \xe2\x80\x93 should have the right to rule on the \'transaction\', which it argued \'threatens to affect significantly competition in the UK retail mobile and wholesale mobile markets\', claiming that its investigation of the takeover would \'avoid duplication and fragmentation\'.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 11 May 2016, the European Commission officially blocked the tie-up of O2 and Three, arguing that the merger would reduce consumer choice and lead to a 2002</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="O2_Priority_and_O2_Refresh">O2 Priority and O2 Refresh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: O2 Priority and O2 Refresh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>"O2 Priority" offers benefits to customers including early access to tickets for events at the O2 Arena and O2 Academy venues.\n</p><p>"O2 Refresh", introduced in 2013, splits monthly billing into two parts so customers pay for their device (phone or tablet) separately from their airtime. From 2017, customers can change their airtime tariff up or down, once a month, during the first 24 months of their contract.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorships">Sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Sponsorships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>O2 currently sponsor the <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England rugby team</a>, and in 2003 launched a mobile video service allowing customers to download or stream video content related to the <a href="/wiki/2003_Rugby_World_Cup" title="2003 Rugby World Cup">2003 Rugby World Cup</a>. In 2005, Telef\xc3\xb3nica O2 extended their involvement in <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a>, signing a new deal with the England rugby team and the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a>, as well as O2 rugbyclass and Premier Rugby Ltd for the <a href="/wiki/English_Premiership_(rugby_union)" class="mw-redirect" title="English Premiership (rugby union)">English Premiership</a>.\n</p><p>Additionally, Telef\xc3\xb3nica O2 had a long-standing relationship with <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal F.C.</a>, being their shirt sponsor until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Arsenal_F.C._season" title="2005\xe2\x80\x9306 Arsenal F.C. season">2005/2006</a> season. In 2005, a three-year deal was signed that saw Telef\xc3\xb3nica O2 become the team\'s exclusive mobile communications partner.\n</p><p>O2 were the main sponsor of the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> reality TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Brother_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Brother (UK)">Big Brother</a></i> from its <a href="/wiki/Big_Brother_2_(UK)" title="Big Brother 2 (UK)">second series</a> (as BT Cellnet) in 2001 until its <a href="/wiki/Big_Brother_4_(UK)" title="Big Brother 4 (UK)">fourth series</a> in 2003. They also sponsored the spin-off shows <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother_2_(UK)" title="Celebrity Big Brother 2 (UK)">Celebrity Big Brother 2</a></i> in 2002 and <i><a href="/wiki/Teen_Big_Brother:_The_Experiment" title="Teen Big Brother: The Experiment">Teen Big Brother</a></i> in 2003. In total O2 sponsored five series of the show.\n</p><p>O2 sponsored music festivals, the <a href="/wiki/Wireless_Festival" title="Wireless Festival">Wireless Festivals</a>, from their inception in 2005 until 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming_rights">Naming rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Naming rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_O2_Arena,_entrance.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/The_O2_Arena%2C_entrance.jpg/220px-The_O2_Arena%2C_entrance.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/The_O2_Arena%2C_entrance.jpg/330px-The_O2_Arena%2C_entrance.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/The_O2_Arena%2C_entrance.jpg/440px-The_O2_Arena%2C_entrance.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_O2_Arena,_entrance.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The O2 entrance</div></div></div>\n<p>O2 is the sponsor of <a href="/wiki/The_O2" title="The O2">The O2</a> in London.  Since its launch in 2007 the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Dome" title="Millennium Dome">Millennium Dome</a> has been transformed and rebranded by O2 into an entertainment venue. O2 customers can get <i>Priority Tickets</i> up to 48 hours before they are released to the general public. The O2 offers exclusive areas for O2 customers, including the <i>blueroom</i> bar and the <i>O2 lounge</i>. Once inside, O2 customers can change their experience of these areas by dictating the wallpaper designs and jukebox requests through text. O2 also contributed \'O2 angels\', a team of people trained to greet and direct visitors. Other smaller touches include changing the lighting, carpets and seats in the arena to the colour blue.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/The_O2_Arena" title="The O2 Arena">The O2 Arena</a> was the site of the artistic gymnastics events and medal rounds in basketball for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.\n</p><p>In February 2017, in a continuation of its agreement with <a href="/wiki/Anschutz_Entertainment_Group" title="Anschutz Entertainment Group">AEG</a>, the developers of the site, O2 announced that it had renewed its naming rights for a further ten years.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Academy_venues">Academy venues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a O2 attained the standard after saving 47,000 tonnes of carbon over the previous three years through its energy efficiency measures, including a \xc2\xa31.4&#160;million distribution of <a href="/wiki/Smart_meter" title="Smart meter">smart metering technology</a> across the company\'s <a href="/wiki/Cell_site" title="Cell site">cell sites</a>, offices and retail stores, and upgrades to more energy efficient systems across its <a href="/wiki/Cellular_network" title="Cellular network">mobile phone network</a>. In addition to distributing energy efficient <a href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode" title="Light-emitting diode">LED</a> lighting and <a href="/wiki/Boiler" title="Boiler">boiler system controls</a>, the company was also able to reduce energy use by removing <a href="/wiki/Air_conditioning" title="Air conditioning">air conditioning</a> units from some of its cell sites and reducing <a href="/wiki/Computer_monitor" title="Computer monitor">computer monitor</a> standby times.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>O2 is a voluntary participant in the <a href="/wiki/10:10" title="10:10">10:10</a> climate change campaign, which required participants to cut their carbon emissions by 10 per cent by the end of 2010, and has since broadened its approach to include a range of projects focused on carbon reduction and <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=O2_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In October 2017, an inquiry was launched in the UK concerning overcharging customers for handset sales integrated with the data and call charges. Responding to questions, O2\'s CEO indicated that the company\'s billing allows for separation of handset charges and telephony/data charges.
