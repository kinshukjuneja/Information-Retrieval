href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=BBC_Radio_Cornwall&amp;params=50_15_45.579_N_5_2_48.0516_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">50.26266083\xc2\xb0N 5.046681000\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">50.26266083; -5.046681000</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>\n</p><p><b>BBC Radio Cornwall</b> is the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Local_Radio" title="BBC Local Radio">BBC Local Radio</a> service for <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It broadcasts from its studios on Phoenix Wharf in <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a> on 95.2 in the east, 96.0 on the Isles of Scilly and 103.9 in the west MHz <a href="/wiki/FM_broadcasting" title="FM broadcasting">FM</a>, as well as on <a href="/wiki/Digital_Audio_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital Audio Broadcasting">DAB</a>.\n</p><p>Prior to its launch on 17 January 1983, BBC regional radio broadcasting for Cornwall amounted to the breakfast show <i><a href="/wiki/Morning_Sou%27West" title="Morning Sou&#39;West">Morning Sou\'West</a></i> on the AM frequencies of Radio 4 in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> and Cornwall. Initially, Radio Cornwall shared an afternoon programme with <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Devon" title="BBC Radio Devon">BBC Radio Devon</a>, but now sustains a full daytime service. The station also broadcasts a short weekly news bulletin in the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish language</a>. As well as broadcasting on FM, BBC Radio Cornwall may also be streamed via internet at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio/player/bbc_radio_cornwall">BBC Radio Cornwall Online</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Cornish_language"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Cornish language</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Programming"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Programming</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Notable_past_presenters"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Notable past presenters</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cornish_language">Cornish language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Radio_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cornish language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Radio Cornwall is one of two radio stations to have broadcast programmes in the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish language</a>. Currently a five-minute news show, An Nowodhow, is broadcast every Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>When Radio Cornwall was first set up Cornish language content was limited to around 2 minutes per week. In 1987, a new weekly 15-minute-long bilingual show, Kroeder Kroghan, detailing Celtic cultural events taking place in Cornwall, was introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Radio_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The majority of the station\'s programming is produced and broadcast from Truro. During off-peak hours, BBC Radio Cornwall also carries regional programming for the South West and West regions, produced from sister stations <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Devon" title="BBC Radio Devon">BBC Radio Devon</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Bristol" title="BBC Radio Bristol">BBC Radio Bristol</a>. As with all BBC Local Radio stations, it also airs the networked weekday evening shows, originating from <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Leeds" title="BBC Radio Leeds">BBC Radio Leeds</a> and produced independently by Wire Free Productions. During the station\'s downtime, BBC Radio Cornwall simulcasts <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_5_Live" title="BBC Radio 5 Live">BBC Radio 5 Live</a> overnight.\n</p><p>The station\'s local presenters include Julie Skentelbery and Pam Spriggs (weekday breakfast), David White (weekday mornings), Laurence Reed (weekday lunchtime), Debbie McCrory (weekday afternoons) and Martin Bailie (weekday drive time).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_past_presenters">Notable past presenters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
