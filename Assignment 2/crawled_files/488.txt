Chief Vexillologist and Trustee of the Flag Institute</div></div></div>\n<p>The Institute was formed out of the Flag Section of <a href="/wiki/The_Heraldry_Society" title="The Heraldry Society">The Heraldry Society</a> on <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St George&#39;s Day">St George\'s Day</a>, 23 April 1971, by <a href="/wiki/William_Crampton" title="William Crampton">William Crampton</a>, later President of <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_Vexillological_Associations" title="International Federation of Vexillological Associations">FIAV</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Kingsley_(yacht_designer)#Evarne" title="Charles Kingsley (yacht designer)">Captain E.M.C. Barraclough</a>, CBE RN, as its chairman.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is a membership-based <a href="/wiki/Vexillological" class="mw-redirect" title="Vexillological">vexillological</a> organisation with over 500 members from all parts of the world, and provides advice and assistance to individuals and organisations including <a href="/wiki/UK_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Government">UK Government</a> departments, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITN" title="ITN">ITN</a>, and many publishers, museums and libraries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Institute maintains the <a href="/wiki/William_Crampton_Library" title="William Crampton Library">William Crampton Library</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Kingston upon Hull</a>, England and named in honour of its co-founder. Comprising over 40,000 flag related volumes it is thought to be one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Vexillology" title="Vexillology">vexillology</a>-based libraries in the world. It publishes a quarterly 24-page full colour journal, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flagmaster&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Flagmaster (page does not exist)">Flagmaster</a></i>, and a virtual magazine called <i>eFlags</i>. Since 2006 it has sponsored an annual public lecture on a flag-related topic, known as the \'<a href="/wiki/William_Gordon_Perrin" title="William Gordon Perrin">Perrin</a> Lecture\'. It holds twice yearly meetings for its members in various locations around the United Kingdom. The Institute itself is governed by a Board of five elected Trustees who are advised by an appointed Council of members.\n</p><p>The Flag Institute was congratulated by the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Communities and Local Government">Secretary of State for Communities and Local Government</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Pickles" title="Eric Pickles">Eric Pickles</a>, on its first 40 years of service to the United Kingdom. In May 2011 he said:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>Let me start by congratulating the Flag Institute on its first 40 years. 40 years as a respected source of help and advice. Not only to the UK Government, but to the United Nations and other organisations around the world. 40 years of bringing together enthusiasts, educating the public, and spreading knowledge.....The UK is very lucky in having \xe2\x80\x93 in the Institute \xe2\x80\x93 a group of dedicated and informed people who do a great deal to make sure that that respect is given. In your first 40 years you have established your credentials not just in this country, but on the world stage.</p></blockquote>\n<p>Following a postal ballot of members, the Institute became a charity at its annual general meeting in Worcester on Saturday 17 November 2012.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Constitution">Constitution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Constitution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Flag Institute\'s constitution<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> states that its objectives are;\n</p>\n<ul><li>to maintain its role as the United Kingdom\'s recognised <a href="/wiki/Flag" title="Flag">flag</a> organisation;</li>\n<li>to unite all those with a professional or personal interest in any aspect of flags and vexillology;</li>\n<li>to educate and increase public awareness of flags and their usage;</li>\n<li>to provide advice and guidance on flags and related matters to the public and organisations, both governmental and non-governmental;</li>\n<li>to promote the study of the history, function, usage, symbolism and development of flags of all kinds; and the development of flag terminology, standards of usage, specifications, colours and descriptive terms.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UK_Flag_Registry">UK Flag Registry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: UK Flag Registry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Under the supervision of their Chief Vexillologist <a href="/wiki/Graham_Bartram" title="Graham Bartram">Graham Bartram</a>, the Institute keeps a registry of flags for the countries, regions and counties of the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-registry_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-registry-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Flag designs with which its officers have been involved include those for the badge and ensign of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Border_Agency" title="UK Border Agency">UK Border Agency</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the flag of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Supreme Court">UK Supreme Court</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>All Scottish flags must, by law, be authorised by <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lyon" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Lyon">Lord Lyon</a> and recorded in the Public Register of All Arms and Bearings in Scotland. The Earl Marshal and the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> are legally responsible for the flying of flags on land in the rest of the UK. The College of Arms and the Court of the Lord Lyon maintain official registers of flags.\n</p><p>With the impending <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence_referendum,_2014" title="Scottish independence referendum, 2014">Scottish independence vote</a> in mind, in 2013 the Flag Institute members were asked for suggested designs of a post-independence UK flag. Five designs were showcased in the media to stimulate discussion, several of them incorporating elements of the Welsh <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_St_David" class="mw-redirect" title="Flag of St David">flag of St David</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_no_scots_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_no_scots-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
