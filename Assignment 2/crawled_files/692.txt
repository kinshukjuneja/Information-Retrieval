title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, England. The council is a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority</a>, and is unusual in the United Kingdom in that its executive function is controlled by a <a href="/wiki/Directly_elected_mayor_of_Bristol" class="mw-redirect" title="Directly elected mayor of Bristol">directly elected mayor of Bristol</a>. href="/w/index.php?title=Bristol_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Bristol_City_Council" title="History of Bristol City Council">History of Bristol City Council</a></div>\n<p>The council was formed by the Local Government Act 1972. It was first <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_local_elections,_1973" title="United Kingdom local elections, 1973">elected in 1973</a>, a year before formally coming into its powers and prior to the creation of the non-metropolitan district of <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a> on 1 April 1974.\n</p><p>It was envisaged through the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> that Bristol as a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district_council" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-metropolitan district council">non-metropolitan district council</a> would share power with the <a href="/wiki/Avon_County_Council" title="Avon County Council">Avon County Council</a>. This arrangement lasted until 1996 when Avon County Council was abolished and Bristol City Council gained responsibility for services that had been provided by the county council.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_composition">Political composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bristol_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayor">Mayor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bristol_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mayor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Bristol" title="Mayor of Bristol">mayor of Bristol</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Bristol#2016" title="Mayor of Bristol">2016 mayoral election</a> is <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Rees" title="Marvin Rees">Marvin Rees</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a>. Rees had previously ran in the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Bristol#2012" title="Mayor of Bristol">first Bristol mayoral election</a>, coming second place to the independent <a href="/wiki/George_Ferguson_(politician)" title="George Ferguson (politician)">George Ferguson</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Councillors">Councillors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bristol_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Councillors">edit</a><span
