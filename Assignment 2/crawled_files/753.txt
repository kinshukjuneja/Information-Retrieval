Lynher</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Linar</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (or <b>St id="Navigation">Navigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Lynher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Navigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Normal_tidal_limit" class="mw-redirect" title="Normal tidal limit">normal tidal limit</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Weir" title="Weir">weir</a> upstream of <a href="/wiki/Notter_Bridge" class="mw-redirect" title="Notter Bridge">Notter Bridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/St_Germans,_Cornwall" title="St Germans, Cornwall">St Germans</a> is a historic fishing village situated on the River Tiddy just upstream of its confluence with the Lynher. The <a href="/wiki/Quay_Sailing_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay Sailing Club">Quay Sailing Club</a> are based at St Germans Quay. The <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Harbour_Master" title="Queen&#39;s Harbour Master">Queen\'s Harbour Master</a> for Plymouth<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is responsible for managing navigation on the tidal Lynher and its tributaries (up to the normal tidal limits).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Lynher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The river is about 21 miles (34&#160;km long), rising at a height of about 920 feet (280m) on <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a> and flowing into the Tamar Estuary near <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>. It has four main tributaries, the largest of which is the <a href="/wiki/River_Tiddy" title="River Tiddy">River Tiddy</a>. The smaller tributaries include <a href="/wiki/Deans_Brook" title="Deans Brook">Deans Brook</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Withey_Brook&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Withey Brook (page does not exist)">Withey Brook</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marke_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marke Valley (page does not exist)">Marke Valley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darleyford" title="Darleyford">Darleyford</a> streams and Kelly Brook.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CRP_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Lynher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Lynher and its tributaries are largely used for rod and line fishing, particularly for <a href="/wiki/Brown_trout" title="Brown trout">brown trout</a> and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_salmon" title="Atlantic salmon">Atlantic salmon</a>. \n</p><p>Its estuary is a designated <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and also a <a href="/wiki/Special_Protection_Area" title="Special Protection Area">Special Protection Area</a>. Many species of animals can be found here, including the <a href="/wiki/Kingfisher" title="Kingfisher">kingfisher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otter" title="Otter">otter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dipper" title="Dipper">dipper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avocet" title="Avocet">avocet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black-tailed_godwit" title="Black-tailed godwit">black-tailed godwit</a>; the triangular club-rush (<a href="/wiki/Scirpus" title="Scirpus">scirpus</a>) also grows on the banks. The estuary, along with the <a href="/wiki/Tamar%E2%80%93Tavy_Estuary" title="Tamar\xe2\x80\x93Tavy Estuary">Tamar\xe2\x80\x93Tavy Estuary</a> system and <a href="/wiki/St_John,_Cornwall" title="St John, Cornwall">St John\'s Lake</a>, form the <i>Tamar Estuaries Complex</i> <a href="/wiki/Special_Protection_Area" title="Special Protection Area">Special Protection Area</a> (SPA),<sup id="cite_ref-SPA_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPA-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> as well as forming part of the <i><a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Sound" title="Plymouth Sound">Plymouth Sound</a> and Estuaries</i> <a href="/wiki/Special_Area_of_Conservation" title="Special Area of Conservation">Special Area of Conservation</a> (SAC).<sup id="cite_ref-SAC_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SAC-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Park Wood, in the Lyhner valley 2 miles (3.2&#160;km) south-west of <a href="/wiki/Callington,_Cornwall" title="Callington, Cornwall">Callington</a> off the <a href="/wiki/A390_road" title="A390 road">A390 road</a>, is also designated an SSSI for its <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">biological</a> interest.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
