href="http://www.fibs.it">www.fibs.it</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Italian Baseball League</b> (IBL; <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i><i lang="it" title="Italian language text">Campionato italiano di baseball</i></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball_league" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball league">league</a> that is governed by <b>FIBS</b> (Italian Baseball &amp; Softball Federation), which has its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. The IBL is a <a href="/wiki/Baseball_bat" title="Baseball bat">wood bat</a> league in which both composite and aluminum bats are prohibited; the official ball of the IBL is the <i><a href="/wiki/Wilson_Sporting_Goods" title="Wilson Sporting Goods">Wilson</a> 1010 Italy</i>. The overall level of the competition is considered the highest in Europe.\n</p><p>Until 2010, the IBL featured a league format that demoted the last place finisher to the minor leagues (Series A2), while the Series A2 champion would be promoted into the IBL.  However, in late 2009 FIBS approved the decision to eliminate the <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion and relegation</a> system starting with the 2010 season and thus now applies a fixed-team <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports_league_organization" title="Professional sports league organization">franchise</a> format similar to that found in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>.\n</p><p>The current IBL consists of eight teams, each contesting 42 games; a team plays two 3-game series against every other team. The four teams that finish with the best regular season record qualify for a <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin</a> playoff. The first and second-place finishers of the round-robin are cast into the best-of-7-games <b>Italian Baseball Series</b> and compete for the championship, referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Scudetto" title="Scudetto">Scudetto</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" id="IBL_players">IBL players</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Italian_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: IBL players">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>For the 2010 season, FIBS maintained the number of sport visas granted to non-<a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> import foreign players at 4 per team. Per EU and Olympic Committee directive, EU citizens will have the same player rights of Italian passport holders. FIBS, however, will continue to enforce the regulation on the Italian School of Baseball that imposes 6 ASI players be on the field at all times of every game, including the pitcher in either game 2 or 3 of each series.\n</p><p>An Italian player is classified as Italian School of Baseball, or ASI, only if he has been developed in Italy or if has played 6 seasons in the Italian Leagues.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/EU_citizen" class="mw-redirect" title="EU citizen">EU citizens</a> have the same rights of the players who hold Italian citizenship but were not developed in Italy.\n</p><p>One particular case of the ASI rule would be a foreigner who developed as a baseball player in Italy (started playing as an U15). This is the case of a foreigner who is also an ASI, but will be able to play only respecting the limitation on import players.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Three-game_series_format">Three-game series format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Italian_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Three-game series format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Game 1 of each three-game series is classified as the <i>Foreign Affair Game</i>, with the pitcher\'s mound free to be occupied by any pitcher including foreign imports.\n</p><p>In either Game 2 or Game 3, an EU pitcher may be the starting pitcher; however, IBL rules require that an ASI be the starting pitcher for one of these two remaining games.  Moreover, whichever game a manager selects the ASI as the starting pitcher, then all subsequent pitchers must be ASI.  Therefore, there may be ASI vs EU match-ups for the first time beginning in 2010.\n</p><p>Half of the teams are based in the <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a> region of Italy.  A team from the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_San_Marino" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of San Marino">Republic of San Marino</a> also plays in the IBL.  Like <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Japanese baseball</a>, many of the official team names contain the name of the team\'s corporate
