href="http://Freeview.co.uk">Freeview.co.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Freeview</b> is the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Digital terrestrial television in the United Kingdom">digital terrestrial television</a> platform. It is operated by <b>DTV Services Ltd</b>, a joint venture between the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_plc" title="ITV plc">ITV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">Sky</a> and transmitter operator <a href="/wiki/Arqiva" title="Arqiva">Arqiva</a>. It was launched in 2002,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> taking over the licence from <a href="/wiki/ITV_Digital" title="ITV Digital">ITV Digital</a> which collapsed that year. The service provides consumer access via an <a href="/wiki/Television_antenna" title="Television antenna">aerial</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television_in_the_United_Kingdom#The_multiplexes" title="Digital terrestrial television in the United Kingdom">six DTT multiplexes</a> covering the United Kingdom. In April 2014 it had some 60 <a href="/wiki/DVB-T" title="DVB-T">DVB-T</a> TV channels, 26 digital radio channels, 10 HD channels, six text services, 11 streamed channels, and one interactive channel.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A number of new HD channels launched in 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> from a new group of multiplexes awarded to Arqiva.<sup id="cite_ref-OfcomJuly2013_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfcomJuly2013-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The new HD channels were launched in selected areas on 10 December 2013 with a further roll-out during 2014.\n</p><p>DTV Services\' delivery of <a href="/wiki/Standard-definition_television" title="Standard-definition television">standard-definition television</a> and radio is labelled <b>Freeview</b>, while its delivery of <a href="/wiki/HDTV" class="mw-redirect" title="HDTV">HDTV</a> is called <b>Freeview HD</b>. Reception of Freeview requires a Freeview <a href="/wiki/Tuner_(television)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tuner (television)">tuner</a>, either in a separate <a href="/wiki/Set-top_box" title="Set-top box">set-top box</a> or built into the TV set. Since 2008 all new TV sets sold in the United Kingdom have a built-in Freeview tuner.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Freeview HD requires a HDTV-capable tuner. <a href="/wiki/Digital_video_recorder" title="Digital video recorder">Digital video recorders</a> (DVRs) with a built-in Freeview tuner are labelled <b>Freeview+</b>. Depending on model, DVRs and HDTV sets with a Freeview tuner may offer standard Freeview or Freeview HD.\n</p><p>The technical specification for Freeview is published and maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Digital_TV_Group" title="Digital TV Group">Digital TV Group</a>, the industry association for digital TV in the UK which also provide the test and conformance regime for Freeview, Freeview + and Freeview HD products. DMOL (DTT Multiplex Operators Ltd.), a company owned by the operators of the six DTT multiplexes (BBC, ITV, C4, and Arqiva) is responsible for technical platform management and policy, including the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_program_guide" title="Electronic program guide">electronic programme guide</a> and channel numbering.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since the completion of the <a href="/wiki/Digital_switchover_dates_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Digital switchover dates in the United Kingdom">digital switch-over</a> on 24 October 2012, there is no terrestrial <a href="/wiki/Analogue_television" class="mw-redirect" title="Analogue television">analogue television</a> being broadcast in the United Kingdom, and all broadcast terrestrial TV is delivered through digital terrestrial television.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" href="/wiki/History_of_Freeview_UK" title="History of Freeview UK">History of Freeview UK</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Freeview_logo.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/29/Freeview_logo.svg/220px-Freeview_logo.svg.png" width="220" height="92" href="/wiki/File:Freeview_logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo used from 2006 to 2015</div></div></div>\n<p>Freeview officially launched on 30 October 2002 at 5 am, when the BBC and Crown Castle (now <a href="/wiki/Arqiva" title="Arqiva">Arqiva</a>) officially took over the <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television" title="Digital terrestrial television">digital terrestrial television</a> (DTT) licences to broadcast on the three <a href="/wiki/Multiplex_(TV)" class="mw-redirect" title="Multiplex (TV)">multiplexes</a> from the defunct <a href="/wiki/ITV_Digital" title="ITV Digital">ITV Digital</a>. The founding members of DTV Services, who trade as Freeview, were the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, Crown Castle UK (now part of <a href="/wiki/Arqiva" title="Arqiva">Arqiva</a>) and <a href="/wiki/British_Sky_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="British Sky Broadcasting">British Sky Broadcasting</a>. On 11 October 2006, <a href="/wiki/ITV_plc" title="ITV plc">ITV plc</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> became equal shareholders. Since then, the Freeview model has been copied in <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(Australia)" title="Freeview (Australia)">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(New_Zealand)" title="Freeview (New Zealand)">New Zealand</a>.\n</p><p>Although all pay channels had been closed down on ITV Digital, many free-to-air channels continued broadcasting, including the five analogue channels and digital channels such as <a href="/wiki/ITV2" title="ITV2">ITV2</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITN_News_Channel" class="mw-redirect" title="ITN News Channel">ITN News Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/S4C2" class="mw-redirect" title="S4C2">S4C2</a>, <a href="/wiki/TV_Travel_Shop" title="TV Travel Shop">TV Travel Shop</a> and <a href="/wiki/QVC_(UK)" title="QVC (UK)">QVC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> With the launch of Freeview other channels were broadcast free-to-air, such as: <a href="/wiki/Sky_Travel" title="Sky Travel">Sky Travel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yesterday_(TV_channel)" title="Yesterday (TV channel)">UK History</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a>, The Hits (now <a href="/wiki/4Music" title="4Music">4Music</a>) and TMF (now <a href="/wiki/Viva_(UK_%26_Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Viva (UK &amp; Ireland)">Viva</a>) were available from the start. <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Red_Button" title="BBC Red Button">the interactive BBC streams</a> were moved to multiplex B.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Under the initial plans, the two multiplexes operated by <a href="/wiki/Crown_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown Castle">Crown Castle</a> would carry eight channels altogether. The seventh stream became shared by <a href="/wiki/UK_Bright_Ideas" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Bright Ideas">UK Bright Ideas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ftn_(TV_channel)" title="Ftn (TV channel)">Ftn</a> which launched in February 2003. The eighth stream was left unused until April 2004 when the shopping channel <a href="/wiki/Ideal_World" title="Ideal World">Ideal World</a> launched on Freeview. There are now 14 streams carried by the two multiplexes, with Multiplex C carrying 6 streams, and Multiplex D carrying 8. It has recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (January 2014)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> been announced that more streams are now available on the multiplexes, and that bidding is under way.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_retune">2009 retune</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeview_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2009 retune">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Freeview service underwent a major upgrade on 30 September 2009, which required 18 million households to retune their Freeview receiving equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The changes, meant to ensure proper reception of <a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(UK)" title="Channel 5 (UK)">Channel 5</a>, led to several thousand complaints from people who lost channels (notably <a href="/wiki/ITV3" title="ITV3">ITV3</a> and <a href="/wiki/ITV4" title="ITV4">ITV4</a>) as a result of retuning their equipment. The Freeview website crashed and the call centre was inundated as a result of the problems. The change involved an update to the NIT (Network Information Table), which some receivers could not accommodate. Many thousands of people could not receive some channels. For all transmissions indoor, loft-mounted, and external aerials are available. In regions of strong signal an indoor aerial may be adequate; in marginal areas a high-gain external aerial mounted high above the ground with an electronic <a href="/wiki/Amplifier" title="Amplifier">amplifier</a> at its top may be needed.\n</p><p>Aerial requirements for analogue (the old standard) and digital reception in the UK are identical; there is no such thing as a special "digital aerial", although installers and suppliers often falsely say one is necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> As the signal degrades, the analogue picture degrades gradually, but the <a href="/wiki/Cliff_effect" title="Cliff effect">digital picture holds up well then suddenly becomes unwatchable</a>; an aerial which gave poor analogue viewing may give unwatchable, rather than poor, digital viewing, and need replacing, at a cost of typically \xc2\xa380 to \xc2\xa3180, most of which is fitting cost. Or if the size permits an external aerial may be fitted indoors on top of a closet or a similar object of the signal is adequate. <sup id="cite_ref-caifaq_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-caifaq-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeview_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Digital TV Group, the industry association for digital television in the UK, is responsible for co-ordination between Freeview and other digital services.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The original Freeview was later expanded with additional facilities (Freeview+), high-definition channels (Freeview HD), and Internet connectivity (Freeview Play). An electronic programme guide was available. Freeview channels are not encrypted and can be received by anyone in the UK. The Top Up TV service was not connected with the Freeview service, but ran alongside it on the DTT platform and was included in the Freeview EPG; programmes could be received on some Freeview set-top boxes and televisions equipped with a card slot or <a href="/wiki/Common_Interface" title="Common Interface">CI</a> slot. Top Up TV was replaced in 2006, by a service that did not run on Freeview equipment.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Freeview_HD">Freeview HD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeview_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Freeview HD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Freeview HD comprises a number of high-definition versions of existing channels. The service was broadcast to all regions by the end of 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4 HD</a> commenced test broadcasts on 25 March 2010 with an animated caption, ahead of its full launch on 30 March 2010, coinciding with the commercial launch of Freeview HD.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/S4C" title="S4C">S4C Clirlun</a> launched on 30 April 2010, in Wales, where Channel 4 HD did not broadcast.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/STV_(TV_channel)#STV_HD" title="STV (TV channel)">STV HD</a> launched in Scotland, where ITV HD does not broadcast, on 6 June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> S4C Clirlun closed on 1 December 2012, allowing Channel 4 HD to begin broadcasting in Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(UK)" title="Channel 5 (UK)">Five HD</a> was due to launch during 2010 but was unable to reach \'key criteria\' to keep its slot.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Spare allocation on multiplex B was handed over to the BBC, two years from the date when it was anticipated that further capacity on multiplex B would revert to the control of the BBC Trust. On 3 November 2010, <a href="/wiki/BBC_One#BBC_One_HD" title="BBC One">BBC One HD</a> launched on Freeview HD.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Initially it was available in addition to the existing BBC HD channel, which continued to show the "best of the rest" of the BBC in HD. provide an additional HD service from between 28 November 2011 and 1 April 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Channel 5 HD was the sole applicant,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> with the aim of launching in spring or early summer 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 15 December 2011, Channel 5 dropped its bid to take the fifth slot after being unable to resolve "issues of commercial importance".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Subject to any future Ofcom decision to re-advertise the slot, the capacity will remain with the BBC and can be used by it for BBC services or services provided by a third party via a commercial arrangement.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The BBC temporarily used the space to broadcast a high definition simulcast of their main Freeview <a href="/wiki/BBC_Red_Button" title="BBC Red Button">red button</a> feed for the duration of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> followed by a channel from Channel 4 for the <a This provided additional spectrum in the 600Mhz band for additional DVB-T2 multiplexes, reaching up to 70% of the UK population.<sup id="cite_ref-OfcomJuly2013_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OfcomJuly2013-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, the BBC announced that they would provide five new HD channels due to the newly available capacity: BBC Three HD, BBC Four HD, CBBC HD, CBeebies HD and BBC News HD.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> BBC Three HD and CBBC HD launched to all viewers on 10 December 2013 using the capacity released by the Red Button HD service, and the other BBC channels launched in some regions, expanding to 70%  UK coverage by June 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical">Technical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeview_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Technical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Digital_TV_Group" title="Digital TV Group">Digital TV Group</a> publishes and maintains the UK technical specification for high-definition services on digital terrestrial television (Freeview) based on the new DVB-T2 standard. The specification is known as the <a href="/wiki/D-book" title="D-book">D-book</a>. Freeview HD is the first operational TV service in the world using the <a href="/wiki/DVB-T2" title="DVB-T2">DVB-T2</a> standard. This standard is incompatible with DVB-T, and can only be received using compatible reception equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Some television receivers sold before the HD launch claimed to be "HD-ready", but this usually implies that the screen can display HD, rather than that DVB-T2 signals can be received&#160;&#8211;&#32; a suitable tuner (typically built into a STB or PVR) is additionally required. Freeview HD set-top boxes and televisions are available. To qualify for the Freeview HD logo, receivers will need to be <a href="/wiki/IPTV" title="IPTV">IPTV</a>-capable and display Freeview branding, including the logo, on the electronic programme guide screen.<sup id="cite_ref-Freeview_HD_goes_live_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freeview_HD_goes_live-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The Freeview HD trademark requirements state that any manufacturer applying for the Freeview HD logo should submit their product to the <a href="/wiki/Digital_TV_Group" title="Digital TV Group">Digital TV Group</a>\'s test centre (DTG Testing) for conformance testing.\n</p><p>On 2 February 2010, <a href="/wiki/Vestel" title="Vestel">Vestel</a> became the first manufacturer to gain Freeview HD certification, for the Vestel T8300 set top box.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Humax" title="Humax">Humax</a> released the first Freeview HD reception equipment, the Humax HD-FOX T2, on 13 February 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It was announced on 10 February 2009, that the signal would be encoded with <a href="/wiki/H.264/MPEG-4_AVC" title="H.264/MPEG-4 AVC">MPEG-4 AVC</a> High Profile Level 4, which supports up to 1080i30/1080p30, so 1080p50 cannot be used.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The system has been designed from the start to allow regional variations in the broadcast schedule. Services are statistically multiplexed&#160;&#8211;&#32; bandwidth is dynamically allocated between channels, depending on the complexity of the images&#160;&#8211;&#32; with the aim of maintaining a consistent quality, rather than a specific bit rate. Video for each channel can range between 3&#160;Mbit/s and 17&#160;Mbit/s. <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Audio_Coding" title="Advanced Audio Coding">AAC</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dolby_Digital_Plus" title="Dolby Digital Plus">Dolby Digital Plus</a> audio is transmitted at 384&#160;<a href="/wiki/Kb/s" class="mw-redirect" title="Kb/s">kb/s</a> for 5.1 surround sound, with stereo audio at 128\xe2\x80\x93192&#160;kbit/s; audio description takes up 64&#160;kbit/s, subtitles 200&#160;kbit/s and the data stream, for interactive applications 50&#160;kbit/s.<sup id="cite_ref-Freeview_HD_goes_live_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freeview_HD_goes_live-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Recording sizes for Freeview HD television transmissions average around 3&#160;GB per hour.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Between 22 and 23 March 2011, an encoder software change allowed the Freeview version of BBC HD to automatically detect progressive material and change encoding mode appropriately, meaning the channel can switch to 1080p25.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> This was extended to all of the other Freeview HD channels in October 2011.\n</p><p>To ensure provision of audio description, broadcasters typically use the AAC codec.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Hardware restrictions allow only a single type of audio decoder to operate at any one time, so the main audio and the audio description must use the same encoding family for them to be successfully combined at the receiver. In the case of BBC HD, the main audio is coded as AAC-LC and only the audio description is encoded as <a href="/wiki/High-Efficiency_Advanced_Audio_Coding" title="High-Efficiency Advanced Audio Coding">HE-AAC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Neither AAC nor Dolby Digital Plus codecs are supported by most home AV equipment, which typically accept <a href="/wiki/Dolby_Digital" title="Dolby Digital">Dolby Digital</a> or <a href="/wiki/DTS_(sound_system)" title="DTS (sound system)">DTS</a>, leaving owners with stereo, rather than <a href="/wiki/Surround_sound" title="Surround sound">surround sound</a>, output. Transcoding from AAC to Dolby Digital or DTS and multi-channel output via <a href="/wiki/HDMI" title="HDMI">HDMI</a> was not originally necessary for Freeview HD certification. As of June 2010 the <a href="/wiki/Digital_TV_Group" title="Digital TV Group">DTG</a> D-Book includes the requirement for mandatory transcoding when sending audio via <a href="/wiki/S/PDIF" title="S/PDIF">S/PDIF</a>, and for either transcoding or multi-channel <a href="/wiki/Pulse-code_modulation" title="Pulse-code modulation">PCM</a> audio when sending it via HDMI in order for manufacturers to gain Freeview HD certification from April 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Thus equipment sold as Freeview HD before April 2011 may not deliver surround sound to audio equipment (some equipment may, but this is not mandatory); later equipment must be capable of surround sound compatible with most suitable audio equipment.\n</p><p>In early February 2011, it was announced that one million Freeview HD set-top boxes had been sold.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Copy_protection">Copy protection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeview_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Copy protection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In August 2009 the BBC wrote to Ofcom after third-party content owners asked the BBC to undertake measures to ensure that all Freeview HD boxes would include <a href="/wiki/Copy_protection" title="Copy protection">copy protection</a> systems as required by the Digital TV Group\'s D-Book, which sets technical standards for digital terrestrial television in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The BBC proposed to ensure compliance with copy-protection standards on the upgraded Freeview HD multiplex by compressing the service information (SI) data, which receivers need to understand the TV services in the data stream. To encourage boxes to adopt copy protection, the BBC made its own look-up tables and decompression algorithm, necessary for decoding the EPG data on high-definition channels, available without charge only to manufacturers who implement the copy-protection technology. This technology would control the way HD films and TV shows are copied onto, for example Blu-ray discs, and shared with others over the internet. No restrictions will be placed on standard-definition services. In a formal written response, Ofcom principal advisor Greg Bensberg said that wording of the licence would probably need to be changed to reflect the fact that this new arrangement is permitted. The BBC had suggested that as an alternative to the SI compression scheme, the Freeview HD multiplex may have to adopt encryption. Bensberg said that it would appear "inappropriate to encrypt public service broadcast content on DTT".\n</p><p>On 14 June 2010, Ofcom agreed to allow the BBC to limit the full availability of its own and other broadcasters\' high definition (HD) Freeview services to receivers that control how HD content can be used.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Ofcom concluded that the decision to accept the BBC\'s request will deliver net benefits to licence-holders by ensuring they have access to the widest possible range of HD television content on DTT.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Freeview_HD_Recorder">Freeview HD Recorder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeview_(UK)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Freeview HD Recorder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b>Freeview HD Recorder</b> (formerly <b>Freeview+</b>, originally named <b>Freeview Playback</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a updated in standby (e.g. scheduled recording starting early)</li>\n<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accurate_Recording&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Accurate Recording (page does
