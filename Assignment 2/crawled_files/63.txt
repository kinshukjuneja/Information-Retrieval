Church, Oxford</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Francis Godolphin Osborne, 5th Duke of Leeds</b>, <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:100%;"><a href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Order_of_the_Garter" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight of the Order of the Garter">KG</a>,&#32;<a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">PC</a></span> (29 January 1751 \xe2\x80\x93 31 January 1799), styled <b>Marquess of Carmarthen</b> until 1789, was a British politician. He notably served as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Foreign_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs">Foreign Secretary</a> under <a href="/wiki/William_Pitt_the_Younger" title="William Pitt the Younger">William Pitt the Younger</a> from 1783 to 1791. He also was <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Scilly Isles">Governor of Scilly</a>. As a statesman he is generally regarded as a failure, and his deep hostility to the newly  independent United States of America damaged relations between the two countries.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" id="Background_and_education">Background and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Osborne,_5th_Duke_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Carmarthen was the only surviving son of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Osborne,_4th_Duke_of_Leeds" title="Thomas Osborne, 4th Duke of Leeds">Thomas Osborne, 4th Duke of Leeds</a>, by his wife <a href="/wiki/Mary_Osborne,_Duchess_of_Leeds" title="Mary Osborne, Duchess of Leeds">Lady Mary</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin,_2nd_Earl_of_Godolphin" title="Francis Godolphin, 2nd Earl of Godolphin">Francis Godolphin, 2nd Earl of Godolphin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Henrietta_Godolphin,_2nd_Duchess_of_Marlborough" title="Henrietta Godolphin, 2nd Duchess of Marlborough">Henrietta Godolphin, 2nd Duchess of Marlborough</a>. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Westminster_School" title="Westminster School">Westminster School</a> and at <a href="/wiki/Christ_Church,_Oxford" title="Christ Church, Oxford">Christ Church, Oxford</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Osborne,_5th_Duke_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Carmarthen was a <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Eye_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Eye (UK Parliament constituency)">Eye</a> in 1774 and for <a href="/wiki/Helston_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Helston (UK Parliament constituency)">Helston</a> from 1774 to 1775; in 1776 having received a <a href="/wiki/Writ_of_acceleration" title="Writ of acceleration">writ of acceleration</a> as <a href="/wiki/Baron_Osborne" class="mw-redirect" title="Baron Osborne">Baron Osborne</a>, he entered the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a>, and in 1777 Lord Chamberlain of <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">the Queen</a>\'s Household and <a href="/wiki/Captain_of_Deal_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="Captain of Deal Castle">Captain of Deal Castle</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a> he was prominent as a determined foe of the prime minister, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_North,_Lord_North" title="Frederick North, Lord North">Lord North</a>, who, after he had resigned his position as chamberlain, deprived him of the office of <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_the_East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of the East Riding of Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant of the East Riding of Yorkshire</a> in 1780. He regained this, however, two years later.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Early in 1783 Carmarthen was selected as ambassador to France, but he did not take up this appointment, becoming instead <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Foreign_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs">Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs</a> under <a href="/wiki/William_Pitt_the_Younger" title="William Pitt the Younger">William Pitt the Younger</a> in December of the same year.  Historian <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Black_(historian)" title="Jeremy Black (historian)">Jeremy Black</a> says that in terms of foreign policy, Pitt  and other leaders were disappointed with his performance as a minister. Leeds, as he became upon his father\'s death in 1789, was anti-French but did not develop an active and aggressive foreign policy. Instead, King George III himself set the main lines of foreign policy before he became  mentally disabled.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Pitt\'s rejection of Leeds\'  anti-Russian policy was the final blow and he left office in April 1791.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Leeds had done nothing to foster good relations with the newly independent <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a>: two future Presidents,  <a href="/wiki/John_Adams" title="John Adams">John Adams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a>, as envoys from the United States, both complained of his obstructive attitude and "aversion to having anything to do with us".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While Adams, who was rather  <a href="/wiki/Anglophile" title="Anglophile">Anglophile</a>  by inclination, was prepared to forgive and forget, Jefferson was not, and it can be argued that Leeds\'s only lasting achievement was to foster Jefferson\'s implacable hostility as <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> to Great Britain and its rulers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Subsequently Leeds took little part in politics: in 1792, hearing rumours that a new coalition might be formed, he unwisely offered himself as its  head and met with a firm rebuff from both Pitt and the King.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Osborne,_5th_Duke_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_Osborne.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Coat_of_arms_of_Osborne.jpg/200px-Coat_of_arms_of_Osborne.jpg" width="200" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c5/Coat_of_arms_of_Osborne.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="225" data-file-height="200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_Osborne.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ancestral arms of the Osborne family, Dukes of Leeds</div></div></div>\n<p>Leeds married firstly in 1773 <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Osborne,_Marchioness_of_Carmarthen" title="Amelia Osborne, Marchioness of Carmarthen">Lady Amelia Darcy</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Darcy,_4th_Earl_of_Holderness" title="Robert Darcy, 4th Earl of Holderness">Robert Darcy, 4th Earl of Holderness</a> on 29 November 1773. Lady Amelia became Baroness Darcy de Knayth and Baroness Conyers in her own right in 1778. They were divorced in 1779. Their marriage produced three children:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/George_Osborne,_6th_Duke_of_Leeds" title="George Osborne, 6th Duke of Leeds"><i>George</i> William Frederick Osborne, Marquess of Carmarthen</a> (21 July 1775 \xe2\x80\x93 10 July 1838), later 6th Duke of Leeds; married Lady Charlotte Townshend, daughter of the <a href="/wiki/George_Townshend,_1st_Marquess_Townshend" title="George Townshend, 1st Marquess Townshend">1st Marquess Townshend</a>, on 17 August 1797 and had issue.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Francis_Osborne,_1st_Baron_Godolphin" title="Francis Osborne, 1st Baron Godolphin">Lord <i>Francis</i> Osborne</a> (18 October 1777 \xe2\x80\x93 15 February 1850), later 1st Baron Godolphin; married The Hon. Elizabeth Eden, third daughter of the <a href="/wiki/William_Eden,_1st_Baron_Auckland" title="William Eden, 1st Baron Auckland">1st Baron Auckland</a>, on 31 March 1800 and had issue.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Mary_Pelham,_Countess_of_Chichester" title="Mary Pelham, Countess of Chichester">Lady <i>Mary</i> Henrietta Juliana Osborne</a> (1776\xe2\x80\x931862); married <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pelham,_2nd_Earl_of_Chichester" title="Thomas Pelham, 2nd Earl of Chichester">Thomas Pelham, 2nd Earl of Chichester</a> (28 April 1756 \xe2\x80\x93 4 July 1826) in 1801 and had issue.</li></ul>\n<p>He married secondly <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Osborne,_Duchess_of_Leeds" title="Catherine Osborne, Duchess of Leeds">Catherine</a>, daughter of Thomas Anguish, in 1788 and had two more children: \n</p>\n<ul><li>Lord Sidney Godolphin Osborne (1789\xe2\x80\x931861); unmarried.</li>\n<li>Lady Catherine Anne Sarah Osborne (1791\xe2\x80\x931878); married <a href="/wiki/George_Whyte-Melville#Life_and_work" title="George Whyte-Melville">Major John Whyte-Melville</a> on 1 June 1819 and had issue.   .</li></ul>\n<p>Leeds died in London in January 1799, aged 48, and was buried in the Osborne family chapel at <a href="/wiki/All_Hallows_Church,_Harthill,_South_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="All Hallows Church, Harthill, South Yorkshire">All Hallows Church, Harthill, South Yorkshire</a>. He was succeeded in the dukedom by his eldest son from his first marriage, <a href="/wiki/George_Osborne,_6th_Duke_of_Leeds" title="George Osborne, 6th Duke of Leeds">George Osborne, 6th Duke of Leeds</a>. His second son from his first marriage, <a href="/wiki/Francis_Osborne,_1st_Baron_Godolphin" title="Francis Osborne, 1st Baron Godolphin">Lord Francis Osborne</a>, was created <a href="/wiki/Baron_Godolphin" title="Baron Godolphin">Baron Godolphin</a> in 1832. The dowager Duchess of Leeds died in October 1837, aged 73.
