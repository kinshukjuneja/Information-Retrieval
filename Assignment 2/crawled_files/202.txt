International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[so\xcb\x88fia \xcb\x88\xc9\xa3omes u\xcb\x88\xc9\xbei\xce\xb2e]</a></span>; born 15 April 1992)<sup id="cite_ref-Gomez_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gomez-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a Colombian <a href="/wiki/Freediver" class="mw-redirect" title="Freediver">freediver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">civil engineer</a>. She holds six national records in three different freediving disciplines ("<a href="/wiki/Finswimming" title="Finswimming">depth apneas</a>"), a <a href="/wiki/Bolivarian_Games" title="Bolivarian Games">Bolivarian Games</a> record set at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Bolivarian_Games" title="2013 Bolivarian Games">2013 Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Trujillo,_Peru" title="Trujillo, Peru">Trujillo</a>, Peru, that won her a gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/Underwater_sports_at_the_2013_Bolivarian_Games" title="Underwater sports at the 2013 Bolivarian Games">Women\'s individual dynamic apnea free-diving event</a>, and a Pan-American record in <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_apnea" title="Dynamic apnea">dynamic apnea</a> with equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-Tiempo_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tiempo-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sof%C3%ADa_G%C3%B3mez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>G\xc3\xb3mez was born in <a href="/wiki/Pereira,_Colombia" title="Pereira, Colombia">Pereira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Risaralda_Department" title="Risaralda Department">Risaralda</a>, Colombia. Her parents Francisco and M\xc3\xb3nica are from <a href="/wiki/Santa_Marta" title="Santa Marta">Santa Marta</a>. G\xc3\xb3mez attended the Immaculate Heart of Mary School. In 2001, she took up <a href="/wiki/Synchronised_swimming" title="Synchronised swimming">synchronised swimming</a> and in 2002, she took up <a href="/wiki/Finswimming" title="Finswimming">finswimming</a>. After finishing high school, G\xc3\xb3mez moved to <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogot\xc3\xa1">Bogot\xc3\xa1</a> for a year and now lives in <a href="/wiki/Medell%C3%ADn" title="Medell\xc3\xadn">Medell\xc3\xadn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EE_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, she graduated from the <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Colombia_at_Medell%C3%ADn" title="National University of Colombia at Medell\xc3\xadn">National University of Colombia at Medell\xc3\xadn</a>, and she now works as a <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineer" title="Civil engineer">civil engineer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gomez_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gomez-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Colombiano_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colombiano-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, G\xc3\xb3mez setup a fundraising campaign to help people in Dominica affected by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">Hurricane Maria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sof%C3%ADa_G%C3%B3mez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 2013, a friend encouraged G\xc3\xb3mez to take up freediving, a sport that involves <a href="/wiki/Underwater_diving" title="Underwater diving">underwater diving</a> without breathing apparatus. After some training, she managed to dive 40 metres (130&#160;ft), and she decided to take up the sport professionally.<sup id="cite_ref-EE_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In her first year, G\xc3\xb3mez attempted to break the Pan-American record for <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_apnea" title="Dynamic apnea">dynamic apnea</a>, but became unconscious after swimming for 180 metres (590&#160;ft).\n</p><p>In 2014, she successfully broke the dynamic apnea record by swimming for 195.76 metres (642.3&#160;ft) in <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>, Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-Tiempo_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tiempo-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, she broke the Pan-American records for the constant weight with fins and free immersion events at the <a href="/wiki/Vertical_Blue" title="Vertical Blue">Vertical Blue</a> competition.<sup id="cite_ref-Colombiano_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colombiano-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017, she broke the <a href="/wiki/Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques" title="Conf\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Mondiale des Activit\xc3\xa9s Subaquatiques">CMAS</a> Constant Weight Bi-Fins freediving world record after descending 83.1 metres (273&#160;ft) in a time of 2 minutes and 43 seconds, beating Alenka Artnik\'s previous record of 82 metres (269&#160;ft) set the previous year in <a href="/wiki/Ka%C5%9F" title="Ka\xc5\x9f">Ka\xc5\x9f</a>, Turkey.<sup id="cite_ref-EE_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Colombiano_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colombiano-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FDC_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FDC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Two days later, she broke her own world record by diving 84 metres (276&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-EE_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FDC_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FDC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EFE_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EFE-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> At the 2017 CMAS World Championship, Artnik and Ukrainian Nataliia Zharkova both dived 85 metres (279&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2018, G\xc3\xb3mez participated in a scientific study to measure her body\'s functions in extreme conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-EFE_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EFE-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
