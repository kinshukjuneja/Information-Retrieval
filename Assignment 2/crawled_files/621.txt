links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versailles,_Kentucky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Versailles was founded on June 23, 1792, on 80 acres (320,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of land owned by Hezekiah Briscoe, at the time only a child. His guardian, Major Marquis Calmes, named the town after <a href="/wiki/Versailles_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Versailles (city)">Versailles</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, in honor of <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_du_Motier,_marquis_de_Lafayette" class="mw-redirect" title="Gilbert du Motier, marquis de Lafayette">General Lafayette</a>, a family friend. In the <a href="/wiki/Bluegrass_Region" class="mw-redirect" title="Bluegrass Region">Bluegrass Region</a> of Kentucky, which became known for its thoroughbred horses and other livestock, the city was officially incorporated on February 13, 1837. It was briefly occupied during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> by both <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Union_(American_Civil_War)" title="Union (American Civil War)">Union</a> forces.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versailles,_Kentucky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the Inner <a href="/wiki/Bluegrass_Region" class="mw-redirect" title="Bluegrass Region">Bluegrass Region</a>, the area is a center for <a href="/wiki/Horse_breeding" title="Horse breeding">horse breeding</a> and training, for <a href="/wiki/Thoroughbred" title="Thoroughbred">thoroughbred</a> and <a href="/wiki/Standardbred" title="Standardbred">standardbred</a> racehorses, and <a href="/wiki/Saddlebred" class="mw-redirect" title="Saddlebred">saddlebred</a> pleasure horses.\n</p><p>Thoroughbred farms include\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Woodburn_Stud" title="Woodburn Stud">Woodburn Stud</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Lane%27s_End_Farm" title="Lane&#39;s End Farm">Lane\'s End Farm</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/WinStar_Farm" title="WinStar Farm">WinStar Farm</a></li></ul>\n<p>Standardbred farms include Brittany Farms. Lenore Farms is for Saddlebred horses.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Woodford_Reserve_Distillery" class="mw-redirect" title="Woodford Reserve Distillery">Woodford Reserve Distillery</a>, a station on the <a href="/wiki/Bourbon_Trail" class="mw-redirect" title="Bourbon Trail">Bourbon Trail</a>, is located here.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_industry">Film industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versailles,_Kentucky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Most of the small-town scenes in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Elizabethtown_(film)" title="Elizabethtown (film)">Elizabethtown (film)</a></i> (2005) locations were filmed here. Versailles was also the setting of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Dreamer_(2005_film)" title="Dreamer (2005 film)">Dreamer: Inspired by a True Story</a></i> (2005). The cemetery scene in the film, <i><a href="/wiki/Secretariat_(film)" title="Secretariat (film)">Secretariat</a></i> (2010) about one of Kentucky\'s major horses, was filmed at Pisgah Pike Church. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flim-Flam_Man" title="The Flim-Flam Man">The Flim-Flam Man</a></i> (1967) was filmed at several locations near Versailles, of them. The opening sequence was filmed at Paynes Depot and a car chase was filmed on Clifton Rd.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0061678/locations">Flim-Flam Man filming locations</a>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versailles,_Kentucky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 2.8 square miles (7.3&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), all land.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versailles,_Kentucky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="toccolours" style="border-spacing: 1px; The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 2,668.7 per square mile (1,030.4/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 3,330 housing units at an average density of 1,183.2 per square mile (456.8/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 96.18% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.67% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.15% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.35% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.01% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.34% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.30% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 4.29% of the population.\n</p><p>There were 3,160 households out of which 29.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 48.7% were married couples living together, 14.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.2% were non-families. 28.4% of all households were made up of individuals and 11.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.37 and the average family size was 2.89.\n</p><p>23.2% of the population was under the age of 18, 10.1% from 18 to 24, 29.4% from 25 to 44, 23.7% from 45 to 64, and 13.6% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females, there were 88.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 86.8 males.\n</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was <a href="/wiki/US_$" class="mw-redirect" title="US $">US $</a>35,052, and the median income for a family was $41,567. Males had a median income of $31,056 versus $24,488 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $18,489. About 11.1% of families and 14.0% of the population were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 16.0% of those under age 18 and 20.9% of those age 65 or over.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versailles,_Kentucky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_Clay_Stiles_Blackburn" title="Joseph Clay Stiles Blackburn">Joseph Clay Stiles Blackburn</a>, U.S. Congressman, U.S. Senator</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Ben_Chandler" title="Ben Chandler">Ben Chandler</a>, Kentucky Attorney General and former U.S. Congressman</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Happy_Chandler" title="Happy Chandler">Happy Chandler</a>, Kentucky Governor and <a href="/wiki/Commissioner_of_Baseball" title="Commissioner of Baseball">Commissioner of Baseball</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Martha_Layne_Collins" title="Martha Layne Collins">Martha Layne Collins</a>, Kentucky Governor</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/John_Conlee" title="John Conlee">John Conlee</a>, Country music singer</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/John_J._Crittenden" title="John J.
