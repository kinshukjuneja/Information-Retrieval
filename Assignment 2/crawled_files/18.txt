href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in the English county of <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>.  The council covers a largely rural area, with a population of 34,900<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in an area of 740 square kilometres (290&#160;sq&#160;mi);<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_districts_by_population" title="List of English districts by population">least populous</a> <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">non-unitary district</a> in England.  According to figures released by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a> in 2009, the population of West Somerset has the oldest average age in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> at 52.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\nThe largest centres of population are the coastal towns of <a href="/wiki/Minehead" title="Minehead">Minehead</a> (population 10,000) and <a href="/wiki/Watchet" title="Watchet">Watchet</a> (4,400).\n</p><p>The council\'s administrative <a href="/wiki/Headquarters" title="Headquarters">headquarters</a> is located in the village of <a href="/wiki/Williton" title="Williton">Williton</a>, with an additional office in Minehead.\n</p><p>In September 2016, West Somerset and <a href="/wiki/Taunton_Deane" title="Taunton Deane">Taunton Deane</a> councils agreed in principle to merge the districts into one (with one council) subject to consultation.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The new district would not be a unitary authority: it would still be part of the Somerset County Council area.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In March 2018 both councils voted in favour of the merger and it will come into effect on 1st April 2019, with the first elections to the new council in May 2019. The new authority will be known as <a href="/wiki/Somerset_West_and_Taunton" title="Somerset West and Taunton">Somerset West and Taunton</a> Council.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Somerset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The district was formed on 1 April 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, by a merger of the previous <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> of Minehead and <a href="/wiki/Watchet" title="Watchet">Watchet</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Dulverton_Rural_District" title="Dulverton Rural District">Dulverton Rural District</a> and <a href="/wiki/Williton_Rural_District" title="Williton Rural District">Williton Rural District</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Listed_buildings">Listed buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Somerset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Listed buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are 33 Grade I <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed buildings</a> in West Somerset: the oldest are <a href="/wiki/Culbone_Church" title="Culbone Church">Culbone Church</a> (one of the smallest churches in England,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and pre-<a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a> in origin)<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Tarr_Steps" title="Tarr Steps">Tarr Steps</a>, which some say originates in the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, although others date them from around 1400.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dunster" title="Dunster">Dunster</a> has the greatest concentration of Grade I listed buildings including <a href="/wiki/Dunster_Castle" title="Dunster Castle">Dunster Castle</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Yarn_Market,_Dunster" title="Yarn Market, Dunster">Yarn Market</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gallox_Bridge,_Dunster" title="Gallox Bridge, Dunster">Gallox Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Priory_Church_of_St_George,_Dunster" title="Priory Church of St George, Dunster">Priory Church of St George</a>. Other sites include manor houses such as <a href="/wiki/Nettlecombe_Court" title="Nettlecombe Court">Nettlecombe Court</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orchard_Wyndham" title="Orchard Wyndham">Orchard Wyndham</a>. The most recent buildings included in the list are <a href="/wiki/Crowcombe_Court" title="Crowcombe Court">Crowcombe Court</a> which was completed in 1739<sup id="cite_ref-crowcombecourt_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crowcombecourt-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St_John_the_Baptist,_Carhampton" title="Church of St John the Baptist, Carhampton">Church of St John the Baptist</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carhampton,_Somerset" title="Carhampton, Somerset">Carhampton</a> which was rebuilt in 1863.<sup id="cite_ref-stjohncrahampton_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stjohncrahampton-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> There are numerous religious structures in Somerset, with the largest number being <a href="/wiki/Anglican" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> parish churches, dating from <a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a> or <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> eras. services.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Somerset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>West Somerset benefits from one of the most scenic landscapes in England. Nearly two-thirds of the western land area of the district forms part of <a href="/wiki/Exmoor" title="Exmoor">Exmoor National Park</a>, designated in 1954, while on the eastern edge of the district are the <a href="/wiki/Quantock_Hills" title="Quantock Hills">Quantock Hills</a>, which in 1956 became the first <a href="/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> created in the UK. Large areas of Exmoor and the Quantock Hills are also designated as <a href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" class="mw-redirect" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest</a> because of their value for wildlife.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/West_Somerset_Railway" title="West Somerset Railway">West Somerset Railway</a> runs through most of the district, from <a href="/wiki/Bishop%27s_Lydeard" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop&#39;s Lydeard">Bishop\'s Lydeard</a> in the south east to Minehead in the north west. Operated mainly as a tourist attraction, the railway runs mostly <a href="/wiki/Steam_locomotive" title="Steam locomotive">steam</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Diesel_locomotive" title="Diesel locomotive">diesel locomotives</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Somerset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/West_Somerset_District_Council_elections" title="West Somerset District Council elections">West Somerset District Council elections</a></div>\n<p>West Somerset District Council is elected every four years, with currently 28 <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">councillors</a> being elected at each election. Following the <a href="/wiki/West_Somerset_District_Council_election,_2011" title="West Somerset District Council election, 2011">election in 2011</a> the Conservatives regained a majority and
