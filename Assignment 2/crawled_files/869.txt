rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.mlbplayers.com">mlbplayers.com</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Major League Baseball Players Association</b> (or <b>MLBPA</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Collective_bargaining" title="Collective bargaining">collective bargaining</a> representative for all current <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> players. All players, <a href="/wiki/Manager_(baseball)" title="Manager (baseball)">managers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coach_(baseball)" title="Coach (baseball)">coaches</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Athletic_trainer" title="Athletic trainer">athletic trainers</a> who hold or have held a signed contract with a Major League club are eligible for membership in the Association.\n</p><p>The MLBPA has three major divisions: a <a href="/wiki/Labor_union" class="mw-redirect" title="Labor union">labor union</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> (Players Choice Group Licensing Program), and a <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charitable foundation</a> (Major League Baseball Players Trust).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The MLBPA primarily serves as a collective bargaining representative for all <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> players, as well as playing significant roles in MLB-related business and non-profit affairs.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="Players_Choice_group_licensing">Players Choice group licensing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Players Choice group licensing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The MLBPA\'s Players Choice group licensing program utilizes collective marketing to assist licensees and sponsors who want to associate their brands and products with that of Major League players, teams, and coaches. Through an individual agreement with each player, the MLBPA holds exclusive right to use, license and sublicense the names, numbers, nicknames, likenesses, signatures and other personal indicia (known as \xc2\x93publicity rights\xc2\x94) of active Major League Baseball players who are its members for use in connection with any product, brand, service or product line when more than two players are involved.\n</p><p>Among its other functions, the Players Choice licensing program also protects the rights of players from exploitation by unauthorized parties.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_League_Baseball_Players_Trust">Major League Baseball Players Trust</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Major League Baseball Players Trust">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Major League Baseball players also formed the Players Trust, a charitable foundation that is the first of its kind in <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports" title="Professional sports">professional sports</a>. Through the Players Trust, Major Leaguers contribute their time, money and fame to call attention to important issues affecting those in need and to help encourage others to get involved in their own communities.\n</p><p>Many programs including Buses for Baseball, City Clinics, Medicines for Humanity, the <a href="/wiki/Players_Choice_Awards" title="Players Choice Awards">Players Choice Awards</a> and Volunteers of America are funded through the foundation.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Action_Team">Action Team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Action Team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2003, the Major League Baseball Players Trust and <a href="/wiki/Volunteers_of_America" title="Volunteers of America">Volunteers of America</a> created the Action Team National Youth Volunteer Program to recruit and train high school students to become volunteers in their communities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Players_Choice_Awards">Players Choice Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Players Choice Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Players_Choice_Awards" title="Players Choice Awards">Players Choice Awards</a></div>\n<p>The Players Choice Awards is an <a href="/wiki/Award_ceremony" class="mw-redirect" title="Award ceremony">award ceremony</a> held to recognize each season\'s best performers, as chosen by the players themselves. Each Players Choice Awards winner designates the charity of his choice to receive a grant from the Player\'s Trust.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The MLBPA was not the first attempt to unionize baseball players. Earlier attempts had included:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Brotherhood of Professional Baseball Players - 1885 (founded by <a href="/wiki/John_Montgomery_Ward" title="John Montgomery Ward">John Montgomery Ward</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Players\' Protective Association - 1900<sup id="cite_ref-mlbplayers.mlb.com_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlbplayers.mlb.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Fraternity of Professional Baseball Players of America - 1912</li>\n<li>National Baseball Players Association of the United States - 1922 (founded by <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Joseph_Cannon" title="Raymond Joseph Cannon">Raymond Joseph Cannon</a>)</li>\n<li>The American Baseball Guild \xe2\x80\x93 1946 (founded by labor lawyer Robert Murphy)</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Unionized">Pre-Unionized</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pre-Unionized">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>President\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bob_Feller" title="Bob Feller">Bob Feller</a>: 1956&#8211;1959</li></ul>\n<p>Executive Director\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frank_Scott_(player_agent)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Frank Scott (player agent) (page does not exist)">Frank Scott</a>: May 1, 1959 &#8211; 1966</li></ul>\n<h4><span id="The_Marvin_Miller_era_.281966.E2.80.9383.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Marvin_Miller_era_(1966\xe2\x80\x9383)">The Marvin Miller era (1966\xe2\x80\x9383)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Marvin Miller era (1966\xe2\x80\x9383)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The organization that would eventually become the MLBPA was conceived in 1953. However, it was not officially recognized as a union until 1966. That year the newly recognized union hired <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Miller" title="Marvin Miller">Marvin Miller</a> from the <a href="/wiki/United_Steel_Workers_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United Steel Workers of America">United Steel Workers of America</a> to head the organization, serving as Executive Director until 1983. During Miller\'s tenure, base salaries, pension funds, licensing rights and revenues increased.\n</p><p>In 1968, Miller negotiated the first collective bargaining agreement (CBA) with the team owners, which raised the minimum salary from $6,000 to $10,000 per year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The 1970 CBA included <a href="/wiki/Arbitration" title="Arbitration">arbitration</a> to resolve disputes.<sup id="cite_ref-mlbplayers.mlb.com_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlbplayers.mlb.com-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1972 the major leagues saw their first <a href="/wiki/1972_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1972 Major League Baseball strike">player strike</a>, in opposition to the owners\' refusal to increase player pension funds.\n</p><p>In 1974, when owner <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Finley" title="Charlie Finley">Charlie Finley</a> failed to make a $50,000 payment into an insurance annuity as called for in <a href="/wiki/Catfish_Hunter" title="Catfish Hunter">Catfish Hunter</a>\'s contract, the MLBPA took the case to <a href="/wiki/Arbitration" title="Arbitration">arbitration</a>. The arbitrator ruled that Hunter could be a <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Andy_Messersmith" title="Andy Messersmith">Andy Messersmith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dave_McNally" title="Dave McNally">Dave McNally</a> had their 1974 contracts automatically renewed by their teams, the MLBPA supported them by challenging the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_clause" title="Reserve clause">reserve clause</a> which was used by team owners to bind players to one team. On December 23, 1975, arbitrator Peter Seitz ruled in favour of the players. Following the <a href="/wiki/Seitz_decision" title="Seitz decision">Seitz decision</a>, the modern <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a> system was created, and the strength of the union was immeasurably increased.\n</p><p>Players and owners failed to come to terms over free agent compensation, which led to <a href="/wiki/1981_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1981 Major League Baseball strike">another strike</a> in 1981. In the late 1980s and early 1990s the MLBPA filed <a href="/wiki/Baseball_collusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball collusion">collusion</a> charges, arguing that team owners had violated the collective bargaining agreement in the 1985\xe2\x80\x931987 seasons. The MLBPA won each case, resulting in "second look" free agents, and over $269 million in owner fines.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Successors">Successors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Successors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>After Miller retired, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ken_Moffett&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ken Moffett (page does not exist)">Ken Moffett</a> became the new executive director in December 1982, but in November 1983 he was dismissed, and Marvin Miller was named interim director. Donald Fehr was named acting director in December 1983. Miller supported a strong antidrug policy, whereas Fehr opposed penalizing players.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>Ken Moffett: He was 51 years old. <a href="/wiki/Tony_Clark" title="Tony Clark">Tony Clark</a>, the Deputy Executive Director, was named Executive Director on December 2, 2013, the first former major league player to hold the position.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016, the MLBPA celebrated its 50th anniversary as a union, commemorating the event at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">Major League Baseball All-Star Game</a> with a redesigned golden logo and merchandise such as T-shirts.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basic_agreements">Basic agreements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Basic agreements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1968, the Major League Baseball Players Association negotiated the first-ever Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) in professional <a href="/wiki/Sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports">sports</a>. Several agreements have been negotiated since the original agreement, the latest of which expired on Dec. 1, 2016.  A new agreement took effect at that time and is set to expire on Dec. 1, 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="MLBPA.2FMLB_Joint_Initiatives"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="MLBPA/MLB_Joint_Initiatives">MLBPA/MLB Joint Initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: MLBPA/MLB Joint Initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_Drug_Agreement">Joint Drug Agreement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Joint Drug Agreement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Joint Drug Agreement went into effect in December 2011 and is scheduled to terminate Dec. 1, 2016, the same date as the Basic Agreement. The <a href="/wiki/Doping_in_sport" title="Doping in sport">prohibited substances</a> section of the Joint Drug Agreement is updated annually.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_Violence_Policy">Domestic Violence Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Domestic Violence Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In August 2015, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> and the MLBPA reached agreement on the Joint Domestic Violence, Sexual Assault and Child Abuse Policy, and is intended to provide a comprehensive policy addressing issues such as protecting the legal rights of players, treating violations seriously, holding players accountable through appropriate disciplinary measures and providing resources for the intervention and care of victims, families and the players themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The terms of this joint policy cover four primary areas: Treatment &amp; Intervention; Investigations; Discipline; and Training, Education &amp; Resources.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_Baseball_initiative">Youth Baseball initiative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Youth Baseball initiative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In June 2016, Executive Director, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Clark" title="Tony Clark">Tony Clark</a> and Commissioner of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Manfred" title="Rob Manfred">Rob Manfred</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Granderson" title="Curtis Granderson">Curtis Granderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_McCutchen" title="Andrew McCutchen">Andrew McCutchen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marquis_Grissom" title="Marquis Grissom">Marquis Grissom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Griffey_Jr." title="Ken Griffey Jr.">Ken Griffey Jr.</a> announced major initiatives within youth baseball in a press conference held at <a href="/wiki/Citi_Field" title="Citi Field">Citi Field</a>.\n</p><p>On top of jointly donating over $2 million several youth-focused initiatives supported by current and former Major League player, other major initiatives included financial contributions to youth <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> projects and the creation of a partnership with Positive Coaching Alliance for the training of coaches and administrators from the Reviving Baseball in Inner Cities program.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellaneous">Miscellaneous</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Miscellaneous">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Salary_cap">Salary cap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Salary cap">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>As of 2017, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> is the only major professional sports league in North America that does not have a <a href="/wiki/Salary_cap" title="Salary cap">salary cap</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">MLS</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> all implement some sort of salary cap.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Steroids">Steroids</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Steroids">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Banned_substances_in_baseball_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Banned substances in baseball in the United States">Banned substances in baseball in the United States</a></div>\n<p>The MLBPA was initially opposed to random steroid testing, claiming it to be a violation of the privacy of players. After enormous negative publicity surrounding the alleged or actual involvement of several star players in the <a href="/wiki/BALCO" class="mw-redirect" title="BALCO">BALCO</a> steroid scandal, the players dropped their opposition to a steroid testing program and developed a consensus that favored testing. Under pressure from <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">US Congress</a> which threatened to pass a law if the MLB\'s drug policy was not strengthened, the baseball union agreed in 2005 to a stricter policy that would include <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_players_suspended_for_performance-enhancing_drugs" title="List of Major League Baseball players suspended for performance-enhancing drugs">50-game, 100-game</a>, and lifetime suspensions.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://mlb.mlb.com/pa/info/faq.jsp#created">"Frequently Asked Questions: \'When was the MLBPA created?<span class="cs1-kern-right">\'</span>"</a>. <i>Major League Baseball</i><span class="reference-accessdate">.
