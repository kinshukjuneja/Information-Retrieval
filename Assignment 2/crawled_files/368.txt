Hills AONB, <a href="/wiki/Leith_Hill" title="Leith Hill">Leith Hill</a> near <a href="/wiki/Coldharbour,_Surrey" title="Coldharbour, Surrey">Coldharbour</a>, is 294 metres (965&#160;ft) above sea level. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Greensand_Ridge" title="Greensand Ridge">Greensand Ridge</a>, which traverses the AONB from west to east, and is the highest point in southeast England.\n</p><p>The Surrey Hills area has three long-distance walks running through it: the <a href="/wiki/North_Downs_Way" title="North Downs Way">North Downs Way</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Greensand_Way" title="Greensand Way">Greensand Way</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pilgrims%27_Way" title="Pilgrims&#39; Way">Pilgrims\' Way</a>. <a href="/wiki/Blackheath_Common" title="Blackheath Common">Blackheath Common</a> is also part of this area.\n</p><p>The northern ridge of these hills, predominantly formed by <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a>, is separated by the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Holmesdale" title="Vale of Holmesdale">Vale of Holmesdale</a> which continues into Kent from the southern ridges which are predominantly <a href="/wiki/Greensand" title="Greensand">greensand</a>. They provide a haven for rare plants and insects.  Parts of the area are owned and managed by the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Ranmore_Common" title="Ranmore Common">Ranmore Common</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leith_Hill" title="Leith Hill">Leith Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Box_Hill,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Box Hill, England">Box Hill</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Chiddingfold_Forest" title="Chiddingfold Forest">Chiddingfold Forest</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI), lies within the area.\n</p><p>Surrey Hills AONB is surrounded by an Area of Great Landscape Value which covers a further 8% of the county.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surrey_Hills_AONB&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Findlater-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-Findlater_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Findlater G (2009). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.surreyhills.org/getdoc/0469961c-8840-4c0e-bd57-ddcd4a37d861/AONB-FULL-STD.aspx">"The State of the Surrey Hills AONB"</a>.
