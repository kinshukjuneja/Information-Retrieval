class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It was originally called <b>Tiverton District</b>, but was renamed in 1978 by resolution of the district council.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Raddon Top">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Raddon_Top" title="Raddon Top">Raddon Top</a> (772&#160;ft.) is the highest point of the Raddon Hills. Excavations at the summit in 1994 uncovered traces of <a href="/wiki/Early_Iron_Age" class="mw-redirect" title="Early Iron Age">Early Iron Age</a> settlement.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grand_Western_Canal">Grand Western Canal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Grand Western Canal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Grand_Western_Canal" title="Grand Western Canal">Grand Western Canal</a> stretches from Canal Hill in Tiverton to just outside <a href="/wiki/Greenham" title="Greenham">Greenham</a> in <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>. It no longer operates for trade purposes, but is a popular tourist location. Visitors are able to walk along its banks or take a trip down the canal in a <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a> drawn <a href="/wiki/Barge" title="Barge">barge</a>. A static barge at the Canal Hill end of the canal offers refreshments. The site is one of two tourism spots owned by <a href="/wiki/Devon_County_Council" title="Devon County Council">Devon County Council</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exmoor">Exmoor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Exmoor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Exmoor_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Exmoor National Park">Exmoor National Park</a> is a <a href="/wiki/National_park" title="National park">national park</a> situated in Mid Devon and <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">South West England</a>. The park covers 267 square miles (692&#160;km\xc2\xb2) of hilly open <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a>. Exmoor is one of the first British National Parks, designated in 1954, and is named after its main river, the River Exe. Several areas of the moor have been declared a  <a href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" class="mw-redirect" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Devon_Railway_Centre">Devon Railway Centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Devon Railway Centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Devon_Railway_Centre" title="Devon Railway Centre">Devon Railway Centre</a> is located at <a href="/wiki/Bickleigh,_Mid_Devon" title="Bickleigh, Mid Devon">Bickleigh</a> in Mid Devon, in a restored Victorian <a href="/wiki/Railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway station">railway station</a> on the closed <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a> branch from <a href="/wiki/Exe_Valley_Railway" title="Exe Valley Railway">Exeter to Dulverton</a>. The Centre operates a 2&#160;ft (610mm) gauge passenger railway and has a large collection of <a href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge" class="mw-redirect" title="Narrow gauge">narrow gauge</a> rolling stock, a <a href="/wiki/Ridable_miniature_railway" title="Ridable miniature railway">miniature railway</a> and a collection of <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_modelling" title="Rail transport modelling">model railways</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coldharbour_Working_Wool_Museum">Coldharbour Working Wool Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mid_Devon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Coldharbour Working Wool Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Coldharbour_Mill" class="mw-redirect" title="Coldharbour Mill">Coldharbour Mill</a> is a Grade II* listed Georgian mill complex in <a href="/wiki/Uffculme" title="Uffculme">Uffculme</a>, close to junction 27 (<a href="/wiki/Tiverton,_Devon" title="Tiverton, Devon">Tiverton</a> turnoff) of the M5. The mill has the largest working waterwheel in the south west, and steams up its stationary steam engines most Bank Holidays. It has a number of other collections, such as dolls\' houses, a large tapestry showing five local parishes, and a wide range of worsted wool spinning and weaving machines.\n</p>\n<h2><span
