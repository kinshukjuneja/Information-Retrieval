International</b> (<b>TI</b>) is a US headquartered <a href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-profit organization">nonprofit</a> educational organization that operates clubs worldwide for the purpose of promoting <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a>, <a href="/wiki/Public_speaking" title="Public speaking">public speaking</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Leadership" title="Leadership">leadership</a> skills.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">8</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toastmasters_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The organization grew out of a single club, <b>Smedley Chapter One Club</b>, which would become the first Toastmasters club. It was founded by <a href="/wiki/Ralph_C._Smedley" title="Ralph C. Smedley">Ralph C. Smedley</a> on October 22, 1924, at the <a href="/wiki/YMCA" title="YMCA">YMCA</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Ana,_California" title="Santa Ana, California">Santa Ana, California</a>, United States.<sup id="cite_ref-OCM1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OCM1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It originated as a set of classes with the aim of improving the communication skills of the young men under his charge.<sup id="cite_ref-Indie1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indie1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Toastmasters International was incorporated under <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">Californian</a> law on December 19, 1932.<sup id="cite_ref-OCM1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OCM1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-KPF1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KPF1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The first international chapter was established in Vancouver, Canada in 1932. The organization began admitting women in 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-OCM1_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OCM1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LaviolaYears_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LaviolaYears-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Throughout its history, Toastmasters has served over four million people, and today the organization serves over 352,000 members in 141 countries, through its 16,400 member clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-Who_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Who-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Toastmasters membership increased rapidly around the turn of the century, nearing 16,000 clubs worldwide by 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-blakeHow_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blakeHow-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meetings">Meetings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toastmasters_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Meetings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Toastmasters International uses a local club-based structure, each having around 20-40 members. Meetings are held every week or other week and usually in the evening, although some clubs meet in the morning or afternoon.<sup id="cite_ref-OCM1_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OCM1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Indie1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indie1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-KPF1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KPF1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Each club operates as a separate entity with a set of requirements leading to chartered status for them to be recognised as official Toastmasters clubs. Chartered status allows for clubs to use the names, promotional material and programme of TI.<sup id="cite_ref-MPN1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MPN1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Table_Topics">Table Topics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toastmasters_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Table Topics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A main part of meetings is the Table Topics, which are off-the-cuff speeches which are assigned on the spot by a Topicsmaster. The goal of this is to think on one\'s feet with minimal preparation. In some clubs attendees are then asked to vote on who they thought gave the best speech.<sup id="cite_ref-OCM1_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OCM1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Indie1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indie1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-KPF1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KPF1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Speeches">Speeches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toastmasters_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Speeches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Every meeting is based around a set of organised speeches. Speakers are evaluated by an experienced member who then gives an impromptu speech with constructive feedback based on their performance.<sup id="cite_ref-AMWA1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMWA1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toastmasters_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Toastmasters International places a large emphasis on building the public speaking and leadership skills of its members.<sup id="cite_ref-AMWA1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMWA1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The current education system consists of ten differing paths suited to their needs and requirements, based around <i>Public Speaking</i>, <i>Interpersonal Communication</i>, <i>Management</i>, <i>Strategic Leadership</i>, and <i>Confidence</i>. Each path consists of 14 projects.<sup id="cite_ref-BP1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Content consists of online and print materials, it being the first time in the organisation\xe2\x80\x99s history to provide modern technology in its programme.<sup id="cite_ref-BP1_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hindu1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hindu1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Content is available in English, French and German.<sup id="cite_ref-Delano1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Delano1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_speaking_championship">Public speaking championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toastmasters_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Public speaking championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Toastmasters runs an international public speaking championship formally known as the Toastmasters International <a href="/wiki/World_Champion_of_Public_Speaking" title="World Champion of Public Speaking">World Champion of Public Speaking</a>, which is held annually at its International Convention in August.<sup id="cite_ref-MooneyChampion_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MooneyChampion-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NasirWords_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NasirWords-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MurphyFlop_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MurphyFlop-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Basheda_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Basheda-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It started in 1938 and involves over 33,000 participants in 141 countries making it the world\'s largest speech contest.<sup id="cite_ref-NasirWords_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NasirWords-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BP2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> There is a six-month process of elimination to reach the semifinals, in which in 2018 there were 106 participants who got that far. There are ten places in the final and speeches are judged on content, gestures, organisation and style.<sup id="cite_ref-BP2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
