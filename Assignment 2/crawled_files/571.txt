href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Penntorr</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> and town on the <a href="/wiki/Rame_Peninsula" title="Rame Peninsula">Rame Peninsula</a> in southeast <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom. It is situated opposite the city of <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Hamoaze" title="Hamoaze">Hamoaze</a> which is the tidal estuary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Tamar" title="River Tamar">River Tamar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Torpoint has a population of 8,457 (2001 census), decreasing to 8,364 at the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Two electoral wards also exist (Torpoint East and West). Their combined populations at the same census were 7,717.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish language</a> Torpoint is called Penntorr (local folk band Pentorr have taken their name from this).\n</p><p>Torpoint is linked to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> (and <a href="/wiki/Devonport,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Devonport, Devon">Devonport</a>) by the <a href="/wiki/Torpoint_Ferry" title="Torpoint Ferry">Torpoint Ferry</a>. The three vessels that operate the service are <a href="/wiki/Cable_ferry" title="Cable ferry">chain ferries</a> &#8211; that is, they are propelled across the river by pulling themselves on fixed chains which lie across the bed of the river. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Torpoint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>It is said that Torpoint\'s name is derived from Tar Point, a name given because of the initial industry on the west bank of the Hamoaze. However this is actually a nickname given by workers, Torpoint meaning "rocky headland".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Torpoint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Torpoint is an eighteenth-century planned town. The grid-based design for the town was commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Reginald_Pole_Carew" title="Reginald Pole Carew">Reginald Pole Carew</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Antony,_Cornwall" title="Antony, Cornwall">Parish of Antony</a> in 1774. His family continue to have a strong influence in the area, having become the Carew Poles in the twentieth century, and still reside at their family seat, <a href="/wiki/Antony_House" title="Antony House">Antony House</a>.\n</p><p>In 1796 Torpoint was the setting for a shooting battle between the crew of a government vessel, the <i>Viper</i>, and a large party of armed liquor <a href="/wiki/Smugglers" class="mw-redirect" title="Smugglers">smugglers</a>, in which one person was killed and five people seriously wounded.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Due to the presence of <a href="/wiki/HMNB_Devonport" title="HMNB Devonport">Devonport Dockyard</a>, the town grew as Dockyard workers settled there. The establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>\'s main training facility, <a href="/wiki/HMS_Raleigh_(shore_establishment)" title="HMS Raleigh (shore establishment)">HMS <i>Raleigh</i></a> also increased the population of Torpoint.<sup id="cite_ref-History_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people_from_Torpoint">Notable people from Torpoint</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Torpoint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable people from Torpoint">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><i>See <a href="/wiki/Category:People_from_Torpoint" title="Category:People from Torpoint">Category:People from Torpoint</a></i>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/John_Langdon_Down" title="John Langdon Down">John Langdon Down</a> was born in Torpoint in 1828, he later described the condition which is now referred to as <a href="/wiki/Down_syndrome" title="Down syndrome">Down syndrome</a>. He was called back on a number of occasions to help his father in his local business until his father\'s death in 1853.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Torpoint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span Junior School</b> &#8212; a large <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary school</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Torpoint_Community_College" title="Torpoint Community College">Torpoint Community College</a>, The Humanities College for South East Cornwall &#8212; the only <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">humanities</a> college in south east Cornwall.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_and_leisure">Sport and leisure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a The Mill.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Twinning">Twinning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
