title="Paperback">Paperback</a>)</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><i><b>Marazan</b></i> is the first published novel by the British author <a href="/wiki/Nevil_Shute" title="Nevil Shute">Nevil Shute</a>. It was originally published in 1926 by <a href="/wiki/Cassell_%26_Co" class="mw-redirect" title="Cassell &amp; Co">Cassell &amp; Co</a>, then republished in 1951 by <a href="/wiki/Heinemann_(book_publisher)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heinemann (book publisher)">William Heinemann</a>. The events of the novel occur, in part, around the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Plot_summary"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Plot summary</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Author&#39;s_later_reflections"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Author\'s later reflections</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marazan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Philip Stenning is a commercial pilot, trained during the First World War.  After his engine fails, he crashes and is rescued by an escaped convict, Denis Compton, who turns out to have been framed for embezzlement by his Italian half-brother, Baron Rodrigo Mattani, who is smuggling drugs into England.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The story tells how Stenning plays a key role in breaking that drug ring.  It involves episodes characteristic of Shute: flying, small boat sailing, and a love story.\n</p><p>Stenning was a major character in Shute\'s first (unpublished) novel <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Morris_(novel)" title="Stephen Morris (novel)"><i>Stephen Morris</i></a>. Stenning also crops up as a comparatively minor character in Shute\'s next two novels <i><a href="/wiki/So_Disdained" title="So Disdained">So Disdained</a></i> (1928) and <i><a href="/wiki/Lonely_Road_(novel)" title="Lonely Road (novel)">Lonely Road</a></i> (1932).\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Author.27s_later_reflections"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Author\'s_later_reflections">Author\'s later reflections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marazan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s later reflections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In his autobiography <i>Slide Rule</i>, Shute recalls writing the book twice over and rewriting large portions a third time. He wrote as a relaxation from his regular work of designing air ships.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>His first two unpublished novels (<a href="/wiki/Stephen_Morris_(novel)" title="Stephen Morris (novel)"><i>Stephen Morris</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Pilotage_(novel)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilotage (novel)"><i>Pilotage</i></a>) were typed on an old Blick portable typewriter: he said <i>it may not be quite a coincidence that my first published novel </i>Marazan<i> was the first that I wrote on a brand new typewriter bought out of my earnings as an engineer</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
