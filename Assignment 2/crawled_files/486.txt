title="Knight of the Bath (1399\xe2\x80\x931725)">KB</a></span> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_parliament" title="Member of parliament">MP</a> (25 December 1605 \xe2\x80\x93 22 March 1667), of <a href="/wiki/Godolphin_Estate" title="Godolphin Estate">Godolphin</a> in Cornwall, was an English nobleman, landowner, politician, and Member of Parliament. His chief claim to fame is that he was the dedicatee of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hobbes" title="Thomas Hobbes">Hobbes\'</a> <a href="/wiki/Leviathan_(Hobbes_book)" title="Leviathan (Hobbes class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin_(1605%E2%80%931667)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/William_Godolphin_(1567%E2%80%931613)" title="William Godolphin (1567\xe2\x80\x931613)">Sir William Godolphin (1567\xe2\x80\x931613)</a> and brother of the poet <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Godolphin_(poet)" title="Sidney Godolphin (poet)">Sidney Godolphin</a>, both of whom were also members of Parliament. He succeeded his father in 1613, inheriting estates which included the lease of the <a href="/wiki/Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Scilly Isles">Scilly Isles</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin_(1605%E2%80%931667)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He represented <a href="/wiki/Helston_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Helston (UK Parliament constituency)">Helston</a> in the Parliament of 1625\xe2\x80\x936, again in the <a href="/wiki/Long_Parliament" title="Long Parliament">Long Parliament</a> and was appointed <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Cornwall" title="High Sheriff of Cornwall">High Sheriff of Cornwall</a> in 1638.\n</p><p>Being a <a href="/wiki/Cavaliers" class="mw-redirect" title="Cavaliers">Royalist</a>, when the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">Civil War</a> broke out he returned to Cornwall, where he secured the Scilly Isles for the King and raised a regiment of which his brother, William, took command. In consequence, he was disbarred from sitting in Parliament in January 1644, and his estates were sequestered. However, after the capture of the <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_England" title="Charles I of England">King</a> he negotiated an honourable capitulation of the Scilly Isles to Parliament, the House of Commons voting <i>"that Mr Godolphin, <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#Lord_Proprietors_of_the_Scilly_Islands" title="Isles of Scilly">governor of Scilly</a>, upon his surrender of that island, with all forts &amp;c, should enjoy his estate and be free from arrest for any acts of war"</i>.\n</p><p>He was elected once more for Helston in the <a href="/wiki/Convention_Parliament_(1660)" title="Convention Parliament (1660)">Convention Parliament of 1660</a>, and following the <a href="/wiki/English_Restoration" class="mw-redirect" title="English Restoration">Restoration</a> he was knighted at <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II\'s</a> coronation. He died in 1667.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_children">Marriage and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for Helston 1665\xe2\x80\x931679, created a baronet in 1661.</li>\n<li><a Godolphin in 1706.</li>\n<li>Dr <a href="/wiki/Henry_Godolphin" title="Henry Godolphin">Henry Godolphin</a>, third son, <a href="/wiki/Dean_of_St_Paul%27s" title="Dean of St Paul&#39;s">Dean of St Paul\'s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Provost_of_Eton_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Provost of Eton College">Provost of Eton College</a>.</li>\n<li>Elizabeth Godolphin (died 30 August 1707),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> eldest daughter, second wife of <a href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Northcote,_2nd_Baronet" title="Sir Arthur Northcote, 2nd
