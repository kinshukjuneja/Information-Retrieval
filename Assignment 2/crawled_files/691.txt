href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> located southeast of <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-os_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-os-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is thought to be the oldest settlement on the island. There is a <a href="/wiki/Church_(building)" title="Church (building)">church</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">pub</a> (the <i>Old Town Inn</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-oldtowninn_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oldtowninn-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> two cafes, a small convenience shop and a <a href="/wiki/Day_care" class="mw-redirect" title="Day care">day nursery</a>.\n</p><p>It is a popular tourist area and is only a short distance from the <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Airport_(Isles_of_Scilly)" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mary&#39;s Airport (Isles of Scilly)">island\'s airport</a>. Three art and pottery studios are located on the lane heading north out of the village. As well as a number of self-catering holiday properties, Old Town also has a large proportion of dwellings permanently occupied by local residents. The <a href="/wiki/Five_Islands_School" title="Five Islands School">Five Islands School</a>, as well as the main sports and fitness centre for St Mary\'s, is located just by Old Town.\n</p><p>Old Town Bay forms a natural harbour and links the village with the open sea; there is an old quay and there continues to be a slipway in use.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" Scilly. The island of St Mary\'s was then known as Ennor, and Old Town was named Porth Ennor or Porthenor (being the main harbour settlement of the island). It declined with the development of The Garrison and neighbouring Hugh Town, which in turn replaced Old Town as the main harbour, administrative centre and settlement of the Isles.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ennor_Castle">Ennor Castle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ennor Castle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On a small but prominent knoll are the fragmentary remains of a small <a href="/wiki/Shell_keep" title="Shell keep">shell keep</a> castle dating from the thirteenth or fourteenth century, known as <b>Ennor Castle</b>, the only medieval castle on the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It became redundant after <a href="/wiki/Star_Castle,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Star Castle, Isles of Scilly">Star Castle</a> (at The Garrison) was built in the late sixteenth century, and much of its stone was then robbed for buildings in Old Town.<sup id="cite_ref-pastscape_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastscape-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Church">Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On the other side of the bay to the settlement lies <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">St Mary\'s Old Church</a> (commonly known as <i>Old Town Church</i>) which is surrounded by an old, terraced graveyard as well as the newer municipal <a href="/wiki/Cemetery" title="Cemetery">cemetery</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Dedication" title="Dedication">dedication</a> of the church to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Mary_the_Virgin" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Mary the Virgin">St Mary</a> then gave that name to the island parish, replacing the older name of Ennor; the naming after the parish church is also the case with a number of other islands in Scilly. Common with other <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornish</a> <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parishes</a>, the church is located a little distance from the main settlement of the parish, and there once was a small "<a href="/wiki/Churchtown,_Cornwall" title="Churchtown, Cornwall">churchtown</a>" surrounding the church; today however there are no other buildings in the vicinity of the church. The <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> of St Mary\'s is now in Hugh Town, also dedicated to St Mary the Virgin, though the Old Church is used for Sunday evening services (Easter-September)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the cemetery belonging to the Council of the Isles of Scilly lies adjacent.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a> <a Town.<sup id="cite_ref-wilson_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wilson-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span
