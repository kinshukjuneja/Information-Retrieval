Swindon</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Historic England Archive</b> is the public <a href="/wiki/Archive" title="Archive">archive</a> of <a href="/wiki/Historic_England" title="Historic England">Historic England</a>, located in The Engine House on Fire Fly Avenue in Swindon, formerly part of the <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Works" title="Swindon Works">Swindon Works</a> of the Great Western Railway.\n</p><p>It is a public archive of architectural and archaeological records and holds over 12 million historic photographs, plans, drawings, reports, records and publications covering England\'s archaeology, architecture, social and local history. It is a dynamic collection, with records being added to this day.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <b>PastScape</b> website allows searching of over 420,000 records (as of 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The roots of the archive go back to 1908 and the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Commission_on_the_Historical_Monuments_of_England" title="Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England">Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England</a> (RCHME) which was set up to compile and publish an inventory of all ancient and historical monuments up to the year 1700 by county and by parish.\n</p><p>Its more immediate forerunner, however, was the <b>National Buildings Record</b> (<b>NBR</b>), an independent body set up in 1940 under the inspiration of <a href="/wiki/Walter_Godfrey" title="Walter Godfrey">Walter Godfrey</a>, its first Director, "to meet the dangers of war then threatening many buildings of national importance".<sup id="cite_ref-NMRleaflet_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NMRleaflet-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Between the declaration of war in 1939 and the first London air-raids in 1940 many moveable works of art, archives and antiquities were evacuated to the relative safety of the countryside. For example, the contents of the <a href="/wiki/National_Gallery" title="National Gallery">National Gallery</a> were taken to <a href="/wiki/English_country_house" title="English country house">country houses</a>, slate mines and quarries in Wales <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, buildings could not be protected in this way, so steps were taken to collect architectural plans, drawings, photographs and other records. The RCHME worked closely with the NBR to carry out the extensive photographic recording of the towns in the eastern and southern counties at risk from enemy action.\n</p><p>Although there were other voluntary and academic bodies at the time that collected some architectural records (including the RCHME, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_British_Architects" title="Royal Institute of British Architects">Royal Institute of British Architects</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Survey_of_London" title="Survey of London">London Survey Committee</a>), they were apparently deemed to be too small or narrowly focused for the sort of comprehensive venture that seemed necessary in the chaos of wartime Britain.<sup id="cite_ref-summerson_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summerson-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The initial core of the collection was formed by the Conway Library of architectural photographs held by the <a href="/wiki/Courtauld_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="Courtauld Institute">Courtauld Institute</a> of Art; the negatives of the Victorian photographer <a href="/wiki/Henry_Taunt" title="Henry Taunt">Henry Taunt</a> of Oxford; items from the University of London and by quickly setting photographers to work recording buildings in London and other vulnerable areas.<sup id="cite_ref-summerson_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summerson-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Over the next few years the NBR attracted donations from amateur photographers, people who possessed drawings and owners of collections of negatives.\n</p><p>By the end of the war the collection had grown to such size and importance that it was continued and was funded by the Treasury.<sup id="cite_ref-NMRleaflet_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NMRleaflet-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The 1944 and 1947 <a href="/wiki/Town_and_Country_Planning_Act_1947" title="Town and Country Planning Act 1947">Town and Country Planning Acts</a> were important for the NBR as they meant that lists of buildings of architectural and historic importance were started, and the NBR had to be informed before an historic building was demolished.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The recession of the late 1940s and early 1950s presented a different challenge from bombing, but no less severe. This time the destruction of significant buildings was due to deliberate demolition because the owners (through new inheritance taxation and shortage of building materials) could no longer afford to maintain them.<sup id="cite_ref-summerson_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summerson-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1946 the RCHME work was extended to include the recording of 18th and 19th century architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-RCHM_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCHM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 1950s the NBR archive acquisitions included the collections of Sydney Pitcher (medieval architecture and vernacular buildings); <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Gernsheim" title="Helmut Gernsheim">Helmut Gernsheim</a> (photographs of tombs in <a href="/wiki/St_Pauls_Cathedral" class="mw-redirect" title="St Pauls Cathedral">St Pauls Cathedral</a> and <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a>) as well as continued recording of architectural details such as glass, fixtures and fittings, woodwork and sculpture.<sup id="cite_ref-summerson_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summerson-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The 1950s also saw the start of aerial photography to discover and record ancient sites, as more intensive agriculture, forestry and gravel extraction threatened historic remains on "marginal land".<sup id="cite_ref-RCHMEhistory_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCHMEhistory-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The NBR was merged with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Commission_on_the_Historical_Monuments_of_England" title="Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England">Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England</a> (RCHME) in 1963. The name was then changed to the <b>National Monuments Record</b> (<b>NMR</b>) to denote the inclusion of archaeological and photographic records.<sup id="cite_ref-NMRleaflet_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NMRleaflet-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During the 1960s, the importance of photographic records grew, and the archive acquired the surviving negatives of architectural photographer Harry <a href="/wiki/Bedford_Lemere" title="Bedford Lemere">Bedford Lemere</a> (1864\xe2\x80\x931944) of houses, public buildings and interior decoration. (The <a href="/wiki/National_Maritime_Museum" title="National Maritime Museum">National Maritime Museum</a> holds another large section of his work.) <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The demolition of the <a href="/wiki/Euston_Arch" title="Euston Arch">Euston Arch</a> <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and City of London <a href="/wiki/Coal_Exchange_(London)" title="Coal Exchange (London)">Coal Exchange</a> demonstrated the lack of regard there was at the time for (then) unfashionable Victorian architecture and the importance of keeping records of their existence.\n</p><p>During the 1970s and 1980s the NMR increased its recording of industrial and commercial building records as well as <a href="/wiki/English_country_house" title="English country house">country houses</a>. The expanding breadth of the archive is shown by the acquisition of Rev Denys Rokeby\xe2\x80\x99s collection of railway photographs, and H. E. S. Simmons\' negatives of water and wind-mills, and John Maltby\xe2\x80\x99s collection of photographs of <a href="/wiki/Odeon_cinema" class="mw-redirect" title="Odeon cinema">Odeon cinemas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-summerson_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summerson-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1983 the <a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey" title="Ordnance Survey">Ordnance Survey</a> Archaeology Division was transferred to the NBR/RCHME which greatly expanded the records available.<sup id="cite_ref-RCHMEhistory_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCHMEhistory-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> These are now digitised and available through the PastScape website. In 1984 the NMR took over the responsibility for the National Library of Aerial Photographs from the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_the_Environment" title="Secretary of State for the Environment">Department of the Environment</a>, together with two million RAF and Ordnance Survey oblique and vertical aerial photographs.<sup id="cite_ref-RCHMEhistory_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCHMEhistory-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A further acquisition was the Industrial Monuments Survey, transferred from the Department of the Environment (DoE) in 1981. This was followed by the RCHME taking on responsibility for the <a href="/wiki/Survey_of_London" title="Survey of London">Survey of London</a> following the abolition of the Greater London Council.<sup id="cite_ref-RCHM_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCHM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1992 the Commission\xe2\x80\x99s terms of reference was extended to include responsibility for creating a central national record of historic wrecks and other archaeological sites within the 12-mile coastal limit.<sup id="cite_ref-RCHM_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RCHM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The RCHME, including the NMR, merged with <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage</a> in April 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  In April 2012 the NMR was renamed the <b>English Heritage Archive</b>. In 2015 English Heritage was divided into two parts: those departments carrying out statutory and advisory functions became Historic England, and the archive, as part of the new body, was renamed the <b>Historic England Archive</b>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contents">Contents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Contents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Its collections arose from a number of sources, including the work of national institutions concerned with the buildings and archaeology of England, and from collections acquired from others. The collections are not only used by historians, architects and designers seeking inspiration but also by family history researchers looking for photographs of where their ancestors lived and worked.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The scope and content of the material is very wide-ranging \xe2\x80\x93 modern and historic photography, including coverage of the whole of England in <a href="/wiki/Aerial_photographs" class="mw-redirect" title="Aerial photographs">aerial photographs</a>; information on most known archaeological sites and <a href="/wiki/Listed_buildings" class="mw-redirect" title="Listed buildings">listed buildings</a>; complete sets of Country Life magazine; Victoria County Histories; the Council of British Archaeology\xe2\x80\x99s Industrial Archaeology index; Professor <a href="/wiki/Goodhart-Rendel" class="mw-redirect" title="Goodhart-Rendel">Goodhart-Rendel</a>\xe2\x80\x99s personal index of late 19th century churches; the typescript report, photographs and drawings of Lt Col G W Meates\xe2\x80\x99s <a href="/wiki/Lullingstone_Roman_villa" class="mw-redirect" title="Lullingstone Roman villa">Lullingstone Roman villa</a> excavation and much more.<sup id="cite_ref-summerson_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-summerson-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There is also a specialist reference library. Although photographs form the largest proportion of the Historic England Archive\xe2\x80\x99s holdings, collections also include plans of historic houses, reports, correspondence and digital files. All of this material is available for public viewing and a substantial number of images from the Historic England Archive are now available online and free to access.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Buildings.2C_drawings_and_surveys"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings,_drawings_and_surveys">Buildings, drawings and surveys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Buildings, drawings and surveys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Historic England Archive holds information on over 70,000 individual buildings, as photographs, drawings, notes, reports from the Royal Commission on Historical Monuments for England (RCHME) and from English Heritage fieldwork.<sup id="cite_ref-drawings_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drawings-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In addition, it holds over 56,000 architectural drawings, including plans, elevations, sketches and watercolours from the RCHME and other sources as well as many plans of English Heritage\'s properties. The original research material (notes, sketches and photographs) for RCHME inventory volumes are also in the archives.<sup id="cite_ref-drawings_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drawings-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There is a selection of brochures advertising the sales of estate and properties across England from the late 19th and 20th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-drawings_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drawings-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_photographs">Historic photographs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Historic photographs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Historic England Archive contains photographic collections dating from the 1850s. These include country house albums by anonymous amateurs; famous photographers such as Roger Fenton and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Brandt" title="Bill Brandt">Bill Brandt</a>; architectural photographers such as Bedford Lemere &amp; Co; commercial photographers like Miller and Harris, <a href="/wiki/John_Gay_(photographer)" title="John Gay (photographer)">John Gay (photographer)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_de_Mare" class="mw-redirect" title="Eric de Mare">Eric de Mare</a>; and the Thames Valley views of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Taunt" title="Henry Taunt">Henry Taunt</a>.\n</p><p>There are also more esoteric collections acquired from, or donated by, individuals who were particularly interested in specialist topics such as medieval stained glass; public parks and windmills.<sup id="cite_ref-photos_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-photos-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Aerial_photographs">Aerial photographs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aerial photographs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The Historic England Archive holds the largest public archive of <a href="/wiki/Aerial_photographs" class="mw-redirect" title="Aerial photographs">aerial photographs</a> in England, organised as two distinct collections \xe2\x80\x93 oblique and vertical \xe2\x80\x93 each acquired from different sources.  More than 680,000 oblique (taken at an angle to the ground) aerial photographs of locations throughout England are available on open access in the Historic England Archive search rooms.  The Historic England Archive also holds more than two million vertical (bird\'s-eye view) aerial photographs, covering the whole of England, including near-complete coverage taken by the <a href="/wiki/RAF" class="mw-redirect" title="RAF">RAF</a> in 1946\xe2\x80\x9348.   These are available via a search request from the Archive Services Team.<sup id="cite_ref-photos_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-photos-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\nThe \xe2\x80\x98Britain from Above\xe2\x80\x99 project, funded by the <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Lottery_Fund" title="Heritage Lottery Fund">Heritage Lottery Fund</a> completed digitisation and cataloguing of 95,000 of the <a href="/wiki/Aerofilms" title="Aerofilms">Aerofilms</a> aerial image negatives from 1919\xe2\x80\x931953 and put them online.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Survey_photographs">Survey photographs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Survey photographs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The photographs taken by the RCHME and English Heritage survey teams, which range from workers\xe2\x80\x99 cottages to power stations, are also available.<sup id="cite_ref-photos_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-photos-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Access">Access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_resources">Online resources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Online resources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Approximately 20% of the archive, i.e. over 2 million records, can be searched online.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The main online resources are:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150415224527/http://archive.historicengland.org.uk/">Historic England Archives</a> \xe2\x80\x93 over a million free online catalogue descriptions of photographs and records of England\'s buildings and heritage sites.</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://historicengland.org.uk/images-books/archive/archive-collections/englands-places/">England\'s Places</a> \xe2\x80\x93 Discover photos of English cities, towns and villages using this online version of the Architectural Red Box Collection from the Historic England Archive.</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.britainfromabove.org.uk/">Britain from Above</a> \xe2\x80\x93 a unique archive of historical aerial photographs across Britain, from the <a href="/wiki/Aerofilms" title="Aerofilms">Aerofilms</a> collection (1919\xe2\x80\x932006)</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/">Images of England</a> \xe2\x80\x93 over 300,000 contemporary photographs of England\xe2\x80\x99s listed buildings and monuments; based on the statutory list as it was in 2001, no longer updated</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" and architecture. With links to historic and modern maps and aerial photographs where available.</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.historicengland.org.uk/listing/the-list/">The National Heritage List for England</a> \xe2\x80\x93 database of all 400,000 nationally designated heritage assets i.e. listed buildings; scheduled monuments; protected wreck sites; registered battlefields and registered parks and gardens.</li>\n<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.heritagegateway.org.uk/gateway/">Heritage Gateway</a> \xe2\x80\x93 a partnership with the Association of Local Government Archaeology Officers (ALGAO) and the Institute of Historic Building Conservation (IHBC) to share local records on listed buildings</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_visitors">Personal visitors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal visitors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>For access to the wider archive, not just the material available online, it is possible to call, write or visit the Historic England Archive in Swindon, Wiltshire.  Although an appointment is not necessary, it is recommended to contact them in advance.\n</p><p>In addition to accessing the collections, visitors to the Historic England Archive can also make use of the reference library.  This includes the entire collection of <i><a href="/wiki/Country_Life_(magazine)" title="Country Life (magazine)">Country Life</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Architects_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Architects Journal">Architects Journal</a></i> magazines going back to the 19th century, both of which feature plans and photographs of thousands of homes, country estates, commercial and civic buildings.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Archive_services">Archive services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England_Archive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Archive services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Historic England Archive offers a remote enquiry service providing a comprehensive search for information on areas of interest. Search requests are handled free of charge provided they fall within standard search criteria (for example, a single building per enquiry).  To request a wider search, contact the Archive Services team for details of services and relevant fees.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
