ribbon">ribbon</a></div></div></div>\n<p>October<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>22 was designated <b>International Stuttering Awareness Day</b> (<b>ISAD</b>) in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-Sugarman_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sugarman-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The day is intended to raise public awareness of the millions of people \xe2\x80\x93 one percent of the world\'s population<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Jewish_Currents_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jewish_Currents-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Stuttering_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>ISAD is organised by three organizations:<sup id="cite_ref-UWE_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/European_League_of_Stuttering_Associations" title="European League of Stuttering Associations">European League of Stuttering Associations</a></li>\n<li>International Fluency Association</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/International_Stuttering_Association" title="International Stuttering Association">International Stuttering Association</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Stuttering_Awareness_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>ISAD includes an online conference,<sup id="cite_ref-Topky_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Topky-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> running annually from October 1 to 22 each year, targeted at people with an interest in stuttering as well as <a href="/wiki/Speech-language_pathologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Speech-language pathologist">speech-language pathologists</a> and their clients.<sup id="cite_ref-UWE_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The conferences, held every year since 1998, are all still available online.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It also includes public awareness events, a media campaign,<sup id="cite_ref-ISAD_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ISAD-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Doncaster_stunt_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doncaster_stunt-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cameroon_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cameroon-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> educational activities and online resources.\n</p><p>In an article published in the UK magazine <i>Community Care</i> to mark International Stuttering Awareness Day, Irina Papencheva from the Bulgarian Stuttering Association and Phil Madden from the European Association of Service Providers for Persons with Disabilities demanded a fresh start in attitudes towards stammering, saying that "everyone has the responsibility to be aware, to be sensitive in our conversations and meetings" and to remember that stuttering is "<i>not</i> funny".<sup id="cite_ref-Community_Care_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Community_Care-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
