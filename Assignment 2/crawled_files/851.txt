series with the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Stockings_(1870%E2%80%9389)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago White Stockings (1870\xe2\x80\x9389)">Chicago White Stockings</a> when Mutual left the The Mutual club declared itself champion.</small>\n</p>\n</td></tr></tbody></table><p>\nThe <b>Mutual Base Ball Club</b> of New York was a leading American <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> club almost throughout its 20-year history. It was established during 1857, the year of the first baseball convention, just too late to be a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Base Ball Players">National Association of Base Ball Players</a>. </p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg/150px-New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg" width="150" height="202" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg/225px-New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg/300px-New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg 2x" data-file-width="398" data-file-height="535" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_York_Mutual_baseball_player.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Picture of an unidentified New York Mutual player</div></div></div><p>It was a charter member of both the first professional league in 1871 and the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> in 1876. Failing on the field and in the coffer, it declined to make its last western trip of the inaugural season. For the transgression it was expelled in December, and soon found itself defunct.\n</p><p>The Mutual club initially played its home games at <a href="/wiki/Elysian_Fields,_Hoboken,_New_Jersey" title="Elysian Fields, Hoboken, New Jersey">Elysian Fields</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hoboken,_New_Jersey" title="Hoboken, New Jersey">Hoboken</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Knickerbockers" title="New York Knickerbockers">New York Knickerbockers</a> and many other <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> clubs, but moved to the enclosed <a href="/wiki/Union_Grounds" title="Union Grounds">Union Grounds</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn</a> in 1868.\n</p><p>The Mutuals chose open professionalism in 1869\xe2\x80\x9370 after <a href="/wiki/NABBP" class="mw-redirect" title="NABBP">NABBP</a> liberalization. They joined the first professional league, the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Professional Base Ball Players">National Association of Professional Base Ball Players</a>, for its <a href="/wiki/1871_in_sports" title="1871 in sports">1871</a> to <a href="/wiki/1875_in_sports" title="1875 in sports">1875</a> duration. In <a href="/wiki/1876_in_sports" title="1876 in sports">1876</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Stockings_(1870%E2%80%9389)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago White Stockings (1870\xe2\x80\x9389)">Chicago White Stockings</a> initiated the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> and recruited its members from West to East, partly to wrest control of professional baseball from Eastern interests. The Mutuals were one of eight charter members, six of whom were from the National Association. Weak (sixth place at 21\xe2\x80\x9335) and cash-poor, the club refused to complete its playing obligations in the West; and was expelled.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On May 13, 1876, the Mutuals executed the first <a href="/wiki/Triple_play" title="Triple play">triple play</a> in major-league history in a game against the Hartford Dark Blues.\n</p><p>Union Grounds proprietor <a href="/wiki/William_Cammeyer" class="mw-redirect" title="William Cammeyer">William Cammeyer</a>, often listed today as the Mutual club owner, signed the <a href="/wiki/Hartford_Dark_Blues" title="Hartford Dark Blues">Hartford Dark Blues</a> to play at his Union Grounds in 1877. The team was effectively a one-year replacement for the defunct Mutuals, and was sometimes called "Hartford of Brooklyn".\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button"
