id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>An <b>independent baseball league</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> organization located in the United States and Canada that is not operated in conjunction with either a <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> team or an affiliated <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> team. Being independent allows teams to be located close to major league teams without their consent. Such leagues have been around for many years and were once known as "outlaw leagues" due to their position outside the rules of affiliated minor league baseball.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Northern_League_(baseball,_1993%E2%80%932010)" title="Northern League (baseball, 1993\xe2\x80\x932010)">Northern League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frontier_League" title="Frontier League">Frontier League</a> both started play in 1993, and the Northern League\'s success paved the way for other independent <a href="/wiki/Sports_league" title="Sports league">leagues</a> like the <a href="/wiki/Central_Baseball_League" title="Central Baseball League">Texas-Louisiana League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canadian-American_Association_of_Professional_Baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian-American Association of Professional Baseball">Northeast League</a>. The Atlantic League has had more marquee players than any other independent league, including <a href="/wiki/Jose_Canseco" title="Jose Canseco">Jose Canseco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rickey_Henderson" title="Rickey Henderson">Rickey Henderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ozzie_Canseco" title="Ozzie Canseco">Ozzie Canseco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Sierra" title="Rub\xc3\xa9n Sierra">Rub\xc3\xa9n Sierra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Baerga" title="Carlos Baerga">Carlos Baerga</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Rocker" title="John Rocker">John Rocker</a>. The Northern League alumni include <a href="/wiki/Leon_Durham" title="Leon Durham">Leon "Bull" Durham</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._D._Drew" title="J. D. Drew">J. D. Drew</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Darryl_Strawberry" title="Darryl Strawberry">Darryl Strawberry</a>.\n</p><p>Independent leagues have flourished in northeastern states, where dense populations can often support multiple <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports_league_organization" title="Professional sports league organization">franchises</a>. Because they are not subject to the territorial limitations imposed on affiliated minor-league teams, independent clubs can relocate as close to affiliated teams (and one another) as they choose to.  For example, the city of <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster, Pennsylvania">Lancaster, Pennsylvania</a> cannot have an affiliated team because of its proximity to the <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg_Senators" title="Harrisburg Senators">Harrisburg Senators</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reading_Fightin_Phils" title="Reading Fightin Phils">Reading Fightin Phils</a>, leaving the Atlantic League to place a team\xe2\x80\x94the <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_Barnstormers" title="Lancaster Barnstormers">Lancaster Barnstormers</a>\xe2\x80\x94to fill the void.  Another example is the greater New York City metropolitan area, where there are many independent teams: the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Ducks" title="Long Island Ducks">Long Island Ducks</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Jackals" title="New Jersey Jackals">New Jersey Jackals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rockland_Boulders" title="Rockland Boulders">Rockland Boulders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somerset_Patriots" title="Somerset Patriots">Somerset Patriots</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sussex_County_Miners" title="Sussex County Miners">Sussex County Miners</a>. The Kansas City area is home not only to the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a>, but also the independent <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_T-Bones" title="Kansas City T-Bones">Kansas City T-Bones</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/St._Paul_Saints" title="St. Paul Saints">St. Paul Saints</a> share a market with the American League\'s <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a>; both teams have frequently worked together to promote baseball in the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Midwest" title="Upper Midwest">Upper Midwest</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox"
