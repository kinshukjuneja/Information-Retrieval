Martin\'s</div></div></div>\n<p>There are three main settlements on the island - <a href="/wiki/Higher_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Higher Town, Isles of Scilly">Higher Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_Town,_St_Martin%27s" title="Middle Town, St Martin&#39;s">Middle Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Lower Town, Isles of Scilly">Lower Town</a> - in addition to a number of scattered farms and cottages, with a total population (2011 census) of 136.\n</p><p>There are two <a href="/wiki/Quay" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay">quays</a> - at Higher Town (the Higher Town Quay, used at <a href="/wiki/High_tide" class="mw-redirect" title="High tide">high tide</a>) and at Lower Town (the Hotel Quay, used at <a href="/wiki/Low_tide" class="mw-redirect" title="Low tide">low tide</a>). In Higher Town there is a post office.\n</p><p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyard</a> on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>To the north, St Martin\'s is joined by a <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">tidal</a> <a href="/wiki/Causeway" title="Causeway">causeway</a> to <a href="/wiki/White_Island,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="White Island, Isles of Scilly">White Island</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Daymark">Daymark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Martin%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Daymark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>At the northeast corner of the island is a large red-and-white <a href="/wiki/Daymark" title="Daymark">daymark</a>. It was erected in 1683 by Thomas Ekins, first steward of the Godophin Family to live on the islands.\n</p><p>It is a rendered <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> circular tower 4.8 metres (16&#160;ft) in diameter and 6.4 metres (21&#160;ft) high, set back to conical termination making it 11 metres (36&#160;ft) high. The blocked arched entrance door contains an incorrect date of 1637. It was painted white until 1822 but by 1833 had been painted red, and is now painted in bands of red and white.\n</p><p>Despite its modernity, the daymark, alongside some prehistoric cairns, is a <a href="/wiki/Scheduled_Ancient_Monument" class="mw-redirect" title="Scheduled Ancient Monument">Scheduled Ancient Monument</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SAM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SAM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The daymark is built on the highest point of St Martin\'s \xe2\x80\x94 and the second-highest point in the Isles of Scilly \xe2\x80\x94 and the islands\' only <a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey" title="Ordnance Survey">Ordnance Survey</a> <a href="/wiki/Triangulation_station" title="Triangulation station">triangulation station</a> is situated by the daymark; the position and relative height mean that the Cornish mainland is visible (when the weather allows) and the daymark is identifiable from the mainland too.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Martin%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are two churches on St Martin\'s: the <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglican</a> <a href="/wiki/St_Martin%27s_Church,_St_Martin%27s" title="St Martin&#39;s Church, St Martin&#39;s">parish church of St Martin\'s</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a> chapel, both located in the Higher Town area. The Methodist chapel dates from about 1845 and is part of the Isles of Scilly Methodist Circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_parish_and_ward">Civil parish and ward</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Martin%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Civil parish and ward">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/220px-St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/330px-St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png/440px-St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Martin%27s_Isles_of_Scilly_UK_parish_locator_map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The isles of Scilly comprising the civil parish and ward of St Martin\'s shown in red.</div></div></div>\n<p>St Martin\'s is one of the five <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parishes</a> of the Isles of Scilly, which are also <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">wards</a>. The civil parish and ward include several uninhabited islands and rocks, including the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Isles" title="Eastern Isles">Eastern Isles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Ganilly" title="Great Ganilly">Great Ganilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_Island,_Isles_of_Scilly" class="mw-redirect" title="English Island, Isles of Scilly">English Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/White_Island,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="White Island, Isles of Scilly">White Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> St Martin\'s returns 2 councillors to the Council of the Isles of Scilly, the same as the other "off-island" wards. The civil parish is not functional however, and there is no <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">council</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parish_meeting" title="Parish meeting">meeting</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Martin%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Source<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div>\n\n&#32;\n<table class="multicol" role="presentation" style="border-collapse: collapse; padding: 0; border: 0; background:transparent; width:100%;"><tbody><tr>\n<td style="width: 33.33%;text-align: left; vertical-align: top;">\n<ul><li>1841 -
