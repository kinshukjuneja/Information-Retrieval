expansion</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>1969 Major League Baseball expansion</b> resulted in the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Expansion_team" title="Expansion team">expansion franchises</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a> in the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Pilots" title="Seattle Pilots">Seattle Pilots</a> began play in the <a href="/wiki/1969_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1969 Major League Baseball season">1969 Major League Baseball season</a>. One of the reasons for expansion was increasing pressure to maintain the sport as the US <a href="/wiki/National_pastime" class="mw-redirect" title="National pastime">national pastime</a>, particularly because of the increasing popularity of professional football.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a result of expansion, the American and National Leagues reorganized. Each league was split into two divisions, forming the <a href="/wiki/American_League_East" title="American League East">American League East</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_League_West" title="American League West">American League West</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_League_East" title="National League East">National League East</a>, and <a href="/wiki/National_League_West" title="National League West">National League West</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJozsa200657_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJozsa200657-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Other candidate cities that were considered in 1967 included <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex" title="Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth metroplex">Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The latter two were rejected because they were close to cities that already had a Major League Baseball team (<a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, respectively).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In May 1966, <a href="/wiki/Commissioner_of_Baseball" title="Commissioner of Baseball">Commissioner of Baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Eckert" title="William Eckert">William Eckert</a> stated that cities that should be considered for expansion included <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves#Milwaukee_(1953\xe2\x80\x931965)" title="Atlanta Braves">Milwaukee Braves</a> had moved to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a> before the <a href="/wiki/1966_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1966 Major League Baseball season">1966 season</a>), <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oakland" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland">Oakland</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, and that expansion would occur in "eight to ten years";<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGadsden1966_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGadsden1966-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> he subsequently stated that the leagues could expand "any time after two years".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_New_York_Times:Eckert_clarifies_stand_on_expansion_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_New_York_Times:Eckert_clarifies_stand_on_expansion-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> By August, Major League Baseball had plans to expand to two 12-team leagues by 1970, and had squelched the possibility of a third league,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClassen1966_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClassen1966-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> such as the <a href="/wiki/Continental_League" title="Continental League">Continental League</a> proposed in 1959 or the proposal made in August 1968 by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Plaza" title="Ron Plaza">Ron Plaza</a> consisting of an Eastern, Central, and Western League.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHodges1968_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHodges1968-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: American League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1960, the American League conducted a survey to determine potential expansion cities. The list included Atlanta, Buffalo, Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth, <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, Oakland, San Diego, Seattle, and Toronto.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESarasota_Herald-Tribune1960_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESarasota_Herald-Tribune1960-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kansas_City_Royals">Kansas City Royals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kansas City Royals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Kansas_City_Royals" title="History of the Kansas City Royals">History of the Kansas City Royals</a></div>\n<p>Kansas City had been the home to the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Oakland_Athletics#Kansas_City_(1955\xe2\x80\x931967)" title="History of the Oakland Athletics">Kansas City Athletics</a> from 1955, when the team moved from <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, to 1967, after which the team moved to <a href="/wiki/Oakland" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland">Oakland</a>. For several years, owner <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Finley" title="Charlie Finley">Charlie Finley</a> had threatened to leave the city: in May and September 1962 to Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Bonham_Daily_Favorite1962_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Bonham_Daily_Favorite1962-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004237_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004237-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> in January 1964 he signed a contract with the state of <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a> to move the team to <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville</a> and rename the team <i>Kentucky Athletics</i>;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_New_York_Times1964_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_New_York_Times1964-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and in February 1964 he wanted to move the team to <a href="/wiki/Oakland" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland">Oakland</a>. A committee of American League owners denied his requests.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAcocella_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAcocella-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In October 1967, with the team\'s lease at <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Stadium_(Kansas_City,_Missouri)" title="Municipal Stadium (Kansas City, Missouri)">Municipal Stadium</a> expired,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004246_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004246-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Finley once again proposed to move the franchise to Oakland, this time at the owners\' meetings in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>. During the first ballot of the committee of American League owners on October 18,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004246_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004246-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> he received six supporting votes, one opposing vote (from the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, and <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Washington_Senators" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the Washington Senators">Washington Senators</a> abstained from voting. American League president <a href="/wiki/Joe_Cronin" title="Joe Cronin">Joe Cronin</a> requested a second ballot, in which the New York Yankees were the only team to change their status, opting to support the move and giving Finley "the seven votes necessary to move the team".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003260_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003260-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In a press release, Cronin announced the move and promised to expand the league by two more clubs no later than 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> began play in the <a href="/wiki/1968_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1968 Major League Baseball season">1968 Major League Baseball season</a>.\n</p><p>Cronin conveyed the news to Kansas City mayor <a href="/wiki/Ilus_W._Davis" title="Ilus W. Davis">Ilus Davis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Symington" title="Stuart Symington">Stuart Symington</a>, a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senator" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senator">US Senator</a> from <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>. Cronin had thought that the compromise to install a new team by 1971 would be acceptable to them as compensation for the loss of the Athletics. Instead, Symington threatened to challenge Major League Baseball\'s <a href="/wiki/Toolson_v._New_York_Yankees#Baseball_antitrust_exemption" class="mw-redirect" title="Toolson v. New York Yankees">antitrust exemption</a> with federal legislation,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003260_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003260-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and to also challenge the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_clause" title="Reserve clause">reserve clause</a>. Davis threatened a lawsuit to block the move.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004246_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004246-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tom_Yawkey" title="Tom Yawkey">Tom Yawkey</a> arranged a meeting of the owners still at the convention, during which the league agreed to accelerate the expansion process and assured that Kansas City would be granted a new franchise to begin play no later than the 1969 season.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This would require another franchise to be established at the same time to ensure the league had an even number of teams for a balanced schedule.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On the <a href="/wiki/Floor_(legislative)" title="Floor (legislative)">floor</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> on October 19, Symington described Finley as "one of the most disreputable characters ever to enter the American sports scene" and said that "Oakland is the luckiest city since Hiroshima".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004233_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArmourLevitt2004233-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ewing_Kauffman" title="Ewing Kauffman">Ewing Kauffman</a> won rights to the franchise, and paid a $5.5 million expansion fee for the Royals, which played games at Municipal Stadium<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJozsa200657_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJozsa200657-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> until the end of the <a href="/wiki/1972_Kansas_City_Royals_season" title="1972 Kansas City Royals season">1972 season</a>, after which the team moved to Royals Stadium, now known as <a href="/wiki/Kauffman_Stadium" title="Kauffman Stadium">Kauffman Stadium</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seattle_Pilots">Seattle Pilots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Seattle Pilots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Pilots" title="Seattle Pilots">Seattle Pilots</a></div>\n<p>By the 1960s, with Seattle\'s population growing, the city became the largest to host a <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> team, the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Rainiers" title="Seattle Rainiers">Seattle Rainiers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The league\'s stature also declined with the move of the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Brooklyn_Dodgers" title="History of the Brooklyn Dodgers">Brooklyn Dodgers</a> to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_New_York_Giants_(NL)" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the New York Giants (NL)">New York Giants</a> to <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, which caused those cities\' PCL teams to relocate to much smaller markets.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 1964, the city purchased <a href="/wiki/Sick%27s_Stadium" title="Sick&#39;s Stadium">Sick\'s Stadium</a> for $1.1 million.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 1965, the Rainiers were sold to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels" title="Los Angeles Angels">Los Angeles Angels</a>, who renamed the team the <i>Seattle Angels</i>.\n</p><p>The city made several attempts to lure a Major League Baseball team. In 1964, <a href="/wiki/William_R._Daley" title="William R. Daley">William R. Daley</a> visited the city when searching for a new home for the Cleveland Indians. He was unimpressed with the stadium, citing it as the primary reason to terminate his quest to move his team.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Charlie Finley also found the stadium inadequate during a 1967 visit, and so rejected Seattle as a potential target for moving the Kansas City Athletics.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Because of this, the city instead tried to lobby for an expansion franchise at the 1967 owners\' meetings in Chicago.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The delegation also had support from two <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">US Senators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_M._Jackson" title="Henry M. Jackson">Henry M. Jackson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warren_Magnuson" title="Warren Magnuson">Warren Magnuson</a>, the latter of whom was the chairman of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_Committee_on_Commerce,_Science_and_Transportation" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate Committee on Commerce, Science and Transportation">Senate Commerce Committee</a>, a committee which has "jurisdiction over the major league\'s business activities".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Coupled with Symington\'s threats related to the move of the Kansas City Athletics, the political influence swayed the American League owners. However, they were reluctant to expand in 1969 without a Seattle stadium bond issue.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Seattle delegation assured the owners that Sick\'s Stadium could be renovated in five months to fulfill the minimum requirements until a new stadium was built; with this, the owners agreed to a 1969 expansion, and approved teams in Kansas City and Seattle.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeterson2003261-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 1967 at the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Meetings" title="Winter Meetings">Winter Meetings</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, the franchise was officially awarded to Pacific Northwest Sports, which received $5.5 million in funding from Daley, who thus had 47% ownership of the venture.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Other owners included Max and <a href="/wiki/Dewey_Soriano" title="Dewey Soriano">Dewey Soriano</a>. The award was contingent on renovation of Sick\'s Stadium to increase its <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seating capacity</a> from 11,000 to 30,000 by the start of the 1969 season.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The Sorianos persuaded notable athletes to advocate for the $40 million <a href="/wiki/King_County,_Washington" title="King County, Washington">King County</a> stadium bond issue, including baseball players <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Mantle" title="Mickey Mantle">Mickey Mantle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Yastrzemski" title="Carl Yastrzemski">Carl Yastrzemski</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Joe_DiMaggio" title="Joe DiMaggio">Joe DiMaggio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a> player <a href="/wiki/Y._A._Tittle" title="Y. A. Tittle">Y. A. Tittle</a>; the bond issue was approved by 62.3% of the electorate.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Spokesman-Review1967_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Spokesman-Review1967-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000_16-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchaefer2000-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_League">National League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The National League had received applications for an expansion franchise from Buffalo, Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth, Milwaukee, Montreal, San Diego, and Toronto as well as an informal bid from Denver.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESpokane_Daily_Chronicle1967_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESpokane_Daily_Chronicle1967-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Montreal_Expos">Montreal Expos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Montreal Expos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos#Franchise_history" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos \xc2\xa7&#160;Franchise history</a></div>\n<p>Montreal had a long history of interest in baseball. The sport became increasingly popular in eastern North America in the 1850s and 1860s, and Montreal "became a special hotbed",<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBjarkman2005_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBjarkman2005-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> so much so that by the early 1860s Montreal authorities banned play in city parks and streets for <a href="/wiki/Public_safety" class="mw-redirect" title="Public safety">public safety</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBjarkman200573_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBjarkman200573-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Minor league baseball became a presence in the city. In 1946, the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Royals" title="Montreal Royals">Montreal Royals</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> team affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Dodgers" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn Dodgers">Brooklyn Dodgers</a> of Major League Baseball, became the first team having any affiliation to MLB to break <a href="/wiki/Baseball_color_line" title="Baseball color line">baseball\'s colour barrier</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Robinson" title="Jackie Robinson">Jackie Robinson</a>, who had been signed to a contract by <a href="/wiki/Branch_Rickey" title="Branch Rickey">Branch Rickey</a> in late 1945, played the entire season for the <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPovich1997_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPovich1997-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He and teammates <a href="/wiki/Roy_Partlow" title="Roy Partlow">Roy Partlow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Wright_(baseball)" title="Johnny Wright (baseball)">Johnny Wright</a> were the first black players in the league since the 1880s.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBjarkman200576_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBjarkman200576-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPennington2006_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPennington2006-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The next year, Robinson became the first black player to play for a Major League Baseball team. In 1951 in <a href="/wiki/Farnham,_Quebec" title="Farnham, Quebec">Farnham</a>, a town east of Montreal, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Bankhead" title="Sam Bankhead">Sam Bankhead</a> became the first black manager in <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBjarkman200576_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBjarkman200576-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> he was a player-manager for the <a href="/wiki/Farnham_Pirates" title="Farnham Pirates">Farnham Pirates</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Provincial_League_(baseball_minor_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Provincial League (baseball minor league)">Provincial League</a> that year.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBaseball_Reference_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBaseball_Reference-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Montreal_City_Council" title="Montreal City Council">Montreal City Councilor</a> Gerry Snyder spoke to <a href="/wiki/Ford_Frick" title="Ford Frick">Ford Frick</a> sometime after the 1962 Major League Baseball expansion, during which he was told Montreal would not receive an expansion franchise unless it had a stadium in which to contest matches.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> At the Winter Meetings in Mexico City on December 2, 1967, Snyder presented a proposal to Major League Baseball owners to establish a franchise in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2004_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2004-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Several influential owners pledged their support for a Montreal franchise in that meeting, including <a href="/wiki/Walter_O%27Malley" title="Walter O&#39;Malley">Walter O\'Malley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hofheinz" title="Roy Hofheinz">Roy Hofheinz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Galbreath" class="mw-redirect" title="John Galbreath">John Galbreath</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Certain that Hofheinz would object to a Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth bid and that the San Diego bid was near certain to be successful, Snyder deemed a bid from Buffalo to be the strongest bid against which to compete.\n</p><p>On 27 May 1968, the National League officially awarded a franchise to Montreal to commence play in the 1969 season.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2004_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2004-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> National League president Warren Giles had encouraged the owners during the meeting, stating "If we\'re going to expand, let\'s really spread it out".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Montreal Expos became the first franchise to be awarded to a city outside the United States. When the news reached Congress, members collectively condemned the decision.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Because of the slow pace of progress in meeting commitments, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Louis_L%C3%A9vesque" title="Jean-Louis L\xc3\xa9vesque">Jean-Louis L\xc3\xa9vesque</a> withdrew his financial support in the franchise on July 31, 1968. Snyder quickly found another investor, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Bronfman" title="Charles Bronfman">Charles Bronfman</a>, and the team met the deadline of 15 August for the initial $1.1 million installment, before which <a href="/wiki/Jarry_Park" title="Jarry Park">Jarry Park</a> was selected as the team\'s stadium for the short term.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Renovations to the park were made by adding uncovered bleacher seats along the right and left field lines, and an electronic scoreboard installed beyond right field.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPurdy2010182_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPurdy2010182-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The team had some issues committing to a new stadium, as required by the franchise award, and it was said that the team had agreed to build a dome at the <a href="/wiki/Autostade" title="Autostade">Autostade</a> and use it as their stadium if a new stadium was not built by 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_17_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_17-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It had originally intended to lease the stadium and expand its seating capacity from 26,000 to 37,000,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_17_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_17-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> but then chose Jarry Park instead.\n</p><p>The ownership group paid $12.5 million for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJozsa200659_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJozsa200659-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_McHale_(baseball)" title="John McHale (baseball)">John McHale</a> was hired as the team\'s first president,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEToronto_Star2008_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEToronto_Star2008-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Fanning" title="Jim Fanning">Jim Fanning</a> its first general manager.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Many names had been considered for the team, including <i>Royals</i> which had a strong association with the city, but the name had already been adopted by the new Kansas City franchise. After rejecting various options, including "Voyageurs" and "Nationals", the name <i>Expos</i> was chosen in honour of <a href="/wiki/Expo_67" title="Expo 67">Expo 67</a><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPurdy2010182_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPurdy2010182-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and because it was the same in both of the city\'s official languages.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> McHale stated that the name would "help Montreal be identified properly as the city that gave the world Expo 67".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMajor_League_Baseball:_History_of_the_Expos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i>The New York Daily</i> mistakenly reported that Buffalo had been awarded a franchise instead of Montreal.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEByrnes2012_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEByrnes2012-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2004_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECanadian_Broadcasting_Corporation2004-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> for a fee of $12.5 million for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJozsa200659_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJozsa200659-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Buzzie_Bavasi" class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_draft">Expansion draft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Expansion draft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1968_Major_League_Baseball_expansion_draft" title="1968 Major League Baseball expansion draft">1968 Major League Baseball expansion draft</a></div>\n<p>In order to stock the <a href="/wiki/Schedule_(workplace)" title="Schedule (workplace)">roster</a> of each team, a draft was held in which each of the extant teams would make available to the expansion franchises some of the players on their major league and minor league rosters. Each expansion team selected 30 players in the draft, and each of the extant teams lost six players. The extant teams were allowed to protect 15 players in their organization from the draft.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1968_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1968-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> After each player lost, another three players could be protected.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERathet1968_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERathet1968-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The extant teams were not informed of the draft selections of the expansion teams until the draft was complete.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 14, 1968, the Montreal Expos and San Diego Padres drafted players exclusively from National League teams. The draft was conducted from the <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Hotel_(Montreal)" title="Windsor Hotel (Montreal)">Windsor Hotel</a> in Montreal, in which four rooms were reserved: one for the Expos, one for the Padres, one for the remaining 10 teams, and one in which draft selections were made.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The Padres won a coin flip,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Spokesman-Review1968_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Spokesman-Review1968-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and so had the first selection, which they used to draft <a href="/wiki/Ollie_Brown_(baseball)" title="Ollie Brown (baseball)">Ollie Brown</a> from the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Spartanburg_Herald1968_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Spartanburg_Herald1968-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Montreal\'s first selection was <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> utility player <a href="/wiki/Manny_Mota" title="Manny Mota">Manny Mota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Spartanburg_Herald1968_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Spartanburg_Herald1968-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Selections were made by general manager Jim Fanning and manager <a href="/wiki/Gene_Mauch" title="Gene Mauch">Gene Mauch</a> for Montreal, and general manager Buzzie Bavasi and manager <a href="/wiki/Preston_G%C3%B3mez" title="Preston G\xc3\xb3mez">Preston G\xc3\xb3mez</a> for San Diego.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1968_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESt._Petersburg_Times1968-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 15 October, the Kansas City Royals and Seattle Pilots drafted players exclusively from American League teams. The draft was conducted in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBlackman1968-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Spartanburg_Herald1968_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Spartanburg_Herald1968-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Sheraton_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Sheraton Hotels and Resorts">Sheraton Hotel</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1969_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Because Buffalo was not awarded an expansion franchise, <a href="/wiki/Erie_County,_New_York" title="Erie County, New York">Erie County</a> legislators reconsidered a $50 million bond issue that was to be used for the construction of a domed <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> for baseball and <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Expansion_reaction_mixed;_some_glad,_some_real_mad1968_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Expansion_reaction_mixed;_some_glad,_some_real_mad1968-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The applicants that submitted a bid for Dallas\xe2\x80\x93Fort Worth claimed that <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hofheinz" title="Roy Hofheinz">Roy Hofheinz</a>, owner of the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>, had blocked their bid in order to preserve Houston\'s radio and television broadcast revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Expansion_reaction_mixed;_some_glad,_some_real_mad1968_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Expansion_reaction_mixed;_some_glad,_some_real_mad1968-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> owner <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Allyn,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur Allyn, Jr.">Arthur Allyn, Jr.</a> complained about his team being in the same division as two expansion franchises.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The addition of four teams resulted in the reconfiguration of the leagues; each was split into an east and a west division.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The American League announced its realignment into two divisions on 28 May 1968;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> the American League East would consist of the Baltimore Orioles, <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>, Cleveland Indians, <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a>, New York Yankees and Washington Senators, and the American League West would consist of the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">California Angels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a>, Kansas City Royals, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a>, Oakland Athletics, and <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Pilots" title="Seattle Pilots">Seattle Pilots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The National League originally resisted a divisional split, with its president <a href="/wiki/Warren_Giles" title="Warren Giles">Warren Giles</a> citing "the tradition and history of baseball".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> By July 10, the National League owners had agreed to a divisional split, in which the National League East would consist of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>, Montreal Expos, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>, and <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a>, and the National League West would consist of the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>, Houston Astros, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>, San Diego Padres, and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Game scheduling issues had to be resolved;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOttawa_Citizen196829-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> league-wide, there would be 1,944 games compared to 1,232 for the <a href="/wiki/1968_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1968 Major League Baseball season">1968 Major League Baseball season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The American League had considered decreasing the number of games played by each team to 152<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEThe_Spokesman-Review1967_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEThe_Spokesman-Review1967-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> or 156 from 162 that had been played each season since 1961,  and the National League either 162 or 165 games,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETri-City_Herald:_Two-division_split_gets_AL_approval1968-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> but the leagues agreed to a 162-game schedule in a meeting on 10 July 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Each team would play 18 games against divisional opponents, and 12 games against league opponents in the other division.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELodi_News-Sentinel1968_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELodi_News-Sentinel1968-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> A best-of-five post-season playoff series between league division winners was also introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGriffin1969-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWolf1968Sports_11-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Kansas City Royals have been the most successful of the four 1969 expansion teams. The franchise fielded competitive teams in the 1970s and 1980s and won the <a href="/wiki/1985_World_Series" title="1985 World Series">1985 World Series</a>. After a 29-year postseason drought, the Royals returned to postseason play in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Kansas_City_Royals_season" title="2014 Kansas City Royals season">2014 season</a>, before winning their second World Series title in <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Series" title="2015 World Series">2015</a>.\n</p><p>The San Diego Padres had nine consecutive losing seasons, and have since made the playoffs five times. The Padres twice made the World Series in <a href="/wiki/1984_World_Series" title="1984 World Series">1984</a> and <a href="/wiki/1998_World_Series" title="1998 World Series">1998</a>, but lost both appearances.\n</p><p>The Montreal Expos had good attendance for the <a href="/wiki/1969_Montreal_Expos_season" title="1969 Montreal Expos season">1969 season</a>, and after having losing records for ten years, were competitive in the 1980s and early 1990s. Its <a href="/wiki/1994_Montreal_Expos_season" title="1994 Montreal Expos season">1994 season</a> put it in first place before the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Major_League_Baseball_strike" title="1994\xe2\x80\x9395 Major League Baseball strike">1994\xe2\x80\x9395 Major League Baseball strike</a> prematurely ended the season. The team\'s fortunes declined thereafter, and the team moved to <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2005_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2005 Major League Baseball season">2005 season</a>, becoming the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a>.\n</p><p>The Seattle Pilots fared worst of the four expansion franchises, playing only in the <a href="/wiki/1969_Seattle_Pilots_season" title="1969 Seattle Pilots season">1969 season</a>. The team fared poorly on the field, and faced financial difficulties owing to no television coverage, a stadium with problems, and the highest ticket and <a href="/wiki/Concession_stand" title="Concession stand">concession</a> prices in the league.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Owners of other American League teams wanted Soriano and Daley to sell the team to a Seattle owner who would improve the team and address issues at Sick\'s Stadium, particularly uncovered seats with bad views.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHogan2007128-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> After several attempts to sell the team to a Seattle owner failed, on April 1, 1970 judge Sidney Volinn declared the team officially bankrupt; it was sold to <a href="/wiki/Bud_Selig" title="Bud Selig">Bud Selig</a>, who moved the team to Milwaukee and renamed it the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2006_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2006-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Selig had negotiated a deal for the purchase with Soriano during <a href="/wiki/1969_World_Series#Game_1" title="1969 World Series">Game 1 of the 1969 World Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEStone2006_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStone2006-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> As a result of the relocation, in 1970 the city of Seattle, King County, and the state of <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a> sued the American League for <a href="/wiki/Breach_of_contract" title="Breach of contract">breach of contract</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECour1999C16_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECour1999C16-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The $32.5 million lawsuit<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERiess2006802_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERiess2006802-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> was settled in 1976 when the American League offered the city a franchise in exchange for termination of the lawsuit.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECour1999C16_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECour1999C16-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> This led to the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> via the <a href="/wiki/1977_Major_League_Baseball_expansion" title="1977 Major League Baseball expansion">1977 Major League Baseball expansion</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
