title="Length">\n<th style="white-space: nowrap;">Length\n</th>\n<td>19&#160;km (12&#160;mi)\n</td></tr>\n\n\n<tr class="mergedrow" style="height: 0.2em;">\n<td colspan="2">\n</td></tr>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n</tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>River Menalhyl</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Dowr Melynheyl</b></i>, meaning <i>river of the estuary mill</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, that flows through the civil parishes of <a href="/wiki/St_Columb_Major" title="St Columb Major">St Columb Major</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Mawgan" title="St Mawgan">Mawgan-in-Pydar</a>. Its length is about 12 miles and it flows in a generally north-west direction.<sup id="cite_ref-CRP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The name comes from the Cornish words <i>melyn</i> meaning mill and <i>heyl</i> meaning estuary - <i>estuary mills</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The name was recorded as <i>Mellynheyl</i> in the 19th century, but it had been known as <i>Glyvion</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The source of the river\'s longest branch is near <a href="/wiki/Nine_Maidens_stone_row" title="Nine Maidens stone row">Nine Maidens</a>, about 2 miles to the north of St Columb,<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=River_Menalhyl&amp;params=50.447_N_4.943_W_region:GB_scale:20000"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">50\xc2\xb026\xe2\x80\xb249\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">4\xc2\xb056\xe2\x80\xb235\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">50.447\xc2\xb0N 4.943\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">50.447; -4.943</span></span></span></a></span>, at a height of approximately 490 feet above mean sea level. The Menalhyl enters the sea at <a href="/wiki/Mawgan_Porth" title="Mawgan Porth">Mawgan Porth</a> on the north coast of the county, <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=River_Menalhyl&amp;params=50.465_N_5.027_W_region:GB_scale:10000"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">50\xc2\xb027\xe2\x80\xb254\xe2\x80\xb3N</span> <span class="longitude">5\xc2\xb001\xe2\x80\xb237\xe2\x80\xb3W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">50.465\xc2\xb0N 5.027\xc2\xb0W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">50.465; -5.027</span></span></span></a></span>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Tributaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Tributaries">Tributaries</a> to the river include one that rises between the village of Tregonetha and <a href="/wiki/Castle_an_Dinas,_St._Columb_Major" class="mw-redirect" title="Castle an Dinas, St. Columb Major">Castle an Dinas</a>, and one whose source is south-east of <a href="/wiki/Tregatillian" title="Tregatillian">Tregatillian</a>. A smaller river, from <a href="/wiki/Talskiddy" title="Talskiddy">Talskiddy</a>, joins the Menalhyl a short distance from its mouth.\n</p><p>The river flows through the settlements of St Columb (the north part of the town, known as <i>Bridge</i>), <a href="/wiki/St_Mawgan" title="St Mawgan">St Mawgan</a> and Mawgan Porth. Its valley, from St Columb downstream, is called the <b>Vale of Lanherne</b>; it is wooded for much of its length and is popular with walkers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There are a small number of disused mills along the length of the Menalhyl, and the river feeds some small lakes and ponds. It is also used for agricultural purposes along most of its course.\n</p><p>The river is fished by anglers, especially in its lower course, and fish species such as <a href="/wiki/Brown_trout" title="Brown trout">brown trout</a>, sea trout, lamprey, eel and minnow have been recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-CRP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The bedrock of the catchment area largely consists of <a href="/wiki/Devonian" title="Devonian">Devonian</a> calcareous slates, grits and limestone.<sup id="cite_ref-CRP_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Menalhyl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-CRP-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-CRP_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-CRP_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-CRP_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cornwallriversproject.org.uk/geography/menalhyl.htm">"River Catchments \xe2\x80\x93 Menalhyl"</a>.
