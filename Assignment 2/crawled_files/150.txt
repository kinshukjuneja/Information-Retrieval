href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in southeast <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, UK. It has a population of 16,184 at the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It lies in the south east of Cornwall, facing <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> over the <a href="/wiki/River_Tamar" title="River Tamar">River Tamar</a>. It was in the <a href="/wiki/Caradon" title="Caradon">Caradon</a> district until March 2009 and is known as "the Gateway to Cornwall". Saltash means <i>ash tree by the salt mill</i>. Saltash is best known for the I.K Brunel Railway Overpass Bridge, a towering 134-foot bridge, constructed in 1854, which frequently transports trains over the River Tamar.  \n</p>\n<div id="toc" href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Bridge" title="Royal Albert Bridge">Royal Albert Bridge</a>, opened by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Albert_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Albert of It takes the railway line across the River Tamar. Alongside it is the <a href="/wiki/Tamar_Bridge" title="Tamar Bridge">Tamar Bridge</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Toll_bridge" title="Toll bridge">toll bridge</a> carrying the <a href="/wiki/A38_road" title="A38 road">A38</a> <a href="/wiki/Trunk_road" title="Trunk road">trunk road</a>, which in 2001 became the first <a href="/wiki/Suspension_bridge" title="Suspension bridge">suspension bridge</a> to be widened whilst remaining open to traffic.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Saltash_railway_station" title="Saltash railway station">Saltash railway station</a> is close to the town centre. It was hoped that the empty buildings could be restored as a visitor centre for the bridge, but they have instead been purchased by a property developer. The station is served by a regular train service, with some direct High Speed services to and from <a href="/wiki/London_Paddington_station" title="London Paddington station">London Paddington</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_South_West" title="Stagecoach South West">Stagecoach South West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Citybus" title="Plymouth Citybus">Plymouth Citybus</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Cornwall_Bus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Go Cornwall Bus (page does not exist)">Go Cornwall Bus</a> operate bus services from Saltash, into Plymouth city centre, Launceston, Liskeard, Looe, and Polperro.\n</p><p>The cottage of Mary Newman, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Francis_Drake" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Francis Drake">Sir Francis Drake</a>\'s first wife, can be found in Saltash on Culver Road downhill from Saltash railway station.\n</p><p>Nearby are the castles at <a href="/wiki/Trematon_Castle" title="Trematon Castle">Trematon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ince_castle" class="mw-redirect" title="Ince castle">Ince</a>, as well as the nature reserve at <a href="/wiki/Churchtown_Farm" title="Churchtown Farm">Churchtown Farm</a>, where there are some wonderful walks, with stunning views of the river. Burrell House near Saltash was built in 1621 and has a wing dated 1636. The entrance side was altered in the 18th century.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The town expanded in the 1990s with the addition of the large new estate Latchbrook, and again with the more recent building of another housing area, Pillmere. In the summer of 2009 the Saltash postcode area was judged as the most desirable place to live in Great Britain in a survey that included statistics from school results and <a href="/wiki/Crime_statistics" title="Crime statistics">crime figures</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saltash_Ferry">Saltash Ferry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Saltash Ferry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The history of Saltash is linked to the <i>passage</i>, or ferrying place across the Tamar. The <a href="/wiki/Saltash_Ferry" title="Saltash Ferry">original ferry</a> became established by fishermen for those passing to and from the <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_Germans,_Cornwall" title="St Germans, Cornwall">St Germans</a> and to <a href="/wiki/Trematon_Castle" title="Trematon Castle">Trematon Castle</a>.\n</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Conquest">Norman Conquest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert,_Count_of_Mortain" title="Robert, Count of Mortain">Robert, Count of Mortain</a>, who held the castle and manor at Trematon, took the market from the canons at St. Germans and established it at Saltash. The Count granted Trematon and other manors to the de Valletorts, who had then to provide for the ferry boat. In the twelfth century a borough was founded (1190), and Saltash became the only franchised seaport between <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth,_Devon" title="Dartmouth, Devon">Dartmouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fowey" title="Fowey">Fowey</a>. This fact has given rise to the Saltash saying, <i>Saltash was a borough town, when <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a> was a fuzzy down</i> (\'fuzzy\' = \'fursy\' i.e. covered in gorse), as the town of Plymouth and its seaport did not exist in 1190. In medieval times the ferry was part of the manor of Trematon, held by the Valletorts.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Antony_Passage_Ferry">Antony Passage Ferry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Antony Passage Ferry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Antony_Passage_(6491).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Antony_Passage_%286491%29.jpg/220px-Antony_Passage_%286491%29.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Antony_Passage_%286491%29.jpg/330px-Antony_Passage_%286491%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Antony_Passage_%286491%29.jpg/440px-Antony_Passage_%286491%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Antony_Passage_(6491).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Antony Passage</div></div></div>\n<p>The Antony Passage Ferry, which is mentioned in documents as early as 1324, was situated within the St Stephens suburb of Saltash civil parish.  \nThe ferry belonged to the Daunay family in the 14th Century, in 1450 it passed to the Carew family, and by the end of the 18th century it was taken over by the Crosley family, who operated the ferry for several generations, until it fell into disuse in the early 1950s.  The route linked Jupiter Point (part of the Antony Estate), Antony Passage and Passage Point (both part of Saltash).\n</p><p>Officially the Antony Passage Ferry is still in existence since, as a public ferry, an Act of Parliament would be necessary to terminate it, and it still appears on Ordnance Survey maps.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Charter_of_Incorporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Charter of Incorporation">Charter of Incorporation</a> refers to the place as \'Essa\' (Latin for \'Ash\'). However, the spelling of the town has changed over the years. For example, in <a href="/wiki/Edward_the_Confessor" title="Edward the Confessor">Edward the Confessor</a>\'s time it was called and spelt \'Aysche\'. In the 1584 Charter it is stated that <i>Essa is now commonly called Saltash</i>. A family called Essa lived in the twelfth century at their property near Ashtor Rock, where the Manor Courts were once held. The \'Salt\' part of the name was added to distinguish it from other places called Ash.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg/220px-Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg/330px-Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg/440px-Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Saltash_Guildhall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1703440.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Saltash Guildhall; data-file-height="427" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Saltash_Parish_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_817015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> of Saltash was, until 1881, <a href="/wiki/St_Stephen%27s_by_Saltash" class="mw-redirect" title="St Stephen&#39;s by Saltash">St Stephen\'s by Saltash</a>, one mile from the town. Though of earlier foundation, the structure of the building is largely 15th century: there are two aisles and the tower is west of the north aisle. The <a href="/wiki/Baptismal_font" title="Baptismal font">font</a> is <a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a>.\n</p><p>This church was probably founded in Norman times by the lords of Trematon Castle. Like the castle it belonged later to the Earls and Dukes of Cornwall; in 1351 King Edward III appropriated it to the Deanery of Windsor so that the benefice became a vicarage. In medieval times there was a chapel at Saltash and private chapels at Shillingham ((1318), Trehan ((1332), Earth (1413) and Trevalward (1395). The chapel at Saltash was dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Nicholas" title="Saint Nicholas">Saint Nicholas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Faith" title="Saint Faith">Saint Faith</a> and became the parish church of the town in 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Church_of_St_Nicholas_and_St_Faith,_Saltash" title="Church of St Nicholas and St Faith, Saltash">St. Nicholas &amp; St. Faith\'s Church</a> has an unbuttressed tower adjoining the former north transept which remains from the original Norman church. Also Norman are the blocked south door, most of the masonry of the chancel, the nave, the south transept and the west wall. A north chancel chapel was added in the 14th century. In the 15th century a north aisle was added to the nave; the wagon roofs of the north aisle and the nave are of the same date. The font is probably Norman and is of an unusual vague shape. There is a monument to three naval officers, John and James Drew and J. W. Drew, who were drowned in 1798 and 1799.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brunel_Primary_School,_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Brunel_Primary_School%2C_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg/220px-Brunel_Primary_School%2C_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Brunel_Primary_School%2C_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg/330px-Brunel_Primary_School%2C_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Brunel_Primary_School%2C_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg/440px-Brunel_Primary_School%2C_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="427" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brunel_Primary_School,_Saltash_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1090879.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Brunel Primary School</div></div></div>\n<p>Primary schools in Saltash include Burraton CP School, Bishop Cornish (V.A) Primary School, Brunel School (previously Longstone Infant School and Saltash Junior School) and St Stephens School, St Germans CP School\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> in Saltash was designated as a Science and Mathematics &amp; Computing Specialist College in September 2004, and renamed <a href="/wiki/Saltash.net_community_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Saltash.net community school">saltash.net community school</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Cornwall_College" title="Cornwall College">Cornwall College</a> has a campus in Saltash.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saltash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Saltash hosts a number of annual festivals: Saltash Mayfair takes place in and around Fore Street during early May, and Saltash Town Regatta brings music and entertainment to the Waterside area in the summer. On the water there are intense Gig races with the best teams taking part, along with Flashboat rowing and dinghy racing, and the now infamous \'England and back\' race between local organisations. For 2008 a new five towns challenge has been added to the list of events, pitting Saltash against <a href="/wiki/Liskeard" title="Liskeard">Liskeard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Callington,_Cornwall" title="Callington, Cornwall">Callington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Looe" title="Looe">Looe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torpoint" title="Torpoint">Torpoint</a>. There are dinghy and yacht races and activities organised for those less competitive.\n</p><p>A BMX and skatepark are available in the Saltmill Park along with pieces of public art. Saltmill is also home to an all-weather pitch available to local organisations.\n</p><p>Saltash Tennis Centre, which is located behind the Leisure centre.
