It launched at 2&#160;pm on 3 September 2007, and broadcasts to 2,100 residents of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. In 2008 the station was featured on the <a href="/wiki/BBC2" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC2">BBC2</a> programme <a href="/wiki/An_Island_Parish" title="An Island Parish">An Island id="Technical">Technical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Technical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>It broadcasts on <a href="/wiki/FM_broadcasting" title="FM broadcasting">FM</a> on 107.9 <a href="/wiki/MHz" class="mw-redirect" title="MHz">MHz</a> from the existing radio mast by the Coastguard Tower at <a href="/wiki/Telegraph,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Telegraph, Isles of Scilly">Telegraph</a> on <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St. Mary\'s</a> island.\nCurrently, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Cornwall" title="BBC Radio Cornwall">BBC Radio Cornwall</a> transmits from there on 96&#160;MHz.\nTests show that all of the Isles of Scilly can be covered from this site, and the transmitter output power is 100 <a href="/wiki/Watt" title="Watt">watts</a> vertical <a href="/wiki/Polarization_(waves)" title="Polarization (waves)">polarisation</a>, although the station is licensed for up to 200 watts mixed <a href="/wiki/Polarization_(waves)" title="Polarization (waves)">polarisation</a>.\n</p><p>It claims to be the world\'s smallest radio station, due to the small number of residents on the islands, and the fact that reception beyond islands is very limited (reception is possible in <a href="/wiki/St_Just_in_Penwith" title="St Just in Penwith">St. Just</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>). However, its licensed service area population is slightly larger than that of the UK\'s smallest licensed station <a href="/wiki/Two_Lochs_Radio" title="Two Lochs Radio">Two Lochs Radio</a> which broadcasts to an official service area population of just 1,681.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">Funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio station</a> is funded in part by a local weekly <a href="/wiki/Lottery" title="Lottery">lottery</a>, licensed by the Council for the Isles of Scilly. It costs \xc2\xa31 a week to enter and there is a weekly prize of \xc2\xa31000. The number of players is limited to 2,000 each week.
