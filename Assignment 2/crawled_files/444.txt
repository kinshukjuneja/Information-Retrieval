(<small>American Spanish:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[\xcb\x88sesa\xc9\xbe ra\xcb\x88ul \xcb\x88mesa \xcb\x88tores]</a></span>; 31 October 1991 \xe2\x80\x93 25 April 2010), commonly referred to by his alias "<b>El Mini 6</b>", was a Mexican suspected assassin of the <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa_Cartel" title="Sinaloa Cartel">Sinaloa Cartel</a>, a criminal group based in <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Meza_Ontiveros" title="Ra\xc3\xbal Meza Ontiveros">Ra\xc3\xbal Meza Ontiveros</a> (alias "El M6"), a leader of the Sinaloa Cartel. By the age of 15, Meza Torres aspired to be like his father and became involved in organized crime. He was popular on social media for posing in pictures with his weapons and for following his father\'s footsteps. Meza Torres\' pictures later served as inspiration for other teenagers aspiring to join organized crime in Mexico.\n</p><p>His criminal career was short-lived, however. Meza Torres was killed at the age of eighteen following a routine traffic stop in <a href="/wiki/Zapopan" title="Zapopan">Zapopan, Jalisco</a>, on 25 April 2010. According to the police, Meza Torres and one of his accomplices were pulled over, but they tried to intimidate the policemen by flashing their weapons. When the officers ordered them to turn themselves in, Meza Torres killed one officer but was mortally wounded by another. He was taken to a private hospital and was pronounced dead that morning.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Meza_Torres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>C\xc3\xa9sar Ra\xc3\xbal Meza Torres was born in <a href="/wiki/Culiac%C3%A1n" title="Culiac\xc3\xa1n">Culiac\xc3\xa1n, Sinaloa</a>, Mexico, on 31 October 1991. His birth certificate was registered in <a href="/wiki/Cosal%C3%A1" title="Cosal\xc3\xa1">Cosal\xc3\xa1, Sinaloa</a>, on 7 February 1992. His parents were <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Meza_Ontiveros" title="Ra\xc3\xbal Meza Ontiveros">Ra\xc3\xbal Meza Ontiveros</a> (father) and Aida Elizabeth Torres F\xc3\xa9lix (mother).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-actadenacimiento_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-actadenacimiento-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His father was reportedly a high-ranking leader of the <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa_Cartel" title="Sinaloa Cartel">Sinaloa Cartel</a>, a criminal group based in <a href="/wiki/Sinaloa" title="Sinaloa">Sinaloa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-capodurangense_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-capodurangense-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His mother was the sister of <a href="/wiki/Javier_Torres_F%C3%A9lix" title="Javier Torres F\xc3\xa9lix">Javier Torres F\xc3\xa9lix</a> (alias "El JT") and <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Torres_F%C3%A9lix" title="Manuel Torres F\xc3\xa9lix">Manuel Torres F\xc3\xa9lix</a> (alias "El M1"), who worked alongside his father.<sup id="cite_ref-cazabanondeado_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cazabanondeado-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Meza Torres was often referred to by his alias El Mini 6, in reference to his father, whose alias was El M6.<sup id="cite_ref-houston_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-houston-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was also nicknamed Raulillo and Raulito, <a href="/wiki/Diminutive" title="Diminutive">diminutives</a> of his middle name Ra\xc3\xbal.<sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Many of Meza Torres\' relatives were involved in drug trafficking just like his father.<sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Several of Meza Torres\' relatives, including his father, uncles, and cousins, have been killed by security forces, rival criminal groups, or arrested for their alleged involvement in organized crime.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Meza Torres was also romantically involved with Teresa Zambada Niebla, sister of imprisoned Sinaloa Cartel leader <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Zambada_Niebla" title="Vicente Zambada Niebla">Vicente Zambada Niebla</a> (alias "El Vicentillo") and daughter of <a href="/wiki/Ismael_Zambada_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Ismael Zambada Garc\xc3\xada">Ismael "El Mayo" Zambada</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-teresita_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teresita-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> a high-ranking Sinaloa Cartel leader who his father and brother-in-laws reported to.<sup id="cite_ref-lapared_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapared-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_career">Criminal career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Meza_Torres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criminal career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Since he was young, Meza Torres aspired to be like his father. By the age of 15, he got involved in the organized crime activities in the Sinaloa Cartel and began following his father\'s footsteps.<sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He joined the Sinaloa Cartel as one of its young assassins.<sup id="cite_ref-houston_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-houston-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a>, he had multiple pictures of himself posing with firearms; most of them were uploaded by him or his friends. His friends admired him and often commented on how he was poised to become as influential as his father.<sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Meza Torres was popular on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=MetroFLOG&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MetroFLOG (page does not exist)">MetroFLOG</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/MetroFLOG" class="extiw" title="es:MetroFLOG">es</a>)</span>,<sup id="cite_ref-metroflog_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metroflog-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and his image later helped inspire other young Mexican boys to pursue careers as organized crime hit men.<sup id="cite_ref-houston_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-houston-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 9 October 2007, a few months after his father was killed,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;b&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Meza Torres (aged 15) was pulled over by the transit police in <a href="/wiki/Las_Quintas_(Culiac%C3%A1n)" title="Las Quintas (Culiac\xc3\xa1n)">Las Quintas</a> neighborhood in Culiac\xc3\xa1n for driving an armored vehicle without <a href="/wiki/License_Plates" class="mw-redirect" title="License Plates">license plates</a>. He was subsequently arrested after not having paperwork that accredited him as the legal owner of the vehicle. He was a minor at that time and was with two other underage boys, Brayan Ra\xc3\xbal Rodr\xc3\xadguez Verdugo (aged 14) and Jes\xc3\xbas Arley P\xc3\xa9rez (aged 17). In the police report, Meza Torres address was in Villa Sat\xc3\xa9lite neighborhood in Culiac\xc3\xa1n.<sup id="cite_ref-poresto_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poresto-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Meza Torres found himself in more legal trouble two years later. On 19 June 2009, he was <a href="/wiki/Drag_racing" title="Drag racing">drag racing</a> in Las Quintas when the transit police showed up. When the police arrived at the scene and ordered the two vehicles to stop, several gunmen got off from their vehicles and began shooting at the officers. A shootout between the transit police and the assailants broke out. The gunmen eventually fled the scene and abandoned one of the two vehicles. When state authorities arrived at the location to investigate the crime scene, they discovered that the abandoned vehicle was owned by Meza Torres, and that there was a license plate inside the vehicle that corresponded to one reported as stolen. At the scene, they discovered <a href="/wiki/FN_5.7%C3%9728mm" title="FN 5.7\xc3\x9728mm">FN 5.7\xc3\x9728mm</a>  (used for the <a href="/wiki/FN_Five-seven" title="FN Five-seven">FN Five-seven</a> pistol), .<a href="/wiki/.223_Remington" title=".223 Remington">223 Remington</a>, <a href="/wiki/.38_Super" title=".38 Super">.38 Super</a>, and <a href="/wiki/.45_ACP" title=".45 ACP">.45 ACP</a> cartridges. One policeman was injured in the attack.<sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Meza_Torres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 25 April 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-25abril2010_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25abril2010-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Meza Torres (aged 18) was killed in a shootout with municipal police officers in <a href="/wiki/Zapopan" title="Zapopan">Zapopan, Jalisco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;c&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The confrontation began in early hours of the morning when police officers were patrolling Las \xc3\x81guilas neighborhood,<sup id="cite_ref-juarez_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-juarez-15">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> in the intersection between Sierra Tapalpa and Avenida 18 de Marzo streets,<sup id="cite_ref-nosedetiene_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nosedetiene-16">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and noticed a vehicle they found suspicious. When the policemen ordered the vehicle to stop for a routine search, Meza Torres and his accomplice Fidel Rojas F\xc3\xa9lix (aged 22) taunted them by pulling their weapons and shooting straight up into the air.<sup id="cite_ref-sobrino_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sobrino-17">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The officers asked them to calm down and turn themselves in, but both men then attacked the officers. Meza Torres killed police officer Jaime Vicente Morales Dom\xc3\xadnguez; he died instantly after being shot in the head. Police officer Jos\xc3\xa9 Luis Monroy suffered three gunshot wound injuries in his hand, leg, and thorax. However, Monroy was able to respond to the aggression and mortally wound Meza Torres.<sup id="cite_ref-informador_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-informador-18">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After seeing that Meza Torres was shot, Rojas F\xc3\xa9lix attempted to flee the scene by jumping onto the rooftop of a property.<sup id="cite_ref-13ejecuciones_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13ejecuciones-19">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He was arrested there by other policemen. The police confirmed that both men were from Sinaloa. At the scene, the police officers confiscated a .38 Super (used by Rojas F\xc3\xa9lix) and <a href="/wiki/9_mm_caliber" title="9 mm caliber">9 mm</a> (used by Meza Torres) handguns, 22 rounds of ammunition, and two cartridges. Rojas F\xc3\xa9lix was taken into custody by state officials from the Intentional Homicide Office of the General Prosecutor, where the government would determine his criminal status.<sup id="cite_ref-blogdelnarco_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blogdelnarco-20">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> When interrogated, Rojas F\xc3\xa9lix stated that Meza Torres and him had met with <a href="/wiki/Ignacio_Coronel_Villarreal" title="Ignacio Coronel Villarreal">Ignacio "Nacho" Coronel Villarreal</a>, a suspected leader of the Sinaloa Cartel, days prior. He also stated that the weapons they were carrying were owned by Coronel Villarreal. This information reached the <a href="/wiki/SEIDO" title="SEIDO">Subprocuradur\xc3\xada de Investigaci\xc3\xb3n Especializada en Delincuencia Organizada</a> (SIEDO), Mexico\'s former organized crime investigatory agency, but they declined to investigate.<sup id="cite_ref-vidabreve_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vidabreve-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zapopan_Municipal_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zapopan Municipal Police (page does not exist)">Zapopan Municipal Police</a> stated they would eventually plan to carry out a ceremony in memory of their fallen officer. Meza Torres was taken in a private vehicle to the hospital to treat his gunshot wounds. He was pronounced dead at 3:40&#160;a.m. after medical intervention. His <a href="/wiki/Autopsy" title="Autopsy">autopsy</a> reported confirmed that Meza Torres had a gunshot wound and multiple blunt injuries in his head, likely produced by <a href="/wiki/Pistol-whipping" title="Pistol-whipping">pistol-whipping</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blogdelnarco_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blogdelnarco-20">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> His body was transferred to Culiac\xc3\xa1n that day, where his family held a <a href="/wiki/Wake_(ceremony)" title="Wake (ceremony)">wake</a> at a funeral home on Emilio Zapata boulevard. His body stayed at the funeral home for three days before it was sent by his family to Meza Torres\' home, where another wake was held. He was buried on 29 April 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-transladan_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-transladan-21">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Meza_Torres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When Meza Torres was a teenager, musicians composed two <a href="/wiki/Narcocorrido" title="Narcocorrido">narcocorridos</a> (drug ballads) about him. The first one was made by Jes\xc3\xbas Arley P\xc3\xa9rez, a long-time friend of his.
