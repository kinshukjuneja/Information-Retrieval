class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Most people in Cornwall indentify their health as \'good\' but the proportion of those in good health is slightly lower than the national average (at 81.2%). The proportion of those indentifying their health as \'bad\' was similar to those over the whole of the England and Wales (5.6%).<sup id="cite_ref-CC-Census2011_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC-Census2011-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>In good health: 78.8% (an increase of 12.6% since 2001)</li>\n<li>In bad health: 6.3% (a decrease of 3.6% since class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li>Limits day to day activities \'a lot\': 10.0%</li>\n<li>Limits day to day activities \'a little\': 11.4%</li>\n<li>Day to day activities \'not limited\': 78.6%</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carers">Carers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a unpaid care every week: 11.9% (an increase of 0.6% since 2001)\n<ul><li>Provides 1-19 hours of unpaid care per week: 7.3%</li>\n<li>Provides 20-49 hours of unpaid care per week:
