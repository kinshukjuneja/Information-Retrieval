href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">parliamentary</a> <a href="/wiki/Constituency" class="mw-redirect" title="Constituency">constituency</a> in <a Its council is based in <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Dorset" title="Dorchester, Dorset">Dorchester</a>.  The district was formed on 1 April 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, and was a merger of the <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">boroughs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bridport" title="Bridport">Bridport</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Municipal_Borough_of_Dorchester&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Municipal Borough of Dorchester (page does not exist)">Dorchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lyme_Regis" title="Lyme Regis">Lyme Regis</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Sherborne" title="Sherborne">Sherborne</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural districts</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Beaminster_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Beaminster Rural District (page does not exist)">Beaminster</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bridport_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bridport Rural District (page does not exist)">Bridport</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dorchester_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dorchester Rural District (page does not exist)">Dorchester</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sherborne_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sherborne Rural District (page does not exist)">Sherborne</a>. In 2006 the district was named 10th best place to live in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>The district and its council will be abolished on 1st April 2019 and, together with the other 4 districts outside the greater Bournemouth area, will form a new <a href="/wiki/Dorset_(unitary_authority)" title="Dorset (unitary authority)">Dorset</a> unitary authority.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" href="/w/index.php?title=West_Dorset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">Census 2001</a> West Dorset registered a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 92,350, estimated to be 94,000 as of  2004<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=West_Dorset&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>.  The population structure reflects the rural nature of the district.  52% of the population are female.  The area is a popular retirement area which also exports young people due the low choice of career options.  This is reflected in the age structure, with 12.3% of the population over 75, compared to 7.5% in England; 51.7% are between 15 and 59, compared to 59.1% in England.  34.4% of dwellings are pensioner households, compared to 23.8% in England.\n</p><p>The district is an example of the low <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_minority" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic minority">ethnic minority</a> populations in rural areas, with 96.7% white British ethnicity, compared to 87.0% in England.\n</p><p>The district has a high level of car ownership, at 83.1% of households compared to 73.2% in England.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Constituency">Constituency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Dorset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Constituency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/West_Dorset_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="West Dorset (UK Parliament constituency)">West Dorset (UK Parliament constituency)</a></div>\n<p>West Dorset is a <a href="/wiki/County_constituency" class="mw-redirect" title="County constituency">county constituency</a>, currently represented in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">British House of Commons</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> front-bencher <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Letwin" title="Oliver Letwin">Oliver Letwin</a>.  Elections in this constituency are usually a two-party contest between the Conservative and <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrat</a> parties. It is almost identical in area with the West Dorset district, but a very small part of West Dorset District lies within the <a href="/wiki/South_Dorset" class="mw-redirect" title="South Dorset">South Dorset</a> county constituency, currently represented in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">British House of Commons</a> by the Conservative Party member <a href="/wiki/Richard_Drax" title="Richard Drax">Richard Drax</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlements">Settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Dorset&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Settlements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The main settlements in West Dorset are <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Dorset" title="Dorchester, Dorset">Dorchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sherborne" title="Sherborne">Sherborne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bridport" title="Bridport">Bridport</a>. Dorchester is in the south of the district and is the county town of Dorset. It has been an important settlement since <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> times. Sherborne is in the north of the district and is an important <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> which was, for a time, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a>. Bridport is in the west of the district and is popular with tourists visiting the <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Coast" title="Jurassic Coast">Jurassic Coast</a>, as is the smaller coastal town of <a
