<b>Appalachian League of Professional Baseball</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Rookie" title="Rookie">Rookie</a>-class <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> league that began play in 1911. It operated as a Class D league (1911&#8211;1914), (1921&#8211;1925), (1937&#8211;1955) and (1957&#8211;1962) before becoming a Rookie league in 1963. Teams are located in the <a href="/wiki/Appalachia" title="Appalachia">Appalachian</a> regions of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>. The league\'s season starts in June, after <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">major league</a> teams have signed players they selected in the annual amateur <a href="/wiki/Draft_(sports)" title="Draft (sports)">draft</a>, and ends in September.\n</p><p>Along with the <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_League_(baseball)" title="Pioneer League (baseball)">Pioneer League</a>, it forms the second-lowest rung on the minor league ladder. Although classified as a Rookie league, the level of play is slightly higher than that of the two Rookie leagues based at the parent clubs\' <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> complexes, the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_League" title="Gulf Coast League">Gulf Coast League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arizona_League" title="Arizona League">Arizona League</a>. Unlike these two leagues, Appalachian League games charge admission and sell concessions.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Appalachian_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The original Appalachian League only existed for four seasons from 1911&#8211;1914 and all teams were independent with no MLB affiliation. The original league consisted of the <a href="/wiki/Asheville,_North_Carolina" title="Asheville, North Carolina">Asheville</a> Moonshiners, the <a href="/wiki/Bristol,_Virginia" title="Bristol, Virginia">Bristol</a> Boosters, the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Tennessee" title="Cleveland, Tennessee">Cleveland</a> Counts, the <a href="/wiki/Johnson_City,_Tennessee" title="Johnson City, Tennessee">Johnson City</a> Soldiers, the <a href="/wiki/Knoxville,_Tennessee" title="Knoxville, Tennessee">Knoxville</a> Appalachians, and the <a href="/wiki/Morristown,_Tennessee" title="Morristown, Tennessee">Morristown</a> Jobbers.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The second Appalachian League existed for five seasons from 1921&#8211;1925, and, as before, it consisted entirely of independent teams: the Bristol State-Liners, the Cleveland Manufacturers, the <a href="/wiki/Greeneville,_Tennessee" title="Greeneville, Tennessee">Greeneville</a> Burley Cats, the second iteration of the Johnson City Soldiers, the <a href="/wiki/Kingsport,_Tennessee" title="Kingsport, Tennessee">Kingsport</a> Indians, and the Knoxville Pioneers. Two of the 1921 locations have present-day teams in the Appalachian League: Kingsport, Tennessee, with the present-day Kingsport Mets, and Greeneville, Tennessee, with the present-day Greeneville Reds.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The third iteration of the Appalachian league, which started in 1937, was shifted to D-level minor league, the lowest level in the pre-1963 MLB. It consisted of four teams: the Elizabethton Betsy Red Sox, the third iteration of the Johnson City Soldiers, the <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Tennessee" title="Newport, Tennessee">Newport</a> Canners, and the <a href="/wiki/Pennington_Gap,_Virginia" title="Pennington Gap, Virginia">Pennington Gap</a> Lee Bears.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_2-2" class="reference"><a
