east and <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a> to the west. A former agricultural market town, it lies at the intersection of the south-north <a href="/wiki/A388_road" title="A388 road">A388</a> Saltash to Launceston road and the east-west <a href="/wiki/A390_road" title="A390 road">A390</a> <a href="/wiki/Tavistock,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Tavistock, Devon">Tavistock</a> to <a href="/wiki/Liskeard" title="Liskeard">Liskeard</a> road.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kit_Hill" title="Kit Hill">Kit Hill</a> is a mile north-east of the town and rises to 333 metres (1,093&#160;ft) with views of Dartmoor, <a href="/wiki/Bodmin_Moor" title="Bodmin Moor">Bodmin Moor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/River_Tamar" title="River Tamar">River Tamar</a>.\n</p><p>The hamlets of Bowling Green, <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Bray" title="Kelly Bray">Kelly Bray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frogwell" title="Frogwell">Frogwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Downgate,_Cornwall" title="Downgate, Cornwall">Downgate</a> are in the parish.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway_station">Railway station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Railway station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Callington_railway_station" title="Callington railway station">Callington railway station</a></div>\n<p>Callington railway station was the terminus of a branch line from <a href="/wiki/Bere_Alston" title="Bere Alston">Bere Alston</a>, the junction with the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Railway (Great Britain)">Southern Railway</a>\'s Tavistock to Plymouth line. The railway line beyond <a href="/wiki/Gunnislake" title="Gunnislake">Gunnislake</a> to the Callington terminus was closed in the 1960s, due to low usage and difficult operating conditions on the final sections of the line due to several severe gradients and speed restrictions. One can still travel by rail on the <a href="/wiki/Tamar_Valley_Line" title="Tamar Valley Line">Tamar Valley Line</a> from Plymouth as far as Gunnislake via Bere Alston, where trains reverse. For most of its journey the line follows the River Tamar. Gunnislake is the nearest railway station to Callington, although the nearest mainline station is at Saltash.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Food manufacturers  <a href="/wiki/Ginsters" title="Ginsters">Ginsters</a> and The Cornwall Bakery (both wholly owned subsidiaries of Samworth Brothers) are the largest employers in the town.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Ginsters uses local produce in many of its products, buying potatoes and other vegetables from local farmers and suppliers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There is also a <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> supermarket, opened in 2010, which employs 200 local people.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_antiquities">History and antiquities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History and antiquities">edit</a><span class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:StMary%27sChurchCallington.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Mary\'s Church, viewed from the southeast</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TombOfRobertWilloughbyCallington.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" Church, Callington, north wall of chancel</div></div></div>\n<p>Callington has been postulated as one of the possible locations of the ancient site of <i><a href="/wiki/Celliwig" title="Celliwig">Celliwig</a></i>, associated with <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Nearby ancient monuments include <a href="/w/index.php?title=Castlewitch_Henge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Castlewitch Henge (page does not exist)">Castlewitch Henge</a> with a diameter of 96m<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Cadsonbury" class="mw-redirect" title="Cadsonbury">Cadsonbury</a> Iron Age <a href="/wiki/Ringfort" title="Ringfort">hillfort</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> as well as <a href="/wiki/Dupath_Well" title="Dupath Well">Dupath Well</a> built in 1510 on the site of an ancient sacred <a href="/wiki/Spring_(hydrology)" title="Spring (hydrology)">spring</a>.\n</p><p>Callington was recorded in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> (1086); the manor had four hides of land and land for thirty ploughs. The lord had land for three ploughs with eleven serfs. Twenty-four villeins and fourteen smallholders had land for fifteen ploughs. There were also one and a half square leagues of pasture and a small amount of woodland. The income of the manor was \xc2\xa36 sterling.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1601 Robert Rolle (died 1633) purchased the manor of Callington, thereby gaining the <a href="/wiki/Pocket_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Pocket borough">pocket borough</a> seat of <a href="/wiki/Callington_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Callington (UK Parliament constituency)">Callington</a> in Parliament,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> which in future served to promote the careers of many Rolles. He nominated to this seat his brother <a href="/wiki/William_Rolle_(died_1652)" title="William Rolle (died 1652)">William Rolle</a> (died 1652) in 1604 and 1614, his son Sir <a href="/wiki/Henry_Rolle" title="Henry Rolle">Henry Rolle</a> (1589\xe2\x80\x931656), of <a href="/wiki/Shapwick,_Somerset" title="Shapwick, Somerset">Shapwick</a>, in 1620 and 1624, his son-in-law <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Wise_(MP_for_Devon)" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas Wise (MP for Devon)">Thomas Wise</a> (died March 1641) of <a href="/wiki/Sydenham_House,_Devon" title="Sydenham House, Devon">Sydenham</a> in Devon, in 1625, and another son <a href="/wiki/John_Rolle_(Parliamentarian)" title="John Rolle (Parliamentarian)">John Rolle</a> (1598\xe2\x80\x931648), \n</p><p>In the 19th-century, Callington was one of the most important mining areas in Great Britain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Deposits of silver were found nearby in Silver Valley. Today, the area is marked by mining remains, but there are no active mines. <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">Granite</a> is still quarried on <a href="/wiki/Hingston_Down" title="Hingston Down">Hingston Down</a>.\n</p><p>The former <a href="/wiki/Callington_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Callington (UK Parliament constituency)">Callington constituency</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rotten_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotten borough">rotten borough</a>, elected two members to the <a href="/wiki/Unreformed_House_of_Commons" title="Unreformed House of Commons">unreformed House of Commons</a> but was abolished by the <a href="/wiki/Reform_Act_1832" title="Reform Act 1832">Reform Act 1832</a>. The town is now in the <a href="/wiki/South_East_Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="South East Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">South East Cornwall constituency</a>.\n</p><p>St Mary\'s Church was originally a chapel of ease to <a href="/wiki/South_Hill,_Cornwall" title="South Hill, Cornwall">South Hill</a>; it was consecrated in 1438 and then had two aisles and a buttressed tower; a second north aisle was added in 1882. Unusually for Cornwall there is a clerestory; the wagon roofs are old. The parish church contains the fine brass of Nicholas Assheton and his wife, 1466.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the churchyard there is a Gothic lantern cross. It was first mentioned by the historian <a href="/wiki/William_Borlase" title="William Borlase">William Borlase</a> in 1752. Each of the four faces of the cross head features a carved figure beneath an ogee arch. The heads of these figures have been chiselled off, no doubt in the Commonwealth period.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Callington is one of a small number of towns to continue to appoint a <a href="/wiki/Portreeve" title="Portreeve">Portreeve</a>; originally a medieval revenue officer and now an honorary title given to the chairman of the town council.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Callington Town Council has twelve members and covers the civil parish of Callington. At the Council elections in 2013 only ten candidates stood, eight Independents and two <a href="/wiki/Mebyon_Kernow" title="Mebyon Kernow">Mebyon Kernow</a> Councillors.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In recent years, the town has seen much residential development with more, including social housing, planned for the next few years.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The neighbouring village of <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Bray" title="Kelly Bray">Kelly Bray</a> has almost doubled in size in recent years with houses still being built in the area.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Twinning">Twinning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Twinning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Callington is <a href="/wiki/Twin_towns_and_sister_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin towns and sister cities">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Guipavas" title="Guipavas">Guipavas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, France, and <a href="/wiki/Barsb%C3%BCttel" title="Barsb\xc3\xbcttel">Barsb\xc3\xbcttel</a> near <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> in Germany.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  It also has unofficial friendship links with <a href="/wiki/Keila" title="Keila">Keila</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> and a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Malaga" class="mw-redirect" title="Malaga">Malaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Callington has both football and cricket teams. Callington Town Football Club (established 1989) has four adult teams playing in the <a href="/wiki/South_West_Peninsula_League" title="South West Peninsula League">South West Peninsula League</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Cornwall_League" title="East Cornwall League">East Cornwall League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duchy_League" title="Duchy League">Duchy League</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_West_Regional_Women%27s_Football_League" title="South West Regional Women&#39;s Football League">South West Regional Women\'s Football League</a>. They all play at Marshfield Parc. Callington Cricket Club has three teams playing in the Cornwall Cricket League and play their games at Moores Park.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Category:People_from_Callington,_Cornwall" title="Category:People from Callington, Cornwall">People from Callington</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Dupath_Well" title="Dupath Well">Dupath Well</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/East_Cornwall_Mineral_Railway" title="East Cornwall Mineral Railway">East Cornwall Mineral Railway</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Callington_Community_College" title="Callington Community College">Callington Community College</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Callington,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140729194902/http://www.magakernow.org.uk/pdf/placename_masterlist.pdf">"List of Place-names agreed by the MAGA Signage Panel"</a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>.
