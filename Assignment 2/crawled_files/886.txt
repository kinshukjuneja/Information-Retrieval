/>.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cotswold</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.  It is named after the wider <a href="/wiki/Cotswolds" title="Cotswolds">Cotswolds</a> region.  Its main town is <a href="/wiki/Cirencester" title="Cirencester">Cirencester</a>.\n</p><p>It was formed on 1 April 1974 by the merger of the urban district of Cirencester with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cirencester_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cirencester Rural District (page does not exist)">Cirencester Rural District</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Cotswold_Rural_District" title="North Cotswold Rural District">North Cotswold Rural District</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Northleach_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Northleach Rural District (page does not exist)">Northleach Rural District</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tetbury_Rural_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tetbury Rural District (page does not exist)">Tetbury Rural District</a>. The population of the District at the time of the 2011 Census was about 83,000.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Eighty per cent of the district lies within the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> catchment area, with the Thames itself and several tributaries including the <a href="/wiki/River_Windrush" title="River Windrush">River Windrush</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Leach" title="River Leach">River Leach</a> running through the district. <a href="/wiki/Lechlade" title="Lechlade">Lechlade</a> in an important point on the river as the upstream limit of navigation. In the <a href="/wiki/2007_floods_in_the_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 floods in the UK">2007 floods in the UK</a>, rivers were the source of flooding of 53 per cent of the locations affected and the Thames at Lechlade reached record levels with over 100 reports of flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The District is spread over 450 square miles, with some 80% of the land located within the Cotswold Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The much larger area referred to as the Cotswolds encompasses nearly 800 square miles,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> over five counties: Gloucestershire, Oxfordshire, Warwickshire, Wiltshire, and Worcestershire.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This large Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty had a population of 139,000 in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a
