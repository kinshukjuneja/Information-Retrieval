href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Airline" title="Airline">airline</a> which operates year-round scheduled services to the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> from <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End_Airport" title="Land&#39;s End Airport">Land\'s End Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newquay_Cornwall_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Newquay Cornwall Airport">Newquay Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, and seasonal scheduled services from <a href="/wiki/Exeter_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Exeter International Airport">Exeter</a>. Their head office is located in the Isles of Scilly Travel Centre in <a href="/wiki/Penzance" title="Penzance">Penzance</a>, Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-hq_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hq-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Isles of Scilly Skybus Limited holds a <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Civil Aviation Authority (United Kingdom)">United Kingdom Civil Aviation Authority</a> Type B Operating Licence; it is permitted to carry passengers, cargo and mail on aircraft with fewer than 20 seats and/or weighing less than 10 tonnes.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The airline is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" title="Isles of Scilly Steamship Company">Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FI_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The parent company operates shipping services to the Isles of Scilly in its own name, and also owns <a href="/wiki/Westward_Airways_(United_Kingdom)" title="Westward Airways (United Kingdom)">Westward Airways</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isles_of_Scilly_Skybus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The airline was established in March 1984 and started operations in August 1984. Isles of Scilly Skybus originated as an air charter operator at Land\'s End aerodrome which operated high frequency services to the Isles of Scilly. Following the withdrawal of <a href="/wiki/Brymon_Airways" title="Brymon Airways">Brymon Airways</a> routes to and from the islands the operator stepped in, initially operating services to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>. This route was later dropped but with the addition of <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada_DHC-6_Twin_Otter" title="De Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter">Twin Otter</a> aircraft the route network expanded to its current make-up. It is wholly owned by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Steamship_Company" title="Isles of Scilly Steamship Company">Isles of Scilly Steamship Company</a> which also runs the passenger ferry, <i><a href="/wiki/Scillonian_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Scillonian III">Scillonian III</a></i>, and freight services from Penzance to the Islands. Skybus celebrated 25 years of service in June 2009, painting the aircraft tails with the 25 years mark. An extra Twin Otter aircraft was sourced for the beginning of the 2013 season following the cessation of helicopter services to and from the islands. Flights to <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Airport" title="Bristol Airport">Bristol Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Airport" title="Southampton Airport">Southampton Airport</a> were dropped at the end of the 2012 season to concentrate on services from Exeter, Newquay and Land\'s End.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
