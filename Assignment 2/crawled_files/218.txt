He also has the highest career earnings in MLB history.</div></div></div>\n<p>The highest-paid player in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) from the <a href="/wiki/2018_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2018 Major League Baseball season">2018 Major League Baseball season</a> is <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>\' <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Kershaw" title="Clayton Kershaw">Clayton Kershaw</a> with an annual salary of $34,000,000 on a 7 year contract for $215,000,000. MLB does not have a hard <a href="/wiki/Salary_cap" title="Salary cap">salary cap</a>, instead employing a <a href="/wiki/Luxury_tax" title="Luxury tax">luxury tax</a> which applies to teams whose total payroll exceeds certain set thresholds for a given season.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">Free agency</a> did not exist in MLB prior to the end of the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_clause" title="Reserve clause">reserve clause</a> in the 1970s, allowing owners before that time to wholly dictate the terms of player negotiations and resulting in significantly lower salaries. Babe Ruth, widely regarded as one of the greatest baseball players ever, earned an estimated $910,696 ($14,654,832 inflation-adjusted from 1931 dollars) over his entire playing career.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When asked whether he thought he deserved to earn $80,000 a year ($1,171,952 inflation-adjusted), while the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">president</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a>, had a $75,000 salary, Ruth famously remarked, "What the hell has Hoover got to do with it? Besides, I had a better year <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Crash_of_1929" title="Wall Street Crash of 1929">than he did</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Alex Rodriguez has signed two record-breaking contracts over the course of his career. First, he signed a $252 million, 10-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> in December 2000 ($358,107,826 inflation-adjusted from 2000 dollars).<sup id="cite_ref-aroddeal1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aroddeal1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Alderson" title="Sandy Alderson">Sandy Alderson</a> called the deal "stupefying", while <i><a href="/wiki/Sports_Illustrated" title="Sports Illustrated">Sports Illustrated</a></i> noted that Rodriguez\'s early salaries under the contract ($21 million) would be greater than the annual payroll of the entire <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> team that year ($15.8 million).<sup id="cite_ref-aroddeal1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aroddeal1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The deal was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_sports_contracts" title="List of largest sports contracts">largest sports contract in history</a>, doubling the total value of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Garnett" title="Kevin Garnett">Kevin Garnett</a>\'s $126 million <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> contract (the previous record holder) and more than doubling <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hampton" title="Mike Hampton">Mike Hampton</a>\'s $121 million contract, the previous MLB record which had been signed just days before.<sup id="cite_ref-aroddeal1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aroddeal1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Rangers later traded Rodriguez to the Yankees in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_Soriano" title="Alfonso Soriano">Alfonso Soriano</a> before the 2004 season, though they agreed to pay $67 million of the $179 million outstanding on the contract.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, he <a href="/wiki/Opt-out" title="Opt-out">opted out</a> of the remainder of his deal after the 2007 season and renegotiated a new $275 million, 10-year agreement with the Yankees, breaking his own record for the largest sports contract.<sup id="cite_ref-aroddeal2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aroddeal2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Under this deal, Rodriguez also receives $6 million each if and when he ties the career <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> totals of <a href="/wiki/Willie_Mays" title="Willie Mays">Willie Mays</a> (660), <a href="/wiki/Babe_Ruth" title="Babe Ruth">Babe Ruth</a> (714), <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron" title="Hank Aaron">Hank Aaron</a> (755), and <a href="/wiki/Barry_Bonds" title="Barry Bonds">Barry Bonds</a> (762), along with another $6 million for breaking Bonds\' mark.<sup id="cite_ref-aroddeal2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aroddeal2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/First_base" class="mw-redirect" title="First base">First base</a> was the highest-paid position in 2010; regular starters at that position earned an average salary of $9,504,165 in compared to an overall average of $3,014,572.<sup id="cite_ref-2010avg_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010avg-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">Pitcher</a> <a href="/wiki/Nolan_Ryan" title="Nolan Ryan">Nolan Ryan</a> was the first player to earn an annual salary above $1 million, signing a $4.5 million, 4-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> in 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-landmarkfa_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-landmarkfa-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kirby_Puckett" title="Kirby Puckett">Kirby Puckett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rickey_Henderson" title="Rickey Henderson">Rickey Henderson</a> signed the first contracts which paid an average of $3 million a year in November 1989, in 1990 <a href="/wiki/Jose_Canseco" title="Jose Canseco">Jose Canseco</a> signed for 5 years and $23.5 million, making him the first player to earn an average of $4 million a year. It was until 2010 when the MLB average salary rose above that same mark.<sup id="cite_ref-2010avg_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010avg-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Five of the twenty highest-paid players in 2013 were members of the Yankees. Their team payroll for 2013 was $228,835,490, roughly $12 million above the second-largest <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Yankees have drawn criticism for their payroll, with some claiming it undermines the <a href="/wiki/Parity_(sports)" title="Parity (sports)">parity</a> of MLB.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a A $20 million/2-year contract would have an average annual value of $10 million, even if the player actually received $9 million one season and $11 million in the other. This also does not include contracts for less than a season <a href="/wiki/Prorate" class="mw-redirect" title="Prorate">prorated</a> in value for a full season such as Roger Clemens\' 2006 and 2007 contracts.<sup id="cite_ref-clemensbref_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clemensbref-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup> All values are listed in <a href="/wiki/Real_versus_nominal_value_(economics)" title="Real versus nominal value (economics)">nominal dollars</a>.</li>\n<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Winfielda"><a href="#ref_Winfielda"><b><sup>a</sup></b></a></span>  Dave Winfield initially negotiated a 10-year deal in 1980 worth a projected $25 million ($2.5 million per year).<sup id="cite_ref-winfield1_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-winfield1-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> However, Yankees owner <a href="/wiki/George_Steinbrenner" title="George Steinbrenner">George Steinbrenner</a> reportedly had not fully understood a <a href="/wiki/Cost_of_living" title="Cost of living">cost of living</a> adjustment provision in it and the 10-year contract was renegotiated a few months later. The final precise value is unclear, although it fell between $20 and $25 million in total value ($2\xe2\x80\x932.5 million a year).<sup id="cite_ref-winfieldbref_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-winfieldbref-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Hershb"><a href="#ref_Hershb"><b><sup>b</sup></b></a></span> Hershiser and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Viola" title="Frank Viola">Frank Viola</a> both won the 1988 <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a> and Viola signed an identically-sized $7.9 million, 3-year contract two months after Hershiser.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_3millc"><a href="#ref_3millc"><b><sup>c</sup></b></a></span> <a href="/wiki/Rickey_Henderson" title="Rickey Henderson">Rickey Henderson</a> signed a $12 million, 4-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> on November 28, 1989, 6 days after Puckett signed his $9 million, 3-year contract.<sup id="cite_ref-langstonnyt_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-langstonnyt-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_Clemens2d"><a href="#ref_Clemens2d"><b><sup>d</sup></b></a></span>  Roger Clemens signed a contract extension during the 2000 season covering 2001 and 2002 along with a player option for 2003. Clemens was paid $10.3 million each year for 2001 and 2002, with the same $10.3 million available if he elected to play in 2003 under his option. However, Clemens also received $10.3 million if he rejected the option. As a result, he could effectively collect the full $30.9 million of contract value for only two years of play ($15.45 million annually).<sup id="cite_ref-clemens0203_87-1" class="reference"><a annually, three-year deal.<sup id="cite_ref-cnnsitable_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnnsitable-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He became a free agent after the 2002 season and ultimately
