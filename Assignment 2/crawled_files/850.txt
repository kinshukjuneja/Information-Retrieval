links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacific_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The circuit was founded as the <b>Taiheiyo Baseball Union</b> in 1949 (the name changing to its current form in 1980). <a href="/wiki/Daiei_Stars" title="Daiei Stars">Daiei Stars</a> owner <a href="/wiki/Masaichi_Nagata" title="Masaichi Nagata">Masaichi Nagata</a> was the first president of the Pacific League.<sup id="cite_ref-Fame_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fame-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The league began with seven teams: four holdovers from the previous iteration, the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Baseball_League" title="Japanese Baseball League">Japanese Baseball League</a> \xe2\x80\x94 the <a href="/wiki/Hankyu_Braves" class="mw-redirect" title="Hankyu Braves">Hankyu Braves</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nankai_Hawks" class="mw-redirect" title="Nankai Hawks">Nankai Hawks</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Daiei_Stars" title="Daiei Stars">Daiei Stars</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Tokyu_Flyers" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokyu Flyers">Tokyu Flyers</a> \xe2\x80\x94 and three new teams \xe2\x80\x94 the <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Pearls" class="mw-redirect" title="Kintetsu Pearls">Kintetsu Pearls</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Orions" class="mw-redirect" title="Mainichi Orions">Mainichi Orions</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Nishitetsu_Clippers" class="mw-redirect" title="Nishitetsu Clippers">Nishitetsu Clippers</a>.\n</p><p>In 1954 an eighth Pacific League team was founded, the <a href="/wiki/Takahashi_Unions" title="Takahashi Unions">Takahashi Unions</a>, to increase the number of teams to eight. Although the team was stocked with players from the other Pacific League teams, the Unions struggled from the outset and finished in the second division every season. In 1957, the Unions were merged with the <a href="/wiki/Daiei_Stars" title="Daiei Stars">Daiei Stars</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Daiei_Unions" class="mw-redirect" title="Daiei Unions">Daiei Unions</a> (and again bringing the number of Pacific League teams down to seven). In their first season, the Unions finished in last place, 43-1/2 games out of first. In 1958, the Unions merged with the <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Orions" class="mw-redirect" title="Mainichi Orions">Mainichi Orions</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Daimai_Orions" class="mw-redirect" title="Daimai Orions">Daimai Orions</a>. This enabled the Pacific League to shrink from the ungainly seven-team arrangement to six teams.\n</p><p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fujio_Nakazawa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fujio Nakazawa (page does not exist)">Fujio Nakazawa</a>, a former player and television commentator, became the PL\'s first full-time president in 1959, serving through 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1973 to 1982, the Pacific League employed a <a href="/wiki/Apertura_and_Clausura" title="Apertura and Clausura">split season</a>, with the first-half winner playing against the second-half winner in a mini-playoff to determine its champion.\n</p><p>Beginning in 1975, the Pacific League began using the <a href="/wiki/Designated_hitter" title="Designated hitter">designated hitter</a> (DH), as in the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. During interleague play (adopted in 2005), the DH is used in Pacific League teams\' home games.\n</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/2004_in_sports" title="2004 in sports">2004</a> season, the <a href="/wiki/Orix_BlueWave" class="mw-redirect" title="Orix BlueWave">Orix BlueWave</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Kintetsu_Buffaloes" title="Osaka Kintetsu Buffaloes">Osaka Kintetsu Buffaloes</a> merged to form the <a href="/wiki/Orix_Buffaloes" title="Orix Buffaloes">Orix Buffaloes</a>.  A franchise was granted to the <a href="/wiki/Tohoku_Rakuten_Golden_Eagles" title="Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles">Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles</a> to fill the void caused by the merger.\n</p><p>Also since 2004, a three-team <a href="/wiki/Playoff" class="mw-redirect" title="Playoff">playoff</a> system was introduced in the Pacific League (Pacific League Championship Series). The teams with the second- and third-best records play in the three-game first stage, with the winner advancing to the five-game final against the top team. The winner becomes the representative of the Pacific League to the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Series" title="Japan Series">Japan Series</a>.\n</p><p>Since the Pacific League won every Japan Series after introducing this system, an identical system was introduced to the <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central League</a> in 2007, and the post-season intra-league games were renamed the "<a href="/wiki/Climax_Series" title="Climax Series">Climax Series</a>" in both leagues. Player statistics and drafting order based on team records are not affected by these postseason games.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_teams">Current teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacific_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Team\n</th>\n<th>Japanese Name\n</th>\n<th>Place\n</th>\n<th>Stadium\n</th>\n<th>Sponsor\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Chiba_Lotte_Marines" title="Chiba Lotte Marines">Chiba Lotte Marines</a>\n</td>\n<td>\xe5\x8d\x83\xe8\x91\x89\xe3\x83\xad\xe3\x83\x83\xe3\x83\x86\xe3\x83\x9e\xe3\x83\xaa\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x83\xb3\xe3\x82\xba<br />Chiba Rotte Mar\xc4\xabnzu\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Chiba,_Chiba" class="mw-redirect" title="Chiba, Chiba">Chiba, Chiba</a>\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Zozo_Marine_Stadium" title="Zozo Marine Stadium">ZOZO Marine Stadium</a>\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Lotte_(conglomerate)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotte (conglomerate)">Lotte</a>\n</td></tr>\n\n<tr>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_SoftBank_Hawks" title="Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks">Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks</a>\n</td>\n<td>\xe7\xa6\x8f\xe5\xb2\xa1\xe3\x82\xbd\xe3\x83\x95\xe3\x83\x88\xe3\x83\x90\xe3\x83\xb3\xe3\x82\xaf\xe3\x83\x9b\xe3\x83\xbc\xe3\x82\xaf\xe3\x82\xb9<br />Fukuoka Sofutobanku H\xc5\x8dkusu\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Prefecture" title="Fukuoka Prefecture">Fukuoka</a>\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Yahuoku!_Dome" title="Fukuoka Yahuoku!
