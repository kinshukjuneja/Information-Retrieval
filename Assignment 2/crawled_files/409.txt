class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><b>Methleigh</b> was the site of a fair and annual market from the year 1066.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After the Norman Conquest, the Bishop of Exeter held the manor of Methleigh, but the Earl of Cornwall possessed the fair. At the time of the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Survey" class="mw-redirect" title="Domesday Survey">Domesday Survey</a> there were 15 acres (6.1&#160;ha) of arable land, 40 acres (16&#160;ha) of pasture and 60 acres (24&#160;ha) of underwood. The population consisted of 15 villeins, 4 smallholders and 3 serfs.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Until 1844 Porthleven was within the parish of <a href="/wiki/Sithney" title="Sithney">Sithney</a>; the parish church of St Bartholomew was built in 1842. The name Porthleven is probably connected with St <a href="/wiki/Elwen" title="Elwen">Elwen</a> or Elwyn, whose chapel here existed before 1270. It was rebuilt c. 1510 but was destroyed in 1549. There were also chapels at Higher Penrose and Lanner Veor (the latter founded in 1377) and a holy well at Venton-Vedna.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/William_Cookworthy" title="William Cookworthy">William Cookworthy</a> acquired leases on <a href="/wiki/Tregonning_Hill" title="Tregonning Hill">Tregonning Hill</a> quarries and shipped <a href="/wiki/Kaolinite" title="Kaolinite">china clay</a> to his <a href="/wiki/Porcelain" title="Porcelain">porcelain</a> factory in Plymouth.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Export of china clay from Porthleven ceased in 1880. Granite was also exported from the quarries at <a href="/wiki/Coverack_Bridges" title="Coverack Bridges">Coverack Bridges</a> and Sithney.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Porthleven\'s most recognisable building is the Bickford-Smith Institute next to the pier and harbour entrance. With a tower about 70 feet (20 m) high, it looks like a church, but currently is used as a snooker club and houses the town council offices. It featured (along with various other scenes from the town) as the incident room in an episode of the TV detective series <a href="/wiki/Wycliffe_(TV_series)" title="Wycliffe (TV series)">Wycliffe</a>. A picture of the building against a large breaking wave sometimes appears in the background of BBC UK weather forecasts, particularly when windy conditions and rough seas are expected.\n</p><p>For local-government purposes, Porthleven was included within the town boundaries of nearby <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">Helston</a>. After years of growth, it now has its own <a href="/wiki/Town_council" title="Town council">town council</a> and its population recorded by the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">United Kingdom Census 2001</a> was 3,190.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Overnight on 12\xe2\x80\x9313 December 1978, Police Constables Joseph James Childs and Martin Ross Reid of <a href="/wiki/Devon_and_Cornwall_Police" title="Devon and Cornwall Police">Devon and Cornwall Police</a> were killed when their patrol car was swept into the harbour during heavy storms. A stone memorial to them was later erected on the south-facing harbour wall.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifeboat">Lifeboat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lifeboat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Due to the <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">prevailing westerly winds</a> it was easy for a ship under sail to get trapped in the bay and be wrecked on the nearby coast. The <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">Royal National Lifeboat Institution</a> stationed a <a href="/wiki/Lifeboat_(rescue)" title="Lifeboat (rescue)">lifeboat</a> at Porthleven in 1863. A boat house was built at Breageside from where the boat was taken to the water on a carriage. A new boat house on the west side of the harbour entrance was opened in 1894 with a slipway to make launching easier. The station was closed in 1929 as the neighbouring stations at <a href="/wiki/The_Lizard_Lifeboat_Station" title="The Lizard Lifeboat Station">The Lizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penlee_Lifeboat_Station" title="Penlee Lifeboat Station">Penlee</a> had been equipped with motor lifeboats that could cover the whole of <a href="/wiki/Mount%27s_Bay" title="Mount&#39;s Bay">Mount\'s Bay</a>. The slipway was dismantled and the boat house was used as a store for a while but has since become the Shipwreck Centre museum.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_and_leisure">Sport and leisure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sport and leisure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Porthleven has exploited its location and exposure to powerful <a href="/wiki/Swell_(ocean)" title="Swell (ocean)">swells</a> to become one of the best-known and highly regarded <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a> spots in <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">Britain</a> and has been described as "Cornwall\'s best reef break". Waves often exceeding 6.6 feet (2.0&#160;m), break on the shallow <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reef</a> that was shaped by blasting the harbour.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Kayaking is also popular. RNLI lifeguards patrol the beach during the holiday season.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The beach is separated from the harbour by a granite pier, which stands in front of the Porthleven institute and clock tower. When the <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">tide</a> is out it is possible to walk east along Porthleven beach for approximately three miles. There is also a coastal path with views of the beach below.\n</p><p>Porthleven Bowling Club is based at Methleigh Parc and is affiliated to Bowls Cornwall and Bowls England. The club was founded in 1959 and has lawn bowling and short-mat bowls facilities.  The club competes against other clubs and individuals within Cornwall and nationally, and there are in-house competitions as well.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Porthleven_F.C." title="Porthleven F.C.">Porthleven</a> has a <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">non-League football</a> club which plays in the <a href="/wiki/South_West_Peninsula_League" title="South West Peninsula League">South West Peninsula League</a>; a league which operates at levels 10 and 11 of the <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a>. Their home ground is at Gala Parc.\n</p><p>The restaurateur <a href="/wiki/Rick_Stein" title="Rick Stein">Rick Stein</a> has a restaurant in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Twinning">Twinning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Twinning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Porthleven is <a href="/wiki/Twin_towns_and_sister_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin towns and sister cities">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Guisseny" class="mw-redirect" title="Guisseny">Guisseny</a> (Gwiseni) in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, France.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Porthleven was the birthplace of the Victorian theologian and religious writer <a href="/wiki/Pender_Hodge_Cudlip" title="Pender Hodge Cudlip">Pender Hodge Cudlip</a> and of <a href="/wiki/David_Jewell" title="David Jewell">David Jewell</a>, a prominent <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent school</a> headmaster during the late 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-education.guardian.co.uk_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-education.guardian.co.uk-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Porthleven was the home town of the <a href="/wiki/Operation_Chastise" title="Operation Chastise">Dambusters</a>\' Commanding Officer, <a href="/wiki/Guy_Gibson" title="Guy Gibson">Guy Gibson</a>, and there is a road named in his memory. Former <a href="/wiki/Motor_racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor racing">motor racing</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Chris_Craft_(racing_driver)" title="Chris Craft (racing driver)">Chris Craft</a>, who competed in the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> race for over a decade, including a third-placed finish in <a href="/wiki/1976_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="1976 24 Hours of Le Mans">1976</a>, as well as two races in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>, was born in Porthleven.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protected_areas">Protected areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Protected areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are four <a href="/wiki/Sites_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" class="mw-redirect" title="Sites of Special Scientific Interest">Sites of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI) sites close to Porthleven and they are also <a href="/wiki/Geological_Conservation_Review" title="Geological Conservation Review">Geological Conservation Review</a> (GCR) sites. Three are designated for their geological interest, they are <b>Porthleven Cliffs SSSI</b>, <b>Porthleven Cliffs East SSSI</b> and <b>Wheal Penrose SSSI</b> The "Giant\'s Rock", within Porthleven Cliffs SSSI, is an <a href="/wiki/Glacial_erratic" title="Glacial erratic">erratic</a> of unknown origin and unknown mode of transport to its present site near the entrance of Porthleven harbour. The Wheal Penrose SSSI is a disused lead mine 550 yards (500&#160;m) to the south with "good examples of typical lead <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zone_mineralisation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zone mineralisation (page does not exist)">zone mineralisation</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The fourth <a href="/wiki/Loe_Pool" class="mw-redirect" title="Loe Pool">Loe Pool SSSI</a> is Cornwall\'s largest natural lake formed by a <a href="/wiki/Barrier_beach" class="mw-redirect" title="Barrier beach">barrier beach</a>, known as Loe Bar, which dams the <a href="/wiki/River_Cober" title="River Cober">River Cober</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Porthleven (like almost a third of Cornwall) lies within the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" title="Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty">Cornwall Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty</a> (AONB).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Porthleven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
