Redruth lies approximately at the junction of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_road_numbering_scheme" title="Great Britain road numbering scheme">A</a>393 and A3047 roads, on the route of the old London to <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End" title="Land&#39;s End">Land\'s End</a> trunk road (now the <a href="/wiki/A30_road" title="A30 road">A30</a>), and is approximately  9 miles (14&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a>, 12 miles (19&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/St_Ives,_Cornwall" title="St Ives, Cornwall">St Ives</a>, 18 miles (29&#160;km) north east of <a href="/wiki/Penzance,_Cornwall" class="mw-redirect" title="Penzance, Cornwall">Penzance</a> and 11 miles (18&#160;km) north west of <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Cornwall" title="Falmouth, Cornwall">Falmouth</a>. <a href="/wiki/Camborne" title="Camborne">Camborne</a> and Redruth together form the largest urban area in Cornwall and before local government reorganisation were an <a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redruth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The name <b>Redruth</b> (pronounced \'Red-<b>rooth\'</b>) derives from its older Cornish name, <i>Rhyd-ruth</i>.  It means Red Ford (literally fordred). The first syllable \'red\' means ford. The second \'ruth\' means red.\n</p><p><i>Rhyd</i> is the older form of \'Res\', which is a Cornish equivalent to a ford (across a river), a common Celtic word; Old Cornish <i>rid</i>; Welsh <i>rhyd</i> (Old Welsh <i>rit</i>); Old Breton <i>rit</i> or <i>ret</i>, Gaulish <i>ritu-</i>, all from Indo-European <i>*prtus</i> derived word in <i>-tu</i> from the root *per " to cross, to go through "; Proto-Germanic <i>*furd\xc3\xbaz</i> (English ford, German <i>Furt</i>); Latin <i>portus</i>, all related to the Celtic word.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>It is the <i>-ruth</i> (and not the <i>Red-</i> part of the name) which means the colour red.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a This location is a steeply wooded valley, with <a href="/wiki/Carn_Brea,_Redruth" title="Carn Brea, Redruth">Carn Brea</a> on one side and the now-called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bullers_Hill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bullers Hill (page does not exist)">Bullers Hill</a> on the other. The presence of shallow <a href="/wiki/Lode" title="Lode">lodes</a> of tin and copper lying east to west made it an advantageous site for extracting metals, including, tin, lead and copper. The first settlers stayed by a crossing in the river and started extracting metal ores, and this process turned the colour of the river red.\n</p><p>Historically, Redruth was a small <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> overshadowed by its neighbours until a boom in the demand for copper <a href="/wiki/Ore" title="Ore">ore</a> during the 18th century. Copper ore had mostly been discarded by the Cornish <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a>-mining industry but was now needed to make <a href="/wiki/Brass" title="Brass">brass</a>, an essential metal in the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. Surrounded by copper ore deposits, Redruth quickly became one of the largest and richest mining areas in Britain and the town\'s population grew markedly, although most miners\' families remained poor.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In the 1880s and 1890s the town end of Clinton Road gained a number of institutions, notably a <a href="/wiki/School_of_Metalliferous_Mining" title="School of Metalliferous Mining">School of Mines and Art School</a> in 1882\xe2\x80\x9383, St. Andrew\'s Church (replacing the chapel in Chapel Street) in 1883 and, opposite, the Free Library, built in 1895. The <a href="/wiki/Mining_Exchange" title="Mining Exchange">Mining Exchange</a> was built in 1880 as a place for the trading of mineral stock. By the turn of the 20th century, Victoria Park had been laid out to commemorate the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Jubilee">Golden Jubilee</a> and this part of town had taken on its present appearance \xe2\x80\x93 a far cry from the jumble of mining activity that had taken place there in the early 19th century. Redruth was making its transition from a market town dominated by mines and industry to a residential centre.\n</p><p>By the end of the 19th century, the Cornish mining industry was in decline and Britain was importing most of its copper ore. To find employment, many miners <a href="/wiki/Emigration" title="Emigration">emigrated</a> to the newer mining industries in the Americas, Pachuca, Mexico, <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a> and South Africa. Cornwall\'s last fully operational mine, <a href="/wiki/South_Crofty" title="South Crofty">South Crofty</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pool,_Cornwall" title="Pool, Cornwall">Pool</a> between Redruth and <a href="/wiki/Camborne" title="Camborne">Camborne</a>, closed in March 1998.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redruth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notable buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Parish Church of St Uny, which is some distance from the town centre, is of <a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a> foundation but was rebuilt in 1756. The patron saint is also honoured at Lelant. The tower is two centuries earlier and the whole church is built of granite. A <a href="/wiki/Chapel_of_ease" title="Chapel of ease">chapel of ease</a> was built in the town in 1828 but it is no longer in use.<sup id="cite_ref-Pevsner,_N._1970_p._150_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pevsner,_N._1970_p._150-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Other places of worship include the Wesleyan Church of 1826, the Free Methodist Church of 1864 (in grand Italianate style) and the Quaker Meeting House of 1833 (no longer in use).<sup id="cite_ref-Pevsner,_N._1970_p._150_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pevsner,_N._1970_p._150-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The former post office in Alma Place is now known as the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Studies_Centre" title="Cornish Studies Centre">Cornish Studies Centre</a>: also housed there is the collection of Tregellas Tapestries which depict the history of Cornwall in embroidery. The Mining Exchange building is now used as a housing advice centre (it was built in 1880 as accommodation for share brokers).\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" Gardens</div></div></div>\n<p>The house now called Murdoch (or, sometimes Murdock) House in the middle of Cross Street was erected in the 1660s as a chapel and it afterwards became a prison. <a href="/wiki/William_Murdoch" title="William Murdoch">William Murdoch</a> lived in it from 1782 to 1798. During this time, he worked on local tin and copper mines, erecting engines on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Boulton_and_Watt" title="Boulton and Watt">Boulton and Watt</a>.\nHe fitted the house out with <a href="/wiki/Gas_lighting" title="Gas lighting">gas lighting</a> from <a href="/wiki/Coal_gas" title="Coal gas">coal gas</a> \xe2\x80\x93 this was the first house in the world with this type of lighting.\n</p><p>In the 19th century, the house was used as a tea room, run by a Mrs Knuckey. In 1931 Mr A. Pearce Jenkin, a leading citizen of Redruth purchased the house and gave it as a gift to the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Friends" class="mw-redirect" title="Society of Friends">Society of Friends</a> (<a href="/wiki/Quakers" title="Quakers">Quakers</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Murdoch House has since been fully restored and is now regularly used by the Redruth <a href="/wiki/Old_Cornwall_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Cornwall Society">Old Cornwall Society</a>, as well as the Cornish-American Connection and the Redruth Story Group. One of the two specialist shopping areas</div></div></div>\n<p>Redruth is a small commercial town, with a population recorded in <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001</a> of 12,352. It is <a href="/wiki/Town_twinning" class="mw-redirect" title="Town twinning">twinned</a> with Plumergat<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Meriadec" class="mw-redirect" title="Meriadec">Meriadec</a> in Brittany, France and <a href="/wiki/Mineral_Point,_Wisconsin" title="Mineral Point, Wisconsin">Mineral Point, Wisconsin</a> in the United States, where Cornish immigrants built many of the stone buildings still standing. A museum organised by the Old Cornwall Society<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> is housed in the Town Council office at the bottom of the main street.\n</p><p>Redruth is also home to <a href="/wiki/Carn_Brea,_Redruth" title="Carn Brea, Redruth">Carn Brea</a>, which has most historical interest. The Carn however is not the highest point in Redruth, beaten slightly by <a href="/wiki/Carnmenellis" title="Carnmenellis">Carnmenellis</a>, south west of the town centre.\n</p><p><i><b>Sport</b></i> <a href="/wiki/Redruth_RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Redruth RFC">Redruth Rugby Football Club</a> currently play in <a href="/wiki/National_League_2_South" title="National League 2 South">National League 2 South</a> (tier 4 of rugby union in England). Players such as <a href="/wiki/Phil_Vickery_(rugby_player)" class="mw-redirect" title="Phil Vickery (rugby player)">Phil Vickery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Thirlby" title="Rob Thirlby">Rob Thirlby</a> have both passed through its ranks. Redruth <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Soccer club</a> has not enjoyed as much success but still thrives in its regular fixtures. On the high street there is a <a href="/wiki/Tang_Soo_Do" title="Tang Soo Do">Tang Soo Do</a> (Korean Karate) and <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">Kickboxing</a> Academy named ISK Martial Arts. <a href="/wiki/Duchy_Hockey_Club" title="Duchy Hockey Club">Duchy Hockey Club</a> are based at Pool Academy on the outskirts of Redruth and compete in the West of England regional leagues.\n</p><p><i><b>Performing arts</b></i> Actress <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Scott_Thomas" title="Kristin Scott Thomas">Kristin Scott Thomas</a>, electronic musician <a href="/wiki/Luke_Vibert" title="Luke Vibert">Luke Vibert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baritone" title="Baritone">baritone</a> <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Luxon" title="Benjamin Luxon">Benjamin Luxon</a>, opera singer <a href="/wiki/Alan_Opie" title="Alan Opie">Alan Opie</a> and the co founder of and drummer with <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Mac" title="Fleetwood Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mick_Fleetwood" title="Mick Fleetwood">Mick Fleetwood</a>, were all born here. The writer and comedian <a href="/wiki/Rory_McGrath" title="Rory McGrath">Rory McGrath</a> was born here and educated at the town\'s secondary school, Cornish artist <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Polkest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jonathan Polkest (page does not exist)">Jonathan Polkest</a> studied art at The Redruth School of Art in Clinton Road, The musician <a href="/wiki/Aphex_Twin" title="Aphex Twin">Aphex Twin</a> (and friend of Vibert) grew up near Redruth. Luke Vibert has released an album called <i>Chicago, Detroit, Redruth</i> and has also recorded under the alias <i>Kerrier District</i>; a reference to name of the district in which Redruth is located. <a href="/wiki/Hedluv_%2B_Passman" title="Hedluv + Passman">Hedluv + Passman</a> both grew up in Redruth, and wrote the song "\'druth" in tribute to the town. The traditional Cornish dance and music group, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hevva&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hevva (page does not exist)">Hevva</a> is based in Redruth.\n</p><p><i><b>Scholars and philanthropists</b></i> The historians <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Hamilton_Jenkin" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenneth Hamilton Jenkin">Kenneth Hamilton Jenkin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Thomas_(historian)" title="Charles Thomas (historian)">Charles Thomas</a> were also born in Redruth, and the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian</a> philanthropist <a href="/wiki/John_Passmore_Edwards" title="John Passmore Edwards">John Passmore Edwards</a> was born in the neighbouring village of Blackwater.\n</p><p><i><b>Town centre shopping</b></i> Key shops and other outlets within the <a href="/wiki/Town_centre" title="Town centre">town centre</a> include a multi-screen <a href="/wiki/Cinema_(place)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cinema (place)">cinema</a>, a covered <a href="/wiki/Market_(place)" class="mw-redirect" title="Market (place)">market</a> way, the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Studies_Centre" title="Cornish Studies Centre">Cornish Studies Centre</a>, an old butter market, various <a href="/wiki/Antique_shop" title="Antique shop">antique shops</a>, a second hand <a href="/wiki/Book_shop" class="mw-redirect" title="Book shop">book shop</a> and two supermarkets, plus Greens Newsagents, The Emporium (formerly John Oliver\'s) which still carries on the tradition of selling music and books (mainly of local historical interest) and antiques, as well as providing other products (gifts, stationery, greeting cards, etc.) and the local cash and carry Jims. Off the main street (<a href="/wiki/Fore_Street" title="Fore Street">Fore Street</a>), there are two separate specialist shopping areas, Bond Street (to the south of the <a href="/wiki/Redruth_railway_station" title="Redruth railway station">railway station</a>) and Green Lane to the north.\n</p><p>The new <i><b>street landscaping</b></i> includes wooden seating, with granite furniture, new <a href="/wiki/Signpost" class="mw-redirect" title="Signpost">signposts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Street_lights" class="mw-redirect" title="Street lights">street lights</a> and <a href="/wiki/Litter_bin" class="mw-redirect" title="Litter bin">litter bins</a>, and two sets of <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> \'dogs\', which were cast from the boots of former tin miners by sculptor David Kemp. The town has a burgundy colour theme, which is in the new <a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_Realm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Public Realm (page does not exist)">Public Realm</a> regeneration work to highlight the town\'s name. A project to light various <a href="/wiki/Public_buildings" class="mw-redirect" title="Public buildings">public buildings</a> around the town with <a href="/wiki/LED" class="mw-redirect" title="LED">LED</a> coloured lights has already commenced. Some of the buildings opposite the railway station and the St. Rumon\'s Gardens have now (April 2008) been completed.\n</p><p><i><b>Environment awards</b></i> On 7 November 2007, Redruth jointly won (with <a href="/wiki/Luton" title="Luton">Luton</a>) the annual UK town centre environment awards, which are run by the BCSC (a retail property consortium).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The judges praised the cast bronze \'dogs\'<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and also liked the large amount of work that had been done to the town in terms of landscaping the central area (mainly Fore St &amp; the opeways).\n</p><p><i><b>Heartlands visitor park</b></i> Just outside the town is a European funded visitor attraction, themed on the Robinson Shaft Mine, it offers parking, an adventure play ground for older children and a pleasant restaurant. At times the last working <a href="/wiki/Cornish_engine" title="Cornish engine">Cornish pumping engine</a> can be seen working.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The museum is an Anchor point on the <a href="/wiki/European_Route_of_Industrial_Heritage" title="European Route of Industrial Heritage">European Route of Industrial Heritage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><i><b>Miscellany</b></i> It is home to Cornwall\'s first ILR Radio Station, <a href="/wiki/Pirate_FM" title="Pirate FM">Pirate FM</a>. On 20 June 2008, the town held its first regular <a href="/wiki/Farmers%27_Market" class="mw-redirect" title="Farmers&#39; Market">Farmers\' Market</a> in Market Place: the Market will now be held every Friday throughout the year.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redruth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg/220px-Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg/330px-Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg/440px-Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Redruth_-_Railway_Station.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Redruth railway station</div></div></div>\n<p>Redruth is an important transport hub. The <a href="/wiki/Redruth_railway_station" title="Redruth railway station">railway station</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Railhead" title="Railhead">railhead</a> for both <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">Helston</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lizard,_Cornwall" class="mw-redirect" title="Lizard, Cornwall">Lizard</a>, and there are frequent buses connecting the three places. The railway station is served by trains from <a href="/wiki/Paddington" title="Paddington">Paddington</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/English_Midlands" class="mw-redirect" title="English Midlands">Midlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">North</a>. Redruth is next to the main <a href="/wiki/A30_road" title="A30 road">A30 road</a> and thus has access to the main route out of the county as well as routes to the <a href="/wiki/Penwith" title="Penwith">far West</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Cornwall" title="North Cornwall">North Cornwall</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_East_Cornwall" class="mw-redirect" title="South East Cornwall">South East Cornwall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>. Another road, the A393, bisects the town in a north-south direction, and links the A30 with the port of <a href="/wiki/Falmouth,_Cornwall" title="Falmouth, Cornwall">Falmouth</a>. A third road, the A3047, links Redruth with <a href="/wiki/Camborne" title="Camborne">Camborne</a>, some four miles (6.4&#160;km) to the west.\n</p><p>The Barncoose by-pass connects between the Redruth Community Hospital and the Barncoose <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Estate" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial Estate">Industrial Estate</a>. It is intended to reduce <a href="/wiki/Heavy_Goods_Vehicle" class="mw-redirect" title="Heavy Goods Vehicle">HGV</a> traffic using the main Camborne road and provide a direct access to the Industrial Estate. It did, however, provoke some controversy, as some residents in Barncoose lost their parking spaces to make way for the new road.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It was extended further towards Camborne in 2016.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redruth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Redruth_R.F.C." title="Redruth R.F.C.">Redruth R.F.C.</a> are Redruth\'s highest-level sports team.
