href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">PC</a></span> (3 September 1678&#160;&#8211;&#32;17 January 1766) was an English nobleman, <a href="/wiki/Courtier" title="Courtier">courtier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropist</a>, styled <b>Viscount Rialton</b> 1706\xe2\x80\x931712. He was among the founding governors of the <a href="/wiki/Foundling_Hospital" title="Foundling Hospital">Foundling Hospital</a>, a charity created in 1739.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Biography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Biography</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Issue"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Issue</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Notes"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Notes</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Godolphin,_2nd_Earl_of_Godolphin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Godolphin was the only child of <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Godolphin,_1st_Earl_of_Godolphin" title="Sidney Godolphin, 1st Earl of Godolphin">Sidney Godolphin, 1st Earl of Godolphin</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Whitehall" title="Whitehall">Whitehall</a>, London, on 3 September 1678, and baptised the same day. His mother, <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Godolphin" title="Margaret Godolphin">Margaret Godolphin</a>, died six days later on 9 September. <a href="/wiki/John_Evelyn" title="John Evelyn">John Evelyn</a>, who had been her most intimate acquaintance, transferred his friendship to her infant son, took charge of the general superintendence of his education, and continued to take an interest in his welfare as he grew.\n</p><p>Francis Godolphin was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a>, and at <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College,_Cambridge" title="King&#39;s College, Cambridge">King\'s College, Cambridge</a>, where he took his <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">M.A. degree</a> in 1705.<sup id="cite_ref-Venn_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Venn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His first public appointment was that of joint <a href="/wiki/Registrar_(law)" title="Registrar (law)">registrar</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_chancery" class="mw-redirect" title="Court of chancery">court of chancery</a> on 29 June 1698, which he held to 20 January 1727, holding also the place of one of the tellers of the <a href="/wiki/Exchequer" title="Exchequer">exchequer</a> from 1699 to 1704. He was chosen a member of parliament for <a href="/wiki/Looe" title="Looe">East Looe</a> in Cornwall on 1 December 1701, but on 4 February 1701\xe2\x80\x932 elected to serve for <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">Helston</a>, and sat for that <a href="/wiki/Constituency" class="mw-redirect" title="Constituency">constituency</a> till 21 September 1710. As <a href="/wiki/Cofferer_of_the_household" class="mw-redirect" title="Cofferer of the household">cofferer of the household</a> he was in office from 1704 to 1711, and acted as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Warden_of_the_Stannaries" title="Lord Warden of the Stannaries">Lord Warden of the Stannaries</a>, high steward of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">duchy of Cornwall</a>, and rider and master <a href="/wiki/Forester" title="Forester">forester</a> of <a href="/wiki/Forest_of_Dartmoor" title="Forest of Dartmoor">Dartmoor</a> from 1705 to 1708. He was known under the courtesy title of <i>Viscount Rialton</i> from 29 December 1706 till 1712. He sat for the <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">county of Oxford</a> from 1708 to 1710, and for <a href="/wiki/Tregony" title="Tregony">Tregony</a> in Cornwall from the latter date until he was elevated to the upper house as second <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Godolphin" title="Earl of Godolphin">Earl of Godolphin</a> on the death of his father on 15 September 1712.\n</p><p>He was again cofferer of the household 1714\xe2\x80\x931723, <a href="/wiki/Lord_lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord lieutenant">lord lieutenant</a> of the county of Oxford 1715\xe2\x80\x931735, <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_bedchamber" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord of the bedchamber">lord of the bedchamber</a> to <a href="/wiki/George_I_of_Great_Britain" title="George I of Great Britain">George I</a> 1716, <a href="/wiki/High_Steward_of_Banbury" title="High Steward of Banbury">High Steward of Banbury</a> 1718, and a <a href="/wiki/Privy_council" title="Privy council">privy councillor</a> 26 May 1723. To <a href="/wiki/George_II_of_Great_Britain" title="George II of Great Britain">George II</a> he was <a href="/wiki/Groom_of_the_stole" class="mw-redirect" title="Groom of the stole">groom of the stole</a>, and first lord of the bedchamber 1727\xe2\x80\x931735. He was named high steward of <a href="/wiki/Woodstock,_Oxfordshire" title="Woodstock, Oxfordshire">Woodstock</a> 18 March 1728 and, on 18 April 1733, appointed <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#Lord_Proprietors_of_the_Scilly_Islands" title="Isles of Scilly">Governor of the Scilly Islands</a>.\n</p><p>On 23 January 1735, he was created <i><a href="/wiki/Baron_Godolphin" title="Baron Godolphin">Baron Godolphin</a> of Helston in Cornwall</i>, with special remainder, in default of his own issue, to the heirs male of his deceased uncle, Dr. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Godolphin" title="Henry Godolphin">Henry Godolphin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_(Christianity)" title="Dean (Christianity)">dean</a> of <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul&#39;s Cathedral">St. Paul\'s</a>. During the king\'s absence from Great Britain in 1723, 1725, and 1727, he acted as one of the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Justice" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Justice">lords justices</a> of the United Kingdom. Finally, as <a href="/wiki/Lord_privy_seal" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord privy seal">lord privy seal</a>, he was in office from 14 May 1735 to 25 April 1740. The <a href="/wiki/Pocket_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Pocket borough">pocket borough</a> of Helston, not far from his ancestral home, <a href="/wiki/Godolphin_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Godolphin House">Godolphin House</a>, was under his <a href="/wiki/Patronage" title="Patronage">patronage</a> for many years, and sent his nominees to parliament. In return for this complaisance, he rebuilt <a href="/w/index.php?title=Churches_and_places_of_interest&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Churches and places of interest (page does not exist)">Helston Church</a> in 1763, at an expense of \xc2\xa36,000, and it was also his custom to pay the rates and taxes for all the electors in the borough. It is said that he only read two works, <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Burnet" title="Gilbert Burnet">Burnet\'s</a> <i>History of my own Time</i> and <a href="/wiki/Colley_Cibber" title="Colley Cibber">Colley Cibber</a>\'s <i>Apology</i>. When he had perused them throughout, he began them again. \n</p><p>He married, in March 1698, <a href="/wiki/Henrietta_Godolphin,_2nd_Duchess_of_Marlborough" title="Henrietta Godolphin, 2nd Duchess of Marlborough">Lady Henrietta Churchill</a>, eldest daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Churchill,_1st_Duke_of_Marlborough" title="John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough">John Churchill, 1st Duke of Marlborough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Jennings" class="mw-redirect" title="Sarah Jennings">Sarah Jennings</a>. She had been born on 20 July 1681, and baptised at <a href="/wiki/St._Martin-in-the-Fields" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Martin-in-the-Fields">St. Martin-in-the-Fields</a>, London on 29 July 1681. On the death of her father, 16 June 1722, she became Duchess of Marlborough, later dying on 24 October 1733, and was buried in <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a> on 9 November. She had acquired much notoriety by her attachment to <a href="/wiki/William_Congreve" title="William Congreve">William Congreve</a>, the dramatist.\n</p><p>Godolphin was one of the founding Governors of the charity called the <a href="/wiki/Foundling_Hospital" title="Foundling Hospital">Foundling Hospital</a>, created in 1739. The charity aimed to tackle the problem of <a href="/wiki/Child_abandonment" title="Child abandonment">child abandonment</a> in London by providing an <a href="/wiki/Orphanage" title="Orphanage">orphanage</a> where parents could leave babies they considered themselves incapable of raising. He also had the distinction of owning one of the founding thoroughbred sires, the <a href="/wiki/Godolphin_Arabian" title="Godolphin Arabian">Godolphin Arabian</a>. Among his proteg\xc3\xa9s was the physician and humourist <a href="/wiki/Messenger_Monsey" title="Messenger Monsey">Messenger Monsey</a>, for whom he obtained the position of physician to <a href="/wiki/Royal_Chelsea_Hospital" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Chelsea Hospital">Royal Chelsea Hospital</a>.\n</p><p>He died at his house in the Stable Yard, St James\'s on 17 January 1766, and was buried in Kensington Church on 25 January, when the earldom of Godolphin, viscounty of Rialton, and barony of Godolphin of Rialton became extinct; the barony of Godolphin of Helston devolved upon his cousin <a href="/wiki/Francis_Godolphin,_2nd_Baron_Godolphin" title="Francis Godolphin, 2nd Baron Godolphin">Francis Godolphin, 2nd Baron Godolphin</a> of Helston.<sup id="cite_ref-2"
