University</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Idris Legbo Kutigi</b> (31 December 1939 \xe2\x80\x93 21 October 2018) was a <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigerian</a> lawyer and judge. He was Attorney General and Commissioner for Justice in Niger State before becoming a high court judge.  Kutigi joined the  <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Nigeria" title="Supreme Court of Nigeria">Supreme Court of Nigeria</a> in 1992 and served as <a href="/wiki/Chief_Justice_of_Nigeria" title="Chief Justice of Nigeria">Chief Justice</a> from  30 January 2007 until 30 December 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-scnbio_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scnbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Education"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Idris_Legbo_Kutigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Born in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kutigi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kutigi (page does not exist)">Kutigi</a>, <a href="/wiki/North-Western_State" title="North-Western State">North-Western State</a> (now located in the <a href="/wiki/Lavun" title="Lavun">Lavun Local Government Area</a> of <a href="/wiki/Niger_State" title="Niger State">Niger State</a>), Kutigi attended elementary school in that town and middle and secondary school in <a href="/wiki/Bida" title="Bida">Bida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-scnbio_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scnbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He then moved on to Government College (now known as <a href="/wiki/Barewa_College" title="Barewa College">Barewa College</a>), and then to <a href="/wiki/Ahmadu_Bello_University" title="Ahmadu Bello University">Ahmadu Bello University</a> (both in <a href="/wiki/Zaria" title="Zaria">Zaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaduna_State" title="Kaduna State">Kaduna State</a>). He left the country for England, where he studied at the <a href="/wiki/School_of_Oriental_and_African_Studies" class="mw-redirect" title="School of Oriental and African Studies">School of Oriental and African Studies</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gibson_and_Weldon" title="Gibson and Weldon">Gibson and Weldon</a>, before returning to attend the <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_Law_School" title="Nigerian Law School">Nigerian Law School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lagos" title="Lagos">Lagos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lagos_State" title="Lagos State">Lagos State</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-scnbio_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scnbio-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  He was called to the bar in approximately 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-bar_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bar-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Idris_Legbo_Kutigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Kutigi served as the Attorney General and Commissioner for Justice in Niger State before becoming a high court judge in 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-ind_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ind-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  He joined the Supreme Court in 1992 and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Nigeria" title="President of Nigeria">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Olusegun_Obasanjo" title="Olusegun Obasanjo">Olusegun Obasanjo</a> appointed him to the position of Chief Justice to succeed the outgoing <a href="/wiki/Salihu_Modibbo_Alfa_Belgore" title="Salihu Modibbo Alfa Belgore">Salihu Alfa Belgore</a>. Belgore retired on 17 January and Kutigi succeeded him on 30 January, after being confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Nigeria" title="Senate of Nigeria">Senate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ind_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ind-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  \n</p><p>Kutigi retired on 30 December 2009, having reached the mandatory retirement age of 70.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  He swore in his successor, <a href="/wiki/Aloysius_Iyorgyer_Katsina-Alu" class="mw-redirect" title="Aloysius Iyorgyer Katsina-Alu">Aloysius Iyorgyer Katsina-Alu</a>. The President of Nigeria usually swears in the Chief Justice, but President <a href="/wiki/Umaru_Musa_Yar%27Adua" title="Umaru Musa Yar&#39;Adua">Umaru Musa Yar\'Adua</a> was not available on this occasion due to ill health.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  He afterwards returned to serve as a high court judge until his death in October 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  Kutingi also continued to attend <a href="/wiki/Council_of_State_(Nigeria)" title="Council of State (Nigeria)">Council of State</a> meetings held at the decision of the president.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2014 he was appointed chairman of the <a href="/wiki/2014_National_Conference,_Nigeria" title="2014 National Conference, Nigeria">National Conference</a> on constitutional matters by President <a href="/wiki/Goodluck_Jonathan" title="Goodluck Jonathan">Goodluck Jonathan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  His appointment was widely welcomed by those on all sides of the Nigerian political spectrum with praise coming for his impartiality and fair handedness.<sup id="cite_ref-bar_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bar-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  On 12 June 2014 he stepped in to separate northern and southern politicians who almost came to blows during a conference meeting over a disagreement on holding a one minute silence to honour those that died during the <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_presidential_election,_1993" title="Nigerian presidential election, 1993">1993 presidential election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Kutigi later described the conference as the "most arduous" to have been held in Nigeria\'s history due to the short length of time, four and a half months, that had been allowed for it.  By its conclusion more than 600 resolutions had been addressed covering points of law, public policy and the constitution.  The findings were presented in a 22-volume, 10,335-page document.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Idris_Legbo_Kutigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Kutigi had 18 children and more than 40 grandchildren. He died in a London hospital on 21 October 2018 following a short period of illness.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A street in the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Capital_Territory,_Nigeria" title="Federal Capital Territory, Nigeria">Federal Capital Territory</a> was named in his honour in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  The Justice Idris Legbo Kutigi International Conference Centre in <a href="/wiki/Minna" title="Minna">Minna</a> is also named for him.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a mark of honour following his death the Nigerian flag was ordered to be flown at half mast at the Supreme Court, the official residence of the chief justice, all judicial institutions and courts of records for seven days.  A book of condolence was opened at the premises of the Supreme Court.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Idris_Legbo_Kutigi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-scnbio-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-scnbio_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-scnbio_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-scnbio_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.scn.gov.ng/portal/detail.php?link=kutigi">"Hon.
