class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Jefferson Street Grounds</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Baseball_park" title="Baseball park">baseball field</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>. It was also known as <b>Jefferson Park</b> and <b>Athletics Park</b>. It was home to the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Athletics_(1860%E2%80%9376)#National_League_(1876)" title="Philadelphia Athletics (1860\xe2\x80\x9376)">Philadelphia Athletics</a> from <a href="/wiki/1871_in_baseball" title="1871 in baseball">1871</a> to <a href="/wiki/1876_in_baseball" title="1876 in baseball">1876</a>, five seasons in the \n<a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Professional Base Ball Players">National Association</a> and one in the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> of Base Ball Clubs. From <a href="/wiki/1873_in_baseball" title="1873 in baseball">1873</a> to 1875, the Athletics shared it with the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_White_Stockings" title="Philadelphia White Stockings">White Stockings</a>, also in the National Association. Later it was home to the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">American Association</a> Athletics beginning <a href="/wiki/1883_in_baseball" title="1883 in baseball">1883</a>, moving in from <a href="/wiki/Oakdale_Park" title="Oakdale Park">Oakdale Park</a>, until <a href="/wiki/1890_in_baseball" title="1890 in baseball">1890</a>.\n</p><p>The grounds were located on a large block bounded by Jefferson Street (north); 25th Street (east); Master Street (south); and 27th Street (west). Although typically listed as one ballpark, the 1870s diamond was located in the opposite corner of the block from the 1880s diamond. The facility opened on May 15, 1871.  The seating capacity was meager, only 5,000 seats.  The inaugural National League game was played there, on Saturday, April 22, 1876, between the Philadelphia Athletics and the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Caps" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Red Caps">Boston Red Caps</a>; Boston won 6-5.<sup id="cite_ref-MLBcarriesOnStrong_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MLBcarriesOnStrong-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  By a quirk of fate, it was the only NL game played that day, all others being rained out. This game is often pointed to as the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball#Differing_definitions_of_MLB&#39;s_founding_year" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MLBcarriesOnStrong_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MLBcarriesOnStrong-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The last major league game played in the park was October 11, 1890.\n</p><p>The ballpark site is currently occupied by various structures including Daniel Boone Public School aka Camelot Academy at 1435 N 26th St, and the Athletic Recreation Center and its ball fields.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_Street_Grounds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-MLBcarriesOnStrong-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-MLBcarriesOnStrong_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-MLBcarriesOnStrong_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Noble, Marty (September 23, 2011). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20110922&amp;content_id=25060814&amp;vkey=news_mlb&amp;c_id=mlb&amp;partnerId=ed-5337375-55428157">"MLB carries on strong, 200,000 games later: Look what they started on a ballfield in Philadelphia in 1876"</a>.
