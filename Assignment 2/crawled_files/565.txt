class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In Canada, each province creates its own system of local government, so terminology varies substantially.\n</p><p>In certain provinces (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>) there is <i>only</i> one level of local government in that province, so no special term is used to describe the situation.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> has only one such municipality, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Rockies_Regional_Municipality" title="Northern Rockies Regional Municipality">Northern Rockies Regional Municipality</a>, which was established in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> the term <b>single-tier municipalities</b> is used, for a similar concept.  Their character varies, and while most function as cities with no upper level of government, some function as counties or <a href="/wiki/Regional_municipality" title="Regional municipality">regional municipalities</a> with no lower municipal subdivisions below them.  They exist as individual <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ontario_census_divisions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Ontario census divisions">census divisions</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ontario_separated_municipalities" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Ontario separated municipalities">separated municipalities</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_Europe">Central Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Central Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In Germany, <a href="/wiki/Kreisfreie_Stadt" class="mw-redirect" title="Kreisfreie Stadt">kreisfreie Stadt</a> (literally  <i>circle-free city</i>) is the equivalent term for a city with the competences of both the <a href="/wiki/Gemeinde_(Germany)" class="mw-redirect" title="Gemeinde (Germany)">Gemeinde</a> (municipality) and the <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Germany" title="Districts of Germany">Kreis</a> (district, literally <i>circle</i>) administrative level. The directly elected chief executive officer of a <i>kreisfreie Stadt</i> is called <i>Oberb\xc3\xbcrgermeister</i> (literally <i>Superior Burgomaster</i>, in English "Chief Mayor" or "Lord Mayor").\nThe British counties have no directly corresponding counterpart in Germany. This German system corresponds to <a href="/wiki/Statutory_city_(Austria)" title="Statutory city (Austria)">statutory cities in Austria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Statutory_city_(Czech_Republic)" title="Statutory city (Czech Republic)">in the Czech Republic</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Denmark">Denmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Denmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Until 1 January 2007, the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Municipality" title="Copenhagen Municipality">Copenhagen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frederiksberg_Municipality" title="Frederiksberg Municipality">Frederiksberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bornholm_Regional_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Bornholm Regional Municipality">Bornholm</a> were not a part of a Danish <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Denmark" title="Counties of Denmark">county</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_New_Zealand" title="Local government in New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, a unitary authority is a <a href="/wiki/Territorial_authorities_of_New_Zealand" title="Territorial authorities of New Zealand">territorial authority</a> (district or city) that also performs the functions of a <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_New_Zealand" title="Regions of New Zealand">regional council</a> (first-level division). There are five unitary authorities; they are (with the year they were constituted): <a href="/wiki/Gisborne_District_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Gisborne District Council">Gisborne District Council</a> (1989), <a href="/wiki/Nelson_City_Council" title="Nelson City Council">Nelson City Council</a> (1992), <a href="/wiki/Tasman_District_Council" title="Tasman District Council">Tasman District Council</a> (1992), <a href="/wiki/Marlborough_District_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Marlborough District Council">Marlborough District Council</a> (1992), and <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Council" title="Auckland Council">Auckland Council</a> (2010).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Islands" title="Chatham Islands">Chatham Islands</a>, located east of the <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a>, have a council with its own special legislation, constituted (1995) with powers similar to those of a regional authority.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Poland">Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Poland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/City_with_powiat_rights" title="City with powiat rights">City with powiat rights</a></div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, a <i>miasto na prawach powiatu</i>, or shortly <i>powiat grodzki</i> (<i>city with <a href="/wiki/Powiat" title="Powiat">powiat</a> rights</i>, or <i>urban county</i> in short) is a, typically big, city which is also responsible for district (poviat) administrative level, being part of no other <i>powiat</i> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Krak\xc3\xb3w">Krak\xc3\xb3w</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="\xc5\x81\xc3\xb3d\xc5\xba">\xc5\x81\xc3\xb3d\xc5\xba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wroc%C5%82aw" title="Wroc\xc5\x82aw">Wroc\xc5\x82aw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pozna%C5%84" title="Pozna\xc5\x84">Pozna\xc5\x84</a>). In total, 65 cities in Poland have this status.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bournemouth,_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Bournemouth%2C_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg/220px-Bournemouth%2C_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Bournemouth%2C_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg/330px-Bournemouth%2C_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Bournemouth%2C_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg/440px-Bournemouth%2C_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bournemouth,_Unitary_Authority_tree_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1038887.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Bournemouth" title="Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a>: Unitary Authority tree. The tree on the left, on the concourse of the <a href="/wiki/File:Bournemouth,_Town_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_637216.jpg" title="File:Bournemouth, Town Hall - geograph.org.uk - 637216.jpg">Bournemouth Town Hall</a>, was planted on 1 April 1997 to mark the occasion of Bournemouth council becoming a unitary authority on that day. This was part of the Local Government Reorganisation of the late 1990s, when certain more urban districts were essentially separated from the relevant county council, with now no services for Bournemouth residents carried out by <a href="/wiki/Dorset_County_Council" title="Dorset County Council">Dorset County Council</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, "unitary authorities" are English local authorities set up in accordance with the Local Government Changes for England Regulations 1994 made under powers conferred by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1992" class="mw-redirect" title="Local Government Act 1992">Local Government Act 1992</a> to form a single tier of <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Local government in the United Kingdom">local government</a> in specified areas and which are responsible for almost all local government functions within such areas. While outwardly appearing to be similar, single-tier authorities formed using older legislation are not <i>Unitary Authorities</i> thus excluding e.g. the Isle of Wight Council or any other single-tier authority formed under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> or older legislation.\n</p><p>This is distinct from the two-tier system of local government which still exists in most of England, where local government functions are divided between <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> (the upper tier) and <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">district</a> or borough councils. Until 1996 two-tier systems existed in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, but these have now been replaced by systems based on a single-tier of local government with some functions shared between groups of adjacent authorities. A single-tier system has existed in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> since 1973.\n</p><p>For many years the description of the number of tiers in UK local government arrangements has routinely ignored any current or previous bodies at the lowest level of authorities elected by the voters within their area such as parish (in England and Wales) or community councils; such bodies do not exist or have not existed in all areas.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="England">England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">Unitary authorities of England</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:NI11w.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/NI11w.jpg/220px-NI11w.jpg" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/NI11w.jpg/330px-NI11w.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/NI11w.jpg/440px-NI11w.jpg 2x" data-file-width="772" data-file-height="616" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:NI11w.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Districts of Northern Ireland</div></div></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Local government in Northern Ireland">Local government in Northern Ireland</a></div>\n<p>Northern Ireland is divided into 11 districts for local government purposes. In Northern Ireland local councils have no responsibility for education, road building or housing (though they do nominate members to the advisory <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Housing_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Ireland Housing Council">Northern Ireland Housing Council</a>). Their functions include <a href="/wiki/Recycling_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Recycling in Northern Ireland">waste and recycling services</a>, leisure and community services, building control and local economic and cultural development. Since their reorganisation in 2015 councils in Northern Ireland have also taken on responsibility for planning functions. The collection of <a href="/wiki/Rates_(tax)" title="Rates (tax)">rates</a> is handled by the <a href="/wiki/Land_and_Property_Services" title="Land and Property Services">Land and Property Services</a> agency.  \n</p>\n<dl><dd><i>Category:</i> <a href="/wiki/Category:Subdivisions_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Category:Subdivisions of Northern Ireland">Subdivisions of Northern Ireland</a></dd></dl>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Local authorities in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> are unitary in nature but not in name. The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_etc._(Scotland)_Act_1994" title="Local Government etc. (Scotland) Act 1994">Local Government etc. (Scotland) Act 1994</a> created a single tier of local government throughout Scotland. On 1 April 1996, 32 local government areas, each with a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Scotland" title="Subdivisions of Scotland">council</a>, replaced the previous <a href="/wiki/Regions_and_districts_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Regions and districts of Scotland">two-tier structure</a>, which had regional, islands and district councils. <a href="/wiki/Comhairle_nan_Eilean_Siar" title="Comhairle nan Eilean Siar">Comhairle nan Eilean Siar</a> (formerly the Western Isles Council) uses the alternative <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Gaelic language">Gaelic</a> designation <i>Comhairle</i>. The phrase "unitary authority" is not used in Scottish legislation (whether from the Scottish Parliament or the UK Parliament), although the term is encountered (used either descriptively or erroneously) in publications<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and in (usually erroneous) use by United Kingdom government departments.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wales">Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Local authorities in <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Wales" title="Local government in Wales">Wales</a> are unitary in nature but are described by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Wales)_Act_1994" title="Local Government (Wales) Act 1994">Local Government (Wales) Act 1994</a> as "principal councils", and their areas as <a href="/wiki/Principal_area" title="Principal area">principal areas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Various other legislation (e.g. s.91(1) Environment Act 1995) includes the counties and county boroughs of Wales within their individual interpretations of the phrase "unitary authority" as an interpretive not a definitive description. In s.2 of the Act each council formed for a county is allocated the respective English and Welsh descriptions of "<a href="/wiki/County_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="County Council">County Council</a>" or "<i>Cyngor Sir</i>", each council formed for a <a href="/wiki/County_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="County Borough">County Borough</a> is allocated the respective descriptions of "County Borough Council" or "<i>Cyngor Bwrdeistref Sirol</i>"; in all cases the shorter alternative forms "Council" or <i>"Cyngor"</i> can be used.\n</p><p>Similar to the civil parishes in England, the lowest tier of local government in Wales are the <a href="/wiki/Community_council" title="Community council">communities</a>. All of the principal councils are fully divided into communities, but not all such communities have established community councils.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are several types of single-tier governments in the United States. In the states of <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, and much of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, county government has been abolished, and the municipalities (known as <a href="/wiki/New_England_town" title="New England town">New England towns</a>) are the only governing tier below the state government, though the former counties still exist in the ceremonial sense.  In some areas, the reverse is true; for example, <a href="/wiki/Howard_County,_Maryland" title="Howard County, Maryland">Howard County, Maryland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arlington_County,_Virginia" title="Arlington County, Virginia">Arlington County, Virginia</a> are examples of counties that, despite being densely developed, have no municipalities and are thus the only tier of general-purpose local government. \n</p><p>In Virginia, all municipalities with <i>city</i> status are, by definition, independent from any county.  Three other cities across the United States are also <a href="/wiki/Independent_city_(United_States)" title="Independent city (United States)">independent of any county government</a>: <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore, Maryland</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis, Missouri</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carson_City,_Nevada" title="Carson City, Nevada">Carson City, Nevada</a>.  There are also several <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_city-county" title="Consolidated city-county">consolidated cities</a> where the county government and municipal government are unified.  <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> are two examples, wherein the city and county are coterminous and have one singular governing body.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Unitary_state" title="Unitary state">Unitary state</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unitary_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://brenthodson.ca/?p=78">"Fort Nelson becomes B.C.\'s first Regional Municipality"</a>.
