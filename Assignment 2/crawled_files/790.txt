front carriage was seen tipped over at an angle of 75 degrees, and most of the fatalities are believed to have been in this carriage.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Survivor accounts claimed that the driver applied the emergency brake multiple times before the incident, and another claimed that the train sped up after the curve.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>All trains in the eastern trunk line were halted in both directions, with replacement bus services being used instead.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Hundreds of medics and firefighters, and 100 army troops responded to the scene.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As of 21:35 local time all passengers, including those deceased had been removed/evacuated from the wreckage.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The derailment is the worst train accident in Taiwan since 1991, when 30 people were killed in a collision near <a href="/wiki/Miaoli" title="Miaoli">Miaoli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Straits_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Straits-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to a press release from the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Railways_Administration" title="Taiwan Railways Administration">Taiwan Railways Administration</a> on 21 October, the cause of the accident is as yet unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-Straits_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Straits-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The train involved in the accident was built by the Japanese company <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Sharyo" title="Nippon Sharyo">Nippon Sharyo</a> in 2011, and underwent major maintenance work in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Victims">Victims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Yilan_train_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Victims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>At least 18 people were killed in the accident, with another 187 injured, all on board. According to reports, one of the injured was a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> citizen.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Six of the deceased victims reportedly were under the age of 18.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigation">Investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Yilan_train_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The cause of the derailment is still under investigation.\n</p><p>On 22 October 2018, it was reported that the driver of the train reported an issue with the main air compressor just before the derailment. However, the Chief Secretary of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Railways_Administration" title="Taiwan Railways Administration">Taiwan Railways Administration</a>, Chu Lai-shun (\xe6\x9c\xb1\xe4\xbe\x86\xe9\xa0\x86), says that a full failure of the main air compressor would cause insufficient power and problems with deceleration, and should not cause a derailment. In addition, the train\'s <a href="/wiki/Automatic_train_protection" title="Automatic train protection">Automatic train protection</a> (ATP) was disabled shortly before the derailment for unknown reasons. According to taiwannews.com.tw, "Investigators believe that without ATPS to keep the train in check, it [the train] reached a highly dangerous speed for the curved section of track, ultimately leading the train to leap off the rails,".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responses">Responses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Yilan_train_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Responses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>President <a href="/wiki/Tsai_Ing-wen" title="Tsai Ing-wen">Tsai Ing-wen</a> called the accident a "major tragedy" and directed the government and military to "step up" rescue efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The President also called for an investigation into the crash, that would "make clear the timing and situation of the whole accident."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a result of the incident, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Progressive_Party" title="Democratic Progressive Party">Democratic Progressive Party</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> halted campaigning for the November local elections.<sup id="cite_ref-Focus_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Focus-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Taipei Blood Donation Center issued a press release, that highlighted local <a href="/wiki/Blood_donation" title="Blood donation">blood donation</a> centers and urged for blood donors to donate.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Memorials">Memorials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Yilan_train_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Memorials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Taiwan President met with relatives of the deceased victims, and those injured on 22 October. She joined <a href="/wiki/Bhikkhu" title="Bhikkhu">Buddhist monks</a> in prayer, at an altar next to the hospital, and told relatives "We are really sorry... you have to stay strong," and "We will do everything we can."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Yilan_train_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_Compostela_derailment" title="Santiago de Compostela derailment">Santiago de Compostela derailment</a>, a similar train accident.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Yilan_train_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-BBC-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_1-3"><sup><i><b>d</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_1-4"><sup><i><b>e</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-BBC_1-5"><sup><i><b>f</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-45932991">"Taiwan train derailment in Yilan County kills at least 18"</a>. <i>BBC News</i>. 21 October 2018<span class="reference-accessdate">.
