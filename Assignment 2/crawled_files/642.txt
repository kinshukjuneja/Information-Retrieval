Clark, 1912</small></li>\n<li><i>Psammechinus pictus</i><small> Verrill, 1867</small></li></ul>\n</td></tr>\n\n</tbody></table>\n<p><i><b>Lytechinus pictus</b></i>, commonly known as the <b>painted urchin</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Sea_urchin" title="Sea urchin">sea urchin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Toxopneustidae" title="Toxopneustidae">Toxopneustidae</a>. It occurs on shallow reefs in the tropical and subtropical eastern Pacific Ocean, off the coasts of California, Central America and  South America as far south as Ecuador.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Taxonomy"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Taxonomy</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Description"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Description</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Distribution_and_habitat"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Distribution and habitat</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Ecology"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Ecology</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lytechinus_pictus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>This sea urchin was <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">first described</a> in 1867 by the American zoologist <a href="/wiki/Addison_Emery_Verrill" title="Addison Emery Verrill">Addison Emery Verrill</a> who gave it the name <i>Psammechinus pictus</i>. It was later transferred to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Lytechinus" title="Lytechinus">Lytechinus</a></i> and became <i>Lytechinus pictus</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-WoRMS_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WoRMS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1912, another species <i>Lytechinus anamesus</i> was described by American zoologist <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Lyman_Clark" title="Hubert Lyman Clark">Hubert Lyman Clark</a> as <i>Lytechinus anamesus</i>. He was the curator of echinoderms at the <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Comparative_Zoology" title="Museum of Comparative Zoology">Museum of Comparative Zoology</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It had long been suspected that <i>L. pictus</i> and <i>L. anamesus</i> were <a href="/wiki/Synonym" title="Synonym">synonymous</a>, and this was confirmed by analysis of <a href="/wiki/Mitochondrial_DNA" title="Mitochondrial DNA">mitochondrial DNA</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-Lawrence_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lawrence-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lytechinus_pictus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Test_(biology)" title="Test (biology)">test</a> of this sea urchin is up to 40&#160;mm (1.6&#160;in) in diameter. The general colour is greyish or pale straw brown, sometimes with a pinkish flush. The  spines are robust, short and blunt, the basal half of each spine usually being white while the rest of the spine varies in colour. Juvenile urchins sometimes have banded spines.<sup id="cite_ref-SCAMIT_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCAMIT-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lytechinus_pictus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>L. pictus</i> is found in the tropical and subtropical eastern Pacific Ocean at depths down to about 300&#160;m (1,000&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Its range extends from central California southwards to Ecuador. In California it tends to inhabit the middle and lower intertidal zone but in the northern part of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_California" title="Gulf of California">Gulf of California</a> it mainly inhabits the <a href="/wiki/Subtidal_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtidal zone">subtidal zone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SCAMIT_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCAMIT-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>These sea urchins tend to form dense aggregations at the edge of, or inside, the <a href="/wiki/Kelp" title="Kelp">kelp</a> beds that line this coast. At <a href="/wiki/San_Onofre,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Onofre, California">San Onofre, California</a> in 1978\xe2\x80\x9379, the density of individuals close to the kelp beds was recorded at 80 per square metre while inside the kelp bed it was 40.<sup id="cite_ref-Lawrence476_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lawrence476-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Anacapa_Island,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Anacapa Island, California">Anacapa Island, California</a>, the highest densities (36 per sq.m.) were found in barren areas with <a href="/wiki/Coralline_algae" title="Coralline algae">coralline algae</a> near the kelp beds.<sup id="cite_ref-Lawrence476_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lawrence476-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lytechinus_pictus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>This sea urchin tends to be more active at night. During the  day it may semi-bury itself in sand, and it sometimes disguises itself with fragments of shell and gravel held in place by its <a href="/wiki/Tube_feet" title="Tube feet">tube feet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SCAMIT_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCAMIT-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It feeds on kelp, particularly <i><a href="/wiki/Macrocystis" title="Macrocystis">Macrocystis</a></i>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gigartina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gigartina (page does not exist)">Gigartina</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Laminaria" title="Laminaria">Laminaria</a></i>, but primarily grazes on young stages rather than fully-grown fronds. In the California kelp forests, the painted urchin is <a href="/wiki/Predation" title="Predation">preyed</a> on by the <a href="/wiki/Bat_star" title="Bat star">bat star</a> (<i>Patiria miniata</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lytechinus_pictus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-WoRMS-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-WoRMS_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-WoRMS_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-WoRMS_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Kroh, Andreas (2018).
