<b>0</b></li></ul>\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Syracuse Stars</b> was a 19th-century American baseball team, as well as the name of the minor league baseball teams which preceded it, based in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a>. They played their home games at <a href="/wiki/Newell_Park" title="Newell Park">Newell Park</a>. (They were unrelated to the <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_Stars_(American_Association)" title="Syracuse Stars (American Association)">Syracuse Stars</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">American Association</a>, who played for one year, in 1890.)\n</p><p>Established as an independent team in <a href="/wiki/1877_in_baseball" title="1877 in baseball">1877</a>, the Stars joined the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_for_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="International Association for Professional Base Ball Players">International Association</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1878_in_baseball" title="1878 in baseball">1878</a> season. They finished second to the pennant-winning <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bisons_(NL)" title="Buffalo Bisons (NL)">Buffalo Bisons</a>, and following the season both teams jumped to the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a>, a devastating blow for the IA, which lasted just two more seasons.\n</p><p>The Stars lone year in the NL, <a href="/wiki/1879_in_baseball" title="1879 in baseball">1879</a>, was not so successful. The team had a record of 22 wins and 48 losses, and did not finish their season, folding on September 10.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  They were 7th in the 8-team <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a>, ahead of the 19-56 Troy Trojans, who also did not complete their schedule. The club officially disbanded at the end of the season.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mike_Dorgan" title="Mike Dorgan">Mike Dorgan</a> managed 43 of the Stars\' games (with a 17-26 record).  <a href="/wiki/Bill_Holbert" title="Bill Holbert">Bill Holbert</a> managed on an interim basis for one game (a loss) and the season was closed out by <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Macullar" title="Jimmy Macullar">Jimmy Macullar</a>, who managed the final 26 games (5-21).\n</p><p>The team had a combined <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a> of .227.  First baseman <a href="/wiki/Jack_Farrell" title="Jack Farrell">Jack Farrell</a> led the team with .303.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
