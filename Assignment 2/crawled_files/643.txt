Hokuloa McKinzie</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Edith Kawelohea Kapule McKinzie</b> (October 22, 1925 \xe2\x80\x93 October 21, 2014) was an American genealogist, educator, author, and an expert in <a href="/wiki/Hula" title="Hula">hula</a> and chant. She published two books on Hawaiian genealogy, was Director of the Hawaiian Language Newspaper Index Project, and taught traditional hula and chant across the United States. In 2004, she was named a <a href="/wiki/Living_Treasure_of_Hawai%27i" class="mw-redirect" title="Living Treasure of Hawai&#39;i">Living Treasure of Hawaii</a> for her contributions to Hawaiian culture and heritage.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Early_life"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Early life</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Academic_career"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Academic career</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Hula"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Hula</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Honours_and_societies"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Honours and societies</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Death"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Death</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edith_Kawelohea_McKinzie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Edith Kawelohea Kapule was born on October 22, 1925, in <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">Honolulu</a>, Hawaii. She was the eldest of three siblings, with a sister named Alexandra and a brother named Harry Jr. Their father, Harry Kawelo Kapule, was a Hawaiian from <a href="/wiki/Holualoa,_Hawaii" title="Holualoa, Hawaii">Holualoa</a>, and their mother, Caroline "Carrie" Costa, was a Portuguese woman living in <a href="/wiki/Pauoa,_Hawaii" title="Pauoa, Hawaii">Pauoa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Kapule graduated from <a href="/wiki/McKinley_High_School_(Honolulu,_Hawaii)" class="mw-redirect" title="McKinley High School (Honolulu, Hawaii)">McKinley High School</a> and went on to study at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">University of Hawaii</a>, earning an undergraduate degree in Hawaiian Studies, a master\'s degree in Education, Curriculum and Instruction,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and a Professional Diploma (P.D.) in Secondary Education.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1946, she married Clayton McKinzie of <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, Missouri. They had one daughter named Joleen Hokuloa McKinzie, and four step-children from Clayton\'s earlier life.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_career">Academic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edith_Kawelohea_McKinzie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>McKinzie lectured at the University of Hawaii\'s College of Continuing Education, teaching local genealogy and <a href="/wiki/Mele_hula_pahu" class="mw-redirect" title="Mele hula pahu">mele hula</a>, and she also taught chanting for the <a href="/w/index.php?title=State_Council_on_Hawaiian_Heritage&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="State Council on Hawaiian Heritage (page does not exist)">State Council on Hawaiian Heritage</a>. She was the first professor of Hawaiian Studies at the <a href="/wiki/Honolulu_Community_College" title="Honolulu Community College">Honolulu Community College</a>, holding the position from 1978 to 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>She was known for her extensive work on Hawaiian genealogy, publishing two volumes of <i>Hawaiian Genealogies.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> McKinzie also researched the genealogy of the chiefs of <a href="/wiki/Kahoolawe" title="Kahoolawe">Kahoolawe</a> for the Kahoolawe Island Reserve Commission.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>McKinzie served as chairwoman for the University of Hawaii\'s Committee for the Preservation and Study of Hawaiian Language, Art and Culture.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Working with the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Museum" title="Bishop Museum">Bishop Museum</a>, McKinzie established the Hawaiian Language Newspaper Index Project, organizing a team of scholars to index and catalogue articles, notices, and advertisements from early 19th\xe2\x80\x93 and 20th\xe2\x80\x93century Hawaiian-language newspapers.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She served as Director of the project.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hula">Hula</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edith_Kawelohea_McKinzie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hula">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>McKinzie first began learning about traditional Hawaiian hula, music and language at home with her family, and was encouraged by her aunt Mary Kapule, who had formerly danced for famed <a href="/wiki/Kumu_hula" class="mw-redirect" title="Kumu hula">Kumu Hula</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anton_Ka%60%C3%B6%60%C3%B6&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anton Ka`\xc3\xb6`\xc3\xb6 (page does not exist)">Anton Ka`\xc3\xb6`\xc3\xb6</a>. At the age of 12, McKinzie began her formal hula and chant training with Joseph `\xc3\x8fl\xc3\xa4l\xc3\xa4`ole.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As an adult, McKinzie taught hula across Hawaii, the U.S. mainland, <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midway_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Midway Island">Midway Island</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After she opened a dance school at Guam, McKinzie was invited by dancers <a href="/wiki/Ted_Shawn" title="Ted Shawn">Ted Shawn</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Meri" title="La Meri">La Meri</a> to teach Hawaiian dance at <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_Pillow_Dance" title="Jacob&#39;s Pillow Dance">Jacob\'s Pillow</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lee,_Massachusetts" title="Lee, Massachusetts">Lee, Massachusetts</a>. She also became a frequent judge in hula competitions.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, McKinzie attended the annual Malia Craver Hula Kahiko Competition as a presenter, awarding the Edith Kawelohea McKinzie Overall Trophy to <a href="/wiki/Kamehameha_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Kamehameha High School">Kamehameha High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours_and_societies">Honours and societies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edith_Kawelohea_McKinzie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honours and societies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>McKinzie joined the <a href="/wiki/Ahahui_Kaahumanu" class="mw-redirect" title="Ahahui Kaahumanu">Ahahui Ka\xe2\x80\x99ahumanu</a> society in 1949. She was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Eastern_Star" title="Order of the Eastern Star">Order of the Eastern Star</a>, and received the title Worthy Matron in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Over the years, McKinzie was recognized for her cultural work with the Pulama Award from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalihi-Palama_Culture_and_Arts_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kalihi-Palama Culture and Arts Society (page does not exist)">Kalihi-Palama Culture and Arts Society</a>, the Order of Distinction from the State Council on Hawaiian Heritage and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ke_Kukui_Malamalama&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ke Kukui Malamalama (page does not exist)">Ke Kukui Malamalama</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Hawaiian_Affairs" title="Office of Hawaiian Affairs">Office of Hawaiian Affairs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She received the Ambassador of Hawaii Award from <a href="/wiki/John_D._Waihee_III" class="mw-redirect" title="John D. Waihee III">Hawaiian Governor John Waihe\xe2\x80\x99e</a>, and the David Malo award from the <a href="/wiki/Rotary_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotary Club">Rotary Club</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oahu" title="Oahu">West Oahu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2004, McKinzie was named a <a href="/wiki/Living_Treasure_of_Hawai%27i" class="mw-redirect" title="Living Treasure of Hawai&#39;i">Living Treasure of Hawaii</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Honpa_Hongwanji_Mission_of_Hawaii" title="Honpa Hongwanji Mission of Hawaii">Honpa Hongwanji Mission of Hawaii</a> for her efforts in fostering traditional Hawaiian culture.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edith_Kawelohea_McKinzie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On October 21, 2014,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> McKinzie died just a few hours before her 89th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She was interred in the <a href="/wiki/Valley_of_the_Temples_Memorial_Park" title="Valley of the Temples Memorial Park">Valley of the Temples Memorial Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Abigail_Kinoiki_Kekaulike_Kaw%C4%81nanakoa" title="Abigail Kinoiki Kekaulike Kaw\xc4\x81nanakoa">Abigail K. Kawananakoa</a>, a member of Hawaii\'s royal family and a longtime friend of McKinzie, released a statement: "Edith McKinzie guided me into the world of genealogy and opened the door to the true history of the Hawaiian people.
