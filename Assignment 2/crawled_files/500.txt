href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It consists of the resort of <a href="/wiki/Weymouth,_Dorset" title="Weymouth, Dorset">Weymouth</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Portland" title="Isle of Portland">Isle of Portland</a>, and includes the areas of <a href="/wiki/Wyke_Regis" title="Wyke Regis">Wyke Regis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preston,_Dorset" title="Preston, Dorset">Preston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melcombe_Regis" title="Melcombe Regis">Melcombe Regis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upwey,_Dorset" title="Upwey, Dorset">Upwey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broadwey" title="Broadwey">Broadwey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southill,_Weymouth" title="Southill, Weymouth">Southill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottington" title="Nottington">Nottington</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Westham,_Dorset&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Westham, Dorset (page does not exist)">Westham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radipole" title="Radipole">Radipole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiswell" title="Chiswell">Chiswell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castletown,_Dorset" title="Castletown, Dorset">Castletown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fortuneswell" title="Fortuneswell">Fortuneswell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weston,_Dorset" title="Weston, Dorset">Weston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southwell,_Dorset" title="Southwell, Dorset">Southwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Easton,_Dorset" title="Easton, Dorset">Easton</a>; the latter six being on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Portland" title="Isle of Portland">Isle of Portland</a>.\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Portland_Harbour" title="Portland Harbour">Portland Harbour</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Weymouth_and_Portland_National_Sailing_Academy" title="Weymouth and Portland National Sailing Academy">Weymouth and Portland National Sailing Academy</a>, where the sailing events at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympic_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Summer Olympic Games">2012 Olympics</a> took place. The main reason that the resort was chosen to be an <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympic_venues" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Summer Olympic venues">Olympic venue</a> was that the Sailing Academy had only recently been built, so no new venue would need to be provided. <a href="/wiki/Weymouth_Bay" title="Weymouth Bay">Weymouth and Portland\'s waters</a> have also been credited by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Yachting_Association" title="Royal Yachting Association">Royal Yachting Association</a> as the best in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">Northern Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wpnsa_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wpnsa-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Weymouth and Portland have been twinned with the town of <a href="/wiki/Holzwickede" title="Holzwickede">Holzwickede</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, since 1986,<sup id="cite_ref-holzwickede_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-holzwickede-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Louviers" title="Louviers">Louviers</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eure" title="Eure">Eure</a> in <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>, since 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-louviers_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-louviers-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weymouth_and_Portland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The district of Weymouth and Portland was formed on 1 April 1974 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, which merged the borough of Weymouth and Melcombe Regis and the nearby <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Portland" title="Isle of Portland">Portland</a> urban district. The district is divided into 15 wards for elections&#8212;12 of them are in Weymouth, and three are on Portland.<sup id="cite_ref-wards_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wards-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Weymouth_and_Portland_Borough_Council_elections" title="Weymouth and Portland Borough Council elections">Elections</a> take place in a four-year cycle; one third of the councillors in all but three wards retire or seek re-election in years one, two and three, and county council elections are held in year four.<sup id="cite_ref-election_cycle_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-election_cycle-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Currently the District Council is a multi-party administration under the leadership of the Conservatives.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Party\n</th>\n<th>Seats\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Conservative\n</td>\n<td>14\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Labour\n</td>\n<td>12\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Liberal Democrat\n</td>\n<td>6\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Independent\n</td>\n<td>2\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>UKIP\n</td>\n<td>1\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Greens\n</td>\n<td>1\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Weymouth and Portland and <a href="/wiki/Purbeck_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Purbeck (district)">Purbeck</a> districts are in the <a href="/wiki/South_Dorset_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="South Dorset (UK Parliament constituency)">South Dorset</a> parliamentary <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_constituencies" title="United Kingdom constituencies">constituency</a>, created in 1885. The constituency elects one <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a>. The current MP for South Dorset is <a href="/wiki/Richard_Drax" title="Richard Drax">Richard Drax</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a>, who was voted in during the 2010 General Election, beating the incumbent <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Jim_Knight" title="Jim Knight">Jim Knight</a>. Weymouth and Portland, the rest of the <a href="/wiki/South_West_England" title="South West England">south west</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> are in the <a href="/wiki/South_West_England_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="South West England (European Parliament constituency)">South West England</a> <a href="/wiki/Constituency" class="mw-redirect" title="Constituency">constituency</a> of the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-euparliament_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-euparliament-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The borough and its council will be abolished on 1st April 2019 and, together with the other 4 districts outside the greater Bournemouth
