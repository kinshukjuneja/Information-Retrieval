Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_sessions&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Quarter sessions">Quarter sessions</a>)</span></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>courts of quarter sessions</b> or <b>quarter sessions</b> were local <a href="/wiki/Court" title="Court">courts</a> traditionally held at four set times each year in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>) from 1388 until 1707, then in 18th-century <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, in the later <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and in other <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">dominions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>.\n</p><p>Quarter sessions generally sat in the <a href="/wiki/County_town" title="County town">seat</a> of each <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a>. They were abolished in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a> in 1972, when the <a href="/wiki/Courts_Act_1971" title="Courts Act 1971">Courts Act 1971</a> replaced them and the <a href="/wiki/Assizes" title="Assizes">assizes</a> with a single permanent <a href="/wiki/Crown_Court_of_England_and_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown Court of England and Wales">Crown Court</a>. In Scotland they survived until 1975, when they were abolished and replaced by <a href="/wiki/District_court_(Scotland)" title="District court (Scotland)">district courts</a> and later by <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_peace_court" title="Justice of the peace court">justice of the peace courts</a>.\n</p><p>The quarter sessions were named after the <a href="/wiki/Quarter_days" title="Quarter days">quarter days</a> on which they met in England and Wales from 1388. These days were later settled as <a href="/wiki/Epiphany_(feast)" class="mw-redirect" title="Epiphany (feast)">Epiphany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midsummer" title="Midsummer">Midsummer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michaelmas" title="Michaelmas">Michaelmas</a> sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reputation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bentley notes in <i>English Criminal Justice in the Nineteenth Century</i> that "the reputation of such courts remained consistently bad throughout the century" due to failure by chairmen to take proper note of <a href="/wiki/Evidence" title="Evidence">evidence</a>, display of open bias against prisoners, and the severity of sentences compared to the assizes. Chairmen of county sessions did not have to be legally qualified.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jurisdiction">Jurisdiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Jurisdiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The quarter sessions generally heard crimes that could not be tried summarily by the <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_peace" title="Justice of the peace">justices of the peace</a> without a jury in <a href="/wiki/Magistrates%27_court_(England_and_Wales)" title="Magistrates&#39; court (England and Wales)">petty sessions</a>, which were sent up by the process of <a href="/wiki/Indictment" title="Indictment">indictment</a> to be heard in quarter sessions.\n</p><p>The quarter sessions did not have jurisdiction to hear the most serious crimes, most notably those subject to <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">capital punishment</a> or later <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a>. These crimes were sent for trial at the periodic <a href="/wiki/Assizes" title="Assizes">assizes</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Staff">Staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The quarter sessions in each county were made up of two or more justices of the peace, presided over by a chairman, who sat with a <a href="/wiki/Jury" title="Jury">jury</a>. <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">County boroughs</a> entitled to their own quarter sessions had a single <a href="/wiki/Recorder_(judge)" title="Recorder (judge)">recorder</a> instead of a bench of justices.\n</p><p>Every court of quarter sessions had a clerk called the <a href="/wiki/Clerk_of_the_peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Clerk of the peace">clerk of the peace</a>. For county quarter sessions, this person was appointed by the <i>custos rotulorum</i> of the county \xe2\x80\x93 the <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_peace" title="Justice of the peace">justice of the peace</a> for the county charged with custody of its rolls and records. There was a large fee income for the clerk, and he was usually a friend or relative of the <i>custos.</i> The clerk rarely discharged the duties of the office himself, but appointed a solicitor to act as his deputy in return for a share of the fees. After 1852, payment by salary was gradually brought in instead of fees.\n</p><p>In some counties there were multiple quarter sessions, quite apart from the urban areas: for example, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> had its <a href="/wiki/North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="North Riding of Yorkshire">North Riding</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding</a>, and <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding</a>; whilst <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Soke_of_Peterborough" title="Soke of Peterborough">Soke of Peterborough</a> was administered separately.  These divisions were carried on to the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_counties" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative counties">administrative counties</a> that county councils covered.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_jurisdiction">Civil jurisdiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Civil jurisdiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The quarter sessions also had some limited civil jurisdiction, and until the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1888" title="Local Government Act 1888">Local Government Act 1888</a> created elected <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a>, also had important administrative functions in their respective counties.\n</p><p>Much of the court\'s administrative business was delegated to committees of <a href="/wiki/Magistrate" title="Magistrate">magistrates</a>, who had specific responsibilities. Most of these administrative functions were transferred to <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> when they were established in 1888.\n</p><p>These functions included:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Repair of <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">roads</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">bridges</a></li>\n<li>Highway diversions</li>\n<li>Construction and maintenance of county buildings</li>\n<li>Administration of the county <a href="/wiki/Gaol" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaol">gaol</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jail" class="mw-redirect" class="mw-redirect" title="Petty sessions">petty sessions</a></li>\n<li>Licensing of <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">public houses</a></li>\n<li>Supervision of the <a href="/wiki/English_Poor_Laws" title="English Poor Laws">English Poor Laws</a> (pre-1834)</li>\n<li>The county militia</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a></li>\n<li>Setting county rates</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes">Changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Quarter sessions were established in Scotland by an Act of the Parliament of Scotland in 1661 (cap. 38), which directed justices of the peace to meet together in each <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Counties of Scotland">county</a> on the first Tuesday of March, May and August, and the last Tuesday of October.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Often Quarter Sessions were delayed, in which case they met as General Sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Quarter Sessions were abolished alongside other local courts by the District Courts (Scotland) Act 1975, which moved justices of the peace to sitting in a uniform series of <a href="/wiki/District_court_(Scotland)" title="District court (Scotland)">district courts</a>, since replaced by <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_peace_court" title="Justice of the peace court">justice of the peace courts</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quarter_session_courts_in_Ireland">Quarter session courts in Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span In <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> city, which had no <a href="/wiki/Assizes_(Ireland)" title="Assizes (Ireland)">assizes</a>, the quarter sessions court had cognizance of all crimes committed within the city\'s boundaries except <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Corporations_(Ireland)_Act_1840" title="Municipal Corporations (Ireland) Act 1840">Municipal Corporations (Ireland) Act 1840</a> abolished many city and borough courts, but Dublin, <a href="/wiki/Galway_(barony)" title="Galway (barony)">Galway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carrickfergus_(barony)" title="Carrickfergus (barony)">Carrickfergus</a> retained their courts of quarter sessions.\n</p><p>In 1867, the <a href="/wiki/Attorney-General_for_Ireland" title="Attorney-General for Ireland">Attorney-General for Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hedges_Eyre_Chatterton" title="Perjury">perjury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Attempted_murder" title="Attempted murder">assault with intent to murder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unlawful_assembly" title="Unlawful assembly">party processions</a>, election <a href="/wiki/Riot_Act" title="Riot Act">riots</a>, and all offences of a <a href="/wiki/Political_crime" title="Political crime">political</a> or <a href="/wiki/Insurrection" class="mw-redirect" title="Insurrection">insurrectionary</a> character.<sup id="cite_ref-McEldowneyO&#39;Higgins1990_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McEldowneyO&#39;Higgins1990-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Quarter Sessions were abolished in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State" title="Irish Free State">Irish Free State</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Courts_of_Justice_Act_1924" title="Courts of Justice Act 1924">Courts of Justice Act 1924</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a title="Circuit Court (Ireland)">Circuit Court</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Courts_of_quarter_sessions_of_the_peace_of_Lower_Canada">Courts of quarter sessions of the peace of Lower chairman in each district. In Montreal the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Montreal" title="Governor of Montreal">Governor of Montreal</a> was replace with the Court of Quarter Sessions Chairman.\n</p><p>List of quarter session courts in Lower Canada from 1763 to 1790:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Montreal_District" title="Montreal District">Montreal District</a></li>\n<li>Quebec District</li>\n<li>Trois-Rivi\xc3\xa8res District</li></ul>\n<p>In 1791 27 districts were created to replace the role of the three founding districts. In 1832 when Montreal was incorporated as a city the role of the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Montreal" title="Mayor of Montreal">Mayor of Montreal</a> replaced the quarter sessions chairman and that of the court by <a href="/wiki/Montreal_City_Council" title="Montreal City Council">Montreal City Council</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Courts_of_quarter_sessions_in_Upper_Canada">Courts of quarter sessions in Upper Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Courts of quarter sessions in Upper Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A Court of Quarter Sessions was held four times a year in each district to oversee the administration of the district and deal with legal cases in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Upper_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Province of Upper Canada">Province of Upper Canada</a> (later Province of <a href="/wiki/Canada_West" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada West">Canada West</a> after 1841). class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Court_of_General_Sessions" title="Court of General Sessions">Court of General Sessions</a></div>\n<p>Courts of Quarter Sessions also existed in North American colonies and were sometimes known as <a href="/wiki/Courts_of_General_Sessions" class="mw-redirect" title="Courts of General Sessions">Courts of General Sessions</a>. When the United States became an independent country, the Courts of General Sessions became independent of Britain and were gradually replaced by other court systems.\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, the Courts of General Sessions in continued until the constitution of that Commonwealth was rewritten in 1968 and the courts\' jurisdiction was placed under the pre-existing <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Courts_of_Common_Pleas" class="mw-redirect" title="Pennsylvania Courts of Common Pleas">Courts of Common Pleas</a> in each county.\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, the Court of Quarter Sessions was established in October 17, 1683, by the first Assembly in New York. It had jurisdiction over both civil and criminal matters until 1691, when it was restricted to felony crimes not punishable by death or life imprisonment. The court was abolished in all counties of New York except <a href="/wiki/New_York_County" class="mw-redirect" title="New York County">New York County</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>). In New York County, the Court of General Sessions continued until 1962 when its scope devolved to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Supreme_Court" title="New York Supreme Court">New York Supreme Court</a> (a trial-level court of general jurisdiction not to be confused with the highest court of the New York system, which is called the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Court_of_Appeals" title="New York Court of Appeals">New York Court of Appeals</a>). At the time when it was abolished, the Court of General Sessions of New York County was the oldest criminal court in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Quarter Sessions were also held in the colony of <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>.  \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="India_and_Malaysia">India and Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quarter_session&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: India and Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, the quarter sessions have evolved into permanent <a href="/wiki/Sessions_court" class="mw-redirect" title="Sessions court">sessions courts</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
