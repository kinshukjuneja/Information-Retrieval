title="Administrative division">administrative division</a> in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> from 1888 to 1974, used for the purposes of <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a>. They are now abolished, although in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> their former areas are used as the basis for <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceremonial county">lieutenancy</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_county&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="England_and_Wales">England and Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_county&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: England and Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Administrative_counties_of_England" title="Administrative counties of England">Administrative counties of England</a></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Subdivisions of Wales">Subdivisions of Wales</a></div>\n<p>The term was introduced for England and Wales by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1888" title="Local Government Act 1888">Local Government Act 1888</a>, which created <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> for various areas, and called them \'administrative counties\' to distinguish them from the continuing statutory <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceremonial counties">counties</a>.\n</p><p>In England and Wales the legislation was repealed in 1974, and entities called \'<a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties</a>\' in England and \'counties\' in Wales were introduced in their place.  Though strictly inaccurate, these are often called \'administrative counties\' to distinguish them from both the historic counties, and the <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial counties</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_county&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Counties of Scotland">Counties of Scotland</a></div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> they were never established as separate entities as they were in England and Wales<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.  For local government purposes Scottish counties were replaced in 1975 with a system of <a href="/wiki/Local_government_areas_of_Scotland_1973_to_1996" class="mw-redirect" title="Local government areas of Scotland 1973 to 1996">regions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Island_council_areas_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Island council areas of Scotland">island council areas</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Administrative_county&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Ireland)_Act_1898" title="Local Government (Ireland) Act 1898">Local Government (Ireland) Act 1898</a> created administrative counties in Ireland on the same model that had been used in England and Wales.\n</p><p>In Northern Ireland the administrative counties were replaced by a system of 26 <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Northern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Districts of Northern Ireland">districts</a> on 1 October 1973. Section 131 of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_(Northern_Ireland)_1972" title="Local Government Act (Northern Ireland) 1972">Local Government Act (Northern Ireland) 1972</a> stated that <i>"every county and every county borough shall cease to be an administrative area for local government purposes"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The areas of the former administrative counties (and county boroughs) remain in use for <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Lieutenant">Lieutenancy</a> purposes, being defined as the areas used  <i>"for local government purposes immediately before 1 October 1973, subject to any subsequent definition of their boundaries..."</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> the legislation that created them remained in force until the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_2001" title="Local Government Act 2001">Local Government Act 2001</a> was passed, which renamed them \'counties\'.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_entities">New entities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
