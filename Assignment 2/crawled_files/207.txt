rubble</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Cromwell\'s Castle</b> is an artillery fort overlooking <a href="/wiki/New_Grimsby_harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="New Grimsby harbour">New Grimsby harbour</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. It comprises a tall, circular gun tower and an adjacent gun platform, and was designed to prevent enemy naval vessels from entering the harbour. The castle was built in two phases; Sir <a href="/wiki/Robert_Blake_(admiral)" title="Robert Blake (admiral)">Robert Blake</a> constructed the tower between 1651 and 1652 in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Roundheads" class="mw-redirect" title="Roundheads">Parliamentary</a> invasion of the islands at the end of the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>, and Master Gunner Abraham Tovey added the gun platform during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_Jenkins%27_Ear" title="War of Jenkins&#39; Ear">War of Jenkins\' Ear</a> around 1739. The tower fell into disuse soon afterwards, and in the 21st century is managed by <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage</a> and open to visitors.\n</p>\n<div History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="17th_century">17th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cromwell%27s_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 17th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Cromwell\'s Castle is an artillery tower, built by Sir <a href="/wiki/Robert_Blake_(admiral)" title="Robert Blake (admiral)">Robert Blake</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliamentary</a> invasion of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> in 1651.<sup id="cite_ref-BowdenBrodieP9_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BowdenBrodieP9-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> between 1642 and 1646, the inhabitants of the Isles had been Royalist supporters of King <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_England" title="Charles I of England">Charles I</a>, and rebelled against Parliament in favour of Charles in 1648.<sup id="cite_ref-BowdenBrodieP9_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BowdenBrodieP9-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Tresco became a base for Royalist <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateers</a> and Parliament became concerned that the Dutch, then hostile to England, might exploit the situation, or that Royalist Irish forces might use the islands for an attack on England.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 1651 Parliament sent Robert Blake and a naval force to retake the island, which had been fortified by the Royalists.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cromwell_Castle_and_King_Charles%27s_Castle.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Cromwell_Castle_and_King_Charles%27s_Castle.jpg/220px-Cromwell_Castle_and_King_Charles%27s_Castle.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Cromwell_Castle_and_King_Charles%27s_Castle.jpg/330px-Cromwell_Castle_and_King_Charles%27s_Castle.jpg 1.5x, data-file-height="426" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cromwell_Castle_and_King_Charles%27s_Castle.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The castle seen from the sea, overlooked by the ruined <a href="/wiki/King_Charles%27s_Castle" title="King Charles&#39;s Castle">King Charles\'s Castle</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Having established control of the islands, between 1651 and 1652 Blake constructed Cromwell\'s Castle on Tresco, named after <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a>, the Parliamentary leader.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was intended to protect the deep water entrance to <a href="/wiki/New_Grimsby_harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="New Grimsby harbour">New Grimsby harbour</a> on the west side of the island, a route which could also allow enemy vessels access through to the other Scilly Isles.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Parliamentarian forces were particularly concerned about any potential Dutch attack.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There were two existing fortifications in this location.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The main existing fort, <a href="/wiki/King_Charles%27s_Castle" title="King Charles&#39;s Castle">King Charles\'s Castle</a>, had been built in the 1550s but was poorly sited and had been blown up by its defenders when Blake\'s forces took the island.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> There was also a small blockhouse beneath it, dating from the 16th century, and the new castle was built on top of this preexisting site.<sup id="cite_ref-bowden29_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bowden29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The castle initially comprised a three-storey circular tower, 13.45 metres (44.1&#160;ft) across and 15.1 metres (50&#160;ft) high, with walls almost 13 feet (4.0&#160;m) thick, made from massive pieces of rubble; a later survey described the tower as "a Huge Mass of Masonry".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Some of the stone used came from the ruins of King Charles\'s Castle.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Six <a href="/wiki/Gun-port" class="mw-redirect" title="Gun-port">gun-ports</a> with broad external splays on the roof allowed the battery a good angle of fire across the channel between the islands of <a href="/wiki/Bryher,_Isles_of_Scilly" class="mw-redirect" title="Bryher, Isles of Scilly">Bryher</a> and Tresco.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> There may have been an adjacent gun platform just beneath the main structure.<sup id="cite_ref-bowden29_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bowden29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The design was old-fashioned, resembling the <a href="/wiki/Device_Fort" class="mw-redirect" title="Device Fort">circular keeps</a> built by <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII</a> in the mid-16th century.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="17th.E2.80.9320th_centuries"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="17th\xe2\x80\x9320th_centuries">17th\xe2\x80\x9320th centuries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cromwell%27s_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 17th\xe2\x80\x9320th centuries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Grimsby_Harbour,_1756.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/New_Grimsby_Harbour%2C_1756.png/300px-New_Grimsby_Harbour%2C_1756.png" width="300" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/New_Grimsby_Harbour%2C_1756.png/450px-New_Grimsby_Harbour%2C_1756.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/New_Grimsby_Harbour%2C_1756.png/600px-New_Grimsby_Harbour%2C_1756.png 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="568" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Grimsby_Harbour,_1756.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>New Grimsby harbour in 1756, showing the castle (centre), overlooked by King Charles\'s Castle (right)</div></div></div>\n<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Restoration_(England)" title="Restoration (England)">restoration</a> of <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II</a> to the throne in 1660, a survey of the castle was carried out, which recommended repairs to the site.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> A survey carried out at the end of the 1730s suggested that the castle had ten artillery positions, but that there were not enough guns on the island to arm both the castle and the other defences, and that some of the guns that were available were unusable.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1739, the <a href="/wiki/War_of_Jenkins%27_Ear" title="War of Jenkins&#39; Ear">War of Jenkins\' Ear</a> broke out between Britain and Spain, and the decision was taken to improve the defences at Cromwell Castle.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Abraham Tovey, the Master Gunner at the nearby fortification known as <a href="/wiki/Star_Castle,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Star Castle, Isles of Scilly">the Garrison</a>, constructed a large gun-platform for a battery of six guns on the south-west side of the tower, possibly on top of any pre-existing 16th-century platform.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> A parapet protected the guns, a new entrance was cut into the main tower and other adjustments made.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Antiquary" class="mw-redirect" title="Antiquary">antiquary</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Borlase" title="William Borlase">William Borlase</a> visited the castle in 1752, noting that the gun platform was armed with 9-pounder (4&#160;kg) cannons and the tower roof with 4-pounder (1.8&#160;kg) guns.<sup id="cite_ref-historicengland47_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historicengland47-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The site was now unmanned, however, and had fallen into decay since the construction work by Tovey.<sup id="cite_ref-historicengland47_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historicengland47-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The writer John Troutbeck, commenting at the end of the century, remarked on the tower having a "special bomb proof" roof, topped with 6-foot (1.8&#160;m) high parapets.<sup id="cite_ref-Troutbeck_1796_126_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Troutbeck_1796_126-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He noted, that although there while still some old iron artillery guns present, the site was still ungarrisoned and in a state of disrepair.<sup id="cite_ref-Troutbeck_1796_126_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Troutbeck_1796_126-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">The Crown</a>, in the form of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a>, had leased the Scilly Isles to the Godolphin family in 1687, followed by Augustus Smith in 1834.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 1922, the lease passed to <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien-Smith" title="Arthur Algernon Dorrien-Smith">Arthur Dorrien-Smith</a>, who agreed to pass several properties on Tresco, including the castle, into the guardianship of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Works_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Works (United Kingdom)">Ministry of Works</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cromwell%27s_Castle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png/300px-Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png" width="300" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png/450px-Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png/600px-Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png 2x" data-file-width="4244" data-file-height="2068" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cromwell%27s_Castle_plan_with_roof.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Plan of the castle, ground floor (left), first floor (right): A \xe2\x80\x93 18th century gun platform; B \xe2\x80\x93 guard house; C \xe2\x80\x93 latrines; D \xe2\x80\x93 17th-century tower</div></div></div>\n<p>In the 21st century, the castle is controlled by <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage</a>, as the successor to the Ministry of Works, and open to visitors. The interior wooden floors have been lost, but the spiral staircase and the stone, <a href="/wiki/Vault_(architecture)" title="Vault (architecture)">vaulted</a> roof remain intact and accessible.<sup id="cite_ref-Ref-2_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ref-2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> English Heritage have commented that the castle is "one of only a few stone fortifications that survive from the Interregnum", and it is protected by UK law as a Grade II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
