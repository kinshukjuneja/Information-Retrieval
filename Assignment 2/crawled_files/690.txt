title="Korean language text">KBO \xeb\xa6\xac\xea\xb7\xb8</span>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> originally called the <b>Korea Baseball Championship</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>&#58;&#160;<span lang="ko-Hang" title="Korean language text">\xed\x95\x9c\xea\xb5\xad\xec\x95\xbc\xea\xb5\xac\xec\x84\xa0\xec\x88\x98\xea\xb6\x8c\xeb\x8c\x80\xed\x9a\x8c</span>; <a href="/wiki/Korean_romanization" class="mw-redirect" title="Korean romanization">Romanization</a>: <i>Hanguk Yagu Seonsukkwon Daehoe</i>), is the highest level league of <a href="/wiki/Baseball_in_South_Korea" title="Baseball in South Korea">baseball in South Korea</a>. The KBO League was founded with six franchises in 1982, and has expanded to ten franchises.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Nine of the ten franchises are named after the companies or business conglomerates which own them, while one sold their naming rights (Nexen Heroes).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The KBO League is the most popular sports league in South Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a> (formerly Haitai Tigers) are the most successful team, having won 11 out of the 36 championships.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBO_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The first game was played on March 27, 1982 between <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a> and the <a href="/wiki/MBC_Chungyong" class="mw-redirect" title="MBC Chungyong">MBC Chungyong</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">LG Twins</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Dongdaemun_Stadium" title="Dongdaemun Stadium">Dongdaemun Baseball Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. Then-president <a href="/wiki/Chun_Doo-hwan" title="Chun Doo-hwan">Chun Doo-hwan</a> threw the first pitch.\n</p><p>The inaugural franchises were: \n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Haitai Tigers</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Gwangju" title="Gwangju">Gwangju</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Lotte_Giants" title="Lotte Giants">Lotte Giants</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">MBC Chungyong</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Doosan_Bears" title="Doosan Bears">OB Bears</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejeon</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Sammi_Superstars" class="mw-redirect" title="Sammi Superstars">Sammi Superstars</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Daegu" title="Daegu">Daegu</a></li></ul>\n<p>In 1985, the <a href="/wiki/Sammi_Superstars" class="mw-redirect" title="Sammi Superstars">Sammi Superstars</a> became known as the <a href="/wiki/Cheongbo_Pintos" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheongbo Pintos">Cheongbo Pintos</a>. The next year, 1986, saw some major changes, with the <a href="/wiki/Doosan_Bears" title="Doosan Bears">OB Bears</a> moving from <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejeon</a> to share the <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Jamsil_Baseball_Stadium" title="Jamsil Baseball Stadium">Jamsil Baseball Stadium</a> with <a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">MBC Chungyong</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. A new franchise, the <a href="/wiki/Hanwha_Eagles" title="Hanwha Eagles">Binggrae Eagles</a>, joined to replace vacancy of <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejeon</a> by OB\'s moving and expanding the league to seven franchises. 1988 saw the Cheongbo Pintos change ownership again, becoming the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Dolphins" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Dolphins">Pacific Dolphins</a>. In 1990, <a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">MBC Chungyong</a> became the <a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">LG Twins</a> and an eighth franchise was added, the <a href="/wiki/Ssangbangwool_Raiders" title="Ssangbangwool Raiders">Ssangbangwool Raiders</a> who represented the <a href="/wiki/Jeollabuk-do" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeollabuk-do">Jeollabuk-do</a> region.\n</p><p>There was little change in the 1990s except for a few major sponsors: in 1993 the Binggrae Eagles became the <a href="/wiki/Hanwha_Eagles" title="Hanwha Eagles">Hanwha Eagles</a>, in 1995 the Pacific Dolphins became the Hyundai Unicorns and the <a href="/wiki/OB_Bears" class="mw-redirect" title="OB Bears">OB Bears</a> in 1999 became the <a href="/wiki/Doosan_Bears" title="Doosan Bears">Doosan Bears</a>. Bigger changes were affected in 2000 when the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Unicorns" title="Hyundai Unicorns">Hyundai Unicorns</a> moved from <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> to <a href="/wiki/Suwon" title="Suwon">Suwon</a>, and a new franchise, the <a href="/wiki/SK_Wyverns" title="SK Wyverns">SK Wyverns</a> took their place in <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Ssangbangwool_Raiders" title="Ssangbangwool Raiders">Ssangbangwool Raiders</a> became defunct. In 2001, the <a href="/wiki/Haitai_Tigers" class="mw-redirect" title="Haitai Tigers">Haitai Tigers</a> became the <a href="/wiki/Kia_Tigers" title="Kia Tigers">Kia Tigers</a>.\n</p><p>In 2008, the Hyundai Unicorns franchise was disbanded, re-founded as the <a href="/wiki/Nexen_Heroes" title="Nexen Heroes">Woori Heroes</a> and moved to Mok-dong in Seoul. Despite winning four championships, the franchise enjoyed little fan support after moving to Suwon. The team\'s naming rights were sold to <a href="/wiki/Nexen_Tire" title="Nexen Tire">Nexen Tire</a> and the team was renamed <a href="/wiki/Nexen_Heroes" title="Nexen Heroes">Nexen Heroes</a>.\n</p><p>Expansion resumed in the 2010s, with the addition of the <a href="/wiki/NC_Dinos" title="NC Dinos">NC Dinos</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Changwon" title="Changwon">Changwon</a>, which joined the league in 2013. It is the first team located in Changwon, the city having previously been the second home of the nearby Lotte Giants.\n</p><p>In 2015, the <a href="/wiki/KT_Wiz" title="KT Wiz">KT Wiz</a> became the league\'s 10th franchise. They play their home games in <a href="/wiki/Suwon" title="Suwon">Suwon</a>, which had not had a team since the Hyundai Unicorns\' disbandment.\n</p><p>The league has recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> enjoyed a surge in popularity, with increased attendance and player salaries. Several KBO players have also successfully transitioned from the KBO to <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">the Major Leagues</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Ryu_Hyun-Jin" class="mw-redirect" title="Ryu Hyun-Jin">Ryu Hyun-Jin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kang_Jung-Ho" class="mw-redirect" title="Kang Jung-Ho">Kang Jung-Ho</a> or <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hyun-soo" class="mw-redirect" title="Kim Hyun-soo">Kim Hyun-soo</a>. This increase in popularity has been accompanied by the building of larger and more modern ballparks, such as <a href="/wiki/Gwangju-Kia_Champions_Field" title="Gwangju-Kia Champions Field">Gwangju-Kia Champions Field</a> (2014), <a href="/wiki/Gocheok_Sky_Dome" title="Gocheok Sky Dome">Gocheok Sky Dome</a> (2016) and <a href="/wiki/Daegu_Samsung_Lions_Park" title="Daegu Samsung Lions Park">Daegu Samsung Lions Park</a> (2016).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_structure">Season structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBO_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Season structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Starting with the 2015 season, each team plays 144 games in the regular season, increased from 128 due to the addition of the <a href="/wiki/KT_Wiz" title="KT Wiz">KT Wiz</a> to the league. Each team plays every other team 16 times.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="KBO_All-Star_Game">KBO All-Star Game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBO_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: KBO All-Star Game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In mid-July of every season, the best players participate in the KBO All-Star Game. The franchises participating are divided into two sets of teams: Dream All-Stars (Doosan, KT, Lotte, Samsung, and SK) and Nanum All-Stars (Kia, Hanwha, LG, NC and Nexen). The KBO All-star game does not determine home-field advantage in the <a href="/wiki/KBO_Korean_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="KBO Korean Series">KBO Korean Series</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Playoffs">Playoffs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBO_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Playoffs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The KBO League\'s season culminates in its championship series, known as the <a href="/wiki/KBO_Korean_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="KBO Korean Series">KBO Korean Series</a>. Currently, the top five teams qualify for the post-season based on win/loss records. The lowest-qualifying teams face off in a step-ladder playoff system, where each winner then faces the next-highest team, culimnating in the Korean Series against the top-ranked team.\n</p>\n<ul><li><i>KBO Wild Card Game</i>: 5th place team vs. 4th place team.</li></ul>\n<dl><dd><dl><dd><i>4th place advances with one win, 5th place must win twice to advance.</i></dd></dl></dd></dl>\n<ul><li><i>KBO Semi Playoffs</i>: KBO Wild Card Game Winner vs. 3rd place team</li></ul>\n<dl><dd><dl><dd><i>First to three games advances.</i></dd></dl></dd></dl>\n<ul><li><i>KBO Playoffs</i>: KBO Semi Playoffs Winner vs. 2nd place team</li></ul>\n<dl><dd><dl><dd><i>First to three games advances</i> (reduced from four games since 2009).</dd></dl></dd></dl>\n<ul><li><i>KBO Korean Series</i>: KBO Playoffs Winner vs. 1st place team</li></ul>\n<dl><dd><dl><dd><i>First to four games wins championship.</i></dd></dl></dd></dl>\n<p>Any playoff games ending in an official tie are replayed, thereby raising the possibility of a close series containing more than the scheduled five or seven games.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KBO_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Traditionally, Korea Professional Baseball games have a maximum number of extra innings before a game is declared an official tie. The KBO abolished this limit for the 2008 season, however it was reinstated in 2009, with a 12-innings limit imposed during regular season, 15-innings limit for playoff games.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \nThe league places a cap on the number of foreign players allowed on club rosters. The foreign player limit is set at three, increased from two players from 2014.\n</p><p>All teams use the <a href="/wiki/Designated_hitter" title="Designated hitter">designated hitter</a> rule.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">Teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
