of the course of the River Hayle</div>\n</td></tr>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n</tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>River Hayle</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Heyl</b></i>, meaning <i>estuary</i>) is a small river in <a href="/wiki/West_Cornwall_(UK_region)" title="West Cornwall (UK region)">West Cornwall</a>, England, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> which issues into <a href="/wiki/St_Ives_Bay" title="St Ives Bay">St Ives Bay</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hayle" title="Hayle">Hayle</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall\'s</a> <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> coast.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The River Hayle is approx 12 miles (19 kilometres) long and it rises south-west of <a href="/wiki/Crowan" title="Crowan">Crowan</a> village. Its course is west for approx 5 miles (which brings the river to within 3 miles of the south coast at <a href="/wiki/Marazion" title="Marazion">Marazion</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mount%27s_Bay" title="Mount&#39;s Bay">Mount\'s Bay</a>). It then flows through a steep wooded valley north of the <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> high ground at Trescowe Common, formerly a mining area, before turning abruptly north near the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Relubbus" title="Relubbus">Relubbus</a>. It then follows a northerly course for the remaining six miles to the <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuary</a>, passing <a href="/wiki/St_Erth" title="St Erth">St Erth</a>.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Hayle_Estuary" title="Hayle Estuary">Hayle Estuary</a> encompasses a disused port on the east bank and a substantial area of <a href="/wiki/Salt_marsh" title="Salt marsh">salt marsh</a> named <a href="/wiki/Lelant" title="Lelant">Lelant Saltings</a> to the west. The port was once of considerable importance to Hayle\'s industry (see main article <a href="/wiki/Hayle" title="Hayle">Hayle</a>). Lelant saltings is an important habitat for birds and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_for_the_Protection_of_Birds" title="Royal Society for the Protection of Birds">Royal Society for the Protection of Birds</a> runs a <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a> there.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Areas around the estuary are designated as <a href="/wiki/SSSI" class="mw-redirect" title="SSSI">sites of special scientific interest</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/Hayle_Estuary_and_Carrack_Gladden_SSSI" title="Hayle Estuary and Carrack Gladden SSSI">Hayle SSSI</a>).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="River_Hayle_location_information">River Hayle location information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
