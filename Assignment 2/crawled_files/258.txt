title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles, Prince of Wales</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Duchy of Cornwall</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Duketh Kernow</i>) is one of two royal <a href="/wiki/Duchies_in_England" title="Duchies in England">duchies in England</a>, the other being the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Lancaster" title="Duchy of Lancaster">Duchy of Lancaster</a>. The eldest son of the reigning <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">British monarch</a> inherits possession of the duchy and title of <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cornwall" title="Duke of Cornwall">Duke of Cornwall</a> at birth or when his parent succeeds to the throne, but may not sell assets for personal benefit and has limited rights and income as a minor. The current duke is <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles, Prince of Wales</a>. If, and when, the current Prince of Wales accedes the throne, <a href="/wiki/Prince_William" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince William">Prince William</a> will become Duke of Cornwall. When the monarch has no male children, the rights and responsibilities of the duchy revert to <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">the Crown</a> and there is no duke. (The <a href="/wiki/Succession_to_the_Crown_Act_2013" title="Succession to the Crown Act 2013">Succession to the Crown Act 2013</a> does not affect the succession to the duchy, so that if the heir apparent is female she cannot succeed to the duchy.)\nThe Duchy Council, called the Prince\'s Council, meets twice a year and is chaired by the duke. The Prince\'s Council is a non-executive body which provides advice to the duke with regard to the management of the Duchy.<sup id="cite_ref-wmn5_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wmn5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The duchy also exercises certain legal rights and privileges across Cornwall, including some that elsewhere in England belong to the Crown. The duke appoints a number of officials in the county and acts as the port authority for the main harbour of the Isles of Scilly.\n</p><p>The government considers the duchy to be a crown body and therefore <a href="/wiki/Tax_exemption" title="Tax exemption">exempt</a> from paying <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_corporation_tax" title="United Kingdom corporation tax">corporation tax</a>. The tax exempt status of the duchy has been challenged;<sup id="cite_ref-publications.parliament.uk_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-publications.parliament.uk-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and, since 1993, the Duke of Cornwall, the Prince of Wales, has voluntarily paid <a href="/wiki/Taxation_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Taxation in the United Kingdom">income tax</a> on the duchy income, less amounts that he considers to be official expenditure.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchy_of_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Financial arrangements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The principal activity of the duchy is the management of its land holdings in England of  135,000 acres or 550&#160;km<sup>2</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-wmn5_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wmn5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This includes just over 2% of the county of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>. The majority of the estate lies outside Cornwall, with half being on <a href="/wiki/Dartmoor" title="Dartmoor">Dartmoor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, with other large holdings in <a href="/wiki/Herefordshire" title="Herefordshire">Herefordshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a> and almost all of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. The duchy also has a portfolio of financial investments.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_year" title="Fiscal year">fiscal year</a> ending 31 March 2013, the duchy was valued at \xc2\xa3763 million, and annual profit was \xc2\xa319 million, a revenue surplus gain of 4.1% from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> For the fiscal year 2011\xe2\x80\x9312 the Duchy was valued at \xc2\xa3728 million with an annual profit of \xc2\xa318.3 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The estate\'s profit for the year ending 31 March 2002<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Duchy_of_Cornwall&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> was over \xc2\xa37.8 million. The administration of the duchy is regulated by the <i><a href="/wiki/Duchies_of_Lancaster_and_Cornwall_(Accounts)_Act_1838" title="Duchies of Lancaster and Cornwall (Accounts) Act 1838">Duchies of Lancaster and Cornwall (Accounts) Act 1838</a></i>, which requires Treasury\'s supervision and for the accounts to be presented to both Houses of Parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The duchy has special legal rights, such as the rules on <i><a href="/wiki/Bona_vacantia" title="Bona vacantia">bona vacantia</a></i>. This right to ownerless property operates in favour of the duchy rather than the Crown, such that the property of anyone who dies in the county of Cornwall without a will or identifiable heirs, and assets belonging to dissolved companies whose registered office was in Cornwall, pass to the duchy.<sup id="cite_ref-TSol_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DOC-BV_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOC-BV-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, \xc2\xa3130,000 was realised from the right of <i>bona vacantia</i>, and given to a charitable fund.\n</p><p>The income of the Duchy of Cornwall accrues to the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Cornwall" title="Duke of Cornwall">Duke of Cornwall</a>, who is the monarch\'s eldest son if heir to the throne (and thus currently <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles, Prince of Wales</a>). The income of the duchy is also used to cover the official expenditure of some members of the Duke\'s immediate family. Presently, the <a href="/wiki/Camilla,_Duchess_of_Cornwall" title="Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall">Duchess of Cornwall</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prince_William,_Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Prince William, Duke of Cambridge">Duke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catherine,_Duchess_of_Cambridge" title="Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge">Duchess of Cambridge</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Harry,_Duke_of_Sussex" title="Prince Harry, Duke of Sussex">Duke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meghan,_Duchess_of_Sussex" title="Meghan, Duchess of Sussex">Duchess of Sussex</a> all have their official expenses paid from Duchy income,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\nassisted by <a href="/wiki/Grants-in-aid" class="mw-redirect" title="Grants-in-aid">Grants-in-aid</a> from the Queen.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> If there is no duke (for example, if the monarch does not have any male children) the duchy income reverts to the monarch and the annual <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_Grant" class="mw-redirect" title="Sovereign Grant">Sovereign Grant</a> is reduced annually by the amount of the duchy\'s income.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchy_of_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The duchy was established on 17 March 1337 by <a href="/wiki/Royal_charters_applying_to_Cornwall" title="Royal charters applying to Cornwall">the Royal Great Charter</a> out of the former <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Cornwall" title="Earl of Cornwall">Earldom of Cornwall</a> by <a href="/wiki/Edward_III_of_England" title="Edward III of England">Edward III</a> for his son, <a href="/wiki/Edward,_the_Black_Prince" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward, the Black Prince">Edward, Prince of Wales, the "Black Prince"</a>, who became the first Duke of Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The charter established that the Duke of Cornwall is to be the eldest surviving son of the monarch and the heir to the throne. Additional charters were issued later by Edward III. The duchy consisted of the title and honour, and the land holdings that supported it financially.<sup id="cite_ref-Official_History_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_History-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The charter afforded the duke certain rights and responsibilities in the county, including the right to appoint the county\'s sheriff and to the profits from the county courts, the <a href="/wiki/Stannaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Stannaries">stannaries</a> and the ports.<sup id="cite_ref-Rural_Econ_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rural_Econ-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The duchy estate, which was based on the holdings of the previous earls, did not comprise the whole of the county, and much of it lay outside Cornwall. The extent of the estate has varied as various holdings have been sold and others acquired over the years, both within Cornwall and in other counties.<sup id="cite_ref-Official_History_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_History-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Under the charter, the manors of the earldom passed to the duchy. The original 17 manors, all in Cornwall, are known as the <i><a href="/wiki/Antiqua_maneria" title="Antiqua maneria">antiqua maneria</a></i>. Those outside Cornwall given to the duchy at its creation are known as the <i>forinseca maneria</i> (foreign manors), with estates incorporated later becoming known as the <i>annexata maneria</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-dcha_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dcha-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ahme_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ahme-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The first duke ordered a survey called "The Caption of <a href="/wiki/Seisin" title="Seisin">Seisin</a> of the Duchy of Cornwall" in May 1337 to determine the extent of duchy holdings of Cornish land including manors, castles and knights\' fees, profits from the <a href="/wiki/Stannary_Courts_and_Parliaments" class="mw-redirect" title="Stannary Courts and Parliaments">Stannary courts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shrievalty" class="mw-redirect" title="Shrievalty">shrievalty</a> of Cornwall, and other revenues.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A subsequent charter of <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_England" title="Henry IV of England">Henry IV</a> to <a href="/wiki/Henry_V_of_England" title="Henry V of England">Prince Henry</a> stated:\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>We have made and created Henry our most dear first-begotten Son, Prince of Wales, Duke of Cornwall and Earl of Chester, and have given and granted, and by our Charter have confirmed to him the said Principality, Duchy, and Earldom, that he may preside there, and by presiding, may direct and defend the said parts. We have invested him with the said Principality, Duchy, and Earldom, <i>per sertum in capite et annulum in digito aureum ac virgam auream juxta morem</i>.</p></blockquote>\n<p>With the death of <a href="/wiki/Arthur,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Arthur, Prince of Wales">Prince Arthur</a> in 1502, the Prince\'s Council became defunct. From 1547 to 1603, there was no male royal heir to hold the title of duke, and the duchy reverted to the Crown, in effect becoming a department of the <a href="/wiki/Exchequer" title="Exchequer">Exchequer</a>. The Duke of Cornwall has the \'<a href="/wiki/Interest_in_possession_trust" title="Interest in possession trust">interest in possession</a>\' of the duchy\'s assets (such as estates) which means they enjoy its net income, do not have its outright ownership and do not have the right to sell capital assets for their own benefit.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010, the duchy generated \xc2\xa317.1 million in income. id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_status_and_additional_rights">Legal status and additional rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchy_of_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legal status and additional rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Both the Duchy of Cornwall and the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Lancaster" title="Duchy of Lancaster">Duchy of Lancaster</a>\xe2\x80\x94since 1399 held by the monarch in a personal capacity\xe2\x80\x94have special legal rights not available to other landed estates: for example, the rules on <i><a href="/wiki/Bona_vacantia" title="Bona vacantia">bona vacantia</a></i>, the right to ownerless property, operate in favour of the holders of the duchies rather than the Crown, such that the property of anyone who dies in the county of Cornwall without a will or identifiable heirs, and assets belonging to dissolved companies whose registered office was in Cornwall, pass to the duchy.<sup id="cite_ref-TSol_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DOC-BV_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOC-BV-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, \xc2\xa3130,000 was realised from the right of <i>bona vacantia</i>, and given to a charitable fund. The duke owns freehold about three-fifths of the Cornish foreshore and the \'fundus\', or bed, of navigable rivers and has <a href="/wiki/Right_of_wreck" class="mw-redirect" title="Right of wreck">right of wreck</a> on all ships wrecked on Cornish shores, including those afloat offshore, and also to <a href="/wiki/Royal_fish" title="Royal fish">royal fish</a>&#8212;i.e. whales, <a href="/wiki/Porpoise" title="Porpoise">porpoises</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sturgeon" title="Sturgeon">sturgeon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The Duchy of Cornwall is the Harbour Authority for <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mary&#39;s Harbour">St Mary\'s Harbour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> There are separate <a href="/wiki/Attorney_general" title="Attorney general">attorneys-general</a> for the duchies. The <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Cornwall" title="High Sheriff of Cornwall">High Sheriff of Cornwall</a> is appointed by the Duke of Cornwall, not the monarch, in contrast to the other counties of England and Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The duke had a ceremonial role in summoning the <a href="/wiki/Stannary_Courts_and_Parliaments" class="mw-redirect" title="Stannary Courts and Parliaments">Cornish Stannary Parliament</a>.\n</p><p>In <i>Bruton v. ICO</i> the first tier tribunal found that the duchy was a public authority for the purposes of the Environmental Information Regulations 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> reported in 2011 that, since 2005, the Prince of Wales has been asked to give his consent to a number of draft bills on matters ranging from <a href="/wiki/Town_planning" class="mw-redirect" title="Town planning">town planning</a> to gambling, because it could affect the interests of the Duchy of Cornwall. <a href="/wiki/Andrew_George_(politician)" title="Andrew George (politician)">Andrew George</a>, Liberal Democrat <a href="/wiki/St_Ives_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="St Ives (UK Parliament constituency)">MP for St Ives</a>, commented that "The duchy asserts that it is merely a private estate. Most people will be astonished to learn that it appears to have effective powers of veto over the government."<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Writing in the <i>Guardian</i>, lawyer David Gollancz commented that: "The duchy exercises a unique range of legal powers, which elsewhere are reserved for the crown.... It seems anomalous, and worrying, that such a huge estate, created and conferred by law and exercising significant legal powers, should be able to escape public scrutiny by calling itself a private estate."<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The requirement for the Prince of Wales to give consent to draft bills that could affect the interests of the Duchy of Cornwall is not a new power granted to Prince Charles, but a centuries-old parliamentary practice that involved the same requirement for consent being conferred on previous Dukes of Cornwall (<i>see <a href="/wiki/Prince%27s_Consent" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince&#39;s Consent">Prince\'s Consent</a></i>).\n</p><p>For some <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish</a> activists, Cornwall itself is described, <i><a href="/wiki/De_jure" title="De jure">de jure</a></i>, as a <a href="/wiki/Duchy" title="Duchy">duchy</a> as opposed to an ordinary county, and the duchy estates are distinguished from the duchy itself, having themselves been annexed and united to "the aforesaid duchy".<sup id="cite_ref-EdCoke_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EdCoke-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Commission_on_the_Constitution_(United_Kingdom)" title="Royal Commission on the Constitution (United Kingdom)">Royal Commission on the Constitution</a> in 1973 recommended that Cornwall be officially referred to as "the duchy" on what it described as "appropriate occasions" given the nature of the county\'s "special relationship" with the Crown.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kbr_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kbr-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The designation is sometimes found used informally in respect of the county as whole.\n</p><p>Historically all justices of the <a href="/wiki/Assizes" title="Assizes">Assizes</a> who visited Cornwall were also permanent members of the Prince\'s Council which oversees the Duchy of Cornwall and advises the Duke. There are on record at least three instances in which the prince overruled the king by instructing his officials to ignore or disobey orders issued to them by the King\'s <a href="/wiki/Court_of_equity" title="Court of equity">Chancery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxation">Taxation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchy_of_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Taxation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The government considers the duchy to be a crown body and therefore <a href="/wiki/Tax_exemption" title="Tax exemption">exempt</a> from paying <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_corporation_tax" title="United Kingdom corporation tax">corporation tax</a>. The tax position of the duchy has been challenged by British republicans.<sup id="cite_ref-publications.parliament.uk_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-publications.parliament.uk-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Following the ruling that the duchy was separate from Prince Charles for the purposes of regulation, <a href="/wiki/Republic_(political_organisation)" title="Republic (political organisation)">Republic</a>, the campaign for an elected head of state, has asked <a href="/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs" title="HM Revenue and Customs">HM Revenue and Customs</a> to investigate if the duchy should still be exempt from tax. The tax exemption is based on the assumption that the duchy estate is inseparable from the tax exempt person of Prince Charles, which is now open to question.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian-20121214_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-20121214-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since 1993, the Prince of Wales has voluntarily paid <a href="/wiki/Taxation_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Taxation in the United Kingdom">income tax</a> on the duchy income less amounts which he considers to be official expenditure.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The Prince paid a voluntary contribution to the <a href="/wiki/HM_Treasury" title="HM Treasury">treasury</a> of 50% of his Duchy income from the time he became eligible for its full income at the age of 21 in 1969, and paid 25% from his marriage in 1981 until the current arrangement commenced in 1993. Tax is calculated after deducting official expenditure, the biggest source of which is the Prince\'s staff of about 110 who assist with his performance of official duties, including private secretaries and a valet working in his office at <a href="/wiki/Clarence_House" title="Clarence House">Clarence House</a> and at <a href="/wiki/Highgrove_House" title="Highgrove House">Highgrove House</a>. The official expenditure of the Prince of Wales is not audited by the National Audit Office.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span
