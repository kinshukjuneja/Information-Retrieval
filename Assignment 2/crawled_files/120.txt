United States, is one of the largest non-profit scientific and educational organizations in the world.  Founded in 1888, its interests include <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Natural_science" title="Natural science">natural science</a>, the promotion of environmental and historical <a href="/wiki/Conservation_movement" title="Conservation movement">conservation</a>, and the study of <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">world culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_history" title="World history">history</a>. The National Geographic Society\'s logo is a yellow <a href="/wiki/Page_orientation" title="Page orientation">portrait</a> frame\xe2\x80\x94rectangular in shape\xe2\x80\x94which appears on the <a href="/wiki/Margin_(typography)" title="Margin (typography)">margins</a> surrounding the front covers of its <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic" title="National Geographic">magazines</a> and as <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="National Geographic (U.S. TV channel)">its television channel</a> logo. In partnership with <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a>, the Society operates the magazine, TV channels, a website that features extra content, worldwide events, and other media operations.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dancer_of_the_Cafes_NGM-v31-p269.jpg" class="image"><img class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dancer_of_the_Cafes_NGM-v31-p269.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A dancer of the cafes, <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, 1917 photograph from <i>National Geographic</i> magazine</div></div></div>\n<p>The National Geographic Society was founded in 1888 "to increase and diffuse geographic knowledge." It is governed by a board of trustees, whose 21 members include distinguished educators, business executives, former government officials and conservationists. The organization sponsors and funds scientific research and exploration. National Geographic maintains a museum for the public in its Washington, D.C., headquarters.\n</p><p>It has helped to sponsor popular traveling exhibits, such as the early 2010s <i>King Tut</i> exhibit featuring artifacts from the tomb of the young <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">Pharaoh</a>; <i>The Cultural Treasures of Afghanistan</i> which opened in May 2008 and traveled to other cities for 18 months; and an exhibition of China\'s <a href="/wiki/Terracotta_Warriors" class="mw-redirect" title="Terracotta Warriors">Terracotta Warriors</a> in its Washington headquarters in 2009\xe2\x80\x9310. Its Education Foundation gives grants to education organizations and individuals to improve geography education.<sup id="cite_ref-ngef_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ngef-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Its Committee for Research and Exploration has awarded more than 11,000 grants for scientific research and exploration.\n</p><p>National Geographic has retail stores in Washington, D.C., London, Sydney, and Panama. The locations outside of the United States are operated by <a href="/wiki/Worldwide_Retail_Store" title="Worldwide Retail Store">Worldwide Retail Store</a> S.L., a Spanish holding company.\n</p><p>The Society\'s media arm is National Geographic Partners, a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a> and the Society, which publishes a journal, <i><a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="National Geographic (magazine)">National Geographic</a></i> in English and nearly 40 local-language editions. It also publishes other magazines, books, school products, maps, and Web and film products in numerous languages and countries.  National Geographic\'s various media properties reach more than 280 million people monthly.<sup id="cite_ref-statedept_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statedept-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Geographic_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Natgeo_old.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Natgeo_old.png/220px-Natgeo_old.png" width="220" height="222" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d1/Natgeo_old.png 1.5x" data-file-width="258" data-file-height="260" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Natgeo_old.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Historical emblem of the National Geographic Society</div></div></div>\n<p>The National Geographic Society began as a club for an elite group of academics and wealthy patrons interested in travel and exploration.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On January 13, 1888, 33 explorers and scientists gathered at the <a href="/wiki/Cosmos_Club" title="Cosmos Club">Cosmos Club</a>, a private club then located on <a href="/wiki/President%27s_Park#Lafayette_Square" title="President&#39;s Park">Lafayette Square</a> in Washington, D.C., to organize "a society for the increase and diffusion of <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geographical</a> knowledge." After preparing a constitution and a plan of organization, the National Geographic Society was incorporated two weeks later on January 27. <a href="/wiki/Gardiner_Greene_Hubbard" title="Gardiner Greene Hubbard">Gardiner Greene Hubbard</a> became its first president and his son-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Graham_Bell" title="Alexander Graham Bell">Alexander Graham Bell</a>, succeeded him in 1897.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1899, Bell\'s son-in-law <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Hovey_Grosvenor" title="Gilbert Hovey Grosvenor">Gilbert Hovey Grosvenor</a> was named the first full-time editor of National Geographic magazine and served the organization for fifty-five years (until 1954), and members of the Grosvenor family have played important roles in the organization since.  Bell and Gilbert Hovey Grosvenor devised the successful marketing notion of Society membership and the first major use of photographs to tell stories in magazines.\n</p><p>The chairman of the National Geographic Society is Jean Case. Michael Ulica is interim president and chief executive. The editor-in-chief of National Geographic magazine is Susan Goldberg. <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Melville_Grosvenor" title="Gilbert Melville Grosvenor">Gilbert Melville Grosvenor</a>, a former chairman, received the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Medal_of_Freedom" title="Presidential Medal of Freedom">Presidential Medal of Freedom</a> in 2005 for his leadership in geography education.\n</p><p>In 2004, the National Geographic Society headquarters in Washington, D.C., was one of the first buildings to receive a "Green" certification<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> from <a href="/wiki/Global_Green_USA" title="Global Green USA">Global Green USA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The National Geographic received the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Asturias_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince of Asturias Award">Prince of Asturias Award</a> for Communication and Humanities in October 2006 in <a href="/wiki/Oviedo,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Oviedo, Spain">Oviedo, Spain</a>.\n</p><p>In 2013 the society was investigated for possible violation of the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act" title="Foreign Corrupt Practices Act">Foreign Corrupt Practices Act</a> relating to their close association with an Egyptian government official responsible for antiquities.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" new company known as National Geographic Partners, which would be majority-owned by <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a> with a 73% stake. This new, for-profit corporation, would own <i>National Geographic</i> and other magazines, as well as its affiliated television networks\xe2\x80\x94most of which were already owned in joint ventures with Fox.<sup id="cite_ref-verge-ngp_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verge-ngp-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> At the time of the deal\'s announcement, <a href="/wiki/James_Murdoch" title="James Murdoch">James Murdoch</a>, the <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> of 21st Century Fox, said in remarks to National Geographic that the pact created "an expanded canvas for the National Geographic brand to grow and reach customers in new ways, and to reach new customers."<sup id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On November 2, 2015, roughly two weeks before the closing of the expanded joint venture deal,<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> National Geographic and 21st Century Fox announced that 9 percent of National Geographic\'s 2,000 employees, approximately 180 people, would be laid off, constituting the biggest staff reduction in the Society\'s history.<sup id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As reported by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, a spokesman for National Geographic in a November 2, 2015, e-mail statement, briefly discussed the rationale for the staff reductions as part of the "... process of reorganizing in order to move forward strategically following the closing the National Geographic Partners deal, which is expected to occur in mid-November."<sup id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Additional specifics were provided to <a href="/wiki/Photo_District_News" title="Photo District News">Photo District News</a> by M. J. Jacobsen, National Geographic\'s SVP of communications, similar to the contents of a formal announcement by the two companies.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> "The National Geographic Society and the National Geographic Channels are in the process of reorganizing in order to move forward strategically following the closing of the NG Partners deal (with Fox), which is expected to occur in mid-November," Jaobsen wrote. "Involuntary separations will represent about 9 percent of the overall workforce reduction, many in shared services and a voluntary separation offer has also been made to eligible employees," he added.\n</p><p>Specifics as to which departments would be affected were not immediately available but <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> reported that the staff reduction appears to affect almost every department including the magazine and the <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel" class="mw-redirect" title="National Geographic Channel">National Geographic Channel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On October 26, 2016, National Geographic announced a rebrand and the television network dropped "Channel" from its name.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On December 14, 2017, in a historic <a href="/wiki/Acquisition_of_21st_Century_Fox_by_Disney" class="mw-redirect" title="Acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney">deal</a> valued at over $52 billion, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> announced it will buy the majority of 21st Century Fox. Disney would assume the controlling interest in the National Geographic partnership.<sup id="cite_ref-FoxDisneyPressRelease_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FoxDisneyPressRelease-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Founders">Founders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Geographic_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Founders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There were 33 original founders in 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Although <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Graham_Bell" title="Alexander Graham Bell">Alexander Graham Bell</a> is sometimes discussed as a founder, he was actually the second president, elected on January 7, 1898, and serving until 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Abbe" title="Cleveland Abbe">Cleveland Abbe</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Frank_Baker_(physician)" title="Frank Baker (physician)">Frank Baker</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Marcus_Baker" title="Marcus Baker">Marcus Baker</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/John_Russell_Bartlett_(naval_officer)" title="John Russell Bartlett (naval officer)">John Russell Bartlett</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_J._Bell_(businessman)" title="Charles J. href="/wiki/File:1915NatGeog.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/1915NatGeog.jpg/220px-1915NatGeog.jpg" width="220" height="305" class="thumbimage" Geographic</i></div></div></div>\n<p><i>The National Geographic Magazine</i>, later shortened to <i>National Geographic</i>, published its first issue in October 1888, nine months after the Society was founded, as the Society\'s official journal, a benefit for joining the tax-exempt National Geographic Society. Starting with the February 1910 (Vol XXI, No. 2) issue, the magazine began using its now famous trademarked yellow border around the edge of its covers.\n</p><p>There are 12 monthly issues of <i>National Geographic</i> per year.  The magazine contains articles about geography, popular science, world history, culture, current events and photography of places and things all over the world and universe. <i>National Geographic</i> magazine is currently published in 40 local-language editions in many countries around the world. Combined English and other language circulation is around 6.8 million monthly, with some 60 million readers.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_publications">Other publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Geographic_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In addition to its flagship magazine, the Society publishes several other periodicals:\n</p>\n<ul><li><i>National Geographic Explorer</i>: Classroom magazine. The <i>National Geographic School Bulletin</i> was launched in 1919 and was replaced by the children\'s magazine <i>National Geographic World</i> in 1975. <i>NG</i> <i>World</i> was separated into the current <i>National Geographic Explorer</i> and <i>National Geographic Kids</i> in 2001.</li>\n<li><i>National Geographic History</i>: Launched in Spring 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Kids" title="National Geographic Kids">National Geographic Kids</a></i>: A version of <i>National Geographic Magazine</i> for children, launched in 1975 under the name <i>National Geographic World</i>. It has a U.S. circulation of over 1.5 million. There are also currently 18 local-language editions of <i>NG Kids</i>, with another half million in circulation. An Arabic edition of the children\'s magazine was launched in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> in early 2007, and more than 42,000 copies are distributed to all the public schools in Egypt, in addition to another 15,000 single copy sales.  More recently, an <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> edition were launched.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Kids#National_Geographic_Little_Kids" title="National Geographic Kids">National Geographic Little Kids</a></i>: For younger children aged 3\xe2\x80\x936</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Traveler" title="National Geographic Traveler">National Geographic Traveler</a></i>: Launched in 1984. There are 18 local-language editions of <i>NG Traveler</i>.</li></ul>\n<p>The Society also runs an online news outlet called <i>National Geographic Daily News</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Additionally, the Society publishes <a href="/wiki/Atlases" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlases">atlases</a>, books, and <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">maps</a>. class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Geographic_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span title="National Geographic Channel">National Geographic Channel</a></div>\n<p>Programs by the National Geographic Society are also broadcast on television. National Geographic <a href="/wiki/Television_specials" class="mw-redirect" title="Television specials">television specials</a> and series have been aired on <a href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a> and other networks in the United States and globally for many years. The <i>Geographic</i> series in the U.S. started on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> in 1964, moved to <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> in 1973, shifted to PBS (produced by <a href="/wiki/WQED_(TV)" title="WQED (TV)">WQED</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>) in 1975, shifted to <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> in 1995, and returned to PBS in 2000.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_Channel" class="mw-redirect" title="National Geographic Channel">National Geographic Channel</a>, launched in January 2001, is a joint venture of National Geographic and <a href="/wiki/Fox_Cable_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="Fox Cable Networks">Fox Cable Networks</a>. It has featured stories on numerous scientific figures such as <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Cousteau" title="Jacques Cousteau">Jacques Cousteau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jane_Goodall" title="Jane Goodall">Jane Goodall</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Leakey" title="Louis Leakey">Louis Leakey</a> that not only featured their work but as well helped make them world-famous and accessible to millions. Most of the specials were narrated by various actors, including <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Close" title="Glenn Close">Glenn Close</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linda_Hunt" title="Linda Hunt">Linda Hunt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Keach" title="Stacy Keach">Stacy Keach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Kiley" title="Richard Kiley">Richard Kiley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burgess_Meredith" title="Burgess Meredith">Burgess Meredith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Scourby" title="Alexander Scourby">Alexander Scourby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Sheen" title="Martin Sheen">Martin Sheen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Strauss" title="Peter Strauss">Peter Strauss</a>. The specials\' theme music, by <a href="/wiki/Elmer_Bernstein" title="Elmer Bernstein">Elmer Bernstein</a>, was also adopted by the National Geographic Channel. The National Geographic Channel has begun to launch a number of sub-branded channels in international markets, such as <a href="/wiki/Nat_Geo_Adventure" class="mw-redirect" title="Nat Geo Adventure">Nat Geo Adventure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nat_Geo_Music" title="Nat Geo Music">Nat Geo Music</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nat_Geo_Wild" title="Nat Geo Wild">Nat Geo Wild</a>.\n</p><p>The National Geographic Partners expanded joint venture with <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a> (to be controlled by the latter) will be concluded in mid-November 2015 with the new for-profit organization to own a 73% stake of the NG affiliated television networks (National Geographic-branded cable TV channels),<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> most of which were already co-owned (with the percentage information not readily available), as per earlier joint ventures with Fox.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Geographic_Museum">National Geographic Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Geographic_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: National Geographic Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Society operates the National Geographic Museum, located at 1145 17th Street, NW (17th and M), in Washington, D.C. About 4 million students a year begin the geography competition locally, which culminates in a national competition of the winners of each state each May in Washington, D.C. Journalist <a href="/wiki/Soledad_O%27Brien" title="Soledad O&#39;Brien">Soledad O\'Brien</a> is the moderator of the Bee. She succeeded <a href="/wiki/Alex_Trebek" title="Alex Trebek">Alex Trebek</a>, host of <i><a href="/wiki/Jeopardy!" title="Jeopardy!">Jeopardy!</a></i>, who moderated the final round of the competition for 25 years, from its inception in 1989 to 2013. Every two years, the Society conducts an international geography competition of competing teams from all over the world. The most recent was held in St. Petersburg, Russia, in July 2013, and had representatives from 19 national teams.
