Typically, it is either geographically distinct from the rest of the country or populated by a <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">national minority</a>. <a href="/wiki/Decentralization" title="Decentralization">Decentralization</a> of self-governing powers and functions to such divisions is a way for a national government to try to increase democratic participation or administrative efficiency or to defuse internal conflicts.  Countries that include autonomous areas may be <a href="/wiki/Federacy" title="Federacy">federacies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federations</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederations</a>.  Autonomous areas can be divided into <a href="/wiki/Territory_(country_subdivision)" class="mw-redirect" title="Territory (country subdivision)">territorial</a> autonomies, subregional territorial autonomies, and local autonomies.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autonomous_administrative_division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Overseas territories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<dl><dt>British Overseas Territories and Crown dependencies</dt></dl>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">British Overseas Territories</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependencies" title="Crown dependencies">Crown dependencies</a>, and <a href="/wiki/British_Islands" title="British Islands">British Islands</a></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> are self-governing <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependencies" title="Crown dependencies">Crown dependencies</a> which are not part of the United Kingdom; however, the UK is responsible for their defence and international affairs. <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> is a self-governing <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">overseas territory of the UK</a>. Most of the other 13 British Overseas Territories also have autonomy in internal affairs through local legislatures.\n</p>\n<dl><dt>New Zealand dependent territories</dt></dl>\n<p><a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> maintains nominal sovereignty over three <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands" title="Pacific Islands">Pacific Island</a> nations. The <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands" title="Cook Islands">Cook Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niue" title="Niue">Niue</a> are self-governing countries in <a href="/wiki/Freely_associated_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Freely associated state">free association</a> with New Zealand that maintain some international relationships in their own name. <a href="/wiki/Tokelau" title="Tokelau">Tokelau</a> remains an autonomous dependency of New Zealand. The <a href="/wiki/Chatham_Islands" title="Chatham Islands">Chatham Islands</a>\xe2\x80\x94despite having the designation of <i>Territory</i>\xe2\x80\x94is an integral part of the country, situated within the New Zealand archipelago. The territory\'s council is not autonomous and has broadly the same powers as other local councils, although notably it can also charge levies on goods entering or leaving the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<dl><dt>Danish constituent countries</dt></dl>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> are two autonomous countries within the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Denmark">Kingdom of Denmark</a>.\n</p>\n<dl><dt>Dutch constituent countries</dt></dl>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Cura\xc3\xa7ao">Cura\xc3\xa7ao</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a> are autonomous countries within the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, each with their own parliament. In addition they enjoy autonomy in taxation matters as well as having their own currencies.\n</p>\n<dl><dt>French overseas collectivities, New Caledonia, and Corsica</dt></dl>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivities" class="mw-redirect" title="Overseas collectivities">Overseas collectivities</a></div>\n<p>The French constitution recognises three autonomous jurisdictions. <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Region_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Region of France">region of France</a>, enjoys a greater degree of autonomy on matters such as tax and education compared to mainland regions. <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a>, a <i>sui generis</i> collectivity, and <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Overseas_collectivity" title="Overseas collectivity">overseas collectivity</a>, are highly autonomous territories with their own government, legislature, currency and constitution. They do not, however, have legislative powers for policy areas relating to law and order, defense, border control or university education. Other smaller overseas collectivities have a lesser degree of autonomy through local legislatures. The five overseas regions, <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guadaloupe" class="mw-redirect" title="Guadaloupe">Guadaloupe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">Martinique</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a> and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="R\xc3\xa9union">R\xc3\xa9union</a>, are generally governed the same as mainland regions; however, they enjoy some additional powers, including certain legislative powers for devolved areas.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethiopian_special_woredas">Ethiopian special woredas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Autonomous_administrative_division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ethiopian special woredas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, "special woredas" are a subgroup of <i><a href="/wiki/Woreda" class="mw-redirect" title="Woreda">woredas</a></i> (districts) that are organized around the traditional homelands of an <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Ethiopia" title="Demographics of Ethiopia">ethnic</a> minority, and are outside the usual hierarchy of a <i><a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Ethiopia" title="Regions of Ethiopia">kilil</a></i>, or region. These <i>woredas</i> have many similarities to autonomous areas in other countries.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_designated_for_indigenous_peoples">Areas designated for indigenous
