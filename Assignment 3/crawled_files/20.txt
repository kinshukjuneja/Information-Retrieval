colspan="2" style="text-align:center;background-color: #efefef">Demographics</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Population</th><td>\n0</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Norrard (Northern) Rocks</b> are a group of small uninhabited granite rocks in the north\xe2\x80\x93western part of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, to the west of <a href="/wiki/Bryher,_Isles_of_Scilly" class="mw-redirect" title="Bryher, Isles of Scilly">Bryher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Samson, Isles of Scilly">Samson</a>. In 1971 they were designated as a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI) for their breeding seabird colonies and they are permanently closed to landings from boat passengers. The vegetation on the islands is limited by the extreme exposure and only six species of flowering plants have been recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-Norrard_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norrard-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nature reserve">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The islands are largely managed as <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserves" class="mw-redirect" title="Nature reserves">nature reserves</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust" title="Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a>, principally for breeding seabirds and grey seals (<i><a href="/wiki/Halichoerus_grypus" class="mw-redirect" title="Halichoerus grypus">Halichoerus grypus</a></i>). The only breeding sites for European Storm\xe2\x80\x93petrel in England are on the Isles of Scilly with eleven colonies and an estimated 1475 occupied sites (i.e.  breeding pairs). There are only three small breeding colonies on the Norrard Rocks; Mincarlo, Illiswilgig and Castle Bryher with a total of 37 pairs.<sup id="cite_ref-Seabird2000_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seabird2000-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Other seabirds breeding within the SSSI are Fulmar (<i><a href="/wiki/Fulmarus_glacialis" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulmarus glacialis">Fulmarus glacialis</a></i>), Guillemot (<i><a href="/wiki/Uria_aalge" class="mw-redirect" title="Uria aalge">Uria aalge</a></i>), Lesser Black\xe2\x80\x93backed Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_fuscus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus fuscus">Larus fuscus</a></i>) and Herring Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_argentatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus argentatus">L argentatus</a></i>). The <a href="/wiki/Rove_beetle" title="Rove beetle">rove beetle</a> (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Omalium_allardi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Omalium allardi (page does not exist)">Omalium allardi</a></i>) has been recorded on several of the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-Norrard_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Norrard-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Individual_islands_and_rocks">Individual islands and rocks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Individual islands and rocks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The islands and rocks are listed below in decreasing order of area.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gweal">Gweal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gweal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Gwithial</b></i>, <i>place of trees</i>) (<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV866152_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV866152</a></span>) 5.82 hectares (14.4 acres) and 32 metres (105&#160;ft) high.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gweal,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Gweal, Isles of Scilly">Gweal</a> is a small rocky island consisting of two hills linked by a boulder beach, just off the west coast of Bryher and is an occasional pupping site for grey seal. Plants recorded are sea beet (<i><a href="/wiki/Sea_Beet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea Beet">Beta vulgaris subsp. maritima</a></i>), rock sea\xe2\x80\x93spurry (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spergularia_rupicola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Spergularia rupicola (page does not exist)">Spergularia rupicola</a></i>), common scurvygrass (<i><a href="/wiki/Cochlearia_officinalis" title="Cochlearia officinalis">Cochlearia officinalis</a></i>), thrift (<i><a href="/wiki/Armeria_maritima" title="Armeria maritima">Armeria maritima</a></i>) and tree mallow (<i><a href="/wiki/Lavatera_arborea" class="mw-redirect" title="Lavatera arborea">Lavatera arborea</a></i>).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scilly_Rock">Scilly Rock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Scilly Rock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV859156_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV859156</a></span>) 1.98 hectares (4.9 acres)\n</p><p>Scilly Rock is just over 1 kilometre (0.62&#160;mi) west of Bryher is a large rock, divided by a deep chasm into two parts with a maximum height of 22 metres (72&#160;ft). Each part has a summit named North Cuckoo and South Cuckoo. Landing is difficult and only possible in calm conditions. It is reputedly the island from which the rest of the archipelago gets its name although there is no evidence or explanation for this. Puffin (<i><a href="/wiki/Fratercula_arctica" class="mw-redirect" title="Fratercula arctica">Fratercula arctica</a></i>) breed and the only plants recorded are rock sea-spurrey and oraches (<i><a href="/wiki/Atriplex" title="Atriplex">Atriplex</a></i>) species.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-north_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-north-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mincarlo">Mincarlo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mincarlo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Men Karleyth</b></i>, <i>rayfish stone</i>) (<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV853129_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV853129</a></span>) 1.82 hectares (4.5 acres)\n</p><p>Mincarlo is the southernmost of the group 2 kilometres (1.2&#160;mi) west of Samson. Several species nest on the island including the largest breeding colony of Cormorant (<i><a href="/wiki/Phalacrocorax_carbo" class="mw-redirect" title="Phalacrocorax carbo">Phalacrocorax carbo</a></i>) in the Isles of Scilly as well as Puffin, Great Black\xe2\x80\x93backed Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_marinus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus marinus">Larus marinus</a></i>), Razorbill (<i><a href="/wiki/Alca_torda" class="mw-redirect" title="Alca torda">Alca torda</a></i>) and Common Shag (<i><a href="/wiki/Phalacrocorax_aristotelis" class="mw-redirect" title="Phalacrocorax aristotelis">Phalacrocorax aristotelis</a></i>). The breeding colony of European Storm\xe2\x80\x93petrel (<i><a href="/wiki/Hydrobates_pelagicus" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydrobates pelagicus">Hydrobates pelagicus</a></i>) occupied 17 sites during the Seabird 2000 survey. Tree mallow, orache, common scurveygrass, rock sea\xe2\x80\x93spurrey and sea beet have all been recorded. The rocks are a main pupping site for grey seal (<i><a href="/wiki/Halichoerus_grypus" class="mw-redirect" title="Halichoerus grypus">Halichoerus grypus</a></i>). The <a href="/wiki/Arachnology" title="Arachnology">Arachnologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/W._S._Bristowe" title="W. S. Bristowe">W S Bristow</a> visited the Isles of Scilly in 1928, 1929 and 1934 and landed on many of the uninhabited islands. On Mincarlo he recorded the following <a href="/wiki/Spiders" class="mw-redirect" title="Spiders">spiders</a>; <i>Trochosa terricola</i>, <i>Oedothorax fuscus</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lepthyphantes_tenuis" class="mw-redirect" title="Lepthyphantes tenuis">Lepthyphantes tenuis</a></i>, <i>Dismodicus bifrons</i>, <i>Xysticus kochi</i> and <i>Halorates reprobus</i> which is associated with the nests of seabirds, especially cormorant and shag.<sup id="cite_ref-Bristowe_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bristowe-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Illiswilgig">Illiswilgig</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Illiswilgig">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Enys Welsek</b></i>, <i>grassy island</i>) (<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV858139_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV858139</a></span>) 0.90 hectares (2.2 acres)\n</p><p>Several species have bred on Illiswilgig, including a small colony of only three pairs of European Storm\xe2\x80\x93petrel, recorded during the Seascape 2000 survey. Plants recorded are tree mallow, thrift, sea beet, rock sea\xe2\x80\x93spurrey, common scurvy grass, orache and English stonecrop (<i><a href="/wiki/Sedum_anglicum" title="Sedum anglicum">Sedum anglicum</a></i>). Illiswilgig is a main pupping and haul\xe2\x80\x93out site for grey seal.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maiden_Bower">Maiden Bower</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Maiden Bower">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Meyn-Meur</b></i>, <i>great stones</i>) (<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV850145_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV850145</a></span>) 0.66 hectares (1.6 acres)\n</p><p>Only a few gulls breed on Maiden Bower and no plants have been recorded.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Castle_Bryher">Castle Bryher</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Castle Bryher">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Kastel Breyer</b></i>, <i>Bryher\'s castle</i>)(<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV863140_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV863140</a></span>) 0.39 hectares (0.96 acres) and 26 metres (85&#160;ft)\n</p><p>The Seabird 2000 survey recorded 17 occupied sites of European Storm\xe2\x80\x93petrel. Puffin and gulls also breed on the island and plants recorded are tree mallow, sea beet, rock sea-spurrey, common scurvygrass and orache.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seal_Rock">Seal Rock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Seal Rock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>(<a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey_National_Grid" title="Ordnance Survey National Grid">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap" class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="https://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Norrard_Rocks&amp;params=SV854141_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SV854141</a></span>) 0.20 hectares (0.49 acres)\n</p><p>An important breeding and haul out site for grey seal and orache is the only plant recorded.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Norrard_Rocks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-Norrard-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-Norrard_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-Norrard_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.sssi.naturalengland.org.uk/citation/citation_photo/1001870.pdf">"Norrard Rocks"</a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>. <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a>. 17 December 1986<span class="reference-accessdate">.
