The <a href="/wiki/Lizard_Lighthouse" title="Lizard Lighthouse">Lizard Lighthouse</a> is situated at Lizard Point. Immediately below the lighthouse, situated in what used to be a hotel, is the <a href="/wiki/Youth_Hostels_Association_(England_%26_Wales)" title="Youth Hostels Association (England &amp; Wales)">YHA</a> Lizard <a href="/wiki/Youth_Hostel" class="mw-redirect" title="Youth Hostel">Youth Hostel</a>. Lizard Point is situated within <i><a href="/wiki/Caerthillian_to_Kennack" title="Caerthillian to Kennack">Caerthillian to Kennack</a> SSSI</i> (<a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>), noted for its <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">biological</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Cornwall" title="Geology of Cornwall">geological</a> interest.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Polpeor Cove is a small cove to the east of Lizard Point.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The area is famous for its carved <a href="/wiki/Serpentinite" title="Serpentinite">serpentine</a> items,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> which range from ornaments to the pump handles in the local <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">public house</a>, the Lizard Inn (The Top House).  The <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Lizard" class="mw-redirect" title="Geology of Lizard">geology of Lizard</a> can be interesting to some, with a number of planned walks available from local tourist authorities to discover more about the local rocks.\n</p><p>The first sighting of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Armada" title="Spanish Armada">Spanish Armada</a> on mainland <a href="/wiki/Early_Modern_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Early Modern Britain">Britain</a> was off Lizard Point at 3&#160;pm on 29 July 1588.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This was one of the greatest invasion fleets in history and consisted of 120 ships armed with over 1,000 cannon and with 29,000 men on board.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_at_the_Lizard" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle at the Lizard">Battle at the Lizard</a>, a naval battle, took place off The Lizard on 21 October 1707.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sinking_of_Ardgarry">Sinking of <i>Ardgarry</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lizard_Point,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sinking of Ardgarry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The bulk <a href="/wiki/Coastal_trading_vessel" title="Coastal trading vessel">coaster</a> carrier <a href="/w/index.php?title=MV_Ardgarry_(1957)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MV Ardgarry (1957) (page does not exist)">MV <i>Ardgarry</i></a> (1957) was lost in a heavy storm, in over 30&#160;ft (9m) high waves, off Lizard Point on 29 December 1962. All 12 crewmen perished and were never found.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She was built by <a href="/wiki/James_Lamont_%26_Co" title="James Lamont &amp; Co">James Lamont &amp; Co</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Port_Glasgow" title="Port Glasgow">Port Glasgow</a> shipyard, and was 221-feet long and weighed 1,074-tons gross.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Ardgarry</i> was carrying coal from <a href="/wiki/Swansea" title="Swansea">Swansea</a> and headed to <a href="/wiki/Rouen" title="Rouen">Rouen</a> in France.  Six of the crew were from Northern Ireland, five from Scotland, and one from Donegal in the Republic of Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Found again in 2006, the ship\'s bell was recovered, and a memorial service was held by family members in August 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> All of the crewmen\'s families were there except one, First Engineer William Shumacher, of Brougham Street, Greenock.  No trace of his family has been found.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bugaled_Breizh_sinking"><i>Bugaled Breizh</i> sinking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lizard_Point,_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bugaled Breizh sinking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 15 January 2004 the French fishing trawler <i><a href="/wiki/Bugaled_Breizh" title="Bugaled Breizh">Bugaled Breizh</a></i> (child of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>) sank off Lizard Point with the loss of five lives.  There were claims at the time by French marine accident experts that the vessel may have been pulled under when her nets became entangled in a British or Dutch submarine which was conducting <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> exercises in the area at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" In a strong gale and dense fog RNLI lifeboat volunteers rescued 456 passengers, including seventy babies.  Crews from <a href="/wiki/The_Lizard" title="The Lizard">The Lizard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cadgwith" title="Cadgwith">Cadgwith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coverack" title="Coverack">Coverack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Porthleven" title="Porthleven">Porthleven</a> rowed out repeatedly for sixteen hours to rescue all of the people on board.
