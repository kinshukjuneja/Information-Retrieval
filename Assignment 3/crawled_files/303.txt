/>/fields<wbr />/details</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Fields Medal</b> is a prize awarded to two, three, or four <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematicians</a> under 40 years of age at the <a href="/wiki/International_Congress_of_Mathematicians" title="International Congress of Mathematicians">International Congress</a> of the <a href="/wiki/International_Mathematical_Union" title="International Mathematical Union">International Mathematical Union</a> (IMU), a meeting that takes place every four years.\n</p><p>The Fields Medal is regarded as one of the highest honors a mathematician can receive, and has been described as the mathematician\'s "<a href="/wiki/List_of_prizes_known_as_the_Nobel_of_a_field" title="List of prizes known as the Nobel of a field">Nobel Prize</a>" <sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>, although there are several key differences, including frequency of award, number of awards, and age limits. According to the annual Academic Excellence Survey by <a href="/wiki/Academic_Ranking_of_World_Universities" title="Academic Ranking of World Universities">ARWU</a>, the Fields Medal is consistently regarded as the top award in the field of mathematics worldwide,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and in another reputation survey conducted by IREG in 2013-14, the Fields Medal came closely after the <a href="/wiki/Abel_Prize" title="Abel Prize">Abel Prize</a> as the second most prestigious international award in mathematics.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The prize comes with a monetary award which, since 2006, has been <span style="white-space: nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">CA$</a></span>15,000.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The name of the award is in honor of <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> mathematician <a href="/wiki/John_Charles_Fields" title="John Charles Fields">John Charles Fields</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fields_Institute_About_Us_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fields_Institute_About_Us-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Fields was instrumental in establishing the award, designing the medal itself, and funding the monetary component.<sup id="cite_ref-Fields_Institute_About_Us_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fields_Institute_About_Us-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The medal was first awarded in 1936 to Finnish mathematician <a href="/wiki/Lars_Ahlfors" title="Lars Ahlfors">Lars Ahlfors</a> and American mathematician <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Douglas" title="Jesse Douglas">Jesse Douglas</a>, and it has been awarded every four years since 1950. Its purpose is to give recognition and support to younger mathematical researchers who have made major contributions. In 2014, the Iranian mathematician <a href="/wiki/Maryam_Mirzakhani" title="Maryam Mirzakhani">Maryam Mirzakhani</a> became the first woman Fields Medalist.<sup id="cite_ref-Farsnews_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Farsnews-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  In all, sixty people have been awarded the Fields Medal.\n</p><p>The most recent group of Fields Medalists received their awards on 1 August 2018 at the opening ceremony of the IMU International Congress, held in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  The medal belonging to one of the four joint winners, Caucher Birkar, was stolen shortly after the event.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  The ICM presented Birkar with a replacement medal a few days later.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div id="Conditions_of_the_award">Conditions of the award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fields_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Conditions of the award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Fields Medal is often described as the "<a href="/wiki/List_of_prizes_known_as_the_Nobel_of_a_field" title="List of prizes known as the Nobel of a field">Nobel Prize of Mathematics</a>" and for a long time has been regarded as the most prestigious award in the field of mathematics.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Unlike the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a>, the Fields Medal is only awarded every four years.  The Fields Medal also has an age limit: a recipient must be under age 40 on 1 January of the year in which the medal is awarded. This is similar to restrictions applicable to the <a href="/wiki/Clark_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="Clark Medal">Clark Medal</a> in economics. The under-40 rule is based on Fields\' desire that "while it was in recognition of work already done, it was at the same time intended to be an encouragement for further achievement on the part of the recipients and a stimulus to renewed effort on the part of others."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  Moreover, an individual can only be awarded one Fields Medal; laureates are ineligible to be awarded future medals.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  This is in contrast with the Nobel Prize which can be, and has been awarded to an individual or an entity more than once, whether in the same category (<a href="/wiki/John_Bardeen" title="John Bardeen">John Bardeen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Sanger" title="Frederick Sanger">Frederick Sanger</a>), or in different categories (<a href="/wiki/Marie_Curie" title="Marie Curie">Marie Curie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linus_Pauling" title="Linus Pauling">Linus Pauling</a>).\n</p><p>The monetary award is much lower than the 8,000,000 <a href="/wiki/Swedish_kronor" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish kronor">Swedish kronor</a> (roughly 1,400,000 Canadian dollars)<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> given with each Nobel prize as of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Other major awards in mathematics, such as the <a href="/wiki/Abel_Prize" title="Abel Prize">Abel Prize</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chern_Medal" title="Chern Medal">Chern Medal</a>, have larger monetary prizes compared to the Fields Medal.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fields_medalists">Fields class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fields_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1954, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Serre" title="Jean-Pierre Serre">Jean-Pierre Serre</a> became the youngest winner of the Fields Medal, at 27. He still retains this distinction.\n</p><p>In 1966, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Grothendieck" title="Alexander Grothendieck">Alexander Grothendieck</a> boycotted the ICM, held in Moscow, to protest Soviet military actions taking place in Eastern Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Motchane" title="L\xc3\xa9on Motchane">L\xc3\xa9on Motchane</a>, founder and director of the <a href="/wiki/Institut_des_Hautes_%C3%89tudes_Scientifiques" title="Institut des Hautes \xc3\x89tudes Scientifiques">Institut des Hautes \xc3\x89tudes Scientifiques</a>, attended and accepted Grothendieck\'s Fields Medal on his behalf.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1970, <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Novikov_(mathematician)" title="Sergei Novikov (mathematician)">Sergei Novikov</a>, because of restrictions placed on him by the Soviet government, was unable to travel to the congress in <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a> to receive his medal.\n</p><p>In 1978, <a href="/wiki/Grigory_Margulis" title="Grigory Margulis">Grigory Margulis</a>, because of restrictions placed on him by the Soviet government, was unable to travel to the congress in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a> to receive his medal. The award was accepted on his behalf by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Tits" title="Jacques Tits">Jacques Tits</a>, who said in his address: "I cannot but express my deep disappointment\xe2\x80\x94no doubt shared by many people here\xe2\x80\x94in the absence of Margulis from this ceremony. In view of the symbolic meaning of this city of Helsinki, I had indeed grounds to hope that I would have a chance at last to meet a mathematician whom I know only through his work and for whom I have the greatest respect and admiration."<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1982, the congress was due to be held in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>  but had to be rescheduled to the next year, because of <a href="/wiki/Martial_law_in_Poland" title="Martial law in Poland">martial law</a> introduced in Poland on 13 December 1981. The awards were announced at the ninth General Assembly of the IMU earlier in the year and awarded at the 1983 Warsaw congress.\n</p><p>In 1990, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Witten" title="Edward Witten">Edward Witten</a> became the first  <a href="/wiki/Physicist" title="Physicist">physicist</a> to win the award.\n</p><p>In 1998, at the ICM, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Wiles" title="Andrew Wiles">Andrew Wiles</a> was presented by the chair of the Fields Medal Committee, <a href="/wiki/Yuri_I._Manin" class="mw-redirect" title="Yuri I. Manin">Yuri I. Manin</a>, with the first-ever IMU silver plaque in recognition of his proof of <a href="/wiki/Fermat%27s_Last_Theorem" title="Fermat&#39;s Last Theorem">Fermat\'s Last Theorem</a>. <a href="/wiki/Don_Zagier" title="Don Zagier">Don Zagier</a> referred to the plaque as a "quantized Fields Medal". Accounts of this award frequently make reference that at the time of the award Wiles was over the age limit for the Fields medal.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup>  Although Wiles was slightly over the age limit in 1994, he was thought to be a favorite to win the medal; however, a gap (later resolved by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Taylor_(mathematician)" title="Richard Taylor (mathematician)">Taylor</a> and Wiles) in the proof was found in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Grigori_Perelman" title="Grigori Perelman">Grigori Perelman</a>, who proved the <a href="/wiki/Poincar%C3%A9_conjecture" title="Poincar\xc3\xa9 conjecture">Poincar\xc3\xa9 conjecture</a>, refused his Fields Medal<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and did not attend the congress.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2014, <a href="/wiki/Maryam_Mirzakhani" title="Maryam Mirzakhani">Maryam Mirzakhani</a> became the first woman as well as the first Iranian, <a href="/wiki/Artur_Avila" title="Artur Avila">Artur Avila</a> the first <a href="/wiki/South_American" class="mw-redirect" title="South American">South American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manjul_Bhargava" title="Manjul Bhargava">Manjul Bhargava</a> the first person of <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">Indian</a> origins to win the Fields Medal.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal">Medal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fields_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Medal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FieldsMedalBack.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/FieldsMedalBack.jpg/220px-FieldsMedalBack.jpg" width="220" height="217" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/FieldsMedalBack.jpg/330px-FieldsMedalBack.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/FieldsMedalBack.jpg/440px-FieldsMedalBack.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2563" data-file-height="2524" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FieldsMedalBack.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The reverse of the Fields Medal</div></div></div>\n<p>The medal was designed by Canadian sculptor <a href="/wiki/R._Tait_McKenzie" title="R. Tait McKenzie">R. Tait McKenzie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>On the obverse is <a href="/wiki/Archimedes" title="Archimedes">Archimedes</a> and a quote attributed to him which reads in Latin: "Transire suum pectus mundoque potiri" ("Rise above oneself and grasp the world").  The date is written in Roman numerals and contains an error ("MCNXXXIII" rather than "MCMXXXIII").<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup> In capital Greek letters the word \xce\x91\xce\xa1XIMH\xce\x94\xce\x9f\xce\xa5\xce\xa3, or "of Archimedes".</li>\n<li>On the reverse is the inscription (in Latin):</li></ul>\n<dl><dd><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">CONGREGATI</i></dd>\n<dd><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">EX TOTO ORBE</i></dd>\n<dd><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">MATHEMATICI</i></dd>\n<dd><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">OB SCRIPTA INSIGNIA</i></dd>\n<dd><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">TRIBUERE</i></dd></dl>\n<p>Translation: "Mathematicians gathered from the entire world have awarded [understood but not written: \'this prize\'] for outstanding writings."\n</p><p>In the background, there is the representation of Archimedes\' <a href="/wiki/Archimedes#Biography" title="Archimedes">tomb</a>, with the carving illustrating his theorem <a href="/wiki/On_the_Sphere_and_Cylinder" title="On the Sphere and Cylinder">On the Sphere and Cylinder</a>,  behind a branch. (This is the mathematical result of which Archimedes was reportedly most proud: Given a sphere and a circumscribed cylinder of the same height and diameter, the ratio between their volumes is equal to \xe2\x85\x94.)\n</p><p>The rim bears the name of the prizewinner.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Female_recipients">Female recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fields_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Female recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In terms of the most prestigious awards in <a href="/wiki/Science,_technology,_engineering,_and_mathematics" title="Science, technology, engineering, and mathematics">STEM</a> fields, only a small proportion have been awarded to women. The Fields Medal has been obtained only one time by a woman, <a href="/wiki/Maryam_Mirzakhani" title="Maryam Mirzakhani">Maryam Mirzakhani</a>, in 2014, out of a total of (currently) 60 medalists.<sup
