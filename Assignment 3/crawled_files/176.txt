class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Talks of expansion began on August 8, 1985, when the players and the owners agreed to a new <a href="/wiki/Collective_bargaining" title="Collective bargaining">collective bargaining agreement</a> (CBA). The basic agreement allowed the National League to expand by two members to match the American League. Details of expansion were hammered out in the 1990 CBA.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ten cities were considered serious candidates for the two spots: <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>; <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>; <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>; <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>; <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>; <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>, Florida; <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>; <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>; <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa</a>-<a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg</a>, Florida; and <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-lakeland_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lakeland-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tropicana_Field" title="Tropicana Field">Florida Suncoast Dome</a> was built in St. Petersburg specifically to lure an existing or expansion MLB franchise.<sup id="cite_ref-chass_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chass-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> expansion committee consisted of <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> Chairman Doug Danfort, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> president <a href="/wiki/Fred_Wilpon" title="Fred Wilpon">Fred Wilpon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> owner <a href="/wiki/John_McMullen_(engineer)" title="John McMullen (engineer)">John McMullen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lakeland_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lakeland-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On December 18, 1990, the committee eliminated Charlotte, Nashville, Phoenix and Sacramento from consideration to cut the list down to six finalists.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> With three sites from <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> remaining in contention it became apparent that one would win out while the other spot would go to a non-Florida team.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Denver.2C_Colorado"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Denver,_Colorado">Denver, Colorado</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Denver, Colorado">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>After previous failed attempts to bring the Major League Baseball to Colorado (most notably the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> nearly relocating to Denver following the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_drug_trials" title="Pittsburgh drug trials">Pittsburgh drug trials</a> in 1985), by the late 1980s a team seemed to be a possibility in Denver. The Colorado Baseball Commission, led by banking executive Larry Varnell, was successful in getting Denver voters to approve a 0.1% sales tax to help finance a new baseball stadium. Also, an advisory committee was formed in 1990 by then-Governor of Colorado <a href="/wiki/Roy_Romer" title="Roy Romer">Roy Romer</a> to recruit an ownership group. The group selected was led by John Antonucci, an Ohio beverage distributor, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_I._Monus" title="Michael I. Monus">Michael I. Monus</a>, the head of the <a href="/wiki/Phar-Mor" title="Phar-Mor">Phar-Mor</a> drugstore chain. Local and regional companies\xe2\x80\x94such as Erie Lake, Hensel Phelps Construction, <a href="/wiki/KOA_(AM)" title="KOA (AM)">KOA Radio</a>, and the <i><a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_News" title="Rocky Mountain News">Rocky Mountain News</a></i>\xe2\x80\x94rounded out the group.\n</p><p>Eugene Orza, associate general counsel of the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Players_Association" title="Major League Baseball Players Association">Major League Baseball Players Association</a>, stated that he expected Denver would receive one of the expansion franchises.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Miami.2C_Florida"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Miami,_Florida">Miami, Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Miami, Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">U.S. Senator</a> <a href="/wiki/Connie_Mack_III" title="Connie Mack III">Connie Mack III</a> from <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_Senators_from_Florida" title="List of United States Senators from Florida">Florida</a>, the grandson of baseball great <a href="/wiki/Connie_Mack" title="Connie Mack">Connie Mack</a> and a member of the Senate Task Force on Major League Baseball, pushed <a href="/wiki/Commissioner_of_Baseball" title="Commissioner of Baseball">Baseball Commissioner</a> <a href="/wiki/Fay_Vincent" title="Fay Vincent">Fay Vincent</a> to expand to Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Finalization_of_expansion_plans">Finalization of expansion plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Finalization of expansion plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In June 1991, the expansion committee accepted the bids of the Miami and Denver groups to debut in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-chass_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chass-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Expansion was approved unanimously by all teams in July 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-chassjuly6_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chassjuly6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Denver group chose to call their franchise the <b>Colorado Rockies</b>, the same name used as the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies_(NHL)" title="Colorado Rockies (NHL)">National Hockey League franchise</a> that played in Denver from 1976 to 1982. The Miami group chose to call themselves the <b>Florida Marlins</b> to broaden their fanbase to the entire state,<sup id="cite_ref-chassjuly6_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chassjuly6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while reviving the nickname of a <a href="/wiki/Miami_Marlins_(IL)" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami Marlins (IL)">defunct minor league team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Expansion_draft" title="Expansion draft">expansion draft</a> to stock both franchises was set for November 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-chassjuly6_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chassjuly6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Though previous expansions allowed the drafting of players from the same league only, Vincent declared that the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> would receive <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>42 million of the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a>\'s $190 million in expansion revenue so that the AL would provide players in the National League expansion draft.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In an attempt to win support in the American League and balance the vote, Vincent decreed that the AL owners were entitled to 22% of the $190 million take. This decision marked the first time in expansion history that leagues were required to share expansion revenue or provide players for another league\'s expansion draft.  Vincent said the owners expanded to raise money to pay their <a href="/wiki/Baseball_collusion" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball collusion">collusion</a> debt.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Tampa Bay Baseball Group sued MLB for allegedly reneging on an agreement to grant an expansion team to Tampa. The suit was settled in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/1992_Major_League_Baseball_expansion_draft" title="1992 Major League Baseball expansion draft">1992 Major League Baseball expansion draft</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_expansion" title="1998 Major League Baseball expansion">1998 Major League Baseball expansion</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Major_League_Baseball_expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
