secondary education for children from 3\xe2\x80\x9316 at five sites across the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sites">Sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Islands_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TrescoBryherSchool.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tresco and Bryher School, Tresco</div></div></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:StAgnesSchool.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" (up to <a href="/wiki/GCSE" class="mw-redirect" title="GCSE">GCSE</a>) is provided only on the most populous island of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>. There, it was based at the Carn Thomas site, on the eastern edge of <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Town" title="Hugh Town">Hugh Town</a>, on the road to Porth Mellon, but in 2011 relocated to a new school complex at Carn Gwaval, adjacent to an existing primary base there, between Hugh Town and <a href="/wiki/Old_Town,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Old Town, Isles of Scilly">Old Town</a>. Secondary pupils from the other islands are given free board at Mundesley Hostel during the school week, returning home at the weekends and holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The four <a href="/wiki/Primary_education" title="Primary education">primary education</a> bases are at Carn Gwaval, near Old Town \xe2\x80\x94 for St Mary\'s pupils (combined since 2011 with the sole secondary education base); on <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Old_Grimsby" title="Old Grimsby">Old Grimsby</a> \xe2\x80\x94 for pupils from that island and the neighbouring island of <a href="/wiki/Bryher" title="Bryher">Bryher</a>; on <a href="/wiki/St_Martin%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Martin&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Martin\'s</a>; and on <a href="/wiki/St_Agnes,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Agnes, Isles of Scilly">St Agnes</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Islands_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School,_St._Mary%27s.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School%2C_St._Mary%27s.jpg/220px-Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School%2C_St._Mary%27s.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School%2C_St._Mary%27s.jpg/330px-Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School%2C_St._Mary%27s.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School%2C_St._Mary%27s.jpg/440px-Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School%2C_St._Mary%27s.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carn_Thomas_Secondary_School,_St._Mary%27s.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The former Carn Thomas Secondary School site, St Mary\'s</div></div></div>\n<p>The secondary school and four primary schools were formerly five separate schools. While the smaller schools were praised by inspectors, the two larger schools on St Mary\'s were judged as inadequate. In response, the government forced a fresh start as a federated school, which opened in April 2002 and subsequently the other off islands bases were also federated.\n</p><p>An inspection in June and July 2003 again found the standard of education at the St Mary\'s sites to be inadequate, and the school was placed in <a href="/wiki/Special_measures" title="Special measures">special measures</a> until 2005.\n</p><p>Under a succession of heads, the school has become a successful and cohesive federation, judged "outstanding" by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_Standards_in_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Office for Standards in Education">Office for Standards in Education</a> (Ofsted) in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\nThe school\'s GCSE results are now the highest in South West England.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2008, the school was given funding of \xc2\xa313 million to build a new 3\xe2\x80\x9316 school complex on a site adjacent to the existing Carn Gwaval primary school.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This development took place 2010\xe2\x80\x9311 and opened in time for the 2011\xe2\x80\x9312 school year, which resulted in the secondary school moving permanently away from the Carn Thomas site, as well as the primary pupils at Carn Gwaval also moving into the new neighbouring school complex. The former primary school building was modernised and extended, becoming the island\'s main indoor sports and fitness centre.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Five_Islands_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.scilly.gov.uk/education/schools/school.htm">"The Five Islands School"</a>.
