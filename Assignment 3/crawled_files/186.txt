scope="row">Population</th><td>\n0</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>White Island</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Ar Nor</b></i>, <i>facing the mainland</i>) is one of the larger unpopulated islands of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and lies off the coast of the northernmost populated island of the group, <a href="/wiki/St_Martin%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Martin&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Martin\'s</a>, to which it is joined by a <a href="/wiki/Tide" title="Tide">tidal</a> <a href="/wiki/Causeway" title="Causeway">causeway</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a>. The island is designated as a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI) and a <a href="/wiki/Geological_Conservation_Review" title="Geological Conservation Review">Geological Conservation Review</a> site and is managed by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust" title="Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a> on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-white_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-white-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Access to the island can be dangerous when the rocky causeway is covered by the sea, as there are strong currents across it.<sup id="cite_ref-mega_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mega-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>This White Island should not be confused with a much smaller island of the same name, which lies off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Samson, Isles of Scilly">Samson</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_Island,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The uninhabited island lies off the north coast of St Martin\'s. It is about 15.25 hectares (37.7 acres)<sup id="cite_ref-parslow_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parslow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and until comparatively recently was part of St Martin\xe2\x80\x99s.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1814 the area of the island was estimated as fifty <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At the north-east end of the island a fragment of altered <a href="/wiki/Killas" title="Killas">killas</a>, which at one time covered a much wider area, could be seen in 1911.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>In common with the larger island, the place names are mostly English with the exception of Camper on the south-east coast (meaning tide race or roost in <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>) and Porthmoren, a place on the west of the shingle and boulder bar that separates the two islands. In Cornish <i>moren</i> is a girl, or maiden, and <i>porth</i> is a landing place. The north-west of the island rises to a height of 21 metres (69&#160;ft) and is topped by a ruined <a href="/wiki/Entrance_grave" title="Entrance grave">entrance grave</a>. There are also other <a href="/wiki/Ancient_monument" title="Ancient monument">ancient monuments</a>, including a <a href="/wiki/Chambered_cairn" title="Chambered cairn">chambered cairn</a> and several other <a href="/wiki/Cairn" title="Cairn">cairns</a>, To the south, and sheltered by the hill, are six small mounds or cairns.<sup id="cite_ref-mega_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mega-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Two walls indicating a bank and ditch <a href="/wiki/Field_system" title="Field system">field system</a> are also present.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> An examination of one cairn in 1975 showed that it was about 3 metres (9.8&#160;ft) across, possibly double walled on the north side and probably too small to be a <a href="/wiki/Hut_circle" title="Hut circle">hut circle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thomas_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thomas-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Only part of the island is scheduled as an Ancient Monument, but the county archaeological unit has recommended that the whole island should be scheduled.<sup id="cite_ref-ratcliffe_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ratcliffe-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_history">Natural history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_Island,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Natural history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>White Island is designated as a SSSI because of the waved maritime heath, maritime grassland, breeding seabirds and for the sequence of <a href="/wiki/Late_Pleistocene" title="Late Pleistocene">Late Pleistocene</a> deposits in the cleft of Chad Girt which almost cuts the island in two.<sup id="cite_ref-parslow_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parslow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The sequence of <a href="/wiki/Quaternary" title="Quaternary">Quaternary</a> deposits is as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-white_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-white-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>a <a href="/wiki/Raised_beach" title="Raised beach">raised beach</a></li>\n<li>granitic <a href="/wiki/Head" title="Head">head</a> known as Porthloo <a href="/wiki/Breccia" title="Breccia">Breccia</a>; named after the site on St Mary\xe2\x80\x99s</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Solifluction" title="Solifluction">soliflucted</a> gravel known as Hell Bay Gravel (Bryher) which consists of <a href="/wiki/Clasts" class="mw-redirect" title="Clasts">clasts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loess" title="Loess">loess</a> from glacial material in the Irish Sea</li>\n<li>head with <a href="/wiki/Glacial_erratic" title="Glacial erratic">erratics</a> named after the nearby site on St Martin\xe2\x80\x99s (Bread and Cheese Breccia).</li></ul>\n<p>Because it lies on the northern edge of the archipelago, the island is particularly exposed to high winds and salt spray. Consequently, the thin <a href="/wiki/Skeletal_soil" class="mw-redirect" title="Skeletal soil">skeletal soil</a> is covered in wind-blown maritime heath made up principally of western gorse (<i><a href="/wiki/Ulex_gallii" title="Ulex gallii">Ulex gallii</a></i>), bell heather (<i><a href="/wiki/Erica_cinerea" title="Erica cinerea">Erica cinerea</a></i>) and heather (<i><a href="/wiki/Calluna_vulgaris" class="mw-redirect" title="Calluna vulgaris">Calluna vulgaris</a></i>). English stonecrop (<i><a href="/wiki/Sedum_anglicum" title="Sedum anglicum">Sedum anglicum</a></i>), bird\'s-foot trefoil (<i><a href="/wiki/Lotus_corniculatus" title="Lotus corniculatus">Lotus corniculatus</a></i>) and heath bedstraw (<i><a href="/wiki/Galium_saxatile" title="Galium saxatile">Galium saxatile</a></i>) can be found growing among the heath and gorse. On the deeper soils, bracken (<i><a href="/wiki/Pteridium_aquilinum" title="Pteridium aquilinum">Pteridium aquilinum</a></i>) dominates, with bramble (<i><a href="/wiki/Rubus_fruticosus" title="Rubus fruticosus">Rubus fruticosus</a></i>) and honeysuckle (<i><a href="/wiki/Lonicera_periclymenum" title="Lonicera periclymenum">Lonicera periclymenum</a></i>). Along the western coast of the island is a small area of maritime grassland with the usual Isles of Scilly species of red fescue (<i><a href="/wiki/Festuca_rubra" title="Festuca rubra">Festuca rubra</a></i>), thrift (<i><a href="/wiki/Armeria_maritima" title="Armeria maritima">Armeria maritima</a></i>), common scurvygrass (<i><a href="/wiki/Cochlearia_officinalis" title="Cochlearia officinalis">Cochlearia officinalis</a></i>), buck\xe2\x80\x99s-horn plantain (<i><a href="/wiki/Plantago_coronopus" title="Plantago coronopus">Plantago coronopus</a></i>) and sea beet (<i><a href="/wiki/Beta_maritima" class="mw-redirect" title="Beta maritima">Beta maritima</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-white_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-white-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In April 2001 the first confirmed Scillonian record (since 1967) of the RDB gilt-edged lichen (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pseudocyphellaria_aurata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pseudocyphellaria aurata (page does not exist)">Pseudocyphellaria aurata</a></i>) was found on White Island. A widespread and frequent species in the tropics, often found in <a href="/wiki/Macaronesia" title="Macaronesia">Macaronesia</a> but rare on mainland Europe, this is a UK Biodiversity Action Plan Priority species and is protected under schedule 8 of the <a href="/wiki/Wildlife_and_Countryside_Act_1981" title="Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981">Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-parslow_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parslow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding_birds">Breeding birds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_Island,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breeding birds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Four species of <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a> and one species of <a href="/wiki/Petrel" title="Petrel">petrel</a> breed on White Island. They are Great Black-backed Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_marinus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus marinus">Larus marinus</a></i>), Lesser Black-backed Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_fuscus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus fuscus">L fuscus</a></i>), Herring Gull (<i><a href="/wiki/Larus_argentatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Larus argentatus">L argentatus</a></i>), Kittiwake (<i><a href="/wiki/Rissa_tridactyla" class="mw-redirect" title="Rissa tridactyla">Rissa tridactyla</a></i>) and the Fulmar (<i><a href="/wiki/Fulmarus_glacialis" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulmarus glacialis">Fulmarus glacialis</a></i>).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
