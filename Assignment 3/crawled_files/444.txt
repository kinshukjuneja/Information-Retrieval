/>/airbus-in-uk<wbr />/</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Airbus UK</b> is a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Airbus" title="Airbus">Airbus</a> which produces wings for the Airbus aircraft family. When Airbus was incorporated as a <a href="/wiki/Joint-stock_company" title="Joint-stock company">joint-stock company</a> in 2001 <a href="/wiki/BAE_Systems" title="BAE Systems">BAE Systems</a> transferred its UK Airbus facilities in return for a 20% share of the new company. These facilities became Airbus UK. Airbus UK has two main sites responsible for the design and manufacture of the high-technology wings for all Airbus models as well as overall design and supply of the fuel system.  For most Airbus models the company is responsible for overall design and supply of landing gear.  The company employs around 13,000 people at two sites: <a href="/wiki/Filton_Airfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Filton Airfield">Filton</a>, where the engineering and design activity takes place along with some manufacturing, and <a href="/wiki/Broughton,_Flintshire" title="Broughton, Flintshire">Broughton</a> where other major wing component manufacturing and all wing assembly takes place.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airbus_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg/250px-Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg" width="250" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg/375px-Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg/500px-Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Filton.bae.hangars.arp.750pix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Airbus UK (Filton)</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Hawker_Siddeley" title="Hawker Siddeley">Hawker Siddeley</a> (which merged with <a href="/wiki/British_Aircraft_Corporation" title="British Aircraft Corporation">British Aircraft Corporation</a> (BAC) in 1977 to form <a href="/wiki/British_Aerospace" title="British Aerospace">British Aerospace</a>) was part of the first Airbus project, the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A300" title="Airbus A300">Airbus A300</a>. The British government withdrew support in 1969 but Hawker Siddeley was allowed to continue as supplier of the aircraft\'s wings due to the advanced stages of design and the reluctance of other nations to take over the wing design. In 1979 BAe rejoined the Airbus consortium. In 2001 Airbus Industrie became Airbus S.A.S., the Airbus Integrated Company.\n</p><p>Airbus UK started work on the wings for the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">Airbus A380</a> in August 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2006 BAE Systems announced its intention to sell its share of Airbus SAS to EADS.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_bae_20060406_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_bae_20060406-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> BAE originally sought to agree a price with EADS through an informal process. However, due to the slow pace of negotiations and disagreements over price, BAE exercised its <a href="/wiki/Put_option" title="Put option">put option</a> which saw investment bank <a href="/wiki/N_M_Rothschild_%26_Sons" title="N M Rothschild &amp; Sons">Rothschild</a> appointed to give an independent valuation.\n</p><p>On 2 July 2006 Rothschild valued BAE\'s stake at \xc2\xa31.9&#160;billion (\xe2\x82\xac2.75&#160;billion); well below the expectation of BAE, analysts and even EADS.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 5 July 2006 BAE appointed independent auditors to study why the value of its share of Airbus had fallen from the original estimates to the Rothschild valuation. On 6 September 2006 BAE agreed to sell its stake in Airbus to EADS for \xc2\xa31.87&#160;billion (\xe2\x82\xac2.75&#160;billion, $3.53&#160;billion), pending BAE shareholder approval.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 4 October shareholders voted in favour of the sale.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sites">Sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airbus_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Airbus wing design and production was assigned to UK largely because of the advanced wing design of <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Siddeley_Trident" title="Hawker Siddeley Trident">Hawker Siddeley Trident</a> (D.H.121), designed by De Havilland. In the early days of Airbus, De Havilland design teams based in <a href="/wiki/Hatfield_Aerodrome" title="Hatfield Aerodrome">Hatfield</a> worked on Airbus wing design and wing assembly was carried out in Broughton (a De Havilland manufacturing site). After <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Siddeley" title="Hawker Siddeley">Hawker Siddeley</a> (the parent company of De Havilland) had <a href="/wiki/Hatfield_Aerodrome#1960s_and_70s" title="Hatfield Aerodrome">become part of</a> <a href="/wiki/British_Aerospace" title="British Aerospace">British Aerospace</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hatfield_Aerodrome#1980s_and_90s" title="Hatfield Aerodrome">Hatfield site was closed</a>. From the 1990s onwards, Airbus design work has been carried out at the Filton site, which was originally the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Aeroplane_Company" title="Bristol Aeroplane Company">Bristol Aeroplane Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filton">Filton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airbus_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Filton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Filton_Aerodrome" class="mw-redirect" title="Filton Aerodrome">Filton site</a> is located on the former <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Aeroplane_Company" title="Bristol Aeroplane Company">Bristol Aeroplane Company</a> site, which was later used as the final assembly line for the British-built <a href="/wiki/Concorde" title="Concorde">Concorde</a> aircraft (the French-built Concordes were assembled in Toulouse, which is also now an Airbus site).\n</p><p>Airbus Filton employs over 4,500 people in a variety of roles. The site is responsible for the design of the wing structure, fuel systems and landing gear integration. Some manufacturing also takes place in Filton, including the wing assembly for the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A400M" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A400M">A400M</a>. Aerodynamics work, research and testing is also carried out.<sup id="cite_ref-Airbus_in_the_UK_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Airbus_in_the_UK-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, Airbus sold most of the component manufacturing activities on the Filton site to <a href="/wiki/GKN" title="GKN">GKN</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> which continues use these facilities to manufacture Airbus parts as a subcontractor. In 2011 Airbus announced the construction of a new office complex, referred to as the "Aerospace Park", at the Filton site.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broughton">Broughton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Airbus_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Broughton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The current <a href="/wiki/Airbus_Broughton" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus Broughton">Airbus Broughton</a> site was founded in 1939 as a <a href="/wiki/British_shadow_factories" title="British shadow factories">shadow factory</a> for the production of the <a href="/wiki/Vickers_Wellington" title="Vickers Wellington">Vickers Wellington</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Avro_Lancaster" title="Avro Lancaster">Avro Lancaster</a>. After the War <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland" title="De Havilland">De Havilland</a> took over the factory and it was used to produce various aircraft, including the <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Mosquito" title="De Havilland Mosquito">Mosquito</a> and the <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Comet" title="De Havilland Comet">Comet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Today Airbus Broughton employs over 6,500 people, mostly in manufacturing roles. The site is responsible for the wing assembly for all Airbus aircraft, with the exception of the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A320">A320s</a> (these wings are assembled in China) and the A400M (assembled in Filton). Airbus wings are transported by <a href="/wiki/Airbus_Beluga" title="Airbus Beluga">Airbus Beluga</a> or ship (in the case of the <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A380" title="Airbus A380">A380</a>) to the final assembly lines at <a href="/wiki/Airbus_Toulouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus Toulouse">Airbus Toulouse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Airbus_in_the_UK_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Airbus_in_the_UK-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft">Aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
