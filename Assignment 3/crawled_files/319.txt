specialising in politics, economics and current affairs. Topics include British, European, and US politics, social issues, art, literature, cinema, science, the media, history, philosophy, and psychology. It features a mixture of lengthy analytic articles, first-person reportage, one-page columns, and shorter, quirky items.\n</p><p>The magazine was launched in October 1995 by <a href="/wiki/David_Goodhart" title="David Goodhart">David Goodhart</a>, then a senior correspondent for the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i>, and chairman Derek Coombs. Goodhart came up with the idea of producing an essay-based monthly general interest magazine\xe2\x80\x94a form then unknown in Britain\xe2\x80\x94while covering German reunification as Bonn correspondent for the <i>FT</i>.\n</p><p>Contributors  include <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Shriver" title="Lionel Shriver">Lionel Shriver</a>, <a href="/wiki/A._C._Grayling" title="A. C. Grayling">A. C. Grayling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_ElBaradei" title="Mohamed ElBaradei">Mohamed ElBaradei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Lind" title="Michael Lind">Michael Lind</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ignatieff" title="Michael Ignatieff">Michael Ignatieff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Dyer" title="Geoff Dyer">Geoff Dyer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francis_Fukuyama" title="Francis Fukuyama">Francis Fukuyama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Scruton" title="Roger Scruton">Roger Scruton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Marr" title="Andrew Marr">Andrew Marr</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Kay_(economist)" title="John Kay (economist)">John Kay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J._M._Coetzee" title="J. M. Coetzee">J. M. Coetzee</a>. Notable features of the magazine include head-to-head debates between two writers with opposing views on a subject; roundtable discussions, in which a series of experts with varying views on a given topic meet for a discussion, an edited transcript of which is published in the magazine; and interviews with major political and cultural figures (examples include <a href="/wiki/Orhan_Pamuk" title="Orhan Pamuk">Orhan Pamuk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wolfowitz" title="Paul Wolfowitz">Paul Wolfowitz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Mantel" title="Hilary Mantel">Hilary Mantel</a>). \n</p><p><i>Prospect</i> received worldwide attention in October 2005 when it published its list of the world\'s top 100 public intellectuals, which included <a href="/wiki/Noam_Chomsky" title="Noam Chomsky">Noam Chomsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Umberto_Eco" title="Umberto Eco">Umberto Eco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dawkins" title="Richard Dawkins">Richard Dawkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Pinker" title="Steven Pinker">Steven Pinker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hitchens" title="Christopher Hitchens">Christopher Hitchens</a>. The magazine asked readers to vote for their top intellectual from the longlist; Chomsky was the eventual winner. Subsequent lists have continued to attract attention. Dawkins claimed the top spot in 2013.\n</p><p><i>Prospect</i> has also attempted to revitalise the art of the <a href="/wiki/Short_story" title="Short story">short story</a> in Britain, by publishing the winning story of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Literature" title="Royal Society of Literature">Royal Society of Literature</a>\'s V. S. Pritchett Memorial Prize since 2009.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Policy_positions"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Policy positions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#The_Think_Tank_Awards"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">The Think Tank Awards</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_positions">Policy positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prospect_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Policy positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The magazine prizes independence over <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">ideology</a> and its articles and authors span the political spectrum. It tends to avoid a "line" on specific policy issues. Perhaps its strongest leaning is "<a href="/wiki/Contrarian" title="Contrarian">contrarian</a>"\xe2\x80\x94it devotes much space to articles debunking the "popular wisdom", on topics ranging from Japan\'s alleged economic crisis to the <a href="/wiki/Mahdi_army" class="mw-redirect" title="Mahdi army">Mahdi army</a> in Iraq.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (January 2012)">original research?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> However, it has been described as left-leaning by the BBC,<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the <i>Prospect</i> contributor <a href="/wiki/Roger_Scruton" title="Roger Scruton">Roger Scruton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-spectator_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spectator-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2009 in a roundtable interview in <i>Prospect</i>, <a href="/wiki/Adair_Turner" class="mw-redirect" title="Adair Turner">Adair Turner</a> supported the idea of new global taxes on financial transactions, warning that a "swollen" financial sector paying excessive salaries has grown too big for society. Turner\'s suggestion that a "<a href="/wiki/Tobin_tax" title="Tobin tax">Tobin tax</a>"\xe2\x80\x94named after the economist <a href="/wiki/James_Tobin" title="James Tobin">James Tobin</a>\xe2\x80\x94should be considered for financial transactions drew international attention.\n</p><p>In recent years<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (April 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the magazine\'s founding editor, <a href="/wiki/David_Goodhart" title="David Goodhart">David Goodhart</a>, has stirred controversy with a series of articles arguing that the increasing diversity of the United Kingdom may weaken the bonds of solidarity on which the welfare state depends. The debate fed into the broader discussions of "<a href="/wiki/Britishness" title="Britishness">Britishness</a>" that have become increasingly common in the public sphere.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Think_Tank_Awards">The Think Tank Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prospect_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Think Tank Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><i>Prospect</i> holds the annual <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120626180350/http://www.thinktankawards.com/">Think Tank Awards</a>, which celebrate and reward the work of <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tanks</a> on a national and global scale. The awards are supported by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Shell</a>. Categories include \'Global Think Tank of the Year\', \'Publication of the Year\', \'North American Think Tank of the Year\', \'European Think Tank of the Year\' (excluding Britain), \'UK Think Tank of the Year\', and many sub-categories for the UK.\nAccording to the official website, "The awards are judged by a cross-party panel looking for evidence of influence on public policy and on the public discourse. The judges will also consider the quality of research and potential of younger and smaller organisations."\nThe awards have been running since 2001, and have been expanding exponentially to include more global awards for international Think Tanks. The winner of the most recent 2017 Think Tank awards (held at the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Directors" title="Institute of Directors">Institute of Directors</a> on 10 July) was the <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Rowntree_Foundation" title="Joseph Rowntree Foundation">Joseph Rowntree Foundation</a>, based in the UK, for its "very strong analytical appraisal of social conditions in Britain".\n</p><p>Previous winners:\n</p><p><b>2017<sup id="cite_ref-prospectmagazine_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prospectmagazine-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></b>\nNorth America Economic
