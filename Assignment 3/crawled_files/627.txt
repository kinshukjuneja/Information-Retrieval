The vowels below are named by the <a href="/wiki/Lexical_set" title="Lexical set">lexical set</a> they belong to:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/KIT_lexical_set" title="KIT lexical set">Kit vowel</a>: The same as in American English, the default <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\xaa]</span>.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/DRESS_lexical_set" title="DRESS lexical set">Dress vowel</a>: The vowel is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x9b]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Trap vowel: This vowel is mostly <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[a]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc3\xa6]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Lot vowel: As mostly of the US, this vowel is usually <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x91]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Strut vowel: It is the same as in the US English, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xca\x8c]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Foot vowel: It is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xca\x8a]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Fleece vowel: It is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[i]</span> or a diphthong <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\xaai]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Face <a href="/wiki/Diphthong" title="Diphthong">diphthong</a>: It is generally <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[e\xc9\xaa]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x9b\xc9\xaa]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Palm vowel: It is mostly <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x91]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Thought vowel: The vowel is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x94]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Goat diphthong: It is generally <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\xb5\xca\x8a]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[o\xca\x8a]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Near diphthong: It is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[e\xc9\x99]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[i\xc9\x99]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Square diphthong: It is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[e\xc9\x99]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Start vowel: It is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x91\xcb\x90]</span>.</li>\n<li>The North diphthong: usually <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x94\xc9\x99]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Force diphthong: usually <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[o\xc9\x99]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Cure diphthong: usually <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[u\xc9\x99]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Bath vowel: This vowel is mostly <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[a]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc3\xa6]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Cloth vowel: It is mostly <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x94]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Nurse vowel: It varies among <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x99]</span>, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x9c]</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x9ci]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Goose vowel: It is mostly <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xca\x89\xcb\x90]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Price/Prize Dithphong: It\'s generally <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x91\xc9\xaa]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Choice diphthong: It is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[o\xc9\xaa]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x91\xc9\xaa]</span>.</li>\n<li>The Mouth diphthong: It varies among <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[ao]</span>, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[a\xc9\xb5]</span> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[a\xc9\x9b]</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\xaa]</span>.</li>\n<li>The lettEr-horsEs-commA vowel is <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xc9\x99]</span> (<a href="/wiki/Schwa" title="Schwa">schwa</a>).</li></ul>\n<p>There is poor distinction between the <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[v]</span> and <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[w]</span> sounds in Bahamian English.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \nThe contrast is often <a href="/wiki/Neutralization_(linguistics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Neutralization (linguistics)">neutralized</a> or merged into <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[v]</span>, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[b]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xce\xb2]</span>, so <i>village</i> sounds like <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[w\xc9\xaal\xc9\xaad\xca\x92]</span>, <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[v\xc9\xaal\xc9\xaad\xca\x92]</span> or <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[\xce\xb2\xc9\xaal\xc9\xaad\xca\x92]</span>. This also happens in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vincentian_English&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vincentian English (page does not exist)">Vincentian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bermudian_English" title="Bermudian English">Bermudian</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_English" title="Caribbean English">Caribbean Englishes</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Dental_fricative" title="Dental fricative">Dental fricatives</a> are usually changed to <a href="/wiki/Alveolar_plosive" class="mw-redirect" title="Alveolar plosive">alveolar plosives</a> (<a href="/wiki/Th-stopping" title="Th-stopping"><i>th</i>-stopping</a>):\n</p>\n<ul><li>Voiced <i>th</i> becomes /d/, e.g. "That" turns into "dat"; "Those" &gt; "Dose"; "There" &gt; "Dere"; "They"
