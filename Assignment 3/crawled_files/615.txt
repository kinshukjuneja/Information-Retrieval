lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">\n\n</p><p><b><a href="/wiki/Public_holiday" title="Public holiday">Public holidays</a> in the United Kingdom</b> are days on which most businesses and non-essential services are closed, although an increasing number of retail businesses (especially the larger ones) do open on some of the public holidays. There are restrictions on trading on Sundays and Christmas Day in England and Wales and on New Year\'s Day and Christmas Day in Scotland. Legally defined holidays, analogous to "public holidays" in many other countries, are usually called <b><a href="/wiki/Bank_holidays" class="mw-redirect" title="Bank holidays">bank holidays</a></b> in the United Kingdom, but can also be referred to as "public holidays"; strictly, however, "public holidays" refer to "common law holidays", the observance of which derive from custom and practice (see <a href="#Terminology">"Terminology"</a> below).\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regional variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>In Scotland, while New Year\'s Day and Christmas Day are national holidays, other bank holidays are not necessarily public holidays, since the Scots instead observe traditional local custom and practice for their public holidays.</li>\n<li>In Northern Ireland, once again, bank holidays other than New Year\'s Day and Christmas Day are not necessarily public holidays.</li>\n<li>Good Friday and Christmas Day are common law holidays, except in Scotland, where they are bank holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherfaqs_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherfaqs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-answerbank_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-answerbank-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-whenis_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whenis-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bis_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bis-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>In Scotland the holiday on 1 January (or 2 January if 1 January is Sunday) is statutory. If New Year\'s Day is Saturday a substitute holiday is given on 4 January by Royal Proclamation. 2 January is given by Royal Proclamation, with a substitute holiday on 4 January if it is Saturday and 3 January if it is Sunday or Monday. Outside Scotland, 1 January is given by Royal Proclamation, or 3 January if it is Saturday and 2 January if it is Sunday.</li>\n<li>In Scotland, 25 December is a statutory holiday (or 26 December if Christmas Day falls on a Sunday). 26 December is given by Royal Proclamation if it is neither Saturday, Sunday or Monday. 27 and 28 December are given by Royal Proclamation if Christmas Day is Saturday. 28 December only is given if Boxing Day is Saturday.</li>\n<li>Outside Scotland, if Christmas Day is a Sunday there is an additional statutory holiday on 27 December. By Royal Proclamation, if Christmas Day is a Saturday there is a substitute holiday on 28 December. If Boxing Day is a Sunday there is again a statutory holiday on 27 December, and if Boxing Day is a Saturday there is a substitute holiday by Royal Proclamation on 28 December. Effectively what happens is that if a holiday falls at the weekend a substitute day is given in lieu.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposals_for_change">Proposals for change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Proposals for change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The United Kingdom has no national day holiday marked and/or celebrated for its formal founding date.  \n</p><p>In general, increasingly, are calls for extra public holidays on the <a href="/wiki/Feast_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Feast day">patron saints\' days</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> (for <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St. George&#39;s Day">St. George\'s Day</a>), <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> (for <a href="/wiki/St._David%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St. David&#39;s Day">St. David\'s Day</a>). This would equal <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> which has <a href="/wiki/St_Patrick%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St Patrick&#39;s Day">St Patrick\'s Day</a> as a holiday. The Scottish Parliament has passed a law creating a special public holiday on <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="St Andrew&#39;s Day">St Andrew\'s Day</a> but it slightly less than many other bank holidays days as must be taken by workers in lieu of another public holiday (bank holiday).<sup id="cite_ref-St_Andrew_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-St_Andrew-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> An online petition to the Prime Minister as to Wales received 11,000 signatures. In <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> are advocates for a public holiday on <a href="/wiki/St_Piran%27s_Day" title="St Piran&#39;s Day">St Piran\'s Day</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The two terms "<b>bank holidays</b>" and "<b>public holidays</b>" are often used interchangeably, although strictly and legally there is a difference. A <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.direct.gov.uk/en/Employment/Employees/Timeoffandholidays/DG_073741">government website</a> describes the difference as follows:\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Bank holidays are holidays when banks and many other businesses are closed for the day. Public holidays are holidays which have been observed through custom and practice.\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>The only date which would seem to qualify nationally as one and not the other is <a href="/wiki/Easter_Sunday" class="mw-redirect" title="Easter Sunday">Easter Sunday</a> on which it would be strange to treat as an ordinary date for great governmental business and many shops reduce their hours further than their normal Sunday routine. However informally on various days in various areas or streets - usually where one religion accounts for most of the population or has a resonance - in Britain other dates are commonly avoided for business opening and treated as local holidays.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_of_holidays">Creation of holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Creation of holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Bank_holiday" title="Bank holiday">Bank holidays</a> may be declared in two ways:\n</p>\n<ul><li>by statute (<b>Statutory holidays</b>) - Holidays specifically listed in the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1971/80">Banking and Financial Dealings Act 1971, Schedule 1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>by <b><a href="/wiki/Royal_proclamation" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal proclamation">Royal proclamation</a></b> - This has been used for annual bank holidays created since 1971, and is also used to move a bank holiday in a given year,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and to create extra one-off bank holidays for special occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> (The Act does not provide for a bank holiday to be suppressed by royal proclamation without appointing another day in its place).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Changes in date">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Unlike the USA, where public holidays falling on a Saturday are sometimes observed on the preceding Friday, British bank holidays are always moved to a <i>later</i> date, not an earlier one.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherfaqs_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherfaqs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-answerbank_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bis-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Workers.27_rights"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Workers\'_rights">Workers\' rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Workers&#039; rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/UK_labour_law" class="mw-redirect" title="UK labour law">UK labour law</a></div>\n<p>Although there is no statutory right for workers to take paid leave on bank holidays, where paid leave is given (either because the business is closed or for other reasons), the bank holiday can count towards the minimum statutory holiday entitlement. Likewise, if people are required to work on a bank holiday, there is no statutory right to an enhanced pay rate nor to a day off <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/in_lieu" class="extiw" title="wikt:in lieu"><i>in lieu</i></a>, although many employers do give either or both. Any rights in this respect depend on the person\'s contract of employment.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The statutory minimum paid holidays is 28 days or 5.6 weeks a year under the <a href="/wiki/Working_Time_Regulations_1998" title="Working Time Regulations 1998">Working Time Regulations 1998</a> (including any bank holidays or public holidays that are taken).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="England.2C_Northern_Ireland_and_Wales"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="England,_Northern_Ireland_and_Wales">England, Northern Ireland and Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Public_holidays_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: England, Northern Ireland and Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Date</th>\n<th>Name</th>\n<th>Notes\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>1 January</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year&#39;s Day">New Year\'s Day</a></td>\n<td>From 1974, by Royal Proclamation. Previously Easter Monday had not been a public holiday in Scotland.\n</p><p>There have been protests about banks opening on 2 January since this decision was taken.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
