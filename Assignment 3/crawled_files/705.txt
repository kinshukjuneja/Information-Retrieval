of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and includes surrounding adjacent urban towns as defined by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BUA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is the largest urban area in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> with a population of 9,787,426 in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-BUA_1-1" class="reference"><a src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/London%2C_United_Kingdom.JPG/220px-London%2C_United_Kingdom.JPG" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" Area.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Greater London Built-up or Urban Area had a population of 9,787,426 and occupied an area of 1,737.9 square kilometres (671.0&#160;sq&#160;mi) at the time of the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-BUA_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>It includes most of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a> region \xe2\x80\x93 omitting most of its woodland, small, <a href="/wiki/Buffer_zone" title="Buffer zone">buffered</a> districts, the <a href="/wiki/Lee_Valley_Park" title="Lee Valley Thames">River Thames</a>. Outside the region\'s administrative boundary, it includes contiguous suburban settlements and a few densely populated <a href="/wiki/Satellite_town" title="Satellite town">outliers</a> connected to it by <a href="/wiki/Ribbon_development" title="Ribbon development">ribbon development</a>. Its outer boundary is constrained by the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Green_Belt" title="Metropolitan Green Belt">Metropolitan Green Belt</a> and it is therefore much smaller than the <a href="/wiki/London_commuter_belt" title="London commuter belt">wider metropolitan area</a> of London.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>As a selective grouping of relatively low- to mid-density (and some high-density) output areas, each consisting of roughly 120 households,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> it can be compared to the <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a> region, which covers 1,572 square kilometres (607&#160;sq&#160;mi) and contained 8,173,194 residents at the time of the 2011 census.\n</p><p>The built-up area of the Greater London region continues beyond the region\'s administrative boundary in some places, while stopping short of it in others. For this reason, the density of the Greater London Built-Up Area is 8.3% higher than that of Greater London, the figure for which includes these outlying rural areas (notably in <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Hillingdon" title="London Borough of Hillingdon">Hillingdon</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Enfield" title="London Borough of Enfield">Enfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Havering" title="London Borough of Havering">Havering</a> and <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bromley" title="London Borough of Bromley">Bromley</a>). All of both areas is drained ultimately by the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a>. The area uses around 4 <a href="/wiki/Gigawatt" class="mw-redirect" title="Gigawatt">gigawatts</a> of electricity power.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a
