<a href="/wiki/Criminal_law" title="Criminal law">criminal</a> <a href="/wiki/Court" title="Court">courts</a> responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Administration_of_justice" title="Administration of justice">administration of justice</a> in Northern Ireland: they are constituted and governed by <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_law" title="Northern Ireland law">Northern Ireland law</a>.\n</p><p>The United Kingdom does not have a single unified judicial system: <a href="/wiki/Courts_of_England_and_Wales" title="Courts of England and Wales">England and Wales</a> have one system, <a href="/wiki/Courts_of_Scotland" title="Courts of Scotland">Scotland</a> another and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> a third. There are exceptions to that rule, for example in immigration law, the jurisdiction of the First Tier Tribunal (Immigration &amp; Asylum Chamber) and the Upper Tribunal covers the whole of the United Kingdom, and in <a href="/wiki/Employment_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Employment law">employment law</a>, there is a single system of <a href="/wiki/Employment_Tribunal" class="mw-redirect" title="Employment Tribunal">Employment Tribunals</a> for England and Wales and Scotland (but not Northern Ireland). Additionally, the <a href="/wiki/Military_Courts_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Military Courts of the United Kingdom">Military Court Service</a> has jurisdiction over all members of the armed forces of the United Kingdom in relation to <a href="/wiki/Offences_against_military_law_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Offences against military law in the United Kingdom">offences against military law</a>.\n</p><p>To overcome problems resulting from the intimidation of jurors and witnesses, the right to a jury trial in Northern Ireland was suspended for certain terrorist offences in 1972, and the so-called "<a href="/wiki/Diplock_courts" title="Diplock courts">Diplock courts</a>" were introduced to try people charged with paramilitary activities. Diplock courts are common in Northern Ireland for crimes connected to terrorism.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Administration of the courts is the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Courts_and_Tribunals_Service" title="Northern Ireland Courts and Tribunals Service">Northern Ireland Courts and Tribunals Service</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a> was created by the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Reform_Act_2005" title="Constitutional Reform Act 2005">Constitutional Reform Act 2005</a>. It took its duties up on 1 October 2009.  It is the highest court of appeal in Northern Ireland, hearing ultimate appeals from all the courts of the United Kingdom, other than Scottish criminal cases.\n</p><p>The Supreme Court has taken over the appellate jurisdiction formerly vested in the <a href="/wiki/Judicial_functions_of_the_House_of_Lords" title="Judicial functions of the House of Lords">House of Lords</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Court_of_Judicature">Court of Judicature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Court of Judicature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Court of Judicature of Northern Ireland is constituted by the Judicature (Northern Ireland) Act 1978. Until 1 October 2009 its name was the Supreme Court of Judicature.  The Court of Judicature is the most important superior court of Northern Ireland. It consists of the following courts:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>The Court of Appeal in Northern Ireland (Court of Appeal, formally "Her Majesty\xe2\x80\x99s Court of Appeal in Northern Ireland"<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>)</li>\n<li>The High Court of Justice in Northern Ireland (High Court, formally "Her Majesty\'s High Court of Justice in Northern Ireland"<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>)</li>\n<li>The Crown Court</li></ul>\n<p>The title of the court was changed<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> on 1 October 2009 when the relevant provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Reform_Act_2005" title="Constitutional Reform Act 2005">Constitutional Reform Act 2005</a> came into force establishing the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Court_of_Appeal">Court of Appeal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Court of Appeal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Court of Appeal is the highest court specifically of Northern Ireland. Appeal from the Court of Appeal lies to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a>. The Court of Appeal hears appeals from the Crown Court, High Court, county courts, courts of summary jurisdiction and tribunals.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="High_Court">High Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: High Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Courts_of_Justice,_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Royal_Courts_of_Justice%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg/220px-Royal_Courts_of_Justice%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Royal_Courts_of_Justice%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg/330px-Royal_Courts_of_Justice%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Royal_Courts_of_Justice%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg/440px-Royal_Courts_of_Justice%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="479" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Courts_of_Justice,_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1304238.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Courts_of_Justice,_Belfast" title="Royal Courts of Justice, Belfast">Royal Courts of Justice, Belfast</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The High Court of Justice (High Court) is, like its <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Justice" title="High Court of Justice">English equivalent</a>, split into three divisions: Queen\'s Bench Division, Family Division and Chancery Division. The High Court is located in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Courts_of_Justice,_Belfast" title="Royal Courts of Justice, Belfast">Royal Courts of Justice, Belfast</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crown_Court">Crown Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Crown Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Crown Courts hear more serious criminal cases. These are <a href="/wiki/Indictable_offence" title="Indictable offence">indictable offences</a> and "either way" offences which are committed for trial in the Crown Courts rather than the magistrates\' courts.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="County_Court">County Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: County Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Downpatrick_Courthouse_(04),_August_2009.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Downpatrick_Courthouse_%2804%29%2C_August_2009.JPG/220px-Downpatrick_Courthouse_%2804%29%2C_August_2009.JPG" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Downpatrick_Courthouse_%2804%29%2C_August_2009.JPG/330px-Downpatrick_Courthouse_%2804%29%2C_August_2009.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Downpatrick_Courthouse_%2804%29%2C_August_2009.JPG/440px-Downpatrick_Courthouse_%2804%29%2C_August_2009.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4272" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Downpatrick_Courthouse_(04),_August_2009.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Downpatrick" title="Downpatrick">Downpatrick</a> Courthouse, August 2009</div></div></div>\n<p>The County Courts are the main civil courts. While higher-value cases are heard in the High Court, the County Courts hear a wide range of civil actions, consumer claims, and appeals from magistrates\' courts. The County Courts are called Family Care Centres when hearing proceedings brought under the Children (Northern Ireland) Order 1995 and appeals from the family proceedings courts. There are seven County Court divisions in Northern Ireland.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subordinate_courts">Subordinate courts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Courts_of_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Subordinate courts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Below the High Court are several classes of courts. Magistrates\' courts (including youth courts, family proceedings courts and domestic proceedings courts) hear less-serious criminal cases and conduct <a href="/wiki/Preliminary_hearing" title="Preliminary hearing">preliminary hearings</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indictable_offence" title="Indictable offence">more serious criminal cases</a>. They are divided into 21 petty sessions districts. The Crown Court hears all serious criminal cases which are committed to trial. When sitting as family proceedings courts the magistrates\' courts hear proceedings brought under the Children (Northern Ireland) Order 1995.\n</p><p>Additionally, there is the Enforcement of Judgments Office, and <a href="/wiki/Coroner" title="Coroner">coroners\' courts</a>, which investigate the circumstances of sudden, violent or unnatural deaths.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
