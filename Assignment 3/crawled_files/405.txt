id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_length_of_coastline" title="List of countries by length of coastline">List of countries by length of coastline</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Length">Length</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coastline_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Length">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The mapping authority for the United Kingdom, the <a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey" title="Ordnance Survey">Ordnance Survey</a>, records the coastline of the main island, Great Britain, as 11,072.76 miles rounding to 11,073 miles (17,820&#160;km).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/British_Cartographic_Society" title="British Cartographic Society">British Cartographic Society</a> points out that "The true answer is: it depends!". If the larger islands are added, the coastline as measured by the standard method at <a href="/wiki/Mean_High_Water_Mark" class="mw-redirect" title="Mean High Water Mark">Mean High Water Mark</a> rises to about 19,491 miles (31,368&#160;km).<sup id="cite_ref-BCS_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BCS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> According to the <i><a href="/wiki/CIA_Factbook" class="mw-redirect" title="CIA Factbook">CIA Factbook</a></i>, the length of the UK coastline is around 12,429&#160;km or 7,723 miles, although no details are provided about how this figure was calculated.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On the basis of <a href="/wiki/EU_Marine_Strategy_Framework_Directive" class="mw-redirect" title="EU Marine Strategy Framework Directive">EU Marine Strategy Framework Directive</a> boundaries,<sup id="cite_ref-Pisces_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pisces-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the UK coastline can be divided into the eastern, Greater North Sea section, which using 1km2 box counts and including islands is 4,994&#160;km, and a western Celtic Seas coastline, which using the same measurement approach and including Northern Ireland is 14,201&#160;km.\n</p><p>Furthermore, the measurement of any coastline is subject to variation depending upon the scale of map used. It is a meaningless statistic without knowing the scale of the map being used and the accuracy of the measurement. A larger map scale and smaller unit of measure will result in more detail being revealed and measured and thus a greater length.<sup id="cite_ref-BCS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BCS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> And because the resultant length increases exponentially faster than the increase of scale of measurement, there is no such thing as "an approximate answer" to this question. This is referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Coastline_paradox" title="Coastline paradox">coastline paradox</a>. A coastline is <a href="/wiki/Fractal" title="Fractal">fractal</a>-like \xe2\x80\x94 which means that it has self-similar properties, similar at every scale \xe2\x80\x94 therefore the closer the observer looks, the more detail is revealed, leading to a greater overall length.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shape">Shape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coastline_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The United Kingdom\'s coastline is more broken than coastlines of many other countries. It has a fractal or <a href="/wiki/Hausdorff_dimension" title="Hausdorff dimension">Hausdorff dimension</a> or \'wiggliness\' of 1.25, which is comparatively high; the Australian coastline for example has a fractal dimension of 1.13, and that of South Africa is 1.02.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a result of this shape and the number of islands, the coastline of the UK is longer than that of similar sized countries. For example, the coastline of Italy is around 9,226&#160;km in length, France 7,330&#160;km and Spain 7,268&#160;km. Greece, which has a large number of islands similar to the UK, has a coastline of around 15,147&#160;km. This means the UK has a relatively high coast/area ratio.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Nowhere in the UK is more than 113 kilometres (70&#160;mi) from the coast. It is estimated that around 3 million people (out of 60 million) live on the coast of the UK. The place furthest from the coast is <a href="/wiki/Coton_in_the_Elms" title="Coton in the Elms">Coton in the Elms</a> in <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>, which is equidistant from <a href="/wiki/Fosdyke" title="Fosdyke">Fosdyke Wash</a> in Lincolnshire; White Sands between <a href="/wiki/Neston" title="Neston">Neston</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flint,_Flintshire" title="Flint, Flintshire">Flint, Flintshire</a> in Wales; and <a href="/wiki/Westbury-on-Severn" title="Westbury-on-Severn">Westbury-on-Severn</a> Gloucestershire.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OS_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coastline_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Islands">Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coastline_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_of_the_British_Isles" title="List of islands of the British Isles">List of islands of the British Isles</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of islands of the United Kingdom">List of islands of the United Kingdom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a></div>\n<p>There are over 1,000 islands within the UK; about 130 are permanently inhabited according to the 2001 Census.
