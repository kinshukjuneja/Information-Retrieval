style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Stella Maris Turk, <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Most_Excellent_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire">MBE</a></b> (1925 \xe2\x80\x93 3 April 2017) was a British <a href="/wiki/Zoologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Zoologist">zoologist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naturalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Naturalist">naturalist</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conservation_biology" title="Conservation biology">conservationist</a>. She was known for her activities in <a href="/wiki/Marine_biology" title="Marine biology">marine biology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marine_conservation" title="Marine conservation">conservation</a>, particularly as it applies to marine <a href="/wiki/Molluscs" class="mw-redirect" title="Molluscs">molluscs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marine_mammal" title="Marine mammal">mammals</a>. Turk became a <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Most_Excellent_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire">Member of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire</a> in 2002, and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Raffles_Award" title="Stamford Raffles Award">Stamford Raffles Award</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Zoological_Society_of_London" title="Zoological Society of London">Zoological Society of London</a> in 1979.\n</p><p>What Stella Turk said about the British <a href="/wiki/Malacologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Malacologist">malacologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Erskine_Ellis" title="Arthur Erskine Ellis">Arthur Erskine Ellis</a> might just as easily be applied to her: "It is difficult to categorise people. Should one even try? We are all multiple in a singular way!"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Publications"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Publications</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Further_reading"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Further reading</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stella_Turk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Stella Turk was born in 1925<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, some distance off the western tip of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. She was born Stella Maris Treharne; her first two names, "Stella Maris", are a <a href="/wiki/Latin_(language)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin (language)">Latin</a> phrase meaning "star of the sea", a title sometimes given to the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Mary" class="mw-redirect" title="Virgin Mary">Virgin Mary</a>. Although Turk lived in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> as a small child, she primarily grew up in Cornwall, and has spent most of her life living in and researching in that county. Turk is a working scientist and a published zoologist. In addition she worked with her late husband biologist <a href="/wiki/Frank_Turk_(biologist)" title="Frank Turk (biologist)">Frank Turk</a> in the field of <a href="/wiki/Adult_education" title="Adult education">adult education</a>.\n</p><p>Stella Turk and Frank Turk founded the "Cornish Biological Record Unit" at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Exeter" title="University of Exeter">University of Exeter</a>\'s, <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Cornish_Studies" title="Institute of Cornish Studies">Institute of Cornish Studies</a>. This was later incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Wildlife_Trust" title="Cornwall Wildlife Trust">Cornwall Wildlife Trust</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Turk served as the British Isles National Recorder for <a href="/wiki/Marine_(ocean)" class="mw-redirect" title="Marine (ocean)">marine</a> <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" class="mw-redirect" title="Mollusc">molluscs</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Conchological_Society_of_Great_Britain_%26_Ireland" title="Conchological Society of Great Britain &amp; Ireland">Conchological Society of Great Britain &amp; Ireland</a>, as well as the Strandings Recorder (i.e. strandings of <a href="/wiki/Marine_mammal" title="Marine mammal">marine mammals</a>, other marine vertebrates, and in fact any unusual organisms) for the Cornish Biological Records Unit. She was a major contributor to the <i>Red Data Book for Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly</i> (editor Adrian Spalding), as the author of thirty-six sections including many of the unusual and under-recorded animals such as Thorn-skins (<a href="/wiki/Kinorhyncha" title="Kinorhyncha">Kinorhyncha</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Entoprocta" title="Entoprocta">Entoprocta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> For many years, she wrote a nature column in the West Briton.<sup id="cite_ref-westBriton_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-westBriton-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1979, Turk was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Raffles_Award" title="Stamford Raffles Award">Stamford Raffles Award</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Zoological_Society_of_London" title="Zoological Society of London">Zoological Society of London</a> "For contributions to the study of <a href="/wiki/Seahorse" title="Seahorse">seahorse</a> life and marine <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" class="mw-redirect" title="Mollusc">molluscs</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1980 Turk was awarded an honorary <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">Master of Science</a> (MSc) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Exeter" title="University of Exeter">University of Exeter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Turk was awarded an <a href="/wiki/MBE" class="mw-redirect" title="MBE">MBE</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2003_New_Year_Honours" title="2003 New Year Honours">2003 New Year Honours</a> List, "For services to Nature Conservation, Cornwall, while holding office as Strandings Recorder".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A film cerebrating her tireless contribution was shown in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-westBriton_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-westBriton-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
