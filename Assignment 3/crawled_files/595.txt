Scotland</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Central Lowlands</b> or <b>Midland Valley</b> is a geologically defined area of relatively low-lying  land in southern <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. It consists of a <a href="/wiki/Rift_valley" title="Rift valley">rift valley</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Highland_Boundary_Fault" title="Highland Boundary Fault">Highland Boundary Fault</a> to the north and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands_Fault" title="Southern Uplands Fault">Southern Uplands Fault</a> to the south.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Central Lowlands are one of the three main geographical sub-divisions of Scotland, the other two being the <a href="/wiki/Highlands_and_Islands" title="Highlands and Islands">Highlands and Islands</a> which lie to the north, northwest and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands" title="Southern Uplands">Southern Uplands</a>, which lie south of the associated second fault line.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" class="mw-headline" id="Geology_and_geomorphology">Geology and geomorphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Lowlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geology and geomorphology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg/220px-Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg/330px-Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg/440px-Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Edinburgh_Arthur_Seat_dsc06165.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arthur\'s Seat in Edinburgh</div></div></div>\n<p>The Central Lowlands is largely underlain by <a href="/wiki/Paleozoic" title="Paleozoic">Paleozoic</a> <a href="/wiki/Formation_(geology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Formation (geology)">formations</a>. Many of these <a href="/wiki/Sedimentary_rock" title="Sedimentary rock">sedimentary rocks</a> have economic significance for it is here that the <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron bearing rocks</a> that fueled Scotland\'s <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial revolution">industrial revolution</a> are to be found. This area has also experienced intense <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arthur%27s_Seat" title="Arthur&#39;s Seat">Arthur\'s Seat</a> in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> being the remnant of a once much larger <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> active in the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Carboniferous</a> period some 300 million years ago. This area is relatively low-lying, although even here hills such as the <a href="/wiki/Ochil_Hills" title="Ochil Hills">Ochils</a> and <a href="/wiki/Campsie_Fells" title="Campsie Fells">Campsie Fells</a> are rarely far from view.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In common with the rest of Scotland the whole region was affected by <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Glaciation" class="mw-redirect" title="Glaciation">glaciations</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boundary_faults">Boundary faults</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Lowlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Boundary faults">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Highland_Boundary_Fault" title="Highland Boundary Fault">Highland Boundary Fault</a> runs from <a href="/wiki/Lochranza" title="Lochranza">Lochranza</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Arran" title="Isle of Arran">Isle of Arran</a> in the south and west through the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Bute" title="Isle of Bute">Isle of Bute</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helensburgh" title="Helensburgh">Helensburgh</a>, then forms the northern boundary of <a href="/wiki/Strathmore,_Angus" title="Strathmore, Angus">Strathmore</a> before reaching <a href="/wiki/Stonehaven" title="Stonehaven">Stonehaven</a> in the north east. The <a href="/wiki/Geologic_fault" class="mw-redirect" title="Geologic fault">fault</a> was active during the <a href="/wiki/Caledonian_orogeny" title="Caledonian orogeny">Caledonian orogeny</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  a <a href="/wiki/Plate_tectonics" title="Plate tectonics">plate tectonic</a> collision which took place from Mid <a href="/wiki/Ordovician" title="Ordovician">Ordovician</a> to Mid <a href="/wiki/Devonian" title="Devonian">Devonian</a> periods (520 to 400 million years ago), during the closure of the <a href="/wiki/Iapetus_Ocean" title="Iapetus Ocean">Iapetus Ocean</a>. The fault  allowed the Midland Valley to descend as a major <a href="/wiki/Rift_(geology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rift (geology)">rift</a> by as much as 4000 metres and there was subsequently <a href="/wiki/Geologic_fault#Strike-slip_faults" class="mw-redirect" title="Geologic fault">vertical movement</a>. This earlier vertical movement was later replaced by a horizontal shear.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands_Fault" title="Southern Uplands Fault">Southern Uplands Fault</a> runs from the <a href="/wiki/Rhins_of_Galloway" title="Rhins of Galloway">Rhins of Galloway</a> in the west towards <a href="/wiki/Dunbar" title="Dunbar">Dunbar</a> on the east coast 30 miles (50&#160;km) from <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_geography">Human geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Lowlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Human geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A productive combination of fertile low-lying agricultural land and significant deposits of economically valuable coal and iron have led to the Central Lowlands being much more densely populated than the rest of Scotland. The major cities of <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stirling" title="Stirling">Stirling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dundee" title="Dundee">Dundee</a> all lie in the Central Lowlands, and over half of <a href="/wiki/Demography_of_Scotland" title="Demography of Scotland">Scotland\'s population</a> lives in this region.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Lowlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Central_Scotland_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Central Scotland (disambiguation)">Central Scotland (disambiguation)</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Scotland" title="Geology of Scotland">Geology of Scotland</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Scottish_Lowlands" title="Scottish Lowlands">Scottish Lowlands</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Lowlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 26em;
