Kingdom</h1>\t\t\t<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p><p>The <b>pharmaceutical industry in the United Kingdom</b> directly employs around 73,000 people and in 2007 contributed \xc2\xa38.4 billion to the UK\'s <a href="/wiki/GDP" class="mw-redirect" title="GDP">GDP</a> and invested a total of \xc2\xa33.9 billion in <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2007 exports of pharmaceutical products from the UK totalled \xc2\xa314.6 billion, creating a trade surplus in pharmaceutical products of \xc2\xa34.3 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>UK Pharmaceutical employment of 73,000 in 2017\n<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\ncompares to 114,000 as of 2015 in Germany, <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 92,000 as of 2014 in France <sup id="cite_ref-efpia.eu_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-efpia.eu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and 723,000 in the European Union as a whole. <sup id="cite_ref-efpia.eu_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-efpia.eu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the United States 281,440 people work in pharmaceutical industry as of 2016. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The UK is home to <a href="/wiki/GlaxoSmithKline" title="GlaxoSmithKline">GlaxoSmithKline</a> and  <a href="/wiki/AstraZeneca" title="AstraZeneca">AstraZeneca</a>, respectively the world\'s fifth- and sixth-largest pharmaceutical companies measured by 2009 market share.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Foreign companies with a major presence in the UK pharmaceutical industry include <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novartis" title="Novartis">Novartis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hoffmann%E2%80%93La_Roche" class="mw-redirect" title="Hoffmann\xe2\x80\x93La Roche">Hoffmann\xe2\x80\x93La Roche</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eisai_(company)" title="Eisai (company)">Eisai</a>. One in five of the world\'s biggest-selling prescription drugs were developed in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-iht_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iht-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div Helens</a></div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pharmaceutical_industry_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1842 <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Beecham_(chemist)" title="Thomas Beecham (chemist)">Thomas Beecham</a> established the <a href="/wiki/Beecham%27s_Pills" title="Beecham&#39;s Pills">Beecham\'s Pills</a> laxative business, which would later become the <a href="/wiki/Beecham_Group" title="Beecham Group">Beecham Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tbeech_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbeech-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> By 1851 UK-based patent medicine companies had combined domestic revenues of around \xc2\xa3250,000.<sup id="cite_ref-pharmindguid_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pharmindguid-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Beecham opened Britain\'s first modern drugs factory in <a href="/wiki/St_Helens,_Merseyside" title="St Helens, Merseyside">St Helens</a> in 1859.<sup id="cite_ref-tbeech_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbeech-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wellcome" title="Henry Wellcome">Henry Wellcome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silas_Mainville_Burroughs,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Silas Mainville Burroughs, Jr.">Silas Burroughs</a> formed a partnership in September 1880, and established an office in Snow Hill in Central London.<sup id="cite_ref-wth_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wth-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The London Wholesale Drug and Chemical Protection Society was formed in 1867, which became the Drug Club in 1891, the forerunner of the present-day <a href="/wiki/Association_of_the_British_Pharmaceutical_Industry" title="Association of the British Pharmaceutical Industry">Association of the British Pharmaceutical Industry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pharmindguid_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pharmindguid-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 1883 Burroughs Wellcome &amp; Co. opened their first factory, at Bell Lane Wharf in <a href="/wiki/Wandsworth" title="Wandsworth">Wandsworth</a>, utilising compressed medicine tablet-making machinery acquired from <a href="/wiki/Wyeth" title="Wyeth">Wyeth</a> of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-wth_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wth-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Burroughs Wellcome &amp; Co. established its first overseas branch in Sydney in 1898.<sup id="cite_ref-wth_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wth-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pharmaceutical_industry_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Glaxo department of Joseph Nathan and Co was established in London in 1908.<sup id="cite_ref-gskhist_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gskhist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Glaxo Laboratories Ltd absorbed Joseph Nathan and Co in 1947 and was listed on the London Stock Exchange in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-gskhist_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gskhist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In order to satisfy regulations then in place in the UK on the importation of medicines, <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a> established a compounding operation in <a href="/wiki/Folkestone" title="Folkestone">Folkestone</a>, Kent in Autumn 1952.<sup id="cite_ref-history1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Pfizer acquired an 80-acre site on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Sandwich,_Kent" title="Sandwich, Kent">Sandwich</a> in 1954 to enable the expansion of its Kent-based activities.<sup id="cite_ref-history1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Glaxo acquired <a href="/wiki/Allen_%26_Hanburys" title="Allen &amp; Hanburys">Allen and Hanburys Ltd.</a> in 1958.<sup id="cite_ref-gskhist_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gskhist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 1981 the bacterial infection treatment Augmentin (<a href="/wiki/Amoxicillin" title="Amoxicillin">amoxicillin/clavulanate potassium</a>) was launched by Beecham; the anti-ulcer treatment Zantac (<a href="/wiki/Ranitidine" title="Ranitidine">ranitidine</a>) was launched by Glaxo; and the antiviral herpes treatment Zovirax (<a href="/wiki/Aciclovir" title="Aciclovir">aciclovir</a>) was launched by Wellcome.<sup id="cite_ref-gskhist_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gskhist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1991 SmithKline Beecham launched Seroxat/Paxil (<a href="/wiki/Paroxetine" title="Paroxetine">paroxetine hydrochloride</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-gskhist_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gskhist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In June 1993 <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Chemical_Industries" title="Imperial Chemical Industries">Imperial Chemical Industries</a> demerged its pharmaceuticals and <a href="/wiki/Agrochemicals" class="mw-redirect" title="Agrochemicals">agrochemicals</a> businesses, forming <a href="/wiki/Zeneca" title="Zeneca">Zeneca Group plc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 1995 Glaxo opened a major research and development facility in <a href="/wiki/Stevenage" title="Stevenage">Stevenage</a>, constructed at a cost of \xc2\xa3700 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In March 1995 the \xc2\xa39 billion acquisition of Wellcome by Glaxo was completed, forming <a href="/wiki/Glaxo_Wellcome" class="mw-redirect" title="Glaxo Wellcome">Glaxo Wellcome</a>, in what was the largest merger in UK corporate history to date.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BASF" title="BASF">BASF</a> completed the acquisition of the pharmaceutical division of <a href="/wiki/Boots_UK" title="Boots UK">The Boots Company</a> in April 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 1997 SmithKline Beecham opened a major new research centre at New Frontiers Science Park in <a href="/wiki/Harlow" title="Harlow">Harlow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gskhist_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gskhist-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 1999 Zeneca Group plc and Sweden-based <a href="/wiki/Astra_AB" title="Astra AB">Astra AB</a> merged to form AstraZeneca plc.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Glaxo Wellcome and SmithKline Beecham announced their intention to merge in January 2000, with the merger completing in December of that year, forming GlaxoSmithKline plc.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pharmaceutical_industry_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In February 2001 the Novartis Respiratory Research Centre, the largest single-site respiratory research centre in the world, opened in Horsham.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In May 2006 AstraZeneca agreed to buy <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Antibody_Technology" title="Cambridge Antibody Technology">Cambridge Antibody Technology</a>, then the largest UK-based biotechnology company, for \xc2\xa3702 million.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In April 2007 AstraZeneca agreed to acquire the U.S.-based biotechnology company <a href="/wiki/MedImmune" title="MedImmune">MedImmune</a> for $15.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In April 2009 GlaxoSmithKline agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/Stiefel_Laboratories" title="Stiefel Laboratories">Stiefel Laboratories</a>, then the world\'s largest independent dermatology company, for US$3.6 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In June 2009 <a href="/wiki/Eisai_(company)" title="Eisai (company)">Eisai</a> opened a major new research and development and manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/Hatfield,_Hertfordshire" title="Hatfield, Hertfordshire">Hatfield</a>, constructed at a cost of over \xc2\xa3100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In November 2009 GlaxoSmithKline and Pfizer combined their respective <a href="/wiki/AIDS" class="mw-redirect" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> divisions into one London-based company, <a href="/wiki/ViiV_Healthcare" title="ViiV Healthcare">ViiV Healthcare</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On 1 February 2011 Pfizer announced that it would be closing its entire research and development facility at <a href="/wiki/Sandwich,_Kent" title="Sandwich, Kent">Sandwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a> within 18\xe2\x80\x9324 months with the loss of 2,400 jobs, as part of a company-wide plan to reduce its spending on research and
