id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The league features a 80 game split-season that runs from June to August. First half winners face the second half winner in a one-game playoff (if needed) for the championship. On top of a salary, each player in the league is also provided housing accommodations and two meals a day six days a week.\n</p><p>During the initial season, two <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>-based teams, the <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Stars" title="Hawaii Stars">Hawaii Stars</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Na_Koa_Ikaika_Maui" title="Na Koa Ikaika Maui">Maui Warriors</a>, played inter-league games against the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Challenge_League" title="Baseball Challenge League">Baseball Challenge League</a> of Japan; California teams played against the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Pro_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Freedom Pro League">Freedom Pro League</a> of Arizona.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Both Hawaii teams, the Stars and the Warriors, ceased operations after playing the 2013 season due to the travel costs of bringing in opponents from Northern California.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/East_Bay_Lumberjacks" title="East Bay Lumberjacks">East Bay Lumberjacks</a> also did not return for a second season.\n</p><p>Two expansion clubs were added in 2014 (<a href="/wiki/Sonoma_Stompers" title="Sonoma Stompers">Sonoma Stompers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pittsburg_Mettle" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburg Mettle">Pittsburg Mettle</a>) bringing the total number of teams to four. Two more teams, Napa Silverados and Martinez Clippers, were added as expansion teams beginning play in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017 San Francisco businessman and entrepreneur Jonathan Stone was named league commissioner.<sup
