navigation-not-searchable">This is a list of institutions with permission to use the prefix <i>Royal</i>. For all institutions that take the name \'Royal College\', see <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Royal College (disambiguation)">Royal College (disambiguation)</a>.</div>\n<p>A <b>Royal College</b> in some <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> countries is technically a <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a> which has received royal patronage and permission to use the prefix <i>Royal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Permission is usually granted through a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The charter normally confers a constitution with perpetual succession and the right to sue or be sued independently of the members. The charter also usually provide for rights of recourse to the <a href="/wiki/Queen_in_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen in Council">Queen in Council</a>. Although incorporation is now cheaply and easily obtainable by registration, the distinction of a Royal Charter means that such charters are still sought by and granted to institutions considered to be in the public interest, typically learned professional societies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Although many institutions are formally Royal Colleges, such as the three royal <a href="/wiki/Public_school_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Public school (UK)">public schools</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> of <a href="/wiki/Westminster_School" title="Westminster School">Westminster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Winchester_College" title="Winchester College">Winchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton</a>, the phrase "The Royal Colleges" is commonly applied to the medical institutions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Physicians_and_Surgeons_of_Glasgow" title="Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Glasgow">Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Glasgow</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Physicians" title="Royal College of Physicians">Royal College of Physicians</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Nursing" title="Royal College of Nursing">Royal College of Nursing</a> and similar institutions in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, and elsewhere.\n</p><p>These colleges enjoy a special status whereby they can confer recognised post-nominal titles comparable to degrees, e.g. Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Glasgow, and they are frequently granted statutory licensing, regulatory and disciplinary powers over their own members and even others.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox"
