The <b>ward</b> is the primary unit of English electoral geography for <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parishes</a> and borough and district councils, <b>electoral ward</b> is the unit used by Welsh principal councils, while the <b>electoral division</b> is the unit used by English <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a>. Each ward/division has an average electorate of about 5,500 people, but ward-population counts can vary substantially.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As at the end of 2014 there were 9,456 electoral wards/divisions in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/London_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="London borough">London boroughs</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan boroughs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan districts</a> (including most <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a>) are divided into wards for local elections. However, <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county council</a> elections (as well as those for several unitary councils which were formerly county councils, such as the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight_Council" title="Isle of Wight Council">Isle of Wight Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shropshire_Council" title="Shropshire Council">Shropshire Council</a>) instead use the term <i>electoral division</i>. In <a href="/wiki/Shire_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Shire county">shire county</a> areas with both wards (used for district council elections) and electoral divisions (used for county council elections), the boundaries of the two types of divisions may sometimes not coincide, but more often the county electoral divisions will be made up of one or more complete wards.\n</p><p>In urban areas the wards within a local authority area typically each contain roughly the same number of electors, and each elect three councillors. In local authorities with mixed urban and rural areas the number of councillors may vary from one to three depending on the size of the electorate. Where civil parishes exist, a ward can be coterminous with a civil parish or consist of groups of civil parishes. Larger civil parishes (such as <a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury" title="Shrewsbury">Shrewsbury</a>) can be divided into two or more wards.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_of_London">City of London</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: City of London">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_City_of_London" title="Wards of the City of London">Wards of the City of London</a></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a> has its own <i><a href="/wiki/Sui_generis" title="Sui generis">sui generis</a></i> form of local government, and is divided into wards, which are ancient and very long-standing sub-divisions of the City.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Isles_of_Scilly">Isles of Scilly</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Isles of Scilly">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Council of the Isles of Scilly">Council of the Isles of Scilly</a>, is also a <i>sui generis</i> unitary authority, has five wards, each returning either 2 or (in the case of St Mary\'s) 13 councillors to the Council of the Isles of Scilly.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_parishes">Civil parishes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Civil parishes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">Civil parishes in England</a> are sometimes divided into wards for elections to the <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish council</a> (or town/city council). They need not bear any relation to wards or electoral divisions at district level.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_use">Historic use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Historic use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The four most northerly <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">ancient counties</a> of England&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Cumberland" title="Cumberland">Cumberland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westmorland" title="Westmorland">Westmorland</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northumberland" title="Northumberland">Northumberland</a>&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 were historically divided into administrative units called wards instead of <a href="/wiki/Hundred_(county_subdivision)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hundred (county subdivision)">hundreds</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wapentakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Wapentakes">wapentakes</a>, as in other counties. Wards were areas originally organised for military purposes, each centred on a castle.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wales">Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In Wales the term <i>electoral ward</i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is used for elections to <a href="/wiki/Principal_areas_of_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Principal areas of Wales">principal councils</a> (county council or county borough council).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  This change replaced the term <i>electoral division</i>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Community_(Wales)" title="Community (Wales)">Communities</a> in Wales (the equivalent to the civil parish in England) are sometimes divided into wards for elections to the community council.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>All of Scotland is divided into wards for local government elections; each ward elects three or four councillors by <a href="/wiki/Single_Transferable_Vote" class="mw-redirect" title="Single Transferable Vote">Single Transferable Vote</a>.\n</p><p>See also: <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Scotland" title="Local government in Scotland">Local government in Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Boundary_Commission_for_Scotland" title="Local Government Boundary Commission for Scotland">Local Government Boundary Commission for Scotland</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Local government in Northern Ireland">Local government in Northern Ireland</a></div>\n<p>Districts in Northern Ireland are divided into electoral areas,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with each electing between five and seven councillors by <a href="/wiki/Single_transferable_vote" title="Single transferable vote">Single Transferable Vote</a>. These are themselves sub-divided into wards, but these wards have no official function.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
