template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Acts of Union 1800</b> (sometimes erroneously referred to as a single <b>Act of Union 1801</b>) were parallel acts of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Ireland" title="Parliament of Ireland">Parliament of Ireland</a> which united the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Ireland" title="Kingdom of Ireland">Kingdom of Ireland</a> (previously in <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a>) to create the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a>. The acts <a href="/wiki/Came_into_force" class="mw-redirect" title="Came into force">came into force</a> on 1 January 1801, and the merged <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> had its  <a href="/wiki/First_Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="First Parliament of the United Kingdom">first meeting</a> on 22 January 1801. \n</p><p>Both acts remain in force, with amendments, in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Northern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and have been repealed in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acts_of_Union_1800&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Two acts were passed in 1800 with the same <a href="/wiki/Long_title" class="mw-redirect" title="Long title">long title</a>, <b>An Act for the Union of Great Britain and Ireland</b>. The <a href="/wiki/Short_title" class="mw-redirect" title="Short title">short title</a> of the act of the British Parliament is <b>Union with Ireland Act 1800</b>, assigned by the <a href="/wiki/Short_Titles_Act_1896" title="Short Titles Act 1896">Short Titles Act 1896</a>. The short title of the act of the Irish Parliament is <b>Act of Union (Ireland) 1800</b>, assigned by a 1951 act of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Parliament of Northern Ireland">Parliament of Northern Ireland</a>, and hence not effective in the Republic of Ireland, where it was referred to by its long title when repealed in 1962.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acts_of_Union_1800&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-unbalanced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Unbalanced scales.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Unbalanced_scales.svg/45px-Unbalanced_scales.svg.png" width="45" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Unbalanced_scales.svg/68px-Unbalanced_scales.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Unbalanced_scales.svg/90px-Unbalanced_scales.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="354" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>may be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view#Undue_weight" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">unbalanced</a> towards certain viewpoints</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Acts_of_Union_1800&amp;action=edit">improve the article</a> by adding information on neglected 2017)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Before these Acts, Ireland had been in <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> with England since 1541, when the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Ireland" title="Parliament of Ireland">Irish Parliament</a> had passed the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Ireland_Act_1542" title="Crown of Ireland Act 1542">Crown of Ireland Act 1542</a>, proclaiming King <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII of England</a> to be <a href="/wiki/King_of_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Ireland">King of Ireland</a>. Since the 12th century, the King of England had been technical overlord of the <a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Ireland" title="Lordship of Ireland">Lordship of Ireland</a>, a papal possession. Both the Kingdoms of Ireland and England later came into personal union with that of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> upon the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_the_Crowns" title="Union of the Crowns">Union of the Crowns</a> in 1603.\n</p><p>In 1707, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Scotland" title="Kingdom of Scotland">Kingdom of Scotland</a> were united into a single kingdom: the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a>. Upon that union, each House of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Ireland" title="Parliament of Ireland">Parliament of Ireland</a> passed a congratulatory address to <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Queen_of_Great_Britain" title="Anne, Queen of Great Britain">Queen Anne</a>, praying that, "May God put it in your royal heart to add greater strength and lustre to your crown, by a still more comprehensive Union".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Irish parliament at that time was subject to a number of restrictions that placed it subservient to the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_England" title="Parliament of England">Parliament of England</a> (and following the union of England and Scotland, the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a>), however Ireland gained effective legislative independence from Great Britain through the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_1782" title="Constitution of 1782">Constitution of 1782</a>.\n</p><p>By this time access to institutional power in Ireland was restricted to a small minority, the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Irish">Anglo-Irish</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Ascendancy" title="Protestant Ascendancy">Protestant Ascendancy</a>, and frustration at the lack of reform among the Catholic majority eventually led, along with other reasons, to a <a href="/wiki/1798_Rebellion" class="mw-redirect" title="1798 Rebellion">rebellion in 1798</a>, involving <a href="/wiki/Expedition_d%27Irlande" class="mw-redirect" title="Expedition d&#39;Irlande">a French invasion of Ireland</a> and the seeking of complete independence from Great Britain. This rebellion was crushed with much bloodshed, and the subsequent drive for union between Great Britain and Ireland that passed in 1800 was motivated at least in part by the belief that the rebellion was caused as much by reactionary loyalist brutality as by the United Irishmen.\n</p><p>Furthermore, Catholic emancipation was being discussed in Great Britain, and fears that a newly enfranchised Catholic majority would drastically change the character of the Irish government and parliament also contributed to a desire from London to merge the parliaments.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passing_the_Acts">Passing the Acts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acts_of_Union_1800&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" Many members of the Irish Parliament jealously guarded this autonomy (notably <a href="/wiki/Henry_Grattan" title="Henry Grattan">Henry Grattan</a>) and a motion for union was legally rejected in 1799.\n</p><p>Only <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Ireland" title="Church of Ireland">Anglicans</a> were permitted to become members of the Parliament of Ireland, though the great majority of the Irish population were <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>, with many <a href="/wiki/Presbyterian" class="mw-redirect" title="Presbyterian">Presbyterians</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ulster" title="Ulster">Ulster</a>. In 1793 Roman Catholics regained the right to vote if they owned or rented property worth \xc2\xa32 p.a. The Catholic hierarchy was strongly in favour of union, hoping for rapid <a href="/wiki/Catholic_emancipation" title="Catholic emancipation">emancipation</a> and the right to sit as MPs \xe2\x80\x93 which was however delayed after the passage of the acts until <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Relief_Act_1829" title="Roman Catholic Relief Act 1829"> 1829</a>.\n</p><p>From the perspective of Great Britain, the union was desirable because of the uncertainty that followed the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rebellion_of_1798" title="Irish Rebellion of 1798">Irish Rebellion of 1798</a> and the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> of 1789; if Ireland adopted Catholic Emancipation, willingly or not, a Roman Catholic parliament could break away from Britain and ally with the French, while the same measure within a united kingdom would exclude that possibility. Also the Irish and British parliaments, when creating a regency during <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">King George III</a>\'s "madness", gave the Prince Regent different powers. These considerations led Great Britain to decide to attempt merger of the two kingdoms and their parliaments.\n</p><p>The final passage of the Act in the Irish Parliament was achieved with substantial majorities, in part according to contemporary documents through bribery, namely the awarding of <a href="/wiki/Peerage" title="Peerage">peerages</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honour" title="Honour">honours</a> to critics to get their votes.<sup id="cite_ref-Ward1994p28_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ward1994p28-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Whereas the first attempt had been defeated in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_House_of_Commons" title="Irish House of Commons">Irish House of Commons</a> by 109 votes against to 104 for, the second vote in 1800 produced a result of 158 to 115.<sup id="cite_ref-Ward1994p28_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ward1994p28-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provisions">Provisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acts_of_Union_1800&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Provisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Acts of Union were two complementary Acts, namely:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <b>Union with Ireland Act 1800</b> (39 &amp; 40 Geo. 3 c. 67),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> an Act of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a>, and</li>\n<li>The <b>Act of Union (Ireland) 1800</b> (40 Geo. 3 c. 38),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> an Act of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Ireland" title="Parliament of Ireland">Parliament of Ireland</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>They were passed on 2 July 1800 and 1 August 1800 respectively, and came into force on 1 January 1801. They ratified eight articles which had been previously agreed by the British and Irish Parliaments:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Articles I\xe2\x80\x93IV dealt with the political aspects of the Union. It created a <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">united parliament</a>.\n<ul><li>In the House of Lords, the existing members of the Parliament of Great Britain were joined by, as <a href="/wiki/Lords_Spiritual" title="Lords Spiritual">Lords Spiritual</a>, four bishops of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Ireland" title="Church of Ireland">Church of Ireland</a>, rotating among the dioceses in each session and as <a href="/wiki/Lords_Temporal" title="Lords Temporal">Lords Temporal</a> 28 <a href="/wiki/Representative_peers" class="mw-redirect" title="Representative peers">representative peers</a> elected for life by the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Ireland" title="Peerage of Ireland">Peerage of Ireland</a>.</li>\n<li>The House of Commons was to include the pre-union representation from Great Britain and 100 members from Ireland: two members from each of the 32 counties and from the two largest boroughs, and one from each of the next 31 boroughs and from <a href="/wiki/Dublin_University_(constituency)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dublin University (constituency)">Dublin University</a>. The other 84 Irish parliamentary boroughs were disfranchised; all were <a href="/wiki/Pocket_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Pocket borough">pocket boroughs</a>, whose patrons received \xc2\xa315,000 compensation for the loss of what was considered their property.</li></ul></li></ul>\n<dl><dd><dl><dd><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_1801" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom general election, 1801">United Kingdom general election, 1801</a></div></dd></dl></dd></dl>\n<ul><li>Article V united the established <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> and Church of Ireland into "one Protestant Episcopal Church, to be called, The United Church of England and Ireland"; but also confirmed the independence of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scotland" title="Church of Scotland">Church of Scotland</a>.</li>\n<li>Article VI created a <a href="/wiki/Customs_union" title="Customs union">customs union</a>, with the exception that customs duties on certain British and Irish goods passing between the two countries would remain for 10 years (a consequence of having trade depressed by the ongoing war with revolutionary France).</li>\n<li>Article VII stated that Ireland would have to contribute two-seventeenths towards the expenditure of the United Kingdom. The figure was a ratio of Irish to British foreign trade.</li>\n<li>Article VIII formalised the legal and judicial aspects of the Union.</li></ul>\n<p>Part of the attraction of the Union for many Irish Catholics was the promise of <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Emancipation" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic Emancipation">Catholic Emancipation</a>, allowing <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> MPs, who had not been allowed in the Irish Parliament. This was however blocked by <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">King George III</a> who argued that emancipating Roman Catholics would breach his <a href="/wiki/Coronation_of_the_British_monarch#Recognition_and_oath" title="Coronation of the British monarch">Coronation Oath</a>, and was not realised until 1829.\n</p><p>The traditionally separate <a href="/wiki/Irish_Army_(Kingdom_of_Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Army (Kingdom of Ireland)">Irish Army</a>, which had been funded by the Irish Parliament, was merged into the larger <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_first_parliament">The first parliament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acts_of_Union_1800&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The first parliament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the first Parliament of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland, the members of the House of Commons were not elected afresh. By royal proclamation authorised by the Act, all the members of the last House of Commons from Great Britain took seats in the new House, and from Ireland <a href="/wiki/Members_of_the_1st_UK_Parliament_from_Ireland" title="Members of the 1st UK Parliament from Ireland">100 members were chosen</a> from the last Irish House of Commons; both members from each of the 32 counties and from the two largest boroughs, and one each (chosen by lot) from the next 31 boroughs and from <a href="/wiki/Dublin_University_(constituency)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dublin University (constituency)">Dublin University</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Union_flag">Union flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
