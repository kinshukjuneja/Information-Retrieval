class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Until the advent of the railways, the United Kingdom used <a href="/wiki/Local_Mean_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Local Mean Time">Local Mean Time</a>. Greenwich Mean Time was adopted first by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a> in 1840 and a few others followed suit in the following years. In 1847 it was adopted by the <a href="/wiki/Railway_Clearing_House" title="Railway Clearing House">Railway Clearing House</a>, and by almost all railway companies by the following year. It was from this initiative that the term "<a href="/wiki/Railway_time" title="Railway time">railway time</a>" was derived. \n</p><p>It was gradually adopted for other purposes, but a legal case in 1858 held "<a href="/wiki/Local_mean_time" title="Local mean time">local mean time</a>" to be the official time.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On 14 May 1880, a letter signed by \'Clerk to Justices\' appeared in \'The Times\', stating that \'Greenwich time is now kept almost throughout England, but it appears that Greenwich time is not legal time.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was changed later in 1880, when Greenwich Mean Time was legally adopted throughout the island of Great Britain under the <b>Statutes (Definition of Time) Act 1880</b> (<a href="/wiki/43_%26_44_Vict." class="mw-redirect" title="43 &amp; 44 Vict.">43 &amp; 44 Vict.</a>). GMT was adopted on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> in 1883, <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> in 1898 and <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a> in 1913. Ireland adopted GMT in 1916, supplanting <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Mean_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Dublin Mean Time">Dublin Mean Time</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Myers_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Myers-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">Daylight saving time</a> was introduced by the <b>Summer Time Act 1916</b> (<a href="/wiki/6_%26_7_Geo._V" class="mw-redirect" title="6 &amp; 7 Geo. V">6 &amp; 7 Geo. V</a>), which was implemented in 1916 as GMT plus one hour and Dublin Mean Time plus one hour. The length of DST could be extended by <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council" title="Order in Council">Order in Council</a>, and was extended for the duration of World War I. For 1916, DST extended from 21 May to 1 October, with transitions at 02:00 standard time. On 1 October 1916, Greenwich Mean Time was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Myers_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Myers-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the beginning of the 20th century, <a href="/wiki/Sandringham_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandringham Time">Sandringham Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B00:30" title="UTC+00:30">UTC+00:30</a>) was used by the royal household. This practice was halted by <a href="/wiki/Edward_VIII_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward VIII of the United Kingdom">King Edward VIII</a>, in an effort to reduce confusions over time.\n</p><p>The dates of British Summer Time are the subject of the <b>Summer Time Act 1972</b>. From 1972 to 1980, the day following the 3rd Saturday in March was the start of British Summer Time (unless that day was Easter Sunday, in which case BST began a week earlier), with the day following the 4th Sunday in October being the end of British Summer Time. From 1981 to 2001, the dates were set in line with various European Directives. Since 2002 the Act has specified the last Sunday in March as the start of British Summer Time with the last Sunday in October being end of British Summer Time.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Experimentations_with_Central_European_Time">Experimentations with Central European Time</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Experimentations with Central European Time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The United Kingdom experimentally adopted <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">Central European Time</a> by maintaining Summer Time throughout the year from 1968 to 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-changetime_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changetime-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In a <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a> debate, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Butler,_17th_Viscount_Mountgarret" title="Richard Butler, 17th Viscount Mountgarret">Richard Butler, 17th Viscount Mountgarret</a> said that the change was welcomed at the time, but the experiment was eventually halted after a debate in 1971, in which the outcome might have been influenced by a major accident on the morning of the debate.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Proposals to adopt CET have been raised by various politicians over the years,<sup id="cite_ref-changetime_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changetime-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> including a proposal in 2011 to conduct an analysis of the costs and benefits.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Devolution">Devolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Devolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Authority over the time zone in Northern Ireland is devolved to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> but the power has never been used.  In Scotland and Wales, time zone is a <a href="/wiki/Reserved_matters_(Westminster)" class="mw-redirect" title="Reserved matters (Westminster)">reserved matter</a>, meaning that only the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> has power to legislate.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="IANA_time_zone_database">IANA time zone database</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: IANA time zone database">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/IANA_time_zone_database" class="mw-redirect" title="IANA time zone database">IANA time zone database</a> contains one zone for the United Kingdom in the file <a href="/wiki/Zone.tab" class="mw-redirect" title="Zone.tab">zone.tab</a>, named <a href="/w/index.php?title=Europe/London&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Europe/London (page does not exist)">Europe/London</a>.\nThis refers to the area having the <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1_alpha-2" title="ISO 3166-1 alpha-2">ISO 3166-1 alpha-2</a> country code "GB". The zone names Europe/Guernsey, Europe/Isle_of_Man and Europe/Jersey exist because they have their own ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 but the zone.tab entries are links to Europe/London. There are several entries for UK possessions around the world.\n</p><p>Data directly from <a href="/wiki/List_of_tz_database_time_zones" title="List of tz database time zones">zone.tab</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tz_database" title="Tz database">IANA time zone database</a>.
