href="/wiki/File:International_Futures_Logo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>International Futures (IFs) Logo</div></div></div>\n<p><b>International Futures (IFs)</b> is a global <a href="/wiki/Integrated_assessment_modelling" title="Integrated assessment modelling">integrated assessment model</a> designed to help in thinking strategically and systematically about key global systems (economic, demographic, education, health, environment, technology, domestic governance, infrastructure, agriculture, energy and environment) housed at the Frederick S. Pardee Center for International Futures. Initially created by <a href="/wiki/Barry_B._Hughes" title="Barry B. Hughes">Barry B. Hughes</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Josef_Korbel_School_of_International_Studies" title="Josef Korbel School of International Studies">Josef Korbel School of International Studies</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Denver" title="University of Denver">University of Denver</a> in <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the model is free for public use in both its online and downloadable forms.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  \n</p><p>The Pardee Center for International Futures has partnered with many organizations to produce forecasts and data analysis.  IFs has been utilized in the <a href="/wiki/National_Intelligence_Council" title="National Intelligence Council">National Intelligence Council\'s</a> <i>Global Trends 2020</i>, <i>Global Trends 2025</i>, and <i>Global Trends 2030</i> report.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The International Futures model has also contributed to the United Nations <i><a href="/wiki/Human_Development_Report" title="Human Development Report">Human Development Report</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Global_Environment_Outlook" title="Global Environment Outlook">Global Environmental Outlook</a></i>.\n</p><p>IFs is hosted free for public use by <a href="/wiki/Google_Public_Data_Explorer" title="Google Public Data Explorer">Google Public Data Explorer</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Council" title="Atlantic Council">Atlantic Council</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Security_Studies" title="Institute for Security Studies">Institute for Security Studies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Futures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Model structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The model incorporates  dynamically linked  sub-models.  They include:  population, economic, agricultural, educational, energy, socio-political, international political, environmental, health, infrastructure and  technology.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  IFs is a unique modeling tool because it endogenizes the impact of such a wide range of global systems for 183 countries.\n</p><p>The help system that accompanies the software<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> provides an extensive overview of the model structure and computer code used to write the model.  IFs has three main functions, all connected to its conceptual treatment of integrated assessment forecasts: data analysis, scenario analysis, and display.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_analysis">Data analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Futures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Data analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Crosssect.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/de/Crosssect.jpg/220px-Crosssect.jpg" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/de/Crosssect.jpg/330px-Crosssect.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/de/Crosssect.jpg/440px-Crosssect.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1194" data-file-height="738" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Crosssect.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Example of cross-sectional analysis of <a href="/wiki/Total_fertility_rate" title="Total fertility rate">total fertility rate</a> and <a href="/wiki/GDP" class="mw-redirect" title="GDP">GDP</a> per capita at <a href="/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity" title="Purchasing power parity">purchasing power parity</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The data analysis section of IFs represents a collection of over 2,000 data series from all major international data gatherers.  It is constantly updated with new data series.  This data forms the foundation of the model structure.  Users can analyze historic data cross-sectionally, longitudinally or on a <a href="/wiki/World_map" title="World map">world map</a>.  Using <a href="/wiki/Cross-sectional_analysis" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross-sectional analysis">cross-sectional analysis</a>, users can  select a <a href="/wiki/Variable_(mathematics)" title="Variable (mathematics)">variables</a> and plot this against up to 5 <a href="/wiki/Independent_variables" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent variables">independent variables</a>.  It is then possible to animate the map to see how the cross-sectional relationship changes across the 40+ years of data in the <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a>.  Longitudinally, users can plot the relationship between a <a href="/wiki/Dependent_variable" class="mw-redirect" title="Dependent variable">dependent variable</a> and time, from 1960 (for most data series) through the most recent data year available.  A world map allows users to display data from any of these series using <a href="/wiki/GIS" class="mw-redirect" title="GIS">GIS</a> options.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scenario_analysis">Scenario analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Futures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scenario analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg/220px-IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg" width="220" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg/330px-IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg/440px-IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1232" data-file-height="897" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:IFsWorldPopulationHistoryForecastII.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Forecast of world population for four <a href="/wiki/UNEP" class="mw-redirect" title="UNEP">UNEP</a> scenarios from 1960&#8211;2100</div></div></div>\n<p>The software allows users to access and change the <a href="/wiki/Parameters" class="mw-redirect" title="Parameters">parameters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Variable_(mathematics)" title="Variable (mathematics)">variables</a> that are used in the model. The <a href="/wiki/Scenario_analysis" title="Scenario analysis">Scenario Analysis</a> display lets users create their own global scenario or load a pre-run global scenario in their field of interest.  For example, to analyze the effects of a policy intervention on different sub-models and variables within the model, make the changes to the appropriate variable and then analyze the results in comparison to the base-case.  Many pre-run scenarios come packaged with the model, including work that has been completed for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Programme" title="United Nations Environment Programme">United Nations Environment Programme</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Intelligence_Council" title="National Intelligence Council">National Intelligence Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Display">Display</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Futures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Display">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mortalitydisplay.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8f/Mortalitydisplay.jpg/220px-Mortalitydisplay.jpg" width="220" height="242" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8f/Mortalitydisplay.jpg/330px-Mortalitydisplay.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8f/Mortalitydisplay.jpg/440px-Mortalitydisplay.jpg 2x" data-file-width="778" data-file-height="855" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mortalitydisplay.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Example of mortality display for Belgium in 2025</div></div></div>\n<p>This portion of the software allows users to display the forecast results of their scenario analyses for different provinces, countries and groups across different issue areas.  Some of the specialized displays include: population, educational attainment, mortality rate, <a href="/wiki/World_Values_Survey" title="World Values Survey">World Values Survey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gini_coefficient" title="Gini coefficient">Gini coefficient</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_accounting_matrix" title="Social accounting matrix">social accounting matrix</a>, advanced sustainability analysis, and <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> financial flows.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pardee_Center_for_International_Futures">Pardee Center for International Futures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Futures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pardee Center for International Futures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The  project received a gift from <a href="/wiki/Frederick_S._Pardee" title="Frederick S. Pardee">Frederick S. Pardee</a>, formerly of <a href="/wiki/RAND" class="mw-redirect" title="RAND">RAND</a>, to construct the <b>Pardee Center for International Futures</b> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Denver" title="University of Denver">University of Denver</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  It is responsible for the further institutionalization of the software, training sessions, and the continued work on the <i>Patterns of Potential Human Progress</i> (PPHP) volumes.  The first PPHP volume discusses reduction in global poverty; the second, global education; the third, health care systems; the fourth, global infrastructure; the fifth, domestic governance.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The journal <a href="/wiki/Poverty_and_Public_Policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty and Public Policy">Poverty and Public Policy</a> reviewed the first PPHP volume, and concluded the following:\n</p>\n<dl><dd>For all their fine effort in data assembly and analysis, Hughes et al. intended that their first volume\xe2\x80\x99s main contribution should be one not of prediction, but of promoting thought about poverty policy. Their attractively presented and lucid volume succeeds in this endeavor; it very usefully fulfills its promise to the student and policymaker.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="African_Futures_Project">African Futures Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Futures&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: African Futures Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The African Futures Project is a collaboration between the <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Security_Studies" title="Institute for Security Studies">Institute for Security Studies</a> and the Pardee Center for International Futures to promote long term strategic planning for African development.
