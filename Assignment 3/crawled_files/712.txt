neighbouring <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Scotland is not one of the twelve leading cricketing nations which play <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test matches</a>, but the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish cricket team">Scottish national team</a> is now allowed to play full <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">One Day Internationals</a> even outside the <a href="/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup" title="Cricket World Cup">Cricket World Cup</a>, in which Scotland competed in 2007. Scotland has a well established recreational cricket structure. In 2016 it was estimated that around 17,000 people play cricket in Scotland<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Administration"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Administration</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Men&#39;s_national_team"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Men\'s national team</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#Domestic_amateur_leagues"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Domestic amateur leagues</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#Notable_Scottish_cricketers"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">Notable Scottish cricketers</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-6"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-7"><a href="#Further_reading"><span class="tocnumber">7</span> <span class="toctext">Further reading</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Cricket in Scotland is at least 225 years old. The first match for which records are available was played in September 1785 at Schaw Park, <a href="/wiki/Alloa" title="Alloa">Alloa</a>.  The game was more generally introduced to Scotland by English soldiers garrisoned in the country in the years following the <a href="/wiki/Jacobite_rising_of_1745" title="Jacobite rising of 1745">Jacobite rising</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Prince_Charlie" class="mw-redirect" title="Bonnie Prince Charlie">Bonnie Prince Charlie</a> in 1745; and it is no coincidence that the oldest known club is Kelso (records date back to 1820), in the Borders, then a garrison town. The origins of cricket in Perth, where cricket was also played at a very early stage, was also for the same reason.\n</p><p>In other areas, including the Borders of Scotland, the game seems to have been introduced by immigrant English workers in the paper, textile and iron industries, while in Edinburgh the game was taken up in the mid nineteenth century by the main Schools in the area, most prominently initially by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_High_School,_Edinburgh" title="Royal High School, Edinburgh">Royal High School</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Academical_Cricket_Club" title="Edinburgh Academical Cricket Club">Edinburgh Academy</a>.\n</p><p>The first representative game of any standing was in 1849, when the All England XI came to Edinburgh to play a 22-man team representing Scotland. This match provoked great interest and similar visits followed over the next few years, representative games also being played in other Cities &amp; Towns. Scotland matches played on an eleven a side basis date from 1865, against a total of more than 30 countries, including all the Test-playing nations, and numerous other select teams, including, of course, the MCC. But the development of Scotland\'s international standing was more-or-less ignored for the best part of the 20th century, with three-day matches against Ireland, MCC, touring Test teams and the occasional County side being the only ones granted \'first-class\' status.\n</p><p>In 1980, Scotland was invited to take part in England\'s <a href="/wiki/Benson_and_Hedges_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Benson and Hedges Cup">Benson and Hedges Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/NatWest_Trophy" class="mw-redirect" title="NatWest Trophy">NatWest Trophy</a> one-day competitions. In a further development, Scotland resigned from membership of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Cricket_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Cricket Council">UK Cricket Council</a> in 1992, effectively severing links with England in the cricketing sense, and in 1994 was elected to Associate Membership of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a> (ICC). This gave Scotland its own voice in world cricket and the national team took part for the first time in the <a href="/wiki/1997_ICC_Trophy" title="1997 ICC Trophy">ICC Trophy</a> held in Kuala Lumpur in March/April 1997. Third place qualified Scotland for the <a href="/wiki/ICC_World_Cup_in_1999" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC World Cup in 1999">ICC World Cup in 1999</a> hosted by England, and Scotland themselves hosted two games (against New Zealand &amp; Bangladesh) at <a href="/wiki/The_Grange_Club" title="The Grange Club">The Grange Club</a> in Edinburgh.\n</p><p>Although failing to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2003_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2003 Cricket World Cup">2003 World Cup</a>, Scotland made significant inroads in professionalising the game in Scotland. The first part of this was Scotland\'s acceptance into the English <a href="/wiki/Sunday_League_(cricket)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunday League (cricket)">Sunday League</a> competition in 2003, for a period of three years. This involved playing a minimum of 18 matches of top class cricket against the English counties, giving both players and administrators the level of experience necessary for Scotland\'s progress to <a href="/wiki/One-Day_International" class="mw-redirect" title="One-Day International">One-Day International</a> status.\n</p><p>In a halcyon period for the Scotland International side, they won the inaugural (three-day) <a href="/wiki/2004_ICC_Intercontinental_Cup" title="2004 ICC Intercontinental Cup">ICC Intercontinental Cup</a> in November 2004, and then the <a href="/wiki/2005_ICC_Trophy" title="2005 ICC Trophy">2005 ICC Trophy</a> in Dublin by beating old rivals Ireland, putting the Scots into 12th place in the global rankings. This gave Scotland qualification for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 World Cup</a> in the West Indies. Placed in the hardest Group, no successes were achieved in the Tournament and there was further disappointment at not achieving qualification for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2011 Cricket World Cup">2011 World Cup</a>.\n</p><p>The International teams then entered a transitional phase, with the retiral of several key players from the previous few years. It was 2011 before Scotland emerged with a fully competitive team, most notably winning four consecutive One Day Internationals against Afghanistan, Netherlands (twice) and Ireland. Following defeat by Sri Lanka, they continued a successful path by beating Northamptonshire &amp; Warwickshire on consecutive days in the <a href="/wiki/CB40_Competition" class="mw-redirect" title="CB40 Competition">CB40 Competition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The governing body for Scottish cricket is <a href="/wiki/Cricket_Scotland" title="Cricket Scotland">Cricket Scotland</a>, which administers women\'s cricket and junior cricket as well as the men\'s game. \n</p><p>The then Scottish Cricket Union resigned from the UK Cricket Council in 1992, effectively severing links with the organisation of cricket in <a href="/wiki/Cricket_in_England" title="Cricket in England">England</a>. In 1994 Scotland became an Associate Member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Men.27s_national_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men\'s_national_team">Men\'s national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Men&#039;s national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Scotland failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2003_cricket_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 cricket World Cup">2003 World Cup</a><sup id="cite_ref-telegicc_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegicc-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but successfully qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 event in the West Indies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Scotland won the qualifying tournament, the ICC Trophy, in Ireland in 2005. Scotland played <a href="/wiki/Australian_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian cricket team">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_African_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="South African cricket team">South Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dutch_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch cricket team">the Netherlands</a> in the opening group stage in 2007. Most of the members of Scotland\'s national team are amateurs, although a few Scots have played professionally in English domestic cricket, and for the England national team, including former captain <a href="/wiki/Mike_Denness" title="Mike Denness">Mike Denness</a>.\n</p><p>In 2003, the Scottish team was granted a place in the English national one day cricket league in the hope that playing against professional cricketers on a regular basis would improve the performance level of the best Scottish cricketers.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Cricket_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Cricket Team">Scottish national team</a> qualified for both the  <a href="/wiki/2009_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2009 ICC World Twenty20">2009 ICC World Twenty20</a> and <a href="/wiki/2016_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2016 ICC World Twenty20">2016 ICC World Twenty20</a> tournaments.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_amateur_leagues">Domestic amateur leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Domestic amateur leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Scotland has a number of different amateur cricket leagues held throughout the country which are governed by the three sub-organisations of <a href="/wiki/Cricket_Scotland" title="Cricket Scotland">Cricket Scotland</a>: \n</p>\n<ul><li>The <b><a href="/wiki/East_of_Scotland_Cricket_Association" title="East of Scotland Cricket Association">East of Scotland Cricket Association</a></b></li>\n<li>The <b>Western District Cricket Union</b></li>\n<li>The <b>Aberdeenshire Cricket Association</b></li></ul>\n<p>Each of these organisations is responsible for running their respective regional leagues. The <b>East of Scotland Cricket Association</b> runs 9 divisions, with the Eastern Premier League containing 10 teams.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, the <b>Western District Cricket Union</b> also runs a 10 team Premier League, with a further 3 divisions with Evening and Sunday Leagues as well.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The <b>Aberdeenshire Cricket Association</b> holds 4 grades of cricket with around 25 clubs fielding over 30 teams throughout summer<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a Scot, <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Jardine" title="Douglas Jardine">Douglas Jardine</a>, the inventor of "<a href="/wiki/Bodyline" title="Bodyline">Leg Theory"</a>. Jardine was born in <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a>, brought up in <a href="/wiki/St_Andrews" title="St Andrews">St Andrews</a>, spent most of his life in England, died in Switzerland and his ashes were scattered in Perthshire.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> His parents were Scottish, and he gave his own children Scottish names.\n</p><p>A number of other prominent <a href="/wiki/Cricketer" class="mw-redirect" title="Cricketer">cricketers</a> have come from <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, including the former <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="English cricket team">England</a> captain, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Denness" title="Mike Denness">Mike Denness</a>, Warwickshire all-rounder <a href="/wiki/Dougie_Brown" title="Dougie Brown">Dougie Brown</a>, and former England test players <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Hamilton_(cricketer)" title="Gavin Hamilton (cricketer)">Gavin Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregor_MacGregor_(sportsman)" title="Gregor MacGregor (sportsman)">Gregor MacGregor</a>, who represented Scotland at rugby as well as England at cricket.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Another notable Scottish cricketer was <a href="/wiki/Brian_Hardie" title="Brian Hardie">Brian Hardie</a>, who was a major contributor to the successful <a href="/wiki/Essex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Essex County Cricket Club">Essex</a> side of the 70\'s and 80\'s.  One of the most successful Scottish spinners, and a respected journalist, was <a href="/wiki/Ian_Peebles" title="Ian Peebles">Ian Peebles</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> who was one of the cricketers of the year in 1931 alongside <a href="/wiki/Donald_Bradman" class="mw-redirect" title="Donald Bradman">Donald Bradman</a>. \n</p><p><a href="/wiki/R._C._Robertson-Glasgow" title="R. C. Robertson-Glasgow">R. C. Robertson-Glasgow</a> played for <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Cricket_Club" title="Oxford University Cricket Club">Oxford University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Somerset_County_Cricket_Club" title="Somerset County Cricket Club">Somerset</a> and later became a prominent cricket writer and correspondent. The South African-born former England captain <a href="/wiki/Tony_Greig" title="Tony Greig">Tony Greig</a> also qualified to play for England due to his Scottish parentage.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Finally Scots have also played for other countries: <a href="/wiki/Tom_Campbell_(South_African_cricketer)" title="Tom Campbell (South African cricketer)">Tom Campbell</a> for South Africa<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Archie_Jackson" title="Archie Jackson">Archie Jackson</a> for Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-36870241">"Sport participation rises in Scotland"</a>. <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News">BBC News</a>. 2016-07-23<span class="reference-accessdate">.
