class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Queen\'s constitutional roles are mostly delegated to the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Tuvalu" title="Governor-General of Tuvalu">Governor-General of Tuvalu</a>.\n</p><p>Royal succession is governed by the English <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Settlement_1701" title="Act of Settlement 1701">Act of Settlement</a> of 1701, which role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: International and domestic role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Fifty-two states are members of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>. Sixteen of these countries are specifically <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realms</a> who recognise, individually, the same person as their Monarch and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Head of State">Head of State</a>; Tuvalu is one of these.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Despite sharing the same person as their respective national monarch, each of the Commonwealth realms\xe2\x80\x94including Tuvalu\xe2\x80\x94is sovereign and independent of the others.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_of_shared_monarchy">Development of shared monarchy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development of shared monarchy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Balfour_Declaration_of_1926" title="Balfour Declaration of 1926">Balfour Declaration</a> of 1926 provided the <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">Dominions</a> the right to be considered equal to Britain, rather than subordinate; an agreement that had the result of, in theory, a shared Crown that operates independently in each realm rather than a unitary British Crown under which all the dominions were secondary. The Monarchy thus ceased to be an exclusively British institution, although it has often been called "British" since this time (in both legal and common language) for reasons historical, legal, and of convenience. The <a href="/wiki/Royal_and_Parliamentary_Titles_Act,_1927" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal and Parliamentary Titles Act, 1927">Royal and Parliamentary Titles Act, 1927</a> was the first indication of this shift in law, further elaborated in the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster 1931</a>.\n</p><p>Tuvalu achieved independence in 1978 but retained the Queen as Head of State. Under the Statute of Westminster, Tuvalu has a common monarchy with Britain and the other Commonwealth realms. \n</p><p>On all matters of the Tuvaluan State, the monarch is advised solely by Tuvaluan <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">ministers</a>. 16 June remains a public holiday as the Queen\'s Official Birthday.\n</p><p>The Queen of Tuvalu and the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Duke of Edinburgh</a> toured Tuvalu between 26\xe2\x80\x9327 October 1982. The royal couple were carried around in ceremonial litters and later served with traditional local dishes on a banquet.<sup id="cite_ref-YT1982S_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YT1982S-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-YT1982V_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YT1982V-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A sheet of commemorative stamps was issued for the royal visit by the <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu_Philatelic_Bureau" title="Tuvalu Philatelic Bureau">Tuvalu Philatelic Bureau</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Title">Title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>By the Act 1 of 1987 of the Parliament of Tuvalu her style and title are: <i>Elizabeth the Second, by the Grace of God, Queen of Tuvalu and of Her other Realms and Territories, Head of the Commonwealth</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-EIItitle_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EIItitle-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>This style communicates Tuvalu\'s status as an independent monarchy, highlighting the Monarch\'s role specifically as Queen of Tuvalu, as well as the shared aspect of the Crown throughout the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_the_Commonwealth" title="Head of the Commonwealth">Commonwealth</a>. Typically, the Sovereign is styled "Queen of Tuvalu," and is addressed as such when in Tuvalu, or performing duties on behalf of Tuvalu abroad.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oath_of_allegiance">Oath of allegiance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Oath of allegiance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small><i>(March 2016)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Under the Constitution of Tuvalu, the oath of allegiance is a declaration of allegiance to <i>Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II, Her Heirs and Successors</i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Constitutional_role">Constitutional role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Constitutional role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Tuvalu" title="Constitution of Tuvalu">Constitution of Tuvalu</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Monarchy_of_Tuvalu_and_the_Governor_General">The Monarchy of Tuvalu and the Governor General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Monarchy of Tuvalu and the Governor General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Monarchy of Tuvalu exists in a framework of a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_system" title="Parliamentary system">parliamentary</a> <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">representative democracy</a>. As a constitutional monarch, The Queen acts entirely on the advice of her Government ministers in Tuvalu.<sup id="cite_ref-EIIrole_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EIIrole-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Head of State is recognised in section 50 of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Tuvalu" title="Constitution of Tuvalu">Constitution of Tuvalu</a>, as a symbol of the unity and identity of Tuvalu. The powers of the Head of State are set out in section 52 (1) of the Constitution.<sup id="cite_ref-pacliiC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pacliiC-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TI_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TI-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Part IV of the Constitution confirms the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Head of State">Head of State</a> of Tuvalu is <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> as the Sovereign of Tuvalu and provides for the rules for succession to the Crown.  As set out in section 54 of the Constitution, the Queen\xe2\x80\x99s representative is the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Tuvalu" title="Governor-General of Tuvalu">governor general</a>. Section 58 of the Constitution requires the governor general to perform the functions of the Head of State when the Sovereign is outside Tuvalu or otherwise incapacitated. The governor general of Tuvalu is appointed by the monarch upon the advice of the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Tuvalu" title="Prime Minister of Tuvalu">Prime Minister of Tuvalu</a>.\n</p><p>In 1986 the Constitution adopted upon independence was amended in order to give attention to Tuvaluan custom and tradition as well as the aspirations and values of the Tuvaluan people.<sup id="cite_ref-SF_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SF-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The changes placed greater emphasis on Tuvaluan community values rather than Western concepts of individual entitlement.<sup id="cite_ref-SL_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SL-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The preamble was change and an introductory \xe2\x80\x98Principles of the Constitution\xe2\x80\x99 was added.\n</p><p>The preamble to the Constitution of Tuvalu recites that the Ellice Islands, after coming under the protection of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> in 1892, had, in January 1916, in conjunction with the Gilbert Islands, become known as the <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_and_Ellice_Islands" title="Gilbert and Ellice Islands">Gilbert and Ellice Islands</a> Colony; and that after the Ellice Islands had been established by Queen Elizabeth as a separate colony in October 1975 under their ancient name of Tuvalu, a constitution had been adopted which was given the force of law by Order in Council, taking effect on 1 October 1978. The constitution now provides that Queen Elizabeth II is the sovereign of Tuvalu and the head of state and that references to the sovereign extend to the sovereign\'s heirs and successors.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Duties">Duties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Duties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small><i>(March 2016)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Most of the Queen\'s domestic duties are performed by the governor-general. The governor-general represents the Queen on ceremonial occasions such as the opening of parliament, the presentation of honours and military parades. Under the constitution, he is given authority to act in some matters, for example in appointing and disciplining officers of the civil service, in proroguing Parliament. As in the other Commonwealth realms, however, the monarch\'s role, and thereby the viceroy\'s role, is almost entirely symbolic and cultural, acting as a symbol of the legal authority under which all governments operate, and the powers that are constitutionally hers are exercised almost wholly upon the advice of the Cabinet, made up of <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_the_Crown" title="Minister of the Crown">Ministers of the Crown</a>. It has been said since the death of <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Queen_of_Great_Britain" title="Anne, Queen of Great Britain">Queen Anne</a> in 1714, the last monarch to head the British Cabinet, that the monarch "reigns" but does not "rule". In exceptional circumstances, however, the monarch or viceroy can act against such advice based upon his or her <a href="/wiki/Reserve_power" title="Reserve power">reserve powers</a>.\n</p><p>There are also a few duties which must be specifically performed by, or bills that require assent by the <a href="/wiki/Queen_regnant" title="Queen regnant">Queen</a>. These include: signing the appointment papers of governors-general, the confirmation of awards of honours, and approving any change in her title.\n</p><p>It is also possible that if the governor-general decided to go against the prime minister\'s or the government\'s advice, the prime Minister could appeal directly to the monarch, or even recommend that the monarch dismiss the governor-general.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession">Succession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Succession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Succession to the British throne">Succession to the British throne</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Charles,_Prince_of_Wales.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c4/Charles%2C_Prince_of_Wales.jpg" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="220" data-file-height="293" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Charles,_Prince_of_Wales.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles, Prince of Wales</a>, the current heir to the throne of Tuvalu</div></div></div>\n<p>The constitution provides that the Queen\'s heirs shall succeed her as head of state. Unlike some realms, but as with others, Tuvalu defers to United Kingdom law to determine the line of succession to the Tuvaluan throne.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As such, succession is by <a href="/wiki/Primogeniture" title="Primogeniture">absolute primogeniture</a> and governed by the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Settlement_1701" title="Act of Settlement 1701">Act of Settlement 1701</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bill_of_Rights_1689" title="Bill of Rights 1689">Bill of Rights 1689</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Succession_to_the_Crown_Act_2013" title="Succession to the Crown Act 2013">Succession to the Crown Act 2013</a>. This legislation lays out the rules that the monarch cannot be a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> and must be in communion with the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> upon ascending the throne.\nThe <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> is Elizabeth II\'s eldest son, <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles</a>, who has no official title outside of the UK, but is accorded his UK title, <i><a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales" title="Prince of Wales">Prince of Wales</a></i>, as a <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_title" title="Courtesy title">courtesy title</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" The viceroy may reserve a bill for the monarch\'s pleasure, that is to say, allow the monarch to make a personal decision on the bill. The monarch has the power to disallow a bill (within a time limit specified by the constitution).\n</p><p>The sovereign is deemed the "fount of justice," and is responsible for rendering justice for all subjects. The sovereign does not personally rule in judicial cases; instead, judicial functions are performed in his or her name.  The common law holds that <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_immunity" title="Sovereign immunity">the sovereign "can do no wrong"</a>; the monarch cannot be prosecuted in his or her own courts for criminal offences. Civil lawsuits against <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">the Crown</a> in its public capacity (that is, lawsuits against the government) are permitted; however, lawsuits against the monarch personally are not cognizable.\n</p><p>The sovereign, and by extension the governor-general, also exercises the "<a href="/wiki/Prerogative_of_mercy" class="mw-redirect" title="Prerogative of mercy">prerogative of mercy</a>," and may <a href="/wiki/Pardon" title="Pardon">pardon</a> offences against the Crown. Pardons may be awarded before, during, or after a trial. The exercise of the \'Power of Mercy\' to grant a pardon and the commutation of prison sentences in described in section 80 of the Constitution.\n</p><p>In Tuvalu the <a href="/wiki/Legal_personality" class="mw-redirect" title="Legal personality">legal personality</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">state</a> is referred to as <i>Her Majesty the Queen in Right of Tuvalu</i>. For example, if a lawsuit is filed against the government, the respondent is formally described as Her Majesty the Queen in Right of Tuvalu. The monarch as an individual takes no more role in such an affair than in any other business of government.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Referendum_of_2008">Referendum of 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Referendum of 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the first years of the 21st century there was a debate about the abolition of the monarchy. Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Saufatu_Sopoanga" title="Saufatu Sopoanga">Saufatu Sopoanga</a> had stated in 2004 that he was in favour of replacing the Queen as Tuvalu\'s head of state, a view supported by popular former Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Ionatana_Ionatana" title="Ionatana Ionatana">Ionatana Ionatana</a>; Sopoanga also stated that public opinion would be evaluated first before taking any further moves.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Former Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Kamuta_Latasi" title="Kamuta Latasi">Kamuta Latasi</a> also supported the idea.\n</p><p>A referendum was held in Tuvalu in 2008, giving voters the option of retaining the monarchy, or abolishing it in favour of a republic. The monarchy was retained with 1,260 votes to 679 (64.98%).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Turnout was low, with about 2,000 voters of a potential 9,000 taking part.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_of_Tuvalu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
