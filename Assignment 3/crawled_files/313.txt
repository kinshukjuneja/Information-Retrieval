logo</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Equality and Human Rights Commission</b> (<b>EHRC</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Non-departmental_public_body" title="Non-departmental public body">non-departmental public body</a> in England and Wales, established by the <a href="/wiki/Equality_Act_2006" title="Equality Act 2006">Equality Act 2006</a> with effect from 1 October 2007. The Commission has responsibility for the promotion and enforcement of equality and non-discrimination laws in England, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. It took over the responsibilities of the <a href="/wiki/Commission_for_Racial_Equality" title="Commission for Racial Equality">Commission for Racial Equality</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Equal_Opportunities_Commission_(United_Kingdom)" title="Equal Opportunities Commission (United Kingdom)">Equal Opportunities Commission</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Disability_Rights_Commission" title="Disability Rights Commission">Disability Rights Commission</a>. It also has responsibility for other aspects of equality law: age, <a href="/wiki/Sexual_orientation" title="Sexual orientation">sexual orientation</a> and religion or belief. A <a href="/wiki/National_human_rights_institutions" class="mw-redirect" title="National human rights institutions">national human rights institution</a>, it seeks to promote and protect human rights in England and Wales.\n</p><p>The EHRC has offices in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, London, Glasgow and <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Non-departmental_public_body" title="Non-departmental public body">non-departmental public body</a> (NDPB) sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Government_Equalities_Office" title="Government Equalities Office">Government Equalities Office</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Education" title="Department for Education">Department for Education</a> (DfE). It is separate from and independent from Government but accountable for its use of public funds. The Chairman of the Commission is David Isaac.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The EHRC\'s functions do not extend to <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, where there is a separate <a href="/wiki/Equality_Commission_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Equality Commission for Northern Ireland">Equality Commission</a> (ECNI) and a <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Human_Rights_Commission" title="Northern Ireland Human Rights Commission">Human Rights Commission</a> (NIHRC), each established under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Belfast_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Belfast Agreement">Belfast Agreement</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers">Powers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Powers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The EHRC derives its powers from the Equality Act 2006, which resulted from the government <a href="/wiki/White_paper" title="White paper">white paper</a>, <i>Fairness for All: A New Commission for Equality and Human Rights</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Section 3 states the EHRC has a general duty to work towards the development of a society where equality and rights are rooted. This is taken to mean,\n</p>\n<blockquote>\n<p>(a) people\'s ability to achieve their potential is not limited by <a href="/wiki/Prejudice" title="Prejudice">prejudice</a> or discrimination,<br /> \n(b) there is respect for and protection of each individual\'s human rights (including respect for the dignity and worth of each individual),<br />\n(c) each person has an equal opportunity to participate in society, and<br />\n(d) there is mutual respect between communities based on understanding and valuing of diversity and on shared respect for equality and human rights.\n</p>\n</blockquote>\n<p>Section 30 strengthens the EHRC\'s ability to apply for judicial review and to intervene in court proceedings, through giving explicit statutory provision for such action. Sections 31\xe2\x80\x932 gives the EHRC a new power to assess public authorities\' compliance with their positive equality duties. It can issue "compliance notices" if it finds a public authority is failing in its duties. Public authorities, importantly, are bound under the <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Act_1998" title="Human Rights Act 1998">Human Rights Act 1998</a> to act in a way compatible with the European Convention on Human Rights (s.6 HRA). The EHRC\'s role is therefore one of catching matters before they lead to the courts. So if you work for a public sector employer (like a local council or the civil service) there are more avenues to enforce equality standards in your favour. This may seem somewhat odd, considering that public sector employers are consistently shown to have excellent workplace practices.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Section 30(3) of the Equality Act 2006 allows the EHRC to bring judicial review proceedings under the HRA against public authorities. This is a stronger tool than usual, because the EHRC is not subject to the normal requirement of being a "victim" of a Human Rights violation.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Under section 24, the EHRC can enter into binding agreements with employers. So for instance, it can agree that an employer will commit to equality best practice audits or avoid discriminatory practices that it may identify, in return for not investigating (a bad thing for employers\' publicity). It can enforce these agreements through injunctions. Previously only the <a href="/wiki/Disability_Rights_Commission" title="Disability Rights Commission">Disability Rights Commission</a> had such powers, the CRE and the EOC were more limited. For instance, the EOC used only to have the power to get injunctions against bodies with a bad track record of discrimination.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Section 20 gives the EHRC the power to carry out investigations when it has the "suspicion" of unlawful discrimination taking place. Before this had been limited to a requirement of "reasonable suspicion" which in effect led the predecessors to be much more cautious. In legal terms this is the difference between an <a href="/wiki/Wednesbury_unreasonableness" class="mw-redirect" title="Wednesbury unreasonableness">irrationality test</a> and a reasonable man test. In other words, a court could not declare an investigation unlawful unless it considered that the EHRC was carrying out an investigation where no reasonable person could have come to the same conclusion. Before a court could declare an investigation unlawful if it thought that the proverbial "<a href="/wiki/The_man_on_the_Clapham_omnibus" title="The man on the Clapham omnibus">man on the Clapham Omnibus</a>" would not regard an employer as being a suspect "discriminator".\n</p><p>There are some complications in relation to the <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Act_1998" title="Human Rights Act 1998">Human Rights Act 1998</a> with the EHRC\'s powers. If it is going to be a "named investigation" (i.e. the employer will probably get shamed by the publication of its name during an investigation), the EHRC cannot start an investigation into a public authority for breaches under the HRA. Also, it cannot support individual cases in tribunals and courts where the issue would concern matters that fall only under the HRA and not under some pre-existing British equality legislation (like the <a href="/wiki/Sex_Discrimination_Act_1975" title="Sex Discrimination Act 1975">Sex Discrimination Act 1975</a>). Practically this will be problematic, not least because if a claim <i>did</i> exist under the HRA, British legislation which did not cover such problems would usually be updated to comply with <a href="/wiki/European_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="European Convention on Human Rights">European Convention</a> rights (these are the ones that the HRA implements). Also, the line between what is in the European Convention, what is actually covered by domestic legislation, is difficult to draw. At any rate, section 28 gives the Minister the power to give authorisation for a discrimination case to be fought if a domestic legislation issue has dropped away, but a purely human rights issue remains.\n</p><p>As a successor body, the EHRC\'s new powers are not dramatic. Some people have called for the changes to go further, for instance, to allow the EHRC to bring proceedings against employers in its own name on any issue (not just human rights ones).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The American, Australian, Belgian, Canadian and New Zealand counterparts can.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_status">International status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Although it operates at sub-national level, the EHRC was in 2009 recognised as a member of the worldwide network of <a href="/wiki/National_human_rights_institutions" class="mw-redirect" title="National human rights institutions">national human rights institutions</a>, securing "A status" accreditation from the <a href="/wiki/International_Co-ordinating_Committee_of_National_Human_Rights_Institutions" class="mw-redirect" title="International Co-ordinating Committee of National Human Rights Institutions">International Co-ordinating Committee of NHRIs</a> (ICC). This gives the Commission enhanced access to the <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Human Rights Council">Human Rights Council</a>, treaty bodies and other United Nations human rights bodies. The EHRC was the second NHRI in the UK, following the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Human_Rights_Commission" title="Northern Ireland Human Rights Commission">Northern Ireland Human Rights Commission</a> (NIHRC) in 1999, and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Human_Rights_Commission" title="Scottish Human Rights Commission">Scottish Human Rights Commission</a> (SHRC) became the third to gain ICC accreditation in 2010.  The three bodies share representation and voting rights in the ICC and its regional network, the <a href="/wiki/European_Group_of_National_Human_Rights_Institutions" title="European Group of National Human Rights Institutions">European Group of NHRIs</a>.\n</p><p>The EHRC has since 2008 engaged in parallel reporting ("shadow reporting") at examinations of the UK under the UN and <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> <a href="/wiki/International_human_rights_instruments" title="International human rights instruments">human rights treaties</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Periodic_Review" title="Universal Periodic Review">Universal Periodic Review</a>. It was designated in 2008 as part of the United Kingdom\'s independent mechanism for promoting, monitoring and protecting implementation in the state of the United Nations <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_the_Rights_of_Persons_with_Disabilities" title="Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities">Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities</a> (CRPD). (It shares that role with the other two NHRIs in the UK \xe2\x80\x93 the NIHRC and SHRC \xe2\x80\x93 and the <a href="/wiki/Equality_Commission_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Equality Commission for Northern Ireland">Equality Commission for Northern Ireland</a>.) The EHRC chairs the CRPD Working Group of the European Group of NHRIs.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaigns">Campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><b>Working Better</b> The Working Better Initiative was launched with a remit of coming up with innovative ways to meet the needs of modern workforce, with a particular focus on flexibility and family life. The Home Front survey<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> formed part of the initial consultation process.\n</p><p><b>Good Relations</b> The Commission aims to provide research and resources and advice to Local Authorities and to enable greater understanding between communities.\n</p><p><b>Care and Support</b> A report<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> produced by the Commission highlighted the need to shift from a "safety net" approach to care to a "springboard". The report suggested ways that individuals could be given greater autonomy over their lives and encouraged to engage in society and make social and economic contributions.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Litigation_against_the_British_National_Party">Litigation against the British National Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Litigation against the British National Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Following the election of 2 <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEPs</a> from the <a href="/wiki/British_National_Party" title="British National Party">British National Party</a> (BNP), a potential issue of public funding was raised by the commission as the BNP constitution states that recruitment is only open to members who are "<i>indigenous Caucasian and defined ethnic groups emanating from that Race</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\nThe Commission\'s legal director <a href="/wiki/John_Wadham" title="John Wadham">John Wadham</a> has stated that \n</p>\n<blockquote>\n<p>"<i>The legal advice we have received indicates that the British National party\'s constitution and membership criteria, employment practices and provision of services to constituents and the public may breach discrimination laws which all political parties are legally obliged to uphold</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-GuardEU_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardEU-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n</blockquote> \n<p>This relates to the <a href="/wiki/Race_Relations_Act_1976" title="Race Relations Act 1976">Race Relations Act 1976</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GuardEU_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardEU-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> which outlaws the refusal or deliberate omission to offer employment on the basis of non-membership of an organisation. The commission sent a letter to the BNP giving them until 20 July to provide written undertakings that there will not be discrimination in its recruitment procedures.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardEU_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardEU-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The BNP responded to the letter by stating that it "intends to clarify the word \'white\' on its website".<sup id="cite_ref-EHRCRel248_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EHRCRel248-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, because the commission believes the BNP will continue to discriminate against potential or actual members on racial grounds, on 24 August 2009 the commission announced that they had issued county court proceedings against the BNP.<sup id="cite_ref-EHRCRel248_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EHRCRel248-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In a statement the commission reduced the grounds on which it was taking action against the BNP, stating\n</p>\n<blockquote>\n<p>"<i>The Commission believes the BNP\'s constitution and membership criteria are discriminatory and, further, that the continued publication of them on the BNP website is unlawful. It has therefore issued county court proceedings against party leader Nick Griffin and two other officials. The Commission has decided not to take action on two further grounds set out in its letter before action in the light of the BNP\'s commitment to comply with the law.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-EHRCRel248_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EHRCRel248-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n</blockquote>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commissioners">Commissioners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commissioners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Commission is made up of 10 commissioners with backgrounds in various fields of equality and human rights. As of  August&#160;2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> they are:<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em;">\n<ul><li>David Isaac (Chairman)<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Caroline Waters OBE (Deputy Chair)</li>\n<li>June Milligan (Wales Commissioner)</li>\n<li>Lesley Sawers (Scotland Commissioner)</li>\n<li>Professor Lorna McGregor</li>\n<li>Susan Johnson OBE</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Chris_Holmes_(swimmer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Holmes (swimmer)">Chris Holmes</a> MBE (Disability Commissioner)</li>\n<li>Evelyn Asante-Mensah OBE</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Laura_L._Carstensen" title="Laura L. The Commissioners included Morag Alexander, Kay Allen, <a href="/wiki/Jane_Campbell,_Baroness_Campbell_of_Surbiton" title="Jane Campbell, Baroness Campbell of Surbiton">Baroness Campbell of Surbiton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeannie_Drake" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeannie Drake">Jeannie Drake</a> CBE, <a href="/wiki/Joel_Edwards_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Joel Edwards (UK)">Joel Edwards</a>, Mike Smith, Professor Kay Hampton, Francesca Klug, Sir <a href="/wiki/Bert_Massie" title="Bert Massie">Bert Massie</a> CBE, <a href="/wiki/Ziauddin_Sardar" title="Ziauddin Sardar">Ziauddin Sardar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Summerskill" title="Ben Summerskill">Ben Summerskill</a> and Dr Neil Wooding. <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Brewer" title="Nicola Brewer">Nicola Brewer</a>, the first chief executive (and ex officio Commissioner), returned to the diplomatic service.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_relations">Industrial relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Industrial relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Six staff were sacked by email on 9 February 2017 and given a day to clear their desks. Since then another two staff have been dismissed and two Directors are serving their notice. Another member of staff is still at risk of dismissal.  Those sacked on 9 February had payment in lieu of notice (PILON) which workers did not agree to because it closes off the opportunity to seek redeployment at the Commission or elsewhere in the civil service.\nIn a letter the union stated: "By imposing PILON you are cutting off this option and effectively consigning BME, disabled, women and trade union members to unemployment. There should only be PILON in cases where the individual concerned has agreed to it."\nCommenting on the cases, PCS general secretary Mark Serwotka said: "It\'s absolutely reprehensible that dedicated staff have been sacked and told to clear their desks with a day\'s notice.  "That this has happened at the government body charged with upholding human rights and fair treatment in our society is an absolute scandal and we will continue to fight it."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Offices">Offices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Offices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The EHRC has four offices:  in London (in Salisbury Square, EC4), in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> (in the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Arndale" title="Manchester Arndale">Arndale Centre</a>), in Cardiff (in Gabalfa), and in Glasgow (on West George Street).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Equality_and_Human_Rights_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.equalityhumanrights.com/en/who-we-are/our-commissioners-committees-and-governance">"Our Commissioners, Committees and Governance | Equality and Human Rights Commission"</a>. <i>www.equalityhumanrights.com</i><span class="reference-accessdate">.
