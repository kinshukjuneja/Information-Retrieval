lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">The organisation issuing 2011 census data for Scotland is the <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_for_Scotland" title="General Register Office for Scotland">General Register Office for Scotland</a> (GROS). For the organisation that took over GROS in April 2011, see <a href="/wiki/National_Records_of_Scotland" title="National Records of Scotland">National Records of Scotland</a> (NRS).</div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_UK_census_logo.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cd/2011_UK_census_logo.svg/140px-2011_UK_census_logo.svg.png" width="140" height="153" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cd/2011_UK_census_logo.svg/210px-2011_UK_census_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cd/2011_UK_census_logo.svg/280px-2011_UK_census_logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="302" data-file-height="331" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_UK_census_logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The English language logo for "2011 Census for England, Northern Ireland and Wales"</div></div></div>\n<p>A <a href="/wiki/Census_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Census in the United Kingdom">census of the population of the United Kingdom</a> is taken every ten years. The <b>2011 census</b> was held in all countries of the UK on 27 March 2011. It was the first UK census which could be completed online via the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a> (ONS) is responsible for the census in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, the <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_for_Scotland" title="General Register Office for Scotland">General Register Office for Scotland</a> (GROS) is responsible for the census in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Statistics_and_Research_Agency" title="Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency">Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency</a> (NISRA) is responsible for the census in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>.\n</p><p>The Office for National Statistics is the executive office of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Statistics_Authority" title="UK Statistics Authority">UK Statistics Authority</a>, a non-ministerial department formed in 2008 and which reports directly to Parliament. ONS is the UK Government\'s single largest statistical producer of independent statistics on the UK\'s economy and society, used to assist the planning and allocation of resources, policy-making and decision-making.<sup id="cite_ref-ons.gov.uk_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ons.gov.uk-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> ONS designs, manages and runs the census in England and Wales. In its capacity as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_national_and_international_statistical_services" title="List of national and international statistical services">national statistics office</a> for the United Kingdom, ONS also compiles and releases census tables for the United Kingdom when the data from England and Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland are complete.\n</p><p>In the run-up to the census both the main UK political parties expressed concerns about the increasing cost and the value for money of the census, and it was suggested that the 2011 census might be the last decennial census to be taken.<sup id="cite_ref-Beyond2011background_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beyond2011background-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The first results from the 2011 census, age and sex, and occupied households estimates for England and Wales and Northern Ireland, were released on 16 July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The first results for Scotland,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the first UK-wide results, were published on 17 December 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> More detailed and specialised data were published from 2013.\n</p>\n<div class="toclimit-3"><div id="toc" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Registrar General John Rickman conducted the first census of Great Britain\'s population, and was responsible for the ten-yearly reports published between 1801 and 1831. During the first 100 years of census-taking the population of England and Wales grew more than threefold, to around 32 million, and that of Scotland, where a separate census has been carried out since 1861, to about 4.5 million.\n</p><p>From 1911 onwards rapid social change, scientific breakthroughs, and major world events affected the structure of the population. A fire that destroyed census records in 1931, and the declaration of war in 1939, made the 1951 census hugely significant in recording 30 years of change over one of the most turbulent periods in British history.\n</p><p>The 1971 census was run by the newly created Office of Population Censuses and Surveys (OPCS), a body formed by the merger of the General Register Office and Government Social Survey. In 1996 the Office for National Statistics (ONS) was formed by merging the Central Statistical Office (CSO), OPCS and the statistics division of the Department of Employment; the first census it ran was in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-ons.gov.uk_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ons.gov.uk-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2008 the UK Statistics Authority was established as an independent body.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">Purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A population census is a key instrument for assessing the needs of local communities. When related to other data sources such as housing or agricultural censuses, or sample surveys, the data becomes even more useful. Most countries of the world take censuses: the United Nations recommends that countries take a census at least once every ten years. \nThe design for the 2011 census reflects changes in society since 2001 and asks questions to help paint a detailed demographic picture of England and Wales, as it stands on census day, 27 March. \nData collected by the census is used to provide statistical outputs which central government uses to plan and allocate local authority services funding, and which local authorities themselves use to identify and meet the needs of their local communities. Other organisations that use census data include healthcare organisations, community groups, researchers and businesses. The questionnaires, including people\'s personal information, are kept confidential for 100 years before being released to the public, providing an important source of information for historical, demographic and genealogy research.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_Census_for_England_and_Wales">2011 class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The 2011 census for England and Wales included around 25 million households. Questionnaires were posted out to all households, using a national address register compiled by the Office for National Statistics (ONS) with the help of local authorities through comparisons of the National Land and Property Gazetteer (NLPG) and the Royal Mail and Ordnance Survey national address products.\n</p><p>People could complete and submit their questionnaire online, or fill it in on paper and post it back in a pre-addressed envelope. Guidance was provided online and through the census helpline. Completed questionnaires were electronically tracked and field staff followed up with households that did not return a questionnaire. Special arrangements were made to count people living in communal establishments such as; boarding schools, prisons, military bases, hospitals, care homes, student halls of residence, hotels, royal apartments and embassies, as well as for particular communities; rough sleepers, travellers and those living on waterways. In these cases field staff delivered and collected questionnaires and, where needed, provided advice or assistance in completing the questionnaire.\n</p><p>There was a legal requirement to complete the 2011 census questionnaire, under the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Census_Act_1920" title="Census Act 1920">Census Act 1920</a>. As at 27 March 2011 everyone who had lived or intended to live in the country for three months or more was required to complete a questionnaire. Failure to return a completed questionnaire could lead to a fine and criminal record.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a href="/wiki/File:UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/02/UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg/140px-UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg" width="140" height="203" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/02/UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg/210px-UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/02/UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg 2x" data-file-width="262" data-file-height="380" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:UK_2011_Census_Form.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Front page of the 2011 census form.</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin_UK" title="Lockheed Martin UK">Lockheed Martin UK</a>, the UK arm of US-based <a href="/wiki/Aerospace_manufacturer" title="Aerospace manufacturer">aerospace</a>, and technology company <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">Lockheed Martin</a> was awarded the contract to provide services for the census comprising questionnaire printing, a customer contact centre and data capture and processing. The Order for the 2011  (including, census date and who should complete the questionnaire) was laid before Parliament in October 2009 and was approved by Parliament and became law in December 2009.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Capita_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Capita Group">Capita Group</a> was contracted by ONS to recruit, train and administer the pay for the 35,000 temporary ONS workers who worked as field staff for the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Costs_and_value_for_money"><span id="Costs_and_value_of_money"></span>Costs and value for money</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Costs and value for money">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The total cost of the 2011 Census in England and Wales over the period from 2004/05 to 2015/16 is estimated to be \xc2\xa3482 million.<sup id="cite_ref-SNSG5230_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SNSG5230-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> This is more than twice the \xc2\xa3210m spent on the 2001 census.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> This breaks down to a cost of 87 pence per person, per year (over the life of the census \xe2\x80\x93 ten years). \xe2\x80\x9cThe cost equates to about 87p a year per person, demonstrating excellent value for money. The per capita costs in the UK are less than for many other European countries that carry out similar censuses. In summary, this census will meet crucial requirements for statistical information that Government and others cannot do without.\xe2\x80\x9d Minister of State, Cabinet Office (Angela E. Smith).<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Both the main UK political parties had expressed concerns about the rising costs and value of a ten-yearly census, and on coming into office the <a href="/wiki/Cameron%E2%80%93Clegg_coalition" title="Cameron\xe2\x80\x93Clegg coalition">UK coalition government</a> had gone as far as suggesting that the 2011 census might be the last of its kind. In July 2010 the UK government asked ONS to explore other methods of measuring the population. In 2011 the three national statistics bodies, ONS, GROS and NISRA set up a co-ordinated research project known as <a href="/wiki/Beyond_2011" title="Beyond 2011">Beyond 2011</a>. The objectives of the programme were to assess the feasibility of improving UK population statistics using integrated data sources to replace or complement existing approaches, and whether alternative data sources could provide the priority statistics on the characteristics of small populations typically provided by a census.<sup id="cite_ref-Beyond2011background_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beyond2011background-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The project reported its findings in March 2014 and recommended that a UK-wide <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2021" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2021">census in 2021</a> should take place, and that better use should be made of other demographic data sources.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_from_2001_census">Changes from 2001 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The United Kingdom Statistics Authority is responsible for coordinating the release of census data by the devolved statistics authorities. It publishes UK-wide census data results via the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ons.gov.uk/ons/guide-method/census/2011/uk-census/index.html">Office for National Statistics (ONS)</a> site. The UK Statistics Authority also provides a central point of reference for all country-specific census data releases via its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.statistics.gov.uk/hub/index.html">Publications Hub</a> site.\n</p><p>Primary responsibility for country-specific 2011 census data rests with the statistical authorities for each of the UK\'s constituent countries. Each authority has at least one dedicated central source from which data can be downloaded or ordered. For England and Wales the ONS provides the access to primary data via its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ons.gov.uk/ons/guide-method/census/2011/index.html">2011 census site</a>. Additionally, data organised by local authority or post code is available on the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/">Neighbourhood Statistics</a> site, and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.nomisweb.co.uk/census/2011">nomis, a source of detailed census results which can be assembled into bespoke data sets</a>. For Scotland the General Registrar Office for Scotland (GROS) part of National Records of Scotland (NRS) to maintain access via its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20100527044844/http://www.scotlandscensus.gov.uk/en/">Scotlands Census</a> site, and for Northern Ireland the Statistics and Research Authority (NISRA) uses the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ninis2.nisra.gov.uk/public/Theme.aspx">Northern Ireland Neighbourhood Information Service (NINIS)</a>.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-defined_statistical_tables">Pre-defined statistical tables</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_census,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=96" title="Edit section: Pre-defined statistical tables">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The format of all the pre-defined statistical tables is standardised as far as practical across the publishing authorities.
