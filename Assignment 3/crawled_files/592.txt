href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university">research university</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, in the United Kingdom. It offers <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Postgraduate degree">postgraduate degrees</a> in <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a> to students from over 60 countries;<sup id="cite_ref-Review_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> it is the only entirely postgraduate art and design university in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-alice_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alice-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap">:<span>118</span></sup> From 2015 onwards, the RCA has consistently placed first in the <a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS World University Rankings</a> in the Art and Design subject area.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_College_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The RCA was founded in <a href="/wiki/Somerset_House" title="Somerset House">Somerset House</a> in 1837 as the <b>Government School of Design</b><sup id="cite_ref-aim25_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aim25-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> or <b>Metropolitan School of Design</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-riba_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riba-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burchett" title="Richard Burchett">Richard Burchett</a> became head of the school in 1852.<sup id="cite_ref-dnb_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 1853 it was expanded and moved to <a href="/wiki/Marlborough_House" title="Marlborough House">Marlborough House</a>, and then, in 1853<sup id="cite_ref-aim25_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aim25-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> or 1857,<sup id="cite_ref-riba_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riba-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dnb_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/South_Kensington" title="South Kensington">South Kensington</a>, on the same site as the <a href="/wiki/South_Kensington_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="South Kensington Museum">South Kensington Museum</a>. It was renamed the <b>Normal Training School of Art</b> in 1857<sup id="cite_ref-riba_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riba-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the <b>National Art Training School</b> in 1863.<sup id="cite_ref-dnb_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> During the later 19th century it was primarily a <a href="/wiki/Teacher_training_college" class="mw-redirect" title="Teacher training college">teacher training college</a>; pupils during this period included <a href="/wiki/George_Clausen" title="George Clausen">George Clausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Dresser" title="Christopher Dresser">Christopher Dresser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luke_Fildes" title="Luke Fildes">Luke Fildes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kate_Greenaway" title="Kate Greenaway">Kate Greenaway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gertrude_Jekyll" title="Gertrude Jekyll">Gertrude Jekyll</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dnb_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dnb-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1896<sup id="cite_ref-aim25_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aim25-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> or 1897<sup id="cite_ref-riba_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riba-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> the school received the name <b>Royal College of Art</b>, and the emphasis of teaching there shifted to the practice of art and design.<sup id="cite_ref-aim25_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aim25-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Teaching of <a href="/wiki/Graphic_design" title="Graphic design">graphic design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_design" title="Industrial design">industrial design</a> and <a href="/wiki/Product_design" title="Product design">product design</a> began in the mid-twentieth century. The school expanded further in the 1960s, and in 1967 it received a  <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a> which gave it the status of an independent university with the power to grant its own degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-aim25_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aim25-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_College_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Darwin_Building,_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London,_spring_2013.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Darwin_Building%2C_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London%2C_spring_2013.JPG/220px-Darwin_Building%2C_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London%2C_spring_2013.JPG" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Darwin_Building%2C_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London%2C_spring_2013.JPG/330px-Darwin_Building%2C_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London%2C_spring_2013.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Darwin_Building%2C_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London%2C_spring_2013.JPG/440px-Darwin_Building%2C_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London%2C_spring_2013.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3168" data-file-height="4752" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Darwin_Building,_Royal_College_of_Art_in_London,_spring_2013.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Darwin Building in <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Gore" title="Kensington Gore">Kensington Gore</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The RCA has two campuses, in <a href="/wiki/South_Kensington" title="South Kensington">South Kensington</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Battersea" title="Battersea">Battersea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rca_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rca-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The Darwin Building in <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Gore" title="Kensington Gore">Kensington Gore</a> dates from the 1960s and is a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>. It was designed by a team of RCA staff members, <a href="/wiki/H._T._Cadbury-Brown" title="H. T. Cadbury-Brown">H. T. Cadbury-Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Casson" title="Hugh Casson">Hugh Casson</a> and Robert Goodden.<sup id="cite_ref-dunnett_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dunnett-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1991 the <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculpture</a> department moved to a converted factory across the river <a href="/wiki/Thames" class="mw-redirect" title="Thames">Thames</a> in Battersea. In the early 2000s the college conceived a substantial second campus being created on the site, with a minibus service linking it to Kensington. Thus, after a successful redevelopment of the premises by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wright_%26_Wright&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wright &amp; Wright (page does not exist)">Wright &amp; Wright</a> (budget \xc2\xa34.3m, floor area 2,500 sq m), the present Sculpture Building opened in January 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A masterplan was commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Haworth_Tompkins" title="Haworth Tompkins">Haworth Tompkins</a>  and phase 1 of their three phase design was completed with the opening of the Sackler Building on 19 November 2009, to house the painting department. Its name commemorates a major gift by <a href="/wiki/Mortimer_Sackler" title="Mortimer Sackler">The Dr Mortimer and Theresa Sackler Foundation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Dyson Building, named in honour of <a href="/wiki/James_Dyson" title="James Dyson">James Dyson</a>, whose educational charity donated \xc2\xa35m to the development, was opened on 24 September 2012. It is the home for <a href="/wiki/Printmaking" title="Printmaking">printmaking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>, and contains an innovation wing where start-up designers can launch their businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Woo Building was opened on 30 September 2015, completing the Battersea project. It is named in honour of <a href="/wiki/Woo_Po-shing" title="Woo Po-shing">Sir Po-Shing and Lady Helen Woo</a>, who have funded scholarships at the RCA since the 1990s. It accommodates the Ceramics &amp; Glass and Jewellery &amp; Metal programmes. The building\'s <a href="/wiki/Anodizing" title="Anodizing">anodised aluminium</a> gates were designed by alumnus Max Lamb.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Courses">Courses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_College_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Courses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The RCA offers Graduate Diploma, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">MA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Philosophy" title="Master of Philosophy">MRes, MPhil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> degrees in twenty-eight subject areas, divided into four schools: <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, arts &amp; humanities, <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">communication</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a>. The <a href="/wiki/History_of_design" class="mw-redirect" title="History of design">history of design</a> programme is in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>; there are two double MA/<a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">MSc</a> programmes in <a href="/wiki/Design_engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Design engineering">design engineering</a> with <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College London</a>, one of which also includes periods of study at <a href="/wiki/Keio_University" title="Keio University">Keio University</a> in Tokyo and at the <a href="/wiki/Pratt_Institute" title="Pratt Institute">Pratt Institute</a> in New York.<sup id="cite_ref-rca2_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rca2-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rankings">Rankings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_College_of_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rankings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Research_Assessment_Exercise" title="Research Assessment Exercise">Research Assessment Exercise</a> of December 2008, 40% of the research output of the school received the highest (4* or "world-leading") assessment, the third-highest rating in the art and design subject area; over all subject areas only about fifty institutions received a higher rating.<sup id="cite_ref-rae_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rae-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In April 2011 the RCA was ranked first on a list of UK graduate art schools compiled by <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Painters_(magazine)" title="Modern Painters (magazine)">Modern Painters</a></i> magazine from a survey of professionals in the art world.<sup id="cite_ref-city_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-city-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In August 2015 it was ranked first on a list of master\'s courses in fashion by Business of Fashion, a fashion website.<sup id="cite_ref-guard2_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard2-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bof_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bof-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015 the RCA placed first in the art and design subject area in the <a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS World University Rankings</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Quacquarelli_Symonds" title="Quacquarelli Symonds">Quacquarelli Symonds</a>. In 2016 and 2017 it held onto the position, increasing its overall score from 96.0/100 to 98.4/100.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qs_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qs-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
