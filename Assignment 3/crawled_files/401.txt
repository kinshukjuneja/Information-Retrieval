constituent college of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_London" title="University of the Arts London">University of the Arts London</a> based in London, UK, and is a leading British art and design institution with an international reputation.<sup id="cite_ref-Best_British_Art_Schools_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Best_British_Art_Schools-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It offers <a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further</a> and higher education courses in fine art, <a href="/wiki/Graphic_design" title="Graphic design">graphic design</a>, interior design, <a href="/wiki/Spatial_design" title="Spatial design">spatial design</a> and <a href="/wiki/Textile_design" title="Textile design">textile design</a> up to PhD level.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chelsea_College_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Polytechnic">Polytechnic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chelsea_College_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Polytechnic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Chelsea College of Arts was originally an integral school of the <b>South-Western Polytechnic</b>, which opened at Manresa Road, <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea</a>, in 1895 to provide scientific and technical education to Londoners. Day and evening classes for men and women were held in <a href="/wiki/Domestic_worker" title="Domestic worker">domestic</a> economy, mathematics, engineering, <a href="/wiki/Natural_science" title="Natural science">natural science</a>, art and music. Art was taught from the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_(United_Kingdom)" title="Polytechnic (United Kingdom)">Polytechnic</a>, and included design, <a href="/wiki/Weaving" title="Weaving">weaving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Embroidery" title="Embroidery">embroidery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electrophoretic_deposition" title="Electrophoretic deposition">electrodeposition</a>. The South-Western Polytechnic became the <b>Chelsea Polytechnic</b> in 1922 and taught a growing number of registered students of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>.\n</p><p>At the beginning of the 1930s, the School of Art began to widen, including courses in <a href="/wiki/Craft" title="Craft">craft</a> training and commercial design from 1931. H.S Williamson, the school\'s appointed headmaster from 1930 to 1958, introduced sculpture shortly after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Notable artists from this period were employed as teachers such as <a href="/wiki/Henry_Moore" title="Henry Moore">Henry Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Sutherland" title="Graham Sutherland">Graham Sutherland</a>. Alumni from this period included <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Frink" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth Frink">Elizabeth Frink</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Burra" title="Edward Burra">Edward Burra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Caulfield" title="Patrick Caulfield">Patrick Caulfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Walker" title="Ethel Walker">Ethel Walker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Bogarde" title="Dirk Bogarde">Dirk Bogarde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Clatworthy_(sculptor)" title="Robert Clatworthy (sculptor)">Robert Clatworthy</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Latham_(artist)" title="John Latham (artist)">John Latham</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Berger" title="John Berger">John Berger</a>.\n</p><p>The School of Science separated and became known as the <b>Chelsea College of Science and Technology</b> in 1957, and was later admitted as a constituent College of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a> in 1966. The Chelsea College of Science and Technology was granted its <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a> in 1971 and merged with <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College_London" title="King&#39;s College London">King\'s College London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_College" title="Queen Elizabeth College">Queen Elizabeth College</a> in 1985.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelsea_School_of_Art">Chelsea School of Art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chelsea_College_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chelsea School of Art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The School of Art merged with the <b>Hammersmith School of Art</b>, founded by Francis Hawke, to form the <b>Chelsea School of Art</b> in 1908. The newly formed school was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/London_County_Council" title="London County Council">London County Council</a> and a new building erected at Lime Grove, which opened with an extended curriculum. A trade school for girls was erected on the same site in 1914.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg/300px-Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg" width="300" height="173" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg/450px-Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg/600px-Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="577" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chelsea_College_of_Art_and_Design_-_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Chelsea College of Art and Design (South Block)</div></div></div>\n<p>The school acquired premises at <a href="/wiki/Great_Titchfield_Street" title="Great Titchfield Street">Great Titchfield Street</a>, and was jointly accommodated with <a href="/wiki/Quintin_Hogg_(merchant)" title="Quintin Hogg (merchant)">Quintin Hogg</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_(United_Kingdom)" title="Polytechnic (United Kingdom)">Polytechnic</a> in <a href="/wiki/Regent_Street" title="Regent Street">Regent Street</a> (a forerunner of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Westminster" title="University of Westminster">University of Westminster</a>). The campus at Manresa Road introduced painting and <a href="/wiki/Graphic_design" title="Graphic design">graphic design</a> in 1963, with both disciplines being particularly successful. During this period, Chelsea had the highest enrolment of fine art students in any school of its kind in the country, producing many notable artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ossip_Zadkine" title="Ossip Zadkine">Ossip Zadkine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Gertler_(artist)" title="Mark Gertler (artist)">Mark Gertler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Nash_(artist)" title="Paul Nash (artist)">Paul Nash</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Gowing" title="Lawrence Gowing">Lawrence Gowing</a>, painter and <a href="/wiki/Art_historian" class="mw-redirect" title="Art historian">art historian</a>, was appointed as the first headmaster of the Chelsea School of Art. He was responsible for the integration of history and theory with practice, employing artists rather than art historians to teach art history and theory. This approach remains intrinsic to Chelsea\'s teaching philosophy today.\n</p><p>Under Gowing, an option programme was introduced, which encompassed workshops in experimental music, poetry, <a href="/wiki/Psychoanalysis" title="Psychoanalysis">psychoanalysis</a>, philosophy and <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">anthropology</a>. A basic design course, pioneered by <a href="/wiki/Victor_Pasmore" title="Victor Pasmore">Victor Pasmore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hamilton_(artist)" title="Richard Hamilton (artist)">Richard Hamilton</a>, was also developed during the same period, becoming the basis of the college\'s current <a href="/wiki/Foundation_degree" title="Foundation degree">foundation course</a> in art and design.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Professor William Callaway, Head of School from 1989 to 1992. Colin Cina, appointed Dean of School of Art, and Bridget Jackson, Dean of School of Design. These three reformed the school and ensured the redevelopment of the entire academic program, introducing courses at multiple levels from <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Diploma" title="Higher National Diploma">HND</a> to accredited Honours and Postgraduate degrees. Initially, these were validated by the UK Council for National Academic Awards; i.e. in the short period prior to the London Institute gaining degree-awarding powers. Bridget Jackson was appointed Head of College in 1993, retiring in 1997 to be succeeded by Professor Colin Cina who led the college until his retirement in 2003.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="London_Institute">London Institute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chelsea_College_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: London Institute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Chelsea School of Art became a constituent College of the <b><a href="/wiki/London_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="London Institute">London Institute</a></b> in 1986, formed by the <a href="/wiki/Inner_London_Education_Authority" title="Inner London Education Authority">Inner London Education Authority</a> to associate London\'s art, design, fashion and media schools into a collegiate structure. The school was renamed <b>Chelsea College of Art and Design</b> in 1989. The London Institute was granted University status and was renamed <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_London" title="University of the Arts London">University of the Arts London</a> in 2004. In 2013, the College was renamed Chelsea College of Arts.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2002\xe2\x80\x932003, Professor Roger Wilson was appointed as the Head of College until his retirement in 2006. He led the relocation to the <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed</a> <b>Royal Army Medical College</b>, renovated as a purpose-built art college by the architects <a href="/wiki/Allies_and_Morrison" title="Allies and Morrison">Allies and Morrison</a> in 2005. With this move, the Chelsea College of Arts presently resides next to <a href="/wiki/Tate_Britain" title="Tate Britain">Tate Britain</a> at <a href="/wiki/Millbank" title="Millbank">Millbank</a>, returning to one standalone campus.<sup id="cite_ref-History_(Official)_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History_(Official)-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-History_(AIM25)_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History_(AIM25)-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exhibition">Exhibition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chelsea_College_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Exhibition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The college comprises three notable on-site exhibition spaces:\n</p>\n<ol><li>Chelsea Space is an international and interdisciplinary platform for professional practitioners to exhibit experimental curatorial projects. The gallery also releases regular publications from participating <a href="/wiki/Authors" class="mw-redirect" title="Authors">authors</a>, artists and designers.</li>\n<li>The Parade Ground, situated within the college, has been transformed into London\'s largest open-air gallery hosting events from film screenings to large scale installations in spring 2008. The exhibition ground had previously been used for students and professionals as an open area platform, notably artist <a href="/wiki/Chris_Burden" title="Chris Burden">Chris Burden</a>\'s \'A Flying Steamroller\' in 2006. Recent exhibitions include <a href="/wiki/Cildo_Meireles" title="Cildo Meireles">Cildo Meireles</a>\'s \'Occasion\', held in association with his exhibition at <a href="/wiki/Tate_Modern" title="Tate Modern">Tate Modern</a> in 2008.</li>\n<li>The Triangle Gallery, pertaining to its name, has been designed as a modern angular shaped space for students to show their work throughout the year.</li></ol>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
