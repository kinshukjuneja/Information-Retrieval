href="/w/index.php?title=Allied_Rapid_Reaction_Corps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ARRC was created on 2 October 1992 in <a href="/wiki/Bielefeld" title="Bielefeld">Bielefeld</a> based on the former <a href="/wiki/I_Corps_(United_Kingdom)" title="I Corps (United Kingdom)">British I Corps</a> (or I (BR) Corps).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It was originally created as the rapid reaction corps sized land force of the Reaction Forces Concept that emerged after the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, with a mission to redeploy and reinforce within Allied Command Europe (ACE) and to conduct <a href="/wiki/Petersberg_tasks" class="mw-redirect" title="Petersberg tasks">Petersberg missions</a> out of NATO territory. The first commander, appointed in 1992 was General Sir <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Mackenzie" title="Jeremy Mackenzie">Jeremy Mackenzie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1994 the ARRC was based in the <a href="/wiki/Rheindahlen_Military_Complex" class="mw-redirect" title="Rheindahlen Military Complex">Rheindahlen Military Complex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It commanded the Land Forces of NATO\'s first ever deployment as part of the <a href="/wiki/IFOR" class="mw-redirect" title="IFOR">IFOR</a> operation in <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia</a> in 1995/6 and was again deployed as the headquarters commanding Land Forces during the <a href="/wiki/Kosovo_War" title="Kosovo War">Kosovo War</a> in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since 2002 however the HQ has been re-roled (with five other corps HQs of other NATO nations) as a High Readiness Force (Land) HQ (HRF(L)) with a broader mission.  The formation HQ is under Operational Command of <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Headquarters_Allied_Powers_Europe" title="Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe">Supreme Allied Commander, Europe</a> (SACEUR). The ARRC has a national Force Pool of Combat, Combat Support and Combat Service Support units with which to train and execute its mission. However, in reality COMARRC commands no forces until he receives an Activation Order from SACEUR. On receipt of ACTORD, forces from troop contributing nations, generated through the NATO Force Generation process are passed into his Operational Command for the duration of the operational deployment.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>ARRC took command of the International Security Assistance Force in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> on 4 May 2006 and then relocated from Rheindahlen to <a href="/wiki/Imjin_Barracks" title="Imjin Barracks">Imjin Barracks</a>, outside <a href="/wiki/Gloucester" title="Gloucester">Gloucester</a> in England, in 2010<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> before deploying to support the <a href="/wiki/ISAF" class="mw-redirect" title="ISAF">ISAF</a> Joint Command Headquarters in Afghanistan in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
