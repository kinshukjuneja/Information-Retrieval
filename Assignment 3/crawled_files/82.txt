/>.gov<wbr />.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>South Gloucestershire Council</b> is the local authority of <a href="/wiki/South_Gloucestershire" title="South Gloucestershire">South Gloucestershire</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority</a> in the South West of England region. As a unitary authority it has the powers of a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_county" title="Non-metropolitan county">non-metropolitan county</a> and <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_England" title="Districts of England">district council</a> combined. As such, it is administratively separate from the rest of <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>.\n</p><p>The council area elects 70 Councillors from 35 wards. Following the May 2015 local elections, South Gloucestershire Council comprises 40 Conservative Councillors, 16 Liberal Democrat Councillors, and 14 Labour Councillors. The current Chair of South Gloucestershire Council is Ian Blair (Liberal Democrat) and the current Vice Chair is Rachael Hunt (Conservative).\n</p><p>From 2019 onwards, South Gloucestershire Council will have revised ward boundaries following the enacted recommendations of the Local Government Boundary Commission for England. South Gloucestershire will be represented by 61 councillors (9 fewer than at present) in 28 wards and those councillors will represent 9 three-councillor wards, 15 two-councillor wards and 4 one-councillor wards.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_functions">Powers and functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Gloucestershire_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Powers and functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The local authority derives its powers and functions from the Local Government Act 1972 and subsequent legislation. For the purposes of local government, South Gloucestershire is a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">non-metropolitan</a> area of England. As a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authority</a>, South Gloucestershire Council has the powers and functions of both a non-metropolitan county and district council combined. In its capacity as a district council it is a <a href="/wiki/Billing_authorities_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Billing authorities in England">billing authority</a> collecting Council Tax and business rates, it processes local planning applications, it is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Council_house" title="Council house">housing</a>, waste collection and environmental health. In its capacity as a county council, it is a <a href="/wiki/Local_education_authority" title="Local education authority">local education authority</a>, responsible for social services, libraries and waste disposal.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
