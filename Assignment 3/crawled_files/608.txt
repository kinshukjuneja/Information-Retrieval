template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Accession of the United Kingdom to the <a href="/wiki/European_Communities" title="European Communities">European Communities (EC)</a></b> \xe2\x80\x93 the collective term for the <a href="/wiki/European_Coal_and_Steel_Community" title="European Coal and Steel Community">European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC)</a>, the <a href="/wiki/European_Economic_Community" title="European Economic Community">European Economic Community (EEC)</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Atomic_Energy_Community" title="European Atomic Energy Community">European Atomic Energy Community (EAEC)</a> \xe2\x80\x93 took effect on 1 January 1973. This followed ratification of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Accession_1972" title="Treaty of Accession 1972">Accession treaty</a> which was signed in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> on 22 January 1972 by the Conservative prime minister <a href="/wiki/Edward_Heath" title="Edward Heath">Edward Heath</a>, who had pursued the UK\'s application to the EEC since the late 1950s. Denmark and Ireland also joined as part of the same <a href="/wiki/1973_enlargement_of_the_European_Communities" title="1973 enlargement of the European Communities">expansion</a> but Norway, who had signed the treaty, declined to ratify it and so it was amended to exclude that country.<sup id="cite_ref-EUED_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EUED-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The ECSC and EEC would later be integrated into the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Maastricht_Treaty" title="Maastricht Treaty">Maastricht</a> and (subsequently) <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Lisbon" title="Treaty of Lisbon">Lisbon</a> treaties.\n</p><p>The UK was the first country to establish a <a href="/wiki/UK_embassy" class="mw-redirect" title="UK embassy">Delegation</a> to the ECSC in 1952 and the first country to sign an <a href="/wiki/EU_Association_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="EU Association Agreement">Association Agreement</a> with the Community in 1954. \n</p><p>The UK had first applied to join in 1961, but this was vetoed by French President <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a>.\n</p><p>After de Gaulle had relinquished the French presidency in 1969, the UK made a third and successful application for membership. The question of sovereignty was discussed at the time in an official <a href="/wiki/Foreign_and_Commonwealth_Office" title="Foreign and Commonwealth Office">Foreign and Commonwealth Office</a> document. It listed among "Areas of policy in which parliamentary freedom to legislate will be affected by entry into the European Communities": Customs duties, Agriculture, Free movement of labour, services and capital, Transport, and Social Security for migrant workers. The document concluded (paragraph 26) that it was advisable to put the considerations of influence and power before those of formal sovereignty.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The UK\'s negotiation team in 1970\xe2\x80\x9372 included <a href="/wiki/Con_O%27Neill_(diplomat)" title="Con O&#39;Neill (diplomat)">Con O\'Neill</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Hannay,_Baron_Hannay_of_Chiswick" title="David Hannay, Baron Hannay of Chiswick">David Hannay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BEEC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BEEC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Accession_1972" title="Treaty of Accession 1972">Treaty of Accession</a> was signed in January 1972 by prime minister <a href="/wiki/Edward_Heath" title="Edward Heath">Edward Heath</a>, leader of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Parliament\'s <a href="/wiki/European_Communities_Act_1972_(UK)" title="European Communities Act 1972 (UK)">European Communities Act 1972</a> was enacted on 17 October, and the UK\'s instrument of ratification was deposited the next day (18 October),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> letting the United Kingdom\'s membership of the EC come into effect on 1 January 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><br />\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Accession_of_the_United_Kingdom_to_the_European_Communities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Shortly after the creation of the ECSC in 1952, the UK became the first country to establish a Delegation in Luxembourg, the seat of the High Authority (present-day European Commission) at the time. On 24 December 1953 the High Authority invited the British Government to enter into negotiations for the establishment of an association. On 29 April 1954 the British Government invited the High Authority to London to begin discussions on the proposed association and on 21 December 1954 the Agreement of Association was signed in London entering into force on 23 September 1955. This was the first example of an <a href="/wiki/EU_Association_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="EU Association Agreement">EU Association Agreement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Agreement of Association established a Standing Council of Association which was intended to provide \'a means for the continuous exchange of information and for consultation in regard to matters of common interest concerning coal and steel\' (Article 6). The first meeting took place on 17 November 1955 in Luxembourg. At the second meeting on 22 March 1956 in London, the High Authority and the British Government agreed to open tariff negotiations.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_EEC_application_and_French_veto">First EEC application and French veto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
