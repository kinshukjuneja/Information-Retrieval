For Canadian TV show, see <a href="/wiki/The_Sunshine_Hour" title="The Sunshine Hour">The Sunshine Hour</a>.</div>\n<p><b>Sunshine duration</b> or <b>sunshine hours</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Climatological" class="mw-redirect" title="Climatological">climatological</a> indicator, measuring duration of <a href="/wiki/Sunshine" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunshine">sunshine</a> in given period (usually, a day or a year) for a given location on Earth, typically expressed as an averaged value over several years. It is a general indicator of <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">cloudiness</a> of a location, and thus differs from <a href="/wiki/Insolation" class="mw-redirect" title="Insolation">insolation</a>, which measures the total <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> delivered by sunlight over a given period.\n</p><p>Sunshine duration is usually expressed in hours per year, or in (average) hours per day. The first measure indicates the general sunniness of a location compared with other places, while the latter allows for comparison of sunshine in various seasons in the same location.<sup id="cite_ref-cimo_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cimo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Another often-used measure is percentage ratio of recorded bright sunshine duration and <a href="#daylight_duration">daylight duration</a> in the observed period.\n</p><p>An important use of sunshine duration data is to characterize the climate of sites, especially of health resorts. This also takes into account the psychological effect of strong solar light on human well-being. It is often used to promote tourist destinations.<sup id="cite_ref-cimo_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cimo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sunshine_duration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Daytime duration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Refracci%C3%B3n.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Refracci%C3%B3n.png/220px-Refracci%C3%B3n.png" width="220" height="82" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Refracci%C3%B3n.png/330px-Refracci%C3%B3n.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5d/Refracci%C3%B3n.png 2x" data-file-width="388" data-file-height="144" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Refracci%C3%B3n.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Diagram showing displacement of the Sun\'s image at sunrise and sunset</div></div></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Daytime_(astronomy)" class="mw-redirect" title="Daytime (astronomy)">Daytime (astronomy)</a></div>\n<p>If the Sun were to be above the horizon 50% of the time for a standard year consisting of 8,760 hours, apparent maximal daytime duration would be 4,380 hours for any point on Earth. However, there are physical and astronomical effects that change that picture. Namely, <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_refraction" title="Atmospheric refraction">atmospheric refraction</a> allows the Sun to be still visible even when it physically sets below the <a href="/wiki/Horizon" title="Horizon">horizon</a>. For that reason, average daytime (disregarding cloud effects) is longest in polar areas, where the apparent Sun spends the most time around the horizon. Places on the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a> have the longest total annual daytime, 4,647 hours, while the <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">North Pole</a> receives 4,575. Because of elliptic nature of the <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_orbit" title="Earth&#39;s orbit">Earth\'s orbit</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a> is not symmetrical: the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Circle" title="Antarctic Circle">Antarctic Circle</a>, with 4,530 hours of daylight, receives five days less of sunshine than its <a href="/wiki/Antipodes" title="Antipodes">antipodes</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">Equator</a> has a total daytime of 4,422 hours per year.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition_and_measurement">Definition and measurement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sunshine_duration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Definition and measurement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg/220px-Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg/330px-Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg/440px-Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1500" data-file-height="1125" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Campbell-Stokes_recorder.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Campbell\xe2\x80\x93Stokes recorder measures sunshine</div></div></div>\n<p>Given the theoretical maximum of daytime duration for a given location, there is also a practical consideration at which point the amount of daylight is sufficient to be treated as a "sunshine hour". "Bright" sunshine hours represent the total hours when the sunlight is stronger than a specified threshold, as opposed to just "visible" hours. "Visible" sunshine, for example, occurs around sunrise and sunset, but is not strong enough to excite the sensor. Measurement is performed by instruments called <a href="/wiki/Sunshine_recorders" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunshine recorders">sunshine recorders</a>. For the specific purpose of sunshine duration recording, <a href="/wiki/Campbell%E2%80%93Stokes_recorder" title="Campbell\xe2\x80\x93Stokes recorder">Campbell\xe2\x80\x93Stokes recorders</a> are used, which use a spherical glass <a href="/wiki/Lens_(optics)" title="Lens (optics)">lens</a> to focus the sun rays on a specially designed tape. When the intensity exceeds a pre-determined threshold, the tape burns. The total length of the burn trace is proportional to the number of bright hours.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Another type of recorder is the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_sunshine_recorder" title="Jordan sunshine recorder">Jordan sunshine recorder</a>. Newer, electronic recorders have more stable sensitivity than that of the paper tape.\n</p><p>In order to harmonize the data measured worldwide, in 1962 the <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">World Meteorological Organization</a> (WMO) defined a standardized design of the Campbell\xe2\x80\x93Stokes recorder, called an Interim Reference Sunshine Recorder (IRSR).<sup id="cite_ref-cimo_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cimo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2003, the sunshine duration was finally defined as the period during which direct solar <a href="/wiki/Irradiance" title="Irradiance">irradiance</a> exceeds a threshold value of 120&#160;W/m\xc2\xb2.<sup id="cite_ref-cimo_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cimo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">Geographic distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span duration follows a general geographic pattern: <a href="/wiki/Horse_latitude" class="mw-redirect" title="Horse latitude">subtropical latitudes</a> (about 25\xc2\xb0 to 40\xc2\xb0 north/south) have the highest sunshine values, because these are the locations of the eastern sides of the <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_high" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical high">subtropical high pressure systems</a>, associated with the large-scale descent of air from the upper-level tropopause. Many of the world\'s driest climates are found adjacent to the eastern sides of the subtropical highs, which create stable atmospheric conditions, little convective overturning, and little moisture and cloud cover. Desert regions, with nearly constant high pressure aloft and rare condensation\xe2\x80\x94like <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">Southwestern United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>\xe2\x80\x94are examples of hot, sunny, dry climates where sunshine duration values are very high.\n</p><p>The two major areas with the highest sunshine duration, measured as annual average, are the central and the eastern <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>\xe2\x80\x94covering vast, mainly desert countries such as <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>\xe2\x80\x94and the Southwestern United States (<a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-sunniest_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunniest-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The city claiming the official title of the sunniest in the world is <a href="/wiki/Yuma,_Arizona" title="Yuma, Arizona">Yuma, Arizona</a>, with over 4,000&#160;hours (about 91% of daylight time) of bright sunshine annually,<sup id="cite_ref-sunniest_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunniest-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but many climatological books suggest there may be sunnier areas in North Africa.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the belt encompassing northern Chad and the <a href="/wiki/Tibesti_Mountains" title="Tibesti Mountains">Tibesti Mountains</a>, northern Sudan, southern Libya, and <a href="/wiki/Upper_Egypt" title="Upper Egypt">Upper Egypt</a>, annual sunshine duration is estimated at over 4,000 hours. There is also a smaller, isolated area of sunshine maximum in the heart of the western section of the Sahara Desert around the Eglab Massif and the <a href="/wiki/Erg_Chech" title="Erg Chech">Erg Chech</a>, along the borders of <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> where the 4,000-hour mark is exceeded, too.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Some places in the interior of the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> receive 3,600\xe2\x80\x933,800&#160;hours of bright sunshine annually. The largest sun-baked region in the world (over 3,000&#160;hours of yearly sunshine) is North Africa. The sunniest month in the world is December in Eastern Antarctica, with almost 23 hours of bright sun daily.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Conversely, higher latitudes (above 50\xc2\xb0 north/south) lying in stormy westerlies have much cloudier and more unstable and <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">rainy</a> weather, and often have the lowest values of sunshine duration annually. Temperate oceanic climates like those in <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_Europe" title="Northwestern Europe">northwestern Europe</a>, the western coast of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, and areas of New Zealand\'s <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a> are examples of cool, cloudy, wet, humid climates where cloudless sunshine duration values are very low. The areas with the lowest sunshine duration annually lie mostly over the polar oceans, as well as parts of northern Europe, southern Alaska, northern Russia, and areas near the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Okhotsk" title="Sea of Okhotsk">Sea of Okhotsk</a>. The cloudiest place in the United States is <a href="/wiki/Cold_Bay,_Alaska" title="Cold Bay, Alaska">Cold Bay, Alaska</a>, with an average of 304 days of heavy overcast (covering over 3/4 of the sky).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In addition to these polar oceanic climates, certain low-latitude basins enclosed by mountains, like the <a href="/wiki/Sichuan_Basin" title="Sichuan Basin">Sichuan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Basin" title="Taipei Basin">Taipei Basins</a>, can have sunshine duration as low as 1,000 hours per year, as cool air consistently sinks to form fogs that winds cannot dissipate.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B3rshavn" title="T\xc3\xb3rshavn">T\xc3\xb3rshavn</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a> is among the cloudiest places in the world with yearly only 840 sunshine hours.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sunshine_duration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_by_sunshine_duration" title="List of cities by sunshine duration">List of cities by sunshine duration</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sunshine_duration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
