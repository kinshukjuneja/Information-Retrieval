links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_and_extent">Name and extent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name and extent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The English name for the area derives from <a href="/wiki/Snowdon" title="Snowdon">Snowdon</a>, which is the highest mountain in Wales at 3560&#160;ft (1,085&#160;m). In <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>, the area is named <i lang="cy" title="Welsh language text">Eryri</i>. A commonly held belief is that the name is derived from <i lang="cy"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/eryr#Welsh" class="extiw" title="wikt:eryr">eryr</a></i> ("eagle"), and thus means \'the abode/land of eagles\',<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but recent evidence is that it means <i>Highlands</i>, and is related to the Latin <i lang="la"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/oriri#Latin" class="extiw" title="wikt:oriri">oriri</a></i> (to rise)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> as leading Welsh scholar Sir <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Ifor_Williams" title="Ifor Williams">Ifor Williams</a></span> proved.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The term <i lang="cy" title="Welsh language text">Eryri</i> first appeared in a manuscript in the 9th-century <i lang="la" title="Latin language text"><a href="/wiki/Historia_Brittonum" title="Historia Brittonum">Historia Brittonum</a></i>, in an account of the downfall of the semi-legendary 5th-century king <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text">Gwrtheyrn</span> (<a href="/wiki/Vortigern" title="Vortigern">Vortigern</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> the title <i>Prince of Wales and Lord of Snowdonia</i> (<i lang="cy" title="Welsh language text">Tywysog Cymru ac Arglwydd Eryri</i>) was used by <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Llywelyn_ap_Gruffudd" title="Llywelyn ap Gruffudd">Llywelyn ap Gruffudd</a></span>; his grandfather <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Llywelyn_Fawr" class="mw-redirect" title="Llywelyn Fawr">Llywelyn Fawr</a></span> used the title <i>Prince of north Wales and Lord of Snowdonia.</i>\n</p><p>Before the boundaries of the national park were designated, "Snowdonia" was generally used to refer to a smaller area, namely the upland area of northern <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Gwynedd" title="Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a></span> centred on the <a href="/wiki/Snowdon" title="Snowdon">Snowdon</a> massif, whereas the national park covers an area more than twice that size extending far to the south into <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Meirionnydd" title="Meirionnydd">Meirionnydd</a></span>. This is apparent in books published prior to 1951, such as the classic travelogue <i>Wild Wales</i> by <a href="/wiki/George_Borrow" title="George Borrow">George Borrow</a> (1862) and <i>The Mountains of Snowdonia</i> by H. Carr &amp; G. Lister (1925). F. J. North, as editor of the book <i>Snowdonia</i> (1949), states "When the Committee delineated provisional boundaries, they included areas some distance beyond Snowdonia proper." The traditional Snowdonia thus includes the ranges of Snowdon and its satellites, the <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Glyderau" title="Glyderau">Glyderau</a></span>, the <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Carneddau" title="Carneddau">Carneddau</a></span> and the <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Moel_Siabod" title="Moel Siabod">Moel Siabod</a></span> group. It does not include the hills to the south of <span lang="cy" style="font-style:normal;" title="Welsh language text"><a href="/wiki/Maentwrog" title="Maentwrog">Maentwrog</a></span>. As <i lang="cy" title="Welsh language text">Eryri</i> (see above), this area has a unique place in Welsh history, tradition and culture.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Snowdonia_National_Park">Snowdonia National Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Snowdonia National Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Snowdonia National Park (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>: <i lang="cy">Parc Cenedlaethol Eryri</i>) was established in 1951 as the third <a href="/wiki/National_parks_of_England_and_Wales" title="National parks of England and Wales">national park</a> in Britain, following the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lake_District" title="Lake District">Lake District</a>. It covers 827 square miles (2,140&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), and has 37 miles (60&#160;km) of coastline.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The park is governed by the <a href="/wiki/Snowdonia_National_Park_Authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Snowdonia National Park Authority">Snowdonia National Park Authority</a>, which is made up of local government and Welsh representatives, and its main offices are at <a href="/wiki/Penrhyndeudraeth" title="Penrhyndeudraeth">Penrhyndeudraeth</a>. Unlike national parks in other countries, Snowdonia (and other such parks in Britain) are made up of both public and private lands under central planning authority. It is governed by the 18-member Snowdonia National Park Authority; nine members are appointed by Gwynedd, 3 by Conwy, and the remaining six by the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_for_Wales" title="National Assembly for Wales">National Assembly for Wales</a> to represent the national interest.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Unusually, Snowdonia National Park has a hole in the middle, around the town of <a href="/wiki/Blaenau_Ffestiniog" title="Blaenau Ffestiniog">Blaenau Ffestiniog</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Slate" title="Slate">slate</a> <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">quarrying</a> centre. This was deliberately excluded from the park when it was set up to allow the development of new light industry to replace the reduced <a href="/wiki/Slate_industry_in_Wales" title="Slate industry in Wales">slate industry</a>. (There is a similar situation in the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Peak District National Park">Peak District National Park</a> where the boundaries were drawn to exclude large built-up areas and industrial sites from the park with the town of Buxton and the adjacent quarries outside but surrounded on three sides by the park.)\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Snowdonia_Society" title="Snowdonia Society">Snowdonia Society</a> is a registered charity formed in 1967. It is a voluntary group of people with an interest in the area and its protection. \n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Amory_Lovins" title="Amory Lovins">Amory Lovins</a> led the successful 1970s opposition to stop <a href="/wiki/Rio_Tinto_Group" title="Rio Tinto Group">Rio Tinto</a> digging up the area for a massive mine.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Research indicates that there were 3.67 million visitors to Snowdonia National Park in 2013, with approximately 9.74 million tourist days spent in the park during that year.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Total tourist expenditure was \xc2\xa3433.6 million in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountain_ranges">Mountain ranges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mountain ranges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Snowdonia may be divided into four areas:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The northernmost area is the most popular with tourists, and includes (from west to east) <a href="/wiki/Moel_Hebog" title="Moel Hebog">Moel Hebog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mynydd_Mawr" title="Mynydd Mawr">Mynydd Mawr</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nantlle_Ridge" title="Nantlle Ridge">Nantlle Ridge</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Snowdon_Massif" title="Snowdon Massif">Snowdon Massif</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Glyderau" title="Glyderau">Glyderau</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Carneddau" title="Carneddau">Carneddau</a>. These last three groups are the highest mountains in Wales, and include all Wales\' <a href="/wiki/Welsh_3000s" title="Welsh 3000s">3000-foot mountains</a>.</li>\n<li>The second area includes peaks such as <a href="/wiki/Moel_Siabod" title="Moel Siabod">Moel Siabod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cnicht" title="Cnicht">Cnicht</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Moelwynion" title="Moelwynion">Moelwynion</a>, and the mountains around <a href="/wiki/Blaenau_Ffestiniog" title="Blaenau Ffestiniog">Blaenau Ffestiniog</a>.</li>\n<li>The third area includes the <a href="/wiki/Rhinogydd" title="Rhinogydd">Rhinogydd</a> in the west as well as the <a href="/wiki/Arenig" title="Arenig">Arenig</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Migneint" title="Migneint">Migneint</a> (this last being an area of bog), and <a href="/wiki/Rhobell_Fawr" title="Rhobell Fawr">Rhobell Fawr</a>. This area is not as popular with tourists as the other areas, due to its remoteness.</li>\n<li>The southernmost area includes <a href="/wiki/Cadair_Idris" title="Cadair Idris">Cadair Idris</a>, the Tarren range, the <a href="/wiki/Dyfi_hills" title="Dyfi hills">Dyfi hills</a>, and the Aran group, including <a href="/wiki/Aran_Fawddwy" title="Aran Fawddwy">Aran Fawddwy</a>, the highest mountain in the United Kingdom south of Snowdon.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Berwyn_range" title="Berwyn range">Berwyn range</a> to the south east, has the western part of it in the park, but the highest summits to the east have been omitted.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountain_walking">Mountain walking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mountain walking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG/220px-Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG/330px-Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG/440px-Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2816" data-file-height="2112" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Badgernet_Snowdonia_walks_1.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Southern edge. Waymarked path near Llyn Barfog in <a href="/wiki/Gwynedd" title="Gwynedd">Gwynedd</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Many of the <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hikers</a> in the area concentrate on <a href="/wiki/Snowdon" title="Snowdon">Snowdon</a> itself. It is regarded as a fine mountain, but at times gets very crowded;<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> in addition the <a href="/wiki/Snowdon_Mountain_Railway" title="Snowdon Mountain Railway">Snowdon Mountain Railway</a> runs to the summit.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The other high mountains with their boulder-strewn summits\xe2\x80\x94as well as <a href="/wiki/Tryfan" title="Tryfan">Tryfan</a>, one of the few mountains in the UK south of Scotland whose ascent needs hands as well as feet\xe2\x80\x94are also very popular. However, there are also some spectacular walks in Snowdonia on the lower mountains, and they tend to be relatively unfrequented. Among hikers\' favourites are <a href="/wiki/Y_Garn_(Glyderau)" title="Y Garn (Glyderau)">Y Garn</a> (east of Llanberis) along the ridge to <a href="/wiki/Elidir_Fawr" title="Elidir Fawr">Elidir Fawr</a>; Mynydd Tal-y-Mignedd (west of Snowdon) along the <a href="/wiki/Nantlle_Ridge" title="Nantlle Ridge">Nantlle Ridge</a> to Mynydd Drws-y-Coed; <a href="/wiki/Moelwyn_Mawr" title="Moelwyn Mawr">Moelwyn Mawr</a> (west of Blaenau Ffestiniog); and <a href="/wiki/Pen_Llithrig_y_Wrach" title="Pen Llithrig y Wrach">Pen Llithrig y Wrach</a> north of <a href="/wiki/Capel_Curig" title="Capel Curig">Capel Curig</a>. Further south are <a href="/wiki/Y_Llethr" title="Y Llethr">Y Llethr</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Rhinogydd" title="Rhinogydd">Rhinogydd</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cadair_Idris" title="Cadair Idris">Cadair Idris</a> near <a href="/wiki/Dolgellau" title="Dolgellau">Dolgellau</a>.\n</p><p>The park has 1,479 miles (2,380&#160;km) of public footpaths, 164 miles (264&#160;km) of public <a href="/wiki/Bridleway" class="mw-redirect" title="Bridleway">bridleways</a>, and 46 miles (74&#160;km) of other public rights of way.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> A large part of the park is also covered by <a href="/wiki/Right_to_Roam" class="mw-redirect" title="Right to Roam">Right to Roam</a> laws.\n</p>\n<h2><span Ddu</a></div></div></div>\n<p>One of the major problems facing the park in recent years has been the growth of <i><a href="/wiki/Rhododendron_ponticum" title="Rhododendron ponticum">Rhododendron ponticum</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> This fast-growing <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> has a tendency to take over and stifle native species. It can form massive towering growths and has a companion fungus that grows on its roots producing toxins that are poisonous to any local flora and fauna for a seven-year period after the <i>Rhododendron</i> infestations have been eradicated. As a result, there are a number of desolate landscapes.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">Wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Wildlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Snowdonia\'s importance in the conservation of habitat and wildlife in the region reflects in the fact that nearly 20% of its total area is protected by UK and European law. Half of that area was set aside by the government under the European Habitats Directive as a Special Area of Conservation.<sup id="cite_ref-Snowdonia_NP_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snowdonia_NP-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Rare mammals in the park include <a href="/wiki/Otters" class="mw-redirect" title="Otters">otters</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_polecat" title="European polecat">polecats</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Feral_goat" title="Feral goat">feral goat</a>, although the <a href="/wiki/Pine_marten" class="mw-redirect" title="Pine marten">pine marten</a> has not been seen for many years.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Rare birds include <a href="/wiki/Raven" title="Raven">raven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red-billed_chough" title="Red-billed chough">red-billed chough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_falcon" title="Peregrine falcon">peregrine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osprey" title="Osprey">osprey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merlin_(bird)" title="Merlin (bird)">merlin</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red_kite" title="Red kite">red kite</a>. Another of Snowdonia\'s famous inhabitants is <a href="/wiki/Chrysolina_cerealis" title="Chrysolina cerealis">the Snowdon or rainbow beetle</a>. The park has three RAMSAR Sites: the Dyfi Estuary Biosphere Reserve, Cwm Idwal and Llyn Tegid.<sup id="cite_ref-Snowdonia_NP_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snowdonia_NP-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Snowdonia is one of the <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Climate of the United Kingdom">wettest parts of the United Kingdom</a>; <a href="/wiki/Crib_Goch" title="Crib Goch">Crib Goch</a> in Snowdonia is the wettest spot in the United Kingdom, with an average rainfall of 4,473 millimetres (176.1&#160;in) a year over the past 30 years.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Snowdonia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.snowdonrailway.co.uk/journey-to-the-summit">"Snowdon Mountain Railway Climb Mount Snowdon by Train"</a>.
