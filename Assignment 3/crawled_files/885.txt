September 1963) is an English composer, best known for his scores to motion pictures. He has been based in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> since 1997 and has composed the scores to over fifty feature films. He is particularly known for his scores for animated films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Antz" title="Antz">Antz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Chicken_Run" title="Chicken Run">Chicken Run</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek" title="Shrek">Shrek</a></i> (all three co-composed with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry Gregson-Williams</a>), <i><a href="/wiki/Robots_(2005_film)" title="Robots (2005 film)">Robots</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Feet" title="Happy Feet">Happy Feet</a></i> (and its <a href="/wiki/Happy_Feet_Two" title="Happy Feet Two">sequel</a>), three <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age_(franchise)" title="Ice Age (franchise)">Ice Age</a></i> sequels, <i><a href="/wiki/Rio_(2011_film)" title="Rio (2011 film)">Rio</a></i>, and the <a href="/wiki/How_to_Train_Your_Dragon_(franchise)" title="How to Train Your Dragon (franchise)"><i>How to Train Your Dragon</i> film series</a>, for which he was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Academy Award for Best Original Score">Academy Award</a> for the <a href="/wiki/How_To_Train_Your_Dragon_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="How To Train Your Dragon (film)">first film</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He has also scored many live-action films, of which his collaborations with directors <a href="/wiki/Doug_Liman" title="Doug Liman">Doug Liman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Greengrass" title="Paul Greengrass">Paul Greengrass</a> are perhaps the best known.  These include the first three and the fifth <a href="/wiki/Bourne_(film_series)" title="Bourne (film series)">Bourne</a> films, <i><a href="/wiki/United_93_(film)" title="United 93 (film)">United 93</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Zone_(film)" title="Green Zone (film)">Green Zone</a></i>.\n</p><p>Powell was a member of <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>\'s music studio, <a href="/wiki/Remote_Control_Productions" title="Remote Control Productions">Remote Control Productions</a>, and has collaborated frequently with other composers from the studio, including <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry Gregson-Williams</a> and Zimmer himself.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Powell_(film_composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Powell was originally trained as a violinist as a child, before studying at London\'s <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College_of_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity College of Music">Trinity College of Music</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He later ventured into jazz and rock music, playing in a soul band called the Faboulistics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On leaving college, he composed music for commercials, which led to a job as an assistant to the composer <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Doyle" title="Patrick Doyle">Patrick Doyle</a> on several film productions, including <i><a href="/wiki/Much_Ado_About_Nothing_(1993_film)" title="Much Ado About Nothing (1993 film)">Much Ado About Nothing</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Powell is an <a href="/wiki/Atheist" class="mw-redirect" title="Atheist">atheist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Spitfire_Interview_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spitfire_Interview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Powell_(film_composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1995, Powell co-founded the London-based commercial music house Independently Thinking Music, which produced scores for more than 100 British and French commercials and independent films.\n</p><p>Powell\'s first film score was for the 1990 film <i>Stay Lucky</i>.  He moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 1997, and scored his first major film, <i><a href="/wiki/Face/Off" title="Face/Off">Face/Off</a></i>. This was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Antz" title="Antz">Antz</a></i> in 1998, the first film produced by <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Animation" title="DreamWorks Animation">DreamWorks Animation</a> which he co-scored with fellow British composer <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gregson-Williams" title="Harry Gregson-Williams">Harry Gregson-Williams</a>.  Two years later the two collaborated again to compose the score to <i><a href="/wiki/Chicken_Run" title="Chicken Run">Chicken Run</a></i>, and again the following year on <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek" title="Shrek">Shrek</a></i>, which won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Animated_Feature" title="Academy Award for Best Animated Feature">Academy Award for Best Animated Feature</a>.  All subsequent Shrek films however, have been scored solely by Gregson-Williams.  During 2001 he also scored <i><a href="/wiki/Evolution_(2001_film)" title="Evolution (2001 film)">Evolution</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Sam" title="I Am Sam">I Am Sam</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Visiting_(film)" title="Just Visiting (film)">Just Visiting</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Rat_Race_(film)" title="Rat Race (film)">Rat Race</a></i>.\n</p><p>In 2002 Powell was hired to score <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Identity_(2002_film)" title="The Bourne Identity (2002 film)">The Bourne Identity</a></i>, after <a href="/wiki/Carter_Burwell" title="Carter Burwell">Carter Burwell</a> left the project, and has gone on to score all of director <a href="/wiki/Doug_Liman" title="Doug Liman">Doug Liman</a>\'s subsequent films.  He also returned to score the other two films in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Supremacy" title="The Bourne Supremacy">The Bourne Supremacy</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bourne_Ultimatum" title="The Bourne Ultimatum">The Bourne Ultimatum</a></i>, which were both directed by British director <a href="/wiki/Paul_Greengrass" title="Paul Greengrass">Paul Greengrass</a>.\n</p><p>Following the Bourne films, Powell collaborated with Liman again to score the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._%26_Mrs._Smith_(2005_film)" title="Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith (2005 film)">Mr. &amp; Mrs. Smith</a></i>.  That year, he also scored <i><a href="/wiki/Robots_(2005_film)" title="Robots (2005 film)">Robots</a></i>, going on to score all but one of subsequent films by <a href="/wiki/Blue_Sky_Studios" title="Blue Sky Studios">Blue Sky Studios</a>.\n</p><p>In 2006, he scored Greengrass\' <i><a href="/wiki/United_93_(film)" title="United 93 (film)">United 93</a></i>.  He also composed music for <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age:_The_Meltdown" title="Ice Age: The Meltdown">Ice Age: The Meltdown</a></i>, following <a href="/wiki/David_Newman_(composer)" title="David Newman (composer)">David Newman</a>, who scored the first <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age_(2002_film)" title="Ice Age (2002 film)">Ice Age</a></i> film, as well as <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_The_Last_Stand" title="X-Men: The Last Stand">X-Men: The Last Stand</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Feet" title="Happy Feet">Happy Feet</a></i>, for which he won a Film &amp; TV Music Award for Best Score for an Animated Feature Film.  The following year he scored <i>The Bourne Ultimatum</i>.  In 2008 he collaborated with composer <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a> to score <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Fu_Panda" title="Kung Fu Panda">Kung Fu Panda</a></i>, and also wrote music that year for <i><a href="/wiki/Jumper_(2008_film)" title="Jumper (2008 film)">Jumper</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Horton_Hears_a_Who!_(film)" title="Horton Hears a Who! (film)">Dr. Seuss\' Horton Hears a Who!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hancock_(film)" title="Hancock (film)">Hancock</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bolt_(2008_film)" title="Bolt (2008 film)">Bolt</a></i>.  In 2009 he scored the third film of <i>Ice Age</i> series; <i><a href="/wiki/Ice_Age:_Dawn_of_the_Dinosaurs" title="Ice Age: Dawn of the Dinosaurs">Dawn of the Dinosaurs</a></i>.\n</p><p>In 2010, Powell composed the <a href="/wiki/How_to_Train_Your_Dragon:_Music_from_the_Motion_Picture" title="How to Train Your Dragon: Music from the Motion Picture">score</a> to <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Train_Your_Dragon_(film)" title="How to Train Your Dragon (film)">How to Train Your Dragon</a></i>.  This was his sixth score for a DreamWorks Animation film, although the first where he composed the whole score himself. It also became his first work to be nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Academy Award for Best Original Score">Academy Award for Best Original Score</a>. That year, he has also scored Greengrass\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Zone_(film)" title="Green Zone (film)">Green Zone</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Knight_and_Day" title="Knight and Day">Knight and Day</a></i>.\n</p><p>In 2013, he took a sabbatical year from film scoring and in April 2014, following the completion of his scores to sequels <i><a href="/wiki/Rio_2" title="Rio 2">Rio 2</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Train_Your_Dragon_2" title="How to Train Your Dragon 2">How to Train Your Dragon 2</a></i>, he announced his decision to take another break to compose concert music, including a 45-minute oratorio to commemorate the 100-year anniversary of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. The piece, named "A Prussian Requiem", premiered on 6 March 2016 at The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Festival_Hall" title="Royal Festival Hall">Royal Festival Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> with <a href="/wiki/Jose_Serebrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Serebrier">Jose Serebrier</a> conducting the <a href="/wiki/Philharmonia_Orchestra" title="Philharmonia
