September 1982), better known by his stage name <b>Skepta</b>, is an English <a href="/wiki/Grime_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Grime music">Grime</a> <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">artist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rapper" class="mw-redirect" title="Rapper">Rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">Songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">Record Producer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_video_director" title="Music video director">Music Video Director</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tottenham" title="Tottenham">Tottenham</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_London" title="North London">North London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Adenuga released his debut studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Skepta_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Skepta album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> in 2007 and his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Microphone_Champion" title="Microphone Champion">Microphone Champion</a></i> in 2009, both independently; while his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Doin%27_It_Again" title="Doin&#39; It Again">Doin\' It Again</a></i> was released in 2011 by <a href="/wiki/All_Around_the_World_Productions" title="All Around the World Productions">AATW</a>. His fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Konnichiwa_(Skepta_album)" title="Konnichiwa (Skepta album)">Konnichiwa</a></i>, was released on 6 May 2016 to critical acclaim, winning that year\'s <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Prize" title="Mercury Prize">Mercury Prize</a>. Skepta\'s younger brother, and Boy Better Know labelmate is the well known grime artist <a href="/wiki/Jme_(MC)" title="Jme (MC)">JME</a>. They have collaborated many times, most recently on his album  <i><a href="/wiki/Konnichiwa_(Skepta_album)" title="Konnichiwa (Skepta album)">Konnichiwa</a></i>. Adenuga is also the brother of <a href="/wiki/Beats_1" title="Beats 1">Beats 1</a> DJ <a href="/wiki/Julie_Adenuga" title="Julie Adenuga">Julie Adenuga</a>. \nSpecial guest appearance on \xe2\x80\x9cText me back\xe2\x80\x9d by Terry \xe2\x80\x98Terreh\xe2\x80\x99 Piper \n</p><p>Adenuga has stated \xe2\x80\x98DIY\xe2\x80\x99 music is the future and told <i>Time Out</i> magazine, "I\xe2\x80\x99m not a rapper. I\xe2\x80\x99m an activist."<sup id="cite_ref-Debretts_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Debretts-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His influence on contemporary <a class="toclevel-1 tocsection-12"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">7</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Skepta was born on 19 September 1982 in <a href="/wiki/Tottenham" title="Tottenham">Tottenham</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_London" title="North London">North London</a> to immigrant parents from <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>. The eldest of four siblings, the family grew up on Meridian Walk Estate, a council estate in Tottenham, North London.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Skepta began MCing shortly before Meridian Crew disbanded in 2005, after which Skepta, alongside his brother <a href="/wiki/Jme_(MC)" title="Jme (MC)">Jme</a>, joined <a href="/wiki/Roll_Deep" title="Roll Deep">Roll Deep</a> for a short period of time before becoming founding members of <a href="/wiki/Boy_Better_Know" title="Boy Better Know">Boy Better Know</a> in 2006. Following the formation of Boy Better Know, Skepta <a href="/wiki/Sound_clash" title="Sound clash">clashed</a> with fellow MC and grime legend Devilman for the DVD Lord of the Mics 2, in what is remembered as one of the biggest clashes in grime history.<sup id="cite_ref-yates_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yates-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thefader.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after the clash, Skepta released a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> entitled "<i>Joseph Junior Adenuga</i>".\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2003.E2.80.9306:_Career_beginnings.2C_Joseph_Junior_Adenuga_and_Greatest_Hits"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2003\xe2\x80\x9306:_Career_beginnings,_Joseph_Junior_Adenuga_and_Greatest_Hits">2003\xe2\x80\x9306: Career beginnings, <i>Joseph Junior Adenuga</i> and <i>Greatest Hits</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2003\xe2\x80\x9306: Career beginnings, Joseph Junior Adenuga and Greatest Hits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Skepta is a British Nigerian.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He is the brother of fellow musician <a href="/wiki/Jme_(MC)" title="Jme (MC)">Jme</a> and radio presenter <a href="/wiki/Julie_Adenuga" title="Julie Adenuga">Julie Adenuga</a>. Skepta was originally a DJ from Tottenham-based grime collective Meridian Crew. Early in his career, Skepta released a number of instrumentals, the most notable being <i>"DTI (Pirate Station Anthem)"</i> and <i>"Private Caller"</i>, the latter of which was released as a single and vocaled by various other members of Meridian Crew.\n</p><p>Skepta began emceeing shortly before Meridian Crew disbanded in 2005, after which Skepta, alongside his brother <a href="/wiki/Jme_(MC)" title="Jme (MC)">Jme</a>, joined <a href="/wiki/Roll_Deep" title="Roll Deep">Roll Deep</a> for a short period of time before becoming founding members of <a href="/wiki/Boy_Better_Know" title="Boy Better Know">Boy Better Know</a> in 2006. Following the formation of Boy Better Know, Skepta <a href="/wiki/Sound_clash" title="Sound clash">clashed</a> with fellow MC Devilman for the DVD Lord of the Mics 2, in what is remembered as one of the biggest clashes in grime history.<sup id="cite_ref-yates_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yates-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thefader.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefader.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after the clash, Skepta released a <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a> entitled "<i>Joseph Junior Adenuga</i>". Skepta publicly released his debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Skepta_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Skepta album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> on 17 September 2007, on his label <a href="/wiki/Boy_Better_Know" title="Boy Better Know">Boy Better Know</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.9311:_Microphone_Champion_and_Doin.27_it_Again"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008\xe2\x80\x9311:_Microphone_Champion_and_Doin\'_it_Again">2008\xe2\x80\x9311: <i>Microphone Champion</i> and <i>Doin\' it Again</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008\xe2\x80\x9311: Microphone Champion and Doin&#039; it Again">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Skepta self-released the first single "<a href="/wiki/Rolex_Sweep" title="Rolex Sweep">Rolex Sweep</a>" in September 2008 and reached number 89 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. Skepta then released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Microphone_Champion" title="Microphone Champion">Microphone Champion</a></i> on 1 June 2009. He then also released single "Too Many Man" with Jme, Wiley, Frisco and Shorty, which went to chart at number 79. Skepta has released five singles off his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Doin%27_It_Again" title="Doin&#39; It Again">Doin\' It Again</a></i> (first with a major label), titled "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Boy_(Skepta_song)" title="Bad Boy (Skepta song)">Bad Boy</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Rescue_Me_(Skepta_song)" title="Rescue Me (Skepta song)">Rescue Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Cross_My_Heart_(Skepta_song)" title="Cross My Heart (Skepta song)">Cross My Heart</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Preeya_Kalidas" title="Preeya Kalidas">Preeya Kalidas</a>, "<a href="/wiki/So_Alive_(Skepta_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="So Alive (Skepta song)">So Alive</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Amnesia_(Skepta_song)" title="Amnesia (Skepta song)">Amnesia</a>", as well as a video for the <a href="/wiki/Hello_Good_Morning#Remixes" title="Hello Good Morning">Hello Good Morning</a> (Grime Remix). Three singles charted in the Top 40 of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, with "<a href="/wiki/Rescue_Me_(Skepta_song)" title="Rescue Me (Skepta song)">Rescue Me</a>" being the most successful at number 14. "Cross My Heart" came in at number 31 and dropped out of the Top 40 a week later. <i>Doin\' it Again</i> spent 3 weeks in the top 100 album charts and debuted and peaked at number 19 on its first week of release. In 2011 Skepta caused a big controversy by releasing a <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_pornography" title="Hardcore pornography">hardcore</a> music video of his single "All over the house".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Blacklisted_and_Konnichiwa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012\xe2\x80\x93present:_Blacklisted_and_Konnichiwa">2012\xe2\x80\x93present: <i>Blacklisted</i> and <i>Konnichiwa</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012\xe2\x80\x93present: Blacklisted and Konnichiwa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg/220px-Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg/330px-Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg/440px-Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4921" data-file-height="3364" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Skepta_2_F.I.B._2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Skepta at the <a href="/wiki/Festival_Internacional_de_Benic%C3%A0ssim" title="Festival Internacional de Benic\xc3\xa0ssim">Festival Internacional de Benic\xc3\xa0ssim</a> 2016</div></div></div>\n<p>In 2012, Skepta released two singles from his fourth forthcoming album. Both "<a href="/wiki/Hold_On_(Skepta_song)" title="Hold On (Skepta song)">Hold On</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Make_Peace_Not_War" title="Make Peace Not War">Make Peace Not War</a>" were Top 40 charting singles in the UK but were a departure from Skepta\'s usual sound. Skepta\'s second major label album was intended to be released in the fourth quarter of 2011, entitled <i>The Honeymoon</i> but was delayed till 2012. After a disappointing response from the first two singles Skepta decided to release a purchasable mixtape, titled <i>Blacklisted</i>. It was released on 2 December 2012 along with music videos to support the release prior to the release.\n</p><p>In March 2014, Skepta provided a verse for the remix of "German Whip" by <a href="/wiki/Meridian_Dan" title="Meridian Dan">Meridian Dan</a>, and in the same month, he released his new single, "<a href="/wiki/That%27s_Not_Me_(Skepta_song)" title="That&#39;s Not Me (Skepta song)">That\'s Not Me</a>", featuring his brother <a href="/wiki/Jme_(MC)" title="Jme (MC)">Jme</a>, peaking at number 21 on the UK charts.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The single\'s music video won the award for Best Video at the 2014 <a href="/wiki/MOBO_Awards" title="MOBO Awards">MOBO Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Upon receiving the award, Skepta stated that the video cost him \xc2\xa380.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In late 2014, Skepta released another single titled "It Ain\'t Safe" featuring A$AP Bari. This was followed in early 2015 with "Shutdown", as well as features on "Red Eye to Paris" by <a href="/wiki/Flatbush_Zombies" title="Flatbush Zombies">Flatbush Zombies</a> and the remix of "<a href="/wiki/Ojuelegba_(Wizkid_song)" title="Ojuelegba (Wizkid song)">Ojuelegba</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Wizkid_(musician)" title="Wizkid (musician)">Wizkid</a>, also featuring <a href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>. Skepta and others joined <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a> at a surprise show in London, performing numerous songs together during a shared set.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was also named one of <i><a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">GQ</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 50 best dressed British men in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In an interview it stated that Skepta\'s making his acting debut in 2015 film <i><a href="/wiki/Anti-Social_(2015_film)" title="Anti-Social (2015 film)">Anti-Social</a></i>, released initially in May.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 14 February 2016 Skepta released "<a href="/wiki/Ladies_Hit_Squad_(song)" title="Ladies Hit Squad (song)">Ladies Hit Squad</a>" featuring grime artist D Double E and American rapper <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Nast" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Nast">A$AP Nast</a>, of the New York-based <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Mob" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Mob">A$AP Mob</a>.\n</p><p>Through a post on Instagram, Skepta announced that his album, <i><a href="/wiki/Konnichiwa_(Skepta_album)" title="Konnichiwa (Skepta album)">Konnichiwa</a></i> was due to be released on 6 May 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <i>Konnichiwa</i> was released to critical acclaim and became Grime\'s highest charting album, peaking at number 2 in the UK music charts.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Konnichiwa features songs such as "<a href="/wiki/That%27s_Not_Me_(Skepta_song)" title="That&#39;s Not Me (Skepta song)">That\'s Not Me"</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Shutdown_(Skepta_song)" title="Shutdown (Skepta song)">Shutdown</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Man_(Skepta_song)" title="Man (Skepta song)">Man</a>". The release of <i>Konnichiwa</i> was celebrated with a launch party on 5 May 2016 held in Tokyo and live streamed on <a href="/wiki/Boiler_Room_(music_project)" title="Boiler Room (music project)">Boiler Room</a>, featuring a live performance of the entire album by Skepta and supporting performances from Japanese trap artists <a href="/wiki/KOHH_(Japanese_artist)" title="KOHH (Japanese artist)">Kohh</a>, Dutch Montana, Loota, and DJ Riki.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Skepta began his "Banned From America" two-part, 14-show tour on April 16, 2017 at the <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival</a>, which covered cities throughout the United States and Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The tour was named as such in reference to Skepta\'s forced cancellation of his 2016 "No Fear" American tour in support of <i>Konnichiwa</i>, when his application for a visa to enter the United States was denied.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Skepta released his <i>Vicious EP</i> on 31 October 2017, with songs featuring <a href="/wiki/A$AP_Rocky" class="mw-redirect" title="A$AP Rocky">A$AP Rocky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lil_B" title="Lil B">Lil B</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Section_Boyz" title="Section Boyz">Section Boyz</a>. In the summer of 2017, rumours on the underground dance scene suggested an imminent collaboration between Skepta and drum\'n\'bass pioneer <a href="/wiki/Goldie" title="Goldie">Goldie</a>. This was confirmed in Goldie\'s 2017 memoir <i>All Things Remembered</i>, which refers to him having done a track with Skepta. It has not yet been released.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Skepta features on A$AP Rocky\'s hit \'Praise The Lord (Da Shine)\' which was released in May 2018. The single charted on Billboard.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discography">Discography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Discography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Skepta_discography" title="Skepta discography">Skepta discography</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Studio_albums">Studio albums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Skepta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Studio albums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Skepta_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Skepta album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> (2007)</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Microphone_Champion" title="Microphone Champion">Microphone Champion</a></i> (2009)</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/Doin%27_It_Again" title="Doin&#39;
