href="#cite_note-FA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Ebenezer Cobb Morley</b> (16 August 1831 \xe2\x80\x93 20 November 1924) was an English sportsman and is regarded as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_persons_considered_father_or_mother_of_a_field" class="mw-redirect" title="List of persons considered father or mother of a field">father</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">the Football Association</a> and modern <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>.\n</p><p>Morley was born at 10 Garden Square, Princess Street<sup id="cite_ref-HDM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a><sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and lived in the city until he was 22.<sup id="cite_ref-HDM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He moved to <a href="/wiki/Barnes,_London" title="Barnes, London">Barnes</a> in 1858<sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> forming <a href="/wiki/Barnes_Football_Club" title="Barnes Football Club">Barnes Football Club</a>, a founding member of the FA, in 1862.<sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1863, as captain of the <a href="/wiki/Mortlake" title="Mortlake">Mortlake</a>-based club, he wrote to <a href="/wiki/Bell%27s_Life_in_London" title="Bell&#39;s Life in London"><i>Bell\'s Life</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspaper</a> proposing a governing body for the sport. This led to the first meeting at the <a href="/wiki/Freemasons%27_Tavern" title="Freemasons&#39; Tavern">Freemasons\' Tavern</a>, that created the FA.<sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>He was the FA\'s first secretary (1863\xe2\x80\x931866) and its second president (1867\xe2\x80\x931874) and drafted the first <a href="/wiki/Laws_of_the_Game_(association_football)" title="Laws of the Game (association football)">Laws of the Game</a> at his home in Barnes.  This house, No 26 <a href="/wiki/The_Terrace,_Barnes" title="The Terrace, Barnes">The Terrace</a>, which had carried a blue plaque to Morley, collapsed "like a tower of cards" in November 2015 during building work.<sup id="cite_ref-plaque_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-plaque-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Search_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Search-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>As a player, he played in the first ever match under FA rules, against <a href="/wiki/Richmond_F.C." title="Richmond F.C.">Richmond</a> in 1863, and scored in the first representative match, between the clubs of London and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_F.C." title="Sheffield F.C.">Sheffield</a> on 31 March 1866.\n</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Solicitor" title="Solicitor">solicitor</a> by profession, Morley was a keen <a href="/wiki/Rowing_(sport)" title="Rowing (sport)">oarsman</a>, founding the Barnes and Mortlake <a href="/wiki/Regatta" title="Regatta">Regatta</a> for which he was also secretary (1862\xe2\x80\x931880). He served on <a href="/wiki/Surrey_County_Council" title="Surrey County Council">Surrey County Council</a> for Barnes (1903\xe2\x80\x931919) and was a <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Peace">Justice of the Peace</a>. Morley is buried<sup id="cite_ref-TO_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TO-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Barnes_Cemetery" title="Barnes Cemetery">Barnes Cemetery</a>, a now abandoned graveyard on <a href="/wiki/Barnes_Common" title="Barnes Common">Barnes Common</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barnes,_London" title="Barnes, London">Barnes</a>.  He had no children.<sup id="cite_ref-TO_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TO-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Morley was the subject of a <a href="/wiki/Google_Doodle" title="Google Doodle">Google Doodle</a> on 16 August 2018, the 187<sup>th</sup> anniversary of his birth.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<div class="thumb
