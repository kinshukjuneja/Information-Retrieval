href="/w/index.php?title=NUTS_statistical_regions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NUTS_2003">NUTS 2003</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NUTS_statistical_regions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: NUTS 2003">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the first version in 2003, North Eastern Scotland (which then included part of Moray) was coded UKM1, and <a href="/wiki/Highlands_and_Islands" title="Highlands and Islands">Highlands and Islands</a> was coded UKM4.\n</p><p>The current NUTS level 1 codes start with "C" (following "UK") rather than "1" because the new list reflected the revised <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_England" title="Regions of England">regions of England</a> and local government changes throughout the UK; "1" to "B" had been used for the 11 regions in the previous coding system.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NUTS_2006">NUTS 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NUTS_statistical_regions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: NUTS 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>NUTS 2006 came into force on 1 January 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-history_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NUTS_2010">NUTS 2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NUTS_statistical_regions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: NUTS 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>NUTS 2010 came into force on 1 January 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-history_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>2010 changes to NUTS 2 also resulting in changes with NUTS 3 regions\n</p>\n<ul><li>The combined area of UKD2 (Cheshire pre-2010) and UKD5 (Merseyside pre-2010) were replaced by UKD6 (Cheshire post-2010) and UKD7 (Merseyside post-2010), due to the transfer of Halton to the Merseyside NUTS region from Cheshire. This resulted in the following changes to the underlying NUTS 3 areas:  UKD22 (Cheshire CC) being split into UKD62 (Cheshire East) and UKD63 (Cheshire West and Chester); The areas of Liverpool, Sefton and Wirral were not changed as NUTS 3 areas however to reflect their transfer within NUTS 2 areas were respectively renumbered from (UKD52 to UKD72; UKD53 to UKD 73 and UKD54 to UKD 74). The two areas of UKD51 (East Merseyside pre 2010) and UKD21 (Halton and Warrington) were amended by the transfer of Halton from the latter to former to form the new areas of UKD71 (East Merseyside post-2010) and UKD61 (Warrington).</li></ul>\n<p>2010 changes to NUTS 3 areas without changes occurring to NUTS 2 areas\n</p>\n<ul><li>UKE43 (Calderdale, Kirklees and Wakefield) was replaced by UKE44 (Calderdale and Kirklees) and UKE45 (Wakefield)</li>\n<li>UKF23 (Northamptonshire) was replaced by UKF24 (West Northamptonshire) and UKF25 (North Northamptonshire)</li>\n<li>UKG34 (Dudley and Sandwell) was replaced by UKG36 (Dudley) and UKG37 (Sandwell)</li>\n<li>UKG35 (Walsall and Wolverhampton) was replaced by UKG38 (Walsall) and UKG39 (Wolverhampton).</li>\n<li>Bedfordshire CC UKH22 was replaced by UKH24 (Bedford) and UKH25 (Central Bedfordshire)</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NUTS_2013">NUTS 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NUTS_statistical_regions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: NUTS 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>NUTS 2013 came into force on 1 January 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-history_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>2015 changes to NUTS 3 areas without changes to NUTS 2 areas:\n</p>\n<ul><li>UKJ42 (Kent CC) was replaced by UKJ43 (Kent Thames Gateway) UKJ44 (East Kent) UKJ45 (Mid Kent) UKJ46 (West Kent)</li>\n<li>UKJ23 (Surrey) was replaced by UKJ25 (West Surrey) and UKJ26 (East Surrey)</li>\n<li>UKJ24 (West Sussex) was replaced by UKJ27 (West Sussex South West) and UKJ28 (West Sussex North East)</li>\n<li>UKJ33 (Hampshire CC) was replaced by UKJ35 (South Hampshire), UKJ36 (Central Hampshire), UKJ37 (North Hampshire)</li>\n<li>UKD43 (Lancashire CC) was replaced by UKD44 (Lancaster and Wyre), UKD45 (Mid Lancashire), UKD46 (East Lancashire) and UKD47 (Chorley and West Lancashire)</li>\n<li>UKD31 (Greater Manchester South) was replaced by UKD33 (Manchester), UKD34 (Greater Manchester South West) and UKD35 (Greater Manchester South East)</li>\n<li>UKD32 (Greater Manchester North) was replaced by UKD36 (Greater Manchester North West) and UKD37 (Greater Manchester North East)</li>\n<li>UKH13 (Norfolk) was replaced by UKH15 (Norwich and East Norfolk), UKH16 (North and West Norfolk) and UKH17 (Breckland and South Norfolk)</li>\n<li>UKH33 (Essex CC) was replaced by UKH34 (Essex Haven Gateway), UKH35 (West Essex), UKH36 (Heart of Essex) and UKH37 (Essex Thames Gateway)</li></ul>\n<p>In 2015 the Greater London NUTS 1 area was left unchanged however the previous NUTS 2 area of inner and outer London were abolished and with the previous NUTS 3 areas becoming NUTS 2 areas. Thus NUTS 2 of Inner London West UKI11 becoming the NUTS 3 area of UKI3 and likewise: Inner London East (from UKI12 to UKI4), Outer London East and North East (from UKI21 to UKI5), Outer London South (from UKI22 to UKI6) and Outer London West and North West (from UKI23 to UKI7). The NUTS 3 areas are now a single or a group of two or three boroughs.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NUTS_2016">NUTS 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=NUTS_statistical_regions_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: NUTS 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li>UKM2 (Eastern Scotland) is replaced with UKM7 (Eastern Scotland) along with associated NUTS 3 areas</li>\n<li>UKM3 (South Western Scotland)
