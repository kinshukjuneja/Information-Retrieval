class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Healthcare in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a></b>, United Kingdom, is now the responsibility of Kernow <a As far as the NHS is concerned, Cornwall includes the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>From 1947 to 1974, NHS services in Cornwall were managed by the South-Western <a href="/wiki/List_of_NHS_Regional_Hospital_Boards_(1947%E2%80%931974)" title="List of NHS Regional Hospital Boards (1947\xe2\x80\x931974)">Regional Hospital Board</a>. In 1974 the Boards were abolished and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Regional_health_authority_(UK)" title="Regional health authority (UK)">Regional Health Authorities</a>. Cornwall came under the South West RHA. Regions were reorganised in 1996 and <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> came under the South and West (Wessex and South Western) Regional Health Authority. Cornwall and Isles of Scilly constituted a <a href="/wiki/District_health_authority" title="District health authority">District health authority</a> from 1974 until 2002 when Cornwall and Isles of Scilly  <a href="/wiki/Primary_care_trust" class="mw-redirect" title="Primary care trust">Primary care trust</a> was established.  This was managed by the South West Peninsula <a href="/wiki/Strategic_health_authority" title="Strategic health authority">Strategic health authority</a> from 2002 until 2006 when that was merged into NHS South West. The PCT and the Strategic health authority were abolished in 2012.\n</p><p>A report by the <a href="/wiki/Care_Quality_Commission" title="Care Quality Commission">Care Quality Commission</a> in October 2017 found that the county\'s health and social care system \xe2\x80\x98is not working well together\xe2\x80\x99, that different parts of the system are not working together to ensure people can move between services as they need them, and that too many people were stuck in hospital waiting for a social care package or a place in a residential establishment.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability_and_transformation_plans"><a href="/wiki/Sustainability_and_transformation_plan" title="Sustainability and transformation plan">Sustainability and transformation plans</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sustainability and transformation plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In July 2015 a devolution deal, the Deal for Cornwall, was announced by the <a href="/wiki/Second_Cameron_ministry" title="Second Cameron ministry">government</a> under which <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> and the Council of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> are to create a plan to bring health and social care services together under local control.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Sustainability and transformation plan says that the 13 community hospitals in the area with a total capacity of 314 beds are not used effectively, not fit for purpose and are not sustainable. It proposes to develop integrated community care hubs primarily for care of the elderly and to integrate <a href="/wiki/NHS_111" title="NHS 111">NHS 111</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Out-of-hours_service" title="Out-of-hours service">out-of-hours service</a>.  The CCG\xe2\x80\x99s savings target for 2017/8 is \xc2\xa329.6 million but it has "no viable plan" to achieve this.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Royal_Cornwall_Hospitals_NHS_Trust" title="Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust">Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Partnership_NHS_Foundation_Trust" title="Cornwall Partnership NHS Foundation Trust">Cornwall Partnership NHS Foundation Trust</a> established a joint board in July 2017 to \xe2\x80\x9cgradually unite clinical and non-clinical services" - a step towards the creation of an <a href="/wiki/Accountable_care_system" title="Accountable care system">accountable care system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In March 2018, after protests, <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> announced that it was abandoning proposals to establish an Accountable Care System but they would continue to support the integration of health and care services.  They said they would strengthen the existing Health and Wellbeing Board to become a more effective decision making body.  Cllr Andy Virr, the Conservative health spokesperson, said that  kind words regarding working together were vacuous without a commitment to integrate and combine budgets.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commissioning">Commissioning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commissioning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Kernow <a href="/wiki/Clinical_Commissioning_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Clinical Commissioning Group">Clinical Commissioning Group</a> was placed under legal directions by <a href="/wiki/NHS_England" title="NHS England">NHS England</a> in December 2015. It has a budget of \xc2\xa3728.7 million and expects a deficit of \xc2\xa314 million for 2015/16.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  It is required to have a turnaround director in place. Keith Pringle, who has previously worked with CCGs in Hampshire and at <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_Hospitals_NHS_Foundation_Trust" title="Gloucestershire Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust">Gloucestershire Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust</a> is employed at a cost of \xc2\xa3396,000 in 2015/6 including agency fees.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Cornwall was one of the 4 areas chosen to trial the integration of specialised commissioning, previously run by <a href="/wiki/NHS_England" title="NHS England">NHS England</a> centrally, in September 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2017 it was forecasting a \xc2\xa337 million deficit and had \xe2\x80\x9cno viable plan\xe2\x80\x9d to hit its control total, a \xc2\xa319.9 million deficit on its allocated \xc2\xa3765 million, in the financial year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The clinical commissioning group proposed in October 2017 that it would no longer pay for non-emergency patient transport to hospital patients who frequently travel to hospital. The policy would particularly impact kidney dialysis patients, who typically have to visit hospital three times a week. The service has been supplied by  E-zec Medical Transport Services Ltd. since summer 2016. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 2017 it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_County_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornwall County Council">Cornwall County Council</a> was to take over the functions of Kernow Clinical Commissioning Group as part of the development of an <a href="/wiki/Accountable_care_system" title="Accountable care system">accountable care system</a> for the county.  Services are to be provided by one or more \xe2\x80\x9caccountable care partnerships\xe2\x80\x9d led by the existing NHS providers.  According to <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council#Cabinet" title="Cornwall Council">Cllr Rotchell</a> \xe2\x80\x9cCornwall Council will ultimately become the strategic commissioner for all healthcare for Cornwall that is commissioned by the CCG. The longer term plan is that the CCG becomes a department within Cornwall Council and we will buy services from the providers.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_and_Community_Care">Primary and Community Care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Primary and Community Care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are 69 GP practices in the county.  St Mary\xe2\x80\x99s hospital on the Isles of Scilly is a small community hospital with a Minor Injuries Unit run by <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Community_Health" title="Peninsula Community Health">Peninsula Community Health</a> which also runs community hospitals all over Cornwall.  The <a href="/wiki/Out-of-hours_service" title="Out-of-hours service">Out-of-hours service</a> contract, worth \xc2\xa38m a year, from 2015 was awarded to Devon Doctors, a GP led social enterprise, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kernow_Health_CIC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kernow Health CIC (page does not exist)">Kernow Health CIC</a>, a community interest company run by member practices. The consortium will operate under the name Cornwall Health.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  In 2017 it was announced that a \xc2\xa348m contract for <a href="/wiki/Out-of-hours_service" title="Out-of-hours service">Out-of-hours service</a> GP and 111 services had been awarded to a consortium of <a href="/wiki/Royal_Cornwall_Hospitals_NHS_Trust" title="Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust">Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust</a>, Kernow Health <a href="/wiki/Community_Interest_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Community Interest Company">Community Interest Company</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vocare" title="Vocare">Vocare</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>Palliative care is provided by <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Hospice_Care" title="Cornwall Hospice Care">Cornwall Hospice Care</a>.\n</p><p>During 2015 there was a programme funded with \xc2\xa344.5m from the <a href="/wiki/Better_Care_Fund" title="Better Care Fund">Better Care Fund</a> intended to ensure care packages were available for patients leaving hospitals in Cornwall but between July and September a total of 3,815 hospital bed days were lost because of problems with social care packages, compared with 2,255 days for the same period in 2014, meaning that there were 69% more delays in discharging people from hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Community services were provided by <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Community_Health" title="Peninsula Community Health">Peninsula Community Health</a> from 2011 but from April 2016 will be taken over by a consortium of Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust, Cornwall Partnership NHS Foundation Trust and Kernow Health CIC.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2017 there was a backlog of 14,000 people unable to register with an NHS dentist in the county. Waiting times for routine appointments were up to eighteen months.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthwatch">Healthwatch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Healthwatch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/HealthWatch_England" class="mw-redirect" title="HealthWatch England">Healthwatch</a> was set up under the <a href="/wiki/Health_and_Social_Care_Act_2012" title="Health and Social Care Act 2012">Health and Social Care Act 2012</a> to act as a voice for patients. There is one Healthwatch for Cornwall and a separate Healthwatch for the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mental_health">Mental health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mental health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>NHS mental health services in the county are largely provided by <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Partnership_NHS_Foundation_Trust" title="Cornwall Partnership NHS Foundation Trust">Cornwall Partnership NHS Foundation Trust</a>. According to the trust between 8 and 12 patients a month have to be sent out of Cornwall, because there is an "inadequate provision" of beds.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hospital_and_acute_care">Hospital and acute care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Healthcare_in_Cornwall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hospital and acute care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Royal_Cornwall_Hospitals_NHS_Trust" title="Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust">Royal Cornwall Hospitals NHS Trust</a> provides hospital care in the west of the county. <a href="/wiki/University_Hospitals_Plymouth_NHS_Trust" title="University Hospitals Plymouth NHS Trust">University Hospitals Plymouth NHS Trust</a> serves the east. Specialised services are provided from other trusts outside the area. <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Air_Ambulance" title="Cornwall Air Ambulance">Cornwall Air Ambulance</a> was the first dedicated helicopter emergency medical service operational in the United Kingdom. The Duchy Hospital in Truro run by <a href="/wiki/Ramsay_Health_Care_UK" title="Ramsay Health Care UK">Ramsay Health Care UK</a> is the only private hospital in the County.
