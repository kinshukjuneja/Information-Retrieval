(disambiguation)">White paper (disambiguation)</a>.</div>\n<p>A <b>white paper</b> is an authoritative report or guide that informs readers concisely about a complex issue and presents the issuing body\'s <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> on the matter. It is meant to help readers understand an issue, solve a problem, or make a decision. \n</p><p>The initial British term concerning a type of government-issued document has proliferated, taking a somewhat new meaning in business. In business, a white paper is closer to a form of marketing presentation, a tool meant to persuade customers and partners and promote a product or viewpoint.<sup id="cite_ref-White_Paper_Guy_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-White_Paper_Guy-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SearchSOA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SearchSOA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> White papers may be considered <a href="/wiki/Grey_literature" title="Grey literature">grey literature</a>.\n</p>\n<div links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_government">In government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The term <i>white paper</i> originated with the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">government</a>, and many point to the <a href="/wiki/Churchill_White_Paper" title="Churchill White Paper">Churchill White Paper</a> of 1922 as the earliest well-known example under this name.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In British government it is usually the less extensive version of the so-called <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_book" title="Blue book">blue book</a></i>, both terms being derived from the colour of the document\'s cover.<sup id="cite_ref-SearchSOA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SearchSOA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>White papers are a "... tool of participatory democracy&#160;... not [an] unalterable policy commitment."<sup id="cite_ref-Doerr_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doerr-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> "White papers have tried to perform the dual role of presenting firm government policies while at the same time inviting opinions upon them."<sup id="cite_ref-Pemberton_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pemberton-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, a white paper is "...a policy document, approved by <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Canada" title="Cabinet of Canada">Cabinet</a>, tabled in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Canada" title="House of Commons of Canada">House of Commons</a> and made available to the general public."<sup id="cite_ref-Doerr2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doerr2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The "provision of policy information through the use of white and green papers can help to create an awareness of policy issues among parliamentarians and the public and to encourage an exchange of information and analysis. They can also serve as educational techniques."<sup id="cite_ref-Doerr3_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Doerr3-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>White papers are a way the government can present policy preferences before it introduces legislation. Publishing a white paper tests public opinion on controversial policy issues and helps the government gauge its probable impact.<sup id="cite_ref-Chapin_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chapin-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By contrast, <a href="/wiki/Green_paper" title="Green paper">green papers</a>, which are issued much more frequently, are more open-ended. Also known as <i>consultation documents</i>, green papers may merely propose a strategy to implement in the details of other legislation, or they may set out proposals on which the government wishes to obtain public views and opinion.\n</p><p>Examples of governmental white papers include, in Australia, the <a href="/wiki/White_Paper_on_Full_Employment_in_Australia" title="White Paper on Full Employment in Australia">White Paper on Full Employment</a> and, in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/White_Paper_of_1939" title="White Paper of 1939">White Paper of 1939</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1966_Defence_White_Paper" title="1966 Defence White Paper">1966 Defence White Paper</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_business-to-business_marketing">In business-to-business marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In business-to-business marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Since the early 1990s, the term "white paper", or "whitepaper", has been applied to documents used as marketing or sales tools in business. These white papers are long-form content designed to promote the products or services from a specific company. As a marketing tool, these papers use selected facts and logical arguments to build a case favorable to the company sponsoring the document. B2B (business-to-business) white papers are often used to generate sales leads, establish <a href="/wiki/Thought_leader" title="Thought leader">thought leadership</a>, make a business case, or inform and persuade prospective customers, channel partners, journalists, analysts, or investors. \n</p><p>White papers are considered to be a form of <a href="/wiki/Content_marketing" title="Content marketing">content marketing</a> or <a href="/wiki/Inbound_marketing" title="Inbound marketing">inbound marketing</a>; in other words, sponsored content available on the web with or without registration, intended to raise the visibility of the sponsor in search engine results and thus build web traffic. Many B2B white papers argue that one particular <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Product_(business)" title="Product (business)">product</a> or method is superior to others for solving a specific business problem. They may also present research findings, list a set of questions or tips about a certain business issue, or highlight a particular product or service from a vendor.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There are, essentially, three main types of commercial white papers:\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>Backgrounder</b>: Describes the technical or business benefits of a certain vendor\'s offering; either a product, service, or methodology. This type of white paper is best used to supplement a product launch, argue a business case, or support a technical evaluation at the bottom of the <a href="/wiki/Sales_funnel" class="mw-redirect" title="Sales funnel">sales funnel</a>.</li>\n<li><b>Numbered list</b>: Presents a set of tips, questions, or points about a certain business issue. This type is best used to get attention with new or provocative views, or cast aspersions on competitors.</li>\n<li><b>Problem/solution</b>: Recommends a new, improved solution to a nagging business problem. This type is best used to generate leads at the top of the sales funnel, build <a href="/wiki/Mind_share" title="Mind share">mind share</a>, or inform and persuade stakeholders, building trust and credibility in the subject.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>While a numbered list may be combined with either other type, it is not workable to combine the detailed product information of a backgrounder with the industry-wide perspective of a problem/solution white paper.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Several variations on the colour theme exist:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Green_paper" title="Green paper">green paper</a> is a proposal or consultative document rather than being authoritative or final.</li></ul>\n<p>Two others are much less well established:\n</p>\n<ul><li>A <b>blue paper</b> sets out technical specifications of a technology or item of equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>A <b>yellow paper</b> is a document containing research that has not yet been formally accepted or published in an academic journal. It is synonymous to the more widely used term <i><a href="/wiki/Preprint" title="Preprint">preprint</a></i>.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Blue_book" title="Blue book">Blue book</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Case_study" title="Case study">Case study</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Green_paper" title="Green paper">Green paper</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Grey_literature" title="Grey literature">Grey literature</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Persuasive_writing" title="Persuasive writing">Persuasive writing</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White_paper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-White_Paper_Guy-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-White_Paper_Guy_1-0">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web">Graham, Gordon. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.thatwhitepaperguy.com/white-paper-faq-frequently-asked-questions/#what_is">"What exactly is a white paper?"</a>.
