href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">Baseball</a> was first introduced to Taiwan during <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_under_Japanese_rule" title="Taiwan under Japanese rule">Japanese rule</a>, and gained popularity when the national little league baseball teams won numerous <a href="/wiki/Little_League_World_Series" title="Little League World Series">Little League World Series</a> championships in the 1970s and 1980s. The <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_National_Baseball_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiwan National Baseball Team">national baseball team</a> also performed exceptionally well in many international competitions. However, the development of baseball in Taiwan was limited due to the lack of a professional league, and therefore many players were reluctant to commit to the sport.\n</p><p>The idea of forming a professional baseball league in Taiwan was first suggested by local <a href="/wiki/Brother_Elephants" class="mw-redirect" title="Brother Elephants">Brother Hotel</a>\'s chairman Hung Teng-Sheng (\xe6\xb4\xaa\xe9\xa8\xb0\xe5\x8b\x9d). He formed his amateur Brother Hotel baseball team in 1984, and intended to professionalize his team and form a professional league within a few years. Throughout 1988 and 1989, Hung visited numerous Taiwanese businesses, trying to convince them to form professional baseball clubs. Most of his requests were rejected, but <a href="/wiki/Wei_Chuan_Dragons" title="Wei Chuan Dragons">Wei Chuan Corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercuries_Tigers" title="Mercuries Tigers">Mercuries Chain Stores</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uni-President_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Uni-President Corporation">Uni-President Corporation</a> all supported the idea and formed teams. The Chinese Professional Baseball League was established on October 23, 1989, with Hung Teng-sheng acting as secretary-general. Because of his contribution to professional baseball in Taiwan, Hung is sometimes referred to as the "Father of the CPBL." Chung Meng-shun (\xe9\x8d\xbe\xe5\xad\x9f\xe8\x88\x9c) designed every original logo of the four founding teams.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taiwan_Major_League">Taiwan Major League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taiwan Major League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Major_League" title="Taiwan Major League">Taiwan Major League</a></div>\n<p>In 1997, the newly founded <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Major_League" title="Taiwan Major League">Taiwan Major League</a> began to compete with the CPBL. The two leagues were often competing with each other, but eventually, the TML merged with the CPBL.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>All teams are owned by and named after large Taiwanese corporations, a similar practice seen in Japan\'s <a href="/wiki/NPB" class="mw-redirect" title="NPB">NPB</a> and South Korea\'s <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">KBO</a>. Each team manages a regional market with a home city, but does not play its games exclusively in that market. Other than the home cities, regular season games are also held in <a href="/wiki/Hsinchu" title="Hsinchu">Hsinchu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Douliou_Baseball_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Douliou Baseball Stadium">Douliou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiayi_Baseball_Field" class="mw-redirect" title="Chiayi Baseball Field">Chiayi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pingtung_Baseball_Field" title="Pingtung Baseball Field">Pingtung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luodong,_Yilan" class="mw-redirect" title="Luodong, Yilan">Luodong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hualien_Baseball_Stadium" title="Hualien Baseball Stadium">Hualien</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taitung_City" title="Taitung City">Taitung</a> with less frequency.\n</p><p>Each season spans from March to October, with a one-week all-star break in June or July, which separates the season into first and second half-seasons. Playoffs are held in late October or early November, with three teams competing in two rounds. A team may qualify for playoffs either by winning a half-season title, or be awarded a wild card berth by attaining the highest place in the seasonal ranks. If a team wins a half-season title, it will not be considered in the seasonal ranks when the winner of the wild card is being decided. If both half seasons were won by the same team, another wild card berth will be given through the same mechanism after the first berth has been awarded.\n</p><p>Due to recent reduction in the number of teams, the regulation above may be modified to better apply to the current situation.\n</p><p>Since 2005 the champion team will represent Taiwan in the <a href="/wiki/Asia_Series" title="Asia Series">Asia Series</a> to compete with other champion teams from <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> (<a href="/wiki/Japan_Series" title="Japan Series">Japan Series</a>), <a href="/wiki/KBO_League" title="KBO League">KBO League</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_Series" title="Korean Series">Korean Series</a>), <a href="/wiki/Australian_Baseball_League" title="Australian Baseball League">Australian Baseball League</a> (<a href="/wiki/Claxton_Shield" title="Claxton Shield">Claxton Shield</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_European_Baseball" title="Confederation of European Baseball">Confederation of European Baseball</a> (<a href="/wiki/European_Champion_Cup_Final_Four" title="European Champion Cup Final Four">European Champion Cup</a>).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_players">Foreign players</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Foreign players">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A typical monthly salary for a foreign player is in between 5,000-12,000 <a href="/wiki/USD" class="mw-redirect" title="USD">USD</a>, these positions are normally filled by AA, AAA, or Japanese minor leaguers. The number of foreign players allowed on a team\'s roster is limited to four. Of the four players only three are allowed to be activated on the major league roster, the remaining foreign player can practice and prepare with the team or play in the minors. A foreign player, once sent to the minor league team, must wait a week before being allowed to be recalled to the major league.\n</p><p>Foreign players, from regions other than Japan and South Korea, are given Chinese <a href="/wiki/Epithets" class="mw-redirect" title="Epithets">epithets</a> to increase familiarity with Taiwanese fans. These epithets, usually two to three characters in length, are generally loose <a href="/wiki/Transliterations" class="mw-redirect" title="Transliterations">transliterations</a> of the players\' names and are generally chosen as terms meant to convey strength or might. One example is Jeff Andra, whose epithet is <i>Feiyong</i> (\xe9\xa3\x9b\xe5\x8b\x87) \xe2\x80\x94 meaning, literally, a flying brave man. Recently however, most foreign players are just simply given a direct Chinese <a href="/wiki/Transcription_(linguistics)" title="Transcription (linguistics)">transcription</a>. Some players (mostly foreign players) have now adopted the custom in the rest of the world by placing their surnames on the back of their jerseys using the <a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" title="Latin alphabet">Latin alphabet</a>. Some teams now have adopted Latin alphabet jerseys, a trend that has picked up in recent years. The <a href="/wiki/Fubon_Guardians" title="Fubon Guardians">Fubon Guardians</a> only have uniforms with such, and the other teams are adopting such jerseys on occasion.\n</p>\n<h3><span href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Despite its young age, professional baseball in Taiwan had suffered game-fixing <a href="/wiki/Scandal" title="Scandal">scandals</a> several times. Such scandals have led to decline in attendance and also the disbanding of some teams.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_August_3_Incident">The August 3 Incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: The August 3 Incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first scandal was the "August 3 Incident", when a group of weapon-carrying local gang members rushed into a <a href="/wiki/Taichung" title="Taichung">Taichung</a> hotel and threatened five Brother Elephants players, including star pitcher <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chen_Yi-Hsin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chen Yi-Hsin (page does not exist)">Chen Yi-Hsin</a> (\xe9\x99\xb3\xe7\xbe\xa9\xe4\xbf\xa1), in order for them to cooperate with the gang and fix games for their gambling business on the evening of August 3, 1996. CPBL reported the incident to the police immediately and the gang members were soon arrested, but rumors about game fixing swirled in Taiwan.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Black_Eagles_Incident">The Black Eagles Incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: The Black Eagles Incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The rumors came true in January 1997, just before the season started. With solid evidence, police arrested several CPBL players suspected of being in illegal gambling. During the process, many players, mostly from the <a href="/wiki/China_Times_Eagles" title="China Times Eagles">China Times Eagles</a>, were found to be involved in the game-fixing and were promptly expelled by CPBL. In June 1997, only two Taiwanese players on the roster of the China Times Eagles were not involved in any sort of scandal, and the rest of the league had to lend their players to the Eagles so that they could finish out the season.\n</p><p>The China Times Eagles were eventually disbanded the next season as a result, and attendance declined sharply, from its peak of 6,000 in 1994 to merely 1,500 in 1998. However, the fiasco did not end there. In August 1997, several <a href="/wiki/Mercuries_Tigers" title="Mercuries Tigers">Mercuries Tigers</a> players were threatened by gambling gangs under similar circumstances as the August 3 Incident. In April 1999, the <a href="/wiki/Wei_Chuan_Dragons" title="Wei Chuan Dragons">Wei Chuan Dragons</a>\' then manager <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hsu_Sheng-ming&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hsu Sheng-ming (page does not exist)">Hsu Sheng-ming</a> (\xe5\xbe\x90\xe7\x94\x9f\xe6\x98\x8e) was attacked near his residence in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>. The Mercuries Tigers and the Wei Chuan Dragons were also badly affected by the financial loss brought on by the scandal and were disbanded after the 1999 season. The scandal was later nicknamed "The Black Eagles Incident", a reference to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sox_Scandal" title="Black Sox Scandal">Black Sox Scandal</a>, and also because the China Times Eagles were the most involved in the scandal.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_game-fixing_scandal">2005 game-fixing scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: 2005 game-fixing scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In July 2005, another gambling-related scandal surfaced when the local <cite><a href="/wiki/Next_Magazine_(Chinese_magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Next Magazine (Chinese magazine)">Next Magazine</a></cite> published a story with photographs of <a href="/wiki/Chinatrust_Whales" title="Chinatrust Whales">Chinatrust Whales</a>\' <a href="/wiki/Australian" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Purcell&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Brad Purcell (page does not exist)">Brad Purcell</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> lounge bar along with several local gang members. This article also pointed out several games which may have been fixed between May and June 2005, and listed nearly ten players involved, mostly from foreign countries.\n</p><p>Local prosecutor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hsu_Wei-Yueh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hsu Wei-Yueh (page does not exist)">Hsu Wei-Yueh</a> (\xe5\xbe\x90\xe7\xb6\xad\xe5\xb6\xbd) took up the case and arrested <a href="/wiki/La_New_Bears" class="mw-redirect" title="La New Bears">La New Bears</a> <a href="/wiki/Catcher" title="Catcher">catcher</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chen_Chao-Ying&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chen Chao-Ying (page does not exist)">Chen Chao-Ying</a> (\xe9\x99\xb3\xe6\x98\xad\xe7\xa9\x8e) and <a href="/wiki/Macoto_Cobras" class="mw-redirect" title="Macoto Cobras">Macoto Cobras</a> coach <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsai_Sheng-Fong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tsai Sheng-Fong (page does not exist)">Tsai Sheng-Fong</a> (\xe8\x94\xa1\xe7\x94\x9f\xe8\xb1\x90) on July 27, both of whom were immediately expelled from the league. However, on August 22, Hsu released the two, stating that "they had made full confessions". Brad Purcell himself had been released earlier by the Chinatrust Whales (and expelled from the league) and fled Taiwan before any trial could start.\n</p><p>Hsu later summoned a total of fifteen players, including <a href="/wiki/Sinon_Bulls" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinon Bulls">Sinon Bulls</a> American <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Andra" title="Jeff Andra">Jeff Andra</a>, La New Bears <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rican</a> <a href="/wiki/Infielder" title="Infielder">infielder</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Victor_Rodriguez_(baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Victor Rodriguez (baseball) (page does not exist)">Victor Rodriguez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chinatrust_Whales" title="Chinatrust Whales">Chinatrust Whales</a> <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Emiliano_Giron" title="Emiliano Giron">Emiliano Giron</a>, and claimed that they were involved in gambling. None of the players admitted to being involved in game-fixing but Hsu ruled that the imprisoned players needed <a href="/wiki/Bail" title="Bail">bail</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Taiwan_dollar" title="New Taiwan dollar">NT $</a>100,000 to be released. CPBL immediately expelled the players who were arrested. Only the Brother Elephants and the Uni-President Lions were unaffected by this scandal.\n</p><p>The circumstances of this scandal remain unclear, with the lack of evidence and confessions, and the fact that Hsu Wei-Yueh himself was later arrested for his involvement in bribery scandals, which led to his trial and sentence in 2005.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="dmedia_T-REX_Scandal">dmedia T-REX Scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: dmedia T-REX Scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On October 8, 2008, <a href="/wiki/Banciao_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Banciao City">Banciao</a> district attorney\'s office called the owner of <a href="/wiki/Dmedia_T-REX" title="Dmedia T-REX">dmedia T-REX</a> Shih Chien-hsin, the team\'s GM, spokesperson, one coach, and three players to the office for allegedly being involved in game-fixing. The three players, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chen_Yuen-chia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chen Yuen-chia (page does not exist)">Chen Yuen-chia</a> (\xe9\x99\xb3\xe5\x85\x83\xe7\x94\xb2), <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chen_Ke-fan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chen Ke-fan (page does not exist)">Chen Ke-fan</a> (\xe9\x99\xb3\xe5\x85\x8b\xe5\xb8\x86), and <a href="/wiki/Cory_Bailey" title="Cory Bailey">Cory Bailey</a>, were later released after posting bail. In accordance with <a href="/wiki/CPBL" class="mw-redirect" title="CPBL">CPBL</a> policy, the three were immediately expelled and they were banned from playing professionally in the league. Due to the involvement of management/operation personnel in the scandal, the team was expelled from CPBL on October 23, 2008.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Chinatrust_Whales" title="Chinatrust Whales">Chinatrust Whales</a> also announced their disbandment on November 11, after playing twelve years in the CPBL. The league now only has 4 teams.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_game-fixing_scandal">2009 game-fixing scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: 2009 game-fixing scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The day after the final game of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Taiwan_Series" title="2009 Taiwan Series">2009 Taiwan Series</a> authorities raided the Brother Elephants\' dormitory in Taipei and ordered several players to report to the prosecutors\' office for questioning regarding game-fixing allegations. In the following weeks, multiple players from the Brother Elephants, Sinon Bulls, and La New Bears were questioned, and some of them were arrested and eventually charged. Furthermore, Elephants manager <a href="/wiki/Shin_Nakagomi" title="Shin Nakagomi">Shin Nakagomi</a> was also charged and later pled guilty in exchange for being released and allowed to return to Japan. Some of the convicted players were given prison sentences ranging from one to four years.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">Criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Original_research" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/40px-Ambox_important.svg.png" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/60px-Ambox_important.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/80px-Ambox_important.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="40" data-file-height="40" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section  <b>possibly contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">original research</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Professional_Baseball_League&amp;action=edit">improve it</a> by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verifying</a> the claims made and adding <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Inline_citations" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">inline citations</a>. Statements consisting only of original research should be removed.</span>  <small><i>(December 2014)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The CPBL has long been criticized for lacking protection of players\' rights, and players do not have a union like the MLB, NPB, and KBO. Players are often given one-year contracts that sometimes include terms that favor team management. Unrestricted <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agency</a> does not exist in the CPBL, as the CPBL fears that teams with lower payrolls could be overtaken by high-payroll teams. If a player wishes to sign with another team for any reason, then he must get the consent of the team he was playing with before. The player\'s team may counteroffer. That does not apply to a player seeking to sign with a team in another country.\n</p><p>The CPBL is governed by a <i>General Managers\' Council</i>, which means the members of the council are the respective <a href="/wiki/General_manager_(baseball)" title="General manager (baseball)">general managers</a> (GMs) of each team. This is unlike any other professional baseball league around the world, where the governing body usually consists of a committee of selected individuals unaffiliated with any other franchise. The CPBL governing structure effectively renders the league powerless in regulation and enforcement of many policies, as GMs can easily veto any major resolutions. Since major reform would require a unanimous approval of the council, it is difficult to initiate major reforms.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span
