href="/wiki/File:AshmoreandCartierIslands.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ashmore and Cartier Islands</div></div></div>\n<p>The <b>Territory of Ashmore and Cartier Islands</b><sup id="cite_ref-Agency2010_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Agency2010-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an uninhabited <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">external territory</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> consisting of four low-lying tropical islands in two separate <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reefs</a>, and the 12 nautical mile <a href="/wiki/Territorial_waters" title="Territorial waters">territorial sea</a> generated by the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The territory is located in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> situated on the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Continental_shelf" title="Continental shelf">continental shelf</a>, about 320&#160;km (199&#160;mi) off the northwest coast of Australia and 144&#160;km (89&#160;mi) south of the <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> island of <a href="/wiki/Rote_Island" title="Rote Island">Rote</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ashmore Reef is called <i>Pulau Pasir</i> by Indonesians and it is called <i>Nusa Solokaek</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Rotenese_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Rotenese language">Rotenese language</a>. links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Territory comprises Ashmore Reef, which includes West, Middle, and <a href="/wiki/East_Island_(Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands)" title="East Island (Ashmore and Cartier Islands)">East Islands</a>, and two <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, and Cartier Reef, which includes <a href="/wiki/Cartier_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartier Islands">Cartier Island</a>. Ashmore Reef covers approximately 150&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (57.9&#160;sq&#160;mi) and Cartier Reef 9&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (3&#160;sq&#160;mi), both to the limits of the reefs.<sup id="cite_ref-YrBk1981_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YrBk1981-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="An Australian Government page cited suggests Ashmore Reef is 239 sq km. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> They have a total of 74.1&#160;km (46&#160;mi) of shoreline, measured along the outer edge of the <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reef</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Which reef? Citation? CIA World Fact Book uses that figure, but does not say how measured. (April 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Australia also claims a 12 nautical mile <a href="/wiki/Territorial_waters" title="Territorial waters">territorial sea</a> generated by the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>West, Middle, and East Islands have a combined land area variously reported as 54 ha, 93 ha, and 112 ha (1 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">hectare</a> is 0.01&#160;km<sup>2</sup>, or about 2.5 <a href="/wiki/Acres" class="mw-redirect" title="Acres">acres</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Europa2004_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Europa2004-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Cartier Island has a reported land area of 0.4 ha.<sup id="cite_ref-Europa2004_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Europa2004-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>By a British <a href="/wiki/Order-in-council" class="mw-redirect" title="Order-in-council">Order-in-council</a> dated 23 July 1931, Ashmore and Cartier Islands were placed under the authority of the Commonwealth of Australia, but Australia officially accepted the Territory on 10 May 1934 when the <i>Ashmore and Cartier Islands Acceptance Act 1933</i> came into operation. The Act authorised the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Western_Australia" title="Governor of Western Australia">Governor of Western Australia</a> to make Ordinances for the Territory. In July 1938 the Territory was annexed to the Northern Territory, then also administered by the Commonwealth, whose laws, ordinances and regulations applied to the Territory. When <a href="/wiki/Self-government" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-government">self-government</a> was granted to the Northern Territory on 1 July 1978, administration of the Territory was retained by the Commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-YrBk1981_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YrBk1981-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1983 the Territory was declared a nature reserve under the <i>National Parks and Wildlife Conservation Act 1975</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> now replaced by the <i><a href="/wiki/Environment_Protection_and_Biodiversity_Conservation_Act_1999" title="Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999">Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>After the islands became a first point of contact with the <a href="/wiki/Australian_migration_zone" title="Australian migration zone">Australian migration zone</a>, in September 2001, the Australian government excised the Ashmore and Cartier Islands from the Australian migration zone.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesian_heritage_and_memorandum">Indonesian heritage and memorandum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Indonesian heritage and memorandum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Ashmore has been regularly visited and fished by Indonesian fishermen since the early eighteenth century. A 1974 Memorandum of Understanding between Australia and Indonesia sets out arrangements by which traditional fishers can access resources in Australia\'s territorial sea in the region. This allows traditional Indonesian fishermen to access parts of Ashmore for shelter, freshwater and to visit grave sites. The area, known as the <a href="/wiki/MOU_Box" title="MOU Box">MOU Box</a>, contains the Ashmore and Cartier Islands Territory.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Some Indonesian groups claim Ashmore Reef to be part of <a href="/wiki/Rote_Ndao_Regency" title="Rote Ndao Regency">Rote Ndao Regency</a> of <a href="/wiki/East_Nusa_Tenggara" title="East Nusa Tenggara">East Nusa Tenggara</a> province.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Today, the Territory is administered from <a href="/wiki/Canberra" title="Canberra">Canberra</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Infrastructure,_Regional_Development_and_Cities" title="Department of Infrastructure, Regional Development and Cities">Department of Infrastructure, Regional Development and Cities</a>, which is also responsible for the administration of the territories of <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Island" title="Christmas Island">Christmas Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocos_(Keeling)_Islands" title="Cocos (Keeling) Islands">Cocos (Keeling) Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Coral_Sea_Islands" title="Coral Sea Islands">Coral Sea Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jervis_Bay_Territory" title="Jervis Bay Territory">Jervis Bay Territory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Island" title="Norfolk Island">Norfolk Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AussieAG_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AussieAG-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Attorney-General%27s_Department_(Australia)" title="Attorney-General&#39;s Department (Australia)">Attorney-General\'s Department</a> had been responsible for the administration of Australian territories until the <a href="/wiki/Australian_federal_election,_2010" title="Australian federal election, 2010">2010 federal election</a>. In that year the responsibility for Australian territories was transferred to the then <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Regional_Australia,_Local_Government,_Arts_and_Sport" title="Department of Regional Australia, Local Government, Arts and Sport">Department of Regional Australia, Local Government, Arts and Sport</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and from 18 September 2013 the Department of Infrastructure and Regional Development has administered Australian territories.\n</p><p>Defence of Ashmore and Cartier Islands is the responsibility of Australia, with periodic visits by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Navy" title="Royal Australian Navy">Royal Australian Navy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Air_Force" title="Royal Australian Air Force">Royal Australian Air Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australian_Customs_and_Border_Protection_Service" title="Australian Customs and Border Protection Service">Australian Customs and Border Protection Service</a>.\n</p><p>Nearby Hibernia Reef, 42&#160;km (26&#160;mi) northeast of Ashmore Reef, is not part of the Territory, but belongs to <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It has no permanently dry land area, although large parts of the reef become exposed during low tide.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment_and_protection">Environment and protection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Environment and protection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Birds_of_Ashmore_Reef" title="Birds of Ashmore Reef">Birds of Ashmore Reef</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flora_of_Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands" title="Flora of Ashmore and Cartier Islands">Flora of Ashmore and Cartier Islands</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cartier_Island.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Cartier_Island.png/220px-Cartier_Island.png" width="220" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Cartier_Island.png/330px-Cartier_Island.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8e/Cartier_Island.png 2x" data-file-width="333" data-file-height="256" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cartier_Island.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cartier Island and surrounding reef (<a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> satellite image)</div></div></div>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Ashmore_Reef_Marine_Park" title="Ashmore Reef Marine Park">Ashmore Reef Marine Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cartier_Island_Marine_Park" title="Cartier Island Marine Park">Cartier Island Marine Park</a> are both classed as strict nature reserves (IUCN Ia) and protect biodiverse areas of significant and international importance, as well as cultural heritage.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There is no economic activity in the Territory, Ashmore and Cartier Islands being uninhabited. Cartier Island is an unvegetated sand island.<sup id="cite_ref-Europa2004_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Europa2004-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Access to Cartier Island is prohibited because of the risk of <a href="/wiki/Unexploded_ordnance" title="Unexploded ordnance">unexploded ordnances</a>. There are no ports or harbours, only offshore anchorage. The customs vessel <a href="/wiki/ACV_Ashmore_Guardian" title="ACV Ashmore Guardian">ACV <i>Ashmore Guardian</i></a> is stationed off the reef for up to 330 days per year.<sup id="cite_ref-FactSheet_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FactSheet-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The islands are also visited by seasonal caretakers and occasional scientific researchers.\n</p><p>The area has been a traditional fishing ground of Indonesian fishermen for centuries, and continues. In the 1850s, American whalers operated in the region. Mining of phosphate deposits took place on Ashmore Island in the latter half of the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Today, all the <a href="/wiki/Water_well" title="Water well">wells</a> in the Territory are infected with <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a> or contaminated and <a href="/wiki/Potable_water" class="mw-redirect" title="Potable water">undrinkable</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Petroleum extraction activities take place at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jabiru_oil_field&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jabiru oil field (page does not exist)">Jabiru</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Challis_oil_field&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Challis oil field (page does not exist)">Challis oil fields</a>, which are adjacent to the Territory, and which are administered by the Northern Territory Department of Mines and Energy on behalf of the Commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-DIRD_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DIRD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Migration">Migration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashmore_and_Cartier_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Migration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As Ashmore Reef is the closest point of Australian territory to Indonesia, it was a popular target for <a href="/wiki/People_smugglers" class="mw-redirect" title="People smugglers">people smugglers</a> transporting <a href="/wiki/Asylum_seekers" class="mw-redirect" title="Asylum seekers">asylum seekers</a> en route to Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Once they had landed on Ashmore Island, asylum seekers could claim to have entered Australian migration zone and request to be processed as refugees. The use of Ashmore Island for this purpose created great notoriety during late 2001, when refugee arrivals became a major political issue in Australia. The Australian Government argued that as Australia was not the country of first asylum for these "<a href="/wiki/Boat_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Boat people">boat people</a>", Australia did not have a responsibility to accept them.\n</p><p>A number of things were done to discourage the use of the Territory for this purpose, such as attempting to have the people smugglers arrested in Indonesia; the so-called <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Solution" title="Pacific Solution">Pacific Solution</a> of processing them in third countries; the boarding and forced turnaround of the boats by Australian military forces; and finally excising the Territory and many other small islands from the <a href="/wiki/Australian_migration_zone" title="Australian migration zone">Australian migration zone</a>.\n</p><p>Two boatloads of asylum seekers were each detained for several days in the lagoon at Ashmore Island after failed attempts by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Navy" title="Royal Australian Navy">Royal Australian Navy</a> to turn them back to Indonesia in October 2001.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
