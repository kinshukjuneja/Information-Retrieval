title="Government">government</a> as the official national <a href="/wiki/Song" title="Song">song</a>, or by <a href="/wiki/Convention_(norm)" title="Convention (norm)">convention</a> through use by the people. The majority of national anthems are <a href="/wiki/March_(music)" title="March (music)">marches</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hymn" title="Hymn">hymns</a> in style. The countries of <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> tend towards more ornate and <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">operatic</a> pieces, while those in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> use a more simplistic <a href="/wiki/Fanfare" title="Fanfare">fanfare</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A national anthem is most often in the <a href="/wiki/National_language" title="National language">national</a> or most common language of the country, whether <a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a> or <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official</a>, there are notable exceptions. Most commonly, states with more than one <a href="/wiki/National_language" title="National language">national language</a> may offer several versions of their anthem, for instance:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The "<a href="/wiki/Swiss_Psalm" title="Swiss Psalm">Swiss Psalm</a>", the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, has different lyrics for each of the country\'s four <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official languages</a> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romansh_language" title="Romansh language">Romansh</a>).</li>\n<li>The national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, "<a href="/wiki/O_Canada" title="O Canada">O Canada</a>", has official lyrics in both English and French which are not translations of each other, and is frequently sung with a mixture of stanzas, representing the country\'s <a href="/wiki/Bilingualism_in_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilingualism in Canada">bilingual nature</a>. The song itself was originally written in French.</li>\n<li>"<a href="/wiki/Amhr%C3%A1n_na_bhFiann" title="Amhr\xc3\xa1n na bhFiann">The Soldier\'s Song</a>", the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, was originally written and adopted in English, but an <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a> translation, although never formally adopted, is nowadays almost always sung instead.</li>\n<li>The current <a href="/wiki/National_anthem_of_South_Africa" title="National anthem of South Africa">South African national anthem</a> is unique in that five of the country\'s eleven official languages are used in the same anthem (the first <a href="/wiki/Stanza" title="Stanza">stanza</a> is divided between two languages, with each of the remaining three stanzas in a different language). It was created by combining two different songs together and then modifying the lyrics and adding new ones.</li>\n<li>One of the two official national anthems of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, "<a href="/wiki/God_Defend_New_Zealand" title="God Defend New Zealand">God Defend New Zealand</a>", is commonly now sung with the first verse in <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_language" title="M\xc4\x81ori language">M\xc4\x81ori</a> ("Aotearoa") and the second in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> ("God Defend New Zealand"). The tune is the same but the words are not a direct translation of each other.</li>\n<li>"<a href="/wiki/God_Bless_Fiji" title="God Bless Fiji">God Bless Fiji</a>" has lyrics in English and <a href="/wiki/Fijian_language" title="Fijian language">Fijian</a> which are not translations of each other. Although official, the Fijian version is rarely sung, and it is usually the English version that is performed at international sporting events.</li>\n<li>Although <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> has four official languages, with English being the current <i>lingua franca</i>, the national anthem, "<a href="/wiki/Majulah_Singapura" title="Majulah Singapura">Majulah Singapura</a>" is in <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> and by law can only be sung with its original Malay lyrics, despite the fact that Malay is a minority language in Singapore. This is because Part XIII of the Constitution of the Republic of Singapore declares, \xe2\x80\x9cthe national language shall be the Malay language and shall be in the Roman script [\xe2\x80\xa6]\xe2\x80\x9d</li>\n<li>There are several countries that do not have official lyrics to their national anthems. One of these is the "<a href="/wiki/Marcha_Real" title="Marcha Real">Marcha Real</a>", the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Although it originally had lyrics those lyrics were discontinued after governmental changes in the early 1980s after <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>\'s dictactorship. In 2007 a national competition to write words was held, but no lyrics were chosen.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Other national anthems with no words include "<a href="/wiki/Inno_Nazionale_della_Repubblica" title="Inno Nazionale della Repubblica">Inno Nazionale della Repubblica</a>", the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>, that of <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> and that of <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Europe_(anthem)" title="Europe (anthem)">Europe</a>".</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/National_Anthem_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="National Anthem of India">national anthem</a> of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Jana_Gana_Mana" title="Jana Gana Mana">Jana Gana Mana</a>", has lyrics in the <a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali language</a>, despite the fact that India has 22 official languages, with <a href="/wiki/Hindi_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindi language">Hindi</a> being the first official language and the most widely spoken of them.</li>\n<li>Despite the most common language in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> being English, the Welsh regional anthem "<a href="/wiki/Hen_Wlad_Fy_Nhadau" title="Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau">Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau</a>" is sung in the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh language</a>.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg/220px-Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg" width="220" height="183" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg/330px-Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg/440px-Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3525" data-file-height="2925" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Handschrift_Brussel_p-37-38.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Early version of the "Wilhelmus" as preserved in a manuscript of 1617 (Brussels, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Library_of_Belgium" title="Royal Library of Belgium">Royal Library</a>, MS 15662, fol. 37v-38r)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>\n<p>National anthems rose to prominence in Europe during the 19th century, but some originated much earlier. The presumed oldest national anthem belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and is called the "<a href="/wiki/Wilhelmus" title="Wilhelmus">Wilhelmus</a>". It was written between 1568 and 1572 during the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Revolt" title="Dutch Revolt">Dutch Revolt</a> and its current melody variant was composed shortly before 1626. It was a popular <a href="/w/index.php?title=Organgism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Organgism (page does not exist)">orangist</a> march during the 17th century but it did not become the official Dutch national anthem until 1932.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> national anthem, "<a href="/wiki/Kimigayo" title="Kimigayo">Kimigayo</a>", has the oldest lyrics, which were taken from a <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period (794\xe2\x80\x931185)</a> poem, yet it was not set to music until 1880.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippine</a> national anthem "<a href="/wiki/Lupang_Hinirang" title="Lupang Hinirang">Lupang Hinirang</a>" was composed in 1898 as wordless <a href="/wiki/Incidental_music" title="Incidental music">incidental music</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Philippine Declaration of Independence">ceremony declaring independence</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines" title="Spanish language in the Philippines">Spanish</a> poem "Filipinas" was written the following year to serve as the anthem\'s lyrics; the current <a href="/wiki/Tagalog_language" title="Tagalog language">Tagalog</a> version dates to 1962.\n</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/God_Save_the_Queen" title="God Save the Queen">God Save the Queen</a>", the national anthem of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_anthem" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal anthem">royal anthem</a> reserved for use in the presence of the Monarch in some Commonwealth realms, was first performed in 1619 under the title "God Save the King". It is not officially the national anthem of the UK, though it became such through custom and usage.\n</p><p>Spain\'s national anthem, the "Marcha Real" (The Royal March), written in 1761, was among the first to be adopted as such, in 1770. <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> adopted the older of its two national anthems, "<a href="/wiki/Kong_Christian_stod_ved_h%C3%B8jen_mast" title="Kong Christian stod ved h\xc3\xb8jen mast">Kong Christian stod ved h\xc3\xb8jen mast</a>", in 1780; and "<a href="/wiki/La_Marseillaise" title="La Marseillaise">La Marseillaise</a>", the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> national anthem, was written in 1792 and adopted in 1795. <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> became the first Eastern European nation to have a national anthem \xe2\x80\x93 "<a href="/wiki/Vostani_Serbije" title="Vostani Serbije">Rise up, Serbia!</a>" \xe2\x80\x93 in 1804.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Ee_Mungu_Nguvu_Yetu" title="Ee Mungu Nguvu Yetu">Ee Mungu Nguvu Yetu</a>", the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, is one of the first national anthems to be specifically commissioned. It was written by the Kenyan Anthem Commission in 1963 to serve as the anthem after independence from the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> regional anthem "<a href="/wiki/Hen_Wlad_Fy_Nhadau" title="Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau">Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau</a>" was the first anthem to be sung at an international sporting event. The anthem was sung in a Rugby game against <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> in <a href="/wiki/Llanelli" title="Llanelli">Llanelli</a>. This was done to counter the famous New Zealand <a href="/wiki/Haka" title="Haka">haka</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg/220px-Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg/330px-Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg/440px-Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="563" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Atat%C3%BCrk_schoolroom_wall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Schoolroom in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> with the words of the "<a href="/wiki/%C4%B0stikl%C3%A2l_Mar%C5%9F%C4%B1" class="mw-redirect" title="\xc4\xb0stikl\xc3\xa2l Mar\xc5\x9f\xc4\xb1">\xc4\xb0stikl\xc3\xa2l Mar\xc5\x9f\xc4\xb1</a>"</div></div></div>\n<p>National anthems are used in a wide array of contexts. Certain etiquette may be involved in the playing of a country\'s anthem. These usually involve military honours, standing up/rising, removing headwear etc. In diplomatic situations the rules may be very formal. There may also be <a href="/wiki/Royal_anthem" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal anthem">royal anthems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Presidential_anthem" class="mw-redirect" title="Presidential anthem">presidential anthems</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_anthem" class="mw-redirect" title="State anthem">state anthems</a> etc. for special occasions.\n</p><p>They are played on <a href="/wiki/National_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="National Day">national holidays</a> and festivals, and have also come to be closely connected with sporting events. Wales was the first country to adopt this, during a rugby game against New Zealand in 1905. Since then during sporting competitions, such as the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>, the national anthem of the <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> winner is played at each <a href="/wiki/Podium" title="Podium">medal ceremony</a>; also played before games in many sports leagues, since being adopted in baseball during World War II.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> When teams from two different nations play each other, the anthems of both nations are played, the host nation\'s anthem being played last.\n</p><p>In some countries, the national anthem is played to students each day at the start of school as an exercise in patriotism, such as in Tanzania.<sup id="cite_ref-aa_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aa-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In other countries the anthem may be played in a theatre before a play or in a cinema before a movie. Many radio and television stations have adopted this and play the national anthem when they <a href="/wiki/Sign_on" class="mw-redirect" title="Sign on">sign on</a> in the morning and again when they <a href="/wiki/Sign_off" class="mw-redirect" title="Sign off">sign off</a> at night. For instance, the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> is played before the broadcast of evening news on <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>\'s local television stations including <a href="/wiki/TVB_Jade" title="TVB Jade">TVB Jade</a> and <a href="/wiki/ATV_Home" title="ATV Home">ATV Home</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, it is a law to play the <a href="/wiki/National_Anthem_of_Colombia" title="National Anthem of Colombia">National Anthem</a> at 6:00 and 18:00 on every public radio and television station, while in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Phleng_Chat" class="mw-redirect" title="Phleng Chat">Phleng Chat</a>" is played at 08:00 and 18:00 nationwide (the <a href="/wiki/Phleng_Sansoen_Phra_Barami" class="mw-redirect" title="Phleng Sansoen Phra Barami">Royal Anthem</a> is used for sign-ons and closedowns instead).\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ROCanthemBySunYatSen.jpg" class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ROCanthemBySunYatSen.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The words of the <a href="/wiki/National_Anthem_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="National Anthem of the Republic of China">Taiwanese national anthem</a> written by <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen" title="Sun Yat-sen">Sun Yat-sen</a></div></div></div>\n<p>The use of a national anthem outside of its country, however, is dependent on the international recognition of that country. For instance, Taiwan has not been <a href="/wiki/Political_status_of_Taiwan" title="Political status of Taiwan">recognized</a> by the Olympics as a separate nation since 1979 and must compete as <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei_Olympic_Committee" title="Chinese Taipei Olympic Committee">Chinese Taipei</a>; its <a href="/wiki/National_Banner_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="National Banner href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">U.S. national anthem</a> as representing respect for dead soldiers and policemen whereas others view it as honoring the country first time</div></div></div>\n<p>Most of the best-known national anthems were written by little-known or unknown composers such as <a href="/wiki/Claude_Joseph_Rouget_de_Lisle" title="Claude Joseph Rouget de Lisle">Claude Joseph Rouget de Lisle</a>, composer of "\'La Marseillaise" and <a href="/wiki/John_Stafford_Smith" title="John Stafford Smith">John Stafford Smith</a> who wrote the tune for "<a href="/wiki/The_Anacreontic_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="The Anacreontic Song">The Anacreontic Song</a>", which became the tune for the U.S. national anthem, "The Star-Spangled Banner". The author of "God Save the Queen", one of the oldest and most well known anthems in the world, is unknown and disputed.\n</p><p>Very few countries have a national anthem written by a world-renowned composer. Exceptions include Germany, whose anthem "<a href="/wiki/Das_Lied_der_Deutschen" class="mw-redirect" title="Das Lied der Deutschen">Das Lied der Deutschen</a>" uses a melody written by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Haydn" title="Joseph Haydn">Joseph Haydn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, whose national anthem "<a href="/wiki/Land_der_Berge,_Land_am_Strome" title="Land der Berge, Land am Strome">Land der Berge, Land am Strome</a>" is sometimes credited to <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a>. The "<a href="/wiki/Anthem_of_the_Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Anthem of the Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic">Anthem of the Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>" was composed by <a href="/wiki/Aram_Khachaturian" title="Aram Khachaturian">Aram Khachaturian</a>. The music of the "<a href="/wiki/Pontifical_Anthem" title="Pontifical Anthem">Pontifical Anthem</a>", anthem of the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a>, was composed in 1869 by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Gounod" title="Charles Gounod">Charles Gounod</a>, for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_jubilee" title="Golden jubilee">golden jubilee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_IX" title="Pope Pius IX">Pope Pius IX</a>\'s priestly ordination.\n</p><p>The committee charged with choosing a <a href="/wiki/Negaraku" title="Negaraku">national anthem for Malaysia</a> at independence decided to invite selected composers of international repute to submit compositions for consideration, including <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Britten" title="Benjamin Britten">Benjamin Britten</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Walton" title="William Walton">William Walton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gian_Carlo_Menotti" title="Gian Carlo Menotti">Gian Carlo Menotti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zubir_Said" title="Zubir Said">Zubir Said</a>, who later composed "<a href="/wiki/Majulah_Singapura" title="Majulah Singapura">Majulah Singapura</a>", the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>. None were deemed suitable.\n</p><p>A few anthems have words by <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel laureates in literature</a>. The first Asian laureate, <a href="/wiki/Rabindranath_Tagore" title="Rabindranath Tagore">Rabindranath Tagore</a>, wrote the words and music of "<a href="/wiki/Jana_Gana_Mana" title="Jana Gana Mana">Jana Gana Mana</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Amar_Shonar_Bangla" title="Amar Shonar Bangla">Amar Shonar Bangla</a>", later adopted as the national anthems of India and <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> respectively. <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B8rnstjerne_Bj%C3%B8rnson" title="Bj\xc3\xb8rnstjerne Bj\xc3\xb8rnson">Bj\xc3\xb8rnstjerne Bj\xc3\xb8rnson</a> wrote the lyrics for the Norwegian national anthem "<a href="/wiki/Ja,_vi_elsker_dette_landet" title="Ja, vi elsker dette landet">Ja, vi elsker dette landet</a>".\n</p><p>Other countries had their anthems composed by locally important people. This is the case for <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/National_Anthem_of_Colombia" title="National Anthem of Colombia">anthem\'s</a> lyrics were written by former president and poet <a href="/wiki/Rafael_N%C3%BA%C3%B1ez_(politician)" title="Rafael N\xc3\xba\xc3\xb1ez (politician)">Rafael Nu\xc3\xb1ez</a>, who also wrote the country\'s first constitution. A similar case is Liberia, the national <a href="/wiki/All_Hail,_Liberia,_Hail!" title="All Hail, Liberia, Hail!">anthem</a> of which was written by its third president, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bashiel_Warner" title="Daniel Bashiel Warner">Daniel Bashiel Warner</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modality">Modality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Modality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=National_anthem&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>.
