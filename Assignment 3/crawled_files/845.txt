&amp; Poor\'s Financial Services LLC</b> (<b>S&amp;P</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Financial_services" title="Financial services">financial services</a> company. It is a division of <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Global" title="S&amp;P Global">S&amp;P Global</a> that publishes financial research and analysis on <a href="/wiki/Stock" title="Stock">stocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bond_(finance)" title="Bond (finance)">bonds</a>, and commodities. S&amp;P is known for its <a href="/wiki/Stock_market_index" title="Stock market index">stock market indices</a> such as the U.S.-based <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a>, the Canadian <a href="/wiki/S%26P/TSX_Composite" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P/TSX Composite">S&amp;P/TSX</a>, and the Australian <a href="/wiki/S%26P/ASX_200" title="S&amp;P/ASX 200">S&amp;P/ASX 200</a>. S&amp;P is considered one of the <a href="/wiki/Big_Three_(credit_rating_agencies)" title="Big Three (credit rating agencies)">Big Three</a> <a href="/wiki/Credit_rating_agency" title="Credit rating agency">credit-rating agencies</a>, which also include <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Investors_Service" title="Moody&#39;s Investors Service">Moody\'s Investors Service</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fitch_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Fitch Group">Fitch Ratings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Its head office is located on <a href="/wiki/55_Water_Street" title="55 Water Street">55 Water Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Manhattan" title="Lower Manhattan">Lower Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Poor%27s_Directory_of_Railroad_Officials_1893_%28Frontispiece%29.jpg/440px-Poor%27s_Directory_of_Railroad_Officials_1893_%28Frontispiece%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1553" data-file-height="2550" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Poor%27s_Directory_of_Railroad_Officials_1893_(Frontispiece).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>"Poor\'s Directory of Railroad Officials", 1893 (frontispiece)</div></div></div><p>The company traces its history back to 1860, with the publication by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Varnum_Poor" title="Henry Varnum Poor">Henry Varnum Poor</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/History_of_Railroads_and_Canals_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Railroads and Canals in the United States">History of Railroads and Canals in the United States</a></i>. This book compiled comprehensive information about the financial and operational state of U.S. railroad companies. In 1868, Henry Varnum Poor established <b>H.V. and H.W. Poor Co.</b> with his son, <a href="/wiki/Henry_William_Poor" title="Henry William Poor">Henry William Poor</a>, and published two annually updated hardback guidebooks, <i>Poor\'s Manual of the Railroads of the United States</i> and <i>Poor\'s Directory of Railway Officials</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-snphistory_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snphistory-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-time-history_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time-history-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1906, Luther Lee Blake founded the <b>Standard Statistics Bureau</b>, with the view to providing financial information on non-railroad companies. Instead of an annually published book, Standard Statistics would use 5-by-7-inch cards, allowing for more frequent updates.<sup id="cite_ref-snphistory_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snphistory-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1941, Paul Talbot Babson purchased Poor\'s Publishing and merged it with Standard Statistics to become <b>Standard &amp; Poor\'s Corp.</b> In 1966, the company was acquired by <a href="/wiki/S%26P_Global" title="S&amp;P Global">The McGraw-Hill Companies</a>, extending McGraw-Hill into the field of financial information services.<sup id="cite_ref-snphistory_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snphistory-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Credit_ratings"><span id="credit_ratings"></span>Credit ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Credit ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Credit_rating_agency" title="Credit rating agency">credit-rating agency (CRA)</a>, the company issues credit ratings for the debt of public and private companies, and other public borrowers such as governments and governmental entities. It is one of several CRAs that have been designated a <a href="/wiki/Nationally_recognized_statistical_rating_organization" title="Nationally recognized statistical rating organization">nationally recognized statistical rating organization</a> by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a>.\n</p><p>S&amp;P issues both short-term and long-term <a href="/wiki/Credit_rating" title="Credit rating">credit ratings</a>. Below is a partial list; see <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://img.en25.com/Web/StandardandPoors/Ratings_Definitions.pdf">S&amp;P\'s website</a> for more information.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Long-term_credit_ratings">Long-term credit ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Long-term credit ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:652px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png/650px-Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png" width="650" height="285" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png/975px-Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png/1300px-Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png 2x" data-file-width="1425" data-file-height="625" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Countries_by_Standard_%26_Poor%27s_Foreign_Rating.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Countries by Standard &amp; Poor\'s Foreign Rating (February 2018) <table class="wikitable">  <tbody><tr> <td style="background: #385e0f">AAA </td> <td style="background: #6e8b3d">AA </td> <td style="background: #bee554">A </td> <td style="background: #ffc125">BBB </td> <td style="background: #ff8600">BB </td> <td style="background: #e04006">B </td> <td style="background: #802a2a">CCC </td> <td style="background: #402a2a">CC/D </td></tr></tbody></table></div></div></div>\n<p>The company rates borrowers on a scale from AAA to D. Intermediate ratings are offered at each level between AA and CCC (e.g., BBB+, BBB and BBB\xe2\x88\x92). For some borrowers, the company may also offer guidance (termed a "credit watch") as to whether it is likely to be upgraded (positive), downgraded (negative) or uncertain (neutral).\n</p><p><b>Investment Grade</b>\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>AAA</b>: An obligor rated \'AAA\' has extremely strong capacity to meet its financial commitments. \'AAA\' is the highest issuer credit rating assigned by Standard &amp; Poor\'s.</li>\n<li><b>AA</b>:  An obligor rated \'AA\' has very strong capacity to meet its financial commitments. It differs from the highest-rated obligors only to a small degree. Includes:\n<ul><li><b>AA+</b>: equivalent to <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s#credit_ratings" class="mw-redirect" title="Moody&#39;s">Moody\'s</a> Aa1 (high quality, with very low credit risk, but susceptibility to long-term risks appears somewhat greater)</li>\n<li><b>AA</b>:  equivalent to Aa2</li>\n<li><b>AA\xe2\x88\x92</b>: equivalent to Aa3</li></ul></li>\n<li><b>A</b>:   An obligor rated \'A\' has strong capacity to meet its financial commitments but is somewhat more susceptible to the adverse effects of changes in circumstances and economic conditions than obligors in higher-rated categories.\n<ul><li><b>A+</b>: equivalent to A1</li>\n<li><b>A</b>:  equivalent to A2</li></ul></li>\n<li><b>BBB</b>: An obligor rated \'BBB\' has adequate capacity to meet its financial commitments. However, adverse economic conditions or changing circumstances are more likely to lead to a weakened capacity of the obligor to meet its financial commitments.</li></ul>\n<p><b>Non-Investment Grade</b> (also known as speculative-grade)\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>BB</b>:  An obligor rated \'BB\' is less vulnerable in the near term than other lower-rated obligors. However, it faces major ongoing uncertainties and exposure to adverse business, financial, or economic conditions, which could lead to the obligor\'s inadequate capacity to meet its financial commitments.</li>\n<li><b>B</b>:   An obligor rated \'B\' is more vulnerable than the obligors rated \'BB\', but the obligor currently has the capacity to meet its financial commitments. Adverse business, financial, or economic conditions will likely impair the obligor\'s capacity or willingness to meet its financial commitments.</li>\n<li><b>CCC</b>: An obligor rated \'CCC\' is currently vulnerable, and is dependent upon favorable business, financial, and economic conditions to meet its financial commitments.</li>\n<li><b>CC</b>:  An obligor rated \'CC\' is currently highly vulnerable.</li>\n<li><b>C</b>:   highly vulnerable, perhaps in bankruptcy or in arrears but still continuing to pay out on obligations</li>\n<li><b>R</b>:   An obligor rated \'R\' is under regulatory supervision owing to its financial condition. During the pendency of the regulatory supervision, the regulators may have the power to favor one class of obligations over others or pay some obligations and not others.</li>\n<li><b>SD</b>:  has selectively defaulted on some obligations</li>\n<li><b>D</b>:   has defaulted on obligations and S&amp;P believes that it will generally default on most or all obligations</li>\n<li><b>NR</b>:  not rated</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Short-term_issue_credit_ratings">Short-term issue credit ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Short-term issue credit ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The company rates specific issues on a scale from A-1 to D. Within the A-1 category it can be designated with a plus sign (+). This indicates that the issuer\'s commitment to meet its obligation is very strong. <a href="/wiki/Country_risk" title="Country risk">Country risk</a> and currency of repayment of the obligor to meet the issue obligation are factored into the credit analysis and reflected in the issue rating.\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>A-1</b>: obligor\'s capacity to meet its financial commitment on the obligation is strong</li>\n<li><b>A-2</b>: is susceptible to adverse economic conditions however the obligor\'s capacity to meet its financial commitment on the obligation is satisfactory</li>\n<li><b>A-3</b>: adverse economic conditions are likely to weaken the obligor\'s capacity to meet its financial commitment on the obligation</li>\n<li><b>B</b>:   has significant speculative characteristics. The obligor currently has the capacity to meet its financial obligation but faces major ongoing uncertainties that could impact its financial commitment on the obligation</li>\n<li><b>C</b>:   currently vulnerable to nonpayment and is dependent upon favorable business, financial and economic conditions for the obligor to meet its financial commitment on the obligation</li>\n<li><b>D</b>:   is in payment default. Obligation not made on due date and grace period may not have expired. The rating is also used upon the filing of a bankruptcy petition.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stock_market_indices">Stock market indices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stock market indices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>It publishes a large number of stock market indices, covering every region of the world, <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market capitalization</a> level and type of investment (e.g., indices for <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_investment_trust" title="Real estate investment trust">REITs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Preferred_stock" title="Preferred stock">preferred stocks</a>)\n</p><p>These indices include:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/S%26P_500" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500">S&amp;P 500</a> \xe2\x80\x93 free-float capitalization-weighted index of the prices of 500 large-capitalization common stocks actively traded in the US.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/S%26P_400" title="S&amp;P 400">S&amp;P 400 MidCap Index</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/S%26P_600" title="S&amp;P 600">S&amp;P 600 SmallCap Index</a><sup id="cite_ref-S&amp;P_SC600_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-S&amp;P_SC600-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance_scores">Governance scores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Governance scores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>S&amp;P has had a variety of approaches to reflecting its opinion of the relative strength of a company\'s <a href="/wiki/Corporate_governance" title="Corporate governance">corporate governance</a> practices. Corporate governance serves as an investor protection against potential governance-related losses of value, or failure to create value.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CGS_scores">CGS scores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: CGS scores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>S&amp;P developed criteria and methodology for assessing corporate governance. It started issuing Corporate Governance Scores (CGS) in 2000. CGS assessed companies\' corporate governance practices. They were assigned at the request of the company being assessed, were non-public (although companies were free to disclose them to and sometimes did) and were limited to public U.S. corporations. In 2005, S&amp;P stopped issuing CGS.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="GAMMA_scores">GAMMA scores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: GAMMA scores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>S&amp;P\'s Governance, Accountability, Management Metrics and Analysis (GAMMA) scores were designed for equity investors in emerging markets and focused on non-financial-risk assessment, and in particular, assessment of corporate-governance risk. S&amp;P discontinued providing stand-alone governance scores in 2011, "while continuing to incorporate governance analysis in global and local scale credit ratings".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Management_and_Governance_criteria">Management and Governance criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Management and Governance criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In November 2012, S&amp;P published its criteria for evaluating insurers and non-financial enterprises\' management and governance credit factors.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> These scores are not standalone, but rather, a component used by S&amp;P in assessing an enterprises\xe2\x80\x99 overall creditworthiness. S&amp;P updated its management and governance scoring methodology as part of a larger effort to include <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_risk_management" title="Enterprise risk management">enterprise risk management</a> analysis in its rating of debt issued by non-financial companies. "Scoring of management and governance is made on a scale of weak, fair, satisfactory or strong, depending on the mix of positive and negative management scores and the existence and severity of governance deficiencies."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Downgrade_of_U.S._long-term_credit_rating">Downgrade of U.S. long-term credit rating</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Downgrade of U.S. long-term credit rating">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/United_States_federal_government_credit_rating_downgrade,_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United States federal government credit rating downgrade, 2011">United States federal government credit rating downgrade, 2011</a></div><p>\nOn August 5, 2011, following enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Budget_Control_Act_of_2011" title="Budget Control Act of 2011">Budget Control Act of 2011</a>, S&amp;P lowered the US\'s sovereign long-term credit rating from AAA to AA+.<sup id="cite_ref-Swann_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swann-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The press release sent with the decision said, in part:</p><blockquote>\n<ul><li>" The downgrade reflects our opinion that the fiscal consolidation plan that Congress and the Administration recently agreed to falls short of what, in our view, would be necessary to stabilize the government\'s medium-term debt dynamics.</li>\n<li>" More broadly, the downgrade reflects our view that the effectiveness, stability, and predictability of American policymaking and political institutions have weakened at a time of ongoing fiscal and economic challenges to a degree more than we envisioned when we assigned a negative outlook to the rating on April 18, 2011.</li>\n<li>" Since then, we have changed our view of the difficulties in bridging the gulf between the political parties over fiscal policy, which makes us pessimistic about the capacity of Congress and the Administration to be able to leverage their agreement this week into a broader fiscal consolidation plan that stabilizes the government\'s debt dynamics any time soon."<sup id="cite_ref-Swann_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swann-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></blockquote>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">United States Department of the Treasury</a>, which had first called S&amp;P\'s attention to its $2 trillion error in calculating the ten-year deficit reduction under the Budget Control Act, commented, "The magnitude of this mistake \xe2\x80\x93 and the haste with which S&amp;P changed its principal rationale for action when presented with this error \xe2\x80\x93 raise fundamental questions about the credibility and integrity of S&amp;P\xe2\x80\x99s ratings action."<sup id="cite_ref-bellows_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bellows-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The following day, S&amp;P acknowledged in writing the US$2 trillion error in its calculations, saying the error "had no impact on the rating decision" and adding:<sup id="cite_ref-clarifyUS_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clarifyUS-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>In taking a longer term horizon of 10 years, the U.S. net general government debt level with the current assumptions would be $20.1 trillion (85% of 2021 GDP).  With the original assumptions, the debt level was projected to be $22.1 trillion (93% of 2021 GDP).<sup id="cite_ref-clarifyUS_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clarifyUS-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<p>In 2013 the Justice Department charged Standard &amp; Poor\'s with fraud in a $5 billion lawsuit: U.S. v. McGraw-Hill Cos et al., U.S. District Court, Central District of California, No. 13-00779. Since it did not charge Fitch and Moody\'s and because the Department did not give access to evidence, there has been speculation whether the lawsuit may have been in retaliation to S&amp;P\'s decision to downgrade. On April 15, 2013, the Department of Justice was ordered to grant S&amp;P access to evidence.<sup id="cite_ref-wsj_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Downgrade_of_France.27s_long-term_credit_rating"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Downgrade_of_France\'s_long-term_credit_rating">Downgrade of France\'s long-term credit rating</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Downgrade of France&#039;s long-term credit rating">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On November 11, 2011 S&amp;P erroneously announced the cut of France\'s triple-A rating (AAA). French leaders said that the error was inexcusable and called for even more regulation of private credit rating agencies (CRA\'s).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  On January 13, 2012 S&amp;P truly cut France\'s AAA rating, lowering it to AA+. This was the first time since 1975 that Europe\'s second-biggest economy, France, had been downgraded to AA+.  The same day S&amp;P downgraded the rating of eight other European countries: <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span id="Downgrade_of_Brazil.27s_sovereign_debt_rating"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Downgrade_of_Brazil\'s_sovereign_debt_rating">Downgrade of Brazil\'s sovereign debt rating</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Downgrade of Brazil&#039;s sovereign debt rating">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On March 11, 2014 S&amp;P downgraded the long-term sovereign debt rating of Latin America\xe2\x80\x99s largest economy, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, by one notch to BBB\xe2\x88\x92, the agency\xe2\x80\x99s lowest investment-grade rating, from BBB. The agency added that Brazil\xe2\x80\x99s fiscal credibility was "systematically weakened" by reductions in the government\'s budget target, while loans issued by state-controlled banks to stimulate spending and increase growth "undermined policy credibility and transparency" instead.\nThis came as a blow to President <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rousseff" title="Dilma Rousseff">Dilma Rousseff</a>, whose efforts to stir the economy from a years-long slump have eroded the country\'s finances.\n"The downgrade reflects the combination of fiscal slippage, the prospect that fiscal execution will remain weak amid subdued growth in the coming years, a constrained ability to adjust policy ahead of the October presidential elections, and some weakening in Brazil\'s external accounts," S&amp;P said.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 9, 2015, Standard &amp; Poor\'s Ratings Services lowered its long-term foreign currency sovereign credit rating on the Federative Republic of Brazil to \'BB+\' from \'BBB\xe2\x88\x92\', and the long-term local currency sovereign credit rating to \'BBB\xe2\x88\x92\' from \'BBB+\'. "The outlook is negative. We also lowered the short-term foreign currency rating to \'B\' from \'A-3\' and the short-term local currency rating to \'A-3\' from \'A-2\'. We also lowered the transfer and convertibility assessment to \'BBB\' from \'BBB+\'. We affirmed the \'brAAA\' national-scale rating and revised the outlook on this rating to negative", S&amp;P said.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The company publishes <i>The Outlook</i>, a weekly investment advisory newsletter for individuals and professional investors, published continuously since 1922.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <i>Credit Week</i> is produced by Standard &amp; Poor\'s Credit Market Services Group. It offers a comprehensive view of the global credit markets, providing credit rating news and analysis. Standard &amp; Poor\'s offers numerous other editorials, investment commentaries and news updates for financial markets, companies, industries, stocks, bonds, funds, economic outlook and investor education. All publications are available to subscribers.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>S&amp;P Dow Jones Indices publishes several blogs that do not require a subscription to access. These include Indexology, VIX Views and Housing Views.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_and_scandal">Criticism and scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Criticism and scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Credit_rating_agency#Criticism" title="Credit rating agency">Credit rating agency \xc2\xa7&#160;Criticism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_public_debt" class="mw-redirect" title="United States public debt">United States public debt</a></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Role_in_the_2007-08_financial_crisis">Role in the 2007-08 financial crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Role in the 2007-08 financial crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Credit rating agencies such as S&amp;P have been cited for contributing to the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%9308" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007\xe2\x80\x9308">financial crisis of 2007\xe2\x80\x9308</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Klein_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Klein-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  Credit ratings of AAA (the highest rating available) were given to large portions of even the riskiest pools of loans in the <a href="/wiki/Collateralized_debt_obligation" title="Collateralized debt obligation">collateralized debt obligation</a> (CDO) market.  When the <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_bubble" title="Real estate bubble">real estate bubble</a> burst in 2007, many loans went bad due to falling housing prices and the inability of bad creditors to refinance.  Investors who had trusted the AAA rating to mean that CDO were low-risk had purchased large amounts that later experienced staggering drops in value or <a href="/wiki/Toxic_assets" class="mw-redirect" title="Toxic assets">could not be sold at any price</a>. For example, institutional investors lost $125&#160;million on $340.7&#160;million worth of CDOs issued by <a href="/wiki/Credit_Suisse_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Credit Suisse Group">Credit Suisse Group</a>, despite being rated AAA by S&amp;P.<sup id="cite_ref-cdo_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cdo-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Klein_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Klein-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Companies pay S&amp;P, Moody\'s and Fitch to rate their debt issues. As a result, some critics have contended that the credit ratings agencies are beholden to these issuers and that their ratings are not as objective as they ought to be, due to this "pay to play" model.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015, Standard and Poor\'s paid $1.5 billion to the U.S. Justice Department, various state governments, and the <a href="/wiki/California_Public_Employees%27_Retirement_System" class="mw-redirect" title="California Public Employees&#39; Retirement System">California Public Employees\' Retirement System</a> to settle lawsuits asserting its inaccurate ratings defrauded investors.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_of_sovereign_debt_ratings">Criticism of sovereign debt ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Criticism of sovereign debt ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In April 2009, the company called for "new faces" in the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Irish government</a>, which was seen as interfering in the democratic process. In a subsequent statement they said they were "misunderstood".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>S&amp;P acknowledged making a US$2 trillion error in its justification for downgrading the credit rating of the United States in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> but stated that it "had no impact on the rating decision".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Jonathan Portes, director of <a href="/wiki/NIESR" class="mw-redirect" title="NIESR">NIESR</a>, Britain\'s longest established independent economic research institute, has observed that "S&amp;P\'s record&#160;.&#160;.&#160;. is remarkable. The agency downgraded Japan\'s credit rating in 2002, since when it has had the lowest long-term interest rates in recorded economic history."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Krugman" title="Paul Krugman">Paul Krugman</a> wrote, "it\xe2\x80\x99s hard to think of anyone less qualified to pass judgment on America than the rating agencies," and, "S&amp;P\xe2\x80\x99s demands suggest that it\xe2\x80\x99s talking nonsense about the US fiscal situation".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The SEC is investigating whether the intent to downgrade the U.S. was leaked prior to the public announcement, since the stock market fell sharply for no apparent reason a day earlier, fed by rumors of an impending downgrade.\n</p><p>In late 2013, S&amp;P downgraded France\'s credit rating. <a href="/wiki/Paul_Krugman" title="Paul Krugman">Paul Krugman</a> commented that the decision was based on politics, rather than sound financial analysis.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_Federal_Court_decision">Australian Federal Court decision</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Australian Federal Court decision">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In November 2012, Jagot J of the Federal Court of Australia found that:\n"A reasonably competent ratings agency could not have rated the Rembrandt 2006-3 CPDO AAA in these circumstances"; and\n"S&amp;P\xe2\x80\x99s rating of AAA of the Rembrandt 2006-2 and 2006-3 CPDO notes was misleading and deceptive and involved the publication of information or statements false in material particulars and otherwise involved negligent misrepresentations to the class of potential investors in Australia, which included LGFS and the councils, because by the AAA rating there was conveyed a representation that in S&amp;P\xe2\x80\x99s opinion the capacity of the notes to meet all financial obligations was \xe2\x80\x9cextremely strong\xe2\x80\x9d and a representation that S&amp;P had reached this opinion based on reasonable grounds and as the result of an exercise of reasonable care when neither was true and S&amp;P also knew not to be true at the time made."\nIn conclusion, Jagot found S&amp;P to be jointly liable along with ABN Amro and LGFS.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Antitrust_review">Antitrust review</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Standard_%26_Poor%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Antitrust review">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In November 2009, ten months after launching an investigation, the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> (EC) formally charged S&amp;P with abusing its position as the sole provider of international securities identification codes for United States of America <a href="/wiki/Security_(finance)" title="Security (finance)">securities</a> by requiring European <a href="/wiki/Financial_institution" title="Financial institution">financial firms</a> and data vendors to pay licensing fees for their use. "This behavior amounts to unfair pricing," the EC said in its statement of objections which lays the groundwork for an adverse finding against S&amp;P. "The (numbers) are indispensable for a number of operations that financial institutions carry out&#160;&#8211; for instance, reporting to authorities or clearing and settlement&#160;&#8211; and cannot be substituted.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>S&amp;P has run the <a href="/wiki/CUSIP" title="CUSIP">CUSIP Service Bureau</a>, the only <a href="/wiki/International_Securities_Identification_Number" title="International Securities Identification Number">International Securities Identification Number</a> (ISIN) issuer in the US, on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/American_Bankers_Association" title="American Bankers Association">American Bankers Association</a>. In its formal statement of objections, the EC alleged "that S&amp;P is abusing this monopoly position by enforcing the payment of licence fees for the use of US ISINs by (a) banks and other financial services providers in the EEA and (b) information service providers in the EEA." It claims that comparable agencies elsewhere in the world either do not charge fees at all, or do so on the basis of distribution cost, rather than usage.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
