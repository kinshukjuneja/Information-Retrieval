<b>third stage, third level, </b>and<b> postsecondary education,</b> is the educational level following the completion of a school providing a <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education" title="Secondary education">secondary education</a>. The <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, for example, defines tertiary education as including universities as well as trade schools and colleges.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">Higher education</a> is taken to include <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate" class="mw-redirect" title="Undergraduate">undergraduate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_education" title="Postgraduate education">postgraduate education</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Vocational_education" title="Vocational education">vocational education</a> beyond secondary education is known as <i><a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further education</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, or <i><a href="/wiki/Continuing_education" title="Continuing education">continuing education</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.\n</p><p>Tertiary education generally culminates in the receipt of <a href="/wiki/Academic_certificate" title="Academic certificate">certificates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">diplomas</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degrees</a>.\n</p><p>The UNESCO stated that tertiary education focuses on learning endeavors in specialized fields. It includes academic and higher vocational education.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" id="In_the_United_Kingdom">In the United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tertiary_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>"Tertiary education" includes <a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further education</a> (FE), as well as <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a> (HE). Since the 1970s specialized FE colleges called \xe2\x80\x9ctertiary colleges\xe2\x80\x9d have been set up to offer courses such as <a href="/wiki/A_Levels" class="mw-redirect" title="A Levels">A Levels</a>, that allow progression to HE, alongside vocational courses. An early example of this which expanded in September 1982 as part of a reorganization of education in the <a href="/wiki/Halesowen" title="Halesowen">Halesowen</a> area which also saw three-tier education axed after just 10 years in force.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In some areas where schools do not universally offer sixth forms, tertiary colleges function as a sixth form college as well as a general FE college.\n</p><p>Unlike sixth form colleges, the staff join lecturers\' rather than teachers\' unions.\n</p><p>Under <a href="/wiki/Devolution_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Devolution in the United Kingdom">devolution in the United Kingdom</a>, education is administered separately in England, Wales, Northern Ireland and Scotland. In 2018 the Welsh Government adopted the term "tertiary education" to refer to post-16 education and training in Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Australia">In Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tertiary_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tertiary_education_in_Australia" title="Tertiary education in Australia">Tertiary education in Australia</a></div>\n<p>Within Australia "Tertiary Education" refers to continuing studies after a students <a href="/wiki/Higher_School_Certificate_(New_South_Wales)" title="Higher School Certificate (New South Wales)">Higher School Certificate</a>. It also refers to any education a student receives after final compulsory schooling, which occurs at the age of 17 within Australia. Tertiary Education options include <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technical_and_further_education" title="Technical and further education">TAFE</a> or private colleges.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_United_States_of_America">In the United States of America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tertiary_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In the United States of America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Higher_education_in_the_United_States" title="Higher education in the United States">higher education system in the United States</a> is decentralized and essentially independent from regulation by the federal government. It is diverse because there are private and public institutions. Some are small and affiliated with religious organizations. Others could be secular, rural, urban, or suburban. In short, there is a proper fit for each student. The United States Department of Education presents a broad-spectrum view of tertiary education and detailed information on the nation\xe2\x80\x99s educational structure, accreditation procedures, and connections to state as well as federal agencies and entities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Carnegie Classifications of Institutions of Higher Education provides an explanation so people will understand how American institutions of higher learning compare to each other. The Carnegie platform separates all accredited schools that give out degrees into categories that describe highest degree granted or special areas of study.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> US tertiary education includes various non-profit organizations promoting professional development of individuals in the field of higher education and helping expand awareness of related issues like international student services and complete campus internationalization.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup
