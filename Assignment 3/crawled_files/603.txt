naval race in a "no limit" game.</div></div></div>\n<p>A <b>national personification</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">anthropomorphism</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> or its people. It may appear in <a href="/wiki/Editorial_cartoon" title="Editorial cartoon">editorial cartoons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Propaganda" title="Propaganda">propaganda</a>.\n</p><p>Some early personifications in the Western world tended to be national manifestations of the majestic wisdom and war goddess <a href="/wiki/Minerva" title="Minerva">Minerva</a>/<a href="/wiki/Athena" title="Athena">Athena</a>, and often took the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> name of the ancient <a href="/wiki/Roman_province" title="Roman province">Roman province</a>. Examples of this type include <a href="/wiki/Britannia_(emblem)" class="mw-redirect" title="Britannia (emblem)">Britannia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germania_(personification)" title="Germania (personification)">Germania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hibernia_(personification)" title="Hibernia (personification)">Hibernia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helvetia" title="Helvetia">Helvetia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polonia_(personification)" title="Polonia (personification)">Polonia</a>. Examples of personifications of the <a href="/wiki/Liberty_(goddess)" title="Liberty (goddess)">Goddess of Liberty</a> include <a href="/wiki/Marianne" title="Marianne">Marianne</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a> (<i>Liberty Enlightening the World</i>), and many examples of United States coinage. Another ancient model was <a href="/wiki/Roma_(mythology)" title="Roma (mythology)">Roma</a>, a female <a href="/wiki/Deity" title="Deity">deity</a> who personified the city of <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and more broadly, the Roman state, and who was revived in the 20th Century as the personification of <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Mussolini</a>\'s "<a href="/wiki/New_Roman_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="New Roman Empire">New Roman Empire</a>". Examples of representations of the <a href="/wiki/Everyman" title="Everyman">everyman</a> or citizenry\xe2\x80\x94rather than of the nation itself\xe2\x80\x94are <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Michel" title="Deutscher Michel">Deutscher Michel</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Bull" title="John Bull">John Bull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Sam" title="Uncle Sam">Uncle Sam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox"
