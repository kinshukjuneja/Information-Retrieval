style="text-align:center;background-color: #efefef">Demographics</th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Population</th><td>\n0</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Rosevear</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Ros Veur</b></i>, <i>great promontory</i>) is the largest  (0.63 hectares (1.6 acres)) of the group of rocks known as the <a href="/wiki/Western_Rocks,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Western Rocks, Isles of Scilly">Western Rocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. The islands are on eastern side of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> on the south-west approaches to the island of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and are renowned for the numerous <a href="/wiki/Shipwrecks" class="mw-redirect" title="Shipwrecks">shipwrecks</a> in the area and the nearby <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Rock,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Bishop Rock, Isles of Scilly">Bishop Rock lighthouse</a>. All the uninhabited islands are owned by the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Cornwall" title="Duchy of Cornwall">Duchy of Cornwall</a> and are managed by the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly_Wildlife_Trust" title="Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust">Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</a>, which looks after the archaeological and historical remains on the islands, as well as the flora and fauna. Landing is not allowed on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Geography"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Geography</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#History"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">History</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#Natural_history"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">Natural history</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosevear,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Rosevear is the largest of the Western Rocks and has a relatively flat top to 5 metres (16&#160;ft). The island is situated within a 3 kilometres (1.9&#160;mi) long group of rocks which stretches from the Round Rock of Crebawethan in the north to <a href="/wiki/Pednathise_Head" title="Pednathise Head">Pednathise Head</a> in the south.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosevear,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The island was used as a base camp, during 1709 and 1710, for the Herbert salvage expedition which worked the wrecks of the <a href="/wiki/HMS_Association_(1697)" class="mw-redirect" title="HMS Association (1697)"><i>Association</i></a> and the other naval vessels in <a href="/wiki/Scilly_naval_disaster_of_1707" title="Scilly naval disaster of 1707">1707</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The ghost of opera singer and actress <a href="/wiki/Ann_Cargill" title="Ann Cargill">Ann Cargill</a>, who died when the British <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a> <a href="/wiki/Packet_ship" class="mw-redirect" title="Packet ship">packet ship</a> <a href="/wiki/Nancy_(1774_EIC_ship)" title="Nancy (1774 EIC ship)"><i> Nancy</i></a>  sank near Rosevear on 24 February 1784, is said to haunt the island. Her body, still clutching her 18 month old son, was originally buried on Rosevear, although she was later moved to <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Old_Church,_St_Mary%27s" title="St Mary&#39;s Old Church, St Mary&#39;s">Old Town Church</a> on <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s</a>, along with her child and Halden, the captain.<sup id="cite_ref-todd_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-todd-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the 1840s and 1850s the island was again inhabited by workmen, this time building the Bishop Rock lighthouse. It was said that they heard mysterious music and were troubled by ghosts.<sup id="cite_ref-todd_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-todd-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following a decision to build a lighthouse on the Bishop Rock in April 1847, <a href="/wiki/Trinity_House" title="Trinity House">Trinity House</a> decided to make Rosevear the base because it was the nearest island (3.2&#160;km) with a relatively flat area. The island was used as a base from 1847\xe2\x80\x931850, for the unsuccessful first attempt  and for the successful construction from 1851\xe2\x80\x9358. Four main elements of the base remain. Two buildings, one a workers lodge and mess and the other a workshop are to the north-west of the complex, a slab-built platform by the east coast and a <a href="/wiki/Forge" title="Forge">smiths hearth</a> between the lodgings and workshop. Another building can be seen further south on the east coast. The surviving walls are from 0.4m to 0.7m high, with the best preserved are up to 3.0m <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A ship\'s funnel lies amongst the rocks of Rosevear; this comes from the <i>Cit\xc3\xa9 de Verdun</i>, which was wrecked on Rosevear in 1925.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_history">Natural history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosevear,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Natural history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Landing on the uninhabited islands is both difficult and discouraged and there are few published records of visits by naturalists.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\nIn 1971, the rocks and islands were designated a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> for their breeding sea birds.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> A visit by <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Grigson" title="Geoffrey Grigson">Geoffrey Grigson</a>, in or around 1947, found a few <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_puffin" title="Atlantic puffin">Atlantic puffin</a> (<i>Fratercula arctica</i>), scores of <a href="/wiki/Razorbill" title="Razorbill">razorbill</a> (<i>Alca torda</i>), <a href="/wiki/European_shag" title="European shag">European shag</a> (<i>Phalacrocorax aristotelis</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Great_black-backed_gull" title="Great black-backed gull">great black-backed gull</a>  (<i>Larus marinus</i>). A heavy fall of migrants was seen on a visit by Rosemary Parslow in October 1990 with dozens of European robin (<i><a href="/wiki/Erithacus_rubecula" class="mw-redirect" title="Erithacus rubecula">Erithacus rubecula</a></i>) and goldcrest (<i><a href="/wiki/Regulus_regulus" class="mw-redirect" title="Regulus regulus">Regulus regulus</a></i>), and several yellow\xe2\x80\x93browed warbler (<i><a href="/wiki/Phylloscopus_inornatus" class="mw-redirect" title="Phylloscopus inornatus">Phylloscopus inornatus</a></i>); all feeding amongst the vegetation. The island has replaced <a href="/wiki/Western_Rocks,_Isles_of_Scilly#Melledgan" title="Western Rocks, Isles of Scilly">Melledgan</a> as the site of the third largest colony of <a href="/wiki/European_storm_petrel" title="European storm petrel">European storm petrel</a>  (<i>Hydrobates pelagicus</i>) in England with fifty-seven occupied sites recorded during the Seabird 2000 survey, increasing to 129 occupied sites in the repeat survey in 2006. The colony of shag on Rosevear and the rest of the Western Rocks is of national importance.<sup id="cite_ref-Parslow_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parslow-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>During storms the sea can wash over the island and there is a shingle community of plants with <a href="/wiki/Lavatera_arborea" class="mw-redirect" title="Lavatera arborea">tree mallow</a> (<i>Lavatera arborea</i>), sea curled dock (<i><a href="/wiki/Rumex_crispus" title="Rumex crispus">Rumex cripus littoreus</a></i>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Atriplex" title="Atriplex">Atriplex</a></i> sp. The only other species recorded are sea beet (<i><a href="/wiki/Sea_Beet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea Beet">Beta vulgaris<i> subsp. </i>maritima</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Cochlearia_officinalis" title="Cochlearia officinalis">common scurvygrass</a> (<i>Cochlearia officinalis</i>), <i><a href="/wiki/Orache" class="mw-redirect" title="Orache">orache</a></i> sp,  and <a href="/wiki/Spergularia" title="Spergularia">rock sea-spurry</a> (<i>Spergularia rupicola</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Sneddon_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sneddon-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosevear,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ios-wildlifetrust.org.uk/node/3209">"Land Management"</a>. <i>Isles of Scilly Wildlife Trust</i>. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20160424055524/http://www.ios-wildlifetrust.org.uk/node/3209">Archived</a> from the original on 24 April 2016<span class="reference-accessdate">.
