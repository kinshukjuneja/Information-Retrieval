<i>Serie del Caribe</i>), also called Caribbean World Series, is the highest tournament for <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> teams in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, featuring the champions of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The tournament location is rotated annually among the countries and is normally played in February after all of the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_leagues" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball leagues">leagues</a> have ended their national tournaments.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/2014_Caribbean_Series" title="2014 Caribbean Series">2014 Caribbean Series</a> was held at <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Nueva_Esparta" title="Estadio Nueva Esparta">Estadio Nueva Esparta</a> in <a href="/wiki/Margarita_Island" title="Margarita Island">Margarita Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> accepted an invitation to take part as a guest in the tournament. The Series would stay with the same five teams in 2015, which was held at <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Hiram_Bithorn" class="mw-redirect" title="Estadio Hiram Bithorn">Estadio Hiram Bithorn</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">San Juan, Puerto Rico</a> and 2016 in <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Quisqueya" title="Estadio Quisqueya">Estadio Quisqueya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo,_Dominican_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic">Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The competition was the brainchild of Venezuelan baseball entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Morales_P%C3%A9rez" title="Pablo Morales P\xc3\xa9rez">Pablo Morales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Prieto_Ortiz" title="Oscar Prieto Ortiz">Oscar Prieto Ortiz</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> his business partner since 1936, who devised the idea after seeing the success of the now extinct <a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serie_Interamericana" class="extiw" title="es:Serie Interamericana">Serie Interamericana</a> in 1946, which featured the clubs <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Bushwicks" title="Brooklyn Bushwicks">Brooklyn Bushwicks</a> from the United States, <a href="/wiki/Leones_del_Caracas" title="Leones del Caracas">Cervecer\xc3\xada Caracas</a> from Venezuela, <a href="/wiki/Sultanes_de_Monterrey" title="Sultanes de Monterrey">Sultanes de Monterrey</a> from Mexico, and an All-Star team composed of Cuban players.\n</p><p>Inspired by the Serie Interamericana and his experience as a former president of the <a href="/wiki/International_Baseball_Federation" title="International Baseball Federation">International Baseball Federation</a>, Morales joined Prieto and presented the idea to baseball representatives of Cuba, Panama and Puerto Rico during a meeting held in <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a> on August 21, 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The representatives then agreed to stage a four-country, <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin tournament</a> 12-game to be known as the <i>Serie del Caribe</i>, to be launched in Cuba from <a href="/wiki/1949_Caribbean_Series" title="1949 Caribbean Series">February 20\xe2\x80\x9325 of 1949</a>.\n</p><p>The Series ran annually from 1949 through 1960, with Cuba winning seven times. However, the event was cancelled after <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">Fidel Castro</a> dissolved all professional baseball in Cuba in 1961. It was not until 1970 when the Caribbean Series was revived. Moreover, the 1981 Caribbean Series was not held due to a Venezuelan League player\'s strike.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leagues_participating">Leagues participating</span><span the champion.
