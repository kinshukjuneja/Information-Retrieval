navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A UNESCO <b>City of Music</b> is a city that has joined the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization</a> (UNESCO) as notable in the field of <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>. The City of Music programme is part of UNESCO\'s <a href="/wiki/Creative_Cities_Network" title="Creative Cities Network">Creative Cities Network</a>, which is aimed at promoting cooperation with and among cities that have identified creativity as a strategic factor for sustainable urban development.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Currently, 30 cities from 23 countries located on six continents bear the title of UNESCO City of Music.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria_for_Cities_of_Music">Criteria for Cities of Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Music_(UNESCO)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criteria for Cities of Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>To be approved as a City of Music, cities need to meet a number of criteria set by UNESCO<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>. Designated UNESCO Cities of Music share similar characteristics:\n</p>\n<ul><li>recognized centres of musical creation and activity;</li>\n<li>experience in hosting <a href="/wiki/Musical_festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical festival">musical festivals</a> and events at a national or international level;</li>\n<li>promotion of the <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">music industry</a> in all its forms;</li>\n<li>music schools, conservatories, academies and higher education institutions specialized in music;</li>\n<li>informal structures for <a href="/wiki/Music_education" title="Music education">music education</a>, including amateur <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choirs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestras</a>;</li>\n<li>domestic or international platforms dedicated to particular <a href="/wiki/Music_genre" title="Music genre">genres of music</a> and/or music from other countries;</li>\n<li>cultural spaces suited for practicing and listening to music, e.g. open-air auditoriums.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_UNESCO_Cities_of_Music">List of
