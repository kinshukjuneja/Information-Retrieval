#ffcccc;">Status: Repealed</div></th></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Local Government (Scotland) Act 1889</b> (52 &amp; 53 Vict. c. 50) is an <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Parliament" title="Act of Parliament">Act</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> which was passed on 26 August 1889.  The main effect of the Act was to establish elected <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.  In this it followed the pattern introduced in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1888" title="Local Government Act 1888">Local Government Act 1888</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#County_councils"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">County councils</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a tocsection-6"><a href="#Notes_and_references"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">Notes and references</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="County_councils">County councils</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: County councils">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Act provided that a county council should be established in each county, consisting of elected councillors.  The county was to be divided into electoral divisions, made up of groupings of parishes, each returning one councillor.  In addition police <a href="/wiki/Burghs" class="mw-redirect" title="Burghs">burghs</a> in the county were also regarded as electoral divisions, though the councillors for these areas were co-opted by the members of the burgh\'s town council.\n</p><p>The chairman of each county council, elected by the members,  was given the title "<a href="/wiki/Convener" class="mw-redirect" title="Convener">Convenor of the county</a>".\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_transferred_to_the_county_councils">Powers transferred to the county councils</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Powers transferred to the county councils">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The county councils received numerous powers and duties previously performed by a number of bodies:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Commissioners_of_Supply" class="mw-redirect" title="Commissioners of Supply">Commissioners of Supply</a> for the county</li>\n<li>County road trustees</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Justices_of_the_peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justices of the peace">Justices of the peace</a> of the county in general or special or quarter sessions</li></ul>\n<p>Among the diverse functions acquired from the justices were inspection of gas meters, regulation of explosive materials, weights and measures, habitual drunkards, control of wild birds and providing visitors to asylums.\n</p><p>In addition the county council exercised authority under the Public Health Acts for the entire county outside burghs.  They also had jurisdiction under legislation dealing with contagious animal diseases and destructive insects.\n</p><p>County councils also took over police functions from burghs with less than 7,000 population.\n</p><p>Section 15 of the Act allowed the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_for_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary for Scotland">Secretary for Scotland</a> to transfer further powers to the councils.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_and_boundaries">Areas and boundaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Areas and boundaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The counties were initially to have the same boundaries as those already existing with some stated exceptions:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The county of <a href="/wiki/Lanarkshire" title="Lanarkshire">Lanark</a> was to be united. Previously, for some administrative purposes it comprised three counties, known as the counties of the lower, middle or upper ward of Lanark.</li>\n<li>The counties of <a href="/wiki/Ross-shire" title="Ross-shire">Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cromartyshire" title="Cromartyshire">Cromarty</a> were to be united "for all purposes whatsoever" as the county of <a href="/wiki/Ross_and_Cromarty" title="Ross and Cromarty">Ross and Cromarty</a>.</li>\n<li>The boundaries of the counties of <a href="/wiki/Dunbartonshire" title="Dunbartonshire">Dumbarton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stirlingshire" title="Stirlingshire">Stirling</a> were to be adjusted, so that the entire parishes of <a href="/wiki/Cumbernauld" title="Cumbernauld">Cumbernauld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirkintilloch" title="Kirkintilloch">Kirkintilloch</a> were to be in <a href="/wiki/Dunbartonshire" title="Dunbartonshire">Dunbartonshire</a>. This area remained an <a href="/wiki/Exclave" class="mw-redirect" title="Exclave">exclave</a> of Dunbartonshire until the county council\'s abolition in 1975. Stirling was gained the lands north of <a href="/wiki/Endrick_Water" title="Endrick Water">Endrick Water</a> as far as the centre-line of <a href="/wiki/Loch_Lomond" title="Loch Lomond">Loch Lomond</a>.</li>\n<li>Part of the county of <a href="/wiki/Banffshire" title="Banffshire">Banff</a> was transferred to the county of <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire" title="Aberdeenshire">Aberdeen</a>; and part of the county of <a href="/wiki/Morayshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Morayshire">Elgin</a> was transferred to the county of <a href="/wiki/Banffshire" title="Banffshire">Banff</a>. These areas had already been administered by the counties in question under highways legislation.</li>\n<li>The county of <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a> and lordship of <a href="/wiki/Shetland" title="Shetland">Zetland</a> were separated to form two counties with those names.</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boundary_changes">Boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>It was recognised in the Act that the boundaries of the counties would need to be altered. Accordingly, section 45 established a body styled the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Boundary_Commissioners_for_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Boundary Commissioners for Scotland (page does not exist)">Boundary Commissioners for Scotland</a> to form county electoral divisions, and to simplify the boundaries of counties, burghs and parishes, so that (1) each burgh and county would be, if expedient, within a single county, (2) no part of a county would be detached therefrom, and (3) no part of a parish would be detached therefrom, and to arbitrate disputes between local authorities arising from boundary changes. All boundary changes made by the commissioners came into full effect on 15 May 1892. For most purposes, however, the bulk of the changes became effective a year earlier, on 15 May 1891.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Subsequent to the commissioners\' dissolution, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_for_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary for Scotland">Secretary for Scotland</a> had the power to vary boundaries on the application of a council.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts_and_district_committees">Districts and district committees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Districts and district committees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Each county (unless it contained fewer than six parishes) was to be divided into districts consisting of groups of electoral divisions. Each district was to have a district committee consisting of the area\'s county councillors, one representative from each parochial board in the district, and one from each burgh. The committees performed the county council\'s functions in respect of the Public Health Acts and highways legislation.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes_and_references">Notes and references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notes and references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">Shennan, Hay (1892). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://scotlandsplaces.gov.uk/hay_shennan/pdfs/ross_and_cromarty-boundaries.pdf"><i>Boundaries of Counties and Parishes in Scotland: as settled by the Boundary Commissioners under the Local Government (Scotland) Act, 1889</i></a> <span class="cs1-format">(PDF)</span>.
