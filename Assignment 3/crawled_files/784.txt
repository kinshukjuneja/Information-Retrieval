Australia">Governor-General of Australia</a>.</li>\n</ol></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Australian Antarctic Territory</b> (<b>AAT</b>) is a part of <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> administered by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Antarctic_Division" title="Australian Antarctic Division">Australian Antarctic Division</a>, an agency of the federal <a href="/wiki/Department_of_the_Environment_and_Energy" title="Department of the Environment and Energy">Department of the Environment and Energy</a>. The territory\'s history dates to a claim on <a href="/wiki/Enderby_Land" title="Enderby Land">Enderby Land</a> made by the United Kingdom in 1841, which was subsequently expanded and eventually transferred to Australia in 1933. It is the largest territory of Antarctica claimed by any nation by area.  In 1961, the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Treaty_System" title="Antarctic Treaty System">Antarctic Treaty</a> came into force. Article 4 deals with territorial claims, and although it does not renounce or diminish any preexisting claims to sovereignty, it also does not prejudice the position of Contracting Parties in their recognition or non-recognition of territorial sovereignty. As a result, only four other countries; New Zealand, United Kingdom, France and Norway recognise Australia\'s claim to sovereignty in Antarctica.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>AAT consists of all the islands and territory south of <a href="/wiki/60th_parallel_south" title="60th parallel south">60\xc2\xb0S</a> and between <a href="/wiki/45th_meridian_east" title="45th meridian east">45\xc2\xb0E</a> and <a href="/wiki/160th_meridian_east" title="160th meridian east">160\xc2\xb0E</a>, except for <a href="/wiki/Ad%C3%A9lie_Land" title="Ad\xc3\xa9lie Land">Ad\xc3\xa9lie territory into Western AAT (the larger portion) and Eastern AAT. It is bounded by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Maud_Land" title="Queen Maud Land">Queen Maud Land</a> in the West and by <a href="/wiki/Ross_Dependency" title="Ross Dependency">Ross Dependency</a> in the East. The area is estimated at 5,896,500&#160;km<sup>2</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-dewha_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dewha-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The territory is inhabited by the staff of research stations. economic zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Exclusive economic zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Australia claims an <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone" title="Exclusive economic zone">exclusive economic zone</a> (EEZ) from the Australian Antarctic Territory. However, the Australian proclamation of an Antarctic EEZ is contested.  The effect of Article IV of the 1959 Antarctic Treaty (which prohibits new territorial claims or the extension of existing claims in the Antarctic) would seem to be that an EEZ cannot be claimed in relation to territory to which that Treaty applies (south of 60\xc2\xb0 South). The provisions of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a> (UNCLOS) define the exclusive economic zone of a coastal state as up to 200 nautical miles (370&#160;km) from the baseline from which the territorial sea is measured.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Whaling">Whaling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Whaling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Whaling" title="Whaling">Whaling</a> in Australian Antarctic territorial waters is controversial and has received international attention.<sup id="cite_ref-keep_out_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-keep_out-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Anti-whaling protest groups, in particular <a href="/wiki/Sea_Shepherd_Conservation_Society" title="Sea Shepherd Conservation Society">Sea Shepherd Conservation Society</a>, have been active within the Australian Antarctic territorial waters. Sea Shepherd small boat crews have had <a href="/wiki/Sea_Shepherd_Conservation_Society_operations" title="Sea Shepherd Conservation Society operations">multiple encounters</a> with <a href="/wiki/Whaling_in_Japan" title="Whaling in Japan">Japanese</a> ships that claim to be on research expeditions while opponents argue this is only a "cover" for banned <a href="/wiki/Commercial_whaling" class="mw-redirect" title="Commercial whaling">commercial whaling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Australian_Whale_Sanctuary" title="Australian Whale Sanctuary">Australian Whale Sanctuary</a>, in Australian Antarctic territory, is not recognised by the government of Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-keep_out_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-keep_out-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Anti-whaling legislation passed by the Australian Government applies to Australian territorial waters.  However, Australia\'s claims of sovereignty over the Australian Antarctic Territory\xe2\x80\x94and thus sovereignty over Australian Antarctic territorial waters\xe2\x80\x94are recognised by only the United Kingdom, New Zealand, France and Norway.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The United Kingdom first claimed <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Land" title="Victoria Land">Victoria Land</a> on 9 January 1841 and then claimed <a href="/wiki/Enderby_Land" title="Enderby Land">Enderby Land</a> in 1930. In 1933, a British imperial order transferred most of the territory south of <a href="/wiki/60th_parallel_south" title="60th parallel south">60\xc2\xb0 S</a> and between <a href="/wiki/Meridian_(geography)" title="Meridian (geography)">meridians</a> <a href="/wiki/160th_meridian_east" title="160th meridian east">160\xc2\xb0 E</a> and <a href="/wiki/45th_meridian_east" title="45th meridian east">45\xc2\xb0 E</a> to Australia.\n</p>\n<blockquote><p><i>That part of His Majesty\'s dominions in the Antarctic Seas which comprises all the islands and territories other than Ad\xc3\xa9lie Land which are situated south of the 60th degree of South Latitude and lying between the 160th degree of East Longitude and the 45th degree of East Longitude is hereby placed under the authority of the Commonwealth of Australia.</i><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<blockquote><p><b>Australian Antarctic Territory Acceptance Act 1933</b><br />\n<i>That part of the territory in the Antarctic seas which comprises all the islands and territories, other than Adelie Land, situated south of the 60th degree south latitude and lying between the 160th degree east longitude and the 45th degree east longitude, is hereby declared to be accepted by the Commonwealth as a Territory under the authority of the Commonwealth, by the name of the Australian Antarctic Territory.</i> <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/">C2004C00416 /  Australian Antarctic Territory Acceptance Act 1933 </a></i> ( Cth )</p></blockquote>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory_(unofficial).svg" class="image"><img alt="" width="220" height="110" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Flag_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory_%28unofficial%29.svg/330px-Flag_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory_%28unofficial%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Flag_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory_%28unofficial%29.svg/440px-Flag_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory_%28unofficial%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory_(unofficial).svg" class="internal" fixed definitively in 1938. In 1947, Britain transferred <a href="/wiki/Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands" title="Heard Island and McDonald Islands">Heard Island and McDonald Islands</a> to the territory. On 13 February 1954,<sup id="cite_ref-mawson_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mawson-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mawson_Station" title="Mawson Station">Mawson Station</a> was established as the first Australian station on the continent proper.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition_of_Australian_sovereignty">Recognition of Australian sovereignty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Recognition of Australian sovereignty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Australia\'s claim to sovereignty over the Australian Antarctic Territory is recognised by the United Kingdom, New Zealand, France and Norway.<sup id="cite_ref-leg_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leg-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Japan does not recognise the Australian claim to the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Antarctic_territorial_waters" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Antarctic territorial waters">Australian Antarctic territorial waters</a> in which Japanese ships conduct whaling.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining_in_Antarctica">Mining in Antarctica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mining in Antarctica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>During the early 1980s there was a brief debate in Australia on whether or not to allow <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">mineral-rich continent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mining_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mining-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Several mining proposals have been discussed and have all been rejected.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On the 9 August 2011, influential Australian think-tank, the <a href="/wiki/Lowy_Institute" title="Lowy Institute">Lowy Institute</a>, published a report warning Canberra against complacency when it comes to its claim.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The global treaty banning resource exploitation becomes reviewable in 2041,<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and some states may then decide to withdraw from it considering the continent\'s mineral deposits. These include coal seams, manganese, iron and uranium, while Antarctica\'s forecast oil reserves are estimated as among the largest in the world after <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. Lowy\'s national security fellow Ellie Fogarty said in the paper that Australia cannot adequately patrol its claim, lacking the kind of ski-planes it needs to reach some areas. It also lacks an ice-breaking ship in the region.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Postage_stamps">Postage stamps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Postage stamps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamps_and_postal_history_of_the_Australian_Antarctic_Territory" title="Postage stamps and postal history of the Australian Antarctic Territory">Postage stamps and postal history of the Australian Antarctic Territory</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg/300px-Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg" width="300" height="170" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg/450px-Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg/600px-Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="409" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Australian_Antarctic_Territory_postal_cover1959.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>This 1959 cover commemorated the opening of the Wilkes post office.</div></div></div>\n<p>Australia issues <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamp" title="Postage stamp">postage stamps</a> for the Australian Antarctic Territory. The first issues came in 1957, and sporadically thereafter, settling into a pattern of an annual issue by the 1990s. All have been Antarctic-themed, and all are valid for postage in Australia and its territories, including Antarctica.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Telephone_connections">Telephone connections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Antarctic_Territory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Telephone connections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Assigned the <a href="/wiki/Country_calling_code" class="mw-redirect" title="Country calling code">country calling code</a> +672 1[0-4] XXXX, the four stations and the Aurora Australis operated by the Australian Antarctic Division can be reached by direct calling from anywhere in the world. The area codes are 10 for Davis, 11 for Mawson, 12 for Casey, 13 for Macquarie Island and 14 for Wilkins and the Aurora Australis, in each case followed by four additional digits.\n</p>\n<h2><span
