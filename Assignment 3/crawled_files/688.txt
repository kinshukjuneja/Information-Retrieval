Theisteanas na h-Alba</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>)  is the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_public_bodies" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish public bodies">executive non-departmental public body</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> responsible for accrediting educational awards. It is partly funded by the <a href="/wiki/Education_and_Lifelong_Learning_Directorate" class="mw-redirect" title="Education and Lifelong Learning Directorate">Education and Lifelong Learning Directorate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a>, and employs approximately 750 staff based in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dalkeith" title="Dalkeith">Dalkeith</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>SQA is best known for the delivery of the annual diet of public examinations within Scotland for school pupils. SQA Higher examinations are the general acceptable level for entry to university, with <a href="/wiki/Scottish_universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish universities">Scottish universities</a> usually requesting a minimum of 3 Highers, all above C level. However, a greater number of candidates of all ages participates in SQA specialist, vocational and higher education qualifications. SQA is accredited by the UK government to offer educational qualifications.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="toclevel-1 tocsection-19"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The SQA\'s functions and responsibilities are laid out in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Education_(Scotland)_Act_1996&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Education (Scotland) Act 1996 (page does not exist)">Education (Scotland) Act 1996</a> as amended by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority_Act_2002&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Scottish Qualifications Authority Act 2002 (page does not exist)">Scottish Qualifications Authority Act 2002</a>. Until their merger, the two major Scottish examination authorities were the SEB (Scottish Examination Board) and the Scottish Vocational Education Council (SCOTVEC). It is the former of the two that issued the school-level examinations, then called Standard Grade, Higher Grade and <a href="/wiki/Certificate_of_Sixth_Year_Studies" title="Certificate of Sixth Year Studies">Certificate of Sixth Year Studies</a> (CSYS). A legacy of its two precursor bodies, the Authority\'s offices remain split over two sites, one in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> and one in <a href="/wiki/Dalkeith" title="Dalkeith">Dalkeith</a>.\n</p><p>Under a major reform of Scottish exams (the National Qualifications or "Higher Still" reforms). The main effect of this was to replace CSYS with a broadly equivalent qualification called Advanced Higher. Some curriculum changes were also made to the Higher grade at this time. The introduction of the reformed examinations system was criticised in the press and by the government after a <a href="/wiki/SQA_examinations_controversy" title="SQA examinations controversy">series of administrative and computer errors</a> led to several thousand incorrect Higher and Intermediate certificates being sent out. The crisis took several months to resolve, and several management figures, including the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Executive">Chief Executive</a> Ron Tuck, resigned or were fired.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification_types">Qualification types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualification types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>SQA has a statutory responsibility to provide public examinations for Scottish state schools, though these are also used more widely. It has a statutory responsibility to <i>accredit</i> vocational qualifications (that is formally scrutinise them and confirm that they conform to agreed UK criteria). None of its qualifications, still less its vocational qualifications, is protected by statute, but the Authority has a largely dominant position within all sectors of qualifications within Scotland. SQA awards are also exported to a number of countries including <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and former Soviet republics and other countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> SQA also provides the licensing certification for many merchant navies throughout the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Qualifications">National Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A National Qualification (NQ) can take the form of Standard Grades or National Courses.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/65/SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png/220px-SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png" width="220" height="323" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/65/SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png/330px-SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/65/SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png/440px-SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png 2x" data-file-width="1002" data-file-height="1471" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SQA_EXAM_LAYOUT.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A specimen front-cover of an old General Level Standard Grade Paper</div></div></div>\n<p><b><a href="/wiki/Standard_Grade" title="Standard Grade">Standard Grades</a></b> have been in existence before the Higher Still reforms. There are three Standard Grade Levels: Foundation, General and Credit. They are normally set at age 14-15 (sometimes at age 16 if birthday is before May), usually when attending <a href="/wiki/High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="High School">High School</a>.\n</p><p><b>National Courses</b> were introduced with the "Higher Still" reforms. There are seven National Courses: National 1, National 2, National 3, National 4, National 5, Higher, Advanced Higher. National Courses can be taken in a wide range of subjects, from the purely academic, such as <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a> - to the purely vocational, such as <a href="/wiki/Accounting" title="Accounting">Accounting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mental_Health" class="mw-redirect" title="Mental Health">Mental Health Care</a>. They combine three National Units, each lasting 40 hours with a Course Assessment, normally taken at the end of a one-year Course in the early summer.\n</p><p>In addition to traditional National Qualification Courses, a new suite of "pre-vocational" courses entitled "Skills for Work" was rolled out.  Primarily available at Intermediate 1 and Intermediate 2 levels, these prevocational courses, aim to give students an awareness of the workplace environment, the skills required for entry to an industry as well as generic employment skills. Each of these Courses is awarded on the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Qualifications_Certificate" title="Scottish Qualifications Certificate">Scottish Qualifications Certificate</a>.\n</p><p>Many faculties that provide <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Qualification_Authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Qualification Authority">SQA</a> National Courses also provide preliminary examinations, or <a href="/wiki/Prelim_exams" class="mw-redirect" title="Prelim exams">prelim exams</a>. These usually take place in January or February.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reorganization_of_National_Qualifications">Reorganization of National Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reorganization of National Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>According to SQA, all National Qualifications will be redesigned as follows in the future:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Previous qualification\n</th>\n<th>New qualification\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Access 1</td>\n<td>National 1\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Access 2</td>\n<td>National 2\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Standard Grade (Foundation level)<br />Access 3</td>\n<td>National 3\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Standard Grade (General level)<br />Intermediate 1</td>\n<td>National 4\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Standard Grade (Credit level)<br />Intermediate 2</td>\n<td>National 5\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td>Higher</td>\n<td>Higher\n</td></tr>\n<tr>\n<td></td>\n<td>Advanced Higher\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>According to the SQA, the new qualifications have "more focus on skills development compared to the existing qualifications [...] There is also a greater emphasis on <a href="/wiki/Deeper_Learning" class="mw-redirect" title="Deeper Learning">\'deeper learning\'</a> by helping learners to think for themselves; to apply and interpret the knowledge and understanding they have developed and to demonstrate the skills they have learned."<sup id="cite_ref-nationalFAQ_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalFAQ-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>National 1-4 will be assessed internally by the student\'s own institution, subject to regulation by the SQA. National 5 courses and above will also have a number of internal assessments, followed by an externally marked exam.<sup id="cite_ref-nationalFAQ_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nationalFAQ-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Assessment_Bank">National Assessment Bank</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: National Assessment Bank">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Since the introduction of Highers, Intermediate 1 and Intermediate 2 courses, the SQA have also introduced a <a href="/wiki/National_Assessment_Bank" title="National Assessment Bank">National Assessment Bank</a> of short examinations for each subject. These are more commonly known as NABs or unit assessments, and contain questions from specific academic units at a basic level. Each candidate must pass a certain number of NABs (usually three) before they can be presented for the final examination, although resits can occur with other NAB materials.\n</p><p>The National Assessment Bank materials are available for download as Word or PDF files on a secure SQA website. The only person who can enter the site is the SQA coordinator of each institution. \nFor national 4 and 5 courses NABs are usually taken 3 times throughout the session when units have been completed and appear on the candidates certificate as a unit pass even if the candidate does not pass the whole course e.g. "No award in national 5 english but passed a unit internally."\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_of_results">Release of results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release of results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Students for <i>National Qualifications</i> receive their results on, generally, the first Tuesday in August. Students signed up for the authority\'s <i>MySQA</i> system are able to check their record online throughout the year and get their exam results by text and/or email on the day that the results are issued. Those signed up to the service received their results one day earlier than the official postal results. This also occurred in subsequent years, however only due to a mistake by the company holding the results. In another incident, the SQA itself sent the results out on the right day, but <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gordon_University" title="Robert Gordon University">Robert Gordon University</a>\'s business school leaked whether students had been let in,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and a technical error with <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_University" class="mw-redirect" title="St Andrew&#39;s University">St Andrew\'s University</a>\'s website also leaked the results of applicants.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Due to the high volume of exam marking with Scottish and British examination boards in general where it is thought that exam marking inaccuracies may occur, there are each year a vast number of students requesting for an exam "re-mark". After the SQA introduced fees (which were exorbitant in many students\' and parents\' opinion), requests for exam remarking plummeted 55,000 students - a decline of 77%.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In order to fight (supposed) grade inflation within the Scottish education sector; SQA has introduced academically and cognitively more rigorous examination standards and stricter marking; this has resulted in strong criticism towards the SQA exam board and in decreases of exam pass rates of up to 15%.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_units_and_programmes">National units and programmes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: National units and programmes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There are the many qualifications often imperfectly referred to as  <b>vocational</b>, though these are frequently stepping-stones for students at Scottish Colleges of Further Education to pursue one- or two- year programmes tailored from a wide-ranging catalogue of <b>National Units</b>. The actual programme may be very rigidly prescribed by employers or be entirely freely chosen by the student to meet particular needs. The prescribed programmes may be recognised by a <b>National Certificate</b> or a <b>Scottish Progression Award</b>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SVQs_and_modern_apprenticeships">SVQs and modern apprenticeships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: SVQs and modern apprenticeships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><b><a href="/wiki/Scottish_Vocational_Qualification" title="Scottish Vocational Qualification">Scottish Vocational Qualifications</a></b> (SVQ) are an award for <a href="/wiki/Vocational_education" title="Vocational education">vocational education</a> and training awarded by the SQA or other approved awarding bodies in conjunction with industry bodies.  Scottish National Qualifications and Scottish Progression Awards are often important in a Modern Apprenticeship scheme along with SVQs. SVQs are developed by <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> <a href="/wiki/Employer" class="mw-redirect" title="Employer">employers</a> in tandem with <a href="/wiki/National_Vocational_Qualification" title="National Vocational Qualification">National Vocational Qualifications</a> (NVQ) for <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>.\n</p><p>SVQ are assessed in the workplace (or closely regulated training workshops) by employers, training providers or colleges approved and monitored by the SQA (or other awarding bodies) accredited by its independent <i>Accreditation Unit</i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Higher_National_Certificates_and_Diplomas">Higher National Certificates and Diplomas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Higher National Certificates and Diplomas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Qualifications aimed at students in their first two years of <a href="/wiki/Higher_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Higher Education">Higher Education</a> include <b>HNCs</b> (<a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Certificate" title="Higher National Certificate">Higher National Certificates</a>)- taken as a one-year full-time course or as a two-year part-time course&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 and <b>HNDs</b> (<a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Diploma" title="Higher National Diploma">Higher National Diplomas</a>). These qualifications are extremely popular in colleges, workplaces and community education centres in Scotland, the rest of the UK and throughout the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Students_with_disabilities">Students with disabilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Students with disabilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>These include specific qualifications for those with severe to moderate difficulties (<b>Access</b>), the right to aid in completing assessments (for example, a scribe) and the right to challenge any unfair or artificial barrier in the rules for any qualification.\nMost notably the case of Louis Webster, a young man with severe learning disabilities who had to have 3 scribes and 4 different computers to do his maths exam. Sadly this story was not all good as he managed to only score 6% in the exam. However the dedication of the SQA staff was simply remarkable and the support from Louis\xe2\x80\x99 friends and family is an inspiration to all.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_as_a_second_language">English as a second language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: English as a second language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There is a suite of National Units addressing the needs of economic migrants, asylum seekers and (the biggest group) those seeking to master English before returning to their own countries.  It has also developed qualifications for those seeking to teach English to refugees.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>SQA is one of the four partner national organisations involved in the <a href="/wiki/Curriculum_for_Excellence" title="Curriculum for Excellence">Curriculum for Excellence</a>. It works with partners on all strands of the development. Its principal role is to contribute to work on qualifications and assessment. SQA\xe2\x80\x99s role in Curriculum for Excellence is to design and develop the new qualifications and assessment.\n</p><p>SQA has joined with Universities Scotland, <a href="/wiki/Quality_Assurance_Agency_for_Higher_Education" title="Quality Assurance Agency for Higher Education">QAA Scotland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> to create the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Credit_and_Qualifications_Framework" title="Scottish Credit and Qualifications Framework">Scottish Credit and Qualifications Framework</a> or <a href="/wiki/SCQF" class="mw-redirect" title="SCQF">SCQF</a>. Every Scottish qualification&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 from the Access level for those with learning difficulties to a Doctorate and including vocational as well as ESOL and BSL qualifications&#160;\xe2\x80\x94 is allocated a level and credit value within this framework, which all partners have agreed to recognise.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_Higher_Maths_exam">2015 Higher Maths exam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2015 Higher Maths exam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Higher Maths exam sat by students in May 2015 was said to be far too difficult.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This evoked heated debates among students, teachers and educationalists; the corresponding grade boundaries for the respective exam were thus adjusted accordingly, with a pass mark as low as 34%.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The SQA later admitted that one of their Higher Maths exam papers had been unusually hard and unfit for purpose.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_exams">2016 exams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2016 exams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Several exams that were set from the SQA in 2016 were criticised by pupils, parents, teachers and MSPs.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="National_5_Maths_exam">National 5 Maths exam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: National 5 Maths exam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The National 5 Maths exam, sat on 12 May 2016, in particular Paper 1 (non-calculator), was also criticised by students after being considered much more difficult than previous years. A petition was created by students which was to be sent to the SQA demanding to know why the exam was exceedingly difficult, and it gained over 25,000 signatures.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="National_5_Computing_Science_exam">National 5 Computing Science exam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: National 5 Computing Science exam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Scottish Green MSP, <a href="/wiki/Ross_Greer_(politician)" title="Ross Greer (politician)">Ross Greer</a> criticised the SQA for mistakes in the National 5 Computing Science exam and called for an investigation into the exam, he defended teachers and students who thought that this was the worst exam ever set by the SQA.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The SQA later admitted that the exam had mistakes.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scottish_Qualifications_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.sqa.org.uk/sqa/45348.2872.html?lang=G">SQA -
