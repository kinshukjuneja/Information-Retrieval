Waterfowl Habitat</b> is an international <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaty</a> for the conservation and sustainable use of <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ramsar_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ramsar-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is also known as the <b>Convention on Wetlands</b>. It is named after the city of <a href="/wiki/Ramsar,_Mazandaran" title="Ramsar, Mazandaran">Ramsar</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, where the Convention was signed in 1971.\n</p><p>Every three years, representatives of the Contracting Parties meet as the Conference of the Contracting Parties (COP), the policy-making organ of the Convention which adopts decisions (Resolutions and Recommendations) to administer the work of the Convention and improve the way in which the Parties are able to implement its objectives. The most recent COP12 was held in <a href="/wiki/Punta_del_Este" title="Punta del Este">Punta del Este</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, in 2015. COP13 will take place in <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, in October 2018. \n</p>\n<div information on each Ramsar Site.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_cooperation">International cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ramsar_transboundary_Site_Wadden_Sea.jpg" href="/wiki/List_of_parties_to_the_Ramsar_Convention" title="List of parties to the Ramsar Convention">List of parties to the Ramsar Convention</a></div>\n<p>As of 2016 there are 18 Transboundary Ramsar Sites, and 15 Ramsar Regional Initiatives covering regions of the Mediterranean, Asia, Africa and South America.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_organization_partners">International organization partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramsar_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International organization partners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Ramsar Convention works closely with six other organisations known as International Organization Partners (IOPs). These are:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">Birdlife International</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> (IUCN)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/International_Water_Management_Institute" title="International Water Management Institute">International Water Management Institute</a> (IWMI)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Wetlands_International" title="Wetlands International">Wetlands International</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF International</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Wildfowl_%26_Wetlands_Trust" title="Wildfowl &amp; Wetlands Trust">Wildfowl &amp; Wetlands Trust</a> (WWT)</li></ul>\n<p>These organizations support the work of the Convention by providing expert technical advice, helping implement field studies, and providing financial support. The IOPs also participate regularly as observers in all meetings of the Conference of the Parties and as full members of the Scientific and Technical Review Panel.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_partners">Other partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramsar_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other partners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Convention collaborates with a network of partners:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Biodiversity-related conventions including the <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_Biological_Diversity" title="Convention on Biological Diversity">Convention on Biological Diversity</a> (CBD), the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_to_Combat_Desertification" title="United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification">Convention to Combat Desertification</a> (UNCCD), <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_the_Conservation_of_Migratory_Species_of_Wild_Animals" title="Convention on the Conservation of Migratory Species of Wild Animals">Convention on Migratory Species (CMS)</a>, the <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Convention" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Convention">World Heritage Convention</a> (WHC), and the <a href="/wiki/CITES" title="CITES">Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species</a> (CITES);</li>\n<li>Project funding bodies including global environmental funds, multilateral development banks and bilateral donors;</li>\n<li>UN agencies such as <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Programme" title="United Nations Environment Programme">UNEP</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Programme" title="United Nations Development Programme">UNDP</a>, <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> and the UN Economic Commission for Europe, and specific programmes such as UNESCO\xe2\x80\x99s Man and the Biosphere (MAB) programme;</li>\n<li>Non-governmental organizations including the <a href="/wiki/The_Nature_Conservancy" title="The Nature Conservancy">Nature Conservancy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conservation_International" title="Conservation International">Conservation International</a>, the Society of Wetland Scientists, the International Association for Impact Assessment, and many others;</li>\n<li>Since 1998 the Convention has also benefited from a strong partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Danone" title="Danone">Danone Group</a> including the <a href="/wiki/Evian" class="mw-redirect" title="Evian">Evian</a> brand, and since 2007 from the Biosphere Connections partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Star_Alliance" title="Star Alliance">Star Alliance</a> airline network.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bodies_established_by_the_Convention">Bodies established by the Convention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramsar_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bodies established by the Convention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dt>Conference of the Parties (COP)</dt></dl>\n<p>Conference of the Parties (COP) is the Convention\'s governing body consisting of all governments that have ratified the treaty. This ultimate authority reviews progress under the Convention, identifies new priorities, and sets work plans for members. The COP can also make amendments to the Convention, create expert advisory bodies, review progress reports by member nations, and collaborate with other international organizations and agreements.\n</p>\n<dl><dt>The Standing Committee</dt></dl>\n<p>The Standing Committee is the intersessional executive body which represents the COP between its triennial meetings, within the framework of the decisions made by the COP. The Contracting Parties that are members of the Standing Committee are elected by each meeting of the COP to serve for the three years until the next one.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ramsar_Convention.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ramsar_Convention.jpg/220px-Ramsar_Convention.jpg" width="220" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ramsar_Convention.jpg/330px-Ramsar_Convention.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ramsar_Convention.jpg/440px-Ramsar_Convention.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3793" data-file-height="2697" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ramsar_Convention.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Ramsar Secretariat offices in Gland, Switzerland</div></div></div>\n<p><b>The Scientific and Technical Review Panel (STRP)</b>\n</p><p>The Scientific and Technical Review Panel provides scientific and technical guidance to the Conference of the Parties, the Standing Committee, and the Ramsar Secretariat.\n</p>\n<dl><dt>The Secretariat</dt></dl>\n<p>The Secretariat carries out the day-to-day coordination of the Convention\xe2\x80\x99s activities. It is based at the headquarters of the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) in <a href="/wiki/Gland,_Switzerland" title="Gland, Switzerland">Gland, Switzerland</a>.\n</p><p>The implementation of the Ramsar Convention is a continuing partnership between the Contracting Parties, the Standing Committee, and the Convention Secretariat, with the advice of the subsidiary expert body, the Scientific and Technical Review Panel (STRP), and the support of the International Organization Partners (IOPs).\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Martha_Rojas_Urrego" title="Martha Rojas Urrego">Martha Rojas Urrego</a> is the sixth Secretary General of the Ramsar Convention on Wetlands.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wetlands_Day">World Wetlands Day</span><span
