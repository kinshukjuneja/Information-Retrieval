class="mw-headline" id="Kansai_Farm_League">Kansai Farm League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_League_(Japanese_baseball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kansai Farm League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The league was created in 1952 as the <b>Kansai Farm League</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-JBcom_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JBcom-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and was initially completely separate from the workings of the Nippon Professional Baseball. It featured the minor league teams of the six professional teams that had their homefields in the western region of Japan, as well one independent team not affiliated with an NPB franchise.\n</p><p>The initial complement of teams:<sup id="cite_ref-JBcom_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JBcom-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Hankyu_Braves" class="mw-redirect" title="Hankyu Braves">Hankyu Braves</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Osaka_Tigers" class="mw-redirect" title="Osaka Tigers">Hanshin Jaguars</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Dragons" class="mw-redirect" title="Nagoya Dragons">Nagoya Dragons</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nankai_Hawks" class="mw-redirect" title="Nankai Hawks">Nankai Hawks</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nishitetsu_Lions" class="mw-redirect" title="Nishitetsu Lions">Nishitetsu Lions</a></li>\n<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanyo_Crowns&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sanyo Crowns (page does not exist)">Sanyo Crowns</a> \xe2\x80\x94 independent</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Shochiku_Robins" title="Shochiku Robins">Shochiku Robins</a></li></ul>\n<p>The Sanyo Crowns were dissolved after the 1952 season. The <a href="/wiki/Shochiku_Robins" title="Shochiku Robins">Shochiku Robins</a> merged with the <a href="/wiki/Taiyo_Whales" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiyo Whales">Taiyo Whales</a> in 1953, but the <a href="/wiki/Kintetsu_Pearls" class="mw-redirect" title="Kintetsu Pearls">Kintetsu Pearls</a>\' <a href="/wiki/Farm_team" title="Farm team">farm team</a> joined the league that year, keeping the number of teams at six.<sup id="cite_ref-JBcom_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JBcom-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1954 the six teams of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central League</a> decided to form their own minor league, called the <b>New Japan League</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-JBcom_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JBcom-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> With the <a href="/wiki/Chunichi_Dragons" title="Chunichi Dragons">Chunichi Dragons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hanshin_Tigers" title="Hanshin Tigers">Hanshin Tigers</a> affiliates dropping out to join the new minor league, only four teams remained in the Kansai Farm League.<sup id="cite_ref-JBcom_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JBcom-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_of_the_Western_League">Formation of the Western <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_League" title="Pacific League">Pacific League</a> were split up to create the <b>Western League</b> and <b><a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_(Japanese_baseball)" title="Eastern League (Japanese baseball)">Eastern League</a></b>, each with seven teams.<sup id="cite_ref-JBcom_1-7" class="reference"><a Braves">Hankyu Braves</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kobe,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" class="mw-redirect" title="Kobe, Hy\xc5\x8dgo">Kobe, Hy\xc5\x8dgo</a>)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hanshin_Tigers" title="Hanshin Tigers">Hanshin Jaguars</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nishinomiya,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" class="mw-redirect" title="Nishinomiya, Hy\xc5\x8dgo">Nishinomiya, Hy\xc5\x8dgo</a>)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hiroshima_Carp" href="/wiki/Fukuoka,_Fukuoka" class="mw-redirect" title="Fukuoka, Fukuoka">Fukuoka, Fukuoka</a>)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nishitetsu_Lions" class="mw-redirect" title="Nishitetsu Lions">Nishitetsu Lions moved their franchise to <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama</a> in 1979 to join the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_(Japanese_baseball)" title="Eastern League
