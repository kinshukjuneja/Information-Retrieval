id="cite_ref-dailymail1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <span photographer. <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Taylor-Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>She was born Samantha Taylor-Wood in <a href="/wiki/Croydon" title="Croydon">Croydon</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her father, David, left the family when she was nine.<sup id="cite_ref-theaustralian1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theaustralian1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Her mother, Geraldine, is a <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">yoga</a> teacher and <a href="/wiki/Astrologer" class="mw-redirect" title="Astrologer">astrologer</a>. She has one younger sister, Ashley, and a maternal half-brother, Kristian.\n</p><p>Taylor-Johnson grew up near <a href="/wiki/Streatham" title="Streatham">Streatham</a> Common in south London until her parents\' divorce.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian2001_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian2001-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The family then moved and lived in an old school house in <a href="/wiki/Jarvis_Brook" title="Jarvis Brook">Jarvis Brook</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_Sussex" title="East Sussex">East Sussex</a>, and Samantha went to <a href="/wiki/Beacon_Community_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Beacon Community College">Beacon Community College</a>. She later attended <a href="/wiki/Goldsmiths,_University_of_London" title="Goldsmiths, University of London">Goldsmiths, University of London</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Taylor-Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fine_art">Fine art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Taylor-Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fine art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Taylor-Johnson began exhibiting fine art photography in the early 1990s. One collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Henry_Bond" title="Henry Bond">Henry Bond</a>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/26_October_1993" title="26 October 1993">26 October 1993</a></i>, featured Bond and Taylor-Wood reprising the roles of <a href="/wiki/Yoko_Ono" title="Yoko Ono">Yoko Ono</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Pastiche" title="Pastiche">pastiche</a> of the photo-portrait made\xe2\x80\x94by photographer <a href="/wiki/Annie_Leibovitz" title="Annie Leibovitz">Annie Leibovitz</a>\xe2\x80\x94a few hours before <a href="/wiki/Death_of_John_Lennon" class="mw-redirect" title="Death of John Lennon">Lennon was assassinated</a>, in 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1994, she exhibited a multi-screen video work titled <i>Killing Time</i>, in which four people mimed to an opera score. From that point multi-screen video works became the main focus of Taylor-Johnson\'s work. Beginning with the video works <i>Travesty of a Mockery</i> and <i>Pent-Up</i> in 1996. One of Taylor-Johnson\'s first United Kingdom solo shows was held at the <a href="/wiki/Chisenhale_Gallery" title="Chisenhale Gallery">Chisenhale Gallery</a>, east London, in September\xe2\x80\x93October 1996. Taylor-Johnson was nominated for the annual <a href="/wiki/Turner_Prize" title="Turner Prize">Turner Prize</a> in 1998, but lost out to the painter <a href="/wiki/Chris_Ofili" title="Chris Ofili">Chris Ofili</a>. She won the Illy Caf\xc3\xa9 Prize for Most Promising Young Artist at the 1997 <a href="/wiki/Venice_Biennale" title="Venice Biennale">Venice Biennale</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2000, Taylor-Johnson created a wraparound, photomural around scaffolding of the London department store <a href="/wiki/Selfridges" title="Selfridges">Selfridges</a> while it was being restored; the mural featured 21 cultural icons including <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, musician <a href="/wiki/Alex_James_(musician)" title="Alex James (musician)">Alex James</a> and actors <a href="/wiki/Richard_E._Grant" title="Richard E. Grant">Richard E. Grant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Winstone" title="Ray Winstone">Ray Winstone</a>. The poses of the figures referenced famous works of art from the past and recent movies.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2002, Taylor-Johnson was commissioned by the <a href="/wiki/National_Portrait_Gallery_(London)" class="mw-redirect" title="National Portrait Gallery (London)">National Portrait Gallery</a> to make a video portrait of <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David Beckham</a>\xe2\x80\x94whom she depicted sleeping. She is perhaps best known for her work entitled \'Crying Men\' which features many of Hollywood\'s <i>glitterati</i> crying, including <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Penn" title="Sean Penn">Sean Penn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Fishburne" title="Laurence Fishburne">Laurence Fishburne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, Taylor-Johnson had a survey exhibition at the BALTIC Centre for Contemporary Art, Gateshead, United Kingdom.\n</p><p>2014 saw a new photographic exhibition by Taylor-Johnson, of the private apartment of Mademoiselle Chanel at <a href="/wiki/Saatchi_Gallery" title="Saatchi Gallery">The Saatchi Gallery</a>. Entitled \xe2\x80\x98Second Floor\xe2\x80\x99, the series of 34 photographs captured the private rooms of <a href="/wiki/Coco_Chanel" title="Coco Chanel">Coco Chanel</a> at 31 Rue Cambon in Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nowhere_Boy"><i>Nowhere Boy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Taylor-Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Nowhere Boy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In August 2008, Taylor-Johnson was chosen to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Nowhere_Boy" title="Nowhere Boy">Nowhere Boy</a></i>, a biopic about the childhood of <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>. Speaking about her experience directing the film, in September 2010, Taylor-Johnson said,\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>I thought, I\'m in too deep and if I mess this up I\'m just never gonna make a film again, and I went into a panic. I got into the car and said, I just have to call these producers and pull out. I got into the car and I put the key into the ignition and Lennon\'s voice came straight out of the radio and it was <a href="/wiki/(Just_Like)_Starting_Over" title="(Just Like) Starting Over">Starting Over</a>. It was one of those moments where I thought it was a sign: OK I\'m gonna do it.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<p>The 53rd annual <a href="/wiki/London_Film_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="London Film Festival">London Film Festival</a> screened the film as its closing presentation on 29 October 2009. The film was released in the UK on <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a>, 2009. to positive reviews. Taylor-Johnson was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> award on 21 January 2010, but lost out to <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Jones" title="Duncan Jones">Duncan Jones</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span id="Other_music.2C_film_and_television_work"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_music,_film_and_television_work">Other music, film and television work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Taylor-Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other music, film and television work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In her 2004 film installation "Strings" at White Cube, ballet dancer <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Putrov" title="Ivan Putrov">Ivan Putrov</a> was suspended by a harness above four musicians playing the slow movement from <a href="/wiki/String_Quartet_No._2_(Tchaikovsky)" title="String Quartet No. 2 (Tchaikovsky)">Tchaikovsky\'s Second String Quartet</a>, filmed in the Crush Bar of the Royal Opera House.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, Taylor-Johnson contributed the short film <i>Death Valley</i> to the British version of <i><a href="/wiki/Destricted" title="Destricted">Destricted</a></i>. In 2008, Taylor-Johnson directed a short film <i><a href="/wiki/Love_You_More_(film)" title="Love You More (film)">Love You More</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Marber" title="Patrick Marber">Patrick Marber</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Minghella" title="Anthony Minghella">Anthony Minghella</a>. The film includes two songs by the <a href="/wiki/Buzzcocks" title="Buzzcocks">Buzzcocks</a> and features a cameo appearance by the band\'s lead singer <a href="/wiki/Pete_Shelley" title="Pete Shelley">Pete Shelley</a>. In February 2009, Taylor-Johnson, collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Sky_Arts" title="Sky Arts">Sky Arts</a> chose to interpret "<a href="/wiki/Vesti_la_giubba" title="Vesti la giubba">Vesti la giubba</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/Pagliacci" title="Pagliacci">Pagliacci</a></i>. She commented: "I\xe2\x80\x99m really happy to be involved in such a great project. I think by capturing one of opera\'s most moving moments in a film short, we have put a modern spin on the aria."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2011, she directed the <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a> music video "<a href="/wiki/%C3%9Cberlin" title="\xc3\x9cberlin">\xc3\x9cberlin</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The clip starred her then-fianc\xc3\xa9 Aaron Johnson, who "throws some kung-fu kicks, attempts some pirouettes, prances, punches the air, chicken walks, tries out some bunny impressions, and, at one point, fondles his bottom."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In September 2011, she collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Solange_Azagury-Partridge" title="Solange Azagury-Partridge">Solange Azagury-Partridge</a> on the short film <i>Daydream</i>. This was aired to support the launch of Azagury-Partridge\'s new jewellery collection, 24:7. Under the direction of Taylor-Johnson, <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Ross" title="Liberty Ross">Liberty Ross</a> plays a beautiful woman in her bedroom, bejewelled by her lover, played by <a href="/wiki/JJ_Feild" title="JJ Feild">JJ Feild</a>. The original music was composed by Oscar winner <a href="/wiki/Atticus_Ross" title="Atticus Ross">Atticus Ross</a>, and the director of photography was BAFTA winner <a href="/wiki/John_Mathieson_(cinematographer)" title="John Mathieson (cinematographer)">John Mathieson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Taylor-Johnson directed the <a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_of_Grey_(film)" title="Fifty Shades of Grey (film)">film adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/E._L._James" title="E. L. James">E. L. James</a>\' best-selling <a href="/wiki/Erotic_literature" title="Erotic literature">erotic novel</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_of_Grey" title="Fifty Shades of Grey">Fifty Shades of Grey</a></i>, made by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" class="mw-redirect" title="Universal Studios">Universal Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Focus_Features" title="Focus Features">Focus Features</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> She was chosen from a list that included <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Angelina Jolie</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Steven_Soderbergh" title="Steven Soderbergh">Steven Soderbergh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Murphy_(writer)" title="Ryan Murphy (writer)">Ryan Murphy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Wright" title="Joe Wright">Joe Wright</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Gus_Van_Sant" title="Gus Van Sant">Gus Van Sant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Taylor-Johnson was in pole position to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_Darker_(film)" title="Fifty Shades Darker (film)">Fifty Shades Darker</a></i> (the sequel to <i>Fifty Shades of Grey</i>), but decided to walk away from the much-discussed franchise after she was involved in a number of disagreements with author E.L. James.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In June 2017, Taylor-Johnson said that she regretted directing the first film.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discography">Discography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sam_Taylor-Johnson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Discography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li>2003: Kiki Kokova \xe2\x80\x93 "<a href="/wiki/Love_To_Love_You_Baby_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Love To Love You Baby (song)">Love To Love You Baby</a>" (Lucky Kunst)</li>\n<li>2008: Sam Taylor-Wood &amp;
