title="Portloe">Portloe</a></div></div></div>\n<p><b>Saint Piran\'s Flag</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Baner Peran</i>) is the flag of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, United Kingdom. The earliest known description of the flag as the <a href="/wiki/Heraldic_standard" class="mw-redirect" title="Heraldic standard">Standard</a> of Cornwall was written in 1838.<sup id="cite_ref-gilbert_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gilbert-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is used by some <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish people</a> as a symbol of identity.<sup id="cite_ref-rendle_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rendle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is a white cross on a black background.\n</p><p>The flag is attributed to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Piran" title="Saint Piran">Saint Piran</a>, a 6th-century Cornish abbot. One early use of a white cross and black background design is the 15th-century coat of arms of the Saint-Peran family.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/Thames_Diamond_Jubilee_Pageant" title="Thames Diamond Jubilee Pageant">Thames Diamond Jubilee Pageant</a> in June 2012, the flag was among those flown on <a href="/wiki/Gloriana_(barge)" title="Gloriana (barge)">the Royal Barge, <i>Gloriana</i></a>, though this was explained as "innocent" and "nothing constitutional".<sup id="cite_ref-Western_Morning_News_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Western_Morning_News-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Piran%27s_Flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are claims that the design dates from prior to 1188 when the flag was used in the <a href="/wiki/Crusades" title="Crusades">Crusades</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-celticleague_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-celticleague-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and an article in the <i><a href="/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A6dia_Britannica" title="Encyclop\xc3\xa6dia Britannica">Encyclop\xc3\xa6dia Britannica</a></i> tells that the flag was carried by the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish</a> contingent at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Agincourt" title="Battle of Agincourt">Battle of Agincourt</a> (1415).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (June 2010)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> However, the reference given by the <i>Encyclop\xc3\xa6dia Britannica</i> seems to have been confused with one that comes from a 1590 poem entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Poly-Olbion" title="Poly-Olbion">Poly-Olbion</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Drayton" title="Michael Drayton">Michael Drayton</a>. It states that the banner carried by the Cornish men at Agincourt depicted two <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Wrestling" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornish Wrestling">Cornish wrestlers</a> in a hitch.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The earliest known evidence of this flag was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Davies_Gilbert" title="Davies Gilbert">Davies Gilbert</a> in his 1838 work: <i>The <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/parochial" class="extiw" title="wikt:parochial">Parochial</a> History of Cornwall</i>, in which he gives reference to\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>a white cross on a black ground [that] was formerly the banner of St Perran and the Standard of Cornwall; probably with some allusion to the black ore and the white metal of tin<sup id="cite_ref-gilbert_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gilbert-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rendle_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rendle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>\n<p>The fact that Gilbert identifies it as being "formerly" a standard of Cornwall implies that he believed it to have been used before 1838.<sup id="cite_ref-gilbert_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gilbert-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, Gilbert did not leave a record of his background research, and referred only to his "recollection".<sup id="cite_ref-rendle_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rendle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>One of the oldest depictions of the flag can be seen in a stained glass window at <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a>. It was unveiled in 1888, in memory of the famous Cornish inventor and engineer <a href="/wiki/Richard_Trevithick" title="Richard Trevithick">Richard Trevithick</a>. The window depicts <a href="/wiki/Michael_(archangel)" title="Michael (archangel)">St Michael</a> at the top and nine Cornish saints, Piran, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petroc" title="Saint Petroc">Petroc</a>, Pinnock,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Germanus, Julian, Cyriacus, <a href="/wiki/Constantine_(British_saint)" title="Constantine (British saint)">Constantine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Non" title="Saint Non">Nonna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geraint_of_Dumnonia" title="Geraint of Dumnonia">Geraint</a> in tiers below. The head of St Piran appears to be a portrait of Trevithick himself, and the figure carries the banner of Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clues_to_its_origin">Clues to its origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Piran%27s_Flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Clues to its origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Saint_Piran%27s_Flag&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span>  <small><i>(June 2010)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>Saint Piran\'s Flag has similarities to the old <a href="/wiki/The_Kroaz_Du_(Black_Cross)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Kroaz Du (Black Cross)">Breton flag</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Saint_David" title="Flag of Saint David">flag of Saint David</a>. The cultural links between <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and Cornwall are well recorded. Saint Piran\'s Flag is the negative image of the old Breton flag, a black cross on a white field. class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Piran%27s_Flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The flag is often displayed on bumper stickers, and flying from buildings, including those of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-suzylou_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-suzylou-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It is flown at most Cornish gatherings, such as the <a href="/wiki/Gorseth_Kernow" class="mw-redirect" title="Gorseth Kernow">Gorseth Kernow</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Piran%27s_Day" title="St Piran&#39;s Day">St Piran\'s Day</a> (5 March), <a href="/wiki/Camborne" title="Camborne">Camborne</a>\'s Trevithick Day (April), <a href="/wiki/Padstow" title="Padstow">Padstow</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/%27Obby_%27Oss_festival" title="&#39;Obby &#39;Oss festival">\'Obby \'Oss festival</a> (May), <a href="/wiki/Helston" title="Helston">Helston</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Furry_Dance" title="Furry Dance">Flora Day</a> (May), and at <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_in_Cornwall" title="Rugby union in Cornwall">Cornish rugby</a> matches. It is regularly seen around Cornwall on car stickers with the word <i><a href="/wiki/Kernow" class="mw-redirect" title="Kernow">Kernow</a></i> (Cornish for Cornwall), and is used around the world as a symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_diaspora" title="Cornish diaspora">Cornish diaspora</a> or overseas Cornish associations. It has been adapted for use in the logos of a number of organisations, such as the Cornwall district of the <a href="/wiki/Methodist_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist Church">Methodist Church</a>, is used by a variety of Cornish businesses such as <a href="/wiki/Ginsters" title="Ginsters">Ginsters</a>, and is seen on the design of the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_All_Blacks" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornish All Blacks">Cornish All Blacks</a> rugby shirts as well as the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Pirates" title="Cornish Pirates">Cornish Pirates</a> rugby logo.<sup id="cite_ref-suzylou_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-suzylou-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Link_rot" title="Wikipedia:Link rot"><span title="&#160;Dead link since June 2015">dead link</span></a></i>&#93;</span></sup>\n</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/Thames_Diamond_Jubilee_Pageant" title="Thames Diamond Jubilee Pageant">Thames Diamond Jubilee Pageant</a> in June 2012, the flag was flown on <a href="/wiki/Gloriana_(barge)" title="Gloriana (barge)">the Royal Rowing Barge</a> alongside the flags representing <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_England" title="Flag of England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Scotland" title="Flag of Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Patrick%27s_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="St Patrick&#39;s Cross">Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Wales" title="Flag of Wales">Wales</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Corporation_of_the_City_of_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Flag of the Corporation of the City of London">City of London</a>. One of the largest flags in the pageant was also St Piran\'s Flag, flown by the St Ives mackerel lugger <i>Barnabas</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The flags of <a href="/wiki/Smith_Island,_Maryland" title="Smith Island, Maryland">Smith Island, Maryland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tangier,_Virginia" title="Tangier, Virginia">Tangier, Virginia</a> incorporate St. Piran\'s cross in the upper-left <a href="/wiki/Canton_(flag)" title="Canton (flag)">canton</a> in recognition of the early settlers who came to the islands from Cornwall and Devon.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gallery">Gallery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
