In <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, local councils do not carry out the same range of functions as those in the rest of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>; for example they have no responsibility for education, road-building or housing (although they do nominate members to the advisory <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Housing_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Ireland Housing Council">Northern Ireland Housing Council</a>). Their functions include <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">planning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Recycling_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Recycling in Northern Ireland">waste and recycling services</a>, leisure and community services, building control and local economic and cultural development.  The collection of <a href="/wiki/Rates_(tax)" title="Rates (tax)">rates</a> is handled centrally by the <a href="/wiki/Land_and_Property_Services" title="Land and Property Services">Land and Property Services</a> agency of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" href="/w/index.php?title=Local_government_in_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The current pattern of 11 local government districts was established on 1 April 2015, as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Reform_of_local_government_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Reform of local government in Northern Ireland">reform process</a> that started in 2005.\n</p><p>The previous pattern of local government in Northern Ireland, with 26 councils, was established in 1973 by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Boundaries)_Act_(Northern_Ireland)_1971" title="Local Government (Boundaries) Act (Northern Ireland) 1971">Local Government (Boundaries) Act (Northern Ireland) 1971</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_(Northern_Ireland)_1972" title="Local Government Act (Northern Ireland) 1972">Local Government Act (Northern Ireland) 1972</a> to replace the previous system established by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Ireland)_Act_1898" title="Local Government (Ireland) Act 1898">Local Government (Ireland) Act 1898</a>.  The system was based on the recommendations of the Macrory Report, of June 1970, which presupposed the continued existence of the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Committee_of_the_Privy_Council_of_Northern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive Committee of the Privy Council of Northern Ireland">Government of Northern Ireland</a> to act as a regional-level authority.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1921 to 1973, Northern Ireland was divided into six <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Counties of Northern Ireland">administrative counties</a> (subdivided into <a href="/wiki/List_of_rural_and_urban_districts_in_Northern_Ireland" title="List of rural and urban districts in Northern Ireland">urban and rural districts</a>) and two <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county boroughs</a>. The counties and county boroughs continue to exist for the purposes of <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Lieutenant">lieutenancy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shrievalty#Northern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Shrievalty">shrievalty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This system, with the abolition of rural districts, remains the model for local government in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>. (See also <a href="/wiki/List_of_rural_and_urban_districts_in_Northern_Ireland" title="List of rural and urban districts in Northern Ireland">List of rural and urban districts in Northern Ireland</a> for more details.)\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Elections">Elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_government_in_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Councillors are elected for a four-year term of office under the <a href="/wiki/Single_transferable_vote" title="Single transferable vote">single transferable vote</a> (STV) system. Elections were last held in May 2014. To qualify for election, a councillor candidate must be:\n</p>\n<ul><li>at least 18 years of age, and</li>\n<li>a <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> or <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> citizen.</li></ul>\n<p>In addition, he or she must either:\n</p>\n<ul><li>be a local elector for the district, or</li>\n<li>have, during the whole of the 12-month period prior to the election, either owned or occupied land in the district, or else resided or worked in the district.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Combinations">Combinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_government_in_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Combinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The districts are combined for various purposes.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eurostat_NUTS_level_3">Eurostat NUTS level href="/w/index.php?title=Local_government_in_Northern_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Reform_of_local_government_in_Northern_Ireland" title="Reform of local government in Northern Ireland">Reform of local government in Northern Ireland</a></div>\n<p>In June 2002, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> established a Review of Public Administration to review the arrangements for the accountability, development, administration and delivery of public services. Among its recommendations were a reduction in the number of districts.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2005 <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hain" title="Peter Hain">Peter Hain</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a>, announced proposals to reduce the number of councils to seven.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The names and boundaries of the seven districts were announced in March 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In March 2008 the restored Northern Executive agreed to create eleven new councils instead of the original seven.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The first elections were due to take place in May 2011. However, by May 2010 disagreements among parties in the executive over district boundaries were expected to delay the reforms until 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In June 2010 the proposed reforms were abandoned following the failure of the Northern Ireland Executive to reach agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> However, on 12 March 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> published its programme for government, which included a commitment to reduce the number of councils in Northern Ireland to 11.<sup id="cite_ref-pfg2015_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pfg2015-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
