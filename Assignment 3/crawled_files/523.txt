The process is called <b>unification</b>. Unification of states that used to be together and are reuniting is referred to as <b>reunification</b>. Unlike a <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> or <a href="/wiki/Real_union" title="Real union">real union</a>, the individual states share a <a href="/wiki/Central_government" title="Central government">central government</a> and the union is <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_recognition" title="Diplomatic recognition">recognized internationally</a> as a single political entity. A political union may also be called a legislative union or state union.\n</p><p>A union may be effected in a number of forms, broadly categorized as:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Incorporating union</li>\n<li>Incorporating <a href="/wiki/Annexation" title="Annexation">annexation</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">Federal</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Confederal" class="mw-redirect" title="Confederal">confederal</a>) union</li>\n<li>Federative annexation</li>\n<li>Mixed unions.</li></ul>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Incorporating union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In an incorporating union a new state is created, the former states being entirely dissolved into the new state (albeit that some aspects may be preserved; see below "<i><a href="/wiki/Political_union#Preservation_of_interests" title="Political union">Preservation of interests</a></i>").\n</p><p><b>Incorporating unions</b> have been present throughout much of History such as the <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_Union_1707" title="Acts of Union 1707">Acts of Union, 1707</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Scotland" title="Kingdom of Scotland">Kingdom of Scotland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a> creating <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, in 1910 the colonies of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape of Good Hope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Natal" title="Colony of Natal">Natal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_River_Colony" title="Orange River Colony">Orange River Colony</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Transvaal_Colony" title="Transvaal Colony">Transvaal</a> were incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">Union of South Africa</a>, between the years of 1037 to 1479 <a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Spain</a> was in the process of Incorporating the <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Crown of Castile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Aragon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre" title="Kingdom of Navarre">Navarre</a> into the Kingdom of Spain, though the process wasn\'t completed until <a href="/wiki/Nueva_Planta_decrees" title="Nueva Planta decrees">1716</a> (Aragon) and <a href="/wiki/1833_territorial_division_of_Spain" title="1833 territorial division of Spain">1833</a> (Navarre), the <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_Union_1800" title="Acts of Union 1800">Acts of Union 1800</a> united the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Ireland" title="Kingdom of Ireland">Kingdom of Ireland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a> into the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom</a>, in 1990 the <a href="/wiki/South_Yemen" title="South Yemen">People\'s Democratic Republic of Yemen</a> united with <a href="/wiki/Yemen_Arab_Republic" title="Yemen Arab Republic">Yemen Arab Republic (North Yemen)</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Republic of Yemen</a>, and in 1783 the <a href="/wiki/Articles_of_Confederation" title="Articles of Confederation">Articles of Confederation</a> were signed by each of the <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a>, uniting them into the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Preservation_of_interests">Preservation of interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preservation of interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Nevertheless, a full incorporating union may preserve the laws and institutions of the former states, as happened in the creating of the United Kingdom. This may be simply a matter of practice or to comply with a guarantee given in the terms of the union. These guarantees may be to ensure the success of a proposed union, or in the least to prevent continuing resistance, as occurred in the union of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 1532 (<a href="/wiki/Union_of_Brittany_and_France" title="Union of Brittany and France">Union of Brittany and France</a>), a guarantee was given as to the continuance of laws and of the <a href="/wiki/Estates_of_Brittany" title="Estates of Brittany">Estates of Brittany</a> (a guarantee revoked in 1789 at the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>). The assurance that institutions are preserved in a union of states can also occur as states realise that whilst a power imbalance exists (such as between the economic conditions of Scotland and England prior to the <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_Union_1707" title="Acts of Union 1707">Acts of Union 1707</a>), it is not so great that it precludes the ability of concessions to be made. The <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_Union_1707" title="Acts of Union 1707">Treaty of Union</a> for creating the unified <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a> in 1707 contained a guarantee of the continuance of the civil laws and the existing courts in Scotland<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (a continuing guarantee), which was significant for both parties. The Scottish, despite economic troubles during the <a href="/wiki/Seven_ill_years" title="Seven ill years">Seven ill years</a> preceding the union, still had remaining negotiating power.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>This marks a delineation of states that are able to ensure a preservation of interests, there has to be some mutually beneficial reasoning behind the formal or informal preservation of interests. In the <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_Union_1801" class="mw-redirect" title="Acts of Union 1801">Union</a> creating the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a> in 1801, no such guarantee was given for the laws and courts of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Ireland" title="Kingdom of Ireland">Kingdom of Ireland</a>, though they were continued as a matter of practice. The informal recognition of such interests represents the different circumstances of the two Unions, the small base of institutional power in Ireland at the time (those who were the beneficiaries of the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Ascendancy" title="Protestant Ascendancy">Protestant Ascendancy</a>) had faced revolution in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rebellion_of_1798" title="Irish Rebellion of 1798">Irish Rebellion of 1798</a>, and as a result there was an institutional drive toward unification, limiting the Irish negotiating power. However, informal guarantees were given to preclude the possibility of further Irish unrest in the period following the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution_of_1789" class="mw-redirect" title="French Revolution of 1789">French Revolution of 1789</a> and the 1798 rebellion. These types of informal arrangements are more susceptible to changes, for example <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tyrol" title="County of Tyrol">Tyrol</a> was guaranteed that its Freisch\xc3\xbctz companies would not be posted to fight outside Tyrol without their consent, a guarantee later revoked by the Austrian republic. This can be juxtaposed with the continued existence of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">Scottish Parliament</a> and a separate body of <a href="/wiki/Scottish_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish law">Scottish Law</a> distinct from <a href="/wiki/English_law" title="English law">English Law</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incorporating_annexation">Incorporating annexation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Incorporating annexation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In an incorporating annexation a state or states is united to and dissolved in an existing state, whose legal existence continues.\n</p><p>Annexation may be voluntary or, more frequently, by conquest.\n</p><p><b>Incorporating annexations</b> have occurred at various points in history such as in 1535 and 1542 under the two <a href="/wiki/Laws_in_Wales_Acts_1535_and_1542" title="Laws in Wales Acts 1535 and 1542">Laws in Wales Acts</a> in which the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a> formally annexed the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Wales" title="Principality of Wales">Principality of Wales</a>, in 1822 the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Spanish_Haiti" title="Republic of Spanish Haiti">Republic of Spanish Haiti</a> was annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Republic of Haiti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unification_of_Germany" title="Unification of Germany">Prussia/Germany</a> used incorporating annexation to unite many of the <a href="/wiki/German_Prince" title="German Prince">German Princes</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Schleswig_War" title="Second Schleswig War">Second Schleswig War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Prussian_War" title="Austro-Prussian War">Austro-Prussian War</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">Franco-Prussian War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Sardinia</a> annexed many of the Duchies and City-states in Italy during the period of <a href="/wiki/Italian_unification" href="/wiki/China" title="China">People\'s Republic of China</a> annexed <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> (1950) and <a href="/wiki/East_Turkestan" title="East Turkestan">East Turkestan (Xinjiang)</a> (1949).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_or_confederal_union">Federal or confederal union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Federal or confederal union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In a federal or confederal union the states continue in existence but place themselves under a new federal authority.  The federal state alone will be the state in international law though the federated states retain an existence in domestic law.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples_of_federal_or_confederal_union">Examples of federal or confederal union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> (federal union from 1995)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a> (1961\xe2\x80\x931970)</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Canada" id="Federal_or_confederal_annexation">Federal or confederal annexation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Federal or confederal annexation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>If a unitary state becomes a federated unit of another existing state, the former continuing its legal existence, then that is a federal annexation. The new federated state thus ceases to be a state in international law but retains its legal existence in domestic law, subsidiary to the federal authority.\n</p><p><b>Federal annexations</b> have occurred in many places, such as <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a> in 1871, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a> in 1873, and <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Newfoundland" title="Dominion of Newfoundland">Newfoundland</a> in 1949 which were all annexed into <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> was annexed into <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> from 1951 to 1962, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> federally annexed <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> in 1815, <a href="/wiki/Saarland" title="Saarland">Saarland</a> was federally annexed by <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> in 1957, <a href="/wiki/Vermont_Republic" title="Vermont Republic">Vermont</a> (1791), <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Texas" title="Republic of Texas">Texas</a> (1846), and <a href="/wiki/California_Republic" title="California Republic">California</a> (1848) all were annexed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Crimea" title="Republic of Crimea">Crimea</a> was annexed into the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian Federation</a> in 2014.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_unions">Mixed unions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mixed unions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The unification of Italy involved a mixture of unions. The kingdom consolidated around the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia" title="Kingdom of Sardinia">Kingdom of Sardinia</a>, with which several states voluntarily united to form the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_(1861%E2%80%931946)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Italy (1861\xe2\x80\x931946)">Kingdom of Italy</a>. Others polities, such as the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Kingdom of the Two Sicilies">Kingdom of the Two Sicilies</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a>, were conquered and annexed. Formally, the union in each territory was sanctioned by a popular referendum where people were formally asked if they agreed to have as their new ruler <a href="/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_II_of_Italy" title="Victor Emmanuel II of Italy">Vittorio Emanuele II of Sardinia</a> and his legitimate heirs.\n</p><p>The unification of Germany was ultimately a confederal union, but it began in earnest when the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Kingdom of Prussia</a> annexed numerous petty states in 1866.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_unions">Historical Some of these organization were inspired, too some extent by the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> for example <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations" title="Association of Southeast Asian Nations">Association of Southeast Asian Nations</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>, the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation_Forum" class="mw-redirect" title="Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation Forum">Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation Forum</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>, and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Union" title="Pacific Union">Pacific Union</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>. Member states are often reluctant to form more centralized unions, the concept of unionism is often present in public debate.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_analysis">Academic analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Political_union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Academic analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The political position of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> is often discussed;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and former states like <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a> (2003\xe2\x80\x932006), the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> (1922\xe2\x80\x931991) and the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Republic" title="United Arab Republic">United Arab Republic</a> (1958\xe2\x80\x931961).\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/John_Lambton,_1st_Earl_of_Durham" title="John Lambton, 1st Earl of Durham">Lord Durham</a> was widely regarded as one of the most important thinkers in the history of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>\'s constitutional evolution.  He articulated clearly the difference between a full legislative union and a <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federation</a>.  In his <a href="/wiki/Report_on_the_Affairs_of_British_North_America_(1839)" class="mw-redirect" title="Report on the Affairs of British North America (1839)">1839 <i>Report</i></a>, in discussing the proposed union of <a href="/wiki/The_Canadas" title="The Canadas">Upper and Lower Canada</a>, he says:\n</p>\n<blockquote><p>Two kinds of union have been proposed \xe2\x80\x93 federal and legislative. By the first, the separate legislature of each province would be preserved in its present form and retain almost all its present attributes of internal legislation, the federal legislature exercising no power save in those matters which may have been expressly ceded to it by the constituent provinces. A legislative union would imply a complete incorporation of the provinces included in it under one legislature, exercising universal and sole legislative authority over all of them in exactly the same manner as the Parliament legislates alone for the whole of the British Isles.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p></blockquote>\n<h2><span
