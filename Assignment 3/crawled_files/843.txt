America</a></td></tr><tr style="display:none"><th colspan="2">\n</th></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>St. George\'s</b> (formally, the <b>Town of St. George</b>, or <b>St. George\'s Town</b>), located on the <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Island,_Bermuda" title="St. George&#39;s Island, Bermuda">island</a> and within the <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Parish,_Bermuda" title="St. George&#39;s Parish, Bermuda">parish</a> of the same names, settled in 1612, was the first permanent English settlement on the islands of <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>. It is often described as the third successful English settlement in the Americas, after <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland" class="mw-redirect" title="St. John&#39;s, Newfoundland">St. John\'s, Newfoundland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown, Virginia</a> and the oldest continuously-inhabited English town in the New World, since the other two settlements were seasonal for a number of years.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._George%27s,_Bermuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bermudian convention, where a <a href="/wiki/Toponym" class="mw-redirect" title="Toponym">toponym</a> contains the name of a person, is to render the person\'s name in the <a href="/wiki/Possessive" title="Possessive">possessive</a> form. The place is rarely treated as equivalent to the person.\n</p><p>Among many examples of such place names in Bermuda are <a href="/wiki/St._David%27s_Island,_Bermuda" title="St. David&#39;s Island, Bermuda">St. David\'s Island</a>, <i>Bailey\'s Bay</i>, <a href="/wiki/Sandys_Parish,_Bermuda" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandys Parish, Bermuda">Sandys\' Parish</a> (named for <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Sandys_(died_1629)" title="Edwin Sandys (died 1629)">Sir Edwin Sandys</a>), Skeeters\' Island (often mistakenly rendered "Skeeter\'s Island", it is named for <i>Edward Skeeters</i> and is also known as <i>Burt\'s Island</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <i>Gibb\'s Hill</i>, <i>Barr\'s Bay</i>, <i>Ackermann\'s Hill</i>, <i>Nelly\'s Island</i>, <a href="/wiki/Cooper%27s_Island,_Bermuda" title="Cooper&#39;s Island, Bermuda">Cooper\'s Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darrell%27s_Island,_Bermuda" title="Darrell&#39;s Island, Bermuda">Darrell\'s Island</a>, <i>Paynter\'s Vale</i>, <i>Abbot\'s Head</i> (or <i>Abbot\'s Cliff</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and Fort St. Catherine\'s. The possessive form is also used for titles, as with <i>Collector\'s Hill</i> (named for the <i>Collector of Taxes</i>).\n</p><p>The use of the possessive form is not exclusive, however, as exemplified by place names such as the names of most of the parishes (which \xe2\x80\x93 other than St. George\'s Parish \xe2\x80\x93 all commemorate historical people), such as <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_Parish,_Bermuda" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamilton Parish, Bermuda">Hamilton Parish</a> (named for <a href="/wiki/James_Hamilton,_2nd_Marquess_of_Hamilton" title="James Hamilton, 2nd Marquess of Hamilton">James Hamilton, 2nd Marquess of Hamilton</a>), Devonshire Parish (named for), and Paget Parish. Some of these exceptions may have originated with changed syntax, as <i>Devonshire Parish</i> may originally have been <i>The Parish of Devonshire</i>. This is seen with the <i>City of Hamilton</i> (named for <a href="/wiki/Henry_Hamilton_(governor)" title="Henry Hamilton (governor)">Henry Hamilton</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Bermuda" title="Governor of Bermuda">Governor of Bermuda</a>). Whereas the City of Hamilton is commonly referred to as <i>Hamilton</i>, The Town of St. George, St. George\'s Parish, St. George\'s Island, and St. George\'s Harbour (and any other toponym usually containing a person\'s name in the possessive form) always remains possessive when shortened, such as in the <i>St. George\'s Foundation</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>By example, Bermudians will always say <i>St. George\'s and St. David\'s are the largest islands in St. George\'s Parish</i>, and never <i>St. George and St. David are the largest islands in St. George Parish</i>. As many maps and other printed materials featuring Bermuda are produced abroad, it is extremely common to find maps, books and other material using the non-possessive forms, but visitors to Bermuda should be aware that using the non-possessive forms when speaking to Bermudians is likely to cause as much offence as saying "Bermudan" (although Bermudians, who pride themselves on politeness, are unlikely to respond rudely).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St._George%27s-6.jpg" class="internal" title="F\xc3\xb6rstora"></a></div></div></div></div>\n<p>Originally called <i>New London</i>, St. George\'s was first settled in 1612. This was three years after the first English settlers landed on St. George\'s Island on their way to <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>. Led by <a href="/wiki/Admiral_Sir_George_Somers" class="mw-redirect" title="Admiral Sir George Somers">Admiral Sir George Somers</a> and Lieutenant-General <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Gates_(governor)" title="Thomas Gates (governor)">Sir Thomas Gates</a>, they had deliberately steered their ship, the <i><a href="/wiki/Sea_Venture" title="Sea Venture">Sea Venture</a>,</i> onto a reef to escape a storm. The survivors built two new ships, the <i>Deliverance</i> and <i>Patience</i>, and most continued their voyage to Jamestown, but the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Company" title="Virginia Company">Virginia Company</a> laid claim to the island.\n</p><p>Two men remained behind to maintain the company\'s possession of the archipelago (a third stayed when the <i>Patience</i> returned later that year). By the Virginia Company\'s Third Charter in 1612, the boundaries of the new colony were extended out to sea to include Bermuda. The company sent a party of 60 new settlers to Bermuda to join the three men left behind by the <i>Sea Venture.</i> After a brief period on neighbouring <a href="/wiki/St._David%27s_Island,_Bermuda" title="St. David&#39;s Island, Bermuda">St. David\'s</a>, the settlers started building structures at St. George\'s, located in a sheltered sound that kept ships protected from bad weather.\n</p><p>In 1615, the shareholders of the Virginia Company created a second company, the <a href="/wiki/Somers_Isles_Company" title="Somers Isles Company">Somers Isles Company</a>. It administered Bermuda separately until the company was dissolved in 1686. (The Virginia Company was dissolved in 1624).\n</p><p>This small town was the capital of Bermuda until 1815; it was linked to the history of colonial America. Ten thousand Bermudians emigrated, primarily to Virginia and the <a href="/wiki/American_Southeast" class="mw-redirect" title="American Southeast">American Southeast</a>, before United States independence made them citizens of separate nations. Branches of wealthy Bermudian merchant families dominated trade in the area\'s ports. Bermudians settled towns in the South, and contributed greatly to the make up of the populations of several US states.\n</p><p>As Bermuda\'s population centre, and only true port during this period, St. George\'s was connected to development in the North American colonies. During the <a href="/wiki/American_War_of_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="American War of Independence">American War of Independence</a>, at the request of <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a>, Bermudians assisted the American rebels in stealing much-needed <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">gunpowder</a> from a St. George\'s magazine, which supplied the forts protecting the port. The powder was carried over the hill to <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_Bay,_Bermuda" title="Tobacco Bay, Bermuda">Tobacco Bay</a>, from where boats transported it to an American ship that lay offshore.\n</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, some British from St. George\'s evaded coastal blockades to provide supplies and munitions to the desperate <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederates</a>. This trade was based in St. George\'s.\n</p><p><a href="/w/index.php?title=King%27s_Square,_St_George%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="King&#39;s Square, St George&#39;s (page does not exist)">King\'s Square</a> forms the centre of St George\'s, where regular 17th-century re-enactments are held throughout the year. Excavations carried out by <a href="/wiki/Bristol_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Bristol University">Bristol University</a> and the Bermuda National Trust discovered the foundations here of the original 1612 governor\'s house. The Bermuda National Trust Museum is located on the square.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Island,_Bermuda" class="mw-redirect" title="Ordnance Island, Bermuda">Ordnance Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Harbour,_Bermuda" title="St. George&#39;s Harbour, Bermuda">St. George\'s Harbour</a>, is situated south of King\'s Square. It holds a replica of the <i>Deliverance</i> (one of the two ships built by the survivors of the Sea Venture), and a life-sized statue of Admiral of the Virginia Company, Sir <a href="/wiki/George_Somers" title="George Somers">George Somers</a>, by Desmond Fountain. Somers, along with Sir <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Gates_(governor)" title="Thomas Gates (governor)">Thomas Gates</a>, had led the survivors of the 1609 wreck.\n</p><p>The town has numerous historical sites, such as the old <a href="/wiki/State_House,_Bermuda" title="State House, Bermuda">State House</a> from 1620. Other than fortifications, this was the first stone building in Bermuda, built to house the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Bermuda" title="Parliament of Bermuda">Parliament</a>. It is the oldest building on the island. Others of note are the Unfinished Church, the Old Rectory, <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Church,_St._George%27s" title="St. Peter&#39;s Church, St. George&#39;s">St. Peter\'s</a> (the oldest surviving <a href="/wiki/Anglican" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> and oldest continuously occupied <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> church in the Western hemisphere), the Tucker House, the Bermuda National Trust Museum, and the St. George\'s Historical Society Museum and the <a href="/wiki/Featherbed_Alley_Printshop" title="Featherbed Alley Printshop">Featherbed Alley Printshop</a> museum (both in the Mitchell House).\n</p><p>In 1996, the town was twinned with <a href="/wiki/Lyme_Regis" title="Lyme Regis">Lyme Regis</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, England, the birthplace of Admiral Sir George Somers. In 2000, the town, together with numerous surrounding fortifications, including the <a href="/wiki/Castle_Islands_Fortifications,_Bermuda" title="Castle Islands Fortifications, Bermuda">Castle Islands Fortifications</a>, was designated as a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_List" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage List">World Heritage Site</a>, formally known as the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage_Site:_Historic_Town_of_St_George_and_Related_Fortifications,_Bermuda" class="mw-redirect" title="UNESCO World Heritage Site: Historic Town of St George and Related Fortifications, Bermuda">Historic Town of St George and Related Fortifications</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span
