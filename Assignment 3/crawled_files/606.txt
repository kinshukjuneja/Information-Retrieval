Although its common law is similar to that in England and Wales, and partially derives from the same sources, there are some important differences in law and procedure between Northern Ireland and England and Wales. While influenced by English law, the Northern Ireland legal system is distinctive for a number of reasons: it has roots in Irish common law prior to Irish independence in 1921; following Irish independence, Northern Ireland became a devolved jurisdiction within the United Kingdom.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Legislation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The current statute law of Northern Ireland comprises those <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_Parliament_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Acts of Parliament in the United Kingdom">Acts</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> that apply to Northern Ireland  and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Acts_of_the_Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="List of Acts of the Northern Ireland Assembly">Acts</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Statutory_instruments" class="mw-redirect" title="Statutory instruments">statutory instruments</a> made by departments of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UK_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Government">UK Government</a>. Also remaining on the statute books are many Acts of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Parliament of Northern Ireland">Parliament of Northern Ireland</a> passed between 1921 and 1972, certain Acts of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Ireland" title="Parliament of Ireland">Parliament of Ireland</a> made before the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Union_1800" class="mw-redirect" title="Act of Union 1800">Act of Union 1800</a>, and Acts of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_England" title="Parliament of England">Parliament of England</a>, and of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a>, extended to Ireland under <a href="/wiki/Poynings%27_Law" title="Poynings&#39; Law">Poynings\' Law</a> between 1494 and 1782.\n</p><p>The expression "<a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_legislation" title="Northern Ireland legislation">Northern Ireland legislation</a>" is defined by statute. The <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a> establishes the legislative competence of the Northern Ireland Assembly. It creates a distinction between excepted matters, reserved matters and other matters (which are transferred i.e. they fall within the NI Assembly\'s competence). The <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a> functions as a constitution for Northern Ireland as indicated in the Robinson case.\n</p><p>The Northern Ireland Parliament was prorogued in 1972; from then until the establishment of the Northern Ireland Assembly following the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a>, the primary method of making legislation for Northern Ireland was by means of <a href="/wiki/Orders_in_council" class="mw-redirect" title="Orders in council">orders in council</a> under the Northern Ireland (Temporary Provisions) Act 1972. A number of important legislative measures were adopted using the order in council procedure: this included the Criminal Evidence (Northern Ireland) Order 1988 restricting the right to silence, the Fair Employment and Treatment Order (Northern Ireland) 1998 on religious and political discrimination.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_publications">Legal publications</span><span These date to the Civil Authorities (Special Powers) Act (Northern Ireland) 1922, commonly called the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Authorities_(Special_Powers)_Act_(Northern_Ireland)_1922" title="Civil Authorities (Special Powers) Act (Northern Ireland) 1922">Special Powers Act</a>. Following the outbreak of violence in the 1960s and 1970s, the Northern Ireland (Emergency Provisions) Act 1973 introduced juryless <a href="/wiki/Diplock_courts" title="Diplock courts">Diplock courts</a> to try terrorism related offences.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Terrorism_Act_2000" title="Terrorism Act 2000">Terrorism Act 2000</a> retains special provisions for Northern Ireland in respect of anti-terrorism law, and retains the possibility to try certain offences without a jury.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_law">Civil law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Civil law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Defamation_Act_2013" title="Defamation Act 2013">Defamation Act 2013</a> does not apply in Northern Ireland. This protections which this Act provides for free expression (e.g. the public interest defence in section 4)  do not therefore apply in Northern Ireland.\n</p><p>Northern Irish courts have issued a small number of <a href="/wiki/Super-injunctions" class="mw-redirect" title="Super-injunctions">super-injunctions</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Discrimination_Law">Discrimination Law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Discrimination Law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Discrimination law in Northern Ireland has evolved somewhat separately to discrimination law elsewhere in the United Kingdom. Most notably, Northern Ireland history of legislation on religious and political discrimination. The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland_Act_1920" title="Government of Ireland Act 1920">Government of Ireland Act 1920</a> prohibited religious discrimination in legislation. In 1976 the UK Parliament passed the Fair Employment (Northern Ireland) Act which prohibited religious and political discrimination in employment. The Fair Employment (Northern Ireland) Act 1989 creates a system to monitor the religious composition of the workforce so as to promote fair participation.\n</p><p>In 1998 the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a> introduced a statutory duty on designated public authorities to promote equality of opportunity on a number of grounds.\n</p><p>While in some aspects Northern Ireland\'s equality law has been in advance of developments elsewhere, there are also examples where it is not as progressive. Racial discrimination in Northern Ireland was only prohibited in 1997. The <a href="/wiki/Equality_Act_2010" title="Equality Act 2010">Equality Act 2010</a> does not apply in Northern Ireland; this means that Northern Ireland\'s equality legislation is split across a large number of Acts and Orders.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
