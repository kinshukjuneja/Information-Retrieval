title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\xcb\x88</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/\xc9\xaa/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">\xc9\xaa</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a double roundabout between the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> towns of <a href="/wiki/Esher" title="Esher">Esher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Thames" title="Kingston upon Thames">Kingston upon Thames</a> (which do not adjoin each other) but which lie on the traditional route of the <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_Road" class="mw-redirect" title="Portsmouth id="History_and_notability">History and notability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scilly_Isles,_Surrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and notability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Since the 19th century there has been a large <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">pub</a> at the junction, named for a prominent <a href="/wiki/Aristocrat" class="mw-redirect" title="Aristocrat">aristocrat</a>-soldier the <a href="/wiki/Marquis_of_Granby" class="mw-redirect" title="Marquis of Granby">Marquis of Granby</a>. The junction acquired its name during the 1930s when the then new Kingston by-pass road rejoined the original route of the Portsmouth Road that was then the <a href="/wiki/A3_road_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="A3 road (Great Britain)">equivalent to today\'s A3</a>. The present road is termed the <a href="/wiki/A307_road" title="A307 road">A307</a> and the <a href="/wiki/A309_road" title="A309 road">A309</a> crosses this and links via a dualled section to the A3 road and the north. A series of <a href="/wiki/Roundabout" title="Roundabout">roundabouts</a> were built at the busy (for the 1930s) intersection, then called "<a href="/wiki/Traffic_island" title="Traffic island">traffic islands</a>". The unfamiliar nature of the double roundabout caused it to be called the "silly islands", which became locally accepted as its name, but this soon evolved into the "<a href="/wiki/Scilly_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Scilly Isles">Scilly Isles</a>", copying the name of the islands to the west of <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>. This name, although intended to be disapproving, thus became an official name, visible on all approaches to the junction and the highest definition maps.\n</p><p>The complexity of the junction combined with traffic greater than the planners envisaged has periodically created tension between those who wish to replace it with a more conventional layout and those who consider it to be a landmark or to reduce speeding in an ingenious way. There are relatively frequent accidents at the junction<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> due to a small minority of users not from the area not reading the road markings or signs along its course - the northern approach is Hampton Court Way that leads to <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Court_Palace" title="Hampton Court Palace">Hampton Court Palace</a>, which adds to such users. The Esher side, being the closer town, has far more priority than the Kingston side (the small roundabout must give way to the bigger, and the latter is to the west).\n</p><p>The nearby <a href="/wiki/Sandown_Park_Racecourse" title="Sandown Park Racecourse">Sandown Park Racecourse</a> hosts the annual <a href="/wiki/Scilly_Isles_Novices%27_Chase" title="Scilly Isles Novices&#39; Chase">Scilly Isles Novices\' Chase</a>.  Littleworth Common and Ditton Marsh to either side are almost undeveloped <a href="/wiki/Common_land" title="Common land">commons</a>, mostly covered by trees and grass/scrub respectively.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Position">Position</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
