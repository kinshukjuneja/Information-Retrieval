href="/wiki/Team_sport" title="Team sport">team sport</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>\'s national side today competes in the annual <a href="/wiki/Six_Nations_Championship" title="Six Nations Championship">Six Nations Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup" title="Rugby World Cup">Rugby World Cup</a>. The first ever international <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby</a> match was played on 27 March 1871, at <a href="/wiki/Raeburn_Place" title="Raeburn Place">Raeburn Place</a> in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Scotland national rugby union team">Scotland</a> defeated <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England</a> in front of 4,000 people. Professional clubs compete in the <a href="/wiki/Pro14" title="Pro14">Pro14</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_Rugby_Champions_Cup" title="European Rugby Champions Cup">European Rugby Champions Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Rugby_Challenge_Cup" title="European Rugby Challenge Cup">European Rugby Challenge Cup</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_League_Championship" title="Scottish League Championship">Scottish League Championship</a> exists for over 200 amateur and semi-professional clubs, as does a knock-out competition, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Cup_(rugby_union)" title="Scottish Cup (rugby union)">Scottish Cup</a>. The governing body, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Rugby_Union" title="Scottish Rugby Union">Scottish Rugby Union</a> (SRU), is one of the ten first-tier member nations of <a href="/wiki/World_Rugby" title="World Rugby">World Rugby</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" operate the <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Scotland national rugby union team">Scottish national team</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Greenyardsmelrose.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nestling beneath the shadow of the <a href="/wiki/Eildon_Hills" class="mw-redirect" title="Eildon Hills">Eildon Hills</a>, the Greenyards at <a href="/wiki/Melrose,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Melrose, Scotland">Melrose</a> in Scotland is the original home of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_sevens" title="Rugby sevens">rugby sevens</a></div></div></div>\n<p>There is a long tradition of "football" games in Scotland, and many of these such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeddart_Ball&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jeddart Ball (page does not exist)">Jeddart Ball</a> bear more resemblance to rugby than <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a>, since passing and carrying by hand play a large part in them. The <a href="/wiki/Kirkwall_Ba_game" title="Kirkwall Ba game">Kirkwall Ba game</a> still takes place, and involves scrummaging. Scottish soccer enthusiasts also cite these games as ancestral to their sport.\n</p><p>There is evidence for schoolboys playing a "football" ball game in Aberdeen in 1633 (some references cite 1636) which is notable as an early allusion to what some have considered to be passing the ball. The word "pass" in the most recent translation is derived from "huc percute" (strike it here) and later <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">repercute pilam</i></i> (strike the ball again) in the original Latin. It is not certain that the ball was being struck between members of the same team.  The original word translated as "goal" is <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">metum</i></i>, literally meaning the "pillar at each end of the circus course" in a <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> chariot race. There is a reference to "get hold of the ball before [another player] does" (Praeripe illi pilam si possis agere) suggesting that handling of the ball was allowed. One sentence states in the original 1930 translation "Throw yourself against him" (<i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">Age, objice te illi</i></i>). It is clear that the game was rough and tackles allowed included the "charging" and pushing/holding of opposing players ("drive that man back" in the original translation, "repelle eum" in original Latin). It has been suggested that this game bears similarities to <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby football</a>.\n</p><p>Contrary to media reports in 2006 there is no reference to forward passing, game rules, marking players or team formation. These reports described it as "an amazing new discovery" but has actually been well documented in football history literature since the early 20th century and available on the internet since at least 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The world\'s oldest continual rugby fixture was first played in 1858 between <a href="/wiki/Merchiston_Castle_School" title="Merchiston Castle School">Merchiston Castle School</a> and the former pupils of <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Academy" title="Edinburgh Academy">The Edinburgh Academy</a>.\n</p><p>Scotland was responsible for organising the very first rugby international when a side representing <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England</a> met the Scottish national side on the cricket field of the Edinburgh Academy at their <a href="/wiki/Raeburn_Place" title="Raeburn Place">Raeburn Place</a> ground on 27 March 1871; Scotland won by one goal. The Scottish Football Union (SFU) - later named SRU - was founded in 1873 (in the Staff Common Room at The Glasgow Academy) and was a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Rugby_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="International Rugby Board">International Rugby Board</a> in 1886 with <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Irish Rugby Football Union">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Rugby_Union" title="Welsh Rugby Union">Wales</a>. (England refused to join until 1890).\n</p><p>Since that time, Scotland have been regular winners of the <a href="/wiki/Calcutta_Cup" title="Calcutta Cup">Calcutta Cup</a>, the five nations championship (discontinued), and have been participants of (having never actually won) the <a href="/wiki/Six_Nations_Championship" title="Six Nations Championship">Six Nations Championship</a>, and every <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup" title="Rugby World Cup">Rugby World Cup</a>. Many of the world\'s most famous players have worn the blue jersey.\n</p><p>Scotland has played a seminal role in the development of rugby, notably in <a href="/wiki/Rugby_sevens" title="Rugby sevens">Rugby sevens</a>, which were initially conceived by <a href="/wiki/Ned_Haig" title="Ned Haig">Ned Haig</a>, a butcher from <a href="/wiki/Melrose,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Melrose, Scotland">Melrose</a> as a fundraising event for his local club in 1883. The first ever officially sanctioned international tournament of rugby occurred at <a href="/wiki/Murrayfield_Stadium" title="Murrayfield Stadium">Murrayfield</a> as part of the "Scottish Rugby Union\'s celebration of rugby" centenary celebrations in 1973. Due to the success of the format, the ongoing <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Sevens" title="Hong Kong Sevens">Hong Kong Sevens</a> was launched three years later. In 1993, the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup_Sevens" title="Rugby World Cup Sevens">Rugby World Cup Sevens</a> was launched and the trophy is known as the <i>Melrose Cup</i> in memory of Ned Haig\'s invention.\n</p><p>In 1924 the SFU changed its name to the Scottish Rugby Union.<sup id="cite_ref-1925GS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1925GS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> International games were played at <a href="/wiki/Inverleith" title="Inverleith">Inverleith</a> from 1899 to 1925 when Murrayfield was opened.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competitions">Competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dd><i>See also</i> <a href="/wiki/Scottish_rugby_union_system" title="Scottish rugby union system">Scottish rugby union system</a></dd></dl>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rugby_Scotland.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Rugby_Scotland.jpg/300px-Rugby_Scotland.jpg" width="300" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Rugby_Scotland.jpg/450px-Rugby_Scotland.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Rugby_Scotland.jpg/600px-Rugby_Scotland.jpg 2x" data-file-width="683" data-file-height="405" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rugby_Scotland.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Murrayfield_Stadium" title="Murrayfield Stadium">Murrayfield</a> rugby stadium</div></div></div>\n<p>Historically rugby union was an <a href="/wiki/Amateur" title="Amateur">amateur</a> sport, but the dawn of professionalism changed the way in which the game was structured. The game is divided into professional and non-professional spheres.\n</p><p>Previously there had been a domestic league that covered the country, the top division of which was essentially the elite of club rugby in Scotland. This league was established in the early 1970s to replace the complicated "unofficial championship" that had been competed for previously. Starting in the 1973\xe2\x80\x9374 season, the clubs were organised into a league of six divisions - what today comprises the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premiership_(rugby)" title="Scottish Premiership (rugby)">Scottish Premiership</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Leagues" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish National Leagues">National League</a> elements of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_League_Championship" title="Scottish League Championship">League Championship</a>. Originally, below the six divisions (but not connected by promotion or relegation) were a series of District Leagues, covering smaller geographical areas, organised by District Unions and sometimes involving second XVs. Over a period of time, these District divisions have been reformed and integrated into the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_rugby_union_system" title="Scottish rugby union system">Scottish rugby union system</a> meaning that today, only four clubs don\'t have their first XVs in the interconnected league structure.\n</p><p>The entire system is sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Bank_of_Scotland" title="Royal Bank of Scotland">Royal Bank of Scotland</a> (or RBS), making it known as the <i>RBS League Championship</i>. This league contains Scottish rugby union\'s traditional big name clubs, such as <a href="/wiki/Melrose_RFC" title="Melrose RFC">Melrose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawick_RFC" title="Hawick RFC">Hawick</a>, as well as major city clubs such as <a href="/wiki/Boroughmuir_RFC" title="Boroughmuir RFC">Boroughmuir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heriots_RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Heriots RFC">Heriots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Watsonians_RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Watsonians RFC">Watsonians</a> from Edinburgh, and <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Hawks" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow Hawks">Glasgow Hawks</a> who were formed from an amalgamation of clubs in the 1990s.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clubs">Clubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Clubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Traditionally, rugby clubs were often formed by universities, ex-pupils of <a href="/wiki/Independent_schools" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent schools">independent schools</a> and large <a href="/wiki/State_schools" class="mw-redirect" title="State schools">state schools</a>, and many clubs names still to this day include abbreviations such as:\n</p>\n<ul><li>\'High School Former Pupils\' (for instance, <a href="/wiki/Dundee_HSFP_RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Dundee HSFP RFC">Dundee HSFP RFC</a>)</li>\n<li>\'Former Pupils\' (for instance, <a href="/wiki/Stewart%27s_Melville_FP_RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Stewart&#39;s Melville FP RFC">Stewart\'s Melville FP RFC</a>)</li>\n<li>\'Grammar School Former Pupils\' (for instance, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_GSFP_RFC" title="Aberdeen GSFP RFC">Aberdeen GSFP RFC</a>)</li>\n<li>\'University\' (<a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_University_Rugby_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Aberdeen University Rugby Football Club">Aberdeen University Rugby Football Club</a> AURFC)</li></ul>\n<p>However, with the introduction of the league system in the 1970s and the resulting increase in competitiveness and standard of play, most of these clubs have had to loosen their participation criteria to include non ex-pupils. In most cases though the clubs squads do still comprise a large proportion of individuals with connections to the schools. Often the clubs will be part-financed, and their grounds maintained or even owned, by the schools themselves.\n</p><p>Amalgamations of clubs are also reasonably frequent, and when this occurs the clubs often combine names, as in <a href="/wiki/Hillhead_Jordanhill_RFC" title="Hillhead Jordanhill RFC">Hillhead/Jordanhill RFC</a>  or Waysiders/Drumpellier RFC.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_leagues">Other leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Scotland is also home to the oldest organised rugby union league in the world,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> the <a href="/wiki/Border_League" title="Border League">Border League</a>, which was formed in 1901. The Border League does not take part in the pyramid structure of the National League, but all its clubs participate in it (and thus the Border League is now effectively a supplementary competition). Two small \'independent\' leagues remain outside the system, the <a href="/wiki/Highland_Alliance_League" title="Highland Alliance League">Highland Alliance League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Grampian_Alliance_League" title="Grampian Alliance League">Grampian Alliance League</a> but they have only four clubs between them (the remaining membership being second XVs of clubs in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_League_Championship" title="Scottish League Championship">League Championship</a>) and are not likely to remain in existence for much longer.\n</p><p>Aside from the schools, the other \'traditional powerhouse\' of rugby in Scotland was the universities, and to this day the Scottish universities have their own <a href="/wiki/League_system" title="League system">league system</a> independent of the <a href="/wiki/British_Universities_%26_Colleges_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="British Universities &amp; Colleges Sport">BUCS</a> system which covers the rest of Great Britain. However, the BUCS Scottish Conference comprises divisions of four or five teams, and therefore not many fixtures each season, so unofficial Saturday University Leagues are organised (somewhat informally) between the universities. As well as having their own leagues the universities often compete in the SRU league structure and cup competitions to a high standard, most notably in 2007\xe2\x80\x9308 <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_University_Rugby_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Aberdeen University Rugby Football Club">Aberdeen University</a> became the first university side to make the SHE SRU finals day winning the Plate competition. The significance of the universities to the history of the SRU is evident when it is noted that four of the oldest 17 SRU affiliated clubs are university teams.\n</p><p>Due to the social and amateur nature of the game, most clubs try to run as many teams as possible so that all their players get games on most weekends, and therefore a large system of what are effectively <a href="/w/index.php?title=Reserve_leagues&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Reserve leagues (page does not exist)">reserve leagues</a> operate. Known as second XV, third XV, fourth XV, etc. depending on the quality of the players making up each team, their competitive activities were formally all supervised by <a href="/wiki/The_Scottish_2nd_XV_League" class="mw-redirect" title="The Scottish 2nd XV League">The Scottish 2nd XV League</a> - however in recent years disputes and breakaways have led to the formation of independent 2nd XV leagues in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Borders" title="Scottish Borders">Scottish Borders</a> and in and around <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.\n</p><p>See <a href="/wiki/University_Leagues_in_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="University Leagues in Scotland">University Leagues in Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/2nd_XV_Leagues_in_Scotland" title="2nd XV Leagues in Scotland">2nd XV Leagues in Scotland</a> for details.  For schools rugby see <a href="/wiki/Brewin_Dolphin_Scottish_Schools_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Brewin Dolphin Scottish Schools Cup">Brewin Dolphin Scottish Schools Cup</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_for_the_professional_era">Changes for the professional era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Changes for the professional era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small><i>(November 2012)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>When professionalism was introduced into rugby union in the 1990s, and the <a href="/wiki/Heineken_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Heineken Cup">Heineken Cup</a> created for clubs across Europe, the SRU decided that the existing clubs operating in the Scottish leagues were not competitive enough. They were predominantly amateur, or at best paid small wages; they had low supports and small old-fashioned venues; and the quality of their play was, by the nature of these factors, comparatively low versus new professional clubs, provinces and regions in other countries. As a rule their players trained only two nights a week.\n</p><p>Scotland has the oldest District provincial rugby sides in the world. The Glasgow - Edinburgh district derby was first played in 1872, hence the <a href="/wiki/1872_Cup" title="1872 Cup">1872 Cup</a> played today. The District sides traditionally drew together the best amateur players from clubs in a given area; and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Inter-District_Championship" title="Scottish Inter-District Championship">Scottish Inter-District Championship</a> was founded in 1953. There were four standard districts \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_District_(rugby_union)" title="Glasgow District (rugby union)">Glasgow District</a>; <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_District_(rugby_union)" title="Edinburgh District (rugby union)">Edinburgh District</a>; <a href="/wiki/North_and_Midlands" title="North and Midlands">North and Midlands</a>; and <a href="/wiki/South_of_Scotland_rugby_union_team" class="mw-redirect" title="South of Scotland rugby union team">South</a> \xe2\x80\x93 they occasionally competed with an Anglo-Scots or <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Exiles_(rugby_union)" title="Scottish Exiles (rugby union)">Exile</a> side as a fifth district in the Championship.\n</p><p>The SRU decided to turn these standard Districts into four professional teams based roughly on the old districts: the <a href="/wiki/Border_Reivers_(rugby)" class="mw-redirect" title="Border Reivers (rugby)">Border Reivers</a> based in Galashiels (with occasional matches elsewhere), the <a href="/wiki/Caledonia_Reds" title="Caledonia Reds">Caledonia Reds</a> based in Aberdeen and Perth, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Rugby" title="Edinburgh Rugby">Edinburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Warriors" title="Glasgow Warriors">Glasgow</a>.\n</p><p>The aim of creating these \'pro-teams\' or \'super-teams\' was ensure that Scotland had fairly competitive sides operating in the European competitions, the <a href="/wiki/Heineken_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Heineken Cup">Heineken Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Challenge_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="European Challenge Cup">European Challenge Cup</a> (as well as the <a href="/wiki/European_Shield" title="European Shield">European Shield</a> during its short existence), and to drive up standards of rugby in the country including developing players for the national side.\n</p><p>Initially the \'pro-teams\' were still competing in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Inter-District_Championship" title="Scottish Inter-District Championship">Scottish Inter-District Championship</a>, but a <a href="/wiki/Welsh-Scottish_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh-Scottish League">Welsh-Scottish League</a> later developed, and from that development came the Celtic League with Ireland\'s introduction (and for a time there was also a cup competition, the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Cup_(rugby_union)" title="Celtic Cup (rugby union)">Celtic Cup</a>). The Celtic League has further expanded and is now known as the <a href="/wiki/Pro14" title="Pro14">Pro14</a> and consists of Scottish, Welsh, Irish, Italian and South African sides.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_four_teams_to_two">From four teams to two</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: From four teams to two">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The four professional teams struggled in European competition and were a heavy financial burden for the union and for the formation of the <a href="/wiki/Pro14" title="Pro14">Celtic League</a> they were amalgamated into Edinburgh Reivers and Glasgow Caledonian Reds playing in Edinburgh and Glasgow respectively, and later renamed simply Edinburgh and Glasgow]].\n</p><p>After a few seasons with two teams, the SRU then reformed a Borders team, initially known as Border Reivers, then renamed The Borders, before reverting to Border Reivers again. At the time of this last change the other two sides were renamed Edinburgh Gunners and Glasgow Warriors. However, the SRU\'s extreme financial difficulties (they were over \xc2\xa320M in debt<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>) forced yet another re-think (especially when the Border Reivers were rooted to the bottom of the Celtic League season after season) \xe2\x80\x93 at the end of season 2005\xe2\x80\x9306, Edinburgh Gunners were sold to a private consortium led by Alex Carruthers, and renamed Edinburgh Rugby.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continuing_difficulties">Continuing difficulties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Continuing difficulties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Ambox current red.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/42px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png" width="42" height="34" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/63px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/84px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="290" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article\'s <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute">factual accuracy</a> may be compromised due to out-of-date information</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span>  <small><i>(May 2013)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>However, even with the running costs of two instead of three teams, the SRU were still struggling. Many attempts were made to find private backers for Glasgow or the Borders (although the only investors interested in the latter wanted to move it to Falkirk, Stirling or Aberdeen) but in the end neither of the teams could be sold. As a result, at the end of 2006\xe2\x80\x9307 the SRU yet again disbanded the <a href="/wiki/Border_Reivers_(rugby)" class="mw-redirect" title="Border Reivers (rugby)">Border Reivers</a>, leaving Scotland with two pro-teams, one under private and one under SRU control.\n</p><p>The relationship with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Carruthers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alex Carruthers (page does not exist)">Alex Carruthers</a> and his ERC Group which owned <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Rugby" title="Edinburgh Rugby">Edinburgh Rugby</a> proved to be very uncomfortable. The SRU defaulted in a number of payments of competition prize money to ERC, requiring the consortium to invest their own additional funds, and the SRU refused to share bar takings from <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Rugby" title="Edinburgh Rugby">Edinburgh Rugby</a> matches at Murrayfield with ERC - at the same time, the SRU was unhappy about the signing policy and the unavailability of players for international team training.\n</p><p>Following a bitter dispute in the press and media during 2007, in which legal action was started, and for a time <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Rugby" title="Edinburgh Rugby">Edinburgh Rugby</a> was banned from participating in matches, the SRU agreed to buy back <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Rugby" title="Edinburgh Rugby">Edinburgh Rugby</a> from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Carruthers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alex Carruthers (page does not exist)">Alex Carruthers</a>. This caused much unrest in the Scottish Borders, as their team had been wound up only months before, when the SRU insisted it couldn\'t finance two pro-teams on its own.\n</p><p>The SRU announced shortly after its buy-back that it intends to rename Edinburgh Rugby as Edinburgh RFC at some point in the future.\n</p><p>From 2014\xe2\x80\x9315. the Heineken Cup and original European Challenge Cup are respectively being replaced by the <a href="/wiki/European_Rugby_Champions_Cup" title="European Rugby Champions Cup">European Rugby Champions Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Rugby_Challenge_Cup" title="European Rugby Challenge Cup">European Rugby Challenge Cup</a>. Scotland is guaranteed one place in the Champions Cup, awarded to the Scottish team that finishes higher in the previous Pro12 season. The other Scottish team can qualify for the Champions Cup if it is one of the three Pro12 clubs with the best record, outside of the top team from each Pro12 nation (Ireland, Italy, Scotland, Wales). Additionally, starting in 2015\xe2\x80\x9316, the second Scottish team can potentially qualify through a play-off involving another Pro12 side and one each from the <a href="/wiki/English_Premiership_(rugby_union)" class="mw-redirect" title="English Premiership (rugby union)">English Premiership</a> and France\'s <a href="/wiki/Top_14" title="Top 14">Top 14</a>. Otherwise, that team will participate in the Challenge Cup.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popularity">Popularity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Popularity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Weststandmurrayfield.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Weststandmurrayfield.jpg/300px-Weststandmurrayfield.jpg" width="300" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Weststandmurrayfield.jpg/450px-Weststandmurrayfield.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Weststandmurrayfield.jpg/600px-Weststandmurrayfield.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Weststandmurrayfield.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The West Stand of <a href="/wiki/Murrayfield_Stadium" title="Murrayfield Stadium">Murrayfield Stadium</a>, demonstrating the popularity of Scottish rugby at international level</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">Rugby union</a> is not the <a href="/wiki/National_sport" title="National sport">national sport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, being far behind <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>. It is most popular in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Borders" title="Scottish Borders">Borders region</a> where it is played widely, although even here ground is being lost to football, with professionalism and migration contributing to the challenges facing the game .<sup id="cite_ref-Kelly_2008_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelly_2008-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the rest of the country rugby tends to be played mainly by private schools.\n</p><p>Whilst attendances at club matches in Scotland are fairly poor, the national team draws a sizeable crowd to <a href="/wiki/Murrayfield_Stadium" title="Murrayfield Stadium">Murrayfield</a> for <a href="/wiki/Six_Nations_Championship" title="Six Nations Championship">Six Nations</a> matches. Some traditionalists claim that in recent years the national rugby union team has become a focal point for football-type sporting nationalism.<sup id="cite_ref-Kelly_2008_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kelly_2008-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Aside from Murrayfield, there are few major rugby stadiums in Scotland. Many clubs in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Borders" title="Scottish Borders">Scottish Borders</a> have grandstands and city sides in Edinburgh and Glasgow also have seated, covered stands.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Statistics">Statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/International_Rugby_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="International Rugby Board">International Rugby Board</a> as of September 2010, Scotland has 241 rugby union clubs; 343 referees; 7,556 pre-teen male players; 13,402 teen male players; 10,556 senior male players (total male players 31,514) as well as 1,303 (total) female players.<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20121228000354/http://www.irb.com/unions/union=11000004/index.html">[1]</a> However, many more pre teen players are not registered with the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Rugby_Union" title="Scottish Rugby Union">SRU</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_in_the_Scottish_Borders" title="Rugby union in the Scottish Borders">Rugby union in the Scottish Borders</a></div>\n<p>Rugby union is particularly popular in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Borders" title="Scottish Borders">Borders</a> region. The towns of <a href="/wiki/Hawick" title="Hawick">Hawick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galashiels" title="Galashiels">Galashiels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jedburgh" title="Jedburgh">Jedburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selkirk,_Scottish_Borders" title="Selkirk, Scottish Borders">Selkirk</a> have produced many international players.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_national_team">The national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_union_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: The national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Scotland national rugby union team">Scotland national rugby union team</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scotland_at_the_Rugby_World_Cup" title="Scotland at the Rugby World Cup">Scotland at the Rugby World Cup</a></div>\n<p>The first international rugby union match in the world was played between England and Scotland in Edinburgh in 1871. Scotland won 4\xe2\x80\x931.  The national side is considered by the IRB to belong in the top tier of nations, although they are not as competitive as the elite sides such as <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_union_team" title="New Zealand national rugby union team">New Zealand</a> or <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_rugby_union_team" title="South Africa national rugby union team">South Africa</a>. They usually play their home matches at <a href="/wiki/Murrayfield_Stadium" title="Murrayfield Stadium">Murrayfield Stadium</a> in the West End of <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.\n</p><p>Scotland contest the <a href="/wiki/Calcutta_Cup" title="Calcutta Cup">Calcutta Cup</a> with England as part of the <a href="/wiki/Six_Nations_Championship" title="Six Nations Championship">Six Nations Championship</a>. The Calcutta Cup was last won by Scotland in the 2018 Six Nations Championship beating England 25\xe2\x80\x9313.\n</p><p>Every four years the <a href="/wiki/British_and_Irish_Lions" title="British and Irish Lions">British and Irish Lions</a> go on tour with players from Scotland as well as England, Ireland and Wales. Scottish players are also regularly selected to represent <a href="/wiki/Barbarian_F.C." title="Barbarian F.C.">The Barbarians</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
