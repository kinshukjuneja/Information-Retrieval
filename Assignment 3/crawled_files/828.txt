href="/wiki/Rugby_league_playing_field" title="Rugby league playing field">Rugby league playing field</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Rugby league football</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Contact_sport" title="Contact sport">full-contact sport</a> played by two teams of thirteen players on a rectangular <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_playing_field" title="Rugby league playing field">field</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RLEF-whatisRL_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RLEF-whatisRL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WADeptRS-dimensions_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WADeptRS-dimensions-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> One of the <a href="/wiki/Comparison_of_rugby_league_and_rugby_union" title="Comparison of rugby league and rugby union">two codes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a>, it originated in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a> in 1895 as a <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league#The_schism_in_England" title="History of rugby league">split</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a> over the issue of payments to players.<sup id="cite_ref-Tony_Collins_2006_p.3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tony_Collins_2006_p.3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Its rules progressively changed with the aim of producing a faster, more entertaining game for spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-WIP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WIP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In rugby league, points are scored by carrying the ball and touching it to the ground beyond the opposing team\'s goal line; this is called a <i><a href="/wiki/Try" title="Try">try</a></i>, and is the primary method of scoring.<sup id="cite_ref-WADeptRS-dimensions_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WADeptRS-dimensions-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The opposing team attempts to stop the attacking side scoring points by <a href="/wiki/Tackle_(football_move)#Rugby_League" title="Tackle (football move)">tackling</a> the player carrying the ball.<sup id="cite_ref-WADeptRS-dimensions_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WADeptRS-dimensions-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In addition to tries, points can be scored by kicking goals. After each try, the scoring team gains a free kick to <i>try at goal</i> with a conversion for further points.<sup id="cite_ref-WADeptRS-dimensions_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WADeptRS-dimensions-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Kicks at goal may also be awarded for penalties, and <a href="/wiki/Drop_goal" title="Drop goal">field goals</a> can be attempted at any time.\n</p><p>Rugby league is a popular sport in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-heartlands_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heartlands-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the states of <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_Australia" title="Rugby league in Australia">Australia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_New_Zealand" title="Rugby league in New Zealand">South Auckland, New Zealand</a>, southwest <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_France" title="Rugby league in France">France</a> and is the national sport of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_Papua_New_Guinea" title="Rugby league in Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PNG_vow_to_upset_World_Cup_odds_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PNG_vow_to_upset_World_Cup_odds-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PNG_seal_2010_Four_Nations_place_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PNG_seal_2010_Four_Nations_place-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The European <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Rugby_League" title="National Rugby League">National Rugby League</a> (NRL) are the premier club competitions. Rugby league is played <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_rugby_league_teams" title="List of international rugby league teams">internationally</a>, predominantly by <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands" title="Pacific Islands">Pacific Island</a> countries, and is governed by the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_International_Federation" title="Rugby League International Federation">Rugby League International Federation</a>. The first <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> was held in France in 1954; the current holders are <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_rugby_league_team" title="Australia national rugby league team">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Rugby league football takes its name from the bodies that split to create a new form of rugby, distinct from that run by the Rugby <a href="/wiki/Football" title="Football">Football</a> Unions, in Britain, Australia and New Zealand between 1895 and 1908.\n</p><p>The first of these, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Rugby_Football_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Rugby Football Union">Northern Rugby Football Union</a>, was established in 1895 as a breakaway faction of England\'s <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a> (RFU). Both organisations played the game under the same rules at first, although the Northern Union began to modify rules almost immediately, thus creating a new faster, stronger paced form of rugby football. Similar breakaway factions split from RFU-affiliated unions in Australia and New Zealand in 1907 and 1908, renaming themselves "rugby football <i>league</i>s" and introducing Northern Union rules.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 1922, the Northern Union also changed its name to the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Rugby Football League</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and thus over time the sport itself became known as "rugby league" football.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a football resulted in the formation of the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Northern Rugby Football Union</a> (NRFU).<sup id="cite_ref-begin_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-begin-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Although many factors played a part in the split, including the success of <a href="/wiki/Working_class" title="Working class">working class</a> northern teams, the main division was caused by the RFU decision to enforce the amateur principle of the sport, preventing "broken time payments" to players who had taken time off work to play rugby. Northern teams typically had more working class players (coal miners, mill workers etc.) who could not afford to play without this compensation, in contrast to affluent southern teams who had other sources of income to sustain the amateur principle.<sup id="cite_ref-Tony_Collins_2006_p.3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tony_Collins_2006_p.3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1895, a decree by the RFU banning the playing of rugby at grounds where entrance fees were charged led to twenty-two clubs (including Stockport who negotiated by telephone) meeting at the <a href="/wiki/George_Hotel,_Huddersfield" title="George Hotel, Huddersfield">George Hotel, Huddersfield</a> on 29 August 1895 and forming the "Northern Rugby Football Union".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Within fifteen years of that first meeting in Huddersfield, more than 200 RFU clubs had left to join the rugby revolution.\n</p><p>In 1897, the <a href="/wiki/Line-out_(rugby_union)" title="Line-out (rugby union)">line-out</a> was abolished<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and in 1898 <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports" title="Professional sports">professionalism</a> introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 1906, the Northern Union changed its rules, reducing teams from 15 to 13 a side and replacing the <a href="/wiki/Playing_rugby_union#Ruck" class="mw-redirect" title="Playing rugby union">ruck</a> formed after every tackle with the <a href="/wiki/Play_the_ball" class="mw-redirect" title="Play the ball">play the ball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A similar schism to that which occurred in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. There, on 8 August 1907 the <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_Rugby_Football_League" class="mw-redirect" title="New South Wales Rugby Football League">New South Wales Rugby Football League</a> was founded at Bateman\'s Hotel in George Street.<sup id="cite_ref-baker_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baker-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Rugby league then went on to displace rugby union as the primary football code in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 5 May 1954 over 100,000 (official figure 102,569) spectators watched the <a href="/wiki/1954_Challenge_Cup" title="1954 Challenge Cup">1953\xe2\x80\x9354</a> <a href="/wiki/Challenge_Cup" title="Challenge Cup">Challenge Cup</a> Final at <a href="/wiki/Odsal_Stadium" title="Odsal Stadium">Odsal Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, setting a new record for attendance at a rugby football match of either code.<sup id="cite_ref-baker_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-baker-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Also in 1954 the Rugby League World Cup, the first for either code of rugby, was formed at the instigation of the French. In 1966, the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_International_Federation" title="Rugby League International Federation">International Board</a> introduced a rule that a team in possession was allowed three play-the-balls and on the fourth tackle a scrum was to be formed. This was increased to six tackles in 1972 and in 1983 the scrum was replaced by a handover.<sup id="cite_ref-collins2006_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-collins2006-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> 1967 saw the first professional Sunday matches of rugby league played.\n</p><p>The first sponsors, <a href="/wiki/Tetley%27s_Brewery" title="Tetley&#39;s Brewery">Joshua Tetley</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Player" class="mw-redirect" title="John Player">John Player</a>, entered the game for the <a href="/wiki/1971%E2%80%9372_Northern_Rugby_Football_League_season" title="1971\xe2\x80\x9372 Northern Rugby Football League season">1971\xe2\x80\x9372 Northern Rugby Football League season</a>. Television would have an enormous impact on the sport of rugby league in the 1990s when <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Murdoch" title="Rupert Murdoch">Rupert Murdoch</a>\'s News Corporation sought worldwide broadcasting rights and refused to take no for an answer. The media giant\'s "Super League" movement saw big changes for the traditional administrators of the game. In Europe, it resulted in a move from a winter sport to a summer one as the new Super League competition tried to expand its market. In Australasia, the <a href="/wiki/Super_League_war" title="Super League war">Super League war</a> resulted in long and costly legal battles and changing loyalties, causing significant damage to the code in an extremely competitive sporting market. In 1997 two competitions were run alongside each other in Australia, after which a peace deal in the form of the National Rugby League was formed. The NRL has since become recognised as the sport\'s flagship competition and since that time has set record TV ratings and crowd figures.<sup id="cite_ref-rleague-attendances-1957-2010_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rleague-attendances-1957-2010-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Laws_of_the_game">Laws of the game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Laws of the game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_gameplay" title="Rugby league gameplay">Rugby league gameplay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laws_of_rugby_league" title="Laws of rugby league">Laws of rugby league</a></div>\n<p>The objective in rugby league is to score more points through <a href="/wiki/Try" title="Try">tries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goal_(sport)" title="Goal (sport)">goals</a> (also known as <a href="/wiki/Try#Conversion" title="Try">conversions</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Drop_goal_(rugby)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drop goal (rugby)">field goals</a> (also known as drop goals) than the opposition within the 80 minutes of play. If after two halves of play, each consisting of forty minutes, the two teams are <a href="/wiki/Tie_(draw)" title="Tie (draw)">drawing</a>, a draw may be declared, or the game may enter extra time under the <a href="/wiki/Golden_point" title="Golden point">golden point</a> rule, depending on the relevant competition\'s format.\n</p><p>The try is the most common form of scoring,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and a team will usually attempt to score one by running and kicking the ball further upfield or passing from player-to-player in order to manoeuvre around the opposition\'s defence. A try involves touching the ball to the ground on or beyond the defending team\'s goal-line and is worth four points. A goal is worth two points and may be gained from a conversion or a penalty. A field goal, or drop goal, is only worth one point and is gained by dropping and then kicking the ball on the <a href="/wiki/Drop_kick" title="Drop kick">half volley</a> between the uprights in open play.\n</p><p>Field position is crucial in rugby league,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> achieved by running with or kicking the ball. Passing in rugby league may only be in a backward or sideways direction. Teammates, therefore, have to remain on-side by not moving ahead of the player with the ball. However the ball may be kicked ahead for teammates, but again, if they are in front of the kicker when the ball is kicked, they are deemed off-side. Tackling is a key component of rugby league play. Only the player holding the ball may be tackled. A tackle is complete, for example, when the player is held by one or more opposing players\nin such a manner that he can make no further progress and cannot part with the ball, or when the player is held by one or more opposing players and the ball or the hand or arm holding the ball comes into contact with the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> An attacking team gets a maximum of six tackles to progress up the field before possession is changed over. Once the tackle is completed, the ball-carrier must be allowed to get to his feet to \'<a href="/wiki/Play-the-ball" class="mw-redirect" title="Play-the-ball">play-the-ball</a>\'. Ball control is also important in rugby league, as a fumble of the ball on the ground forces a handover, unless the ball is fumbled backwards. The ball can also be turned over by going over the sideline.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Positions">Positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg/300px-Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg" width="300" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg/450px-Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg/600px-Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leeds_Rhinos1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Leeds_Rhinos" title="Leeds Rhinos">Leeds</a> playing at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a> friendly against <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Trinity" title="Wakefield Trinity">Wakefield Trinity</a> at <a href="/wiki/Headingley_Rugby_Stadium" title="Headingley Rugby Stadium">Headingley</a></div></div></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_positions" title="Rugby league positions">Rugby league positions</a></div>\n<p>Players on the pitch are divided into <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_positions#Forwards" title="Rugby league positions">forwards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_positions#Backs" title="Rugby league positions">backs</a>, although the game\'s rules apply to all players the same way. Each position has a designated number to identify himself from other players. These numbers help to identify which position a person is playing. The system of numbering players is different depending on which country the match is played in. In Australia and New Zealand, each player is usually given a number corresponding to their playing position on the field. However, since 1996 European teams have been able to grant players specific squad numbers,  which they keep without regard to the position they play, similarly to <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SLnumbers_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SLnumbers-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Substitutes (generally referred to as "the bench") are allowed in the sport, and are typically used when a player gets <a href="/wiki/Fatigue_(medical)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fatigue (medical)">tired</a> or <a href="/wiki/Injury" title="Injury">injured</a>, although they can also be used tactically. Each team is currently allowed four substitutes, and in Australia and New Zealand, these players occupy shirt numbers 14 to 22.<sup id="cite_ref-SLnumbers2_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SLnumbers2-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> There are no limitations on which players must occupy these interchangeable slots. Generally, twelve interchanges are allowed in any game from each team, although in the National Rugby League, this was reduced to ten prior to the 2008 season<sup id="cite_ref-NRLtensubs_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRLtensubs-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and further reduced to eight prior to the 2016 season. If a team has to interchange a player due to the <a href="/wiki/Blood_replacement" title="Blood replacement">blood bin</a> rule or due to injury, and this was the result of misconduct from the opposing team, the compromised team does not have to use one of its allocated interchanges to take the player in question off the field.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Backs">Backs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Backs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The backs are generally smaller, faster and more agile than the forwards. They are often the most creative and evasive players on the field, relying on running, kicking and handling skills, as well as tactics and set plays, to break the defensive line, instead of brute force. Generally forwards do the majority of the work (hit-ups/tackling).\n</p>\n<ul><li>The title of <i><a href="/wiki/Fullback_(rugby_league)" title="Fullback (rugby league)">fullback</a></i> (numbered <b>1</b>) comes from the fullback\'s defensive position where the player drops out of the defensive line to cover the rear from kicks and runners breaking the line. They therefore usually are good ball catchers and clinical tacklers. In attack, the fullback will typically make runs into the attack or support a runner in anticipation of a pass out of the tackle. Fullbacks can play a role in attack similar to a halfback or five-eighth and the fact that the fullback does not have to defend in the first defensive line means that a coach can keep a playmaker from the tackling responsibilities of the first line whilst allowing them to retain their attacking role.</li>\n<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_positions#Wingers" title="Rugby league positions">wingers</a></i> (numbered <b>2</b> and <b>5</b>) are normally the fastest players in a team and play on the far left and right fringes of the field (the wings). Their main task is to receive passes and score tries. The wingers also drop back on the last tackle to cover the left and right sides of the field for kicks while the fullback covers the middle.</li>\n<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Center_(rugby)" class="mw-redirect" title="Center (rugby)">centres</a></i> (numbered <b>3</b> and <b>4</b>) are positioned one in from the wings and together complete what is known as the three-quarter line. Usually the best mixture of power and vision, their main role is to try to create attacking opportunities for their team and defend against those of the opposition. Along with the wingers, the centres score plenty of tries throughout a season. They usually have a large build and therefore can often play in the <a href="/wiki/Second-row_forward" class="mw-redirect" title="Second-row forward">second row forwards</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>Usually, the stand-off/five-eighth and scrum-half/half-back are a team\'s creative unit or \'playmakers\'. During the interactions between a team\'s \'key\' players (five-eighth, half-back, fullback, lock forward, and hooker), the five-eighth and half-back will usually be involved in most passing moves. These two positions are commonly called the "halves".\n</p>\n<ul><li>The stand-off half, or <a href="/wiki/Five-eighth" title="Five-eighth">five-eighth</a> (numbered <b>6</b>): There is not much difference between the stand-off half and the scrum half (<a href="/wiki/Scrum-half_(rugby_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Scrum-half (rugby league)">halfback</a>), in that both players may operate in front of the pack during \'forward play\' (as prime receiver [<b>7</b>] and shadow receiver [<b>6</b>], one on each side of the ruck, or both on same side of the ruck), and both players may operate in front of the backs during \'back play\' (as prime pivot [<b>6</b>] and shadow pivot [<b>7</b>], one on each side of the pack, or both on same side of the ruck / pack). The Five-Eighth position is named with regard to the distance that the player stands in relevance to the team.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Scrum-half_(rugby_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Scrum-half (rugby league)">halfback</a> (numbered <b>7</b>): There is not much difference between the halfback and the five-eighth, in that both players may operate in front of the pack during \'forward play\' (as prime receiver [<b>7</b>] and shadow receiver [<b>6</b>], one on each side of the ruck, or both on same side of the ruck). Both players may operate in front of the backs during \'back play\' (as prime pivot [<b>6</b>] and shadow pivot [<b>7</b>], one on each side of the ruck/pack, or both on same side of the ruck/pack). The halfback position is named with regard to halfway between the fullback and the forwards.</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Forwards">Forwards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Forwards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ftwins.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Ftwins.jpg/300px-Ftwins.jpg" width="300" height="195" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Ftwins.jpg/450px-Ftwins.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Ftwins.jpg/600px-Ftwins.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1231" data-file-height="802" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ftwins.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rugby league is noted for its hard physical play</div></div></div>\n<p>The forwards\' two responsibilities can be broken into "normal play" and "scrum play". For information on a forward\'s role in the scrum see <a href="/wiki/Scrum_(rugby)#Rugby_league" title="Scrum (rugby)">rugby league scrummage</a>. Forward positions are traditionally named after the player\'s position in the scrum yet are equal with respect to "normal play" with the exception of the hooker. Forward positions are traditionally assigned as follows:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Prop_forward" class="mw-redirect" title="Prop forward">props or front-row forwards</a></i> (numbered <b>8</b> and <b>10</b>) are normally the largest players on field. They are positioned in the centre of the line. The prop will be an "enforcer", dissuading the opposition from attacking the centre of the defensive line and, in attack, will give the team momentum by taking the ball up to the defence aggressively.</li>\n<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Hooker_(rugby_league)" title="Hooker (rugby league)">hooker</a></i> (numbered <b>9</b>) is most likely to play the role of <a href="/wiki/Dummy_half" class="mw-redirect" title="Dummy half">dummy-half</a>. In defence the hooker usually defends in the middle of the line against the opposition\'s props and second-rowers. The hooker will be responsible for organising the defence in the middle of the field. In attack as dummy-half this player is responsible for starting the play from every play-the-ball by either passing the ball to the right player, or, at opportune moments, running from dummy-half. It is vital that the hooker can pass very well. Traditionally, hookers "hooked" the ball in the scrum. Hookers also make probably more tackles than any other player on the field. The hooker is always involved in the play and needs to be very fit. They need to have a very good knowledge of the game and the players around them.</li>\n<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Second-row_forward" class="mw-redirect" title="Second-row forward">second row forwards</a></i> (numbered <b>11</b> and <b>12</b>) The modern day second row is very similar to a centre and is expected to be faster, more mobile and have more skills than the prop and will play amongst the three-quarters, providing strength in attack and defence when the ball is passed out to the wings. Good second-rowers combine the skills and responsibilities of props and centres in the course of the game.</li>\n<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Lock_(rugby_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lock (rugby league)">Loose forward or Lock</a></i> (numbered <b>13</b>) is the only forward in the third (last) row of the scrum. They are usually among the fittest players on the field, covering the entire field on both attacking and defending duties. Typically they are big ball-runners who can occasionally slot in as a passing link or kick option; it is not uncommon for locks to have the skills of a <a href="/wiki/Five-eighth" title="Five-eighth">five-eighth</a> and to play a similar role in the team.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_league_worldwide">Rugby league worldwide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Rugby league worldwide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_rugby_league_competitions" title="List of rugby league competitions">List of rugby league competitions</a></div>\n<p>Rugby league is played in over 70 nations throughout the world, Australia, Canada, England, France, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea and Wales have teams that play at a professional level while the rest are completely amateur, 36 are ranked by the RLIF and a further 32 are officially recognized and unranked.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The strongest rugby league nations are <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_rugby_league_team" title="Australia national rugby league team">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_league_team" title="New Zealand national rugby league team">New Zealand</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> is the highest form of representative rugby league and currently features 14 teams. Those which have contested World Cups are; <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_rugby_league_team" title="Australia national rugby league team">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_league_team" title="New Zealand national rugby league team">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_league_team" title="France national rugby league team">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji_national_rugby_league_team" title="Fiji national rugby league team">Fiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_league_team" title="Wales national rugby league team">Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea_national_rugby_league_team" title="Papua New Guinea national rugby league team">Papua New Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa_national_rugby_league_team" title="Samoa national rugby league team">Samoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_rugby_league_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Ireland national rugby league team">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_national_rugby_league_team" title="United States national rugby league team">USA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_league_team" title="Scotland national rugby league team">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_rugby_league_team" title="Italy national rugby league team">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tonga_national_rugby_league_team" title="Tonga national rugby league team">Tonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands_national_rugby_league_team" title="Cook Islands national rugby league team">Cook Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon_national_rugby_league_team" title="Lebanon national rugby league team">Lebanon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_rugby_league_team" title="Russia national rugby league team">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_rugby_league_team" title="South Africa national rugby league team">South Africa</a>. The current World Champions are Australia, who won the <a href="/wiki/2017_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2017 Rugby League World Cup">2017 Rugby League World Cup</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oceania_and_South_Pacific">Oceania and South Pacific</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Oceania and South Pacific">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Rugby_League_Confederation" title="Asia-Pacific Rugby League Confederation">Asia-Pacific Rugby League Confederation</a>\'s purpose is to spread the sport of rugby league throughout their region along with other governing bodies such as the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Rugby_League_Commission" title="Australian Rugby League Commission">ARL</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Rugby_League" title="New Zealand Rugby League">NZRL</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Since rugby league was introduced to Australia in 1908,\nit has become the largest television sport and 3rd most attended sport in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_Papua_New_Guinea" title="Rugby league in Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> is one of two countries to have rugby league as its <a class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_European_Federation" title="Rugby League European Federation">Rugby League European Federation</a> are responsible for developing rugby league in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In England, rugby league has traditionally been associated with the northern counties of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a> where the game originated, especially in towns along the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 corridor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-heartlands_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heartlands-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Its popularity has also increased elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Currently, only one of the twelve <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> teams is based outside of these traditional counties: <a href="/wiki/Catalans_Dragons" title="Catalans Dragons">Catalans Dragons</a> (<a href="/wiki/Perpignan" title="Perpignan">Perpignan</a>). Two other teams from outside the United Kingdom, the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Wolfpack" title="Toronto Wolfpack">Toronto Wolfpack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_Olympique" title="Toulouse Olympique">Toulouse Olympique</a>, also compete in the English Rugby League system. Both teams will play in the Rugby League Championship in 2018. \n</p><p>Super League average attendances are in the 8 to 9,500 range. The average Super League match attendance in 2014 was 8,365.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Ranked the <a href="/wiki/Sport_in_the_United_Kingdom#Popularity" title="Sport in the United Kingdom">eighth most popular sport in the UK</a> overall,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> rugby league is the 27th most popular participation sport in England according to figures released by <a href="/wiki/Sport_England" title="Sport England">Sport England</a>; the total number of rugby league participants in England aged 16 and over was 44,900 in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Participation_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Participation-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> This is a 39% drop from 10 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-Participation_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Participation-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> While the sport is largely concentrated in the north of England there have been complaints about its lack of profile in the British media. the collapse of the Vichy regime, although it was still actively marginalised by the French authorities until the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCFranceHistory_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCFranceHistory-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, the national side appeared in the finals of the 1954 and 1968 World Cups, and the country hosted the 1954 event.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> In 1996, a French team, <a The success of the Dragons in Super League has initiated a renaissance in French rugby league, with new-found enthusiasm for the sport in the south of the country where most of the <a href="/wiki/Elite_One_Championship" title="Elite One Championship">Elite One Championship</a> teams are based. In other parts of Europe, the game is played at semi-professional and amateur level.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_league&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Wolfpack" title="Toronto Wolfpack">Toronto Wolfpack</a> are North America\xe2\x80\x99s only professional Rugby League team, competing in the English Rugby League system. The Wolfpack won the 2017 Kingstone Press League 1 in their inaugural season and earned promotion to the 2018 Rugby League Championship. The Wolfpack play their home games at Lamport Stadium in Toronto.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_professional_competitions">Domestic professional competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a two most prominent full-time professional leagues are the <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasian</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Rugby_League" title="National Rugby League">National Rugby League</a> and the European <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> and to a lesser extent the semi professional French <a href="/wiki/Elite_One_Championship" Domestic leagues, with some full-time exceptions, exist at a semi-professional level below the NRL and Super League, in Australia the <a href="/wiki/Queensland_Cup" title="Queensland Cup">Queensland Cup</a> (which includes a team from Papua New Guinea) and <a href="/wiki/NSW_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="NSW Cup">NSW Cup</a>, which provides players to various NRL teams. In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> below Super League is the <a href="/wiki/Championship_(rugby_league)" title="Championship (rugby league)">Championship</a> and <a href="/wiki/Championship_1" class="mw-redirect" title="Championship 1">Championship 1</a> (the professional system includes 2 <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> teams, 2 <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> and 1 <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> team). \nThe <a href="/wiki/PNGNRL" class="mw-redirect" title="PNGNRL">Papua New Guinea National Rugby League</a> operates as a semi-professional competition as does the <a href="/wiki/USA_Rugby_League" title="USA Rugby League">USA Rugby League</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> runs semi-professional clubs to some extent in providing supported accommodation for foreign players.
