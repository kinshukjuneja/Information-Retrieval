template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Formula One World Constructors\' Championship (WCC)</b> is awarded by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="F\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> to the most successful <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_constructors" title="List of Formula One constructors">constructor</a> over a season, as determined by a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points system</a> based on <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Grands_Prix" title="List of Formula One Grands Prix">Grand Prix</a> results. The Constructors\' Championship was first awarded, as the <b>International Cup for F1 Manufacturers</b>, in <a href="/wiki/1958_Formula_One_season" title="1958 Formula One season">1958</a> to <a href="/wiki/Vanwall" title="Vanwall">Vanwall</a>.\n</p><p>Constructors\' Championship points are calculated by adding points scored in each race by any driver for that constructor. Up until <a href="/wiki/1979_Formula_One_season" title="1979 Formula One season">1979</a>, most seasons saw only the highest-scoring driver in each race for each constructor contributing points towards the Championship. On only ten occasions has the World Constructors\' Champion team not contained the World Drivers\' Champion for that season.\n</p><p>In the 58 seasons the Championship has been awarded, only 15 different constructors have won it, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> the most successful, with 16 titles including 6 consecutive from <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a> to <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2004 Formula One World Championship">2004</a>. Only five countries have produced winning constructors: <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (33 championships with 10 different constructors), <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> (16 with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>), <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> (4 with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>), <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (3 with two constructors) and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> (4 with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>). However, all Austrian, French and German titles have seen the winning cars designed and built (except <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a> in <a href="/wiki/1969_Formula_One_season" title="1969 Formula One season">1969</a>) and run by teams based in the United Kingdom. Among drivers that have contributed with at least a single point to the constructors\' title, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> has the unofficial record, having been involved with seven such titles, six of those consecutively with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher won the world drivers\' title on six of those seven occasions.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button"
