class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to class="mw-empty-elt">\n\n</p>\n<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Literature_Wales_logo.png" class="image"><img alt="Literature Wales logo.png" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/7c/Literature_Wales_logo.png" width="320" height="106" data-file-width="320" data-file-height="106" /></a></div>\n<p><b>Literature Wales</b> is the Welsh national literature promotion agency and society of writers, existing to promote <a href="/wiki/Welsh-language_literature" title="Welsh-language literature">Welsh-language</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_literature_in_English" title="Welsh literature in English">English-language</a> literature in Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Literature Wales publishes a <a href="/wiki/Literary_magazine" title="Literary magazine">literary magazine</a> called <i>Taliesin</i> and a bilingual listings magazine called <i>A470</i>. It offers bursaries for writing projects, runs literary events and lectures, and provides financial assistance for creative mentoring and other literary-based ventures. The organisation also selects the <a href="/wiki/National_Poet_for_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="National Poet for Wales">National Poet for Wales</a>, and manages competitions including <a href="/wiki/Wales_Book_of_the_Year" title="Wales Book of the Year">Wales Book of the Year</a>, the Cardiff International Poetry Competition, and the <a href="/wiki/Rhys_Davies_(writer)" title="Rhys Davies (writer)">Rhys Davies</a> Short Story Competition.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Literature_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Ty_N.jpg" class="image" title="T\xc5\xb7 Newydd Writing Centre"><img alt="T\xc5\xb7 Newydd Writing Centre" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Ty_N.jpg/200px-Ty_N.jpg" width="200" height="134" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Ty_N.jpg/300px-Ty_N.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Ty_N.jpg/400px-Ty_N.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a></div>\n<p>The Academi was initially formed as a <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh language</a> society in 1959, following a public discussion between <a href="/wiki/Bobi_Jones" title="Bobi Jones">Robert Maynard Jones</a> and poet <a href="/wiki/Waldo_Williams" title="Waldo Williams">Waldo Williams</a>. The Academi attracted notable Welsh writers and academics into its society from the start, and while a Welsh language-only publication, had as its chairmen <a href="/wiki/Iorwerth_Peate" title="Iorwerth Peate">Iorwerth Peate</a> (1959\xe2\x80\x9363), <a href="/wiki/T._J._Morgan" title="T. J. Morgan">T. J. Morgan</a> (1963\xe2\x80\x9366) and <a href="/wiki/J._E._Caerwyn_Williams" title="J. E. Caerwyn Williams">J. E. Caerwyn Williams</a> (1966\xe2\x80\x931975).<sup id="cite_ref-CttLoW_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CttLoW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> An English language section was added in 1968 through the initiative of editor and linguist <a href="/wiki/Meic_Stephens" title="Meic Stephens">Meic Stephens</a> in his then capacity as literature director of the <a href="/wiki/Arts_Council_of_Wales" title="Arts Council of Wales">Welsh Arts Council</a>. The first chairman of the English language section was <a href="/wiki/Glyn_Jones_(Welsh_writer)" title="Glyn Jones (Welsh writer)">Glyn Jones</a> who held the post until 1973. He was followed by <a href="/wiki/Roland_Mathias" title="Roland Mathias">Roland Mathias</a> (1973\xe2\x80\x9378), <a href="/wiki/Gwyn_Jones_(physicist)" title="Gwyn Jones (physicist)">G.O. Jones</a> (1978\xe2\x80\x9381), Sam Adams (1981\xe2\x80\x9384), <a href="/wiki/Tony_Curtis_(Welsh_poet)" title="Tony Curtis (Welsh poet)">Tony Curtis</a> (1984\xe2\x80\x9387), <a href="/wiki/Gillian_Clarke" title="Gillian Clarke">Gillian Clarke</a> (1987\xe2\x80\x931993) and <a href="/wiki/Sally_Roberts_Jones" title="Sally Roberts Jones">Sally Roberts Jones</a> (1993\xe2\x80\x931997). The Welsh language chairmen after J. E. Caerwyn Williams were R.M. Jones (1975\xe2\x80\x9378), Alun Llywellyn-Williams (1978\xe2\x80\x9382), <a href="/w/index.php?title=R._Gerallt_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="R. Gerallt Jones (page does not exist)">R. Gerallt Jones</a> (1982\xe2\x80\x9387), <a href="/wiki/R._Geraint_Gruffydd" title="R. Geraint Gruffydd">R. Geraint Gruffydd</a> (1987\xe2\x80\x9390), <a href="/wiki/Gwenlyn_Parry" title="Gwenlyn Parry">Gwenlyn Parry</a> (1990\xe2\x80\x9391), <a href="/wiki/Harri_Pritchard_Jones" class="mw-redirect" title="Harri Pritchard Jones">Harri Pritchard Jones</a> (1991\xe2\x80\x9396) and Nesta Wyn Jones (1996\xe2\x80\x9398).<sup id="cite_ref-CttLoW_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CttLoW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Academi has been constitutionally independent since 1978 and took on an enlarged role in 1998 when it was reformed as a single organisation operating through two languages and won the <a href="/wiki/Arts_Council_of_Wales" title="Arts Council of Wales">Arts Council of Wales</a>\' franchise to provide the national literature support service for Wales.\n</p><p>On 1 April 2011, the Academi changed its title to Literature Wales, simultaneously merging with the writers\' centre <a href="/wiki/T%C5%B7_Newydd" title="T\xc5\xb7 Newydd">T\xc5\xb7 Newydd</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Literature_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Academi_at_WMC.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Academi_at_WMC.jpg/220px-Academi_at_WMC.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Academi_at_WMC.jpg/330px-Academi_at_WMC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Academi_at_WMC.jpg/440px-Academi_at_WMC.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2816" data-file-height="2112" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Academi_at_WMC.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Literature Wales (previously the Academi at the Wales Millennium Centre</div></div></div>\n<p>Literature Wales operates the "Writers on Tour" scheme, which facilitates and helps to fund the placement of writers in schools, interest groups and organisations across Wales for literary lectures, discussions and workshops. It also administers the <a href="/wiki/National_Poet_for_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="National Poet for Wales">National Poet for Wales</a> post (<a href="/wiki/Gwyneth_Lewis" title="Gwyneth Lewis">Gwyneth Lewis</a> \xe2\x80\x93 2005, <a href="/wiki/Gwyn_Thomas_(poet)" title="Gwyn Thomas (poet)">Gwyn Thomas</a> \xe2\x80\x93 2006), <i>Bardd Plant Cymru</i> (<a href="/wiki/Gwyneth_Glyn" title="Gwyneth Glyn">Gwyneth Glyn</a>); the Welsh-language Children\'s Laureate joint scheme between <a href="/wiki/S4C" title="S4C">S4C</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Books_Council" title="Welsh Books Council">Welsh Books Council</a>, Literature Wales and <a href="/wiki/Urdd_Gobaith_Cymru" title="Urdd Gobaith Cymru">Urdd Gobaith Cymru</a>, and in 2005, administered the Cardiff Capital Poet project (<a href="/wiki/Gillian_Clarke" title="Gillian Clarke">Gillian Clarke</a>). Managed by Literature Wales, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Glyn_Jones_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Glyn Jones Centre (page does not exist)">Glyn Jones Centre</a>, based in the <a href="/wiki/Wales_Millennium_Centre" title="Wales Millennium Centre">Wales Millennium Centre</a>, provides a space for visitors and writers to gain and exchange information on the practice, publication and promotion of writing. The centre houses a large collection of Welsh-interest literature. \n</p><p>Following a <a href="/wiki/National_Lottery_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Lottery (United Kingdom)">lottery grant</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Arts_Council_of_Wales" title="Arts Council of Wales">Arts Council of Wales</a>, Literature Wales produced The Welsh Academy Encyclopaedia of Wales (published by <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wales_Press" title="University of Wales Press">University of Wales Press</a> in 2008). The encyclopaedia was published in both Welsh and English and aims to combine the coverage of a <a href="/wiki/Gazetteer" title="Gazetteer">gazetteer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biographical_dictionary" title="Biographical dictionary">biographical dictionary</a> with the authority of a historical dictionary and the utility of a <a href="/wiki/Guide_book" title="Guide book">guide book</a>.\n</p><p>Literature Wales\'s website provides a service as a guide to literary activity in Wales and beyond, including a database of all known writers, funding bodies, writers\xe2\x80\x99 groups, publishers and periodicals currently active in Wales, listings of current poetry and story writing competitions and details of opportunities for writers. It also offers advice on getting published and publishing in general, as well as providing information on the other literature bodies and organisations in Wales.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Literature_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The organisation of the awards presentation of the Academi\'s Wales Book of the Year competition 2008 was criticised after then Culture Minister <a href="/wiki/Rhodri_Glyn_Thomas" title="Rhodri Glyn Thomas">Rhodri Glyn Thomas</a> announced the wrong winner, although it was a simple mistake.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  \n</p><p>Literature Wales has campaigned against the Welsh Journals Online project of the <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Wales" title="National Library of Wales">National Library of Wales</a> because it argues that authors should be paid in return for permission to allow their material to be digitised.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Literature_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">registered charity</a>, Literature Wales is funded from public sources (largely the Arts Council of Wales, local authorities and a number of trusts), from members\' subscriptions and to a lesser degree, from trading and literary activity. Its main offices are in Cardiff, sited at the Wales Millennium Centre and Mount Stuart Square, and there is also a small office based at Ty Newydd in <a href="/wiki/Llanystumdwy" title="Llanystumdwy">Llanystumdwy</a> in north Wales.     \n</p><p>Literature Wales\'s chief executive is Lleucu Siencyn<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> who replaces the previous chief executive, the poet and author <a href="/wiki/Peter_Finch_(poet)" title="Peter Finch (poet)">Peter Finch</a>. The organisation has a staff of twelve, ten of whom are based in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> with the other two employed as literature field-workers in north Wales. \n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Literature_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.literaturewales.org/">Official website</a></span>\n</li>\n<li id="cite_note-CttLoW-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-CttLoW_2-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-CttLoW_2-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book"><a href="/wiki/Meic_Stephens" title="Meic Stephens">Stephens, Meic</a>, ed.
