It has taken place in several locations around the world. The first conference, then known as <b>TechEd</b>, happened in 1993 in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, the last took place in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> in 2014. Microsoft altered its conference schedule and introduced the <b>Microsoft Ignite</b> name from 2015 on.\n</p><p>The conference normally lasts between three and five days, and consists of presentation and whiteboard sessions and hands-on labs. It offers opportunities to meet Microsoft experts, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Most_Valuable_Professional" title="Microsoft Most Valuable Professional">MVPs</a> and community members. Networking is enhanced through parties, community areas and "Ask the Expert" sessions. The event also includes an exhibition area where vendors can show off technologies and sell products. There is a vast content catalog from which attendees can select sessions that will be most beneficial. An agenda is published online before the conference begins.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" <a href="/wiki/Sun_City,_North_West" title="Sun City, North West">Sun City</a>, after which it was moved to the <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a> International Conference Centre (ICC) class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Each year for the European event changes to a new location.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Microsoft TechEd India has been an annual fixture since 2003 except for 2008 which was the only year when Microsoft TechEd India event was not organized.\n</p><p>In 2006, TechEd was held in New Delhi, Pune, Mumbai, Chennai, Hyderabad and Bangalore during the month of June. There were two TechEd events held in each city, the first one delved into architectural aspects of technology and laid emphasis on the needs of the Architect audience alongside four tracks focused on Platform and Tools for Developers. The second event was geared towards technology professionals working on IT infrastructure.\n</p><p>In 2007, TechEd was held in Bangalore.\t\n</p><p>In 2009, TechEd was held in Hyderabad in the month of May. Rechristened Microsoft TechMela, the name change was necessitated by an urge to Indianize the event and its content so that it aligned with the ethos of every Indian. It was attended by over 2,500 CXOs, TDMs, BDMs, Developers, IT Professionals, Architects, Designers, faculty Members and Technology Students.\n</p><p>TechEd-India 2009 was keynoted by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Ballmer" title="Steve Ballmer">Steve Ballmer</a>, the only TechEd event worldwide where Steve Ballmer was to speak. There were 28 Technical Tracks that spanned more than 112 breakout sessions, 24 instructor-led Labs, more than 20 Chalk-Talk sessions. The tracks were designed as a navigational tool which assisted in finding the sessions and labs best suited for individual needs.\n</p><p>In 2011, TechEd India was held in Bangalore from March 23\xe2\x80\x9325 at Hotel Lalit Ashok. The event included keynotes by Senior Microsoft Executives, breakout sessions, hands-on-labs, instructor-led labs, product team tents, free Microsoft certification, community programs and solution expo. The event was keynoted by <a href="/wiki/Qi_Lu_(computer_scientist)" title="Qi Lu (computer scientist)">Qi Lu</a>.\n</p><p>In 2012, 2013, and 2014, Tech Ed India was held in Bangalore at The LaLiT Hotels, Palaces and Resorts.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Israel">Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>TechEd conferences in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> were held in <a href="/wiki/Eilat" title="Eilat">Eilat</a>. The first TechEd conference was held in 2001, with more than 1,000 participants. Six more conferences were held until 2010.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_America">Latin America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Latin America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The Latin American TechEd has always been held in S\xc3\xa3o Paulo, Brazil. But there were an edition of it in Mexico in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_East">Middle East</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Middle East">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>First started in 2010 and hosted in Dubai.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>TechEd New Zealand is the largest IT Conference in New Zealand and is held annually in Auckland.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Each year for the North American event changes to a new location.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TechEd_Online">TechEd Online</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: TechEd Online">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>TechEd Online was an online supplement to the in-person events, which contained video interviews recorded at the event, videos of sessions and keynotes, blogs and speaker blog aggregation. Much of this content can now be found on Microsoft\'s <a href="/wiki/Channel_9_(Microsoft)" title="Channel 9 (Microsoft)">Channel 9</a>.\n</p><p>For TechEd North America, video recordings, session slide decks, and supplemental content can be found on myTechEd.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="myTechEd">myTechEd</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Ignite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: myTechEd">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Microsoft myTechEd is an online community and social networking tool where conference attendees can participate in technical discussions, access recordings of past year\'s sessions, and connect with speakers and other attendees through a directory. myTechEd is a free resource available for both in-person attendees and virtual attendees who are
