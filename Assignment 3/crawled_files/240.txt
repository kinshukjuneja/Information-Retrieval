The development, via <a href="/wiki/Plant_breeding" title="Plant breeding">plant breeding</a>, of new varieties is a major occupation of floriculturists.\n</p><p>Floriculture crops include bedding plants, <a href="/wiki/Houseplant" title="Houseplant">houseplants</a>, flowering garden and <a href="/wiki/Pot_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Pot plant">pot plants</a>, cut cultivated greens, and <a href="/wiki/Cut_flower" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut flower">cut flowers</a>. As distinguished from nursery crops, floriculture crops are generally <a href="/wiki/Herbaceous" class="mw-redirect" title="Herbaceous">herbaceous</a>. Bedding and garden plants consist of young flowering plants (annuals and <a href="/wiki/Perennial" class="mw-redirect" title="Perennial">perennials</a>) and vegetable plants. They are grown in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cell_pack&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cell pack (page does not exist)">cell packs</a> (in flats or trays), in pots, or in hanging baskets, usually inside a controlled environment, and sold largely for gardens and landscaping. <i><a href="/wiki/Pelargonium" title="Pelargonium">Pelargonium</a></i> ("geraniums"), <i><a href="/wiki/Impatiens" title="Impatiens">Impatiens</a></i> ("busy lizzies"), and <i><a href="/wiki/Petunia" title="Petunia">Petunia</a></i> are the best-selling bedding plants. The many <a href="/wiki/Cultivar" title="Cultivar">cultivars</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum" title="Chrysanthemum">Chrysanthemum</a></i> are the major perennial garden plant in the United States.\n</p><p>Flowering plants are largely sold in pots for indoor use. The major flowering plants are <a href="/wiki/Poinsettia" title="Poinsettia">poinsettias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orchid" class="mw-redirect" title="Orchid">orchids</a>, florist <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum" title="Chrysanthemum">chrysanthemums</a>, and finished florist <a href="/wiki/Azalea" title="Azalea">azaleas</a>. Foliage plants are also sold in pots and hanging baskets for indoor and patio use, including larger specimens for office, hotel, and restaurant interiors.\n</p><p>Cut flowers are usually sold in bunches or as bouquets with cut foliage. The production of cut flowers is specifically known as the <b>cut flower industry</b>. Farming flowers and foliage employs special aspects of floriculture, such as spacing, training and pruning plants for optimal flower harvest; and post-harvest treatment such as chemical treatments, storage, preservation and packaging. In Australia and the United States some species are harvested from the wild for the cut flower market.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox"
