scope="row">Population</th><td>\n0</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Samson</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw"><b>Enys Samson</b></i>) is the largest uninhabited island of the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, off the southwestern tip of the Cornish peninsula of Great Britain. It is 38 hectares (0.15&#160;sq&#160;mi) in size.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The island consists of two hills, North Hill and South Hill, which are connected by an <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a> on which the former inhabitants built many of their sturdy stone cottages.<sup id="cite_ref-Tourist_information_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tourist_information-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Samson was named after <a href="/wiki/Samson_of_Dol" title="Samson of Dol">Samson of Dol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="tocnumber">6</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The twin hills of Samson were formerly associated with <a href="/wiki/Breast#The_breast_as_an_anthropomorphic_geographic_feature" title="Breast">breasts</a>, in a similar way to the <a href="/wiki/Paps_of_Jura" title="Paps of Jura">Paps of Jura</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Paps_of_Anu" title="Paps of Anu">Paps of Anu</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. There are large ancient <a href="/wiki/Burial" title="Burial">burial</a> grounds both on the North Hill and South Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The island was inhabited until 1855, when the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Proprietor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Proprietor">Lord Proprietor</a> <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> removed the remaining population from the island.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> By this point, the population was found to be suffering from severe deprivation\xe2\x80\x94particularly due to a diet of <a href="/wiki/Limpet" title="Limpet">limpets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potatoes</a>\xe2\x80\x94and consisted of only two families: the Woodcocks and the Webbers. Smith then built a deer park on the island, but the deer escaped from their stone walled enclosure, and some attempted to wade across to Tresco (at low tide). In August 1933 a major fire occurred which was put out by the staff of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien-Smith" title="Arthur Algernon Dorrien-Smith">Major Dorrien-Smith</a>, by digging ditches to stop the spread.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In recent times, the area has become a protected wildlife site. The island houses many different birds such as <a href="/wiki/Tern" title="Tern">terns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gannets" class="mw-redirect" title="Gannets">gannets</a>, and many wild flowers. In 1971 the island, along with the nearby islands of Green Island, Puffin Island, Stony Island, and White Island, was designated a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> (SSSI) for their <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">biological</a> characteristics.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<ul><li>1669: one family<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>1715: only three men fit to carry arms</li>\n<li>1751: two households</li>\n<li>1794: six households</li>\n<li>1816: forty people</li>\n<li>1822: seven households (thirty-four people)</li>\n<li>1851: three households<sup id="cite_ref-census1851_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census1851-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>1855: <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> removed the remaining inhabitants which consisted of two families.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visiting_the_island">Visiting the island</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Visiting the island">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Boat trips to Samson are regularly available May through September. There is no <a href="/wiki/Quay" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay">quay</a>, so visitors disembark via wooden plank. The remains of the old cottages can be explored, and there are also the remains of Smith\'s deer park and prehistoric entrance graves.<sup id="cite_ref-megalithic_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-megalithic-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  There are no amenities or services available, but guided walks are led by local experts.<sup id="cite_ref-scillywalks_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scillywalks-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Literary_associations">Literary associations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Samson,_Isles_of_Scilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Literary associations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The island is featured in the <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_literature" title="Children&#39;s literature">children\'s story</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Why_the_Whales_Came" title="Why the Whales Came">Why the Whales Came</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Morpurgo" title="Michael Morpurgo">Michael Morpurgo</a>. In the book, Samson is under a curse that needs to be lifted.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The island also featured in <i>Armorel of Lyonesse</i> by <a href="/wiki/Walter_Besant" title="Walter Besant">Walter Besant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Many have made reference to Webber\'s Cottage on Samson as Armorel\'s house.<sup id="cite_ref-Tourist_information_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tourist_information-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Samson features in the fantasy novel \'Stone In The Blood\' by Colin Jordan &amp; David England.
