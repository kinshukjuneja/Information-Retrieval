class="mw-body-content">\n\t\t\t\t<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Commonwealth_of_Nations.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Commonwealth_of_Nations.svg/350px-Commonwealth_of_Nations.svg.png" members (green), former members (orange), and <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">British Overseas Territories</a> and Crown Dependencies (light blue)</div></div></div>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_association" title="Voluntary association">voluntary association</a> of 53 sovereign states. Nearly all of them are former <a href="/wiki/British_colony" class="mw-redirect" title="British colony">British colonies</a> or dependencies of those colonies.\n</p><p>No one government in the Commonwealth exercises power over the others, as is the case in a <a href="/wiki/Political_union" title="Political union">political union</a>. Rather, the Commonwealth is an <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">international organisation</a> in which countries with diverse social, political, and economic backgrounds are regarded as equal in status, and cooperate within a framework of common values and goals, as outlined in the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Declaration" title="Singapore Declaration">Singapore Declaration</a> issued in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Such common values and goals include the promotion of <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Good_governance" title="Good governance">good governance</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rule_of_law" title="Rule of law">rule of law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Individual_liberty" class="mw-redirect" title="Individual liberty">individual liberty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egalitarianism" title="Egalitarianism">egalitarianism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Free_trade" title="Free trade">free trade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Multilateralism" title="Multilateralism">multilateralism</a>, and <a href="/wiki/World_peace" title="World peace">world peace</a>, which are promoted through multilateral projects and meetings, such as the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>, held once every four years.<sup id="cite_ref-Singapore_Declaration_text_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Singapore_Declaration_text-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The symbol of this free association is <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, who serves as the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_the_Commonwealth" title="Head of the Commonwealth">Head of the Commonwealth</a>. This position, however, does not imbue her with any political or executive power over any Commonwealth member states; the position is purely symbolic, and it is the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Secretary-General" title="Commonwealth Secretary-General">Commonwealth Secretary-General</a> who is the chief executive of the Commonwealth.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Commonwealth was first officially formed in 1931 when the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster</a> gave legal recognition to the sovereignty of dominions. Known as the "British Commonwealth", the original members were the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_New_Zealand" title="Dominion of New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish Free State</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Newfoundland" title="Dominion of Newfoundland">Newfoundland</a>, although Australia and New Zealand did not adopt the statute until 1942 and 1947 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1949, the <a href="/wiki/London_Declaration" title="London Declaration">London Declaration</a> was signed and marked the birth of the modern Commonwealth and the adoption of its present name.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The newest member is <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, which joined on 29 November 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-rwanda_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rwanda-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The most recent departure was the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a>, which severed its connection with the Commonwealth on 13 October 2016.\n</p><p>As of April 2017, of the states that are members of the Commonwealth of Nations, three are in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, twelve in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, one in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, nineteen in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, seven in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, and eleven in <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. There are seven former members, four of which no longer exist as independent entities (but form part of current member states). The members have a combined population of 2.4 billion, almost a third of the world population, of whom 1.21 billion live in India, and 95% live in Asia and Africa combined.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook_population_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook_population-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Currently sixteen of the member states are <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realms</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_the_Commonwealth" title="Head of the Commonwealth">Head of the Commonwealth</a> as their head of state. Five others are <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">monarchies</a> with their own individual monarchs (<a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a>) and the rest are republics. <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> (from 1949), <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> (2003), and <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> (2016) are former members of the Commonwealth. <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">The Gambia</a> left and later rejoined the Commonwealth.\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<div id="toc"
