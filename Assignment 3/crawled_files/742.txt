Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>: television, radio, newspapers, magazines and websites. The country also has a strong <a href="/wiki/Music_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Music of the United Kingdom">music industry</a>. The United Kingdom has a diverse range of providers, the most prominent being the publicly-owned <a href="/wiki/Public_service_broadcaster" class="mw-redirect" title="Public service broadcaster">public service broadcaster</a>, the British Broadcasting Corporation (<a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>). The BBC\'s largest competitors are <a href="/wiki/ITV_plc" title="ITV plc">ITV plc</a>, which operates 13 of the 15 regional television broadcasters that make up the ITV Network, and <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a>, which holds a large stake in the satellite broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">Sky plc</a>. Regional media is covered by local radio, television and print newspapers. <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Mirror" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity Mirror">Trinity Mirror</a> operates 240 local and regional newspapers, as well as national newspapers such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Mirror" title="Sunday Mirror">Sunday class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mediacity_at_night_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mediacity_at_night_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/MediaCityUK" title="MediaCityUK">Mediacity</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>, privately funded and publicly backed by the BBC is the largest media-production facility in the United Kingdom.</div></div></div>\n<p>In 2009 it was estimated that individuals viewed a mean of 3.75 hours of television per day and listened to 2.81 hours of radio. The main BBC <a href="/wiki/Public_service_broadcasting_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Public service broadcasting in the United Kingdom">public service broadcasting</a> channels accounted for and estimated 28.4% of all television viewing; the three main independent channels accounted for 29.5% and the increasingly important other satellite and digital channels for the remaining 42.1%.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Sales of newspapers have fallen since the 1970s and in 2009 42% of people reported reading a daily national newspaper.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, 82.5% of the United Kingdom population were Internet users, the highest proportion amongst the 20 countries with the largest total number of users in that year.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017, <a href="/wiki/European_Broadcasting_Union" title="European Broadcasting Union">European Broadcasting Union</a> research found that people in the United Kingdom trusted the written press least of any European country, by a considerable margin. Trust in television and the radio was closer to the European average, though still below average.<sup id="cite_ref-pg-20170526_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg-20170526-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_media_centres_and_organisations">National media centres and organisations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National media centres and organisations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="London">London</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: London">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Media_in_London" title="Media in London">Media in London</a></div>\n<p>London dominates the media sector in the United Kingdom as national newspapers, television and radio networks are largely based there. Notable centres include <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Street" title="Fleet Street">Fleet Street</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_Broadcasting_House" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Broadcasting House">BBC Broadcasting House</a>. \n</p><p>Specialist local paper <a href="/wiki/City_A.M." title="City A.M.">City A.M.</a> is a free, business-focused newspaper published in print Monday to Friday. It is typically available from around 6<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am at London commuter stations and is handed out at key points in the City, Canary Wharf and other central London locations.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greater_Manchester">Greater Manchester</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Greater Manchester">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Media_in_Manchester" title="Media in Manchester">Media in Manchester</a></div>\n<p>Greater Manchester is also a significant national media hub. Notable centres include <a href="/wiki/MediaCityUK" title="MediaCityUK">MediaCityUK</a> a 200-acre (80ha) media production facility in <a href="/wiki/City_of_Salford" title="City of Salford">Salford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trafford" title="Trafford">Trafford</a>.\n</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> national newspaper was founded in Manchester in 1821, and was known as the <i>Manchester Guardian</i> until 1959. In the 1950s, coinciding with the growth in television, the <a href="/wiki/Granada_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Granada Television">Granada Television</a> franchise was set up by <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Bernstein,_Baron_Bernstein" title="Sidney Bernstein, Baron Bernstein">Sidney Bernstein</a>. Consequently, the <a href="/wiki/Granada_Studios" title="Granada Studios">Granada Studios</a> were the first purpose-built television studios in the United Kingdom. The franchise produced television programmes such as <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Up_Series" title="Up Series">Up Series</a></i>. The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> currently has two of its six major business divisions based here <a href="/wiki/BBC_North" title="BBC North">BBC North Group</a> that comprises a number of important departments including <a href="/wiki/BBC_Breakfast" title="BBC Breakfast">BBC Breakfast</a>, <a href="/wiki/CBBC" title="CBBC">BBC Children\'s</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC Sport</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_5_Live" title="BBC Radio 5 Live">BBC Radio 5</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_North_West" title="BBC North West">BBC North West</a>. The other division is <a href="/wiki/BBC_Future_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Future Media">BBC Future Media</a>. In addition <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> has two major divisions of its business based here <a href="/wiki/ITV_Studios" title="ITV Studios">ITV Studios</a> responsible for UK and international network production and <a href="/wiki/ITV_Granada" title="ITV Granada">ITV Granada</a> its regional service provider. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salford" title="University of Salford">University of Salford</a> also has a media campus and research center based at media city.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The United Kingdom is known for its large music industry, along with its new and upcoming artists. In the UK, media is spread through the forms of TV, newspapers, magazines, websites, and radio. \n</p><p>The <i>Daily Mirror</i> was founded in 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Sunday Mirror</i>, the sister paper to the <i>Daily Mirror</i>, was started in 1915.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Key_centres">Other Key centres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other Key centres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Edinburgh and Glasgow, and Cardiff, are important centres of newspaper and broadcasting production in Scotland and Wales respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The BBC, founded in 1922, is the United Kingdom\'s publicly funded radio, television and Internet broadcasting corporation, and is the oldest and largest broadcaster in the world. It operates numerous television and radio stations in the United Kingdom and abroad and its domestic services are funded by the <a href="/wiki/Television_licensing_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television licensing in the United Kingdom">television licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MediaNewsline_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MediaNewsline-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Other major players in the United Kingdom media include <a href="/wiki/ITV_plc" title="ITV plc">ITV plc</a>, which operates 11 of the 15 regional television broadcasters that make up the ITV Network,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation" title="News Corporation">News Corporation</a>, which owns a number of national newspapers through <a href="/wiki/News_International" class="mw-redirect" title="News International">News International</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i> and "<a href="/wiki/Broadsheet" title="Broadsheet">broadsheet</a>" <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> as well as holding a large stake in satellite broadcaster <a href="/wiki/British_Sky_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="British Sky Broadcasting">British Sky Broadcasting</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Print">Print</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Print">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The United Kingdom print publishing sector, including books, server, directories and databases, journals, magazines and business media, newspapers and news agencies, has a combined turnover of around \xc2\xa320 billion and employs around 167,000 people. The print media sector is entirely regulating itself and there are no specific statutory rules regulating the print media.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newspapers">Newspapers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Newspapers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/List_of_newspapers_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of newspapers in the United Kingdom">List of newspapers in the United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_British_newspapers" title="History of British newspapers">History of British newspapers</a></div>\n<p>Traditionally British newspapers have been divided into "quality", serious-minded newspapers (usually referred to as "<a href="/wiki/Broadsheet" title="Broadsheet">broadsheets</a>" because of their large size) and the more populist, "<a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a>" varieties. For convenience of reading many traditional broadsheets have switched to a more <a href="/wiki/Compact_(newspaper)" title="Compact (newspaper)">compact</a>-sized format, traditionally used by tabloids. In 2008 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i> had the highest circulation of any daily newspaper in the United Kingdom at 3.1&#160;million, approximately a quarter of the market.<sup id="cite_ref-paper_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Its sister paper, the <i><a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">News of the World</a></i>, had the highest circulation in the Sunday newspaper market,<sup id="cite_ref-paper_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  and traditionally focused on celebrity-led stories until its closure in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>, a centre-right broadsheet paper, is the highest-selling of the "quality" newspapers.<sup id="cite_ref-paper_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paper-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> While <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> is a centre-left "quality" broadsheet and the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i> is the main business newspaper, printed on distinctive salmon-pink broadsheet paper.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Mirror" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity Mirror">Trinity Mirror</a> operates 240 local and regional newspapers in the United Kingdom, as well as national newspapers such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Mirror" title="Sunday Mirror">Sunday Mirror</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Scotland has a distinct tradition of newspaper readership (see <a href="/wiki/List_of_newspapers_in_Scotland" title="List of newspapers in Scotland">list of newspapers in Scotland</a>). The tabloid <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Record_(Scotland)" title="Daily Record (Scotland)">Daily Record</a></i> has the highest circulation of any daily newspaper, outselling <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scottish_Sun" class="mw-redirect" title="The Scottish Sun">The Scottish Sun</a></i> by four to one, while its sister paper the <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Mail_(Scotland)" title="Sunday Mail (Scotland)">Sunday Mail</a></i> similarly leads the Sunday newspaper market. The leading "quality" daily newspaper in Scotland is <i><a href="/wiki/The_Herald_(Glasgow)" title="The Herald (Glasgow)">The Herald</a></i>, though it is the sister paper of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scotsman" title="The Scotsman">The Scotsman</a></i>, and the <i><a href="/wiki/Scotland_on_Sunday" title="Scotland on Sunday">Scotland on Sunday</a></i> that leads in the Sunday newspaper market.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In November 2014 a new newspaper was launched in Scotland called <i><a href="/wiki/The_National_(Scotland)" title="The National (Scotland)">The National</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Magazines">Magazines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Magazines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A large range of <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazines</a> are sold in the United Kingdom covering most interests and potential topics. British magazines and journals that have achieved worldwide circulation include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)">Nature</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/New_Scientist" title="New Scientist">New Scientist</a></i>,  <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Eye" title="Private Eye">Private Eye</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hello!" class="mw-redirect" title="Hello!">Hello!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spectator" title="The Spectator">The Spectator</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_Times" title="Radio Times">Radio Times</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Books_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Books in the United Kingdom">Books in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting">Broadcasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Broadcasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Truetone-Radio.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Truetone-Radio.jpg/220px-Truetone-Radio.jpg" width="220" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Truetone-Radio.jpg/330px-Truetone-Radio.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Truetone-Radio.jpg/440px-Truetone-Radio.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1150" data-file-height="1297" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Truetone-Radio.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Picture of a Truetone brand radio</div></div></div>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Radio_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Radio in the United Kingdom">Radio in the United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_radio_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Digital radio in the United Kingdom">Digital radio in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<p>Radio in the United Kingdom is dominated by the BBC, which operates <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio" title="BBC Radio">radio</a> stations both in the United Kingdom and abroad. The <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_Service" title="BBC World Service">BBC World Service</a> radio network is broadcast in 33 languages globally. Domestically the BBC also operates ten national networks and over 40 local radio stations including services in Welsh on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Cymru" title="BBC Radio Cymru">BBC Radio Cymru</a>, Gaelic on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_nan_G%C3%A0idheal" title="BBC Radio nan G\xc3\xa0idheal">BBC Radio nan G\xc3\xa0idheal</a> in Scotland and Irish in Northern Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The domestic services of the BBC are funded by the <a href="/wiki/Television_licence" title="Television licence">television licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TV_Licence_Fee:_facts_&amp;_figures_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TV_Licence_Fee:_facts_&amp;_figures-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The internationally targeted BBC World Service Radio is funded by the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_and_Commonwealth_Office" title="Foreign and Commonwealth Office">Foreign and Commonwealth Office</a>, though from 2014 it will be funded by the television licence.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The most popular radio station by number of listeners is <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a>, closely followed by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>. Advances in <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio_broadcast" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital audio broadcast">digital radio</a> technology have enabled the launch of several new stations by the Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Rather than operating as independent entities, many commercial local radio stations are owned by large radio groups which broadcast a similar format to many areas. The largest operator of radio stations is <a href="/wiki/Global_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Global Radio">Global Radio</a>, owner of the major <a href="/wiki/Heart_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="Heart Network">Heart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Galaxy Radio">Galaxy</a> radio brands. It also owns <a href="/wiki/Classic_FM_(UK)" title="Classic FM (UK)">Classic FM</a> and London\'s most popular commercial radio station, <a href="/wiki/95.8_CapitalFM" class="mw-redirect" title="95.8 CapitalFM">95.8 CapitalFM</a>. Other owners are <a href="/wiki/UTV_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="UTV Radio">UTV Radio</a>, with stations broadcasting in large city areas and <a href="/wiki/Bauer_Radio" title="Bauer Radio">Bauer Radio</a>, holding radio in the North of England. There are also regional stations, like <a href="/wiki/Real_Radio" title="Real Radio">Real Radio</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Century_Network" title="Century Network">Century Network</a>, broadcasting in some main parts of England, Wales and Scotland,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and a number of licensed <a href="/wiki/Radio_in_the_United_Kingdom#Community_radio" title="Radio in the United Kingdom">community radio</a> stations<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> which broadcast to local audiences.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television in the United Kingdom">Television in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg/220px-Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg/330px-Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg/440px-Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Channel_4_Building_-_Horseferry_Road_-_London_-_310504.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> building in London</div></div></div>\n<p>The United Kingdom has no analogue <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>. A free to air digital service is made up of two <a href="/wiki/Public_broadcasting" title="Public broadcasting">chartered</a> public broadcasting companies, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> and two franchised commercial television companies, (<a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(UK)" title="Channel 5 (UK)">Channel 5</a>). In addition to this, the United Kingdom\'s free-to-air <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a> service runs a large number of Entertainment, Music, Sport and Shopping channels from the likes of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>, <a href="/wiki/UKTV" title="UKTV">UKTV</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Sky_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="British Sky Broadcasting">British Sky Broadcasting</a>. There are five major nationwide <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television in the United Kingdom">television channels</a>: <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(UK)" title="Channel 5 (UK)">Channel 5</a>\xe2\x80\x94currently transmitted by digital terrestrial, free-to-air signals with the latter three channels funded by commercial advertising. The vast majority of digital cable television services are provided by <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a> with satellite television available from <a href="/wiki/Freesat" title="Freesat">Freesat</a> or <a href="/wiki/British_Sky_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="British Sky Broadcasting">British Sky Broadcasting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Free-to-air" title="Free-to-air">free-to-air</a> digital terrestrial television by <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a>. The entire country <a href="/wiki/Digit_Al" class="mw-redirect" title="Digit Al">switched to digital</a> (from a previously analogue signal) in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-What_is_digital_switchover_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-What_is_digital_switchover-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> operates several <a href="/wiki/BBC_Television" title="BBC Television">television channels</a> in the United Kingdom and abroad. The BBC\'s international television news service, <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_News" title="BBC World News">BBC World News</a>, is broadcast throughout the world. The domestic services of the BBC are funded by the <a href="/wiki/Television_licence" title="Television licence">television licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TV_Licence_Fee:_facts_&amp;_figures_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TV_Licence_Fee:_facts_&amp;_figures-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The international television broadcast services are operated by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Worldwide" title="BBC Worldwide">BBC Worldwide</a> on a commercial subscription basis over cable and satellite services. This commercial arm of the BBC also forms half of <a href="/wiki/UKTV" title="UKTV">UKTV</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Channel 4 is similarly chartered to the BBC, with a remit to provide public service broadcasting and schools programs, however it runs commercial <a href="/wiki/Advertisement" class="mw-redirect" title="Advertisement">advertisements</a> to provide a revenue stream. It produces a number of digital channels, branded as <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/E4_(TV_channel)" title="E4 (TV channel)">E4</a>, <a href="/wiki/More_4" class="mw-redirect" title="More 4">More 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film4" title="Film4">Film4</a>.\n</p><p>The commercial operators rely on advertising for their revenue, and are run as commercial ventures, in contrast to the public service operators. The <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> franchise transmits the 3 different networks in different parts of the country. These are known as <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_channel)" title="ITV (TV channel)">ITV</a> (in England, Wales, Scottish Borders, Isle of Man and <a href="/wiki/Channel_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Channel Television">Channel Islands</a>), <a href="/wiki/STV_(TV_network)" class="mw-redirect" title="STV (TV network)">STV</a> (In Central and Northern Scotland), and <a href="/wiki/UTV_(TV_channel)" title="UTV (TV channel)">UTV</a> in Northern Ireland.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet">Internet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Media_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Internet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Internet_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Internet in the United Kingdom">Internet in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<p>The UK\'s country code top-level domain (<a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">ccTLD</a>) is <a href="/wiki/.uk" title=".uk">.uk</a>. The most visited ".uk" website is the British version of <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a>, <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">eBay</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Online" title="BBC Online">BBC Online</a> and <a href="/wiki/MailOnline" title="MailOnline">MailOnline</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28"
