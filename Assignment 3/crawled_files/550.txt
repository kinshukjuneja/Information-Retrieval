country">island country</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, are known through national <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">censuses</a>, which have been analysed by various statistical bureaus since the 1920s. The <a href="/wiki/Nauru_Bureau_of_Statistics" class="mw-redirect" title="Nauru Bureau of Statistics">Nauru Bureau of Statistics</a> have conducted this task since 1977\xe2\x80\x94the first census since Nauru gained independence in 1968. The most recent census of Nauru was in 2011, when population had reached ten thousand. The population density is 478 inhabitants per square kilometre (185 per square mile), and the overall <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life expectancy</a> is 59.7 years. The population rose steadily from the 1960s until 2006 when the Government of Nauru repatriated thousands of Tuvaluan and <a href="/wiki/I-Kiribati" class="mw-redirect" title="I-Kiribati">I-Kiribati</a> workers from the country. Since 1992, Nauru\'s <a href="/wiki/Birth_rate" title="Birth rate">birth rate</a> has exceeded its <a href="/wiki/Death_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Death rate">death rate</a>; the natural growth rate is positive. In terms of age structure, the population is dominated by the 15\xe2\x80\x9364-year-old segment (65.6%). The median age of the population is 21.5, and the estimated gender ratio of the population is 0.91 males per one female.\n</p><p>Nauru is inhabited mostly by <a href="/wiki/Nauruan_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Nauruan people">Nauruans</a> (93.6%), while minorities include I-Kiribati (1.8%), Chinese (1.5%) and other (3.1%). The demographic history of Nauru is marked by several migrations: the area was first inhabited by Micronesian people about 3,000 years ago. The first European to find the island was <a href="/wiki/John_Fearn_(whaler)" title="John Fearn (whaler)">John Fearn</a> in 1798. Then, the country was annexed by Germany in the 1888. The next was when Japanese occupied the island during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in the 1942. During this time, the Japanese deported several thousands of Nauruans to other islands. In the 1960s, the country gained independence, where the percentage of Nauruans started to increase. The most recent demographic switch was in the 2000s, when the government repatriated several non-Nauruan population from the country.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Nauruan_language" title="Nauruan language">Nauruan language</a> is the official language of Nauru, but English is often used in the country. Nauruan is declared as the primary language of 95.3% of the population. The 2011 census revealed that 66.0% of the population spoke English and 11.9% another language. The main religions of Nauru are <a href="/wiki/Nauru_Congregational_Church" title="Nauru Congregational Church">Nauru Congregational Church</a> (35.71%) and <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> (32.96%). The <a href="/wiki/Literacy_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Literacy rate">literacy rate</a> in Nauru is 96.5%. The proportion of the country\'s population aged 15 and over attaining <a href="/wiki/Academic_degrees" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic degrees">academic degrees</a> is one of the lowest in the world, reaching 7.9% in 2011. An estimated 10.7% of the gross domestic product (GDP) is spent on education. Nauru has a <a href="/wiki/Universal_health_care" title="Universal health care">universal health care</a> system, and in 2012, an estimated 7.5% of its GDP was spent on healthcare. Nauru has the highest <a href="/wiki/Obesity" title="Obesity">obesity</a> ranking in the world; 97 per cent of men and 93 per cent of women are obese. In 2006, the average net monthly income was <a href="/wiki/Australian_dollar" title="Australian dollar">A$</a>2,597 (A$2905 in 2014). The most significant sources of employment are <a href="/wiki/Phosphate_mining_in_Nauru" title="Phosphate mining in Nauru">phosphate mining</a>, banking industries, and various <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> products. In 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Unemployment_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Unemployment rate">unemployment rate</a> was 23%. The 2011 census enumerated 1,647 total households, averaging 6.0 persons per house. Average <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanisation rate</a> in Nauru is 100%.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" The overall <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_life_expectancy" title="List of countries by life expectancy">life expectancy</a> in Nauru at birth is 59.7 years. The <a href="/wiki/Total_fertility_rate" title="Total fertility rate">total fertility rate</a> of 3.70 children per mother is one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependent_territories_by_fertility_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="List of sovereign states and dependent territories by fertility rate">highest in the Oceania</a>. The United Nations projects the population will stay around 10,000 in the 2020s,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the Nauru Bureau of Statistics estimates the population will increase to 20,000 in 2038.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In Nauru\'s history, there have been six major demographics changes. The island was first inhabited by Micronesian people roughly 3,000 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The first European to find the island was <a href="/wiki/John_Fearn_(whaler)" title="John Fearn (whaler)">John Fearn</a> in 1798. In 1888, the country was annexed by Germany. The next demographic change came when <a href="/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_Nauru" title="Japanese occupation of Nauru">Japanese occupied the island</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in the 1940s.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> During this time, the Japanese deported several thousands of Nauruans to other islands.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The next major demographic change was in the 1960s; the country gained independence, where the percentage of Nauruans started to increase.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The last major demographics change was in 2006 when the Government of Nauru repatriated almost all of the remaining <a href="/wiki/Tuvaluan_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Tuvaluan people">Tuvaluan</a> and <a href="/wiki/I-Kiribati" class="mw-redirect" title="I-Kiribati">I-Kiribati</a> workers, following large scale reduction from the <a href="/wiki/Nauru_Phosphate_Corporation" title="Nauru Phosphate Corporation">Republic of Nauru Phosphate Corporation</a> (RONPhos) and government workers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The census of 2006 stated 9,233 people were in Nauru: down 2.13% per year from the previous census of 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-nbs11-7_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nbs11-7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 2002\xe2\x80\x9311, there has been negative net migration, with an annual negative 109 net immigrants from 2006\xe2\x80\x9311.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2009 there were 1,820 arrivals and 1,736 departures, for a positive rate of 84 immigrants. This was the first time since collecting data in 2002, there was a positive rate.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Data on arrivals and departures collected by the Nauruan Customs and Immigration Office is not available, so specific immigration data is unavailable.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As of the 2011 census, 57% of the population over 15 years old were legally or <i>de facto</i> married, 35% were never married, while 7% were either widowed, separated, or divorced.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> There are 1,647 households in Nauru, making an average household size of 6.0 persons per household.<sup id="cite_ref-2011cen_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011cen-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vital_statistics">Vital The biggest changes from 2002\xe2\x80\x9311 were an increase from 0 to 1,291 (Assemblies of God) and 1,417 to 282 (Other).<sup id="cite_ref-CIANAURU_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIANAURU-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Public holidays include New Year\'s Day (1 January), Independence Day (31 January), Good Friday, Easter Monday, Easter Tuesday, Constitution Day (17 May), National Youth Day (25 September), Christmas Day, and Boxer Day.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Nauruan Independent was the predominant religion in Nauru before the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, when foreign missionaries introduced Christianity to the island. It is still practised by 9.5% of the population, according to 2011 census. There are a few active Christian missionary organisations, including representatives of Anglicanism, Methodism, and Catholicism. The Constitution provides for <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_religion" title="Freedom of religion">freedom of religion</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-const_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-const-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> however, the Government restricted this right in some circumstances. The government has restricted the religious practices of <a href="/wiki/The_Church_of_Jesus_Christ_of_Latter-day_Saints" title="The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints">The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Jehovah&#39;s Witnesses">Jehovah\'s Witnesses</a>, most of whom are foreign workers employed by RONPhos.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span Nauru</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a secondary school, 2010</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Literacy_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Literacy rate">Literacy rate</a> in Nauru, defined as "people who are currently enrolled in school and/or have reached at least grade 5 of primary education",<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> is 96.5%, as of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-2011cen_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011cen-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> There are 3,190 students and 104 teachers, as of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-edu_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edu-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The 2011 census stated 4 percent of the population aged 15 years or older have a primary education, 91 percent have a secondary education, and 5 percent have a tertiary education.<sup id="cite_ref-2011cen_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011cen-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Education is based on the British system, which requires attendance from 5 to 16 years old. Students spend three years at preschool, six years of primary education, and four years of secondary school. Tertiary school is not required.<sup id="cite_ref-icde_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icde-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> An estimated 10.7 percent of the GDP was spend on education in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> As of 2013, there are five preschools, four primary schools, three secondary schools, and one tertiary school.<sup id="cite_ref-edu_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edu-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The lone college, <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="University of South Pacific">University of South Pacific</a>, opened in the 1970s via distance courses, and in 1987 a campus was built on the island. It offers accounting, management, primary education, and English studies as majors.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The education system had a near-collapse in 2000\xe2\x80\x932005. During this time, exams were not held, teachers were not paid, and schools did not have enough funding to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-icde_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icde-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> As a result, over half of the schools closed.<sup id="cite_ref-edu_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edu-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Government">Australian Government</a> partnered with the Nauruan Department of Education to help. This agreement resulted in a 5.7% increase in students, teachers with a degree increased from 30% to 93%, and over A$11 million was used to construct a new secondary school.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Obesity_in_Nauru" title="Obesity in Nauru">Obesity in Nauru</a></div>\n<p>Nauru has a <a href="/wiki/Universal_health_care" title="Universal health care">universal health care</a> system, which is provided to all citizens for free.<sup id="cite_ref-brit_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brit-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> There is a single hospital, the Republic of Nauru Hospital in Yaren. It offers basic medical and surgical care, along with <a href="/wiki/Radiology" title="Radiology">radiological</a>, laboratory, pharmacy, and dental services. The Nauru Public Health Centre offers treatments for diabetes and other obesity-related diseases. This is the only form of specialized medical care in Nauru. Patients who require higher treatment are flown to Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, an estimated 7.5% of its GDP was spent on healthcare.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> As of 2010, there are 50 hospital beds.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, there were 149 physicians and 557 nurses per 100,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, it was estimated 26.2% and 22.1% of the population under 15 years of age consumed tobacco and cigarettes, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> <i>The World Factbook</i> estimates the population has a life expectancy of 66.4 years at birth, which ranks Nauru 169th in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2013, there were 366 live births: 193 male and 173 female.<sup id="cite_ref-births_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-births-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The average age of mothers at first birth, as of a 2007 estimate, was 22.1 years.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> General fertility rate, i.e. numbers of births per 1,000 women aged 15\xe2\x80\x9349 is 105.<sup id="cite_ref-2011cen_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011cen-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, 75 people died in Nauru.<sup id="cite_ref-deaths_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deaths-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cardiovascular_disease" title="Cardiovascular disease">Cardiovascular disease</a> (44%) and cancer (10%) were the primary causes of deaths in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In the 2007\xe2\x80\x9311 period, the birth rate was 27.2, exceeding the death rate of 7.5.<sup id="cite_ref-2011cen_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011cen-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The estimated infant mortality rate was 8.21 deaths per live births in 2014. In terms of age structure, the population of Nauru is dominated by the 15\xe2\x80\x9364 rate (65.6%), while the size of the population younger than 15 and older than 64 is relatively small (32.5% and 1.8% respectively). The median age of the population is 21.5. The sex ratio of the population is 0.91 males per 1 female.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Nauru has the highest <a href="/wiki/Obesity" title="Obesity">obesity</a> ranking in the world, 97 per cent of men and 93 per cent of women are obese.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, the average <a href="/wiki/Body_mass_index" title="Body mass index">body mass index</a> was around 34.5, which is greater than obese (30+). According to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i> the main reason for the obesity is result of the importation of Western foods.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_indicators">Economic indicators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Nauru&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economic indicators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Nauru" title="Economy of Nauru">Economy of Nauru</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru,_1968-2001.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru%2C_1968-2001.svg/220px-Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru%2C_1968-2001.svg.png" width="220" height="111" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru%2C_1968-2001.svg/330px-Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru%2C_1968-2001.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru%2C_1968-2001.svg/440px-Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru%2C_1968-2001.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="380" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phosphate_exports_in_Nauru,_1968-2001.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Phosphate exports (in millions of tonnes) in Nauru from 1968\xe2\x80\x932001.</div></div></div>\n<p>Net monthly income in 2006 averaged <a href="/wiki/Australian_dollar" title="Australian dollar">A$</a>2,597 (A$2,905 in 2014<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-AU_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-AU-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>). In the same year, gross monthly income averaged A$9,554 (A$10,685 in 2014<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-AU_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-AU-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>). This was calculated during the mini-census of 2006, which featured 54.4% response rate of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The income was calculated using the following factors: first job salary, subsistence, other business income, second job salary, services to other households, benefits, house gifts consumed and received, and other income.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Compared to other countries that use the Australian dollar\xe2\x80\x94Kiribati, Australia, and Tuvalu\xe2\x80\x94Nauru ranks number one in terms of income.<sup class="reference" id="ref_AAA"><a href="#endnote_AAA">[A]</a></sup> Since 2013, Nauru does not have a <a href="/wiki/Minimum_wage" title="Minimum wage">minimum wage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Nauru\'s number of employed people has steadily risen and fallen. According to the 2011 census, there are 908 employed persons and 2,883 unemployed persons, making an unemployment rate of 23%.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Nauru Bureau of Statistics predicted the unemployment rate will decrease to 22% in FY2014/15.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The gross domestic product of Nauru was A$69.55 million in 2009, an increase of 40% increase from 2008. The GDP is broken down into three categories: primary (18.7%\xe2\x80\x94agriculture, hunting, forestry, fishing, mining, and quarrying), secondary (36.5%\xe2\x80\x94manufacturing, electric, gas, water, and construction), and tertiary (44.8%\xe2\x80\x94trade, hotel, restaurants, and various services) industries.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A majority of the population are employed in phosphate mining, public administration, education, and transportation.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Nauru_detention_centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Nauru detention centre">detention centre</a> was closed in 2008, which caused the unemployment rate to rise to 30%, and approximately ten percent of the population relied on working at the centre.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The centre reopened in 2012<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> and currently serves 1,162 prisoners, as of May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> During the 1990s, Nauru was famous for operating offshore banks, helping with <a href="/wiki/Money_laundering" title="Money laundering">money laundering</a>. The United States State Department\'s International Narcotics Control Strategy Report estimated there were 400 offshore finance centres laundering an estimated $70 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Phosphate_mining_in_Nauru" title="Phosphate mining in Nauru">Phosphate mining in Nauru</a> originally made Nauru the richest per capita nation in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> In 1968, the <a href="/wiki/Nauru_Phosphate_Royalties_Trust" title="Nauru Phosphate Royalties Trust">Nauru Phosphate Royalties Trust</a> (NPRT) was created to invest profits from mining, so Nauru would have money after the mining was exhausted.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The owners of the trust purchased a fleet of ships and aircraft, a brewery in the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>, hotels around the world, and real estate in Australia, the United States and Britain, which causes the trust to bankrupt.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Phosphate exports peaked in 1973 with 2.3 million tonnes, but has decreased to 0.2 million tonnes in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, mining of a secondary layer of phosphate began.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
