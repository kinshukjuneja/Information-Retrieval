over 100 square miles (260&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of the <a href="/wiki/East_Devon" title="East Devon">East Devon</a> countryside (<a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>).\n</p><p>This countryside includes eighteen miles (29 km) of Heritage coastline. The designated area covers: twenty-nine parishes and borders the coastal towns of <a href="/wiki/Exmouth,_Devon" class="mw-redirect" title="Exmouth, Devon">Exmouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seaton,_Devon" title="Seaton, Devon">Seaton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sidmouth" title="Sidmouth">Sidmouth</a> but includes the entire resort of <a href="/wiki/Budleigh_Salterton" title="Budleigh Salterton">Budleigh Salterton</a>.\n</p><p>East Devon has two <a href="/wiki/Areas_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" class="mw-redirect" title="Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty">AONBs</a> within its catchment area which includes the <a href="/wiki/Blackdown_Hills" title="Blackdown Hills">Blackdown Hills</a> (designated 1991) and East Devon AONB (designated 1963), both AONBs make up over 66% of the district.\n</p><p>East Devon AONB Partnership is a joint initiative funded by Defra (Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs), East Devon District Council and Devon County Council.  Through ventures such as community projects and project grants East Devon AONB Partnership helps to conserve and manage the East Devon AONB.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
