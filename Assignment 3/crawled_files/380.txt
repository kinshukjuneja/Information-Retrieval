href="/w/index.php?title=British_Army_Training_Unit_Suffield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:X_21_A_in_ERV.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/X_21_A_in_ERV.JPG/220px-X_21_A_in_ERV.JPG" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/X_21_A_in_ERV.JPG/330px-X_21_A_in_ERV.JPG 1.5x, class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:X_21_A_in_ERV.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Armoured_Vehicle_Royal_Engineers#Chieftain_.22CHAVRE.22_AVRE" title="Armoured Vehicle Royal Engineers">CHAVRE</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a> in Canada in 2005.</div></div></div>\n<p>A British chemical weapons testing facility was located in <a href="/wiki/French_Algeria" title="French Algeria">French-controlled Algeria</a>. However, upon the conquest of France by Germany, the facility was lost. The British could find no suitable location in the UK, and an agreement was signed between Canada and the UK to allow the Suffield area to become available to British scientists for testing. Consequently, British and Canadian forces employed the area for a variety of experiments. Upon the end of World War II, the British departed the Suffield area and it was formally taken over by the Canadian <a href="/wiki/Defence_Research_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="Defence Research Board">Defence Research Board</a>.\n</p><p>In 1969, <a href="/wiki/Muammar_al-Gaddafi" class="mw-redirect" title="Muammar al-Gaddafi">Colonel Gaddafi</a> led a coup in Libya, took control of the country and proceeded, after negotiations, to close down British military installations located at <a href="/wiki/El_Adem" class="mw-redirect" title="El Adem">El Adem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tobruk" title="Tobruk">Tobruk</a>, and American installations located near <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a>. This presented the UK with a problem, as there were no areas large enough to allow the British Army to undertake large-scale armoured warfare exercises in Europe. In 1971 a 10-year lease was signed between the British and Canadian Governments that authorized battle group training to take place in the Suffield area by the British Army. In January 1972, the British Army Training Unit Suffield was formally established. In July, the first live rounds were fired by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Tank_Regiment" title="Royal Tank Regiment">4th Royal Tank Regiment</a> Battle Group. In 1981, the lease for Suffield was extended, and in 1991, the lease was again extended. In 2006, on the expiration of this lease, the British and Canadian governments concluded an agreement that would allow British forces to maintain their training practices in Canada indefinitely.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current">Current</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Army_Training_Unit_Suffield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg/310px-Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg" width="310" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg/465px-Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg/620px-Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1231" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Challenger_2_Tank_Firing_at_BATUS_MOD_45157782.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A row of Challenger II on a firing range at BATUS</div></div></div>\n<p>Due to the hostile winters, BATUS conducts training from May to October each year. This normally consists of four to six battle groups (BGs) each exercising for around 24 days each, supported by the BATUS permanent and temporary staff and a dedicated enemy (traditionally provided by a single nominated regiment). There are relatively few servicemen permanently posted to BATUS, but their numbers are significantly increased by temporary staff who form the bulk of the Range Safety and Control Group (RSCG) (which provides live fire safety and training delivery) as well a large proportion of the camp based supporting organisations, including a dedicated logistics squadron and a REME workshop.\n</p><p>During the winter months, some specialist <a href="/wiki/Arctic_warfare" class="mw-redirect" title="Arctic warfare">Arctic warfare</a> training is conducted on the Suffield Block by the Canadian Forces while the UK vehicle fleet is prepared for the following year. Around 22 <a href="/wiki/Challenger_2" title="Challenger 2">Challenger 2</a> tanks, 80 <a href="/wiki/Warrior_tracked_armoured_vehicle" title="Warrior tracked armoured vehicle">Warrior tracked armoured vehicles</a>, and a similar number of CVR(T) and Bulldog Armoured Fighting Vehicles (AFVs), a fleet of Engineer vehicles including the latest TITAN <a href="/wiki/Armoured_Vehicle_Launched_Bridge" class="mw-redirect" title="Armoured Vehicle Launched Bridge">AVLB</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trojan_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trojan (vehicle)">TROJAN</a> <a href="/wiki/Armoured_Vehicle_Royal_Engineers" title="Armoured Vehicle Royal Engineers">AVRE</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale_Gazelle" title="A\xc3\xa9rospatiale Gazelle">Gazelle</a> helicopters making up <a href="/wiki/29_(BATUS)_Flight_AAC" class="mw-redirect" title="29 (BATUS) Flight AAC">29 (BATUS) Flight AAC</a> are based at BATUS.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li><b>Stationed Units</b>\n<ul><li>HQ BATUS</li>\n<li>Resident OPFOR - rotated every year.
