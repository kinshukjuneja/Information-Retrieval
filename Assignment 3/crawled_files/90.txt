class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" Austelles is nothing notable but the paroch chirch".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Not long after <a href="/wiki/William_Cookworthy" title="William Cookworthy">William Cookworthy</a> discovered <a href="/wiki/Kaolinite" title="Kaolinite">china clay</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tregonning_Hill" title="Tregonning Hill">Tregonning Hill</a> in west Cornwall, the same mineral was found in greater quantity in <a href="/wiki/Hensbarrow" title="Hensbarrow">Hensbarrow downs</a> north of St Austell.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Clay_pit" title="Clay pit">Clay mining</a> soon took over from <a href="/wiki/Tin" title="Tin">tin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> mining as the principal industry in the area, and this eventually contributed enormously to the growth of the town. The clay industry really only came into its own during the mid 19th to early 20th century, at a time when the falling prices of tin and other metals forced many mines to close down or convert to clay mining. The success and high profitability of the industry attracted many families whose breadwinner had been put out of work by the depression in the local metal mining industry, and increased the population of the town considerably. This meant that more shops and businesses took root, providing more jobs and improving trade. This, along with other factors, led to St Austell becoming one of the ten most important commercial centres of Cornwall.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redevelopment">Redevelopment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Redevelopment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Work began in 1963 on a brutalist-style pedestrian precinct which included shops, offices and flats. The design was by Alister MacDonald &amp; Partners and the materials reinforced concrete with some stone facing.<sup id="cite_ref-Pevsner,_N._1970_pp._156\xe2\x80\x93158_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pevsner,_N._1970_pp._156\xe2\x80\x93158-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In the 2000s this area of the town had become very outdated, and underwent a \xc2\xa375 million redevelopment process. In August 2007, developers David McLean and demolition team Gilpin moved onto the town centre site to complete the preparation, with the Filmcentre which was originally an <a href="/wiki/Odeon_Cinemas" title="Odeon Cinemas">Odeon</a> cinema dating back to 1936, being demolished in late September/early October.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In October 2007, the <a href="/wiki/South_West_of_England_Regional_Development_Agency" title="South West of England Regional Development Agency">South West of England Regional Development Agency</a> (SWRDA) announced the new development would be named <i>White River Place</i>. It was also announced that 50% of shop units had been leased to High Street stores,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/New_Look_(clothing_retailer)" class="mw-redirect" title="New Look (clothing retailer)">New Look</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peacocks_(clothing)" title="Peacocks (clothing)">Peacocks</a>, Bonmarch\xc3\xa9 and <a href="/wiki/Wilko_(retailer)" title="Wilko (retailer)">Wilko</a> opening new stores. This would mean New Look relocating from its current premises in Fore Street and the return of Peacocks to St Austell following the demolition of its old store to make way for the new development. Bonmarche has since closed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In October 2008, it was announced that the developer David McLean Developments had gone into administration and concern was expressed that this could jeopardise the completion of the project.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In December 2008, the new White River Cinema opened its doors for the first time: the cinema is technically advanced and the first purpose-built cinema in Cornwall for over 60 years. The Torchlight Carnival was revived<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Revived from when? (November 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> in November 2009 as a direct result of public demand through a survey conducted with local residents. The Torchlight Procession has become an important event in the town\'s calendar, heralding in the Winter celebrations and drawing thousands of people from across Cornwall and Devon. The event is run by a small group of non affiliated volunteers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/St_Austell_and_Clay_Country_Eco-town" title="St Austell and Clay Country Eco-town">St Austell and Clay Country Eco-town</a> is a plan for several new settlements around St Austell on old Imerys sites. It was given outline government approval in July 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> strategic planning committee voted to approve a \xc2\xa3250 million <a href="/wiki/Beach_resort" class="mw-redirect" title="Beach resort">beach resort</a> scheme at <a href="/wiki/Carlyon_Bay" title="Carlyon Bay">Carlyon Bay</a>, St Austell. The development was initially proposed in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a represented by 9 councillors.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>Before this date the area had been an <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">unparished area</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>St Austell is the main centre of the china clay industry in Cornwall and employs around 2,200 people as of  2006<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, with sales of \xc2\xa3195&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/St_Austell_Brewery" title="St Austell Brewery">St Austell Brewery</a>, which celebrated its 150th anniversary in 2001, supplies <a href="/wiki/Cask_ale" title="Cask ale">cask ale</a> to pubs in Cornwall and other parts of the country. Its flagship beer is <i>St Austell Tribute</i>; a number of other ales are brewed but are less commonly sold outside Cornwall. St Austell Brewery\'s first public house, The Seven Stars Inn, purchased in 1863, still stands today on East Hill in the town. Tregonissey House, the site of the company\'s first steam Brewery, built in 1870, can also be seen in Market Hill. A brewery museum and visitor centre is open to the public on the present brewery site in Trevarthian Road.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg/220px-Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg" width="220" height="69" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg/330px-Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg/440px-Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3700" data-file-height="1155" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Eden_Project_geodesic_domes_panorama.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Panoramic view of the geodesic biome domes at the Eden Project</div></div></div>\n<p>As in much of Cornwall and neighbouring counties, tourism is increasingly important to St Austell\'s economy. Tourists are drawn to the area by nearby beaches and attractions such as the <a href="/wiki/Eden_Project" title="Eden Project">Eden Project</a>, sited in a former <a href="/wiki/Clay_pit" title="Clay pit">clay pit</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Lost_Gardens_of_Heligan" title="Lost Gardens of Heligan">Lost Gardens of Heligan</a>. The China Clay Country Park, in a former china-clay pit two miles north of the town, tells the story of the men, women and children who lived, worked and played in the shadow of the clay tips around St Austell.\n</p><p>St Austell is home to several <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">public houses</a>, numerous <a href="/wiki/High_street" class="mw-redirect" title="High street">high street</a> retailers, and several independent shops, many of which cater for tourists. The town has a small museum which is situated in the Market House. A Brewery Museum and Visitor Centre is situated on the site of the St Austell Brewery in Trevarthian Road.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newspaper_and_radio">Newspaper and radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Newspaper and radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The town has two weekly newspapers:\n</p>\n<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/St_Austell_Guardian" class="mw-redirect" title="St Austell Guardian">St Austell Guardian</a></i>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Guardian" title="Cornish Guardian">Cornish Guardian</a> series published by <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_and_Devon_Media" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornwall and Devon Media">Cornwall and Devon Media</a> Ltd, has a long history in the town and is published on Friday.</li>\n<li><i><a href="/wiki/St_Austell_Voice" title="St Austell Voice">St Austell Voice</a></i>, sister paper to the <i><a href="/wiki/Newquay_Voice" title="Newquay Voice">Newquay Voice</a></i>, had offices close to the town centre in Truro Road, but has since moved to Old Vicarage Place. Two branch lines west of the town were later opened to serve the <a href="/wiki/China_clay" class="mw-redirect" title="China clay">china clay</a> industry; the <a href="/wiki/Newquay_and_Cornwall_Junction_Railway" title="Newquay and Cornwall Junction Railway">Newquay and Cornwall Junction Railway</a> which is still partly open, and the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Trenance_Valley_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Trenance Valley line">Trenance Valley line</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The independent <a href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge" class="mw-redirect" title="Narrow gauge">narrow gauge</a> <a href="/wiki/Pentewan_Railway" title="Pentewan Railway">Pentewan Railway</a> ran from West Hill to the coast at <a href="/wiki/Pentewan" title="Pentewan">Pentewan</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Main_Line" title="Cornish Main Line">Cornish Main Line</a> in St Austell is quite renowned for its <a href="/wiki/Viaduct" title="Viaduct">viaduct</a> which passes through the Gover Valley and Trenance areas of the town. the original timber structure was designed by <a href="/wiki/Isambard_Kingdom_Brunel" title="Isambard Kingdom Brunel">Isambard Kingdom Brunel</a>, it was 115 feet (35&#160;m) high, 720 feet (220&#160;m) long on 10 piers; it was replaced by a new stone viaduct in 1899.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>There was a siding located west of the viaduct. In the early years trains from St Austell had to push wagons over the tall, curving viaduct to shunt this siding. The <a href="/wiki/Great_Western_Railway" title="Great Western Railway">Great Western Railway</a>\'s instructions stated that: "Trucks may be pushed from St Austell to the Siding, but when this is done the speed of the Train between the two places must not exceed 8 miles an hour, and the head Guard must ride on the leading vehicle, unless it be a bonnet end one, in which case he must ride in the first low sided vehicle from it, to keep a good look out, and be prepared to give a signal to the Driver either by Day or Night, as may be required".  Train services today operate west to <a href="/wiki/Truro_railway_station" title="Truro railway station">Truro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penzance_railway_station" title="Penzance railway station">Penzance</a>, and east to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_railway_station" title="Plymouth railway station">Plymouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paddington_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Paddington railway station">London</a>. There are also three <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> services on most days to the North of England and Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/St_Austell_bus_station" class="mw-redirect" title="St Austell bus station">town\'s bus station</a> faces the entrance to the railway station to offer an easy interchange between buses and trains. <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> coach services call here, a dedicated link operates to the <a href="/wiki/Eden_Project" title="Eden Project">Eden Project</a>, and local buses operate to villages such as <a href="/wiki/Fowey" title="Fowey">Fowey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mevagissey" title="Mevagissey">Mevagissey</a>. The town can be accessed by the <a href="/wiki/A390_road" title="A390 road">A390</a> which by-passes the town to the south on its way from <a href="/wiki/Liskeard" title="Liskeard">Liskeard</a> to <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a>, or by the <a href="/wiki/A391_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A391 road">A391</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bodmin" title="Bodmin">Bodmin</a>, or by the <a href="/wiki/A3058_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A3058 road">A3058</a> from <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">Newquay</a>. In addition there are the B3273 to Mevagissey, the B3274 to <a href="/wiki/Padstow" title="Padstow">Padstow</a> and the A3082 to Fowey.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="St_Austell_bus_station">St Austell bus station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: St Austell bus station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_(R908RYO).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_%28R908RYO%29.jpg/220px-St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_%28R908RYO%29.jpg" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_%28R908RYO%29.jpg/330px-St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_%28R908RYO%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_%28R908RYO%29.jpg/440px-St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_%28R908RYO%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="614" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Austell_Bus_Station_-_First_31828_(R908RYO).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Austell bus station in June 2013</div></div></div>\n<p>St Austell bus station is the main bus and coach terminus for the town. The hospital provides some urgent treatment at its minor injury unit, with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Cornwall_Hospital" title="Royal Cornwall Hospital">Royal Cornwall Hospital</a> at Treliske, Truro handling more serious cases.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_sites">Religious sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Religious sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The church was originally dedicated to St <a href="/wiki/Austol" title="Austol">Austol</a>, a Breton saint associated with St Meven, but is now <a href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity_Church,_St_Austell" title="Holy Trinity Church, St Austell">dedicated to the Holy Trinity</a>. By 1150 it had been appropriated to the <a href="/wiki/Tywardreath_Priory" class="mw-redirect" title="Tywardreath Priory">Priory of Tywardreath</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Cardinham" title="Cardinham">Cardinhams</a>: this continued until 1535. There was originally a Norman church here, of which some remains may be seen. \n</p><p>The present church is of the 15th century and is large because the mediaeval parish was also a large one: the tower is impressive. All four outside walls bear sculptural groups in carved niches: the Twelve Apostles in three groups on the north, east and south; the Holy Trinity above the Annunciation and below that the Risen Christ between two saints on the west. The tower can be dated to between 1478 and 1487 by the arms of Bishop Courtenay, and the walls are faced in <a href="/wiki/Pentewan_stone" class="mw-redirect" title="Pentewan stone">Pentewan stone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>The tower and other parts of the church have an interior lining of granite<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On the south side of the church, a formerly separate <a href="/wiki/Chantry" title="Chantry">chantry</a> has been incorporated into the church when it was extended. (The chantry itself was abolished in 1543.) \n</p><p>There are holy wells at Menacuddle and Towan.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> A new organ was placed on the north side of the chancel in 1880 and the first recital was held on 22 April. The organ was built by Messrs <a href="/wiki/Henry_Bryceson" title="Henry Bryceson">Bryceson Brothers and Ellis</a> and cost circa \xc2\xa3600.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The church is dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Trinity">Holy Trinity</a>, is Grade I listed, and seats 300.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> There is a Cornish cross in the churchyard which was found buried in the ground on the manor of Treverbyn in 1879.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> This cross was erected in the churchyard on a new base in 1879. Another cross is in the grounds of a house originally known as Moor Cottage. This house was built in 1819; the cross was brought from Hewas in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Ladock" title="Ladock">Ladock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<table role="presentation" class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">\n<tbody><tr>\n<td class="mbox-image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Commons-logo.svg/30px-Commons-logo.svg.png" width="30" height="40" class="noviewer" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Commons-logo.svg/45px-Commons-logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4a/Commons-logo.svg/59px-Commons-logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="1376" /></td>\n<td class="mbox-text plainlist">Wikimedia Commons has media related to <i><b><a href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Churches_in_St_Austell" class="extiw" title="commons:Category:Churches in St Austell">Churches in St Austell</a></b></i>.</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The parish of St Austell was part of the <a href="/wiki/Archdeacon_of_Cornwall" title="Archdeacon of Cornwall">archdeaconry of Cornwall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Exeter" title="Diocese of Exeter">Diocese of Exeter</a> until 1876 when the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Truro" title="Diocese of Truro">Diocese of Truro</a> was established. A new rural deanery of St Austell was established in 1875. The style of worship of the parish church is in the <a href="/wiki/Low_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Low Church">Evangelical</a> tradition of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>. The two chapels-of-ease are All Saints, <a href="/wiki/Pentewan" title="Pentewan">Pentewan</a>, and St Levan\'s, <a href="/wiki/Higher_Porthpean" title="Higher Porthpean">Higher Porthpean</a>. In the 19th century the following parishes were created out of St Austell parish: St Blazey (1845); Charlestown (1846), Treverbyn (1847), and Par (1846 out of St Blazey and Tywardreath).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quakers">Quakers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Quakers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>There was formerly a Quaker burial ground at Tregongeeves, just outside the town on the <a href="/wiki/A390_road" title="A390 road">Truro Road</a>. It was covered by about 6&#160;ft (1.8&#160;m) of earth removed from the building of the new road in the 1960s. A high stone wall bounds the remaining acre of land; access can be gained through a wrought iron gate. \n</p><p>Approximately forty of the headstones from Tregongeeves were removed and are now located at the Friends meeting house in the High Cross Street in St Austell, just below the high wall which surrounds St Austell railway station. That meeting house is still in use.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span>  <small><i>(April 2018)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Speedway">Speedway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Speedway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_speedway" title="Motorcycle speedway">Speedway</a> racing first took place a venue called Rocky Park, under the name "<a href="/wiki/St_Austell_Gulls" title="St Austell Gulls">St Austell Gulls</a>". The sport was a hit during various years, between 1949 and 1963 at the Cornish Stadium &#8211; now Stadium Retail Park, home of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cornish_Market_World&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cornish Market World (page does not exist)">Cornish Market World</a>. The sport returned to the area in the late 1990s, in the Clay Country Moto Parc, located at Old Pound, <a href="/wiki/Nanpean" title="Nanpean">Nanpean</a>.\n</p><p>The club operated as the St Austell Gulls for four years, until the club changed ownership, and moved up a league to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_(speedway)" title="Premier League (speedway)">Premier League</a>, re-formed as the <a href="/wiki/Trelawny_Tigers" title="Trelawny Tigers">Trelawny JAG Tigers</a>, until site owners Imerys Minerals Ltd ended the lease. Speedway has not been held in Cornwall since. Many attempts have been made to re-introduce the sport, but none have got past planning permission. The two highest profile bids were at Par Moor Motor Museum and St Eval Raceway. The owner of the land for the Par Moor bid confirmed that he would rent the land for speedway but locals objected. The St Eval bid failed after residents expressed fears about noise.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stock_car_racing">Stock car racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Stock car racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">Stock car racing</a>, promoted by 1950s Kiwi Speedway star <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Redmond" title="Trevor Redmond">Trevor Redmond</a>, ran side by side with speedway on numerous occasions. Numerous championships were run here, including the 1972 <a href="/wiki/British_Stock_Car_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="British Stock Car Association">BriSCA</a> World Championship for Formula 2 cars, won by Jimmy Murray from N.Ireland. It closed its doors in 1987.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/St_Austell_F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="St Austell F.C.">St Austell Football Club</a> was formed on 17 September 1890. In 1908 the club won its first trophy: the Cornwall Charity Cup. The club achieved some success in the late 1920s and 1930s, winning the Senior Cup and Charity Cup twice. In May 2009, the team won the Senior Cup with a closely fought 3&#8211;2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Saltash_United_F.C." title="Saltash United F.C.">Saltash United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_and_tennis">Rugby and tennis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Rugby and tennis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Tregorrick Park is the home of <a href="/wiki/St_Austell_RFC" title="St Austell RFC">St Austell RFC</a>, St Austell Tennis Club and Cornwall Table Tennis Centre. <a href="/wiki/St_Austell_RFC" title="St Austell RFC">St Austell RFC</a> play in the <a href="/wiki/Tribute_Western_Counties_West" title="Tribute Western Counties West">Tribute Western Counties West</a> league and the club supports two senior teams, a ladies team and 14 youth teams covering most age groups. Founded in 1963 St Austell RFC has played at the Tregorrick Park ground since their move from Cromwell Road in the 1980s to make way for the <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a> supermarket.\n</p><p>Tregorrick Park also hosts a gym, sports hall, squash courts, bar, function room and holds local events such as firework displays and schools cross country competitions.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cricket">Cricket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Cricket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Wheal_Eliza" title="Wheal Eliza">Wheal Eliza</a> cricket ground is the home of <a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell_Cricket_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="St Austell Cricket Club (page does not exist)">St Austell Cricket Club</a>, and is also used for Minor Counties matches. The club supports four senior teams, a ladies\' team and youth teams. Facilities at <a href="/wiki/Wheal_Eliza" title="Wheal Eliza">Wheal Eliza</a> includes two playing fields with their own changing room facilities enabling the club to hold two competitive matches every match day. The club also has a pavilion, scorebox, artificial and grass nets.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball">Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Austell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 2017, the St Austell Claycutters baseball club was established to compete in the South West Baseball League. They are considered to be the first baseball team in Cornwall.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> While the team are named and associated with St. Austell, all outdoor training and home games are held on the sports fields at <a href="/wiki/Fowey_River_Academy" title="Fowey River Academy">Fowey River Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fowey" title="Fowey">Fowey</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
