Pop career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Mansell was the lead singer and guitarist of <a href="/wiki/Pop_Will_Eat_Itself" title="Pop Will Eat Itself">Pop Will Eat Itself</a>. When PWEI signed to <a href="/wiki/Nothing_Records" title="Nothing Records">Nothing Records</a>, Mansell became friends with <a href="/wiki/Trent_Reznor" title="Trent Reznor">Trent Reznor</a> of Nine Inch Nails<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and performed backing vocals on the band\'s 1999 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fragile_(Nine_Inch_Nails_album)" title="The Fragile (Nine Inch Nails album)">The Fragile</a></i>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack_composer">Soundtrack composer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clint_Mansell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soundtrack composer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Mansell started composing film soundtracks when director <a href="/wiki/Darren_Aronofsky" title="Darren Aronofsky">Darren Aronofsky</a> hired him to score his debut film, <i><a href="/wiki/Pi_(film)" title="Pi (film)">Pi</a></i>, in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-ANT_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANT-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also wrote the <a href="/wiki/Requiem_for_a_Dream_(soundtrack)" title="Requiem for a Dream (soundtrack)">score</a> for Aronofsky\'s next film, <i><a href="/wiki/Requiem_for_a_Dream" title="Requiem for a Dream">Requiem for a Dream</a></i>, which became a <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult</a> hit.<sup id="cite_ref-ANT_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ANT-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The film\'s primary composition "<a href="/wiki/Lux_Aeterna_(Mansell)" title="Lux Aeterna (Mansell)">Lux Aeterna</a>" was popular, appearing in adverts, <a href="/wiki/Film_trailer" class="mw-redirect" title="Film trailer">film trailers</a><sup id="cite_ref-LOTR_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOTR-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DAVI_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DAVI-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> videos labelled as Epic Music. Mansell has composed the music to every subsequent Aronofsky film.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_trailers">Film trailers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clint_Mansell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit Two Towers">The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers</a></i> used an orchestral and choral arrangement of "Lux Aeterna", <sup id="cite_ref-LOTR_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOTR-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> called "<a href="/wiki/Requiem_for_a_Tower" title="Requiem for a Tower">Requiem for a Tower</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-LOTR_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOTR-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This orchestration was arranged by Simone Benyacar, Dan Nielsen and <a href="/wiki/Veigar_Margeirsson" title="Veigar Margeirsson">Veigar Margeirsson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LOTR_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOTR-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The original "Lux Aeterna" and "Requiem for a Tower" arrangements have been used in advertisements for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Red Sox</a>\xe2\x80\x93<a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">Yankees</a>, <i><a <i><a href="/wiki/Babylon_A.D." title="Babylon A.D.">Babylon A.D.</a></i> , <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(2004_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lost (2004 TV series)">Lost</a></i>, <i><a
