losses</th></tr><tr><td style="width:50%;border-right:1px dotted #aaa;">\nNone</td><td style="width:50%;padding-left:0.25em">\nNone</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Three Hundred and Thirty Five Years\' War</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Driehonderdvijfendertigjarige Oorlog</i>) was an alleged theoretical state of war between the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> (located off the southwest coast of England). The truth that this war ever existed is disputed. It is said to have been <a href="/wiki/List_of_wars_extended_by_diplomatic_irregularity" title="List of wars extended by diplomatic irregularity">extended by the lack of a peace treaty</a> for 335 years without a single shot being fired, which would make it one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_longest_wars" class="mw-redirect" title="List of longest wars">world\'s longest wars</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Bloodless_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Bloodless war">bloodless war</a>. Despite the uncertain validity of the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_war" title="Declaration of war">declaration of war</a>, and thus uncertainty about whether or not a state of war ever actually existed in the first place, peace was finally declared in 1986, bringing an end to any hypothetical war that may have been legally considered to exist.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The origins of the war can be found in the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>, fought between the <a href="/wiki/Cavalier" title="Cavalier">Royalists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roundhead" title="Roundhead">Parliamentarians</a> from 1642 to 1651. <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a> had fought the Royalists to the edges of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a>. In the West of England this meant that <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> was the last Royalist stronghold. In 1648, Cromwell pushed on until mainland Cornwall was in the hands of the Parliamentarians.\n</p><p>The Royalist Navy was forced to retreat to the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, which lay off the Cornish coast and were under the ownership of Royalist <a href="/wiki/John_Granville,_1st_Earl_of_Bath" title="John Granville, 1st Earl of Bath">John Granville</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dutch_Navy_alliance">Dutch Navy alliance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dutch Navy alliance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch Navy">navy</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="United Provinces of the Netherlands">United Provinces of the Netherlands</a> was allied with the Parliamentarians. The Netherlands had been assisted by the English under a number of rulers in the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years&#39; War">Eighty Years\' War</a> (1568\xe2\x80\x931648), starting with Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I of England</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_M%C3%BCnster" title="Peace of M\xc3\xbcnster">Treaty of M\xc3\xbcnster</a> (30 January 1648) had confirmed Dutch independence from Spain. The Netherlands sought to maintain their alliance with England and had chosen to ally with the side likely to win the Civil War.\n</p><p>The Dutch Navy was suffering heavy losses from the Royalist fleet based in Scilly. On 30 March 1651, <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Maarten_Harpertszoon_Tromp" class="mw-redirect" title="Maarten Harpertszoon Tromp">Maarten Harpertszoon Tromp</a> arrived in Scilly to demand reparation from the Royalist fleet for the Dutch ships and goods taken by them.\n</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Bulstrode_Whitelocke" title="Bulstrode Whitelocke">Whitelocke\'s</a> Memorials (cited in Bowley, 2001), a letter of 17 April 1651 explains: "Tromp came to <a href="/wiki/Pendennis_Castle" title="Pendennis Castle">Pendennis</a> and related that he had been to Scilly to demand reparation for the Dutch ships and goods taken by them; and receiving no satisfactory answer, he had, according to his Commission, declared war on them".\n</p><p>As most of England was now in parliamentarian hands, war was declared specifically upon the Isles of Scilly.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Royalist_surrender">Royalist surrender</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Royalist surrender">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In June 1651, soon after the declaration of war, the Parliamentarian forces under Admiral <a href="/wiki/Robert_Blake_(admiral)" title="Robert Blake (admiral)">Robert Blake</a> forced the Royalist fleet to surrender. The Dutch fleet, no longer under threat, left without firing a shot. Due to the obscurity of one nation\'s declaration of war against a small part of another, the Dutch did not officially declare peace.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peace_treaty">Peace treaty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Peace treaty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1986, Roy Duncan, historian and Chairman of the Isles of Scilly Council, wrote to the Dutch Embassy in London to dispose of the myth that the islands were still at war. Embassy staff found the myth to be accurate and Duncan invited the Dutch ambassador <a href="/wiki/Jonkheer" title="Jonkheer">Jonkheer</a> Rein Huydecoper to visit the islands and sign a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a>. Peace was declared on 17 April 1986, 335 years after the "war" began. The Dutch ambassador joked that it must have been harrowing to the Scillonians "to know we could have attacked at any moment."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Authenticity">Authenticity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Authenticity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bowley (2001) argues that the letter in Whitelock\'s Memorials is the probable origin of the "declaring war" legend: "Tromp had no \'Commission\' from his government to declare war on the rebels in Scilly; but he did come to try \xe2\x80\x94 by a show of force, threats and even by violence perhaps, although this never happened \xe2\x80\x94 to seek reparation for Royalist piracies, but short of resorting to any action which might offend the Commonwealth. ... even if [a war] had occurred in 1651, all matters pertaining would have been resolved in 1654 as a part of the treaty between England and the United Provinces at the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_Dutch_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First Dutch War">First Dutch War</a>".\n</p><p>The reality of this war is disputed by Graeme Donald. In his book <i>Loose Cannons: 101 Myths, Mishaps and Misadventurers of Military History</i><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> he argues that no such war could have existed because the Isles were not an independent nation. The Netherlands could not sign a peace treaty with islands that are only a part of the United Kingdom.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/List_of_wars_extended_by_diplomatic_irregularity" title="List of wars extended by diplomatic irregularity">List of wars extended by diplomatic irregularity</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Arauco_War" title="Arauco War">Arauco War</a> (1536\xe2\x80\x931881), another example of a very long war</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Anglo-Zanzibar_War" title="Anglo-Zanzibar War">Anglo\xe2\x80\x93Zanzibar War</a>, generally considered the world\'s shortest war</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Three_Hundred_and_Thirty_Five_Years%27_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dt>Notes</dt></dl>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation web"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,961310,00.html">"Britain: Peace in Our Time"</a>.
