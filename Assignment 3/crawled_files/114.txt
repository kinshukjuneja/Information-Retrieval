colspan="2">\n</td></tr><tr style="display:none"><td colspan="2">\n</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Zheng Xiaosong</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh-Hans">\xe9\x83\x91\xe6\x99\x93\xe6\x9d\xbe</span>; September 1959 \xe2\x80\x93 20 October 2018) was a Chinese politician and diplomat. He was Director of the <a href="/wiki/Macau_Liaison_Office" title="Macau Liaison Office">Macau Liaison Office</a>, a ministerial-level position, until he fell to his death from his residence in October 2018. He formerly served as Deputy Director of the <a href="/wiki/International_Liaison_Department_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="International Liaison Department of the Communist Party of China">International Liaison Department of the Communist Party of China</a>, Vice Governor of <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a> Province, and secretary-general<sup id="cite_ref-YouQuan01_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YouQuan01-1">&#91;n 1&#93;</a></sup> of the <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Fujian" title="Politics of Fujian">Fujian Provincial Committee</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> (CPC).\n</p><p>Zheng was a member of the <a href="/wiki/19th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="19th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">19th</a> <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">Central Committee of the Communist Party of China</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and a delegate to the <a href="/wiki/13th_National_People%27s_Congress" title="13th National People&#39;s Congress">13th National People\'s Congress</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" href="/w/index.php?title=Zheng_Xiaosong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Zheng was born in September 1959 in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China. He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oslo" title="University of Oslo">University of Oslo</a> with a degree in Norwegian language, and received diplomatic training at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> from 1996 to 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>For most of his career Zheng served in diplomatic and international finance positions, including first-grade secretary of the Western Europe Bureau of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People&#39;s Republic of China">Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs</a>, Director of the International Department of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Finance_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Finance of the People&#39;s Republic of China">Chinese Ministry of Finance</a>, Assistant Finance Minister, and Chinese Executive Director of the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Development_Bank" title="Asian Development Bank">Asian Development Bank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Zheng was promoted to major political posts after President <a href="/wiki/Xi_Jinping" title="Xi Jinping">Xi Jinping</a> came to power. He was appointed Vice Governor of <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a> Province in July 2013, and later became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Provincial_party_standing_committee" title="Provincial party standing committee">standing committee</a> and secretary-general<sup id="cite_ref-YouQuan01_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YouQuan01-1">&#91;n 1&#93;</a></sup> of the <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Fujian" title="Politics of Fujian">Fujian Provincial Committee</a> of the Communist Party of China (CPC).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-China_Vitae_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-China_Vitae-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2016, Zheng was transferred back to Beijing to serve as Deputy Director of the <a href="/wiki/International_Liaison_Department_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="International Liaison Department of the Communist Party of China">International Liaison Department of the Communist Party of China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A month before the <a href="/wiki/19th_National_Congress_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="19th National Congress of the Communist Party of China">19th National Congress of the Communist Party of China</a> (CPC), he was promoted to Director of the <a href="/wiki/Macau_Liaison_Office" title="Macau Liaison Office">Macau Liaison Office</a>, a ministerial-level<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> position in charge of relations between the central government and the <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a> Special Administrative Region.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was elected as a member of the <a href="/wiki/19th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="19th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">19th Central Committee of the CPC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zheng_Xiaosong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>On 20 October 2018, Zheng died in Macau after falling from a tall building where he lived.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was 59. The <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_and_Macau_Affairs_Office" title="Hong Kong and Macau Affairs Office">Hong Kong and Macau Affairs Office</a> in Beijing issued a statement saying that he had suffered from depression, with the implication that he had committed suicide.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On Chinese social media many expressed concern and sadness about his depression, but others noted that at least 7 other Chinese officials have fallen from buildings this year, with one injured and at least 6 dead.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Although there is no
