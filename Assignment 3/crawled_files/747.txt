The area had population of around 1 million based on the 2001 census, and estimated population of over 1.5 million in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-hatherley_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatherley-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is the most populated part of <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">South East England</a>, excluding <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.\n</p><p>The area is sometimes referred to as <b>Solent City</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> but the term is controversial.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <i>Solent Local Enterprise Partnership</i>, a collaboration between local authorities and businesses set up in 2011 by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Business,_Innovation_and_Skills" title="Department for Business, Innovation and Skills">Department for Business, Innovation and Skills</a>, has a population of over 1.3 million (2011).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 12 November 2013 the government announced the second wave of <i>City Deals</i>, with a successful joint bid of Southampton and Portsmouth providing \xc2\xa3953 million of investment into the Solent LEP.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div href="/w/index.php?title=South_Hampshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Harold_Wilson" title="Harold Wilson">Harold Wilson</a>\'s Labour government commissioned town planner <a href="/wiki/Colin_Buchanan_(town_planner)" title="Colin Buchanan (town planner)">Colin Buchanan</a> in 1965 to study the region. He found a region of growing economic importance, in desperate need of proper planning to avoid unplanned sprawl, and suggested the construction of a <a href="/wiki/Modern_architecture#Urban_design_and_mass_housing" title="Modern architecture">modernist</a> urban area between Southampton and Portsmouth.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However this was resisted by local authorities who occupied the proposed development sites, and Buchanan\'s plans were never put into effect.<sup id="cite_ref-hatherley_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatherley-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Instead, as a result of high-tech industry and services, the area grew largely without overall planning to become perhaps the most densely populated region of the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-hatherley_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatherley-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the late 20th century mass manufacturing of ordinary commodities declined leaving several town and city neighbourhoods impoverished and suffering unemployment, however by the early 21st century high income and retirement properties came to dominate some neighbourhoods, particularly close to universities, and many suburbs and exurbs. Centrally planned building programmes included large estates, carefully connected by new road networks, of commercial and residential use by <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_County_Council" title="Hampshire County Council">Hampshire County Council</a>\'s architects, led by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Stansfield_Smith" title="Colin Stansfield Smith">Colin Stansfield Smith</a>. Otherwise development has tended to be a case-by-case basis for retail parks, business parks and housing.<sup id="cite_ref-hatherley_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hatherley-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Hampshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The region may be subdivided into two conurbations, one around Southampton and one around Portsmouth. Most of the area is in the Hampshire districts of <a href="/wiki/Gosport" title="Gosport">Gosport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Fareham" title="Borough of Fareham">Fareham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Havant" title="Borough of Havant">Havant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Eastleigh" title="Borough of Eastleigh">Eastleigh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth" title="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a>. All of these districts have high <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_districts_by_population_density" title="List of English districts by population density">population densities</a>, with Portsmouth and Southampton being the most densely populated districts in England outside <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Other parts of the area are in the <a href="/wiki/Test_Valley" title="Test Valley">Test Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Hampshire" title="East Hampshire">East Hampshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/City_of_Winchester" title="City of Winchester">City of Winchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Forest_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="New Forest (district)">New Forest</a> districts all of which have comparatively lower population densities. Small parts of the area are also in the <a href="/wiki/West_Sussex" title="West Sussex">West Sussex</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Chichester_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chichester (district)">Chichester</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
