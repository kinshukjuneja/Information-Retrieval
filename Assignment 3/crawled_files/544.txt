The <a href="/wiki/British_monarch" class="mw-redirect" title="British monarch">British monarch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, remained <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> as <a href="/wiki/Queen_of_Uganda" title="Queen of Uganda">Queen of Uganda</a> until the link with the British monarchy was severed on 9 October 1963 and the <i>Kabaka</i> (King) of Uganda, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Mutesa_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Mutesa II">Edward Mutesa II</a>, became the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_Uganda" title="President of Uganda">President of Uganda</a>.\n</p><p>Direct British rule of the <a href="/wiki/Uganda_Protectorate" title="Uganda Protectorate">Uganda Protectorate</a> ended in 1962 with the Uganda Independence Act, which granted independence of the protectorate under the name "Uganda" but retained the British monarch, Elizabeth II, as nominal head of state and <a href="/wiki/Queen_of_Uganda" title="Queen of Uganda">Queen of Uganda</a>. Her constitutional roles as head of state were mostly delegated to the <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Uganda#Governor-General_of_Uganda,_1962\xe2\x80\x931963" title="List of governors of Uganda">Governor-General of Uganda</a> <a href="/wiki/Walter_Coutts" title="Walter Coutts">Sir Walter Coutts</a>, who was the only holder of the office.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Milton_Obote" title="Milton Obote">Milton Obote</a> held office as <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">prime minister</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a>.\n</p><p>In 1963, Uganda adopted a new constitution which abolished the links with the British monarchy. Uganda became a <a href="/wiki/Republics_in_the_Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Republics in the Commonwealth of Nations">republic within the Commonwealth</a>. However, the new Ugandan state was deliberately referred to as a state rather than a republic, and the constituent native kingdoms (such as <a href="/wiki/Buganda" title="Buganda">Buganda</a>) continued in existence.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The description "State" implied that the country was not a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a> but instead a federation of tribal kingdoms. Following the proclamation of the State of Uganda on 9 October 1963, the <i>Kabaka</i> (King) of Uganda, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Mutesa_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Mutesa II">Edward Mutesa II</a>, became the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_Uganda" title="President of Uganda">President of Uganda</a>.
