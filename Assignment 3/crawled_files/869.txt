The First Minister is responsible for the exercise of functions by the Cabinet of the Welsh Government; policy development and coordination; relationships with the rest of the United Kingdom, Europe and Wales abroad; and staffing.\n</p><p>The official office of the First Minister is in <a href="/wiki/T%C5%B7_Hywel" title="T\xc5\xb7 Hywel">T\xc5\xb7 Hywel</a>, previously known as Crickhowell House, and the <a href="/wiki/Senedd" title="Senedd">Senedd</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_Bay" title="Cardiff Bay">Cardiff Bay</a>. An office is also kept at the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Buildings,_Cathays_Park" title="Crown Buildings, Cathays Park">Crown Buildings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cathays_Park" title="Cathays Park">Cathays Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>When initially set up under the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Wales_Act_1998" title="Government of Wales Act 1998">Government of Wales Act 1998</a>, Section 51(1), the post was known as <i>Assembly First Secretary</i> (in <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> <i>Prif Ysgrifennydd Cymru</i>), as Wales was given a less powerful assembly and executive than either <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> or <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. The choice of title was also attributed to the fact that the Welsh term for <i>First Minister</i>, <i>Prif Weinidog</i>, may also be translated as <i>Prime Minister</i>, so a different title was chosen to avoid confusion with the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a>. The change of title occurred after the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrats</a> <a href="/wiki/First_Morgan_government" title="First Morgan government">formed</a> a <a href="/wiki/Coalition_government" title="Coalition government">coalition government</a> with <a href="/wiki/Wales_Labour_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Wales Labour Party">Labour</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Assembly" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh Assembly">Welsh Assembly</a> in October 2000. The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Wales_Act_2006" title="Government of Wales Act 2006">Government of Wales Act 2006</a> allowed for the post to be officially known as the First Minister and also made the First Minister <i><a href="/wiki/Welsh_Seal" title="Welsh Seal">Keeper of the Welsh Seal</a></i>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Minister_of_Wales&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Under the arrangements in the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Wales_Act_1998" title="Government of Wales Act 1998">Government of Wales Act 1998</a>, executive functions are conferred on the National Assembly for Wales and then separately delegated to the First Minister and to other Cabinet Ministers and staff as appropriate.\n</p><p>Until the Government of Wales Act 2006, these were delegated powers of the <a href="/wiki/UK_government" class="mw-redirect" title="UK government">UK government</a>. Since that Act came into force in May 2007, however, the First Minister is appointed by the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">monarch</a> and represents <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">the Crown</a> in Wales. Whilst this has little practical difference, it was a huge symbolic shift as for the first time the head of government in Wales is appointed by the Crown on the advice of the elected representatives of the Welsh people.\n</p><p>The First Minister appoints the Welsh Ministers, Deputy Welsh Ministers and the <a href="/wiki/Counsel_General_for_Wales" title="Counsel General for Wales">Counsel General for Wales</a> (collectively known as the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Government" title="Welsh Government">Welsh Government</a>), with the approval of Her Majesty.\n</p><p>Following separation between the legislative and the executive on the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Wales_Act_2006" title="Government of Wales Act 2006">Government of Wales Act 2006</a> (upon appointment of the First Minister, 25 May 2007), the Welsh Ministers exercise functions in their own right.  Any further transfers of executive functions from the UK Government will be made directly to the Welsh Ministers (with their consent) by an Order in Council approved by Parliament.\n</p><p>The First Minister is accountable and responsible for:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Exercise of functions by the Cabinet of the Welsh Government.</li>\n<li>Policy development and coordination of policy.</li>\n<li>The relationships with the rest of the United Kingdom, Europe and Wales Abroad.</li>\n<li>Staffing/Civil Service</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline"
