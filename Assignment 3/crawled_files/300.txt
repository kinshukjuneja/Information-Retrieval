This is conservative, because the system is 99% sure that the player\'s skill is actually higher than what is displayed as their rank.\n</p><p>The system can be used with arbitrary scales, but Microsoft uses a scale from 0 to 50 for Xbox Live. Hence, players start with a rank of <span class="mwe-math-element"><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"  alttext="{\\displaystyle R=25-3\\cdot {\\frac {25}{3}}=0}">\n  <semantics>\n    <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n      <mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">\n        <mi>R</mi>\n        <mo>=</mo>\n        <mn>25</mn>\n        <mo>&#x2212;<!-- \xe2\x88\x92 --></mo>\n        <mn>3</mn>\n        <mo>&#x22C5;<!-- \xe2\x8b\x85 --></mo>\n        <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n          <mfrac>\n            <mn>25</mn>\n            <mn>3</mn>\n          </mfrac>\n        </mrow>\n        <mo>=</mo>\n        <mn>0</mn>\n      </mstyle>\n    </mrow>\n    <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\\displaystyle R=25-3\\cdot {\\frac {25}{3}}=0}</annotation>\n  </semantics>\n</math></span><img src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/76c87cc4a60ed58a063f022d1492831191c317c0" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" aria-hidden="true" style="vertical-align: -1.838ex; width:20.291ex; height:5.176ex;" alt="R=25-3\\cdot {\\frac  {25}{3}}=0"/></span>. This means that a new player\'s defeat results in a large sigma loss, which partially or completely compensates their mu loss. This explains why people may gain ranks from losses.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_in_other_projects">Use in other projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
