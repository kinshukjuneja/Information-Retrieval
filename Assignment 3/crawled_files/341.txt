Endurance Championship</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">auto racing</a> world championship organized by the <a href="/wiki/Automobile_Club_de_l%27Ouest" title="Automobile Club de l&#39;Ouest">Automobile Club de l\'Ouest</a> (ACO) and sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="F\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">F\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Internationale de l\'Automobile</a> (FIA).  The series supersedes the ACO\'s former <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_Le_Mans_Cup" title="Intercontinental Le Mans Cup">Intercontinental Le Mans Cup</a> which began in <a href="/wiki/2010_Intercontinental_Le_Mans_Cup" title="2010 Intercontinental Le Mans Cup">2010</a>, and is the first endurance series of world championship status since the demise of the <a href="/wiki/World_Sportscar_Championship" title="World Sportscar Championship">World Sportscar Championship</a> at the end of <a href="/wiki/1992_World_Sportscar_Championship" title="1992 World Sportscar Championship">1992</a>.  The World Endurance Championship name was previously used by the FIA from <a href="/wiki/World_Sportscar_Championship" title="World Sportscar Championship">1981 to 1985</a>.\n</p><p>The series features multiple classes of cars competing in <a href="/wiki/Endurance_racing_(motorsport)" title="Endurance racing (motorsport)">endurance races</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Sports_prototype" title="Sports prototype">sports prototypes</a> competing in the <a href="/wiki/Le_Mans_Prototype" title="Le Mans Prototype">Le Mans Prototype</a> categories, and production-based <a href="/wiki/Grand_tourer" title="Grand tourer">grand tourers</a> competing in the <a href="/wiki/LM_GTE" title="LM GTE">LM GTE</a> categories.  World champion titles are awarded to the top scoring manufacturers and drivers over the season,<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_WEC_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_WEC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIA_WMSC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_WMSC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> while other cups and trophies will be awarded for drivers and <a href="/wiki/Privateer_(motorsport)" title="Privateer (motorsport)">private teams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ACO_WEC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACO_WEC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FIA_World_Endurance_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The World Endurance Championship was first run in 2012 as a replacement for the <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_Le_Mans_Cup" title="Intercontinental Le Mans Cup">Intercontinental Le Mans Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The World Endurance Championship follows much of the format of the Intercontinental Le Mans Cup and features nine endurance races across the world, including the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a>, with all races being at least six hours in duration.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_WMSC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_WMSC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The calendar includes four races in Europe, two in the Americas, two in Asia and one in the Middle East,<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_WEC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_WEC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> with a possible future expansion.<sup id="cite_ref-PLM_WEC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PLM_WEC-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  There are four categories: LMP1 and LMP2 prototypes and the GTE category, divided into GTE Pro for teams with professional driver line-ups, and GTE Am for teams featuring a mixture of amateur drivers.\n</p><p>Six titles are awarded each season based on total point tally, with two being deemed world championships: Manufacturers\' World Endurance Champion and Drivers\' World Champion.  The GTE Pro class champion are awarded a World Cup, while the leaders in LMP2 and GTE Am are awarded a Trophy.  The final Trophy is awarded to the best <a href="/wiki/Privateer_(motorsport)" title="Privateer (motorsport)">privateer</a> team amongst all four classes.<sup id="cite_ref-ACO_WEC_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ACO_WEC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The points system is similar to that used in the FIA\'s other world championships, awarding points to the top ten finishers on a sliding point margin scale from first to tenth.  Cars finishing the race but classified eleventh or further are awarded a half point. For the 24 Hours of Le Mans points are worth roughly 1.5x as much (i.e. 25 points for a win is worth 38 points at Le Mans).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FIA_World_Endurance_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Faced with declining manufacturer interest, the FIA commissioned a study into the future regulations of the category. Known as "Hypercar", the proposal called for move away from <a href="/wiki/Le_Mans_Prototype" title="Le Mans Prototype">Le Mans Prototype</a> entries and less reliance on hybrid technologies. The proposal was designed to make the championship more appealing to car manufacturers and cited flagship models such as the <a href="/wiki/Aston_Martin_Vulcan" title="Aston Martin Vulcan">Aston Martin Vulcan</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren_Senna" title="McLaren Senna">McLaren Senna GTR</a> as examples of the cars the category was hoping to attract.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Races">Races</span><span
