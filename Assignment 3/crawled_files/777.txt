Forces Cyprus)</th><td>\nMajor-General <a href="/wiki/James_Illingworth" title="James Illingworth">James Illingworth</a> OBE</td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>British Forces Cyprus</b> (<b>BFC</b>) is the name given to the <a href="/wiki/British_Armed_Forces" title="British Armed Forces">British Armed Forces</a> stationed in the UK <a href="/wiki/Akrotiri_and_Dhekelia" title="Akrotiri and Dhekelia">Sovereign Base Areas of Akrotiri and Dhekelia</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Cyprus" title="Geography of Cyprus">island of Cyprus</a> and at a number of related \'retained sites\' in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Cyprus" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Cyprus">Republic of Cyprus</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> retains a military presence on the island in order to keep a strategic location at the eastern end of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, for use as a staging point for forces sent to locations in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. BFC is a tri-service <a href="/wiki/Command_(military_formation)" title="Command (military formation)">command</a>, with all three services based on the island reporting to it. At present, there are approximately 3,500 personnel serving in Cyprus.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" Garrison</div></div></div>\n<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus#Independence" title="Cyprus">establishment of the Republic of Cyprus</a> in 1960, the UK retained two Sovereign Base Areas in Akrotiri and Dhekelia and an RAF <a href="/wiki/Air_marshal" title="Air marshal">air marshal</a> was appointed as the Administrator of the Sovereign Base Areas.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Treaty of Establishment also provided British access to 40 \'retained sites\' in the republic of Cyprus; these included numerous radar stations, several ports, a range of accommodation and support facilities and a firing range.<sup id="cite_ref-treaty_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-treaty-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The following year the <b>British Forces Near East</b> organization was created, the command of which was then held concurrently with that of the post of Administrator.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On 1 March 1961 the Southern Group of Middle East Air Force became <a href="/wiki/Near_East_Air_Force_(Royal_Air_Force)" title="Near East Air Force (Royal Air Force)">Near East Air Force</a> and was based in <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>. By 1962 the title <i>British Forces in Cyprus</i> was in official use.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> British Forces Cyprus retains the right to use 13 retained sites with the remaining 27 having been returned to Cyprus after the Ministry of Defence no longer required them. The most recent sites to be returned were the Berengaria Married Quarters in 2011 because they had become obsolete and the firing range on the Akamas peninsula in 1999-2001 because the areas training value was deemed less important than the environmental damage inflicted on an ecologically important area and the consequent political liability to British Forces Cyprus.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div style="clear:both;"></div>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Command">Command</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Forces_Cyprus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Command">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Episkopi is the current command centre of British Forces Cyprus. The commander of the Sovereign Base Areas/British Forces Cyprus (CBF) is a <a href="/wiki/Two-star_rank" title="Two-star rank">two-star</a> appointment, alternating every three years between the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">Army</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">RAF</a>. Consequently, the Chief of Staff British Forces Cyprus (COS) is a <a href="/wiki/One-star_rank" title="One-star rank">one-star</a> appointment from the opposite service of the commander. <a href="/wiki/Episkopi_Cantonment" title="Episkopi Cantonment">Episkopi Cantonment</a> is home to the <a href="/wiki/Akrotiri_and_Dhekelia" title="Akrotiri and Dhekelia">Sovereign Base Areas Administration</a>, the civilian authority in the territory.<sup id="cite_ref-treaty_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-treaty-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
