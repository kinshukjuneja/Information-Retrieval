href="/wiki/Language" title="Language">language</a> spoken in an area of a <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a>, whether it be a small area, a <a href="/wiki/Federalism" title="Federalism">federal</a> <a href="/wiki/State_(administrative_division)" title="State (administrative division)">state</a> or <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a>, or some wider area.\n</p><p>Internationally, for the purposes of the <a href="/wiki/European_Charter_for_Regional_or_Minority_Languages" title="European Charter for Regional or Minority Languages">European Charter for Regional or Minority Languages</a>, <i>"regional or minority languages" means languages that are: </i>\n</p>\n<ol><li><i>traditionally used within a given territory of a State by nationals of that State who form a group numerically smaller than the rest of the State\'s population; and </i></li>\n<li><i>different from the official language(s) of that State</i><sup id="cite_ref-COE-Charter_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COE-Charter-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ol>\n<p>Recognition of regional or minority languages must not be confused with recognition as an <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" speakers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Influence of number of speakers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are many cases when a regional language can claim greater numbers of speakers than certain languages which happen to be <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official languages</a> of sovereign states. For example, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a> (a regional language of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, albeit the <a href="/wiki/National_language" title="National language">national language</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a>) has more speakers than <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a> or <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Catalan only has 4M L1 speakers (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  A similar situation exists in China, where <a href="/wiki/Wu_Chinese" title="Wu Chinese">Wu</a>, spoken in southern <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a>, northern, and the general area of <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a> by more than 90 million speakers, is spoken natively by more speakers than <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>; <a href="/wiki/Yue_Chinese" title="Yue Chinese">Yue Chinese</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Varieties_of_Chinese" title="Varieties of Chinese">Chinese regional variety</a> spoken in <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and nearby areas in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> with more than 60 million local and overseas speakers (North America, parts of <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>), outnumbers <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> in number of speakers. Dialects of the <a href="/wiki/Min_Chinese" title="Min Chinese">Min</a> dialect group have over 70 million speakers, mainly in <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a> and in nearby <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, but also in the <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="Southeast Asian">Southeast Asian</a> countries of <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_official_languages">Relationship with official languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regional_language&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relationship with official languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In some cases, a regional language may be closely related to the state\'s main language or <a href="/wiki/Official_language" title="Official language">official language</a>. For example:\n</p>\n<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Frisian_languages" title="Frisian languages">Frisian languages</a> spoken in the Netherlands and Germany, which belong to the <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic</a> family.</li>\n<li>The <a href="/wiki/Modern_Gutnish" class="mw-redirect" title="Modern Gutnish">Gutnish language</a>, a regional language spoken in <a href="/wiki/Gotland" title="Gotland">Gotland</a> and related to the Swedish language.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Westrobothnian" title="Westrobothnian">Westrobothnian</a> is another Swedish regional language native to historical <a href="/wiki/Westrobothnia" class="mw-redirect" title="Westrobothnia">Westrobothnia</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kurdish_languages" title="Kurdish languages">Kurdish</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan" title="Kurdistan">Kurdistan</a>, which is an autonomous region in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Iraq" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Iraq">Northern Iraq</a>, Northwestern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and southeastern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Assyrian_Neo-Aramaic" title="Assyrian Neo-Aramaic">Assyrian Neo-Aramaic</a> in northern Iraq, northeastern <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, southeastern Turkey and northwestern Iran.</li>\n<li>Regional varieties in China are considered part of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Macrolanguage" class="mw-redirect" title="Macrolanguage">macrolanguage</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin</a> being the most commonly spoken and <a href="/wiki/Standard_Chinese" title="Standard Chinese">Standard Chinese</a> based on the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_dialect" title="Beijing dialect">Beijing dialect</a>.\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Wu_Chinese" title="Wu Chinese">Wu</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a> and eastern <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Yue_Chinese" title="Yue Chinese">Yue Chinese</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>, parts of <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> is the regional standard variety for Yue-speaking areas</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Hakka_Chinese" title="Hakka Chinese">Hakka</a>, in parts of <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Min_Chinese" title="Min Chinese">Min</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Xiang_Chinese" title="Xiang Chinese">Xiang</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Gan_Chinese" title="Gan Chinese">Gan</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Jiangxi" title="Jiangxi">Jiangxi</a>.</li></ul></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kashubian_language" title="Kashubian language">Kashubian</a>, a regional language of <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Limburgish" title="Limburgish">Limburgish</a>, a regional language in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the <a language</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Occitan" class="mw-redirect" title="Occitan">Occitan</a>, most widely spoken across the Pyrenees in France and Catalonia, together with Catalan, forms a subgroup
