href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">crimes</a>. The <b>criminal justice system</b> is a series of <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> agencies and institutions whose goal is to identify and catch the law-breakers and to inflict a form of punishment on them. Other goals include the <a href="/wiki/Rehabilitation_(penology)" title="Rehabilitation (penology)">rehabilitation</a> of offenders, preventing other crimes, and moral support for victims. The primary institutions of the criminal justice system are the <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prosecutor" title="Prosecutor">prosecution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Criminal_defense_lawyer" title="Criminal defense lawyer">defense</a> lawyers, the <a href="/wiki/Court" title="Court">courts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prison" The purpose of law is to provide an objective set of rules for governing conduct and maintaining order in a society.\n</p><p>The oldest known codified law is the <a href="/wiki/Code_of_Hammurabi" title="Code of Hammurabi">Code of Hammurabi</a>, dating back to about 1754 BC. The preface directly credits the laws to the code of hammurabi  of <a href="/wiki/Ur" title="Ur">Ur</a>. In different parts of the world, law could be established by <a href="/wiki/Legalism_(Chinese_philosophy)" title="Legalism (Chinese philosophy)">philosophers</a> or <a href="/wiki/Theocracy" title="Theocracy">religion</a>. In the modern world, laws are typically created and enforced by governments.  These codified laws may coexist with or contradict other forms of social control, such as religious proscriptions, professional rules and ethics, or the cultural mores and customs of a society.\n</p><p>Within the realm of codified law, there are generally two forms of law that the courts are concerned with.  <a href="/wiki/Private_law" title="Private law">Civil laws</a> are rules and regulations which govern transactions and grievances between individual citizens.  <a href="/wiki/Criminal_law" title="Criminal law">Criminal law</a> is concerned with actions which are dangerous or harmful to society as a whole, in which prosecution is pursued not by an individual but rather by the state. The purpose of criminal law is to provide the specific definition of what constitutes a crime and to prescribe punishments for committing such a crime.  No criminal law can be valid unless it includes both of these factors.  The subject of criminal justice is, of course, primarily concerned with the enforcement of criminal law.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminal_justice_system">Criminal justice system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criminal justice <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prisons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">probation</a> agencies.</li></ol>\n<p>In the criminal justice system, these distinct agencies operate together as the principal means of maintaining the <a href="/wiki/Rule_of_law" title="Rule of law">rule of law</a> within <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" href="/wiki/File:Adult_Criminal_Justice_System.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>This image shows the procedure in the criminal justice system</div></div></div>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_enforcement">Law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement" title="Law enforcement">Law enforcement</a></div>\n<p>The first contact a defendant has with the criminal justice system is usually with the <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> (or <i>law enforcement</i>) who investigate the suspected wrongdoing and make an <a href="/wiki/Arrest" title="Arrest">arrest</a>, but if the suspect is dangerous to the whole nation, a national level <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement agency</a> is called in. When warranted, law enforcement agencies or police officers are empowered to use force and other forms of legal coercion and means to effect public and <a href="/wiki/Social_order" title="Social order">social order</a>. The term is most commonly associated with police departments of a <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">state</a> that are authorized to exercise the <a href="/wiki/Police_power_(United_States_constitutional_law)" title="Police power (United States constitutional law)">police power</a> of that state within a defined legal or territorial area of responsibility. The word comes from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>politia</i> ("civil administration"), which itself derives from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> \xcf\x80\xcf\x8c\xce\xbb\xce\xb9\xcf\x82, for <i>polis</i> ("city").<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The first police force comparable to the present-day police was established in 1667 under King <a href="/wiki/Louis_XIV" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis XIV">Louis XIV</a> in France, although modern police usually trace their origins to the 1800 establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Marine_Police_Force" title="Marine Police Force">Marine Police</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Glasgow_Police" title="City of Glasgow Police">Glasgow Police</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleonic</a> <a href="/wiki/Prefecture_of_Police" title="Prefecture of Police">police of Paris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Police are primarily concerned with keeping the peace and enforcing <a href="/wiki/Criminal_law" title="Criminal law">criminal law</a> based on their particular mission and jurisdiction. Formed in 1908, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">Federal Bureau of Investigation</a> began as an entity which could investigate and enforce specific federal laws as an investigative and "<a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement agency</a>" in the United States;<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> this, however, has constituted only a small portion of overall policing activity.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Policing has included an array of activities in different contexts, but the predominant ones are concerned with <a href="/wiki/Public_order" class="mw-redirect" title="Public order">order maintenance</a> and the provision of services.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  During modern times, such endeavors contribute toward fulfilling a shared mission among law enforcement organizations with respect to the traditional policing mission of deterring crime and maintaining societal order.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Courts">Courts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Courts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Court" title="Court">Court</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg/220px-Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg/330px-Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg/440px-Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg 2x" data-file-width="828" data-file-height="626" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Old_Bailey_Microcosm_edited.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A trial at the <a href="/wiki/Old_Bailey" title="Old Bailey">Old Bailey</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, c. 1808</div></div></div>\n<p>The courts serve as the venue where disputes are then settled and justice is administered.  With regard to criminal justice, there are a number of critical people in any court setting. These critical people are referred to as the courtroom work group and include both professional and non professional individuals.  These include the <a href="/wiki/Judge" title="Judge">judge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prosecutor" title="Prosecutor">prosecutor</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Defense_attorney" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense attorney">defense attorney</a>.  The judge, or magistrate, is a person, elected or appointed, who is knowledgeable in the law, and whose function is to objectively administer the legal proceedings and offer a final decision to dispose of a case.\n</p><p>In the U.S. and in a growing number of nations, <a href="/wiki/Guilt_(law)" title="Guilt (law)">guilt</a> or innocence (although in the U.S. a jury can never find a defendant "innocent" but rather "not guilty") is decided through the <a href="/wiki/Adversarial_system" title="Adversarial system">adversarial system</a>.  In this system, two parties will both offer their version of events and <a href="/wiki/Argument" title="Argument">argue</a> their case before the court (sometimes before a judge or panel of judges, sometimes before a jury).  The case should be decided in favor of the party who offers the most sound and compelling arguments based on the law as applied to the facts of the case.\n</p><p>The prosecutor, or district attorney, is a <a href="/wiki/Lawyer" title="Lawyer">lawyer</a> who brings charges against a person, persons or corporate entity.  It is the prosecutor\'s duty to explain to the court what crime was committed and to detail what <a href="/wiki/Evidence" title="Evidence">evidence</a> has been found which incriminates the accused.  The prosecutor should not be confused with a <a href="/wiki/Plaintiff" title="Plaintiff">plaintiff</a> or plaintiff\'s counsel.  Although both serve the function of bringing a complaint before the court, the prosecutor is a servant of the state who makes accusations on behalf of the state in criminal proceedings, while the plaintiff is the complaining party in civil proceedings.\n</p><p>A defense attorney counsels the accused on the legal process, likely outcomes for the accused and suggests strategies.  The accused, not the lawyer, has the right to make final decisions regarding a number of fundamental points, including whether to testify, and to accept a plea offer or demand a jury trial in appropriate cases.  It is the defense attorney\'s duty to represent the interests of the client, raise procedural and evidentiary issues, and hold the prosecution to its burden of proving guilt beyond a reasonable doubt. Defense counsel may challenge evidence presented by the prosecution or present exculpatory evidence and argue on behalf of their client.  At trial, the defense attorney may attempt to offer a <a href="/wiki/Rebuttal" title="Rebuttal">rebuttal</a> to the prosecutor\'s accusations.\n</p><p>In the U.S., an accused person is entitled to a government-paid defense attorney if he or she is in jeopardy of losing his or her life and/or liberty. Those who cannot afford a private attorney may be provided one by the state.  Historically, however, the right to a defense attorney has not always been universal.  For example, in <a href="/wiki/Tudor_period" title="Tudor period">Tudor</a> England criminals accused of <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a> were not permitted to offer arguments in their defense.  In many jurisdictions, there is no right to an appointed attorney, if the accused is not in jeopardy of losing his or her liberty.\n</p><p>The final determination of guilt or innocence is typically made by a third party, who is supposed to be disinterested. This function may be performed by a judge, a panel of judges, or a <a href="/wiki/Jury" title="Jury">jury</a> panel composed of unbiased citizens. This process varies depending on the laws of the specific jurisdiction. In some places the panel (be it judges or a jury) is required to issue a unanimous decision, while in others only a majority <a href="/wiki/Vote" class="mw-redirect" title="Vote">vote</a> is required. In America, this process depends on the state, level of court, and even agreements between the prosecuting and defending parties.  Some nations do not use juries at all, or rely on theological or military authorities to issue verdicts.\n</p><p>Some cases can be disposed of without the need for a trial.  In fact, the vast majority are.  If the accused confesses his or her guilt, a shorter process may be employed and a judgment may be rendered more quickly. Some nations, such as America, allow <a href="/wiki/Plea_bargaining" class="mw-redirect" title="Plea bargaining">plea bargaining</a> in which the accused pleads guilty, <a href="/wiki/Nolo_contendere" title="Nolo contendere">nolo contendere</a> or not guilty, and may accept a diversion program or reduced punishment, where the prosecution\'s case is weak or in exchange for the cooperation of the accused against other people. This reduced sentence is sometimes a reward for sparing the state the expense of a formal trial. Many nations do not permit the use of plea bargaining, believing that it coerces innocent people to plead guilty in an attempt to avoid a harsh punishment.\n</p><p>The entire trial process, whatever the country, is fraught with problems and subject to criticism.  <a href="/wiki/Bias" title="Bias">Bias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Discrimination" title="Discrimination">discrimination</a> form an ever-present threat to an objective decision.  Any <a href="/wiki/Prejudice" title="Prejudice">prejudice</a> on the part of the lawyers, the judge, or jury members threatens to destroy the court\'s credibility. Some people argue that the often Byzantine rules governing courtroom conduct and processes restrict a layman\'s ability to participate, essentially reducing the legal process to a battle between the lawyers. In this case, the criticism is that the decision is based less on sound justice and more on the lawyer\'s eloquence and <a href="/wiki/Charisma" title="Charisma">charisma</a>. This is a particular problem when the lawyer performs in a substandard manner. The jury process is another area of frequent criticism, as there are few mechanisms to guard against poor judgment or incompetence on the part of the layman jurors. Judges themselves are very subject to bias subject to things as ordinary as the length of time since their last break.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Manipulations of the court system by defense and prosecution attorneys, law enforcement as well as the defendants have occurred and there have been cases where justice was denied.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a authorities, from the court system after the accused has been found guilty.  Like all other aspects of criminal justice, the administration of <a href="/wiki/Punishment" title="Punishment">punishment</a> has taken many different forms throughout history.  Early on, when civilizations lacked the resources necessary to construct and maintain prisons, <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Execution" class="mw-redirect" title="Execution">execution</a> were the primary forms of punishment.  Historically <a href="/wiki/Shame" title="Shame">shame</a> punishments and <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exile</a> have also been used as forms of censure.\n</p><p>The most publicly visible form of punishment in the modern era is the <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prison</a>.  Prisons may serve as detention centers for prisoners after trial. For containment of the accused, jails are used.  Early prisons were used primarily to sequester criminals and little thought was given to living conditions within their walls.  In America, the <a href="/wiki/Quaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Quaker">Quaker</a> movement is commonly credited with establishing the idea that prisons should be used to reform criminals. Faisalabad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Faisalabad" title="Faisalabad">Faisalabad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Punishment (in the form of prison time) may serve a variety of purposes.  First, and most obviously, the incarceration of criminals removes them from the general population and inhibits their ability to perpetrate further crimes. A new goal of prison punishments is to offer criminals a chance to be rehabilitated.  Many modern prisons offer schooling or job training to prisoners as a chance to learn a vocation and thereby earn a legitimate living when they are returned to society.  Religious institutions also have a presence in many prisons, with the goal of teaching ethics and instilling a sense of morality in the prisoners. If a prisoner is released before his time is served, he is released as a parole. This means that they are released, but the restrictions are greater than that of someone on probation.\n</p><p>There are numerous other forms of punishment which are commonly used in conjunction with or in place of prison terms.  Monetary <a href="/wiki/Fine_(penalty)" title="Fine (penalty)">fines</a> are one of the oldest forms of punishment still used today.  These fines may be paid to the state or to the victims as a form of reparation.  <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">Probation</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_arrest" title="House arrest">house arrest</a> are also sanctions which seek to limit a person\'s mobility and his or her opportunities to commit crimes without actually placing them in a prison setting. Furthermore, many jurisdictions may require some form of public or community service as a form of reparations for lesser offenses.  In Corrections, the Department ensures court-ordered, pre-sentence chemical dependency assessments, related Drug Offender Sentencing Alternative specific examinations and treatment will occur for offenders sentenced to Drug Offender Sentencing Alternative in compliance with RCW 9.94A.660.\n</p><p>Execution or <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">capital punishment</a> is still used around the world.  Its use is one of the most heavily debated aspects of the criminal justice system.  Some societies are willing to use executions as a form of political control, or for relatively minor misdeeds.  Other societies reserve execution for only the most sinister and brutal offenses.  Others still have discontinued the practice entirely, believing the use of execution to be excessively cruel.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_discipline">Academic discipline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Academic discipline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The functional study of criminal justice is distinct from <a href="/wiki/Criminology" title="Criminology">criminology</a>, which involves the study of crime as a social phenomenon, causes of crime, criminal behavior, and other aspects of crime. It emerged as an academic discipline in the 1920s, beginning with <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley</a> police chief <a href="/wiki/August_Vollmer" title="August Vollmer">August Vollmer</a> who established a criminal justice program at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a> in 1916.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  Vollmer\'s work was carried on by his student, <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Winfield_Wilson" class="mw-redirect" title="Orlando Winfield Wilson">O.W. Wilson</a>, who led efforts to professionalize policing and reduce <a href="/wiki/Police_corruption" title="Police corruption">corruption</a>.  Other programs were established in the United States at <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_(Bloomington)" class="mw-redirect" title="Indiana University (Bloomington)">Indiana University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan_State_University" title="Michigan State University">Michigan State University</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_State_University" title="San Jose State University">San Jose State University</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-savelsberg_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-savelsberg-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  As of 1950, criminal justice students were estimated to number less than 1,000.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Until the 1960s, the primary focus of criminal justice in the United States was on policing and police science.\n</p><p>Throughout the 1960s and 1970s, crime rates soared and social issues took center stage in the public eye.  A number of new laws and studies focused federal resources on researching new approaches to crime control.  The <a href="/wiki/Warren_Court" title="Warren Court">Warren Court</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court</a> under <a href="/wiki/Chief_Justice_of_the_United_States" title="Chief Justice of the United States">Chief Justice</a> <a href="/wiki/Earl_Warren" title="Earl Warren">Earl Warren</a>), issued a series of rulings which redefined citizen\'s rights and substantially altered the powers and responsibilities of police and the courts.  The Civil Rights Era offered significant legal and ethical challenges to the <i><a href="/wiki/Status_quo" title="Status quo">status quo</a></i>.\n</p><p>In the late 1960s, with the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Law_Enforcement_Assistance_Administration" title="Law Enforcement Assistance Administration">Law Enforcement Assistance Administration</a> (LEAA) and associated policy changes that resulted with the <a href="/wiki/Omnibus_Crime_Control_and_Safe_Streets_Act_of_1968" title="Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968">Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968</a>. The LEAA provided <a href="/wiki/Grant_(money)" title="Grant (money)">grants</a> for criminology research, focusing on social aspects of crime.  By the 1970s, there were 729 academic programs in criminology and criminal justice in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-savelsberg_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-savelsberg-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  Largely thanks to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Law_Enforcement_Education_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Law Enforcement Education Program (page does not exist)">Law Enforcement Education Program</a>, criminal justice students numbered over 100,000 by 1975. Over time, scholars of criminal justice began to include <a href="/wiki/Criminology" title="Criminology">criminology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">sociology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a>, among others, to provide a more comprehensive view of the criminal justice system and the root causes of crime.  Criminal justice studies now combine the practical and technical policing skills with a study of social deviance as a whole.\n</p><p>Criminal justice degree programs at four-year institutions typically include coursework in statistics, methods of research, criminal justice, policing, U.S court systems, criminal courts, corrections, community corrections, criminal procedure, criminal law, victimology, juvenile justice, and a variety of special topics. A number of universities offer a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Criminal_Justice" title="Bachelor of Criminal Justice">Bachelor of Criminal Justice</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_criminal_justice" title="History of criminal justice">History of criminal justice</a></div>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Prisoners_whipped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Prisoners_whipped.jpg/180px-Prisoners_whipped.jpg" width="180" height="264" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Prisoners_whipped.jpg/270px-Prisoners_whipped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Prisoners_whipped.jpg/360px-Prisoners_whipped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1018" data-file-height="1494" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Prisoners_whipped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Prisoners at a <a href="/wiki/Flagellation" title="Flagellation">whipping</a> post in a <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a> prison, c. 1907</div></div></div>\n<p>The modern criminal justice system has evolved since <a href="/wiki/Ancient" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient">ancient</a> times, with new forms of <a href="/wiki/Punishment" title="Punishment">punishment</a>, added <a href="/wiki/Rights" title="Rights">rights</a> for <a href="/wiki/Offender" class="mw-redirect" title="Offender">offenders</a> and victims, and <a href="/wiki/Policing" class="mw-redirect" title="Policing">policing</a> reforms. These developments have reflected changing <a href="/wiki/Customs" title="Customs">customs</a>, political ideals, and economic conditions.  In ancient times through the Middle Ages, <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exile</a> was a common form of punishment.  During the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, payment to the victim (or the victim\'s family), known as <a href="/wiki/Wergild" class="mw-redirect" title="Wergild">wergild</a>, was another common punishment, including for violent crimes.  For those who could not afford to buy their way out of punishment, harsh penalties included various forms of <a href="/wiki/Corporal_punishment" title="Corporal punishment">corporal punishment</a>.  These included <a href="/wiki/Mutilation" title="Mutilation">mutilation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_branding" title="Human branding">branding</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Flogging" class="mw-redirect" title="Flogging">flogging</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Execution" class="mw-redirect" title="Execution">execution</a>.\n</p><p>Though a prison, <a href="/wiki/Stinche_Prison" title="Stinche Prison">Le Stinche</a>, existed as early as the 14th century in <a href="/wiki/Florence,_Italy" class="mw-redirect" title="Florence, Italy">Florence, Italy</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Incarceration" class="mw-redirect" title="Incarceration">incarceration</a> was not widely used until the 19th century.  Correctional reform in the United States was first initiated by <a href="/wiki/William_Penn" title="William Penn">William Penn</a>, towards the end of the 17th century.  For a time, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>\'s criminal code was revised to forbid <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">torture</a> and other forms of cruel punishment, with <a href="/wiki/Jail" class="mw-redirect" title="Jail">jails</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prisons</a> replacing corporal punishment.  These reforms were reverted, upon Penn\'s death in 1718.  Under pressure from a group of <a href="/wiki/Quakers" title="Quakers">Quakers</a>, these reforms were revived in Pennsylvania toward the end of the 18th century, and led to a marked drop in Pennsylvania\'s crime rate.  <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Colquhoun" title="Patrick Colquhoun">Patrick Colquhoun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Fielding" title="Henry Fielding">Henry Fielding</a> and others led significant reforms during the late eighteenth and early nineteenth centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The first official criminal justice systems was created by the British during the American revolution, they created the system to primarily justify hangings to the citizens of their government. In each selected area or/and district there was a magistrate that in today\'s time would be known as a judge. These individuals were in charge of determining if the Crown or also known as the British government had enough evidence to hang an individual for a crime. The British would not always hang an individual for committing a crime, there would also be trials for punishments that would be carried out by cleaning ships, prison ships, or be locked up on British mainland. During the American revolution the primary type of punishment was to be hanged or sent to prison ships such as the notorious HMS Jersey. After the American revolution the British-based criminal justice system was then adopted by other developing nations (Such as the United States).\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_police">Modern police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Modern police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The first modern police force is commonly said to be the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police_Service" title="Metropolitan Police Service">Metropolitan Police</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, established in 1829 by Sir <a href="/wiki/Robert_Peel" title="Robert Peel">Robert Peel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Based on the <a href="/wiki/Peelian_principles" title="Peelian principles">Peelian principles</a>, it promoted the <a href="/wiki/Preventive_police" title="Preventive police">preventive</a> role of police as a deterrent to urban <a href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">crime</a> and disorder.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UKP_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UKP-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  In the United States, police departments were first established in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> in 1838, and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1844.  Early on, police were not respected by the community, as <a href="/wiki/Police_corruption" title="Police corruption">corruption</a> was rampant.\n</p><p>In the 1920s, led by <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley, California</a> police chief, <a href="/wiki/August_Vollmer" title="August Vollmer">August Vollmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/O.W._Wilson" class="mw-redirect" title="O.W. Wilson">O.W. Wilson</a>, police began to professionalize, adopt new technologies, and place emphasis on training and professional qualifications of new hires.  Despite such reforms, police agencies were led by highly autocratic leaders, and there remained a lack of respect between police and the community.  Following urban unrest in the 1960s, police placed more emphasis on community relations, enacted reforms such as increased diversity in hiring, and many police agencies adopted <a href="/wiki/Community_policing" title="Community policing">community policing</a> strategies.\n</p><p>In the 1990s, <a href="/wiki/CompStat" title="CompStat">CompStat</a> was developed by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Police_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Police Department">New York Police Department</a> as an information-based system for tracking and <a href="/wiki/Crime_mapping" title="Crime mapping">mapping</a> crime patterns and trends, and holding police accountable for dealing with crime problems.  CompStat has since been replicated in police departments across the United States and around the world, with <a href="/wiki/Problem-oriented_policing" title="Problem-oriented policing">problem-oriented policing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Intelligence-led_policing" title="Intelligence-led policing">intelligence-led policing</a>, and other information-led policing strategies also adopted.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criminal_justice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Outline_of_criminal_justice" title="Outline of criminal justice">Outline of criminal justice</a> &#8211;
