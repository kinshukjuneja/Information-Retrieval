The other UK territories in the South Atlantic, <a href="/wiki/South_Georgia_and_the_South_Sandwich_Islands" title="South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands">South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands</a>, fall under the protection of <b>British Forces South Atlantic Islands</b> (BFSAI), formerly known as <b>British Forces Falkland Islands</b> (BFFI), which includes commitments from the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They are headed by the Commander of the British Forces South Atlantic Islands (CBFSAI).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Argentina invaded and took control of the Falklands on 2 April 1982. After recapturing the territory in June 1982, the UK invested heavily in the defence of the islands, the centrepiece of which was a new airfield at <a href="/wiki/RAF_Mount_Pleasant" title="RAF Mount Pleasant">RAF Mount Pleasant</a>, 27 miles (43&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Stanley,_Falkland_Islands" title="Stanley, Falkland Islands">Stanley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The base was opened in 1985, and became fully operational in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Military_of_the_Falkland_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Falkland Islands Defence Force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Defence_Force" title="Falkland Islands Defence Force">Falkland Islands Defence Force</a></div>\n<p>The Falkland Islands maintains its own part-time volunteer force, the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands_Defence_Force" title="Falkland Islands Defence Force">Falkland Islands Defence Force</a> (FIDF), previously known as the Falkland Islands Volunteer Corps. Although this unit existed in 1982 as a reinforcement for the Governor\'s detachment of Royal Marines, it did not play any part in the main conflict during the war of 1982, its members having spent the duration of the hostilities under house arrest by the Argentines after their surrender on the Argentine capture of the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The FIDF is now a company-strength <a href="/wiki/Light_infantry" title="Light infantry">light infantry</a> force with a permanent training Warrant Officer seconded from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Marines" title="Royal Marines">Royal Marines</a>. The FIDF operates in a number of roles and is fully integrated into the defence scheme for the islands. The FIDF has been trained by the Royal Navy to operate <a href="/wiki/Oerlikon_20_mm_cannon" title="Oerlikon 20 mm cannon">Oerlikon 20 mm cannon</a> and to board vessels suspected of fishery poaching.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Navy">Royal Navy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Military_of_the_Falkland_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Royal Navy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg/220px-HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg/330px-HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg/440px-HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1351" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HMS_Dauntless-1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/HMS_Dauntless_(D33)" title="HMS Dauntless (D33)">HMS <i>Dauntless</i></a>, a <a href="/wiki/Type_45_Destroyer" class="mw-redirect" title="Type 45 Destroyer">Type 45</a> <a href="/wiki/Guided_Missile_Destroyer" class="mw-redirect" title="Guided Missile Destroyer">Guided Missile Destroyer</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>RAF Mount Pleasant has its own port facility<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> called <a href="/wiki/Mare_Harbour" title="Mare Harbour">Mare Harbour</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/Naval_Party_2010" class="mw-redirect" title="Naval Party 2010">Naval Party 2010</a> (NP2010). The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> maintains a presence in the area with a <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a> or <a href="/wiki/Guided_missile_destroyer" title="Guided missile destroyer">guided missile destroyer</a> accompanied by an <a href="/wiki/Royal_Fleet_Auxiliary" title="Royal Fleet Auxiliary">RFA</a> vessel in the South Atlantic and a patrol ship, currently <a href="/wiki/HMS_Clyde_(P257)" title="HMS Clyde (P257)">HMS <i>Clyde</i></a>, permanently close to the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In addition, an Ice Patrol Ship, <a href="/wiki/HMS_Protector_(A173)" title="HMS Protector (A173)">HMS&#160;<i>Protector</i></a>, is on station close to <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> for six months of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The warship and RFA vessel carry out the <a href="/wiki/Standing_Royal_Navy_deployments" title="Standing Royal Navy deployments">Atlantic Patrol Task (South)</a> mission, which "provides a maritime presence to protect the UK\'s interests in the region". The <a href="/wiki/Type_42_destroyer" title="Type 42 destroyer">Type 42 destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Edinburgh_(D97)" title="HMS Edinburgh (D97)">HMS <i>Edinburgh</i></a> took over the South Atlantic Patrol Task in October 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> replacing <a href="/wiki/HMS_Southampton_(D90)" title="HMS Southampton (D90)">HMS <i>Southampton</i></a>. Prior to <i>Southampton\'s</i> deployment in August 2005, the role was filled by <a href="/wiki/HMS_Cardiff_(D108)" title="HMS Cardiff (D108)">HMS <i>Cardiff</i></a>, which was decommissioned on return to the UK. As of February 2010, the on-station warship was the <a href="/wiki/Type_42_destroyer" title="Type 42 destroyer">Type 42 destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_York_(D98)" title="HMS York (D98)">HMS <i>York</i></a>. In late April 2010, HMS York was relieved by the <a href="/wiki/Type_23_frigate" title="Type 23 frigate">Type 23 frigate</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Portland_(F79)" title="HMS Portland (F79)">HMS <i>Portland</i></a>. In August 2010, HMS Portland was relieved by the <a href="/wiki/Type_42_destroyer" title="Type 42 destroyer">Type 42 destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Gloucester_(D96)" title="HMS Gloucester (D96)">HMS <i>Gloucester</i></a>. On 21 April 2011, <a href="/wiki/HMS_York_(D98)" title="HMS York (D98)">HMS <i>York</i></a> arrived at the <a href="/wiki/East_Cove_Military_Port" class="mw-redirect" title="East Cove Military Port">East Cove Military Port</a> in the Falkland Islands, beginning patrol duties for the islands.<sup id="cite_ref-NN_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NN-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> October 2011 saw the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Type_23_frigate" title="Type 23 frigate">Type 23 frigate</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Montrose_(F236)" title="HMS Montrose (F236)">HMS <i>Montrose</i></a>, generating a statement from UNASUR <a href="/wiki/UNASUR" class="mw-redirect" title="UNASUR">(Union of South American Nations)</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Type_45_Destroyer" class="mw-redirect" title="Type 45 Destroyer">Type 45</a> <a href="/wiki/Guided_Missile_Destroyer" class="mw-redirect" title="Guided Missile Destroyer">Guided Missile Destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Dauntless_(D33)" title="HMS Dauntless (D33)">HMS <i>Dauntless</i></a> replaced HMS Montrose as of April 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In early August 2013, HMS Richmond was deployed to be the ship for the Royal Navy\'s Atlantic Patrol.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> HMS Portland was again deployed in January 2014.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg/220px-HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg/330px-HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg/440px-HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HMS_Torbay_S90.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/HMS_Torbay_(S90)" title="HMS Torbay (S90)">HMS <i>Torbay</i></a>, a <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar_class_submarine" class="mw-redirect" title="Trafalgar class submarine"><i>Trafalgar</i> -class</a> <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_submarine" title="Nuclear submarine">nuclear submarine</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>The Falkland Islands Patrol ship is a <a href="/wiki/River_class_patrol_vessel" class="mw-redirect" title="River class patrol vessel">River class patrol vessel</a>, which replaced the previous <a href="/wiki/Castle_class_patrol_vessel" class="mw-redirect" title="Castle class patrol vessel">Castle class patrol vessel</a>. The threat from submarines to hostile ships was demonstrated during the <a href="/wiki/Falklands_War" title="Falklands War">Falklands War</a> when <a href="/wiki/HMS_Conqueror_(S48)" title="HMS Conqueror (S48)">HMS <i>Conqueror</i></a> sank the Argentine cruiser <a href="/wiki/ARA_General_Belgrano" title="ARA General Belgrano">ARA <i>General Belgrano</i></a>. missiles</a>, which have a range of 1,500 miles (2,400&#160;km) and can strike at targets within an enemy country. <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The March 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In February 2012, a <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar_class_submarine" class="mw-redirect" title="Trafalgar
