lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\nThe <b>Major League Baseball Authentication Program</b>, or <b>MLB Authentication Program</b>, is a program run by Major League Baseball Properties, the product licensing arm of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>, to guarantee the authenticity of baseball merchandise and memorabilia. The centerpiece of the system is a <a href="/wiki/Tamper-evident_technology#Seals_and_signatures" title="Tamper-evident technology">tamper-resistant</a> <a href="/wiki/Security_tape" title="Security tape">security tape</a> (sticker) with an <a href="/wiki/Security_hologram" title="Security hologram">embedded hologram</a>.  Each sticker carries a unique alphanumeric code. The sticker is affixed to all game-used merchandise and memorabilia, while information about the item is entered into a computer database. Between 500,000 and 600,000 items are authenticated each season.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Authentication_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Major League Baseball started running an authentication program in 2001 after the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">FBI</a> discovered during an investigation that 75 percent of autographs purported to be from MLB players and other personnel were fake.<sup id="cite_ref-st_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-st-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-digest_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digest-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The organization has had a long-running issue with counterfeiting in general, which peaks during the postseason. For example, during the <a href="/wiki/2011_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2011 National League Championship Series">2011 National League Championship Series</a> between the St Louis Cardinals and the Milwaukee Brewers, MLB officials confiscated over 5,000 counterfeited items, with more than 80 percent being found in the vicinity of <a href="/wiki/Busch_Stadium" title="Busch Stadium">Busch Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-shira_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shira-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The largest haul is during the <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a>, when thousands of knock-off jerseys, caps, bags, and other items of merchandise are seized.<sup id="cite_ref-stl_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stl-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>\xe2\x80\x99 <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Series" title="2014 World Series">2014 World Series</a> race, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Immigration_and_Customs_Enforcement" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement">United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement</a> agents seized over 2,700 pieces of counterfeit MLB merchandise in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Program_description">Program description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Authentication_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Program description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The authentication system centers on a <a href="/wiki/Hologram" class="mw-redirect" title="Hologram">hologram</a> sticker by OpSec, which carries its own individual alphanumeric code.<sup id="cite_ref-shira_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shira-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The sticker is <a href="/wiki/Tamper_resistance" title="Tamper resistance">tamper-resistant</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Security_hologram" title="Security hologram">embedded hologram</a> is ruined if the <a href="/wiki/Security_tape" title="Security tape">sticker</a> is removed from the item.<sup id="cite_ref-digest_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digest-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-si_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>MLB employs approximately 150 authenticators, with four being appointed to each team.<sup id="cite_ref-st_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-st-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> An MLB authenticator will be present at every MLB game as well as the <a href="/wiki/World_Baseball_Classic" title="World Baseball Classic">World Baseball Classic</a> and team-specific events.<sup id="cite_ref-st_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-st-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_postseason" title="Major League Baseball postseason">MLB postseason</a> games will have three or more authenticators.<sup id="cite_ref-si_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The authenticators all have a background in law enforcement and are recommended to the role by their local police departments.<sup id="cite_ref-st_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-st-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The authenticator usually sits in the first base camera well.<sup id="cite_ref-si_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Every ball that is taken out of play, without leaving the ballpark, is handed to the authenticator, who enters information about that ball into a computer database\xe2\x80\x94such as "the pitcher, batter, inning and the reason the ball came out of play"\xe2\x80\x94and then affixes the hologram.<sup id="cite_ref-digest_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digest-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>While anything that goes into the stands is generally considered outside their jurisdiction, if there is a batter\'s milestone occurring, the authenticators will often work with the umpires to mark up baseballs to ensure that even if the milestone ball is hit into the stands, it can be tracked and later authenticated.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Items_authenticated">Items authenticated</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Authentication_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Items authenticated">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg/220px-Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg/330px-Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg/440px-Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2210" data-file-height="1475" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Lagares_Autographed_Baseball.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Baseball autographed by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Lagares" title="Juan Lagares">Juan Lagares</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Usually the MLB Authentication Program authenticates items used during baseball games, with baseballs and <a href="/wiki/Baseball_bat" title="Baseball bat">baseball bats</a> being the most common. Other game-used items which are authenticated include "player jerseys, locker tags, lineup cards, the pitching rubber, home plate, broken bats", and base pads.<sup id="cite_ref-digest_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digest-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-shira_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shira-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Any player can request that any item be authenticated, with the request usually being passed to the authenticators by the relevant clubhouse manager.<sup id="cite_ref-si_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Any item with an MLB association can be authenticated. One example of this was when the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a> got the remains of a dugout telephone that was smashed by the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/David_Ortiz" title="David Ortiz">David Ortiz</a> authenticated.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> They then presented the phone to Ortiz during his retirement season.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>\' Game 7 win in the <a href="/wiki/2017_World_Series" title="2017 World Series">2017 World Series</a> at <a href="/wiki/Dodger_Stadium" title="Dodger Stadium">Dodger Stadium</a>, MLB authenticated jars of dirt taken from the field.<sup id="cite_ref-st_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-st-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Between 500,000 and 600,000 items are authenticated each season.<sup id="cite_ref-digest_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digest-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-knight_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knight-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Game-used memorabilia remains the property of each team, which authenticates items for players who have reached certain milestones, for sale to fans at the team store or on the MLB online store, and for sale to retailers and other marketers of authenticated MLB merchandise.<sup id="cite_ref-digest_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digest-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-si_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Occasionally items are sent for inclusion in the <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-si_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2001 MLB Properties contracted with the <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Andersen" title="Arthur Andersen">Arthur Andersen</a> accounting firm to oversee and authenticate private autographing sessions for balls, bats, base pads, and other items. The <a href="/wiki/Deloitte_%26_Touche" class="mw-redirect" title="Deloitte &amp; Touche">Deloitte &amp; Touche</a> accounting firm took over this role in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-knight_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knight-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Major_League_Baseball_Authentication_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n<ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-st-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-st_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-st_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-st_1-2"><sup><i><b>c</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-st_1-3"><sup><i><b>d</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-st_1-4"><sup><i><b>e</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation news">Ryan, Megan (September 9, 2014). <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.startribune.com/keeping-it-real-mlb-authenticators-ensure-legitimacy-of-game-used-memorabilia/274421831/">"Keeping it real: MLB authenticators ensure legitimacy of game-used memorabilia"</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Tribune" title="Star Tribune">Star Tribune</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>, Minnesota<span class="reference-accessdate">.
