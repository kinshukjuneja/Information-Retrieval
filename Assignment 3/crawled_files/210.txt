Tiddy</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Teudhi</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a small <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> in south-east <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, the main tributary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Lynher" title="River Lynher">River Lynher</a>. The Tiddy rises near <a href="/wiki/Pensilva" title="Pensilva">Pensilva</a> and flows south east past the village of <a href="/wiki/Tideford" title="Tideford">Tideford</a> until it joins the Lynher just after passing <a href="/wiki/St_Germans,_Cornwall" title="St Germans, Cornwall">St Germans</a>. The name of Tideford derives from its location on the river, literally meaning "Ford on the River Tiddy".\n</p><p>St Germans is a historic fishing village situated on the Tiddy just upstream of its confluence with the Lynher. The <a href="/wiki/Quay_Sailing_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay Sailing Club">Quay Sailing Club</a> are based at St Germans Quay.
