id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0(Redirected from <a lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\nA <b>train operating company</b> (<b>TOC</b>) is a business operating <a href="/wiki/Train#Passenger_trains" title="Train">passenger trains</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Great_Britain" title="Rail transport in Great Britain">railway system of Great Britain</a> under the collective <a href="/wiki/National_Rail" title="National Rail">National Rail</a> brand. TOCs have existed since the <a href="/wiki/Privatisation_of_British_Rail" title="Privatisation of British Rail">privatisation</a> of the network under the <a href="/wiki/Railways_Act_1993" title="Railways Act 1993">Railways Act 1993</a>.\n</p><p>There are two types of TOC: most hold <a href="/wiki/Passenger_rail_franchising_in_Great_Britain" title="Passenger rail franchising in Great Britain">franchises</a> let by the government, following bids from various companies, to operate services on certain routes for a specified duration, while a small number of <a href="/wiki/Open_access_operator" class="mw-redirect" title="Open access operator">open access operators</a> hold licences to provide supplementary services on chosen routes. These operators can run services for the duration of the licence validity. The franchised operators have changed considerably since privatisation: previous franchises have been divided, merged, re-let to new operators, or renamed.\n</p><p>The term is also sometimes used to describe companies operating passenger or freight rail services over tracks that are owned by another company or a national network owner.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Franchises were initially let by the <a href="/wiki/Director_of_Passenger_Rail_Franchising" title="Director of Passenger Rail Franchising">Office of Passenger Rail Franchising</a> (OPRAF). This was in turn replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Rail_Authority" title="Strategic Rail Authority">Strategic Rail Authority</a>, which has since been abolished. For England and Wales, franchising is now the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">Department for Transport</a> in the majority of cases. In <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, it is the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a>. In two parts of England, local government agencies are responsible: in <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Merseytravel" title="Merseytravel">Merseyside Passenger Transport Executive</a> lets the <a href="/wiki/Merseyrail" title="Merseyrail">Merseyrail</a> franchise, while in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_London" title="Transport for London">Transport for London</a> oversees the new <a href="/wiki/London_Overground" title="London Overground">London Overground</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crossrail" title="Crossrail">Crossrail</a> concessions.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Rail_Delivery_Group" title="Rail Delivery Group">Rail Delivery Group</a> (RDG) (formerly the Association of Train Operating Companies) provides a commonality for the TOCs and provides some centralised co-ordination. Its activities include the provision of a national timetable and online journey planner facility, and the operation of the various <a href="/wiki/Concessionary_fares_on_the_British_railway_network#Railcards" title="Concessionary fares on the British railway network">Railcard</a> discount schemes. <a href="/wiki/Eurostar" title="Eurostar">Eurostar</a> is also a member of the RDG, though it is not itself a TOC.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>For historical and geographical reasons the railway network of the United Kingdom is split into two independent systems: one in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> (including the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a>), and one in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, which is closely linked to the railway system of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Great_Britain">Great Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Great Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In Great Britain, passenger train services are operated by a number of companies, referred to as <i>Train Operating Companies</i> or TOCs, normally on the basis of regional franchises awarded by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">Department for Transport</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.dft.gov.uk/stellent/groups/dft_railways/documents/sectionhomepage/dft_railways_page.hcsp">Rail Group</a>. Until 2005 this role was performed by the <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Rail_Authority" title="Strategic Rail Authority">Strategic Rail Authority</a>. The infrastructure of the <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">railways</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 including tracks and <a href="/wiki/Railway_signalling" title="Railway signalling">signalling</a>&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 is owned and operated not by the train companies but by <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a>, which took over responsibility from <a href="/wiki/Railtrack" title="Railtrack">Railtrack</a> in 2002. Most passenger trains are owned by a small number of <a href="/wiki/Rolling_stock_operating_company" title="Rolling stock operating company">Rolling Stock Companies</a> and are leased to the individual TOCs. However, a handful of TOCs own and maintain some of their own rolling stock. Train operating companies also operate most of the network\'s <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">stations</a>, in their role as station facility owners (SFO), in which they lease the buildings and associated land from Network Rail.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Network Rail manages some <a href="/wiki/Category:Network_Rail_managed_stations" title="Category:Network Rail managed stations">major railway stations</a> and several stations are operated by <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> or other companies.\n</p><p>All passenger TOCs in Great Britain are privately owned. The majority of these hold <a href="/wiki/Rail_franchising_in_Great_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Rail franchising in Great Britain">franchises</a> to operate rail services on specific parts of the railway and come under the auspices of the National Rail brand. In addition, companies are able to bid for "paths" (specific parts of the overall National Rail timetable) to operate their own services, which the franchises do not operate&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 these operators are classed as <a href="/wiki/Open-access_operator" title="Open-access operator">open-access operators</a> and are not franchise holders. Currently in Great Britain, there are two open-access operators: <a href="/wiki/Hull_Trains" title="Hull Trains">Hull Trains</a> runs services between <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King\'s Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hull_Paragon_Interchange" title="Hull Paragon Interchange">Hull</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a>, which operates between London King\'s Cross and <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunderland railway station">Sunderland</a> and between London King\'s Cross and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford</a>. In addition, there are operators that fall outside the purview of National Rail, which operate specific services which are recent additions to Britain\'s railways. The main examples are <a href="/wiki/Eurostar" title="Eurostar">Eurostar</a>, which operates to the continent via the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Tunnel" title="Channel Tunnel">Channel Tunnel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heathrow_Express" title="Heathrow Express">Heathrow Express</a>, which runs fast services from London to <a href="/wiki/Heathrow_Airport" title="Heathrow Airport">Heathrow Airport</a>.\n</p><p>A number of metropolitan railways on the network are operated by the local franchise holder in conjunction with the <a href="/wiki/Passenger_transport_executive" title="Passenger transport executive">passenger transport executive</a> or other civic body responsible for administering public transport. One of these bodies, the <a href="/wiki/Merseytravel" title="Merseytravel">Merseyside Passenger Transport Executive</a>, is responsible for one of three National Rail franchises not awarded by central government, namely the <a href="/wiki/Merseyrail" title="Merseyrail">Merseyrail</a> franchise, while certain National Rail services in North London came under the control of <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_London" title="Transport for London">Transport for London</a> in November 2007 as <a href="/wiki/London_Overground" title="London Overground">London Overground</a>. Two other franchises, the Scottish national franchise, currently operated by <a href="/wiki/Abellio_ScotRail" title="Abellio ScotRail">Abellio ScotRail</a>, and the Welsh domestic franchise, operated by <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Trains_Wales" title="Arriva Trains Wales">Arriva Trains Wales</a>, are awarded by the devolved governments of the two constituent nations.\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Rail_Delivery_Group" title="Rail Delivery Group">Rail Delivery Group</a> is the coordinating body of the train operating companies in Great Britain and owns the National Rail brand, which uses the former <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a> double-arrow logo and organises the common ticketing structure. href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Railtours">Railtours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: Railtours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Railtour" title="Railtour">Railtour</a></div>\n<p>The privatisation of British Rail allowed the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Open-access_operator" title="Open-access operator">open-access operators</a>, in which companies, upon payment of a fee, could purchase individual slots on the mainline. This has led to the growth in companies offering charter trains, and to the <a href="/wiki/Railtour" title="Railtour">railtour</a>. Most railtour operators run services in part of the country; however, there are a handful that operate services nationwide. Usually, these will see a train made up of ex BR rolling stock pulled by a hired locomotive from one of the freight companies. Occasionally, a preserved ex BR locomotive that is certified to run on the mainline will be made available for such charters.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sea_links">Sea links</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: Sea links">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>A number of coastal railway stations in the United Kingdom serve to provide connections to ferry services to a number of destinations. Most of the ferry operators in these cases set their timetable to run in conjunction with the arrivals and departures of rail services from the stations serving the ferry terminals. A handful of these even offer integrated pricing for both rail and ferry travel&#160;\xe2\x80\x93 because the <a href="/wiki/Island_Line,_Isle_of_Wight" title="Island Line, Isle of Wight">Island Line</a> is part of the <a href="/wiki/National_Rail" title="National Rail">National Rail</a> network, passengers can purchase tickets for travel to any of the stations on the Isle of Wight from any other station in Great Britain. This ticket also covers the cost of passage on the <a href="/wiki/Wightlink" title="Wightlink">Wightlink</a> catamaran from <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_Harbour_railway_station" title="Portsmouth Harbour railway station">Portsmouth Harbour</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ryde_Pier_Head_railway_station" title="Ryde Pier Head railway station">Ryde Pier Head</a>. It is also possible to purchase ferry inclusive tickets from any station in Great Britain to <a href="/wiki/Cowes" title="Cowes">Cowes</a> or <a href="/wiki/East_Cowes" title="East Cowes">East Cowes</a> on the Isle of Wight using <a href="/wiki/Red_Funnel" title="Red Funnel">Red Funnel</a> ferries, although there are no rail connections from these towns.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_operators">International operators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=40" title="Edit section: International operators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>There are two main international services which operate on the railways in the United Kingdom:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Eurostar" title="Eurostar">Eurostar</a> \xe2\x80\x93 runs between <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">London St Pancras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gare_du_Nord" title="Gare du Nord">Paris Gare du Nord</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruxelles_Gare_du_Midi_/_Brussel-Zuid_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Bruxelles Gare du Midi / Brussel-Zuid railway station">Bruxelles Gare du Midi/Brussel Zuidstation</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Tunnel" title="Channel Tunnel">Channel Tunnel</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Enterprise_(train)" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise (train)">Enterprise</a> \xe2\x80\x93 operates on the Irish network between <a href="/wiki/Belfast_Central_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Belfast Central railway station">Belfast Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Connolly_railway_station" title="Dublin Connolly railway station">Dublin Connolly</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>A third service which is worth mentioning is <a href="/wiki/Dutchflyer" title="Dutchflyer">Dutchflyer</a> (GoLondon in the Netherlands). This is not a separate rail service in itself, as the others are, but a collaboration between <a href="/wiki/Greater_Anglia_(train_operating_company)" title="Greater Anglia (train operating company)">Greater Anglia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stena_Line" title="Stena Line">Stena Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nederlandse_Spoorwegen" title="Nederlandse Spoorwegen">Nederlandse Spoorwegen</a> to provide an integrated rail/sea/rail service between eastern England (<a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Street_station" title="Liverpool Street station">London Liverpool Street</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_railway_station" title="Cambridge railway station">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwich_railway_station" title="Norwich railway station">Norwich</a>) and The Netherlands (<a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Centraal" class="mw-redirect" title="Amsterdam Centraal">Amsterdam Centraal</a>) using a single ticket.\n</p><p>A further international service is provided by <a href="/wiki/Venice_Simplon_Orient_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Venice Simplon Orient Express">Venice Simplon Orient Express</a>. Although this is primarily a railtour operator, with special trains to various locations in the United Kingdom, it also operates the scheduled Orient Express service to destinations in Europe. This involves two separate trains; the <i>British Pullman</i> departs from <a href="/wiki/London_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="London Victoria">London Victoria</a> and terminates at <a href="/wiki/Folkestone_West_railway_station" title="Folkestone West railway station">Folkestone West</a>, where passengers transfer by coach through the Channel Tunnel to Calais; at <a href="/wiki/Gare_de_Calais-Ville" title="Gare de Calais-Ville">Gare de Calais-Ville</a>, they then join the <i>Orient Express</i> which then calls at various destinations including Paris, Vienna, Innsbruck, Venice and Rome.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_countries">In other countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Train_operating_company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: In other countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The differentiation between train operating companies and railway infrastructure companies was enforced by <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> legislation and can be found in all EU member countries.\n</p><p>In Germany, train operating companies (<i>Eisenbahnverkehrsunternehmen</i> \xe2\x80\x93 EVU) are defined by General Railways Act 1993, s. 2(1) (Allgemeines Eisenbahngesetz (AEG), enacted 27 December 1993) as companies providing train services. They are distinct from <i>Eisenbahninfrastrukturunternehmen</i> (EIU), which own and maintain the railway infrastructure. While there are many private EVU, that have obtained regional franchises, only a handful of long-distance EVU exist (the largest by fare being <a href="/wiki/DB_Fernverkehr" title="DB Fernverkehr">DB Fernverkehr</a>); the infrastructure is also almost completely owned by <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn" title="Deutsche Bahn">Deutsche Bahn</a> subsidiaries.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
