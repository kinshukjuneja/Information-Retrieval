Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>\n<b>Peredur I. Lynch</b> (born 13 January 1963) is a <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> academic specialising in Welsh literary history.\n</p><p>Lynch spent much of his early life in <a href="/wiki/Carrog" title="Carrog">Carrog</a> in northern Wales and obtained a degree at <a href="/wiki/Bangor_University" title="Bangor University">Bangor University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has won a number of <a href="/wiki/Eisteddfod" title="Eisteddfod">Eisteddfod</a> Chairs, beginning with the <a href="/wiki/Urdd_National_Eisteddfod" title="Urdd National Eisteddfod">Urdd National Eisteddfod</a> Chair at Maesteg in 1979. His <i><a href="/wiki/Awdl" title="Awdl">awdl</a></i> was received so well that in 1980 he was lauded as "one of the great potentials of Welsh poetry".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Lynch, however, became best known for his academic career and his scholarly work on Welsh literary history. In 1985, he became a Research Fellow at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wales" title="University of Wales">University of Wales</a> Centre for Advanced Welsh and Celtic Studies in Aberystwyth (1985-1990), moved to <a href="/wiki/Swansea_University" title="Swansea University">Swansea</a> to become lecturer in Welsh and in 1995 returned to his alma mater (Bangor), where he was also appointed as lecturer in Welsh.<sup id="cite_ref-WA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2003 he became head of the Welsh Department at Bangor (2003-2006), and in 2005 was appointed to a professor\'s chair.<sup id="cite_ref-WA_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bangor_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bangor-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>His scholarly contributions have focused on medieval and early modern Welsh poetry, notably that of the <a href="/wiki/Gogynfeirdd" class="mw-redirect" title="Gogynfeirdd">Gogynfeirdd</a> or Poets of the Princes.<sup id="cite_ref-Bangor_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bangor-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He assisted <a href="/wiki/J._E._Caerwyn_Williams" title="J. E. Caerwyn Williams">J. E. Caerwyn Williams</a> in preparing an edition of Gogynfeirdd poetry, the first to be published for Cyfres Beirdd y Tywysogion (\'The Poets of the Princes series\'), and made further contributions to the series. On the topic of prophecy in Welsh poetry, he has both led a research project (2002-2005) and read a paper in the 2004 J. E. Caerwyn and Gwen Williams Memorial Lecture.<sup id="cite_ref-Bangor_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bangor-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is one of the editors of the <i><a href="/wiki/Welsh_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh Academy">Welsh Academy</a> Encyclopaedia of Wales</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-WA_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bangor_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bangor-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2001, Lynch published <i>Cywion Nell</i>, a Welsh adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Barnard" title="Robert Barnard">Robert Barnard</a>\'s 1981 novel <i>Mother\'s Boys</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_works">Selected works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peredur_Lynch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Selected works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<dl><dt>Academic</dt></dl>\n<ul><li>1994.
