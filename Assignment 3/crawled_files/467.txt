id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>\t\t\t\t<div id="contentSub"></div>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>\n\t\t\t\t<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>\n\t\t\t\t<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>\xc3\x85land convention</b>, refers to two conventions regarding the demilitarization and neutralization of the <a href="/wiki/%C3%85land_Islands" title="\xc3\x85land Islands">\xc3\x85land Islands</a>.\n</p><p>\xc3\x85land convention held in Paris, France.\n</p><p>The <b>\xc3\x85land convention of 1856</b> was signed on 30 March 1856, following the Russian defeat in the <a href="/wiki/Crimean_War" title="Crimean War">Crimean War</a> against the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> agreed not to militarise the \xc3\x85land Islands, which was confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1856)" title="Treaty of Paris (1856)">Treaty of Paris (1856)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-uneasy_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uneasy-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>However, the Russians militarized the islands in 1916, a move that alarmed the Swedes.\n</p><p>The <b>\xc3\x85land convention of 1921</b> was signed on 20 October 1921 by <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weimar_Republic" title="Weimar Republic">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Second_Polish_Republic" title="Second Polish Republic">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>.
