For the racehorse, see <a href="/wiki/Royal_Anthem_(horse)" title="Royal Anthem (horse)">Royal Anthem (horse)</a>.</div>\n<p>The <b>honors music</b> for a person, office or rank is music played on formal or ceremonial occasions in the presence of the person, office-holder, or rank-holder, especially by a <a href="/wiki/Military_band" title="Military band">military band</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> in many countries is honored with a prescribed piece of music; in some countries the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> serves this purpose, while others have a separate <b>royal</b>, <b>presidential</b>, or, historically, <b>imperial anthem</b>. Other officials may also have anthems, such as the <b>vice-regal salute</b> in several <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realms</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor-General">Governor-General</a>, Governor, or Lieutenant Governor. <a href="/wiki/Ruffles_and_flourishes" title="Ruffles and flourishes">Ruffles and flourishes</a> may be played instead of, or preceding, honors music.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox"
