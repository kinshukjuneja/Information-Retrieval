Islands</b><sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook._1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook.-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (abbreviated as <b>HIMI</b><sup id="cite_ref-abbr_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-abbr-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>) is an Australian <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">external territory</a> comprising a <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_group" title="Volcanic group">volcanic group</a> of barren <a href="/wiki/Antarctic" title="Antarctic">Antarctic</a> islands, about two-thirds of the way from <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> to <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a>. The group\'s overall size is 372 square kilometres (144&#160;sq&#160;mi) in area and it has 101.9&#160;km (63&#160;mi) of coastline. Discovered in the mid-19th century, the islands have been an Australian territory since 1947 and contain the country\'s only two active <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a>. The summit of one, <a href="/wiki/Mawson_Peak" title="Mawson Peak">Mawson Peak</a>, is higher than any mountain on the Australian mainland. The islands lie on the <a href="/wiki/Kerguelen_Plateau" title="Kerguelen Plateau">Kerguelen Plateau</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>.\n</p><p>The islands are among the <a href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_Earth#Remoteness" title="Extreme points of Earth">most remote places on Earth</a>: They are located approximately 4,099&#160;km (2,547&#160;mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ga_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ga-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 3,845&#160;km (2,389&#160;mi) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Leeuwin" title="Cape Leeuwin">Cape Leeuwin</a>, Australia, 4,200&#160;km (2,600&#160;mi) southeast of South Africa, 3,830&#160;km (2,380&#160;mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, 1,630&#160;km (1,010&#160;mi) north of Antarctica, and 450&#160;km (280&#160;mi) southeast of the <a href="/wiki/Kerguelen_Islands" title="Kerguelen Islands">Kerguelen Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-distancefromto_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-distancefromto-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The islands are currently uninhabited.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" Its mountains are covered by 41 glaciers<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> (the island is 80% covered with ice<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook._1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook.-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) and dominated by <a href="/wiki/Mawson_Peak" title="Mawson Peak">Mawson Peak</a>, a 2,745-metre-high (9,006&#160;ft) <a href="/wiki/Complex_volcano" title="Complex volcano">complex volcano</a> which forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Big_Ben_(Heard_Island)" title="Big Ben (Heard Island)">Big Ben</a> <a href="/wiki/Massif" title="Massif">massif</a>. A July 2000 satellite image from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">University of Hawaii</a>\'s Institute of Geophysics and Planetology (HIGP) Thermal Alert Team showed an active 2-kilometre-long (1.2&#160;mi) and 50- to 90-metre-wide (164\xe2\x80\x93295 ft) lava flow trending south-west from the summit of Big Ben.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/McDonald_Islands_(volcano)" title="McDonald Islands (volcano)">McDonald Islands</a> are located 44 kilometres (27&#160;mi) to the west of <a href="/wiki/Heard_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Heard Island">Heard Island</a> at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;params=53_02_20_S_72_36_04_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">53\xc2\xb002\xe2\x80\xb220\xe2\x80\xb3S</span> <span class="longitude">72\xc2\xb036\xe2\x80\xb204\xe2\x80\xb3E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">53.03889\xc2\xb0S 72.60111\xc2\xb0E</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">-53.03889; 72.60111</span></span></span></a></span>. The islands are small and rocky. In 1980 they consisted of McDonald Island (186 metres (610&#160;ft) high), Flat Island (55 metres (180&#160;ft) high) and Meyer Rock (170 metres (560&#160;ft) high). They totalled approximately 2.5 square kilometres (1.0&#160;sq&#160;mi) in area, where McDonald Island was 1.13 square kilometres (0.4&#160;sq&#160;mi). There is a small group of islets and rocks about 10 kilometres (6&#160;mi) north of Heard Island, consisting of Shag Islet, Sail Rock, Morgan Island and Black Rock. They total about 1.1 square kilometres (0.4&#160;sq&#160;mi) in area.\n</p><p>Mawson Peak and McDonald Island are the only two active volcanoes in Australian territory. Mawson Peak is also one of the highest Australian mountains (higher than <a href="/wiki/Mount_Kosciuszko" title="Mount Kosciuszko">Mount Kosciuszko</a>); surpassed only by <a href="/wiki/Mount_McClintock" title="Mount McClintock">Mount McClintock</a> range in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Antarctic_Territory" title="Australian Antarctic Territory">Antarctic territory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Mawson Peak has erupted several times in the last decade; the most recent eruption was filmed on 2 February 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The volcano on McDonald Island, after being dormant for 75,000 years, became active in 1992 and has erupted several times since, the most recent in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Heard Island and the McDonald Islands have no ports or harbours; ships must anchor offshore. The coastline is 101.9 kilometres (63.3&#160;mi), and a 12-nautical-mile (22&#160;km; 14&#160;mi) territorial sea and 200-nautical-mile (370&#160;km; 230&#160;mi) exclusive fishing zone are claimed.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook._1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook.-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg/220px-Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg" width="220" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg/330px-Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg/440px-Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1401" data-file-height="1221" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heard_Island_Karman_vortex_street.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Vortex_shedding" title="Vortex shedding">Vortex shedding</a> as winds pass Heard Island resulted in this <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A1rm%C3%A1n_vortex_street" title="K\xc3\xa1rm\xc3\xa1n vortex street">K\xc3\xa1rm\xc3\xa1n vortex street</a> in the clouds</div></div></div>\n<p>The islands have an Antarctic climate, tempered by their maritime setting. The weather is marked by low seasonal and daily temperature ranges, persistent and generally low cloud cover, frequent precipitation and strong winds. Snowfall occurs throughout the year. Monthly average temperatures at Atlas Cove (at the northwestern end of Heard Island) range from 0.0 to 4.2&#160;\xc2\xb0C (32.0 to 39.6&#160;\xc2\xb0F), with an average daily range of 3.7 to 5.2&#160;\xc2\xb0C (38.7 to 41.4&#160;\xc2\xb0F) in summer and \xe2\x88\x920.8 to 0.3&#160;\xc2\xb0C (30.6 to 32.5&#160;\xc2\xb0F) in winter. The winds are predominantly westerly and persistently strong. At Atlas Cove, monthly average wind speeds range between around 26 to 33.5&#160;km/h (16.2 to 20.8&#160;mph). Gusts in excess of 180&#160;km/h (110&#160;mph) have been recorded. Annual precipitation at sea level on Heard Island is in the order of 1,300 to 1,900&#160;mm (51.2 to 74.8&#160;in); rain or snow falls on about 3 out of 4 days.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Meteorological records at Heard Island are incomplete.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora">Flora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. In this cold climate plant life is mainly limited to grasses, lichens, and <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">mosses</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Low plant diversity reflects the islands\xe2\x80\x99 isolation, small size, severe climate, the short, cool growing season and, for Heard Island, substantial permanent ice cover. The main environmental determinants of vegetation on subantarctic islands are wind exposure, water availability, parent soil composition, salt spray exposure, nutrient availability, disturbance by trampling (from seabirds and seals) and, possibly, altitude. At Heard Island, exposure to salt spray and the presence of breeding and moulting seabirds and seals are particularly strong influences on vegetation composition and structure in coastal areas.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Evidence from microfossil records indicates that ferns and woody plants were present on Heard Island during the <a href="/wiki/Tertiary" title="Tertiary">Tertiary</a> (a period with a cool and moist climate). Neither group of plants is present today, although potential Tertiary survivors include the vascular plant <i><a href="/wiki/Pringlea" title="Pringlea">Pringlea antiscorbutica</a></i> and six moss species. Volcanic activity has altered the distribution and abundance of the vegetation. The vascular flora covers a range of environments and, although only six species are currently widespread, glacial retreat and the consequent connection of previously separate ice-free areas is providing opportunities for further distribution of vegetation into adjacent areas.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Flowering_plants_and_ferns">Flowering plants and ferns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Flowering plants and ferns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Low-growing <a href="/wiki/Herbaceous_plant" title="Herbaceous plant">herbaceous</a> flowering plants and <a href="/wiki/Bryophyte" title="Bryophyte">bryophytes</a> are the major vegetation components. The vascular flora comprises the smallest number of species of any major subantarctic island group, reflecting its isolation, small ice-free area and severe climate. Twelve vascular species are known from Heard Island, of which five have also been recorded on McDonald Island. None of the vascular species is endemic, although <i><a href="/wiki/Pringlea_antiscorbutica" class="mw-redirect" title="Pringlea antiscorbutica">Pringlea antiscorbutica</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Colobanthus_kerguelensis" title="Colobanthus kerguelensis">Colobanthus kerguelensis</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Poa_kerguelensis" title="Poa kerguelensis">Poa kerguelensis</a></i> occur only on subantarctic islands in the southern Indian Ocean.\n</p><p>The plants are typically subantarctic, but with a higher abundance of the cushion-forming <i><a href="/wiki/Azorella_selago" title="Azorella selago">Azorella selago</a></i> than other subantarctic islands. Heard Island is the largest subantarctic island with no confirmed human-introduced plants. Areas available for plant colonisation on Heard Island are generally the result of retreating glaciers or new ice-free land created by lava flows. Today, substantial vegetation covers over 20&#160;km<sup>2</sup> of Heard Island, and is best developed on coastal areas at elevations below 250&#160;m.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mosses_and_liverworts">Mosses and liverworts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mosses and liverworts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Bryophytes (mosses and liverworts) contribute substantially to the overall biodiversity of Heard Island, with 43 mosses and 19 liverworts being recorded, often occupying habitats unsuitable for vascular plants, such as cliff faces. Bryophytes are present in most of the major vegetation communities including several soil and moss-inhabiting species. A 1980 survey of McDonald Island found lower diversity than that on Heard Island; four mosses and a number of algal species are recorded from there.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Algae">Algae</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Algae">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>At least 100 species of terrestrial algae are known from Heard Island, commonly in permanently moist and ephemeral habitats. Forests of the giant Antarctic kelp <i><a href="/wiki/Durvillaea_antarctica" title="Durvillaea antarctica">Durvillaea antarctica</a></i> occur at a number of sites around Heard Island and at least 17 other species of seaweed are known, with more to be added following the identification of recent collections. Low seaweed diversity is due to the island\'s isolation from other land masses, unsuitable beach habitat, constant abrasion by waves, tides and small stones, and the extension of glaciers into the sea in many areas.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Vegetation_communities">Vegetation communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vegetation communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Heard Island has a range of terrestrial environments in which vegetation occurs. Seven general vegetation communities are currently recognised, although vegetation composition is considered more of a continuum than discrete units:\n</p>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Subantarctic" title="Subantarctic">Subantarctic</a> vegetation is minimal and includes small types of shrubbery. Including mosses and liverworts.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cushion_plant" title="Cushion plant">Open cushionfield</a> vegetation is the most widespread and abundant vegetation type on Heard Island. It is characterised by <i><a href="/wiki/Azorella_selago" title="Azorella selago">Azorella selago</a></i> cushions interspersed with bryophytes, small vascular species and bare ground with 20\xe2\x80\x9375% cover, and found mainly at altitudes between 30\xe2\x80\x9370m asl.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Fellfield" title="Fellfield">Fellfield</a> describes vegetation with abundant bare ground and less than 50% plant cover. Fellfield may occur as a result of harsh climatic and/or edaphic factors, or recent <a href="/wiki/Deglaciation" title="Deglaciation">deglaciation</a> which has exposed bare ground.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">Mossy</a> <a href="/wiki/Fellfield" title="Fellfield">fellfield</a> is a community with high species richness and consists of bryophytes and small <i>Azorella selago</i> cushions. It is found at altitudes between 30\xe2\x80\x93150&#160;m in areas with intermediate exposure.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Herbfield" title="Herbfield">Wet mixed herbfield</a> occurs on moist substrate, mostly on moraines and moist lee slopes (often in association with burrowing petrels colonies) at low altitude (&lt; 40&#160;m) where the water table is at or close to the surface. Species richness is highest here of all the communities, with dominant species being <i><a href="/wiki/Poa_cookii" title="Poa cookii">Poa cookii</a></i>, <i>Azorella selago</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pringlea_antiscorbutica" class="mw-redirect" title="Pringlea antiscorbutica">Pringlea antiscorbutica</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Acaena_magellanica" title="Acaena magellanica">Acaena magellanica</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Deschampsia_antarctica" title="Deschampsia antarctica">Deschampsia antarctica</a></i>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Coastal_Strand" title="Coastal Strand">Coastal biotic</a> vegetation is dominated by <i>Poa cookii</i> and <i>Azorella selago</i>, occurring mainly on coastal sites of moderate exposure and in areas subject to significant influence from seals and seabirds.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Supralittoral_zone" title="Supralittoral zone">Saltspray vegetation</a> is dominated by the salt-tolerant moss <i><a href="/wiki/Muelleriella_crassifolia" class="mw-redirect" title="Muelleriella crassifolia">Muelleriella crassifolia</a></i> and limited in extent, being found at low elevations on lavas in exposed coastal sites.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Cushion_plant" title="Cushion plant">Closed cushionfield</a> is found on moraines and sand at altitudes mostly below 60&#160;m, and is dominated almost entirely by <i>Azorella selago</i> cushions that often grow together to form continuous carpets which can be subject to burrowing by seabirds.</li></ul>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Outlook">Outlook</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Outlook">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>One of the most rapidly changing physical settings in the subantarctic has been produced on Heard Island by a combination of rapid glacial recession and climate warming.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The consequent increase in habitat available for plant colonisation, plus the coalescing of previously discrete ice-free areas, has led to marked changes in the vegetation of Heard Island in the last 20 years or so. Other species and vegetation communities found on subantarctic islands north of the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_Convergence" title="Antarctic Convergence">Antarctic Convergence</a> now absent from the Heard Island flora may colonise the island if climate change produces more favourable conditions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>Some plant species are spreading and modifying the structure and composition of communities, some of which are also increasing in distribution. It is likely that further changes will occur, and possibly at an accelerated rate. Changes in population numbers of seal and seabird species are also expected to affect the vegetation by changing nutrient availability and disturbance through trampling.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>One plant species on Heard Island, <i><a href="/wiki/Poa_annua" title="Poa annua">Poa annua</a></i>, a cosmopolitan grass native to Europe, was possibly introduced by humans, though is more likely to have arrived naturally, probably by <a href="/wiki/Skua" title="Skua">skuas</a> from the Kerguelen Islands where it is widespread. It was initially recorded in 1987 in two deglaciated areas of Heard Island not previously exposed to human visitors, while being absent from known sites of past human habitation. Since 1987 <i>Poa annua</i> populations have increased in density and abundance within the original areas and have expanded beyond them. Expeditioner boot traffic during the Australian Antarctic program expedition in 1987 may be at least partly responsible for the spread, but it is probably mainly due to dispersal by wind and the movement of seabirds and seals around the island.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The potential for introducing plant species (including invasive species not previously found on subantarctic islands) by both natural and human-induced means is high. This is due to the combination of low species diversity and climatic amelioration. During the 2003/04 summer a new plant species, <i><a href="/wiki/Cotula_plumosa" class="mw-redirect" title="Cotula plumosa">Cotula plumosa</a></i>, was recorded. Only one small specimen was found growing on a coastal river terrace that had experienced substantial development and expansion of vegetation over the past decade. The species has a circumantarctic distribution and occurs on many subantarctic islands.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fungi">Fungi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Fungi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>71 species of <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichens</a> have been recorded from Heard Island and they are common on exposed rock, dominating the vegetation in some areas.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> As with plants, a 1980 survey of McDonald Island found lower diversity there, with just eight lichen species and a number of non-lichenized fungi recorded.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fauna">Fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The main indigenous animals are insects along with large populations of ocean-going <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabirds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">seals</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penguin" title="Penguin">penguins</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote seal">elephant seals</a> on Heard Island \xe2\x80\x93 a 19th-century scene</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Seal_hunting" title="Seal hunting">Sealing</a> at Heard Island lasted from 1855 to 1910, during which time 67 sealing vessels are recorded visiting, nine of which were wrecked off the coast.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Relics that survive from that time include trypots, casks, hut ruins, graves and inscriptions. This caused the seal populations there to either become locally extinct or reduced to levels too low to exploit economically. Modern sealers visited from Cape Town in the 1920s.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Since then the populations have generally increased and are protected. Seals breeding on Heard include the <a href="/wiki/Southern_elephant_seal" title="Southern elephant seal">southern elephant seal</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_fur_seal" title="Antarctic fur seal">Antarctic fur seal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Subantarctic_fur_seal" title="Subantarctic fur seal">subantarctic fur seal</a>. <a href="/wiki/Leopard_seal" title="Leopard seal">Leopard seals</a> visit regularly in winter to <a href="/wiki/Hauling-out" title="Hauling-out">haul-out</a> though they do not breed on the islands. <a href="/wiki/Crabeater_seal" title="Crabeater seal">Crabeater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ross_seal" title="Ross seal">Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weddell_seal" title="Weddell seal">Weddell seals</a> are occasional visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Birds">Birds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Birds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_birds_of_Heard_and_McDonald_Islands" title="List of birds of Heard and McDonald Islands">List of birds of Heard and McDonald Islands</a></div>\n<p>Heard Island and the McDonald Islands are free from introduced predators and provide crucial breeding habitat in the middle of the vast Southern Ocean for a range of birds. The surrounding waters are important feeding areas for birds and some scavenging species also derive sustenance from their cohabitants on the islands. The islands have been identified by <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">Important Bird Area</a> because they support very large numbers of nesting seabirds.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Nineteen species of birds have been recorded as breeding on Heard Island<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and the McDonald Islands, although recent volcanic activity at the McDonald Islands in the last decade is likely to have reduced vegetated and un-vegetated nesting areas.<sup id="cite_ref-Woehler,_E.J._2006_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Woehler,_E.J._2006-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Penguins are by far the most abundant birds on the islands, with four breeding species present, comprising <a href="/wiki/King_penguin" title="King penguin">king</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gentoo_penguin" title="Gentoo penguin">gentoo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macaroni_penguin" title="Macaroni penguin">macaroni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_rockhopper_penguin" title="Eastern rockhopper penguin">eastern rockhopper penguins</a>. The penguins mostly colonise the coastal tussock and grasslands of Heard Island, and have previously been recorded as occupying the flats and gullies on McDonald Island.\n</p><p>Other seabirds recorded as breeding at Heard Island include three species of albatross (<a href="/wiki/Wandering_albatross" title="Wandering albatross">wandering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black-browed_albatross" title="Black-browed albatross">black-browed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Light-mantled_albatross" title="Light-mantled albatross">light-mantled albatrosses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_giant_petrel" title="Southern giant petrel">southern giant petrels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_petrel" title="Cape petrel">Cape petrels</a>, four species of burrowing petrels <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_prion" title="Antarctic prion">Antarctic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fulmar_prion" title="Fulmar prion">Fulmar prions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_diving-petrel" class="mw-redirect" title="Common diving-petrel">common</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Georgian_diving-petrel" class="mw-redirect" title="South Georgian diving-petrel">South Georgian diving-petrels</a>), <a href="/wiki/Wilson%27s_storm-petrel" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilson&#39;s storm-petrel">Wilson\'s storm-petrels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelp_gull" title="Kelp gull">kelp gulls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Subantarctic_skua" class="mw-redirect" title="Subantarctic skua">subantarctic skuas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antarctic_tern" title="Antarctic tern">Antarctic terns</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Heard_shag" class="mw-redirect" title="Heard shag">Heard shag</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Although not a true seabird, the Heard Island subspecies of the <a href="/wiki/Black-faced_sheathbill" title="Black-faced sheathbill">black-faced sheathbill</a> also breeds on the island. Both the shag and the sheathbill are endemic to Heard Island.\n</p><p>A further 28 seabird species are recorded as either non-breeding visitors or have been noted during \'at-sea surveys\' of the islands. All recorded breeding species, other than the Heard Island sheathbill, are listed marine species under the Australian Environmental Protection and Biodiversity Act (1999, four are listed as threatened species and five are listed migratory species. Under the EPBC Act a recovery plan has been made for albatrosses and giant petrels, which calls for ongoing population monitoring of the species found at HIMI, and at the time of preparing this plan a draft recovery plan has also been made for the <a href="/wiki/Heard_Island_shag" title="Heard Island shag">Heard Island cormorant (or shag)</a> and Antarctic tern.\n</p><p>The recorded populations of some seabird species found in the Reserve have shown marked change. The king penguin population is the best studied seabird species on Heard Island and has shown a dramatic increase since first recorded in 1947/48, with the population doubling every five years or so for more than 50 years.\n</p><p>A paper reviewing population data for the black-browed albatross between 1947 and 2000/01 suggested that the breeding population had increased to approximately three times that present in the late 1940s,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> although a Convention for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources CCAMLR) Working Group was cautious about the interpretation of the increasing trend given the disparate nature of the data,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> as discussed in the paper. The discovery of a large, previously unknown, colony of Heard shags in 2000/01 at Cape Pillar raised the known breeding population from 200 pairs to over 1000 pairs.<sup id="cite_ref-Woehler,_E.J._2006_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Woehler,_E.J._2006-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The breeding population of southern giant petrels decreased by more than 50% between the early 1950s and the late 1980s.\n</p>\n<h4><span id="Terrestrial.2C_freshwater_and_coastal_invertebrates"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Terrestrial,_freshwater_and_coastal_invertebrates">Terrestrial, freshwater and coastal invertebrates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Terrestrial, freshwater and coastal invertebrates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div An exotic species of earthworm <i><a href="/wiki/Dendrodrilus_rubidus" title="Dendrodrilus rubidus">Dendrodrilus rubidus</a></i> was also collected in 1929 from a dump near Atlas Cove, and has recently been collected from a variety of habitats including wallows, streams and lakes on Heard Island.\n</p><p>The arthropods of Heard Island are comparatively well known with 54 species of <a href="/wiki/Mite" title="Mite">mite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tick" title="Tick">tick</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Spider" title="Spider">spider</a> and eight <a href="/wiki/Springtail" title="Springtail">springtails</a> recorded. A study over summer at Atlas Cove in 1987/88 showed overall densities of up to 60 000 individual springtails per square metre in soil under stands of <i><a href="/wiki/Pringlea_antiscorbutica" class="mw-redirect" title="Pringlea antiscorbutica">Pringlea antiscorbutica</a></i>. Despite a few recent surveys, the non-arthropod invertebrate fauna of Heard Island remain poorly known.\n</p><p>Beetles and flies dominate Heard Island\'s known insect fauna, which comprises up to 21 species of ectoparasite (associated with birds and seals) and up to 13 free-living species. Approximately half of the free-living insects are habitat-specific, while the remainder are generalists found in a variety of habitats, being associated with either supralittoral or intertidal zones, <i><a href="/wiki/Poa_cookii" title="Poa cookii">Poa cookii</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pringlea_antiscorbutica" class="mw-redirect" title="Pringlea antiscorbutica">Pringlea antiscorbutica</a></i> stands, <a href="/wiki/Bryophyte" title="Bryophyte">bryophytes</a>, lichen-covered rocks, exposed rock faces or the underside of rocks. There is a pronounced seasonality to the insect fauna, with densities in winter months dropping to a small percentage (between 0.75%) of the summer maximum. Distinct differences in relative abundances of species between habitats has also been shown, including a negative relationship between altitude and body size for Heard Island weevils.\n</p><p>The fauna of the freshwater pools, lakes, streams and mires found in the coastal areas of Heard Island are broadly similar to those on other subantarctic islands of the southern Indian Ocean. Many species reported from Heard Island are found elsewhere. Some sampling of freshwater fauna has been undertaken during recent expeditions and records to date indicate that the freshwater fauna includes a species of <a href="/wiki/Protista" class="mw-redirect" title="Protista">Protista</a>, a gastrotrich, two species of <a href="/wiki/Tardigrade" title="Tardigrade">tardigrade</a>, at least four species of nematode, 26 species of rotifer, six species of annelid and 14 species of arthropod.\n</p><p>As with the other shore biota, the marine macro-invertebrate fauna of Heard Island is similar in composition and local distribution to other subantarctic islands, although relatively little is known about the Heard Island communities compared with the well-studied fauna of some other islands in the subantarctic region, such as <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Island" title="Macquarie Island">Macquarie</a> and Kerguelen.\n</p><p>Despite Heard Island\'s isolation, species richness is considered to be moderate, rather than depauperate, although the number of endemic species reported is low. The large macro-alga <i><a href="/wiki/Durvillaea_antarctica" title="Durvillaea antarctica">Durvillaea antarctica</a></i> supports a diverse array of invertebrate taxa and may play an important role in transporting some of this fauna to Heard Island.\n</p><p>The rocky shores of Heard Island exhibit a clear demarcation between fauna of the lower kelp holdfast zone and the upper shore zone community, probably due to effects of desiccation, predation and freezing in the higher areas. The limpet <i>Nacella kerguelensis</i> is abundant in the lower part of the shore, being found on rock surfaces and on kelp holdfasts. Other common but less abundant species in this habitat include the chiton <i><a href="/wiki/Hemiarthrum_setulosum" class="mw-redirect" title="Hemiarthrum setulosum">Hemiarthrum setulosum</a></i> and the starfish <i>Anasterias mawsoni</i>. The amphipod <i>Hyale</i> sp. and the isopod <i>Cassidinopsis</i> sp. are closely associated with the kelp. Above the kelp holdfast zone, the littornid <i>Laevilitorina (Corneolitorina) heardensis</i> and the bivalve mollusc <i>Kidderia bicolor</i> are found in well-sheltered situations, and another bivalve <i>Gaimardia trapesina trapesina</i> has been recorded from immediately above the holdfast zone. Oligochaetes are also abundant in areas supporting porous and spongy layers of algal mat.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Wetlands">Wetlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Wetlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Heard Island has a number of small wetland sites scattered around its coastal perimeter, including areas of wetland vegetation, lagoons or lagoon complexes, rocky shores and sandy shores, including the Elephant Spit. Many of these wetland areas are separated by active glaciers. There are also several short glacier-fed streams and glacial pools. Some wetland areas have been recorded on McDonald Island but, due to substantial volcanic activity since the last landing was made in 1980, their present extent is unknown.\n</p><p>The HIMI wetland is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Directory_of_Important_Wetlands_in_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Directory of Important Wetlands in Australia">Directory of Important Wetlands in Australia</a> and, in a recent analysis of Commonwealth-managed wetlands, was ranked highest for nomination under the <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_Convention" title="Ramsar Convention">Convention on Wetlands of International Importance Especially as Waterfowl Habitat (Ramsar Convention)</a> as an internationally important wetland.\n</p><p>Six wetland types have been identified from HIMI covering approximately 1860 ha: coastal \xe2\x80\x98pool complex\xe2\x80\x99 (237 ha); inland \xe2\x80\x98pool complex\xe2\x80\x99 (105 ha); vegetated seeps mostly on recent glaciated areas (18 ha); glacial lagoons (1103 ha); non-glacial lagoons (97ha); Elephant Spit (300 ha) plus some coastal areas. On Heard Island, the majority of these types suites are found below 150&#160;m asl. The wetland vegetation occurs in the \xe2\x80\x98wet mixed herbfield\xe2\x80\x99 and \xe2\x80\x98coastal biotic vegetation\xe2\x80\x99 communities described above.\n</p><p>The wetlands provide important breeding and feeding habitat for a number of Antarctic and subantarctic wetland animals. These include the southern elephant seal and macaroni, gentoo, king and southern rockhopper penguins, considered to be wetland species under the Ramsar Convention. Non-wetland vegetated parts of the islands also support penguin and other seabird colonies.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_and_McDonald_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg/220px-FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg" width="220" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg/330px-FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg/440px-FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="1947" data-file-height="1382" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FMIB_46007_Antarctic_Sea-Elephant_Fishery.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Heard Island 1887 by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Wood_Elliott" title="Henry Wood Elliott">Henry Wood Elliott</a><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>\n<p>Neither island-cluster had recorded visitors until the mid-1850s. Peter Kemp, a British sailor, may have become the first person to see the island.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (January 2013)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On 27 November 1833, he spotted it from the <a href="/wiki/Brig" title="Brig">brig</a> <i>Magnet</i> during a voyage from the <a href="/wiki/Kerguelen_Islands" title="Kerguelen Islands">Kerguelen Islands</a> to the Antarctic and was believed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (January 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> to have entered the island on his 1833 chart.\n</p><p>An American sailor, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_John_Heard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Captain John Heard (page does not exist)">Captain John Heard</a>, on the ship <i>Oriental</i>, sighted Heard Island on 25 November 1853, en route from <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> to Melbourne. He reported the discovery one month later and had the island named after him. Captain William McDonald aboard the <i>Samarang</i> <a href="/wiki/1854_in_Australia#Exploration_and_settlement" title="1854 in Australia">discovered the nearby McDonald Islands</a> six weeks later, on 4 January 1854.\n</p><p>No landing took place on the islands until March 1855, when sealers from the <i>Corinthian</i>, led by Captain <a href="/w/index.php?title=Erasmus_Darwin_Rogers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Erasmus Darwin Rogers (page does not exist)">Erasmus Darwin Rogers</a>, went ashore at a place called Oil Barrel Point. In the sailing period from 1855 to 1880 a number of American sealers spent a year or more on the island, living in appalling conditions in dark smelly huts, also at Oil Barrel Point. At its peak the community consisted of 200 people. By 1880 sealers had wiped out most of the seal population and then left the island. In all the islands furnished more than 100,000 barrels of <a href="/wiki/Elephant_seal" title="Elephant seal">elephant-seal</a> oil during this period.\n</p><p>A number of wrecks have occurred in the vicinity of the islands. There is also a discarded building left from John Heard\'s sealing station which is situated near Atlas Cove.\n</p><p>The first recorded landing on McDonald Island was made by Australian scientists Grahame Budd and Hugh Thelander on 12 February 1971, using a helicopter.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-aad_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aad-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The islands have been a territory of Australia since 1947, when they were transferred from the UK<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook._1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook.-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The archipelago became a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> in 1997.\n</p><p>Several <a href="/wiki/Amateur_radio" title="Amateur radio">amateur radio</a> operators have visited Heard, often associated with scientific expeditions. The first activity there was in 1947 by Alan Campbell-Drury. Amateur radio <a href="/wiki/DXpedition" class="mw-redirect" title="DXpedition">DXpeditions</a> to the island took place in 1983 (two) and January 1997. Another expedition began operation on 23 March 2016 organised by Cordell Expeditions.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1991, the islands were the location for the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heard_Island_feasibility_test&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Heard Island feasibility test (page does not exist)">Heard Island feasibility test</a>, an experiment in very long distance transmission of low frequency sound through the ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The US Navy vessels <a href="/wiki/MV_Cory_Chouest" title="MV Cory Chouest">MV&#160;<i>Cory Chouest</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/MV_Amy_Chouest" class="mw-redirect" title="MV Amy Chouest"><i>Amy Chouest</i></a> were used to transmit signals which could be detected as far away as both ocean coasts of the US and Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Cory Chouest</i> was chosen because of its central <a href="/wiki/Moon_pool" title="Moon pool">moon pool</a> and because it was already equipped with an array of low frequency transmitters. A <a href="/wiki/Phase_modulation" title="Phase modulation">phase-modulated</a> 57Hz signal was used. The experiment was successful and demonstrated that such sound waves could travel as far as the <a href="/wiki/Antipodes" title="Antipodes">antipodes</a>. Planned transmissions had been for ten days, although owing to the bad weather conditions and the high failure rate of the transmitter elements, used at a frequency below their design frequency, the transmissions were terminated on the sixth day, when only two of the original ten transducers were still working.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration_and_economy">Administration class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Heard Island, 1929.</div></div></div>\n<p>The United Kingdom formally established its claim to Heard Island in 1910, marked by the raising of the Union Flag and the erection of a beacon by Captain Evensen, master of the <i>Mangoro</i>. Effective government, administration and control of Heard Island and the McDonald Islands was transferred to the Australian government on 26 December 1947 at the commencement of the first <a href="/wiki/Australian_National_Antarctic_Research_Expeditions" title="Australian National Antarctic Research Expeditions">Australian National Antarctic Research Expedition</a> (ANARE) to Heard Island, with a formal declaration that took place at Atlas Cove. The transfer was confirmed by an exchange of letters between the two governments on 19 December 1950.\n</p><p>The islands are a territory (Territory of Heard Island and McDonald Islands) of Australia administered from <a href="/wiki/Hobart" title="Hobart">Hobart</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Antarctic_Division" title="Australian Antarctic Division">Australian Antarctic Division</a> of the Australian <a href="/wiki/Department_of_the_Environment_and_Energy" title="Department of the Environment and Energy">Department of the Environment and Energy</a>. The administration of the territory is established in the <i>Heard Island and McDonald Islands Act 1953</i>, which places it under the laws of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Australian Capital Territory">Australian Capital Territory</a> and the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Supreme Court of the Australian Capital Territory">Supreme Court of the Australian Capital Territory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The islands are contained within a 65,000-square-kilometre (25,000&#160;sq&#160;mi) marine reserve and are primarily visited for research, meaning that there is no permanent human habitation.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook._1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook.-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>From 1947 until 1955 there were camps of visiting scientists on Heard Island (at <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Cove" title="Atlas Cove">Atlas Cove</a> in the northwest, which was in 1969 again occupied by American scientists and expanded in 1971 by French scientists) and in 1971 on McDonald Island (at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Williams_Bay,_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Williams Bay, Australia (page does not exist)">Williams Bay</a>). Later expeditions used a temporary base at <a href="/wiki/Spit_Bay" title="Spit Bay">Spit Bay</a> in the east, such as in 1988, 1992\xe2\x80\x9393 and 2004\xe2\x80\x9305.\n</p><p>The islands\' only natural resource is fish; the Australian government allows limited fishing in the surrounding waters.<sup id="cite_ref-CIA_World_Factbook._1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_World_Factbook.-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Despite the lack of population, the islands have been assigned the <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country code</a> HM in <a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1" title="ISO 3166-1">ISO 3166-1</a> (<a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-2:HM" title="ISO 3166-2:HM">ISO 3166-2:HM</a>) and therefore the Internet <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> <a href="/wiki/.hm" title=".hm">.hm</a>. The timezone of the islands is UTC+5.<sup id="cite_ref-timegenie_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timegenie-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
