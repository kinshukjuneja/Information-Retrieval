class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="/ju\xcb\x90/: &#39;u&#39; in &#39;cute&#39;">ju\xcb\x90</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Cornish_language" title="Cornish language">Cornish</a>: <i lang="kw">Porthbud</i><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">seaside resort</a> town in north <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, England, UK, in the civil parish of <a href="/wiki/Bude-Stratton" class="mw-redirect" title="Bude-Stratton">Bude-Stratton</a> and at the mouth of the River Neet (also known locally as the River Strat). It was sometimes formerly known as Bude Haven.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated66_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated66-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It lies southwest of <a href="/wiki/Stratton,_Cornwall" title="Stratton, Cornwall">Stratton</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Flexbury" class="mw-redirect" title="Flexbury">Flexbury</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poughill" title="Poughill">Poughill</a>, and north of <a href="/wiki/Widemouth_Bay" title="Widemouth Bay">Widemouth Bay</a> and is located along the A3073 road off the <a href="/wiki/A39_road" title="A39 road">A39</a>. Bude is <a href="/wiki/Twin_towns_and_sister_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin towns and sister cities">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ergu%C3%A9-Gab%C3%A9ric" title="Ergu\xc3\xa9-Gab\xc3\xa9ric">Ergu\xc3\xa9-Gab\xc3\xa9ric</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, France.<sup id="cite_ref-bude_people_1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bude_people_1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Bude\'s coast faces Bude Bay in the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Sea" title="Celtic Sea">Celtic Sea</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. The population of the civil parish can be found under <a href="/wiki/Bude-Stratton" class="mw-redirect" title="Bude-Stratton">Bude-Stratton</a>.\n</p><p>Its earlier importance was as a harbour, and then a source of sea sand useful for improving the moorland soil. The <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorians</a> favoured it as a watering place, and it was a popular seaside destination in the 20th century.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>It lies just west of <a href="/wiki/Stratton,_Cornwall" title="Stratton, Cornwall">Stratton</a> and north of <a href="/wiki/Widemouth_Bay" title="Widemouth Bay">Widemouth Bay</a> and is located along the A3073 road off the <a href="/wiki/A39_road" title="A39 road">A39 road</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coastline">Coastline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coastline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>A section of Bude\'s coast which is located between Compass Cove to the south and Furzey Cove to the north, is a SSSI (<a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a>) noted for its <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Cornwall" title="Geology of Cornwall">geological</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna_of_Cornwall" title="Flora and fauna of Cornwall">biological</a> interest.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Carboniferous</a> <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> cliffs surround Bude. During the <a href="/wiki/Variscan_Orogeny" class="mw-redirect" title="Variscan Orogeny">Variscan Orogeny</a> the strata were heavily faulted and folded. As the sands and cliffs around Bude contain calcium carbonate (a natural fertiliser), farmers used to take sand from the beach, for spreading on their fields. The cliffs around Bude are the only ones in Cornwall that are made of Carboniferous sandstone, as most of the Cornish coast is formed of <a href="/wiki/Devonian" title="Devonian">Devonian</a> <a href="/wiki/Slate" title="Slate">slate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Precambrian" title="Precambrian">Precambrian</a> <a href="/wiki/Metamorphic_rocks" class="mw-redirect" title="Metamorphic rocks">metamorphic rocks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SSSI_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SSSI-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The stratified cliffs of Bude give their name to a <a href="/wiki/Rock_unit" class="mw-redirect" title="Rock unit">sequence of rocks</a> called the Bude Formation. Many formations can be viewed from the <a href="/wiki/South_West_Coast_Path" title="South West Coast Path">South West Coast Path</a> which passes through the town.\n</p><p>Many ships have been wrecked on the jagged reefs which fringe the base of the cliffs. The figurehead of one of these, the <i>Bencoolen</i>, a barque whose wrecking in 1862 resulted in the drowning of most of the crew, was preserved in the churchyard but was transferred to the town museum to save it from further decay.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The aftermath of the wreck of the <i>Bencoolen</i> was described by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stephen_Hawker" title="Robert Stephen Hawker">Robert Stephen Hawker</a> in letters which were published in Hawker\'s <i>Poetical Works</i> (1879).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Like the rest of the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> and South West England, Bude experiences a <a href="/wiki/Maritime_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime climate">maritime climate</a> with cool summers and mild winters. Another chapel existed at Chapel Rock which was dedicated to Holy Trinity and St Michael.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated66_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated66-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bude_Canal" title="Bude Canal">Bude Canal</a>, which once ran to <a href="/wiki/Launceston,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Launceston, England">Launceston</a>, now runs only a few miles inland. Several historic wharf buildings were demolished in the 1980s, but since then the canal has undergone restoration.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Until the start of the 20th century, the neighbouring town of <a href="/wiki/Stratton,_Cornwall" title="Stratton, Cornwall">Stratton</a> was dominant, and a local saying is "Stratton was a market town when Bude was just a furzy down", meaning Stratton was long established when Bude was just gorse-covered downland. (A similar saying is current at Saltash about Plymouth.)\n</p><p>On 10 October 1844, during an exercise, the unnamed Bude <a href="/wiki/Lifeboat_(rescue)" title="Lifeboat (rescue)">Lifeboat</a> capsized when the steering oar broke followed by four on the port side, and two of the crew were drowned.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The local senior school <a href="/wiki/Budehaven_Community_School" title="Budehaven Community School">Budehaven Community School</a> suffered a major fire in October 1999, destroying most of the older parts of the school. The school was forced to close for several weeks until temporary classrooms could be brought in. The damaged part of the school was rebuilt with interactive classrooms.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Present-day Bude has two beaches with broad sands close to the town, and is a good centre for adjacent beaches. Its sea front faces west and the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> rollers make for good surfing when conditions are right.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The main access road into and out of Bude is the <a href="/wiki/A39_road" title="A39 road">Atlantic Highway (A39)</a>.\n</p><p>Stagecoach South West  operates numerous bus services in and around Bude, with direct services to local towns, such as Holsworthy, Wadebridge Bideford, and Barnstaple.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Victorian_resort">Victorian resort</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Victorian resort">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bude_outer_harbour.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bude_outer_harbour.jpg/220px-Bude_outer_harbour.jpg" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bude_outer_harbour.jpg/330px-Bude_outer_harbour.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bude_outer_harbour.jpg/440px-Bude_outer_harbour.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1709" data-file-height="1303" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bude_outer_harbour.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Haven, the Atlantic Ocean and the beach at Bude</div></div></div>\n<p>In the latter part of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>\'s reign, the middle classes were discovering the attractions of sea bathing, and the romantic movement encouraged an appreciation of wild scenery and the <a href="/wiki/Matter_of_Britain" title="Matter of Britain">Arthurian Legend</a>. To serve this desire, a railway line was extended to Bude in 1898. This encouraged the holiday trade, but Bude never rivalled <a href="/wiki/Newquay" title="Newquay">Newquay</a> or the resorts in south Cornwall and <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beaches">Beaches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a are a number of good beaches in the Bude area, many of which offer good <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfing</a> conditions. Bude was the founder club in British Surf Life Saving.\n</p>\n<ul><li>Summerleaze, Crooklets and \'middle\' beach, are all within the town;</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Widemouth_Bay" title="Widemouth Bay">Widemouth Bay</a> is a few miles south of the town and offers a long, wide sandy beach;</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Sandymouth_Beach" title="Sandymouth Beach">Sandymouth Beach</a> is owned by the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a>, and has spectacular cliffs and rock formations with shingle below the cliffs and a large expanse of sand at low tide. There are also a number of other coves and beaches to be found and explored in the local area.</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bude_Harbour_and_Canal">Bude Harbour and Canal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bude Harbour and Canal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the 18th century there was a small unprotected tidal harbour at Bude, but it was difficult whenever the sea was up. The Bude Canal Company built a canal and improved the harbour. Around twenty small boats use the tidal moorings of the original harbour during the summer months. Most are sport fishermen, but there is also some small-scale, semi-commercial, fishing for crab and lobster.\n</p><p>There is a wharf on the <a href="/wiki/Bude_Canal" title="Bude Canal">Bude Canal</a> about half a mile from the sea lock that links the canal to the tidal haven. This can be opened only at or near high tide, and then only when sea conditions allow. <a href="/wiki/North_Cornwall_District_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="North Cornwall District Council">North Cornwall District Council</a> administered the canal, harbour and lock gates until its abolition in March 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> These gates were renewed after the originals were damaged in a storm in 2008. They are the only manually operated sea lock gates in England. The pier head by the locks is a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed structure</a>.\n</p><p>The canal is one of the few of note in south-west England. Its original purpose was to take small <a href="/wiki/Tub_boat" title="Tub boat">tub boats</a> of mineral-rich sand from the beaches at Bude and carry them inland for agricultural use on fields. A series of <a href="/wiki/Canal_inclined_plane" title="Canal inclined plane">inclined planes</a> carried the boats over 400 vertical feet (120 m) to <a href="/wiki/Red_Post" title="Red Post">Red Post</a>, where the canal branched south along the upper Tamar Valley towards Launceston, east to Holsworthy and north to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tamar_Lakes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tamar Lakes (page does not exist)">Tamar Lakes</a>, that fed the canal. The enterprise was always in financial difficulty, but it carried considerable volumes of sand and also coal from south Wales. The arrival of the railway at Holsworthy and the production of cheap manufactured fertiliser undermined the canal\'s commercial purpose, and it was closed down and sold to the district municipal water company. However the wharf area and harbour enjoyed longer success, and coastal sailing ships carried grain across to Wales and coal back to Cornwall.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_buildings">Notable buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notable buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg/220px-Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg/330px-Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg/440px-Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="412" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bude_castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1457352.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bude Castle</div></div></div>\n<p>Notable buildings include the Perpendicular medieval parish church (<a href="/wiki/St_Olaf%27s_Church,_Poughill" title="St Olaf&#39;s Church, Poughill">St Olaf\'s</a>) in the village of <a href="/wiki/Poughill" title="Poughill">Poughill</a> just outside Bude; the parish church of St Michael and All Angels, built in 1835 and enlarged in 1876 (the architect was <a href="/wiki/George_Wightwick" title="George Wightwick">George Wightwick</a>), Ebbingford Manor,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and the town\'s oldest house, Quay Cottage in the centre of town. Bude Castle was built about 1830 for Victorian inventor Sir <a href="/wiki/Goldsworthy_Gurney" title="Goldsworthy Gurney">Goldsworthy Gurney</a> and is now a heritage centre.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>At the northernmost point of Efford Down Farm, overlooking Summerleaze Beach and the breakwater, a former coastguard lookout stands. Known as Compass Point and built by the Acland family in 1840 of local sandstone, it is based on the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_the_Winds" title="Tower of the Winds">Temple of Winds in Athens</a>. It was moved to its current position in 1880. It is so called as it has points of the compass carved in each of its octagonal sides.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2017, the so-called "Bude Tunnel", a 70 metre Perspex walkway at the Bude <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury\'s</a> supermarket site gained notoriety after becoming the town\'s top-rated attraction on <a href="/wiki/TripAdvisor" title="TripAdvisor">TripAdvisor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Internet_troll" title="Internet troll">Pranksters</a> spammed the page with over 170 positive reviews of the site, whereas critics retorted that this prank detracted from Bude\'s "genuine" tourist attractions.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway">Railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>From 1879 Bude\'s nearest railway station was at <a href="/wiki/Holsworthy_railway_station" title="Holsworthy railway station">Holsworthy</a>, ten miles away. The railway came to Bude itself in 1898.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The line was built by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_South_Western_Railway" title="London and South Western Railway">London and South Western Railway</a>, but was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Railway (Great Britain)">Southern Railway</a> in 1923 and <a href="/wiki/British_Railways" class="mw-redirect" title="British Railways">British Railways</a> in 1948.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bude_railway_station" title="Bude railway station">Bude railway station</a> was served by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Coast_Express" title="Atlantic Coast Express">Atlantic Coast Express</a>, providing a direct service to/from <a href="/wiki/London_Waterloo_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="London Waterloo railway station">London (Waterloo)</a>; the "ACE" was discontinued in 1964. Bude station and the entire <a href="/wiki/Okehampton_to_Bude_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Okehampton to Bude Line">Bude branch line</a> closed on 1 October 1966 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Beeching_Axe" class="mw-redirect" title="Beeching Axe">Beeching Axe</a>.\n</p><p>Bude and neighbouring Stratton are more distant from the rail network than any other towns in England and Wales.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Barnstaple (35&#160;miles north east), Bodmin Parkway (32&#160;miles south) and Gunnislake (32&#160;miles sse) are the nearest <a href="/wiki/National_Rail" title="National Rail">National Rail</a> stations with regular services. Okehampton (29&#160;miles) has occasional summer-only trains from Exeter. A "rail link" coach runs from <a href="/wiki/Exeter_St_Davids_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Exeter St Davids railway station">Exeter St Davids railway station</a> to Bude Strand via <a href="/wiki/Okehampton" title="Okehampton">Okehampton</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Tourism is the main industry in the Bude area whilst some fishing is carried on. In the past, the staple trade was the export of sand, which, being highly charged with <a href="/wiki/Calcium_carbonate" title="Calcium carbonate">calcium carbonate</a>, was much used as fertiliser. There are also golf links in the town.\n</p><p>The UK\'s top manufacturer of air rifle tuning kits, Tinbum Tuning (TbT) are based just outside of the town in the village of Marhamchurch.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>The largest employer associated with Bude is <a href="/wiki/GCHQ_Bude" title="GCHQ Bude">GCHQ Bude</a>, with over 250 civil servants and contractors, sited north of the town near <a href="/wiki/Morwenstow" title="Morwenstow">Morwenstow</a>.\n</p><p>Bude has an industrial estate which houses Bott Ltd, which manufactures racking and tool-holding accessories and storage systems for vans and workshops. It was the home of Tripos Receptor Research, which produced prototypes of drugs for the pharmaceutical industry until it ceased trading in 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Bude is in the <a href="/wiki/North_Cornwall_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="North Cornwall (UK Parliament constituency)">North Cornwall parliamentary constituency</a> which is represented by Scott Mann MP. It developed from the much older market town of <a href="/wiki/Stratton,_Cornwall" title="Stratton, Cornwall">Stratton</a>, 1&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span>&#160;miles inland to the east. Since <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> became a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority#England" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> in 2009 there has been a two-tier structure of local government: <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a> (administers, for example, schools and highways, housing, social services, canal and harbour, refuse and recycling collection, street cleanliness); and Bude-Stratton Town Council (local children\'s playground, Bude "castle"). There was some local argument when the town council adopted the name Bude-Stratton, as it was previously Stratton-Bude. Bude\'s population in 1901 was 2308; by 2001 it had risen to 4674.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The town is home to a number of sports teams including <a href="/wiki/Bude_RFC" title="Bude RFC">Bude RFC</a> \xe2\x80\x94 the town\'s rugby club, and Bude Town FC \xe2\x80\x94 the local football club. Bude is the host town of the North Cornwall Cup, a large youth football event held every August. Bude &amp; North Cornwall Golf Club is ideally situated within the town centre. Bude is also home to the Bude Cricket Club.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bude&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>.
