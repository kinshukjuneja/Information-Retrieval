and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a> data), year 2005; Number of inhabitants in thousands.</div></div></div>\n<p>According to the 2016 census, Lesotho has a total population of 2,007,201.<sup id="cite_ref-census_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Of the population, 34.17 percent lived in urban and 65.83 percent in rural areas.<sup id="cite_ref-census_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The country\'s capital, <a href="/wiki/Maseru" title="Maseru">Maseru</a>, accounts for around half of the total urban population. The sex distribution is 982,133 male and 1,025,068 female, or around 96 males for each 100 females.<sup id="cite_ref-census_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The average population density in the country is around 66,1 people per square kilometer. The density is lower in the <a href="/wiki/Lesotho_Highlands" title="Lesotho Highlands">Lesotho Highlands</a> than in the western lowlands. Although the majority of the population\xe2\x80\x9456.1 percent\xe2\x80\x94is between 15 and 64 years of age, Lesotho has a substantial youth population numbering around 37.8 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The annual population growth rate is estimated at 0.13%<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>According to the 2017 revision of the World Population Prospects<sup id="cite_ref-UN_WPP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_WPP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the total population was 2,203,821 in 2016, compared to only  734 000 in 1950. The proportion of children below the age of 15 in 2010 was 37.4%, 58.3% was between 15 and 65 years of age, while 4.3% was 65 years or older\n.<sup id="cite_ref-WPP_2010_4-0" class="reference"><a history as a unified nation, that continued even through <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> colonial rule, the ethnic makeup of the country is very homogenous. Lesotho\'s ethno-linguistic structure consists almost entirely of the <a href="/wiki/Basotho" title="Basotho">Basotho</a> (singular <i>Mosotho</i>), a Bantu-speaking people: an estimate of 99.7 percent of the people identify as Basotho.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Kwena (Bakoena) are the largest subgroup of the Sotho; other Basotho subgroups include the Natal (North) Nguni, Batloung (the Tlou), Baphuthi (the Phuti), Bafokeng, Bataung (the Tau), Bats\'oeneng (the tso\'ene) and the Cape (South) Nguni (Thembu). Other ethnic groups include Europeans, numbering in the thousands, and several hundred Asians.<sup id="cite_ref-commonwealth_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commonwealth-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sotho_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Sotho language">Sesotho</a> (Southern Sotho) and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> languages are both official.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zulu_language" title="Zulu language">Zulu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xhosa_language" title="Xhosa language">Xhosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> are also spoken.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Lesotho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Religion_in_Lesotho" title="Religion in Lesotho">Religion in Lesotho</a></div>\n<p>The population of Lesotho is estimated to be around 90 percent <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-religion_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-religion-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholics</a>, the largest religious group, make up around 45 percent of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-religion_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-religion-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Evangelicalism" title="Evangelicalism">Evangelicals</a> comprise 26 percent of the population, and <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican</a> and other Christian groups an additional 19 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-religion_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-religion-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Muslims</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hindus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baha%27i_Faith" class="mw-redirect" title="Baha&#39;i Faith">Baha\'i</a>, and members of traditional indigenous religions comprise the remaining 10 percent of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-religion_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-religion-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_literacy">Education and literacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Lesotho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education and literacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>According to recent estimates, 85 percent of the population 15 and over was literate. Among women the literacy rate was around 95 percent, and among men around 75 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-factbook_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-factbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As such, Lesotho boasts one of the higher literacy rates in Africa.  Although education is not compulsory, the Government of Lesotho is incrementally implementing a programme for free primary education. It was expected that the program would be fully in place by 2006. The <a href="/wiki/National_University_of_Lesotho" title="National University of Lesotho">National University of Lesotho</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Roma,_Lesotho" title="Roma, Lesotho">Roma</a> and the Limkokwing University of Creative Technology located in the heart of Maseru, Moshoeshoe 2 are the only universities in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-inhea_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inhea-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the country has almost 20 other public and 15 private institutes giving tertiary education.<sup id="cite_ref-inhea_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inhea-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span
