href="http://www.cmas.org/">http://www.cmas.org/</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p><b>Conf\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Mondiale des Activit\xc3\xa9s Subaquatiques</b> (CMAS) is an international federation that represents underwater activities in <a href="/wiki/Underwater_sports" title="Underwater sports">underwater sport</a> and underwater sciences, and oversees an international system of recreational <a href="/wiki/Snorkelling" class="mw-redirect" title="Snorkelling">snorkel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuba_diving" title="Scuba diving">scuba diver</a> training and recognition.  It is also known by its English name, the <i>World Underwater Federation</i>, and its Spanish name, <i>Confederacion Mundial De Actividades Subacuaticas</i>. Its foundation in <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> during January 1959 makes it one of the world\'s oldest <a href="/wiki/Underwater_diving" title="Underwater diving">underwater diving</a> organisations.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> on 28 September 1958. National\ndelegates attended from following countries: Belgium, Brazil, France, the Federal Republic of Germany, Greece, Italy, Monaco, Portugal, Switzerland, the United States of America and the former <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. Following a decision at that congress, a meeting was held in <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> on 9\xe2\x80\x9311 January 1959, which officially established the World Underwater Federation, with an acronym based on its French title as <i>CMAS</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-aboutcmas_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aboutcmas-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>A founding member and key proponent of <i>CMAS</i> was the French underwater explorer and diving pioneer <a href="/wiki/Jacques-Yves_Cousteau" class="mw-redirect" title="Jacques-Yves Cousteau">Jacques-Yves Cousteau</a> who was chosen to be the inaugural president with Luigi Ferraro, Italian underwater pioneer, appointed as vice-president.<sup id="cite_ref-Luigi_Ferraro;_C.M.A.S._3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luigi_Ferraro;_C.M.A.S.-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>CMAS succeeded the <i>Comit\xc3\xa9 des Sports Sous-Marins</i> (Underwater Sports Committee) of the <a href="/wiki/Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_la_P%C3%AAche_Sportive" title="Conf\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Internationale de la P\xc3\xaache Sportive">Conf\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration Internationale de la P\xc3\xaache Sportive</a> (CIPS) (International Confederation of Sport Fishing), which was founded on 22 February 1952.<sup id="cite_ref-aboutcmas_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aboutcmas-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Luigi_Ferraro;_C.M.A.S._3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Luigi_Ferraro;_C.M.A.S.-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>CMAS consists of 3 major committees - sport, technical and scientific.<sup id="cite_ref-technical_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-technical-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sport_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sport-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-scientific_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scientific-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  These committees are overseen by a board of directors (BoD) elected periodically at the annually convened general assembly. The BoD, the sport committee and the scientific committee oversee sub-committees known as commissions. Day-to-day operation is overseen by a steering committee appointed from the BoD. Its headquarters is currently located in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_steering_committee">The steering committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The steering committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The steering committee consists of seven members. Committee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The role of the Technical Committee is the provision of \xe2\x80\x98safe diving for CMAS members\xe2\x80\x99 and seeks to achieve this by \xe2\x80\x98promoting world class standards for all aspects of Scuba Diving and ensuring adherence of them by member federations and dive providers\xe2\x80\x99.<sup id="cite_ref-About-CMAS-TC_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About-CMAS-TC-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  Its officers who are elected from persons nominated at the CMAS General Assembly by affiliated national diving federations include the following positions - President, Secretary, Standards Director, Education Director, Technical Director, Diving Security Director, Special Tasks Director and a number of general members.  It oversees the two following systems - a diver training standards system known as the <i>CMAS International Diver Training Standards</i> and a certification system known as <i>CMAS International Diver Certificates</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-About-CMAS-TC_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About-CMAS-TC-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Learn_To_Dive_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Learn_To_Dive-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Since CMAS effectively started as a volunteer organisation for hobbyists, its courses tend to reflect the full range of European and world diving standards. Compared to other diving organisations which may be more geared towards holiday and tropical water diving. While organisations like <a href="/wiki/PADI" class="mw-redirect" title="PADI">PADI</a> or <a href="/wiki/Scuba_Schools_International" title="Scuba Schools International">SSI</a> tend to bring divers into the water immediately, CMAS entry-level training is more extensive, featuring more "classroom" delivered theory.<sup id="cite_ref-dtp_16-0" class="reference"><a recognised worldwide particularly in countries where CMAS affiliated federations exist. The system includes a double sided certification card format where one side depicts the achieved CMAS standard while the other side has details of the issuing organisation and the diver.<sup id="cite_ref-Learn_To_Dive_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Learn_To_Dive-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Certificate_format_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Certificate_format-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>CMAS itself does not provide training or conduct the issuing of certifications - this is available from two sources. The instructor is qualified to conduct practical lessons in a swimming pool and to teach the CMAS 1 star snorkel diver certificate.  The instructor can be used as an assistant instructor in open water, but cannot plan an open water dive and lead a group of divers on his own.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><b>Two Star Snorkel Diver Instructor</b> - \xe2\x80\x9can experienced one star snorkel diver instructor who has the knowledge, skills, and experience required to lead and instruct 1, 2 and 3 Star snorkel diver students in the classroom, swimming pool, and open water.  The experienced CMAS 2 Star snorkel diver instructor may assist in the training and education of CMAS 1 Star snorkel diver instructors.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-Snorkel_Diver_Standards_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snorkel_Diver_Standards-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><b>One Star Instructor</b> - \xe2\x80\x9ca three star or four star diver who has demonstrated a knowledge of the techniques of diving instruction and has proven, under evaluation, to be competent in practical instructional skills and diving safety procedures: he or she is qualified to train and certify novice diving students in a full CMAS One Star Diver scuba program.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><b>Two Star Instructor</b> - \xe2\x80\x9can experienced one star instructor who has the knowledge, skills, and experience required to teach groups of divers in the classroom, pool, and in open water, and to train qualified dive supervisors, assistant instructors and assist in the training &amp; evaluation of One Star Instructors. He is qualified to teach and certify all CMAS diver levels including snorkel Instructor levels.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li><b>Three Star Instructor</b> - \xe2\x80\x9ca highly experienced two star instructor who is competent to train all grades of divers and instructors and able to take responsibility for instructor certification programs and the conduct of diving schools/centres and specialised training courses or events.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-diver_definitions_25-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-diver_definitions-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n</div>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Committee considers its main task is to bring to the attention of the world underwater diving community, the important issues concerning the marine environment and how divers can play a major role in protecting it by serving as frontline observers of its overall health, particularly in respect to invasive species, coastal ecosystems and biodiversity.<sup id="cite_ref-about_scientific_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about_scientific-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\nIts officers who are elected from persons nominated at the CMAS General Assembly by affiliated national diving federations include the following positions - President, Secretary, a number of general members and Presidents of the following commissions - marine biology, marine archaeology, geology and professional relationships.<sup id="cite_ref-scientific_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scientific-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_diving_codes">Scientific diving codes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Scientific diving codes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Over a 10-year period from 1977, it was responsible for the development of the "Code of Practice for Scientific Diving" for <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a><sup id="cite_ref-Flemming_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Flemming-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  in cooperation with <a href="/wiki/National_Sea_Grant_College_Program" title="National Sea Grant College Program">Sea Grant</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifications_2">Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Mondiale_des_Activit%C3%A9s_Subaquatiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The CMAS Scientific Committee oversees a system of diving standards and certification that operates in parallel to the CMAS International Diver Training Certification System.\nThe system was developed to which recognize the status of a diver who is qualified to dive in the course of research whilst employed.  This internationally recognized standard of competence is a distinct advantage for working scientists who wish to travel between laboratories and institutes in different countries.\nKnown as the CMAS Scientific Diver Standard, the system consists of the following diver and instructor grades:\n</p>\n<ul><li>CMAS Scientific Diver (CSD) (pre-requisite of CMAS 2 Star Diver plus scientific diver training)</li>\n<li>CMAS Advanced Scientific Diver (CASD) (pre-requisite of CMAS 3 Star Diver plus advanced scientific diver training)</li>\n<li>CMAS Scientific Diving Instructor (pre-requisite: CMAS 2 Star Instructor)</li>\n<li>CMAS Confirmed Scientific Diving Instructor (pre-requisite: CMAS 3 Star Instructor)</li></ul>\n<p>A programme of specialist courses both at entry and advanced levels in <a href="/wiki/Underwater_archaeology" title="Underwater archaeology">underwater archaeology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freshwater_biology" title="Freshwater biology">freshwater biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marine_biology" title="Marine biology">marine biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marine_geology" title="Marine geology">marine geology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oceanology" class="mw-redirect" title="Oceanology">oceanology</a> is also offered.\nTraining and certification (also known as brevets) for the above qualifications is available from organisations known as CMAS Scientific Centres (CSC).<sup id="cite_ref-Scientific_diver_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scientific_diver-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
