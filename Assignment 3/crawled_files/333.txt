<i><a href="/wiki/Another_Place_(sculpture)" title="Another Place (sculpture)">Another Place</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Crosby_Beach" title="Crosby Beach">Crosby Beach</a> near <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Event_Horizon_(sculpture)" title="Event Horizon (sculpture)">Event Horizon</a></i>, a multi-part site installation which premiered in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> in 2007, around <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square" class="mw-redirect" title="Madison Square">Madison Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, in 2010, in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="S\xc3\xa3o Paulo">S\xc3\xa3o Paulo</a>, Brazil, in 2012, and in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Antony_Gormley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The youngest of seven children born to a German mother and an Irish father,<sup id="cite_ref-Guard250605_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard250605-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Gormley has stated that his parents chose his initials, "AMDG" to have the inference <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">Ad maiorem Dei gloriam</i></i> - "to the greater glory of God".<sup id="cite_ref-AGBH_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AGBH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gormley" title="Gormley">Gormley</a> grew up in a <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> family living in <a href="/wiki/Hampstead_Garden_Suburb" title="Hampstead Garden Suburb">Hampstead Garden Suburb</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Guard250605_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard250605-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Ampleforth_College" title="Ampleforth College">Ampleforth College</a> a Benedictine boarding school in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Guard250605_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard250605-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> before reading archaeology, anthropology and the history of art at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge" title="Trinity College, Cambridge">Trinity College, Cambridge</a>, from 1968 to 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-Guard250605_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard250605-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He travelled to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> to learn more about <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> between 1971 and 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-Guard250605_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard250605-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> After attending <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin%27s_School_of_Art" title="Saint Martin&#39;s School of Art">Saint Martin\'s School of Art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Goldsmiths" class="mw-redirect" title="Goldsmiths">Goldsmiths</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> from 1974, he completed his studies with a postgraduate course in sculpture at the <a href="/wiki/Slade_School_of_Fine_Art" title="Slade School of Fine Art">Slade School of Fine Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_College,_London" class="mw-redirect" title="University College, London">University College, London</a>, between 1977 and 1979.\n</p><p>While at the Slade, he met <a href="/wiki/Vicken_Parsons" title="Vicken Parsons">Vicken Parsons</a>, who was to become his assistant and, in 1980, his wife, as well as a successful artist in her own right.<sup id="cite_ref-AGBH_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AGBH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Phillips_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Gormley said of her:<sup id="cite_ref-AGBH_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AGBH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>For the first 15 years she was my primary assistant. She did all of the body moulding... I think there are a lot of myths that art is made by, usually, lone men... I just feel so lucky and so blessed really, that I have such a strong supporter, and lover, and fellow artist.\n</p></blockquote>\n<p>The couple have three children, a daughter and two sons.<sup id="cite_ref-NAgain_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NAgain-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Jones_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jones-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antony_Gormley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gormley_pair-341.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Gormley_pair-341.JPG/225px-Gormley_pair-341.JPG" width="225" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Gormley_pair-341.JPG/338px-Gormley_pair-341.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Gormley_pair-341.JPG/450px-Gormley_pair-341.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gormley_pair-341.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pair of figures separated by plate glass, <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Place" title="Regent&#39;s Place">Regent\'s Place</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a></div></div></div>\n<p>Gormley\'s career began with a solo exhibition at the <a href="/wiki/Whitechapel_Art_Gallery" class="mw-redirect" title="Whitechapel Art Gallery">Whitechapel Art Gallery</a> in 1981. Almost all his work takes the human body as its subject, with his own body used in many works as the basis for <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metal</a> <a href="/wiki/Casting" title="Casting">casts</a>.\n</p><p>Gormley describes his work as "an attempt to materialise the place at the other side of appearance where we all live."<sup id="cite_ref-Making_Space_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Making_Space-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Many of his works are based on moulds taken from his own body, or "the closest experience of matter that I will ever have and the only part of the material world that I live inside."<sup id="cite_ref-Making_Space_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Making_Space-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His work attempts to treat the body not as an object but a place and in making works that enclose the space of a particular body to identify a condition common to all human beings. The work is not symbolic but indexical \xe2\x80\x93 a trace of a real event of a real body in time.\n</p><p>The 2006 <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Biennale" class="mw-redirect" title="Sydney Biennale">Sydney Biennale</a> featured Gormley\'s <i>Asian Field</i>, an installation of 180,000 small clay figurines crafted by 350 Chinese villagers in five days from 100 tons of red clay.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The appropriation of others\' works caused minor controversy and some of the figurines were stolen in protest.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Also in 2006, the burning of Gormley\'s 25-metre high <i>The Waste Man</i> formed the zenith of the <a href="/wiki/Margate_Exodus" class="mw-redirect" title="Margate Exodus">Margate Exodus</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p>In 2007, Gormley\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Event_Horizon_(sculpture)" title="Event Horizon (sculpture)">Event Horizon</a></i>, consisting of 31 life-size and anatomically correct casts of his body, four in <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">cast iron</a> and 27 in <a href="/wiki/Fiberglass" title="Fiberglass">fiberglass</a>, was installed on top of prominent buildings along London\'s <a href="/wiki/South_Bank" title="South Bank">South Bank</a>, and installed in locations around New York City\'s <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square" class="mw-redirect" title="Madison Square">Madison Square</a> in 2010.  Gormley said of the New York site that "Within the condensed environment of Manhattan\'s topography, the level of tension between the palpable, the perceivable and the imaginable is heightened because of the density and scale of the buildings" and that in this context, the project should "activate the skyline in order to encourage people to look around.  In this process of looking and finding, or looking and seeking, one perhaps re-assess one\'s own position in the world and becomes aware of one\'s status of embedment."<sup id="cite_ref-msq_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-msq-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Critic Howard Halle said that "Using distance and attendant shifts of scale within the very fabric of the city, [<i>Event Horizon</i>] creates a metaphor for urban life and all the contradictory associations \xe2\x80\x93 alienation, ambition, anonymity, fame \xe2\x80\x93 it entails."<sup id="cite_ref-msq_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-msq-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In July 2009, Gormley presented <i><a href="/wiki/One_%26_Other" title="One &amp; Other">One &amp; Other</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Plinth" class="mw-redirect" title="Fourth Plinth">Fourth Plinth</a> commission, an invitation for members of the public, chosen by lot, to spend one hour on the vacant plinth in Trafalgar Square in  London.<sup id="cite_ref-O&amp;O_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-O&amp;O-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This "living art" happening initially attracted much media attention. It even became a topic of discussion on the long-running BBC radio drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archers" title="The Archers">The Archers</a></i>, where Gormley made an appearance as himself.<sup id="cite_ref-Tel280609_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tel280609-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2012, Gormley began making sculptures that could be termed as "digital-cubism".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Through solid steel cubes the human form is rendered into an array of different postures and poses, boldly standing in a white gallery space.\n</p><p>In March 2014 Gormley appeared in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a> series <i>What Do Artists Do All Day?</i> in an episode which followed him and his team in their Kings Cross studio, preparing a new work \xe2\x80\x93 a group of 60 enormous steel figures \xe2\x80\x93 called <i>Expansion Field</i>. The work was shown at the <a href="/wiki/Zentrum_Paul_Klee" title="Zentrum Paul Klee">Zentrum Paul Klee</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In May 2015 five life-sized sculptures, <i>Land</i>, were placed near the centre and at four compass points of the UK in a commission by the <a href="/wiki/Landmark_Trust" title="Landmark Trust">Landmark Trust</a> to celebrate its 50th anniversary. They are at <a href="/wiki/Lowsonford" title="Lowsonford">Lowsonford</a> (<a href="/wiki/Warwickshire" title="Warwickshire">Warwickshire</a>), <a href="/wiki/Lundy" title="Lundy">Lundy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Channel" title="Bristol Channel">Bristol Channel</a>), <a href="/wiki/Saddell_Bay" title="Saddell Bay">Saddell Bay</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>), <a href="/wiki/Aldeburgh#Martello_Tower" title="Aldeburgh">the Martello Tower</a> (<a href="/wiki/Aldeburgh" title="Aldeburgh">Aldeburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Clavell_Tower" title="Clavell Tower">Clavell Tower</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kimmeridge_Bay" title="Kimmeridge Bay">Kimmeridge Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-landmark_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-landmark-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcland_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcland-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The Dorset sculpture was knocked over into Kimmeridge Bay by a storm in September 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>\nOn 6 September 2015, <i>Another Place</i> saw its 10th anniversary at Crosby Beach in Liverpool. Talking of their 10th birthday.</p><blockquote><p>I\'m just delighted by the barnacles!</p></blockquote><blockquote><p>Every time I\'m there, just like any other visitor, you\'re encouraged to linger a bit longer seeing the tide come in and how many of them disappear. And then you\'re encouraged to linger further until they\'re revealed again.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote><p>In September 2015, Gormley had his first sculpture installed in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Stay_(sculptures)" title="Stay (sculptures)">Stay</a></i> are identical cast iron human form sculptures, with the first installed in the <a href="/wiki/Avon_River_(Canterbury)" title="Avon River (Canterbury)">Avon River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a>\'s <a href="/wiki/Christchurch_Central_City" title="Christchurch Central City">central city</a>, and the other sculpture to be installed in the nearby <a href="/wiki/Christchurch_Arts_Centre" title="Christchurch Arts Centre">Arts Centre</a> in early 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Gormley_statue_installed_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gormley_statue_installed-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antony_Gormley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Gormley won the <a href="/wiki/Turner_Prize" title="Turner Prize">Turner Prize</a> in 1994 with <i><a href="/wiki/Field_(sculpture)" title="Field (sculpture)">Field for the British Isles</a></i>. He was quoted as saying that he was "embarrassed and guilty to have won...In the moment of winning there is a sense the others have been diminished. I know artists who\'ve been seriously knocked off their perches through disappointment."<sup id="cite_ref-Guard080907_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard080907-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Gormley has been a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Arts" title="Royal Academy of Arts">Royal Academician</a> since 2003, and was a Trustee of the <a href="/wiki/British_Museum" title="British Museum">British Museum</a> from 2007 to 2015. He is an Honorary Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Arts" title="Royal Society of Arts">Royal Society of Arts</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_British_Architects" title="Royal Institute of British Architects">Royal Institute of British Architects</a>, honorary doctor of the universities of <a href="/wiki/Teesside_University" title="Teesside University">Teesside</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Liverpool" title="University of Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_College_London" title="University College London">University College London</a>, and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, and a fellow of <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge" title="Trinity College, Cambridge">Trinity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jesus_College,_Cambridge" title="Jesus College, Cambridge">Jesus Colleges</a>, Cambridge.  In October 2010, he and 100 other leading artists signed an open letter to the Culture Minister <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Hunt_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeremy Hunt (politician)">Jeremy Hunt</a> protesting against cutbacks in the arts.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 13 March 2011, Gormley was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Outstanding_Achievement_in_Dance" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Outstanding Achievement in Dance">Laurence Olivier Award for Outstanding Achievement in Dance</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Set_design" class="mw-redirect" title="Set design">set design</a> for <i>Babel (Words)</i> at <a href="/wiki/Sadler%27s_Wells" class="mw-redirect" title="Sadler&#39;s Wells">Sadler\'s Wells</a> in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Sidi_Larbi_Cherkaoui" title="Sidi Larbi Cherkaoui">Sidi Larbi Cherkaoui</a> and Damien Jalet.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He was the recipient of the Obayashi Prize in 2012 and is the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Praemium_Imperiale" title="Praemium Imperiale">Praemium Imperiale</a> laureate for sculpture. Gormley was <a href="/wiki/Knight_Bachelor" title="Knight Bachelor">knighted</a> in the 2014 New Year Honours for services to the arts.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Art_market">Art market</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antony_Gormley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Art market">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Gormley\'s auction record is \xc2\xa33,401,250 for a <a href="/wiki/Maquette" title="Maquette">maquette</a> of the <i>Angel of the North</i>, set at <a href="/wiki/Christie%27s" title="Christie&#39;s">Christie\'s</a>, London, on 14 October 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_works">Major works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antony_Gormley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Major works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="infobox" style="width:22em;width: 210px;
