Kingdom)">Ministry of Defence</a>, the <a href="/wiki/British_government_departments" title="British government departments">executive department</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Armed_Forces" title="British Armed Forces">British Armed Forces</a>. The office is a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Cabinet of the United Kingdom">British Cabinet</a>\xe2\x80\x93level position. \n</p><p>The post was created in 1964 as successor to the posts of <b>Minister for Coordination of Defence</b> (1936\xe2\x80\x931940) and <b>Minister of Defence</b> (1940\xe2\x80\x931964). It replaced the positions of <a href="/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty" title="First Lord of the Admiralty">First Lord of the Admiralty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_War" title="Secretary of State for War">Secretary of State for War</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Air" title="Secretary of State for Air">Secretary of State for Air</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/Admiralty" title="Admiralty">Admiralty</a>, <a href="/wiki/War_Office" title="War Office">War Office</a> and <a href="/wiki/Air_Ministry" title="Air Ministry">Air Ministry</a> were merged into the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)">Ministry of Defence</a> (the Secretary of State for War had already ceased to be a cabinet position in 1946, with the creation of the cabinet-level <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Defence_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister of Defence (UK)">Minister of Defence</a>).\n</p>\n<div id="toc" id="Ministers_for_Co-ordination_of_Defence_(1936\xe2\x80\x931940)">Ministers for Co-ordination of Defence (1936\xe2\x80\x931940)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secretary_of_State_for_Defence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ministers for Co-ordination of Defence (1936\xe2\x80\x931940)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The position of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Co-ordination_of_Defence" title="Minister for Co-ordination of Defence">Minister for Co-ordination of Defence</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Cabinet of the United Kingdom">British Cabinet</a>-level position established in 1936 to oversee and co-ordinate the rearmament of Britain\'s defences.\n</p><p>The position was established by the Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Baldwin" title="Stanley Baldwin">Stanley Baldwin</a> in response to criticism that Britain\'s armed forces were understrength compared to those of <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>. This campaign had been led by <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> and many expected him to be appointed as the new minister, though nearly every other senior figure in the <a href="/wiki/National_Government_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Government (United Kingdom)">National Government</a> was also speculated upon by politicians and commentators. Despite this, Baldwin\'s choice of the <a href="/wiki/Attorney_General_for_England_and_Wales" title="Attorney General for England and Wales">Attorney General</a> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Inskip,_1st_Viscount_Caldecote" title="Thomas Inskip, 1st Viscount Caldecote">Sir Thomas Inskip</a> provoked widespread astonishment. A famous comment made in response to Inskip\'s appointment was "This is the most cynical appointment since <a href="/wiki/Caligula" title="Caligula">Caligula</a> made <a href="/wiki/Incitatus" title="Incitatus">his horse</a> a consul".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The appointment is now regarded as a sign of caution by Baldwin who did not wish to appoint someone like Churchill who would have been interpreted by foreign powers as a sign of the United Kingdom preparing for war, as well as a desire to avoid taking on board a controversial and radical minister.\n</p><p>In 1939 Inskip was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/First_Sea_Lord" title="First Sea Lord">First Sea Lord</a> <a href="/wiki/Ernle_Chatfield,_1st_Baron_Chatfield" title="Ernle Chatfield, 1st Baron Chatfield">Lord Chatfield</a>. When the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> broke out, the new Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain" title="Neville Chamberlain">Neville Chamberlain</a> formed a small <a href="/wiki/War_Cabinet" class="mw-redirect" title="War Cabinet">War Cabinet</a> and it was expected that Chatfield would serve as a spokesperson for the three service ministers, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_War" title="Secretary of State for War">Secretary of State for War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty" title="First Lord of the Admiralty">First Lord of the Admiralty</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Air" title="Secretary of State for Air">Secretary of State for Air</a>; however, political considerations resulted in all three posts being included in the Cabinet, and Chatfield\'s role proved increasingly redundant. In April 1940 the position was formally wound up and the functions transferred to other Ministers.\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable" style="text-align:center">\n<caption>Minister for Co-ordination of (1940\xe2\x80\x931964)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The post of <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Defence" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister of Defence">Minister of Defence</a> was responsible for co-ordination of defence and security from its creation in 1940 until its abolition in 1964. The post was a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Cabinet of the United Kingdom">Cabinet</a> level post and generally ranked above the three service ministers, some of whom, however, continued to also serve in Cabinet.\n</p><p>On his appointment as Prime Minister in May 1940, <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> created for himself the new post of Minister of Defence. The post was created in response to previous criticism that there had been no clear single minister in charge of the prosecution of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. In 1946, the post became the only cabinet-level post representing the military, with the three service ministers \xe2\x80\x93 the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_War" title="Secretary of State for War">Secretary of State for War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty" title="First Lord of the Admiralty">First Lord of the Admiralty</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Air" title="Secretary of State for Air">Secretary of State for Air</a>, now formally subordinated to the Minister of Defence.\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable" The former Cabinet positions of <a href="/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty" title="First Lord of the Admiralty">First Lord of the Admiralty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_War" Force</a> respectively) were incorporated into it and the offices of the <a href="/wiki/Admiralty" title="Admiralty">Admiralty</a>, <a href="/wiki/War_Office" title="War Office">War Office</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Air_Ministry" title="Air Ministry">Air Ministry</a> were abolished and their functions transferred to an expanded <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of
