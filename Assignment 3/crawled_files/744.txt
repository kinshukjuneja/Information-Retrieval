href="/wiki/%C3%81ras_an_Uachtar%C3%A1in" title="\xc3\x81ras an Uachtar\xc3\xa1in">Viceregal Lodge</a></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>From its foundation on 6 December 1922 until 11 December 1936, the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State" title="Irish Free State">Irish Free State</a> was in accordance with its <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Constitution of the Irish Free State">constitution</a>, governed formally under a form of <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a>. The monarch exercised a number of important duties, including appointing the <a href="/wiki/Executive_Council_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Executive Council of the Irish Free State">cabinet</a>, dissolving the legislature and promulgating laws. Nonetheless, by convention the monarch\'s role was largely ceremonial and exercised on his behalf by his official representative, the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Governor-General of the Irish Free State">governor-general</a>. The monarch\'s role and duties under the constitution were ended under a <a href="/wiki/Constitution_(Amendment_No._27)_Act_1936" title="Constitution (Amendment No. 27) Act 1936">constitutional amendment adopted in 1936</a>. From that point, the monarch no longer played any role in appointing the cabinet, dissolving the legislature or promulgating laws. Nor was the monarch mentioned anywhere in the constitution. Under <a href="/wiki/Executive_Authority_(External_Relations)_Act_1936" title="Executive Authority (External Relations) Act 1936">separate legislation also adopted in 1936</a> it was provided that Irish diplomatic representatives would be appointed on the authority of the cabinet alone and international agreements would be concluded with the authority of the cabinet alone. At the same time, that legislation also created a new role for the king in his capacity as the "symbol of [the] co-operation" of Australia, Canada, Great Britain, New Zealand, and South Africa for so long as the Irish Free State was associated with those states. The new role of the king in that capacity alone was to act on behalf of the Irish government with respect to the appointment of diplomatic and consular representatives and the conclusion of international agreements when advised by the cabinet so to do. The role of the king in acting on behalf of the Irish government with respect to appointing diplomats was not ended until 1949<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and under United Kingdom law the king is regarded as having been the sovereign until that time but not under Irish law.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_the_United_Kingdom_(1837-1952).svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Coat_of_arms_of_the_United_Kingdom_%281837-1952%29.svg/200px-Coat_of_arms_of_the_United_Kingdom_%281837-1952%29.svg.png" width="200" data-file-height="1550" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_the_United_Kingdom_(1837-1952).svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Royal_coat_of_arms_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Royal coat of arms of the United Kingdom">Royal arms</a> used by George V, Edward VIII and George VI.</div></div></div>\n<p>The monarch\'s title in the Irish Free State was exactly the same as it was elsewhere in the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>, being\n</p>\n<ul><li>6 December 1922 \xe2\x80\x93 13 May 1927 \xe2\x80\x93 <i>By the Grace of God, of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland and of the British Dominions beyond the Seas King, Defender of the Faith, Emperor of India</i></li>\n<li>13 May 1927 \xe2\x80\x93 29 December 1937 \xe2\x80\x93 <i>By the Grace of God, of Great Britain, Ireland and the British Dominions beyond the Seas King, Defender of the Faith, Emperor of India</i></li></ul>\n<p>The reason the monarch\'s title changed in 1927 was because the term "<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a>" had been superseded by the establishment of the Irish Free State and the renaming of the UK as the "United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland". Rather than draw attention to the <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_Ireland" title="Partition of Ireland">partition of Ireland</a>, the monarch\'s title simply referred to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. This change did not mean the monarch adopted different crowns for different realms; that development did not formally occur until 1953.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Duties_and_functions">Duties and functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_in_the_Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Duties and functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<ul><li><b>Executive authority:</b> The <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">executive authority</a> of the state was formally <i>vested</i> in the monarch but \'exercised\' by the governor-general, on the \'advice\' of the Executive Council.</li>\n<li><b>Appointment of the cabinet:</b> The <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Executive_Council_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="President of the Executive Council of the Irish Free State">President of the Executive Council</a> (prime minister) was appointed by governor-general after being selected by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann_(Irish_Free_State)" title="D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann (Irish Free State)">D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann</a> (the lower house of parliament). The remaining ministers were appointed on the nomination of the president, subject to a vote of consent in the D\xc3\xa1il.</li>\n<li><b>Convention and dissolution of the legislature</b>: The governor-general, on behalf of the monarch, convened and dissolved the <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Oireachtas of the Irish Free State">Oireachtas</a> on the advice of the Executive Council.</li>\n<li><b>Signing bills into law:</b> The monarch was formally, along with the D\xc3\xa1il and the <a href="/wiki/Seanad_%C3%89ireann_(Irish_Free_State)" title="Seanad \xc3\x89ireann (Irish Free State)">Senate</a>, one of three tiers of the Oireachtas. No bill could become law until it received the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Assent" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Assent">Royal Assent</a>, given by the governor-general on behalf of the monarch. The governor-general theoretically had the right to veto a bill or reserve it "for the signification of the King\'s pleasure", in effect postponing a decision on whether or not to enact the bill, for a maximum of one year. However neither of these two actions was ever taken.</li>\n<li><b>Representative of the state in foreign affairs:</b> The monarch accredited ambassadors and received the letters of credence of foreign diplomats; ministers signed international treaties in his name. The role of the monarch in the Free State\'s foreign affairs was the only function retained by him after the constitutional changes of 1936.</li>\n<li><b>Appointment of judges</b>: All judges were appointed by the governor-general, on the advice of the Executive Council.</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oath_of_Allegiance">Oath of Allegiance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_in_the_Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Oath of Allegiance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Oath_of_Allegiance_(Ireland)" title="Oath of Allegiance (Ireland)">Oath of Allegiance (Ireland)</a></div>\n<p>Under the Free State constitution, members of the Oireachtas were required to take an Oath of Allegiance to the Irish Free State with a promise of fidelity\xe2\x80\x94but not allegiance\xe2\x80\x94to the monarch before being permitted to assume their seats. This oath was strongly objected to by many <a href="/wiki/Irish_republicanism" title="Irish republicanism">republicans</a> and was one of the causes of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Civil_War" title="Irish Civil War">Irish Civil War</a>. The oath was eventually abolished in 1933. The Oath of Allegiance read as follows:\n</p>\n<dl><dd><i>I ................ do solemnly swear true faith and allegiance to the Constitution of the Irish Free State as by law established, and that I will be faithful to H. M. King George V., his heirs and successors by law in virtue of the common citizenship of Ireland with Great Britain and her adherence to and membership of the group of nations forming the British Commonwealth of Nations.</i></dd></dl>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diminished_role">Diminished role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_in_the_Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diminished role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg/220px-Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg" width="220" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg/330px-Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg/440px-Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg 2x" data-file-width="748" data-file-height="549" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leinster_House_-_1911.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Leinster_House" title="Leinster House">Leinster House</a>, decorated for the visit of King <a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">George V</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Teck" title="Mary of Teck">Queen Mary</a> in 1911; within a decade, it became the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas_of_the_Irish_Free_State" title="Oireachtas of the Irish Free State">Oireachtas of the Irish Free State</a></div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna F\xc3\xa1il">Fianna F\xc3\xa1il</a> came to power after the <a href="/wiki/Irish_general_election,_1932" title="Irish general election, 1932">1932 election</a> and reduced the role of the monarchy.  The <a href="/wiki/Oath_of_Allegiance_(Ireland)" title="Oath of Allegiance (Ireland)">oath of fidelity to the king</a> required of legislators and ministers was abolished. A compliant governor-general was appointed,  <a href="/wiki/Domhnall_Ua_Buachalla" title="Domhnall Ua Buachalla">Domhnall Ua Buachalla</a>, who withdrew from all public and ceremonial roles, performing in a perfunctory manner the minimum duties required by the Constitution. No treaties requiring the assent of the king as head of state were signed from 1931 to 1937; two methods were used to circumvent this: bilateral treaties were concluded at government rather than head-of-state level; for multilateral treaties, the Free State chose, not to enrol at inauguration via the king\'s signature, but instead to accede a few months later via the signature of the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_External_Affairs_(Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister for External Affairs (Ireland)">Minister for External Affairs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stewart1938_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stewart1938-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1936, the  government of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89amon_de_Valera" title="\xc3\x89amon de Valera">\xc3\x89amon de Valera</a> carried out a major revision of the constitution aimed at all but eliminating the role of the monarch in the Irish state. The parliament passed the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_(Amendment_No._27)_Act_1936" title="Constitution (Amendment No. 27) Act 1936">Constitution (Amendment No. 27) Act 1936</a>, which removed all explicit reference to the monarch from the constitution, abolished the office of governor-general, and shared all of the monarch\'s former functions amongst various other organs of government.\n</p><p>However, without mentioning him by name, the amendment also introduced a provision permitting the government to "avail of" the monarch as a "constitutional organ" for the "appointment of diplomatic and consular agents and the conclusion of international agreements". Thus, henceforth, the sovereign\'s role was restricted to diplomatic and foreign affairs, a standard head of state role. The monarch retained no other constitutional role internally in the life of the Irish state and was relegated in the 1937 <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Ireland" title="Constitution of Ireland">Constitution of Ireland</a> to being an unnamed "organ" used by the state should it choose in <a href="/wiki/Statute_law" class="mw-redirect" title="Statute law">statute law</a> to do so. The role continued until the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_Act_1948" title="Republic of Ireland Act 1948">Republic of Ireland Act 1948</a>, by which it was transferred to the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President of Ireland</a>. At that time, the new republic also ceased to be a member of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">British Commonwealth</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_monarchs">List of monarchs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monarchy_in_the_Irish_Free_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: List of monarchs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="wikitable" style="width:100%;">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>No.\n</th>\n<th>Name<br /><span style="font-size:85%;">(Birth\xe2\x80\x93Death)</span>\n</th>\n<th colspan="2">Reign\n</th>\n<th>Governors-General\n</th>\n<th>Presidents of the Executive Council\n</th></tr>\n<tr>\n<td style="text-align:center;">1.\n</td>\n<td style="text-align:center;"><b><a href="/wiki/George_V" title="George V">George V</a></b><br /><small>(1865\xe2\x80\x931936)\n</small></td>\n<td>6 December 1922\n</td>\n<td>20 January 1936\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/Tim_Healy_(politician)" title="Tim Healy (politician)">Timothy Healy</a> <span style="font-size:85%;">(1922\xe2\x80\x9328)</span><br /><a href="/wiki/James_McNeill" title="James McNeill">James McNeill</a> <span style="font-size:85%;">(1928\xe2\x80\x9332)</span><br /><a href="/wiki/Domhnall_Ua_Buachalla" title="Domhnall Ua Buachalla">Domhnall Ua Buachalla</a> <span style="font-size:85%;">(1932\xe2\x80\x9336)</span>\n</td>\n<td><a href="/wiki/W._T._Cosgrave" title="W.
