class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bell_Edison_logo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Bell_Edison_logo.jpg/220px-Bell_Edison_logo.jpg" width="220" height="219" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Bell_Edison_logo.jpg/330px-Bell_Edison_logo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Bell_Edison_logo.jpg/440px-Bell_Edison_logo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1681" data-file-height="1672" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bell_Edison_logo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Company logo on porch of <a href="/wiki/17_%26_19_Newhall_Street,_Birmingham" title="17 &amp; 19 Newhall Street, Birmingham">17 &amp; 19 Newhall Street, Birmingham</a> (former <i>Central</i> exchange)</div></div></div>\n<p><a href="/wiki/National_Telephone_Company" title="National Telephone Company">National Telephone Company</a> (NTC) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> telephone company from 1881 until 1911 which brought together smaller local companies in the early years of the telephone. Under the <a href="/wiki/Telephone_Transfer_Act_1911" title="Telephone Transfer Act 1911">Telephone Transfer Act 1911</a> it was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/General_Post_Office" title="General Post Office">General Post Office (GPO)</a> in 1912.\n</p><p>Until 1982, the main civil telecommunications system in the UK was a state <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a> known (since reorganisation in 1969) as <a href="/wiki/Post_Office_Telecommunications" title="Post Office Telecommunications">Post Office Telecommunications</a>. Broadcasting of radio and television was a duopoly of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Independent_Broadcasting_Authority" title="Independent Broadcasting Authority">Independent Broadcasting Authority</a> (IBA): these two organisations controlled all broadcast services, and directly owned and operated the broadcast transmitter sites. Mobile phone and Internet services did not then exist. The civil telecoms monopoly ended when <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Communications" title="Mercury Communications">Mercury Communications</a> arrived in 1983. The Post Office system evolved into <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">British Telecom</a> and was privatised in 1984. \nBroadcast transmitters, which belonged to the BBC and IBA, were privatised during the 1990s and now belong to <a href="/wiki/Babcock_International" title="Babcock International">Babcock International</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arqiva" title="Arqiva">Arqiva</a>.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Telecommunications" title="British Rail Telecommunications">British Rail Telecommunications</a> was created in 1992 by <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a> (BR). It was the largest private telecoms network in Britain, consisting of 17,000 route kilometres of <a href="/wiki/Optical_fiber" title="Optical fiber">fibre optic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> cable which connected every major city and town in the country and provided links to continental <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Tunnel" title="Channel Tunnel">Channel Tunnel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> BR also operated its own national trunked radio network providing dedicated train-to-shore mobile communications, and in the early 1980s BR helped establish Mercury Communications\xe2\x80\x99, now C&amp;WC, core infrastructure by laying a resilient \xe2\x80\x98figure-of-eight\xe2\x80\x99 fibre optic network alongside Britain\xe2\x80\x99s railway lines, spanning London, Bristol, Birmingham, Leeds and Manchester.\n</p><p>Regulation of communications has changed many times during the same period, and most of the bodies have been merged into <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a>, the independent regulator and competition authority for the UK communications industries.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_trunk_infrastructure">Domestic trunk infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Domestic trunk infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>All communications <a href="/wiki/Trunk_(telecommunications)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trunk (telecommunications)">trunks</a> are now <a href="/wiki/Digital_data" title="Digital data">digital</a>. Most are carried via national <a href="/wiki/Optical_fiber" title="Optical fiber">optical fibre</a> networks. There are several companies with national fibre networks, including <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">BT</a>, <a href="/wiki/Level_3_Communications" title="Level 3 Communications">Level 3 Communications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cable_%26_Wireless_Worldwide" title="Cable &amp; Wireless Worldwide">Cable &amp; Wireless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Easynet" title="Easynet">Easynet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thus_(company)" title="Thus (company)">Thus</a>. <a href="/wiki/British_Telecom_microwave_network" title="British Telecom microwave network">Microwave</a> links are used up to the 155 <a href="/wiki/Data_rate_units" class="mw-redirect" title="Data rate units">Mbit/s</a> level, but are seldom cost-effective at higher <a href="/wiki/Bit_rate" title="Bit rate">bit rates</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_trunks">International trunks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International trunks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The UK is a focal point for many of the world\'s <a href="/wiki/Submarine_communications_cable" title="Submarine communications cable">submarine communications cables</a>, which are now mostly digital <a href="/wiki/Optical_fiber" title="Optical fiber">optical fibre</a> cables. There are many satellite links too, but these now provide a relatively small part of the international bandwidth.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast_transmission">Broadcast transmission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Broadcast transmission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Most broadcasting organisations, BBC and commercial, lease transmission facilities from one or more of the transmission companies. The main exception is the smaller local radio stations, some of which find it more cost-effective to provide their own.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fixed_phone_lines">Fixed phone lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fixed phone lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>BT is still the main provider of fixed telephones lines, both POTS and ISDN, and it has a <a href="/wiki/Universal_service" title="Universal service">universal service obligation</a>, although companies can now contract <a href="/wiki/Openreach" title="Openreach">Openreach</a> to install a phoneline on their behalf, rather than telling the customer to get BT to install it, then transfer over.\n</p><p>Virgin Media is the second biggest player in the residential telephone line market.\n</p><p>Other companies provide fixed telephone lines too, but mainly to large companies in the major cities.\n</p><p>There are many other providers who sell fixed telephone services carried over BT lines. They have no network infrastructure of their own.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_phone_networks">Mobile phone networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mobile phone networks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="First_generation_networks">First generation networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: First generation networks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<ul><li>Cellnet was originally jointly owned by <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">British Telecom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Securicor" title="Securicor">Securicor</a>. BT eventually bought out Securicor\'s stake. The network became BT Cellnet and was then demerged to become <a href="/wiki/O2_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="O2 (United Kingdom)">O2</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Vodafone_UK" title="Vodafone UK">Vodafone</a></li></ul>\n<p>Both companies ran <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Mobile_Phone_System" title="Advanced Mobile Phone System">ETACS</a> analogue mobile phone networks.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="First_and_second_generation_networks">First and second generation networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: First and second generation networks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/O2_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="O2 (United Kingdom)">O2</a> - runs a GSM-900 network, owned by <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telef\xc3\xb3nica">Telef\xc3\xb3nica</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Vodafone_UK" title="Vodafone UK">Vodafone</a> - runs a GSM-900 network.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/EE_Limited" title="EE Limited">EE</a> - runs a GSM-1800 network. Formerly this was two separate companies: <a href="/wiki/Orange_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Orange (UK)">Orange</a> and <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_UK" title="T-Mobile UK">T-Mobile</a>, which was originally called One-2-One.</li></ul>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Third_generation_networks">Third generation networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Third generation networks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>The four 2G companies all won 3G licences in a competitive auction, as did new entrant known as <a href="/wiki/Hutchison_3G" class="mw-redirect" title="Hutchison 3G">Hutchison 3G</a>, which branded its network as <a href="/wiki/Three_UK" title="Three UK">3</a>. They have now rolled out their networks. Hutchison 3G does not operate a 2G network, previously having agreements with Orange and O2 to allow roaming on their 2G networks.\n</p><p>The third generation stems from technological improvements and is in essence an improvement of the available bandwidth, enabling new services to be provided to customers. Such services include streaming of live radio or video, video calls and live TV.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fourth_generation_networks">Fourth generation networks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Fourth generation networks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>Long-term evolution (<a href="/wiki/LTE_(telecommunication)" title="LTE (telecommunication)">LTE</a>) services are currently being rolled out. EE launched their 4G network in October 2012, using part of their existing 1800&#160;MHz spectrum. O2 and Vodafone will use the 800&#160;MHz and 2600&#160;MHz bands for their services. O2 launched its 4G network on 29 August 2013, initially in London, Leeds and Bradford with a further 13 cities added by the end of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Vodafone  commenced its 4G services on 29 August 2013, initially in London with 12 more cities to be added by the end of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 3 commenced LTE services in London, Birmingham, Manchester, Reading, Wolverhampton and the Black country in December 2013 albeit with a limited number of subscribers to evaluate its implementation. Full rollout to remaining subscribers commenced on 5 February 2014 on a phased basis via a silent <a href="/wiki/Subscriber_identity_module" title="Subscriber identity module">SIM</a> update.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A 50 further cities and over 200 towns are scheduled to receive LTE coverage by the end of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> As a condition of acquiring part of EE\'s 1800&#160;MHz spectrum for 4G use, 3 were unable to use it until October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Telephones">Telephones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Telephones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fixed_telephones">Fixed telephones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Fixed telephones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>In the UK, there were 35 million (2002) main line telephones.\n</p><p>The telephone service in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> was originally provided by private companies and local city councils, but by 1912\xe2\x80\x9313 <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> all except the telephone service of <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Kingston upon Hull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a> had been bought out by the <a href="/wiki/General_Post_Office" title="General Post Office">General Post Office</a>. Post Office Telephones also operated telephone services in <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> until 1969 when the islands took over responsibility for their own postal and telephone services.\n</p><p>Post Office Telephones was reorganised in 1980\xe2\x80\x9381 <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.bt.com/archives/history/19811983.htm#1981">[1]</a> as <i>British Telecommunications</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/British_Telecom" class="mw-redirect" title="British Telecom">British Telecom</a></i>, or <i>BT</i>), and was the first nationalised industry to be privatised by the Conservative government. The Hull Telephone Department was itself sold by Hull City Council as <i><a href="/wiki/Kingston_Communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingston Communications">Kingston Communications</a></i> in the late 1990s and celebrated its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20050829033031/http://www.kcom.com/centenary/">centenary in 2004</a>.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_telephones">Mobile telephones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Mobile telephones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<p>There are more <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a> than people in the UK. In 2011 there were 82 million subscriptions in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-ofcom_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ofcom-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> There were 76 million in 2008<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and 55 million in January 2005.\n</p><p>Each of the main network operators sells mobile phone services to the public. In addition, companies such as <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Mobile_UK" title="Virgin Mobile UK">Virgin Mobile UK</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tesco_Mobile" title="Tesco Mobile">Tesco Mobile</a> act as <a href="/wiki/Mobile_virtual_network_operator" title="Mobile virtual network operator">mobile virtual network operators</a>, using the infrastructure of other companies.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Numbering">Numbering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Numbering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Telephone_numbers_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Telephone numbers in the United Kingdom">Telephone numbers in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<p>There is a set numbering plan for phone numbers within the United Kingdom, which is regulated by the Office of Communications (<a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a>), which replaced the Office of Telecommunications (<a href="/wiki/Oftel" title="Oftel">Oftel</a>) in 2003. Each number consists of an area code\xe2\x80\x94one for each of the large towns and cities and their surroundings\xe2\x80\x94and a subscriber number\xe2\x80\x94the individual number.\nMobile IMSI is the actual number assigned to it the mobile telephone number, and provided with individual license to the MNOs.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_radio_broadcasting">Television and radio broadcasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Television and radio broadcasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Radio_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Radio in the United Kingdom">Radio in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<p>In 1998 there were 663 radio broadcast stations: 219 on <a href="/wiki/Amplitude_modulation" title="Amplitude modulation">AM</a>, 431 on <a href="/wiki/Frequency_modulation" title="Frequency modulation">FM</a> and 3 on shortwave. There were 84.5 million radio receiver sets (1997). Today there are around 600 licensed radio stations in the UK.\n</p>\n<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television in the United Kingdom">Television in the United Kingdom</a></div>\n<p>In 1997 there were 30.5 million households with television sets.\n</p><p>Analogue television broadcasts ceased in the UK in 2012, replaced by the Digital Terrestrial Service <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a> which operates via the <a href="/wiki/DVB-T" title="DVB-T">DVB-T</a> and <a href="/wiki/DVB-T2" title="DVB-T2">DVB-T2</a> (for HD broadcasts) standards. Digital Satellite is provided by <a href="/wiki/BSkyB" class="mw-redirect" title="BSkyB">BSkyB</a> (subscription and free services) and <a href="/wiki/Freesat" title="Freesat">Freesat</a> (free to air services only) from services at <a href="/wiki/Astra_28.2%C2%B0E" title="Astra 28.2\xc2\xb0E">28.2\xc2\xb0 East</a>. Digital cable is primarily provided by <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet">Internet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telecommunications_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: In 1999, there were 364 <a href="/wiki/Internet_service_provider" title="Internet service provider">Internet service providers</a> (ISPs). <a href="/wiki/Public_libraries" class="mw-redirect" title="Public libraries">Public libraries</a> also provide access to the internet, sometimes for a fee.\n</p><p>In 2017, 90% of households were reported to have access to an internet connection.
