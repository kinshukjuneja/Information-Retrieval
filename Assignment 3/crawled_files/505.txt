id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claim_of_Right_Act_1689&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Glorious_Revolution" title="Glorious Revolution">Glorious Revolution</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">William of Orange</a> landed with his army in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> on 5 November 1688. King <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_England" title="James II of England">James VII of Scotland</a>, who was also <a href="/wiki/King_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="King of England">King of England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> as James II, attempted to resist the invasion. He then sent representatives to negotiate, and he finally fled England on 23 December 1688.\n</p><p>Whilst the <a href="/wiki/Convention_Parliament_(1689)" title="Convention Parliament (1689)">Convention Parliament</a> in England declared that James, as King of England, had abdicated the Government, and issued an <a href="/wiki/Bill_of_Rights_1689" title="Bill of Rights 1689">English Bill of Rights</a> on 13 February 1689 offering the Crown of England to William and <a href="/wiki/Mary_II_of_England" title="Mary II of England">Mary</a>, the Scots found themselves facing a more difficult constitutional problem. As James had not been present in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> during the crisis and had not fled from Scottish territory in December, it would be highly dubious to claim that he had <em>abdicated</em> the Scottish throne.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Process">Process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claim_of_Right_Act_1689&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Therefore, a <a href="/wiki/Convention_of_Estates_(1689)" title="Convention of Estates (1689)">Convention of the Scottish Estates</a> met to consider letters received on 16 March 1689 from the two contenders for the Crown. On 4 April they voted to remove James VII from office, drawing on <a href="/wiki/George_Buchanan" title="George Buchanan">George Buchanan</a>\'s argument on the contractual nature of monarchy.<sup id="cite_ref-lynch92_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lynch92-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Later that month, the Convention adopted the Claim of Right and the Article of Grievances, enumerating what they saw as the contemporary requirements of Scottish constitutional law. It also declared that, because of his actions in violation of these laws, James had forfeited the Scottish throne.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The effect of the Claim of Right was to "bolster the position of parliament within the Scottish constitution at the expense of the royal prerogative".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Convention proceeded to offer the crown on the basis of these documents to William and Mary, who accepted it on 11 May 1689, and were proclaimed King and Queen of the Scots as William II and Mary II, though with subsequent controversy over whether the Claim of Right articles against <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_polity" title="Episcopal polity">Episcopacy</a> were fully accepted by the new monarchy.<sup id="cite_ref-lynch92_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lynch92-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claim_of_Right_Act_1689&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-lynch92-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-lynch92_1-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-lynch92_1-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text"><cite class="citation book">Lynch, Michael (1992). <i>Scotland: A New History</i>.
