class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Languages_of_Sudan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_endangered_languages_in_Sudan" title="List of endangered languages in Sudan">List of endangered languages in Sudan</a></div>\n<p>Approximately 114 languages are native to <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>. More than 500 accents are spoken in Sudan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sudanese_Arabic" title="Sudanese Arabic">Sudanese Arabic</a> is the most widely spoken language in the country. It is the <a href="/wiki/Varieties_of_Arabic" title="Varieties of Arabic">variety</a> of <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> spoken throughout northern Sudan. It has much borrowed vocabulary from the local languages (<i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotana_(languages)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rotana (languages) (page does not exist)">El Rotana</a></i>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. This has resulted in a variety of Arabic that is unique to Sudan, reflecting the way in which the country has been influenced by both <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Africa" title="Culture of Africa">African</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arab_culture" title="Arab culture">Arab cultures</a>. Some of the tribes in Sudan still have similar accents to the ones in <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>. Other important languages include <a href="/wiki/Beja_language" title="Beja language">Beja</a> (<span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">AKA</span></span> Bedawi) along the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, with perhaps 2 million speakers; <a href="/wiki/Tigre_language" title="Tigre language">Tigre</a> is another language spoken by some Beja tribes; <a href="/wiki/Fur_language" title="Fur language">Fur</a> in the west (<a href="/wiki/Darfur" title="Darfur">Darfur</a>), with perhaps a million speakers; and various <a href="/wiki/Nubian_languages" title="Nubian languages">Nubian languages</a> along the Nile in the north, with half a million or so speakers. The most linguistically diverse region in the country are the <a href="/wiki/Nuba_Hills" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuba Hills">Nuba Hills</a> in Kordofan, inhabited by speakers of multiple language families, with Darfur and the Ethiopian border regions being second. There are many different accents or languages in Sudan.\n</p><p>Beja is the sole <a href="/wiki/Cushitic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Cushitic language">Cushitic language</a> in Sudan. Arabic is <a href="/wiki/Semitic_languages" title="Semitic languages">Semitic</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Niger%E2%80%93Congo" class="mw-redirect" title="Niger\xe2\x80\x93Congo">Niger\xe2\x80\x93Congo</a> family is represented by many of the <a href="/wiki/Kordofanian_languages" title="Kordofanian languages">Kordofanian languages</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_languages" title="Indo-European languages">Indo-European</a> by <a href="/wiki/Domari_language" title="Domari language">Domari</a> (Gypsy) and English. Historically, <a href="/wiki/Old_Nubian" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Nubian">Old Nubian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Coptic_language" title="Coptic language">Coptic</a> (Egyptian) were the languages of <a href="/wiki/Christian_Nubia" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian Nubia">Christian Nubia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meroitic_language" title="Meroitic language">Meroitic</a> the language of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kush" title="Kingdom of Kush">Kingdom of Kush</a> which conquered Egypt.\n</p><p>Sudan also has multiple <a href="/wiki/Sudanese_sign_languages" title="Sudanese sign languages">regional sign languages</a>, which are not <a href="/wiki/Mutually_intelligible" class="mw-redirect" title="Mutually intelligible">mutually intelligible</a>. By 2009 a proposal for a unified language had been worked out, but is not widely known.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The most used languages are:\n</p>\n<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> in all Sudan, along with the tribal languages.\n<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Sudanese_Arabic" title="Sudanese Arabic">Sudanese Arabic</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Najdi_Arabic" title="Najdi Arabic">Najdi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hejazi_Arabic" title="Hejazi Arabic">Hejazi</a> Arabic, mainly in mid-northern and mid-eastern regions.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Chadic_Arabic" class="mw-redirect" title="Chadic Arabic">Chadic Arabic</a> in western region, mainly spoken by Baggara and various Arabized African tribes.</li></ol></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nubian_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Nubian language">Nubian language</a> in far north, mainly spoken by Nubians of Mahas, Dongola and Halfa.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Dinka_language" title="Dinka language">Dinka language</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Beja_language" title="Beja language">Beja language</a> (also known as Bedawit) in far east alongside Red Sea, mainly spoken by Beja of Hadandawa, Amar\'ar Ababda and Bisharin.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Tigrinya_language" title="Tigrinya language">Tigray, Beni Amer Rotana</a>, spoken by Beni Amer and Habab tribes in Tokar, Aqiq, and Kassala.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Fur_language" title="Fur language">Fur language</a> in far west, mainly spoken by <a href="/wiki/Fur_people" title="Fur people">Fur people</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Massalit_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Massalit language">Massalit language</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Maban_languages" title="Maban languages">Maban languages</a> in Darfur</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Zaghawa_language" title="Zaghawa language">Zaghawa language</a> primarily spoken in <a href="/wiki/Darfur" title="Darfur">Darfur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Zaghawa_people" title="Zaghawa people">Zaghawa people</a>. Also spoken by the diaspora in <a href="/wiki/Omdurman" title="Omdurman">Omdurman</a>.</li>\n<li>Dajue language primarily spoken in Darfur and Chad by Dajue people.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Dazaga_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Dazaga language">Daza language</a> (also known as Gorane or Gouran) is primarily spoken in <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, but there is also a small community of about 3000 speakers in Omdurman.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Kordofanian_languages" title="Kordofanian languages">Kordofanian languages</a> consist of numerous languages like <a href="/wiki/Kadu_languages" title="Kadu languages">Kadu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katla_languages" title="Katla languages">Katla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mande_languages" title="Mande languages">Mandi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rashad_languages" title="Rashad languages">Rashad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lafofa_languages" title="Lafofa languages">Lafofa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talodi%E2%80%93Heiban_languages" title="Talodi\xe2\x80\x93Heiban languages">Talodi\xe2\x80\x93Heiban</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Nuba_Mountains" title="Nuba Mountains">Nuba Mountains</a> languages in Southern region, different ethnic groups speak different languages.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Temein_languages" title="Temein languages">Temein languages</a>.</li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Domari_language" title="Domari language">Domari language</a>, mainly spoken by Halabi/Nawar and Ghajar clans.</li>\n<li>Various <a href="/wiki/Niger%E2%80%93Congo_languages" title="Niger\xe2\x80\x93Congo languages">Niger\xe2\x80\x93Congo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chadic_languages" title="Chadic languages">Chadic</a> languages, mainly spoken by <a href="/wiki/Western_Africa" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Africa">Western African</a> tribes like Fallata, also known as <a href="/wiki/Fulani" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulani">Fulani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hausa_people" title="Hausa people">Hausa</a>.</li>\n<li>Other tribal languages in all Sudan with some people speaking English.</li></ol>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Language_policies">Language policies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Languages_of_Sudan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Language policies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Under the 1998 constitution, only Arabic was the official language.<sup id="cite_ref-LeClerc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeClerc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The 2005 constitution designated Arabic and English as the official languages of Sudan.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Article 8:\n</p>\n<ol><li>All indigenous languages of Sudan are national languages and shall be respected, developed and promoted.</li>\n<li>Arabic is a widely spoken national language in Sudan.</li>\n<li>Arabic, as a major language at the national level and English shall be the official working languages of the national government and the languages of instruction for higher education.</li>\n<li>In addition to Arabic and English, the legislature of any sub-national level of government may adopt any other national language as an additional official working language at its level.</li>\n<li>There shall be no discrimination against the use of either Arabic or English at any level of government or stage of education.</li></ol>\n<p>As such Sudan is the only country in the <a href="/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab world</a> to have English as a co-official language.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline"
