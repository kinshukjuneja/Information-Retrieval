<i>Argent, a mascle sable over all a fess of the last</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>\n<p><b>Joseph Hunkin</b> (1610-1661)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> of Gatherleigh<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Lifton,_Devon" title="Lifton, Devon">Lifton</a> in Devon, was <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_Scilly" title="List of Governors of Scilly">Governor of Scilly</a> between 1651 and 1660, during Parliamentary control in the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">Civil War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Hunkin_(Governor_of_Scilly)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He was the son and heir of John Hunkin (d.1629) of Gatherleigh, High Steward of the Borough of <a href="/wiki/Liskeard" title="Liskeard">Liskeard</a> in Cornwall (son of John Hunkin (d.1608) first <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayor_of_Liskeard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mayor of Liskeard (page does not exist)">Mayor of Liskeard</a> elected on the re-incorporation of that Borough on 6 July 1587) by his first wife Grace Clobery, a daughter of Andrew Clobery (d.1596/7)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> of Lifton<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> in Devon,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 2nd son of John Clobery of <a href="/wiki/Bradstone" title="Bradstone">Bradstone</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Devon, near the Cornish border.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Hunkin_(Governor_of_Scilly)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He was Governor during the construction of <a href="/wiki/Cromwell%27s_Castle" title="Cromwell&#39;s Castle">Cromwell\'s Castle</a>, an artillery fort overlooking <a href="/wiki/New_Grimsby_harbour" class="mw-redirect" title="New Grimsby harbour">New Grimsby harbour</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. According to the Acts and Ordnances of the Interregnum 1642 - 1657 he was a commissioner for Devonshire for December 1649, December 1652 and June 1657.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Hunkin_(Governor_of_Scilly)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1630 at Boyton, he married Frances Loveys (d.1657),<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> a daughter of Robert Loveys of Beardon in Cornwall. Her mural monument, a simple slate tablet, survives on the external wall of Old Town Church, <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St Mary&#39;s, Isles of Scilly">St Mary\'s, Isles of Scilly</a>, inscribed as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><br />\n</p>\n<dl><dd><i>"To the memory of Francys, the wife of Joseph Hunkyn of Gatherly in the parish of Lifton in Devon, Governour of the Iland of Silly. She was the daughter of Robert Lovyes of Beardon in the parish of Boydon in Cornwall Esqu. She dyed the 30 day of March 1657 about the 46 (?) yeer of her age. Blest Soul thy race is run whilst we behind Strive for that crown WCH thou prepar\'d didst find By Christ for thee, here shall thy body rest Till with thy soul it be for ever blest"</i>.</dd></dl>\n<h2><span id="Death_.26_burial"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_&amp;_burial">Death &amp; burial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Hunkin_(Governor_of_Scilly)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death &amp; burial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>He was buried on 15 August 1661 at Lifton in Devon.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> A later prominent member of the Hunkin family was <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Hunkin_(Bishop_of_Truro)" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Hunkin (Bishop of Truro)">Joseph Hunkin</a> (1887-1950), born in <a href="/wiki/Truro" title="Truro">Truro</a>, Cornwall, the 8th <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Truro" title="Bishop of Truro">Bishop of Truro</a> from 1935 to 1950. His monument in <a href="/wiki/Truro_Cathedral" title="Truro Cathedral">Truro Cathedral</a> displays the arms of Hunkin of Gatherleigh, namely: <i>Argent, a mascle sable over all a fess of the last</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Hunkin_(Governor_of_Scilly)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">\n<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Vivian, Lt.Col.
