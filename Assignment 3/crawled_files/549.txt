template">e</abbr></a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Northern Ireland Executive</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Devolution" title="Devolution">devolved</a> government of Northern Ireland, an administrative branch of the <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislature</a> <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a>. It is answerable to the Assembly and was established according to the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Act_1998" title="Northern Ireland Act 1998">Northern Ireland Act 1998</a>, which followed the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a> (or Belfast Agreement). The <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">executive</a> is referred to in the legislation as the <b>Executive Committee</b> of the Assembly and is an example of a <a href="/wiki/Consociationalist_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Consociationalist government">consociationalist government</a>.\n</p><p>The Northern Ireland Executive consists of the <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="First Minister and deputy First Minister">First Minister and deputy First Minister</a> and various ministers with individual portfolios and remits. The main Assembly parties appoint most ministers in the executive, except for the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Justice_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Department of Justice (Northern Ireland)">Minister of Justice</a> who is elected by a <a href="/wiki/Cross-community_vote" title="Cross-community vote">cross-community vote</a>. It is one of three devolved governments in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the others being the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Government" title="Welsh Government">Welsh Governments</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_Executive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" width="50" height="39" class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span>  <small><i>(December 2013)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>In contrast with <a href="/wiki/Westminster_system" title="Westminster system">Westminster system</a> cabinets, which generally need only be backed by a <a href="/wiki/Majority" title="Majority">majority</a> of legislators, ministerial positions in the Northern Ireland Executive are allocated to parties with significant representation in the Assembly.  With the exception of justice, the number of ministries to which each party is entitled is determined by the <a href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_system" class="mw-redirect" title="D&#39;Hondt system">D\'Hondt system</a>.\n</p><p>In effect, major parties cannot be excluded from participation in government and power-sharing is enforced by the system. The form of government is therefore known as <i>mandatory coalition</i> as opposed to <i>voluntary coalition</i> where parties negotiate an agreement to share power. The <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a> (DUP), the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a> (UUP), the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party of Northern Ireland</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">Social Democratic and Labour Party</a> (SDLP) members favour a move towards voluntary coalition in the longer term but this is currently opposed by <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a>.\n</p><p>The Executive cannot function if either of the two largest parties refuse to take part, as these parties are allocated the First Minister and deputy First Minister positions.  However, other parties are not required to enter the Executive even if they are entitled to do so; instead, they can choose to go into <a href="/wiki/Opposition_(parliamentary)" title="Opposition (parliamentary)">opposition</a> if they wish. There were some calls for the SDLP and the UUP to enter opposition after the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election,_2007" title="Northern Ireland Assembly election, 2007">2007 Assembly elections</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-uupruleout_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uupruleout-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but ultimately the two parties chose to take the seats in the Executive to which they were entitled.\n</p><p>In 2010, an exception to the D\'Hondt system for allocating the number of ministerial portfolios was made under the <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Castle_Agreement" title="Hillsborough Castle Agreement">Hillsborough Castle Agreement</a> to allow the cross-community <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party of Northern Ireland</a> to hold the politically contentious policing and justice brief when most of those powers were devolved to the Assembly.  Devolution took place on 12 April 2010.\n</p><p>Under D\'Hondt, the SDLP would have been entitled to the extra ministerial seat on the revised Executive created by the devolution of policing and justice. Accordingly, both the UUP and SDLP protested that Alliance was not entitled, under the rules of the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a>, to fill the portfolio and refused to support this move. However, Alliance leader <a href="/wiki/David_Ford" title="David Ford">David Ford</a> was elected Minister with the support of the DUP and Sinn F\xc3\xa9in.\n</p><p>On 26 August 2015, the UUP announced it would withdraw from the Executive and form an opposition after all, in response to the assassination of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_McGuigan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kevin McGuigan (page does not exist)">Kevin McGuigan</a>.\n</p><p>On 25 May 2016 a new executive was announced (three weeks after assembly election). For the first time in the assembly\'s history, parties that were entitled to ministries (i.e. UUP, SDLP and Alliance) chose instead to go into opposition following a recent bill providing parties with this choice. This meant that the executive was formed only by the two major parties, the DUP and Sinn F\xc3\xa9in, and thus giving them more seats in the Executive (with the exception of the Department of Justice which was given to an <a href="/wiki/Independent_Unionist" title="Independent Unionist">Independent Unionist</a> MLA, <a href="/wiki/Claire_Sugden" title="Claire Sugden">Claire Sugden</a>, due to this appointment needing cross-community support).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Procedure">Procedure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_Executive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Procedure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg/220px-Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg" width="220" height="174" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg/330px-Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg/440px-Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="505" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stormont_Castle_-_geograph.org.uk_-_964434.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Stormont_Castle" title="Stormont Castle">Stormont Castle</a>, seat of the Executive</div></div></div>\n<p>The Executive is co-chaired by the First Minister and deputy First Minister. Its official functions are:\n</p>\n<ul><li>acting as a forum for the discussion of, and agreement on, issues which cut across the responsibilities of two or more ministers;</li>\n<li>prioritising executive and legislative proposals;</li>\n<li>discussing and agreeing upon significant or controversial matters; and</li>\n<li>recommending a common position where necessary (e.g. in dealing with external relationships).<sup id="cite_ref-legislation.gov.uk_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-legislation.gov.uk-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>Executive meetings are normally held fortnightly, compared to weekly meetings of the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Cabinet of the United Kingdom">British Cabinet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Irish Government</a>. Under the Executive\'s Ministerial Code, ministers are obliged to:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<ul><li>operate within the framework of the Programme for Government;</li>\n<li>support all decisions of the Executive and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a>; and</li>\n<li>participate fully in the Executive, the <a href="/wiki/North/South_Ministerial_Council" title="North/South Ministerial Council">North/South Ministerial Council</a> and the <a href="/wiki/British-Irish_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="British-Irish Council">British-Irish Council</a>.</li></ul>\n<p>The Ministerial Code allows any three ministers to request a cross-community vote. The quorum for voting is seven ministers. At present, the Executive consists of six unionist, five nationalist and two \'other\' (Alliance Party) ministers.\n</p>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg/220px-Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg/330px-Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg/440px-Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg 2x" data-file-width="623" data-file-height="384" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dundonald_House_-_3724956_f8d69b20.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Dundonald_House" title="Dundonald House">Dundonald House</a>, home to various government agencies</div></div></div>\n<p>The current system of devolution has succeeded long periods of direct rule (1974&#8211;1999 and 2002&#8211;2007), when the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Civil_Service" title="Northern Ireland Civil Service">Northern Ireland Civil Service</a> had a considerable influence on government policy. The legislation which established new departments in 1999 affirmed that "the functions of a department shall at all times be exercised subject to the direction and control of the Minister".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Ministerial powers can be conferred by an Act of the Assembly<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and ministers can also exercise executive powers which are vested in the <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">Crown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ministers are also subject to several limitations, including the <a href="/wiki/European_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="European Convention on Human Rights">European Convention on Human Rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_Union_law" title="European Union law">European Union law</a>, other international obligations of the UK,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> a requirement not to discriminate on religious or political grounds,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and having no power over <a href="/wiki/Reserved_and_excepted_matters" title="Reserved and excepted matters">reserved and excepted matters</a> (which are held by the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Government">United Kingdom Government</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ministerial decisions can be challenged by a petition of 30 <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly" title="Northern Ireland Assembly">Northern Ireland Assembly</a> members.  This action can be taken for alleged breaches of the Ministerial Code and on "matters of public importance". The Speaker of the Assembly must consult political party leaders in the Assembly (who are often also ministers) before deciding whether the subject is a matter of public importance. Successful petitions will then be considered by the Executive.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The number of ministers and their responsibilities can be changed when a department is being established or dissolved. The proposal must be made by the First Minister and the deputy First Minister and be carried by a cross-community vote in the Assembly. The number of departments was initially limited to 10 but this increased to 11 upon the devolution of justice.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ministers are disqualified from holding office if appointed to the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Government of Ireland</a> or as the chairman or deputy chairman of an <a href="/wiki/Oireachtas" title="Oireachtas">Oireachtas</a> (Irish Parliament) committee.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a It comprised a voluntary coalition between the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_and_Labour_Party" title="Social Democratic and Labour Party">Social Democratic and Labour Party</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party of Northern Ireland</a>, with the UUP\'s Brian Faulkner in the position of Chief Executive. Designates for <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_and_Deputy_First_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="First Minister and Deputy First Minister">First Minister and Deputy First Minister</a> were appointed on 1 July 1998 by the UUP and SDLP, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> A full Executive was nominated on 29 November 1999 and took office on 2 December 1999, comprising the UUP, SDLP, <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">Democratic Unionist Party</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>\nDevolution was suspended for four periods, during which the departments came under the responsibility of <a href="/wiki/Direct_rule_over_Northern_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct rule over Northern Ireland">direct rule</a> ministers from the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Office" title="Northern Ireland Office">Northern Ireland Office</a>:\n</p>\n<ul><li>between 12 February 2000<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and 30 May 2000;<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>on 11 August 2001;<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>on 22 September 2001;<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>between 15 October 2002<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and 8 May 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<p>The 2002\xe2\x80\x932007 suspension followed the refusal of the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a> to share power with <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a> after a high-profile <a href="/wiki/Police_Service_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Police Service of Northern Ireland">Police Service of Northern Ireland</a> investigation into an alleged <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Irish_Republican_Army" title="Provisional Irish Republican Army">Provisional Irish Republican Army</a> spy ring.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2007.E2.80.932011"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2007\xe2\x80\x932011">2007&#8211;2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_Executive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2007\xe2\x80\x932011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Since 8 May 2007, devolution had operated without interruption. The second Executive formed in 2007 was led by the DUP and Sinn F\xc3\xa9in, with the UUP and SDLP also securing ministerial roles.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>However, the Executive did not meet between 19 June 2008 and 20 November 2008 due to a boycott by <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a>. This took place during a dispute between the DUP and Sinn F\xc3\xa9in over the devolution of policing and justice powers.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Policing and justice powers were devolved on 12 April 2010, with the new <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Justice_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Department of Justice (Northern Ireland)">Minister of Justice</a> won by <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance</a> in a cross-community vote.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932016"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011\xe2\x80\x932016">2011\xe2\x80\x932016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_Executive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2011\xe2\x80\x932016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election,_2011" title="Northern Ireland Assembly election, 2011">Northern Ireland Assembly election</a> held on 5 May 2011, a third Executive was formed on 16 May 2011 with the same five parties represented.  Alliance for the first time gained administration of a department under the D\'Hondt system, in addition to the Department of Justice.\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peter_Robinson_(Northern_Ireland_politician)" title="Peter Robinson (Northern Ireland politician)">Peter Robinson</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">DUP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_McGuinness" title="Martin McGuinness">Martin McGuinness</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn F\xc3\xa9in">Sinn F\xc3\xa9in</a> were nominated by their parties and appointed as <a href="/wiki/First_Minister_and_deputy_First_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="First Minister and deputy First Minister">First Minister and deputy First Minister</a> on 12 May 2011. <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Unionist_Voice" title="Traditional Unionist Voice">Traditional Unionist Voice</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Jim_Allister" title="Jim Allister">Jim Allister</a> opposed the joint appointment.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On 16 May 2011, 10 other Executive ministers (with the exception of the Minister of Justice) and two junior ministers were appointed by their political parties. The Minister of Justice was then elected by the Assembly via a cross-community vote.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On 26 August 2015, the UUP withdrew from the Executive in protest over the alleged involvement of members of the Provisional IRA in the murder of Kevin McGuigan Sr.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Danny Kennedy MLA\'s position as Minister for Regional Development was later taken over by the DUP, thereby leaving four Northern Irish parties in the power sharing agreement. On 10 September 2015 Peter Robinson stepped down as First Minister, although he did not officially resign. Arlene Foster took over as acting First Minister.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Robinson subsequently resumed his duties as First Minister again on 20 October 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Following the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Fresh_Start_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Fresh Start Agreement">Fresh Start Agreement</a>, Peter Robinson announced his intention to stand down as leader of the DUP and First Minister of Northern Ireland. He subsequently resigned as DUP leader on 18 December 2015, being succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Arlene_Foster" title="Arlene Foster">Arlene Foster</a>. Foster then took office as First Minister on 11 January 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932017"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016\xe2\x80\x932017">2016\xe2\x80\x932017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Ireland_Executive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2016\xe2\x80\x932017">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The fourth Executive was formed following the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election,_2016" title="Northern Ireland Assembly election, 2016">May 2016 election</a>. The <a href="/wiki/SDLP" class="mw-redirect" title="SDLP">SDLP</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">UUP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Party_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Alliance Party of Northern Ireland">Alliance Party</a> left the Executive and formed the Official Opposition for the first time. Ministerial positions were proportionally allocated between the DUP and Sinn F\xc3\xa9in, with independent unionist <a href="/wiki/Claire_Sugden" title="Claire Sugden">Claire Sugden</a> serving as Minister of Justice. The government collapsed on 16 January 2017, after <a href="/wiki/Martin_McGuinness" title="Martin McGuinness">Martin McGuinness</a> resigned in protest over the <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Heat_Incentive_scandal" title="Renewable Heat Incentive scandal">Renewable Heat Incentive scandal</a>. His resignation sparked a <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Assembly_election,_2017" title="Northern Ireland Assembly election, 2017">snap election</a> as Sinn F\xc3\xa9in refused to re-nominate a deputy First Minister.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Committee">Executive Committee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
