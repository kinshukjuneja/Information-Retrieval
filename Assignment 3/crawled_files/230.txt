lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <b>local enterprise partnerships</b> (<b>LEPs</b>) are voluntary partnerships between <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_England" title="Local government in England">local authorities</a> and businesses set up in 2011 by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Business,_Innovation_and_Skills" title="Department for Business, Innovation and Skills">Department for Business, Innovation and Skills</a> to help determine local economic priorities and lead economic growth and job creation within the local area. They carry out some of the functions previously carried out by the <a href="/wiki/Regional_development_agency" title="Regional development agency">regional development agencies</a> which were abolished in March 2012. In March 2017 Northamptonshire LEP merged with <a href="/wiki/South_East_Midlands_Local_Enterprise_Partnership" title="South East Midlands Local Enterprise Partnership">South East Midlands LEP</a> under the name and aegis of the latter, so that to date there are now 38 local enterprise partnerships in operation. Further mergers may take place in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_enterprise_partnership&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The abolition of <a href="/wiki/Regional_development_agency" title="Regional development agency">regional development agencies</a> and the creation of local enterprise partnerships were announced as part of the <a href="/wiki/June_2010_United_Kingdom_budget" title="June 2010 United Kingdom budget">June 2010 United Kingdom budget</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Budget_June_2010_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Budget_June_2010-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On 29 June 2010 a letter was sent from the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for Communities and Local Government">Department for Communities and Local Government</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Business,_Innovation_and_Skills" title="Department for Business, Innovation and Skills">Department for Business, Innovation and Skills</a> to local authority and business leaders, inviting proposals to replace regional development agencies in their areas by 6 September 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-DCLG_1626854_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DCLG_1626854-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On 7 September 2010, details were released of 56 proposals for local enterprise partnerships that had been received.<sup id="cite_ref-NDS_415344_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NDS_415344-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 6 October 2010, during the Conservative Party Conference, it was revealed that 22 had been given the provisional \'green light\' to proceed and others might later be accepted with amendments.<sup id="cite_ref-LGC_5020212_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LGC_5020212-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 24 bids were announced as successful on 28 October 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-sub-national_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sub-national-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LGC_5018558_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LGC_5018558-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p><p>LEPs were set up on a volunteer basis without any public funding and struggled to make progress.  A report by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Heseltine" title="Michael Heseltine">Michael Heseltine</a> in October 2012, <i>No Stone Unturned</i>, was largely accepted by Government, and proposed delegating funds from central government to LEPs, including:\n</p>\n<ul><li>a share of a \xc2\xa31,400m Local Growth Fund to generate growth, allocated through competitive bidding;</li>\n<li>getting LEPs to draw up plans for local growth as the basis for negotiation on the money in the Fund</li>\n<li>realigning the management of the EU Structural and Investment Funds in England to follow the plans made by LEPs.</li></ul>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_deals">City deals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a areas of Greater Birmingham and Solihull, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Region" title="Leeds City Region">Leeds City Region</a>, North Eastern, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_City_Region" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield City Region">Sheffield City Region</a>, and West of England were included in the first wave of \'city deals\' in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_LEPs">List of LEPs</span><span
