href="/wiki/Independent_baseball_league" title="Independent baseball league">independent baseball league</a> located primarily in the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid-Atlantic states">Mid-Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">Northeastern</a> United States, especially the greater metropolitan areas of the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_megalopolis" title="Northeast megalopolis">Northeast megalopolis</a>, with one team located in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>.  League offices are located in <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Pennsylvania" title="Lancaster, Pennsylvania">Lancaster, Pennsylvania</a>.\n</p><p>The Atlantic League operates in cities not served by <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major</a> or <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League Baseball</a> teams and is not affiliated with either; most of its teams are within <a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburbs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Exurb" class="mw-redirect" title="Exurb">exurbs</a> too close to other teams in the organized baseball system to have minor league franchises of their own.  The Atlantic League requires cities to have the market for a 4,000 to 7,500-seat ballpark and for the facility to be maintained at or above AAA standards.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When Atlantic League professionals are signed by MLB clubs, they usually start in their Double-A or Triple-A affiliates.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The league uses a <a href="/wiki/Pitch_clock" title="Pitch clock">pitch clock</a> of 12 seconds and a maximum time between innings of two minutes, five seconds in an effort to speed up the game.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Atlantic_League_of_Professional_Baseball&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In 1998, the Atlantic League of Professional Baseball played its inaugural season, with teams in <a href="/wiki/Bridgewater,_New_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Bridgewater, New Jersey">Bridgewater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">Atlantic City, New Jersey</a>; <a href="/wiki/Nashua,_New_Hampshire" title="Nashua, New Hampshire">Nashua, New Hampshire</a>; <a href="/wiki/Newburgh,_New_York" title="Newburgh, New York">Newburgh, New York</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport, Connecticut</a>. The creation of the league was the result of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>\' objection to <a href="/wiki/Frank_Boulton" title="Frank Boulton">Frank Boulton</a>\'s proposal to move the former <a href="/wiki/Albany_A%27s" title="Albany A&#39;s">Albany-Colonie Yankees</a> because of its territorial rights to the region. Boulton, a Long Island native, decided to create a new league that would have a higher salary cap for its players and a longer season than most of the other independent baseball organizations. He modeled the Atlantic League after the older <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a>, with facilities that exceed <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">AAA-level</a> standards. Boulton also emphasized signing players of Major League Baseball experience for all Atlantic League teams, raising the level of play above other independent leagues.\n</p><p>In 2010, the league announced that it would be expanding to <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Land,_Texas" title="Sugar Land, Texas">Sugar Land, Texas</a> and adding its first franchise not located in an <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">Atlantic coast</a> state.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Land_Skeeters" title="Sugar Land Skeeters">Sugar Land Skeeters</a> began play in 2012. In 2010, amid financial struggles, the <a href="/wiki/Newark_Bears" title="Newark Bears">Newark Bears</a> moved from the Atlantic League to the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_American_Association_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Canadian American Association of Professional Baseball">Can-Am League</a>, leaving the <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport_Bluefish" title="Bridgeport Bluefish">Bridgeport Bluefish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Somerset_Patriots" title="Somerset Patriots">Somerset Patriots</a> as the only teams remaining from the league\'s inaugural season.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the summer of 2013, then-ALPB President Frank Boulton announced that he would be resigning so that he could devote more time to operating the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Ducks" title="Long Island Ducks">Long Island Ducks</a>. He was replaced by longtime high-ranking Major League Baseball executive Rick White.<sup id="cite_ref-white_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-white-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On July 8, 2015, the Atlantic League began using <a href="/wiki/Rawlings_(company)" title="Rawlings (company)">Rawlings</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseballs" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseballs">baseballs</a> with red and blue seams, virtually unused in the sport since the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> swapped the blue in their seams for red in 1934.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>On September 1, 2015, the Atlantic League announced conditional approval for an expansion team or a relocated team to play in <a href="/wiki/New_Britain,_Connecticut" title="New Britain, Connecticut">New Britain, Connecticut</a> for the 2016 season.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On October 21, 2015, the Camden Riversharks announced they would cease operations immediately due to the inability to reach an agreement on lease terms with the owner of Campbell\'s Field, the Camden County Improvement Authority.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The team was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/New_Britain_Bees" title="New Britain Bees">New Britain Bees</a> for the 2016 season.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On May 29, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Jennie_Finch" title="Jennie Finch">Jennie Finch</a> was the guest manager for the league\'s Bridgeport Bluefish, thus becoming the first woman to manage a professional baseball team.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Shortly before the conclusion of the 2017 season, the city of <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">Bridgeport, Connecticut</a> voted to not continue with professional baseball in the city and announced plans to convert <a href="/wiki/The_Ballpark_at_Harbor_Yard" title="The Ballpark at Harbor Yard">The Ballpark at Harbor Yard</a> into a music amphitheater; the <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport_Bluefish" title="Bridgeport Bluefish">Bridgeport Bluefish</a> announced plans to relocate to <a href="/wiki/High_Point,_North_Carolina" title="High Point, North Carolina">High Point, North Carolina</a> in 2019 when the construction of a new multipurpose facility in High Point is completed.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> League officials announced the return of the <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Road_Warriors" title="Pennsylvania Road Warriors">Pennsylvania Road Warriors</a>, an all road game team, to keep the league at an even eight teams while the Bluefish go inactive for the 2018 season.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The Atlantic League is generally regarded as the most successful and highest level of baseball among independent leagues.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Two former Atlantic League players have been inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Baseball Hall of Fame</a>: <a href="/wiki/Tim_Raines" title="Tim Raines">Tim Raines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rickey_Henderson" title="Rickey Henderson">Rickey Henderson</a>. Other notable former and future Major League ballplayers who have played in the league include <a href="/wiki/Roger_Clemens" title="Roger Clemens">Roger Clemens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Kazmir" title="Scott Kazmir">Scott Kazmir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dontrelle_Willis" title="Dontrelle Willis">Dontrelle Willis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Gonz%C3%A1lez_(baseball)" title="Juan Gonz\xc3\xa1lez (baseball)">Juan Gonz\xc3\xa1lez</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Rocker" title="John Rocker">John Rocker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Canseco" class="mw-redirect" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Canseco">Jos\xc3\xa9 Canseco</a>, and several others have coached or managed, including <a href="/wiki/Gary_Carter" title="Gary Carter">Gary Carter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_John" title="Tommy John">Tommy John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bud_Harrelson" title="Bud Harrelson">Bud Harrelson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Gaetti" title="Gary Gaetti">Gary Gaetti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sparky_Lyle" title="Sparky Lyle">Sparky Lyle</a>. The Atlantic League has consistently posted higher per game and per season attendance numbers than other independent circuits including the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_of_Independent_Professional_Baseball" title="American Association of Independent Professional Baseball">American Association</a>, <a href="/wiki/Can-Am_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Can-Am League">Can-Am League</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frontier_League" title="Frontier League">Frontier League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015, the Atlantic League experienced a watershed moment for independent baseball when it signed a formal agreement with Major League Baseball which put into writing the rules which the ALPB would follow in selling its players\' contracts to MLB clubs and their affiliates. This marked the first time that MLB, which has enjoyed a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Supreme Court">U.S. Supreme Court</a>-granted <a href="/wiki/Federal_Baseball_Club_v._National_League" title="Federal Baseball Club v. National League">antitrust exemption</a> since 1922, had made any formal agreement with or acknowledgment of an independent baseball league.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a
