id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>It is charged with the collection and publication of statistics related to the economy, population and society of the UK; responsibility for some areas of statistics in Scotland, Northern Ireland and Wales is devolved to the <a href="/wiki/Devolution_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Devolution in the United Kingdom">devolved governments</a> for those areas.  The ONS functions as the executive office of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistician" title="National Statistician">National Statistician</a>, who is also the UK Statistics Authority\'s Chief Executive and principal statistical adviser to the UK\'s National Statistics Institute,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the \'Head Office\' of the <a href="/wiki/Government_Statistical_Service" title="Government Statistical Service">Government Statistical Service</a> (GSS). Its main office is in Newport near the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Intellectual_Property_Office" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Intellectual Property Office">United Kingdom Intellectual Property Office</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tredegar_House" title="Tredegar House">Tredegar House</a>, but another significant office is in <a href="/wiki/Titchfield" title="Titchfield">Titchfield</a> in Hampshire, and a small office is in London. ONS co-ordinates data collection with the respective bodies in Northern Ireland and Scotland, namely <a href="/wiki/NISRA" class="mw-redirect" title="NISRA">NISRA</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Records_of_Scotland" title="National Records of Scotland">NRS</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ONS was formed on 1 April 1996 by the merger of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Statistical_Office,_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Statistical Office, UK">Central Statistical Office</a> (CSO) and the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Population_Censuses_and_Surveys" title="Office of Population Censuses and Surveys">Office of Population Censuses and Surveys</a> (OPCS).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Following the <a href="/wiki/Statistics_and_Registration_Service_Act_2007" title="Statistics and Registration Service Act 2007">Statistics and Registration Service Act 2007</a>, the United Kingdom Statistics Authority became a <a href="/wiki/Non-ministerial_department" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-ministerial department">non-ministerial department</a> on 1 April 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose_and_scope">Purpose and scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Purpose and scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>ONS produces and publishes a wide range of the information about the United Kingdom that can be used for social and economic policy-making as well as painting a portrait of the country as its population evolves over time. This is often produced in ways that make comparison with other societies and economies possible. Much of the data on which policy-makers depend is produced by ONS through a combination of a decennial population census, samples and surveys and analysis of data generated by businesses and organisations such as the National Health Service and the register of births, marriages and deaths. Its publications, and analyses by other users based on its published data, are reported and discussed daily in the media as the basis for the public understanding of the country in which they live.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications_of_data">Applications of data</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Applications of data">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The reliance on some of these data by government (both local and national) makes ONS material central to debates about the determination of priorities, the allocation of resources and for decisions on interest rates or borrowing. The complexity and degree and speed of change in the society, combined with the challenge of measuring some of these (e.g. in relation to longevity, migration or illness patterns or fine movements in inflation or other aspects of national accounts) give rise to periodic debates about some of its indicators and portrayals. Many of these rely on sources which are outside ONS, while some of its own sources need to be supplemented, for example between censuses, by updated but less rigorously obtained information from other sources. Consequently, unexpected or incomplete data or occasional errors or disputes about its analysis can also attract considerable attention. \n</p><p>ONS data can also be used in <a href="/wiki/Epidemiology" title="Epidemiology">epidemiologic</a> studies such as <a href="/wiki/Survival_analysis" title="Survival analysis">survival analysis</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a>, then Chancellor of the Exchequer, announced on 28 November 2005,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> that the government intended to publish plans in early 2006 to legislate to render the ONS and the statistics it generates independent of government on a model based on the independence of the Monetary Policy Committee of the Bank of England.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This was originally a 1997 Labour manifesto commitment<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and was also the policy of the Liberal Democrat<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and Conservative<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> parties. Such independence was also sought by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Statistical_Society" title="Royal Statistical Society">Royal Statistical Society</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Statistics_Commission" title="Statistics Commission">Statistics Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> \nThe <a href="/wiki/National_Statistician" title="National Statistician">National Statistician</a> would be directly accountable to Parliament through a more widely constituted independent governing Statistics Board.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The ONS would be a non-ministerial government department so that the staff, including the Director, would remain as civil servants but without being under direct ministerial control.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> \nThe then National Statistician, <a href="/wiki/Karen_Dunnell" title="Karen Dunnell">Dame Karen Dunnell</a>, stated that legislation would help improve public trust in official statistics<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> although the ONS already acts independently according to its own published guidelines, the National Statistics Code of Practice,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> which sets out the key principles and standards that official statisticians, including those in other parts of the government statistical service, are expected to follow and uphold.\n</p><p>The details of the plans for independence were considered in Parliament during the 2006/2007 session and resulted in the <a href="/wiki/Statistics_and_Registration_Service_Act_2007" title="Statistics and Registration Service Act 2007">Statistics and Registration Service Act 2007</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In July 2007, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Scholar" title="Michael Scholar">Sir Michael Scholar</a> was nominated by the government to be the three-day-a-week non-executive chairman of the Statistics Board which, with the intention of re-establishing faith in the integrity of government statistics, was to take on statutory responsibility for oversight of UK statistics in April 2008 and oversee the Office for National Statistics; also having a duty to assess all UK government statistics. Following Gordon Brown\'s announcement of new constitutional arrangements for public appointments, Sir Michael also became, on 18 July, the first such nominee to appear before the House of Commons Treasury Committee and to have his nomination subject to confirmation by the House.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 7 February 2008, following the first meeting of the shadow board, it was announced that it would be known as the <a href="/wiki/UK_Statistics_Authority" title="UK Statistics Authority">UK Statistics Authority</a> (UKSA).\n</p><p>In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Dilnot" title="Andrew Dilnot">Andrew Dilnot</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Michael_Scholar" title="Michael Scholar">Michael Scholar</a> as chairman of the Authority.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heads_of_the_Office_and_the_National_Statistician">Heads of the Office and the National Statistician</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Heads of the Office and the National Statistician">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Since its establishment, ONS has had five Directors: Professor <a href="/wiki/Tim_Holt_(statistician)" title="Tim Holt (statistician)">Tim Holt</a>; <a href="/wiki/Len_Cook" title="Len Cook">Len Cook</a>; <a href="/wiki/Karen_Dunnell" title="Karen Dunnell">Karen Dunnell</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jil_Matheson" title="Jil Matheson">Jil Matheson</a>; and, from October 2012, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Glen_Watson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Glen Watson (page does not exist)">Glen Watson</a>. Len Cook was the first Director to hold the newly created role of National Statistician.  The roles of Director of ONS and National Statistician were combined until 2012 when Jil Matheson continued as National Statistician while Glen Watson became Director of the ONS. <a href="/wiki/John_Pullinger" title="John Pullinger">John Pullinger</a> replaced Jil Matheson as National Statistician (and Chief Executive of the UK Statistics Authority) in July 2014.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_of_the_ONS">Work of the ONS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Work of the ONS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The work of the ONS covers the collection of data and the analysis and publication of statistics covering the economy, population, and society of the UK.  \n</p><p>Where data is broken down by geographical area, this is usually done by the areas defined in the <a href="/wiki/ONS_coding_system" title="ONS coding system">ONS geographical coding system</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_collection">Data collection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Data collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The principal areas of data collection include:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Agriculture and Environment</li>\n<li>Business and Energy</li>\n<li>Children, Education and Skills</li>\n<li>Crime and Justice</li>\n<li>Economy</li>\n<li>Government</li>\n<li>Health and Social Care</li>\n<li>Labour Market</li>\n<li>People and Places</li>\n<li>Population</li>\n<li>Travel and Transport</li></ul>\n<p>Statisticians are also employed by many other Government departments and agencies, and these statisticians often collect and publish data. They are members of the <a href="/wiki/Government_Statistical_Service" title="Government Statistical Service">Government Statistical Service</a> and are the professional responsibility of the head of the service, who is also the National Statistician. Each department has a statistical service <i>Head of Profession</i>. For example, data on Agriculture, Fishing and Forestry comes primarily from the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a>. Along with economic data on which the Treasury and Bank of England rely for decision-making, many of the statistics that receive widespread media attention are issued by the Home Office, the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Health_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Health (United Kingdom)">Department of Health</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Education_and_Skills_(United_Kingdom)" title="Department for Education and Skills (United Kingdom)">Department for Education and Skills</a>. ONS is also responsible for the maintenance of the <i>Inter-Departmental Business Register</i> and the <i>Business Structure Database</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_departments">Former departments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Former departments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Prior to the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Statistics_Authority" title="UK Statistics Authority">UK Statistics Authority</a>, the statistical work of ONS, since June 2000, was scrutinised by the <a href="/wiki/Statistics_Commission" title="Statistics Commission">Statistics Commission</a>, an independent body with its own chairman and small staff. This ceased to operate from 1 April 2008. The <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office" title="General Register Office">General Register Office</a> and the post of <a href="/wiki/Registrar-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Registrar-General">Registrar-General</a> for England &amp; Wales ceased to be part of ONS from that date but remains subject to ministerial accountability within the <a href="/wiki/Home_Office" title="Home Office">Home Office</a>.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Blue_Book">The Blue Book</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: The Blue Book">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_National_Accounts_-_The_Blue_Book" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom National Accounts - The Blue Book">United Kingdom National Accounts - The Blue Book</a></div>\n<p>The annual <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_National_Accounts_-_The_Blue_Book" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom National Accounts - The Blue Book">United Kingdom National Accounts</a> are published in an online publication (The Blue Book<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>) by the Office for National Statistics. It records and describes economic activity in the United Kingdom and as such is used by government, banks, academics and industries to formulate the economic and social policies and monitor the economic progress of the United Kingdom. It also allows international comparisons to be made. The Blue Book is published alongside the United Kingdom Balance of Payments \xe2\x80\x93 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pink_Book_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pink Book (journal) (page does not exist)">The Pink Book</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_of_statisticians">Education of statisticians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education of statisticians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Office for National Statistics collaborates with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southampton" title="University of Southampton">University of Southampton</a> in the teaching of an MSc in Official Statistics, the programme has been running since 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Virtual_Microdata_Laboratory">Virtual Microdata Laboratory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Virtual Microdata Laboratory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The Virtual Microdata Laboratory (VML) was established in 2004 to allow researchers access to business data.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It is a secure facility within the Office for National Statistics where both government officials and academic researchers can analyse sensitive, detailed data for statistical purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The researchers cannot download the data or take any copies out of the laboratory and the results of the analysis is checked for statistical disclosure.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_locations">Office locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Office locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The ONS has a head office in the city of <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>, South Wales, and other offices in <a href="/wiki/Pimlico" title="Pimlico">Pimlico</a> in London and <a href="/wiki/Titchfield" title="Titchfield">Titchfield</a> in Hampshire.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Family_Records_Centre" title="Family Records Centre">Family Records Centre</a> in Myddelton Street in <a href="/wiki/Islington" title="Islington">Islington</a>, London, moved to the National Archives in Kew in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> They also have an Archive Storage site located in <a href="/wiki/Christchurch,_Dorset" title="Christchurch, Dorset">Christchurch</a>, Dorset.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_headquarters">Former headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Former headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The London (Pimlico) office was the head office until April 2006 when the corporate headquarters was moved to Newport<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> following the Lyons Review<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> on public sector relocation. Since May 2011 the London office has been located on the 2nd floor of the former Drummond Gate headquarters and houses the methodology consultancy service, the virtual microdata laboratory and media briefings.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gradual_move_of_functions_to_South_Wales">Gradual move of functions to South Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Gradual move of functions to South Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The ONS asserted that recruitment and training of quality staff in South Wales, where data collection and analysis already took place, would ensure that there was no risk to the quality of its services and that it managed the risks associated with the changes.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> However the plan to discontinue statistical activity in London proved controversial amid claims that the shift of functions from London and the closure of the London office would have serious implications for the future of certain sets of statistics. These include health statistics, National Accounts, Retail and Consumer Prices and Labour Market Statistics. These risks were stated to derive from the fact that few of the experienced staff working in these highly technical areas were expected to relocate to Newport, resulting in a substantial loss of expertise and a consequent threat to the continued quality of the statistics.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In a submission to the Parliamentary Treasury Sub Committee, the Bank of England expressed concern over the relocation of the ONS to <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>, saying, that <i>"the relocation programme poses serious risks to the maintenance of the quality of macroeconomic data. If substantial numbers of ONS staff are unwilling to relocate, the loss of skilled individuals could have a severe impact on a range of statistics."</i><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The director of ONS at the time vigorously defended the implementation of government policy on civil service relocation and the decision to concentrate staff in the three locations outside London.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_of_the_ONS">Criticism of the ONS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Office_for_National_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Criticism of the ONS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Occasional errors and revisions account for some past criticism,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Many of the most controversial topics for statistics issued by government do not come from the ONS though they are expected to meet <i>National Statistics</i> standards.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague.
