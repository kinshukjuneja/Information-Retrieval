href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">state</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">country</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Blacks_1-0" id="Publication_and_organization">Publication and organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication and organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>In virtually all countries, newly enacted statutes are published in some kind of journal, gazette, or chronological compilation, which is then distributed so that everyone can look up the statutory law.\n</p><p>A universal problem encountered by lawmakers throughout human history is how to organize published statutes.  Such publications have a habit of starting small but growing rapidly over time, as new statutes are enacted in response to the exigencies of the moment.  Eventually, persons trying to find the law are forced to sort through an enormous number of statutes enacted at various points in time to determine which portions are still in effect.\n</p><p>The solution adopted in many countries is to organize existing statutory law in topical arrangements (or <a href="/wiki/Codification_(law)" title="Codification (law)">"codified"</a>) within publications called <a href="/wiki/Code_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Code (law)">codes</a>, then ensure that new statutes are consistently drafted so that they add, amend, repeal or move various code sections. In turn, in theory, the code will thenceforth reflect the current cumulative state of the statutory law in that jurisdiction.  In many nations <a href="/wiki/Statutory_law" title="Statutory law">statutory law</a> is distinguished from and subordinate to <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_law" title="Constitutional law">constitutional law</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_meanings">Alternative meanings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alternative meanings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_law">International law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The term <b>statute</b> is also used to refer to an International <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaty</a> that establishes an <a href="/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">institution</a>, such as the Statute of the <a href="/wiki/European_Central_Bank" title="European Central Bank">European Central Bank</a>, a protocol to the international courts as well, such as the Statute of the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a> and the Rome Statute of the <a href="/wiki/International_Criminal_Court" title="International Criminal Court">International Criminal Court</a>. Statute is also another word for law. The term was adapted from England in about the 18th century.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Autonomy_statute">Autonomy statute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Autonomy statute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Communities_of_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous Communities of Spain">Autonomous Communities of Spain</a>, the autonomy statute is a legal document similar to a state constitution in a <a href="/wiki/Federated_state" title="Federated state">federated state</a>. The autonomies statutes in Spain have the rank of "Ley Organica", a category of special laws reserved only for the main institutions and issues and mentioned in the Constitution (the highest ranking legal instrument in Spain). Leyes Organicas rank between the Constitution and ordinary laws. The name was chosen, among others, to avoid confusion with the term Constitution (i.e. the Spanish Constitution of 1978).\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_statutes">Religious statutes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religious statutes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biblical_terminology">Biblical terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Biblical terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In biblical terminology,  statute (Hebrew <i>chok</i>) refers to a law given without any reason or justification. The classic example is the statute regarding the <a href="/wiki/Red_Heifer" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Heifer">Red Heifer</a>.(Numbers 19:2)\n</p><p>The opposite of a chok is a <i>mishpat</i>, a law given for a specified reason, e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Shabbat" title="Shabbat">Sabbath</a> laws, which were given because "God created the world in six days, but on the seventh day He rested" (<a href="/wiki/Book_of_Genesis" title="Book of Genesis">Genesis</a> 2:2-3).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dharma">Dharma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Statute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Dharma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p><i>That which upholds, supports or maintains <a href="/wiki/Rta" class="mw-redirect" title="Rta">the regulatory order of the universe</a></i> meaning the <i>Law</i> or <i>Natural Law</i>. This is a concept of central importance in <a href="/wiki/Indian_philosophy" title="Indian philosophy">Indian philosophy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indian_religions" title="Indian religions">religion</a>.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a
