lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>The Stoke-on-Trent Built-up Area</b> or <b>The Potteries Urban Area</b> or <a href="/wiki/Colloquially" class="mw-redirect" title="Colloquially">colloquially</a>, simply "<b>The Potteries"</b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Staffordshire" title="North Staffordshire">North Staffordshire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_region" class="mw-redirect" title="West Midlands region">West Midlands region</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.\n</p><p>It includes the <a href="/wiki/Stoke-on-Trent" title="Stoke-on-Trent">City of Stoke-on-Trent</a>, and parts of the boroughs of <a href="/wiki/Newcastle-under-Lyme" title="Newcastle-under-Lyme">Newcastle-under-Lyme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kidsgrove" title="Kidsgrove">Kidsgrove</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire_Moorlands" title="Staffordshire Moorlands">Staffordshire Moorlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CIL%20Viability%20Assessment%20-%20February%202013.pdf_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CIL_Viability_Assessment_-_February_2013.pdf-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The area had a population of 372,101<sup id="cite_ref-BUA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in 2011, a small increase from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 census</a> figure of 362,403 with Stoke-on-Trent making up over 70% of this population. It is sometimes called the Potteries Urban Area due to the area\'s fame for pottery manufacture ever since the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial revolution">industrial revolution</a>.\n</p>\n<table class="wikitable">\n\n<tbody><tr>\n<th>Subdivision<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a
