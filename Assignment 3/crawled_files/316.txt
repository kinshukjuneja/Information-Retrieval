organisation which promotes coordinated conservation in the UK Overseas Territories and Crown Dependencies (UKOTs and CDs). It is a not-for-profit organisation supported by grants, donations and subscriptions, and a registered charity and company.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>UKOTCF is a non-profit organisation, constituted as a Charitable Company under the laws of England &amp; Wales.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The governing document is the Memorandum and Articles of Association. Under the Memorandum and Articles and subsidiary documents, UKOTCF is run by a Council of up to 12 persons elected by the member organisations. Council members are elected for 3-year, renewable terms, each year four places come up for election on rotation.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims">Aims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The UKOTs (also known as the <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">British Overseas Territories</a>) are internationally recognised for their exceptionally rich and varied natural environments. They contain an estimated 90% \nof the biodiversity found within the UK and the territories combined.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The UK and territory governments bear joint responsibility for conserving and protecting the rich natural heritage of the UKOTs, and work together to meet obligations under international environmental agreements.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> UKOTCF is the only organisation working solely on cross-territory UKOT environmental issues, providing assistance in the form of expertise, information and liaison between non-governmental organisations and governments, both in the UK and in the territories themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_organisations">Member organisations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Website">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The UKOTCF website and database contain extensive information on conservation priorities for the UKOTs, as well as funding sources, environmental education resources and specific information on projects carried out in the UKOTs. The database was incorporated in response to requests from the territories, designed so that information can be added and updated by partner organisations. The database modules help to track critical sites (and common issues across sites), conservation priorities, projects within the UKOTs/CDs, environmental education resources and other information on a wide variety of subjects. The database has been described as \' <i>a valuable resource for conservation practitioners in the Overseas Territories</i> \' by the Institute of Zoology.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In addition to an annual report, UKOTCF also publishes the <i>Forum News</i> newsletter several times a year.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> These can also be found on the UKOTCF website and provide the latest news relevant to the UKOTs.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2018, a new website was launched at <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.ukotcf.org.uk">www.ukotcf.org.uk</a><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Virtual_tours">Virtual tours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Virtual tours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The UKOTs are important parts of the UK, not foreign countries.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>  Although small in size, they support far more endemic taxa and other globally important biodiversity than does Britain and Ireland. \n<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> They are also important for their historical and cultural heritage, both in their own right, and their historical links for UK. However, there is little public awareness, either within UKOTs or in mainland UK, of the biodiversity and cultural importance of the UKOTs, and the challenges they face.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> UKOTCF raises awareness of the territories and the UK\'s responsibility to them through its \'Virtual Tours\' of the territories. The virtual tours draw attention to the biodiversity and cultural value, and highlight the challenges they face and the opportunities to protect and conserve their important features.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>UKOTCF has organised six international environment conferences since 1999, the most recent being held in Gibraltar in 2015, with support from HM Government of Gibraltar and the <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar_Ornithological_%26_Natural_History_Society" title="Gibraltar Ornithological &amp; Natural History Society">Gibraltar Ornithological &amp; Natural History Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> UKOTCF conferences bring together conservation professionals from across the UKOTs, CDs and beyond, providing a forum in which to share information and best practice, and forge stronger environmental links between UK Overseas Territories, Crown Dependencies and other small island nations.  The latest conference included involvement of environment ministers from several territories.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Proceedings of all UKOTCF-organised conferences are publicly available on the UKOTCF website.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<p><b>2015 - It is hoped that Overseas Territories, Crown Dependencies and other small island communities that share similar environmental problems benefit from learning about one another\xe2\x80\x99s history and experience of planning and conservation initiatives. The overall aim was to draw on similarities and differences in experience across the territories, to provide insights into common challenges, and leave participants better equipped to address local needs.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<p><b>2009 - Jersey</b>: <b>Biodiversity that Matters: a conference on conservation in UK Overseas Territories and other small island communities</b><sup id="cite_ref-States_of_Jersey_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-States_of_Jersey-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Organised by: UK Overseas Territories Conservation Forum, with the support of the Overseas Territories Environment Programme, and hosted \nby the Jersey conservation bodies.\n</p><p>This conference was designed to be of help in some of the priority issues identified by workers in small territories. Main sessions were:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Environmental education and the UKOTs</li>\n<li>Environment Charters and strategic planning</li>\n<li>Integration of conservation and sustainable livelihoods: terrestrial and marine, including fisheries</li>\n<li>Obtaining and Using Resources (not just money)</li>\n<li>Species conservation issues: dealing with alien invasive species and species recovery including captive breeding</li></ul>\n<p><b>2003 - Bermuda</b>: <b>A Sense of Direction: a conference on conservation in UK Overseas Territories and other small island communities</b><sup id="cite_ref-Tania_Theriault_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tania_Theriault-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Organised by Bermuda Ministry of Environment, Bermuda National Trust, Bermuda Zoological Society, Bermuda Audubon Society and UK Overseas Territories Conservation Forum.\n</p><p>The conference provided a forum for government environmental agencies and NGOs to discuss key conservation issues, to highlight success stories, exchange ideas, and to forge partnerships. It was planned that Overseas Territories, and other small island communities that share similar environmental problems, should benefit from Bermuda\xe2\x80\x99s experiences and history of planning and conservation initiatives.  Bermuda planned to learn from the success of environmental programmes tried and tested elsewhere.\n</p><p><b>2000 - Gibraltar</b>: <b>Calpe 2000: Linking the Fragments of Paradise: an international conference on environmental conservation in small territories</b><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Sponsored by the Government of Gibraltar, organised by the Gibraltar Ornithological &amp; Natural History Society, with the support of the UK Overseas Territories Conservation Forum.\n</p><p>This conference formed one of a new series sponsored by the Government of Gibraltar, under the series title "Calpe", which is the old Roman name for Gibraltar.  This particular conference addressed the very topical issue of environmental conservation. Its title reflects one of the first publications (<i>Fragments of Paradise: A Guide for Conservation Action in the U.K. Dependent Territories, 1987</i>) highlighting the immense biodiversity value of the UK Overseas Territories, and the need to provide for increased exchange of knowledge between them and other areas.\n</p><p><b>1999 - London</b>: <b>A Breath of Fresh Air: an international conference on environmental conservation in small territories</b><sup id="cite_ref-Anon_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Anon-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The conference formed part of the process of taking forward the issues highlighted in the environmental chapter of UK Government\'s White Paper of March 1999 \'Partnership for Progress and Prosperity \xe2\x80\x94 Britain and the Overseas Territories\'. It was organised by the Foreign &amp; Commonwealth Office\xe2\x80\x99s (FCO) Environment, Science &amp; Energy Department (ESED) with the support of the UK Overseas Territories Conservation Forum. The meeting had 3 main purposes:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Bring together the governmental, NGO and other organisations in the UK, the Overseas Territories (OTs) and elsewhere who are stakeholders in the OTs\xe2\x80\x99 environment, thus facilitating joint approaches to the challenging work needed to conserve these important natural assets and resources, for the people of the OTs themselves and for the wider world.</li>\n<li>Identify the key priority needs as seen by people working in the OTs.</li>\n<li>Further clarify ways for them to access the skills and commitment of the range of UK government departments and agencies (co-ordinated by the FCO) and NGOs (co-ordinated through UKOTCF).</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Volunteers">Volunteers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Volunteers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>As a non-profit organisation, UKOTCF draws largely on the efforts of highly skilled volunteers. Their specialisms include environmental education, invasive species, wetlands management, planning and development, ornithology, ecology, biodiversity strategies and climate change, to name a few.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> An important aspect of UKOTCF\'s work involves organising specialist volunteer support for UKOTs and deployment of volunteers to support capacity building in local NGOs.\n</p><p>Previous placements include:\n</p>\n<ul><li>Dhekelia Turtle Watch (Cyprus SBA)<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>St Helena National Trust</li>\n<li>Akrotiri Environmental Education Centre (Cyprus SBA)<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Turks &amp; Caicos National Trust<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup></li>\n<li>Cross-territory support</li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_activities">Other activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Overseas_Territories_Conservation_Forum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p><b>Working Groups</b>\n</p><p>UKOTCF runs Working Groups for the different UKOT regions, namely the Southern Oceans Working Group (SOWG),<sup id="cite_ref-Chagos_News42_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chagos_News42-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Europe Territories Working Group (ETWG)<sup id="cite_ref-IIEM_ETWG_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IIEM_ETWG-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> and Wider Caribbean Working Group (WCWG). Through e-Newsletters and regular Skype meetings, these provide a way for Territories to exchange expert knowledge and information, and a platform for determining common issues/requirements and coordinating activities.<sup id="cite_ref-Chagos_News42_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chagos_News42-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IIEM_ETWG_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IIEM_ETWG-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><b>Workshops</b>\n</p><p>UKOTCF held a workshop in Montserrat in May 2016. This was held in association with Montserrat\'s Department of the Environment and looked to gather information from a range of attendees as to what work could potentially be done in the south of the Island. This forms part of a wider project which is looking to guarantee the preservation of the Territory\'s endemic flora and fauna. One problem identified as being particularly important was the need to address the feral livestock issue in South Montserrat. Other issues that would need to be addressed to carry out work in the area were also discussed, e.g. access and safety, important considerations in light of the volcanic activity in the south.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In partnership with UKOTCF, the Montserrat National Trust and Montserrat Department of Environment held a workshop in January 2015 on <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Impact_Assessment" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental Impact Assessment">Environmental Impact Assessment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In January 2014, at the request of the House of Commons Environmental Audit Committee (EAC), UKOTCF organised a workshop to mark the publication of the EAC report <i>Sustainability in the UK Overseas Territories</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2010 and 2011, UKOTCF organised workshops on biodiversity strategies, trying to complement the UK Government biodiversity \'strategy\' for UKOTs. Following the publication of the UK Government\'s 2012 White Paper, UKOTCF analysed the White Paper and produced a review, which was later followed by a workshop titled \'Environmental conservation and UK Government\xe2\x80\x99s June 2012 White Paper The Overseas Territories: Security, Success and Sustainability\'.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><b>Britain\'s Treasure Islands</b>\n</p><p>UKOTCF Officers contributed towards naturalist <a href="/wiki/Stewart_McPherson_(geographer)" title="Stewart McPherson (geographer)">Stewart McPherson</a>\'s <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.britainstreasureislands.com/">Britain\'s Treasure Islands</a></i> series.<sup id="cite_ref-Treasure_Islands_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Treasure_Islands-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The aim of this project was to demonstrate the enormous variety in natural and cultural heritage of the UKOTs as well as elements of their histories.<sup id="cite_ref-Treasure_Islands_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Treasure_Islands-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> This culminated in the production of a natural history book, a series of 42 mini-documentaries, and a TV documentary series<sup id="cite_ref-Treasure_Islands_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Treasure_Islands-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> that was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> Four in April 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In association with Stewart McPherson, UKOTCF also helped to organise an event held on 24 March 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Geographical_Society" title="Royal Geographical Society">Royal Geographical Society</a>. During this evening event, Stewart provided an account of his travels to all of the UKOTs and across the incredible variety of ecosystems and biodiversity that they have to offer.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><b>YouTube videos</b>\n</p><p>UKOTCF have a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel which is used occasionally to add film of the UKOTs.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><b>Turks &amp; Caicos Islands Bird Tours</b>\n</p><p>UKOTCF in collaboration with the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_National_Museum" title="Turks and Caicos National Museum">Turks and Caicos National Museum</a> organised a \'Bird Week\' which ran from 11\xe2\x80\x9315 July 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> A Bird Walking Tour and a Bird Driving Tour on <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">Grand Turk</a> were both officially opened during this week, made possible thanks to a grant from the Carnival/TCInvest/TCIGovernment Infrastructure Fund.
