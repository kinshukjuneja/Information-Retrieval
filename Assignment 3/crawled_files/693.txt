href="http://nmc.org.uk/">nmc<wbr />.org<wbr />.uk</a></span></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<p>The <b>Nursing and Midwifery Council</b> (<b>NMC</b>) is the regulator for nursing and midwifery professions in the UK. The NMC maintains a register of all <a href="/wiki/Nurses" class="mw-redirect" title="Nurses">nurses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midwives" class="mw-redirect" title="Midwives">midwives</a> and specialist community <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> nurses eligible to practise within the UK. It sets and reviews standards for their education, training, conduct and performance. The NMC also investigates allegations of impaired fitness to practise (i.e. where these standards are not met).\n</p><p>It has been a statutory body since 2002, with a stated aim to protect the health and well-being of the public. The NMC is also a charity registered with the <a href="/wiki/Charity_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Charity Commission">Charity Commission</a>, charity number 1091434 and in Scotland with the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Scottish_Charity_Regulator" title="Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator">Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator</a>, charity number SC038362. All Council members are trustees of the charity.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UKCC">UKCC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: UKCC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In 1983, the <b>United Kingdom Central Council for Nursing, Midwifery and Health Visiting</b> (<b>UKCC</b>) was set up, replacing the General Nursing Council for England and Wales established by the <a href="/wiki/Nurses_Registration_Act_1919" title="Nurses Registration Act 1919">Nurses Registration Act 1919</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Midwives_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Midwives Board">Central Midwives Board</a> in London and seven other bodies.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The UKCC was expected to maintain a register of UK nurses, midwives and health visitors, provide guidance to registrants, and handle professional misconduct complaints. At the same time, National Boards were created for each of the UK countries. Their main functions were to monitor the quality of nursing and midwifery education courses, and to maintain the training records of students on these courses.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment_of_NMC">Establishment of NMC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Establishment of NMC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>This structure of the UKCC survived with minor modifications up to April 2002, when the UKCC ceased to exist and its functions were taken over by a new Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC). This was legislated for in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a>, through the Nursing and Midwifery Order 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The English National Board was also abolished and its quality assurance function was taken on board by the NMC. The other National Boards were also abolished, but new bodies were created in each country to take over their functions.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Council">Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The NMC Council has two key roles: setting the strategic direction for the NMC and overseeing the work of senior NMC staff.\n</p><p>The Council ensures that the NMC complies with all relevant legislation, including the Nursing and Midwifery Order 2001 and the Charities Act 1993.\n</p><p>The Council is made up of 12 lay and registrant members, including one member from each of the four UK countries. They are appointed by the Privy Council. Registrant members are from a nursing or midwifery background; lay members are selected for their expertise in various fields and strategic experience.\n</p><p>The current Council took office on 1 May 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The NMC\'s role is to:\n</p>\n<ul><li>protect the health and wellbeing of the public</li>\n<li>set standards of education, training, conduct and performance so that nurses and midwives can deliver high quality healthcare consistently throughout their careers</li>\n<li>ensure that nurses and midwives keep their skills and knowledge up to date and uphold the NMC\xe2\x80\x99s professional standards, and</li>\n<li>have clear and transparent processes to investigate nurses and midwives who fall short of the NMC\xe2\x80\x99s standards.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The NMC has an annual income in excess of \xc2\xa352&#160;million and employs over 400 staff.<sup id="cite_ref-CHRE_review2012_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CHRE_review2012-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> To fulfill the activities of the NMC it charges a registration fee to its members.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The NMC set standards, guidance and requirements for nursing and midwifery education across the UK. These standards help to shape the content and design of programmes and state what a registered nurse or midwife needs to know and be able to do.\n</p><p>The NMC approves higher education institutions to deliver programmes. They currently accredit 1000 programmes in 79 education institutions across the UK.\n</p><p>When students successfully complete their programme, their education institution will let the NMC know that they have met the education and practice standards and are of good health and good character. If they are deemed fit to practise they will then be eligible to apply to join the register. Each year the NMC receives over 22,000 newly qualified nurses and midwives from education institutions.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Registering_nurses_and_midwives">Registering nurses and midwives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Registering nurses and midwives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>As of  March 2017<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> there are 690,773 nurses and midwives on the NMC register,<sup id="cite_ref-NT_8Oct2015_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NT_8Oct2015-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> meaning the NMC is the body responsible for regulating the largest number of healthcare professionals in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-CHRE_review2012_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CHRE_review2012-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Nurses and midwives must be on the NMC register to practise in the UK. Anyone is able to search the NMC register.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Setting_standards">Setting standards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Setting standards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The NMC\'s revised code became effective on 31 March 2015. The code contains professional standards of practice and behaviour that all nurses and midwives must keep to.\nFour key sections describe what nurses and midwives are expected to do:\n\xe2\x80\xa2 prioritise people\n\xe2\x80\xa2 practise effectively \n\xe2\x80\xa2 preserve safety, and \n\xe2\x80\xa2 promote professionalism and trust.\n</p><p>The Code was updated to reflect changes in healthcare and society since its last publication in 2008. The updates include new requirements on the fundamentals of care, the duty of candour, raising concerns and social media use.\n</p><p>The introduction to the code states: \xe2\x80\x9cWhen joining our register, and then renewing their registration, nurses and midwives commit to upholding these standards. This commitment to professional standards is fundamental to being part of a profession. We can take action if registered nurses or midwives fail to uphold the Code. In serious cases, this can include removing them from the register. The Code should be useful for everyone who cares about good nursing and midwifery.\xe2\x80\x9d<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Revalidation">Revalidation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Revalidation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The NMC has committed to developing and implementing a system of revalidation.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In October 2015, the NMC introduced the new system which all nurses and midwives in the UK must go through to remain on the NMC register.<sup id="cite_ref-NT_8Oct2015_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NT_8Oct2015-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Nurses and midwives will need to renew their registration every three years and confirm that they continue to remain fit to practise by meeting the principles of the revised Code. They must demonstrate that they have completed the required hours of practice and learning activity through continuing professional development, have used feedback to review and improve the way they work, and have received confirmation from someone well placed to comment on their continuing fitness to practise.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The NMC ran a public consultation from January to March 2014 on how revalidation can be applied in practice.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Pilots involving over 2,000 nurses and midwives across the UK would inform the final plans.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Revalidation for nurses and midwives will start on 1 April 2016, with approximately 16,000 nurses and midwives going through the process initially. All nurses on the NMC register will eventually go through revalidation when their registration is up for renewal.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In January 2016, the NMC launched a microsite to assist nurses and midwives through the revalidation process.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Managing_complaints_about_nurses_and_midwives">Managing complaints about nurses and midwives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Managing complaints about nurses and midwives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The NMC handles complaints made about nurses and midwives, and investigates allegations where appropriate. The NMC has the power to restrict a nurse or midwife\'s practice or strike them off their register.\n</p><p>The NMC produced guidance for nurses and midwives on raising concerns which aims to help them take action in the public interest when needed. It includes information on legislation that offers protection to whistleblowers and information on organisations nurses and midwives can go to for further advice.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Helene Donnelly, ambassador for cultural change at Staffordshire and Stoke-on-Trent Partnership NHS Trust, spoke to NMC Council about raising concerns and gave her support to the guidance.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislation">Legislation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Legislation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes_to_healthcare_regulation">Changes to healthcare regulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Changes to healthcare regulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 2 April 2014, the Law Commission published its draft bill, Regulation of Health and Social Care Professionals. The bill aims "to aim to sweep away the out-dated and inflexible decision-making processes associated with the current legislation."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The bill would give healthcare regulators in the UK more autonomy.\n</p><p>The NMC\'s Chief Executive and Registrar Jackie Smith welcomed the publication of the draft bill, but stated how the NMC needs the bill to become law quickly if it is able to modernise and become an efficient regulator. In its current legislative framework, the NMC spends nearly 80% of its income of fitness to practise hearings.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Affecting_regulation_of_professionals_within_the_EU">Affecting regulation of professionals within the EU</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Affecting regulation of professionals within the EU">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The revised Mutual Recognition of Professional Qualifications (MRPQ) Directive, which came into effect 19 January 2016, will bring in a number of changes that aim to further facilitate the free movement of professionals within the EU, including amendments to language controls.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The changes will enable the NMC to ensure, on a case-by-case basis, that a nurse or midwife from the EU has sufficient knowledge and command of English to practise safely and effectively.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accusations_of_bullying_and_racism">Accusations of bullying and racism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Accusations of bullying and racism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 11 March 2008 two <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Members of Parliament</a> <a href="/wiki/Jim_Devine" title="Jim Devine">Jim Devine</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Smith_(Welsh_politician)" title="John Smith (Welsh politician)">John Smith</a> made accusations in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a> of bullying and racism within the NMC. The accusations have been denied by the NMC.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The government set up an independent inquiry asking the <a href="/wiki/Council_for_Healthcare_Regulatory_Excellence" class="mw-redirect" title="Council for Healthcare Regulatory Excellence">Council for Healthcare Regulatory Excellence</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Charity_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Charity Commission">Charity Commission</a> to investigate.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Panorama_controversy">Panorama controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Panorama controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>On 16 April 2009, nurse Margaret Haywood was barred from practising as a Nurse in the UK following a ruling by the NMC Conduct and Competence committee panel.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> This followed Haywood\'s contribution to a <a href="/wiki/Panorama_(TV_series)" title="Panorama (TV series)">BBC Panorama</a> television programme, exposing significant deficiencies in care at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Sussex_County_Hospital" title="Royal Sussex County Hospital">Royal Sussex County Hospital</a>, an acute <a href="/wiki/Teaching_hospital" title="Teaching hospital">teaching hospital</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">Brighton</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The public and media response was generally antagonistic towards the NMC, the response being described by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Nursing" title="Royal College of Nursing">Royal College of Nursing</a> as \'unduly harsh\'.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 12 October 2009, the striking off order was dropped, and replaced with a one-year caution.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulatory_criticism">Regulatory criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Regulatory criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In an early July 2012 report, the <a href="/wiki/Council_for_Healthcare_Regulatory_Excellence" class="mw-redirect" title="Council for Healthcare Regulatory Excellence">Council for Healthcare Regulatory Excellence</a> (CHRE), now known as the <a href="/wiki/Professional_Standards_Authority_for_Health_and_Social_Care" title="Professional Standards Authority for Health and Social Care">Professional Standards Authority for Health and Social Care</a> (PSA),<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> critically examined the leadership of the Nursing and Midwifery Council.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In late July 2012, a new chair for the NMC, Mark Addison, was appointed by the privy council, an appointment which was subject to criticism by the NMC Council, who described their "grave concern" with regard to what they claimed was a "lack of an open, transparent and equal opportunities process" in regards to the appointment.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In contrast, the CHRE stated that they were pleased with the appointment of a new Chair, and that they would "look forward to working with Mark Addison".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On 26 June 2015, the PSA reported in their performance review 2014\xe2\x80\x932015 report that the NMC had made overall improvements in their performance.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Data_loss">Data loss</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Data loss">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>In March 2013 the NMC was fined \xc2\xa3150,000 by the <a href="/wiki/Information_Commissioner%27s_Office" title="Information Commissioner&#39;s Office">Information Commissioner\'s Office</a> after it admitted that personal data, including "highly sensitive" information about nurses and about vulnerable children, had been placed on DVD without being <a href="/wiki/Encrypted" class="mw-redirect" title="Encrypted">encrypted</a>, sent by <a href="/wiki/Courier" title="Courier">courier</a> and then lost. The ICO said that "it should have been obvious ... that such a contravention would be of a kind likely to cause substantial distress to the data subjects due to the nature of the data involved".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Increase_in_membership_cost">Increase in membership cost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Increase in membership cost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The cost charged to remain registered on the NMC has increased from \xc2\xa376 per year in 2013, to \xc2\xa3100 in 2014. Since 2015 the registration fee is \xc2\xa3120.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The annual registration fee to the NMC is an allowable expense for UK income tax, however a survey by the NMC discovered that more than 75 percent of NMC registrants do not claim tax relief on their registration.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Morecambe_Bay">Morecambe Bay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Morecambe Bay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The council was criticised by Bill Kirkup for the way it handled the investigation resulting from events at <a href="/wiki/University_Hospitals_of_Morecambe_Bay_NHS_Foundation_Trust" title="University Hospitals of Morecambe Bay NHS Foundation Trust">University Hospitals of Morecambe Bay NHS Foundation Trust</a> relating to misconduct by two midwives involved in the <a href="/wiki/Furness_General_Hospital_scandal" title="Furness General Hospital scandal">Furness General Hospital scandal</a>.  He said their response to his report had "fallen far short of expectations". The <a href="/wiki/Professional_Standards_Authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional Standards Authority">Professional Standards Authority</a> said the regulator\'s investigation was "deficient" because evidence was not presented to the panel by the council, even though it had it in its possession.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>  In 2018 the authority reported that the council had ignored more than 20 concerns raised by Cumbria police in 2012 for two years and that its handling of complaints was \xe2\x80\x9cfrequently incompetent\xe2\x80\x9d. There was poor behaviour by staff towards families, reluctance to take concerns seriously and a lack of openness about mistakes it had made.  The chief executive, Jackie Smith, resigned.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulator">Regulator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nursing_and_Midwifery_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Regulator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>The <a href="/wiki/Professional_Standards_Authority_for_Health_and_Social_Care" title="Professional Standards Authority for Health and Social Care">Professional Standards Authority for Health and Social Care</a> (PSA), is an independent body accountable to the UK Parliament, which promotes the health and wellbeing of the public and oversees the nine UK healthcare regulators, including the Nursing and Midwifery Council.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015 it was suggested that the costs of regulating the Nursing and Midwifery Council would be paid for by the membership of the NMC i.e. nurses and midwives.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015 the PSA published the 2014 audit it had undertaken of the NMC which found that whilst it had improved, it was still failing in areas.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See
