He is a self-taught multi-instrumental musician who began his career in the 1980s, working notably with <a href="/wiki/The_Lotus_Eaters_(band)" title="The Lotus Eaters (band)">The Lotus Eaters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Lang_(singer)" title="Thomas Lang (singer)">Thomas Lang</a>, Gary Wall and <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Br%C3%BCcken" title="Claudia Br\xc3\xbccken">Claudia Br\xc3\xbccken</a>. Since the beginning of his career, he has collaborated numerous times with several directors, mainly <a href="/wiki/Vadim_Jean" title="Vadim Jean">Vadim Jean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boyle" title="Danny Boyle">Danny Boyle</a>. He has received praise through the years and some of his awards include the Silver Award (1st Prize) at the Cannes Film Festival, a British D&amp;AD Award, and a BMI Award.\n</p><p>Murphy gained recognition in the film industry while working with <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a> on his film <i><a href="/wiki/Lock,_Stock_and_Two_Smoking_Barrels" title="Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels">Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Mann_(director)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Mann (director)">Michael Mann</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Miami_Vice_(film)" title="Miami Vice (film)">Miami Vice</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Vaughn" title="Matthew Vaughn">Matthew Vaughn</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(film)" title="Kick-Ass (film)">Kick-Ass</a></i> and scoring various films by Danny Boyle. His instrumental tracks "<a href="/wiki/In_the_House_%E2%80%93_In_a_Heartbeat" class="mw-redirect" title="In the House \xe2\x80\x93 In a Heartbeat">In the House \xe2\x80\x93 In a Heartbeat</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/28_Days_Later" title="28 Days Later">28 Days Later</a></i> and "<a href="/wiki/Sunshine_(soundtrack)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunshine (soundtrack)">Adagio in D Minor</a>" from <a href="/wiki/Sunshine_(2007_film)" title="Sunshine (2007 film)"><i>Sunshine</i></a> have been featured in a variety of TV shows, commercials and <a href="/wiki/Film_trailers" class="mw-redirect" title="Film trailers">film trailers</a>.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Murphy_(composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, England, Murphy began composing music for films in the early 1990s and scored his first hit with <i><a href="/wiki/Leon_the_Pig_Farmer" title="Leon the Pig Farmer">Leon the Pig Farmer</a></i>. Together with former <a href="/wiki/Orchestral_Manoeuvres_in_the_Dark" title="Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark">OMD</a> member <a href="/wiki/David_Hughes_(musician)" title="David Hughes (musician)">David Hughes</a>, he worked on several successful British movies, enjoying particular success with the soundtrack to 1998\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Lock,_Stock_and_Two_Smoking_Barrels" title="Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels">Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels</a></i>.\n</p><p>Since 2000\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Snatch_(film)" title="Snatch (film)">Snatch</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Shooters_(2001_film)" title="Shooters (2001 film)">Shooters</a></i>, Murphy has been mainly based in Los Angeles. His successes include <i><a href="/wiki/City_by_the_Sea" title="City by the Sea">City by the Sea</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boyle" title="Danny Boyle">Danny Boyle</a>\'s box-office success <i><a href="/wiki/28_Days_Later" title="28 Days Later">28 Days Later</a></i> and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/28_Weeks_Later" title="28 Weeks Later">28 Weeks Later</a></i>. He also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Underworld_(band)" title="Underworld (band)">Underworld</a> to score Danny Boyle\'s science fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Sunshine_(2007_film)" title="Sunshine (2007 film)">Sunshine</a></i>. In 2006 Murphy composed the score for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Mann_(director)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Mann (director)">Michael Mann</a>\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Miami_Vice_(film)" title="Miami Vice (film)">Miami Vice</a></i>. In 2009, he composed the music for the <a href="/wiki/The_Last_House_on_the_Left_(2009_film)" title="The Last House on the Left (2009 film)">2009 remake</a> of the 1972 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_House_on_the_Left_(1972_film)" title="The Last House on the Left (1972 film)">The Last House on the Left</a></i>, followed by 2010\'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(film)" title="Kick-Ass (film)">Kick-Ass</a></i>, based on <a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(comic_book)" title="Kick-Ass (comic book)">the comic book of the same name</a>. Other excerpts from his scores have been released for advertising, in particular "<a href="/wiki/In_the_House_%E2%80%93_In_a_Heartbeat" class="mw-redirect" title="In the House \xe2\x80\x93 In a Heartbeat">In the House \xe2\x80\x93 In a Heartbeat</a>" from <i>28 Days Later</i>, and "Adagio in D minor" from <i>Sunshine</i>.\n</p><p>In 2014, he released an album <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anonymous_Rejected_Filmscore&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anonymous Rejected Filmscore (page does not exist)">Anonymous Rejected Filmscore</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-FMM:_ANO_review_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FMM:_ANO_review-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> that had been, as the title suggests, composed for a film whose studio head had rejected it.<sup id="cite_ref-Film_Score_Monthly:_ANO_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Film_Score_Monthly:_ANO-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FMM:_ANO_interview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FMM:_ANO_interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Murphy promised the director  that he wouldn\'t name the film,<sup id="cite_ref-FMM:_ANO_interview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FMM:_ANO_interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but described it as being 6 or 7 years previous, so around 2006\xe2\x80\x9307.<sup id="cite_ref-Film_Score_Monthly:_ANO_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Film_Score_Monthly:_ANO-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The score was allowed to develop in directions unconstrained by the original film\'s narrative<sup id="cite_ref-FMM:_ANO_interview_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FMM:_ANO_interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the cover art is a photograph of Murphy\'s son, taken by his wife through her sunglasses.<sup id="cite_ref-FMM:_ANO_interview_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FMM:_ANO_interview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span
