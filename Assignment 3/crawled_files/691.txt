The <a href="/wiki/Erinyes" title="Erinyes">erinyes</a> represent the guilt for murdering <a href="/wiki/Clytemnestra" title="Clytemnestra">his mother</a>.</div></div></div>\n<p>In <a href="/wiki/Criminal_law" title="Criminal law">criminal law</a>, <b>guilt</b> is the state of being responsible for the commission of an offense.<sup id="cite_ref-thefreedictionary.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefreedictionary.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Legal guilt is entirely externally defined by the <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">state</a>, or more generally a "court of law". Being "guilty" of a <a href="/wiki/Criminal_offense" class="mw-redirect" title="Criminal offense">criminal offense</a> means that one has committed a violation of <a href="/wiki/Criminal_law" title="Criminal law">criminal law</a>, or performed all the elements of the offense set out by a criminal <a href="/wiki/Statute" title="Statute">statute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The determination that one has committed that violation is made by an external body (a "court of law") and is, therefore, as definitive as the record-keeping of the body. So  the most basic definition is fundamentally circular: a person is guilty of violating a law, if a court says so.\n</p><p>Philosophically, guilt in criminal law is a reflection of a functioning society and its ability to condemn individuals\' actions. It rests fundamentally on a presumption of <a href="/wiki/Free_will" title="Free will">free will</a>, in which individuals choose actions and are, therefore, subjected to external judgement of the rightness or wrongness of those actions.\n</p>\n<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>An adjudication of guilt is more than a factual determination that the defendant pulled a trigger, took a bicycle, or sold heroin. It is a moral judgment that the individual is blameworthy. Our collective conscience does not allow punishment where it cannot impose blame. Our concept of blameworthiness rests on assumptions that are older than the Republic: man is naturally endowed with these two great faculties, understanding and liberty of will. Historically, our substantive criminal law is based on a theory of punishing the viscious&#32;&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>&#93; will. It postulates a free agent confronted with a choice between doing right and wrong, and choosing freely to do wrong.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p></blockquote>\n<p><i>See also</i> Cotton, Michael,  A FOOLISH CONSISTENCY: KEEPING DETERMINISM OUT OF THE CRIMINAL LAW, 15 B.U. Pub. Int. L.J. 1 ("A substantial body of scholarship has concerned itself with the importance of free will to the theory of the criminal law. Even given the importance of the subject, the quantity of attention is surprising because of the lack of fundamental disagreement among scholars, who overwhelmingly endorse the criminal law\'s assumption of free will.")\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc"><input type="checkbox" role="button" id="toctogglecheckbox" class="toctogglecheckbox" style="display:none" /><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctogglespan"><label class="toctogglelabel" for="toctogglecheckbox"></label></span></div>\n<ul>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a href="#Moral_and_legal_definitions"><span class="tocnumber">1</span> <span class="toctext">Moral and legal definitions</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a href="#Remedies"><span class="tocnumber">2</span> <span class="toctext">Remedies</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3"><a href="#See_also"><span class="tocnumber">3</span> <span class="toctext">See also</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-4"><a href="#References"><span class="tocnumber">4</span> <span class="toctext">References</span></a></li>\n<li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-5"><a href="#External_links"><span class="tocnumber">5</span> <span class="toctext">External links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Moral_and_legal_definitions">Moral and legal definitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guilt_(law)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Moral and legal definitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>"Guilt" is the obligation of a person who has violated a moral standard to bear the sanctions imposed by that moral standard. In legal terms, guilt means having been found to have violated a criminal law,<sup id="cite_ref-thefreedictionary.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thefreedictionary.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> though law also raises \'the issue of defences, pleas, the mitigation of offences, and the defeasibility of claims\'.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p><a href="/wiki/Les_Parrott" title="Les Parrott">Les Parrott</a> draws a three-fold distinction between "<i>objective</i> or <i>legal</i> guilt, which occurs when society\'s laws have been broken... <i>social</i> guilt...[over] an unwritten law of social expectation", and finally the way "<i>personal</i> guilt occurs when someone compromises one\'s own standards".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remedies">Remedies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guilt_(law)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Remedies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Guilt can sometimes be remedied by: <a href="/wiki/Punishment" title="Punishment">punishment</a> (a common action and advised or required in many <a href="/wiki/Legal_code" class="mw-redirect" title="Legal code">legal</a> and moral codes); <a href="/wiki/Forgiveness" title="Forgiveness">forgiveness</a> (as in <a href="/wiki/Transformative_justice" title="Transformative justice">transformative justice</a>); making amends (see <a href="/wiki/Reparation_(legal)" title="Reparation (legal)">reparation</a> or <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_reparation" title="Acts of reparation">acts of reparation</a>), or "restitution&#160;... an important step in finding freedom from real guilt\';<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> or by sincere <a href="/wiki/Remorse" title="Remorse">remorse</a> (as with <a href="/wiki/Confession_(religion)" title="Confession (religion)">confession</a> in <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholicism">Catholicism</a> or <a href="/wiki/Restorative_justice" title="Restorative justice">restorative justice</a>). Guilt can also be remedied through <a href="/wiki/Intellectualisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Intellectualisation">intellectualisation</a> or cognition <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> (the understanding that the source of the guilty feelings was illogical or irrelevant). Helping other people can also help relieve guilt feelings: "thus guilty people are often helpful people&#160;... helping, like receiving an external reward, seemed to get people feeling better".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> There are also the so-called "<a href="/wiki/Don_Juan" title="Don Juan">Don Juans</a> of achievement&#160;... who pay the installments due their superego not by suffering but by achievements.... Since no achievement succeeds in really undoing the unconscious guilt, these persons are compelled to run from one achievement to another".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Law does not usually accept the agent\'s <a href="/wiki/Self-punishment" class="mw-redirect" title="Self-punishment">self-punishment</a>, but some ancient codes did: in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, the accused could propose their own remedy, which could, in fact, be a reward, while the <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/accusation" class="extiw" title="wikt:accusation">accuser</a> proposed another, and the <a href="/wiki/Jury" title="Jury">jury</a> chose something in-between. This forced the accused to effectively bet on his support in the community, as <a href="/wiki/Socrates" title="Socrates">Socrates</a> did when he proposed "room and board in the town hall" as his fate. He lost and drank <a href="/wiki/Conium" title="Conium">hemlock</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poison</a>, as advised by his accuser.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guilt_(law)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<table role="presentation" class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">\n<tbody><tr>\n<td class="mbox-image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Wikiquote-logo.svg/34px-Wikiquote-logo.svg.png" width="34" height="40" class="noviewer" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Wikiquote-logo.svg/51px-Wikiquote-logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Wikiquote-logo.svg/68px-Wikiquote-logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="355" /></td>\n<td class="mbox-text plainlist">Wikiquote has quotations related to: <i><b><a href="https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/Guilt" class="extiw" title="q:Guilt">Guilt</a></b></i></td></tr></tbody></table>\n<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Culpability" title="Culpability">Culpability</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Erinyes" title="Erinyes">Erinyes</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Malum_in_se" title="Malum in se">Malum in se</a></li>\n<li><a href="/wiki/Malum_prohibitum" title="Malum prohibitum">Malum prohibitum</a></li></ul>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guilt_(law)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
