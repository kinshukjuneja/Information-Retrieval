links</span></a></li>\n</ul>\n</div>\n\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marjorie_Chibnall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Born to a farming family at <a href="/wiki/Atcham" title="Atcham">Atcham</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shropshire" title="Shropshire">Shropshire</a> in 1915, Chibnall was educated at Shrewsbury Priory County Girls School and <a href="/wiki/Lady_Margaret_Hall,_Oxford" title="Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford">Lady Margaret Hall, Oxford</a>, where she was taught by <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Jamison" title="Evelyn Jamison">Evelyn Jamison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Hunter_Galbraith" title="Vivian Hunter Galbraith">V. H. Galbraith</a> and <a href="/wiki/F._M._Powicke" title="F. M. Powicke">F. M. Powicke</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 1947, she married the biochemist and amateur medieval historian <a href="/wiki/Charles_Chibnall" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Chibnall">Charles Chibnall</a>, who died in 1988. Chibnall died in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> on 23 June 2012, at the age of 96,<sup id="cite_ref-Times_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was survived by their son and daughter, Mary and John, and by two step-daughters, Joan and Cicely.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scholarly_life">Scholarly life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marjorie_Chibnall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scholarly life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Marjorie Chibnall took her <a href="/wiki/BLitt" class="mw-redirect" title="BLitt">BLitt</a> at the University of Cambridge on the topic of ecclesiastical law, before moving to a study of the relations between the mighty <a href="/wiki/Bec_Abbey" title="Bec Abbey">Bec Abbey</a> in Normandy and its dependent English priories for her doctorate. She completed her doctorate in 1939 under the supervision of the renowned economic historian <a href="/wiki/Eileen_Power" title="Eileen Power">Eileen Power</a>. Her early career was spent teaching at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southampton" title="University of Southampton">University of Southampton</a> (1941\xe2\x80\x9343) and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Aberdeen" title="University of Aberdeen">University of Aberdeen</a> (1943\xe2\x80\x9347).\n</p><p>Chibnall was from 1947 a lecturer in history at <a href="/wiki/Girton_College,_Cambridge" title="Girton College, Cambridge">Girton College, Cambridge</a>, and from 1953 a <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">fellow</a> of the college, but she relinquished her positions there in 1965 in order to complete her editorial work on the <i>Historia Ecclesiastica</i> of <a href="/wiki/Orderic_Vitalis" title="Orderic Vitalis">Orderic Vitalis</a>. Four years later she was made a research fellow and subsequently a fellow of <a href="/wiki/Clare_Hall,_Cambridge" title="Clare Hall, Cambridge">Clare Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, and an <a href="/wiki/Honorary_fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="Honorary fellow">honorary fellow</a> of Girton College.\n</p><p>In a career spanning more than six decades, Marjorie Chibnall worked extensively on Anglo-Norman and Norman history. She encouraged much scholarship on these topics, as an active participant at the Battle Conferences on Anglo-Norman history and an editor of their proceedings. Chibnall\'s editions of the writings of Orderic Vitalis and of Atcham were acclaimed works, as was her biography of the <a href="/wiki/Empress_Matilda" title="Empress Matilda">Empress Matilda</a>. She continued to publish when she was well into her nineties. Her last book, a short account of the Normans, was published in 2000.\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marjorie_Chibnall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<p>Chibnall was elected a Fellow of the British Academy in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1979, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Birmingham" title="University of Birmingham">University of Birmingham</a> granted her an honorary doctorate.
